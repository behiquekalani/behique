3. **Event-to-online pipeline.** Randy meets customers face-to-face. Every event should feed his email list and social following. Right now those interactions end at the sale.
4. **AI automation for operations.** Randy is a one-person operation. Automating social posting, email sequences, and product descriptions would free him to focus on product creation and events.

## 2. PRODUCT STRATEGY

### Original Products Must Be the Priority

Randy currently has 2 original products out of 29. That is a 7% original product ratio. This needs to flip.

Resale items (Pokemon binders, San-X plush, Birduyen) serve a purpose: they fill the catalog, attract search traffic, and give customers reasons to add items to cart. But they cannot be the business. Margins on resale kawaii goods are typically 20-35% after shipping and platform fees. Original products can hit 60-80% margins.

**Recommended original product expansion:**
- Sticker packs (he already does this, scale it). Themed sets: ghost types, water types, fairy types, food kawaii, seasonal.
- Enamel pins. $1.50-3.00 to produce, sell for $10-15. Kawaii pin collectors are a massive community.
- Art prints (5x7, 8x10). Low production cost, high perceived value.
- Washi tape. Extremely popular in the kawaii/stationery space. $2-4 to produce, sells for $8-12.
- Tote bags with original art. $5-8 to produce, sells for $18-25.

**Target:** 50% original products within 6 months.

### Blind Box Strategy

Blind boxes are Randy's secret weapon and he is not using them.

The blind box market is projected to exceed $40 billion globally. On TikTok, #blindbox has over 15 billion views. #unboxing has over 60 billion.

**What Randy should do:**
- Stock 3-5 blind box lines consistently (Sonny Angel, POP MART, Tokidoki are top sellers).
- Create "mystery bundles" using his own inventory. A $25 "Kawaii Mystery Box" with $30+ retail value of mixed items. This solves two problems: moves slow inventory and creates content.
- Film every unboxing. Every single one.

### Bundle Strategy

Individual items under $10 have high cart abandonment. Bundles solve this.

**Recommended bundles:**
- "Pokemon Collector Starter Kit" -- binder + sleeves + deck box, $35-45 (vs. $50+ individual)
- "Kawaii Desk Set" -- stickers + stationery + small plush, $20-25
- "Rilakkuma Fan Pack" -- plush + charm + sticker, $30-40
- "Mystery Kawaii Box" -- 4-6 items, $25 (builds excitement, clears slow stock)
- "Event Exclusive Bundle" -- only available at cons/flea markets, creates urgency

### Price Analysis

| Category | Current Range | Market Comparison | Assessment |
|----------|--------------|-------------------|------------|
| Pokemon TCG binders | $25-60 | $20-65 (Amazon/TCGplayer) | Competitive, slightly high on low end |
| Sleeves/deck boxes | $9-15 | $8-14 | In range |
| San-X plush | $22-40 | $18-45 (depending on size/rarity) | Fair |
| Blind boxes | $15-16 | $12-18 | In range |
| Stationery | $3-4 | $2-6 | Good entry price point |
| Original stickers | $3 | $3-5 (Etsy average) | Could go to $4-5 for packs |

**Key finding:** Prices are generally market-rate, but "On Sale" badges undercut perceived value. Remove sale badges from items that are already at market price. Only use sales for genuine clearance events or limited-time promotions.

## 3. CONTENT & MARKETING PLAN

### TikTok (Priority 1 -- Start Immediately)

This is the single most impactful channel Randy is not using. The kawaii and collectibles community on TikTok is enormous and highly engaged.

**Content pillars (post 1-2x daily):**
1. **Blind box unboxings.** Film the reaction. The suspense of not knowing what you get is inherently viral. No editing skills required for the first videos.
2. **"Pack with me" videos.** Packing orders, preparing for events. These perform extremely well in the small business community.
3. **Event vlogs.** Walk through flea markets, comic cons. Show the booth setup, customer reactions, hauls.
4. **Product showcases.** 15-second clips of new arrivals with trending audio.
5. **"What sold today" recaps.** Transparency content builds community.
6. **Thrift haul to resale flip.** Randy already thrifts. Documenting the process of finding items and flipping them is proven content.

**Technical setup:**
- Ring light ($20-30)
- Phone tripod with overhead mount ($15-25)
- Clean pastel background (a $5 foam board works)
- CapCut for editing (free)

**Target:** 1,000 followers in 30 days, 5,000 in 90 days. First viral video (10K+ views) within 2 weeks of consistent posting.

### Instagram (Priority 2)

Randy has an Instagram presence. It needs to become a curated kawaii aesthetic feed.

**Strategy:**
- Grid aesthetic matters. Plan 9-post grids with consistent pink/pastel color palette.
- Reels: repurpose TikTok content (remove watermark with SnapTik or similar).
- Stories: daily behind-the-scenes, polls ("which blind box should I open?"), countdown to events.
- Highlights: organize by category (New Arrivals, Blind Boxes, Events, Reviews, Behind the Scenes).
- Collaborate with micro-influencers (1K-10K followers) in the kawaii space. Send free product for a post. Cost: $20-40 in product for potential reach of 5K-10K.

### Pinterest (Priority 3 -- Currently Missing Entirely)

Pinterest is the #2 platform for kawaii product discovery after TikTok. It drives direct purchase intent.

**Setup checklist:**
- Business account with claimed website
- Rich pins enabled (auto-pulls product data from Shopify)
- Boards: "Kawaii Stationery," "Pokemon TCG Accessories," "Blind Box Collection," "Desk Aesthetic," "Gift Ideas for Anime Fans"
- Pin every product with keyword-rich descriptions
- Create "idea pins" (Pinterest's version of Reels) showing products in use

**Pinterest drives long-tail traffic.** A pin posted today can drive traffic for 6-12 months. This is the opposite of TikTok's flash-and-fade model. Both are needed.

### Event Marketing (Capture Every Customer)

Randy's in-person events are a goldmine he is leaving money on the table at.

**Current problem:** Customer buys at a flea market, walks away, never connects with the brand again.

**Solution -- Event Capture System:**
1. **QR code on every table.** Links to a landing page: "Join the Oni-Puni Club -- 10% off your next online order." Captures email + phone.
2. **Business cards with QR code.** Every bag gets one. Cost: $20 for 500 cards.
3. **"Follow us on TikTok" sign.** With the handle prominently displayed.
4. **Text-to-join option.** "Text KAWAII to [number]" for SMS list (use a service like Postscript or SMSBump, both integrate with Shopify).
5. **Post-event email.** "Thanks for visiting us at [event name]! Here's what's new in the store." Sent within 24 hours.

**Target:** Capture 20-30 emails per event. At 2 events per month, that is 40-60 new email subscribers monthly.

### Email Marketing

**Current state:** Newsletter signup exists but no evidence of active email campaigns.

**Required sequences:**
1. **Welcome series (3 emails):**
   - Email 1 (immediate): Welcome + 10% discount code + brand story
   - Email 2 (Day 3): Best sellers + "Why kawaii?" content
   - Email 3 (Day 7): Social proof + "Follow us on TikTok"

2. **Abandoned cart (3 emails):**
   - Email 1 (1 hour): "You left something cute behind"
   - Email 2 (24 hours): Social proof + urgency
   - Email 3 (72 hours): Final discount offer

3. **Post-purchase (2 emails):**
   - Email 1 (Day 2): Order confirmation + "Share your haul on Instagram"
   - Email 2 (Day 14): Review request (this solves the 2-review problem)

4. **Monthly newsletter:**
   - New arrivals, upcoming events, behind-the-scenes content, exclusive deals

**Tool:** Klaviyo (free up to 250 contacts, integrates natively with Shopify). Upgrade to paid when list exceeds 250.

## 4. STORE IMPROVEMENTS

### Theme & Design

The Shopify Boost theme is functional but generic. The store needs to feel like Oni-Puni, not like a Shopify template.

**Priority fixes:**
- **Homepage hero banner.** Custom graphic with brand personality. Not a product grid. A statement: "Your kawaii obsession starts here" or similar.
- **Brand story section.** Who is Randy? Why kawaii? Customers buy from people, not stores. A 3-sentence "About" blurb on the homepage builds connection.
- **Custom favicon.** The browser tab should show the Oni-Puni logo, not a generic Shopify icon.
- **Consistent product photography.** Same background, same lighting, same angle for every product. This is non-negotiable for perceived professionalism.
- **Remove excessive "On Sale" badges.** Keep them for genuine markdowns only.
- **Add a "Best Sellers" collection.** Social proof through popularity.
- **Add a "New Arrivals" collection.** Gives repeat visitors a reason to come back.
- **Add an "Event Exclusive" collection.** Creates FOMO for online buyers.

### Product Descriptions

Current descriptions are likely manufacturer copy or minimal. Every product needs:

1. **A hook.** First sentence should create desire, not describe the item.
   - Bad: "This is a 9-pocket binder for Pokemon cards."
   - Good: "Protect your pulls in style. This 9-pocket binder holds up to 360 cards and looks clean doing it."

2. **Specifications.** Dimensions, materials, capacity. Bullet points.

3. **Use case.** Who is this for? "Perfect for tournament players who want to show off their collection between rounds."

4. **Social proof element.** "One of our best sellers at Milwaukee Comic Con" or similar.

### Review Strategy

2 reviews across 29 products is the most urgent trust problem on the store.

**Immediate actions:**
- **Judge.me post-purchase email.** Automate review requests 14 days after delivery. Judge.me (already installed) supports this natively.
- **Incentivize reviews.** "Leave a review, get 10% off your next order." This is standard practice.
- **Photo reviews.** Offer 15% off for reviews with photos. Photo reviews convert 2x better than text-only.
- **Ask event customers.** "If you love what you got, drop us a review at onipuni.com." Include this on the business card or follow-up email.
- **Import reviews from other platforms.** If Randy sells on eBay or Mercari and has reviews there, reference them (cannot import directly, but can screenshot and use as social proof in marketing).

**Target:** 20+ reviews within 60 days. 50+ within 6 months.

### SEO

**Current issues:**
- Product titles are functional but not search-optimized.
- No blog content.
- No collection descriptions.
- Meta descriptions likely auto-generated.

**Fixes:**
- **Product titles.** Include searchable keywords. "Pokemon 9-Pocket Card Binder" becomes "Pokemon Card Binder - 9-Pocket TCG Portfolio for Trading Cards."
- **Collection descriptions.** 150-200 words per collection page with target keywords.
- **Blog.** One post per week. Topics: "Best Pokemon TCG Accessories for 2026," "What is Rilakkuma? A Beginner's Guide," "Blind Box Collecting 101." Each post targets a long-tail keyword.
- **Alt text on images.** Every product image needs descriptive alt text.
- **Meta descriptions.** Custom for every page. 155 characters, include a call to action.

**Target keywords:**
- kawaii accessories online
- pokemon tcg binder
- blind box shop
- rilakkuma plush usa
- kawaii stickers
- anime collectibles milwaukee
- kawaii gift shop
