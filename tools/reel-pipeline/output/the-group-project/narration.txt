The professor says group project and your stomach drops. Not because the work is hard. Because you already know exactly what's about to happen. Four names on the slide. One person doing everything. You send the first message in the group chat. Hey, when should we meet? Silence. Two days pass. You send another message. Maybe we should divide the sections? One person responds with a thumbs up emoji. The night before it's due, someone sends their part. Two paragraphs. Wrong topic. And somehow, you still get it done. Because you always do. That's the real project. Learning to carry people who won't carry themselves.
