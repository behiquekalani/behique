We used to need extra chairs at Thanksgiving.

We used to need extra chairs at Thanksgiving. Folding chairs from the garage, a bench from the porch, anything to fit everyone. The table was loud. Arguments about politics, kids running underneath, someone always spilling something. Now there are empty seats. We don't need the folding chairs anymore. The food is the same but the room feels twice as big. Nobody talks about the empty chair. But everyone looks at it at least once. And that silence is the loudest thing at the table.
