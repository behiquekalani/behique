My dad put glow-in-the-dark stars on my ceiling when I was six. He stood on the bed with a chair cushion under his feet because we didn't own a ladder. He let me pick where each one went. I pointed and he pressed them up there one by one. Some of them fell off over the years. First the big ones, then the little ones, one by one, like real stars burning out. I'm twenty-five now. I moved back home for a while. And they're still there. Barely glowing. You have to let your eyes adjust in the dark to even see them. But they're there. And every night, for a few minutes, I'm six again. And my dad is standing on the bed, reaching for the ceiling, making me a sky.
