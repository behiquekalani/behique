My dad spent every weekend in the garage.

My dad spent every weekend in the garage. He could fix anything. Lawnmowers, car engines, broken radios, wobbly table legs. He had a solution for everything that was broken. Except himself. He never talked about what hurt him. He just went to the garage, turned on the radio, and worked with his hands until the sun went down. I didn't understand it then. Now I catch myself doing the same thing. Staying busy so I don't have to feel. He taught me how to fix everything but that.
