I used to wait at this bus stop every morning with my dad. He'd hold my hand even when I was old enough to be embarrassed by it. He always got there ten minutes early because he said the bus doesn't wait for anybody. I remember the smell of his cologne mixed with morning air. He'd quiz me on multiplication tables while we waited. Now I drive past that bus stop every day on my way to work. The bench is different. The sign is new. But I still slow down when I pass it. And some mornings, I swear I can smell his cologne.
