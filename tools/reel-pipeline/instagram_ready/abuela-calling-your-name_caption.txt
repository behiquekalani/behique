She never texted.

She never texted. She never called your phone. She just stood on the porch and yelled your name. And somehow, three blocks away, you heard it. She had this way of stretching the vowels. Turning two syllables into five. Like your name was a song only she knew how to sing. You'd drop whatever you were doing and run. Not because you were hungry. Because the way she said it made you feel like the most important person in the world. She's been gone seven years now. But sometimes, right before I fall asleep, I swear I can still hear it. Somewhere far away. Calling me home.
