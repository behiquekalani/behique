The key didn't work right.

The key didn't work right. You had to jiggle it, lift the door handle, and turn it at the same time. The landlord said he'd fix it. He never did. The apartment was tiny. One window. Smelled like fresh paint and someone else's life. I didn't have furniture yet. Just a mattress on the floor and a backpack full of everything I owned. That first night, I lay there staring at the ceiling. No one telling me to turn the lights off. No noise through the walls I recognized. Just silence. My silence. And for the first time in my life, I felt completely free. I never fixed that lock either. I kind of loved it.
