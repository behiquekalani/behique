My mom made me a birthday cake every single year.

My mom made me a birthday cake every single year. It was never pretty. The frosting was always uneven, the layers always slightly lopsided. One year the letters in my name slid right off the side. But I remember blowing out those candles and looking up at her face. She was so proud. I've had expensive cakes since then, from bakeries with perfect fondant and clean edges. None of them tasted like hers. Because hers tasted like someone stayed up until midnight just for me.
