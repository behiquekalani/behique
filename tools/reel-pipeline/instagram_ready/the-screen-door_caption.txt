The screen door at my grandparents' house had this specific sound when it slammed shut. A sharp crack followed by a rattle. Every summer, that sound meant freedom. Running outside barefoot, catching fireflies, staying out until the streetlights came on. I heard a screen door slam at a gas station last week. And for three seconds I was eight years old again, standing in wet grass, with nowhere to be and no reason to worry.
