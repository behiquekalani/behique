I drove through the old neighborhood last week.

I drove through the old neighborhood last week. The corner store is a laundromat now. The park where we played tag has new benches nobody sits on. Someone painted over the wall where we drew our names in chalk every summer. The house looks smaller than I remember. The mango tree in the backyard is gone. I parked for a minute and just sat there. A kid rode past on a bike and looked at me like I was a stranger. I guess I am now. But for a second, sitting in that car, I was ten years old again. And nothing had changed yet.
