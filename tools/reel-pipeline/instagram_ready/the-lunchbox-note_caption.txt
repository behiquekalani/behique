My mom used to put little notes in my lunchbox. Just simple things. "You're going to do great today." "I'm proud of you." I used to hide them so my friends wouldn't see. I was embarrassed. Now I'm thirty-two and I found one folded inside an old textbook. Her handwriting, slightly rushed, blue ink on a torn piece of notebook paper. I held it like it was made of glass. I would give anything to be embarrassed by her love one more time.
