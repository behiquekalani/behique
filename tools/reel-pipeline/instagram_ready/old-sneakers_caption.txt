These shoes have seen everything.

These shoes have seen everything. First day of high school. The court where I played until the streetlights came on. The night I walked three miles home because I missed the last bus. They're falling apart now. The sole is peeling and the laces have been replaced twice. But I can't throw them away. My mom bought them with money she didn't have. She said they were an investment in me. Every time I lace up a new pair, I think about her counting bills at the kitchen table. These shoes didn't just carry me. They carried her belief that I was going somewhere.
