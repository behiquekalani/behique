I kept this old recipe card for twenty years.

I kept this old recipe card for twenty years. Not because I ever learned to cook her arroz con pollo. The card is stained with oil and faded at the edges. Her handwriting is barely legible now. But every time I hold it, I can smell her kitchen. The sound of the radio playing boleros. The way she hummed while she stirred. She passed when I was twelve. I never got to tell her that her food was the only thing that made Sundays feel like home.
