My dad had this old Ford that hadn't run in years. Rust eating through the wheel wells, paint fading in the sun. He refused to sell it. Every now and then, after a long day, he'd just go sit in it. Engine off. Windows down. Hands on the wheel like he was driving somewhere far away. We never understood it. After he passed, my mom asked me to clean it out. Under the seat I found empty coffee cups, a flashlight, and a cassette tape still in the player. I pressed play. It was my mom's favorite song. He'd been sitting out there listening to her all along.
