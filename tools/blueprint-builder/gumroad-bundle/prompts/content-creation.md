# Content Creation Prompts
## 10 Battle-Tested Prompts for Social, Blog, Email, and Ads

---

### 1. Social Media Post Generator (Platform-Specific)

**When to use:** You need a week's worth of social content and don't want every post to sound the same.

**Prompt:**
```
You are a social media strategist for [BUSINESS TYPE] targeting [TARGET AUDIENCE].

Create 7 social media posts for [PLATFORM: Instagram/Twitter/LinkedIn] for this week.

My brand voice is [DESCRIBE: casual and direct / professional but warm / bold and irreverent].

Topic focus this week: [TOPIC OR PRODUCT].

Rules:
- No generic motivational fluff
- Each post must have a specific hook in the first line
- Include a call to action that isn't "link in bio"
- Vary the format: 2 text-only, 2 with image suggestions, 2 carousel concepts, 1 story/reel idea
- Keep character counts appropriate for the platform

For each post, include:
1. The post copy (ready to paste)
2. Best time to post
3. Hashtag set (5-10, mix of broad and niche)
4. Engagement prompt (question or poll to add in comments)
```

**Expected output:** 7 complete posts with metadata, ready to schedule.

### 2. Blog Post Outline + Draft

**When to use:** You know the topic but staring at a blank page isn't working. This gets you from zero to structured draft.

**Prompt:**
```
Write a blog post for [BUSINESS NAME] about [TOPIC].

Target reader: [WHO - be specific, e.g., "freelance designers making $3-8K/month who want to add AI services"].

Goal of this post: [WHAT SHOULD THE READER DO AFTER - e.g., "sign up for our free workflow audit"].

Structure requirements:
- Title: specific and benefit-driven, not clickbait
- Opening: start with a relatable problem or bold claim, no "In today's fast-paced world..."
- 5-7 subheadings that could stand alone as takeaways
- Include at least 2 concrete examples or case studies (real or realistic)
- End with a specific next step, not a vague "start your journey"

Length: [800/1200/2000] words.
Tone: [conversational / authoritative / technical but accessible].

Do NOT use these phrases: "game-changer", "leverage", "in today's digital landscape", "unlock your potential".
```

**Expected output:** Full blog post draft, formatted with headers, ready for editing.

### 3. Email Welcome Sequence

**When to use:** Someone just signed up for your list and you need to turn them from stranger to buyer in 5 emails.

**Prompt:**
```
Create a 5-email welcome sequence for [BUSINESS NAME].

What we sell: [PRODUCT/SERVICE + PRICE RANGE].
Who signs up: [HOW THEY FOUND US - e.g., "downloaded our free AI automation checklist"].
Main objection to buying: [BIGGEST HESITATION - e.g., "they think AI is too technical for them"].

Email sequence:
1. Welcome + deliver the lead magnet + set expectations (send immediately)
2. Our story + why we built this (Day 2)
3. The biggest mistake our audience makes + how to avoid it (Day 4)
4. Case study or proof that our approach works (Day 6)
5. Direct offer with urgency (Day 8)

For each email provide:
- Subject line (and one A/B variant)
- Preview text
- Full email body (under 300 words each)
- CTA button text

Tone: like a smart friend who happens to know a lot about [INDUSTRY], not a marketer.
```

**Expected output:** 5 complete emails with subject lines, preview text, and body copy.

### 4. Product Description Writer

**When to use:** You have a product listing (eBay, Shopify, Amazon, Gumroad) and need copy that actually sells.

**Prompt:**
```
Write a product description for [PRODUCT NAME].

Platform: [eBay / Shopify / Amazon / Gumroad].
Price: [PRICE].
Target buyer: [WHO IS BUYING THIS AND WHY].

Product details:
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]
- [Key differentiator from competitors]

Write:
1. A headline that includes the main benefit (not just the product name)
2. A 2-3 sentence hook that addresses the buyer's problem
3. Bullet-point feature list (benefit-first, then feature)
4. A short "who this is for" section
5. A "what's included" section if applicable
6. A closing line with soft urgency (no fake scarcity)

Optimize for [PLATFORM] search. Include relevant keywords naturally.
Do not use all caps, excessive exclamation marks, or the word "premium."
```

**Expected output:** Complete listing copy, platform-optimized, ready to paste.

### 5. Ad Copy Generator (Meta/Google)

**When to use:** You're running paid ads and need multiple angles to test.

**Prompt:**
```
Create 5 ad variations for [PRODUCT/SERVICE] on [META ADS / GOOGLE ADS].

Target audience: [DEMOGRAPHIC + PSYCHOGRAPHIC - e.g., "small business owners, 25-45, frustrated with manual processes"].
Budget range: [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH - affects the offer framing].
Landing page URL: [URL or describe what's on the page].

For Meta Ads, each variation needs:
- Primary text (125 characters max for feed)
- Headline (40 characters)
- Description (30 characters)
- Image/video concept suggestion

For Google Ads, each variation needs:
- 3 headlines (30 characters each)
- 2 descriptions (90 characters each)

Use these 5 angles:
1. Pain point (what sucks about the current way)
2. Transformation (before/after)
3. Social proof (real or realistic testimonial)
4. Curiosity (make them click to find out)
5. Direct offer (straightforward value proposition)

No clickbait. No false promises. These need to convert AND get approved.
```

**Expected output:** 5 complete ad variations with creative direction, ready to upload.

### 6. Content Repurposing Engine

**When to use:** You created one piece of content and want to squeeze 10 pieces out of it.

**Prompt:**
```
I have this [BLOG POST / VIDEO SCRIPT / PODCAST EPISODE / NEWSLETTER]:

"""
[PASTE YOUR CONTENT HERE]
"""

Repurpose this into:
1. 3 Twitter/X posts (different angles, not just quotes)
2. 1 LinkedIn post (professional framing, 150-200 words)
3. 1 Instagram carousel (6-8 slides, text for each slide)
4. 5 Instagram/TikTok Reel hooks (first 3 seconds, text overlay + visual suggestion)
5. 1 email newsletter snippet (3 paragraphs + CTA)
6. 1 YouTube community post
7. 3 quote graphics (text that works on a simple background)

For each piece, note which part of the original content it draws from.
Adapt the tone for each platform. Don't just copy-paste with different formatting.
```

**Expected output:** 15+ content pieces derived from one source, platform-adapted.

### 7. Newsletter Writer

**When to use:** Weekly newsletter due, brain empty. This gives you structure and substance.

**Prompt:**
```
Write this week's newsletter for [BUSINESS/BRAND NAME].

Newsletter name: [NAME].
Audience: [WHO READS IT].
This week's main topic: [TOPIC].

Format:
- Subject line (and A/B variant) - must create curiosity without clickbait
- Opening hook (1-2 sentences, personal or observational)
- Main section (300-400 words, teach one actionable thing)
- "Quick hits" section (3-4 bullet points: tool recommendations, industry news, or tips)
- Closing (personal note + what's coming next week)
- P.S. line (secondary CTA or fun aside)

Tone: [DESCRIBE - e.g., "like a weekly memo from a colleague who's always two steps ahead"].
Include at least one specific example, number, or case study. No fluff padding.
```

**Expected output:** Complete newsletter draft, ready to paste into your email tool.

### 8. SEO Content Brief Generator

**When to use:** Before writing any blog or landing page. This ensures you're targeting the right keywords and structure.

**Prompt:**
```
Create an SEO content brief for the keyword: "[TARGET KEYWORD]"

My site: [DOMAIN].
Industry: [INDUSTRY].
Current domain authority (approximate): [LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH or number if known].

Provide:
1. Primary keyword + search intent (informational/transactional/navigational)
2. 5-8 secondary keywords to include naturally
3. 3 long-tail variations to target
4. Suggested title tag (under 60 characters)
5. Meta description (under 155 characters)
6. Recommended word count based on competition
7. Content outline with H2/H3 structure
8. 3 internal linking opportunities (suggest page types to link to/from)
9. 2-3 external authority sources to reference
10. Featured snippet opportunity (if applicable, format the answer)

Analyze what the top 3 ranking pages do well and what they miss. My content should fill those gaps.
```

**Expected output:** Complete content brief that a writer (human or AI) can execute against.

### 9. Video Script Writer

**When to use:** You need a script for a YouTube video, Reel, or TikTok that doesn't sound like it was written by a robot.

**Prompt:**
```
Write a video script for [PLATFORM: YouTube / Instagram Reel / TikTok].

Topic: [TOPIC].
Length: [30 seconds / 60 seconds / 5-10 minutes].
Style: [talking head / voiceover / screen recording / b-roll heavy].

My personality on camera: [DESCRIBE - e.g., "dry humor, fast-paced, lots of examples"].

Script format:
- HOOK (first 3 seconds - this determines if they scroll or stay)
- SETUP (why this matters, 10-15 seconds)
- CONTENT (main points, use numbered steps or story structure)
- CTA (what to do next, natural not pushy)

Rules:
- Write for spoken word, not written word. Short sentences. Contractions.
- Include [VISUAL] notes for what should be on screen
- No "hey guys welcome back to my channel"
- The hook should make a claim or ask a question that demands an answer
- If it's a Reel/TikTok, the entire script should be under [30/60] seconds when read aloud
```

**Expected output:** Ready-to-film script with visual cues and timing notes.

### 10. Brand Voice Documentation

**When to use:** Once, when setting up your AI systems. This becomes the reference doc you paste into every future prompt.

**Prompt:**
```
Help me document my brand voice for [BUSINESS NAME].

Here are 3-5 examples of content I've written that sounds like me:

Example 1: """[PASTE]"""
Example 2: """[PASTE]"""
Example 3: """[PASTE]"""

Analyze these and create a Brand Voice Guide with:

1. Voice characteristics (3-5 adjectives with examples)
2. Tone spectrum (when do we get serious vs. playful?)
3. Vocabulary preferences:
   - Words we use
   - Words we never use
   - Industry jargon: use it or translate it?
4. Sentence structure patterns (short and punchy? Long and flowing? Mixed?)
5. Formatting preferences (bullets vs. paragraphs? Headers style?)
6. Brand personality: "If our brand were a person at a party, they would..."
7. 3 "do this, not that" examples showing voice-correct vs. voice-wrong versions

Output this as a reference document I can paste at the top of future prompts.
```

**Expected output:** A reusable brand voice document that standardizes all future AI-generated content.
