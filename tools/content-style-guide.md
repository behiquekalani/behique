## WRITING RULES (check 2-3 per post)

1. ACTIVE VOICE. Numbers. Pattern interrupts. Imply a problem or question.
2. CONFIDENT TONE. No "maybe", "I think", "could be". Use absolutes.
3. UNIQUE PERSPECTIVE. From experience, values, or research. Not generic.
4. HIT A PAIN POINT or benefit people feel in their daily life.
5. READABLE. Line breaks. Rhythm. Visual structure. Lists taper long-to-short.
6. ANSWER "What's in it for me?" so they want to engage.
7. EDUCATE, ENTERTAIN, or INSPIRE. Hit at least two of three.

## TONE

- Dan Koe: direct, minimalist, confident, short-to-mid form
- Apple: clean, serif headlines, no clutter
- Journalism: front-load key info, inverted pyramid
- NO em dashes. Use periods or commas.
- NO hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing", "insane")
- NO generic CTAs ("Drop your take below", "What do you think?")

## LONG-FORM TEMPLATE (Content Multiplication Template)

### Planning
- Topic: What are you talking about?
- Research: 2-3 existing posts/videos on same topic
- Main Problem: What big problem are you solving?
- Key Points: 3-10 steps to solve it
- Transformation: Point A -> Point B

### Intro
- Headline: Sum up the transformation. Use numbers.
- Hook: 2-3 sentences. Relatable. "Tell me if this sounds familiar..."
- Lead: Imagery-full description of the problem.

### Body
- Story (optional): Personal experience, brief
- Key Points: Each one answers WHO/WHAT/WHERE/WHEN/WHY/HOW
- Practical: Be specific. No vague advice.

### Conclusion
- Summarize the transformation
- Paint a picture of their life after solving this
- End with conviction, not a question

## FOR AI NEWS SPECIFICALLY

When writing about AI news, apply these OPB angles:
- IMPORTANCE: "Why [this AI news] matters for builders"
- HARSH TRUTH: "Everyone is talking about [X] but missing [Y]"
- PRINCIPLES: "[Company] just dropped [product]. Here's what it means:"
- PAIN RESOLUTION: "If you're struggling to keep up with AI, here's your filter"

Always connect news back to the reader's life:
- What can they DO with this information?
- How does this affect their business/career/projects?
- What should they build/learn/avoid based on this?

News without context is noise. Context without action is philosophy.
Our job is to give both.
