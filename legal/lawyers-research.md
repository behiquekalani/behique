## FREE Option: Pro Bono

### Federal Circuit Bar Association National Clearinghouse
- USPTO's nationwide pro bono program
- Matches qualifying inventors with volunteer attorneys
- **Income-based qualification** (must be under certain income threshold)
- Worth checking if you qualify as a student
- URL: https://www.federalcircuitbar.org/programs/pro-bono

## Recommendation

**For your budget right now:**

1. **Copyright:** File yourself at copyright.gov ($85). It's straightforward.
   The filing prep doc has everything pre-filled.

2. **Trademark:** Either:
   - DIY ($350 total) using the filing prep doc. The TEAS Plus form is simple.
   - JPG Legal (~$199 + $350 = $549 total) if you want an attorney to review.
   - Check the pro bono program first since you're a student.

3. **Later when revenue starts:** Get a local PR attorney (Solid Rep or Alice Cabrera)
   on retainer for ongoing IP protection.
