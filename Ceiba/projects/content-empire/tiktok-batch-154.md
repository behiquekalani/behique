## Script 1: The Reason Most Side Projects Die
**Hook (0-3s):** Your side project isn't dying because you're lazy. It's dying because it has no deadline.
**Body:** Open-ended projects expand indefinitely. Without a constraint, nothing ships. The fix is a hard deadline with a defined output. Not "finish the course eventually." Try: "Draft three modules by Friday." The constraint forces prioritization. You stop trying to make it perfect and start trying to make it done. Momentum is not motivation. Momentum is what happens when you define the next action so clearly that starting requires no thought. Deadline first, quality second.
**CTA:** Set one deadline in the comments right now. I'll check on you.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "No deadline = no ship"
- "Constraint creates momentum"
- "Define it clearly to start instantly"
- "Done beats forever-in-progress"

**Hook (0-3s):** Not all digital products sell equally. These three types consistently convert.

## Script 3: Why Your Niche Is Not Too Specific
**Hook (0-3s):** The more specific your niche, the faster you grow. That's not a theory.
**Body:** When you write for everyone you reach no one. When you write for twenty-three-year-old freelance designers who want to build passive income while still taking clients, people who match that description feel like you read their mind. They follow instantly. They share because finally someone gets it. The algorithm also rewards specificity because watch time increases when content feels relevant. Broad niches produce broad, forgettable content. Specific niches produce content that people screenshot and send to their friends.
**CTA:** State your niche in the comments as specifically as possible.

## Script 4: What Working for Yourself Actually Teaches You
**Hook (0-3s):** Working for yourself teaches you that you were the problem all along.
**Body:** Not in a harsh way. In an honest way. Nobody is making the decision for you. Nobody sets the deadline. Nobody tells you what to prioritize. And suddenly you realize how many of your productivity habits were borrowed from a structure someone else built. Working for yourself reveals your actual defaults: what you avoid, what you procrastinate on, what energizes you, and what drains you. That data is more valuable than any productivity course. The business is the diagnosis.

## Script 5: The Problem With Waiting Until You're Ready
**Hook (0-3s):** Ready is a feeling, not a state. It never arrives on its own.

The creator who started posting before they felt confident now has two years of compounding data on what resonates with their audience. The person who waited is still workshopping the first post. You don't need more preparation. You need one shipped thing that generates real feedback. Nothing prepares you for the thing like doing the thing.

## Script 7: The Money Conversation Most Creators Avoid
**Hook (0-3s):** Most creators are afraid to ask for money because they don't believe they've earned it yet.
**Body:** The earning threshold in your head is arbitrary. You set it. And you keep moving it forward. First it was: once I have a hundred followers. Then a thousand. Then a real audience. The truth is that the moment someone finds value in something you made, that value exists regardless of your follower count. Price the value, not the audience size. Ten engaged buyers who got real results are more proof than ten thousand passive followers who never paid. Start charging before you feel ready.

Strategy comes later. Habit is what gets you to the point where strategy matters.
