## Topic: How to Reprice Yourself Higher Without Losing Customers

## TikTok 1: The Truth About Why You're Undercharging

**Hook (0-2s):**
"You're not undercharging because of the market. You're undercharging because of you."

**Script:**
The market will pay more than you're asking.

I know this because every time someone raises their prices with the right positioning, the conversion rate stays the same or goes up.

What changes is the quality of the conversation.

Higher-priced offers attract people who take the problem seriously. They show up. They do the work. They get results. They refer others.

The real reason you're undercharging is because you're afraid that raising prices will expose some gap between what you charge and what you deliver.

But here's the thing. Your price is part of what you deliver.

If you're charging $50 for something and someone is charging $500 for something similar, the person paying $500 has more skin in the game. They're more likely to follow through. Their result is better. They credit your offer.

Raise the price. The customers who leave weren't the ones getting results anyway.

Follow. The next video is about how to frame the price increase.

## TikTok 2: How to Raise Prices Without an Awkward Conversation

**Hook (0-2s):**
"Raising your prices doesn't require an announcement. It requires positioning."

**Script:**
The mistake is making the price increase the news.

"I'm raising my prices on Friday" is not positioning. It's a warning.

The right way to reprice is to change what the offer is, not just what it costs.

Add a component that justifies the new price. A live session. A template library. A 30-day check-in. Something tangible that the old price would have felt expensive for.

Now you're not raising the price. You're launching an expanded offer.

Existing customers get grandfathered in at the old rate for a limited time. New customers see the new price from the start.

Nobody feels blindsided. The value story is clear. And you never have to explain why you cost more because the offer itself makes the price obvious.

Save this. You'll use it the next time you reprice.

## TikTok 3: The Clients Who Leave When You Raise Prices

**Hook (0-2s):**
"When you raise your prices, some clients will leave. That's the point."

**Script:**
The clients who are most sensitive to price are usually the ones who take the most of your time and give the least back.

They question every deliverable. They ask for revisions that go beyond scope. They pay late. They bring the most anxiety into your business.

Higher prices act as a natural filter.

When you charge more, you attract clients who trust your judgment, move faster, and treat the engagement like a priority.

This is not a theory. It's a pattern every service provider notices after they raise prices once and stick with it.

You might lose two or three clients in the transition. But the ones who stay, and the new ones who find you at the new price, operate differently.

Your work improves because your client relationships improve.

Raise the price. Improve the client. The business gets easier.

## TikTok 4: Proof That Your Current Price Is Wrong

**Hook (0-2s):**
"If no one has ever said your price is too high, your price is too low."

**Script:**
You want a mix of reactions when you quote a price.

Some people should say yes immediately. Some should need a moment to think. A few should push back or say it's too much.

If everyone says yes without hesitation, you've left money on the table.

Price resistance is feedback, not failure. It tells you that your price is significant to the buyer, which means the value conversation matters.

The goal isn't a price that never causes hesitation. It's a price where the hesitation resolves in your favor when you can articulate the value.

Test your price by raising it 30 percent on the next three conversations. Track the response.

If all three still convert, raise it again.

Keep raising until you find the edge. That edge is where your real market price lives.

**CTA:**
What would change about your business if you doubled your prices tomorrow?

## TikTok 5: The Positioning Shift That Makes Higher Prices Feel Obvious

**Hook (0-2s):**
"One sentence changed how I talk about what I do. And it doubled my prices."

**Script:**
The sentence was this: stop describing what you do and start describing what changes for the client.

Old positioning: "I build websites."
New positioning: "I turn unclear service businesses into ones that close leads from their first phone call."

Same skill. Different sentence. Completely different price conversation.

The first one invites comparison. How does your price compare to other web developers?

The second one invites investment. What is that outcome worth to your business?

When you describe outcomes instead of activities, the buyer calculates ROI instead of shopping for the cheapest option.

Change your positioning and the price conversation changes with it. You don't even have to raise your rates formally. You just stop attracting people who were shopping on price.
