## TikTok 2: Why Your AI Content Sounds Generic
**Hook (0-2s):**
Your AI content sounds like everyone else's. Here's why.

**Script:**
The problem isn't the tool. It's the prompt.

Most people give AI zero context. No voice. No audience. No point of view. So it gives you the average of everything it's been trained on. Which is exactly what you don't want.

Here's the fix. Before you write a single prompt, define three things. One: what you actually believe about your topic. Two: who you're writing for specifically. Three: what tone sounds like you on your worst day, not your best.

That last one matters. Your best day is polished. Your worst day is honest. AI needs honest.

Feed those three things into every prompt. The output will stop sounding like a press release and start sounding like a person.

Your audience can tell the difference. They've been trained by bad AI content too.

**CTA:**
Save this. Your next content session starts with those three things.

## TikTok 4: How to Sound Like Yourself Using AI
**Hook (0-2s):**
AI can write in your voice. Most people never set it up correctly.

**Script:**
Here's the setup that takes 20 minutes and saves you hours every week.

Open a doc. Write 500 words about anything you actually care about. Don't edit. Don't structure. Just write.

Then take three pieces of content you've already made that performed well. Drop all of it into your AI tool. Say: "Analyze the voice in these samples. List the patterns. Then use those patterns for everything I write."

You've just given the AI a voice profile. Now every time you prompt it, you start with: "Write in the voice of these examples."

The output won't be perfect. But it'll be close enough to edit in 10 minutes instead of rewrite from scratch.

Voice is trainable. Yours is in your past work. Go get it.

**CTA:**
Try this today. Take 20 minutes. Build your voice profile.

## TikTok 5: One AI Tool That Changed How I Research Content
**Hook (0-2s):**
I stopped spending hours on content research. This is how.

**Script:**
Content research used to mean opening 12 tabs, reading for an hour, and then forgetting half of it before I started writing.

Now I use AI for the research layer, but not the way most people think.

I don't ask it to research for me. I ask it to help me ask better questions.

"What are the three most contested ideas in this topic right now? What does the mainstream believe that experts disagree with? What question does my audience have that nobody is answering honestly?"

Those three prompts give me better angles than any amount of tab-browsing. Then I go verify the specifics myself.

AI speeds up the thinking. I still own the conclusions.

That's the mental model. Research faster. Think harder. Write with a point of view.
