## TikTok 2: The Exact TikTok to Email Funnel in Four Steps

**Hook (0-2s):**
Four steps. TikTok follower to email subscriber. Here's the system.

**Script:**
Step one: lead magnet. Create a free resource that solves one specific problem for your audience. A checklist, a template, a short guide. One page is enough. It has to be immediately useful.

Step two: link in bio. Your bio has one job: point to the lead magnet. Not your Linktree with twenty options. One link. One destination. One action.

Step three: content with intent. Every video you post should end with a reason to click that link. Not "link in bio" generically. A specific reason: "the template I mentioned is free in my bio."

Step four: nurture sequence. When someone joins your list, send them three to five emails that deepen the relationship. Give more value. Build more trust. Then introduce your paid product.

The funnel is not complicated. Most people overcomplicate it because they're procrastinating building it.

One lead magnet. One link. One call to action per video. Three emails.

## TikTok 3: What Makes a Lead Magnet People Actually Download

**Hook (0-2s):**
Most lead magnets fail before they're even clicked. Here's why.

**Script:**
The lead magnet has one job: remove one specific friction for one specific person right now.

Not "everything you need to know about content marketing." That's a course, not a lead magnet. The scope is too big. The value proposition is too vague.

The lead magnet that works sounds like: "the exact email sequence I used to get my first ten customers" or "the five-minute morning routine that replaced my therapy bills" or "the template I use to write one week of TikTok content in ninety minutes."

Specific. Immediate. Actionable.

The format matters less than the specificity. A one-page PDF beats a thirty-page ebook if the one page solves a real problem. People don't download things to learn. They download things to do something.

What's the one thing your audience keeps struggling with that you solved? That's your lead magnet.

## TikTok 4: How Often Should You Promote Your Email List on TikTok?

**Hook (0-2s):**
How often should you mention your email list on TikTok? More than you think.

**Script:**
The common fear is that mentioning your lead magnet too often feels salesy or repetitive.

Here's the reality: most people need to see the same offer multiple times before they act on it. The marketing research on this is decades old. Multiple exposures drive conversions.

More importantly, your audience is not watching every video. TikTok shows each video to a different subset of your followers. The person watching today probably didn't see the video you posted last Thursday.

You are not repeating yourself to the same person. You are reaching different people with the same message.

Mention your lead magnet in every video that logically connects to it. When the content topic maps to the resource you're offering, make the CTA. Don't save it for once a week.

The creators who build email lists fast are the ones who mention their lead magnet constantly and without apology.

## TikTok 5: What to Do With Your Email List Once You Build It

**Hook (0-2s):**
Building an email list is step one. Here's what to actually do with it.

**Script:**
Most people build an email list and then don't email the people on it.

They're afraid of annoying people. They're waiting until they have something perfect to say. They send one welcome email and then go silent for three months.

The people on your list forget who you are. When you finally email them again, the open rate tanks. You've wasted the asset.

Here's what to do instead.

Email weekly. Every week. Doesn't need to be long. It needs to be consistent and useful.

One idea, one insight, one recommendation. Three hundred words is enough. The goal is presence. You want to be the name they recognize in their inbox every week.

Then, when you launch something, they're warm. They know you. The sale is not cold.

The list that gets emailed weekly converts at a multiple of the list that doesn't. Consistency is the entire strategy.
