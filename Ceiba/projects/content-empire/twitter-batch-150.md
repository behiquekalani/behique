# Twitter/X Batch 150 — Milestone: 10 Truths About Building Online

---

**Tweet 1 (punchy single)**
Batch 150. Here are the 10 truths I wish someone had told me before I started building online.

---

**Tweet 2 (punchy single)**
Truth 1:
The first product will embarrass you in three years.

Ship it anyway. The gap between what you imagine and what you build only closes by building.

---

**Tweet 3 (punchy single)**
Truth 2:
Audience size is a vanity metric until the audience trusts you.

1,000 people who believe you will outperform 100,000 people who follow you for entertainment.

---

**Tweet 4 — 3-tweet thread**

Tweet 1/3:
Truth 3: Most people stall not because they don't know enough.

They stall because taking action makes failure feel real. Researching keeps it theoretical.

This is the mechanism behind 90% of "I'm not ready yet."

Tweet 2/3:
Preparation has a point of diminishing returns.

At some point the only new information available is the kind you can only get from doing the thing.

That information is worth more than anything you can read or watch.

Tweet 3/3:
The move is to compress the gap between "I'm thinking about this" and "I shipped a version of this."

Not perfect. Not complete. A version.

Versions teach you what plans never will.

---

**Tweet 5 (punchy single)**
Truth 4:
The business model matters more than the niche.

A bad model in a great niche fails slower.
A great model in a small niche still works.

Study the mechanics before you study the market.

---

**Tweet 6 (punchy single)**
Truth 5:
Consistency compounds, but only if what you're consistent about is the right thing.

Consistently posting bad content, making offers to the wrong audience, or solving problems no one has -- that compounds too.

Direction matters more than discipline.

---

**Tweet 7 — 8-tweet thread**

Tweet 1/8:
150 batches in. Here are the remaining 10 truths about building online.

Not motivation. Not hype. Things I've had to learn by doing this long enough.

Tweet 2/8:
Truth 6: Your pricing tells a story before your buyer reads a word.

Low prices say: "I'm not sure this is worth much."
High prices say: "I built something I believe in."

Neither guarantees a sale. But only one of them positions you for a real business.

Tweet 3/8:
Truth 7: The content you think is too simple is usually the content that performs best.

You've been living in your area of expertise long enough that the basics feel boring.

To someone just entering your world, the basics are everything.

Never graduate past teaching fundamentals.

Tweet 4/8:
Truth 8: Most online business failures are distribution failures, not product failures.

The product worked. Nobody saw it.

Build your audience before you build the thing. Or build them in parallel. But never assume good work finds its own audience.

Tweet 5/8:
Truth 9: The first year is for data collection, not income.

Every post that lands, every offer that converts, every question you get asked repeatedly -- that's data.

The business is built from that data.

People who quit in the first year are quitting during the data collection phase and calling it failure.

Tweet 6/8:
Truth 10: You don't find your angle. You build it.

The creator with a distinctive voice didn't download it fully formed. They wrote, iterated, got feedback, cut what didn't work, kept what did.

Identity online is constructed, not discovered.

Start building the construction before you know what the finished thing looks like.

Tweet 7/8:
One thing no one talks about: the psychological weight of building in public.

Silence from the algorithm. Posts that go nowhere. Comparing your chapter 3 to someone else's chapter 30.

The mental game is harder than the tactical game.

Most people who quit don't quit because they lacked skill. They quit because they ran out of reasons to keep going.

Know your reasons before you need them.

Tweet 8/8:
150 batches. The summary:

Trust compounds. Clarity sells. Consistency is only valuable pointed in the right direction. Ship before you're ready. Price with confidence. Teach the basics always. Build audience and product simultaneously. The first year is a research phase. Voice is built, not found. Know why you're doing this before you need to remember.

Thank you for being here. The work continues.

**Tweet 8 (punchy single)**
The people still building at year 3 aren't smarter or more talented than the ones who quit.

They just found better reasons to stay.

---

**Tweet 9 (punchy single)**
150 batches ago I didn't have the answers.

I had questions and the discipline to keep showing up while I found them.

That's still the only system that works.

**Tweet 10 — 3-tweet thread**

Tweet 1/3:
The most underrated truth about building online:

The audience you build is a reflection of who you've become.

If you want a better audience, become a better thinker. The work is internal before it's external.

Tweet 2/3:
You cannot consistently publish ideas you haven't thought through and expect people who think deeply to follow you.

The content is the proof of the work.

The work is everything that happens before you write a word.

Tweet 3/3:
150 batches.

The model is simple: think seriously, share clearly, build consistently, serve genuinely.

Everything else is tactics layered on top of that foundation.

The foundation is what holds.
