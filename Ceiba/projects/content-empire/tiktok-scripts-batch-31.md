**Script 4**
**Title:** AI tools for people who hate AI tools
**Hook (0-3s):** You tried ChatGPT, got generic output, and gave up. Makes sense.
**Body (3-45s):**
Most people quit AI tools because they tested them wrong. They asked vague questions and got vague answers. Then they concluded the tool was useless.

The tool wasn't useless. The prompt was.

Here's the shift: treat every AI interaction like briefing a contractor. The more specific context you give, the more specific output you get.

Bad prompt: "Write me a product description."
Better prompt: "Write a product description for a $29 PDF guide on eBay dropshipping. The buyer is a 25-35 year old who already sells on eBay but wants to scale. Tone: direct, practical, no hype. 150 words max."

Same tool. Completely different result.

If AI tools have disappointed you, try changing the input before abandoning the tool.
