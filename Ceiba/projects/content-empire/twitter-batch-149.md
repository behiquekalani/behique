**Tweet 6 — 8-tweet thread**

Tweet 1/8:
Here's the email sequence framework I use after every digital product sale.

It's five emails. It runs over 10-14 days. It does three things: reduces refunds, increases results, and warms people for the next offer.

Tweet 2/8:
Email 1: The activation email.
Sent within 15 minutes of purchase.

Not just a delivery receipt. It includes:
- Product access link
- One specific thing to do first (not everything, one thing)
- A sentence about what result to expect and by when

This email sets the frame for the whole relationship.

Tweet 3/8:
Email 2: The quick win email.
Sent day 2.

Walk them through the smallest actionable unit of the product.

The goal: get them a win before they lose momentum.

Early wins are the primary predictor of whether someone finishes a product and comes back for more.

Tweet 4/8:
Email 3: The belief email.
Sent day 3 or 4.

This is the mindset shift.

Most products fail not because the strategy is wrong, but because the buyer carries old assumptions that contradict the new approach.

This email addresses the belief that needs to change for the product to work.

Tweet 5/8:
Email 4: The proof email.
Sent day 5-7.

Share a story. Yours or a customer's.

This email does two things:
1. It reinforces that the method works.
2. It re-engages anyone who went quiet after the first email.

Stories reactivate. Data informs. Use stories here.

Tweet 6/8:
Email 5: The next step email.
Sent day 10-14.

By now they've had time to engage with the product.

This is where you introduce:
- Your second product (if they're ready for the upgrade)
- Your community (if you have one)
- A question asking how it's going (opens a conversation)

Don't sell hard here. Just show them the door.

Tweet 7/8:
What this sequence does:

- Reduces refunds by keeping buyers engaged
- Creates early results which create loyalty
- Warms buyers for future offers without cold pitching
- Positions you as someone who cares about outcomes, not just sales

This is the infrastructure of a real digital product business.

Tweet 8/8:
Most creators send the receipt and disappear.

The ones who build real businesses treat the post-purchase moment as the beginning of the relationship.

Five emails. Start to finish: two weeks.

It's the highest ROI thing you can build after your first product is live.

**Tweet 9 — 3-tweet thread**

Tweet 1/3:
You don't need a 20-email sequence to start.

You need 5 clear, purposeful emails that move a buyer from "just purchased" to "I got a result and I trust this person."

That's the job. Keep it simple.

Tweet 2/3:
Mistakes to avoid in post-purchase email:

- Too many links, too many options
- Overwhelming them with everything the product offers on day 1
- Pitching hard before they've had a single result
- Going silent after day 1

One thing per email. One job per email.

Tweet 3/3:
The test of a good post-purchase sequence:

Would a buyer who never touched the product still feel supported, guided, and seen after going through these emails?

If yes, you've built trust infrastructure.

If no, you've just built a receipt system.
