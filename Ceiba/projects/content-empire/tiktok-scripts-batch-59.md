# Topic: What Nobody Tells You About Digital Products

## Script 59-1: The Real Reason Products Do Not Sell

**Hook (0-3s):**
Your digital product is not selling. The product is probably fine. The problem is something else.

**Body:**
Nobody buys information. They buy transformation.

If your product description talks about what is inside, pages, chapters, frameworks, you have already lost the sale.

People buy because they believe their life will be different after. They are not purchasing a PDF. They are purchasing the version of themselves that has the outcome the PDF promises.

Rewrite every product description to lead with the transformation. What does the person look like on the other side. What problem disappears. What becomes possible.

That is what sells.

**CTA:**
Go rewrite your product page with that frame.

## Script 59-2: The Validation Shortcut

**Hook (0-3s):**
I spent six weeks building a product nobody wanted. Here is what I do instead now.

**Body:**
Validate before you build.

The simplest validation: post about the problem your product solves, not the product itself. If nobody engages, the problem is either not painful enough or you are not reaching the right people.

If people respond, DM them. Ask if they would pay for a solution. Ask what they have already tried.

Then build what they told you they need. Not what you think they should want.

The creators who launch successfully are not smarter. They do their research in public before they build in private.

**CTA:**
Test the pain before you build the solution.

## Script 59-3: Why Low Prices Kill Products

**Hook (0-3s):**
Pricing your product at seven dollars is not a strategy. It is avoidance.

**Body:**
Low prices communicate low value. Customers who pay the least complain the most and implement the least.

When you charge more, the buyer has skin in the game. They are more likely to use it, get results, and leave a review.

The math also matters. To make $5,000 at $7 per product, you need 714 sales. At $97 per product, you need 52 sales.

Which distribution problem would you rather solve.

Price based on the value of the outcome, not the cost of your time or how nervous you are about selling.

**CTA:**
Raise your price. Your product is worth more than you think.

## Script 59-4: The Launch Nobody Talks About

**Hook (0-3s):**
Your first launch will not go the way you planned. That is not failure. That is data.

**Body:**
Most first-time digital product creators expect a launch to be an event. Big revenue day one. Viral moment. Instant proof it works.

The reality: your first launch is a test. You learn what your audience actually responds to, what objections come up, and what part of your messaging is landing.

The creators who quit after a bad first launch were treating a data collection exercise as a final exam.

The creators who succeed treat every launch as a feedback loop. They adjust and relaunch.

Ship it. Learn from it. Ship it again better.

**CTA:**
One bad launch beats zero launches every time.

## Script 59-5: The Product That Sells Itself

**Hook (0-3s):**
The best digital products have something in common. And it is not production quality.

**Body:**
They solve a specific, painful problem for a specific person.

Not a broad guide for everyone. A targeted solution for someone who is frustrated right now and searching for an answer.

When the product is that specific, word of mouth happens naturally. The buyer tells the five other people they know who have the same problem.

Generalist products need marketing to work. Specific products get recommended.

The mistake is trying to reach everyone. A product that helps one type of person solve one type of problem is easier to sell, easier to market, and easier to scale.

**CTA:**
Make it more specific. Not less.
