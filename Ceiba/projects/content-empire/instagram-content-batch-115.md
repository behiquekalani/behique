## Post 1 — The Worst Advice for New Creators in 2026

**Hook:** "Post everywhere" is the fastest way to burn out and build nothing.

**Slide 1:** "Post everywhere" is the fastest way to burn out and build nothing.

**Slide 2:** The advice sounds like scale. It is actually just scatter. Five mediocre presences are worse than one strong one.

**Slide 3:** Every platform has a different content format, algorithm, culture, and audience behavior. You cannot master them all at once.

**Slide 4:** The first 6 months should be spent winning on one platform. Not dabbling on five.

**Slide 5:** Pick the platform where your audience already lives and where your natural content format fits. Double down there first.

**Slide 6:** Master one. Then expand. Follow @behikeai for the full platform hierarchy.

**Caption:** The creator who is everywhere in year one is usually nowhere in year two.

Platform mastery takes time. The algorithm needs to learn you. Your audience needs to find you. Your content style needs to develop through repetition and feedback.

None of that happens when you are producing half-effort content across six platforms simultaneously.

Pick one. Learn it deeply. Build real traction. Then expand from a position of strength.

## Post 2 — The 2026 Platform Hierarchy (Based on ROI for Solopreneurs)

**Hook:** Not all platforms pay off equally for solo creators. Here is the actual hierarchy.

**Slide 1:** Not all platforms pay off equally for solo creators. Here is the actual hierarchy.

**Slide 2:** Tier 1: YouTube. Longest shelf life. Content stays discoverable for years. Highest trust. Best for complex products and high-ticket offers.

**Slide 3:** Tier 2: Instagram. Visual-first. Strong for brand building, product showcasing, and community. Reels driving most new growth.

**Slide 4:** Tier 3: X (Twitter). Best for intellectual authority and B2B relationships. Lower volume, higher intent audience.

**Slide 5:** Tier 4: TikTok. Fastest distribution. Lowest shelf life. Best for top-of-funnel awareness if your audience skews younger.

**Slide 6:** Email is not a platform. It is the destination for all of them. Build it from day one. Follow @behikeai.

**Caption:** The platform hierarchy for solopreneurs in 2026 is shaped by one question: how long does this content keep working after you publish it?

YouTube videos from two years ago still drive sales. A TikTok from last week is already buried.

For a solo creator with limited time, long-shelf-life content is worth more than high-distribution content. A YouTube video that converts for 18 months beats a TikTok that goes viral for 48 hours.

That said, your specific niche and audience matter. If your buyers are on TikTok and you hate YouTube, the hierarchy shifts.

Start with the platform where your content will have the longest useful life and where your audience is most likely to make a purchase. Everything else is secondary.

## Post 3 — Why Instagram Is Still the Best Platform for Product Creators in 2026

**Hook:** Instagram is not dying. It is evolving. And product creators who adapt are winning.

**Slide 1:** Instagram is not dying. It is evolving. And product creators who adapt are winning.

**Slide 2:** The algorithm in 2026 rewards: Reels for discovery, Carousels for saves and shares, Stories for retention and trust.

**Slide 3:** For digital product creators: Reels bring in new followers, Carousels build trust and demonstrate expertise, Stories convert to DMs and link clicks.

**Slide 4:** The mistake: treating Instagram like a portfolio. It is a conversation platform now. The creators winning are having real interactions with real followers.

**Slide 5:** If you sell digital products under $100, Instagram is still the highest-ROI single platform for most niches.

**Slide 6:** The Behike brand runs on Instagram. Follow @behikeai to see the system.

**Caption:** The "Instagram is dead" narrative circulates every year. It is never accurate.

What is true is that the tactics change. Static posts alone do not build accounts anymore. Reels are the primary discovery engine. Carousels drive saves, which signal value to the algorithm. Stories maintain the relationship with people who already follow you.

The system that works in 2026: produce 3 to 5 Reels per week for distribution, 2 to 3 Carousels per week for depth and trust, 5 to 7 Stories per week for proximity and conversion.

That is 10 to 15 pieces of content per week. With AI and batch production, that is 4 to 6 hours total. Very buildable for a solo creator.

Instagram rewards consistency and variety of format. Give it both.

## Post 4 — When to Add a Second Platform (And What to Add)

**Hook:** The right time to expand to a second platform is when the first one is running without you.

**Slide 1:** The right time to expand to a second platform is when the first one is running without you.

**Slide 2:** Running without you means: you have a content batch system that produces 4 weeks of content in 2 days. Your posting is scheduled. Your DMs are manageable.

**Slide 3:** The right second platform is not the most popular one. It is the one that repurposes your existing content most efficiently.

**Slide 4:** Instagram creator adds YouTube: convert carousels into long-form videos, use Reels as YouTube Shorts.

**Slide 5:** Instagram creator adds X: turn carousel slides into thread tweets. Same ideas, different format, different audience.

**Slide 6:** Always repurpose before you recreate. Follow @behikeai for the repurposing framework.

**Caption:** Platform expansion is a leverage decision, not an ambition decision.

The worst way to expand to a second platform is to start creating original content for it from scratch while your first platform is still not running efficiently. That is just spreading thin in a more deliberate way.

The right way is to build a batch production system on platform one that produces more content than you need, then use the surplus to seed platform two.

The content already exists. The effort is in reformatting. A carousel becomes a thread. A Reel becomes a Short. A caption becomes a newsletter intro.

Your job is to design a content factory where the work flows down the pipeline automatically. One production effort. Multiple platform outputs.

## Post 5 — The Only Platform That Matters More Than Social Media

**Hook:** You can build a massive following and still have a fragile business. One platform fixes that.

**Slide 1:** You can build a massive following and still have a fragile business. One platform fixes that.

**Slide 2:** Social media platforms are rented land. You build there. They own the infrastructure. They can change the rules or shut you out at any time.

**Slide 3:** Email is owned land. Your list is yours. You can move it to any platform. No algorithm controls your access to it.

**Slide 4:** The hierarchy: build social first for distribution and trust. Move followers to email. Sell to the email list.

**Slide 5:** Social media is the top of the funnel. Email is the bottom. A business without email is a funnel with no bucket.

**Slide 6:** Every platform strategy should end with the same question: how does this grow my email list? Follow @behikeai.

**Caption:** Platform algorithms change. Platform rules change. Platforms shut down.

In 2026, the social media graveyard includes platforms that once had millions of active creators who built audiences they thought they owned. Those audiences disappeared when the platform did.

Email cannot be taken from you. It moves with you. It does not disappear if a platform changes its algorithm or decides to reduce organic reach.

Every piece of social media content you produce should have a path to your email list. Not every post needs a CTA. But your strategy needs to be oriented toward the destination, not just the distribution.

Social media is how people find you. Email is how they become customers. Build both, but never confuse which one is the actual foundation.
