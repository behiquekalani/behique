## Post 1 — The Writing Tool That Matters Most Is Not an App

**Hook:** The best writing tool you can use costs nothing and takes 30 minutes a day.

**Slide 1:** The Writing Tool That Matters Most Is Not an App

**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Reading. Reading is the tool. Writers who read widely write with more range, more precision, and better intuition for what works.
- Slide 3: Read one long-form piece every morning before you write anything. Essays, journalism, books. Not social media. Not newsletters.
- Slide 4: Your writing absorbs the rhythm, logic structure, and vocabulary of what you read. The input directly shapes the output.
- Slide 5: Grammarly can clean up grammar. Reading builds the instincts that generate ideas worth writing about in the first place.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for writing and content frameworks built for solopreneurs.

**Caption:** Most people looking to improve their writing reach for a tool. There are good tools. Some of them are on this list. But the single highest-leverage writing habit is not software. It is reading. Specifically, reading long-form writing from people who are better than you at it. Essays, journalism, long-form reporting, books that are dense with ideas. The writers you read become the training data for your own voice. Your sentence structure, your argumentation, your transitions, your sense of when to be concise and when to expand. All of it is shaped by what you consume. Read for 30 minutes before you write anything. Do it every day. In three months your writing will sound different. Not because you learned a new grammar rule. Because you absorbed 90 hours of excellent models.

## Post 2 — Hemingway Editor Changes How You See Your Own Writing

**Hook:** Most writers do not realize how much they are padding their sentences until they see it highlighted in red.

**Slide 1:** Hemingway Editor Changes How You See Your Own Writing

**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Hemingway Editor (free, web-based) highlights passive voice, complex sentences, adverbs, and readability grade level in real time.
- Slide 3: Paste your draft in. Look at what lights up. Every highlighted sentence is a place where a reader might lose momentum.
- Slide 4: The goal is not to make everything grade 6. It is to make every sentence earn its complexity. Short sentences create pace. Long sentences build weight.
- Slide 5: Use it as a diagnostic, not a rulebook. Some sentences should be long. The tool tells you which ones to question.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for tools and systems that improve your content without overcomplicating the process.

**Caption:** Hemingway Editor is one of the few free writing tools that actually changes how you see your own drafts. It does not tell you what to think. It shows you where your writing is doing work and where it is coasting. The passive voice highlights alone will shift how you write captions and product copy in the first week. Passive voice creates distance. Active voice creates energy. When you can see the passive constructions highlighted in your draft, you start catching them automatically before you even open the tool. That is the real value: it trains your eye. Use it on every piece of content you write for 30 days and your default style will be cleaner, tighter, and more direct without conscious effort.

## Post 3 — Notion as a Writing System, Not Just a Note App

**Hook:** The problem with most writers is not a lack of ideas. It is that the ideas are scattered and never get used.

**Slide 1:** Notion as a Writing System, Not Just a Note App

**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Build one Notion database for content: every idea, draft, published piece, and related resource in one searchable place.
- Slide 3: When you capture ideas into a system with status tags, nothing falls through the cracks. You always have raw material for your next piece.
- Slide 4: Link ideas to each other. A newsletter topic links to a Twitter thread. A Twitter thread links to a carousel post. One idea becomes five pieces.
- Slide 5: The system removes the blank page problem. You never start from nothing. You start from a bank of pre-filtered ideas.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for solopreneur systems that turn scattered thinking into consistent output.

**Caption:** Most writers use some version of a note-taking app to capture ideas. The problem is that the capture system and the writing system are disconnected. You have a note full of ideas that never become drafts, drafts that never become published pieces, and published pieces that never link back to the ideas they spawned. Notion fixes this when you treat it as a database rather than a notebook. One table. Status column: idea, draft, editing, published. One tag column for topic. One linked field connecting it to your publishing calendar. Now your entire content operation is visible in one place. Ideas become drafts with a status change. Drafts get published with a date stamp. Published pieces get linked to future repurposing opportunities. The system does the thinking for you.

## Post 4 — Claude and ChatGPT Are Drafting Tools, Not Writing Replacements

**Hook:** AI will not make you a better writer. Using AI with editorial judgment will.

**Slide 1:** Claude and ChatGPT Are Drafting Tools, Not Writing Replacements

**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Use AI to produce the ugly first draft. The one with the right structure but wrong voice. Then rewrite it in your own language.
- Slide 3: The skill is prompt engineering. The better your prompt, the less editing you need. Learn to prompt with your voice, your format, your audience.
- Slide 4: AI is best at structure, transitions, and rephrasing. It is worst at nuance, specific experience, and original ideas. Combine both.
- Slide 5: The writers who use AI effectively will outproduce those who do not. The writers who use it as a shortcut will produce forgettable work.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for AI writing frameworks that keep your voice at the center.

**Caption:** The conversation about AI and writing tends toward two extremes. Either AI is going to replace writers entirely, or it is a cheat code for people who cannot write. Neither is accurate. The real frame is: AI is a drafting tool that collapses the blank page problem and handles structural work that is tedious to do manually. What it cannot do is know what you actually think, what experiences have shaped your perspective, or why your specific audience should trust your voice over someone else's. Those things are yours. The workflow that works is: think the idea clearly first, prompt the AI for structure and a rough draft, then rewrite every paragraph in your own language. The output should sound like you. If it sounds like a chatbot, you skipped the rewrite step.

## Post 5 — The Swipe File Is the Oldest Writing Tool and Still the Best

**Hook:** Every great writer keeps a swipe file. Most beginners do not know what one is.

**Slide 1:** The Swipe File Is the Oldest Writing Tool and Still the Best

**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: A swipe file is a collection of writing that stopped you in your tracks. Hooks, headlines, openings, arguments, closing lines. Anything that worked.
- Slide 3: When you need to write something compelling, you do not start from a blank page. You browse your swipe file for the pattern that fits.
- Slide 4: You are not copying. You are borrowing structure. The same hook format works with a hundred different topics.
- Slide 5: Build it over time: a Notion page, a folder in Apple Notes, a saved posts folder on Instagram. Save what stops your scroll.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for content systems that make consistent output feel like a system, not a grind.

**Caption:** Professional copywriters have kept swipe files for decades. The concept is simple: when you encounter writing that works, you save it. Hooks that stop you. Headlines that made you click. Opening paragraphs that pulled you in against your will. Arguments that changed your mind. You are not saving it to plagiarize. You are saving it to study and adapt. When you have 200 pieces of writing that worked, you start to see the patterns. What makes a hook feel urgent. What makes an argument feel inevitable. What makes a CTA feel like the natural next step instead of a sales pitch. You can replicate those patterns with your own content and your own voice. A well-curated swipe file is worth more than most writing courses you could take.
