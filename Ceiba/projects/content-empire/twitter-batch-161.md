**Tweet 7 (6-tweet thread)**
Most people positioned themselves wrong in the attention economy. Here's the framework I use instead.

Thread.

1/6

---

There are two types of attention: borrowed and earned.

Borrowed attention comes from trends, virality, controversy. It spikes and disappears.

Earned attention comes from consistent, specific, useful thinking. It compounds.

Build for earned.

2/6

---

The mistake most solopreneurs make: they chase borrowed attention thinking it'll convert.

It rarely does. Viral posts attract the wrong audience. They come for entertainment. They leave when you try to sell.

3/6

---

Positioning in the attention economy means this: you become the person people think of when a specific problem surfaces in their life.

Not "I follow this person." But "I need to handle X, let me check what this person said."

4/6

---

To get there, you need three things:
1. A specific problem you solve
2. A consistent perspective on that problem
3. Evidence that your perspective works

That's it. The algorithm finds you. You don't have to chase it.

5/6

---

Summary:
- Stop optimizing for reach
- Start optimizing for specificity
- Be the go-to for one problem, for one person
- Document your results

The attention economy rewards position, not presence.

6/6

**Tweet 9 (single)**

**Tweet 10 (3-tweet thread)**
The attention economy is not broken. You're just playing the wrong version of it.

1/3

---

Most people play the reach game: post more, grow faster, reach bigger numbers.

But reach without resonance is noise. You're building an audience that doesn't care.

The better game: depth over width. 100 people who trust you beat 10,000 who scroll past.

2/3

---

Shift the goal from "how many people saw this" to "how many people saved this."

Saves are intent. Saves are trust. Saves are future buyers.

Optimize for saves. The algorithm follows.

3/3
