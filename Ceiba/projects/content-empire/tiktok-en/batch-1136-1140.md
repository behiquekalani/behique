## TikTok 1138: Why Your Audience Doesn't Grow
**Hook (0-3s):** You're posting content. You're not building relationships.
**Duration:** 45 seconds
**Script:**
There's a difference between broadcasting and building. Broadcasting is throwing content into the void and hoping someone cares. Building is going where your audience already is and adding value to conversations they're already having. Comment on posts in your niche. Answer questions in forums. Show up in other people's comment sections with something actually useful. The algorithm rewards engagement. More importantly, people notice who adds value without asking for anything. Be that person consistently. Your audience grows from the relationships you start, not just the content you post.
**On-screen text:** [Broadcasting vs building] [Show up in others' spaces] [Add value, ask nothing] [Relationships drive growth]
**Sound:** Chill, community-feel lo-fi

## TikTok 1140: The Real Cost of Staying Employed Forever
**Hook (0-3s):** A salary feels safe until you realize what you traded for it.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:**
A salary is a trade. You trade your time, your energy, and your ceiling for predictability. For some people, that trade is perfect. For others, it's a slow suffocation. If you feel the second one, pay attention to it. The cost of staying employed forever is not just money. It's the version of yourself that never built anything. Never took the risk. Never found out what you were actually capable of. I'm not saying quit your job tomorrow. I'm saying build something on the side while you have the safety net. Then make the decision with data, not just fear.
**On-screen text:** [Salary = predictability trade] [The cost is more than money] [Build while you still have safety] [Decide with data, not fear]
**Sound:** Reflective, serious ambient
