### Script 1 — Low Price, Low Trust

**HOOK**
When you charged $50, they questioned your ability. When you charged $500, they didn't ask.

**BODY**
Price is a signal before it's a number.

Point 1: Premium buyers associate high prices with high outcomes. A low price suggests low confidence.

Point 2: The client who haggles your $50 rate will also micromanage your work. The client who pays $500 trusts you.

Point 3: The work is often identical. What changes is who you attract.

Your price filters your clients before the first conversation.

### Script 2 — The Price Anchor Shift

**HOOK**
You're not raising your prices. You're correcting them.

**BODY**
Most creators undercharge not because of the market, but because of their own belief about what they're worth.

Point 1: Your rate was set when you had less evidence, less results, and less confidence. It's probably outdated.

Point 2: Premium pricing requires premium positioning. Update your bio, your case studies, your framing.

Point 3: The market doesn't set your price. You do. And then the market responds.

Raise the number. Let the wrong clients leave. Watch what arrives.

### Script 3 — What Premium Clients Actually Buy

**HOOK**
Premium clients are not buying your service. They're buying certainty.

**BODY**
Understanding this changes how you sell.

Point 1: They want to know the outcome, not the process. Lead with results.

Point 2: They want low friction. Complicated onboarding, unclear timelines, and too many options push them away.

Point 3: They want someone who has done this before. Social proof and case studies are not optional at this level.

Point 4: They want to feel like they found you, not like you found them.

Premium positioning creates that dynamic.

### Script 4 — The Discovery Call Mistake

**HOOK**
You're losing premium clients in the first 5 minutes of the call.

**BODY**
The discovery call is not a sales pitch. It's a diagnostic.

Point 1: Ask about their situation before you ever mention your service.

Point 2: Let the silence after their answer breathe. Premium clients respect thoughtful pauses.

Point 3: When you do present your offer, tie every element directly to what they just told you.

Point 4: End by asking if they're ready to move forward, not by sending a follow-up proposal.

Confidence in the close is part of the premium signal.

### Script 5 — Raise the Price, Reduce the Volume

**HOOK**
I took on fewer clients, charged more, and made more money. Here's the math.

**BODY**
Volume-based thinking keeps most solopreneurs underpaid and overworked.

Point 1: 10 clients at $200 is $2,000 and a calendar full of low-trust relationships.

Point 2: 2 clients at $1,500 is $3,000 and space to actually do great work.

Point 3: The quality of your work improves when you have fewer clients. Your results improve. Your case studies improve. Your price goes up again.

Premium pricing is a flywheel, not a one-time adjustment.
