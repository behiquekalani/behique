# TikTok EN — batch-1286

**Topic:** Creator economy — niche down
**Duration:** ~53 seconds

---

Broad topics kill creator businesses.

"I make content about productivity" gets you nothing. "I help night-shift nurses build morning routines that don't destroy their sleep" gets you a community that pays.

Specificity is the unlock. The more specific your niche, the less competition, the higher the trust, and the faster your audience grows.

People scroll past generic advice. They stop and screenshot when something feels like it was written for them specifically.

So here's the exercise. Take your current topic and ask: who exactly is this for? Not "entrepreneurs." Not "people who want to be healthier." Get uncomfortable with how specific you go.

The creator economy rewards precision. General advice is everywhere. Specific solutions are rare.

Pick your specific person this week. Write one piece of content for only them.
