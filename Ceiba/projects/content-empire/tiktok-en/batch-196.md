### Script 1 — The 8-Hour Lie

**HOOK**
You think you work 8 hours a day. You don't. You work about 2.

**BODY**
I tracked every hour for 7 days. The result was uncomfortable.

Point 1: 3 hours go to email, Slack, and notifications — tasks that feel like work but produce nothing.

Point 2: 1.5 hours go to decision fatigue. Choosing what to work on instead of working.

Point 3: 45 minutes go to context switching. Every tab change costs you 23 minutes of focus.

What's left? Real output: maybe 2 hours if you're lucky.

The solopreneur who controls those 2 hours beats the one grinding 10.

### Script 2 — Track Before You Fix

**HOOK**
Stop optimizing your schedule. You haven't measured it yet.

**BODY**
Most productivity advice skips step one: the audit.

Point 1: For 3 days, write down what you actually do in 30-minute blocks. Not what you planned. What happened.

Point 2: Sort each block into three categories: income-generating, growth-building, or noise.

Point 3: Most people discover that 60% of their day is noise wearing a productive costume.

Point 4: You can't cut what you haven't named.

The audit isn't about shame. It's about data.

Point 3: When you see where the hours go, you stop scheduling more and start protecting less.

### Script 4 — The 4 Categories

**HOOK**
There are only 4 things you can do with your time. Most people only know 1.

**BODY**
After years of tracking, every task falls into one of four buckets.

Point 1: Genius work. The 2-3 things only you can do and that directly generate revenue or growth.

Point 2: Support work. Things that need to happen but don't require your brain at full power.

Point 3: Learning work. Input that sharpens your output.

Point 4: Drift. Scrolling, busy admin, reactive tasks.

The goal of a time audit is to push more hours into genius work and out of drift.
