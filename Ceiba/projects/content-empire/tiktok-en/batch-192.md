## Topic: How to find your niche in 24 hours

---

### Script 192-1

**Hook:**
You do not need months to find your niche. You need 24 hours and a pen.

**Body:**
Most people overthink this because they treat niche selection like a permanent tattoo. It is not. It is a starting hypothesis.

Here is the 24-hour framework. List every problem you have personally solved in the last 3 years. Not skills, problems. Problems connect to people who have the same problem.

Then ask: who else has this problem and would pay to skip the part I already figured out? That intersection is your niche.

Pick the one with the most overlap between your experience and someone else's pain. Start there. Refine later.

### Script 192-2

**Hook:**
The reason you cannot find your niche: you are looking at skills, not pain.

**Body:**
Skills are what you can do. Pain is what someone else cannot figure out. The gap between those two things is where money lives.

Step one: write down the 5 most frustrating things you had to figure out on your own. Step two: search who else is complaining about those things online. Reddit, forums, comment sections. Step three: pick the one where you are two steps ahead of the person asking.

Two steps ahead is your niche. You do not need to be an expert. You need to be useful.

This takes 24 hours if you do the work instead of thinking about doing the work.

**CTA:**
Comment the problem you solved that nobody taught you. That might be your niche.

### Script 192-3

**Hook:**
Every niche is taken. That is actually good news.

**Body:**
Beginners think a crowded niche is a dead niche. Wrong. A crowded niche means people are already spending money there. The market is proven.

Your job is not to create a new market. Your job is to serve a specific slice of an existing one.

Narrow the niche until it fits your exact background. Fitness for startup founders. Productivity for homeschool parents. Copywriting for SaaS companies. Each of these is a subset of a crowded market.

Specificity wins because it feels personal. Generic loses because it feels like noise.

Your niche is not what you are good at. It is who you used to be.
