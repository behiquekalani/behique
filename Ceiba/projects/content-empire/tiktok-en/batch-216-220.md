## script-217
**Topic:** How to price a digital product
**Format:** Talking head with on-screen text

[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
You are underpricing your digital product. Here is why.

[BODY - 0:03-0:52]
Most creators price based on what they would pay. That is the wrong question.

Price based on the outcome you deliver. If your product saves someone 10 hours of research, what is that hour worth to them? If your product helps someone close one client, what does that client pay them?

The math reframes everything.

A $27 guide on writing better cold emails sounds cheap. But if it helps someone land a $2,000 project, the ROI is 7,300 percent. Suddenly $47 feels like a discount.

Here is how to price better. Start at the transformation. Work backward. Add a multiplier for your time saved. Price at the low end of that range to build trust fast. Then raise it as social proof accumulates.

The creators stuck at $9 products are not stuck because the market is cheap. They are stuck because they have not connected price to outcome in their own mind.

## script-220
**Topic:** The compounding effect of consistent output
**Format:** Talking head

[HOOK - 0:00-0:03]
Consistency is not a discipline hack. It is a math problem.

[BODY - 0:03-0:50]
Here is how the math works.

Post once a week for a year, you have 52 pieces of content. Each one has a small chance of finding the right person. Each one builds the pattern recognition in the algorithm that your account is active and worth showing.

But here is the part most people miss. The posts are not independent. They compound. Video 52 gets found by someone who then watches videos 1 through 51. A library of 52 videos does not perform like 52 videos. It performs like a catalog.

The creator who posted 50 times with average quality will outperform the one who posted 5 times with perfect quality. Every time. Because distribution is a volume game on the front end and a quality game on the back end.

Start producing before you are ready. Improve while in motion. The people waiting for the perfect post will still be waiting when the person who shipped 200 imperfect ones hits their first $10,000 month.
