# TikTok EN — batch-1299

**Topic:** Creator economy — consistency over quality
**Duration:** ~50 seconds

---

Done beats perfect every single time.

I've watched creators spend three weeks on one video that gets 800 views. Meanwhile, someone posting daily with B-minus content builds 50,000 followers in the same three weeks.

The algorithm rewards consistency. The audience rewards familiarity. Neither rewards perfection.

This doesn't mean post garbage. It means set a quality floor and post above it every time. Don't set a quality ceiling that keeps you from posting.

The creator who posts 365 times a year improves more, reaches more people, and learns the algorithm faster than the creator who posts 12 perfect pieces.

Volume beats precision at the start. You can't optimize what you don't publish.

Set your quality floor. Post consistently above it. Every day.

The compound effect kicks in at month three. Most people quit at month two.

Follow to stay in the game.
