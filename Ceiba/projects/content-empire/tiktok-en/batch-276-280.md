**Script 279**

[Hook — 0:00-0:03]
Price anchoring is not a trick. It is a communication strategy.

[Body — 0:03-0:50]
When someone sees your price with no context, they evaluate it against nothing. That means they compare it to zero, which always feels expensive. Price anchoring gives context. You show a higher option first, which recalibrates what the buyer considers reasonable. You show what the problem costs if left unsolved, which reframes your solution as an investment. You compare your price to something the buyer already spends money on routinely. None of this is manipulation. It is helping the buyer understand value in a frame that makes sense. The creator who never anchors their price is making the buyer do all the work of justifying the purchase. Most buyers will not do that work. They will just leave. Anchoring does the framing for them.

[CTA — 0:50-0:57]
Look at your product page. Add one anchor before the price. A comparison, a cost of the problem, or a higher tier.
