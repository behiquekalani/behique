## Video 621
**Hook (0-3s):** "The real reason your content gets no engagement."
**Type:** Talking head
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:** It's not the algorithm. It's not your follower count. It's not even the time you post. The real reason your content gets no engagement is that it doesn't make people feel anything. Safe content gets ignored. Content that challenges, surprises, or validates a deeply held belief gets shared. Ask yourself before every post: does this make someone laugh, think, or feel less alone? If the answer is no, rewrite it. You're not trying to inform people. You're trying to move them. Movement is what creates engagement. Emotion drives action.

## Video 622
**Hook (0-3s):** "What a $27 product taught me about pricing psychology."
**Type:** Voiceover
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** I launched the same product twice. First at $47. Then at $27 after it stalled. The $27 version outsold the $47 version five to one. Not because people wanted cheap. Because $27 feels like a no-brainer and $47 feels like a decision. The threshold between impulse buy and considered purchase sits right around $30 for most digital products. Below that line, people don't need to think. Above it, they want more proof. Know where your product sits and build your sales page accordingly. Price is not just a number. It's a psychological signal.
**Caption:** What a $27 product taught me about pricing psychology. #pricingpsychology #digitalproducts #creatorbusiness #onlinebusiness #behike

## Video 623
**Hook (0-3s):** "You don't need more motivation. You need a better system."
**Type:** Talking head
**Duration:** 45s
**Script:** Motivation is unreliable. It shows up when things are new and exciting, then vanishes when the work gets boring and repetitive. If your content strategy depends on feeling inspired, you will post inconsistently forever. The solution is not more motivation. It's a system that removes the decision of whether to post. Batch your content on Sundays. Use templates. Set your posting time the same every day. Systems work when motivation doesn't. Build the system when you're motivated, so it runs when you're not.
**Caption:** You don't need more motivation. Build a system. #productivity #contentcreation #creatortips #systemsthinking #behike

## Video 624
**Hook (0-3s):** "I stopped posting for two weeks. Here's what happened to my growth."
**Type:** Talking head
**Duration:** 60s
**Script:** I took two weeks off from posting. No content, no stories, no engagement. I came back expecting to restart from zero. The numbers surprised me. My follower count barely moved. Saves and profile visits on old posts kept coming in. The content I had already made was still working. That taught me two things. First, old content has a longer life than most creators think. Second, the best thing you can do before a break is batch strong content so it runs on schedule while you're gone. Build the machine. Then take the break.
**Caption:** I took 2 weeks off. Here's what happened to my numbers. #contentcreator #socialmedia #creatortips #batching #behike

## Video 625
**Hook (0-3s):** "Every broke creator makes this same mistake with their content."
**Type:** Voiceover
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:** They create for vanity metrics. Views, likes, followers. Those feel good but they don't pay. The creators making real money optimize for one thing: clicks to their offer. Every piece of content should do one of three things. Build trust. Showcase authority. Or drive someone toward a product or email list. If your content does none of those three things, you're entertaining an audience, not building a business. Entertainment is fine as a strategy. But it only pays when you have a monetization layer behind it. Know your content's job before you make it.
