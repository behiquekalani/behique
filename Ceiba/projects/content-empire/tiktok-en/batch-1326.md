**Topic:** Nobody tells you this about freelancing

---

Freelancing truth that took me way too long to learn.

Your rate isn't about what you need. It's about what the outcome is worth to the client.

If you help someone generate $50K, charging $500 is not "fair." It's cheap. And cheap signals low confidence.

Stop pricing based on hours. Start pricing based on results.

"I charge $1,500 to set up your email system" beats "I charge $25 an hour" every single time. One is a commodity. The other is a solution.

Raise your rate. Watch the quality of clients go up.

Follow for real business thinking, no fluff.
