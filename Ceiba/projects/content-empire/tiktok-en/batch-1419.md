**Topic:** How to Write Copy That Sells

---

Copy that sells. Four things that make the difference.

Talk to one person. Not everyone. Pick one specific person with one specific problem and write directly to them. Generic copy gets ignored.

Lead with the outcome, not the feature. They do not care what your product does. They care what their life looks like after they use it.

Use specific numbers. "Save time" does nothing. "Cut your content planning from 3 hours to 20 minutes" stops the scroll.

End with urgency that is real. Not fake countdown timers. A real reason to act now. Limited spots, a bonus expiring, a price going up.

These four rules wrote more sales for me than any tool or tactic. Master them before adding anything else.

Good copy is not clever. It is clear.
