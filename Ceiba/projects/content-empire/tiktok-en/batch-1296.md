Stop managing your time. Manage your energy.

You have 24 hours a day. But not every hour is the same. A forced 6 AM work session when you're a night person produces mediocre work. Two hours at your peak time produces exceptional work.

Figure out when you're sharpest. Most people know but ignore it because of social pressure to be a morning person. Forget that.

Schedule your hardest, most creative tasks at your peak. Schedule admin, emails, and low-brain tasks when your energy dips.

Three hours at peak beats eight hours fighting your biology.

This one change can double your output without adding a single hour to your day.

Track your energy for one week. Notice the pattern. Then redesign your schedule around it.

Stop fighting your own biology. Work with it.
