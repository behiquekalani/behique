**Script 2:**
Hook: This pricing mistake is costing you sales and clients.
Body: The mistake is anchoring your price to what you're comfortable asking for instead of what the solution is worth. Your discomfort with a higher number is not market data. The market doesn't care how you feel about your price. It cares about the result relative to the cost. If your product or service helps someone generate ten thousand dollars, charging five hundred for it is not expensive. It's a ten-to-one return. If it saves someone forty hours over a month, what is that time worth to them. Price to the value of the result. Not to your confidence level at this moment. Your confidence will grow. Your price should reflect the value you deliver today, not the confidence you'll have in two years.
