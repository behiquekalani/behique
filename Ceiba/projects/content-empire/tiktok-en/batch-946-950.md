## TikTok 947
**Hook:** Your niche is too broad. That's why you're invisible.
**On-screen text:** The niching problem | The riches in the niches | How narrow to go

"Content creator helping entrepreneurs" is not a niche. It's a category.

Here's the test. If ten thousand people could describe their job the same way, you're not niche enough.

Go narrow. Then go narrower.

Not "helping entrepreneurs grow online." Try "helping solo consultants book clients without posting on social media."

One type of person. One specific problem. One promised result. That's a niche.

The counter-intuitive truth: the more specific you get, the more people feel like you're talking directly to them. Specific content gets shared. Generic content gets ignored.

And niches don't limit your income. They increase it. Specialists charge more than generalists. Always.

Write your niche statement in this format. I help [specific person] achieve [specific result] without [specific frustration].

That statement is your brand in one sentence.

The difference is not talent. Not luck. Not connections.

It's calls to action.

The paid creator asks for the sale. Directly. Clearly. Every single time. "Click the link. Buy the guide. Book a call."

## TikTok 949
**Hook:** You don't need a brand deal. Build your own offer first.
**On-screen text:** Brand deal trap | Your own offer > sponsorship | Do this instead

Brand deals feel like winning. They're actually a ceiling.

You spend months building an audience. A brand offers you five hundred dollars. You take it because it feels like validation. Now you're creating content for someone else's product.

Here's what that deal cost you. Your audience's trust. Your content direction. Three to five posts you could have used to sell your own thing.

Before you take any brand deal, ask this question. Do I have an offer of my own that I'm promoting consistently? If the answer is no, fix that first.

A one hundred dollar digital product sold to five percent of your audience every month beats any sponsorship deal. You keep the margin. You control the message. You own the relationship.

Brand deals are a feature of a mature business. Not the foundation of one.

Build your offer. Prove it converts. Then add sponsorships on top.
