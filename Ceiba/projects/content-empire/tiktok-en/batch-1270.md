**Topic:** Pricing your knowledge correctly
**Duration:** 45-60 seconds

---

You're underpricing yourself. Almost guaranteed.

Most first-time creators price out of fear. $7 for a guide. $12 for a course. Priced to avoid rejection, not to reflect value.

Here's the truth: Low prices don't make it easier to sell. They make people trust the product less. A $7 guide feels like an afterthought. A $47 guide feels like a real resource.

Price based on the result you're delivering, not the hours you spent making it.

If your product saves someone ten hours of struggle, what is ten hours worth to them? Price accordingly.

Start at $27. Test. Raise to $47. Test again. Most creators find the sweet spot around $37 to $97 for entry-level products.

You're not too expensive. You're under-positioned.
