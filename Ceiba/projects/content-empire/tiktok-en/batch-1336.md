**Topic:** The focus trick that beats any timer

---

Forget the Pomodoro timer. This works better.

Before you start a work block, write down the one specific output you want at the end. Not "work on project." Write "finish the introduction section" or "reply to 5 emails."

A clear output pulls your attention forward. A vague task lets your brain drift.

The timer keeps time. The defined output keeps focus.

When you know exactly what done looks like, distractions become obvious. You notice when you're off track because you know where the track is.

Define the output first. Start the work second.

Follow for focus methods that actually cut through noise.
