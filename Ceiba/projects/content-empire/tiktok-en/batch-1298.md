**Topic:** Solopreneur — saying no

Every yes costs you a no somewhere else.

When you're a solopreneur, your time is finite and your focus is the business. Every client project you don't want, every collaboration that doesn't fit, every request that pulls you off your path is stealing from the thing you're actually building.

Learning to say no is not just a mindset exercise. It's a business survival skill.

The filter I use: does this move me closer to my one-year goal or away from it? If the answer isn't a fast yes, it's a no.

Harsh? Maybe. Effective? Completely.

The solopreneurs who build the fastest are the ones who protect their attention like money. Because it is money.

Audit your commitments this week. What's on your list that doesn't belong there?

Cut it. Your real work is waiting.
