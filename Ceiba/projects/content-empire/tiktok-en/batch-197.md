### Script 1 — The Wrong Order

**HOOK**
You built the product first. That's why it didn't sell.

**BODY**
Most solo builders have the sequence backwards.

Point 1: A product without an audience is a solution looking for a problem no one told you they had.

Point 2: An audience tells you what they want before you build it. You're not guessing. You're responding.

Point 3: Distribution is harder than creation. A post from a trusted creator converts. A post from a stranger gets ignored.

Build the relationship first. The product becomes the natural next step.

### Script 3 — Your Audience is Market Research

**HOOK**
The best product research is just talking to your audience for 6 months.

**BODY**

Point 1: The comments told me the exact pain points I would have missed if I'd built in silence.

Point 2: The questions people asked became my sales page copy, almost word for word.

Point 3: When I launched, people recognized themselves in the product description because they'd written it without knowing.

Audience first is not a delay tactic. It's the most efficient path.

### Script 5 — The Cold Launch Problem

**HOOK**
Most product launches fail in the first 72 hours. Here's why.

**BODY**
A cold launch means you're asking strangers for money before you've given them a reason to trust you.

Point 1: Trust is built through consistent value delivery over time. One post doesn't do it.

Point 2: The algorithm rewards engagement. An audience that already knows you will engage. Cold traffic won't.

Point 3: The difference between a $0 launch and a $2,000 launch is almost always the relationship you built before the link went live.

Audience is the asset. The product is the conversion event.
