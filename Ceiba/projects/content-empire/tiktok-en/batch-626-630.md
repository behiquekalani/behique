**Script:** Creators blame the algorithm for everything. Bad reach. Low views. Slow growth. But the algorithm is doing exactly what it is designed to do. ## Video 628
**Hook (0-3s):** "How to turn one idea into a month of content."
**Type:** Voiceover
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** One idea has more content inside it than you think. Take the idea "email lists are more valuable than social followers." That's a TikTok. A Twitter thread. A LinkedIn post. A short YouTube. A newsletter issue. A product about email list building. Six pieces of content from one insight. The framework is called content atomization. You create the core idea once, then distribute it across every format and platform. This is how full-time creators stay consistent without burning out. They don't have more ideas. They squeeze more from each one.

## Video 629
**Hook (0-3s):** "What your most successful post has in common with every viral video ever."
**Type:** Talking head
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:** Every viral video, no matter the niche, does one of four things. It challenges a commonly held belief. It shows a surprising result. It validates something the audience secretly thinks. Or it gives immediately actionable information. Your most successful post probably hit at least one of those. Most creators produce content that does none of them. They inform without challenging. They share without surprising. They list without validating. Study your best post. Find the mechanic. Repeat it across different topics and formats. You already know what works. Use it again.
**Caption:** Every viral video has this in common. Study your best post. #viralcontent #contentcreation #creatortips #socialmedia #behike
