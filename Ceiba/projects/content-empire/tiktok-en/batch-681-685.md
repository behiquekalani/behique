## TikTok 681: The Three-Hour Creator Workday
**Hook (0-3s):** I work three hours a day on my creator business. That's not a flex, it's a design.
**Body (3-45s):** Hour one: create. Write, record, think. Hour two: distribute. Post, repurpose, schedule. Hour three: build. Product, system, funnel. Everything else is noise. The creators who work 12-hour days are usually filling the hours with tasks that don't move the needle. Three focused hours beats eight distracted ones. Design your day around outputs, not effort.

## TikTok 682: Your Price is a Message
**Hook (0-3s):** Your price doesn't just set revenue. It sets expectations.
**Body (3-45s):** A $7 product says "I don't fully believe in this." A $97 product says "this will change something for you." Same content. Completely different buyer psychology. Cheap prices attract people who argue about refunds. Right prices attract people who implement and get results. Price your product based on the transformation it delivers, not the hours you spent making it. Value is not cost-plus.

## TikTok 683: The Only Metric That Matters
**Hook (0-3s):** Followers, views, likes. None of those pay rent. This does.
**Body (3-45s):** Revenue per subscriber. That's the metric. A creator with 1,000 true buyers earns more than one with 100,000 passive followers. Vanity metrics feel good. Revenue metrics build freedom. Every piece of content you make should have a job: attract someone, build trust, or point toward a purchase. If it doesn't do one of those three, why are you making it?
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow if you care about revenue, not just reach.
**Caption:** Stop optimizing for followers. Optimize for buyers. #creatortips #creatoreconomy #moneymindset #buildingonline #digitalproducts
