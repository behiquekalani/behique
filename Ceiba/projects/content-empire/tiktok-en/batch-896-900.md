## TikTok 900
**Hook:** Most creators fail at month three. This is why.
**On-screen text:** [MONTH 1: EXCITED] → [MONTH 2: GRINDING] → [MONTH 3: GONE]

Month one. Everything is exciting. You post every day. The algorithm rewards new accounts. You see some early traction. You're hooked.

Month two. Harder. Growth slows. Some posts flop. You start comparing your numbers to creators with three years of posts. Doubt creeps in.

Month three. The excitement is gone. The discipline wasn't built. You post less. Then stop entirely. The account dies quietly.

This cycle is not a talent problem. It's an expectation problem.

Real traction on most platforms takes six to twelve months of consistent output. Not thirty days. Not sixty. Six to twelve. The creators who win simply outlast the ones who quit at month three.

So set your expectation correctly. Month three is where you earn the right to month twelve. It's not a sign to stop. It's a test.

Most people fail it. That's why it works for the ones who don't.
