## script-209
**Topic: Creator Systems**
**Hook type: Story opening**

---

[0-3s HOOK]
Six months ago I had 300 followers and zero systems. Here's what I actually changed.

[BODY]
Not consistency. Not aesthetics. Not niche.

Systems.

Let me walk you through what a creator system actually looks like when it works.

Capture layer: every idea that comes to you gets dropped somewhere immediately. Doesn't matter if it's voice memo, a note, a voice-to-text. The point is nothing gets lost.

Processing layer: once a week, you take those raw ideas and find the ones with tension — the ones where you have a real opinion, not just information.

Production layer: you build a template for your format and stick to it. Same structure every time. Hook, point, proof, CTA. You're not starting from scratch each post.

Distribution layer: each piece of content gets repurposed into at least two formats. One long, one short. One for search, one for feed.

The reason most creators stall is they're doing all four layers in real time, inside every single piece of content. That's not creation. That's chaos.

Build the layers. Separate the steps. Output goes up. Stress goes down.

## script-210
**Topic: Income Diversification**
**Hook type: Data-driven opener**

---

[0-3s HOOK]
One income stream is a liability, not a foundation.

[BODY]
I want to push back on the "focus on one thing" advice that gets passed around constantly.

Focusing on one skill is smart. Focusing on one income source is fragile.

Here's how I think about diversification as a solopreneur:

There are three types of income worth building in parallel.

Active income: services, consulting, done-for-you work. High cash flow, high effort.

Passive income: digital products, templates, courses. Lower effort per sale, scales without your time.

Compounding income: content that keeps working — YouTube, newsletters, SEO. Takes longest to build, highest long-term leverage.

The mistake people make is thinking you have to pick one. You don't. You stack them in order.

Start with active income to fund your time. Use that time to build passive products. Use those products to fund the creation of compounding content.

Each layer funds the next. That's not diversification for its own sake. That's architecture.
