## TikTok 702: How to Write a Hook That Stops the Scroll
**Hook (0-3s):** Your first sentence either earns attention or loses it forever.
**Body (3-45s):**
On TikTok you have three seconds. That's it.
The best hooks do one of three things: make a bold claim, ask a burning question, or show a surprising result.
Wrong: "Today I want to talk about content creation."
Right: "I made $4,000 from one TikTok with 800 views. Here's how."
The hook isn't about you. It's about what the viewer gets for staying.
Lead with value or lead with curiosity. Never lead with your agenda.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this and rewrite your next hook before you post.
**Caption:** Three seconds to earn attention. Here's how to win them. #contenttips #tiktoktips #contentcreator #writingtips #creatorlife

## TikTok 704: What Your Niche Actually Means
**Hook (0-3s):** "Find your niche" is terrible advice. Here's what they actually mean.
**Body (3-45s):**
Your niche is not a topic. It's a person.
Not "productivity" but "burnt-out college students who want to study smarter."
Not "fitness" but "new dads trying to stay consistent with 20-minute workouts."
The more specific the person, the faster they find you and trust you.
Broad topics have broad competition. Specific people have specific problems. Specific problems pay specific money.
Niche down until it feels uncomfortably specific. That's usually where the money is.
