## TikTok 1206: The Compound Effect of Daily Reps
**Hook (0-3s):** Small work every day beats big work sometimes.
**Duration:** 45 seconds
**Script:** A post a day for a year is 365 pieces of content. Most people don't post 365 times in five years. That gap is not about talent. It's about daily reps compounding over time. Every time you ship something, you get a tiny bit better. Every time you get feedback, you adjust. Every time you push past discomfort, your threshold rises. The people crushing it online are not more creative. They just showed up when it was boring. When nobody was watching. When it felt pointless. That's the actual work. Do it anyway. Let the compound effect do the rest.
**On-screen text:** "365 posts beats sporadic brilliance" / "Ship when it feels pointless" / "Adjust from feedback" / "Compound is quiet but real"
**Sound:** Steady, building instrumental

## TikTok 1207: AI Will Not Replace You
**Hook (0-3s):** AI is not stealing your job. It's revealing what you're actually worth.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:** The fear about AI is backwards. AI doesn't replace people who think. It replaces people who only execute. If your value is clicking buttons and following instructions, yes, something is coming for that. But if your value is judgment, creativity, relationships, original ideas. AI makes you faster, not obsolete. The people panicking about AI are the same people who never invested in building real skills. The people thriving are using AI as a multiplier on work they already knew how to do. The question isn't whether AI will take your job. It's whether you've built something it can't replace.
**On-screen text:** "AI replaces execution, not thinking" / "Judgment stays human" / "Use AI as a multiplier" / "Build what it can't replace"
**Sound:** Sharp, modern instrumental

## TikTok 1208: The Real Reason You're Not Making Money Online
**Hook (0-3s):** You're not broke. You're invisible.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** Nobody knows you exist. That's the actual problem. Not your product. Not your price. Not your skills. You have all of those. But you haven't shown up enough times in enough places for people to find you, trust you, and buy from you. Visibility solves most online business problems. When people know you exist, they can decide if they like you. When they like you, they follow. When they follow long enough, they buy. The funnel is simple. What breaks it is inconsistency. You post for a week. Nothing happens. You stop. That's where everyone quits. Keep going for six months and the story changes.
**On-screen text:** "Problem: invisibility, not skill" / "Trust takes time and reps" / "Six months changes everything" / "Consistency is the strategy"
**Sound:** Calm beat that builds slightly

## TikTok 1209: Build in Public Without Oversharing
**Hook (0-3s):** Building in public doesn't mean sharing everything.
**Duration:** 45 seconds
**Script:** Building in public is one of the most powerful things you can do as a creator. But most people do it wrong. They either overshare personal chaos or they only share wins. Neither builds real trust. What works is sharing the process. The decisions. The problems you're solving. The things you figured out this week. Not the emotional lows. Not the highlights. The actual work, narrated in real time. This is what creates an audience that feels like they're on the journey with you. They root for you. They buy when you launch. Start documenting the process, not just the outcomes.
**On-screen text:** "Share the process, not the chaos" / "Document decisions and problems" / "Audience = co-travelers" / "Narrate the real work"
**Sound:** Warm, focused lo-fi

## TikTok 1210: The Student-to-Builder Shift
**Hook (0-3s):** Stop being a student. Start being a builder.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:** There's a point where taking another course becomes the problem, not the solution. You have enough information. You need to make something. The student mindset says learn more before you start. The builder mindset says start and learn from what breaks. Most people are stuck in permanent student mode because building feels uncomfortable. You might fail publicly. People might not care. The thing might not work. All of that is true. It's also how you actually get good. Every builder you admire spent years making things that didn't work. That's the curriculum. You can't skip it by buying more courses.
**On-screen text:** "More courses = more delay" / "Builder mindset: start and learn" / "Public failure is the curriculum" / "You have enough info to start"
**Sound:** Direct, slightly energetic beat
