# TikTok EN — batch-1287

**Topic:** Digital products — pricing psychology
**Duration:** ~50 seconds

---

Cheap prices attract broke clients.

This sounds harsh but it's true. When you price a digital product at $3, you signal that it's not serious. The people who buy it often don't use it, don't get results, and don't come back.

Price it at $27 or $47 and something shifts. Buyers take it more seriously. They implement it. They get results. They buy your next thing.

The sweet spot for a first digital product is $17 to $47. Not $3. Not $297 unless you have case studies to back it up.

Here's the rule: your price should match the transformation you're promising. If it saves someone five hours of work, it's worth at least $27. If it helps them make their first $500, it's worth $47.

Stop undervaluing what you've built.

Raise the price. Watch the quality of your buyers change.
