# TikTok EN — Batch 203: The Digital Product That Writes Itself

---

## Script 203-1

**Hook:**
The best digital product you will ever make is already inside your head. You just have not extracted it yet.

**Body:**
- A self-writing digital product starts with one question: what do people keep asking you about?
- Those repeated questions are a product outline. Every question is a chapter.
- You are not creating knowledge. You are packaging what you already know.
- The extraction process takes a weekend. The product sells indefinitely.

**CTA:**
Follow if you want to turn what you already know into a product that generates income while you are offline.

## Script 203-2

**Hook:**
I built a digital product in 48 hours without writing a single word from scratch. Here is how.

**Body:**
- I audited 90 days of content. Questions in comments became a FAQ document.
- FAQ document became a table of contents. Table of contents became a product outline.
- Each section was written by expanding an answer I had already given publicly.
- The product existed in the conversation before I packaged it.

**CTA:**
Follow and I will walk you through the full extraction process this week.

## Script 203-3

**Hook:**
You do not need a course platform, a team, or a large following to sell a digital product this week.

**Body:**
- A PDF sold through a single link is a viable product. It has been for years.
- The barrier is not technical. The barrier is the belief that your knowledge is not worth selling.
- Solve one specific problem for one specific person. Package it. Price it.
- You do not need 10,000 followers. You need 10 people to pay $27.

**CTA:**
Follow for the no-overhead digital product model. Simple, direct, profitable.

## Script 203-4

**Hook:**
The digital product market rewards specificity, not breadth. Here is the narrow niche principle.

**Body:**
- A guide called "How to Build a Profitable Newsletter" is too broad.
- A guide called "How a Freelance Designer Gets 3 New Clients Per Month from a 500-Person Newsletter" sells.
- Specificity creates resonance. Broad topics create skepticism.
- The more narrow the promise, the more the right person believes it applies to them.

**CTA:**
Follow and I will show you how to niche down your product idea without shrinking the market.

---

## Script 203-5

**Hook:**
Most digital products fail not at creation. They fail at positioning. One sentence can fix that.

**Body:**
- Your product needs a positioning sentence: For [specific person] who [specific problem] so they can [specific outcome].
- Every word in that sentence filters out wrong buyers and attracts right ones.
- Wrong buyers create refund requests and bad reviews. Right buyers create testimonials.
- Spend more time on the sentence than on the cover design.

**CTA:**
Follow for digital product positioning strategy. Write the sentence first. Build the product second.
