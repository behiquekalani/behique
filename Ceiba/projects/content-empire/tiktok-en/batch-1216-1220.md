## TikTok 1216: How to Price Your Knowledge
**Hook (0-3s):** You're undercharging. Not because you're bad, because you're afraid.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:** The number one pricing mistake people make is asking what feels comfortable instead of asking what the result is worth. If your knowledge saves someone ten hours a week, it's worth more than $20. If your system helps someone make their first sale, it's worth more than a coffee. The price you charge signals the value you believe you're delivering. Low prices attract people who don't take action and then blame your product. Higher prices attract people who are serious. Raise your price. Watch the quality of your customers improve. You're not overcharging. You're still undercharging.
**On-screen text:** "Price = signal of value" / "Low price attracts skeptics" / "Charge what the result is worth" / "You're still undercharging"

## TikTok 1218: Identity Over Goals
**Hook (0-3s):** Goals don't change behavior. Identity does.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:** Setting a goal to make money online doesn't make you someone who builds things. Deciding you're a builder does. The goal is an outcome. The identity is what drives daily behavior. When you identify as a creator, you create even when there's no reward yet. When you identify as a builder, you ship even when nobody's watching. Goals are things you aim at. Identity is who you become in the process of aiming. The shortcut isn't better goals. It's choosing who you're going to be and letting the work follow from that choice. Pick the identity first. The results come after.
**On-screen text:** "Goals = targets. Identity = engine" / "Behavior follows identity" / "Who are you becoming?" / "Identity first, results second"
**Sound:** Thoughtful, mid-tempo instrumental

## TikTok 1219: The 24-Hour Product Challenge
**Hook (0-3s):** You can build something worth selling in 24 hours.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** You don't need months to create a digital product. You need one focused day. Hour one: pick one specific problem you know how to solve. Hours two through four: outline the solution in steps. Hours five through eight: write it, record it, or build it. Hour nine: set up a simple payment link. Hour ten: tell people about it. That's it. The product doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to solve one problem clearly. Everything else is polish you can add after someone buys. The biggest mistake is waiting until it's perfect before it exists. Get it out. Improve it from feedback.
**On-screen text:** "One problem, one day, one product" / "Payment link beats perfect product" / "Sell first, polish after" / "Ship today. Improve tomorrow."
**Sound:** Energetic, focused production beat

## TikTok 1220: What Nobody Tells You About Building Alone
**Hook (0-3s):** Building alone is hard. Nobody tells you about the invisible tax.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** When you're building solo, there's no coworker to reality-check your ideas. No manager to tell you what's worth doing. No team meeting to give you structure. You make every decision alone. That freedom is real and it costs you something. The invisible tax is the mental load of choosing every single day what matters most. Most solo builders don't burn out from working too much. They burn out from deciding too much. The fix is structure. A weekly review. A simple decision filter. A list of what matters this week and nothing else. Not complicated. Just deliberate. Build the structure that replaces the team.
**On-screen text:** "Solo building = decision fatigue" / "Freedom costs mental load" / "Weekly review = your team meeting" / "Build the structure yourself"
**Sound:** Honest, grounded lo-fi
