## Topic: The 3 digital products that sell the most

---

### Script 193-1

**Hook:**
Three digital products consistently outsell everything else. Most creators ignore two of them.

**Body:**
Number one: templates. People want to skip the blank page. Give them a starting point and they will pay for it without hesitation. Notion templates, Canva packs, spreadsheet systems. All of it sells.

Number two: short, specific guides. Not 300-page ebooks. A 15-page PDF that solves one specific problem completely. Specific beats comprehensive every time.

Number three: cohort courses or workshops. Not pre-recorded libraries. A live, time-limited event where people pay for accountability and community, not just information.

All three share one trait: they solve a clear, immediate problem.

### Script 193-2

**Hook:**
You are selling the wrong digital product. Here is what actually moves.

**Body:**
The mistake most beginners make is building what they want to teach instead of what the market wants to learn.

Templates sell because they collapse time. The buyer gets a finished structure without the work of building from zero.

Specific guides sell because they feel like the exact answer to a specific search. Broad ebooks sit unread. Focused PDFs get read the same day they are downloaded.

Live workshops sell because people pay for accountability. The same content delivered live converts better than the same content on demand, because live has a deadline.

Pick the format that matches how your audience prefers to consume.

### Script 193-3

**Hook:**
The digital product that sells with zero audience: templates.

**Body:**
Templates are the best entry-level digital product because they do not require authority. They require usefulness.

A brand new creator can build a Notion dashboard for freelancers and sell it on Gumroad before they have 100 followers. The product speaks for itself because the value is immediately visible.

Templates also have low refund rates. Buyers see exactly what they are getting before they buy. There is no expectation gap.

If you have zero audience and want to validate a product idea fast, build a template. Solve one workflow. Price it between $9 and $27. Put it up.

### Script 193-4

**Hook:**
A $27 PDF can outsell a $997 course. Here is the math.

**Body:**
High price does not mean high volume. A course at $997 needs 10 sales to hit $10K. A guide at $27 needs 370 sales. Both are possible. Different audiences, different decision speeds.

Low-ticket digital products convert faster because the risk for the buyer is lower. They buy on impulse with confidence. High-ticket requires trust, proof, and usually a sales call.

The strategy that works: start with a $27 product to build buyers. Then offer the $997 product to people who already bought and trust you.

The $27 PDF funds your marketing. The $997 course funds your business.

### Script 193-5

**Hook:**
The third most profitable digital product nobody is talking about: workshops.

**Body:**
Pre-recorded courses have a completion rate of about 5%. Workshops have a completion rate closer to 80%. Because people show up when they paid and there is a set time.

A 2-hour live workshop delivered three times a year can generate more revenue than a course you spent months building, because it requires no ongoing marketing. You announce it, people buy, you deliver.

The preparation is minimal compared to a course. You teach what you know in a structured session. You answer questions live. People feel like they got more value than they paid for.

Then record it and sell the replay as a product.
