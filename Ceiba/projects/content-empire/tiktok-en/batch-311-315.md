## Script 312
**Topic:** Solopreneur systems — The 3-hour work week for content

**Hook (0-3 sec):**
I know a creator who posts every day and works 3 hours a week on content.
Not automation. A system.

**Body:**
Most creators work in scattered blocks.
Open phone. Think of something. Film it. Post it. Repeat tomorrow.

That model burns you out and keeps your output low.

Here is what a system looks like instead.

Monday: 90 minutes. Record 7 short videos in one block.
Tuesday: 30 minutes. Write captions for all 7.
Wednesday through Sunday: scheduled posts go out automatically.

That is it.

The insight is that creation and distribution are different modes of work.
When you batch creation, you get into a flow state faster and produce better content.
When you automate distribution, you free your attention for everything else.

The system did not take long to build.
It took one afternoon to set up and runs itself now.

## Script 315
**Topic:** Money — Why passive income is real but takes active building first

**Hook (0-3 sec):**
"Passive income" is real.
But the passive part comes after a lot of active work.
Here is the honest version.

**Body:**
The phrase passive income has been so misused that most people either believe it is a scam or a shortcut.
Neither is accurate.

Passive income is deferred active income.
You do the work once. The asset keeps producing after.

A digital product takes real time to create.
Writing it, designing it, building the sales page, writing the emails, setting up the funnel.
That is 20 to 60 hours of work upfront.

After that, a sale can happen at 3am while you are asleep.
That is the passive part.

The mistake is expecting passive without doing the active.
Or giving up after the active phase before the passive phase starts working.

The asset needs to exist before it can produce.
The audience needs to exist before the asset can sell.

Both take time. Neither is a shortcut.
But both are real if you build them correctly.
