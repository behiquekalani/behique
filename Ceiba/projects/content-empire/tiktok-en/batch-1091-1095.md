## Script 1091

**Hook:** Reading without doing is just entertainment with extra steps.

**Duration:** 40-50 seconds

**Script:**
Books don't change your life.
Action changes your life.
Books are just compressed action plans that require execution.
Point one: Most people finish a book and feel like they've done something. They haven't.
Point two: The test of whether you understood a book is whether your behavior changes.
Point three: Read one book. Apply one idea. Then read the next.
Reading ten books and applying nothing is just an expensive hobby.
Apply first. Collect knowledge second.
Follow for the unsexy version of self-education.

**On-screen text:**
- "reading without doing"
- "= entertainment"
- "apply 1 idea per book"

**Sound:** Minimal lo-fi, dry and direct

## Script 1092

**Hook:** You don't need more time. You need fewer commitments.

**Duration:** 45-55 seconds

**Script:**
Everyone says they don't have time.
But everyone has 24 hours.
The problem isn't time. It's what you've filled it with.
Point one: Every yes to something unimportant is a no to something that matters.
Point two: Most people's schedules are full of obligations they never consciously chose.
Point three: Audit your week. Remove two things. See what becomes possible.
Time doesn't expand. Your priorities do.
Follow for the uncomfortable version of time management.

**On-screen text:**
- "not a time problem"
- "a commitment problem"
- "remove 2 things this week"

**Sound:** Clean lo-fi, steady pace

## Script 1093

**Hook:** The version of you that succeeds is uncomfortable most of the time.

**Duration:** 45-55 seconds

**Script:**
Comfort is the enemy disguised as a friend.
It keeps you safe. It keeps you small.
The future version of you that has what you want got there through discomfort.
Point one: Every skill you want requires a period of being bad at it. That's uncomfortable.
Point two: Every relationship you want requires a conversation you've been avoiding.
Point three: Every result you want is on the other side of something you don't want to do.
Stop waiting to feel ready.
Uncomfortable is the path, not the obstacle.
Follow for truth nobody enjoys hearing.

**On-screen text:**

- "is uncomfortable most of the time"
- "discomfort = the path"

You learn more from one published piece than a hundred planned ones.
Stop hoarding potential.
Follow for the output-first mindset.

## Script 1095

**Hook:** Most advice online is optimized for engagement, not results.

**Duration:** 45-55 seconds

**Script:**
Engagement bait feels inspiring.
It gets likes. It spreads.
It rarely produces behavior change.
Point one: Advice that feels good is usually advice that confirms what you already believe.
Point two: Advice that actually works usually feels uncomfortable or counterintuitive.
Point three: Filter your feed by asking: does this make me act, or does this make me feel good about not acting?
Good content challenges you.
Great content changes you.
Follow for the kind that actually costs you something.

**On-screen text:**
- "advice online"
- "optimized for engagement, not results"
- "does it make you ACT?"

**Sound:** Slow burn lo-fi, thoughtful
