## Script 1: The Productivity Trap Nobody Talks About
**Hook (0-3s):** You're not unproductive. You're just working on the wrong things.
**Body:** Most people optimize their schedule when they should be optimizing their decision list. The problem isn't that you're slow. It's that half your task list shouldn't exist. Every morning, ask: if I only got three things done today, what moves the needle? Everything else is noise. You don't need a better calendar. You need a shorter list of things that actually matter. That's the difference between busy and productive.
**CTA:** Save this if you've ever felt productive all day but accomplished nothing.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Busy ≠ productive"
- "Cut the list first"
- "3 real tasks per day"
- "Wrong work = zero progress"

## Script 3: Why Most Content Fails Before It's Published
**Hook (0-3s):** Your content is failing because you're writing for yourself.
**Body:** The biggest content mistake is writing about what you know instead of what your audience is searching for. You finish a topic, feel good about it, post it, silence. Because nobody was looking for that. Effective content starts with a question someone is already asking and works backward to your answer. Your knowledge is the vehicle. Their problem is the destination. Start from the destination every time and your content will find its audience without paid promotion.
**CTA:** Comment "question" if you want a framework for finding what your audience actually needs.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Write for them, not you"
- "Start with their question"
- "Problem first, knowledge second"
- "They search, you answer"

## Script 6: Build in Public Without Oversharing
**Hook (0-3s):** Building in public doesn't mean showing everything.
**Body:** Most people either hide their work entirely or share so much they turn their business into a reality show. Neither works. The version that builds an audience is specific enough to be useful and restrained enough to protect what matters. Share the framework, not the client list. Share the result, not the contracts. Share the decision process, not the internal drama. Useful transparency builds trust. Emotional transparency builds followers, not buyers. Know the difference.
**CTA:** Follow if you're building something and want to document it without burning it down.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Share the process, not the chaos"
- "Useful > emotional transparency"
- "Framework, not client names"
- "Trust comes from utility"

## Script 7: The Compounding Content System
**Hook (0-3s):** Every piece of content you make should feed the next three.
**Body:** Compounding content isn't about posting more. It's about making content that references other content, builds on prior ideas, and creates a trail the algorithm and your audience can follow. A long tweet becomes a thread. A thread becomes a short video. A video becomes an email. The email drives back to the thread. You wrote one idea. Now it lives in six places and sends traffic to itself. Stop creating in isolation. Create in ecosystems.
**CTA:** Save this. This is the system that replaced my content calendar.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "One idea, six formats"
- "Content ecosystems > calendars"
- "Let content feed itself"
- "Compounding = less work, more reach"

## Script 8: The Real Cost of Staying Employed
**Hook (0-3s):** The paycheck is safe. The ceiling is the problem.
**Body:** Employment trades certainty for cap. You know what's coming in, but you also know you can't go above what they decided you're worth. That's the deal. Most people are comfortable with it until they're not. The shift happens when you realize the ceiling isn't just financial. It's on your time, your ideas, your risk tolerance. Building something on the side doesn't require quitting. It requires acknowledging that the cap exists and deciding if you're okay with it. Most people are, until they're not.

- "They priced your time for you"
