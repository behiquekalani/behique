## Post 2 — The 3 Types of Passive Income (And Which One to Start With)

**Hook:** Not all passive income is built the same way.

**Slide 1:**
There are 3 types of passive income online.
Most people start with the wrong one.

**Slide 2:**
Type 1: Asset-based
You create something once and sell it repeatedly.
Examples: ebooks, templates, courses, digital guides.
Startup cost: low. Timeline: 30-90 days to first sale.

**Slide 3:**
Type 2: Audience-based
You build a following that sends traffic to your assets.
Examples: email list, YouTube, newsletter.
Startup cost: zero cash, high time. Timeline: 6-18 months.

**Slide 4:**
Type 3: System-based
You build automations that run without you.
Examples: email funnels, affiliate flows, automated delivery.
Startup cost: medium. Requires types 1 and 2 first.

**Slide 5:**
The order matters:
1. Build the asset first (something to sell)
2. Build the audience (someone to sell to)
3. Build the system (make it run without you)

Skip step 1 and you have no income.
Skip step 2 and you have no customers.
Skip step 3 and you have a job, not a business.

**Slide 6:**
Start with the product.
@behikeai has the templates, guides, and systems to get you there.

**Caption:**
Most passive income advice skips the most important question: which type should you build first, and in what order? The answer changes everything about your timeline and your results.

Asset-based income is the right starting point for most people because it requires the least upfront investment and creates something concrete. A digital product you can sell for $9 or $49 or $99 is the foundation everything else builds on. Without it, an audience has nowhere to land.

Audience-based income is the multiplier. Once you have a product, an email list or a consistent content channel is what turns a one-time sale into a recurring revenue stream. This takes longer but compounds over time.

System-based income is what converts the first two into something that runs without you actively managing it. Email automations, delivery systems, affiliate structures. This is the part that actually feels passive.

Build in that order. Most people try to start at step 3. That is why they fail.

## Post 4 — The Passive Income Math Nobody Shows You

**Hook:** Here is what $1,000 a month in passive income actually looks like.

**Slide 1:**
$1,000/month in passive income.
What does it actually take to get there?

**Slide 2:**
Path A: One product at $47
You need 22 sales per month.
At a 2% conversion rate, you need 1,100 visitors per month.
At 100 visitors per post: 11 posts hitting consistently.

**Slide 3:**
Path B: Multiple products at different price points
3 products: $9, $27, $47
Sell 30 units of the $9 product = $270
Sell 20 units of the $27 product = $540
Sell 5 units of the $47 product = $235
Total: ~$1,000

**Slide 4:**
The hidden variable: traffic.
Every number above assumes consistent traffic.
Traffic comes from:
- Content (free, slow)
- Email list (free after building)
- Ads (fast, costs money)
- SEO (slow, compounds)

**Slide 5:**
The actual timeline to $1K/month:
Month 1-2: Build product
Month 3-4: Build content and list
Month 5-6: First consistent sales
Month 6-12: Optimize and scale

This is conservative. Some do it faster. Most take longer.

**Slide 6:**
The math works. The question is whether you will do the work to get there.
@behikeai — the real playbook, step by step.

**Caption:**
Most passive income content shows you screenshots of dashboards but never breaks down the actual math behind those numbers. So here it is.

One thousand dollars a month from a digital product at $47 requires roughly 22 sales per month. At a realistic 2% store conversion rate, you need about 1,100 visitors. That is the concrete number you are building toward, not some vague idea about "building an audience."

Understanding your numbers changes how you make decisions. When you know you need 1,100 visitors, you know whether your current content output can get you there. When you know your conversion rate, you know whether the issue is traffic or product positioning.

Most solopreneurs fail at passive income not because the model does not work, but because they never do this math. They post content and hope. Hope is not a strategy.

Map your numbers before you start. Know what $1,000 requires. Then reverse-engineer your monthly actions from that target. That is how you stop hoping and start building.

**Slide 5:**
The trap is not laziness.
It is the illusion of progress.

Research feels like work.
Tool setup feels like work.
Revising feels like work.

None of it is the work.

The work is shipping.

**Slide 6:**
Break the loop.
@behikeai — products, templates, and systems to help you actually ship.

**Caption:**
The passive income trap is real, and most of the people stuck in it are genuinely working hard. That is what makes it so frustrating to name. You are not being lazy. You are staying busy with everything except the one action that would actually move you forward.
