# TikTok Batch 181 — High-Ticket vs Low-Ticket (Part 1)

## Script 1: The Pricing Trap Most Solopreneurs Fall Into
**Hook (0-3s):** Selling a $27 product is not a business. It is a job with extra steps.
**Body:** Here is the math nobody shows you. To make $5,000 a month at $27, you need 185 customers. At $500, you need 10. Same outcome. Completely different workload. Low-ticket is not a stepping stone. It is a ceiling you build for yourself. The audience you attract at $27 expects $27 forever. They will not upgrade. They will not refer. They will tell you the $500 offer is too expensive. You trained them to think that. High-ticket is not about greed. It is about what your time is worth and what transformation you can actually deliver. Price is a signal. Low prices signal low stakes. Low stakes attract people who are not serious. Non-serious people get no results. No results means no testimonials. No testimonials means you stay stuck at $27.

## Script 2: Why Low-Ticket Builds the Wrong Audience
**Hook (0-3s):** Your audience is a direct reflection of what you charged them to enter.
**Body:** Every price point filters for a type of buyer. Free gets you the curious. $27 gets you bargain hunters. $500 gets you committed people who will actually do the work. When you build an audience around cheap offers, you build an audience optimized for cheap offers. These are not bad people. They are just not buyers at higher price points. So when you try to move upstream, they do not follow. They get offended. They leave. You start over. The mistake was not launching the high-ticket offer. The mistake was spending 18 months building an audience that was never going to buy it.
**CTA:** Save this if you are planning to raise your prices.
**On-screen text:** ["Price = filter", "Cheap audience = wrong fit", "18 months building the wrong crowd", "Start with the right filter"]

## Script 3: One Premium Client Changes Everything
**Hook (0-3s):** One client at $2,000 changed how I thought about content forever.
**Body:** When you have ten clients paying $200 each, you are managing ten relationships. When you have one client paying $2,000, you go deep on one transformation. Depth creates results. Results create case studies. Case studies build trust at scale. The second thing that happens is you stop creating content to fill volume. You start creating content to attract the specific person who can afford to act on your ideas. That precision makes your hooks sharper. Your calls to action cleaner. One real client doing real work is worth more to your brand than fifty people who bought something cheap and forgot about it.

## Script 4: Price Is Not the Objection. Trust Is.
**Hook (0-3s):** You are not losing sales because your price is too high. You are losing them because trust is too low.
**Body:** Most digital product creators assume price is the objection. It is almost never the real objection. The visitor landed on your page. They do not know you. They do not believe you can deliver. And your $27 price is not low enough to overcome that doubt. Now flip it. If you built genuine trust first, through content, through proof, through consistency, then price becomes almost irrelevant within reason. People pay what they believe is worth paying. And belief comes from relationship. Low-ticket does not build trust faster than high-ticket. What builds trust is sustained presence and evidence of results.

**On-screen text:** ["Price is not the problem", "Trust is the problem", "Belief drives purchase decisions", "Fix trust first, pricing solves itself"]

## Script 5: When Low-Ticket Actually Makes Sense
**Hook (0-3s):** There is one situation where low-ticket is the right move. Most people are not in it.
**Body:** Low-ticket works when you already have an audience. You have built trust. You have proof of concept. You need to remove friction for people who want to enter before committing to something bigger. That is a specific scenario. Most creators start with low-ticket because they are afraid to charge more. Fear is not a pricing strategy. If you are starting from zero, high-ticket forces you to get good fast. You have to deliver. You have to talk to your clients. You learn what actually works. That feedback loop is worth more than 200 low-ticket sales where nobody tells you anything.
**CTA:** Follow if you are building from zero right now.
**On-screen text:** ["Low-ticket has one specific use case", "Starting from fear is not strategy", "High-ticket = faster feedback loop", "Get good fast or get stuck"]

## Script 6: The Compounding Effect of One Premium Result
**Hook (0-3s):** One premium client does more for your business than 100 cheap sales.
**Body:** One premium client who gets results becomes a case study. That case study goes on your sales page. It gets shared. It attracts three more people exactly like them. One of those three refers someone from their network. That referral closes without a single sales call because the case study did the work. Now trace the same path with 100 cheap sales. You got 100 transactions. Maybe a few nice reviews. No deep transformation stories. No referrals because nobody got enough value to talk about it. The compounding effect of high-ticket is not just revenue. It is the social proof infrastructure that makes the next sale easier.
**CTA:** Save this if you are thinking about your first premium offer.
**On-screen text:** ["1 premium result compounds", "Case study builds pipeline", "Cheap sales don't generate referrals", "Social proof is infrastructure"]

## Script 7: Why Solopreneurs Undercharge Their Expertise
**Hook (0-3s):** You undercharge because you do not see what you know the way a stranger sees it.
**Body:** You have been inside your knowledge for years. It feels obvious to you. So you price it like it is obvious. The person buying has never had access to what is in your head. To them, it is not obvious. It is transformative. The gap between what you know and what they know is the price. Most solopreneurs discount that gap because of impostor syndrome or because they compare themselves to people charging more and assume those people are more qualified. They are usually not more qualified. They just decided to charge more. Pricing is a decision. You make it. Then you build evidence to back it up.
**CTA:** Comment "gap" if you have been undercharging.
**On-screen text:** ["You discount your own knowledge gap", "Their ignorance is your value", "Pricing is a decision, not a credential", "Higher stakes = faster evidence"]

## Script 8: How to Move from Low to High-Ticket the Right Way
**Hook (0-3s):** Do not just raise your price. Change what you are selling entirely.
**Body:** The biggest mistake when moving from low to high-ticket is taking the same thing and adding a zero. It does not work. Your audience will not pay it. Your content does not support it. The right move is to build a new offer for a new segment. Keep the low-ticket product if it is working. Let it run. Build the high-ticket offer as a separate track for people who want more. Different landing page. Different content pillar. Different proof points. Over six to twelve months, shift your content to attract the high-ticket buyer. Let the audience self-select. You did not burn anyone. You just built a second lane.
