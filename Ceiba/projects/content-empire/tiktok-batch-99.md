## TikTok 1: The Solopreneur Automation Stack That Actually Works
**Hook (0-2s):**
I run a one-person business with four automations. Here's what they are.

**Script:**
Most automation advice is either too simple or too technical. Here's what's actually in my stack and why.

First: email sequences. When someone buys a product or joins my list, a sequence goes out automatically. Welcome, value, offer. No daily manual sends.

Second: content scheduling. I batch content once a week. One session. Everything queues up and posts on its own.

Third: delivery automation. Every product I sell delivers itself the moment the payment clears. No manual fulfillment.

Fourth: analytics. One dashboard shows me what's working. I check it once a week. Not daily. Not hourly.

Four systems. Run in the background. The business moves while I'm working on the next thing.

That's the goal. Not to remove yourself from the business. To remove yourself from the repetitive parts of it.

**CTA:**
Which of these four do you still do manually? That's your first automation.

## TikTok 2: Automations That Pay for Themselves on Day One
**Hook (0-2s):**
Some automations save time. These ones make money while you sleep.

**Script:**
There's a difference between automations that reduce your workload and automations that generate revenue. You want both, but you should build the second kind first.

An email welcome sequence that introduces your product makes money. Build that first.

An abandoned cart sequence that follows up on incomplete purchases makes money. Build that second.

A content scheduler saves time but doesn't directly generate revenue. Build that later.

The mental model is simple. If the automation sits between a potential buyer and a purchase, it earns its cost immediately. Everything else is support infrastructure.

Map out the path from "stranger discovers you" to "purchase complete." Every gap in that path is an automation opportunity. Plug the revenue gaps first.

**CTA:**
Draw that path out. Where does a potential buyer fall off right now?

## TikTok 3: Why Most Solopreneurs Automate the Wrong Things
**Hook (0-2s):**
You automated the wrong part of your business. Here's how to tell.

**Script:**
Automating something that shouldn't exist yet is one of the most common productivity mistakes.

You spend three days building an automation for a process you run once a month. Then you spend three more days debugging it. You've spent a week on something that would have taken four hours to do manually for the rest of the year.

The right automation target is: high frequency, low complexity, and currently eating your time.

Answering the same FAQ ten times a week. Manually sending the same onboarding email. Posting the same type of content on the same schedule.

Those are automation targets. One-off tasks, creative decisions, and strategy calls are not.

Before you automate anything, ask: how often does this happen? How much of my actual thinking does it require? If the answer is "often" and "none," automate it.

**CTA:**
What do you do every week that requires zero thinking? That's your next automation.

## TikTok 4: The Simplest Automation Setup for Beginners
**Hook (0-2s):**
You don't need to know how to code to automate your business.

**Script:**
Here's the minimum viable automation setup. Three tools. All no-code.

One: a form or landing page that captures leads. Something simple. Name and email.

Two: an email platform that triggers a sequence when someone fills out that form. Welcome email goes out automatically. Day three email goes out automatically. Day seven too.

Three: a scheduler for your social content. Write everything on Sunday. It posts throughout the week.

That's it. You've automated lead capture, follow-up, and content distribution. Three tools. Probably less than $50 a month combined.

The automation doesn't have to be complex to work. It has to run reliably when you're not watching it.

Start simple. Add complexity only when you hit a ceiling.

**CTA:**
Are you still manually doing any of those three things? Which one are you fixing first?

## TikTok 5: How I Use AI to Automate Content Without Losing My Voice
**Hook (0-2s):**
I use AI in my content workflow. My audience can't tell. Here's the system.

**Script:**
The goal isn't to have AI write everything. The goal is to have AI handle the parts of content creation that don't require your judgment.

Structuring drafts: AI does this. Given a thesis and three points, it can scaffold an outline in 30 seconds.

Generating variations: AI does this. Five different hooks for the same piece, and I pick the one that sounds most like me.

First draft of repurposed content: AI does this. I give it the source, it translates it to a new format, I edit the voice.

What AI doesn't do in my workflow: choose the thesis, decide the angle, or edit the final draft. Those are judgment calls. They require knowing my audience.

Humans decide what to say. AI handles a significant portion of the how. That's the right split.

**CTA:**
Where in your content process are you still doing things AI could handle in a minute?
