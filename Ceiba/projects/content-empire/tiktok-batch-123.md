# TikTok EN Batch 123
**Topic: The difference between a landing page and a sales page**

## TikTok 1: You're Sending People to the Wrong Page
**Hook (0-2s):**
If you're sending cold traffic to a sales page, you're burning money. Here's why.

**Script:**
A landing page and a sales page are not the same thing. Using them interchangeably is one of the most common and most expensive mistakes in digital marketing.

A landing page has one job: capture attention and get a micro-commitment. That's it. Usually an email address in exchange for something useful. No price. No purchase decision. One ask.

A sales page has one job: convert someone who already knows you into a buyer. It handles objections, presents the offer fully, and asks for money.

Sending someone cold to a sales page is like proposing on a first date. The relationship is not there yet.

Build the landing page first. Get the email. Warm them up over a sequence. Then send them to the sales page.

The order matters more than either page.

**CTA:**
Which page are you sending people to first? Drop it in the comments.

## TikTok 2: What Every Landing Page Needs to Work
**Hook (0-2s):**
Most landing pages fail because they're asking for too much too soon.

**Script:**
A landing page that converts has four elements. Only four.

One headline that names the specific outcome. Not "learn how to grow online." Try "get 100 email subscribers in 14 days without running ads."

One sentence that tells them who this is for and who it's not for. "This is for creators who already have a product but can't get their first 100 customers."

One clear value offer. What do they get when they sign up? Make it a specific deliverable, not a vague promise.

One call to action. One button. One form field. Not a menu. Not links. Not a footer with ten options.

That's the whole page. Every extra element you add reduces your conversion rate. Simplicity is the strategy.

**CTA:**
Go look at your landing page right now. How many elements does it have? Cut everything that isn't on this list.

## TikTok 3: What Every Sales Page Needs That Most Don't Have
**Hook (0-2s):**
The one section missing from most sales pages is the reason nobody buys.

**Script:**
Sales pages usually cover the what and the how. The product, the features, the bonuses, the price.

What they skip is the why now.

People delay purchases indefinitely unless you give them a reason to decide today. Not a fake countdown timer. Not manufactured urgency. A real reason.

Maybe it's a cohort that starts on a specific date. Maybe it's a price that increases after a launch window because you'll have case studies to charge more. Maybe it's a bonus that's only available during a defined period.

The "why now" section is where most sales pages go quiet. The reader thinks "I'll come back to this" and never does.

Write the "why now" section last. Make it real. If you don't have a real reason, create a real reason.

**CTA:**
Check your sales page. Does it have a "why now" section? If not, that's your conversion problem.

## TikTok 4: The Length Debate Is Fake
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Long sales pages don't work anymore." That's wrong, and here's why.

**Script:**
The debate about long versus short sales pages misses the point entirely.

A sales page should be exactly as long as it needs to be to overcome every objection a qualified buyer has.

If your product is $7, the page can be short. The reader doesn't need much convincing for a $7 decision.

If your product is $497, the page needs to be long. The reader has real objections. They want proof. They want to understand exactly what they're getting. They need to trust the investment.

Match the length of your page to the price of your offer and the level of trust required for that purchase.

Short pages for low-ticket, warm audiences. Long pages for high-ticket or cold audiences. That's the actual rule.

**CTA:**
What's the price of your offer? That should tell you how long your page needs to be.

## TikTok 5: The Difference That Actually Changes Conversions
**Hook (0-2s):**
I changed one thing on my sales page and conversion rate went up 40%. It wasn't the headline.

**Script:**
Landing pages and sales pages both live or die by one thing: clarity about who the page is for.

Most pages try to speak to everyone. "This is for anyone who wants to build a business online." That person doesn't exist. Everyone who reads that thinks it might not be for them.

Write your page for one specific person. Name their exact situation. Describe where they are right now in painful detail. Then describe where they want to be.

The more precisely you describe the gap, the more the right person says "this is exactly me." And the more they feel that, the more they trust you can close the gap.

This works on landing pages. It works on sales pages. It's not a hack. It's the whole job of a page that converts.

**CTA:**
Write one sentence that describes your ideal buyer's exact current situation. That sentence should be at the top of every page you own.
