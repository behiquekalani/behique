# LinkedIn Batch 31 — Affiliate Marketing in a Solopreneur Income Stack

---

## Post 1

Most solopreneurs treat affiliate marketing like a side project.

That's why most solopreneurs make $50/year from it.

The ones who do it well treat it as a second product line. They build around it. They pick programs with recurring commissions. They write reviews instead of dropping links. They think about which tools their audience already buys.

The difference isn't effort. It's positioning.

Affiliate income doesn't replace your core offer. It sits next to it. Your course teaches the skill. Your affiliate links to the tools that help them apply it. One audience, two revenue streams, almost no extra work.

That's the stack.

---

## Post 2

The solopreneur income stack, from least to most scalable:

1. Services (time-for-money, high income, not scalable)
2. Digital products (scalable, requires audience, one-time purchase)
3. Affiliate income (scalable, compounds over time, requires trust first)

Most people start at the top and never get to the bottom because they think you need a huge audience first.

You don't.

You need a small, trusted audience and the right programs.

A newsletter with 500 engaged readers earning 30% recurring on a $50/month tool is $75/month per referral. Ten referrals is $750/month in recurring income from that one program.

Do that with three programs. That's your affiliate layer.

---

## Post 3

The thing that kills affiliate income before it starts: recommending things you don't use.

Your readers can tell. The specificity is gone. The writing sounds like a product page. Nothing converts.

The filter I use before joining any affiliate program:

Would I recommend this to a friend without getting paid?

If yes, join. If no, skip, no matter the commission rate.

Your reputation is worth more than a 40% commission on something that disappoints people.

The trust you've built is the mechanism that makes affiliate income work. Protect it.

---

## Post 4

Affiliate marketing timeline that nobody tells you:

- Month 1-3: Setup, first content, zero income
- Month 3-6: First clicks, maybe $20-50/month
- Month 6-12: Compounding starts, $100-300/month possible
- Month 12-18: Real money if you've been consistent, $300-800/month
- Month 18-24: The stack is working, $1,000+/month becomes realistic

Most people quit in month 3.

They look at $20 and decide it isn't worth it.

The people who stick until month 18 are earning from content they wrote 18 months ago while also creating new content. That's the compounding everyone talks about but almost nobody sees because they left before it started.
