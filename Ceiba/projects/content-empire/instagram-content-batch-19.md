**Slide 1 (Cover):**
Headline: Why most Gumroad products do not sell.
Body: And the four things that fix it.

**Slide 2:**
Headline: Problem 1 — Vague title.
Body: "The Complete AI Guide" tells the buyer nothing. "How I use ChatGPT to write 10 emails in 20 minutes" tells them everything. Be specific in the title.

**Slide 3:**
Headline: Problem 2 — No proof the product works.
Body: One screenshot. One result. One sentence from someone who used it. Social proof does not require a big following. It requires one honest example.

**Slide 4:**
Headline: Problem 3 — Wrong price anchor.
Body: If you price too low, buyers think it is low quality. If you price too high with no context, they leave. Show what they are getting before the price.

**Slide 5:**
Headline: Problem 4 — No urgency in the listing.
Body: "Available forever" creates no reason to buy today. Add a limited bundle, a bonus for early buyers, or a price that increases. Give them a reason to act now.

**Slide 6:**
Headline: The listing structure that converts.
Body: Line 1: The result the buyer wants. Line 2: What makes this different. Line 3: What is included. Line 4: Who it is for. Line 5: Price and CTA.

**Slide 7:**
Headline: The thumbnail matters more than most creators think.
Body: Your cover image is your ad. It shows up in feeds, search results, and emails. A clean, readable image with a bold claim outperforms every fancy design.

**Slide 8:**
Headline: Traffic is the last problem, not the first.
Body: Fix the conversion before you chase the audience. If the listing does not convert cold traffic, more traffic is just more waste.

**Slide 9 (CTA):**
Headline: Share this with someone building their first product.
Body: Or save it for when you write your next Gumroad listing.

**Title:** The pricing mistake that kills Gumroad sales before they start.
**Recommended posting time:** Thursday, 12:00 PM EST
**Hashtags:** #gumroad #digitalproducts #pricingstrategy #solopreneur #passiveincome #onlinebusiness #buildinpublic #creatoreconomy #sellonline #indiehacker #productcreator #digitalmarketing #makemoneyonline #conversiontips #solopreneurlife

**Caption:**
Most new creators price their first digital product too low. They think $5 or $7 is safe. What it actually signals is "I don't believe this is worth much."

Buyers use price as a quality filter. A $5 guide feels like a weekend project. A $27 guide feels like real research.

If your product solves a specific problem, price it based on what that problem costs the buyer to leave unsolved. Not based on how long it took you to make.

The $5 product and the $27 product take the same time to sell. One of them builds a real business.

**Title:** Three Gumroad listing tweaks that doubled my conversions.
**Recommended posting time:** Sunday, 9:00 AM EST
**Hashtags:** #gumroad #digitalproducts #conversionoptimization #solopreneur #passiveincome #onlinebusiness #buildinpublic #creatoreconomy #sellonline #indiehacker #productcreator #digitalmarketing #productlisting #copywriting #solopreneurlife

**Caption:**
Three changes to my Gumroad listing that changed what happened when people landed on the page.

First: I rewrote the first line to start with a result, not a description. "Stop spending 3 hours on emails" instead of "This is a guide to email writing."

Second: I added one screenshot of the product itself. Not a testimonial. Just what the inside looks like. Buyers want to know what they are getting.

Third: I moved the price above the fold. I was hiding it. Hiding the price creates doubt. Showing it early filters for people who are actually ready.

All three changes together. One afternoon of work.
