## Script 1: The Real Reason You Can't Focus
**Hook (0-3s):** You can't focus because your brain knows you haven't decided what matters.
**Body:** Focus is not a willpower problem. It's a clarity problem. When you sit down to work and can't concentrate, it's because your brain is still running in the background trying to figure out what the priority actually is. The fix is a pre-work decision ritual. Before you start, you write: what is the one output that, if done by the end of this block, means today was a success? One thing. Not a list. The moment you define it clearly, your brain has permission to engage. Clarity creates focus.
**CTA:** Try this before your next work block and comment what changed.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Focus = clarity, not willpower"
- "Define the one output first"
- "Unclear priority = distracted brain"
- "Permission to engage"

## Script 3: The Wrong Way to Use Social Media for Business
**Hook (0-3s):** Using social media to sell is the wrong use of social media.
**Body:** Social media is for building trust. Not for selling. The moment someone follows you, they're not ready to buy. They're watching. They need to see you show up consistently, deliver real value, and demonstrate you know what you're talking about. The sale happens after enough trust is built that the product feels like a natural next step. If every post is a sales pitch, the audience leaves because there's no reason to stay. Provide ten times before you ask once. The ask works when the trust is there.
**CTA:** Save this if your content feels like it's not converting.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Social = trust building"
- "Sell after the trust is built"
- "10 value posts, then the ask"

## Script 7: The Number That Tells You If Your Business Idea Is Working
**Hook (0-3s):** One number tells you if your idea is working. Most people never track it.
**Body:** Conversion rate. How many people who see your product actually buy it. Not followers. Not impressions. Not likes. Buyers divided by viewers. If a hundred people see your product page and one buys, that's one percent. Industry average for cold traffic is around one to three percent. If you're below that, the issue is either the offer, the price, or the page. If you're above it, scale. Everything else, the content, the growth, the branding, exists to increase the number of people who see that page. Conversion rate is the only metric that's actually connected to money.
**CTA:** Comment your current conversion rate if you know it.
**On-screen text suggestions:**
- "Buyers / viewers = conversion rate"
- "Followers ≠ revenue"
- "Track the one number that matters"
- "Below 1%? Fix the offer first"

## Script 8: How to Escape the Time-for-Money Trap
**Hook (0-3s):** Trading time for money has a ceiling. Digital products don't.
**Body:** Every service business has an invisible cap: the number of hours you can work. Beyond that cap, revenue stops growing unless you raise prices or hire. Digital products break the cap. You create once and the revenue doesn't depend on your time. That's not a get-rich-quick pitch. That's basic math about leverage. The shift doesn't happen overnight. It happens when you start putting a percentage of your service income into building the asset. Build the product in the margins. Then let the product grow the margins. Eventually the product supports the business and you choose which clients you take.
**CTA:** Save this for when you're stuck in hourly billing.
