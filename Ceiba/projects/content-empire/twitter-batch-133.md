## Tweet 5 (6-tweet thread)
Just-in-time learning: the framework for building without going back to school:

**[1/6]**
Traditional education is just-in-case.

Learn it all now so you'll have it if you need it.
This makes sense when you don't know your path yet.

But when you're building something specific, the model breaks.
You don't need everything. You need the right thing at the right moment.

**[2/6]**
Just-in-time learning works like this:

1. Start building
2. Hit a specific obstacle
3. Learn exactly what you need to get past it
4. Return to building

The obstacle is the teacher. Not the course. Not the book.

**[3/6]**
What just-in-time learning is not:

It's not skipping fundamentals. It's not cutting corners.
It's deliberately sequencing your education around real blockers, not hypothetical ones.

If you don't know Python and you're building a Python tool, you learn Python.
You don't learn Go because it might be useful later.

**[4/6]**
The just-in-case trap looks like:

- 7 half-finished courses
- 200 saved articles you'll "read later"
- A notes app full of ideas with no products behind them
- Knowing a lot about building but not actually building

Recognize it. It's very comfortable. That's the problem.

**[5/6]**
The question to ask before you open any course, book, or tutorial:

"What specific problem am I trying to solve right now?"

If you can't answer that precisely, you're learning for comfort, not progress.

**[6/6]**
Build the thing. Hit the wall. Learn the answer. Repeat.

That's the solopreneur's MBA.
No campus. No waitlist.
Just the work.

## Tweet 8 (3-tweet thread)
The information diet of a productive solopreneur:

**[1/3]**
Consume less than you think you should.

The person who reads 40 newsletters, listens to 10 podcasts, and takes every course is not ahead of you.
They're buried in input and producing no output.

**[2/3]**
The productive filter:

Before consuming anything, ask: "Does this help me solve a problem I have right now?"

If yes, consume it and apply it immediately.
If no, skip it. Not save it. Skip it.

**[3/3]**
Your goal isn't to be the most informed person.

It's to produce the most useful work.
Less input, more output.
The work is the education.
