### 10. API Routing / Model Arbitrage Service

**What it is.** Build a service that routes AI requests to the cheapest model that can handle the task. Sell access to businesses that don't want to manage multiple AI providers.

**How it works.**
- The price spread between AI models is insane: Gemini Flash at $0.15/1M tokens vs Claude Opus at $15/1M tokens. That's a 100x difference.
- Build a routing layer that classifies incoming requests by complexity
- Simple tasks (summarization, formatting) go to cheap models (GPT-5 Nano, Gemini Flash)
- Complex tasks (reasoning, coding) go to expensive models (Claude, GPT-5)
- Charge a flat rate that's cheaper than always using the expensive model, but more expensive than the cheap model
- Your margin is the routing intelligence

**Revenue potential.** $1,000-$10,000/month depending on volume. Hard to predict. Could be huge if you land even one mid-size company.

**Setup difficulty.** 5/5

**Human hours/week after setup.** 3-5 hours (monitoring, optimizing routing, adding new models)

**Why most people don't do it.** It requires real engineering. You need to build classification logic, handle API failures, manage billing, and convince businesses to trust your proxy layer. But the economics are compelling. A company spending $5,000/month on Claude could cut that to $1,500 with smart routing, and you keep 20%.

**Tools needed.** Python, FastAPI, multiple AI API keys, Stripe, Cobo (your GPU machine) for local model hosting via Ollama

**Keep private or share?** KEEP PRIVATE. If this works, it's a real business with defensible margins because you're running local models on your own hardware.

**Confidence level.** 5/10. The economics work on paper. Execution is hard. But your 3-computer setup with Ollama gives you an edge most people don't have.

## OPPORTUNITIES I INVESTIGATED AND REJECTED

These sound good on YouTube but have serious problems.

### AI-Generated Stock Images
**Why rejected.** 47% of Adobe Stock is now AI images. 29 million new AI images uploaded per month. The market is completely saturated. Earnings per image have cratered. Not worth your time unless you find an extremely specific niche.

### AI Music Generation for Stock Libraries
**Why rejected.** Copyright situation is a legal minefield. Platforms are starting to crack down. Revenue per track is tiny ($0.10-$1.00 per download). You need thousands of tracks to make meaningful money, and the AI music tools aren't consistent enough yet.

### Selling "AI Courses" Teaching Others How to Use AI
**Why rejected.** The market is saturated with low-quality AI courses. The people who make money selling AI courses are the ones who already have large audiences. Without an audience, you're just another "make money with AI" course in a sea of thousands.

### Data Labeling Arbitrage (outsourcing to cheaper labor)
**Why rejected.** Scale AI, Appen, and other platforms have locked this market. The margins are thin. The quality requirements are high. And you're competing with organized operations in lower-cost countries. Not a good solo play.

### Faceless YouTube Channels (Fully AI-Generated)
**Why rejected for TIER 1.** Not rejected entirely, but overhyped. The "I made $50k/month with a faceless channel" stories are mostly from 2024 when the competition was lower. YouTube's algorithm now detects and deprioritizes AI-generated content more aggressively. It can still work, but it takes 6-12 months to monetize, and the success rate is low. Better as a long-term play alongside other revenue streams, not as your primary bet.
