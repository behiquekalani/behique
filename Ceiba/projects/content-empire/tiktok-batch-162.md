# TikTok Batch 162 — The Subscription Model for Solopreneurs

## Script 1: Why Subscriptions Beat One-Time Sales
**Hook (0-3s):** One-time sales make you work every month. Subscriptions make money while you sleep.
**Body:** When you sell a product once, you start every month at zero. No customers from last month carry over. You hustle to hit the same number again. Subscriptions break that cycle. If you have 100 people paying $20 a month, you start the month with $2,000 already on the table. You still sell, but you build on a floor instead of starting from nothing. The math changes everything. The stress changes too. Founders with subscription revenue make decisions differently. They build longer. They think further ahead. They do not panic. You can build a subscription around almost anything: content, coaching, templates, community, software, or a combination.

## Script 2: The Minimum Viable Subscription
**Hook (0-3s):** You do not need software to run a subscription business.
**Body:** Substack, Gumroad, and Patreon exist. They handle billing. They handle delivery. You write a thing once a week, a month, or however often you promise. People pay to receive it. That is the entire business model. The minimum viable version is a paid newsletter at $5 to $10 a month. You need 200 paid subscribers to make $1,000 a month recurring. Two hundred people is nothing. That is your Instagram following from three months ago. Most solopreneurs already have the audience. They just have not asked those people to pay for the thing they give away for free. The first step is deciding what your paid version actually includes.
**CTA:** Save this for when you are ready to price your subscription.
**On-screen text:** ["No software needed", "Substack/Gumroad handle billing", "200 subscribers @ $5 = $1K/mo", "You already have the audience"]

## Script 3: Tiered Subscriptions for Solo Creators
**Hook (0-3s):** One subscription tier leaves most of your revenue on the table.
**Body:** Some people in your audience will pay $5 a month. Others will pay $50. A small number will pay $200. If you only offer the $5 tier, the people who would have paid $200 are paying $5 or nothing. Tiers fix this. A basic tier gives access to content. A mid tier adds templates or resources. A premium tier adds personal access, calls, or feedback. You do the same work. The top tier pays 40 times more per person. Ten people at the top tier of a $200 plan is $2,000 a month from ten people. Build three tiers. Most of your subscribers will be at the bottom. Most of your revenue will come from the top.
**CTA:** Follow for the three-tier pricing model that fits solopreneurs.
**On-screen text:** ["Tiers capture full willingness to pay", "Some people will pay $200/mo", "10 premium subs = $2K/mo", "Most revenue from smallest group"]

## Script 4: What to Include in a Paid Subscription
**Hook (0-3s):** The mistake most creators make with subscriptions is giving too much.
**Body:** You do not need to produce a mountain of content every month to justify a paid subscription. You need to produce something specific that someone cannot easily get elsewhere. That could be a weekly breakdown of one industry trend. It could be a monthly toolkit of templates. It could be access to a community where you are present and respond. It could be early access to your work before it is public. The value is not volume. The value is access, specificity, or proximity to you. Pick one of those three things and build the subscription around it. Do not build a library. Build a relationship.
**CTA:** Comment what you would build a subscription around.
**On-screen text:** ["Less content, more specificity", "Access, specificity, or proximity", "Value is not volume", "Build a relationship, not a library"]

## Script 5: Churn Is the Enemy
**Hook (0-3s):** You can grow your subscription list and still make less money if your churn is too high.
**Body:** Churn is the percentage of subscribers who cancel each month. If you add 50 new subscribers but 40 cancel, you only grew by 10. The top lever for reducing churn is making your subscribers feel progress. If they join and feel like they are getting smarter, building something, or moving toward a goal, they stay. If they join and feel like they are watching content, they leave. The shift is from content delivery to outcome delivery. Ask: what does my subscriber accomplish this month because they pay me? If you cannot answer that, the churn rate will tell you.

**On-screen text:** ["Grow and still lose money", "Churn kills compounding", "Outcome > content delivery", "What does your subscriber accomplish?"]

## Script 6: Annual vs. Monthly Pricing
**Hook (0-3s):** Annual subscribers are worth three times more and churn eight times less.
**Body:** A monthly subscriber at $10 might stay for four months on average. That is $40 total lifetime value. An annual subscriber at $80 paid upfront and already committed for a year. The math is not close. Most subscription businesses that work well do 30 to 50 percent of their revenue on annual plans. To push people toward annual, you offer a discount. Two months free is the standard offer. $10 a month or $80 a year. The person who takes the annual plan intends to use your thing. They made a decision. The monthly person is still deciding. Price and position to pull people into annual from the start.
**CTA:** Save this and follow for the annual pricing conversion script.
**On-screen text:** ["Annual subs churn 8x less", "Lifetime value: $40 vs $80+", "2 months free = standard offer", "Annual = decision maker"]

## Script 7: Subscription Stacking
**Hook (0-3s):** The most resilient subscription businesses are not built on one product.
**Body:** Subscription stacking means building multiple subscription income streams that serve the same audience. A newsletter plus a community. A community plus a template library. A template library plus a coaching program. Each subscriber may pay for one or all three. The audience is the same. The offers expand. A person who pays for your newsletter might upgrade to the community six months later. A community member might add the template library. Each offer deepens the relationship and adds another line of recurring revenue. You do not need a large audience to do this. You need a trusting audience and multiple ways to serve them at different price points.

**On-screen text:** ["Multiple streams, same audience", "Newsletter + community + resources", "Deepens relationship over time", "Small audience, multiple offers"]

## Script 8: The First 10 Subscribers
**Hook (0-3s):** Getting your first 10 paying subscribers is the hardest and most important step.
**Body:** Ten paying subscribers proves the model works. It shows people will pay for what you make. It gives you real feedback on what they value. And it deposits real money in your account, which changes how seriously you take the business. To get the first 10: tell the people who already follow your free content that you are opening a paid tier. Send a direct message to the 20 people most engaged with your work. Offer a founding member discount. The first 10 do not come from algorithms. They come from direct, personal asks. Most creators never do this because it feels uncomfortable to ask people for money. That discomfort is the gate. Cross it.
**CTA:** Follow if you are building a subscription from scratch.
**On-screen text:** ["10 paid subs = proof of concept", "DM your most engaged followers", "Founding member discount works", "Cross the discomfort gate"]
