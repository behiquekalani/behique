## Script 1: The 30-Day Rule That Changed My Creator Strategy
**Hook (0-3s):** Thirty days is the minimum sample size for any content strategy. Not ninety. Not a year. Thirty.
**Body:** Most people try something for a week, decide it's not working, and switch. That's not a test. That's a reflex. A real content experiment requires thirty days of consistent execution before you look at the numbers. Why thirty? Because the algorithm takes two weeks to understand your content. Your audience takes two weeks to adjust to your posting cadence. Your own skill improves significantly in thirty days of reps. When you quit at day seven, you never get the data. You never learn. You just keep starting over. Thirty days is where real information lives. Commit to it.
**CTA:** Comment "30" if you're starting your experiment today.

## Script 2: How to Design a 30-Day Content Experiment
**Hook (0-3s):** A content experiment without a hypothesis is just posting. Here's the difference.
**Body:** Step one: state your hypothesis. "If I post short-form educational content every day for 30 days on the topic of freelance pricing, I will grow my email list by 100 subscribers." Step two: define your variables. Format, frequency, topic, platform. Step three: post consistently without changing variables mid-experiment. Step four: measure at day 30. What worked? What didn't? What surprised you? Step five: iterate and run the next experiment. This structure turns content into a learning machine. You're not just posting. You're gathering data that tells you how to post better. Treat your creator journey like a scientist, not a performer.
**CTA:** Follow for the full 30-day experiment framework.
**On-screen text:** ["State your hypothesis first", "Don't change variables mid-run", "Measure at day 30 only", "Creator as scientist not performer"]

## Script 4: The 30-Day Experiment on a Single Topic
**Hook (0-3s):** One topic. Thirty days. Every variation possible. Here's what happens.
**Body:** Pick one topic you know well. Spend 30 days posting every variation of that topic you can think of. Different angles. Different formats. Different hooks. Different examples. You'll find three things. The variations that land, the ones that flop, and one angle you didn't expect to resonate that becomes your content pillar. That unexpected angle is gold. It's the thing your audience wants that you didn't know to offer. You only find it through volume and variation. Single-topic experiments also train the algorithm to classify you clearly. By day 30, the platform knows exactly who to show your content to. That's worth more than any follower tactic.

## Script 5: What Most People Do Wrong in the First 30 Days
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people ruin their 30-day experiment by doing this on day ten.
**Body:** They check their metrics too early and let them dictate the strategy. Day three, low views, they change the topic. Day seven, low engagement, they change the format. Day ten, still slow, they give up. Every time they touch the variable, they reset the experiment. The data at day ten means almost nothing. The platform hasn't amplified yet. The audience hasn't found you yet. You haven't found your stride yet. The rule is: look at data only at day 30. Do not optimize mid-experiment. Post consistently, stay the course, and review at the end. The worst thing you can do is interfere with your own test.
**CTA:** Follow to run a real 30-day experiment without self-sabotage.
**On-screen text:** ["Don't check metrics before day 30", "Changing variables resets the test", "Day 10 data is meaningless", "Don't interfere with your own test"]

## Script 6: The 30-Day Experiment Results Framework
**Hook (0-3s):** Day 30 is here. Now what? Here's exactly how to read your results.
**Body:** Look at your top 3 performing posts. What do they have in common? Topic? Format? Hook style? Tone? That commonality is your signal. Look at your bottom 3. Same analysis. Now look at the middle. These are your baseline. Your strategy for the next 30 days is simple. Do more of what the top 3 had in common. Cut what the bottom 3 had in common. Test one new variable. This review takes 20 minutes and it makes your next month materially better than your last. The creators who improve fastest are not the most talented. They're the most rigorous about reviewing and applying their data.
**CTA:** Save this review framework for your day 30 analysis.
**On-screen text:** ["Top 3 = find the common thread", "Bottom 3 = what to cut", "Next 30 = apply the signal", "Rigorous review beats raw talent"]
