## Threads

**6. How to pick your first digital product**

1/ Choosing your first digital product doesn't require a market research sprint. It requires answering four questions honestly:

2/ What problem have I already solved for myself? What do people ask me about most often? What knowledge do I have that took me longer than it should have? What could I explain well in under 10 pages?

3/ The intersection of those answers is your first product. It won't be perfect. It will be better than anything built through pure theory.

4/ Most people overthink product selection because they're afraid of building the wrong thing. The cost of building the wrong thing is a few weeks. The cost of building nothing is the whole opportunity.

---

**7. Why the one-person model scales**

1/ One-person businesses used to hit a ceiling because humans have 24 hours. That ceiling has moved.

2/ AI handles research, drafts, and formatting. Automation handles distribution, follow-up, and delivery. Systems handle the customer experience when you're not there.

3/ This doesn't mean the business runs itself. It means you can operate at a level of output that previously required a small team.

4/ The one-person model doesn't scale because of hustle. It scales because you become the architect of systems instead of the executor of tasks.

---

**8. The content to customer pipeline**

1/ The content-to-customer pipeline has four stages. Most people only build two of them.

2/ Awareness: broad content that gets you discovered. Education: deeper content that builds trust and demonstrates expertise. Offer: the moment you make the ask clearly. Delivery: the experience after they buy that produces referrals.

3/ Most creators build awareness content well. They never build the education layer. So the audience grows but the conversion never comes.

4/ The education layer is where you write the specific, longer, more useful content that makes someone think "this person clearly knows what they're doing." That's the trust that converts.

---

**9. On systems vs willpower**

1/ If your productivity depends on motivation, your business will be inconsistent. Motivation is a variable. Systems are constants.

2/ A system is just a decision made in advance. You've already decided when content gets written, when products get built, when outreach happens. You don't need to decide again every day.

3/ This is why professionals in any field don't wait to "feel like it." The feeling follows the action, not the other way around.

4/ Build systems for the outputs you need most. Protect those systems from your own moods. That's the discipline.

---

**10. On publishing before you're ready**

1/ "Not ready to publish" is usually not a quality problem. It's a fear problem.

2/ The post you're holding back because it's "not good enough" is probably better than 80% of what's already out there. The bar for useful is lower than the bar you've set in your head.

3/ Perfectionism in content creation is a way of staying safe. Nothing can be criticized if nothing is published.

4/ Publish the thing. The feedback is the data you need. The alternative is waiting until the moment feels right, which, for most people, never comes.
