# Twitter/X Batch 120

## Tweet 1
The one-person business model is not about working less.

It's about making sure every hour of work has a chance to compound.

That's a fundamentally different relationship with time than a salary.

## Tweet 2
1/

Most people quit before they reach what I call the "proof point."

This is the moment when your work starts to attract people who found you without being told to.

2/

Before the proof point, everything feels like it's not working.

After it, everything feels like it was always going to work.

The proof point is usually somewhere between month 8 and month 18 of consistent, focused effort.

3/

Almost everyone quits between month 3 and month 6.

That's the only reason the proof point is rare. Not because it's hard to reach. Because most people stop just before they would have reached it.

## Tweet 3
The most underrated skill in the creator economy is curation.

There is more information than anyone can process. The person who filters it with taste and judgment provides enormous value.

## Tweet 4
Consistency is not a personality trait. It's a system output.

If you're inconsistent, your system is broken. Not your character.

Fix the system.

## Tweet 5
1/

Here is the only content strategy you need:

Pick a specific person. Understand their specific problem. Write one thing per day that proves you understand it.

2/

Do that for a year.

You won't need a strategy after that. You'll have a body of work that self-selects the right audience.

3/

The problem is that this sounds too simple.

So people keep searching for the more sophisticated version, not realizing the simplicity is the sophistication.

## Tweet 6
Digital products have an asymmetric risk profile.

Downside: time spent building something that doesn't sell.

Upside: something that earns indefinitely with near-zero marginal cost.

Most businesses can't offer that ratio.

## Tweet 7
The person who creates from a genuine point of view will outlast the person who creates for the algorithm every time.

Algorithms change. Points of view compound.

## Tweet 8
1/

What separates solopreneurs who scale from those who plateau is almost always the same thing:

The ones who scale figured out how to systematize their thinking.

2/

Not their execution. Their thinking.

They built frameworks for the decisions they make repeatedly.

Then they turned those frameworks into content, products, and offers.

3/

Your lived experience solving a problem is a framework in rough form.

The work is extraction: pulling the repeatable structure out of the specific experience and making it transferable.

That's what a product is. That's what a course is. That's what a newsletter issue is.

## Tweet 9
Stop asking "what should I post."

Start asking "what do I believe that most people in my space don't say out loud."

That's where the interesting content lives.

## Tweet 10
Your first 100 pieces of content are not for an audience.

They are for you. They are how you find your voice, your thesis, and the ideas worth building on.

Most people want the audience before they've done the work of finding their voice.

It doesn't work that way.
