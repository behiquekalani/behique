**Slide 6:** @behikeai posts what works. Save this if you are thinking about walking away.

**Caption:** The opportunity cost of quitting is almost never discussed honestly.

Everyone says "do not quit." But nobody breaks down what quitting at month 6 actually costs versus staying for month 12.

**Hook:** There are three types of content creators. Only one of them builds a real business.

**Slide 1:** There are three types of content creators. Only one of them builds a real business.

**Slide 2:** Type 1 — The Sprinter.
Posts 30 times in January. Disappears in March. Returns in June with "I'm back."
Has 4 followers at month 18.
This is the majority.

**Slide 3:** Type 2 — The Optimizer.
Chases every trend. Changes niche every 8 weeks based on what is performing. Builds no identity, no trust, no library.
Has engagement but no authority at month 18.

**Slide 4:** Type 3 — The Builder.
Posts 3-5 times a week with clear niche focus. Does not chase trends. Does not restart.
Tracks one metric per quarter and adjusts slowly.
Has a recognizable presence and growing revenue at month 18.

**Slide 5:** The Builder does not win because they are the most talented.
They win because they never gave the algorithm a reason to forget them.
Consistency is not a character trait. It is a system. You build it, then you follow it.

**Slide 6:** @behikeai is the Builder's playbook. Follow for the systems that make consistency automatic.

**Caption:** The content creator landscape is not a talent competition. It is a consistency competition.

The most talented people in your niche will quit before the math works. The people with average content and above-average consistency will outlast them every single time.

This is not an inspiring hypothesis. It is observable. Go look at the accounts with 100K followers. Read their oldest posts from 3 years ago. The content was not better than what you are making now. It was consistent.
