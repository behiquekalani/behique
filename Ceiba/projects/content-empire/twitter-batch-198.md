## Threads

**Thread 1: The real difference between social media and email**

1/ Social media and email are not the same type of tool. Using them interchangeably is one of the most expensive mistakes online creators make.

2/ Social media is rented land. You build on it, but you don't own it. The platform sets the rules. The algorithm decides your reach.

3/ Email is owned land. You have a direct, unmediated line to your reader. No algorithm. No reach throttle. No platform between you and them.

4/ In 2012, Facebook organic reach was 16%. Today it's under 2%. Every platform follows the same trajectory: attract creators, then charge them to reach the audience they built.

5/ Email has not fundamentally changed in 30 years. It still delivers. It still converts. It still belongs to the person who built the list.

6/ Build on rented land if you have to. But own your list. Always.

---

**Thread 2: How to start building an email list from zero**

1/ "I'll build my email list later" has killed more online businesses than bad products ever did. Here's how to start today.

2/ Step 1: Choose a platform. ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or MailerLite for beginners. Set up a free account in under an hour.

3/ Step 2: Create one lead magnet. Not five. One. It should solve one specific problem in under 15 minutes. PDF, checklist, email course.

4/ Step 3: Create a simple landing page. One headline, one sentence of description, one form. That's it.

5/ Step 4: Put the link in every piece of content you publish. Every post, every thread, every bio. Drive traffic to the list.

6/ Step 5: Email your list every week. Consistency compounds. A list you email weekly outperforms a list you email quarterly by 10x in engagement.

---

**Thread 3: What to send your email list (and how often)**

1/ Most people build an email list and then freeze because they don't know what to send. Here's a framework that eliminates that.

2/ The goal of every email: give value first, then ask for something. Not every email needs a pitch. Most shouldn't have one.

3/ A simple weekly rhythm: 3 emails per week. One long-form insight. One short observation. One curated resource or recommendation.

4/ Monthly: one email that pitches a product, a service, or a paid offer. Just one. The other 11 emails earn the right to send it.

5/ The creators who say "email doesn't work" are the ones who only email when they're selling something. That's not a list. That's a broadcast channel.

6/ Send something worth reading. Do it consistently. The results will follow in 3-6 months, not 3-6 days.

---

**Thread 4: The conversion math that makes email non-negotiable**

1/ Let me show you why I care more about 500 email subscribers than 50,000 Twitter followers.

2/ Scenario A: 50,000 Twitter followers. 2% organic reach = 1,000 people see your offer post. 1% click rate = 10 buyers at $47 = $470.

3/ Scenario B: 500 email subscribers with 35% open rate = 175 readers. 5% conversion = 8.75 buyers at $97 = $849.

4/ The email list is 100x smaller. It generates nearly 2x the revenue per send.

5/ Email compounds differently than social. Every subscriber you add increases your base. Every product you launch goes to the same list. The list gets more valuable over time.

6/ The math on building an email list is not a close call.

---

**Thread 5: The welcome sequence that turns subscribers into buyers**

1/ Most people send one welcome email and then wait. The welcome sequence is the highest-leverage thing you can build for your list.

2/ Email 1 (Day 0): Deliver the lead magnet. Say who you are in one paragraph. Set expectations for what they'll receive.

3/ Email 2 (Day 2): Tell your origin story. Not your resume. The moment you realized what you know now that you didn't know before.

4/ Email 3 (Day 4): Share your single most useful insight. The thing that, if someone understood it, would change how they approach their problem.

5/ Email 4 (Day 6): Introduce one paid product. Not with a hard sell. With a "this is what I built for people who want to go deeper."

6/ Email 5 (Day 8): Ask a question. "What's the biggest challenge you're facing with [topic]?" The replies will tell you what to build next.
