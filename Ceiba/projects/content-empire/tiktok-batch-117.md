## Topic: Why You Should Start With Services Before Products

## TikTok 1: The Product Trap Nobody Warns You About

**Hook (0-2s):**
"Building a product before you have customers is just expensive guessing."

**Script:**
Here's the problem with starting with a product.

You spend weeks building it. You design the cover, set the price, write the sales page. You launch.

And then you hear nothing.

Not because the product is bad. Because you built it for an imaginary customer.

Services fix this.

When you offer a service, you talk to real people with real problems. They tell you exactly what they need. You deliver it. You learn what worked and what didn't.

After ten clients, you know more about your market than any product researcher could tell you. You know the exact words people use to describe their problem. You know what they've already tried. You know what they're willing to pay.

Now you build the product. And it sells because it was built from real conversations, not assumptions.

Services first. Products second. That's the order.

## TikTok 2: Services Are Market Research You Get Paid For

**Hook (0-2s):**
"What if your market research paid you $2,000?"

**Script:**
That's what a service client is.

You're not just earning money. You're getting direct access to the mind of your ideal customer.

Every client you work with tells you:
What problem they had before they found you.
What they've tried before.
What confused them about the solution.
What results they actually wanted.

All of that is product research.

After five clients, you have enough data to write a product description that converts. After ten, you can write an email sequence that sounds exactly like your market talks to itself.

This is why the big creators you follow had services before they had courses. The service funded the research. The research built the product. The product scaled the income.

You don't have to guess when you have clients telling you the answer.

## TikTok 3: You Only Need One Client to Start

**Hook (0-2s):**
"You don't need a website, a logo, or a social media presence to land your first client."

**Script:**
You need a skill and a message.

The skill is the thing you can do that helps someone get a result they want. You already have one.

The message is: "I help [specific person] do [specific result]. Here's proof."

That's it.

Post that in three places: your LinkedIn, your Twitter, your Instagram bio. Then DM five people who might know someone who needs that result.

One of them will say yes or forward your message.

Do the work. Do it better than they expected. Ask them to refer you.

Repeat ten times.

Now you have case studies, testimonials, and direct insight into the market. No product launch risk. No inventory. No ads.

One client is the business model. The product comes later when you know exactly what to build.

## TikTok 4: When to Switch From Services to Products

**Hook (0-2s):**
"There's a specific moment to stop doing services and start building products. Most people miss it."

**Script:**
You stay in services until you notice a pattern.

Three clients have the same problem. They ask the same questions. They need the same process.

That's the moment.

That pattern is the outline of your product. The questions they ask are your FAQ. The process you use to solve their problem is your curriculum.

You don't switch fully from services to products. You add the product as a lower-priced entry point. Some people want the product. Some want you personally. You offer both.

The service keeps money coming in. The product scales without your time.

Over time, the product income grows. The service becomes selective, higher-priced, and more fulfilling because you only take clients you actually want.

That's the transition. Stay in services until the pattern repeats three times. Then build.

## TikTok 5: The Real Reason People Skip Services

**Hook (0-2s):**
"The real reason you want to skip services and go straight to products is fear."

**Script:**
Hear me out.

A product feels safer. If nobody buys it, you can tell yourself it was the marketing. Or the timing. Or the algorithm.

A service means a real person either says yes or no to you directly. That's scary.

But that fear is also the feedback loop you need.

When someone says no to your service offer, they're telling you something your product launch never will. Too expensive. Wrong audience. Not the right framing.

That data is worth more than any course on marketing.

The people building sustainable businesses did the uncomfortable part first. They pitched themselves. They got rejected. They adjusted. They got better.

The product came after. And it was built on a foundation of real evidence.

Stop hiding behind product launches. Go talk to someone who needs help.
