## What Happens Next

Here is the full timeline from here to handoff:

**Before the build session**
You gather the access credentials and process documentation listed below. Takes about 30-60 minutes. Nothing technical required.

**Week 1: Build session 1**
We meet via screen share. I connect your tools, build the first workflow, and walk you through how it works before we close out. You will see the automation running against real data before the session ends.

**Week 2: Testing**
The system runs on live inputs. You and your team use it under real conditions. I check in to adjust anything that does not match how your operation actually works.

**Week 3: Handoff**
Final confirmation that everything is working as described. You receive written documentation, a walkthrough video, and a support contact for questions. The 30-day support window begins on handoff day.

*If your package includes more than one workflow, the timeline extends by one week per additional workflow. I will confirm the exact schedule on our first call.*

## How to Prepare for the Build Session

**Show up with time.** The first hour of the session requires your full attention. After that, you can step away while I build. Block at least 90 minutes on your calendar.

**Have the process fresh in your mind.** I will ask clarifying questions about edge cases. Knowing the details of how exceptions are handled (what happens when X, what do you do if Y) speeds things up significantly.

**Avoid back-to-back meetings.** You will want a few minutes after the session to test the automation before your next call.

**Have another browser tab open with your email or the relevant tool.** I will likely ask you to send a test message or trigger the workflow manually so we can confirm it runs correctly.

## What the Build Session Looks Like

I share my screen. You watch.

Here is what we do:

1. I connect your accounts to the automation platform (n8n or Zapier).
2. I build the first workflow step by step, explaining what each piece does as I go. This is not a lecture. I keep it short. The point is that you understand the basic logic so you can recognize when something is working and when it is not.
3. We test the workflow together using a real input. You trigger it, we watch it run, I confirm the output looks right.
4. If something does not match your actual process, we adjust before ending the session.
5. I show you where to check on the workflow status (the dashboard, logs, or error alerts, depending on the platform).

The session ends when the automation is confirmed working and you have seen it run at least once on real data.

## Common Questions New Clients Ask

**Can I make changes to the workflow after it is built?**
Yes. Minor changes (updating a response template, changing a schedule, adding a new email address to a list) are usually simple. I will show you how to do those yourself during handoff. More significant changes (adding a new step, connecting a new platform) are outside the support window scope and would be quoted separately.

**What if my team does not use the tool I connected?**
Tell me before the build session. It is easier to change platforms before the workflow is built than after. If you discover this during the session, we can pivot, but it adds time.

**Do I need to keep paying for n8n or Zapier after the engagement?**
Yes. The automation platform is a third-party subscription. Most clients pay $20-50 per month for n8n or Zapier depending on volume. This is billed directly to you and is not included in my fee. I will tell you which tier you need before the session so there are no surprises.

**What if I want to add a workflow later?**
After the engagement closes, any new work is quoted as a new project. I offer returning clients a lower rate than new clients. The 2-Hour AI Install at $499 is a common entry point for adding single workflows later.

**What if the automation breaks after the support window closes?**
Automations built with n8n or Zapier are stable if the platforms they connect to do not change their APIs. If a connected platform makes a breaking change (which does happen, rarely), I will quote a fix. The documentation I provide will help you or another developer diagnose the issue if needed.
