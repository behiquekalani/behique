# Testimonial Collection Templates

Use these as-is. They are intentionally short. Long DM requests get ignored.

---

## DM Scripts for Asking Buyers

### Script 1 - Low-ticket product ($4.99-$9.99)

> Hey [name], saw you picked up [product name]. Hope it's useful. Quick question: what was the main thing you got out of it? Even one sentence is helpful. No pressure.

### Script 2 - Mid-ticket product ($12.99-$14.99)

> Hey [name], thanks for grabbing [product name]. Curious how it's landing for you so far. Is there anything specific that clicked or anything you'd have done differently? Honest answer is more useful than a positive one.

### Script 3 - Premium product ($19.99)

> Hey [name], you picked up [product name] recently. I'd love to know what drew you to it and whether it matched what you expected. If you got value from it, I might ask if I can quote you in future materials, but no pressure either way. What stood out?

### Script 4 - Post-application or hands-on result

> Hey [name], it's been a couple weeks since you grabbed [product name]. Have you had a chance to apply anything from it? Even a small result or a "here's what I tried" is the kind of thing that helps other people decide if it's right for them.

### Script 5 - Content creator or public figure buyer

> Hey [name], noticed you bought [product name]. If you've found it worth talking about, I'd love it if you shared your take publicly, even just a sentence. Happy to return the favor and share your content with the Behike audience. No obligation.

## Follow-Up Scripts (No Response After 7 Days)

### Follow-up 1 - General

> Hey, following up on my last message. No stress if you haven't gotten to it yet. If you ever have thoughts on [product name], I'm interested. Otherwise no worries.

### Follow-up 2 - Reframe as feedback

> Hey [name], I'm not chasing a five-star review. Even if something didn't work for you, that's useful. What was your experience with [product name]?

### Follow-up 3 - Final, closes the loop

> Last message on this, I promise. If you have 30 seconds to leave a rating on the Gumroad page for [product name], I'd appreciate it. That's it. Link: [product URL].

## Testimonial Format for Landing Pages

When formatting a testimonial on a product landing page, include:

1. The quote. Keep it under 50 words. If the original is longer, cut to the most specific, concrete part. Generic praise ("this was great") is worth less than specific result ("I set up the budget tracker in 20 minutes and found two subscriptions I forgot I was paying for").
2. First name and last initial. (e.g., "Marcus R.")
3. Brief descriptor. One phrase that tells the reader who this person is relative to the product. (e.g., "freelance designer," "Shopify store owner," "computer science student")
4. Optional: platform or handle if the person consented to being named publicly.

**Layout:**

> "Specific, concrete thing the product did for them. Ideally with a number, a before/after, or a specific decision it helped them make."
>
> -- First Name L., descriptor

Do not use stock photos to represent real people. Do not fabricate testimonials. Do not edit quotes to change their meaning. Trimming for length and clarity is acceptable; changing what the person said is not.

## Case Study Template (300 words)

Use this to turn a buyer's result into a short story. Fill in the brackets.

---

**[First name] [brief descriptor, e.g., "runs a one-person design studio in Austin"].**

Before picking up [product name], [describe the specific problem or situation in 1-2 sentences. What were they struggling with? What were they trying to figure out?].

[Quote from the buyer about the problem or their situation before using the product.]

[Describe what they did with the product. Not just "they read it." What specifically did they apply or try? 2-3 sentences.]

The result: [specific outcome. If there's a number, use it. If not, describe the qualitative change clearly. One sentence.]

[Quote from the buyer about the result or experience.]

[Close with one sentence about where they are now or what they're doing next that this made possible.]

---

Maria runs a small content agency in Miami. Before picking up the Solopreneur OS, she was handling five clients, writing every deliverable herself, and burning out by Thursday each week.

"I knew I needed systems but every business book I tried was written for a team of ten. I don't have a team. I needed something written for how I actually work."

She went through Module 3 (the ADHD Builder's Edge) first, built her first content scheduling system using the framework in Module 4, and within two weeks had batched three weeks of client content in two afternoons.

The result: she cut her active working hours by roughly 30% in the first month without dropping a client.

"The Content Waterfall module alone justified the price. I stopped thinking about what to make and started thinking about when to make it."

She's now in the process of building a second income stream using the Value Ladder framework from Module 7.

## Buyer Review Email Sequence (3 emails)

These go to buyers automatically after purchase. Subject lines are plain and direct.

---

### Email 1 - Day 3 After Purchase

**Subject:** Quick check-in on [product name]

---

Hey,

You picked up [product name] a few days ago.

Have you had a chance to go through it? If something clicked or you have a question, just reply here.

If you want to leave a quick rating on Gumroad, the link is: [product URL]

Thanks for buying.

Kalani

---

### Email 2 - Day 7 After Purchase

**Subject:** One question

---

Hey,

It's been a week since you grabbed [product name].

One question: what's one thing you got from it, or one thing you tried based on it?

I read every reply. The honest answers, good or bad, are what help me make better products.

If you have a positive experience worth sharing, I'd also ask if I can quote you. If yes, just say so in your reply.

Kalani

---

### Email 3 - Day 14 After Purchase

**Subject:** Last follow-up

---

Hey,

Last email on this.

If [product name] was useful, a short review on the Gumroad page goes a long way. It helps other people find it. Takes about 90 seconds.

Link: [product URL]

If something didn't work for you, I'd genuinely like to know. Reply and tell me. No defensiveness on my end.

Either way, thanks for the purchase.

Kalani

---

*End of templates. These can be loaded directly into any email tool that supports merge tags. Replace [product name] and [product URL] with the relevant product on send.*
