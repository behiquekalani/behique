**Topic: How to create an upsell that feels natural**

## TikTok 1: Why Your Upsells Feel Desperate
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Wait, before you go..." is not an upsell strategy. Here's what actually works.

**Script:**
Most upsells feel gross because they're designed to extract money, not solve a problem.

A natural upsell answers a question the buyer is already asking the moment they finish the purchase. "Okay, I bought this. What do I do next?"

That next thing is your upsell.

If your product teaches someone how to build a landing page, the natural next question is "what do I do with the leads I capture?" That's your upsell. An email sequence template. A follow-up framework. Whatever answers the "what's next" question.

The mistake is offering something unrelated or something that feels like filler content just to add a price.

Find the question your buyer is asking at checkout. Build something that answers it. That's the whole formula.

**CTA:**
What question does your buyer have immediately after purchasing your product? That question is your upsell brief.

## TikTok 2: The 3-Part Upsell Formula
**Hook (0-2s):**
A good upsell has three parts. Most people only use one.

**Script:**
Here's how to structure an upsell that converts without feeling like a squeeze.

Part one: timing. The offer comes immediately after the purchase decision, not before. The buyer is already in buying mode. Their psychology is primed. This is the highest-trust moment you will have with a new customer. Use it well.

Part two: logical continuation. The upsell must feel like the obvious next step, not a separate product. "You just bought the map. You might also want the compass." Same journey. Next tool.

Part three: reduced friction. The upsell checkout should not require re-entering credit card information. One click. Pre-filled. The moment you add friction, you lose the window.

These three parts together create an upsell that doesn't feel like an upsell. It feels like good product design.

**CTA:**
Does your upsell flow have all three of these? Which one is missing?

## TikTok 3: Order Bumps vs. Upsells (Most People Confuse These)
**Hook (0-2s):**
An order bump and an upsell are not the same thing. Using the wrong one costs you money.

**Script:**
An order bump is a small, impulsive addition shown on the checkout page before purchase. Think of it like the candy bar at the grocery checkout. Low price, immediate value, requires almost no thought. Usually $7 to $27.

An upsell is a higher-value offer shown after the purchase. The buyer has already committed. You're offering them a way to get a better, faster, or more complete result. Usually $47 to $197.

Most solopreneurs skip the order bump and try to upsell at checkout. That's too much decision at once. The buyer is already processing the main purchase. Adding a big second decision at that moment creates confusion and abandonment.

Sequence matters: order bump on the checkout page, upsell on the thank-you page.

Get both right and your average order value goes up 30 to 60% without a single new customer.

**CTA:**
What's a small, obvious add-on to your main product that would make a good order bump? Think about it.

## TikTok 4: The Upsell That Converts 20%+
**Hook (0-2s):**
The highest-converting upsell I've seen converts at 25%. It has one specific characteristic.

**Script:**
The highest-converting upsells share one thing: they solve the most obvious problem the buyer will face while implementing the main product.

Not a new feature. Not extra content. A solution to the obstacle that's coming.

If your product is a template kit for building a digital product funnel, the highest-converting upsell is probably a done-for-you version of the first email sequence. Because after they download the templates, the first thing they'll get stuck on is writing emails.

You're not guessing at what they might want. You're seeing around the corner. "Here's the thing that's going to slow you down next. I already solved it."

That's the psychology that gets 20%+ conversion on an upsell. Anticipation of the next obstacle, not expansion of the current offer.

**CTA:**
What's the first obstacle your customer will hit after buying your product? Build that into your upsell.

## TikTok 5: The Upsell Page That Writes Itself
**Hook (0-2s):**
Your upsell page should take 30 minutes to write. Here's the template.

**Script:**
Upsell page structure. Five elements. Nothing else.

One: "You just made a smart decision." One sentence confirming their purchase. This closes the purchase psychologically.

Two: "Here's the one thing that will get in your way." Name the specific obstacle they're about to face. This creates immediate relevance.

Three: "Here's how [Product Name] solves that." Two to four sentences, no more. Clear, specific, fast.

Four: One sentence of social proof. Not a testimonial wall. One quote from someone who used it and got a result.

Five: The offer. Price, what they get, one-click button.

That's the whole page. No long-form copy. No multiple sections. No testimonial carousels.

The key is that every element earns its place by being relevant to the thing they just bought. If it drifts from that, cut it.

**CTA:**
Build this page this week. If you have a product, you have everything you need to write this in 30 minutes.
