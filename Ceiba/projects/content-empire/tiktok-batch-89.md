## TikTok 5: What Makes Content Go Viral
**Hook:**
Content goes viral for one reason: it makes people want to share their reaction.

**Script:**
People share content that helps them express something they already believed but couldn't articulate. They share content that makes them look smart, informed, or relatable to their audience. They share content that surprises them. When you create, ask yourself: if someone forwarded this to a friend, what would they say? "This is exactly how I feel." Or "you have to see this." Or "this explains something I've been thinking about." If you can't answer that question, the content probably won't spread. Design for the share, not just the view. The view is passive. The share is active. Active sharing is what moves the needle.

**CTA:**

## TikTok 8: AI Writing vs Human Writing
**Hook:**
Here's the real difference between AI content and human content.

**Script:**
AI is very good at producing competent content. It can write a solid blog post, a serviceable email, a decent product description. What it can't do is have an opinion that comes from lived experience. It can't describe the specific failure you had in March that taught you something. It can't reference the exact book that changed your perspective. It can't convey the frustration of a real moment with a real outcome. Those specific details are what make content feel real and build actual trust. The best way to use AI is to produce the structure and the baseline draft, then add the specific human details that make it yours. Competent plus specific equals compelling.

## TikTok 9: The Four-Hour Content Week
**Hook:**
I run a full content operation in four hours a week. Here's the breakdown.

**Script:**
One hour on Monday: plan the week. Five topics, five hooks, five CTAs. One hour on Tuesday: record everything in one session, back to back. One hour on Wednesday: edit and schedule. One hour on Friday: review what performed, adjust next week's plan. That's it. The key is ruthless constraint. You don't need to post three times a day to build an audience. You need to post consistently and improve with every batch. Four high-quality posts a week, done every week for a year, beats twenty posts a week for two months followed by a burnout break. Sustainable beats optimal every time.
