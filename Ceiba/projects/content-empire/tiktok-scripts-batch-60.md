# TikTok Scripts — Batch 60
# Topic: The Content Strategy That Built My Audience From Zero
# Voice: Dan Koe tone, direct, no em dashes, no hype, no exclamation marks

---

## Script 60-1: The Only Content Strategy You Need

**Hook (0-3s):**
I grew from zero without a viral video, without dancing, and without posting every hour.

**Body:**
The strategy: pick one problem your ideal reader has. Write about that problem every day from a slightly different angle.

That is it.

Most people fail at content because they try to be interesting to everyone. You cannot build an audience that way. You build an audience by being the most useful resource for a specific type of person.

Every piece of content should make one person think: this creator understands my situation better than anyone else.

If you are doing that consistently, growth is just a matter of time and distribution.

**CTA:**
What is the one problem you want to be known for solving. Comment it.

## Script 60-2: Why Consistency Beats Quality Early

**Hook (0-3s):**
Your first 100 pieces of content are not for your audience. They are for you.

**Body:**
This is what nobody says when they tell you to be consistent.

The early content is how you figure out your voice, your angle, and what actually resonates. You cannot know that in theory. You learn it by publishing and watching what happens.

The creators who grow are not the ones who waited until they had the perfect system. They are the ones who shipped imperfect content until they developed a good system through iteration.

Consistency compounds in two ways: your skill improves, and the algorithm starts distributing your work further.

Both require the same input: showing up.

**CTA:**
Post something today. It does not have to be perfect.

## Script 60-3: The Hook Is Ninety Percent of the Result

**Hook (0-3s):**
The difference between a post with 200 views and one with 200,000 is usually the first three seconds.

**Body:**
The hook decides whether someone stops or scrolls.

The body decides whether they finish or leave.

The CTA decides whether they act or forget.

But none of that matters if the hook does not work.

Spend as much time on your opening line as you do on the entire rest of the post. Test different angles on the same idea. Same content, different hook, completely different results.

The most important writing skill in short-form content is not storytelling. It is pattern interruption.

**CTA:**
Write five different hooks for your next post before you pick one.

## Script 60-4: The Niche Trap

**Hook (0-3s):**
You do not need to niche down as much as everyone tells you.

**Body:**
Here is what actually matters: you need a consistent perspective, not a narrow topic.

The creators with the biggest audiences are not the ones who only talk about one thing. They are the ones who have a clear worldview and filter every topic through it.

Dan Koe talks about design, philosophy, money, and fitness. The consistency is not the topic. It is the lens.

Your lens is what you believe about how the world works, how success happens, how people should think.

Develop a strong perspective. Apply it to multiple relevant topics. That is a more durable content strategy than picking one narrow niche and staying trapped inside it.

**CTA:**
What is your worldview. Start there.

---

## Script 60-5: The Compounding Content Archive

**Hook (0-3s):**
A post I wrote nine months ago brought in customers this week.

**Body:**
This is what people miss about content marketing.

Every piece of content you create is a permanent asset. It keeps getting discovered through search, shares, and algorithm recommendations long after you posted it.

The creator who has been publishing for two years has hundreds of assets working for them simultaneously. The one who started last week has three.

The gap compounds over time. Which means the best time to start was last year. The second best time is today.

Consistency is not about daily willpower. It is about building an archive that works while you sleep.

Every post is a small bet. Most will not hit. A few will keep paying out for years.

**CTA:**
Start the archive. You will thank yourself in twelve months.
