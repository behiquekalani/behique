## Script 1: You Already Have What You Need

**Hook (0-3s):** The skills you use for free every day are the skills someone will pay for.

**Body:**
The most common lie people tell themselves about starting a business is "I don't have anything valuable to offer."

Think about the last five times someone came to you for help.

What did they ask? What problem did you solve? What did you explain in a way that finally clicked for them?

That is your product.

The accountant friend everyone texts for tax questions. The person everyone calls to fix their computer. The one people ask how they learned to edit videos.

Those casual moments of being useful are pointing directly at what the market will pay for.

You have not been building a business around your skills because you do not see your own competence clearly.

The skills you take for granted are valuable to someone who doesn't have them.

That asymmetry is the foundation of a business.

## Script 2: The Skills Audit That Reveals Your Business

**Hook (0-3s):** Do this one exercise and your business idea will become obvious.

**Body:**
Write down every skill you have. Not just professional skills. Everything.

Can you write well? Can you edit video? Can you organize systems? Can you cook specific food? Can you speak another language? Can you code even a little? Can you negotiate? Can you build habits?

Now next to each skill, write the first person you'd sell it to.

Not a market. A person. A specific person you know or can imagine.

Now circle the ones where the gap between your skill level and their current ability is largest.

That gap is value. The wider the gap, the more they'll pay to cross it faster.

The business is not in your favorite skills. It's in the skills where the gap is widest between you and your customer.

Do this audit. It takes twenty minutes. It will show you what to build.

## Script 3: How to Package a Skill Into a Product in One Weekend

**Hook (0-3s):** You can turn a skill into a sellable product in 48 hours. Here's how.

**Body:**
Friday night: define the one specific outcome your skill delivers. Not "I help with social media." "I help freelancers write client proposals that close 60% of the time."

Saturday morning: write down every step you take when you use this skill to get that outcome. Don't organize it yet. Just dump every step on paper.

Saturday afternoon: organize those steps into a logical sequence. Number them. Remove anything that's not essential for a beginner.

Saturday evening: decide on the format. A written guide. A checklist. A video walkthrough. A template. Pick the simplest one that communicates the steps.

Sunday: produce the product. First draft. Imperfect. Done.

Sunday evening: write a one-paragraph description of the problem it solves and who it's for.

That's your product. It is not perfect. But perfect does not sell. Done sells.

**On-screen text:**

- "Saturday → process dump + organize"
- "Sunday → produce + describe"
- "Done > perfect"

## Script 5: Why Experience Beats Credentials Every Time Online

**Hook (0-3s):** Nobody checked my credentials. They checked my results.

**Body:**
The credential economy does not transfer to the internet in the way people think.

Online, people buy from people who have solved the exact problem they're trying to solve.

Not from people with the most certifications. Not from people with the most followers. From people who have credible, specific, relevant results.

You don't need a degree in nutrition to help people build a meal prep system. You need to have actually built one that worked for you.

You don't need a marketing certification to help small business owners get more clients. You need to have gotten clients for a small business.

Your lived experience is your credential online.

The challenge is that we've been trained to discount lived experience in favor of formal credentials. That training works against you when building an online business.

Document what you've done. Show the results. Be specific.

That's the credential that converts.

## Script 6: The Consulting Shortcut Nobody Talks About

**Hook (0-3s):** The fastest way to turn a skill into income is not a course. It's a consulting offer.

**Body:**
Building a course takes weeks. Recording, editing, hosting, sales page, marketing.

A consulting offer takes an afternoon.

Here's how: write down the specific result you produce using your skill. Write a short description of who you help and how. Set a price. Tell three to five people.

That's it. You're consulting.

Your first client will come through a conversation, not through a sales funnel. It will come from someone in your network who has the problem you solve and trusts you.

Use those first few clients to refine exactly what you do and how you do it. Document the process. Notice what works.

Then, once you've delivered results for real clients, build the course. You now have case studies, you know the questions, and you understand the process deeply enough to teach it.

Start with consulting. Build the course later with the evidence and expertise the consulting gave you.

## Script 7: Stacking Skills Into a Business Nobody Can Copy

**Hook (0-3s):** Your most defensible business is built from an unusual combination of skills, not one exceptional skill.

**Body:**
One skill is easy to compete with. Someone who writes well. Someone who edits video. Someone who understands SEO.

But the combination of skills you personally have is statistically unlikely to be replicated.

You write well and understand engineering and speak Spanish. That combination is your niche. Nobody else has the exact same stack.

This is called a skill stack. Popularized by Scott Adams, the Dilbert creator, who said he was not the funniest cartoonist, not the best artist, not the best writer. But the combination made him unique.

What's your stack?

Write down your top three to five skills. Then write down what problem you can solve that requires all of them together.

That problem is your niche. That business is essentially yours.
