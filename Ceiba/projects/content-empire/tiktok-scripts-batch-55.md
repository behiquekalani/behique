## Script 2: The Tool That Replaced My Customer Support Workflow
**Hook (0-3s):** I stopped answering the same email questions three weeks ago. Here is what I did instead.

**Script:**
I stopped answering the same email questions three weeks ago. Here is what I did instead.

First, I listed every question I had received more than twice. There were about 12. Those 12 questions become your FAQ page and your email templates.

Then I built a simple knowledge base using Notion. Every common question has a clear answer, with screenshots where needed. I linked this from every product delivery email.

Then I set up a Gmail filter that auto-responds to certain keywords with the relevant Notion link.

Now when someone asks a question I have already answered, they get a response in 30 seconds with the exact info they need. I did not type a single word.

The tools used: Notion, Gmail filters, and a good product FAQ page. Free or near-free.

This is not about being cold to customers. It is about getting them answers faster than you can type them manually.

**CTA:** Build your FAQ this week. 12 questions. One hour. Set it up once.

## Script 3: What Replaced My Social Media Manager
**Hook (0-3s):** Paying someone to post your content is one of the easiest things to automate.

**Script:**
Paying someone to post your content is one of the easiest things to automate.

Here is my current setup.

I write all content in one document. One day per week. I get everything done in a 2-hour session.

Then I use Buffer to schedule everything across platforms for the next 7 days. It posts automatically. I am not involved.

For repurposing, I use a simple n8n workflow that takes a long caption and outputs a short version for a different platform. Built it once. Runs whenever I trigger it.

For ideation, I use Claude with a prompt that pulls ideas from my existing content pillars and spits out 20 topic angles in 90 seconds. I pick the ones that feel right and write them in the same session.

The whole content operation: 2 hours on Sunday. That is it.

A social media manager would cost $400 to $1500 a month for the same output. These tools cost me about $20 combined.

**CTA:** Save this. This is your content system for under $25 a month.

## Script 4: The Research Tool That Replaced Hours of Manual Work
**Hook (0-3s):** I used to spend 3 hours researching a topic before writing anything. Not anymore.

**Script:**
I used to spend 3 hours researching a topic before writing anything. Not anymore.

Here is the shift.

I now use Perplexity for any research task that used to take me a browser rabbit hole. It pulls from current sources, gives me citations, and summarizes in 60 seconds what used to take me an hour to compile.

Then I paste the summary into Claude with my voice guide and ask it to outline the content piece based on the research.

Then I write from the outline. My job is judgment and voice. Not information gathering.

The research-to-draft cycle went from 3 hours to under 45 minutes. For every piece of content.

That is not a small efficiency gain. Over a month, that is days of time returned to me.

The tools are not doing my thinking. They are removing the friction between having an idea and executing on it.

That friction is what kills most creative projects before they ship.

**CTA:** Try Perplexity on your next research task and tell me how long it took.
