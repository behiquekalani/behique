# Issue 1402: One Product, One Audience, One Year

**Subject:** The math behind $100K from a single digital product

---

People overcomplicate online business because they think revenue requires complexity. Multiple income streams. A funnel with seven steps. An email sequence that branches twelve ways. But the creators quietly hitting $100K a year often have one product, one platform, and one consistent message.

Here's the math. If you have a $97 digital product and you want to make $100K in a year, you need to sell 1,031 copies. That's about 86 copies a month. If your conversion rate is 2%, you need 4,300 people seeing that offer each month. That's roughly 143 people per day landing on your sales page.

That is not an impossible number. It's a traffic problem, not a creativity problem.

The hard part is that building the machine to generate that traffic takes 6 to 12 months of consistent work before it compounds. Most people quit during that lag phase because they can't see the compounding happening yet. They try a different product, a different platform, a different price. The clock resets every time.

One product. One platform. One message. Long enough to get to the compounding phase.

**Tactical takeaway:** Define your one primary product and your one primary distribution channel this week. Write down the exact traffic number you need monthly to hit your revenue goal. Post that number somewhere visible. Now your job is simple: drive that traffic, improve that conversion rate.

Everything else is a distraction from that equation.
