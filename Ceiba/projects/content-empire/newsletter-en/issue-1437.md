# Issue 1437 — Why Your Niche Isn't Too Small

**Subject:** The fear of a small audience is costing you a real business

---

The most common thing holding creators back from committing to a specific niche is the belief that it's too small to support a real business. A freelance designer who works with therapists, a productivity coach for single parents, a copywriter who only works with food brands. These feel narrow. But narrow is exactly what makes them viable.

Here's the math that most people skip: you don't need a million followers to generate significant income from digital products. You need a few hundred buyers per year. If your product costs $97, you need 1,031 buyers to hit $100k. You do not need a massive audience for that. You need a highly specific audience that has the problem you solve.

The narrower the niche, the lower the customer acquisition cost, the higher the conversion rate, and the more powerful the word-of-mouth. People talk about solutions to their specific problems. They don't talk about generalist content.

Fear of a small audience is actually fear of being known for one thing. That fear keeps creators in the middle, talking to everyone, converting no one.

**Tactical takeaway:** Name the most specific possible version of your audience. Not "entrepreneurs" but "first-year freelance designers who are losing clients to scope creep." Write your next three pieces of content exclusively for them. Notice what happens to engagement quality.

Smaller is more specific. Specific is more powerful.
