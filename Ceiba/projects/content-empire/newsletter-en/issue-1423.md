# Issue 1423 — One Skill, Infinite Products

**Subject:** How solopreneurs build product libraries from a single skill

---

The most durable business model for a one-person operation is one skill expressed at multiple price points. Not ten different skills. One skill, packaged differently for different levels of urgency and commitment.

A writing coach, for example, has one core skill. That skill can become a $9 guide on fixing bad hooks, a $49 template pack, a $197 course on writing a 30-day newsletter, and a $2,000 coaching offer for someone who needs it done right now. Same knowledge. Different packaging, different buyer psychology, different conversion path.

The mistake most creators make is treating each product as a separate identity. They start over each time. The better model is a curriculum: each product is a step in a staircase, and each satisfied buyer naturally moves up to the next level. Your cheapest product builds the trust needed to sell your most expensive one.

You don't need to create ten different things. You need to create the same thing ten different ways, at ten different depths.

**Tactical takeaway:** Map your core skill into three price tiers: a low-cost entry product (under $30), a mid-tier system product ($97-$197), and a premium implementation offer ($500+). Build the cheapest one first. It funds the rest.

One skill is enough. Package it well.
