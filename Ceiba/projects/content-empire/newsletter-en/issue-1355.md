# Issue 1355 — The Creator Economy Is Splitting

**Subject:** Two types of creators are emerging. One is winning.

---

The creator economy is separating into two camps, and the gap between them is growing. The first camp is creating content for platforms, chasing algorithms, optimizing for views and reach, building audiences that the platform owns. The second camp is building businesses where content is the marketing, not the product.

The first camp is vulnerable. Algorithm changes, platform shutdowns, shadowbans, policy updates. Every few months there's a new event that wipes out years of someone's work. These are not rare exceptions. They're built into the structure of platform dependency. When your income is tied to a metric that a company controls, you're an employee without the benefits.

The second camp operates differently. Content drives people to something they own: a list, a product, a community. The platform is a discovery mechanism, not the foundation. A bad quarter on Instagram doesn't tank the business because the business lives somewhere the platform can't touch.

**Tactical takeaway:** Audit your content ecosystem right now. What percentage of your audience is in places you own versus places you rent? If you have 10,000 followers and 400 email subscribers, your real audience is 400. Make every content decision this month in service of growing the owned number, not the borrowed one.

Borrow reach. Build ownership.
