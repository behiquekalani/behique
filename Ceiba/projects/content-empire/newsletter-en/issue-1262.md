# Issue 1262: Digital Products Are Not Passive. They're Presold Labor.

Everyone sells you on "passive income." The honest version is different.

Digital products are presold labor. You work hard once. Then the work keeps paying. That's not passive, that's asymmetric. And asymmetric is what you should be chasing.

The mistake most solopreneurs make is treating a digital product like a side project. They build something, throw it on Gumroad, and wait. Nothing happens. Then they call digital products a scam and go back to trading time for money.

What actually works is treating each product like a small business. It has a customer. It solves a specific problem. It has a sales page that addresses objections. It has traffic directed to it. It has a follow-up product for buyers who want more. When you build that structure around even a $27 PDF, it converts. It compounds. It turns into a catalog.

The catalog is where the real income lives. Not one product. Fifteen. Each one solving a different problem for the same type of person.

**Tactical takeaway:** Before you build the next product, write one paragraph describing the exact person who would buy it and the exact result they'd get. If you can't write that paragraph clearly, the product isn't ready to build.

Clarity before creation.
