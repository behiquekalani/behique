# Issue 1415: The Three-Tier Offer Stack

**Subject:** Why one product is leaving money on the table

---

Most creators operate with a single product at a single price. Someone either buys it or they don't. That's a missed opportunity on both ends: people who can't afford the full offer, and people who would happily pay more for a deeper experience.

The three-tier offer stack solves this. You have an entry product, a core product, and a premium tier. Each tier serves a different level of commitment and budget, and each one feeds the next.

The entry product is low friction and low price. Its job is not revenue generation. Its job is customer acquisition. Getting someone to pay even $10 for something you made converts a stranger into a buyer. Buyers behave differently from free subscribers. They've made a commitment. They're more engaged, more likely to implement, and far more likely to buy again.

The core product is your main offer. This is where the majority of your revenue lives. It solves the primary problem your audience has at a price that reflects genuine value.

The premium tier serves the people who want the outcome but also want your time, your direct input, or a more intensive experience. Coaching, done-with-you services, mastermind groups. This tier has higher price points and lower volume, but it funds the operation and generates your best testimonials.

**Tactical takeaway:** Map your current offers against this framework. If you only have one tier, identify what the other two would be. You don't need all three on day one. But know the direction. Your next product launch should deliberately fill the gap that creates the most natural progression for your existing customers.
