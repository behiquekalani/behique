# Issue 1374: The Niche Trap Most Creators Fall Into

**Subject:** You picked a niche. It might be the wrong kind.

---

The standard advice is to niche down. And the standard advice is right. But there are two types of niches, and one of them works much better than the other. Most creators accidentally pick the wrong one.

A topic niche is: "I make content about productivity." A person niche is: "I make content for first-generation college students trying to build skills their schools didn't teach them." The first describes what you talk about. The second describes who you serve. Topic niches attract people who are interested in the subject. Person niches attract people who feel deeply understood.

People buy from creators who understand them. When your content clearly speaks to a specific type of person in a specific situation, that person feels a connection that no amount of high-quality generic content can create. They think, "this person gets it." That's the beginning of a buyer relationship.

The person niche also makes content decisions far easier. Every time you sit down to write, the question is: what does this person need to hear right now? You're not thinking abstractly about a topic. You're thinking concretely about a person. That specificity transfers directly into the writing.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Write a one-paragraph description of the exact person your content is for. Not demographics. Situation, struggle, and aspiration. Put that description above your desk and read it before you write anything. Your content will sharpen immediately.

Serve one person well before you try to reach everyone.
