# Issue 1440 — The Only Metric That Actually Matters

**Subject:** Stop tracking vanity. Start tracking this.

---

Solopreneurs track everything: followers, impressions, open rates, click-through rates, time on page. Most of it is vanity data. It feels like progress because the numbers are moving. But movement is not direction.

The only metric that actually matters in a solopreneur business is revenue per email subscriber. Everything else is a proxy for this number, and most proxies are loose at best. A massive social following with a small, unengaged email list will generate far less revenue than a modest but targeted list of people who trust you.

Revenue per subscriber tells you how well your entire system is working: the quality of your audience, the relevance of your product, the effectiveness of your email sequence, and the strength of your positioning. When this number goes up, everything is working. When it stays flat despite list growth, something in the middle is broken.

For most solopreneurs at early stages, even $1 per subscriber per month is a healthy benchmark. At $3-5, the business is working well. At $10+, you've built something exceptional.

**Tactical takeaway:** Calculate your revenue per subscriber today. Divide your last 30 days of revenue by your current list size. If the number is below $0.50, focus on product quality and relevance before growing the list. If it's above $2, focus on list growth.

One number. Total clarity.
