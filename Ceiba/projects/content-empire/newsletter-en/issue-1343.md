# Issue 1343 — What AI Tools Are Actually Good For

**Subject:** Stop using AI wrong. Here's what it's actually built for.

---

People either treat AI as a magic wand that should write everything for them, or they dismiss it entirely because the first output wasn't good enough. Both reactions miss the point, and both leave real value on the table.

AI tools are not good at thinking for you. They're very good at executing once you've done the thinking. If you can write a clear brief, a sharp angle, a specific audience description, the output quality goes up dramatically. The problem most people have with AI writing is not the tool. It's that they haven't done the upstream work of defining what they actually want to say. Garbage in, garbage out. That hasn't changed.

What AI is genuinely excellent at: turning one piece of content into five formats, generating twenty angle variations so you can pick the two worth developing, drafting first versions of repetitive content like product descriptions and email sequences, and helping you stress-test ideas by arguing against them. These are all execution tasks, not thinking tasks. Keep the thinking yours. Use AI for the execution volume.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your last good newsletter or post. Feed it into your AI tool with this prompt: "Give me 10 different angles on this topic for different audiences." Pick the two that surprise you. Develop those into new content. You just turned one piece of thinking into a week of content without losing your voice.

Think first. Then multiply.
