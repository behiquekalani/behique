# Issue 1432 — The Wrong Reason to Start a Business

**Subject:** If you're building to escape, you'll rebuild the same cage

---

A lot of people get into solopreneurship for the wrong reason and don't realize it until they've been at it for a year. The reason is escape. Escape from a bad job, a controlling boss, a schedule that doesn't fit their life. The motivation is real, but it's the wrong foundation.

When you build to escape something, you optimize your business around the absence of what you hated. You make sure you have no boss. You make sure no one controls your hours. You avoid every structure that reminded you of the old life. The problem is that some of those structures were load-bearing. Remove them without a replacement and the business becomes chaotic.

The better foundation is something you're building toward, not away from. A specific outcome. A specific person you want to serve. A specific problem you want to solve at scale. The "toward" framing changes your decisions. You stop asking "does this feel like a job?" and start asking "does this build toward the thing?"

The emotional relief of leaving a bad situation fades within months. What sustains the work is the clarity of what you're building and why it matters beyond your personal comfort.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write one sentence about what you are building toward, not away from. If you can't write it, that's the problem to solve before the business strategy problem.

Build toward something. Not away from everything.
