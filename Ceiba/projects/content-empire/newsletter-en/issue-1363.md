# Issue 1363: Build Your Personal Brand Like a Journalist, Not a Marketer

**Subject:** Stop pitching. Start reporting.

---

The creator economy has a storytelling problem. Most people building personal brands are writing marketing copy disguised as personal content. "Here's why you need X." "5 reasons to do Y." It's transparent, and readers feel it.

The most durable personal brands are built more like journalism than advertising. They report on what they're actually doing. They share what they're learning in real time. They make specific observations about things they've seen firsthand. The reader feels like a fly on the wall, not a target in a funnel.

This works because specificity builds trust faster than any pitch. When you say "I tried posting at 3 different times for 30 days and here's what I actually found," that's more credible than any claim about "cracking the algorithm." The data is yours. It can't be argued with. It can't be replicated by anyone else because it came from your life.

The practical shift is small: before writing any piece of content, ask yourself "what did I observe or test this week?" Start there instead of starting from a list of tips.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Pick one thing you did this week, report on the result honestly, and publish it. No hype. No manufactured insight. Just what happened. That's content that compounds.

Trust is the only brand metric that matters.
