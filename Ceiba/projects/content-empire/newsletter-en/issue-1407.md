# Issue 1407: Digital Products Are Not Passive

**Subject:** The truth about "passive income" that nobody tells you upfront

---

The phrase "passive income" sold a lot of people on the idea that digital products run themselves. List the product once, collect money in your sleep, spend your days at the beach. The marketing around passive income has always been more aspirational than accurate.

Digital products are not passive. They require active selling, which means active marketing, which means consistent content creation and audience building. The "passive" part is that you're not trading time for money on a per-unit basis. You create the product once and it can sell many times. That's real and valuable. But the machine that drives those sales is not passive. It demands constant input.

Here's the model that actually works: you spend the majority of your time building audience and trust through content, and a minority of your time maintaining and improving products. The products exist to convert the trust you've built through content. Without the content engine running, product sales dry up quickly.

This framing matters because it changes what you optimize for. You're not looking for a product that sells itself. You're building a relationship with an audience that trusts your recommendations, and then making sure your products are worth that trust.

**Tactical takeaway:** Calculate what percentage of your weekly time goes into content and audience building versus product maintenance. If it's less than 60% audience-building, rebalance. Your content is your sales team. Under-invest there and even a great product underperforms.

The product is the reward for the relationship. Build the relationship first.
