# Issue 1403: Your Personal Brand Is a Filter

**Subject:** Stop trying to appeal to everyone. It's killing your conversions.

---

The instinct to make your personal brand broadly appealing is understandable. You want more people to like your content. More followers, more reach, more potential customers. So you sand off the edges. You avoid strong opinions. You make your messaging vague enough that nobody could possibly disagree.

What you actually create is a brand that nobody feels strongly about.

Strong personal brands are filters, not megaphones. They clearly communicate who the content is for and, implicitly, who it's not for. That specificity creates trust. When someone lands on your profile and immediately thinks "this is exactly what I've been looking for," they follow, they buy, they share. When someone lands and thinks "this seems fine," they scroll on.

The math works in your favor when you're specific. A creator with 3,000 hyper-targeted followers who trust their recommendations will outperform a creator with 50,000 generalist followers every single time, in both conversions and long-term relationship quality.

Your opinions are your differentiation. Your specific experience is your moat. Your willingness to say "this is not for everyone" is what makes it essential for someone.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write down the exact person your brand is designed to speak to. Get specific. Not "entrepreneurs" but "solo founders building their first digital product while working a day job." Now audit your last ten pieces of content. How many of them would resonate deeply with that specific person? Adjust your next content sprint accordingly.

Niche down until it feels too narrow. Then go a little further.
