# Issue 1434 — The Solopreneur's Hiring Decision

**Subject:** When to stop doing everything yourself

---

There's a moment every growing solopreneur faces: the business is generating real revenue, and the bottleneck is no longer ideas or products. The bottleneck is time. Every hour spent on admin, editing, scheduling, or customer support is an hour not spent on the work that generated the revenue in the first place.

The decision to bring in help is not a "when I'm successful enough" decision. It's a math problem. If you earn $100 per hour of your highest-value work, and you're spending 10 hours a week on $15-per-hour tasks, the cost is not the contractor's fee. The cost is $850 per week in lost output.

The resistance is usually about control. The thought that no one can do it quite the way you do it. That's true for some tasks and irrelevant for most. The question is not "can someone do this as well as me?" It's "does this task require my specific judgment, or just someone competent?"

Most solopreneurs wait too long to delegate. They stay in the $15-per-hour tasks long after the business can afford to outsource them, and they wonder why growth stalls.

**Tactical takeaway:** Track your tasks for one week. Mark each one with a simple label: "only me" or "not necessarily me." Calculate the hours in the second column. Those hours are your first hire.

Protect the hours that only you can fill.
