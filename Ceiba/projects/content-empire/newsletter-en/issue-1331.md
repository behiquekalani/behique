# Issue 1331: What Nobody Tells You About Building in Public

Building in public became a strategy around 2020. Like most strategies, it got cargo-culted into something that looks the same on the surface but does not work.

The original premise was honest: share your real journey, including failures, so people trust you as someone who figured things out the hard way. That worked because it was rare and it was true. Now everyone "builds in public" but most of the content is either polished wins that look accidental or performative vulnerability that does not feel real.

The readers who follow building-in-public content are more sophisticated now. They can tell the difference between someone genuinely sharing the process and someone doing a content strategy. When it feels like a strategy, it stops building trust.

The version of this that still works is specific and uncomfortable. Not "I made $10K this month, here is how" but "I priced my product wrong three times before understanding why it was not converting, here is exactly what I got wrong and how I figured it out." The specificity of the failure is what makes it credible. Anyone can talk about wins. Very few people are willing to diagnose their own mistakes in public with enough precision to be useful.

That precision is the asset. It signals that you actually understand your domain, not just that you got lucky.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write one post this week that starts with a real mistake you made in the past 90 days. Describe exactly what you did, what you assumed, and what the outcome taught you. No silver lining required. Just the clear-eyed debrief. Post it and see what the response is.

Real process builds real trust. The rest is just content.
