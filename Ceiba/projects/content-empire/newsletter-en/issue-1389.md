# Issue 1389: The Business Model Most Creators Overlook: Licensing Your Systems

**Subject:** You have already built something other people will pay to use

---

Most creators think in terms of two business models: sell your knowledge as a product, or sell your time as a service. There is a third model that fits in between and often outperforms both: licensing your systems.

If you have built a repeatable process, a workflow, a set of templates, a decision framework, you have something other people in adjacent spaces would pay for. Not the raw information inside it, but the system itself. The packaged, ready-to-run version that saves someone from building it from scratch.

This works especially well for solopreneurs who have solved operational problems in their own business. The content calendar you built for yourself. The client onboarding flow. The AI prompt stack that cuts your production time in half. These are not just tools. They are products that happen to live in your own business. The extraction work is minor. The upside is asymmetric.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write down three systems you have built in your own business that actually work. For each one, ask: "Would I have paid $50-200 for this when I needed it?" If yes, you have a product. Package the best one and put it on a sales page this week. It does not need to be perfect to be valuable.

Your operating system is someone else's shortcut.
