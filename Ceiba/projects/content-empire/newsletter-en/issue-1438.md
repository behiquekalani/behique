# Issue 1438 — The Launch Isn't the Hard Part

**Subject:** What actually determines whether a product succeeds long-term

---

Everyone obsesses over the launch. The launch post, the launch email, the launch discount, the launch timeline. Launch week gets treated as the moment of truth. But for most digital products, launch week is the smallest revenue week in the product's lifetime.

The hard part is what happens after the launch. The evergreen funnel, the referral loop, the content that keeps finding new buyers. Most creators put all their energy into launch week and then wonder why sales drop off to zero in week two.

A product that sells consistently for 24 months generates more total revenue than a product that spikes for 72 hours and dies. The spike feels more dramatic. But the long tail is the actual business.

Building for the long tail means a few specific things: a clear SEO entry point, a referral structure that rewards buyers for sharing, a content calendar that keeps surfacing the product without feeling like repetitive promotion, and an email sequence that converts new subscribers weeks after they first joined.

**Tactical takeaway:** For your most recent product, write one piece of content that exists purely to attract cold traffic to that product three months from now. Not a launch post. A problem-awareness post that leads naturally toward the solution your product provides.

The launch is a day. The funnel is a machine.
