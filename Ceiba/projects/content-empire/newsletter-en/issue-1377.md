# Issue 1377: How to Repurpose Without Watering Down

**Subject:** Repurposing done wrong just makes noise

---

Repurposing content is one of the highest-leverage activities for a solo creator. One idea, multiple platforms, multiple entry points to your work. The theory is right. The execution is where most people get it wrong.

The wrong way to repurpose: take a newsletter, paste it into a Twitter thread, paste the thread into an Instagram caption, paste the caption into a LinkedIn post. This produces one idea poorly adapted to four different contexts. Every platform has a different native format, and content that doesn't fit the format signals to the reader that you're not paying attention to them. The engagement dies.

The right way to repurpose: start with a core idea, then ask what the native expression of that idea looks like on each platform. On Twitter it might be one sharp contrarian sentence. On Instagram it might be a carousel with five visuals. In a newsletter it might be 400 words with a real story. On LinkedIn it might be a personal reflection. Same idea. Four genuinely different executions.

This takes more thought per platform, but far less time overall because you're working from a single well-considered idea rather than generating four separate ones.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Pick your best-performing piece of content this month. Write out the core idea in one sentence. Then build a native version of that idea for each platform you use. Publish all four. Compare the results to your copy-paste approach.

Repurposing is translation, not copying.
