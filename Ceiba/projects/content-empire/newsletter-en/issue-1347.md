# Issue 1347 — Content Strategy for People Who Hate Content Strategy

**Subject:** A content system so simple you'll actually use it

---

Most content strategy advice is designed for marketing teams. Editorial calendars, content pillars, audience personas, quarterly themes. All of it adds planning overhead that solo operators don't have the bandwidth for. So they skip planning entirely and post reactively, which means the content is inconsistent and the message never accumulates.

There's a simpler version that works. Pick one problem your reader has. Pick one outcome they want. Write about the space between those two things, every time, in a different way. That's your content strategy. Not five pillars. Not a content matrix. One problem, one outcome, infinite angles.

The reason this works is compounding. Every piece of content that lives in the same problem space reinforces every other piece. A reader who sees post number seven and finds it resonant will go back and find posts one through six useful too. Your archive becomes a body of work instead of a random collection of topics. That archive is what eventually converts strangers into buyers without you doing anything new.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write one sentence: "I help [specific person] go from [specific problem] to [specific outcome]." Every piece of content you make this month should be answering one question that person has along that journey. Delete anything that doesn't fit. The discipline of the constraint is the strategy.

One reader. One journey. Every post.
