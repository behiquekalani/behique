# Issue 1276: Build the Offer Before You Build the Audience

The conventional wisdom says build an audience first, then figure out what to sell them. This works but it's slow. It can also leave you with a large following and no clear offer, which is its own frustrating problem.

The faster path: validate the offer first. Talk to ten people in your target market. Describe the product you want to build. Watch their reaction. If they lean forward and ask when they can get it, you have something. If they nod politely and change the subject, go back to the problem.

Once the offer is validated, content strategy becomes simple. Everything you post is designed to attract the person who would buy this specific thing. You're not creating content for followers. You're creating content for buyers. Followers are just the by-product.

This changes the content you make, the platforms you prioritize, and the calls to action you include. Everything points toward the offer. The audience grows around the offer instead of the offer being retrofitted onto the audience.

The side benefit is that you build a business with a clear purpose from day one. Every decision filters through: does this help me reach and serve the person who buys this offer? The clarity makes you faster and more decisive.

**Tactical takeaway:** Before you create content this week, write down one sentence describing the offer you're ultimately trying to sell. Now read your planned content. Does it attract the person who would buy that offer? If not, rewrite the content with that person in mind.

Content is marketing. Market something specific.
