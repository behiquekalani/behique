# Issue 1421 — The Real Cost of a Generic Digital Product

**Subject:** Why your digital product isn't selling (it's not the price)

---

Most creators price their digital products wrong and blame the market. The actual problem is simpler. Your product looks like everything else on the shelf.

Generic products sell when distribution is strong enough to overwhelm the sameness. Most solopreneurs don't have that distribution yet. What they do have is the ability to get specific. Specificity is the only competitive advantage available to a one-person business at the start.

Think about it this way: a PDF called "Productivity System" competes with a thousand others. A PDF called "Productivity System for Founders Who Can't Afford to Miss a Week" competes with almost none. The audience is smaller, yes. But the buyer intent is higher, the refund rate is lower, and word of mouth is faster because it feels like it was made *for them*.

The shift is not about creating more content. It is about narrowing the promise until your specific buyer feels slightly uncomfortable being left out.

**Tactical takeaway:** Pick your existing product. Write three different versions of its title, each one more specific than the last. The third title is usually the one that sells.

Your product isn't the problem. The promise is.
