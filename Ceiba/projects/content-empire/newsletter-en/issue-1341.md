# Issue 1341 — Your Product Is Not the Problem

**Subject:** The real reason your digital product isn't selling

---

You built something. You spent weeks on it. You know it's good. And it's just sitting there, collecting zero sales and a lot of silence.

The instinct is to fix the product. Add more pages, more templates, more sections. Make it bigger. Make it shinier. That instinct is wrong.

The product is rarely the problem. The problem is that nobody outside your immediate circle knows it exists. Distribution is the variable most creators ignore until they're frustrated enough to actually pay attention to it. You don't need a better product. You need more people seeing the one you already have. One sale from a cold audience is worth more information than fifty compliments from friends.

The shift that actually moves the needle is treating content as your distribution system, not your brand play. Every piece of content you publish is either pointing people toward a specific problem, or it isn't. Vague value posts about "the creator economy" help nobody buy anything. Specific posts that name an exact pain, then connect it to an exact solution, convert. That's the only distinction that matters at your stage.

**Tactical takeaway:** Pick one product. Write ten pieces of content this week where every single one ends with a clear sentence about who the product is for and what it solves. Not a pitch. A direction. You'll know more about what's working after seven days of that than after seven months of tweaking the product itself.

Stop building. Start pointing.
