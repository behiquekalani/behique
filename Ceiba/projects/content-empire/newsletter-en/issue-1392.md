# Issue 1392: How Solopreneurs Should Think About Pricing (Most Get It Backwards)

**Subject:** Stop anchoring your prices to your costs. Anchor them to outcomes.

---

The instinct most people have when pricing a product is to think about what it cost them to make, then add a margin. This is how physical goods work. It is a terrible model for digital products and knowledge-based services.

Your cost to create a PDF is essentially zero after the first copy. But the value of that PDF to the person who buys it might be a transformed business, saved hours every week, or avoided mistakes that cost thousands of dollars. Pricing to your cost ignores all of that. It leaves enormous value on the table and signals to buyers that what you have made is not particularly valuable.

The right model is outcome-based pricing. What is the problem this solves? What is that problem worth to the person who has it? What would they pay a consultant to walk them through this? Your price should live somewhere between "clearly cheap for what it delivers" and "worth every dollar to the right buyer." That range is almost always higher than your gut says.

**Tactical takeaway:** For your next product or service, research what competitors charge and ignore it. Instead, interview three potential buyers and ask: "What would you pay to have this problem solved?" Their answers will recalibrate your pricing instincts in one conversation.

Price the outcome. Not the artifact.
