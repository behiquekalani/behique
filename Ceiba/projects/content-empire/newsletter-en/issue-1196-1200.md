## Issue 1196 — The Attention Economy Is Real. Here Is How to Win It.

**Subject:** Attention is the new currency. You are either earning it or losing it.
**Preview:** The builders who understand attention will own the next decade.

Attention is finite. There are only so many hours in a day and only so many things a person can focus on. Every platform is competing for those hours. Every creator is competing for that attention. The question is not whether you are in that competition. You are. The question is whether you are competing deliberately.

Winning attention does not mean being loud. It means being specific and consistent enough that the right people always choose you over the noise. The creator who posts twice a week with sharp, specific ideas will consistently outperform the one who posts daily with generic content.

The attention economy rewards depth over breadth. One person who reads every single thing you write is worth more than a hundred who skim. Build for the former. Write as if you are speaking directly to one specific reader who is waiting for exactly this.

The creators who will win the next decade are the ones who treat attention as sacred. They do not waste it with filler content. They show up with something worth stopping for, every single time.

**This week:** Review your last 10 posts. How many would you stop scrolling for if you were seeing them for the first time? Be honest.

## Issue 1197 — Why Most People Stay Stuck at the Starting Line

**Subject:** The real reason you have not started yet.
**Preview:** It is not resources or knowledge. It is something more uncomfortable than that.

You have enough knowledge to start. You have enough tools. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is almost never a resource gap. It is a permission gap.

You are waiting for someone to tell you that your idea is good enough, that you have enough experience, that the timing is right. That permission is never coming from outside. The people you are waiting for are also waiting. Everyone is waiting for someone else to go first.

The builders who move are the ones who gave themselves permission without ceremony. No announcement. No waiting for the stars to align. They made the decision privately, started quietly, and let the work become the proof of concept.

Fear does not go away before you start. It goes away because you started and nothing catastrophic happened. Every day you wait, the fear gets a little more comfortable living in your head. Every day you work, the fear gets a little smaller.

The most powerful thing you can do right now is start something that is not finished. Not perfect. Not ready. Just started.

**This week:** Give yourself permission to start one thing you have been deferring. Set a 25-minute timer. Work. Stop. Notice what happened.

## Issue 1198 — The Power of Building in Public

**Subject:** Building in public is the most underrated growth strategy.
**Preview:** Sharing the process attracts people before the product exists.

Building in public means documenting what you are creating before it is finished. It means sharing the decisions, the setbacks, the pivots, and the progress. It feels uncomfortable because it exposes the process, not just the outcome. That discomfort is exactly what makes it work.

People are more invested in a story unfolding than in a finished product announced. When they follow your build, they become stakeholders in the outcome. They root for you. They share your updates. They become your launch team before you have anything to launch.

The practical version is simple. Once a week, share one honest update about what you are building. What went well, what did not, what you learned, what is next. That is it. No polish required. The rawness is the point.

Build in public long enough and you will have an engaged audience before your product is done. That audience will tell you whether the product is right before you finish it. That feedback loop is worth more than any market research you could pay for.

**This week:** Post one honest build update. What are you working on? What is hard about it right now? What did you learn this week?

## Issue 1199 — How to Think About Money as a Creator

**Subject:** Most creators have an unhealthy relationship with money. Here is the fix.
**Preview:** Money is not the goal. It is the score that tells you how much value you created.

Most creators have one of two unhealthy relationships with money. Either they are obsessed with it and it distorts every creative decision, or they are uncomfortable with it and they avoid thinking about it until it becomes a crisis. Neither works.

The healthy relationship with money as a creator is to treat it as a signal. Money tells you whether the value you created actually landed. It tells you whether the right people found your work. It tells you whether your offer is clear and compelling. It is feedback, not a goal.

When you stop making money the goal and start making value the goal, something shifts. You make better creative decisions because they are not distorted by short-term revenue pressure. You build more honest products because you are solving real problems rather than chasing perceived demand.

The money follows when the value is real. Not always immediately. Not always in the form you expected. But the creator who consistently solves real problems for real people does not stay broke for long.

**This week:** Audit one thing you are currently building. Does it solve a real problem for a real person? Or does it solve a problem you invented?

## Issue 1200 — 200 Issues In. What This Has Taught Me.

**Subject:** 200 issues. Here is the one thing I keep relearning.
**Preview:** Every issue comes back to the same uncomfortable truth about what actually works.

Two hundred issues. That is a lot of writing. Looking back at all of it, one theme keeps surfacing in different forms across every topic. Whether the issue was about branding, monetization, systems, or mindset, the underlying principle was always the same.

Clarity beats cleverness. Every single time. The clearest message wins over the most clever one. The most direct offer wins over the most sophisticated one. The simplest system wins over the most elaborate one. When in doubt, make it clearer.

Most creators, including me, default to complexity when things are not working. They add more steps, more platforms, more products, more strategy layers. Rarely is that the fix. Usually the fix is stripping something away. Going back to the one thing that actually works and doing it better.

The other thing two hundred issues has confirmed: the people who read this regularly are not looking for shortcuts. They are looking for permission and direction. Permission to start. Direction for where to go next. That is what this has always been for.

Thank you for reading. Keep building.

**This week:** Look back at the last 30 days of your work. What one thing actually moved the needle? Double down on exactly that.
