# Issue 1369: The Content That Builds the Most Trust

**Subject:** Nobody trusts advice. They trust evidence.

---

There are two types of content you can publish: advice content and evidence content. Most creators default to advice content because it's easier to write. "Here are five ways to grow your audience." The problem is that everyone is writing advice content, and readers have developed a defense against it.

Evidence content is different. It shows your work. It documents what you tried, what happened, what you learned. It doesn't claim to have the answer. It shows the answer in progress. And because it's grounded in your specific experience, it can't be replicated. No other creator has your numbers, your timeline, your specific context.

The most effective evidence content doesn't even need a strong lesson. "I posted every day for 90 days and here's what my account looked like at day 1, 30, 60, and 90" is more compelling than any tactical breakdown. People see themselves in the journey. They believe it because they can track the progression.

The lesson can come at the end or not at all. The evidence does the persuasion. This is why case studies convert better than tutorials, and why personal stories beat generic tips every time.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Document one ongoing experiment publicly. Share updates at regular intervals. No manufactured conclusions. Just honest reporting on what's happening. This is the fastest trust-building content format available to a solo creator.

Show the work. The work speaks.
