# Issue 1332: The Productivity System That Solopreneurs Actually Need

Most productivity advice was built for employees managing tasks inside a larger machine. Solopreneurs have a different problem.

The employee's productivity challenge is completing assigned work efficiently. The solopreneur's challenge is deciding what to work on at all. The whole company is one person. Strategy, execution, marketing, admin, and vision all live in the same brain. No productivity system designed for knowledge workers at companies directly solves that.

What works for solopreneurs is a decision filter, not a task manager. The filter has one question: does this move my core metric this week? If the answer is no, it goes on a maybe list that you look at once a week. If yes, it goes on a short list of three to five things you will actually do.

The short list matters more than the full system. Most solopreneurs run on a list of 30 tasks that creates the feeling of being busy without producing much. A list of three things that actually need to happen this week, done with full focus, consistently outperforms the 30-item list in results.

Time blocking is the mechanical support for this. Assign specific work to specific time slots. Do not make the block more than 90 minutes. Take a real break. Your deep work output over a four-hour session with real breaks is higher than an eight-hour session where you are half-present.

**Tactical takeaway:** At the start of tomorrow, write your three most important outputs for the day. Not tasks. Outputs. What would exist at the end of the day that would not exist today? Work on output one until it is done, then move to two. If you get all three done, everything else is a bonus.

Three things done beats thirty things started.
