# Issue 1315: The Revenue Model That Beats the Single-Product Business Every Time

Selling one product is a job. Selling a product suite is a business.

The math on a single product is simple and limited. You have one price point and one type of customer. You can grow by finding more customers or raising the price. Both have friction. But the creator with three products at different price points has a completely different business. The same customer can enter at $27, ascend to $97, and eventually invest in $497. Your revenue grows without finding new people.

This is the ladder model. Every creator who has crossed $10K/month is running some version of it. The entry product filters for your ideal customer. The mid-tier product delivers the core transformation. The premium product does the deepest work and commands the highest price. Each rung exists because a portion of your customers always want more after they got the last thing.

The mistake is building the ladder all at once. You don't need three products today. You need one good one. But you should build it with the ladder in mind. What is the natural next question your customer will have after completing this? That's your second product. What does the person who wants to go deep really need? That's your third.

**Tactical takeaway:** Map your existing products or ideas onto a ladder: entry, core, premium. Identify the biggest gap. Fill that one next.

The best time to sell your customer something is right after they bought from you.
