# Issue 1408: Building in Public Without Oversharing

**Subject:** The right way to document your journey without burning yourself out

---

Building in public has become one of the most powerful growth strategies for creators. Sharing your process, your numbers, your failures, your pivots, it builds trust faster than polished marketing content ever could. People connect with the journey, not the destination.

But there's a version of building in public that becomes performance rather than documentation. You start posting updates because you feel obligated to your audience rather than because you have something genuine to share. The content gets hollow. The metrics become the story rather than the work behind them.

The distinction is subtle but important. Building in public works when you're documenting real decisions, real results, and real lessons, even uncomfortable ones. It breaks down when you're manufacturing updates to maintain an image or feed an algorithm.

Sustainable building in public looks like this: share when you have a genuine insight, a meaningful result, or a decision that others might learn from. Don't share every step. Don't narrate every day. Give your audience the condensed version of what you're learning, not a live feed of your work sessions.

**Tactical takeaway:** Choose one recurring thing you're actively building right now. Decide what the meaningful milestones are, not daily activity, but actual turning points. Commit to sharing those milestones with full transparency: what you tried, what happened, what you'd do differently. That cadence, one genuine post per real milestone, builds more trust than daily progress theater.

Authenticity doesn't mean constant exposure. It means what you share is real.
