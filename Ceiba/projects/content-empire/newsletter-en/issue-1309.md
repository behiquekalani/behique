# Issue 1309: Pricing Is a Positioning Decision, Not a Math Problem

Most creators price by instinct or fear. They pick a number that feels safe and hope people buy. That is not pricing. That is guessing.

Pricing is positioning. The price you set tells your customer what category your product is in before they read a single word of your sales page. A $7 product says "impulse buy." A $97 product says "real investment." A $497 product says "this will change something." The content of the product matters less than you think at the moment of the pricing decision, because pricing happens before the customer knows the content.

The most common mistake is underpricing because of imposter syndrome. You don't believe the product is worth $97 so you charge $19. Then you resent the sales that come in at $19 because you're doing $97 worth of work for $19 worth of pay. And your customers undervalue what they bought because it cost them nothing to risk.

Higher prices also attract better customers. Not richer customers. Better ones. People who invest more take the product more seriously. They get better results. They leave better testimonials. The feedback loop improves your product and your brand simultaneously.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your current lowest-priced product and double the price for 30 days. Track whether conversion rate drops enough to offset the per-sale gain. Most creators find it doesn't.

Charge what you'd pay if you needed this as badly as your customer does.
