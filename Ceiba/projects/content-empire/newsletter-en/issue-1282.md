# Issue 1282: Why Your Content Gets Ignored (It's Not the Algorithm)

Everyone blames the algorithm when their content gets no traction. The algorithm is not your problem. The algorithm shows content to people. If people scroll past yours, that's a message.

The real issue is almost always specificity. Generic content competes with every other generic creator in your niche. "5 productivity tips" competes with 50,000 other pieces about productivity tips. "How I stopped losing 2 hours a day to Slack by doing one thing on Monday morning" competes with almost nothing, because it's specific enough to be real.

Specificity signals truth. When you say something specific, people believe you lived it. "I grew my newsletter to 10,000 subscribers" is forgettable. "I grew my newsletter from 847 to 10,412 subscribers in 5 months by sending one extra email per week at 6am on Thursdays" is a story. It has texture. People read texture.

The second reason content gets ignored is weak opening lines. Most creators bury the point. They write three warm-up sentences before saying anything worth reading. Online, you have one sentence to earn the next sentence. Write your best sentence first. Delete everything before it.

The third reason is misaligned distribution. You can write the most useful post in the world, but if you publish it in a place where your ideal reader doesn't go, it vanishes. Choose one platform where your specific reader already spends time. Master that before spreading thin.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your last three pieces of content that underperformed. Rewrite just the first line of each. Make it more specific, more direct, more charged with either curiosity or usefulness. Then republish as new posts. Measure the difference.

Attention follows specificity. Always has.
