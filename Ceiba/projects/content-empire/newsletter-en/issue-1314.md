# Issue 1314: Why Your Free Content Is Undermining Your Paid Products

There is a version of free content that builds demand for your paid work. And there is a version that kills it. Most creators are running the second version without realizing it.

The version that kills demand is giving away the complete answer for free. Not frameworks, not principles, not your perspective, but the actual step-by-step solution. When someone gets everything for free, there is no reason to buy. You trained your audience to expect full value at zero cost and then you're confused when your product launch underperforms.

The version that builds demand is free content that raises awareness of a problem and demonstrates that you know how to solve it, without solving it fully. You show the diagnosis. You show what the solution looks like at a high level. You show proof that it works. The full implementation lives in the paid product.

Think of your free content as a trailer, not the film. The trailer shows enough to create desire. It does not show the ending. Most creators are releasing the full film for free and then asking people to pay for the extras.

This does not mean withhold value. It means be strategic about which type of value you give where. Insight is free. Implementation is paid.

**Tactical takeaway:** Review your last 5 pieces of free content. For each one, ask: does this make someone want to learn more, or does it satisfy the need entirely? Adjust accordingly.

Give them the why for free. Charge for the how.
