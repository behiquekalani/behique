# Building in public without giving everything away

Building in public is one of the most effective growth strategies available to a solo creator right now. It creates accountability, attracts an audience, and builds trust in real time. But most people who try it make the same error: they confuse transparency with exposure.

Transparency is showing your process. Exposure is sharing the parts that make you vulnerable without serving anyone.

## What to share

Share the decision, not just the outcome. When you chose to pivot, price higher, or cut a feature, share the reasoning. This is useful. People learn how you think, not just what you did.

Share the failures after you've processed them. Not in the middle of the spiral. A failure shared in real time is often just venting. A failure shared with reflection is a lesson.

Share the numbers when they tell a story. Revenue, growth rate, churn. But only when the numbers serve the narrative. Not to brag. Not to perform humility. To give context.

## What not to share

Do not share client details, even anonymously if the details are specific enough to identify them.

Do not share business decisions that are still in play. Share what you decided. Not what you're deciding. The indecision loop is noise for your audience and confusion for you.

Do not share every metric you track. Sharing vanity metrics trains your audience to expect vanity. Share the metrics that actually matter to your business health.

## The frame that makes it work

Every piece of building-in-public content should answer one question: what can the person reading this learn or apply? If the honest answer is "nothing, I just needed to say it," it belongs in a journal, not a newsletter.

Your audience is not your therapist. They are people who trust you to make your chaos useful to them.

## The action step

Write one building-in-public post this week about a real decision you made recently. Include: the context, the options you considered, the choice you made, and what you'd change. That structure creates signal. Everything else is noise.
