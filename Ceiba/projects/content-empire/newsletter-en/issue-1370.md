# Issue 1370: Pricing Is a Signal

**Subject:** Your price tells people how to feel about your product

---

Most first-time digital product creators underprice because they're afraid. Afraid no one will pay more. Afraid they'll be judged. Afraid their product isn't "worth" a high price. This fear produces exactly the result they're afraid of: low sales, because the price is signaling low quality.

Pricing is not just a financial decision. It's a positioning decision. A $7 ebook and a $97 guide can contain identical information. But the $7 version will be dismissed, downloaded, and never read. The $97 version will be opened, worked through, and referred to. Price shapes how people engage with what they buy.

This doesn't mean you should charge whatever you want with no basis. It means you should price based on the value of the outcome, not the cost of production. A PDF that saves someone 40 hours of research has value worth far more than the hour it took you to write it. If you price it like the hour, you're leaving serious money on the table and telling your buyer the outcome isn't worth much.

Raise your price. Then write better copy that explains the outcome clearly. The price and the copy work together. A high price with vague copy fails. A high price with specific outcome copy converts.

**Tactical Takeaway:** If your product is under $30 and doesn't convert well, try doubling the price before changing anything else. Watch what happens to the quality of your buyers.

Price what the outcome is worth. Not what the file weighs.
