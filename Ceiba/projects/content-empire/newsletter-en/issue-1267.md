# Issue 1267: The Content Strategy Nobody Talks About: Depth Over Volume

Everyone is chasing the algorithm. More posts. More reels. More consistency. The implicit promise is that volume wins.

Sometimes it does. But the creators who build real audiences that buy things, not just follow things, win on depth.

A single piece of content that genuinely changes how someone thinks about a problem is worth 50 posts that entertain and disappear. The person who reads the deep piece becomes a follower who actually pays attention. They recommend you to people they know. They buy when you sell something. They stick around after the algorithm forgets about your last post.

Depth is hard. It requires sitting with an idea long enough to say something real about it. Most creators don't do this because it's uncomfortable and slow. That's exactly why it works as a differentiation strategy.

You don't need to choose between depth and volume entirely. The model that works: one deep piece per week that anchors your thinking, then repurpose that depth into shorter posts for volume. The shallow posts feed the algorithm. The deep piece builds the relationship.

**Tactical takeaway:** Pick one idea you've been circling around but haven't fully written out. Give it 90 minutes. Write it without worrying about length or format. Just think on paper until the idea is fully expressed. That piece becomes your anchor content for the week.

Go deep once. Then spread it.
