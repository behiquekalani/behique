# Issue 1358 — Passive Income Is Not Passive

**Subject:** The truth about digital products and what "passive" actually means

---

"Passive income" has done more damage to creator expectations than almost any other phrase in the space. It implies that you build something once and money appears indefinitely with no further effort. That's not what happens. What actually happens is more useful, but it requires a different mental model.

Digital products are not passive. They require content to drive traffic to them. They require updates when the information gets stale. They require customer support when someone can't access their download. They require a sales page that converts, which requires testing and iteration. None of that is passive. What is real is that the income is decoupled from your time in a way that hourly service work is not. You can make a sale at 3am without being awake for it. That's the actual value proposition.

The frame that works better: digital products buy you time leverage. Not freedom from work. Leverage over how your time connects to revenue. One hour of product creation or content can generate $50 in sales spread across a week. That same hour of service work generates $50 once. Over a year, those two outcomes look completely different.

**Tactical takeaway:** Calculate your current hourly rate from any service work you do. Then estimate how many copies of your digital product would need to sell in a month to match that income. That number is your sales target. Work backward from it to figure out how many people need to see the product per day to hit it. That's your distribution goal.

Not passive. Leveraged. Big difference.
