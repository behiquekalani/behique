# Issue 1272: Content Strategy Starts With Distribution, Not Creation

Most creators plan what to make and then figure out distribution. The ones building real audiences do it in reverse.

Start with where the audience already lives and what they already consume there. Then build content that fits that context. A LinkedIn article is not a repurposed Twitter thread. A YouTube video is not a podcast with a camera pointed at you. Each platform has a native content form. Work with it, not against it.

The distribution question is: where is the right audience, and what do they engage with on that platform? Once you answer that, your content decisions become obvious. You're not guessing what to make. You're building what the audience already wants, in the format they already consume, on the platform they already use.

This sounds mechanical. It isn't. Your voice and perspective make the content yours. The platform just dictates the form. A strong point of view adapts to any format. A weak one sounds flat in all of them.

The creators who grow fastest right now are those who pick one platform, learn its native mechanics deeply, and execute consistently. Not three platforms half-heartedly. One platform completely.

**Tactical takeaway:** List every platform you're currently posting to. Pick the one where you have the most clarity about the audience and the most energy for the format. Cut the others temporarily. Go deep on one for 60 days. Then reassess.

One platform. Full commitment. Sixty days.
