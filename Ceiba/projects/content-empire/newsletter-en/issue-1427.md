# Issue 1427 — Personal Brand Is Not About You

**Subject:** The counterintuitive truth about building a personal brand

---

Most people think a personal brand is about broadcasting who they are. They post about their morning routine, their values, their journey. This is the most common mistake in creator marketing, and it's understandable because the word "personal" implies self-disclosure.

A personal brand is actually about what your audience becomes by engaging with you. The best ones make the audience feel smarter, clearer, more capable, or more seen. Your story is a vehicle for that transformation, not the destination. When you make your content about yourself, people admire you. When you make it about them, they follow you.

The practical shift: every time you share a story about yourself, ask what the reader is supposed to take away. Not what they're supposed to think about you. What they're supposed to think about themselves. The anchor is always the reader's situation, not yours.

This is why the most shareable content is not "look at what I did." It's "here's what I learned that you can use." The second format scales. The first one runs out of stories.

**Tactical takeaway:** Rewrite your bio. Remove the list of credentials. Replace it with one sentence about what your reader gains by following you. Test it. The click-through rate will tell you if it's working.

Your brand is their transformation. Act accordingly.
