# Issue 1366: The One-Product Trap

**Subject:** Why adding products won't fix your revenue problem

---

There's a predictable moment in every creator's journey where they decide the solution to low sales is more products. The logic seems sound. More products, more chances to hit. But it almost never works, and the reason is counterintuitive.

When you add products before your first one is working, you split your energy without increasing your audience. You end up with three half-promoted products instead of one properly positioned one. Your messaging gets muddier. Your audience gets confused. The product that could have worked with focused attention never gets the push it needs.

The creators who build real product income almost always started with one thing, sold it to everyone who would buy it, and then built the next product based on what their existing customers asked for. Not what they thought would sell. Not what they saw someone else do. What the people who already trusted them wanted next.

This is the product ladder, and it only works in sequence. You can't skip straight to the top. The second product exists to serve the people who completed the first. The third serves the people who completed the second. Build it backward.

**Tactical Takeaway:** If you have multiple products and none are converting, pick the one with the clearest promise and cut everything else from your bio and homepage for 30 days. Focus converts. Optionality confuses.

Sell one thing until you know exactly who it's for.
