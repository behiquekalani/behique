# Issue 1311: The Personal Brand Mistake That's Costing You Credibility

Most people think personal brand is about being likable. It is not. It is about being clear.

Likability without clarity is entertainment. People enjoy your content but they don't know what to buy or why to buy it from you specifically. Clarity without warmth is a commercial. People understand what you sell but don't feel connected enough to trust you. The combination of both is what builds something durable.

The mistake most creators make is chasing engagement over consistency of perspective. They post whatever performs. This week it's a productivity hack. Next week it's a relationship take. The week after that, a political opinion. Their audience grows confused. Confusion kills purchase intent.

Your personal brand is not your aesthetic. It is your consistent point of view on a consistent set of topics. When people know exactly what you think about a specific thing, they start to seek you out for that thing. That's the position you want to occupy. Not "creator" in general. A specific voice on a specific set of ideas.

Decide what you believe. Say it clearly and often. Let the people who disagree leave. The people who stay are your audience.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write your brand positioning in one sentence: "I help [specific person] do [specific thing] through [specific method or lens]." If it takes more than one sentence, you don't have clarity yet.

Clear is more valuable than clever.
