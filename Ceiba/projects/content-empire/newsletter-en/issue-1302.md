# Issue 1302: Why Your Second Product Should Cost More Than Your First

The creator playbook says start cheap to build trust. That's partially right and mostly wrong.

Starting cheap to get your first customers is fine. The mistake is staying cheap. Your second product should cost more than your first, not because you got greedy, but because you now have proof. You have testimonials. You have data. You understand what your audience actually needs. That knowledge is worth more, and the product you build with it will be better.

Price anchors identity. When you price low forever, your audience learns to expect low prices. Raising prices later feels like a betrayal to them and an act of courage for you. Neither should be true. Build the expectation of increasing value from the start. Your first product is the introduction. The second is the relationship.

The real trap is the creator who has 5 products all priced at $27. They're working 5x as hard for the same revenue they could get from one well-positioned $97 product. Volume-first thinking is how you build a business that never scales.

**Tactical takeaway:** Before you create your next product, decide the price first. Then build to that price point. This forces you to create something worth the number you chose.

Price is a decision, not a discovery.
