# Issue 1362: Why Most Digital Products Never Sell

**Subject:** The real reason your product isn't moving

---

You spent weeks building something. You pressed publish. Nothing happened. Most creators chalk this up to audience size. "I don't have enough followers." But that's almost never the actual problem.

The real issue is specificity. Most digital products are built around a vague outcome. "Get more productive." "Grow your brand." "Make money online." These promises don't trigger a purchase because they don't describe a person. A buyer needs to read your product description and think, "That's exactly my problem."

The fix is surgical. Instead of "productivity system," you write "daily planning system for ADHD creatives who can't stick to routines." Instead of "grow your brand," you write "Instagram content system for coaches who hate showing up on camera." One sentence. One person. One outcome.

When you get this right, the product almost sells itself. The copy writes itself because the buyer feels seen, not pitched. And people buy from clarity, not from features.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Look at your current product description. Swap out every vague word for a specific one. Name the person, name the situation, name the result. Then repost it and watch what changes.

Specificity is the cheapest form of marketing.
