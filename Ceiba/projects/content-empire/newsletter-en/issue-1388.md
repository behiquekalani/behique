# Issue 1388: Personal Brand Is Not About Being Famous. It's About Being Findable.

**Subject:** The low-pressure way to think about building your brand online

---

When people hear "personal brand" they imagine a highlight reel. Perfect photos, polished videos, a curated persona that performs success. This version of personal branding is exhausting and, for most people, completely unsustainable.

The practical version is simpler and more durable. A personal brand is just the answer to: when someone in your niche needs what you offer, do they think of you? That is it. You do not need to be famous. You need to be findable by the right people. Specific enough that the person who needs exactly what you do can recognize you immediately.

This reframe matters because it changes what you optimize for. Instead of optimizing for reach, you optimize for resonance. Instead of trying to appeal to everyone, you speak directly to the person who has the exact problem you solve. A hundred people who deeply trust you are worth more than ten thousand passive followers who vaguely know your name.

**Tactical takeaway:** Audit your profile across one platform today. Does a stranger know within ten seconds what you do, who you help, and what problem you solve? If not, rewrite your bio with those three elements. Specificity attracts. Vagueness repels.

Be found by the right people. The rest is vanity.
