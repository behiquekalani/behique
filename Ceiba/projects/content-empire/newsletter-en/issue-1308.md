# Issue 1308: What "Passive Income" Actually Requires

Everyone wants passive income. Almost no one wants what it takes to build it.

Passive income from digital products is real. But it is not passive at the start. It requires an upfront investment of time, thinking, and often money that most people dramatically underestimate. The person making $5K a month from a course they made two years ago spent months building trust, building an audience, and refining that course before the passive part kicked in.

The better framing is deferred income. You work hard now so that work pays you later without requiring more work. The math on this is excellent. The psychology is hard. Most people want the income before they've done the work. That's not how it functions.

The three things that make a digital product truly passive: a clear customer acquisition path that runs on its own, a product that needs no fulfillment or support after purchase, and a funnel that converts cold traffic. Building any one of these takes effort. Building all three is a real project.

Once it's built though, you can sleep and make money. That's worth the project.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write down every manual task you currently do to sell your product. For each one, ask: can this be automated, eliminated, or documented into a system? Start with the one that takes the most time.

Passive income is a result, not a starting point.
