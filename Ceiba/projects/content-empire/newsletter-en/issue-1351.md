# Issue 1351 — Niche Down or Die Vague

**Subject:** The creator who tries to help everyone helps no one

---

The broad approach feels safer. If you target everyone who's interested in productivity, or entrepreneurship, or personal development, you have a larger potential audience, right? In theory. In practice, you're invisible to all of them because you're not speaking to any of them specifically enough to feel relevant.

Specificity is not a limitation. It's a magnet. When someone reads your content and thinks "this was written for me," they subscribe, share, and buy. When they read it and think "this is generally good advice," they scroll past. The brain is wired to filter generic. It stops to pay attention to specific. That's not a preference. It's cognitive architecture.

The objection most creators raise is that going specific will shrink their audience. It does shrink the number of people who casually encounter your content. But it increases the percentage of people who actually care. And 1,000 readers who genuinely care convert at 10x the rate of 10,000 passive followers who found you mildly interesting once.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write the most specific possible version of your niche statement. Not "I help entrepreneurs." Try: "I help solo service providers package their expertise into digital products so they stop trading time for money." Read it out loud. If it doesn't make you slightly nervous that it's too narrow, it's still too broad.

Narrow is not small. Narrow is focused.
