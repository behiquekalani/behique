# Issue 1273: How to Price Your Digital Products Without Guessing

Most solopreneurs price by gut feel. They pick a number that feels fair and hope it converts. This leaves money on the table or kills the sale entirely.

Pricing is a communication tool. The price signals who the product is for, what transformation it delivers, and how seriously you take your own work. A $7 product says something different than a $97 product, even if the content is identical.

The starting point is not "what feels comfortable." It's "what problem does this solve and what is that problem worth to the buyer?" A product that saves someone ten hours of work per month is worth more than $27. A template that helps someone close their first client is worth more than $47. Price to the outcome, not to the production cost.

The second factor is positioning. A higher price requires a clearer promise. If your sales page is vague, a low price covers for the ambiguity. But a specific, confident promise earns a specific, confident price.

Test anchor pricing. Show a higher-tier option next to your main offer. The main offer looks like the smart middle choice. Conversion rates almost always improve with proper anchoring.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your current flagship product. Write down the specific outcome a buyer gets in 30 days if they use it correctly. Now look at your price. Does the price reflect that outcome? If you'd charge more for a service that delivered the same outcome, your product is underpriced.

Price to the outcome.
