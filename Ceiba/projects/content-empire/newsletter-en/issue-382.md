# Newsletter EN — Issue 382

**Subject:** The 20% of content that actually drives sales

**Preview text:** Most content builds brand. Only some of it builds revenue. Here's the difference.

---

There is a reason some creators seem to effortlessly sell while others produce enormous volumes of content and convert almost nothing.

It is not luck. It is not audience size. It is content type.

Most creators default to educational content because it feels safe and generous. Teach, teach, teach. And teaching does build trust. But teaching alone does not move product. There is a specific type of content that closes the gap between trust and transaction, and most creators either don't know what it is or avoid it because it feels uncomfortable.

**The 80/20 of content**

80% of your content should build awareness, trust, and authority. This is what most creators already do. Frameworks, insights, opinions, hot takes. This is the foundation.

The other 20% has one job: make the reader feel the cost of not acting.

Not manipulation. Not manufactured urgency. Just clarity. When your reader finishes a piece of content and feels the specific weight of their unsolved problem, and then sees that you have a solution, the sale becomes logical rather than emotional.

**What that 20% looks like**

Case studies with real numbers. Before and after transformations that are specific enough to sting. Honest breakdowns of what didn't work and what finally did. Content that names the exact problem so precisely that readers feel called out.

Generic educational content keeps people comfortable. Revenue-generating content makes the status quo feel costly.

**The practical application**

Look at your last 30 posts. Identify which ones led to direct questions, replies with buying signals, or actual purchases. Most creators will find 3-5 posts responsible for the majority of their conversion activity.

Those posts are your templates. Study why they worked. What was the specific problem they named? What was the pain point? Write 10 variations of each.

You do not need more content. You need more of the right content.

The distinction is not about being salesy. It is about being clear. The best selling content does not feel like selling. It feels like someone finally understanding your problem and showing you the way out.

Build toward that.
