# Issue 1352 — The Monetization Sequence That Works

**Subject:** Stop monetizing too early. Or too late. Here's the timing.

---

Two failure modes. The creator who monetizes on day one and wonders why nobody buys, because there's no trust yet. And the creator who builds an audience for two years, refuses to sell anything, and then wonders why nobody buys when they finally do, because they've trained the audience that this is a free value channel.

Trust is required for sales, but trust alone is not sufficient. The audience also needs to understand that buying from you is something you do. That it's normal, expected, and good for them. If you never mention your products, you're not being humble. You're depriving your readers of the next step they might need.

The right sequence: spend the first 30-60 days giving value with no ask. Build proof that you understand the reader's problem and can say useful things about it. Then introduce your lowest-barrier offer, whether that's a free lead magnet or a low-priced product, and mention it regularly. Not every post, but every few. Make it part of the content ecosystem, not a break from it.

**Tactical takeaway:** Count how many times you've mentioned your product or offer in the last 30 days of content. If the answer is fewer than five, double it next month. Mentioning what you sell is not selling out. It's completing the service. The reader who needed it will thank you.

Give freely. Sell clearly. Both at the same time.
