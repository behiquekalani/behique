# Issue 1296: How to Validate a Digital Product Before You Build It

Building for 40 hours and then finding out no one wants it is the most common expensive mistake in the creator economy. The solution exists. Most people skip it because validation feels slower than building.

It is not slower. Validation that takes two days and saves you from building the wrong thing is faster than two weeks of building followed by a failed launch.

The minimum viable validation has three steps. First, describe the product in one sentence to ten people in your target audience. Not friends. Actual potential buyers. Watch their reaction. If their eyes go flat, the product doesn't resonate. If they lean in and ask how to buy it, you have something.

Second, write the sales page before you build the product. This sounds backwards. It's not. Writing the sales page forces you to articulate the outcome, the audience, and the value. If you can't write a compelling sales page for the product, the product concept isn't clear yet. Clarify the concept, then build.

Third, pre-sell it. Post about the product. Set a specific window. Offer early access at a discount. If you can get 10 pre-sales at $47, you've validated the concept, funded the build, and have a cohort of buyers who will give you feedback when it launches. If you get zero pre-sales after genuine promotion, you've learned a $47 lesson instead of a 40-hour one.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your next product idea and write the sales page for it today. If you can't finish it, the concept needs more definition. If you finish it and it sounds compelling to you, post a pre-sale offer. See what happens before you build a single slide.

The market will tell you what to build. Let it.
