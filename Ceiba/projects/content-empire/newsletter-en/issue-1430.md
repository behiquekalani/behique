# Issue 1430 — The 100-Day Rule

**Subject:** What separates creators who make it from those who quit

---

There's a point in every creator's journey, usually somewhere between day 60 and day 90, where the work feels pointless. The output is consistent. The quality is real. But the numbers don't reflect it yet. This is the point where most people stop.

The 100-day rule is not a motivational concept. It's an observation about how audience growth actually works. In most cases, the feedback loop is delayed. The people who will eventually find your work are not finding it yet because the algorithm hasn't understood your content pattern, your audience hasn't grown enough to share your work, and your own skill hasn't compounded into something remarkable.

Quitting at day 70 is the single most common mistake in the creator economy. You stop right before the feedback becomes visible. The creators who break through are not necessarily more talented. They are more patient. They treat the first 100 days as infrastructure, not as evidence of success or failure.

The question is not "is this working?" during the first 100 days. The question is "am I getting better?" If yes, keep going. Getting better always precedes getting big.

**Tactical takeaway:** If you're under 100 days of consistent effort, remove the analytics. Stop checking the numbers. Check the quality of your thinking instead. The numbers will reflect the quality eventually. Not before.

Stay. The algorithm catches up.
