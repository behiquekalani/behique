# Issue 1435 — The Quiet Advantage of Building in Public

**Subject:** Why showing your process is better content than showing your results

---

"Building in public" has become a content category, and like most content categories, it's been diluted. The common version is revenue screenshots and milestone announcements. That's not building in public. That's performance.

Real building in public is documenting the decisions, not just the outcomes. The moment you chose to pivot. The product that flopped and the specific reason you think it flopped. The week nothing worked and what you tried anyway. This kind of content does something revenue screenshots can't: it makes the reader trust that you actually understand the problem, not just that you got lucky.

The audience for this kind of content is also different. People who follow because of your results will leave when the results slow down. People who follow because of your thinking will stay through the hard periods because the thinking doesn't stop being useful.

There's also a practical business benefit. Documenting your process creates product ideas. The thing you figured out that took you three weeks to figure out is exactly the thing someone else will pay to skip. The content is market research.

**Tactical takeaway:** This week, share one decision you made in your business. Not the result. The reasoning. What you considered, what you rejected, and why you went the direction you did. It will be your best-performing post of the month.

Show the thinking. The results will speak for themselves later.
