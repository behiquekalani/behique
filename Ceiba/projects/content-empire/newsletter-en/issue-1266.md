# Issue 1266: Why Solopreneurs Who Niche Down Make More Money Without More Work

The generalist solopreneur works twice as hard to make half as much. This is not an opinion. It shows up in the pricing, the conversion rate, and the referrals.

When you serve everyone, you're competing on price with everyone. There is always someone cheaper. When you serve a specific person with a specific problem, you're competing on fit. Price becomes a secondary concern for a buyer who believes you understand their exact situation.

The fear of niching down is real. It feels like you're cutting off potential customers. You are. You're also cutting off the 90% of people who would never buy from you anyway and replacing them with the 10% who will pay more, refer more, and complain less.

Specificity also makes marketing easier. When you know exactly who you're talking to, you know exactly what to write. The content writes itself. The offer writes itself. You stop guessing and start connecting.

The niche doesn't have to be a demographic. It can be a problem. "I help people who make good money but feel like they have nothing to show for it." That's a niche. It's psychographic. It's specific. It attracts.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write down the three clients or customers who made you happiest to work with. What do they have in common? That intersection is your niche. Build your next piece of content or product specifically for that person.

Specific is profitable.
