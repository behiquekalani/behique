# Issue 1304: Nobody Builds an Audience Anymore. They Borrow One.

Organic growth is real. It's also slow. The fastest way to grow an audience in 2025 is not to build one from scratch. It's to borrow someone else's.

This means collaborations, not ads. Guest newsletters. Podcast appearances. Partnerships where both parties have something the other's audience wants. The creator who shows up in front of 50,000 aligned subscribers once will grow faster than the one posting daily for a year to 200 followers.

The key word is aligned. Borrowing a mismatched audience is worse than building slowly. If your audience is productivity-focused solopreneurs and you appear on a fitness podcast, you'll get followers who don't buy from you. Quality of borrowed audience matters more than size.

To borrow an audience well, you need two things: a clear value proposition and a compelling reason for the host to say yes. The value proposition is what you bring to their audience. The reason to say yes is what you offer them, whether that's reciprocal promotion, a free product, or simply a great episode. Make it easy for them to agree.

**Tactical takeaway:** Write a list of 5 creators in adjacent niches whose audience overlaps with your ideal customer. Draft a one-paragraph pitch for each. Send one this week.

Your next 1,000 followers are already subscribed to someone else.
