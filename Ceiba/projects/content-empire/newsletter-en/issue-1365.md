# Issue 1365: The AI Tool You're Actually Underusing

**Subject:** Most people are using AI like a search engine

---

The common complaint about AI tools is that they produce generic output. And for most people, that's true. But the reason isn't the tool. It's the input.

Most creators use AI like a faster Google. "Give me 10 content ideas for a productivity creator." The output is average because the input is average. The tool has no context about who you are, who your audience is, or what specific problem you're trying to solve. It's filling a blank with the most statistically likely answer.

The unlock is context loading. Before you ask for anything, give the tool your voice, your audience, your past work, your current goal. "I write for early-career software engineers who want to go independent within 2 years. My tone is direct and no-hype. Here's a post I wrote that resonated: [paste]. Now help me write three hooks for a piece about rate negotiation." The output is now specific, on-brand, and actually useful.

This is the difference between a vending machine and a thinking partner. You don't change the tool. You change how you enter the conversation.

**Tactical Takeaway:** Before your next AI session, write a 3-sentence context block: who you are, who you write for, and what your tone sounds like. Paste it before every prompt. The output will be immediately different.

Better inputs produce better thinking. That's true of tools and of people.
