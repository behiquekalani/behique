# Issue 1356 — How to Build a Product Suite, Not Just a Product

**Subject:** Why one product is a bet. Three products are a business.

---

A single product is fragile. If the market shifts, if a better competitor emerges, if the audience's priorities change, you have no fallback. The creator who built a business around one product finds out how fragile it was the first time something disrupts the demand.

A product suite changes the risk profile. It also changes the customer relationship. When a buyer gets value from your $19 product, they want more from you. If the next step is a $97 product that goes deeper, you haven't sold them something new. You've given them the logical continuation of the journey they already started. The hard part of sales, establishing trust, is already done.

The right way to think about a suite is entry, depth, and transformation. Entry is the lowest-barrier product that proves you can solve the problem. Depth is the comprehensive version for serious buyers. Transformation is the high-touch offer for people who want the result, not the information. You don't need all three on day one. But knowing where you're going means every product you build fits the map.

**Tactical takeaway:** Sketch a three-tier product map. Don't create anything new yet. Just write down: what would a $15-$29 entry product look like? A $97-$147 depth product? A $300-$500 transformation product? Most creators find they already have the materials for at least two of these. The suite is already there. It just needs to be named.

Name the ladder. Then build the rungs.
