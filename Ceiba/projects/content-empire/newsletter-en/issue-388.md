# Newsletter EN — Issue 388
## The Lead Magnet Formula That Builds Real Lists

**Subject line:** Your freebie is attracting the wrong people.

**Preview text:** The problem isn't the size of your list. It's the quality.

---

You have a lead magnet. You promoted it. People downloaded it. Your list grew. Then you sent an offer and heard silence.

The list was real. The problem was who was on it.

**Why most lead magnets fail the test**

The goal of a lead magnet is not to collect email addresses. It is to attract the right person, demonstrate your value, and create momentum toward a purchase. When the magnet is too broad, too generic, or too disconnected from your paid offer, it fails all three.

A "free productivity checklist" attracts everyone interested in productivity. That's students, executives, people who just discovered Notion, retirees, and your actual buyer all mixed together. You can't write one email sequence for all of them. You can't make one offer that resonates with all of them.

**The filtering function**

A good lead magnet is a filter, not a net. It is designed to attract one type of person by being so specific that anyone outside that profile ignores it.

"Free guide: how to get your first $1,000 month as a freelance writer" does not appeal to everyone. It appeals to aspiring freelance writers who want to earn money, not just improve their writing. That specificity is the mechanism. The people who download it are already pre-qualified.

**The formula**

Three elements determine whether a lead magnet builds a real list.

Specific audience. Not "creators" or "entrepreneurs." A real person with a real job title, real frustration, and a specific outcome they want. The tighter the definition, the more qualified the download.

Urgent, small problem. The best lead magnets solve one frustration fast. Not a comprehensive guide to everything. A two-page fix for one specific bottleneck. Speed of value matters. If someone downloads it and gets a result in 15 minutes, they trust you more than any long course could create.

Logical bridge to the paid offer. The magnet should make the reader want more. It solves problem A and naturally points to problem B, which your paid product addresses. The download is chapter one. The purchase is the rest of the book.

**What this looks like in practice**

You sell a course on building a personal brand as a developer. Your lead magnet is not "10 personal branding tips." It is a one-page audit: "Is your GitHub profile killing your career? 5 things developers miss." It solves a small problem, attracts your exact buyer, and creates the desire for the full framework.

That's the formula. Specificity creates quality. Quality creates buyers.
