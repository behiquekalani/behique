# Issue 1431 — Digital Products That Age Well

**Subject:** The difference between products that keep selling and products that die fast

---

Most digital products have a shelf life problem. A course about a specific tool becomes outdated when the tool updates. A guide about a trend becomes irrelevant when the trend fades. The creators who build sustainable passive income are the ones who understand which type of knowledge ages slowly.

Evergreen knowledge is tied to human behavior, not software features. How to write clearly. How to build trust with an audience. How to structure an offer. How to manage time when you work alone. These problems existed before the internet and they will outlast every platform update. Products built on this foundation keep selling for years.

The contrast is tactical knowledge, which is useful right now but depreciates fast. A tutorial on a specific platform's interface, for example, has a 12-18 month window before it needs updating. That's fine if you plan for it, but most creators don't and then wonder why their product stopped selling.

The rule: the more specific the software, the faster it ages. The more specific the human problem, the longer it sells.

**Tactical takeaway:** Audit your product ideas. Sort them into two columns: "ages fast" and "ages slowly." Prioritize building from the second column. Then layer tactical products on top once the evergreen base is generating revenue.

Build what lasts. Then build what's timely.
