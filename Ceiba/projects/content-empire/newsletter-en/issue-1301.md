# Issue 1301: The One Number Every Creator Ignores (And Why It's Killing Their Income)

Most creators obsess over follower count. Wrong metric.

The number that actually determines your income is conversion rate. Specifically: what percentage of people who see your offer buy it. You can have 10K followers and a 3% conversion rate and out-earn someone with 100K followers at 0.1%. The math is simple. The application is not.

Conversion rate is a function of three things: trust, specificity, and timing. Trust is built through consistent, honest content over time. Specificity means your offer solves a named problem for a named person. Timing means you're in front of the right person when they're ready to buy, not just when you feel like posting.

Most creators fail on specificity. They build a broad audience and then try to sell to everyone. When you sell to everyone, you sell to no one. A product page that says "for creators who want to grow" will always underperform one that says "for solopreneurs doing $0-$2K/month who want to hit $5K through digital products."

Narrow your promise. Sharpen your audience. Watch the number that matters go up.

**Tactical takeaway:** Audit your last 3 offers. For each one, write down exactly who it's for and what specific problem it solves in one sentence. If you can't, that's your conversion problem.

You can't optimize what you haven't defined.
