# Issue 1390: Why Most "Niching Down" Advice Is Wrong

**Subject:** The niche you actually want is not smaller. It is sharper.

---

"Niche down" has become the default advice for anyone struggling with audience growth. And it is not wrong exactly, but most people apply it incorrectly. They make their topic smaller when they should be making their angle sharper.

The difference matters. Making your topic smaller might mean going from "business" to "e-commerce" to "Shopify for women over 40." That works, but it is still just demographic targeting. Making your angle sharper means having a specific belief or methodology that differentiates you within any topic. Not just "I help people build businesses" but "I think the hustle culture model is broken and here is the quieter, more sustainable alternative."

The second version is not smaller. It is distinct. It attracts people who share a worldview, not just a demographic. That kind of audience is more loyal, more engaged, and more likely to buy because they are not following you for information. They are following you because you articulate something they already felt but couldn't say.

**Tactical takeaway:** Identify one belief about your industry that most mainstream voices would disagree with. Write a 300-word post explaining why you believe it and what evidence you have. That post will perform better than any "tips and tricks" content you create this month.

A sharp angle cuts through. A small topic just limits reach.
