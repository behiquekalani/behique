# Issue 1360 — Build the Business Before You Need It

**Subject:** The best time to build your digital presence was yesterday. Today works.

---

There's always a reason to wait. The niche isn't clear yet. The product isn't ready. The audience isn't big enough. The design isn't polished. The timing isn't right. These objections are real, and they're also indefinitely renewable. There will always be another version of them available when you need an excuse to stay comfortable.

The creators who build real businesses share one trait more than any other: they started before they were ready and got ready by going. The product gets better after the first sale, not before it. The content gets sharper after the first 100 posts, not in the planning stage. The audience grows because you started, not because you waited until you had something worth growing an audience for.

The cost of waiting is invisible in real time. You don't see the compound growth you missed. You don't feel the subscribers who didn't join because you hadn't started yet. The cost only becomes visible later, when you look back and can measure what two years of consistency would have produced, if you'd started two years ago.

**Tactical takeaway:** Pick the one thing you've been planning to start for longer than 30 days. Give yourself 48 hours to publish a minimum viable version of it. Not the final version. The version that exists. A product that's 60% of what you imagined is infinitely more useful than a perfect product that doesn't exist yet. Ship, then improve.

Imperfect and published beats perfect and waiting.
