# Newsletter EN — Issue 385

**Subject:** Email list vs. social following: the math most creators ignore

**Preview text:** The numbers make a clear argument. Most creators are building on the wrong foundation.

---

There is a persistent belief in the creator space that follower count is the primary measure of audience value. This belief costs creators real money every year.

The math tells a different story.

**The actual comparison**

Average organic reach on Instagram: 3-8% of followers.
Average email open rate: 25-45% of subscribers.

If you have 10,000 Instagram followers, roughly 300-800 people see any given post. If you have 1,000 email subscribers, 250-450 people open every email you send.

The list of 1,000 reaches nearly as many people as the following of 10,000. With a more intentional, higher-trust audience.

Now run conversion rates. An email subscriber converts to buyer at roughly 1-3%. A social follower converts at a fraction of that, typically 0.1-0.5% depending on the platform and offer.

The math is not close. Email wins by an order of magnitude on both reach efficiency and conversion probability.

**Why most creators still prioritize social**

Social followings are visible. They confer social proof. A large follower count signals credibility to new audiences in a way that a list of email subscribers does not, because subscribers are invisible to everyone except you.

This visibility bias pushes creators to optimize for the vanity metric at the expense of the asset.

It is a rational short-term decision and an irrational long-term one.

**The compounding case for email**

Social platforms change. Algorithms shift. Accounts get restricted. Reach declines. These are not edge cases. They are recurring events in the history of every major platform.

Your email list is not subject to any of that. You own the contact. You control the send. No algorithm stands between you and your reader.

A list built over five years compounds the same way an investment does. It grows, it becomes more segmented, and each segment becomes more valuable as you understand what they need.

The decision is not whether to build a social following. It is whether you treat social as a destination or a pipeline. The answer should always be: pipeline.

Every piece of content on every platform has one ultimate goal: move the right person onto your list. That is where the durable business lives.
