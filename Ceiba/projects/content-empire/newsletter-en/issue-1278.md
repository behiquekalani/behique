# Issue 1278: The Repurposing Strategy That Actually Works

Repurposing content sounds like an efficiency hack. Done wrong, it's just spam across multiple platforms. Done right, it's your distribution engine.

The wrong way: copy-paste a tweet into an Instagram caption. Take the audio from a YouTube video and post it as a podcast without editing. Share the same LinkedIn post word-for-word on every platform. Same content, different box. The audience on each platform can feel when content wasn't made for them.

The right way: start with one core idea expressed fully in your long-form anchor piece. Then extract specific points from that piece and reformat them for each platform's native style. The idea stays the same. The expression changes completely.

A newsletter issue becomes five tweets. Each tweet covers one insight from the issue, written in tweet-native language. Not shortened newsletter sentences. Actual tweets. The YouTube video becomes a short thread pulling the three most counterintuitive moments. The thread becomes a LinkedIn post built around the most practical point.

The anchor piece does the thinking. The repurposed pieces do the distribution. You think once. You reach multiple audiences. The key discipline is resisting the urge to just copy-paste. Take the extra ten minutes to write natively for each format.

**Tactical takeaway:** Take your best piece of content from the past month. Identify three distinct insights from it. Write one native post for each insight on one platform. Do not copy from the original. Write fresh, in the voice of that platform. Three posts from one piece of thinking.

One idea. Three native expressions.
