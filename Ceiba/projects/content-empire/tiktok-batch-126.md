## TikTok 2: The Consistency Lie
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Consistency will not make you money. This will."

**Script:**
Everyone tells you to be consistent. Post every day. Show up. Trust the process.

That is not wrong. But it is incomplete.

Consistency without conversion is just noise. You can be the most consistent creator on the platform and still make nothing if every video ends with no next step.

The creators making their first thousand dollars are not the most consistent. They are the most intentional.

Every piece of content they make answers one question: does this move someone closer to buying something?

Not every post needs to be a sales pitch. But every post should be part of a system that eventually leads somewhere.

Build the system first. Then be consistent inside it.

## TikTok 3: The Follower Fantasy
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Waiting until you have 10K followers to monetize is the reason you're broke."

**Script:**
There is a story creators tell themselves. "Once I hit 10K, I'll start selling."

Then they hit 10K. And they push it to 50K.

This is not strategy. It is procrastination with a number attached to it.

The truth is you can make your first thousand dollars with under a thousand followers. I have seen it happen. It happens because of one thing: those creators made an offer before they felt ready.

Your audience does not need to be big. It needs to be warm and it needs somewhere to go.

Stop waiting for permission from a follower counter. Make the thing. Sell the thing.

## TikTok 4: The Wrong First Product
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your first product idea is probably the wrong one. Here's how to find the right one."

**Script:**
Most creators who try to monetize make the same mistake. They build the product they want to make instead of the product their audience is already asking for.

You post about productivity. People comment asking about your note-taking system. You build a mindset course.

Nobody buys it.

The right first product is hiding in your comment section. It is the question people ask you most. It is the thing they already want from you.

Stop building from your ego. Build from their problems.

Go look at your last twenty posts right now. Find the most asked question. That is your first product.

## TikTok 5: The Price Problem
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your product isn't too expensive. It's too vague."

**Script:**
Creators who can not make their first sale almost always have the same problem. They made something real and then described it in a way that sounds like everything else.

"A guide to growing your business." "A course on content creation." "A system for productivity."

None of those tell me what changes in my life after I buy it.

Price is never the real objection. Clarity is. When someone understands exactly what they get and why it matters to them, price becomes a small detail.

Your job before launching anything is to be able to answer this in one sentence: "After you buy this, you will be able to do what you could not do before."

If you cannot answer that, you do not have a product. You have a document.
