## POST 13 [CONFIDENT ADVICE] (Authority)

How to turn any skill into a digital product in 7 days:

1. Write down the 5 questions people ask you most about your skill
2. Answer each one in 500 words
3. Add a checklist, template, or framework they can use
4. Package it as a PDF or interactive HTML
5. List it on Gumroad for $10-20
6. Post about it. Let the market decide.
7. If it sells, make version 2. If not, make something else.

Your first product will probably be bad. Ship it anyway. The second one won't be.

## POST 14 [PRINCIPLES] (Authority)

The 4 rules of building in public:

- Show the process, not just the result. People connect with the struggle.
- Be specific. "I made $47 today" hits harder than "business is growing."
- Share failures faster than wins. Nobody trusts someone who only wins.
- Build what you'd use yourself. The best marketing is being your own customer.

Building in public is not content strategy. It's accountability with an audience.

## POST 18 [CONFIDENT ADVICE] (Authenticity - ADHD)

How I actually get things done with ADHD:

1. I don't use to-do lists. I use a "next action" sticky note. One thing.
2. I build systems that trigger themselves. If I have to remember it, it won't happen.
3. I work in 25-minute blocks. Not because Pomodoro says so. Because that's my natural window.
4. I forgive myself when I drift. Then I come back. No drama.
5. I use AI to handle the parts my brain refuses to do (scheduling, organizing, follow-ups).

ADHD is not about lacking focus. It's about having focus that doesn't obey you. So you build rails for it.
