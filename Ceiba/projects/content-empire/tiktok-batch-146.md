## TikTok 1: The Only Model That Scales Without You
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Most people make $0 online because they're selling the wrong thing."

**Script:**
Here's the model I'd use to build $5K a month in digital products if I had to start from zero.

Step one: pick one specific problem a specific person has. Not "make money online." Something like "freelance designers who lose clients after the first project."

Step two: build one product that solves exactly that. A template. A guide. A system. Something they can use without you being there.

Step three: write content that attracts that person every single day. Short-form video. Newsletter. Whatever you can stay consistent with.

Step four: send every lead into an email list. That list is the business. Social is just the pipeline.

Step five: sell the product through email. Automated. Repeatable. Scalable.

This model works because you build once and sell forever. You are not trading time for money. You are building a machine that runs while you sleep.

The first $5K is proof of concept. After that, you add a second product or raise the price.

Simple. Not easy. But completely doable if you actually execute.

One specific audience. One product between $47 and $197. One content channel to grow the list. One email sequence to convert.

That is the whole business. Everything else is optional.

## TikTok 3: The Product That Sells Itself
**Hook (0-2s):**
"The best digital product doesn't need a sales call."

**Script:**
If you have to convince someone to buy your product, the product is wrong or the audience is wrong.

The best digital products sell because they describe the problem better than the buyer can describe it themselves. When someone reads your product page and thinks "this is exactly what I needed," the sale is already done.

How do you get there?

Go into forums, comment sections, Reddit threads, and Facebook groups where your target audience talks. Copy their exact words. The phrases they use. The frustrations they express. The thing they keep coming back to.

Now write your product page using their language, not yours.

"I help creators monetize their audience" is your language.

"I show you how to turn followers who never buy into customers in 30 days" is theirs.

The second one sells. The first one gets ignored.

Your product should feel like it was written by someone who has been inside their head for a year. Because you did the research to make it feel that way.

Save this. Go find three pieces of your audience's language today.

## TikTok 4: You Don't Need a Big Audience for $5K
**Hook (0-2s):**
"1,000 followers is enough. Here's the math."

**Script:**
Let me show you the math that most people never run.

You have 1,000 followers. Industry average email opt-in rate from social: roughly 2 to 5 percent if you push it. Let's say 3 percent. That's 30 people on your list.

Email conversion rate for a warm list with a good offer: 1 to 3 percent per campaign.

You send 10 emails over a month. That's 10 opportunities to convert.

At 2 percent of 30 people, you get one sale per campaign. At $97 per product, that's $970 over 10 emails.

Now scale that. Get to 500 subscribers. Same conversion rates. You're looking at $4,850 a month.

500 email subscribers. That is your real target, not 100K followers.

Most people are chasing the vanity number instead of building the list that converts.

Grow a small, engaged audience. Capture emails aggressively. Sell one good product repeatedly. That's the business.

**CTA:**
If you don't have an email list yet, that is your task for this week. Start one.

## TikTok 5: The First Product You Should Build
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your first digital product should take you one weekend to build."

**Script:**
I see people spend six months building a course before they've validated anything. That is how you burn out before you ever make a dollar.

Your first product should be a PDF guide, a template pack, or a short tutorial video series. Something you can complete in a weekend.

The point of the first product is not to make $5K. The point is to learn whether people will pay for what you're offering. If they won't pay $27, they won't pay $297 for the bigger version.

So here's what you do. Identify the single most painful problem your audience has. Write a 10 to 20 page guide that solves it completely. Price it between $17 and $47.

Send it to your email list. Post about it on social. If it sells, you have proof. If it doesn't, you have data. Either way you learn faster than someone still building their course.

The first product teaches you more about your audience than any research session will. Ship it ugly. Price it low. Learn fast.

What problem would your first product solve? Write it in the comments.
