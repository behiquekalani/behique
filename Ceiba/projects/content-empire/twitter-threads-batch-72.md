All of it free. All of it proven.

Don't obsess over analytics before you have enough traffic to make them meaningful.

## Thread 72-4: When to Pay for Tools (and When Not To)

**Tweet 1 (hook):**
Most solopreneurs pay for tools they don't need.

And delay paying for tools that would actually move the needle.

Here's how to decide:

**Tweet 2:**
Don't pay for a tool if:

- You haven't validated the use case with the free version
- You're paying to feel productive instead of to be productive
- The tool solves a problem you don't actually have yet

Tool costs compound. Audit yours every 90 days.

**Tweet 3:**
Pay for a tool when:

- The free version's limits are actively blocking revenue
- You've used the free version enough to know you'll use the paid version
- The ROI calculation is obvious (time saved x your hourly rate)

Upgrade when the cost is smaller than the friction it removes.

**Tweet 4:**
The most common tool mistakes:

1. Paying for Notion AI before you have a system in Notion
2. Paying for premium design tools before you have a design need
3. Paying for advanced email tools before you have a list

**Tweet 5:**
The tool that's almost always worth paying for first:

A good email service provider once you hit the free tier limit.

Your email list is your most valuable asset. Don't cheap out on it once it starts growing.

**Tweet 6:**
The tool category most solopreneurs overpay for:

Analytics and SEO tools.

You don't need $99/month in analytics when you have fewer than 10,000 monthly visitors. Free Google Search Console tells you everything you need.

**Tweet 7:**
The better question than "which tool should I pay for?":

"What is the biggest bottleneck in my business right now?"

Pay to solve that bottleneck. Everything else is optional.

## Thread 72-5: The Minimal Stack That Does Everything

**Tweet 1 (hook):**
The most productive solopreneurs I know use fewer tools, not more.

Here's the minimal stack that handles everything:

**Tweet 2:**
1 notes app: Obsidian (free, local, yours forever)

1 writing environment: iA Writer or VS Code

That's your thinking and creation layer. Nothing else needed.

**Tweet 3:**
1 publishing platform: Newsletter (Beehiiv or Substack)
1 social platform: wherever your audience is
1 long-form platform: blog, YouTube, or podcast

That's your distribution layer.

**Tweet 4:**
1 payment processor: Gumroad or Stripe
1 product delivery: Gumroad or email
1 CRM: Notion database

That's your revenue layer.

**Tweet 5:**
1 automation tool: n8n (self-hosted) or Make.com free tier
1 scheduling tool: Buffer free tier

That's your operations layer.

Total monthly cost if you do it right: $0.

Total monthly cost once you have revenue and need to scale: under $50.

The barrier to starting is not financial. It never was.

**Tweet 7:**
The hidden cost of too many tools:

Context switching. Decision fatigue. Maintenance overhead.

Every tool you add is a tool you have to learn, update, and remember to use.
