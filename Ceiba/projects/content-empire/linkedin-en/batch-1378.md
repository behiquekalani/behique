# LinkedIn Post 1378

The creator who posts every day for a year will outperform the one who posts perfectly once a week.

I know because I was the second type for eighteen months.

I spent hours on every post. Drafted, revised, scrapped, rewrote. Posted it and got decent engagement. Then disappeared for a week because I was "preparing the next one."

The algorithm did not reward my perfectionism. Neither did the audience.

When I switched to daily publishing, the quality of individual posts dropped. The overall growth was three times faster.

Here is the math: 365 average posts will teach you more about your audience than 52 perfect posts. More reps. More data. More understanding of what actually lands.

Quantity is a path to quality. Not instead of it, but through it.

The creator who has published 1,000 posts knows things that cannot be taught in any course. They earned that knowledge through repetition.

You do not get better at writing by thinking about writing. You get better by writing.

What would your content output look like if you removed the word "perfect" from your process?
