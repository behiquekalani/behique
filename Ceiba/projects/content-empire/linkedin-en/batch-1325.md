Nobody talks about the six months before the breakthrough.

The posts that get shared are the wins. The revenue screenshots. The "I quit my job" announcements.

But the six months before that? That's where the actual work happens. That's where most people quit.

I've been building in public long enough to see the pattern. The people who break through are not necessarily the most talented. They're the ones who stayed consistent past the point where it felt pointless.

That's not motivational fluff. It's math.

Building an audience, a product catalog, or a service reputation is a compounding game. The first 90 days produce almost nothing visible. The next 90 days build on that nothing. By month six, the curve starts to bend.

But you have to be in it to see it bend.

The problem isn't that people give up too early. The problem is they have no signal to tell them they're on the right track. So silence feels like failure.

Silence is not failure. Silence is the compounding phase.

Keep building. Keep shipping. Keep showing up.

The curve will bend. You just have to outlast the silence.
