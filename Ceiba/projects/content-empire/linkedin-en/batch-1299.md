I spent two years trying to be consistent before I understood what consistency actually means.

I thought it meant showing up every single day, no exceptions. The posts had to come out. The newsletter had to send. The streak could not break. When it did break, the whole thing felt like a failure.

That definition of consistency is designed to make you feel like you are failing.

Real consistency is showing up on a schedule your life can sustain. Not a schedule that looks impressive on paper. Not the schedule you would keep if everything went perfectly. The schedule that survives a bad week, a sick kid, a project deadline, a hard month.

The creator who posts three times a week and has kept that up for two years is infinitely more consistent than the one who posted every day for forty days and disappeared.

Frequency is not consistency. Sustainability is consistency.

When I stopped optimizing for volume and started optimizing for staying in the game long enough for the work to compound, the actual output per year went up. Because I was not burning out and restarting. I was running the same race at a pace I could actually maintain.

Design your content practice around your real life, not the ideal version of it.
