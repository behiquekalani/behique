I had a conversation last month that changed how I think about pricing.

A consultant told me he'd raised his rates three times in two years. Each time he lost some clients. Each time the clients he kept were better than the ones who left.

He didn't raise his rates because he got better. He raised them because he finally believed what his results were worth. The price change came from inside, not from the market.

Most people underprice not because of market conditions but because of self-perception. They look at what others charge, anchor to the lower end, and assume that's where they belong.

Here's the thing about price: it signals your confidence in your own value. If you aren't sure you're worth more, your price will say so before you speak a word.

The way to fix underpricing isn't to study competitors more. It's to get specific about results. Not "I help with marketing" but "my last three clients saw their email open rates go from 18% to 41% in six weeks." Specificity earns rates.

Know the outcome you deliver. Price the outcome, not the hours.

What would change if you raised your rates 20% today?
