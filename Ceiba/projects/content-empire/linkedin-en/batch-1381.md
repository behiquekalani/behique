# LinkedIn Post 1381

I used to think productivity was about doing more.

More tasks. More hours. More hustle.

Then I hit a wall so hard I couldn't get out of bed for two days.

That's when I realized I wasn't managing time. I was managing energy, badly.

The shift happened when I stopped asking "what can I fit in today?" and started asking "what is the one thing that moves everything else forward?"

One question. Different results.

Here's what changed:
- I stopped checking email first thing in the morning
- I blocked my two highest-focus hours before anything else touched my calendar
- I built a shutdown ritual that actually let my brain rest

The output went up. The exhaustion went down.

You don't have a time problem. You have a priority problem.

Most people know this. Few actually restructure their day around it.

The ones who do stop spinning and start building.
