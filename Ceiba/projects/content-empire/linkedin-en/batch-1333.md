The one question I ask before building anything new: who is already looking for this?

Not "could this be useful?" Useful things fail all the time.

Not "is this a good idea?" Good ideas are cheap.

The question is whether there's an existing group of people actively searching for a solution to the exact problem this solves.

If yes, build it and put it in front of them.
If no, the first job is to build the audience before building the product.

This seems obvious. But most builders skip it because they fall in love with the thing they're building instead of the person it's supposed to help.

The market doesn't care about your vision. The market cares about its own pain.

When you build something that matches existing demand, distribution is easier. SEO works faster. Referrals happen naturally. People share it because it solves something real.

When you build something ahead of demand, you have to educate and convince at the same time. That's expensive in time and money.

Find the existing demand. Build exactly for that. Ship.

The creative work is in how you solve it, not whether people want it solved.
