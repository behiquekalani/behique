# LinkedIn Post 1391

The best investment I made this year wasn't a course.

It was building one system that eliminated a recurring problem.

Every week I spent 3 hours doing the same manual work. I spent one weekend automating it. That's it.

3 hours every week for 52 weeks is 156 hours a year.

156 hours I now spend building instead of maintaining.

Most people know this math. Most people never do it because "I'll get to it later."

Later doesn't come. The manual work does.

Pick one recurring task that costs you more than an hour a week. Automate it, delegate it, or eliminate it this month.

Not next quarter. This month.

The first automation pays for itself within weeks. The discipline of doing it once builds the habit of doing it repeatedly.

That's how you buy back your time without earning more money.

What's the one task you do manually every week that you know should be automated?
