# LinkedIn Post 1393

Confidence isn't something you find. It's something you build through evidence.

Every time you do the thing you said you would, you deposit trust in yourself.

Every time you don't, you withdraw.

Most people who struggle with confidence aren't dealing with a mindset problem. They're dealing with a bank account problem. Years of small withdrawals have left the balance near zero.

The fix isn't affirmations. The fix is small, consistent, kept promises to yourself.

You tell yourself you'll write for 10 minutes. You write for 10 minutes. You deposit.

You tell yourself you'll work on the project at 7am. You do. Deposit.

Do that for 30 days and something shifts. Not because of a motivational speech. Because you have evidence.

Evidence that you follow through. Evidence that you can be trusted. By yourself.

That's what confidence is built on.

Start this week. One small promise. Keep it. Then make another.

The size of the promise doesn't matter as much as the keeping of it.

What's one promise to yourself you've been breaking that you could start keeping today?
