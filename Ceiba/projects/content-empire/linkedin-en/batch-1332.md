Most people design their life around their income instead of designing their income around their life.

That's not a philosophy lecture. That's a practical observation about where time goes.

If you take whatever job pays the most, you end up building your life around the schedule, the location, and the identity that job requires. Everything else fits in the margins.

The alternative is harder to start but much harder to lose.

When you build income around what you want your days to look like, you make different decisions. You price your time differently. You say no to things that pay well but cost you in other ways.

This isn't about rejecting employment. Some jobs are worth it. It's about being honest about the trade you're making.

You're trading your hours, attention, and energy for money. The question is whether the terms of that trade are ones you consciously chose or ones you drifted into.

Most people drift. A small number decide.

The ones who decide don't always make more money in the short term. But they end up with more control of where they're going.

Control compounds. Start designing on purpose.
