My first digital product took 3 weeks to build and sold for $19.

I treated it like a failure.

But here's what I didn't understand at the time: that $19 was not the point. The point was that a stranger, somewhere, decided my work was worth exchanging money for.

That is a different kind of proof than likes or comments or followers. It's proof that you made something real enough that someone opened their wallet.

Most creators stop before they get that proof. They're waiting for the perfect product, the perfect niche, the perfect price. They're building in private, convinced the market isn't ready.

The market is always ready for something specific and honest.

What kills most first products is not competition. It's the creator never finishing them. Or finishing but never shipping. Or shipping but immediately moving on without telling anyone it exists.

I spent two weeks telling nobody about that $19 product. Then I mentioned it once in a post. Sold three copies that day.

The market doesn't find you. You go to the market.

Finish the thing. Ship it. Tell people it exists. That's the whole strategy.
