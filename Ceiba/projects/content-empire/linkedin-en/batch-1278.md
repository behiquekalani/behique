I've had two kinds of mentors in my life.

The first kind told me what to do. Follow this path. Do it this way. Here's the map.

The second kind showed me how to think. Asked better questions. Pointed at patterns. Gave me tools I could use in situations they couldn't predict.

The first kind helped me in the short term. The second kind changed how I operate permanently.

The creator space is full of the first kind. Courses that tell you exactly what to post, when to post it, which platform, which format. Follow the steps and you'll get results.

Sometimes you do. But you never really know why. And when the steps stop working, you're back to square one.

What I look for now is not people who tell me what to do. It's people who give me frameworks for deciding what to do myself.

A good framework outlives the advice. It works in markets that didn't exist when the framework was created. It adapts.

Most of the best things I've learned came from books, not courses. From watching how exceptional people think, not following what they did.

Learn to think before you learn to do.
