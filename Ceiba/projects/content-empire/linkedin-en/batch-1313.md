I got fired from a freelance client in year one of my business.

They said my communication was poor and my timelines were inconsistent. They were right.

At the time I was bitter. I thought the client was difficult. I told myself the project was unclear and the expectations were unreasonable.

Then I got the same feedback from a second client. That's when I stopped blaming the external and looked at the pattern.

I wasn't bad at the work. I was bad at managing expectations. I'd say yes to deadlines without checking my actual capacity. I'd go silent when a project got complicated instead of flagging it early. I let problems grow because I was afraid to deliver bad news.

These are learnable things. I learned them. But only after I stopped defending myself and started listening.

The hardest feedback is the kind that's true. The feedback you want to argue with is usually the feedback worth sitting with longest.

Build a habit of asking clients at project end: "What could I have done differently?" Most won't tell you unprompted. Ask directly and you'll learn more about your business in an hour than you would in a month of guessing.

What's a piece of feedback you received that turned out to be right?
