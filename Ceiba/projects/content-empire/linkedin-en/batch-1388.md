# LinkedIn Post 1388

I deleted my to-do list last year.

Not metaphorically. I actually deleted the app and stopped using it.

Because a to-do list is a collection of possibilities. It doesn't tell you what to actually do next.

I replaced it with three things:

1. One MIT (Most Important Task) per day. One. Not three. Not five. One.
2. A weekly review every Sunday that decides the MITs for the week.
3. A "someday" list that captures everything else without it cluttering my attention.

The result: I do fewer things and finish more of them.

Productivity isn't about throughput. It's about completion.

A finished thing has value. A started-but-not-finished thing has almost none.

The human brain is wired to feel incomplete things more strongly than completed ones. That's called the Zeigarnik effect. Your half-finished projects are draining energy you could be using on the one thing that matters.

One thing. Done. Then the next.

Try it for one week and tell me what happens.

What's the one thing that would actually change your trajectory if you finished it this week?
