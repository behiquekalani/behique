Most people think learning faster means consuming more content.

It means the opposite.

I read fewer books this year than any previous year. I learned more.

The difference: I applied immediately.

Read something. Try it within 24 hours. Notice what worked. Notice what didn't. Adjust. That loop is where learning actually happens.

The people who consume the most content often make the least progress. They're optimizing for feeling informed instead of becoming capable.

Feeling informed is comfortable. Becoming capable requires failure, which is uncomfortable.

If your information diet is mostly passive (podcasts, videos, articles) with no active component (projects, experiments, creation), you're accumulating knowledge with no roots. It won't hold when you need it.

The test of learning is not "can I remember this?" It's "can I use this when it matters?"

Pick one thing you learned this week and use it today. Even if the use is small. Even if it's imperfect.

That's the whole method.

What's one thing you learned recently that you actually applied?
