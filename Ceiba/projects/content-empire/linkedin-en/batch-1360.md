**Hook:** The most underrated competitive advantage in the creator economy is showing up for years when everyone else shows up for months.

I've watched dozens of creators launch in the same space I was building in. Big announcements. Strong early content. Real momentum.

Six months later, most are gone.

Not because they failed. Because they had a timeline. They expected results by a certain date. The results didn't come fast enough. They moved on.

Longevity is a strategy, and it's one most people don't choose consciously.

The creators with large, loyal audiences didn't get there by having better content. They got there by being the last one standing in their niche after the first wave of enthusiasm died.

If you can publish consistently for two years in a focused topic area, you will almost certainly become a recognized voice. Not because two years is magic. Because almost nobody does it.

The asset you're building isn't followers. It's catalog density and time-in-market. Those two things make you findable, trustworthy, and hard to compete with.

You don't need to go viral. You need to not quit.

What's your honest timeline for the work you're doing right now?
