I'm a computer engineering student in Puerto Rico building a digital business at night.

No VC money. No startup capital. No team.

Just a laptop, some tools, and a clear understanding that the internet gives ordinary people access to extraordinary distribution.

A product I build once can sell to someone in Japan while I'm asleep. That's not a fantasy. That's the economics of digital goods.

The reason most people don't build this way isn't that they can't. It's that they've never seen someone who looks like them do it. So it feels like something for other people.

I'm here to say: it's not.

You don't need the perfect background. You don't need the right city or the right connections. You need a skill, a specific audience, and the discipline to keep shipping.

The entry point to digital income has never been lower. AI tools handle what used to require a team. Platforms give you global reach for free. The only scarce resource is the willingness to start.

If you're waiting for permission or the right circumstances, they're not coming. Start with what you have where you are.

What's the one thing standing between you and your first digital product?
