The best decision I made as a builder was to stop trying to make everyone's problem my problem.

There are 8 billion people. You cannot serve all of them. You cannot even serve most of them.

You can serve a specific person with a specific problem in a specific situation better than anyone else in the world.

That specificity feels like a limitation when you're starting. It's actually a competitive advantage.

"AI tools for everyone" is noise.

"AI workflow templates for solo freelancers who bill by the hour and hate admin work" is a product.

The narrower the problem, the clearer the solution. The clearer the solution, the easier it is to find the person who needs it. The easier it is to find them, the less you have to spend on marketing.

Niche is not a trap. Niche is a launchpad.

Most builders are afraid to be specific because specificity feels like rejection of everyone else. Flip that. Specificity is the deepest form of respect for the person you actually serve.

Who is the one person your work is specifically for?
