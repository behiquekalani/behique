I built a business while being a full-time student.

Not because I had extra time. I didn't. Because I decided that waiting for the right conditions was a story I was telling myself to avoid starting.

There is no right time. There's just time, and what you decide to do with it.

I used to look at successful creators and assume they had freedoms I didn't. More hours, fewer obligations, better circumstances. Then I started paying attention to what they actually said about how they started.

Most of them started with less. With night hours and early mornings. With borrowed Wi-Fi and one good idea they refused to let die.

The difference between starting and not starting isn't circumstance. It's tolerance for discomfort.

Building something from nothing is uncomfortable. Doing creative work when you're tired is uncomfortable. Shipping something before it's perfect is uncomfortable.

You don't get past the discomfort. You get used to it. And the more used to it you get, the faster you move.

I'm not where I want to be yet. But I'm in motion, and motion is the only prerequisite for progress.

Start before you're ready. Adjust as you go.
