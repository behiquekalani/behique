# LinkedIn Post 1389

You don't need more information. You need a decision.

Every course you're considering, every book in your queue, every podcast you'll listen to someday: none of it moves you forward until you make a decision and act on it.

Information without action is just entertainment.

I stopped adding things to my "learn later" list and started asking: what is the minimum I need to know to take the next step?

Usually it's less than I think.

The gap between where you are and where you want to be isn't filled with information. It's filled with action you haven't taken yet.

Here's the exercise: write down the next step in your most important project. Not the whole plan. Just the next step.

If you already know what it is, why haven't you done it?

That answer is more valuable than any course.

The bottleneck is rarely knowledge. It's almost always execution.

Pick the next step. Do it before you open another tab.

What is the one next step you've been avoiding?
