There's a question I ask anyone who tells me they're stuck:

"What's the smallest possible version of this you could test in the next 48 hours?"

Most people who are stuck aren't lacking information. They're waiting for conditions that won't arrive. More capital. More time. More certainty. More followers.

The minimum viable test breaks the waiting loop.

You don't need a finished course to find out if people will pay for what you know. You can teach it live first. You don't need a polished app to know if your solution works. You can do it manually first.

The goal of the first version is not to be good. It's to be real. Real gives you feedback. Imaginary gives you anxiety.

I've watched people spend months building products in their heads that would have failed in their first week on the market. The failure would have been a gift. It would have redirected them fast. But they never released it, so they never knew.

Release the smallest version. Today if possible. Tomorrow if not.

What's something you could test this week that you've been treating as a much bigger project?
