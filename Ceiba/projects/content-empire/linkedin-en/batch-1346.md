**Hook:** A framework I use every morning that takes 90 seconds and prevents 90% of wasted days.

Before I open anything, I ask three questions:

1. What is the one thing I need to ship today for this day to count?
2. What am I avoiding that I know I should do?
3. What am I doing today that isn't actually mine to do?

That last question is the one most people skip.

So much of what fills a workday is other people's urgency. Notifications designed by someone else's algorithm. Requests that feel important because someone asked loudly. Tasks that exist because they've always existed.

The 90-second audit strips all of that.

What's mine? What moves my actual goals? What's noise dressed up as work?

The answers aren't always comfortable. Sometimes I realize I've built a busy day around avoiding the one file I actually need to open.

Productivity isn't time management. It's honesty about what you're actually doing versus what you keep saying you'll do.

Three questions. Ninety seconds. Run it tomorrow and see what you find.

What's the thing you're avoiding that you already know you need to do?
