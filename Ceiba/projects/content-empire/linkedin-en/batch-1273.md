The best tool I use costs nothing.

It's a plain text file where I write every idea the moment it shows up. Not an app. Not a system. A file.

Ideas are slippery. The ones that feel obvious in the shower are gone by the time you reach your desk. The ones that seem too small to write down are sometimes the best ones.

I used to let ideas disappear because I assumed I'd remember them. I never did. I lost years of potential because I trusted a brain that was running 30 other processes at the same time.

Now the rule is simple: if it crosses my mind, it goes in the file. Immediately. No filtering, no editing, no judgment. Just capture.

Then once a week I go through it. Most ideas are garbage in the daylight. A few are worth developing. Occasionally there's one that changes the direction of something I'm working on.

The system is not the file. The system is the habit of not trusting your memory with things that matter.

Every product I've shipped started in a file like this. Every piece of content worth reading. Every decision that moved things forward.

Capture everything. The cost of missing a good idea is higher than the cost of writing down a bad one.
