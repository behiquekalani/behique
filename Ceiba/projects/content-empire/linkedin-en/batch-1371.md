# LinkedIn Post 1371

The single biggest difference between people who finish things and people who do not: they stop waiting to feel ready.

I have started 23 projects. Finished 11. The 12 I did not finish all died at the same point: somewhere between "this is exciting" and "this is hard and I am not sure it is working."

The gap is not motivation. Motivation runs out for everyone. The gap is commitment to a system that runs even when motivation disappears.

Here is what I do now:
- Set a ship date before I start anything
- Make the first version intentionally small enough to finish in 2 weeks
- Tell someone about it publicly on day one

That last one is the most uncomfortable. It is also the most effective.

The finish line moves when no one is watching. When someone is watching, it stays fixed.

You do not need more knowledge to start. You need a deadline and a witness.

If you are sitting on something half-built right now, what would it take to actually ship it in the next 14 days?

Tell me what it is in the comments. I will check back in two weeks.
