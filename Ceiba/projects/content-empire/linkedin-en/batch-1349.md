**Hook:** The version of you five years from now is not impressed by how busy you were. That version cares about what you built.

I keep a file called "Five Year Audit." One column: things I was busy with. One column: things I shipped.

The busy column is always long. The shipped column is what actually mattered.

Busyness is the default mode. It's socially rewarded. It signals effort and commitment. You can be legitimately exhausted from work and have nothing to show for it.

Shipping is uncomfortable. It requires finishing. It requires showing people something that might not land. It requires the vulnerability of saying "this is what I made."

The gap between busy builders and compounding builders is not talent. It's the willingness to close loops.

A closed loop is a file that ships. A published piece. A product live on a store. A system someone else is using.

Open loops consume mental energy without producing anything permanent.

Start auditing your loops. How many are open that should be closed? How many things are "in progress" that have been in progress for months?

Close one loop this week. One real thing. Then watch how that changes the next week.

What open loop have you been carrying that you could close this week?
