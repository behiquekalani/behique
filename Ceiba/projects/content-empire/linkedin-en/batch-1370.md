# LinkedIn Post 1370

Puerto Rico taught me something about resilience that I cannot find in any business book.

We have been through hurricanes, earthquakes, economic collapse, and federal neglect. The island keeps moving. Not because we are naive about the problems. Because we have no choice but to keep going.

That is the exact energy required to build something from scratch.

The entrepreneurs I admire most are not the ones who had perfect conditions. They are the ones who built in spite of impossible conditions. The ones who found a way when the map did not include one.

Coming from PR means I learned early that no one is coming to save you. The infrastructure will fail. The support will not arrive on time. The system was not designed with you in mind.

So you build your own.

I am not asking for sympathy. I am saying that adversity is not an excuse to stay small. It is the reason you have skills most people in comfortable environments never develop.

Your difficult background is not a handicap. It is a competitive advantage if you use it right.

What challenge from your background became a strength in business?
