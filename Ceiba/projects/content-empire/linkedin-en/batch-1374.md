# LinkedIn Post 1374

Nobody is going to give you permission to start.

Not your parents. Not your professor. Not your manager. Not the market.

You have been waiting for a signal that never comes. The signal is always going to be: wait until you are more ready. Wait until conditions improve. Wait until someone validates the idea.

I watched my dad work for someone else his entire life waiting for recognition that came in tiny doses, always just enough to keep him there but never enough to set him free.

I decided at 18 I was not doing that.

The cost of waiting is not visible the same way a failed launch is. A failed launch you can see and measure and recover from. Waiting costs you years and you never see the bill until you are much older and much further from the start.

Start the thing. It does not need to be ready. It just needs to exist.

The version you launch today, no matter how rough, will be better than the version you keep polishing in your head forever.

What is the thing you have been waiting to start? What would it take to do one concrete step today, not next week?
