# LinkedIn Post 1363

I am a computer engineering student building a six-figure business on the side.

Not because I am special. Because I chose a different constraint.

Most students try to separate school from business. They wait until they graduate. They think the credential is the permission slip.

I used school as a laboratory. Every class gave me a problem to solve. Every project was a product prototype. Every professor was a potential mentor or customer.

The students who will run companies in ten years are not the ones grinding only for GPA. They are the ones who are already shipping, already failing, already learning what no course teaches.

Here is the truth: your campus is one of the most underused resources you have. You have access to smart people, cheap experimentation, and enough structure to actually finish things.

Use it.

Build the thing during school. Fail fast while the stakes are low. The diploma is a credential. The business is leverage.

If you are a student building something right now, I want to hear about it. Drop it in the comments.
