# LinkedIn Post — batch-1414

The skill gap is smaller than you think. The consistency gap is enormous.

I've watched people with average skills outperform talented people consistently. The pattern is always the same.

Talented person: starts strong, slows down when it gets hard, stops when it stops being exciting.

Consistent person: starts slow, keeps going when it gets hard, shows up even when it's boring.

Six months in, the consistent person has 180 pieces of content. The talented person has 30, then went quiet.

Which one do you think the algorithm rewards? Which one do you think has the trust of an audience?

Skills can be learned. Anyone can get better at writing, designing, selling. Those are learnable with enough repetition.

Consistency is rarer. It requires you to keep going when there's no immediate feedback loop. When the post doesn't perform. When the product doesn't sell. When it feels like you're talking to no one.

The people who cross to the other side of that silence are the ones who build something real.

Consistency over talent, every time.

What does your consistency look like on paper right now? Posts per week, products shipped per month, emails sent. The numbers don't lie.
