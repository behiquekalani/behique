The $97 product and the $9.97 product take almost the same energy to build.

The price difference is not about materials. It's about positioning.

A template sold as "a template" is worth $9.

The same template sold as "the system that saved my client 10 hours a week and generated $3K in new revenue" is worth $97. Or more.

Value is not inherent in the thing. Value is understood by the buyer through the story you tell.

This is not manipulation. It's communication.

If you genuinely believe your product changes something for the person who uses it, it is your obligation to explain how. Clearly. Specifically. With evidence.

The market does not reward modest packaging of great work.

Stop underselling. Raise your prices. Rewrite your description to lead with the outcome, not the feature.

"12 Notion templates" is a feature list.

"The productivity system used to go from reactive to focused in 7 days" is a result.

Sell the result.

What's something you've been underselling that deserves a higher price and a better story?
