## Post 86-1

**Topic:** Content Compounding Effect — The Infrastructure Thesis

Most people treat content creation like a sprint. The people who win treat it like compound interest.

Here is what they understand that most do not:

Your first 30 posts are not the performance. They are the foundation. Every post after that builds on what came before, trains the algorithm, and stays in the index indefinitely. A post you wrote in month two can surface in month nine and become your best-performing piece.

This is why creators who stay consistent for 90 days outperform creators who go viral once and stop. The algorithm rewards predictability. Your audience rewards familiarity. And both rewards compound over time.

Three things that compound in content:
- Trust: each post reinforces your positioning
- Reach: older content keeps attracting new readers
- Monetization: authority converts to income at higher rates over time

The compounding threshold is real. Most creators quit 30 posts before they cross it.

## Post 86-2

**Topic:** Content Compounding Effect — The Body of Work Argument

Viral posts are events. A body of work is an asset.

The distinction matters more than most creators realize.

An event-driven content strategy means you are only as relevant as your last post. One weak week and your metrics drop. You are always starting from scratch, always chasing the next spike.

An asset-driven content strategy means you have a searchable, shareable library of thinking on a specific topic. People discover post 17 from eight months ago. Post 17 leads them to post 31. Post 31 leads them to your product page. That is a sales funnel you built once and never had to maintain.

The shift from event thinking to asset thinking is the single most important mental model change for long-term creators.

Start treating every post as a permanent brick in a structure. Not as a daily lottery ticket.

## Post 86-3

**Topic:** Content Compounding Effect — The 90-Day Framework

If I had to give a new creator one practical directive, it would be this: post consistently for 90 days before evaluating whether content creation is working.

Why 90 days?

Because 90 days is the minimum time required for three things to happen:
1. The algorithm develops a meaningful understanding of your content and audience
2. You produce enough volume for statistical patterns to emerge (what resonates, what does not)
3. Your audience develops an expectation of you, which is the beginning of trust

Most creators evaluate at day 14 or day 30. Both are too early. The data is too sparse. The algorithm has not indexed you properly. The audience barely knows you exist.

Commit to 90 days of consistent posting on one topic, one platform, one format. Then evaluate.

The results at day 90 will look nothing like the results at day 30.
