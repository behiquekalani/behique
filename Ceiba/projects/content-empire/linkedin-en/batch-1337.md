The best business model I've found for a solo builder: sell a solution to one painful, specific problem to one clearly defined person.

Not a general-purpose tool. Not a community. Not a course that covers everything.

One problem. One person. One clear outcome.

The reason this works is distribution. When your product is specific, you know exactly where your buyer is. You can find them. Speak to them. Solve exactly what they need without building something bloated.

The reason most builders resist this is scope. They want to help everyone. They're afraid of leaving money on the table by being too niche.

But niche is not a constraint. Niche is a targeting strategy. Once you own a specific problem for a specific person, you can expand. The audience is yours. The trust is built.

The mistake is trying to be broad before being deep. Deep comes first.

Define the most painful, specific problem your audience has. Build the cleanest solution to that problem. Sell it to the people who have that problem right now.

That's the entire playbook. Most of the complexity people add is noise.

What's the one specific problem you're uniquely positioned to solve?
