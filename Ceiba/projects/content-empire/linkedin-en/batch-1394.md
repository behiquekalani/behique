# LinkedIn Post 1394

The biggest thing holding most creators back isn't skill. It's publishing frequency.

You get better at writing by writing and publishing.
You get better at video by recording and posting.
You get better at building by shipping and iterating.

The feedback loop only works if you close it.

Creators who publish once a month and creators who publish every day are playing completely different games. The daily publisher has 12x the data, 12x the reps, 12x the feedback.

That gap compounds over years into a skill difference that looks like talent.

It's not talent. It's reps.

You don't need to write perfectly. You need to write enough that you figure out what works for your audience, your voice, your niche.

That only happens through volume.

Commit to a publishing cadence you can actually sustain. Not the aspirational one. The real one.

Then protect it like it's your most important meeting.

Because it is.

What's the publishing cadence you know you could actually sustain for the next 90 days?
