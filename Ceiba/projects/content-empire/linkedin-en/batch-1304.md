The best piece of writing advice I ever got wasn't about writing.

It was about reading.

A mentor told me to read like a thief. Not for enjoyment, not for information. To steal.

Find a sentence that stops you. Ask why it works. Find a structure that moves fast. Reverse-engineer it. Find a paragraph that makes you feel something. Dissect it until you understand the mechanism.

Most people read passively. They absorb the surface. They might remember a few ideas. But they don't come away knowing how to replicate the effect.

Reading actively changes what you produce. You start noticing when your own writing is vague because you've seen what precision looks like. You notice when your hook is weak because you've studied what makes a hook land.

The writers who improve fastest aren't the ones who write the most. They're the ones who study the craft with the same intensity they bring to producing it.

Pick one piece of writing this week, something you wish you'd written, and spend thirty minutes taking it apart. Structure, sentences, word choice, pacing. Understand every decision.

Then write something. You'll feel the difference.

Who are you reading right now that's making you better?
