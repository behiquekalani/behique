# LinkedIn Post — batch-1410

The uncomfortable truth about building an audience: most of your posts will be ignored.

That's not failure. That's how it works.

The creators who grow consistently aren't the ones who go viral once. They're the ones who show up 300 times while most of their posts get 12 views, and they keep going because they understand the game they're playing.

Audience building is not a sprint. It's the accumulation of trust over time.

Every post that doesn't hit big still does something:
- Someone reads it and remembers your name
- An algorithm notes your consistency
- You practice your voice and get sharper
- One person saves it and finds you months later

The sum of those "failed" posts is what builds the foundation for the one that breaks through.

Most people stop before that moment. They post 20 times, get low numbers, decide it isn't working, and disappear. Then they watch someone else blow up and think they got lucky.

They didn't get lucky. They just lasted longer.

Commit to the long game. Not six weeks. Two years. What you build in that time is yours forever.

Are you building for the next post or for the next two years?
