I grew up watching my family work jobs they didn't love so I could have options they didn't.

That's not a sad story. That's the foundation everything I build is sitting on.

When you understand who you're building for, the hard days are different. They don't disappear. But they have weight behind them instead of just friction.

A lot of builder content talks about freedom and laptops and exotic locations.

That stuff is real for some people. For me the motivation is simpler and more specific. I want to build things that work while I sleep so the people I love don't have to work until they break.

That's the goal. Everything else is logistics.

If you're building something right now, get clear on who it's for. Not just your customer. The real answer. The person whose name you'd put on it if you could.

That clarity will carry you through the days when the algorithm is quiet and the sales are slow and the doubt is loud.

Build for someone specific.

Who are you building for?
