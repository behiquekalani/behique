I almost quit three times in the first year of building for myself.

Not because the work was too hard. Because the timeline was nothing like I had imagined.

I had consumed enough creator success stories to have a completely distorted picture of what early traction looked like. Six months in, with almost nothing to show for it in terms of revenue, I was convinced I had made a mistake.

What kept me going was not a pep talk or a mindset shift. It was getting specific about what I was actually measuring. Revenue was one metric. But I was also building skills I did not have before. I was developing an understanding of what my audience cared about. I was learning how to communicate clearly. I was creating infrastructure that would still be there when the revenue started moving.

None of those things appeared in my bank account. But they were real assets. They just had a longer conversion timeline than I had expected.

The honest version of creator business timelines is this: most people who succeed at this took two to four years before it became financially meaningful. Most people stop after six months because nothing has happened yet.

The gap between those two numbers is where the competition disappears.

If you are still in the early, ugly, nothing-is-working phase, you are not behind. You are exactly where almost everyone who eventually makes it has been.

Stay in it.
