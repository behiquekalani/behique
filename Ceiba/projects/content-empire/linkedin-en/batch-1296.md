Everyone who builds something eventually has to answer this question: Am I building this because I want to, or because I think I should?

The difference matters more than it seems.

The things you build because you want to have a different energy in them. The ideas come more easily. The problems feel like puzzles instead of obstacles. When you hit a wall, you find another route because you are genuinely curious about what is on the other side.

The things you build because you think you should tend to stall. Not dramatically, but slowly. You work on them less. You delay decisions. You tell yourself it is because you are busy, but if you are honest, you know the real answer is that your interest never fully committed.

I have started projects in both categories. The ones I forced myself to build because they seemed smart or profitable or what someone like me was supposed to do almost all died without shipping. The ones I built because I was genuinely trying to solve something for myself, or could not stop thinking about, almost all shipped regardless of how hard they were to finish.

You cannot fake the underlying interest. The market picks up on it eventually. Your own work ethic certainly picks up on it immediately.

Build toward what you are actually drawn to. The commercial angle can be figured out from there. Going the other direction rarely works.

What is one project you are working on that you are not really excited about?
