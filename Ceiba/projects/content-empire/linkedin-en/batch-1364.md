# LinkedIn Post 1364

The best investment I made this year cost $27.

It was a digital product from a creator I had been following for six months. It taught me a framework I applied the next day. The result was worth 100 times the price.

I think about this whenever someone tells me their audience will not pay for information.

They are wrong about the reason. People pay for organized, applied thinking that saves them time. They do not pay for raw information. Information is free everywhere. What is scarce is clarity.

The creator who packaged that framework understood this. They did not give me facts. They gave me a system. A sequence. A way to think about a problem I had been approaching backwards.

That is the product. That is what sells.

If you are sitting on knowledge and wondering why no one is buying, ask yourself honestly: are you selling information or are you selling a transformation?

There is a wide gap between those two things.
