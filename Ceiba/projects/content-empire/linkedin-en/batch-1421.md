I spent 3 years building skills nobody asked for.

Data pipelines. Automation scripts. AI workflows nobody at my university was talking about.

My professors thought I was wasting time.

Here's what I learned: the market doesn't reward what you studied. It rewards what you built while everyone else was just studying.

The gap between a student who graduates and one who ships is not GPA. It's not connections either.

It's proof of work.

Every project you complete teaches you something a classroom cannot. How to handle ambiguity. How to finish when motivation disappears. How to communicate what you built so someone else can understand it.

That's the real curriculum.

If you're a student right now, stop waiting for permission to build. Start building systems, tools, content, businesses. The degree is a floor, not a ceiling.

The ceiling is yours to set.
