The hardest thing about building in public is not the criticism. It's the silence.

You post something you spent real time on. Something honest. Something you actually believe. And it gets four likes, two of which are from people who like everything.

Most people read that silence as confirmation they're not good enough and stop.

The builders who make it read it differently. They see silence as data: either the framing was wrong, the platform doesn't match the audience, or the timing was off.

Then they adjust and post again.

The compounding effect of consistent content is real. But it only works if you don't quit during the silent phase. And the silent phase can last months.

What separates the builders who push through from the ones who don't is not talent. It's the story they tell about silence.

If silence means failure, you quit. If silence means iteration, you keep going.

The people whose content eventually resonates are not the ones who were born knowing what to say. They're the ones who posted consistently long enough to figure it out.

Keep posting. The silence won't last forever.
