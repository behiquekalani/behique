I used to plan my week on Sunday night and feel productive before Monday even started.

By Wednesday, the plan was gone. Not because I was lazy. Because the plan didn't account for how I actually think.

Most productivity systems are designed for linear minds. Step 1, step 2, step 3. But most builders don't work that way. They work in bursts. They follow energy. They circle back.

So I stopped fighting my nature and started building systems that fit it.

The shift was simple: instead of a rigid schedule, I built a decision tree. A set of trigger conditions that tell me what to work on based on energy, time, and priority.

High energy + 2 hours = deep build work.
Low energy + 30 minutes = admin, replies, review.
Blocked on one thing = switch context, come back.

This isn't an excuse to avoid hard work. It's a map that makes sure hard work actually happens.

You don't need more discipline. You need a system that works with your brain, not against it.

If you're building something on the side, that system matters more than the idea itself.

What does your decision tree look like?
