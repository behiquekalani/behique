Writing online is the highest-leverage thing a builder can do. Most builders don't do it.

Here's what writing does that no other marketing channel does: it proves you can think.

Anyone can post an image. Anyone can record a video. But a post that takes a clear position, backs it with a real story, and lands on a genuine insight tells the reader something important: this person has thought about this.

That matters to buyers. It matters to clients. It matters to collaborators who want to work with someone trustworthy.

I've seen people with smaller followings close bigger deals because their writing made them look like a serious thinker. And I've seen people with massive audiences fail to convert because their content was noise.

The size of your audience is less important than the quality of your thinking.

Writing online is how you put your thinking in a place where the right people can find it.

Start with one honest observation per week. Something you've noticed that others haven't said yet. Ship it.

It doesn't have to be perfect. It has to be true.

What's one thing you've learned this month that would help someone else?
