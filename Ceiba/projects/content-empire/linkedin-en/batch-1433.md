If AI is going to replace your job, it won't happen all at once.

It'll happen task by task.

The spreadsheet analysis you do manually. The first draft you always write from scratch. The research you spend three hours on. The scheduling, the formatting, the categorizing.

Each of those tasks is already being handed to AI by someone in your industry.

The question is not "will AI change my work?" The question is "which parts of my work am I building identity around that a machine can already do better?"

That question is uncomfortable. It's supposed to be.

The builders who win in this era are the ones who offload the mechanical so they can go deeper on the human. Judgment. Context. Taste. Relationships. The things that are genuinely hard to automate.

You don't fight the wave. You understand where it's going and get there first.

Learn what AI can do. Use it. Build your career on what it can't.

What's one part of your job that you'd be relieved to hand off to a good tool?
