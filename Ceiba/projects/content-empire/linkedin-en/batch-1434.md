I've made more money from things I didn't plan than things I planned perfectly.

Planning is necessary. Over-planning is avoidance with a respectable disguise.

The plan gives you a direction. It does not give you certainty. Nothing does.

The people who ship fast make something, put it in front of real humans, watch what happens, and adjust. They're not reckless. They're efficient with uncertainty.

The people who plan forever are optimizing for a confidence they will never reach because certainty doesn't exist until you ship.

Here's the real cost of over-planning: every week you spend refining a plan is a week you don't have market feedback. And market feedback is the only data that actually matters.

Your plan is a hypothesis. The world is the experiment.

Ship faster. Learn faster. Adjust. Repeat.

The plan that works is rarely the first one. But you have to ship the first one to get to the one that works.

What's something you've been planning for too long that needs to just ship?
