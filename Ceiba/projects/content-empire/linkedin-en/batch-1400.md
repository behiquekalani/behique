# LinkedIn Post 1400

Every builder I know who quit had one thing in common.

They stopped seeing the connection between their daily actions and their long-term vision.

The work felt random. The progress was invisible. The goal seemed too far away.

So they stopped.

This is why vision alone isn't enough. You need a bridge between today's work and the future you're building toward.

That bridge is your weekly review.

Every Sunday I ask three questions:
1. What did I actually complete this week?
2. Did what I did this week move me closer to where I want to be in 90 days?
3. What is the one thing that needs to happen next week no matter what?

Three questions. Thirty minutes. Every week.

This is what keeps the daily work from feeling meaningless.

You can see the trajectory. You can see the progress. You can correct course before you drift too far.

The builders who last aren't more talented. They're more connected to their own direction.

Build the bridge. Review your progress. Stay in the game.

What does your weekly review look like right now?
