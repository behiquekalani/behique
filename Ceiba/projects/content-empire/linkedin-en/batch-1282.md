My first digital product made $0 in the first three weeks.

I had spent six days building it. The landing page was clean. The product was solid. I posted it twice. Nothing.

I told myself the market was wrong. Too saturated. Wrong timing. Wrong platform. The whole story a creator tells themselves when they don't want to look at the real problem.

The real problem was that I had built the product first and found the audience second. Or rather, I had not found an audience at all. I just posted into the void and expected it to respond.

Everything changed when I reversed the order. I started writing about problems I had solved for myself. Not "here is a product," but "here is what I used to struggle with and here is how I think about it now." People started responding. Asking questions. Sharing their own versions of the same problem.

By the time I had something to sell, there were people who already trusted that I understood their situation.

The product was almost secondary. The relationship was the thing.

Most creators skip the relationship phase because it takes longer and you cannot measure it easily. But it is the only part that makes the sale feel natural instead of forced.

Build the relationship before you build the product. Or at minimum, build them at the same time.

What relationship are you building right now, separate from anything you are trying to sell?
