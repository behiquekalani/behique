I automated 80% of my content workflow in a weekend.

Not because I'm a genius. Because I was tired of doing the same thing manually 40 times.

Here's the honest version of that story:

Friday night I mapped out every step I repeated when producing content. Writing, formatting, saving, categorizing, scheduling. Most of it was mechanical. None of it required creativity.

Saturday I built scripts to handle the mechanical parts.

Sunday I tested, broke everything, fixed it, and ran the first real batch.

Monday I had 20 pieces of content staged and ready. It took me the same time it usually took to make 3.

The tools aren't the point. The point is that most "work" is not actually work. It's process. And process can be designed, optimized, or eliminated.

If you're doing the same thing more than three times a week, you should be asking: can this be systematized?

Usually the answer is yes.

What's one task you keep doing manually that probably shouldn't be? Serious question.
