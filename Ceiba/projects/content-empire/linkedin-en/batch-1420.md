# LinkedIn Post — batch-1420

Most people are one year of consistent output away from a completely different life.

Not five years. One year.

The math isn't complicated. 52 weeks of showing up, publishing, refining your product, building your list, and shipping even when it feels pointless.

By month three, your voice gets sharper.
By month six, you have enough content that people start finding you.
By month twelve, the compounding kicks in and old work starts pulling in new people every day.

None of this requires viral moments. None of it requires a big break. It requires the boring discipline of doing the same unsexy things long enough for the results to catch up to the work.

The problem is that a year feels like forever when you're in month two with no results yet. But a year from now you'll be a year older either way. The question is whether you'll have built something or just watched another twelve months pass.

This is the post I would have needed to read two years ago. Maybe it's the one you need today.

One year. Consistent. Start now.

What would your content and product business look like if you committed to one full year of unbroken output starting today?
