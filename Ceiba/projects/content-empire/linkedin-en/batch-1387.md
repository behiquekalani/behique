# LinkedIn Post 1387

Most people build systems for their best days.

The problem is that your worst days are the ones that determine whether you actually succeed.

On your best day, you'll do the work regardless. The systems don't matter.

On your worst day, low energy, bad news, no motivation, that's when the system is the only thing standing between you and quitting.

Good systems are designed for bad days.

This means:
- The habit is so small you can do it sick. Write one paragraph. Send one email. Do five minutes.
- The environment is set up to reduce friction. Notebook on the desk. Tools already open. No decisions required.
- The minimum is non-negotiable. The maximum is optional.

When I build a new habit, I ask: what's the smallest possible version of this I could do on the day everything goes wrong?

That's the actual habit. Everything else is extra.

Protect your bad day version. That's what keeps you in the game long enough to win.

What does your system look like on your worst day?
