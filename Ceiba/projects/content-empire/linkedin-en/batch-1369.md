# LinkedIn Post 1369

The content that grows your audience is not the content you think is your best.

I spent two days on a detailed essay about AI automation systems. 400 views.

I wrote a three-paragraph observation about something I noticed while making coffee. 14,000 views.

I have tested this pattern across channels and it holds every time. The content that feels small and obvious to you is the content that hits.

Here is why: you are too close to your own expertise. What seems basic to you is genuinely useful to someone three steps behind you. The "obvious" thing you know is a treasure to someone who does not know it yet.

Stop saving your best observations for the big post. Put them out. The one you almost did not share because "it is too simple" is usually the one that lands.

The mechanism is simple: relatable beats sophisticated. Specific beats comprehensive. One clear idea beats ten connected ideas.

Your next viral post is probably already written in your head. You just talked yourself out of posting it.

Post it.

What is the most obvious thing in your field that nobody seems to say out loud?
