Here's the truth about consistency that nobody wants to hear:

Motivation is not required.

You will not want to write the post. You will not want to record the video. You will not want to open the laptop on the days when nothing is working and everything feels hard.

Consistency is not about waiting until you want to. It's about building a habit so automatic that wanting has nothing to do with it.

You brush your teeth without wanting to. You put on a seatbelt without motivating yourself. These things happen because they are built into the structure of your day.

Your creative work needs that same kind of structure.

Not inspiration. Infrastructure.

A time. A place. A minimum viable output. Something small enough to start even on the worst day.

On bad days you write 50 words. On good days you write 500. Both days count.

The compound effect of showing up 365 times a year is not visible in any single week. It's visible after three years.

Set the habit. Remove the decision. Show up whether you feel it or not.

The feeling follows the action. Not the other way around.

What's one creative habit you want to build but haven't systematized yet?
