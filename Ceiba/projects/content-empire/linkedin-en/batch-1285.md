The hardest part of going solo is not the workload.

It is the absence of external validation for months at a time.

When you work inside a company, feedback is built into the structure. Your manager tells you how you are doing. Your paycheck arrives on a schedule. There are metrics dashboards and quarterly reviews and colleagues who react to your contributions. The feedback loop is continuous even when it is imperfect.

When you build for yourself, that loop goes quiet. You post and hear nothing. You ship and the download counter reads zero. You send the email and get no reply. The silence does not mean the work is bad. It usually just means you have not found the right people yet. But that distinction is very hard to hold onto at 11pm when you are questioning whether any of this was a reasonable idea.

I have learned to manufacture my own validation signals. Not fake ones, but real ones I can control. Did I write today? Did I ship something this week? Did I learn one thing I did not know before? Did I have one honest conversation with someone building something similar?

These are not metrics of success. They are proof of motion. And proof of motion is sometimes the only thing that keeps you in the game long enough for success to become possible.

If you are in the silent phase right now, it is not evidence that you should stop. It is evidence that you are building.

Keep building.
