I've watched smart people fail at the same thing repeatedly and I've finally figured out why.

It's not bad luck. It's the refusal to do the boring work that sits between the idea and the result.

The idea phase is exciting. You see the potential. You feel the energy. You can picture it working.

Then comes the part where you have to build landing pages, write copy that actually converts, answer support emails, fix bugs that only happen to one user, and redo things that didn't work the first time.

That's where most smart people exit. Because they're used to being rewarded for being smart, and this work doesn't care how smart you are. It just needs to get done.

Building a business is not an intelligence test. It's an endurance test with skill requirements.

The skill that matters most is not coding or design or writing. It's the ability to keep doing unglamorous work when the excitement has worn off and nothing is giving you positive feedback yet.

That skill can be developed. But you have to want to develop it.

What's the boring task you've been avoiding that would move things forward?
