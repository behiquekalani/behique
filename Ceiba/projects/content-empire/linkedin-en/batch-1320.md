The question I ask myself every Sunday that changed how I work:

"Did I do my most important work, or did I do the most visible work?"

These are often different things.

Most visible: posting, replying, engaging, attending calls, being active in communities. These create the feeling of productivity. They produce likes and comments. They're easy to justify.

Most important: building the thing, writing the piece, finishing the system, having the conversation that's scary to have. This work is often private. It doesn't show up on metrics. It compounds slowly.

There's a pull toward visible work that's hard to resist because visible work gives you immediate feedback. You post and something happens. Important work often goes quiet for weeks before you see anything from it.

The best use of your week is usually doing more of the second category than feels comfortable, and less of the first category than feels natural.

Ask yourself: if no one could see what I was doing this week, what would I choose to do?

That answer points at your most important work.

Do more of that.

What's something important you've been deferring in favor of visible activity?
