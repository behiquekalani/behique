**Hook:** The creator who taught me the most about pricing never talked about pricing. She talked about belief.

She said: "People don't pay what the thing is worth. They pay what they believe they'll get from it."

That changed how I think about every number I put on a product.

Price is not about the hours you put in. It's not about what competitors charge. It's about what the buyer believes their outcome will be worth.

A $19 template feels expensive if someone doesn't believe it will help them. A $197 system feels like a deal if someone genuinely believes it will save them weeks.

The price resistance you face is almost never about the number. It's about the gap between what you believe about your product and what the buyer currently believes.

Your job isn't to justify the price. It's to build enough belief that the price becomes obvious.

That's done with case studies, specificity, demonstrated results, and honest communication about what happens after the purchase.

Stop negotiating on price. Start investing in belief.

What could you add to your product page that would make the price feel small compared to the outcome?
