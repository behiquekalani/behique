Three months ago I had 102 products built and zero listed for sale.

I'm going to tell you why, because it's the same reason most builders stall before launch.

It wasn't technical. I knew how to list a product. It wasn't confidence in quality. The products were solid.

It was the fear that shipping it made it real. And if it's real, it can fail. And if it fails, the story I told myself about being a builder gets tested.

That's the real blocker. Not the platform. Not the pricing page. Not the copy.

The fear that shipping closes the loop on whether you're actually good enough.

So people keep building. Because building feels productive without exposing you to rejection.

I caught myself doing this. And calling it out is the only thing that breaks the loop.

The products are real whether they're listed or not. The only thing that changes when you list them is that they have a chance to help someone and make money.

Nothing loses by shipping. Everything loses by staying in draft mode forever.

What have you been sitting on that needs to go live this week?
