# LinkedIn Post — batch-1406

I used to think I needed to build the perfect product before selling it.

I was wrong. That thinking cost me months.

Here's what actually works: sell first, build second.

Not in a dishonest way. In a smart way. Validate before you invest weeks building something nobody wants.

Create a clear description of what the product will do. Put a price on it. Tell ten people. See if any of them want to pay.

If no one pulls out a wallet, you haven't found product-market fit. You've just found an idea that sounds good.

If someone pays, you have a customer before you have a product. Now build exactly what they told you they needed.

This approach changes everything:

- You stop guessing what people want
- You eliminate the risk of building in isolation
- You have a deadline and a real reason to finish

Most digital products fail not because they're bad. They fail because nobody wanted them before they were built, and nobody wanted them after either.

The market will tell you the truth. You just have to ask it before you spend 200 hours on something.

What product idea are you sitting on that you could test this week?
