The reason most content doesn't convert has nothing to do with quality. It's about specificity.

Generic content gets consumed and forgotten. Specific content gets saved and acted on.

"Here's how to be more productive" is forgettable. "Here's the exact trigger-based system I use when I have two hours and low motivation" is bookmarked.

The specificity signals to the reader: this person has actually been in my situation. This isn't recycled advice. This is real.

That trust is what converts. Not production value. Not follower count. Not posting frequency.

The trap is writing for the largest possible audience. When you write for everyone, the person with the exact problem you can solve doesn't recognize themselves in your content.

Write for the person in the specific situation you know well. Make it so specific they feel like you're describing their exact week.

That person will share it with the five people in their life who have the same problem. That's how content grows.

Start with one person in one specific situation. Go deep before going broad.

What's the most specific version of the problem your content is trying to solve?
