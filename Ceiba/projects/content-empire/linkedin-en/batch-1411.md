# LinkedIn Post — batch-1411

One email list beats 10,000 followers on any social platform.

Strong statement. Here's why it's true.

You don't own your social following. The platform does. They change the algorithm and your reach drops 70% overnight. That's happened to creators over and over. TikTok gets banned. Instagram buries your posts. X tanks your impressions.

Your email list is yours. No algorithm between you and your readers. No platform that can decide tomorrow you're less relevant.

I started building an email list before I had anything to sell. That felt backwards at first. But by the time I had a product ready, I had people who'd been reading my thinking for months. The first launch wasn't cold. It was warm.

Building an email list in 2026:
- Give away something genuinely useful for free
- Write weekly with one real insight, not a newsletter that reads like a company memo
- Build trust before you pitch anything

The list compounds. Early subscribers refer others. Content you wrote two years ago still brings people in. The asset grows while you sleep.

Social media is the billboard. Email is the relationship.

Do you have an email list? If not, what would it take to start one this week?
