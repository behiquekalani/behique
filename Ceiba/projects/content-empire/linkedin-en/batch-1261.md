I used to think productivity meant doing more.

So I'd stack tools on top of tools. Notion for tasks. Linear for projects. A separate calendar. A habit tracker. A time blocker. A focus timer.

And I was constantly busy managing the system instead of doing the work.

The turning point came when I started working on my first digital product. I had no time for complexity. I had a laptop, one folder, and a deadline I gave myself.

That product made money. The ones I built while managing elaborate systems mostly didn't.

There's a version of "being organized" that is just procrastination with better aesthetics. You feel productive. You're optimizing workflows. But nothing ships.

The real metric isn't how clean your system is. It's how often something leaves your brain and reaches the world.

One doc. One task. One hour. That's the only system that has never failed me.

Every tool I use now has to justify itself by making me ship faster, not feel more organized.

Simplicity is a competitive advantage most people overlook because it doesn't feel like work.
