Pricing is the most misunderstood part of selling digital products.

Most first-time builders underprice because they're afraid of rejection. They think: lower price = more buyers = more proof that people want it.

But low pricing doesn't reduce rejection. It changes the type of buyer you attract.

A $7 product attracts browsers. A $47 product attracts buyers who expect results. A $97 product attracts people who are serious about solving the problem.

Higher-ticket buyers complain less, consume the product more thoroughly, and get better results. Better results mean better testimonials. Better testimonials improve conversion for future buyers.

Low-price products create a race to zero. You need volume to make it work, and volume requires an audience most new builders don't have.

The better strategy: charge more, deliver more, take care of your buyers, and let the results speak.

You can always lower your price. You almost never successfully raise it on the same product with the same audience.

Start at a price that respects the value you deliver. Then deliver on that respect.

What price does your product actually deserve?
