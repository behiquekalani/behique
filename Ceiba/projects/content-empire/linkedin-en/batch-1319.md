I track one metric above all others in my business.

Not followers. Not revenue. Not email list size.

Conversations.

How many real back-and-forth conversations did I have this week with someone in my audience?

This sounds soft until you understand what it produces. Every time I've made a product that sold well, I can trace it to a conversation I had weeks or months before launch. Someone told me exactly what they needed. I built it. Then I described it back to them in their own words.

Every failed product I've made, I can trace to isolation. I thought I knew what people needed. I didn't ask. I was wrong.

Conversations are your market research, your customer development, your product validation, and your marketing copy, all at once. And most creators skip them because they feel less scalable than a post that might go viral.

Viral posts don't tell you what to build. Conversations do.

Send three messages this week to people in your audience. Ask one question: "What's the thing you're stuck on right now that you wish you had a clear answer to?"

Then listen.

What's a conversation that's changed the direction of your work?
