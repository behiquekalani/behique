# LinkedIn Post — batch-1417

The hardest part of building something new isn't the technical work.

It's doing the work when nothing has validated it yet.

Before the first sale, before the first follower who wasn't a friend, before anyone in your life takes it seriously, you're building in a void. No feedback. No momentum. No proof it will ever amount to anything.

That phase breaks most people. Not because the work is impossible. Because the silence is unbearable.

What I've learned from staying in it: the silence is the filter. It's the thing that separates the people who will build something real from the people who were only willing to try if success was guaranteed first.

No successful creator got a guarantee before they started. They decided to keep going in the silence and let the work accumulate until it was impossible to ignore.

If you're in that phase right now, sitting with posts that aren't getting traction, products that haven't sold yet, an audience that's still tiny, I want you to know that's not a sign it isn't working.

It's a sign you're in the part most people quit.

Keep building.
