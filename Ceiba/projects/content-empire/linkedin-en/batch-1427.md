The most underrated career skill in 2026 is not coding.

It's not prompt engineering either.

It's the ability to think in systems.

A systems thinker doesn't just solve the problem in front of them. They ask: why does this problem keep happening? What created the conditions for it? What would have to be true for it to stop happening permanently?

That kind of thinking is rare. Most people solve symptoms. Systems thinkers fix roots.

In practice this looks like:

- Not just fixing the bug, but asking why the bug was able to exist
- Not just writing the email, but designing the process that makes future emails unnecessary
- Not just completing the task, but asking whether the task should exist at all

AI is making execution faster. The people who win in that world are the ones who decide what to execute and why.

That's a thinking problem, not a technical one.

Systems thinking is not something you're born with. It's trained. Start by asking "why" one more time than feels necessary.

What problem in your work keeps coming back that probably has a systemic root?
