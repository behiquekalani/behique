My most profitable insight from two years of building online:

Price is rarely the objection. Clarity is.

When someone says "it's too expensive," they usually mean "I'm not sure enough about the outcome to justify this."

Remove the uncertainty and the price objection disappears.

Here's how to remove uncertainty:
1. Show them exactly what they'll get, step by step
2. Let them see social proof from someone who looked like them before buying
3. Reduce their risk with a guarantee
4. Make the transformation concrete and specific

A $97 product that's clearly described and delivers a specific result will outsell a $27 product that's vague about what it does.

People make value judgments, not price judgments. If the value is clear, the math takes care of itself.

I raised prices on two products last year. Sales went up. Why? Because a higher price, paired with better framing, actually increased perceived value and reduced doubt.

The market is not looking for cheap. It's looking for certain.

What would you need to change about how you present your product to make the outcome feel inevitable?
