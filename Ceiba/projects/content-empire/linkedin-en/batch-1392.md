# LinkedIn Post 1392

I built my first digital product in a weekend.

It wasn't pretty. The design was basic. The copy was rough.

It sold.

Because people don't buy polished. They buy solutions.

Here's what I've learned from building and selling digital products:

The spec that matters most is specificity. Vague products for general audiences sell poorly. Specific solutions for specific people sell.

"Productivity system" is vague. "Morning routine for founders who have ADHD and struggle with task initiation" is specific.

Same underlying content. Completely different sales conversion.

The more specific you get, the smaller the audience feels but the higher the purchase rate becomes. Because the right person reads your description and thinks "this is exactly for me."

That feeling is what sells.

Stop trying to appeal to everyone. Start solving one real problem for one real person and write for that person.

Then let distribution do its job.
