I spent two years building an audience before I understood what I was actually selling.

Not products. Not services. Trust.

Every post, every email, every piece of content is a small transaction. You give someone something useful. They give you a few minutes of their day. Do that consistently and something compounds.

The mistake I see most creators make is treating content like advertising instead of relationship-building. They post when they have something to sell. Go quiet when they don't.

That's not how trust works.

Trust is built in the quiet periods. When you show up not because you need something but because you have something to give. When you write the honest post instead of the polished one. When you admit the thing that isn't working instead of only sharing the wins.

The creators with loyal audiences are not the ones with the best strategies. They're the ones people feel like they actually know.

Closeness is the real product. Everything else is just the delivery mechanism.

Write like you're talking to one person. Ship consistently. Be honest more than you're impressive.

The audience that trusts you will follow you anywhere. The audience that just follows you will leave when someone shinier shows up.
