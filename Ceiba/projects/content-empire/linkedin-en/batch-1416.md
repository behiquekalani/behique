# LinkedIn Post — batch-1416

Four years ago I didn't know what a funnel was. Now my products sell while I'm in class.

The gap between those two points wasn't talent or connections. It was a series of decisions to learn one thing at a time and apply it before moving to the next.

Most people consume endlessly and apply nothing. They watch ten YouTube videos about email marketing without ever writing their first email. They read three books about copywriting without ever writing one sales page.

Learning without doing is entertainment, not education.

The framework that actually worked for me:

1. Learn one concept
2. Apply it immediately, imperfectly
3. See what the market says
4. Adjust based on reality, not theory
5. Then learn the next thing

This loop, repeated enough times, produces skill. Real skill. The kind that generates income, not the kind that wins trivia nights.

You don't need a 12-week course before your first product. You need to build the first version, ship it, and let the feedback teach you what no course can.

What's one thing you've been learning about without applying yet?
