# LinkedIn Post 1365

Nobody tells you that building in public means people will watch you fail.

I posted my first revenue number. It was small. Embarrassingly small for the time I had put in. The comments were kind. I was still ashamed.

But I kept posting. Week after week. The number grew. The shame turned into something else.

Here is what building in public actually does: it creates accountability you cannot fake. When you tell an audience you are building something, quitting becomes public. Progress becomes visible. You are suddenly responsible to more than just yourself.

For someone with ADHD, that external structure is not a nice-to-have. It is a requirement for follow-through.

The fear of public failure pushed me harder than any motivational content I have ever consumed. Real stakes. Real timeline. Real eyes watching.

If you are struggling to stay consistent on something, consider making it visible. Not for the likes. For the pressure.

The internet is a terrible place to hide from accountability. Use that.

What are you building right now that you have not told anyone about yet?
