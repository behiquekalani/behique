I asked a mentor once what separated the creators who made it from the ones who did not.

He did not say talent. He did not say timing. He said tolerance for the awkward middle.

Every creator path has one. It is the phase after you have committed but before anything is working. You have told people you are doing this. You have started shipping. Nothing has clicked yet. You are too invested to quit without real pain, but not successful enough to have evidence that continuing is the right call.

Most people exit during the awkward middle. Not in a dramatic way. They just quietly shift focus. They start a new project. They take on more consulting work. They tell themselves they are pivoting when they are actually retreating.

I have been in the awkward middle twice. Both times I came close to retreating. What stopped me the first time was a spreadsheet where I tracked every small signal of progress that was not revenue. New email subscribers. A conversation with someone who said a post resonated. A piece of content that outperformed my average. None of it was income. All of it was evidence that momentum was building in a direction.

The awkward middle is not permanent. But you have to stay in it long enough to find out.

Map your progress with metrics that are honest but not just about money. That map might be the only thing that gets you to the other side.

Where are you in your current awkward middle?
