# LinkedIn Post 1384

The most valuable skill nobody teaches in school: learning how to learn.

Not memorizing. Not test-taking. Actually acquiring a skill fast enough to use it.

I've picked up skills in web dev, AI, copywriting, design, and finance in the past two years outside of any formal curriculum.

Here's the method I keep coming back to:

1. Find the 20% of knowledge that produces 80% of results. Skip the rest until you need it.
2. Build something ugly with it immediately. Ugly ships. Perfect doesn't.
3. Teach it to someone else within a week. Teaching reveals every gap.
4. Come back to the theory only when you hit a wall you can't get around.

The traditional model is: learn, then do. That's backwards.

The faster model is: do a little, learn from the doing, repeat.

Every skill I actually use, I learned by using it first.

Every skill that's still in a browser tab I never closed, I learned by reading about it.

Close the tab. Open a blank file. Start building.

What skill have you been "learning" for more than three months without shipping anything with it?
