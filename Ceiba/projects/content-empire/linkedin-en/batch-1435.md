The loneliest part of building is not the failure.

It's the in-between.

Failure is sharp. You feel it, you process it, you move. The in-between is slower. You're not winning. You're not losing. You're just working in a direction you can't yet confirm is right.

Most people quit in the in-between. Not because it's painful, but because it's quiet.

Nobody is cheering. The metrics are flat. The validation you need doesn't come until after you've already decided to keep going.

That's the design. The gate is patience under ambiguity.

The builders who make it through are not the most talented. They're the ones who can tolerate uncertainty longer than anyone else. Who can keep executing when the feedback loop is broken.

If you're in the in-between right now, I want you to know: this is exactly where the outcome is being decided.

Not in the launch. Not in the moment it works.

Right now. In the quiet.

Keep going.

What helped you most when you were in the in-between?
