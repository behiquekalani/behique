# LinkedIn Post 1383

Nobody told me that building a business and finishing a degree at the same time would feel like running two operating systems on one machine.

Constant switching. Constant lag.

The solution wasn't working harder. It was building better defaults.

A default is a decision you make once so you don't have to make it again.

Default writing time: 6am, before class, before email.
Default review time: Sunday night, 30 minutes, no exceptions.
Default shutdown trigger: 9pm, no screens, no tasks.

You stop negotiating with yourself every day and start executing the plan you already made.

The people who seem superhuman aren't doing more. They've just removed more decisions from their daily loop.

Decision fatigue is real. Every choice costs something. Spend that energy on the work that matters.

Build your defaults. Protect them. Watch what happens over 90 days.

What's one default you could set this week that would change the rest of your year?
