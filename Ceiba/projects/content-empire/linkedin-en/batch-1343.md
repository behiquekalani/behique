**Hook:** The friend who changed how I think about time wasn't a productivity guru. He was a carpenter.

He told me: "I don't work hard. I work irreversibly."

That word stuck. Irreversibly.

Every hour he spent on a joint was an hour that couldn't be undone. The wood was cut. The piece was shaped. Something permanent existed that didn't exist before.

Most knowledge workers, including me at 22, spend hours that are completely reversible. We write notes we never revisit. We plan projects we never start. We draft ideas that stay in drafts.

Nothing irreversible happens.

The compounding in a career doesn't come from working harder. It comes from creating more things that can't be taken back. Published content. Shipped code. Filed systems. Products that exist.

Every irreversible action is a brick. Every reversible one is just carrying a brick somewhere and setting it down.

What did you do this week that can't be undone? That's your real output.

Stack enough of those and the compound effect becomes unavoidable.

What's one thing you've been doing that's completely reversible when it should be shipped?
