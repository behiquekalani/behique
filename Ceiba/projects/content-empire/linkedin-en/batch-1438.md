I stopped asking "what should I build next?" and started asking "who is not being served well right now?"

That one shift changed everything.

The first question is about me. The second is about the market.

When you build from the first question, you make what excites you. Sometimes that matches what people need. Often it doesn't.

When you build from the second question, you start with evidence. You look at complaints. Reviews. Forum posts. The things people say they wish existed. The workarounds they've invented because no clean solution exists.

Then you build the clean solution.

This is not a new idea. It's just one most builders resist because it feels less creative. It isn't. Constraints are creative fuel.

The best products I've seen weren't born from inspiration. They were born from frustration. Someone was tired of a problem and built the thing that solved it.

Find the frustration. Build the thing.

What's a problem you've run into recently that has no good solution yet?
