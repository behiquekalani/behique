Writing online changed how I think more than anything else I've done.

Not because the audience teaches you. Because the act of writing forces clarity.

You cannot write vaguely about something you understand well. And you cannot hide how little you know about something when you have to write a clear sentence about it.

Writing is a thinking tool before it is a communication tool.

When I write a post, I'm not documenting what I already know. I'm discovering what I actually believe. The gaps show up fast. The contradictions become visible. The fuzzy ideas get tested against actual sentences.

That process is uncomfortable and invaluable.

If you want to think more clearly, write more publicly. Not for the likes. Not for the followers.

For the discipline that comes from knowing someone might read it.

The audience is almost incidental. The thinking is the product.

Start a writing habit. Even private. Especially public.

What's something you believed until you tried to write about it clearly?
