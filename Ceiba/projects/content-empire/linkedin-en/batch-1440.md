I am a computer engineering student building an AI-powered content and product business from Puerto Rico.

I tell people that and they usually say: "That's cool, good luck."

What they mean is: "That sounds hard and unlikely."

They're right that it's hard.

They're wrong that it's unlikely.

The tools available to a 20-something builder today would have required a team of 20 and $500K in funding five years ago. That is not an exaggeration.

AI handles research, drafting, image generation, automation, code review. What used to take a team now takes one person with the right systems.

The barrier is not resources anymore. It's clarity of vision and consistency of execution.

I'm not succeeding yet by most financial metrics. But I'm building. And the building is teaching me things that no job ever would have.

If you're a young builder sitting somewhere that doesn't feel like the right place to build, I want you to know: the place doesn't matter as much as the decision.

Decide to build. Build publicly. Keep going.

The location is just background noise.

Where are you building from?
