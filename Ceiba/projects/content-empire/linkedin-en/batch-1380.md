# LinkedIn Post 1380

Three years ago I could not explain what I was building.

Not to my family. Not to my friends. Not to myself.

I just knew the traditional path felt wrong. Degree, job, salary, wait for retirement. That sequence made sense to people who built it. It made no sense to me.

So I started building in the dark. Testing ideas with no audience. Making products with no customers. Writing content with no feedback.

The dark period is real. Nobody tells you about it. You are doing the work and the results are invisible and every person in your life is confused about why you are not focused on "more important things."

The answer is: this is the most important thing.

Not because it will definitely work. But because the person you become by trying, failing, learning, and trying again is the only version of you capable of building something real.

The dark period is not failure. It is foundation.

If you are in that phase right now, building something no one in your life fully understands yet, stay in it. The clarity comes later. The momentum comes later. But only if you kept going.

You are not behind. You are exactly where you need to be.
