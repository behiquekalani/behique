**Hook:** When is the last time you audited what your business actually needs versus what you think it needs?

The sequence matters as much as the effort.

The goal isn't to work more. It's to align your hours with what your business actually needs at this stage.

What would you stop doing this week if you ran this audit honestly?

## Post 289
**Type:** List
**Hook:** 5 things your LinkedIn profile should do that most profiles completely ignore.

Your profile is a landing page. Treat it like one:

1. Your headline should speak to what you do for people, not your job title. "Helping solopreneurs turn expertise into products" beats "Founder | Creator | Coach."

2. Your About section should tell a story, not list credentials. Credentials create awareness. Stories create trust.

3. A featured section filled with social proof, product links, or your best-performing content. Empty featured sections are wasted real estate.

4. Consistent posting so your recent activity shows momentum. A cold profile with no recent posts signals inactivity to potential buyers.

5. A clear call to action. What do you want someone to do after reading your profile? Most profiles have no answer.

Profiles that convert look different from profiles that exist.

Which of these will you fix today?

## Post 290
**Type:** Insight
**Hook:** The metric that matters most in the first year of a creator business is not followers. It's trust.

Follower count is visible. Trust is invisible and worth more.

You can buy attention. You cannot buy trust. Trust is built through a hundred small interactions where you showed up, delivered value, and didn't ask for anything in return.

Trust is what makes someone buy your product before they read the full description. It's what makes them recommend you to a friend without prompting. It's what survives an algorithm change.

In year one, every post should be optimized for trust-building, not reach.

That means being honest about what you know and don't know. Sharing the failure, not just the win. Delivering real insight in every post instead of vague inspiration.

The audience that trusts you is small but mighty. It scales. The audience that just follows you is fragile.

Build slowly. Build trust. The growth comes after.
