I shipped 100 products before I made real money online.

Not 100 successful products. 100 attempts. Most of them were small. Some were terrible. A few sold a handful of copies.

People hear "100" and think that's discouraging. I think it's the most encouraging number I've ever heard.

It means you don't need to get it right on the first try. You don't need to get it right on the fiftieth try. You just need to keep going long enough to run into the attempt that works.

The creator economy is not a lottery. It rewards volume and iteration. The people at the top are not smarter. They've just made more attempts and kept the lessons from each one.

Every product I built taught me something specific. About pricing. About packaging. About what problems people actually pay to solve versus what they say they want.

You can't learn that from a course. You learn it by shipping.

The fastest path to one good product is ten mediocre ones. The fastest path to ten mediocre ones is starting today.

Don't optimize before you have data. Go make some.
