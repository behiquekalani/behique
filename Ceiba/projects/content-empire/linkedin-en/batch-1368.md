# LinkedIn Post 1368

I sent 47 cold emails last month. Got 3 replies. Closed 1 client.

Most people would call that a bad conversion rate. I call it a $1,200 lesson in what messaging works.

Here is what I learned from the 44 that did not reply:

1. I was talking about what I do, not what they get.
2. I was opening with my story instead of their problem.
3. I was asking for a call before I had given any reason to say yes.

The one that replied and paid started differently. First line: "I noticed your [specific thing] and I think there is a gap I can close for you in the next 30 days."

One sentence. Specific. Outcome-focused. No performance.

Cold outreach is a skill most people practice wrong. They copy templates from gurus who last did outreach ten years ago. The game has changed. Your prospects get 50 pitches a week. Standing out is not about being louder. It is about being more specific than everyone else.

If you are doing outreach right now and it is not converting, reply to this. I will look at your opening line and give you one concrete improvement.
