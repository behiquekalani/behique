**Hook:** I didn't quit my job to be free. I quit because I realized employment was teaching me to need permission.

That sounds dramatic. Let me explain.

Every system I worked in had a version of: wait for approval. Submit the form. Schedule the meeting. Let someone above you decide if your idea is worth pursuing.

And I was good at the system. Fast. Organized. Got promoted.

But I noticed something after two years: I had stopped having original thoughts. Not because I wasn't smart. Because the environment punished unsanctioned ideas and rewarded execution within existing lanes.

The muscle you use to generate new things atrophies when you don't use it.

Going independent wasn't about freedom from work. It was about recovering the capacity to originate. To decide what gets built. To test ideas without a committee.

Not everyone should go independent. But everyone should audit what permission they're waiting on that they could just... give themselves.

The idea you've been drafting for six months. The product you keep saying you'll build when the time is right. The content you're afraid to post.

You already have permission. You're just waiting for someone to tell you that you do.

What have you been waiting for permission to start?
