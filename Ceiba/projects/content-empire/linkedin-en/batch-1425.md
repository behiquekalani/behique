The advice "follow your passion" has ruined more careers than bad luck.

Here's why: passion follows mastery, not the other way around.

You don't fall in love with something before you're good at it. You fall in love with it as you get better. The feeling of competence creates the feeling of passion.

This means the question isn't "what am I passionate about?"

The question is: "What am I willing to be bad at long enough to get good at?"

That's a harder question. Most people won't sit with it.

But the people who answer it honestly are the ones who end up building careers they actually love, because they built skills first and let the love develop through the work.

I wasn't passionate about AI tools at the start. I was curious. I built things. I got better. Now I can't stop.

Curiosity is the seed. Skill is the soil. Passion is what grows.

Start with curiosity. The passion will come.

What were you willing to be bad at for long enough to get good? I'd love to hear it.
