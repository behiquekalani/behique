# LinkedIn Post 1396

Your network is a reflection of the conversations you've been willing to have.

Every connection that has opened a door for me started with me saying something real.

Not a pitch. Not a cold template. Something real.

An observation. A question. A genuine reaction to something they built or wrote.

People can feel the difference between someone who wants something from them and someone who is genuinely interested.

Be the second person.

Building a network isn't about collecting contacts. It's about building relationships where both people actually show up for each other.

That takes time. It takes consistency. It means commenting on their work when nothing is in it for you.

Over time, those deposits build something that can't be bought.

The people in my corner today are there because I showed up for them first, repeatedly, with nothing to gain.

Stop optimizing for reach. Start optimizing for depth.

Ten deep relationships will do more for your career than ten thousand shallow followers.

Who is one person in your network you could show up for this week with no agenda?
