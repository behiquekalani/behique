# LinkedIn Post 1373

I spent six months trying to be Dan Koe. Then I realized he was already taken.

Every creator goes through the imitation phase. You find someone whose work resonates and you absorb everything they do. Style, format, topics, tone. You rebuild yourself in their image.

It works. For a while. Then you hit a wall and you cannot figure out why your content feels hollow even when the structure is solid.

The reason: you are borrowing the vehicle but the fuel is theirs, not yours. Their specific stories. Their specific journey. Their specific perspective on the world.

The only thing you have that no one else has is your actual experience. Where you came from. What you survived. What you built and broke and rebuilt. What you believe when no one is watching.

That is the content that compounds. That is the content people come back for.

Be influenced by everyone. Be a copy of no one.

The fastest path to a real audience is the most uncomfortable one: being exactly yourself, clearly, consistently, without apology.

Who influenced you most early in your creator journey, and how did you eventually find your own voice?
