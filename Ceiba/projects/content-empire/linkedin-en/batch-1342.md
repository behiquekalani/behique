**Hook:** My first digital product made $0. My second made $0. My third made $0. Then I stopped guessing what people wanted.

The pattern nobody tells you about digital products: the first few fail not because the product is bad. They fail because you're solving problems you assumed existed.

I spent months building things I thought were valuable. Smart systems. Clean designs. Useful frameworks. Zero buyers. Not because the work was bad. Because I never asked a single real person if they needed it.

The turn happened when I started distributing content before building the product. I wrote about the problem. I watched what got shared. I noticed what questions came back. Then I built the thing that answered the most repeated question.

That's the actual product-market fit process for solos. It's not surveys. It's content. Content is market research that pays you while you do it.

Build in public. Let the audience tell you what they'll pay for. Then build that.

The market is always talking. Most builders just aren't listening.

What problem have you been assuming exists without confirming it?
