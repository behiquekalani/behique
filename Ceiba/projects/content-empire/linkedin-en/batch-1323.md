The most dangerous career advice I ever received: "Get good at one thing."

Not because specialization is wrong. Because the advice was missing the second half.

Get good at one thing. Then build a system around it so that one thing can scale without you doing more hours.

Most skilled people I know are trapped. They're excellent at their craft and completely dependent on trading hours for income. The skill ceiling is high. The revenue ceiling is lower.

The shift happens when you stop selling access to your skill and start packaging the outcome your skill produces.

A developer who charges $150/hour is selling time. A developer who builds a product that solves one recurring problem and sells it for $49/month is building an asset.

Same skill. Completely different model.

The solopreneur era isn't about everyone becoming a content creator. It's about skilled people finally having the tools to own what they build.

You already have the skills. The question is whether you're packaging them in a way that compounds.

What skill do you have that could become a product?
