**Hook:** I spent $4,000 on a course that taught me one sentence. Worth every dollar.

"Systems that survive contact with your actual life are the only ones that matter."

Not the ones that look clean in Notion. Not the ones that a productivity YouTuber uses in their optimal-life setup video. The ones that you actually run when you're tired, behind, distracted.

Every system I've ever built that failed was built for an idealized version of me. The version that wakes up at 5am rested, executes perfectly, has no friction.

That version doesn't exist. Real me has ADHD and variable energy and three open browser tabs and a project that's been 80% done for six weeks.

The systems that work for real me are ugly. They have redundancies. They account for low-energy days. They have an "emergency simple version" built in.

The emergency simple version is often the only version that runs.

Stop building systems for the person you want to be. Build them for the person you are on your worst productive day. If that version of you can run the system, you have a system.

What would your workflow look like if you designed it around your worst productive day instead of your best?
