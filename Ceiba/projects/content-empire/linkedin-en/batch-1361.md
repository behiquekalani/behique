# LinkedIn Post 1361

I failed my first product launch at 19.

Zero sales. Three weeks of work. I sat staring at the Gumroad dashboard at 2am thinking I had wasted everything.

Here is what I did not understand then: the product was fine. The positioning was wrong.

I was selling "a productivity template" when I should have been selling "three hours back in your week." Nobody buys templates. People buy time. People buy relief. People buy the feeling that they finally have control.

That shift changed everything. Same product. Different framing. The next launch converted.

Most creators kill good products before they get the chance to prove themselves. They write the copy wrong, blame the offer, and start over. Rinse, repeat, never figure out why.

Before you trash your next launch, ask one question: am I selling what this does, or am I selling what it feels like to have it?

The answer will tell you everything.
