I've shipped over 100 digital products.

Revenue from them: $0.

That's the honest part of the story most builders skip.

Here's the less honest but more common version: "I built this incredible thing and now everything is perfect."

My version: I built 100 products, learned 100 things about what actually makes something sell, and now I'm applying those lessons one by one.

The gap between building and selling is not talent. It's not effort either.

It's distribution. And distribution is a skill you have to learn separately from building.

Most builders are great at making things. We're terrible at putting them in front of people. Not because we're lazy, but because we were never taught that making something is only half the job.

The other half is getting it seen.

I'm working on the second half now. It's uncomfortable in a different way than building was. But the lessons are just as valuable.

If you've built something that nobody has seen yet, this is your sign to fix the distribution, not the product.

What have you built that deserves more eyes on it?
