The biggest income killers aren't bad luck or bad ideas.

They're small, invisible beliefs. The ones you don't notice until you look for them.

"I'm not an expert yet."
"Someone already did this."
"I don't know enough about marketing."
"What if no one buys?"

These aren't facts. They're fear wearing the mask of logic.

I had all of them. What changed wasn't my circumstances. It was choosing to act before the fear resolved itself, because the fear never fully resolves.

Every creator you admire shipped things before they were confident. They weren't sure it would work. They did it anyway. And the doing is what built the confidence, not the other way around.

You don't become confident and then start. You start and confidence follows.

The beliefs that are holding you back feel protective. Like they're keeping you from failure. What they're actually doing is guaranteeing it by keeping you from beginning.

Write down the belief that's been whispering "not yet" to you. Look at it directly. Ask if it's true, or if it's just fear with good vocabulary.

Most of the time it's the second.
