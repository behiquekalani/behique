# LinkedIn Post 1398

Most people spend their twenties learning what they want to spend their thirties building.

That's not a flaw. That's the process.

But you can compress it.

The fastest way to figure out what you want: build things, ship them, pay attention to what energizes you and what drains you.

Not what you think should energize you. What actually does.

I've built projects that looked impressive on paper and left me empty.

I've built small tools that nobody saw that I couldn't stop working on.

The second category tells you something.

Follow the energy. Not the external validation. Not the money (yet). The genuine pull you feel when you're working on something.

That pull is data about who you are and what you're actually built for.

You don't have to have it figured out. You just have to keep building and keep paying attention.

Clarity comes from doing, not from planning.

What are you building right now that you find yourself working on even when you don't have to?
