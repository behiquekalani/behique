# LinkedIn Post — batch-1310

A student asked me how to stand out when everyone is posting the same content.

My answer: stop trying to be interesting. Start trying to be useful.

Interesting fades fast. Useful compounds over time.

The problem with trying to be interesting is that interesting is defined by the audience's current mood. You can't predict it, you can't control it, and when the mood shifts your content becomes invisible.

Useful is different. Useful solves a problem someone already has. Useful is searchable. Useful gets saved. Useful gets sent to a friend who needs it.

The creator who posts three useful things a week that people bookmark will outperform the creator who posts daily trying to be interesting. Not in vanity metrics, maybe. But in business outcomes, almost always.

Ask a different question before every piece of content. Not "what would people find interesting?" but "what problem does my audience have that I can address in two minutes of reading?"

Answer that question consistently for six months and you won't need to worry about standing out. Your usefulness will do it for you.

What's the most useful piece of content you've published this year? Not most liked. Most useful.
