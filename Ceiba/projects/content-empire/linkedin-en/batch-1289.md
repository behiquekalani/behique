I have launched seven digital products. Two of them account for about eighty percent of total revenue.

I did not predict which two. I thought I knew. I was wrong.

This is the part of the creator economy that the income reports obscure. The process is not linear. You do not build the thing, price it correctly, market it well, and watch it steadily grow. You build a lot of things, most of them teach you something without generating much money, and occasionally one connects in a way you did not fully engineer.

What you can engineer is the volume. More experiments means more surface area for one of them to land. This is not an argument for shipping carelessly. It is an argument for shipping often, because careful shipping that takes six months per product gives you two shots per year. Thoughtful but faster shipping gives you twelve.

The creators who stay in the game long enough learn to read signals early. When something gets more engagement than expected, when people ask follow-up questions, when a post about a specific problem gets saved and shared more than others, that is signal. Build toward that signal.

Most creators ignore signal because they are too attached to the product they already planned to build.

Stay loose. Watch what people respond to. Build toward what the market is already trying to tell you.
