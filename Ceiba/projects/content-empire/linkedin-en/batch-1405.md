# LinkedIn Post — batch-1405

Your content is being ignored because it's trying to teach everyone.

Content that performs well does one thing: it makes a specific person feel like you wrote it for them.

When you write for "entrepreneurs," you write for no one. When you write for "a 23-year-old student building their first digital product at night after class," you write for someone who feels it.

I tested this directly. Generic posts: low engagement. Specific, personal posts: 4x the reach and real conversations in the comments.

The fear is that being specific shrinks your audience. The reality is the opposite. Specificity is magnetic. It draws people in rather than bouncing them off.

Here's the framework I use before writing any piece of content:

- Who is the one person I'm writing this for?
- What are they feeling right now that I once felt?
- What do I know now that would have helped me then?

Answer those three questions and the content almost writes itself.

Most people skip this step. They open a blank document and start typing. Then wonder why nothing resonates.

The work before the writing is the most important part.

Who is the one person your content is actually for?
