**Hook:** Nobody talks about the month before something works. That month is where most people quit.

I call it the Trough. Not a failure. Not a plateau. A trough.

You've been putting in consistent work. The compound hasn't shown yet. The metrics are flat or declining. The enthusiasm that launched the thing has worn off. The novelty is gone.

This is the most dangerous window in any creative or business project.

The people who quit in the Trough never find out if they had something. The people who push through discover one of two things: either the trough was a lag and the compound arrives, or the work genuinely needs a pivot.

Both outcomes are better than quitting blind.

What gets me through the Trough: I stop measuring outcomes and start measuring inputs. Did I ship today? Did I write today? Did I post today? That's the only scoreboard that matters until the compound shows.

You can control inputs. You can't control outcomes. Obsessing over flat metrics during the Trough is the surest way to talk yourself out of something that could have worked.

Trust your inputs. Survive the Trough. Let the lag do its work.

Where are you in your current project, and are you measuring the right things?
