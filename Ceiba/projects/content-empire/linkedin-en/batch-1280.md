The question I get most often from people starting out is: "Where do I begin?"

My answer is always the same: with the problem you've already solved.

Not the problem you think is interesting. Not the problem you've heard is profitable. The problem you've personally lived through and figured out.

That's where your credibility is. That's where your specific knowledge lives. That's where you can speak with honesty instead of theory.

Every creator who builds something real starts from that place. They had a problem. They found a solution. They shared it.

The sharing is the product.

You don't need to manufacture expertise. You need to extract it from your own experience and make it accessible to someone who's where you were two years ago.

The gap between who you are now and who you were then is your business.

Most people underestimate this gap because they moved through it gradually and forgot how hard it was at the start.

Go back to that version of yourself. What did they need? What would have saved them months? What do you wish someone had just explained clearly?

Build that. Price it fairly. Tell people it exists.

That's a business. And it started with you, not with a market research document.
