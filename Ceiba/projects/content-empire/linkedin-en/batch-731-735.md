## Post 731

Your network is not who you know. It is who knows what you do.

Most professionals invest in relationships but not in visibility. They meet people at events and then disappear. Their name comes up only when someone happens to remember them.

The fix is simple. Stay visible. Share what you are building. Write about what you are learning. Make it easy for people to know how to think of you.

Visibility is not vanity. It is the infrastructure of opportunity.

## Post 732

Burnout is not a sign you worked too hard. It is usually a sign you worked long in the wrong direction.

People who love what they do still get tired. But they recover. They come back. The work refills them as much as it drains them.

The people who burn out completely are usually chasing a version of success they do not actually want. They borrowed someone else's goal and sprinted toward it.

Check the direction before you blame the pace.

## Post 733

The most underrated professional skill: knowing when to stop gathering information and start making a decision.

More research is almost always available. There is always one more article, one more opinion, one more dataset.

At some point the next piece of information will not change your decision. You already know enough. What you are really doing is delaying.

Set a decision deadline. Force the choice. Learn from the result.

#DecisionMaking #Leadership #Execution

## Post 734

Nobody becomes an expert by accident.

Expertise is the result of deliberate repetition with feedback. You do the thing. You review what happened. You adjust. You do it again.

Most people skip the review. They accumulate experience without extracting lessons from it. That is how someone can have ten years of experience and still be a beginner.

Reflect often. Improve systematically. Expertise is a practice, not a destination.

#LearningAndDevelopment #GrowthMindset #Expertise

## Post 735

The problem with most goals is that they are too vague to be useful.

"Grow my business" gives you nothing to do on Monday morning. "Sign two new clients by April 30" gives you a target, a timeline, and a starting point.

Vague goals feel ambitious. Specific goals feel scary. That fear is a feature. It means the goal is real enough to fail at.

Write a goal that is specific enough to make you a little nervous. Then work it.

#GoalSetting #Entrepreneurship #Accountability
