# LinkedIn Post — batch-1315

Your network is not your net worth.

That phrase gets repeated until it's meaningless. What it actually means is more specific than the slogan suggests.

The right relationships don't come from collecting contacts at events and sending connection requests to people with big followings. They come from consistently showing up in a way that makes people think of you when something relevant crosses their path.

Networking as most people practice it is transactional. You show up when you want something and disappear when you don't. People remember this pattern even when they don't articulate it.

Relationships that actually produce results are built through genuine interest over time. Reading what someone publishes and engaging thoughtfully. Sharing their work when you find it genuinely good. Reaching out with something valuable before you need anything.

This isn't a strategy. It's a character trait. You either care about other people's work or you don't. If you genuinely do, your network will grow because people can feel the difference.

The creators with the best opportunities aren't always the most talented. They're the ones other people want to help. That reputation is built one real interaction at a time.

Who's someone in your network you could add value to today, with zero expectation of return?
