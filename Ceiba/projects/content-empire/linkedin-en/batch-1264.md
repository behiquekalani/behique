There's a skill nobody talks about in creator communities.

It's the skill of ignoring good advice.

Every week someone tells you to post more. Post less. Go long-form. Go short. Pick one platform. Be everywhere. Niche down. Stay broad.

All of it is good advice. For someone. It's also completely wrong. For you.

The people giving it built their audiences in different markets, different years, with different resources. They're telling you what worked for them. But you are not them.

The hardest thing I've had to learn is that information is not the bottleneck. I have more frameworks, strategies, and systems than I could use in a decade.

The bottleneck is deciding what to ignore.

When I stopped consuming content about how to build an audience and just started building one, things moved. Not because I found the secret. Because I stopped looking for it and started generating data of my own.

Your situation is unique. Your output will tell you more than anyone else's advice ever could.

Pick a direction. Go. Adjust based on what your own data tells you.

The guru's playbook was built for the guru.
