Most people treat their attention like it's free.

It's not. It's actually the most expensive thing you have.

Every hour you spend consuming content about building a business is an hour you didn't spend building one. Every hour in a meeting that could have been a message is gone. Every context switch costs you 20 minutes of recovery before you're deep again.

I started tracking where my attention actually went versus where I thought it went. The gap was embarrassing.

I thought I was working 6 hours a day on my business. I was actually working about 90 minutes. The rest was research, reading, tweaking systems, and convincing myself it all counted.

Attention is what turns ideas into output. Without it, you have a collection of intentions.

What changed things for me was a simple rule: no consumption before creation. Before I read anything, watch anything, or listen to anything, I have to produce something first.

Even if it's rough. Even if it's short. The act of creating first changes how the rest of the day goes.

You stop being a spectator and become a participant. And participants build things. Spectators watch others build things.

Guard your attention like it's money. Because it converts into money.
