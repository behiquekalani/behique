# LinkedIn EN — Post 1423

Nobody tells you that building in public is terrifying until you actually do it.

You post your first project. Ten people see it. Nobody comments. You feel like you're shouting into a void.

Then one person DMs you: "I built something similar, want to connect?"

That one message changed the trajectory of my work more than any job application I ever sent.

Here's what building in public actually does that nobody explains:

It forces clarity. You can't explain something you don't understand. When you write about what you're building, the gaps in your thinking become obvious.

It creates surface area for luck. The right person has to know you exist. They can't find you if you're invisible.

It compounds. Post 1 gets 5 views. Post 100 gets 500. The math is slow but it's real.

You don't need a massive audience to get massive results. You need the right five people to see the right five posts.

Start posting. Be specific. Be consistent.

The void eventually echoes back.
