The thing about building from a small island is that you stop waiting for permission.

Puerto Rico has 3.2 million people and an unemployment rate that makes ambition feel like a survival skill.

Growing up here, you learn quickly that the career paths people celebrate on the mainland aren't always available. The companies aren't here. The network isn't here. The funding isn't here.

What is here: resourcefulness. The ability to build something from very little. A work ethic shaped by knowing you don't have a safety net.

The internet made geography irrelevant. A digital product sold from Caguas reaches the same buyer as one sold from San Francisco. A newsletter built in Ponce competes on the same playing field as one built in New York.

That realization changed everything for me. Not because I wanted to escape where I'm from. Because I wanted to prove that where you're from doesn't determine what you can build.

If you're building from a place where the traditional path feels closed off, that's not a disadvantage. That's the reason your hunger is real.

Use it.
