Pricing is where most creators destroy their own confidence.

They build something real, spend weeks on it, and then list it at $9 because they're afraid nobody will pay more.

Here's what that actually communicates: you don't believe in it. And if the creator doesn't believe in it, why would the buyer?

Low prices don't attract buyers. They attract the wrong buyers. The ones who want everything for nothing, who ask for refunds over small issues, who never implement anything and then say the product didn't work.

The person who pays $97 for something has already made a commitment. They take it seriously because it cost them something. They're more likely to use it, get results, and come back for more.

I raised my prices once as an experiment. Sales went down slightly. Revenue went up significantly. And the quality of feedback I received improved completely.

Price is a signal. It tells people what kind of product this is and what kind of buyer it's for.

What is your work worth to someone whose problem it genuinely solves? Start there. Not at the floor, not at "what I think people will pay." At the actual value.

Underpricing is not humility. It's a hidden fear of rejection.
