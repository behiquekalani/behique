Consistency is not about volume. It's about frequency.

I've watched creators burn out by posting every day. They start strong, run out of substance, start padding their content with filler, then disappear entirely when the quality drops and the algorithm stops rewarding them.

Posting daily with nothing to say is worse than posting twice a week with something real to say.

Frequency matters more than volume because it creates a rhythm your audience can count on. They know you'll be there. That predictability is a form of trust.

What breaks creators is conflating busyness with productivity. They measure output in posts per week instead of impact per post.

One post that makes someone think differently about their work is worth 20 posts that fill a feed.

Find the frequency you can maintain while doing quality work. Not the frequency that feels impressive on a content calendar. The one that leaves you with something to say each time you show up.

Then protect that frequency. Don't let a bad week turn into three weeks of silence. Show up even if it's shorter than usual. The habit matters more than the individual piece.

Slow and consistent beats fast and scattered every time.
