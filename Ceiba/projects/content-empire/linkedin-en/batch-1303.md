Nobody told me that the hardest part of working for yourself isn't the work.

It's the silence between the work and the result.

When you have a job, there's a feedback loop. You do the task, your manager notices, you get a performance review. The feedback is imperfect, but it's there. You know where you stand.

When you work for yourself, especially early on, you can spend weeks doing the right things and see nothing. You post, you build, you reach out. And the silence comes back.

Most people quit during that silence. They assume it means they're doing something wrong. So they switch strategies, start over, or give up entirely.

The silence is not a signal. It's a phase.

Every creator I've watched build something real went through a period where their output looked identical to someone who was failing. The difference was internal. They decided to trust the process past the point where trust felt reasonable.

You can't always control when results arrive. You can control whether you're still there when they do.

Show up for another week. Then another. The compounding isn't visible until it is.

Has there been a moment when you almost quit but didn't? What kept you going?
