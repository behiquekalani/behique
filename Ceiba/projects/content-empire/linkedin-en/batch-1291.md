The creator economy loves the word "niche" but rarely explains what finding one actually costs.

When you niche down, you are not just choosing what to write about. You are choosing what to stop writing about. You are choosing who to stop talking to. You are deciding that being relevant to a specific group of people matters more than being interesting to everyone.

That decision is harder than it sounds.

For most of my early content career, I wrote about everything I found interesting. It was intellectually satisfying and strategically useless. Interesting to no one in particular. Remembered by almost no one.

The first time I committed to a specific problem for a specific person, the content got worse before it got better. I had fewer ideas. The posts felt smaller. I kept wanting to break out of the constraint and write about something broader.

Staying inside the constraint anyway is what eventually worked. The specificity forced me to go deeper on fewer things. The depth created genuine insight. Genuine insight is what people share, return to, and eventually pay for.

Broad content attracts passive readers. Specific content attracts people with a problem who feel like you understand them.

Those are not the same people, and they are not worth the same thing to your business.

What problem are you the most honest and specific version of yourself about?
