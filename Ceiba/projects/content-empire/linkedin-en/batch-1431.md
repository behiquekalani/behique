I used to think rest was the enemy of output.

I was wrong. Rest is part of the system.

The highest-output periods of my life have always followed periods of intentional recovery. Not accidental collapse. Intentional rest.

The difference matters.

Accidental collapse is when you run until you can't. You don't choose it. Your body forces it. The recovery is slow because you're also dealing with the guilt of stopping.

Intentional rest is scheduled. You protect it. You don't apologize for it. You return from it with energy instead of just absence of exhaustion.

Output is not continuous. It's cyclical.

The people who understand this build careers that last decades. The ones who treat every week like a sprint burn out inside three years.

If you're in a heavy execution phase right now, schedule your next rest period the same way you schedule your next project. Give it a date. Protect it.

The work will be better for it. You will be better for it.

When did you last take real, intentional rest? Not collapse. Actual recovery.
