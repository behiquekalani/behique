Most productivity systems were designed for knowledge workers inside organizations. They were not designed for a solo creator who is also the product manager, the marketer, the customer service team, and the accountant.

That mismatch causes a lot of unnecessary suffering.

You read about Inbox Zero. You try to apply it. But your inbox contains client questions, collaboration pitches, random ideas you emailed to yourself, and invoices that need following up on. There is no clean separation between work categories because there is no organizational structure around you to create it.

The creator's version of productivity is different. It has to be.

What works for me is thinking in three levels every week. What needs to ship. What needs to grow. What needs to maintain. Ship means output. Grow means relationships, audience, learning. Maintain means everything that keeps the operation running.

If all three categories get some attention, the week was productive even if it felt chaotic. If one category gets neglected for too long, something breaks.

This is not a framework I read somewhere. It is something I arrived at by watching what actually happens when I ignore one of the three for an extended period.

Your system needs to match your actual life, not the life the productivity book was written for.

Build your own. Steal parts of others. Keep only what works for how you actually operate.
