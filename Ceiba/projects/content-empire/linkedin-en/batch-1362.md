# LinkedIn Post 1362

Most people treat their phone like a slot machine.

Pick it up. Scroll. Put it down. Pick it up again ten minutes later. Never once deciding what they wanted from the interaction.

I tracked my screen time for 30 days and found something uncomfortable: I was spending 2.5 hours a day consuming content from people I would never meet, about topics I did not care about deeply, in exchange for a vague feeling of being "informed."

That is 900 hours a year. 37 full days.

I made one rule: every time I pick up my phone, I have to know why before I unlock it.

It sounds small. It changed my output completely.

The best ideas I have come when I protect the space between inputs. The problem is most of us never create that space. We fill every gap with noise and wonder why we cannot think clearly.

Your phone is not the enemy. Unconscious use is.

Define the purpose before you unlock. Give yourself a reason. See what happens to your thinking in 30 days.
