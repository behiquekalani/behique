I spent two years building an audience on a platform I didn't own.

Then the algorithm changed. Reach dropped sixty percent overnight. The work didn't disappear but the distribution did.

That experience taught me something I try to pass on to every creator I talk to: build the asset, not just the channel.

A social following is rented. An email list is owned. A YouTube channel can be demonetized. A course you host on your own domain cannot be taken from you.

This isn't about abandoning social media. It's about understanding the game you're playing. Social gives you discovery. Owned channels give you durability.

Every piece of content you publish should have a path that moves people toward something you control. An email opt-in, a direct community, a product they buy directly from you.

If tomorrow your main platform shut down, what would survive? That's the thing worth building toward.

The creators who last aren't necessarily the most talented. They're the ones who built infrastructure while the building was good. They didn't wait until they needed it.

Start building the owned asset now. Use the algorithm while you have it, but don't depend on it.
