# LinkedIn Post — batch-1409

AI didn't replace creators. It separated the good ones from the lazy ones.

Here's what I mean.

People who used AI to think less got worse. Generic output, no perspective, content that blends into the feed and disappears.

People who used AI to think more got better. Faster drafts that they then rewrote in their voice. Research that freed up time for deeper ideas. Distribution that let one insight reach more people.

The tool is the same. The operator determines the result.

Your unique perspective, lived experience, and specific way of seeing the world are not replicable. An AI can write a paragraph. It can't have a conversation with your grandmother that changed the way you think about money. It can't know what it feels like to build something with no safety net.

That specificity is what makes content worth reading. The AI handles the production. Your life is the source material.

If your content sounds like it could have been written by anyone, that's the problem to fix. Not the tool you're using.

What's one experience from your life that would make a piece of content no AI could replicate?
