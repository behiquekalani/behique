Three years ago I launched a product that flopped completely.

Not a slow death. A fast one. Zero sales in the first week. I had the landing page, the email list, the social posts. I had everything except proof that anyone actually wanted what I built.

I built it because I thought it was a good idea. I never asked.

The product I launched after that one sold on day one. The difference wasn't quality. The difference was that I spent four weeks talking to people before I wrote a single line of copy. I found out what they were already trying to solve, what they'd already bought, what had let them down.

Then I built something that fit into a gap that already existed in their life.

Market research doesn't have to be formal. It can be ten conversations, twenty Reddit threads, and a close read of one-star reviews on competing products. That's it. That's enough to know if you're building toward something real.

Most people skip this step because creating feels more productive than listening. But the creating is just ego until you know you're building something someone actually needs.

Listen first. Build second. Then launch with confidence instead of hope.

What's your process for validating before you build?
