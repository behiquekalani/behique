**Hook:** The person who taught me the most about AI wasn't a researcher. It was a student who refused to use it as a shortcut.

She used AI differently than everyone else in the room.

While classmates used it to finish assignments faster, she used it to go deeper. She'd take an AI-generated answer and then spend an hour asking why. Verifying. Pushing back. Building her own understanding on top of the scaffold.

Her output took longer. Her understanding was permanent.

There's a version of AI use that makes you dependent. You outsource the thinking. The reasoning atrophies. You get faster at producing work that doesn't represent what you actually know.

Then there's the version that amplifies. You use AI as a starting point, not an ending point. You treat it like a research assistant who's fast but not always right. You stay in the loop.

The builders who will compound over the next decade are not the ones who automate the most. They're the ones who understand the most and use AI to go further with that understanding.

Don't use AI to skip the hard parts. Use it to reach harder parts faster.

How are you using AI to go deeper, not just faster?
