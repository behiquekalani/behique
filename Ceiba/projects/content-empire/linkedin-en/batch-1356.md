**Hook:** The difference between someone who builds one product and someone who builds a catalog is one thing: they don't wait to understand everything before starting the next one.

Catalog builders have a rhythm. They finish, reflect briefly, start again. The learning from one product is input for the next. The system compounds.

One-product builders wait for clarity that never fully arrives. They polish. They second-guess. They compare to others. By the time they're satisfied, the window has moved.

The insight isn't "done is better than perfect." That's too simple.

The real insight is that clarity comes from doing, not from waiting. Every product you ship teaches you something that no amount of planning can. Your market speaks through purchases and non-purchases in a language that research can only approximate.

The builders with large catalogs didn't build them because they had more time. They built them because they had a different relationship with completion. Good enough to ship became a standard they could repeat.

You need a repeatable completion threshold, not a perfect product.

What would your production rhythm look like if you cut your self-review cycle in half?
