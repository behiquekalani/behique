# LinkedIn Post — batch-1413

I tracked every hour I spent on content for 30 days. Here's what the data said.

60% of my time went to creation. 40% to distribution.

The results flipped when I reversed it.

Most creators spend all their energy making the thing and almost none getting it in front of people. Then they wonder why nothing performs.

Distribution is the actual work. Content without distribution is just a private journal.

What changed when I flipped the ratio:
- Old content started getting found again
- New posts got more traction on day one
- I built relationships with other creators who amplified my work
- I spent less time creating and got more out of every piece

You don't need to create more. You need to distribute better.

That means: repurposing, cross-posting, reaching out to collaborators, getting on podcasts, writing guest posts, being in the communities where your audience already is.

One insight, five formats, ten destinations. That's more efficient than five new insights with no distribution plan.

What's the ratio between creating and distributing in your work right now?
