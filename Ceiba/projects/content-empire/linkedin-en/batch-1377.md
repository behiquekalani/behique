# LinkedIn Post 1377

I read 40 books last year. The ROI on 38 of them was zero.

Not because the books were bad. Because I read without a purpose and applied nothing.

This is the consumption trap. We convince ourselves that intake equals progress. More books, more courses, more podcasts. We confuse learning with action. We feel productive without producing anything.

The two books that returned real value were the ones I read with a specific problem in mind. I was stuck on pricing. I found a book on pricing psychology. I read it with a highlighter and a notebook open, writing down one thing I would change by Friday.

Knowledge with no assigned application date is entertainment, not education.

Before your next book or course, define the problem it is supposed to solve. Set a date for when you will apply the first principle. Then ship something based on it.

The goal is not to know more. The goal is to do more with what you know.

What is one book that actually changed how you work, and what specifically did you do differently after reading it?
