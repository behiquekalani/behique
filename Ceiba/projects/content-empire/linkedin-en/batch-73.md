# LinkedIn EN — Batch 73
## Topic: Content Strategy for Solopreneurs

---

### Post 1

The content strategy for a solopreneur is not a scaled-down version of a brand content strategy. It's a fundamentally different model.

A brand creates content to build awareness. A solopreneur creates content to build trust with a specific person who might eventually buy something specific.

That difference changes everything:

Tone: Personal, first-person, honest about the journey. Not polished brand voice.
Topics: Your process, your frameworks, your failures, your opinions. Not broad industry coverage.
Goal per piece: Move one person from "I've seen this person before" to "I trust this person's judgment."
Frequency: Consistent enough to stay relevant. Not so frequent that quality degrades.

The solopreneur content strategy has one core question underneath every piece: "What would make someone who reads this trust me more and understand what I'm building?"

If a post answers that question, publish it. If it doesn't, reconsider.

The trap is treating content like performance. The solopreneur who performs loses credibility over time. The one who shares genuine process builds it.

### Post 2

Five content formats that work for solopreneurs on LinkedIn, ranked by effort-to-result ratio:

1. Contrarian take on a widely held belief in your domain. Low effort, high reach. Pick something you genuinely disagree with and explain your reasoning in clear terms.

2. Breakdown of a mistake you made and what you learned. Medium effort, high trust-building. Vulnerability with a lesson lands better than success theater.

3. Step-by-step framework for solving a specific problem. Higher effort, high value per post. This is the format that gets saved and shared most.

4. Decision you made recently and why. Low effort, high authenticity signal. Shows the thinking, not just the outcome.

5. Observation from your work that challenges an assumption. Medium effort, positions you as someone who thinks, not just someone who does.

Most solopreneurs overthink format and underthink frequency. Post consistently with medium-quality content before you optimize for great content with inconsistent presence.

### Post 3

The solopreneur who posts consistently for 12 months builds something that no single viral post can replicate: a body of work.

A body of work is what converts cold audiences into warm ones, warm audiences into buyers, and buyers into repeat customers.

Here's what 12 months of consistent content creates:
- A searchable library of your thinking on a specific domain
- Proof that you show up, which signals reliability before a sale
- Multiple entry points for different types of potential buyers
- A feedback loop from engagement that improves every piece you write

The trap is optimizing for individual post performance instead of cumulative trust.

One post that performs well is a spike. Twelve months of consistent posting is a ramp.

The solopreneurs who complain that content "doesn't work" usually stopped before the compounding phase started. The compounding phase starts somewhere between months three and six for most.

Start now. Post with intention. Review quarterly. Adjust based on what resonates. Do not stop.
