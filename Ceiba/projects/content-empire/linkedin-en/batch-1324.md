I was broke, building products nobody asked for, and convinced I just needed one more feature.

That's not a hustle story. That's a trap.

The moment things changed wasn't when I got smarter or worked harder. It was when I stopped building first and started listening first.

Real builders don't build what they want to build. They build what the market is already trying to buy.

You find that by going to the places where people complain. Reddit threads. One-star reviews. DMs from frustrated buyers. Discord servers where people vent.

People will literally tell you what they need. Most builders are too busy building to listen.

Once I started paying attention to what problems kept surfacing, the products I made started selling on launch day instead of collecting dust.

The product wasn't better. The targeting was.

If your products aren't selling, the problem isn't the product. It's that you're solving a problem that doesn't hurt enough.

Find the pain first. Build the relief second.

What's the last complaint you heard that sparked a product idea?
