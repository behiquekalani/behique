I made $0 from content for the first eighteen months.

People find that hard to believe when I tell them what I make now. They ask what changed.

The honest answer is: almost nothing changed about the strategy. What changed was that I kept going past the point most people stop.

The creator economy rewards patience more than talent. Not because talent doesn't matter, but because most people with talent quit before the compounding kicks in.

Here's the math that nobody shows you: an audience that takes eighteen months to build is far more durable than one built in six through aggressive growth hacks. Slow audiences are loyal audiences. They found you before you were popular. That matters to them.

The problem is that eighteen months of near-zero results is genuinely hard. You look around and see people growing faster. You question your approach. You consider pivoting or stopping entirely.

The pivot often kills the compounding. You restart the clock.

Unless something is fundamentally broken, the answer is almost always: keep going, refine the craft, serve the people who are already there.

Eighteen months feels like failure until month nineteen.

What month are you on?
