# Post 42 - Mindset: Consistency Over Intensity
## Caption
A 2-hour work session every day for 30 days beats a 16-hour sprint followed by two weeks of burnout.

Every time.

Intensity feels heroic. You grind for 14 hours, post about it, get the dopamine hit, and crash. The project sits untouched for a week while you recover.

Consistency is boring. Nobody celebrates a quiet 2-hour session. But those sessions compound. In 30 days you have 60 hours of focused work. In 90 days you have a product.

The sprint-and-crash cycle is not discipline. It is avoidance disguised as work ethic. You work hard for a day so you can justify resting for a week.

Real builders show up at the same time, do the same work, and do not need to feel motivated to do it.

Build the habit. Protect the schedule. Trust the compound effect.

The people who win are not the ones who work the hardest on any given day. They are the ones who show up the most days.
