# Post 20 - Business Tip: The Two-Hour Rule
## Caption
If a task takes more than two hours and does not directly lead to revenue, it goes to the backlog.

This is the two-hour rule. It is the single most effective filter for solopreneur productivity.

Redesigning your website header? Backlog. Researching the perfect email tool? Backlog. Creating a brand guidelines document? Backlog.

Writing a sales email to a warm lead? Do it now. Building the checkout page for your product? Do it now. Recording a video that drives traffic to your offer? Do it now.

Revenue-adjacent tasks first. Everything else is maintenance and can wait.

The two-hour rule forces you to ask one question before starting any task: does this move the sale forward?

If the answer is not a clear yes, it waits. Not forever. Just until the revenue work is done.

Protect your best hours for your highest-leverage work.
