## TikTok 1: Why You're Underpricing Your Digital Products
**Hook (0-2s):**
Your digital product is probably priced wrong. And it's costing you more than you think.

**Script:**
There's a pricing mistake almost every first-time creator makes. They price based on what they'd pay for it themselves.

That's wrong. You already know what's inside. You built it. Of course it feels like less to you.

Price based on the outcome. What's the result worth to the person who needs it most?

If your ebook saves someone 20 hours of research, and their time is worth $50 an hour, the outcome is worth $1,000. You're not charging $1,000. But you could probably charge $47 instead of $9.

The gap between $9 and $47 isn't effort. It's positioning. It's the language you use to describe the result.

People don't pay for information. They pay for transformation. Price the transformation, not the PDF.

**CTA:**
What's your digital product priced at right now? Think about the outcome it delivers.

## TikTok 2: The Pricing Mistake That Kills Trust
**Hook (0-2s):**
Pricing too low doesn't attract more buyers. It scares them away.

**Script:**
There's a psychological reality that most creators ignore. When something is too cheap, people assume it's low quality before they even open it.

A $5 ebook and a $47 ebook can have identical content. But the $5 one will get lower completion rates, more refund requests, and fewer testimonials.

Because the price signals the value before the buyer experiences it.

This isn't just theory. Look at your own behavior. When you buy something expensive, you pay attention. When it's nearly free, you treat it like it's nearly worthless.

You want buyers who are invested in the result. A higher price filters for that. It selects for people who take it seriously.

Raise your price. The right buyers will find it. The wrong ones will leave. That's actually what you want.

**CTA:**
Have you ever bought something cheap and never used it? That's what's happening to your products.

## TikTok 3: How to Price a Digital Product From Scratch
**Hook (0-2s):**
Here's a pricing framework that takes five minutes.

**Script:**
Step one. Write down the three most painful problems your product solves. Be specific.

Step two. For each problem, estimate the cost of not solving it. Time wasted, money lost, stress created.

Step three. Add those up. That's the full value of the outcome.

Step four. Price at 5 to 10 percent of that value.

If the outcome is worth $500, your product should probably be $27 to $47. If the outcome is worth $5,000, you have room to charge $197 to $497.

Most people skip steps one and two entirely. They just guess a number that feels comfortable. Then they wonder why it's not converting.

The price should follow the outcome. Work backwards from there.

**CTA:**
Run your current product through this framework right now. Does the math hold?

## TikTok 4: Tiered Pricing for Solopreneurs
**Hook (0-2s):**
One product. Three price points. More revenue without more products.

**Script:**
You don't need more products. You need more ways to buy the one you have.

Tiered pricing is how you capture buyers at every level of commitment.

Tier one: the entry point. Lowest price. Core value only. PDF or basic download. Gets people in the door.

Tier two: the full product. Everything they need plus templates, checklists, or a swipe file. This is your main offer.

Tier three: the premium experience. The product plus direct access to you. A Q&A call, a community, or an implementation guide.

Most people buy tier two. Some people buy tier one and upgrade. A few people buy tier three and they pay for half your revenue alone.

The same idea. Three different ways to say yes.

**CTA:**
Does your current product have more than one entry point? If not, this is your next move.

## TikTok 5: When to Raise Your Prices
**Hook (0-2s):**
There are three signs it's time to raise your price. Most people ignore all of them.

**Script:**
Sign one: you're getting sales without promoting. If buyers are finding you organically and converting, your product is underpriced relative to the demand.

Sign two: people compliment the value immediately after buying. "This is worth so much more than what I paid" is a sign. Believe them.

Sign three: your refund rate is near zero. People who feel they overpaid ask for refunds. Zero refunds means the perceived value exceeds the price.

When you see two or three of these, test a higher price. Not forever. For 30 days. See if conversion holds.

It usually does. Because the people who already bought were not price-sensitive. The ones coming after them won't be either.

Raise the price. Watch what happens.

**CTA:**
Which of those three signs are you seeing right now?
