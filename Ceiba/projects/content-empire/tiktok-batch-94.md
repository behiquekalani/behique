## TikTok 1: The One-Person Business Model Explained
**Hook (0-2s):**
"One person. No employees. Multiple income streams. This is the model."

**Script:**
The one-person business is not a new idea. But the tools available now make it more viable than ever.

Here's the structure.

You have one core expertise. One thing you know better than most people.

From that expertise, you build three layers.

Layer one: free content. TikToks, tweets, newsletter issues, blog posts. This builds trust and brings traffic.

Layer two: low-ticket products. Templates, guides, mini-courses. $9 to $49. Converts the trust into revenue without a sales call.

Layer three: high-ticket. A service, a coaching program, a consulting retainer. $500 to $5,000. For the people who want the implementation, not just the information.

Most solopreneurs get stuck because they try to build all three at once. They end up with nothing finished.

The right order: build the free content first until you have a consistent audience. Then add layer two. Then layer three once you have buyers.

This model does not require venture capital. It requires depth and patience.

**CTA:**
What's your core expertise? Write it in the comments in one sentence.

## TikTok 2: How to Price Your Digital Products
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your digital product is underpriced. Here's how to know."

**Script:**
Most first-time creators price their products based on how long it took to make them. That's the wrong variable.

Price based on the outcome, not the effort.

If your product helps someone save 10 hours a week, it's worth at least $200. If it helps someone make $500, it should cost at least $50.

The formula: price equals roughly 10 percent of the outcome value.

Here's a common mistake I see.

Someone builds a guide for getting freelance clients. They price it at $7. But freelance clients are worth thousands of dollars. The guide should cost $97 minimum.

Lower prices do not always mean more sales. They often mean less trust.

Pricing signals quality. A $7 product signals that the outcome is worth $70. A $97 product signals that the outcome is worth nearly $1,000.

If you've been underpricing, test raising your price by 3x. Watch what happens to your conversion rate. You might be surprised.

**CTA:**
What's your product priced at? Drop it in the comments and I'll tell you if it's underpriced.

## TikTok 3: The Email Strategy That Outperforms Social
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Email has a higher ROI than every social platform. Most people ignore it."

**Script:**
Average email open rate: 25 to 40 percent.
Average Instagram reach: 3 to 5 percent of your followers.

That math is not close.

Email is the only platform where you own the relationship. You can't be shadowbanned. The algorithm doesn't decide who sees your content. You send, they receive.

Here's how to start building an email list if you have zero subscribers.

Step one: create one free resource that solves a specific problem. A checklist. A template. A short guide. Five pages is enough.

Step two: set up a free ConvertKit or Beehiiv account. Create a landing page with one sentence describing what the freebie does.

Step three: mention it once per week at the end of your TikToks. "I have a free [X] at the link in my bio."

Within 30 days you can have 100 to 500 subscribers if your content is in a specific niche.

Then you email them weekly. You build trust. You make offers. You earn.

Social builds attention. Email builds relationships. Relationships build income.

**CTA:**
Start your list this week. Free tools. Twenty minutes. No excuses.

## TikTok 4: What Separates 6-Figure Solopreneurs
**Hook (0-2s):**
"I studied 20 six-figure solopreneurs. They all do this one thing."

**Script:**
They say no more than they say yes.

This sounds counterintuitive. Don't you grow by doing more things?

No. You grow by doing fewer things better.

Every six-figure solopreneur I've studied has ruthless focus on one primary channel, one primary offer, and one primary audience.

They don't spread across five platforms. They own one.
They don't sell ten products. They sell one or two.
They don't serve everyone. They serve one specific person extremely well.

The people stuck at $2,000 a month are usually trying to do five things at 60 percent capacity. The people at $10,000 a month are doing two things at 95 percent capacity.

Capacity, not effort, is the constraint.

When you spread thin, you hit the ceiling of what's possible at 60 percent.

When you go deep, you break through ceilings you didn't know were there.

**CTA:**
What's the one thing you would do if you could only do one thing? That's your answer.

## TikTok 5: The 30-Day Content Experiment
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Post every day for 30 days. Here's what actually happens."

**Script:**
I've done this twice now. Here's the honest version.

Days 1 through 7: you're motivated. You feel productive. Engagement is average.

Days 8 through 15: you start running low on easy ideas. You get your first piece of content that flops. You question whether this is working.

Days 16 through 22: something shifts. You find your rhythm. You stop trying to make every post viral and start just expressing what's actually on your mind. The quality goes up.

Days 23 through 30: the algorithm starts recognizing your consistency. A few posts punch above their weight. You have more ideas than time.

After 30 days: you have a library of 30 pieces of content. You've tested formats, hooks, and topics. You know what your audience responds to.

The value of 30 days is not the individual posts. It's the data and the habit.

Most people quit on day 10. That's the day before the shift happens.

**CTA:**
Start today. Day one. Don't optimize. Just post.
