### Short 4 — How to Write a Landing Page in 30 Minutes

**Hook (0-3s):** Your landing page doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to do one thing.

**Content (3-50s):**
Most people overthink landing pages. They spend days on design, headline variations, and color palettes.

Here's a 30-minute framework that converts.

Five sections.

One: the headline. One sentence. Who it's for and what they get.

Two: the problem. Three sentences maximum. Describe the pain they have right now so well that they feel understood.

Three: the solution. What your product does and how it works. Not features. Outcomes.

Four: proof. One testimonial or one specific result. If you don't have one yet, use a specific before-and-after scenario.

Five: the offer. Price. What's included. The buy button.

That's it.

No video required. No fancy graphics. No 20-section page.

The page that converts is the page that makes the visitor feel understood in two seconds and shows them the solution in the next ten.

Design is secondary to clarity.

Write it in 30 minutes. Publish it. Improve it based on real feedback.

### Short 8 — The Only 3 Metrics That Matter for a Digital Product

**Hook (0-3s):** You're tracking the wrong numbers. Here are the only three that matter.

**Content (3-50s):**
Vanity metrics will waste your time.

Page views, follower count, impressions. They feel good. They don't tell you if the business is working.

Three numbers tell you everything you need to know.

First: conversion rate. Of everyone who visits your sales page, what percentage buys. This tells you if your offer and copy are working. Industry average for warm traffic is 1 to 3 percent. If you're under one, fix the page.

Second: average order value. How much does a buyer spend on average. This is where the product stack matters. One product, one number. Three products, the number can compound.

Third: customer acquisition cost. How much does it cost to get one paying customer. If you're running ads, this is your spend divided by sales. If you're using content, it's your time.

If conversion rate is healthy, average order value grows with your product stack, and acquisition cost stays lower than what each customer spends, the business works.

Every other metric supports these three or it's a distraction.

Know these three numbers every week.
