# Instagram Content Batch 59
**Topic: The 3 types of content that actually build business**
**Brand: Behike | Voice: Dan Koe-style, direct, no hype**

---

**Post 1**
**Hook:** Most content gets attention. Three types of content get customers.
**Caption:** The content that goes viral is not usually the content that converts. The content that builds a real business operates at three levels: it attracts, it builds trust, and it converts. Most creators only ever make attract content because it gets the most feedback. But without the trust layer and the conversion layer, attention is just vanity. Build all three or accept that you're running a hobby.

**Post 2**
**Hook:** Attract content gets eyes on you. It does nothing else.
**Caption:** Hooks, trends, relatable moments, hot takes. This content grows your audience by reaching people who didn't know you existed. It's necessary and it's overrated at the same time. The mistake is thinking that more reach automatically leads to more revenue. It doesn't. People who found you from a trending post have no idea why they should trust your opinion or buy what you're selling. Attract content opens a door. That's all it does.

**Post 3**
**Hook:** Trust content is the layer most creators skip entirely.
**Caption:** Trust content is where you prove that your knowledge is real, your framework is tested, and your perspective is worth paying attention to. It's not flashy. It doesn't go viral. It gets saved. It gets shared to someone specific because it was exactly what they needed. That save means they're coming back. That share is a referral. Trust content is the slowest to grow and the most valuable thing you can make. Make more of it.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #solopreneur #digitalproducts #contentmarketing #creatoreconomy #buildingonline #audiencegrowth #entrepreneurship

**Post 4**
**Hook:** Conversion content doesn't need to be a sales post to sell.
**Caption:** A case study is conversion content. A walkthrough of how you use your own product is conversion content. A post that compares your solution to doing it manually is conversion content. None of those feel like ads, but all of them move people from passive followers to active buyers. The goal isn't to hide that you're selling. It's to make the value so clear that the sale is the obvious next step.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #solopreneur #digitalproducts #gumroad #contentmarketing #copywriting #creatoreconomy #buildingonline

**Post 5**
**Hook:** The ratio that works: 50% attract, 30% trust, 20% convert.
**Caption:** This isn't a law, it's a starting point. If your numbers look like 90% attract and 10% convert with nothing in the trust layer, you have an audience that likes your content and doesn't buy. If you're 80% convert, you have an audience that feels sold to and stops growing. The balance creates a flywheel: new people find you, start trusting you, then buy from you. Audit your last 30 posts and see which category each one falls into.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #solopreneur #contentmarketing #creatoreconomy #digitalcreator #buildingonline #entrepreneurship #audiencegrowth

**Post 6**
**Hook:** Educational content isn't automatically trust content. Most of it is just attract content with extra steps.
**Caption:** Teaching a concept attracts people who want to learn. Demonstrating that you've applied that concept, with real context and real results, is what builds trust. The difference between a creator who posts "5 tips for writing better copy" and one who posts "this is the specific headline I rewrote, the before, the after, and the conversion lift" is the difference between information and proof. Proof builds trust. Information gets saves.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #solopreneur #contentmarketing #creatoreconomy #digitalproducts #buildingonline #entrepreneurship #digitalcreator

**Post 7**
**Hook:** The highest-converting content type is the one most creators are afraid to make.
**Caption:** Failure content. Not performative "I failed and learned so much" content. Actual documentation of a decision that didn't work, what the data showed, and what you changed because of it. This is the content that converts because it destroys the "this person makes it look easy" objection. Buyers trust people who show them the real process, not just the result. Most creators are too afraid of looking imperfect to make it.
**Hashtags:** #buildinpublic #solopreneur #creatoreconomy #contentstrategy #contentmarketing #entrepreneurmindset #digitalcreator #transparency

**Post 8**
**Hook:** Repurposing content across formats isn't lazy. It's strategy.
**Caption:** One insight, four formats. A thread for the people who read. A short video for the people who scroll. A saved post for the people who want to return to it. A newsletter section for the people who want depth. The insight doesn't change. The format matches where that person lives. Most creators post one format and wonder why their reach plateaus. Distribution is part of the content strategy, not separate from it.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #contentrepurposing #solopreneur #creatoreconomy #digitalcreator #contentmarketing #buildingonline #entrepreneurship

**Post 9**
**Hook:** The content calendar should be built around the customer journey, not your content ideas.
**Caption:** If someone discovers you on Monday and buys your product by Friday, what does that path look like in terms of the content they consumed. Work backwards from that. What did they see first, what built trust, what made the purchase obvious. Most content calendars are built around "what should I post this week" rather than "what does my customer need to see next." One optimizes for output. The other optimizes for revenue.
**Hashtags:** #contentstrategy #solopreneur #contentmarketing #customerjourney #creatoreconomy #digitalproducts #buildingonline #entrepreneurship
---

**Post 10**
**Hook:** Every piece of content you make should have a job. Most don't.
**Caption:** Before you post, ask: is this meant to reach new people, build trust with existing followers, or move someone toward a purchase. If the answer is "I just thought it was interesting," that's fine, but know what you're trading for. Consistent content without intention is just noise with good design. Give each post a job. Attract. Trust. Convert. Measure whether it did the job. Iterate. That's the whole system.
