## Tweet 5
Thread: How to write content that actually converts (1/3)

Most content is too broad.
"10 tips for productivity" helps everyone, converts no one.
Specific problems convert.
"Why you lose focus at 2pm every day" speaks to someone.

(2/3)
The formula:
Name a specific problem.
Explain why the common solution fails.
Offer a different frame.
Let the product close.
The content doesn't sell. It qualifies.

(3/3)
You're not trying to convince anyone.
You're trying to identify people who already believe what you believe.
Content is a filter, not a pitch.

## Tweet 9
Thread: What I learned from shipping 5 digital products (1/4)

The first product teaches you how to make a product.
The second product teaches you what people actually want.
The third product makes money.

(2/4)
Most people quit after the first product fails.
They call it a failed experiment.
The successful ones call it research.
Same outcome. Different interpretation. Completely different next action.

(3/4)
Price higher than you're comfortable with.
The people who pay full price take it more seriously.
They get better results.
Better results become testimonials.
Testimonials become sales.

(4/4)
The goal is not a product.
The goal is a customer who trusts you enough to buy twice.
Everything before that is lead generation.
