"If I were starting a content business from scratch today, I would not start on TikTok. Here's what I'd do instead."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "The problem with TikTok as a starting point"]
High reach, low retention, algorithm-dependent, zero owned audience. You can go viral and have nothing to show for it three months later.

[on-screen: "The format I'd start with: long-form on YouTube"]
YouTube videos have the longest shelf life of any content format. A video published today can rank and drive traffic for five years. TikToks have a 48-hour window.

[on-screen: "The second layer: email from day one"]

[on-screen: "The third layer: repurpose everywhere else"]
Once the long-form content exists, everything else is a clip, a thread, a post. Short-form becomes distribution, not the primary asset.

[on-screen: "The thesis"]
Build assets that compound. Distribute everywhere. Own the relationship. That's the format that works in year two when algorithms shift.

## Script 3: The Metric That Actually Matters

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Followers, views, likes. None of them pay rent. Here's the only number that actually measures business growth."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "The vanity metric problem"]
Vanity metrics feel good and change nothing. A million views on a video that drives zero purchases is a marketing loss dressed up as a win.

[on-screen: "The metric: revenue per subscriber or follower"]
Take your monthly revenue. Divide it by your total audience size across all channels. That number tells you how effectively your content is converting into income.

[on-screen: "What good looks like"]
$1 per subscriber per month is a healthy benchmark for a content-led business. If you have 1,000 email subscribers and you're making $1,000/month from them, your system is working.

[on-screen: "How to improve it"]
Better lead magnets bring in higher-intent subscribers. Better content nurtures them faster. A clearer offer with less friction converts them more efficiently. Fix the funnel, not the follower count.

[on-screen: "The second metric that matters"]
Email open rate. If people open your emails, they trust you. Trust converts. Everything else is upstream noise.

**CTA (50-60s):**

**Caption:** Stop chasing followers. Start tracking revenue per subscriber. That one metric shift will change how you create, what you create, and who you create it for.

## Script 4: What 80 Batches Taught Me About Content

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Eighty batches of content. Here are the three things I know now that I didn't know at batch one."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "Lesson 1: The audience doesn't find you. You find them."]
You don't post and wait. You study where your audience already spends time, what they already search for, and what language they use. Then you show up in those places with that language.

[on-screen: "Lesson 2: Specificity is kindness"]
Vague content is lazy content. The more specific your examples, numbers, and cases, the more the right person feels like you're talking directly to them. Niche content builds trust faster than broad content.

[on-screen: "Lesson 3: The output compounds only if the system is right"]
Creating more content without a distribution system is just shouting louder. The content has to go somewhere that matters. Email, SEO, a community. Output without distribution is invisible.

[on-screen: "The one thing that never changed"]
Serve the audience before you sell to them. Every single time. Without exception.
