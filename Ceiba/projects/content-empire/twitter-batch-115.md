## Tweet 2
Thread: Why I build in public even when it's uncomfortable (1/4)

Transparency creates accountability.
When I say what I'm building, I have to build it.
The audience becomes the contract.

(2/4)
There's a second reason.
Watching someone figure something out in real time is more compelling than watching someone who already figured it out.
Process beats result in the attention economy.

(3/4)
Third reason: I learn faster.
When I explain what I'm doing publicly, I find the gaps in my own thinking immediately.
Writing is thinking.
Public writing is faster thinking.

(4/4)
The discomfort doesn't go away.
You just build tolerance for it.
And the compound value of 365 days of public building is not linear.
It's exponential.

## Tweet 5
Thread: The solopreneur's week that actually works (1/3)

Monday-Tuesday: create. Deep work. No meetings.
Wednesday: systems. Fix what broke, automate what repeated itself.
Thursday-Friday: distribute and respond.

(2/3)
This only works if you protect Monday and Tuesday.
No calls. No DMs. No distractions.
The creation window is the most fragile part of the week.
Guard it.

(3/3)
Sunday evening: review the week and set one intention for Monday.
Not a to-do list.
One thing.
The most important thing.
Do that first.

## Tweet 9
Thread: The three types of digital products and when to use each (1/4)

1. The quick win product. Under $30.
Solves one specific problem. Short. Actionable.
Purpose: build trust and collect buyers.
Not to get rich. To start a relationship.

(2/4)
2. The core offer. $47-$197.
Teaches a system. Multiple modules or a deep guide.
This is where your actual framework lives.
Most of your revenue will come from here.

(3/4)
3. The premium product. $300+.
Done-with-you or done-for-you.
Your time is in the price.
But leverage comes from making it productized, not custom.

(4/4)
Start with the quick win.
It builds your list, proves demand, and funds the next one.
Most people try to start with the premium product.
They get stuck and quit.
Build in order.
