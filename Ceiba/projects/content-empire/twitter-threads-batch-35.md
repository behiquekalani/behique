**Thread 3**
**Topic:** The content you publish today is still working in year 3

Tweet 1: Most people think of content as perishable. They post, it gets 24 hours of life, then it dies. That's the social media model. But the compounding content model works completely differently.

Tweet 2: A YouTube video about a specific, searchable topic will generate views in year 3. An essay that answers a specific question will rank in search results long after you wrote it. A Twitter thread that captures real insight gets shared by people who find it months later.

Tweet 3: The content that compounds is not content chasing trends. Trend content has a 48-hour shelf life. Evergreen content that solves a persistent problem has a 3-year shelf life. Same effort, 500x the return.

Tweet 4: The question to ask before publishing: "Will someone searching for this problem in 18 months find this useful?" If yes, the content has compounding potential. If no, it's trend content and it expires.

Tweet 5: Shift your content calendar toward evergreen. Fewer posts, but posts that keep working. One well-researched guide published monthly outperforms daily reactive posts over any 12-month period.

Tweet 6: Follow @behikeai for more on building content that compounds instead of content that expires.
