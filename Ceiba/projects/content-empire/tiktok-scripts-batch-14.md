### Script 1 — 5 Digital Products You Can Sell This Week

**Hook (0-3s):** You already know enough to sell a digital product. Here are five ideas you can ship this week.

**Setup (3-10s):** Not complicated courses. Not software. Simple, high-value files people will pay for.

**Content (10-55s):**
One — A swipe file. Collect 50 of the best examples of something in your niche. Email subject lines, Instagram hooks, cold outreach templates. Package it. Sell it for $7-15.

Two — A checklist. Write out the exact step-by-step process you use for something. Not an ebook. A clean, tight checklist. Canva template, one page. Priced at $5-9.

Three — A Notion template. Build the system you use. Content calendar, budget tracker, project manager. Export it. People pay $15-35 for good templates.

Four — A one-pager explainer. Pick a topic that confuses beginners. Write the clearest possible explanation. One page. $5. The volume makes it work.

Five — A resource list. Curate the 20 best free tools, sites, or links in your niche. Add a short description of each. One PDF. $7.

All five can be made in Canva or Notion. All five can be listed on Gumroad today.

**CTA (55-60s):** Which one are you building. Comment it.

**On-screen text:** "5 digital products you can build this week" / "Swipe file / Checklist / Notion / Explainer / Resource list" / "Gumroad = free to list"

**Trending sound direction:** Fast-paced but clear, upbeat without being chaotic, idea generation energy

### Script 2 — Why Notion Templates Print Money for Solo Creators

**Hook (0-3s):** Notion templates are one of the easiest digital products to create and one of the most consistent to sell.

**Setup (3-10s):** Here's why they work and what makes a template sell versus one that gets ignored.

**Content (10-55s):**
Why they sell:
People love Notion but hate building from scratch. They will pay to skip the setup.
The price point is low enough to impulse buy. High enough to profit at scale.
One template can sell thousands of times with zero extra work after you build it.

What makes a template actually sell:
It solves a specific pain. Not "a project manager." A project manager for freelance designers who bill by the hour.
It looks good on preview. Your Gumroad thumbnail is your entire sales pitch.
It comes with a short setup guide. People want to open it and immediately understand it.

Where to list it:
Gumroad. Etsy. Your own site if you have traffic.

How to price it:
Simple templates: $9-15.
Complex systems with multiple databases: $27-47.
Template packs (5+ templates): $47-97.

You can build a solid template in 2-3 hours. Price it at $15. Sell 10 a month. That's $150 from one weekend of work.

**On-screen text:** "Notion templates = passive digital income" / "Specific niche = more sales" / "One template, unlimited downloads"

### Script 3 — The Digital Product Category That Sells the Easiest

**Hook (0-3s):** If I had to choose one category to start with for digital products, it would be this.

**Setup (3-10s):** Templates. Not ebooks. Not courses. Templates.

**Content (10-55s):**
Here's why templates win for beginners.
No writing a 40-page guide. No recording video. No building a course platform.
You build the thing once. You hand it to someone else who immediately uses it.

The top-performing categories right now:
Social media templates — Canva carousel templates, Instagram grid layouts, TikTok thumbnail packs.
Business templates — Proposal templates, invoice formats, onboarding documents.
Life system templates — Budget trackers, weekly planners, habit systems.

What they all have in common:
They save someone time doing something repetitive.
They make something look better than the person could make it themselves.
The buyer sees the value instantly. No convincing needed.

How to find what to make:
Go to Etsy. Search your niche plus "template." Sort by bestsellers. See what's selling. Build a better version or a more specific version.

You're not copying. You're researching demand before building supply.

**CTA (55-60s):** Save this. And follow for the full template business breakdown.

**On-screen text:** "Templates = easiest digital product to sell" / "Save time + look better = sale" / "Research Etsy before building"

**Trending sound direction:** Clear and instructional, moderate tempo, background track stays out of the way

### Script 4 — Why Your Ebook Is Not Selling (and What to Do Instead)

**Hook (0-3s):** Your ebook is not selling. Not because it's bad. Because you're competing with free.

**Setup (3-10s):** Here's how to repackage what you know into something people actually buy.

**Content (10-55s):**
The problem with ebooks:
You wrote a guide. So did 10,000 other people. And some of them put theirs on Medium for free.
The information is not the product. The format and transformation is the product.

What sells instead of a flat ebook:
A workbook. Add exercises, fill-in prompts, reflection sections. Now it's interactive. Now it's $27 instead of $7.
A challenge kit. Turn the ebook into a 5-day challenge with daily actions. People pay for structure.
A done-with-you guide. Add templates, checklists, and swipe files inside the ebook. Now it's a toolkit. Price it at $37-97.

The rule: don't sell information. Sell transformation.
The buyer doesn't want to know how to do something. They want to actually do the thing.
Make your product do as much of the work for them as possible.

**CTA (55-60s):** Which format are you switching to. Drop it below.

**On-screen text:** "Why your ebook isn't selling" / "Information vs transformation" / "Workbook / Challenge / Toolkit"

**Trending sound direction:** Direct and pointed, slightly urgent, clean background music with forward momentum

### Script 5 — The $9 to $97 Digital Product Ladder

**Hook (0-3s):** The goal is not to sell one product. The goal is to build a product ladder.

**Setup (3-10s):** A product ladder means every sale leads naturally to the next purchase.

**Content (10-55s):**
Here's the structure:
$0 — Free lead magnet. One-page PDF. Solves one problem. Captures the email.
$9 — Entry product. Quick win. Checklist, swipe file, simple template. Converts email subscribers into buyers.
$27 — Core product. Deeper guide, Notion template system, or workbook.
$47-97 — Premium product. Bundle. Multiple templates, guide plus workbook, full toolkit.
$150+ — Service. Do it for them. Use the products as proof of expertise.

Why this matters:
Someone who buys a $9 product is seven times more likely to buy a $27 product from you than a cold visitor is.
Your buyer list is your most valuable asset. Not your follower count.

You don't need a huge audience. You need a well-designed ladder and consistent traffic into the free entry point.

Build the ladder once. Then drive traffic to the bottom of it.

**CTA (55-60s):** Follow. I break down exactly how to build traffic into this in the next video.

**On-screen text:** "$0 / $9 / $27 / $47-97 / $150+" / "Product ladder = repeat customers" / "Buyer list beats follower count"

**Trending sound direction:** Systematic and confident, building energy, background instrumental, progressive tempo
