Every business I've built started with less than most people spend on a weekend.

The constraint forced the creativity.

## Script 05: "Why Digital Products Beat Freelancing for Students" [EN]

**Hook (0-3s):**
Freelancing trades time for money. Digital products don't.

**Body (3-50s):**
I have done both. Here's the honest comparison.

Freelancing: you get paid when you work. You stop working, income stops. Good for learning, bad for scaling. Also: you're always selling yourself, which takes energy.

Digital products: you build once, sell many times. The guide I wrote in January still sells without me doing anything. That's a different relationship with time.

The downside: it takes longer to see money. A freelance job pays this week. A digital product might take 3 months to get traction.

For a student with flexible time and low fixed costs, the math favors digital products long-term.

Start small. One product. One specific problem solved. See if anyone buys it.

The answer to that question is worth more than any course.

**CTA (last 5s):**
Link in bio. My products are live, all under $20.

## Script 08: "Lo que aprendí perdiendo dinero en eBay" [ES]

**Hook (0-3s):**
Perdí dinero en eBay. Aquí está lo que aprendí.

**Body (3-50s):**
Compré tres productos sin investigar bien la demanda. Los listé. No se vendieron.

Dos meses después los vendí a pérdida solo para recuperar algo.

Lo que faltó: validación antes de comprar.

Ahora el proceso es al revés. Primero busco el producto en eBay y veo cuántos se han vendido en los últimos 30 días. Si hay ventas recientes, hay demanda. Si no hay, no compro.

Parece obvio. Pero la mayoría de los que empiezan en reselling lo saltan porque están emocionados con el producto.

La emoción es una señal para pausar y verificar, no para actuar.

El error me costó como $60. La lección vale mucho más.

**CTA (last 5s):**
Sígueme. Sigo testeando y sigo posteando lo que funciona y lo que no.

## Script 09: "Gumroad From Puerto Rico — What Nobody Tells You" [EN]

**Hook (0-3s):**
Gumroad works from Puerto Rico. Here's the actual setup.

**Body (3-50s):**
People ask me constantly if digital product platforms work if you're not in the US mainland.

Short answer: most of them do. Gumroad included.

Puerto Rico is a US territory. Stripe processes payments. Gumroad deposits directly. Taxes follow US rules because we're under US federal tax law.

The part that took me time to figure out: you need a US bank account or at least a US-compatible account. Most people here already have one. If not, a Wise account solves it.

What you also need: a product people want, a way to describe it clearly, and some distribution. That part has nothing to do with where you live.

I went from zero products to 9 listed in about 6 weeks. All from a laptop in PR.
