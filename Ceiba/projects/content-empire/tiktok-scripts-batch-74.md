## Script 74.1 — Creator Burnout Is a Systems Problem

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Creator burnout is not a motivation problem. It is a systems problem. Here is the difference."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: BURNOUT = SYSTEMS FAILURE, NOT WEAKNESS]
When you are posting every day and running out of ideas and energy at the same time, the instinct is to push harder. But pushing harder inside a broken system just breaks you faster.

[on-screen: WORKING HARDER INSIDE A BROKEN SYSTEM]
The real issue is usually one of three things. You are creating and distributing and engaging all on the same day. You have no idea bank, so every piece of content starts from zero. Or your output goals are tied to feelings instead of a schedule.

[on-screen: 3 ROOT CAUSES]
The fix is not a break. A break fixes the symptom. The fix is separating creation from distribution, building a topic bank before you need it, and setting output goals you can hit on your worst day.

[on-screen: SEPARATE CREATION FROM DISTRIBUTION]
Sustainable systems do not require motivation. They run on structure.

[on-screen: STRUCTURE REMOVES THE NEED FOR MOTIVATION]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Tell me one thing in your content process that drains you most. I will tell you which system is broken."

**Caption:** Burnout is not a motivation problem. It is a systems problem. Fix the system, not yourself. (93 chars)

## Script 74.2 — The Energy Management Approach to Content

**Hook (0-3s):**
"The best content creators do not manage their time. They manage their energy."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: TIME MANAGEMENT IS THE WRONG GAME]
You have the same twenty-four hours as everyone else. But you do not have the same energy every hour of every day. Treating all time as equal is why you produce low-quality work at 9pm after a full day.

[on-screen: ENERGY IS NOT EQUAL ACROSS THE DAY]
The framework: know when your creative energy is highest. For most people, it is the first two to three hours after waking. That window is for creation. Writing, filming, ideating.

[on-screen: CREATIVE HOURS = MORNING BLOCK]
The rest of the day is for execution. Editing, scheduling, responding to comments, admin. These tasks require less cognitive peak but still get done.

[on-screen: EXECUTION HOURS = EVERYTHING ELSE]
One more piece: protect your creative window like it is a meeting with your most important client. No notifications, no email, no social. Creation first.

[on-screen: PROTECT THE CREATIVE WINDOW]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Block your highest energy hours this week for creation only. See what changes in output quality."

**Caption:** Stop managing time. Start managing energy. Your best work happens in a two-hour window. Protect it. (100 chars)

## Script 74.3 — Content Sprints vs. Daily Posting

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Daily posting is killing your creativity and you think it is building your discipline."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: DAILY POSTING VS. CONTENT SPRINTS]
The conventional advice is post every day to stay relevant. And there is truth there. Consistency signals reliability. But daily posting when you are also running a business, building a product, and living a life burns through creative reserves fast.

[on-screen: DAILY POSTING BURNS CREATIVE RESERVES]
Content sprints are the alternative. One day per week, or one intensive week per month, dedicated entirely to producing content in bulk.

[on-screen: SPRINT = ONE HIGH-OUTPUT SESSION]
The psychology: constraints create creativity. Knowing you have six hours to produce thirty pieces of content removes the open-ended procrastination of "I will post something later today."

[on-screen: CONSTRAINTS PRODUCE CREATIVITY]
The output from a three-hour sprint, done in a creative peak state, will outperform five days of daily posting done half-heartedly.

[on-screen: 3-HOUR SPRINT > 5 DAYS OF HALF-OUTPUT]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Schedule one content sprint this week. Three hours. One topic set. See how much you produce."

**Caption:** Daily posting is a discipline story you tell yourself. Content sprints are a system that actually works. (103 chars)

## Script 74.4 — Automating Your Content Distribution

**Hook (0-3s):**
"If you are manually posting to every platform every day, you are doing the lowest-value work in your content business."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: DISTRIBUTION IS INFRASTRUCTURE, NOT WORK]
Creating the content is the work. Distributing it is infrastructure. Infrastructure should run without you once it is built.

[on-screen: BUILD ONCE, DISTRIBUTE ALWAYS]
The basic stack: schedule long-form content on Buffer or Later. Use Repurpose.io to auto-clip and cross-post. Set up a Make or Zapier workflow to push new content to your newsletter automatically.

[on-screen: BUFFER / REPURPOSE.IO / MAKE]
That covers YouTube to Shorts, LinkedIn to Twitter, newsletter repurposing, and social scheduling with almost no daily manual work.

[on-screen: WHAT GETS AUTOMATED]
What you keep doing manually: engaging with comments, responding to DMs, adjusting strategy based on data. The human layer stays. The logistics layer does not.

[on-screen: AUTOMATE LOGISTICS. KEEP THE HUMAN LAYER]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"One automation you can set up today: schedule your next five posts on Buffer. Do it before you close this app."

**Caption:** Distribution is infrastructure. Automate the logistics. Keep the human layer. That is the system. (100 chars)

## Script 74.5 — The Batch Day That Saves Your Week

**Hook (0-3s):**
"One day of focused batching gives you a full week of content. Here is exactly how to run it."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: THE ANATOMY OF A BATCH DAY]
A batch day is not a full workday. It is a three to four hour sprint with a clear input and a clear output.

[on-screen: 3-4 HOURS. CLEAR INPUT. CLEAR OUTPUT]
Input: your topic bank. Before batch day, you should already have fifteen to twenty topics written down. You pull from that list. You do not generate ideas on batch day. That is a different cognitive mode.

[on-screen: INPUT = TOPIC BANK, PREPARED IN ADVANCE]
Output: rough drafts for every piece you will post that week. Not polished. Not perfect. Rough. Editing is a different session, a different day.

[on-screen: OUTPUT = ROUGH DRAFTS ONLY]
The session structure: thirty minutes of warm-up writing, two hours of focused script or caption drafting, thirty minutes of review and rough scheduling.

[on-screen: 30 MIN WARMUP + 2 HRS DRAFT + 30 MIN REVIEW]
One batch day a week. That is the system that lets you stop thinking about content the other six days.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Block a batch day this week. Tell me the day you are doing it in the comments. Accountability starts now."

**Caption:** One batch day gives you a week of content. Here is the structure that makes it work every time. (97 chars)
