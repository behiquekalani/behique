# LinkedIn Batch 36 — EN
**Topic: Why I Focus on Email Over Social Media (The Ownership Argument)**
**5 Posts | 150-300 words each**

---

## Post 1: The Tenant Problem

Social media reach is not yours.

You have 10,000 followers. The platform shows your post to 400 of them. Next month they change the algorithm. Now it reaches 200.

You had no say in that decision. You cannot appeal it. You cannot prevent the next one.

This is the tenant problem. You are building on land someone else owns. The landlord can raise the rent, change the rules, or close the building entirely. Your followers, your content, your years of work, all of it is subject to conditions you did not set and cannot renegotiate.

Email is different.

When someone subscribes to your email list, they give you permission to put a message directly in their inbox. No algorithm decides who sees it. No platform throttles your reach based on advertising inventory.

The open rate on a healthy email list runs 25-40%. The organic reach of a social post on most platforms runs 3-7%.

I am not saying abandon social media. I use it for discovery and distribution. But I am not building my business on it.

The business lives on the list. Social is the funnel that fills it.

If you are building a content business and you do not have an email list yet, you are building with rented tools on rented land.

Start the list today. The asset compounds in a way that social following does not.

## Post 2: The Comparison No One Makes

Here is something most creators never calculate.

A social following of 10,000 people generates roughly 300-700 views per post organically.

An email list of 2,000 people generates 500-800 opens per email.

The email list is one-fifth the size and produces comparable or better reach.

And the email list actually earns revenue.

A 1% purchase rate on a $49 product, to a list of 2,000 people, is twenty sales per campaign. That is $980 from a single email. With no ad spend. No algorithm dependency. No platform risk.

What does a 10,000-follower social account generate in a single post? Maybe a few affiliate clicks if you are lucky. Almost certainly less than $200.

I am not anti-social media. I use it. But I am clear on what it is for: discovery and awareness. It is the top of the funnel.

The business, the actual revenue-generating operation, lives in the inbox. It always has for the creators who figure this out early.

The metric that matters is not follower count. It is the number of people who trust you enough to give you their email address and keep opening what you send.

That is the real audience. Everything else is reach.

## Post 3: Why I Quit Chasing Viral

In 2022 I spent three months trying to grow a social following.

I studied formats. I reverse-engineered trending posts. I posted daily. I chased every dopamine hit of a post that performed slightly better than average.

At the end of three months I had 1,200 more followers and no business.

Then I shifted all of that energy toward growing an email list. Same three months. Same creative energy. Different infrastructure.

At the end of those three months I had 800 email subscribers and a product that had sold $1,400 in its first week.

The followers produced engagement. The email list produced revenue.

I am not saying social media does not work. It works extremely well for what it is designed to do, which is keep you on the platform. It is not designed to help you build a business off the platform.

Email was designed for direct communication. It has worked for direct communication for thirty years. Every evolution in social media algorithms has made social less reliable for business. Email has gotten more reliable.

I still use social. But I measure my business health by list size, open rate, and revenue per subscriber. Not by follower count.

The shift in measurement changed everything.

## Post 4: The Metrics That Actually Predict Revenue

If I look at a creator's business and I want to predict their revenue potential, I do not look at their follower count.

I look at three things.

Email list size. Open rate. Average revenue per subscriber.

A creator with 3,000 email subscribers, a 35% open rate, and a $2/subscriber monthly revenue is generating $6,000/month. That is a real business.

A creator with 50,000 Instagram followers and no email list is generating $0 in recurring predictable revenue. Maybe some sponsorship income. Maybe some affiliate commissions. But nothing that compounds and nothing they own.

Follower count is a vanity metric unless it is paired with an owned distribution channel.

The math is simple. Revenue = audience you own x conversion rate x product value.

If the audience you own is zero, the formula produces zero regardless of how large the rented audience is.

I built my email list before I built anything else because it is the only number in that formula I fully control.

Start with ownership. Build reach on top of it.

## Post 5: What I Tell Every Creator Starting Out

Build the list first.

Not the channel. Not the brand. Not the product. The list.

Here is why.

The channel takes twelve months to grow. The brand takes two years to develop. The product can be built in a week if you know who you are selling it to.

If you spend your first year building an audience you own through email, you arrive at month twelve with a real asset. You have people who trust you. You have a channel to sell through. You have data on what they care about.

If you spend your first year building a social following, you arrive at month twelve with a number that does not translate directly to revenue without the missing piece.

The missing piece is always the email list.

I am not saying social is worthless. Discovery on social is real. But convert that discovery into something you own as fast as possible.

The sequence: social drives discovery, discovery drives email sign-ups, email drives revenue.

Each step matters. But the one that does not compound without the others is social reach. Email is where the compounding actually happens.

If you are a creator reading this and you have not started a list yet, the only question worth asking is: what am I waiting for?
