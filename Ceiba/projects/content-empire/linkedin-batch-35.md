# LinkedIn Batch 35 — Milestone: 35 Batches. 5 Things I'd Tell Myself About Building Online.

---

## Post 1

35 batches of content.

Not going to make it bigger than it is. But it's a thing worth noting, because when I started this I didn't know if I'd have 35 things to say.

Turns out you always have more to say than you think. The problem was never ideas. It was sitting down.

The one thing that made the difference: treating content creation as a system, not a motivation-dependent activity.

If you're waiting to feel inspired before you write, you'll write four times a year.

If you have a system, you write forty times a year and find that the inspiration comes from the act of sitting down, not before it.

---

## Post 2

The first thing I'd tell myself about building online: distribution is the product.

For the first year, I thought the product was the product. Make something good enough and people will find it.

They won't.

The best product with no distribution loses to a mediocre product with a real audience every single time. This is uncomfortable to accept because it feels like the world is rewarding the wrong thing.

It isn't. Distribution is a skill. Like any skill, people who develop it outperform people who assume they don't need it.

Build the audience first. Or build the product and the distribution at the same time. Never build the product alone and hope the rest follows.

---

## Post 3

The second thing I'd tell myself: the niche will emerge, not appear.

I spent months trying to define my niche before I started creating. Who is my audience? What's my unique angle? What makes me different from everyone else already doing this?

Those questions are not answerable before you ship something.

You find out who your audience is by publishing and seeing who responds. You find your angle by watching which posts land and which don't. You find what makes you different by doing the work and letting the pattern reveal itself.

Start before it's clear. Clarity comes from action, not from planning.

---

## Post 4

The third and fourth things:

Third: money takes longer than you think, but compounds harder than you expect. The first year is a tax on the next five years. Pay it without resentment.

Fourth: ignore the tools, obsess over the fundamentals.

The platform changes. The algorithm changes. The formats change. What doesn't change: writing that makes people think, content that solves real problems, trust built slowly over time.

Every month there's a new thing that's going to change everything. Most of those things are noise.

The fundamentals are the same now as they were in 2015. Go deep on those.

---

## Post 5

The fifth thing, and the one I think about most:

Build for the person you're talking to, not the person you want to impress.

The worst content I've ever produced was the content I made to look smart. It performs fine on vanity metrics and converts terribly.

The best content came from explaining something I genuinely understood to someone who genuinely needed it.

That's the whole model. Find the people who need what you know. Teach it to them clearly and honestly. Charge fairly for the premium version.

Everything else is details.

35 batches in. Still building.
