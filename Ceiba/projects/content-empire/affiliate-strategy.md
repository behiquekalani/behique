# Affiliate Strategy
## Two Sides: Running an Affiliate Program + Being an Affiliate

---

## PART 1: Running the Behike Affiliate Program

### Why This Matters

Affiliates are a multiplier. Every affiliate you recruit is a sales channel you didn't have to build. One well-placed affiliate in a newsletter with 5,000 subscribers can outsell a month of your own posting. And unlike paid ads, affiliates only cost you money when they actually make you money.

### Program Structure

- Platform: Gumroad's native affiliate system (no extra setup needed, already available on your account)
- Commission: 30% on all products
- Cookie window: 30 days
- Payment: automatic via Gumroad (they handle the payouts)
- Minimum payout threshold: $10
- Payment schedule: monthly, handled by Gumroad
- Tracking: Gumroad affiliate dashboard shows clicks, conversions, and revenue per affiliate

### Who to Recruit as Affiliates

Priority order based on conversion rate and ease of reaching them:

1. Former buyers who got results. These are your best affiliates because the testimonial is baked in. They already believe in the product. When they recommend it, it's a genuine referral, not a paid pitch. Reach out to anyone who left a positive review or DM'd you about results.

2. Small YouTube creators (1K-50K subscribers) in the solopreneur, productivity, or AI tools niche. Small creators have more engaged audiences than mega-creators. Their recommendation carries more weight per viewer. They're also more likely to respond to your DM.

3. Newsletter writers with 500-5,000 subscribers in the AI, tools, or builder space. Newsletter audiences are high-intent. A single mention in a relevant newsletter can drive $200-500 in sales from one email.

4. Twitter/X accounts in the builder and solopreneur community. Look for accounts that post about "tools I use" or "what I'm building." These people are already in recommendation mode.

5. Podcast hosts in the solopreneur space. Even small podcasts (1K-5K downloads/episode) can drive consistent affiliate sales over time because podcast content doesn't expire the way social media posts do.

6. Other Gumroad creators in adjacent niches. Reach out to creators selling productivity templates, design resources, or AI prompt packs. You promote their stuff, they promote yours. Cross-promotion is underused.

### Affiliate Recruitment Message Template

Use this as the starting point. Personalize the first two sentences based on their specific content. Never send a generic blast.

---

Subject: Quick partnership idea (Behike affiliate)

Hey [Name],

I've been following your [channel/newsletter/account] for a while. [Specific thing you liked, e.g., "Your breakdown of n8n automations last month was exactly what I needed."]

I run Behike, and I create digital guides for solopreneurs who want to use AI to work faster. My products are on Gumroad (guides on AI employees, social media pipelines, automation setups). They're priced $19-$35, so they're easy impulse buys for your audience.

I'd love to set you up as an affiliate. 30% commission, 30-day cookie, Gumroad handles all the tracking and payouts automatically.

I'll send over swipe copy, screenshots, and your affiliate link. You use it when it fits naturally in your content. No quotas, no pressure.

If you're interested, just reply and I'll get you set up in five minutes.

[Your name]

---

Notes on the template:
- Keep it under 150 words. Longer = lower response rate.
- Never say "I think your audience would love this." It sounds like every other pitch.
- Lead with what you liked about their work before asking for anything.
- Emphasize that Gumroad handles the logistics. People hate affiliate programs with manual tracking or delayed payouts.

### Affiliate Resources to Provide

When someone agrees to be an affiliate, send them a folder (Google Drive or Notion page) with:

- Their unique Gumroad affiliate link (generate this in your Gumroad affiliate settings)
- 3 email swipe copy variations (different angles: curiosity, results-focused, tool-focused)
- 2 Twitter thread swipe copy variations (one as a "tools I use" thread, one as a mini-review)
- 2 Instagram caption swipe copy variations (one short punchy, one storytelling format)
- Product screenshots: cover images, inside screenshots, results if available
- A one-paragraph "what is Behike" blurb they can use as context
- FTC disclosure language they can copy-paste ("This post contains affiliate links. I earn a commission if you purchase through my link at no extra cost to you.")

The easier you make it, the more they'll actually promote. Most affiliates won't write their own copy. Give them everything ready to paste.

### Tracking and Optimization

Gumroad's affiliate dashboard shows you:
- Clicks per affiliate link
- Conversion rate per affiliate
- Revenue generated per affiliate
- Which products each affiliate is driving sales for

Review this monthly. Look for:
- Affiliates driving clicks but no conversions (their audience might not be the right fit, or they need better swipe copy)
- Affiliates driving conversions but no clicks (they have high-intent audiences, worth recruiting more like them)
- Which products convert best through affiliates vs. direct traffic (this tells you what to promote in your own content too)

### Top Affiliate Bonus Structure

Once you have affiliates generating meaningful revenue, create a tiered bonus:

- Bronze (first sale): nothing extra, just the standard 30%
- Silver (10+ sales/month): 35% commission that month
- Gold (25+ sales/month): 40% commission + a personal shoutout in your newsletter
- Partner tier (50+ sales/month): revenue share conversation, co-created product possibility

Don't build this out immediately. Wait until you have 5+ active affiliates generating sales. Then introduce it.

## PART 2: Being an Affiliate

### The Principle First

Only recommend tools you actually use. Your audience trusts you because you're honest. The moment you push something you don't believe in, they can tell. One fake recommendation can cost you more in lost trust than the commission was worth.

The goal is to build a layer of passive revenue that comes from genuinely useful recommendations, not to squeeze affiliate income from every piece of content.

### How to Promote Without Losing Trust

The honest recommendation approach works in three steps:

1. Use the product first. Actually use it in your workflow. Have a real opinion.

2. Show how you use it, not just that you use it. "I use n8n" is weak. "I use n8n to automatically pull trending products from Google Trends and format them into my research doc every morning" is compelling. The specific use case is the proof.

3. Disclose the affiliate relationship naturally. "Link in the description is an affiliate link, meaning I get a small commission if you sign up. Doesn't change my recommendation. I've used this for six months."

Never structure your content around the affiliate link. Structure it around the genuine value. The link is just how you get paid for the recommendation you were going to make anyway.

### Where to Place Affiliate Links

**YouTube (highest conversion potential):**
- Pin the affiliate link in the description
- Say it once in the video at a natural moment ("I'll link this in the description")
- Add a card at the moment in the video where you reference the tool
- Include in the pinned comment
- Disclosure at the start of description: "Some links below are affiliate links. I earn a small commission at no extra cost to you."

**Newsletter:**
- "Tool I've been using" section. Keep it to one tool per issue max. More than that and it reads as a sponsored email.
- Inline mention during a tip: "I set this up in n8n [affiliate link]. Took about 20 minutes."
- Monthly "stack roundup" where you list everything you use, all with affiliate links, disclosed clearly.

**Social media:**
- Link in bio via Linktree. Keep your most relevant affiliate links (2-3 max) in the main Linktree alongside your product links.
- Story mention with "link in bio" for tools you've genuinely been using that week.
- Don't put affiliate links in Instagram post captions. It looks promotional. Save them for Stories and bio.

**Guides and products:**
- Any guide that references a specific tool should have an affiliate link on that tool.
- The AI Employee Guide, social media pipeline guide, and self-hosted store guide all have natural places to include affiliate links.
- Disclose at the start of the product: "Some links in this guide are affiliate links."

### Revenue Projections

Affiliate income is slow to start and compounds over time. Realistic numbers:

**Month 3 (small but growing audience):**
- 5 n8n Cloud signups: 5 x $20/mo x 20% = $20/mo recurring
- 3 Hostinger signups: 3 x $50 one-time x 60% = $90
- 2 ConvertKit signups: 2 x $29/mo x 30% = $17.40/mo recurring
- 2 NordVPN signups: 2 x $100 x 40% = $80 one-time
- Month 3 total: ~$200 (mix of one-time and recurring baseline)

**Month 6 (audience growing, content compounding):**
- 20 tool signups/month across all programs
- Average $15 commission per signup
- Recurring from previous months: ~$100/mo
- Month 6 total: ~$400/mo

**Month 12 (established audience, YouTube monetized):**
- 50+ signups/month across all programs
- Recurring commissions from previous months compounding
- YouTube descriptions driving passive signups on old videos
- Month 12 total: ~$800-1,500/mo

The compounding effect is real. A video you publish today will keep driving affiliate signups for 2-3 years. Every piece of content you publish is a long-term revenue asset.

### Non-Negotiable Rules

1. Only recommend tools you actually use or have tested thoroughly. No exceptions.
2. Always disclose the affiliate relationship. This is both an FTC legal requirement and a trust-builder.
3. Never change a recommendation because the commission is higher. If a better free alternative exists, recommend it alongside the paid option.
4. Affiliate revenue is a bonus, not the content strategy. Content comes first. If the content stops being useful, the affiliate revenue disappears too.
5. Check annually whether the tools you're promoting are still the best option. The AI tools space moves fast. Don't keep promoting something that has been surpassed.
