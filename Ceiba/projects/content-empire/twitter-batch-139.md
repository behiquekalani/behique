# Twitter/X Batch 139
## Topic: Why most people never start (and how to fix that)

## Tweet 1
Most people don't have a knowledge problem. They have a starting problem.

The fix isn't more research. It's a smaller first step.

## Tweet 2
You're not procrastinating. You're protecting yourself from the discomfort of being a beginner in public.

The solution is the same either way: do it publicly before you feel ready.

## Tweet 3
1/
The real reason people don't start:

2/
They've decided in their head what "good" looks like. Their first attempt will obviously fall short of that. So they don't attempt.

3/
The fix: decide that your first attempt IS good. Good means shipped. Good means exists. Good means you did it when you didn't have to.

## Tweet 4
"I'll start when I have more time" is rarely about time.

It's about the fact that time creates the perfect excuse to stay comfortable.

## Tweet 5
1/
The cost of not starting compounds too.

2/
Every week you don't post, someone else is building the audience in your niche.
Every week you don't ship, someone else is capturing the buyer you would have had.

3/
Not starting is not neutral. It's actively falling behind.

## Tweet 6
Perfect conditions for starting: non-existent.

Minimum conditions for starting: a laptop, a specific person you can help, and 2 hours.

## Tweet 7
1/
Starting is hard because you're conflating two separate decisions:

2/
Decision 1: Will I commit to doing this for 2 years?
Decision 2: Will I take one action today?

3/
You don't need to answer decision 1 to take action on decision 2. Stop asking yourself to decide the whole thing at once.

## Tweet 8
The person you want to become is not waiting at the end of a plan.

They're built incrementally through each day you chose to ship something instead of waiting.

## Tweet 9
1/
How to actually fix the starting problem:

2/
Make the unit of work embarrassingly small.
Not "launch my business this weekend."
Just: "write one thing today that I could post."

3/
Then post it.
Then do it again tomorrow.
The momentum builds faster than you expect.

## Tweet 10
1/
Most people are waiting for permission.

2/
Permission from the audience: start posting.
Permission from the market: ship something.
Permission from themselves: this is the hardest one.

3/
Nobody is coming to give you permission. That's the point. That's also the advantage. You can start right now.
