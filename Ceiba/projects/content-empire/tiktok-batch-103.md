## TikTok 2: The Solopreneur's Minimum Viable Calendar
**Hook (0-2s):**
"You don't need 30 days of planned content. You need 3 buckets."

**Script:**
Every piece of content you create fits into one of three buckets.

Bucket one: Proof. Document what you're doing. Show the process, the results, the failures. This builds trust.

Bucket two: Education. Teach one thing your audience needs to know. Keep it simple. One insight per post.

Bucket three: Perspective. Share an opinion. Take a stance. Disagree with something common. This builds loyalty.

If you post one from each bucket per week, you have a complete content strategy.

That's three posts. Not thirty. Not a launch calendar. Not a repurposing system with twelve moving parts.

Three posts. Every week. From three buckets.

The complexity can come later. Consistency comes first.

**CTA:**
Save this. Pick one bucket and post from it today.

## TikTok 3: Why You Keep Running Out of Content Ideas
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Running out of ideas isn't a creativity problem. It's a system problem."

**Script:**
You run out of content ideas because you try to come up with them in the moment.

That's the wrong approach.

Ideas don't come from sitting down and trying to think. They come from living, reading, working, and noticing things.

The fix: collect ideas as they happen. Every time you have a thought, a reaction, an insight, capture it somewhere. Don't try to write it out. Just capture the seed.

Then, when you sit down to create, you're not starting from zero. You're choosing from a list.

The solopreneur who never runs out of ideas doesn't have more creativity. They have a better capture system.

Your phone is already in your hand. Use it to capture the next idea you have in the next 24 hours.

## TikTok 4: Batch Your Content or Lose Your Mind
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Creating content every single day is unsustainable. Do this instead."

**Script:**
Daily content is a lie they sell to people who don't know better.

Not because posting daily is wrong. Because creating daily is impossible to sustain.

The solution: batch creation.

One day per week. Two to three hours. You write, script, or outline everything you plan to post that week.

Then you spend the rest of the week actually running your business.

Batching works because creative work requires a different mental state than operational work. Switching between the two, multiple times per day, costs you more than you realize.

When you batch, you get into a flow. Your ideas compound. Your tenth post in a session is always sharper than your first.

Batch on Sunday. Post every day. Look like you're always creating.

## TikTok 5: The Content Calendar for People Who Hate Calendars
**Hook (0-2s):**
"I don't use a content calendar. I use a content menu."

**Script:**
A calendar tells you what to post and when. That's rigid. It breaks when life does.

A menu gives you options. You pick based on what you have available that day.

Here's how a content menu works.

You have five to seven topics you always talk about. Those are your menu items.

Every time you create, you pick one. You write about it from a different angle, with a different format, for a different audience slice.

You never run out because the topics don't expire. You never get stuck because you always have something to choose from.

A calendar is a commitment you break. A menu is a system you use.

Build the menu once. Use it indefinitely.
