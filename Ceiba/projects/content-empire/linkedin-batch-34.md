# LinkedIn Batch 34 — The Creator's Guide to Choosing Platforms

---

## Post 1

The platform question is one of the most consistently mishandled decisions in creator businesses.

People pick platforms based on where everyone else is. Or based on which platform is growing fastest. Or based on where they think their audience is, without actually checking.

None of those are the right filter.

The right filter: where can you produce the best output given your specific strengths?

A writer who forces themselves onto YouTube will produce worse content than a writer who builds a Substack. The platform advantage never overcomes the output quality gap.

Your strengths determine your platform. Not the other way around.

---

## Post 2

The honest platform breakdown for solopreneurs in 2026:

Writing: Substack, LinkedIn, X/Twitter. Long-form builds authority. LinkedIn is still underused relative to the professional audience there. Substack compounds well over time.

Short video: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts. High discovery, low per-follower value, works best as a top-of-funnel feeding something else.

Long video: YouTube. The only short-form equivalent that also builds deep trust over time. Slow to start, durable once established.

Audio: Podcasts are saturated at the top, but niche podcasts with focused audiences still convert well for products and services.

Email: Not a platform. Infrastructure. Everyone needs it regardless of what else they do.

---

## Post 3

The multi-platform trap:

You start a newsletter. It starts growing slowly. Someone says you should be on TikTok. You start TikTok. It grows slowly. Someone says you should have a YouTube channel. You start a channel.

Now you have three platforms, all mediocre, none of them working.

The math on focus is not subtle.

One platform done well generates 10x the results of three platforms done poorly. This is not a rough estimate. It's what you see when you look at creators who actually make money.

Pick one. Get good at it. Add a second only when the first is running on a system.

---

## Post 4

How to know if you've picked the wrong platform:

You dread creating for it. Not occasionally. Consistently.

You produce output only when you have to, not when you have something to say.

Your engagement is flat after six months of consistent posting.

You're mimicking other creators rather than developing your own voice.

Any of these is a signal, not a verdict. Platforms can be changed. But most people ignore these signals for a year before admitting the fit isn't there.

The faster you admit it, the faster you find the thing that works.

---

## Post 5

The platform that tends to be most underestimated by solopreneurs: LinkedIn.

Most people treat it as a resume site. It's not.

LinkedIn's organic reach is better than almost any other platform right now for professional content. A post that would reach 2% of your followers on Instagram can reach 10-20% on LinkedIn. The algorithm still rewards good content because the competition is lower.

The audience is buyers. Professionals who make purchasing decisions, have disposable income, and consume content during work hours when they're in a decision-making mindset.

If you're selling anything to professionals, you should be on LinkedIn before you're on most other platforms.
