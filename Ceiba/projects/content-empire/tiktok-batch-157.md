## Script 2: The Only Writing Framework You Need
**Hook (0-3s):** There is one writing formula behind almost every sale online.
**Body:** Problem. Agitation. Solution. That is it. You name a problem your reader has. You remind them how much it costs them. Then you offer the solution. This is not manipulation. It is clarity. You are helping someone connect the dots between their pain and your answer. Most people skip the agitation step. They name the problem and jump to the offer. That gap is where you lose the sale. Sit in the problem longer. Let them feel it.
**CTA:** Save this. It is worth more than most courses.
**On-screen text:** ["Problem. Agitation. Solution.", "Skip agitation = lose the sale", "Sit in the problem longer", "PAS framework"]

## Script 3: Your Bio Is a Sales Page
**Hook (0-3s):** Your Instagram bio loses you money every single day.
**Body:** Most people write their bio like a resume. Name. Title. Emoji. Location. That tells me who you are. It does not tell me why I should follow you. A good bio answers one question: what do you give me? Not what you do. What the reader gets. Rewrite your bio as an offer. Tell me exactly what I will learn, get, or become by following you. That one change can double your follow rate.
**CTA:** Go rewrite your bio right now. Then come back and tell me what changed.
**On-screen text:** ["Bio = sales page", "What does the reader GET?", "Not who you are. What they get.", "Rewrite the bio, double the follows"]

## Script 4: Short Sentences Print Money
**Hook (0-3s):** Long sentences kill conversions. Here is why.
**Body:** Every extra word is a decision point. The reader has to decide whether to keep going. Short sentences make that decision easy. Yes. Keep going. Long ones make them work. And online, when something feels like work, people stop. This is especially true on mobile. People are scrolling in line at the grocery store. Write for that person. One idea. One sentence. Then the next one. Your writing starts converting when it stops feeling like reading.
**CTA:** Follow for more writing tactics that actually move money.
**On-screen text:** ["Long sentences = decision fatigue", "Short = easy to keep reading", "One idea, one sentence", "Write for the grocery store line"]

## Script 6: The Testimonial Does the Selling
**Hook (0-3s):** Your customer's words sell better than yours ever will.
**Body:** You saying your product is good means nothing. A stranger saying it means everything. Social proof works because the buyer identifies with the reviewer, not the seller. When someone reads a testimonial and thinks "that sounds like me," the sale is already close. Your job is to collect specific testimonials. Not "great product." But "I made $400 in my first week after using this guide." Specificity is credibility.
**CTA:** Save this and go collect one specific testimonial today.

## Script 7: Write Like You Talk
**Hook (0-3s):** The most converting copy sounds like a person, not a brand.
**Body:** Brands write things like "we are committed to delivering exceptional value." Nobody talks like that. Nobody trusts that. People buy from people. When your copy sounds like a real human having a real conversation, the guard comes down. The resistance drops. Read your writing out loud. If you would not say it in a conversation, cut it. The conversational tone is not unprofessional. It is just honest.
**CTA:** Follow for copy that sounds human and converts.
**On-screen text:** ["Sound like a person", "Read it out loud test", "Conversational = honest", "Nobody trusts brand speak"]

## Script 8: The Hook Is 80% of the Work
**Hook (0-3s):** If the first line is bad, nothing else matters.
**Body:** 80% of people decide whether to keep reading after the first sentence. That means 80% of your writing ROI lives in the hook. Not the body. Not the close. The hook. A good hook creates a pattern interrupt. It says something the reader did not expect. Or asks a question they cannot not answer. Or makes a bold claim they want to verify. Spend more time on the first sentence than on anything else. Then everything else has a chance.
