# Instagram Batch 44: Pricing Yourself Right
# Account: @behikeai
# Topic: Underpricing is worse than overpricing

---

**Post 1**
**Hook:** Underpricing does not make you more accessible. It makes you less credible.
**Caption:** There is a psychological threshold below which a price signals low quality regardless of what the product actually delivers. People do not buy the cheapest option when they care about the outcome. They buy at the price point that matches the result they expect. If you charge $10 for a $200 result, the buyer does not think "great deal." They think "what is wrong with this." Price is the first signal your market receives about what you think your work is worth.

**Post 2**
**Hook:** The price you set is a statement about how seriously you take your own work.
**Caption:** Every time you discount before anyone asks, you are signaling that you do not believe the full price is justified. Every time you apologize for your rates, you are giving the other person permission to negotiate. Confidence in your price comes from clarity on what the outcome is worth to the buyer. Not what the work cost you to produce. What the result is worth to them. When you anchor your price to the buyer's outcome, the number becomes easier to defend.
**Hashtags:** #pricing #solopreneur #pricingconfidence #productpricing #builderonline #digitalproducts #creatoreconomy #valueprice #onlinebusiness #priceright

**Post 3**
**Hook:** Cheap customers are the most expensive customers you will ever have.
**Caption:** The buyer who haggles your price down by 40 percent will also demand 40 percent more support, question every deliverable, and leave the most detailed negative review when the outcome does not match their inflated expectations. Low price attracts low-trust buyers. High price filters for people who are serious about the outcome and willing to do their part to get there. Raise your prices. You will do less work and get better results for your clients at the same time.

**Post 4**
**Hook:** The price objection is almost never actually about the price.
**Caption:** When someone says "that is too expensive," they are usually saying one of three things: they do not believe the outcome is real, they do not believe you specifically can deliver it, or the timing is wrong. None of those are solved by lowering the price. Lowering the price confirms their doubt. The real work is in the copy, the proof, and the positioning that comes before the number. If you are getting consistent price objections, the problem is upstream from the price tag.
**Hashtags:** #pricing #solopreneur #salespsychology #priceobjection #productpricing #builderonline #digitalproducts #creatoreconomy #copywriting #onlinebusiness

**Post 5**
**Hook:** Raise your prices by 30 percent today and see what happens.
**Caption:** Most people discover that nothing terrible happens. Some people say no. The ones who say yes are better clients, generate the same or more revenue, and take less time to serve. The mental block around raising prices is almost always bigger than the actual market resistance. The market is willing to pay more than you think. You are the constraint, not the customer. Set a number that feels slightly uncomfortable to say out loud. That is usually close to where you should be.

**Post 6**
**Hook:** A $27 product and a $97 product require the same amount of effort to create. The math is not close.
**Caption:** You write the guide. You design the PDF. You build the sales page. You set up the payment processor. You handle the delivery. All of that work is identical whether the price is $27 or $97. At $97 you need 28 sales to hit $2,700. At $27 you need 100 sales. You are doing 3.5 times the marketing work for the same revenue. Price is leverage. Use it. There is no award for having the most affordable product in your category.

**Post 7**
**Hook:** The person who pays more uses the product more. That is not a coincidence.
**Caption:** When something costs you $200 you use it. When it costs $9 you forget it exists. This matters for creators because results drive referrals. A $200 course that people actually complete generates testimonials, word-of-mouth, and repeat buyers. A $9 course that sits untouched generates refund requests and silence. Pricing your work appropriately is not just about your revenue. It is about creating the conditions where your buyers actually get the result you promised them.

**Post 8**
**Hook:** Overpricing a bad product teaches you something. Underpricing a good product teaches you nothing.
**Caption:** If you price high and the market rejects it, you get real feedback. Maybe the product needs more proof of concept. Maybe the positioning is off. Maybe the audience you are targeting does not have that budget. All of that is useful information. If you underprice and sell well, all you learn is that you can sell cheaply. You never learn what your ceiling is. You never build the confidence that comes from commanding a real price. Test the upside. You can always come down.
**Hashtags:** #pricing #solopreneur #productpricing #pricetest #builderonline #creatoreconomy #digitalproducts #onlinebusiness #businesslearning #pricestrategy

**Post 9**
**Hook:** Your price is part of your brand. Low price brands are hard to move upstream.
**Caption:** Once you establish yourself as the affordable option in a market, that becomes the expectation. Moving up in price requires resetting the audience's perception of who you are, which is significantly harder than establishing the right positioning from the beginning. The builders who succeed long-term stake out a clear value position early and hold it. Being known as "the serious option" is a better brand position than "the cheap option" in almost every category.
**Hashtags:** #pricing #solopreneur #brandpositioning #productpricing #builderonline #creatoreconomy #digitalproducts #onlinebusiness #brandbuilding #pricestrategy

**Post 10**
**Hook:** The real cost of underpricing is not lost revenue. It is the ceiling it puts on your entire business.
**Caption:** When you underprice, you build a customer base that expects low prices. You train your market that your work is in the budget tier. You make it functionally impossible to raise prices without losing your existing audience. And you do all of this work, all of this marketing, all of this content creation, to build something that cannot grow past a certain point because the math never works. The short-term comfort of easy sales is the long-term trap. Price for where you want to be, not where you are.
