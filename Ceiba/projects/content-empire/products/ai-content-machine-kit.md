## Section 1: The Content Machine Concept

The core problem with most content strategies is that they treat every piece of content as a separate creative act. Every post starts from zero. Every tweet requires a spark of inspiration. Every newsletter requires a new idea.

That is not how output-focused creators work.

The content machine operates on a different model: one input generates multiple outputs across multiple formats and platforms. A single long-form idea becomes a YouTube video, five tweets, three Instagram carousels, a LinkedIn post, two TikTok scripts, and a newsletter section. All from one source.

This is not repurposing in the lazy sense. It is transformation. Each piece of content is adapted for the platform, the format, and the audience behavior on that platform. A tweet is not a truncated newsletter paragraph. A TikTok script is not a blog post read aloud. The raw material is the same. The execution is platform-native.

**How the machine works:**

```
ONE CORE IDEA
     |
     v
Long-form anchor content (1,500-3,000 words)
     |
     +---> YouTube video script (8-12 min)
     +---> 5 Twitter/X threads (200-400 words each)
     +---> 3 Instagram carousels (10 slides each)
     +---> 2 TikTok scripts (60-90 seconds)
     +---> 1 LinkedIn post (500-800 words)
     +---> 1 Newsletter section (300-500 words)
```

From one idea, properly developed, you get 13+ pieces of content. That is a week of posting across platforms from a single Sunday session.

**Time budget for the full machine:**

| Phase | Time |
|-------|------|
| Idea selection + core development | 30 min |
| AI prompt session (anchor content) | 20 min |

| Scheduling | 15 min |
| **Total per week** | **~2.5-3 hours** |

Run this four times a month. That is 30 days of content in three hours of actual work per week, spread across four sessions.

## Section 2: Your Content Core

Before you touch any AI tool, you need three things defined. Without these, you are generating noise. With them, every piece of content you produce compounds toward something.

**Step 1: Pick your three core topics**

Your content should live at the intersection of:
- What you know deeply
- What your audience is trying to solve
- What you can talk about consistently without burning out

Three topics. Not ten. Not two. Three.

Example for a solopreneur in the creator economy:
1. Building digital products (the business)
2. AI tools for productivity (the how)
3. Working independently without burning out (the philosophy)

Every piece of content you produce should connect to at least one of these three.

**Step 2: Define your three angles**

Within each topic, you have angles. An angle is how you approach the topic. It determines the frame of the content, which determines whether someone reads it or scrolls past.

The three angles that consistently perform:
- **Contrarian** ("Everyone says X. Here is why they are wrong.")
- **How-to** ("Here is the exact process I used to achieve Y.")
- **Story** ("I failed at Z. Here is what I learned.")

Most content should use the contrarian or how-to angle. Stories work best when you have a specific lesson to anchor them to.

**Step 3: Build your content bank**

Before your Sunday batch session, you need a running list of ideas. This is your content bank.

The content bank is not a brainstorm dump. It is a curated list of ideas that have already passed a basic filter:

1. Does this connect to one of my three topics?
2. Can I say something specific and non-obvious about this?
3. Is there a clear outcome the reader gets from this piece?

If the answer to all three is yes, it goes in the bank. If not, it stays in drafts until it develops.

**Minimum viable content bank:** 20 ideas before you start batch creating. At 13 pieces per idea, that is 260 pieces of content. More than enough runway to build momentum without running dry.

## Section 3: The AI Workflow

This is the core of the system. The prompts below are sequenced to take you from a raw idea to a full week of platform-ready content.

**The workflow runs in three phases:**

**Phase 1: Anchor development (20 min)**

This is the long-form piece that everything else gets derived from. Do not skip this. The quality of your anchor content determines the quality of everything downstream.

```
PROMPT 1 — Anchor content development

You are a content strategist helping me develop a long-form anchor piece.

Topic: [YOUR TOPIC]
My three core topics: [TOPIC 1], [TOPIC 2], [TOPIC 3]
Target audience: [WHO READS YOUR CONTENT]
Angle: [contrarian / how-to / story]

Write a comprehensive, structured piece (1,500-2,000 words) covering:
- A specific, non-obvious insight as the lead
- 3-5 supporting points with concrete examples
- A practical takeaway for each point
- A closing that connects back to the bigger picture

Tone: Direct, no hype. Write for a builder audience that can spot empty content.
No filler sentences. Every paragraph must earn its place.
```

**Phase 2: Platform adaptation (45 min)**

Once you have your anchor, run it through each platform prompt in sequence.

```
PROMPT 2 — Twitter/X thread

Using the anchor content below, write 5 Twitter/X threads.

Each thread should:
- Start with a single hook tweet (max 280 chars) that creates a knowledge gap
- Include 6-10 tweets per thread
- Use specific numbers, examples, or contrarian claims in the hook
- End with a reply-bait question or a clear summary

Vary the entry points: each thread should highlight a different insight from the anchor.
Do not copy paragraphs. Rewrite each point for the Twitter format.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

```
PROMPT 3 — Instagram carousels

Using the anchor content below, write 3 Instagram carousel scripts.

For each carousel:
- Slide 1: Hook (bold claim or contrarian statement, max 15 words)
- Slides 2-9: One point per slide, kept to 2-3 sentences max
- Slide 10: CTA (what should they do next)

Each carousel should have a different focus. Pull different sub-topics from the anchor.
Keep slide copy clean. No paragraphs. Scannable at a glance.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

```
PROMPT 4 — TikTok scripts

Using the anchor content, write 2 TikTok/Reels scripts.

Each script should be 60-90 seconds when read at a natural pace.

Structure:
- Hook (0-3 seconds): pattern interrupt, bold statement, or question
- Problem setup (3-15 seconds): why this matters
- Core value (15-75 seconds): the main insight, step by step
- CTA (75-90 seconds): follow for more, comment, or share prompt

Write the full script as spoken word. Natural language. Not bullet points.
No corporate language. This should sound like a person talking, not a webinar.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

```
PROMPT 5 — LinkedIn post

Using the anchor content, write 1 LinkedIn post (500-800 words).

LinkedIn format:
- First line must be a hook that stops the scroll (no fluff opening)
- Short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences max
- Use line breaks strategically for rhythm
- Include a practical takeaway that makes the reader look competent for sharing it
- End with a question to drive comments

Audience: operators, founders, solopreneurs, creators who build things.
Avoid buzzwords and corporate language.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

```
PROMPT 6 — Newsletter section

Using the anchor content, write 1 newsletter section (300-500 words).

Newsletter format:
- Section title (3-6 words)
- Lead paragraph that earns the reader's attention immediately
- The main insight broken into 2-3 supporting points
- One concrete action the reader can take today
- No soft endings. Close with something they will remember.

Write for an audience that opened the email because they trust you.
Do not repeat what you said in the subject line. Go deeper.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

**Phase 3: YouTube script (if applicable)**

Run this separately if you are producing YouTube content that week.

```
PROMPT 7 — YouTube video script

Using the anchor content, write a full YouTube video script (8-12 minutes when read at speaking pace).

Structure:
- Hook (0-30 seconds): A specific claim or question that tells the viewer exactly what they will get
- Intro (30-90 seconds): Why this matters, who it is for, what they will leave with
- Section 1-3 (main content): Each section has a clear sub-topic, specific examples, and a mini-conclusion
- Outro (60-90 seconds): Summary, next step, subscribe ask tied to specific value

Include [B-ROLL] markers where visuals would help.
Include [PAUSE] markers for natural breathing points.
Write in spoken word style. Not essay style.

[PASTE ANCHOR CONTENT]
```

## Section 4: Platform Breakdown

Each platform has different audience behavior. Content that works on LinkedIn will not work on TikTok. Here is what actually matters on each one.

**Instagram**

Format priority: Carousels first, then Reels, then static posts.

Carousels are the highest-leverage format on Instagram right now. They get saved, which signals to the algorithm that the content has long-term value. They also get shared to Stories.

What performs: actionable lists, step-by-step frameworks, before/after comparisons, myth-busting slides.

What does not: generic inspiration, low-information posts, anything that could have been a tweet.

Posting cadence: 4-5 times per week. At least 3 of those should be carousels.

**Twitter/X**

Format priority: Threads first, then single tweets.

Single tweets work for observations and takes. Threads work for teaching. If you are building an audience as a creator or solopreneur, threads are where you grow.

Hook quality is everything. If your opening tweet does not create a knowledge gap or make a specific claim, the thread dies before it starts.

What performs: contrarian takes with evidence, numbered frameworks, personal failure + lesson stories, specific data points.

Posting cadence: 1 thread per day, 3-5 single tweets per day.

**TikTok**

Format priority: Talking head or screen recording with voiceover.

You do not need production quality. You need a hook in the first three seconds and a reason to keep watching. TikTok is about momentum, not polish.

What performs: "I did X and here is what happened," "Stop doing X, do this instead," specific tutorials with real results.

Length: 45-90 seconds for educational content. Under 30 seconds for pure hooks or observations.

Posting cadence: 1-2 per day if you are building, 5 per week minimum.

**YouTube**

Format priority: Long-form evergreen content (8-20 min), then Shorts.

YouTube is a search engine first. Every video should target a specific search term or question your audience is already looking up.

Shorts work as a top-of-funnel discovery tool. They do not replace long-form. They feed it.

What performs: tutorials, case studies, specific how-to videos with clear outcomes in the title.

Posting cadence: 1-2 long-form per week, 3-5 Shorts.

**LinkedIn**

Format priority: Text posts, then document posts (carousels), then video.

LinkedIn rewards professional insight and personal story more than any other platform. The audience there is in work mode. They want things they can apply, share with colleagues, or use to think better about their business.

What performs: lessons from specific failures, frameworks with professional application, counterintuitive business observations.

Posting cadence: 3-5 times per week.

**Newsletter**

This is the only platform you own. Everything else is borrowed attention.

Your newsletter is where you go deeper, show more of your thinking, and build the kind of relationship that makes someone actually buy what you sell.

Frequency: Weekly minimum. Twice weekly if you have the content.

Structure: Keep it consistent. Same sections every issue. Readers should know what they are getting before they open it.

## Section 5: The Weekly Production Sprint

The sprint runs on Sunday. One session, three hours maximum. By the end, the full week is scheduled.

**Sunday Session Structure**

**9:00 - 9:30 | Idea selection and core development**

Pull from your content bank. Pick one idea for the anchor. Do not deliberate. If it made it into the bank, it is good enough.

Run it through the anchor development prompt. Read the output. Edit what sounds wrong. Add any specific examples or data points that you know but the AI did not have.

This editing step is not optional. AI-generated anchor content without your real perspective is generic. Your examples, your numbers, your stories are what make it worth reading.

**9:30 - 10:15 | Platform adaptation**

Run through prompts 2-6 in sequence. Do not stop to review each one deeply. Get the raw output first.

**10:15 - 10:45 | Review pass**

Read every piece with this filter: does this sound like me? Cut anything that sounds like a press release, a LinkedIn influencer parody, or a motivational poster.

Replace generic claims with specific ones. "AI can save you time" becomes "This prompt sequence cut my content production from 4 hours to 45 minutes." Specificity is credibility.

**10:45 - 11:00 | Scheduling**

Load everything into your scheduling tool of choice (see Section 8). Set the times. Walk away.

**The 30-day math:**

Four Sunday sessions x 13 pieces per session = 52 pieces of content per month. That is enough for:
- Instagram: 5 posts per week
- Twitter: 1 thread per day
- TikTok: 3 posts per week
- LinkedIn: 3 posts per week
- Newsletter: 4 issues per month
- YouTube: 2-4 videos per month

All from four three-hour sessions.

## Section 6: Repurposing Matrix

This is the breakdown of how each content type feeds the others. Use this as a reference when you have existing content you want to extend.

| Source | Becomes |
|--------|---------|
| YouTube video (10 min) | 5 Twitter threads, 3 carousels, 2 TikToks, 1 newsletter |
| Newsletter issue | 3 carousels, 2 Twitter threads, 1 LinkedIn post |
| Twitter thread | 1 carousel, 1 TikTok script, 1 LinkedIn post |
| Podcast episode | YouTube video script, 5 Twitter threads, 3 carousels |
| Blog post | YouTube script, 5 Twitter threads, 3 carousels, 2 TikToks |

**Long-form to short-form to micro-content:**

Level 1 (Long-form): YouTube video, blog post, podcast episode, newsletter issue. 1,000-3,000+ words/8+ minutes.

Level 2 (Short-form): Twitter thread, Instagram carousel, LinkedIn post. 200-800 words.

Level 3 (Micro-content): Single tweet, Instagram story, TikTok clip, YouTube Short. Under 100 words or under 60 seconds.

One Level 1 piece produces 3-5 Level 2 pieces. Each Level 2 piece produces 3-5 Level 3 pieces.

That is a 1-to-25 ratio from a single anchor piece.

## Section 8: Scheduling Stack

**Free tools**

**Buffer (free tier):** 3 channels, 10 scheduled posts per channel. Good for starting out. Clean interface. Covers Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, Facebook.

**Later (free tier):** Best visual planning for Instagram. Drag-and-drop calendar. You can see the grid layout before posting. Limited on Twitter and LinkedIn.

**TweetDeck:** Free, Twitter-only, unlimited scheduling. If Twitter is your main platform, this is all you need.

**Meta Business Suite:** Free for Instagram and Facebook. Allows scheduling Reels, carousels, and stories. Native scheduling has zero algorithmic penalty (unlike third-party tools).

**Paid tools (if you scale)**

**Buffer Essentials ($6/month):** Removes the post limit. Worth it once you are posting consistently across 3+ platforms.

**Hypefury ($19/month):** Twitter/X specific. Auto-retweet top posts, thread scheduling, auto-DM sequences. Worth it if Twitter is your primary platform and you are posting daily.

**Metricool ($22/month):** All-in-one. Covers every platform, analytics, best time to post based on your own data. The most complete free alternative has decent functionality but limits.

**What actually works**

Native scheduling where it exists (Meta Business Suite for Instagram/Facebook, LinkedIn's built-in scheduler, TweetDeck for Twitter) has no algorithmic disadvantage and is free.

For a creator posting 5 platforms with weekly batching, the minimal stack is:
- Meta Business Suite (Instagram, free)
- TweetDeck or Buffer (Twitter, free)
- LinkedIn native scheduler (free)
- Later or Buffer (TikTok, once you hit the limit, $6/month)

Total cost to start: zero. Total cost when you are posting daily across all platforms: $6-22/month.

## Section 9: Metrics That Matter

Most creators track the wrong things. Follower count is a vanity metric. Likes are a weak signal. Here is what to actually watch.

**What matters**

**Saves (Instagram):** The single most important metric on Instagram. A save means someone thought this was worth coming back to. The algorithm weights saves heavily because they indicate long-term value.

**Click-through rate (newsletter):** Tells you if your content is actually driving action. Open rate tells you if your subject line works. Click-through tells you if your content works.

**Reply rate (newsletter):** If people reply to your newsletter, you have built something real. This is the strongest signal of genuine connection.

**Profile visits (Twitter, Instagram, TikTok):** When someone visits your profile after seeing a post, that post did its job. Track which content types drive the most profile visits.

**Watch time percentage (YouTube, TikTok):** Did people watch to the end? If your average watch time is below 40%, your content is losing people in the middle. Find where they drop off and fix it.

**Link clicks (across platforms):** If you are trying to drive traffic to a product, page, or email list, this is the metric that feeds your business.

**What to ignore**

- Total follower count (lagging indicator, can be gamed)
- Impressions (reach means nothing without action)
- Likes on their own (low-friction, low-signal)
- Comments that say "great post" (no information content)

**Review cadence**

Weekly: check saves, click-throughs, and profile visits. Note which content performed best.

Monthly: look at trend lines. Are saves increasing? Is reply rate growing? Which platform is converting best?

Quarterly: adjust your content mix based on what is actually working. If carousels are outperforming threads, shift the ratio.

## Section 10: The 90-Day Plan

This plan assumes you are starting from zero or near-zero. It is designed to build the machine before trying to scale it.

**Week 1-2: Foundation**

- Define your three core topics and three angles
- Build your content bank to 20 ideas
- Set up your scheduling accounts (free tools)
- Run one full Sunday sprint
- Publish everything for the first week manually to understand each platform

No audience analysis yet. No optimization. Just ship.

**Week 3-4: First batch**

- Run two Sunday sprints
- You now have two full weeks of content scheduled ahead
- Start tracking: saves, profile visits, reply rates
- Identify your strongest performing platform (the one where posts are getting the most saves or engagement per follower)

**Month 2: Doubling down**

- Run four Sunday sprints
- You now have a full month of content live or scheduled
- Focus 60% of your energy on your strongest platform
- Maintain presence on the others at minimum viable frequency
- Add YouTube Shorts to the mix (short-form video compounds differently than text)
- Start building your email list: add a sign-up CTA to every content piece

**Month 3: Optimization**

- You now have real data. Use it.
- Look at your top 10 posts across all platforms. What do they have in common?
- Run more variations of what works. Kill what does not.
- Add a lead magnet if you do not have one: one free resource that solves a specific problem for your audience
- Begin connecting content to a product or service offer

**Week 12 benchmark (reasonable, not guaranteed):**

| Platform | Target |
|----------|--------|
| Instagram | 300-800 followers |
| Twitter/X | 200-500 followers |
| Newsletter | 100-300 subscribers |
| TikTok | 500-2,000 followers |
| LinkedIn | 200-500 connections/followers |

These are conservative. If one platform spikes, lean into it. If another one stays flat after three months of consistent quality posting, deprioritize it.

The machine does not run itself. You still have to show up, do the thinking, and make editorial decisions. What changes is the ratio: your one hour of deep thinking now produces thirty days of content instead of one post.

The biggest failure mode is optimizing the system before proving it works. Run four Sunday sprints with this exact workflow before you change anything. See what the data says. Then adjust.

The content that builds an audience is specific, non-obvious, and consistent. The AI tools handle the volume. You handle the specificity. That division of labor is what makes this work.
