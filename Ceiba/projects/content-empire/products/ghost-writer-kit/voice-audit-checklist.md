# Voice Audit Checklist
# 12-Point Review for AI-Generated Content
# Behike Ghost Writer Kit

---

## HOW TO USE

Run this checklist on every AI-generated draft before publishing.

The goal is not to make it sound less like AI. The goal is to make it sound more like you.

Go through all 12 points. If any fail, revise that specific element. You do not need to rewrite the whole draft. Find the sentence or phrase that breaks the voice and fix only that.

---

## THE 12 POINTS

### 1. BANNED WORDS
Scan for every word on your banned list (Section 3 of your Voice Bible).

- [ ] No words from your permanent banned list appear
- [ ] No words from your personal banned list appear
- [ ] No sentences begin with your banned openers

**Common offenders to scan for first:**
leverage, unlock, game-changing, powerful, transform, hustle, grind, passive, guru, utilize, journey, space, synergy, amazing, incredible, epic

---

### 2. SENTENCE LENGTH
Read the first 5 sentences out loud.

- [ ] Sentences match your average length from Section 4
- [ ] No sentence exceeds your maximum length threshold
- [ ] Short sentences are used for emphasis in the right places (not scattered randomly)

**Fix:** Break any sentence that feels too long into two. The break point is usually where you naturally pause when reading aloud.

---

### 3. PARAGRAPH LENGTH
Scan for paragraphs longer than your stated maximum.

- [ ] No paragraph exceeds your defined maximum
- [ ] Paragraphs do not start with filler context ("In today's digital landscape...")
- [ ] Each paragraph has one main idea

**Fix:** Split long paragraphs at the point where the second idea starts.

---

### 4. HOOK CHECK
Read the first 1-2 sentences.

- [ ] The hook follows your formula from Section 5
- [ ] The hook is specific, not generic
- [ ] The hook creates a question in the reader's mind

**Fail pattern:** "Content marketing is important for any business today."
**Pass pattern:** "Most creators spend 80 percent of their time on logistics. That is backwards."

---

### 5. STRUCTURE MATCH
Compare the draft's structure to your template in Section 5.

- [ ] The overall structure matches your short-form or long-form template
- [ ] Main points are stated first, then explained (not buried at the end)
- [ ] The closing follows your closing formula

---

### 6. ANTI-PATTERN SCAN
Check for each anti-pattern in your Section 6 list.

- [ ] No bullet list of 10+ items without narrative
- [ ] No "Bonus tip:" at the end of a list
- [ ] No "Here's what I learned:" as an opener
- [ ] No filler context before the point
- [ ] No generic advice that applies to anyone without specificity
- [ ] No "counterintuitive" claims that are actually obvious
- [ ] No "most people" without specifics

---

### 7. SPECIFICITY CHECK
Pick 3 claims at random from the draft.

- [ ] Each claim includes at least one specific detail (number, name, example, timeline)
- [ ] No claim relies on vague authority ("experts say," "research shows," "many people")
- [ ] Examples are concrete, not hypothetical

**Fail pattern:** "Many entrepreneurs struggle with time management."
**Pass pattern:** "I spent 3 hours last Tuesday reformatting one Instagram post for four platforms."

---

### 8. VOICE EXAMPLES COMPARISON
Read one paragraph from your Section 7 examples, then read the corresponding section of the draft.

- [ ] The draft has a similar rhythm to your examples
- [ ] The word-level choices feel consistent (same vocabulary range, same register)
- [ ] The level of intimacy is consistent (is it writing to one person or to a crowd?)

**Fix:** Find the paragraph in the draft that feels most unlike your examples. Rewrite just that paragraph to match.

---

### 9. PUNCTUATION CONSISTENCY
Scan for your flagged punctuation patterns.

- [ ] Em dashes appear or are absent according to your preference
- [ ] Exclamation marks appear or are absent according to your preference
- [ ] Semicolons appear or are absent according to your preference
- [ ] Contractions are used or avoided according to your preference

---

### 10. OPENING AND CLOSING
Read only the first sentence and the last sentence.

- [ ] First sentence could stand alone as a hook
- [ ] Last sentence is a takeaway, not a summary
- [ ] Neither sentence uses a filler phrase or generic closer

**Fail patterns for closings:** "I hope this was helpful." / "Let me know what you think in the comments." / "That is all for today."

---

### 11. READ ALOUD TEST
Read the full draft aloud at your normal speaking pace.

- [ ] Nothing trips you up while reading
- [ ] No sentence requires a second reading to understand
- [ ] The rhythm matches how you would actually explain this to someone in a conversation

**Note:** If you stumble on a sentence, that sentence needs revision. Your readers will stumble too.

---

### 12. THE ONE-PERSON TEST
As you read, imagine you are writing to exactly one specific person (your target reader from Section 1).

- [ ] Every sentence is relevant to that one person
- [ ] No sentence is addressing a general "you" that could be anyone
- [ ] The level of assumed knowledge matches that person's background

---

## SCORING

**12 of 12:** Publish as-is.

**10-11:** Minor fixes. Usually one or two sentences. Take 5 minutes.

**7-9:** Medium revision. One or two sections need rewriting. Usually 15-20 minutes.

**Below 7:** Heavy revision or regenerate. The system may have drifted from your Voice Bible constraints. Check that your Section 8 constraint summary is loaded correctly in the Ghost Writer workflow.

---

## COMMON FAILURE PATTERNS AND QUICK FIXES

| Failure | Quick Fix |
|---------|-----------|
| Too formal | Replace one formal word per paragraph with your natural word |
| Too generic | Add one specific number, name, or example to each vague claim |
| Too long | Cut the last sentence of every paragraph |
| Sounds like a list | Convert three of the list items into one narrative sentence |
| Wrong opening | Delete the first sentence. Does the second one work better as an opener? |
| Weak closing | Replace the closing with one sentence from the middle of the draft that captures the main insight |
