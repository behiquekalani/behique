# The Zero-Dollar AI Stack v2

### 15 free tools that run a real digital business. What each one replaces. How they connect. Where to start.

*By Kalani, Founder of Behike*

---

## Why This Exists

The original Zero-Dollar AI Stack I put out covered ten tools. People liked it. They also asked the same question repeatedly: "How do these actually connect? How do you go from a list of tools to a working system?"

This is the updated version. Fifteen tools. Each one with a real use case, a setup note, and how I actually use it. Not how the marketing copy says it works. How it works when you are building a one-person digital business at 11 PM with no team and no budget.

The total cost of this stack is zero dollars per month. Some tools have paid tiers. You do not need them to start. The free tiers cover everything I describe here.

I run my entire operation on this stack. One person. Multiple products. Automations running overnight. AI handling the volume work. Me handling the strategy.

Here is the toolkit.

## The Stack

---

### 1. Claude.ai (Free Tier)

**What it is:** A conversational AI built by Anthropic. The free tier gives you access to Claude Sonnet, which is fast, accurate, and handles most tasks without hitting limitations.

**What it replaces:** ChatGPT Plus at $20/month.

**How I actually use it:** Claude is my thinking partner. When I am writing product copy and I am stuck, I paste what I have and describe the problem. It does not write it for me. It asks questions that clarify my own thinking, then helps me finish. I also use it for editing. Paste a draft, ask it to tighten the logic and cut the redundancy. The output is almost always tighter and faster to read.

It also reads PDFs, analyzes data, writes code, and handles research tasks. The free tier has a usage limit per day but I have never hit it during normal work.

**Concrete example output:** I fed it the Reddit research from a niche validation session and asked it to summarize the top three frustrations people expressed and the exact language patterns they used. It returned a two-paragraph brief that became the opening of my product description.

**Get it:** claude.ai

---

### 2. Bing Image Creator

**What it is:** Microsoft's AI image generation tool, built on DALL-E 3. No subscription required. You get a batch of fast credits per day and then slower generation indefinitely.

**What it replaces:** Midjourney at $10/month.

**How I actually use it:** Product cover concepts and social media visual ideas. I describe the style and scene I want, generate four options, pick the one closest to what I need, then refine it in Canva. I do not use AI images as final products. I use them as starting points that cut design time in half.

**Concrete example output:** "A clean flat-lay of a laptop with a minimal desk setup, no clutter, morning light, top-down angle" produced four options in about 30 seconds. One of them became the base image for a product cover after a Canva crop and text overlay.

**Get it:** bing.com/images/create

---

### 3. Canva Free Tier

**What it is:** Browser-based design tool with templates for everything. The free tier covers ebooks, social media graphics, presentations, and basic video.

**What it replaces:** Adobe suite subscriptions, which run $55+/month for full access.

**How I actually use it:** Every PDF product I sell was designed in Canva. Every Instagram post. Every Gumroad cover image. The free template library is large enough that I have never needed to build a design from scratch. I pick the closest template, strip out what I do not need, replace the text and colors with mine, and export.

**Concrete example output:** A 15-page ebook designed from a free Canva template in about two hours, including the cover, section headers, and a consistent layout across every page.

**Get it:** canva.com

---

### 4. CapCut (Free Desktop and Mobile)

**What it is:** Video editing software owned by ByteDance. The free version is full-featured. Auto-captioning, speed controls, transitions, text overlays, and a growing library of AI editing tools.

**What it replaces:** Adobe Premiere Elements or DaVinci Resolve for creators who want speed over granular control.

**How I actually use it:** Short-form video for Instagram Reels and repurposed content. Record a talking-head video on my phone, import it to CapCut, run auto-captions, trim the dead air, add a text overlay with the hook, export. Start to finished video in under 20 minutes.

**Concrete example output:** A 60-second Reel walking through one tool from this stack, captioned automatically, with a branded intro card and end screen, exported at 1080p.

**Get it:** capcut.com

---

### 5. n8n Self-Hosted

**What it is:** Open-source workflow automation with a visual node editor. Self-host it on any server. No per-execution charges. No usage caps.

**What it replaces:** Zapier Pro at $20/month, or Make (Integromat) at a similar price.

**How I actually use it:** The automation layer between everything else in this stack. When I capture a new idea in Telegram, n8n classifies it and saves it to Notion. When a new subscriber joins my email list, n8n sends the lead magnet file, tags the subscriber, and logs the event. When I want to check trending products, n8n runs the research script and sends me a summary.

**Setup note:** You need a VPS or a spare machine to self-host. A $4/month VPS from Hetzner or DigitalOcean works. Install with Docker, two commands. If you have no server, the n8n cloud free tier gives you a limited number of executions per month. Enough to learn the tool and build your first five workflows.

**Concrete example output:** A workflow that monitors a Gumroad product page for new sales, logs each sale to a Notion database with the date and product, and sends me a Telegram notification within 30 seconds of the transaction.

**Get it:** n8n.io

### 6. Gumroad Free Tier

**What it is:** A platform for selling digital products. The free tier has zero monthly cost. Gumroad takes a flat 10% transaction fee on each sale.

**What it replaces:** Shopify at $39/month plus payment processing fees, for digital product sales specifically.

**How I actually use it:** Every paid digital product I sell goes through Gumroad. The storefront is simple, the checkout is clean, and file delivery is automatic. I upload the product file, write the description, set the price, and it is live. No configuration beyond that.

**The fee math:** At 10% per sale, I keep $9 on a $9.99 product. Shopify keeps $39 per month regardless of sales, plus 2.9% + $0.30 per transaction. Gumroad is cheaper until you are doing serious volume. At that point you can evaluate switching. Start here.

**Concrete example output:** A fully functional product page with file delivery, sales tracking, and the option to add upsells and discount codes, all on the free tier.

**Get it:** gumroad.com

### 7. GitHub Pages

**What it is:** Free static site hosting from GitHub. Any public repository with an index.html file can be a website. Custom domain support included.

**What it replaces:** Web hosting at $10/month, Netlify paid tier, or any other static hosting service.

**How I actually use it:** Landing pages for every product. I write a one-page HTML file using the template structure from my existing landing pages. Push to GitHub. GitHub builds and serves it automatically. The site is live in under two minutes.

**Setup note:** You need a GitHub account (free) and basic git knowledge. If you know enough to create a repository and push files, you have everything you need. The landing page template is simple HTML and CSS. No build tools, no frameworks.

**Concrete example output:** A product landing page at username.github.io/product-name with a headline, problem description, product contents, and a Gumroad CTA button. Loads in under a second.

**Get it:** pages.github.com

### 8. Notion Free Tier

**What it is:** A workspace that combines docs, databases, kanban boards, and wikis. The free tier supports unlimited pages and basic database features.

**What it replaces:** Airtable free tier is limiting, paid tier at $20/month per seat. Notion free covers more ground for a single operator.

**How I actually use it:** Central knowledge base for my business. I have databases for products, content calendar, research notes, and business metrics. The key is that Notion has an API. That means n8n can read from and write to my Notion databases automatically. New ideas flow in from BehiqueBot. Sales data flows in from Gumroad via n8n. I do not update Notion manually for operational data. The automations do that.

**Concrete example output:** A product pipeline database with columns for product name, status (idea, drafting, live, archived), Gumroad URL, revenue to date, and linked content. Updated automatically when sales come in.

**Get it:** notion.so

### 9. Whisper (Local via Whisper.cpp)

**What it is:** OpenAI's open-source speech-to-text model. The Whisper.cpp implementation runs locally on CPU or GPU. No API calls, no cost per minute, full privacy.

**What it replaces:** Otter.ai at $8/month, or paying for the OpenAI Whisper API at $0.006 per minute.

**How I actually use it:** Transcribing voice notes I record on my phone throughout the day. I capture ideas as audio when I am not near a keyboard. Those recordings sync to my Mac, Whisper processes them, and the text goes into my system. I also use it to transcribe long YouTube videos or podcast episodes I want to reference without rewatching.

**Setup note:** Install via Homebrew on Mac: `brew install whisper-cpp`. Download the model of your choice (tiny or base model works for most transcription). Run from terminal with one command. First-time setup takes about ten minutes.

**Concrete example output:** A five-minute voice note about a new product idea transcribed in about 20 seconds on CPU, with near-perfect accuracy for clear speech.

**Get it:** github.com/ggerganov/whisper.cpp

### 10. ElevenLabs Free Tier

**What it is:** AI text-to-speech with natural-sounding voices. The free tier gives you 10,000 characters per month.

**What it replaces:** Paid TTS services for content creation, which run $10 to $30/month for comparable quality.

**How I actually use it:** Voiceovers for short Reels where I want audio but am not recording a talking-head video. I write the script, paste it into ElevenLabs, generate the audio, and sync it to the video in CapCut. Clean, natural, zero performance anxiety. The 10,000 free characters is enough for roughly ten to fifteen short scripts per month.

**Concrete example output:** A 45-second voiceover for a Reel about one of the tools in this stack, using a natural male voice, exported as MP3, synced to text overlay animation in CapCut.

**Get it:** elevenlabs.io

### 11. Kling AI Free Tier

**What it is:** AI video generation tool that creates short clips from text prompts or images. The free tier includes a limited number of generations per day.

**What it replaces:** Runway Gen-3 at $15/month, or Sora for high-quality AI video generation.

**How I actually use it:** B-roll and concept visuals for Reels and YouTube thumbnails. I am not generating full AI videos yet. I use Kling for 3 to 5 second clips of a concept or scene that would be impractical to film. A desk setup timelapse. A product idea visualization. Background motion for a text-focused video.

**The realistic expectation:** Free tier generations are slower and limited. Plan them ahead. Generate a few options the night before you need them. Pick the best one.

**Concrete example output:** A five-second clip of a glowing circuit board turning into a flowing document, used as opening B-roll for a Reel about AI automation.

**Get it:** klingai.com

### 12. Telegram Bot API

**What it is:** A free API for building bots that live inside Telegram. Your bot can receive messages, send messages, process files, and integrate with any external system.

**What it replaces:** Slack at $7/month for a small team, or paid notification services for automation alerts.

**How I actually use it:** BehiqueBot is my idea capture system. I send it a voice note or text message from anywhere. It transcribes, classifies, and saves. n8n uses a Telegram bot to send me daily briefings and automation alerts. I use another bot instance as a private channel for logging business metrics. All three bots cost zero dollars.

**Setup note:** Create a bot via BotFather on Telegram in about five minutes. You get a bot token. Build the logic in Python using the python-telegram-bot library. Host on Railway free tier. The entire setup, first bot to first message, takes an afternoon.

**Concrete example output:** A morning briefing bot that sends me a formatted message at 7 AM with yesterday's sales, today's tasks, and any flagged automation alerts. All data pulled automatically by n8n and formatted by a simple Python script.

**Get it:** core.telegram.org/bots

### 13. Obsidian Free Tier

**What it is:** A local-first markdown note-taking app. Notes are plain text files on your computer. No cloud required. No subscription for the core product.

**What it replaces:** Roam Research at $15/month, or any premium note-taking app that charges for local storage and linking.

**How I actually use it:** Long-form thinking and reference material that does not belong in Notion. Notion is my operational database. Obsidian is my thinking environment. Research notes, book highlights, content outlines, strategy documents. The backlink system lets me connect ideas across months of notes without organizing them into folders.

**Concrete example output:** A connected note system where my research on content strategy links automatically to my product ideas, which link to my launch timelines. Finding a note about an old product idea surfaces all the related research I did at the time.

**Get it:** obsidian.md

---

### 14. Warp (Free Tier)

**What it is:** A modern terminal for Mac with AI command suggestions, command history search, and a block-based interface that makes terminal output readable.

**What it replaces:** iTerm2 or the default Mac terminal for developers who spend significant time in the command line. No direct paid equivalent at the same level.

**How I actually use it:** Running all my local tools. Whisper transcriptions, n8n local instance, Python scripts, git operations. Warp's AI assistant lets me type what I want to do in plain language and get the right command. "Delete all .DS_Store files recursively from this folder." It writes the find command. I review and run it. This has saved me a lot of time on commands I use infrequently and would otherwise have to look up.

**Concrete example output:** A session where I ran five different commands to process a batch of voice notes through Whisper, none of which I had to look up, because Warp's AI suggestion handled the flags and syntax.

**Get it:** warp.dev

### 15. Railway Free Tier

**What it is:** A cloud hosting platform with a free tier that includes enough compute and storage to run small applications continuously.

**What it replaces:** Heroku (now paid-only), DigitalOcean App Platform free tier, or any other small-app hosting service.

**How I actually use it:** Hosting BehiqueBot. The bot runs 24/7 on Railway without any intervention from me. When I push a new version to GitHub, Railway detects the change and redeploys automatically. The free tier covers the compute needs of a Python bot handling moderate message volume.

**Setup note:** Connect your GitHub repository. Set your environment variables (bot token, API keys). Click deploy. Railway handles the rest. Zero server configuration.

**Concrete example output:** A Telegram bot deployed and responding to messages within four minutes of pushing the first working version to GitHub.

**Get it:** railway.app

## How These Connect

A list of tools is not a system. Here is how the fifteen tools above connect into one operational workflow.

**Capture layer:** You have an idea. You send a voice note to your Telegram bot. Whisper transcribes it. n8n classifies and saves it to Notion. Claude helps you develop it when you sit down to work.

**Creation layer:** You are building a product. You design it in Canva. You write it with Claude's help. You export as PDF. You record the promo video with your phone and edit it in CapCut, adding ElevenLabs audio or Kling B-roll.

**Distribution layer:** You publish the product on Gumroad. You build the landing page on GitHub Pages. You post the promo content on Instagram, sourced from the Bing Image Creator visuals you created.

**Automation layer:** n8n watches your Gumroad for new sales. Logs them to Notion. Sends you a Telegram notification. Adds the subscriber to your list trigger sequence. All without you touching anything.

**Knowledge layer:** You reflect on what is working in Obsidian. You reference research in Notion. You use Warp to run maintenance scripts and Whisper to process field recordings.

Every tool in this stack talks to at least one other tool. That is what makes it a system instead of a collection.

## How to Prioritize If Starting From Zero

You do not need all fifteen tools on day one. Here is the order that makes sense.

**Week 1 (Get your foundation):**
- Claude.ai. Your thinking partner for everything.
- Canva. You will design your first product and first social post this week.
- Gumroad. Create your account even if you have no product yet. Get familiar with the platform.

**Week 2 (Build your pipeline):**
- GitHub Pages. Get a landing page live.
- Notion. Start one database. Your product pipeline.
- CapCut. Record and edit your first piece of content.

**Week 3 (Add automation):**
- Telegram bot. Build a basic idea capture bot.
- n8n. Build one workflow that connects any two tools you already use.
- Railway. Deploy your Telegram bot so it runs without your laptop being open.

**Week 4 (Add depth):**
- Whisper. Start transcribing voice notes.
- Obsidian. Move long-form thinking out of Notion and into a local system.
- Bing Image Creator and ElevenLabs. Start producing content at higher volume.

**When you have a product and some revenue:**
- Kling AI. Add video generation to your content process.
- Warp. Worth switching to once you are spending real time in the terminal.

The goal of this stack is not to use all fifteen tools. The goal is to have zero excuses. Every capability you need to run a digital product business from scratch is covered here. For free.

Build the system. The rest follows.
