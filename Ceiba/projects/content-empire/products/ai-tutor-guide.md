# AI Tutor — Product Guide

## What AI Tutor is

AI Tutor is a SaaS tool that converts any document into a structured, interactive learning experience. You upload a PDF or text file. The system reads it, generates a learning path, builds quiz questions from the content, and gives you an AI tutor that answers questions exclusively from your material.

It is not a chatbot. It is not a course platform. It sits between the two: you bring the content, the system structures it and teaches it back to you.

---

## The core problem it solves

### Passive reading does not produce retention

Reading a textbook or study guide is passive. The brain treats passive reading as exposure, not learning. Without active recall, most of what you read is gone within 48 hours. Highlighting and re-reading feel productive but have low impact on long-term retention.

AI Tutor forces active engagement. The quizzes require retrieval. The XP system rewards showing up. The structured lesson path makes you move through material in a sequence rather than drifting.

### Generic AI tools do not know your material

When you ask ChatGPT or any general AI about a topic, it answers from its training data. That training data may not match your textbook, your professor's notes, your proprietary certification study guide, or your company's internal documentation. The answers sound plausible but may contradict what you actually need to learn.

AI Tutor indexes only your document. Every answer comes from that source and cites it. If you ask something the document does not cover, the system says so instead of inventing an answer.

### No accountability means no consistency

Most study tools have no mechanism for keeping you on track. You open the tool once, use it for 30 minutes, and never return. There is no cost to stopping and no reward for continuing.

AI Tutor uses XP, streaks, and progress history to create a reason to come back. Not because gamification is a trick, but because the problem is not intelligence, it is behavior. The system creates a feedback loop that makes studying feel like progress rather than a chore.

## Feature breakdown

### Source-locked AI

The AI is constrained to your uploaded document at the architecture level. When you ask a question, the system searches your document for relevant passages and uses only those passages to construct an answer. It cannot access the internet or its general training for content questions.

This matters for two reasons. First, accuracy: the answers reflect your specific material, not a generalized version of the topic. Second, trust: if the document says something specific, the AI will say the same thing, not a paraphrase from a different source.

Example: You upload a cardiology textbook chapter. You ask, "What is the first-line treatment for atrial fibrillation according to this chapter?" The AI answers from that chapter specifically, not from general medical knowledge that might contradict it.

### Auto-quiz generation

When you upload a document, the system analyzes the content and generates quiz questions automatically. It identifies key terms, important facts, causal relationships, and definitions. It builds multiple choice and short answer questions from those.

You do not write the questions. You do not configure anything. The questions appear as part of the lesson structure the system generates from your document.

Example: You upload notes from a JavaScript course. The system generates questions like "What is the difference between let and const?" and "What does the spread operator do?" based on what the notes actually say.

### XP and streak system

Every completed lesson earns XP. Every correct quiz answer earns XP. Completing sessions on consecutive days builds a streak.

The XP system is not cosmetic. It creates a visible record of effort over time and makes the cost of stopping concrete. Breaking a 14-day streak feels different from simply not opening an app.

Streaks also help students with ADHD and executive function challenges. They create external structure when internal motivation is inconsistent.

### Study break reminders

The system tracks session length and prompts you to take breaks at defined intervals. The default is 25 minutes of study, 5 minutes off. You can adjust this.

Short breaks between study blocks improve retention. This is not opinion, it is documented in cognitive science research going back decades. The tool does not lecture you about it. It just prompts you when the interval arrives.

### Progress history

Every session is saved. You can see which lessons you completed, how you scored on quizzes, which questions you got wrong, and how your performance changed over time. If you uploaded a document two weeks ago and studied it four times, you can see the arc of those sessions.

This is useful for exam preparation, where understanding your weak areas is more valuable than knowing your average score.

### Export your notes

At the end of a session you can export a summary. The export includes the AI answers you found most useful during the session and your quiz results. The format is clean text or PDF.

This output is shareable. If you export your notes with AI Tutor attribution, that attribution links back to the product. That is the viral loop for the free tier.

## Use cases

### College students

The clearest use case. A student uploads lecture notes or a textbook chapter before an exam. The system generates a mini course from the material and quizzes them on it. The student studies actively instead of passively. The streak keeps them coming back in the days before the exam instead of cramming the night before.

Works especially well for students with ADHD, who need external structure and clear rewards to sustain focus over multiple study sessions.

### Professionals studying for certifications

Certification exams are document-based. The official study guide is the source of truth. Uploading it and studying from it directly, with quizzes generated from the exact material, is more targeted than using a generic flashcard app or re-reading the guide.

Applies to: CPA, PMP, CompTIA Security+, AWS certifications, bar exam prep, medical licensing exams, and anything else that has a defined study corpus.

### Language learners studying from a specific text

A language learner uploading a text in their target language can ask the AI to explain vocabulary, grammar structures, and idiomatic expressions that appear in the document. The AI explains them in context, using examples from the text itself.

This is different from Duolingo or a phrasebook. The content comes from whatever the learner is actually trying to read, not a pre-designed curriculum.

### Professionals doing continuing education

Many fields require continuing education for license renewal. The materials are often dense, boring, and not designed for engagement. Uploading those materials and studying them through AI Tutor makes the process active rather than passive.

Also applicable to on-the-job training. A new employee uploads their company's onboarding documentation. The system generates a learning path from it, quizzes them on key policies and procedures, and tracks their progress.

## Pricing and how to choose a plan

### Free tier

Good for: first-time users, students with one active course, anyone who wants to try the product before committing.

Limitations: one document at a time, 100-page maximum, basic chat only. No quiz generation, no XP, no progress history.

The free tier is a functional product, not a preview. You can use it to study one document indefinitely. The limits are designed to let you experience the core value before deciding whether to upgrade.

### Solo ($9.99/month)

Good for: active students, professionals studying for certifications, anyone with more than one document or course.

Includes everything the free tier does not: unlimited documents, quiz generation, XP and streak system, progress history, note export, and study break reminders.

This is the right plan for most individual users. The per-month cost is less than a single textbook chapter in most university bookstores.

### Team ($19.99/month)

Good for: study groups, classrooms, small teams doing shared training.

One person uploads the material. Up to five teammates get access to the same document library. The leaderboard lets team members see each other's progress and creates a mild competitive dynamic that increases engagement.

Also works for: small businesses running internal training, tutors who want to build a shared library for their students, study groups preparing for the same exam.

## FAQ

**What file types does it support?**
PDF and plain text (.txt) currently. Word document (.docx) and markdown support are on the roadmap.

**Does it work on mobile?**
Yes. The interface is responsive and works in any mobile browser.

**Is my data private?**
Your documents are stored securely and are not used to train any AI model. You can delete them at any time.

**Can the AI answer questions not in my document?**
No. That is intentional. If you ask something the document does not cover, the AI will tell you rather than answering from general knowledge.

**Can I use the same document across multiple sessions?**
Yes. Your documents stay in your library. You can return to them at any time.

**What happens if I cancel?**
Your documents and progress history are retained for 30 days after cancellation. You can export your notes during that period.

## The technical approach

AI Tutor uses retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). When you upload a document, the system splits it into chunks and creates vector embeddings for each chunk. When you ask a question, the system retrieves the most relevant chunks from your document and passes them to the language model as context.

The model is instructed to answer only from the provided context and to indicate when the document does not contain relevant information. It does not have access to its general training data for content responses.

This approach produces two practical benefits. First, the answers are accurate to your specific source material. Second, the AI cannot fabricate content that is not in the document, which matters in high-stakes contexts like exam preparation or professional certification.

Quiz generation works differently. The system uses the full document to identify important concepts, then generates questions using a structured prompt that asks for questions at multiple difficulty levels. The questions are reviewed for coherence before being presented to the user.

The XP and streak logic is simple arithmetic. Lesson completion, quiz performance, and session consistency each contribute points. The streak counter resets if you miss a day. This is the same basic mechanism used in language learning apps because it works.
