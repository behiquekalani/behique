# Chapter 1: The 60-Minute Promise

You do not need a brand. You do not need a website. You do not need an audience of thousands. You do not need to spend three weeks "perfecting" something before anyone sees it.

You need one thing: knowledge that someone else wants, packaged in a simple format, and listed on a platform where people can buy it.

That is what this kit does. In 60 minutes, you will go from "I want to sell something digital" to "here is the link, go buy it."

This is not theory. This is a checklist. Follow it step by step.

# Chapter 2: Pick Your Topic (10 Minutes)

This is where most people get stuck for weeks. They think they need to be a world-class expert. They don't.

You need to know more than the person buying it. That's the bar. If you've done something that someone else hasn't done yet, you can teach it.

## The "Already Know" Test

Answer these three questions. Write the first thing that comes to mind. Don't filter.

1. What do people ask you for help with? (Could be friends, coworkers, family, strangers online.)
2. What have you figured out through trial and error that took longer than it should have?
3. What process do you follow regularly that feels obvious to you but confuses others?

Your product topic is hiding in one of those answers.

## Examples of Simple Products That Sell

- "How I organize my week using Notion" (template + guide)
- "5 email templates for cold outreach that got me replies"
- "My meal prep system for busy people who hate cooking"
- "How to set up a simple budget spreadsheet in 30 minutes"
- "The resume format that got me 3 interviews in one week"

Notice something? None of these require a degree. None require years of experience. They require one thing: a process someone else can follow.

## Pick One Topic Right Now

Write it down in one sentence: "I will teach someone how to _______________."

If you cannot finish that sentence in 30 seconds, you are overthinking it. Pick the first idea that felt right and move on. You can always make a second product later. The goal right now is to finish, not to be perfect.

Timer check: you should be at 10 minutes.

# Chapter 3: Write Your Guide (20 Minutes)

You are not writing a book. You are writing a short, clear guide that solves one specific problem. The target length is 1,500 to 3,000 words. That is 4 to 8 pages in a PDF.

## The Simple Structure

Every good short guide follows this structure:

1. The problem (why does this matter, who is this for)
2. The solution overview (what they will learn)
3. Step-by-step instructions (the actual content)
4. Common mistakes to avoid
5. Quick reference or cheat sheet

That's it. Five sections. You already know the content because you've lived it.

## How to Write It Fast

Open a blank document. Set a 20-minute timer. Write using these rules:

- Write like you are explaining this to a friend over coffee
- Use short sentences. One idea per sentence.
- Use bullet points and numbered lists instead of long paragraphs
- Do not edit while you write. Get the ideas down first.
- If you get stuck on a section, skip it and come back

## Template for Your Guide

Use this exact structure. Fill in the brackets.

**Title:** How to [achieve specific result]

**Section 1: The Problem**
Most people struggle with [problem] because [reason]. This guide fixes that.

**Section 2: What You Will Learn**
By the end of this guide, you will be able to:
- [Outcome 1]
- [Outcome 2]
- [Outcome 3]

**Section 3: Step-by-Step Process**
Step 1: [First action]
- Details and explanation
- Pro tip or shortcut

Step 2: [Second action]
- Details and explanation
- Pro tip or shortcut

(Repeat for 3 to 7 steps total. More than 7 and you are overcomplicating it.)

**Section 4: Common Mistakes**
- Mistake 1: [What people do wrong] / Fix: [What to do instead]
- Mistake 2: [What people do wrong] / Fix: [What to do instead]
- Mistake 3: [What people do wrong] / Fix: [What to do instead]

**Section 5: Quick Reference**
A one-page summary of the key steps. Think of it as the "cheat sheet" someone can print out and pin to their wall.

## What If My Topic Is a Template?

If you are selling a template (spreadsheet, Notion template, Canva design, email scripts), your guide becomes:

1. What this template does and who it is for
2. How to use it (walkthrough with examples)
3. How to customize it for your situation
4. Common mistakes when using templates like this

The template IS the product. The guide is the instruction manual. Both go in the same download.

Timer check: you should be at 30 minutes.

# Chapter 4: Format It as a PDF (5 Minutes)

Keep this simple. You do not need fancy design software.

## Option 1: Google Docs (Fastest)

1. Open Google Docs
2. Paste your guide text
3. Format: Title in large bold, section headers in Heading 2, body text in normal
4. Add page breaks between major sections (Insert > Break > Page break)
5. File > Download > PDF Document

Done. That is a sellable PDF.

## Option 2: Canva (Better Looking)

1. Go to canva.com and search for "ebook template" or "lead magnet template"
2. Pick a clean, minimal template
3. Replace the placeholder text with your guide
4. Download as PDF

Canva has free templates that look professional. Do not spend more than 10 minutes on design. The content is what people are buying, not the font choice.

## Option 3: Notion (If You Already Use It)

1. Write your guide in Notion
2. Click the three dots menu in the top right
3. Export as PDF

## Design Tips That Take 30 Seconds

- Use one font. Two maximum.
- Leave white space. Do not cram text wall to wall.
- Use bold for key points so people can scan
- Add a cover page with the title, your name, and one line about what it does
- Number your pages

Timer check: you should be at 35 minutes.

# Chapter 5: Set Up Your Gumroad Account (5 Minutes)

If you already have a Gumroad account, skip to Chapter 6.

## Step-by-Step Account Setup

**Step 1: Go to gumroad.com and click "Start Selling"**

You will see the signup page. Use your email or sign up with Google/Twitter. Pick whatever is fastest.

**Step 2: Fill in your profile**

- Display name: Use your brand name or your real name. Keep it simple.
- Bio: One sentence about what you create. Example: "I make simple guides for people who want to [your niche]."
- Profile picture: Use a headshot or a clean logo. If you don't have either, use your initials on a solid color background (Canva can make this in 2 minutes).

**Step 3: Connect your payment method**

Gumroad will ask you to connect either Stripe or PayPal so they can pay you. Do this now. If you skip it, you cannot receive payments.

- Click Settings > Payments
- Connect Stripe (recommended) or PayPal
- Follow the prompts to verify your identity

**Step 4: Set your store URL**

Under Settings, you can customize your Gumroad URL. It will look like: yourusername.gumroad.com. Pick something clean and recognizable.

That is it. Your account is live. You are now a Gumroad seller.

Timer check: you should be at 40 minutes.

# Chapter 6: Write Your Listing Copy (10 Minutes)

Your listing copy is the sales page for your product. This is what convinces someone to click "I want this." It does not need to be long. It needs to be clear.

## The 5-Part Listing Formula

Use this exact formula. Fill in the blanks.

**Line 1: The Hook**
"[Target audience], this is the [type of product] that shows you how to [specific result]."

Example: "Freelancers, this is the guide that shows you how to land your first 3 clients in 30 days without cold calling."

**Line 2: The Problem**
"If you have been struggling with [problem], it is probably because [common reason]. Most advice out there tells you to [bad advice]. That does not work."

**Line 3: What Is Inside**
List 3 to 5 bullet points of what the buyer gets. Be specific.

- The exact [number]-step process I used to [result]
- A ready-to-use template for [specific task]
- The [number] mistakes most people make (and how to avoid them)
- A quick-reference cheat sheet you can use immediately

**Line 4: Who This Is For**
"This is for you if you are [description of ideal buyer] and you want [desired outcome] without [common frustration]."

**Line 5: The Close**
"Grab it now. You will have [the result] by [timeframe]."

## Writing the Product Name

Your product name should be clear, not clever. The buyer should know exactly what they are getting from the title alone.

Good: "The Cold Email Template Pack: 10 Emails That Get Replies"
Bad: "Email Mastery Pro 2.0 Ultimate Edition"

Good: "Freelancer's First Client Playbook"
Bad: "The Revenue Acceleration System"

## Adding a Cover Image

Gumroad lets you upload a product thumbnail. This shows up in search results and on your product page.

- Use Canva to make a simple cover (1280 x 720 pixels works well)
- Include the product title in large text
- Use a clean background, one or two colors
- Do not put too much text on the cover

Timer check: you should be at 50 minutes.

# Chapter 7: Price, Upload, and Publish (5 Minutes)

## Setting Your Price

For your first product, price it between $5 and $15. Here is the logic:

- $5 to $9: Low barrier. Good for short guides, templates, cheat sheets. Buyers feel safe trying something cheap from an unknown creator.
- $10 to $15: Still accessible. Works for more comprehensive guides, template bundles, or anything with multiple deliverables.
- $0 (free with email): Use this only if your goal is building an email list, not making money. Gumroad lets you set a $0 price and collect emails.

Do not price it at $1. It signals low value. Do not price it at $50 unless you have proof that the content delivers serious results. Start at $5 to $9 for your first product.

You can also enable "Pay what you want" with a minimum price. This lets generous buyers pay more. Some will.

## Uploading Your Product

**Step 1: From your Gumroad dashboard, click "New Product"**

**Step 2: Choose product type**

Select "Digital product" for PDFs, templates, and guides.

**Step 3: Fill in the details**

- Name: Your product title
- Price: Set your price (or minimum price if using pay-what-you-want)
- Description: Paste your listing copy from Chapter 6
- Cover image: Upload the thumbnail you made
- Product file: Upload your PDF (and any bonus files like templates)

**Step 4: Configure settings**

- Content delivery: "Digital download" is the default. Leave it.
- PDF stamping: Optional. This adds the buyer's email to the PDF. Helps prevent sharing.

**Step 5: Click "Publish"**

Your product is now live. Gumroad gives you a direct link to your product page. Copy that link. You will need it in the next chapter.

Timer check: you should be at 55 minutes.

# Chapter 8: Share It and Get Your First Sale (5 Minutes)

Your product is live. Nobody knows it exists yet. Time to fix that.

## The First 5 Places to Share

Pick at least 3 of these and post within the next 5 minutes.

**1. Your personal social media**

Post on Twitter/X, Instagram, LinkedIn, or wherever you have any following at all. Even 50 followers is enough. Use this format:

"I just made [product name]. It is a [short description] for [target audience]. Link in bio / link below."

That is it. Do not write an essay. Do not apologize for selling something. State what it is and link it.

**2. Relevant Reddit or forum communities**

Find 1 to 2 subreddits where your target audience hangs out. Do NOT just drop your link. Write a short post that provides value on the topic, then mention your product naturally at the end.

Example: "I put together a guide on [topic] after doing it myself for [time period]. Here is a quick summary of what I learned: [3 key points]. I also put together a more detailed version with templates if anyone wants it: [link]."

**3. Facebook groups**

Same approach as Reddit. Find groups where your audience is. Provide value first, link second.

**4. Direct messages to 5 to 10 people**

Think of 5 to 10 people who would genuinely benefit from your product. Send them a message: "Hey, I made a guide on [topic]. I think it would help you with [specific thing]. Here is the link if you want to check it out: [link]."

This is not spam. You are reaching out to specific people with something relevant. If they are not interested, no problem.

**5. Your email list (if you have one)**

If you have any kind of email list, even a small one, send a simple email:

Subject: I made something for you
Body: "I just published [product name]. It is a [description]. I made it because [reason]. Here is the link: [link]."

## The Mindset Shift

Your first product will probably not make you $1,000. That is fine. The point of your first product is:

- Proving to yourself that you can do it
- Learning the process from start to finish
- Getting real feedback from real buyers
- Building momentum for product #2, #3, and beyond

The person who has published one imperfect product is 100x further ahead than the person who has been "planning" their perfect product for six months.

Timer check: 60 minutes. You are done.

# Chapter 10: After You Launch - What Comes Next

You did it. You have a live product. Here is what to do in the first week after launch.

## Day 1 to 3: Collect Feedback

Reach out to your first buyers (even if it is just 1 or 2 people) and ask:

- Was the guide clear?
- What was the most useful part?
- What was missing or confusing?

Use their answers to improve the product. Gumroad lets you update the file at any time. Buyers automatically get access to the updated version.

## Day 4 to 7: Double Down on Distribution

Your product will not sell itself. In the first week:

- Post about it at least 3 more times on social media (different angles each time)
- Share it in 2 to 3 new communities
- Create one piece of free content related to your product topic (a tweet thread, a short video, or a blog post) and link to the product at the end

## Week 2 and Beyond: Build the Flywheel

Once your first product is live, you have proof of concept. Now:

- Create product #2. The process gets faster every time.
- Bundle products together at a higher price point
- Start collecting emails through free content and $0 products
- Use buyer feedback to create exactly what people are asking for

The first product is the hardest. Not because the work is hard, but because your brain will try to convince you it is not good enough, not ready, not worth selling. Ignore that voice. Ship it.

## Revenue Milestones to Aim For

- $1: Your first sale. Proof that someone will pay for what you know.
- $100: You have a real product. Not a fluke.
- $500: Time to think about scaling. More products, better distribution, email list.
- $1,000: You have a digital product business. Keep going.

Every person selling digital products online started exactly where you are right now. The difference between the ones who made it and the ones who didn't is simple: the ones who made it published.

You just published.

Now do it again.
