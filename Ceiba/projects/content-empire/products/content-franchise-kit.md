## CHAPTER 2: CHOOSING YOUR NICHE

### The 3 Requirements

Not every niche works for automated content. Some need too much original research. Some do not have a buyable audience. Some do not have enough recurring news to feed the machine.

Your niche needs three things:

**1. Searchable topics.** There must be a constant stream of new things to talk about. If your niche only has 20 topics total, the machine runs out of fuel in a month.

Test: Search your niche on Google News. Are there at least 5 new articles per week? If yes, it passes.

**2. Recurring news.** The niche needs events, updates, releases, changes. Things that happen on a schedule. Product launches, regulatory changes, seasonal trends, industry reports.

Test: Can you name 3 things that happened in this niche in the last 30 days? If yes, it passes.

**3. Buyable audience.** The people who care about this niche must spend money. They buy products, services, courses, tools. If the audience is purely hobbyist with no spending behavior, monetization will be painful.

Test: Are there at least 5 products or services you could recommend as an affiliate? If yes, it passes.

### Niches That Do NOT Work (And Why)

Not every interest is a viable content niche. These common choices fail the 3-requirement test:

**Ultra-specific hobbies with small audiences.** "Vintage typewriter restoration" is fascinating but the addressable audience is too small. You will max out at 2,000 followers and struggle to monetize.

**One-event niches.** If your niche only produces news once a year (like "eclipse photography"), the machine starves between events. You need weekly fuel.

**Purely entertainment niches without products.** "Funny cat videos" has a massive audience but no buyable behavior. The audience watches and scrolls. They do not buy guides, tools, or services.

**Niches where free information is perfect.** "How to tie a tie" does not need a $20 guide. The free YouTube video is sufficient. Your niche needs problems complex enough that people will pay for structured solutions.

**Niches you do not actually care about.** The machine handles execution, but you still need to review, edit, and add your perspective. If you are bored by the topic, your content will feel hollow. Your audience will sense it immediately.

### How to Validate Your Own Niche

If your niche is not on this list, run the 3-requirement test yourself. Then do this:

1. Search Reddit for your niche subreddit. Does it have 50,000+ members? Good sign.
2. Search Amazon for products in your niche. Are there products with 1,000+ reviews? Money is being spent.
3. Search YouTube for your niche. Are the top videos getting 100,000+ views? Attention exists.
4. Search Google Trends for your niche keyword. Is it stable or growing over the past 5 years? Sustainable.

If all four pass, your niche works. Set it up.

## CHAPTER 4: THE CONTENT PIPELINE

### Step 1: RSS Feed Setup

RSS feeds are how your machine knows what is happening in your niche. They are free, fast, and reliable. Every major news site, blog, and publication has one.

**What you need:**
- A free RSS reader (Feedly free tier, or self-hosted FreshRSS)
- 10-20 RSS feeds relevant to your niche (see Appendix A for pre-built lists)

**Setup (10 minutes):**

1. Go to feedly.com and create a free account
2. Create a folder named "[Your Niche] Pipeline"
3. Add RSS feeds from your niche (Appendix A has curated lists for 10 niches)
4. Enable notifications for new items

For self-hosted (advanced, $0/month):
1. Install FreshRSS on any server or Raspberry Pi
2. Import the OPML file included with this kit
3. Configure polling interval (every 6 hours is good)

**How the pipeline uses RSS:**
Every morning, the system checks your RSS feeds for new items. It filters by relevance (keywords you define). It ranks by recency and engagement signals. The top 5 items become your content source for the day.

### Step 2: Configure the News Tracker

The news tracker is a Python script that pulls from your RSS feeds, filters content, and outputs a daily brief. You do not need to know Python. You need to run one command.

**Installation:**

```bash
# Clone the content pipeline
git clone https://github.com/behike/content-pipeline.git
cd content-pipeline

# Install dependencies
pip install -r requirements.txt

# Configure your niche
cp config.example.yaml config.yaml
```

**Configuration (config.yaml):**

```yaml
niche:
  name: "your-niche-name"
  keywords:
    primary: ["keyword1", "keyword2", "keyword3"]
    secondary: ["related1", "related2"]
    exclude: ["spam-term", "irrelevant-term"]

sources:
  rss_feeds:
    - url: "https://example.com/feed"
      priority: high
    - url: "https://example.com/rss"
      priority: medium

schedule:
  fetch_time: "06:00"
  timezone: "America/New_York"

output:
  daily_brief: true
  max_topics: 5
  format: "markdown"
```

**Running it:**

```bash
# Fetch today's topics
python fetch_topics.py

# Output: daily_brief.md with 5 ranked topics
```

That is it. Every morning you have 5 fresh, relevant topics waiting for you. No scrolling. No searching. No "what should I post about today?"

### Step 3: Caption Templates

Captions are where most people waste time. They stare at a blank screen and try to write something clever. The machine does not stare. It uses templates.

Each template is a structure with blanks. The system fills the blanks with topic-specific content, filtered through your Voice Bible.

**5 Universal Caption Templates:**

**Template 1: The Breakdown**
```
[HEADLINE: Specific claim or stat]

Here is what happened:
[2-3 sentences explaining the news/topic]

Here is what it means for you:
[2-3 sentences on practical implications]

[CTA: Question or action]
```

**Template 2: The Hot Take**
```
[CONTRARIAN OPENING: Challenge a common belief]

[2-3 sentences explaining why the common belief is wrong]

What actually works:
[2-3 sentences with your alternative view]

[CTA: Agree/disagree prompt]
```

**Template 3: The Tutorial Snippet**
```
How to [SPECIFIC RESULT] in [TIME FRAME]:

1. [Step 1 with specific detail]
2. [Step 2 with specific detail]
3. [Step 3 with specific detail]

[BONUS TIP or common mistake to avoid]

Save this for later.
```

**Template 4: The Story**
```
[HOOK: Something unexpected that happened to you]

[3-4 sentences telling the story with specific details]

The lesson:
[1-2 sentences with the takeaway]

[CTA: Has this happened to you?]
```

**Template 5: The List**
```
[NUMBER] [THINGS] I [LEARNED/STOPPED/STARTED] this [TIME PERIOD]:

1. [Item with one-sentence explanation]
2. [Item with one-sentence explanation]
3. [Item with one-sentence explanation]
[...]

Which one surprised you?
```

### Step 4: Carousel Generation

Carousels get 3x the reach of single images on Instagram. The system generates them automatically.

**How it works:**

1. The script takes a topic from your daily brief
2. It generates carousel text (title slide, 5-8 content slides, CTA slide)
3. It applies your brand colors and fonts to a template
4. It exports as individual images ready to upload

**Configuration:**

```yaml
carousel:
  brand_colors:
    primary: "#F5A623"    # Your accent color
    background: "#1A1A1A"  # Your background
    text: "#FFFFFF"        # Your text color

  font:
    heading: "Inter Bold"
    body: "Inter Regular"

  slides:
    min: 5
    max: 10
    words_per_slide: 30

  style: "minimal"  # Options: minimal, bold, editorial
```

**Running it:**

```bash
# Generate carousel from today's brief
python generate_carousel.py --topic 1

# Output: /output/carousels/2026-03-22/topic-1/
# Contains: slide-1.png, slide-2.png, ... slide-n.png
```

Review the slides. Edit any text that feels off. Upload to Instagram. Done.

## CHAPTER 6: THE SELF-IMPROVING LOOP

### Why This Chapter Matters More Than You Think

Most content systems are static. You set them up, they produce the same quality forever. This system gets better over time because it tracks what works and feeds that data back into the generation process.

This is the real moat. Anyone can set up RSS feeds and caption templates. Very few people build a feedback loop that compounds.

### Setting Up Performance Tracking

**What to track (per post):**

| Metric | Why It Matters |
|--------|---------------|
| Saves | People want to come back. Strongest signal. |
| Shares | People want others to see it. Second strongest. |
| Comments | People felt something. Engagement signal. |
| Reach | How many people saw it. Volume metric. |
| Profile visits | Content drove curiosity. Conversion metric. |
| Follows | Content was good enough to commit. Growth metric. |

**What NOT to track:**
- Likes. They mean almost nothing. Someone tapped their screen. That is the lowest bar.

**Setup:**

1. Open your posting calendar Google Sheet
2. Go to the Performance Log tab
3. Every Sunday, fill in metrics for the previous week's posts
4. The sheet auto-calculates: best format, best topic category, best posting time, best template

**The formula:**

```
Post Score = (Saves x 3) + (Shares x 2) + (Comments x 1.5) + (Reach / 100)
```

Saves are weighted highest because they indicate real value. Shares are next because they drive organic growth. Comments show engagement. Reach is divided by 100 to normalize against the other metrics.

### How the System Learns

Every month, the system reviews performance data and adjusts three things:

**1. Topic weighting.** Topics that score above average get more representation in the daily brief. Topics that consistently underperform get deprioritized.

Example: If "AI tool reviews" consistently outperform "AI industry news" in your niche, the system fetches more tool reviews and fewer industry news pieces.

**2. Template selection.** Templates that produce higher-scoring posts get used more often. Templates that underperform get rotated out or rewritten.

Example: If "The Breakdown" template scores 2x higher than "The Hot Take" for your audience, the system shifts the ratio.

**3. Voice evolution.** Phrases and styles that correlate with high performance get reinforced. Phrases that correlate with low performance get added to the soft-ban list.

Example: If posts that start with a question consistently outperform posts that start with a stat, the system adjusts headline preferences.

### Monthly Optimization Checklist

Run this on the first Sunday of every month. Time: 2 hours.

- [ ] Export last month's performance data from each platform
- [ ] Enter data into Performance Log tab
- [ ] Review Top 5 posts: what patterns do they share?
- [ ] Review Bottom 5 posts: what patterns do they share?
- [ ] Adjust topic keywords in config.yaml (add trending, remove stale)
- [ ] Adjust template rotation based on performance

- [ ] Review Voice Bible: any new tonal observations?

- [ ] Update posting times if analytics show different optimal windows
- [ ] Set 3 content goals for next month (specific, measurable)
- [ ] Archive last month's posting calendar to a new tab

### The Value Ladder for Your Niche

Content is not the product. Content is the trust engine. The product is what you sell to the people who trust you.

Here is how to build a value ladder in any niche:

**Rung 0: Free Content (What You Are Building)**
- Your posts, carousels, reels, threads
- Purpose: build audience, build trust, build authority
- Revenue: $0 (this is the investment)

**Rung 1: Low-Ticket Digital Product ($5-$20)**
- A guide, template, checklist, or toolkit
- Solves one specific problem in your niche
- Example (cybersecurity niche): "The Small Business Security Checklist" for $9.99
- Example (finance niche): "The Student Loan Payoff Spreadsheet" for $12.99
- Example (music niche): "10 Royalty-Free Drum Kits for Lo-Fi Producers" for $7.99

**Rung 2: Mid-Ticket Bundle ($50-$100)**
- Multiple products packaged as a system
- Solves a complete problem, not just one piece
- Example: "The Complete Small Business Security Kit" (checklist + audit template + incident response plan + 30-day hardening guide) for $79.99

**Rung 3: High-Ticket Service ($200-$1,000)**
- You do it with them or for them
- A call, an audit, a custom build, a consulting session
- Example: "90-Minute Security Audit of Your Business" for $299
- Example: "Custom AI Content Pipeline Setup" for $499

**Rung 4: Recurring ($50-$500/month)**
- Community, membership, ongoing service
- Example: Monthly mastermind group for your niche
- Example: Managed content service (you run the pipeline for them)

### Creating Products from Your Content

Your best content is your product research lab. Here is the process:

1. **Identify your top 10 posts by saves.** These are the topics people want to reference later. That means they want more depth.

2. **Group them by theme.** You will see 2-3 clusters. Each cluster is a potential product.

3. **Expand each cluster into a guide.** Your carousel had 8 slides? The guide has 8 chapters. Each slide becomes 500-1,000 words of depth, examples, and actionable steps.

4. **Package with templates.** Every guide should include at least one fillable template, checklist, or tool. Something the buyer can use immediately.

5. **Price based on outcome, not length.** A 3-page spreadsheet that saves someone 5 hours per week is worth more than a 100-page PDF they never finish.

### Affiliate Marketing Integration

Affiliate revenue is passive income from recommending tools you already use. Here is how to integrate it into your content system:

**Setup:**
1. Sign up for affiliate programs for tools in your niche
2. Create a "Tools I Use" page on your website
3. Use affiliate links in relevant posts (with disclosure)
4. Track clicks and conversions

**Best affiliate programs by niche:**

| Niche | Programs |
|-------|----------|
| AI/Tech | Anthropic (Claude), OpenAI, hosting providers, SaaS tools |
| Finance | Brokerage apps, budgeting tools, bank sign-up bonuses |

| Ecommerce | Shopify, hosting, email marketing tools |

**Rule:** Only recommend things you actually use. Your audience will know if you are shilling something you have never touched. One dishonest recommendation destroys months of trust.

### The Product Creation Process (Detailed)

Here is the exact process to go from "I have content that performs" to "I have a product for sale":

**Week 1: Research.** Export your top 20 posts by saves. Group them by topic. You will see 2-3 clusters emerge. Each cluster is a product category.

**Week 2: Outline.** Pick the strongest cluster (most saves, most engagement). Write a 10-point outline expanding each post into a full section. Each point should be specific enough that you could explain it to a friend in 2 minutes.

**Week 3: Write.** Write 500-1,000 words per section. Use your Voice Bible. Include at least one template, checklist, or actionable framework per section. Total guide length: 5,000-10,000 words.

**Week 4: Package.** Create a cover image using your brand colors. Export as PDF. Write a product description (use the Breakdown caption template adapted for product copy). Set up on Gumroad or your own store. Price it based on outcome: a guide that saves someone 5 hours/week is worth $20+, not $4.99.

**The pricing rule:** Price by the problem solved, not the page count. A 3-page spreadsheet that replaces a $200/month tool is worth $50. A 100-page PDF that nobody finishes is worth $0. Be honest about what your product actually delivers.

### When to Offer Services

Start with products. They scale. Services do not.

Offer services when:
- You have at least 1,000 followers on one platform
- You have sold at least 20 copies of a product (proves demand)
- You have a waitlist or people asking you to help them directly
- You can charge at least $200/hour for your time

Do not offer services when:
- You are still building your audience
- You have not validated demand with product sales

### Troubleshooting Common Issues

**"Ollama is slow on my machine."**
Use a smaller model. `llama3.1:8b` runs on most machines with 8GB RAM. If you have 16GB, you can run `llama3.1:70b` for better quality. If your machine has less than 8GB RAM, use the cloud option (free tier of Claude or ChatGPT) instead of local.

**"The carousel text is too long for slides."**
Adjust `words_per_slide` in your config.yaml. Default is 30. For Instagram, 15-20 words per slide works better. For LinkedIn carousels, 30-40 is fine.

**"n8n is confusing to set up."**
Start with Buffer free tier instead. It handles 3 accounts and 10 scheduled posts each. That covers your first month easily. Migrate to n8n when you want full automation control.

**"My RSS feeds are not updating."**
Check if the feed URLs are still active. Some sites change their feed paths. Use a tool like feedvalidator.org to verify. Also check your polling interval: if it is set to 24 hours, you might be checking at the wrong time.

**"The AI output still sounds generic."**
Your Voice Bible needs more specificity. Add more banned phrases. Write a longer "WHO IS SPEAKING" section. Include specific examples of what your voice sounds like vs. what it does not sound like. The more constraints you give the system, the more unique the output becomes.

### Common Launch Week Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Perfecting before posting.** Your first post does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist. The system improves over time. Post it.

**Mistake 2: Posting without engaging.** If you post and close the app, the algorithm buries you. Spend as much time commenting on others' posts as you spend creating your own. Minimum 30 minutes/day on engagement.

**Mistake 3: Changing your niche on Day 3.** You will feel like your niche is wrong on Day 3. It is not. You have not given it enough time. Commit to 30 days before evaluating.

**Mistake 4: Ignoring the Voice Bible.** If your content sounds generic, the Voice Bible needs work. Go back to Chapter 3. Add more banned phrases. Be more specific about your tone. The Voice Bible is a living document. Update it every week during the first month.

**Mistake 5: Comparing your Day 1 to someone else's Year 3.** The accounts you admire posted 500+ times before they "blew up." You are on post 5. Keep going.

### How to Get Your First 100 Followers

Followers do not come from posting. They come from engaging.

**The 10-10-10 Method (30 minutes/day):**

1. Find 10 accounts in your niche with 1,000-10,000 followers
2. Leave 10 genuine, thoughtful comments on their posts (not "great post!" but actual thoughts)
3. Follow 10 people who commented on those accounts (they are your target audience)

Do this every day for 30 days. You will have 100+ followers and, more importantly, you will be visible in your niche's comment sections. People click profiles of people who leave smart comments.

**What NOT to do:**
- Do not buy followers. Ever. It destroys your engagement rate.
- Do not use follow/unfollow. Platforms detect it and suppress your reach.
- Do not post and disappear. The algorithm rewards people who participate, not just broadcast.
- Do not DM strangers asking them to follow you. Instant block.

## CHAPTER 10: SCALING

### The Scaling Mindset

Scaling does not mean "do more." It means "get more output from the same input." Before adding platforms, languages, or people, ask: am I getting everything I can from what I already have?

If your single Instagram account is posting 5 times per week and averaging 200 reach per post, the problem is not scale. The problem is content quality or audience targeting. Fix that first.

Scale when: your current system is running smoothly, your engagement is growing, and your bottleneck is reach, not quality.

### Adding Languages (Bilingual Content)

If you speak two languages, you have double the addressable audience. The system supports multilingual content with minimal extra effort.

**How to add a second language:**

1. Create a second Voice Bible for the new language
2. Add language-specific RSS feeds to config.yaml
3. Duplicate your posting calendar for the new account
4. Run the pipeline twice: once per language

**Important considerations:**
- Do not just translate. Rewrite. Cultural context matters.
- Some topics trend in one language but not another
- Hashtags are completely different per language
- Posting times differ by geography

**Config addition:**

```yaml
languages:
  primary:
    code: "en"
    voice_bible: "voice_bible_en.md"
    rss_feeds: "feeds_en.yaml"
  secondary:
    code: "es"
    voice_bible: "voice_bible_es.md"
    rss_feeds: "feeds_es.yaml"
```

### Adding Platforms

You built the machine for Instagram. Now you want to be on YouTube, TikTok, and X.

**Adaptation rules by platform:**

| Original (Instagram) | YouTube Shorts | TikTok | X/Twitter |
|----------------------|----------------|--------|-----------|
| Carousel | Short video (narrate slides) | Short video (text overlay) | Thread (1 tweet per slide) |
| Single image | Thumbnail + short commentary | Duet/stitch format | Single tweet with image |
| Reel | YouTube Short (same file) | TikTok (same file, re-edit) | Video tweet |
| Caption | Description + timestamps | On-screen text | Thread or single tweet |

**The repurposing workflow:**

1. Create for Instagram first (highest production value)
2. Export carousel slides as video (narrate with Kokoro TTS or your voice)
3. Upload video to YouTube Shorts and TikTok (adjust aspect ratio if needed)
4. Convert caption to Twitter thread (one slide per tweet)
5. Total extra time: 15-20 minutes per piece of content

### When to Hire (Or Keep Automating)

The machine replaces a content team. But at some point, growth requires people.

**Keep automating when:**
- You are under 10,000 followers total
- Content quality is consistent
- You are hitting your posting schedule
- Revenue is under $2,000/month
- You enjoy the process

**Consider hiring when:**
- You are missing posts because you cannot review fast enough
- DMs and comments are piling up unanswered
- Revenue exceeds $3,000/month (you can afford help)
- You want to add video content but do not have time to produce it
- You are spending more time on content management than on your business

**Who to hire first:**
1. A virtual assistant for engagement (comments, DMs, community) at $400-$800/month
2. A video editor for short-form content at $500-$1,000/month
3. A second operator who runs the pipeline for a second niche or language

**Who NOT to hire:**
- A "social media manager" who uses Canva templates and posts generic content. You already have a machine that does that better.
- A "content strategist" when you already have the strategy (this kit). You need executors, not more strategists.

### Revenue Milestones and What to Do at Each

**$0-$500/month:**
Focus entirely on content and audience building. Do not optimize monetization yet. Your job is to reach 1,000 real followers who care about your niche.

**$500-$2,000/month:**
Launch your first low-ticket product. Use your top-performing content as the product outline. Keep posting consistently. Start an email list.

**$2,000-$5,000/month:**
Add a mid-ticket bundle. Offer your first service (consulting call or done-with-you session). Start an email sequence that nurtures buyers up the value ladder.

**$5,000-$10,000/month:**
Add a second niche or language. Hire your first VA for engagement. Create a recurring revenue offer (community, membership, or managed service).

**$10,000+/month:**
You have a real business. Consider incorporating. Hire a video editor. Build a team. Or keep it lean and keep automating. Both paths work.
