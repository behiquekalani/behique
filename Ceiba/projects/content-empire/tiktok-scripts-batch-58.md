## Script 58-1: My Actual Daily Stack

**Hook (0-3s):**
The tools I actually use every day to run a solo business. No affiliate links.

**Body:**
First: a notes app. I use Obsidian. Everything lives there. Ideas, projects, decisions, drafts.

Second: n8n for automation. I do not do repetitive work manually. If it happens more than twice, it gets automated.

Third: Claude for thinking. Not writing for me. Thinking with me. It challenges my logic, helps me structure ideas, speeds up research.

Fourth: a calendar with blocked time. The tool does not matter. The behavior does.

These four things handle 80 percent of what it takes to run a content business solo.

**CTA:**
Simple stack. Consistent execution. That is the whole game.

## Script 58-2: Stop Buying Tools You Do Not Need

**Hook (0-3s):**
The tool is not the problem. You are.

**Body:**
Most solopreneurs have fifteen tools and use four of them consistently.

The rest are either never set up properly, duplicated by something else, or bought as a proxy for progress.

Buying Notion does not make you organized. Using Notion every day does.

Before you add a new tool, ask: what is the specific behavior this tool is supposed to support. If you cannot answer that precisely, do not buy it.

Complexity kills execution. The less friction between you and the work, the more work gets done.

**CTA:**
Audit your tools this week. Delete anything you have not used in 30 days.

## Script 58-4: One Tool Most Creators Ignore

**Hook (0-3s):**
Most creators skip this tool and wonder why their business stays small.

**Body:**
A proper email service provider.

Not because email is sexy. Because it is the only channel where you own your audience completely.

The creators who survive platform shifts are the ones who converted followers to subscribers.

I use ConvertKit. The specific tool matters less than the habit of sending consistently and giving people a reason to stay subscribed.

Every piece of content should have one job: move someone from rented platform to owned list.

## Script 58-5: The Free Tool I Would Not Give Up

**Hook (0-3s):**
This free tool saves me four hours every week. Most people already have it and do not use it right.

**Body:**
Google Calendar with time blocking.

Not as a scheduling tool. As a commitment device.

Every task I want to complete gets a block. Not a to-do list. A scheduled appointment with myself.

If it is not on the calendar, it does not exist as a real commitment. Ideas live on lists. Work lives on the calendar.

This single habit shift is what separates people who are productive from people who are busy.

Busy has a full to-do list. Productive has a full calendar with the right things on it.

**CTA:**
Block three hours this week for your most important project. Watch what happens.
