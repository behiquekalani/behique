### POST 3
THEME: ADHD and parallel project tracking

HOOK: Five projects running. None of them stalled.

CAPTION:
The advice is always to focus on one thing. For some brains, that advice is correct. For mine, one thing is a cage.

Running multiple projects in parallel works for ADHD when the projects are at different stages. One in the idea phase. One being built. One ready to ship. One already live and being iterated. The brain gets to move between them based on where energy is, not where a schedule says to be.

The failure mode is five projects all at the same stage requiring the same type of work at the same time. That creates the scattered feeling people associate with ADHD. The fix is staggering the stages, not reducing the number.

This is not permission to avoid finishing. It is a different operating structure that works better than the linear alternative.

HOOK: External accountability is not weakness.

CAPTION:
The internal drive to finish things is real. It is also unreliable when the task is boring, the feedback loop is long, or the stakes feel abstract.

External accountability patches those gaps. A deadline someone else set. A public commitment. A bot that asks what got done today. These are not crutches. They are systems that compensate for a known gap in internal regulation.

Building your own accountability system is not overthinking it. It is treating your attention like the finite resource it is.

### POST 13
THEME: The catalog revenue model

HOOK: 25 products. Here is the math.

CAPTION:
If each product averages $14.99 and sells once per day across the catalog, that is $374.75 per day. That is the ceiling assumption, not the floor.

The real number starts much lower. Some products sell every day. Most sell a few times per week. A few sit untouched for a month and then spike after a single post references them.

The insight from running a catalog is that the average is not the useful number. The useful number is how many products are selling at any given time. A catalog where 8 out of 25 are actively generating revenue is more stable than a single product generating the same total.

Diversification within a catalog is real risk management. Not just a content strategy.

### POST 22
THEME: AST timezone advantages

HOOK: AST is an underrated timezone.

CAPTION:
Atlantic Standard Time sits one hour ahead of EST and does not observe daylight saving time. In winter, Puerto Rico is one hour ahead of New York. In summer, the same.

For an async business, this creates a natural structure. The US market wakes up after two hours of work time are already done. Post something at 7 AM and it hits US feeds at peak morning engagement two hours later.

The practical workflow builds around this. Deep work from 6 to 9 AM before any US notifications arrive. Content goes live at 9. The day is already productive before the inbox opens.

Timezone is infrastructure. Most people do not think about it that way.
