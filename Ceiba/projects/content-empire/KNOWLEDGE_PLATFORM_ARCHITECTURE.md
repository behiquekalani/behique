## THE THESIS

Behike becomes the place where non-technical people learn AI. Not hype. Not fear. Not "10 AI tools that will replace your job." Real skills, taught by someone who actually builds with these tools every day.

The free content is genuinely useful. People learn real skills from it. They share it because it helped them, not because they were tricked into a funnel. When someone wants to go deeper, structured paid products exist. When someone wants implementation, services exist.

The business model is a trust engine. Free builds trust. Trust creates buyers. Buyers become advocates. Advocates bring more people to the free content. The loop compounds.

This document is the master plan. The grand-slam-offers.md defines the offer architecture. The product-listings-v2.md defines the catalog. The VOICE_BIBLE.md defines the tone. This document connects all of it into a single system.

## 1. THE FREE TIER (Building Trust + Audience)

The free tier has three components: a structured knowledge base, interactive tools, and lead magnets. Each serves a different purpose. The knowledge base builds authority and SEO. The tools create viral traffic magnets. The lead magnets capture emails.

### 1.1 Free Knowledge Base (Hosted on Behike Hub Website)

The knowledge base is a structured curriculum that lives permanently on the Behike website. 26 lessons across 3 tracks. Free forever. No login required. No gating. No "sign up to continue reading."

The only ask: at the end of each lesson, a natural bridge to a relevant paid product. Not a popup. Not a hard sell. A single line like "If you want the full system behind this, I built [Product Name] for exactly that."

## APPENDIX A: LESSON WRITING GUIDELINES

Every lesson must follow these rules (derived from VOICE_BIBLE.md):

1. **800-1200 words.** Not 500. Not 2,000. Enough to teach, short enough to finish in one sitting.
2. **One idea per paragraph.** Max 3 sentences per paragraph.
3. **First sentence hooks.** If the first sentence is boring, delete it and start with the second.
4. **Specific over vague.** "Use Ollama to run Llama 3 locally" not "use a local AI tool."
5. **Exercise must be doable today.** Not "think about" or "consider." Do something. Right now.
6. **Bridge is natural.** One sentence. Not a popup. Not a banner. A sentence that connects the lesson to a paid product the way a friend would mention something helpful.
7. **No banned words.** Check every lesson against the VOICE_BIBLE.md banned words list before publishing.
8. **No em dashes.** Periods and commas only.
9. **Conversational.** Write like you are texting someone smart. Not like you are writing a textbook.
10. **Include one opinion.** Content without opinions is furniture. Take a stance on something. Some people should disagree with you.
