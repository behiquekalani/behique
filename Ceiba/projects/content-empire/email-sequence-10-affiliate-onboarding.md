A few things that help conversion:

Tell people who it's for before you say what it is. "If you want to X" does more work than "this product is about Y."

Mention the price. It qualifies the buyer and removes the friction of clicking just to find out. $19.99 is not a hesitation price.

Include the disclosure. "Affiliate link" or "I earn a commission if you buy" in the caption or in a comment. Short and clear is fine.

You don't need a photo of the product. A relevant screenshot, a quote from inside it, or even a plain text slide works. The copy carries it.

If you have a newsletter, that's where I'd post first. Your subscribers have the highest intent. After that, Instagram or X.

Let me know when you post. I'll engage with it.

## Email 3 - Day 5 - Optimization

**Subject:** What to look at in Gumroad

---

By now you've either posted or you haven't. Either way, here's how to read the data.

In your Gumroad dashboard under Affiliates, you can see:

- Clicks: how many people clicked your link
- Conversions: how many of those clicks became purchases
- Conversion rate: clicks divided by sales

A normal conversion rate for cold traffic (people who don't know the product) is 1-3%. For warm traffic (your own audience, your newsletter) it's 3-8% or higher.

If you have 100 clicks and 0 sales, the link is working but something in your post isn't connecting. Most common issues: the audience isn't the right fit for the product, or the post explains what the product is without explaining why someone should care.

If you have 10 clicks total, the post didn't reach many people. That's a distribution problem, not a conversion problem. More posts, different formats, different platforms.

The fastest fix for low conversions: be more specific about who the product is for. Instead of "great for entrepreneurs," try "if you're a freelancer who wants to stop trading time for money, this is the guide I'd start with." Specificity beats broad appeal.

If you're seeing traction on a specific product, double down on that one before spreading to the full catalog. One product that converts beats ten products that don't.

Reply with your numbers if you want a second set of eyes.

- Kalani / Behike

## Email 4 - Day 10 - Advanced

**Subject:** Three things that move the needle past post #1

---

You've done the basics. Here's what separates affiliates who make $50 from affiliates who make $500+.

**1. Promote the bundle, not just single products.**

The Builder's Bundle (Solopreneur OS + Behike Method v2, $29.99) has a higher average order value and a stronger value proposition. "Two guides for $29 instead of $33" is a concrete reason to buy now. Your commission is $9.00 per bundle sale, which is 50% more per transaction than a single product sale.

**2. Seasonal and context timing matters.**

The AI and business products do well when there's a relevant news hook: a big AI announcement, a viral "I quit my job" post, or any moment when people are actively thinking about their situation. Keep a note of your best-performing content timing. Most digital product affiliates don't track this and leave obvious patterns on the table.

**3. Build a list from your affiliate posts.**

If you're creating content around these products, you're also creating content that attracts a specific type of person (builders, solopreneurs, AI-curious people). That audience is valuable beyond affiliate commissions. Start capturing emails now, even just a simple "reply to get my resource list." The people who engage with affiliate content are pre-qualified buyers for anything you sell later.

The math on this is straightforward: 100 email subscribers who trust you are worth more than 10,000 random followers over a 12-month horizon.

Try one of these this week. Let me know what you're working on.

- Kalani / Behike
