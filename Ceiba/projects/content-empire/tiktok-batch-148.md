# TikTok EN Batch 148
**Topic: The email welcome sequence that converts new subscribers to buyers**

## TikTok 1: Most Welcome Emails Do Nothing. Here's Why.
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your welcome email is the most important email you'll ever send. And most people waste it."

**Script:**
When someone subscribes to your list, they are at peak interest. They just saw something from you that made them want more. That moment is the highest open rate and the highest click rate you will ever get from that person.

And most creators send a generic "thanks for subscribing" email that says absolutely nothing.

Your welcome email should do four things. One: confirm who you are and why they should care. Two: tell them exactly what they're going to get. Three: give them something valuable right now, not later. Four: move them toward a next step.

That next step could be clicking a free resource. Replying to a question. Or visiting your product page.

The welcome email is not a formality. It is the first impression that determines whether someone becomes a reader or a buyer. Treat it like the most important email in your business. Because it is.

**CTA:**
Screenshot your current welcome email. Does it do any of those four things?

## TikTok 2: The 5-Email Welcome Sequence That Converts
**Hook (0-2s):**
"5 emails. That's all you need to convert a stranger into a buyer."

**Script:**
Here is the structure I use for a welcome sequence that actually sells.

Email one: delivered immediately. Give them the freebie or the promise you made. Make it better than they expected. End with a question: "What's your biggest challenge with X?"

Email two: delivered day two. Tell your story. Not your whole life. One specific moment where you faced their exact problem. Show that you understand their situation from the inside.

Email three: day three. Teach something genuinely useful. A tactic, a framework, a perspective shift. No selling yet. Build trust first.

Email four: day five. Social proof. A result you got. A result someone else got using your approach. Not fabricated. Real. Let results do the selling.

Email five: day seven. Introduce your product. Not as a pitch. As the natural next step. "If you want to go deeper on this, here's what I built for you."

Five emails. Seven days. Done. That sequence runs on autopilot while you create new content.

**CTA:**
Save this sequence and start writing email one today.

## TikTok 3: The One Question That Changes Everything
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Add this one line to your welcome email and watch your replies multiply."

**Script:**
Here is the simplest thing you can do to your welcome sequence to make it significantly better.

At the end of your very first email, ask one specific question. "What's the number one thing you're struggling with right now when it comes to X?"

Then actually read the replies.

This does two things. First, it tells Gmail and other email providers that your subscribers want to hear from you, which improves deliverability. Second, it gives you market research that money cannot buy.

The answers to that question are your next five content pieces. They are the language you use on your sales page. They are the exact problem your next product should solve.

Most email list owners never talk to their subscribers. The ones who do have an unfair advantage. They know exactly what to say because their audience told them.

One question. Infinite insight. Add it to your welcome email today.

**CTA:**
Tell me: what would you ask your new subscribers in that first email?

## TikTok 4: Why Your Welcome Sequence Needs a Clear Villain
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Great welcome sequences don't just introduce you. They name the enemy."

**Script:**
Every compelling story has a villain. In your welcome sequence, the villain is not a person. It is the bad advice, the broken system, or the flawed approach that keeps your audience stuck.

When you name the villain early, subscribers feel understood. They have been trying that bad advice. They have been stuck in that system. They thought it was their fault. You are telling them it is not.

"The reason you're not growing your audience isn't your content quality. It's that nobody taught you distribution."

That one sentence creates a micro-shift. The subscriber goes from "I must be bad at this" to "the system was wrong and this person gets it."

Now they trust you. Now they want to keep reading. Now when you introduce your product as the alternative to the villain's approach, it lands.

Your welcome sequence is a story arc. Know the villain before you write a single word.

**CTA:**
What is the villain in your niche? Write it in the comments.

## TikTok 5: The Metrics That Tell You If Your Sequence Is Working
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Here are the numbers you should be watching in your email sequence."

**Script:**
If you have a welcome sequence running, here are the metrics that tell you whether it is actually working.

Open rate on email one should be 50 percent or above. If it's below that, your subject line is failing or your deliverability is broken.

Open rate on emails two through five should be 30 to 40 percent. If it drops dramatically, you are losing people early and your first email isn't setting the right expectations.

Click rate on the product email should be 3 to 5 percent minimum. If it's below 1 percent, your offer is wrong for the audience or your positioning is off.

Reply rate on email one should be at least 1 to 2 percent. If you get zero replies, your question isn't compelling enough or the relationship isn't warm enough yet.

These are not arbitrary targets. They are benchmarks built from real campaigns. If you're hitting them, your sequence is converting. If you're not, now you know which email to fix first.

**CTA:**
Check your sequence analytics today. Which email is underperforming?
