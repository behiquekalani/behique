## TikTok 1: The Newsletter Strategy Nobody Tells You
**Hook (0-2s):**
"A newsletter with 500 subscribers can make more money than one with 50,000."

**Script:**
Everyone is chasing follower counts. They want 10K, 100K, a million.

But follower count is a vanity metric. What pays is trust.

Here's the math that changes how you think about newsletters.

A creator with 500 highly engaged subscribers in a specific niche. They trust you. They buy from you. Average conversion rate: 5 to 10 percent.

That's 25 to 50 buyers per email. At a $37 product, that's $925 to $1,850 per send.

A creator with 50,000 cold subscribers from a giveaway. Zero trust. Conversion rate: 0.1 percent.

That's 50 buyers. Same outcome, but you're managing 100x more people.

The lesson: build a small list of people who actually want what you sell. Do not grow for growth's sake.

One niche. One specific promise. One consistent offer.

Small and trusted beats big and indifferent every single time.

**CTA:**
If you want to build a newsletter that actually converts, the link in bio breaks down the whole system.

## TikTok 2: Three AI Tools I Use Every Single Day
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Three AI tools. Under $50 a month. I use all three every day."

**Script:**
ChatGPT for thinking. Claude for writing. Perplexity for research.

Here's how they work together.

When I have an idea for a post or product, I open Perplexity first. I search the topic and ask it to find me the most recent data, contrarian takes, and underreported angles. This takes five minutes.

Then I bring that research into Claude. I write my draft with Claude because its output sounds more like a human than any other model I've tested. I give it my tone, my structure, my thesis. It fills in the body.

Then I bring the draft into ChatGPT for stress-testing. I paste the draft and say: "Argue against every claim in this piece." If the counterarguments expose weaknesses, I fix them. If they don't, I know the piece is solid.

The whole process takes 20 to 30 minutes for a piece that used to take two hours.

You don't need expensive tools. You need the right combination.

## TikTok 3: Why Most Solopreneurs Stay Broke
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Most solopreneurs stay broke. Here's the pattern I keep seeing."

**Script:**
They build before they sell.

They spend three months creating a course, a product, a service. They pour everything into it. They launch. Nobody buys.

Not because the product is bad. Because nobody knew it was coming.

Here's what actually works.

Sell before you build.

You write about the problem publicly. Every week for at least four weeks. You build an audience of people who recognize the problem.

Then you announce a product that solves it. You take pre-orders. If you get ten, you build it. If you get zero, you learned something valuable before spending three months on the wrong thing.

This is called validation. Most people skip it because they want to create, not sell.

But selling is creating. You're creating the market first.

The most successful solopreneurs I've studied launched with an audience, not a product.

**CTA:**
Start writing before you build. One post, today. That's how it starts.

## TikTok 4: The Content System That Creates Itself
**Hook (0-2s):**
"I have more content ideas than I can post. Here's the system."

**Script:**
Most creators run out of ideas because they're trying to create from scratch.

Here is a better model.

Everything I consume gets recycled into content.

When I read an article, I write one sentence about what I disagreed with.
When I finish a book, I pick the one idea that changed how I think.
When I make a mistake in my business, I write down what I assumed was true that wasn't.

That's the content. Disagreements. Contrarian takeaways. Public learning.

These are not things you have to manufacture. You're already thinking them. You're just not capturing them.

I use a simple voice memo habit. Any time I have a reaction to something I read or see, I record a 30-second voice note. At the end of the week, I have ten to fifteen raw ideas waiting to be written.

Most of them are two-sentence TikToks. Some of them become essays.

You do not have a content idea problem. You have a capture problem.

**CTA:**
Start a voice memo habit this week. You'll thank yourself in two months.

## TikTok 5: The $0 to First Sale Framework
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Zero dollars, zero audience, zero experience. Here's how to get your first sale."

**Script:**
Step one: pick one specific problem you know how to solve better than most people. Not a broad topic. A specific pain point.

Step two: create one piece of content that teaches the solution, but not completely. You give the framework, not the implementation.

Step three: mention at the end that you have a template, checklist, or short guide that makes implementation faster. Price it between $7 and $15.

Step four: post it on TikTok, Twitter, and one relevant Reddit or Facebook group.

Step five: respond to every comment for 48 hours. The engagement tells the algorithm the content is worth pushing.

Step six: DM everyone who commented with a link to the product.

This is not a funnel. This is a conversation at scale.

The first sale is the hardest because you haven't proven to yourself that people will pay for your thinking. Once you see the first $9 come in, the belief shifts permanently.

**CTA:**
Tell me in the comments: what's one specific problem you know how to solve?
