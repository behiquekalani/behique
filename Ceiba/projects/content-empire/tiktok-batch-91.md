**Hook (0-2s):**
"You're using ChatGPT wrong — and it's costing you hours."

**Script:**
Everyone treats ChatGPT like a search engine. Type a question, get an answer, move on.

That's not how you use it.

The people actually saving time use it as a thinking partner. You give it context. You give it constraints. You give it your draft and tell it to punch holes in it.

Here's a workflow that takes 20 minutes off my content day:

Step one. Paste your rough idea into ChatGPT and say: "What's the strongest version of this argument?"

Step two. Take the response and say: "Now give me 3 counterarguments."

Step three. Write your final piece using the counterarguments as your structure.

You stop writing fluff because you already know the objections. Your content gets sharper because you're arguing against something real.

That's not AI replacing you. That's AI making you better.

## TikTok 4: The System That Replaced My Todo List
**Hook (0-2s):**
"I stopped using todo lists. Here's what I do instead."

**Script:**
Todo lists are a trap. You add 20 things, do 4, and feel like a failure every night.

Here's the system I switched to.

Each Sunday I write three things. Three outcomes I want to be true by Friday.

Not tasks. Outcomes.

Not "write 5 blog posts." But "have 5 blog posts ready to publish."

The difference is subtle but changes how you work. An outcome has a clear finish line. A task can be done halfway.

From those three outcomes, I pick the one highest-leverage action I can do Monday morning. That single action gets a time block. Nothing else goes in that block.

The rest of the week fills in naturally because I know exactly what I'm moving toward.

My output doubled. My stress dropped. I stopped feeling behind.

Most productivity systems fail because they track effort. This one tracks progress.
