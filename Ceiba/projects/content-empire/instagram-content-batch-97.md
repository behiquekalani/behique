Freelance copywriters at $50-200/hour.
Content agencies at $500-2000/month.

Not your thinking. Never your thinking.

**Slide 6:**

That's the only sustainable use of AI writing.
Follow @behikeai for the full prompt library.

**Caption:**
The complaint about AI writing is always the same: "It sounds like AI." That's a prompt problem, not a tool problem.

The people who get great output from AI writing tools do two things differently. They put their own opinions, frameworks, and specific examples into the prompt before asking for a draft. And they edit the output aggressively instead of publishing it raw.

AI writing is a first draft accelerator. It's not a replacement for your perspective, your lived experience, or your voice. The tools that replace expensive copywriters only replace them when a human with a point of view is guiding the process.

The workflow matters more than the tool. Claude or ChatGPT at $20/month plus 30 minutes of editing beats a $500 content brief every time, if you know how to use it.

**Slide 6:**

Follow @behikeai for the full automation playbook.

**Caption:**
Virtual assistants are valuable. But most solo operators in the early stages are paying for tasks that automation now handles better, faster, and without turnover.

The data you need is available for free or near free.

**Caption:**
Analytics platforms are one of the easiest places to over-invest. The enterprise tools are built for teams with dedicated analysts. Solo operators need four numbers: traffic, source, engagement, and conversion.

Every tool at the enterprise tier has a free or $10/month alternative that surfaces those same four numbers. Google Analytics 4 is genuinely powerful and free. Plausible gives you a cleaner view at $9/month. Beehiiv and ConvertKit have email analytics built in at the free tier.
