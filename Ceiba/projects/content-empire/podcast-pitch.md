## ANGLE A: The $0 AI Business (Entrepreneurship Podcasts)

### Subject Line
21-year-old CS student built an AI business with 25 products, 80+ agents, and $0 in costs

### Pitch (3 sentences)
I'm Kalani Andre, a computer engineering student in Puerto Rico who built a complete AI business for $0. I run 80+ AI agents across three machines using open-source models and local hardware. No venture capital, no cloud subscriptions, no team. Just one person, old laptops, and a system that proves you don't need money to build something real.

### Bio
Kalani Andre is a 21-year-old computer engineering student in Puerto Rico and the founder of Behike, an AI tools and education company. He built a catalog of 25 digital products in one week using local AI agents, spending $0 on infrastructure. His mission is to make AI accessible to people who can't afford the subscription stack that most creators recommend.

### Talking Points
1. **The $0 infrastructure breakdown.** What specific tools and hardware I use, why local AI beats cloud for bootstrapped founders, and the actual setup process anyone can follow.
2. **Building 25 products in 7 days.** How I used AI agents as a production team, what worked, what didn't, and why quality control is the human job.
3. **The gap between building and selling.** Honest take on having a complete product catalog and $0 in revenue. Why building is the comfortable part and selling is the real skill.
4. **Value ladder architecture.** How $4.99 products become a $66,000/year business when you design the right offers around them.
5. **Building from Puerto Rico.** Why being outside a tech hub is a feature, not a bug. How the island mentality shapes the business philosophy.

### What the Audience Gets
A concrete, step-by-step understanding of how to build an AI-powered business with no startup capital. Not theory. Real numbers, real tools, real challenges. Plus an honest look at what the "build in public" journey actually feels like when you're at $0 revenue.

## ANGLE B: ADHD as a Business Superpower (Mental Health / Productivity Podcasts)

### Subject Line
I turned my psychologist's ADHD framework into a Telegram bot that runs my business

### Pitch (3 sentences)
I was diagnosed with ADHD at 20 and my psychologist gave me a framework for managing attention, emotional patterns, and accountability. Instead of just following the framework, I coded it into a Telegram bot called BehiqueBot that classifies my ideas, tracks my emotional state, and holds me accountable automatically. Then I applied the same principle to my entire business: designing systems for my brain, not against it.

### Bio
Kalani Andre is a 21-year-old computer engineering student in Puerto Rico, diagnosed with ADHD and building an AI business designed around his neurodivergent brain. He works with a psychologist whose framework he turned into software, and he's built systems that treat ADHD as a different operating system rather than a deficit. His company Behike creates AI tools and education for people building businesses on their own terms.

### Talking Points
1. **The diagnosis story.** What changed after getting diagnosed, and why "nothing and everything" is the honest answer.
2. **The psychologist's framework.** Small tasks, concrete next steps, capture ideas immediately, validate effort over results. How this applies to business, not just personal life.
3. **BehiqueBot.** The Telegram bot that classifies ideas into 5 categories, tags them with life pillars (health, wealth, relationships), and runs accountability check-ins. Built from a real treatment plan.
4. **ADHD-native workflows.** Parallel tracks instead of sequential tasks. Short sprints instead of long projects. Automated maintenance instead of manual upkeep. How building 5 things at once is the feature, not the bug.
5. **Recognizing avoidance patterns.** My AI system (Ceiba) tracks my behavioral patterns and identified "infrastructure building as a proxy for not starting the scary thing." How naming the pattern breaks the cycle.
6. **Faith and family as anchors.** How having a clear "why" that's bigger than the business keeps you grounded when the ADHD brain wants to jump to the next shiny thing.

### What the Audience Gets
A real, non-romanticized look at building a business with ADHD. Not "ADHD is a superpower" toxic positivity. Not "ADHD is a curse" victim framing. The practical middle ground: understanding how your brain works and engineering your systems to match. Specific tools and frameworks your listeners can implement immediately.

## ANGLE C: The Local AI Revolution (Tech Podcasts)

### Subject Line
Why I stopped paying for cloud AI and run everything locally on old laptops

### Pitch (3 sentences)
I run a fleet of 80+ AI agents across a MacBook Air M4, a desktop with a GTX 1080 Ti, and a dedicated server. Monthly cloud cost: $0. I use Ollama for local inference, open-source models for every task, and a custom orchestration system called Ceiba that connects everything. This is the future of AI for small businesses, and nobody's talking about it.

### Bio
Kalani Andre is a 21-year-old computer engineering student in Puerto Rico who runs a production AI infrastructure entirely on local hardware. He operates Behike, an AI tools company where he builds and sells AI systems designed to run without cloud subscriptions. His local-first philosophy comes from both technical conviction and economic necessity, and he believes the next wave of AI adoption will be local, not cloud.

### Talking Points
1. **The local AI stack in detail.** Ollama setup, model selection (which models for which tasks), multi-machine orchestration, and performance benchmarks on consumer hardware.
2. **Why local beats cloud for most use cases.** Privacy (data never leaves the building), cost ($0 vs. $200+/month), reliability (no API outages), and control (no terms of service changes).
3. **The Ceiba system.** A personal AI system that serves as memory, thinking partner, and execution assistant. How it's architected, what it connects, and why persistent AI context matters more than raw model performance.
4. **Running AI on a GTX 1080 Ti in 2026.** The card cost $200 used. It runs 7B and 13B models faster than most people's cloud API calls return. Consumer GPU AI is massively underestimated.
5. **Multi-machine orchestration.** How I route tasks between machines based on capability. Heavy inference goes to the GPU machine (Cobo). Light tasks stay on the MacBook (Ceiba). Background jobs run on the server (Hutia).
6. **What this means for small businesses.** Most businesses already have hardware that can run useful AI models. The gap is knowledge, not capability. Setup takes one afternoon. The ROI starts the next day.

### What the Audience Gets
A technical but accessible breakdown of running production AI workloads on consumer hardware. Real benchmarks, real architecture decisions, real tradeoffs. Your listeners will leave knowing exactly what hardware they need, what software to install, and what performance to expect.

## OUTREACH PLAN

### Phase 1: Weeks 1-2
- Research each podcast (listen to at least 1 episode each)
- Start with podcasts 8-13 (ADHD angle). Smallest audience, highest fit, best for practice.
- Send 3 pitches per week. Personalize each one.

### Phase 2: Weeks 3-4
- Follow up on Phase 1 pitches (one follow-up email, 7 days after initial pitch)
- Start pitching podcasts 14-20 (Tech angle). Medium audience, strong differentiation.
- Send 3 pitches per week.

### Phase 3: Weeks 5-8
- Follow up on Phase 2 pitches
- Start pitching podcasts 1-7 (Entrepreneurship angle). Largest audience, hardest to land.
- Send 2 pitches per week (these take more personalization)

### Phase 4: Ongoing
- Track responses in a simple spreadsheet: podcast name, date pitched, response, follow-up date, recording date
- After each appearance, ask the host for introductions to other podcasters
- Repurpose every podcast appearance into 5+ pieces of content (clips, quotes, social posts)

### Rules
- Never pitch more than one angle to the same podcast
- Never follow up more than twice
- Always listen to at least one episode before pitching
- Always mention a specific episode or segment in the pitch
- If rejected, respond graciously. The podcasting world is small.
