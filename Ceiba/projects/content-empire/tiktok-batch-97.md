## TikTok 1: The Content Repurposing Framework Nobody Talks About
**Hook (0-2s):**
One piece of content. Seven posts. Here's the system.

**Script:**
Most people repurpose content by cutting it up. That's not repurposing. That's chopping.

Real repurposing is format translation. The same idea expressed differently for different contexts.

Here's the framework. Start with one long-form piece. A blog post, a newsletter, a YouTube video. That's your source.

From that one piece, extract the core thesis. That becomes a tweet or short post.

Extract the best example inside it. That becomes a TikTok or Reel.

Extract the lesson. That becomes a carousel.

Extract the question it answers. That becomes a poll or Q&A.

You haven't copied anything. You've translated one idea into four formats. Four pieces of content from one hour of thinking.

The output multiplies. The thinking happens once.

**CTA:**
Save this framework. Use it on your last piece of content today.

## TikTok 2: Why Most Repurposed Content Flops
**Hook (0-2s):**
You repurposed your content. It flopped anyway. Here's why.

**Script:**
The problem with most repurposed content is that it assumes every platform is the same.

Instagram wants a strong visual thesis. Twitter wants a sharp observation. TikTok wants to feel like a conversation.

When you take a LinkedIn post and paste it into a TikTok caption, it dies. Not because the idea is bad. Because the format is wrong for the room.

Every platform has a native language. Native LinkedIn sounds like a business insight. Native TikTok sounds like something a friend told you at a coffee shop.

Same idea. Different delivery. That's the actual translation work.

Before you repurpose, ask: what does this platform's best content sound like? Then write it in that language.

The idea travels. The format doesn't.

**CTA:**
Which platform are you worst at translating to? Drop it below.

## TikTok 3: The One Asset That Repurposes Into Everything
**Hook (0-2s):**
If you build this first, everything else gets easier.

**Script:**
The most repurposable piece of content you can create is a long-form video with your voice on it.

Not because video is king. Because voice carries tone, rhythm, and personality in a way text can't.

You transcribe it: blog post.
You pull clips: TikToks and Reels.
You pull quotes: Twitter and LinkedIn posts.
You pull chapters: newsletter sections.
You pull the outline: YouTube Shorts.

One recording session. Six months of content.

The people who seem to be everywhere aren't producing more. They're building from a single source that splits in every direction.

Podcast. Long video. In-depth interview. Pick one format where you talk. That's your root. Everything else grows from there.

**CTA:**
What's your root format? The thing you do that splits into everything else?

## TikTok 4: How to Repurpose Without Sounding Repetitive
**Hook (0-2s):**
Your audience follows you everywhere. You need this.

**Script:**
If your audience follows you on Instagram, LinkedIn, and Twitter, they'll see the same idea three times. That sounds like a problem. It's not, if you do this right.

Each platform gets a different angle on the same idea.

Same topic: why most people price their products too low.

LinkedIn angle: professional credibility and business strategy.
Twitter angle: one observation, one counterintuitive insight.
Instagram angle: personal story, emotional hook, lesson at the end.

The idea is the same. The entry point is different. Your audience doesn't experience it as repetition. They experience it as depth.

You're not repeating yourself. You're showing the same thing from three different sides.

**CTA:**
Take your last post. Right now. How would you say it differently on another platform?

## TikTok 5: Repurposing for People Who Hate Creating Content
**Hook (0-2s):**
You don't hate creating content. You hate starting from zero.

**Script:**
The blank page problem is the real problem. Not creativity. Not ideas. The terror of starting from nothing.

Repurposing fixes this permanently.

Once you have 30 pieces of content, you never start from zero again. You start from something that already worked.

Pick your best performing post from the last 90 days. Ask: why did this do well? What was the core idea?

Now express that same idea with a new example. Or from a different angle. Or for a different audience segment.

You're not recycling. You're iterating. Every good idea has at least five versions in it.

The best creators aren't the most original. They're the most thorough. They squeeze every idea until it's dry.

**CTA:**
Go find your best post from last month. That's your next three posts right there.
