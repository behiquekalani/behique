## Script 2: "The 3 mistakes that kill Gumroad sales before anyone even reads your page"

**Caption:** 3 Gumroad mistakes killing your sales before anyone reads the page.
**Hashtags:** #gumroad #digitalprod #solopreneur #onlinebusiness #sellonline #passiveincome #conversiontips

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
Your Gumroad product is not selling. It is probably not the product. It is one of these three things.

[B-roll: Gumroad product page with zero sales showing]

[0:06] **MISTAKE 1**
Bad cover image. Your cover is the only thing buyers see before they decide to click. If it looks like a Google Slides default, they do not click. The cover needs to communicate the outcome in under two seconds. Not the topic. The result.

[B-roll: two product covers side by side, one generic, one clean and outcome-focused]

[0:22] **MISTAKE 2**
A title that describes the product instead of the transformation. "AI Prompts for Business" tells me what it is. "Get a week of content written in 30 minutes using AI" tells me what I get. Buyers purchase outcomes, not features.

[B-roll: split screen of weak title vs strong title in Gumroad editor]

[0:40] **MISTAKE 3**
No social proof on the page. Not a single screenshot, quote, or result. Buyers do not know you. They need to see that someone else trusted this product and got something from it. Even two or three testimonials change the conversion rate significantly.

[B-roll: Gumroad product page with testimonial section added, before/after]

[0:58]
Fix those three things before you spend any time on ads or promotion. The traffic is not the problem. The page is.

[B-roll: person editing Gumroad page, making changes]

## Script 3: "How to price a digital product when you have no audience"

**Caption:** How to price a digital product with zero audience. Honest answer.
**Hashtags:** #digitalprod #solopreneur #gumroad #productpricing #onlinebusiness #passiveincome #buildinpublic

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
No audience. No testimonials. No track record. Here is how you price a digital product so it still sells.

[B-roll: blank Gumroad product listing, price field empty]

[0:06] **THE TRAP**
Most people with no audience price too high because they think the product is worth it, or too low because they are afraid no one will buy. Both are the wrong frame. Price should be based on the buyer's risk, not your effort.

[B-roll: pricing comparison visual, high vs low, with conversion indicators]

[0:22] **THE LOGIC**
When you have no audience, the buyer's risk is high. They do not know you. They have no evidence this works. So you want the price to be low enough that clicking "buy" feels like a no-brainer, not a decision.

[B-roll: buyer on phone, hesitating at $97 vs clicking fast at $7]

[0:36]
For a first product with no proof, $7 to $17 is the right zone. Low enough to remove friction. High enough to signal it is not junk. Your goal at this stage is not revenue. It is proof of concept and first testimonials.

[B-roll: Gumroad page at $9 with purchases coming in]

[0:52]
Once you have ten buyers and three good reviews, raise the price. You now have social proof. That is what justified the higher price, not the product getting better.

[B-roll: Gumroad page being edited, price going up, testimonials section visible]

[1:05] **CTA**
Pricing is a conversation, not a calculation. My product guide covers the full framework. Link in bio.

[B-roll: phone showing bio link]

## Script 5: "The difference between a product that sells and one that does not"

**Caption:** The difference between a digital product that sells and one that sits.
**Hashtags:** #digitalprod #solopreneur #gumroad #onlinebusiness #productcreation #passiveincome #conversiontips

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
Two people build the same type of digital product. One sells consistently. One gets zero purchases. The content is almost identical. Here is what is different.

[B-roll: two Gumroad products side by side, one with sales, one without]

[0:08] **THE SURFACE ANSWER**
Most people say it is marketing. That is part of it. But I have seen products with no promotion sell and products with heavy promotion fail. Marketing amplifies the product. It does not fix it.

[B-roll: ad campaign running with no conversions showing]

[0:22] **THE REAL DIFFERENCE**
Specificity. The product that sells solves a specific problem for a specific person at a specific moment. The product that does not sell is useful to everyone in general, which means it feels essential to no one in particular.

[B-roll: broad generic product title vs hyper-specific product title on Gumroad]

[0:40]
"Social media tips for businesses" sells to nobody. "How to write a week of LinkedIn posts in two hours if you hate writing" sells to the exact person who has been putting it off.

[B-roll: two product descriptions, vague vs specific, side by side]

[0:55]
The more specific you are, the smaller the audience feels. But the smaller the audience, the higher the conversion rate, because you are talking directly to someone instead of past them.

[B-roll: product page with specific pain point called out in headline]

[1:05] **CTA**
Specificity is a skill you build. I cover how in my newsletter. Link in bio.

[B-roll: phone showing newsletter link in bio]
