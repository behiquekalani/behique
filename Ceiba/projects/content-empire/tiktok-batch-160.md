Not the website traffic. One dollar from a stranger is more validating than a thousand likes from friends. When you hit $1K, you stop theorizing and start iterating. The first thousand is the hardest. Every thousand after that is a repeat.

## Script 2: The Fastest Path to $1K
**Hook (0-3s):** There is a faster path to your first $1K online than you think.
**Body:** Solve one specific problem. Package the solution into a simple digital product. Price it between $17 and $47. Put it in front of the people who have that problem. Ten sales at $100. Twenty-five sales at $40. Sixty sales at $17. That is the math. The question is not whether the math works. It is whether you have identified a real problem and a real audience. That research takes a weekend. The product takes a week. The $1K takes a month.
**CTA:** Save this and map your first product using this math.
**On-screen text:** ["One problem. One product.", "Price: $17-$47", "10 sales at $100 = $1K", "Research, build, sell"]

## Script 3: Services First, Products Second
**Hook (0-3s):** If you have zero audience, sell a service before you sell a product.
**Body:** A product requires people to find it. A service can be sold to three people you already know. Services are faster to the first dollar because you are trading time for money directly, not waiting for discovery. Do that until you have cash, testimonials, and clarity on what problems you keep solving. Then productize the most common service. You already know it works. You have already been paid for it. The product is just the service at scale.
**CTA:** Follow if you are starting from zero.
**On-screen text:** ["Service → Product", "Trade time first, then scale", "Testimonials from service = product proof", "Productize what already works"]

## Script 5: Stop Waiting to Be Ready
**Hook (0-3s):** Ready is a feeling, not a status. You will never feel it.
**Body:** The creator who waits until they feel ready launches in year three. The creator who ships before they feel ready launches in month two. Both started at the same time. The difference: one made a decision. Readiness is not a pre-condition for starting. It is a result of starting. You get ready by doing the thing imperfectly and learning from what happens. The fear does not go away before launch. It goes away after the first sale.
