## Script 1: No Audience, No Problem. Here Is Where to Start.
**Hook (0-3s):** Zero followers does not mean zero sales. Here is proof.

**Script:**
Zero followers does not mean zero sales. Here is proof.

Most people wait to build an audience before selling anything. That is backwards.

Here is the order that works.

First, build the product or offer. Even if it is small. A guide, a service, a template. Something specific that solves a specific problem.

Second, find where the people who have that problem already gather. Reddit, Facebook groups, Discord servers, niche forums. Those communities already exist.

Third, show up there with value. Answer questions. Be useful. When it is appropriate and relevant, mention what you built.

You do not need 10,000 followers to make your first 10 sales. You need to be in the right room with the right offer.

Audience building is a long game worth playing. But it is not a prerequisite for revenue.

Most people never start because they are waiting for a number that does not need to come first.

**CTA:** Tell me your offer and I will tell you which community to go find.

## Script 2: The Cold DM Strategy That Actually Works
**Hook (0-3s):** Cold DMs get ignored because people do it wrong. Here is the version that works.

**Script:**
Cold DMs get ignored because people do it wrong. Here is the version that works.

Most cold DMs are pitches. "Hey, I noticed your business could use X, want to hop on a call?"

No one wants that.

The cold DM that works is a genuine observation. You actually looked at their content or product. You mention something specific. You ask a question or make an observation that shows you paid attention.

No pitch. No link. No ask.

If they reply, you have a conversation. The sale comes later, after trust is established.

Here is the structure. Open with a specific compliment or observation. Ask one relevant question. Close with something that gives them an easy yes or no.

The goal of the first message is not to sell. It is to get a reply. That is it.

Running a business with no audience means doing this kind of outreach manually until you have built enough to attract inbound. It is not glamorous. It is effective.

**CTA:** Save this. Write your next DM using this structure.

## Script 3: Post Before You Are Ready. Here Is Why.
**Hook (0-3s):** Waiting until your content is perfect is why you still have no audience.

**Script:**
Waiting until your content is perfect is why you still have no audience.

The algorithm does not reward perfection. It rewards consistency and engagement.

Your first 50 posts are practice. They are not meant to go viral. They are meant to build the skill of showing up and communicating clearly about what you do.

Most people treat post one like it is their launch. It is not. It is day one of a long training cycle.

Here is what to post when you have nothing to say.

Document what you are building. Share the process, not just the result. Post a lesson you learned this week. Share a mistake you made and what it taught you. Take a position on something in your space.

None of that requires expertise. It requires honesty and a consistent point of view.

The people who built audiences did not have better content in week one. They just did not stop.

Show up before you are ready. The readiness comes from showing up.

**CTA:** Post something today. Tell me what topic in the comments.

## Script 4: How to Get Your First 100 Followers Without Spending a Dollar
**Hook (0-3s):** Your first 100 followers are already in your phone.

**Script:**
Your first 100 followers are already in your phone.

Not through cold outreach. Through connection.

Every person you know has a network. When you start building something publicly and you tell the people already in your life, some of them will follow. Some of them will share. And their network becomes your second layer.

This is not about spamming your contacts. It is about being clear about what you are building and inviting people who are genuinely interested.

Tell 10 people this week. Say "I just started posting about X, check it out if you are curious." Not a pitch. An invitation.

Then engage genuinely on other accounts in your space. Comment with actual thought, not empty compliments. Follow people whose content you find useful. The algorithm notices, and so do the people you engage with.

One hundred followers from real engagement is worth more than 10,000 from a giveaway. The first group trusts you. The second does not know you.

Build slow and real at the start. It compounds.

**CTA:** Tell me your page and I will drop a genuine comment.

## Script 5: The Content Flywheel for People Starting from Zero
**Hook (0-3s):** One piece of content done right can fuel a whole week of posts.

**Script:**
One piece of content done right can fuel a whole week of posts.

Here is the flywheel.

Start with a long-form piece. A blog post, a video, a detailed thread. Something with depth that required thought.

Then extract from it. Pull a quote and make it a graphic. Pull the main point and make it a short video. Pull three sub-points and make three separate posts. Pull a question from it and use it as a poll or conversation starter.

That is five pieces of content from one original idea.

You do not need more ideas. You need to extract more from the good ideas you have.

When you are starting from zero and have limited time, this model is sustainable. You think once. You distribute multiple times. The algorithm sees consistency. Your audience sees variety.

Most people are trying to have five original ideas a week. That burns out fast. One good idea executed across five formats lasts the same amount of time with a fraction of the mental load.

**CTA:** What topic could you build a flywheel around this week.
