## Post 4 — The One-Hour Newsletter Writing System

**Hook:** Writing a great newsletter issue should take one hour. If it's taking you longer, you don't have a writing problem. You have a structure problem.

**Slide 1:** Writing a great newsletter issue should take one hour. If it's taking you longer, you don't have a writing problem. You have a structure problem.

**Slide 2:** The one-hour newsletter structure:
- 10 min: Write the core idea in one sentence
- 15 min: Expand into 3-4 main points
- 20 min: Add one concrete example per point
- 10 min: Write the intro and CTA
- 5 min: Edit for clarity

**Slide 3:** The intro formula:
One sentence that names the problem.
One sentence that says why it matters now.
One sentence that tells them what they'll get from reading.
Done. Under 50 words.

**Slide 4:** The CTA formula:
One action. Not five.
"Click here to get X" or "Reply with Y" or "Forward this to someone who needs it."
The weaker your CTA, the lower your engagement rate.

**Slide 5:** What separates a good newsletter from a great one:
Not length. Not design. Not frequency.
One original idea, stated clearly, with one piece of proof.
Your unique perspective on a problem your reader is currently facing.

**Slide 6:** The Ghost Writer Kit has newsletter templates included.
Write better issues in less time.
behike.gumroad.com | Link in bio.

**Caption:** Most newsletter writers spend too much time writing and not enough time structuring.

The one-hour constraint forces you to get to the point. It forces you to decide what the issue is actually about before you start writing. It forces you to cut the parts that don't serve the main idea.

The best newsletter issues I've received are not the longest ones. They're the clearest ones. One idea, stated in full, with a concrete example and a clear action.

The reader doesn't want a comprehensive guide every week. They want one thing to think about, one thing to try, and the feeling that you wrote it specifically for them.

Achieve that in 600 words. Then stop.

The one-hour system forces the clarity that makes newsletters worth reading.

Multiple revenue streams active simultaneously
