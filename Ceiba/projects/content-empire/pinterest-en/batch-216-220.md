## Pin 216: LinkedIn for Creators in 2025: What Actually Works

**Description:**
LinkedIn is not a resume platform anymore. For creators, it is the highest-signal distribution channel available for professional audiences. The people growing on LinkedIn in 2025 share one thing in common: they post their own perspective on real problems, not industry news or motivational content without specifics. The format that works is a strong first line, short paragraphs, and a clear point of view at the end. Posting three times a week consistently for six months builds more than sporadic perfect content. But the most underused strategy is commenting, leaving five-sentence responses on posts by accounts with larger followings exposes your thinking to new readers without needing to publish anything. This breakdown covers format, topics, and the consistency structure that compounds.

## Pin 217: Substack vs Self-Hosted Newsletter: How to Make the Right Call

**Description:**
Substack reduces friction for new newsletter creators, zero setup, built-in payments, and a discovery network. But it charges roughly 10% of subscription revenue and limits data ownership and design control. A self-hosted newsletter built on ConvertKit, Beehiiv, or Ghost gives you full data ownership, higher margin on paid subscriptions, and complete integration flexibility. The fixed monthly cost replaces the percentage cut. The real decision depends on your stage: under 1,000 subscribers with no paid product yet, Substack is a reasonable starting point. With traction, a clear offer, and committed subscribers, the economics of migration favor moving early. The cost of migrating grows over time. This breakdown covers the real numbers and the criteria to make the decision with clarity instead of preference.

## Pin 218: Ghostwriting as a Serious Business: How to Build It from Scratch

**Description:**
Ghostwriting is not writing for others because you lack your own platform. It is offering communication capacity to people who have deep knowledge but not the time, skill, or process to express it as content. The market is large and growing. Executives need LinkedIn presence but cannot write for the platform. Founders want newsletters but do not have the habit or system. The entry point that works is a specific niche and platform combination, not generic content writing. The process that produces quality output is a 45-minute extraction call, a transformation phase, and a light revision. Monthly retainers at $1,000 to $2,000 are more sustainable than per-piece pricing. This guide covers how to find first clients, price the work, and build a practice that compounds.

## Pin 219: The Annual Business Review That Actually Changes Your Direction

**Description:**
Most annual reviews are lists of accomplishments and goals. They do not change behavior because they do not diagnose anything. A review that works asks four questions: what worked that you did not scale, what you stopped doing that was working, where you invested energy with low return, and what you consistently avoided. The process has three parts: a full inventory of everything you did, an energy-versus-result analysis mapping each item to a two-by-two grid, and a clear allocation decision about what to stop, maintain, or scale. The most important categories in the analysis are high effort with low return and low effort with high return. One is where you are losing. The other is where your untapped advantage lives. This breakdown includes the full framework and the relationship inventory most creators skip.

## Pin 220: Paid Workshops vs Full Courses: Which One Should You Build First

**Description:**
The answer depends on your stage and how much you know about what your audience actually needs. Workshops have faster production cycles, lower purchase friction, and the direct client contact that shows you what parts of your expertise create the most impact. A well-run paid workshop at $97 to $197 can outperform a full course in volume conversion and deliver richer feedback for future product development. Full courses are long-term assets, but they carry higher production risk when built on assumptions rather than validated content. The strategy that works: use workshops to test ideas, identify the sections with the highest impact, and build your course from proven material. This breakdown covers the financial comparison, the production process for both formats, and how to sequence them for compounding revenue.
