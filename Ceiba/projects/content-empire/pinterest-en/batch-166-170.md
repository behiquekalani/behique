## Pin 169

**Title:** Why Solopreneurs Stay Broke (And How to Fix It)

**Description:**
Most solopreneurs aren't broke because their niche is wrong or their content isn't good enough.

They're broke because of three structural errors:

1. No product. They're selling time only. Time is finite. Products scale.
2. No list. They're building on borrowed platforms. When the algorithm changes, they lose reach overnight.
3. No clear offer. Their audience doesn't know what to buy or why it's for them.

Fix these three things and the revenue problem changes.

Create one product. Start one email list. Make one offer that names the specific problem it solves and who it's for.

That's the foundation. Everything else, content, traffic, growth, is optimization on top of that foundation.

## Pin 170

**Title:** The Simple Rule for Pricing Your Digital Products

**Description:**
The most common digital product pricing mistake: pricing based on how long it took to create instead of the outcome it delivers.

A $27 ebook that saves someone 40 hours of trial and error is underpriced by almost any measure. A $97 course that helps someone avoid a $5,000 mistake is a bargain.

The buyer isn't paying for your creation time. They're paying for the result they get.

To price correctly, answer this question: what is the specific outcome of this product, and what is that outcome worth to the person who needs it most?

Start there. Price from value, not effort. The number will likely be higher than your instinct says, and the right buyers will agree.
