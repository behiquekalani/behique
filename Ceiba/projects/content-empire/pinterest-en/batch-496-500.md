## Pin 498: Time Blocking for Maximum Focus

**Title:** Time Blocking System That Actually Sticks (For Scattered Brains)

**Description:** Time blocking sounds simple. Reserve blocks of time for specific types of work. In practice, most people try it for a week and abandon it because their blocks are too rigid, too optimistic, or too detailed to survive contact with a real workday. The time blocking system that sticks uses a different approach. Block by category, not by task. Create three to four categories for your workweek: deep creative work, client delivery, admin and communication, and personal recharge. Assign specific windows to each category based on your natural energy peaks. Deep creative work belongs in your highest-energy window. Admin belongs in your lowest. Then within each block, use a simple task list rather than a precise schedule. This gives you structure without rigidity. The system adapts to reality without falling apart. Save this and try the category-based approach for two weeks before deciding if time blocking works for you.
