**Thread 4**
**Topic:** The wrong way to use AI for content (and what to do instead)

Tweet 1:
Using AI to write your content from scratch and posting it unchanged is not leverage. It's a shortcut to sounding like everyone else. Your audience follows you for your specific thinking, not for averaged internet prose.

Tweet 2:
AI is not the author. You are. AI is the editor, the formatter, the first draft generator, the options machine. You bring the idea, the angle, the real experience. AI helps you get it out of your head faster.

Tweet 3:
The right workflow: you write the core idea in your own rough words. You give that to AI with context about your voice and audience. AI expands, formats, or tightens it. You review and inject the pieces only you could add.

Tweet 4:
The test: could someone else have written this output? If yes, it lacks your specific voice, story, or point of view. Add the layer only you bring. That layer is what builds an audience, not the polished prose around it.

Tweet 5:
Build your voice document. A page that describes how you write: your sentence rhythm, the phrases you use, the topics you avoid, the tone. Give that to AI every time. The output will be closer to yours and still produced at speed.
