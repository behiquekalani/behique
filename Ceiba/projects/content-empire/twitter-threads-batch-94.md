## Thread 94-2: LinkedIn Is the Best Kept Secret in 2026

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
Most creators post on LinkedIn like it's a resume site.
The ones who treat it like a content platform are quietly building massive audiences.

Here's what they know:

**Tweet 2**
1/ LinkedIn's feed heavily favors text posts.

Paradoxically, the platform built on professional networking rewards raw, personal, honest writing more than polished graphics.
A thoughtful 200-word post will outperform a designed carousel in most cases.

**Tweet 3**
2/ The audience is different from every other platform.

Higher average income. Higher purchase intent. Longer attention span.
One LinkedIn follower converts at a higher rate than 10 Twitter followers in most niches.
Quality of reach matters more than quantity.

**Tweet 4**
3/ First-line hook is everything.

LinkedIn shows only the first line before the "see more" break.
If that line doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.
Write the first line. Then write the post.

**Tweet 5**
4/ The comment section multiplies reach.

A post with 30 comments gets pushed to second and third-degree connections.
Reply to every comment in the first hour.
The algorithm reads this as high engagement and amplifies distribution.

**Tweet 6**
5/ Long posts outperform short ones on LinkedIn.

The opposite of Twitter.
LinkedIn rewards posts that take 90+ seconds to read.
Depth signals credibility in a way that short posts don't.

**Tweet 7**
6/ The opportunity window may not last.

LinkedIn was a late-mover for content creators and the platform is still catching up.
That gap means less competition and more organic reach than anywhere else right now.
Get in while the window is open.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
If you're ignoring LinkedIn in 2026, you're leaving organic reach on the table.
Start posting. The audience is there and they're ready to buy.

## Thread 94-3: YouTube Shorts: The Underrated Asymmetric Bet

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
You can get 200,000 views on YouTube with 47 subscribers.
That's not hypothetical. It happened last week to a creator I follow.

Here's why YouTube Shorts is the biggest organic opportunity right now:

**Tweet 2**
1/ YouTube is subsidizing Shorts to compete with TikTok.

That means they're pushing Shorts to non-subscribers at a rate no other platform is doing for established creators.
This is a manufactured organic reach window. It will close.

**Tweet 3**
2/ Shorts discovery is algorithm-first, subscriber-second.

Subscribers help. They're not the mechanism.
The mechanism is watch completion rate.
A 45-second Short that gets watched fully beats a 5M-subscriber channel's Short that gets skipped.

**Tweet 4**
3/ The conversion path from Shorts is improving.

YouTube now lets you link to long-form content, to products, to your channel.
Someone who watches 15 of your Shorts is a warm lead.
The funnel is there if you build it.

**Tweet 5**
4/ The skills transfer from other platforms.

If you're already scripting Instagram Reels or TikToks, your content works on Shorts.
Same format, same hooks, larger potential distribution.
There's no reason not to cross-post.

**Tweet 6**
5/ Shorts stack your channel over time.

Each Short is indexed on YouTube search forever.
A Short you made 8 months ago can still drive views today.
It's a library that compounds.

**Tweet 7**
6/ The one thing to avoid: posting Shorts with TikTok watermarks.

YouTube's algorithm penalizes repurposed content with visible competitor branding.
Use a clean export or a removal tool before uploading.
Small detail. Significant impact on reach.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
If you're creating short-form content and not uploading it to YouTube Shorts, you're losing free reach.
The window is open right now. Use it.

## Thread 94-5: How to Pick the Right Platform for Your Stage

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
The wrong platform for your stage wastes months of effort.
The right one compounds everything.

Here's how to pick:

**Tweet 2**
1/ Stage 1: Building (0-500 followers anywhere)

Focus: one platform, one format, deep understanding.
Goal: learn what resonates and build initial proof.
Best options: X for writers, YouTube Shorts for video, LinkedIn for B2B topics.

**Tweet 3**
2/ Stage 2: Growing (500-5,000)

Your first platform is giving you signals.
Now you understand what works.
Add one more platform and repurpose your best-performing content there.
Do not start fresh on platform two. Extract from platform one.

**Tweet 4**
3/ Stage 3: Scaling (5,000+)

At this stage you should have email as your primary asset.
Social platforms become distribution channels for the email list.
You're not building on platforms. You're building through them.

**Tweet 5**
4/ The format question matters as much as the platform question.

Are you a writer? Build on text-first platforms.
Are you a talker? Build on video-first platforms.
Working against your natural format increases friction. Increase friction and you'll stop.

**Tweet 6**
5/ Ask one question before picking a platform.

"Where is my specific buyer spending time right now?"
Not "where do I want to be."
Not "where is the most traffic."
Where is your buyer?

**Tweet 7**
6/ Evaluate every 90 days, not every week.

90 days is the minimum time to see whether a platform is working.
Jumping platforms every 3 weeks is how you stay a beginner on every platform indefinitely.
Commit to a cycle. Evaluate honestly. Pivot with data.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
One platform, mastered, beats five platforms abandoned.
Pick your platform.
Post consistently for 90 days.
Then evaluate.

That's the whole strategy.
