## Essay 1 (Makeup Palette)
I've kept a small makeup palette in my vanity for over five years because it reminds me of when I first started getting into skincare and makeup. It was one of the first palettes I ever bought with my own money, and even though the colors are mostly used up by now, I can't bring myself to throw it away. Every time I see it, it takes me back to when I was just learning how to blend and trying different looks in front of the mirror for hours. That palette reminds me of a time when I was discovering something that made me feel creative and confident. Makeup became more than just a hobby for me, it became a way to express myself and take care of myself at the same time. Even though I have way better products now, that little palette will always have a special place because it represents the beginning of something I'm still passionate about today.

## Essay 6 (Boxing Tickets)

My family member is also a boxing fan, so I was taken to see a boxing match live at that arena and that experience will forever be burned in my mind because the whole building was filled with such anticipation and excitement from each person, cheering for the boxer that they wanted to see win. When I returned home after the boxing match, I made sure that I saved my tickets instead of discarding them like many others did; I knew at that moment my tickets would always remind me of the fun I had that evening and the time I spent with my own favorite family member enjoying a night of boxing together.

## Essay 8 (Two-Dollar Bills)

I've kept several two-dollar bills tucked away in a special envelope for a long time because they remind me of the importance of valuing things that are rare. I first began collecting them years ago when I realized they weren't commonly found in daily circulation, making every one I encountered feel like a lucky find. To me, these bills are more than just currency; they represent a long-term treasure that I believe will gain significant historical and emotional value as time passes. Much like the lists of "good things" that Sister Helen's students kept for decades, these bills serve as a reminder that just because something is hidden or uncommon doesn't mean it lacks worth. Holding onto them gives me a sense of patience and the hope that preserving unique pieces of the past will eventually pay off in the future.

Whenever I see it, it takes me back to that simple day with no responsibilities, just being a kid and enjoying the moment. It reminds me that not every meaningful memory has to be big or dramatic; sometimes it's just a quiet afternoon that stays with you.

## Essay 16 (Grandma's Recipe Card)
I have kept a small, yellowed index card with Grandma's handwritten recipe for rice, beans, and fried pork chops in my kitchen drawer for many years because it reminds me of an experience I treasure deeply. I received this card during the final summer before I moved away due to hurricane Maria destroying my home and moving in with her. I remember sitting at her small wooden table, watching her seasoned hands move with precision as she seared the chuletas and perfectly seasoned the sofrito. She didn't usually use measurements, but that afternoon, she insisted I write every step down so I would always have a "taste of home" wherever I went. Today, the card is frazzled at the edges and stained with oil spots and a faint smudge of orange achiote, making it look as worn as the lists carried by Sister Helen's students. To me, this souvenir is not just about food; it represents Grandma's transition from provider to teacher, ensuring her love and heritage would sustain me even in her absence. Every time I pull it out to cook, I can almost hear the lid of the caldero clinking and her voice reminding me to never let the rice burn.

## Essay 19 (Football Championship Trophy)
