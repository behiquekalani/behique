**Hook (0-3s):** Every creator who blew up chasing trends had to find a new trend immediately after. That's not a business. That's a treadmill.

**Body (4-50s):**

The people who benefit most from trends already have positioning. They ride the wave with momentum they built elsewhere.

For everyone else, chasing trends means starting from zero each time.

And it trains your audience to follow you for novelty, not for what you know. That's a fragile relationship. The moment you slow down, they leave.

It also destroys your positioning.

One creator writes about deep work every week for a year. Another covers whatever is trending. After 12 months, one is known for something specific. The other is vaguely familiar.

The fix is not to ignore trends. It's to filter them through your existing position.

Does covering this trend bring you closer to what you want to be known for, or further?

Answer that honestly first.

## Short 5: You Don't Have a Content Problem
**Hook (0-3s):** Every creator who says "I don't know what to post" had thirty ideas this week they let evaporate.

**Body (4-50s):**
You don't have a content problem. You have a capture problem.

Your brain is good at recognizing patterns and making connections. It is terrible at storing raw ideas reliably.

And every idea you have competes with your inbox anxiety, your grocery list, and whatever you read this morning. The idea almost always loses.

The fix is a second brain. Not an app. A system.

Here's the simple version:

One place where everything goes immediately. No categories, no organization. Just capture.

Once a week, go through it. Move two things into development. Archive the rest.

Before you write anything, spend five minutes reading connected notes.

That's it.

The creator who does this never sits down to write without material. The blank page problem disappears.

And the output compounds. Captured ideas get developed. Developed ideas become content. Content generates new ideas. The loop feeds itself.

Start with one thing today: capture one idea before you open social media tomorrow morning.

## Short 9: The Weekly Review That Prevents Content Burnout
**Hook (0-3s):** Most creators burn out because they're building new content every day from scratch. The fix takes 30 minutes a week.

**Body (4-50s):**
Content burnout usually comes from one thing: starting from zero every time you sit down to write.

The weekly review breaks that pattern.

Here's how it works.

Every week, 30 minutes. Go through everything you captured that week: ideas, observations, screenshots, voice memos.

For each item, one decision: is this worth developing? If yes, move it to your "in progress" folder. If no, archive or delete.

Then look at what's already in development. Pick two things to move forward. Add a paragraph, expand a note, write a rough draft.

That's it.

By the time you sit down to write a full piece, you're not starting from zero. You're finishing something that's been building for days or weeks.

The work of writing drops. The quality goes up. The burnout drops.

You're not producing content anymore. You're refining what already exists.

## Short 10: Stop Optimizing for Reach, Start Optimizing for Resonance
**Hook (0-3s):** The creator with 100,000 followers and no sales has an audience. The creator with 3,000 fans and consistent income has a business. Here's the difference.

**Body (4-50s):**
Reach gets people to see you once.

Resonance gets people to trust you enough to buy.

They're built differently.

Reach comes from optimizing for the algorithm. Trending topics, posting frequency, broad appeal.

Resonance comes from saying something specific enough that someone feels seen, understood, or challenged in a way they needed.

The creator optimizing for reach asks: how do I get this in front of more people?

The creator building resonance asks: does this actually say something true?

The paradox: the more specific your content, the fewer people it appeals to initially. And the more deeply those people care.

Depth of connection beats width of reach every time when you're building income, not influence.

Your goal for every piece of content: move one more person from passive follower to active fan.

Not impress everyone who scrolls past. Convert one person who was already paying attention.
