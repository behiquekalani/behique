# Behike Newsletter Welcome Sequence - Strategy Guide

## Overview

7-email welcome sequence for Beehiiv. Triggers when someone signs up via free lead magnet download or storefront browse. Goal: build trust, establish authority, convert to $97 Behike Operating System sale.

## Timing

| Email | Day | Type | Goal |
|-------|-----|------|------|
| 01 - Welcome | 0 | Delivery | Deliver lead magnet, set expectations |
| 02 - Story | 2 | Connection | Build trust through authenticity |
| 03 - Value | 4 | Education | Teach the OS concept, create desire |
| 04 - Proof | 6 | Credibility | Show results, build confidence |
| 05 - Offer | 8 | Sales | Direct pitch with value stack |
| 06 - Objections | 11 | Sales | Handle resistance, reframe value |
| 07 - Last Chance | 14 | Close | Final pitch, graceful exit to weekly |

## Strategy

**Days 0-4: Trust phase.** No selling. Deliver value, tell the real story, teach something useful. The reader should feel like they found someone who gets it.

**Days 6-8: Bridge phase.** Show proof, then make the offer. The proof email (Day 6) does the heavy lifting so the offer email (Day 8) feels like a natural next step, not a cold pitch.

**Days 11-14: Close phase.** Handle objections honestly, then give a clean final ask. No fake urgency. No countdown timers. Just a direct "here it is, here's why, your call."

## Key Principles

1. **No emojis.** Clean, text-forward emails.
2. **Under 300 words each.** Respect the reader's time.
3. **One CTA per email.** Never split attention.
4. **Plain text style.** Beehiiv supports rich formatting but these should read like a real person writing, not a marketing template.
5. **Sign off consistent.** Always "- Kalani, Behike"
6. **Reply-friendly.** Mention "reply to this email" at least once in the sequence to train engagement.

## Beehiiv Setup Notes

- Set up as an Automation sequence, not a broadcast
- Trigger: new subscriber (any source)
- Delays: use the day numbers above (0, 2, 4, 6, 8, 11, 14)
- After sequence completes, subscriber moves to regular weekly newsletter
- Tag subscribers who complete the sequence: "welcome-complete"
- Tag subscribers who click the buy link: "os-interested" (for retargeting)

## Placeholder Links to Replace

- `[DOWNLOAD YOUR FREE BLUEPRINT]` - link to actual lead magnet delivery
- `[SEE THE FULL OPERATING SYSTEM OVERVIEW]` - link to blog post or landing page
- `[BROWSE FREE RESOURCES]` - link to Behike storefront (Gumroad or Shopify)
- `[GET THE BEHIKE OPERATING SYSTEM - $97]` - link to product purchase page

## Metrics to Track

- Open rate per email (benchmark: 40%+ for welcome sequences)
- Click rate on CTAs (benchmark: 5-10%)
- Unsubscribe rate (should stay under 2% per email)
- Conversion rate on Email 05, 06, 07 (the sales emails)
- Reply rate (especially on Email 05 where replies are invited)

## Iteration Notes

After 100 subscribers complete the sequence, review:
- Which email has the lowest open rate? Rewrite that subject line.
- Which CTA gets the most clicks? Double down on that angle.
- Where do most unsubscribes happen? That email needs softening or cutting.
