## Script 3: The Three Questions That Run the Whole System
**Hook (0-3s):** Three questions. That is the entire daily review. Nothing else required.
**Body:** Question one: what actually moved forward today. Not what you worked on. What actually moved forward. There is a difference. You can work for 8 hours and move nothing forward if the work was not connected to the right output. Question two: what did I avoid and what is the real reason. Not the excuse. The real reason. Are you afraid of it? Is it unclear what to do next? Is it a task that does not actually need to happen? Question three: what is the single most important action for tomorrow. Not a list. One thing. The thing that makes tomorrow feel like progress no matter what else happens.

## Script 7: What to Do When the Review Reveals a Problem
**Hook (0-3s):** The daily review is going to show you something uncomfortable. That is exactly the point.
**Body:** Most people avoid the review because they do not want to see that they wasted the day. But the discomfort of seeing a wasted day is the mechanism that fixes the next one. If you sit with the discomfort without reacting to it with self-criticism, and instead just ask what needs to change, the review becomes a tool for adjustment rather than a source of shame. The rule is simple: no self-judgment during the review. Only analysis. What happened. Why it happened. What to change. That is it. The moment you add emotional weight to the review, you will stop doing it. Keep it clinical. Keep it honest. Keep it short.
