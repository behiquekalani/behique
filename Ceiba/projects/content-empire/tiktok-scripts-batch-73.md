## Script 73.1 — The Gumroad Launch Checklist

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Most people launch their first digital product without doing five things that would have doubled their sales."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: GUMROAD LAUNCH CHECKLIST]
One: The product page copy answers "who is this for and what will they be able to do after buying." If your page does not answer both, fix it before you launch.

[on-screen: 1. PAGE COPY ANSWERS WHO + OUTCOME]
Two: You have at least one testimonial or social proof element. A screenshot of a DM counts. A quote from someone who tried your method counts.

[on-screen: 2. ONE PROOF ELEMENT]
Three: The price ends in a 7 or 9. Not because it is magic, but because it anchors perceived value differently than round numbers.

[on-screen: 3. PRICE ANCHORING]
Four: You have a reason to buy today. A launch discount, a bonus that expires, a cohort limit. Without urgency, decisions get deferred indefinitely.

[on-screen: 4. URGENCY ELEMENT]
Five: You have told your audience at least three times before launch day. One announcement is not a launch.

[on-screen: 5. THREE TOUCHES BEFORE LAUNCH]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Save this checklist. If your Gumroad page has all five, you are ready. If not, fix what is missing first."

**Caption:** Before you launch your Gumroad product, check these five things. Most people skip at least three. (99 chars)

## Script 73.2 — Pricing Digital Products With Confidence

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Underpricing your digital product is not humility. It is a tax you pay for not understanding value."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: LOW PRICES SIGNAL LOW VALUE]
When you price low, you are not making it easier to buy. You are making it easier to dismiss. Price is a signal. A ten-dollar ebook and a ninety-seven dollar ebook are read differently before anyone reads a word.

[on-screen: PRICE = PERCEPTION]
The framework for pricing with confidence: what is the outcome worth to the buyer. Not what it cost you to make. What the transformation is worth to them.

[on-screen: PRICE THE OUTCOME, NOT THE EFFORT]
If your guide saves someone two hours a week, over a year that is a hundred hours. What is an hour of their time worth. Price relative to that.

[on-screen: VALUE = TIME SAVED OR PROBLEM SOLVED]
Start at the price that makes you slightly uncomfortable. That discomfort is usually your old story about money, not data about the market.

[on-screen: DISCOMFORT IS DATA ABOUT YOU, NOT THE MARKET]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"What is your product and what problem does it solve. Comment below. Let us figure out what it should actually cost."

**Caption:** Underpricing is not modesty. It is mispositioning. Price the outcome, not the effort. (87 chars)

## Script 73.3 — How to Validate Before You Build

**Hook (0-3s):**
"The fastest way to waste three months is to build a product nobody asked for. Here is how to stop doing that."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: BUILD AFTER YOU VALIDATE, NOT BEFORE]
Validation is not a survey. A survey tells you what people say they want. Validation tells you what people will pay for.

[on-screen: VALIDATION = WILLINGNESS TO PAY]
Step one: Post about the problem your product solves. Not the product. The problem. Count how many people engage, comment, save, share.

[on-screen: 1. POST THE PROBLEM FIRST]
Step two: DM the people who engaged most. Ask one question: "Would you buy a guide that helps you solve X. Yes, maybe, or no." The maybes are nos.

[on-screen: 2. DM THE ENGAGED ONES]
Step three: Presell it. Put up a Gumroad page with a waitlist price. If ten people buy before you build, you have validation. If zero people buy, you have data.

[on-screen: 3. PRESELL BEFORE YOU BUILD]
Zero sales is not failure. It is the cheapest possible course correction.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"What product are you thinking about building. Comment the problem it solves. Let us validate it together."

**Caption:** Build after you validate. Post the problem. DM the engaged. Presell before you build. (87 chars)

**Hashtags:** #validatebeforeyoubuild #digitalproducts #productvalidation #solopreneur #onlinebusiness #creatoreconomy #gumroad #buildinsecret #startuplessons #behike

## Script 73.4 — The Minimum Viable Product for Digital Creators

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Your first digital product does not need to be a course. It can be something you make in an afternoon."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: MVP = MINIMUM VIABLE PRODUCT]
The minimum viable product for a digital creator is not the most complete version of your idea. It is the smallest version that delivers the core transformation.

[on-screen: SMALLEST VERSION, CORE TRANSFORMATION]
A checklist is a product. A template is a product. A three-thousand-word guide is a product. A recorded thirty-minute workshop is a product.

[on-screen: FORMATS THAT TAKE ONE DAY TO BUILD]
You are not building a university course. You are solving one specific problem for one specific person, at a price they will pay without thinking too hard.

[on-screen: ONE PROBLEM. ONE PERSON. ONE AFTERNOON]
The mistake is waiting until you have enough content to justify selling it. You do not need more content. You need clearer positioning.

[on-screen: CLARITY > VOLUME]
Launch the MVP. Let buyers tell you what is missing. Build version two with their money.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"What is the one problem you could solve in a PDF or checklist format this week. Drop it below."

**Caption:** Your first digital product does not need to be a course. A checklist that solves one problem is enough. (104 chars)

## Script 73.5 — Turning Comments Into Content Ideas

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Your audience is already telling you what to make. You are just not listening to the right channel."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: COMMENTS = FREE MARKET RESEARCH]
Every comment on your post is a data point. Not just the likes and follows. The specific words people use when they respond.

[on-screen: READ THE EXACT WORDS THEY USE]
When someone says "I never thought about it that way," that is a hook. When someone says "I struggle with this so much," that is a product idea. When someone asks a follow-up question, that is your next video.

[on-screen: COMMENT TYPES AND WHAT THEY SIGNAL]
The system: after every post, spend five minutes reading every comment. Write down any question, complaint, or confusion you see. That becomes your topic bank.

[on-screen: FIVE MINUTES. EVERY COMMENT. EVERY POST]
The best content does not come from what you want to say. It comes from what your audience needs to hear. Those two things are only the same when you are paying attention.

[on-screen: WHAT THEY NEED VS. WHAT YOU WANT TO SAY]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Go check your last post's comments right now. Find one question someone asked. That is your next piece of content."

**Caption:** Your comments are a content calendar. Read every one. Write down the questions. Post the answers. (98 chars)
