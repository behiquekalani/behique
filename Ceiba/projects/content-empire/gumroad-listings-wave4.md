**Full description:**

Your screen is working against your brain.

Blue light in the evening suppresses melatonin. Unstructured ambient noise activates stress responses. No built-in Pomodoro means your focus blocks run until you crash. Most wellness apps try to fix this with subscriptions, accounts, and a dozen separate tools.

Lumina is one browser tab that handles all of it.

The Pomodoro timer is built in. The breathing exercise module walks you through structured breathwork patterns. Everything is configurable. Nothing requires an account or internet connection after the first load.

People who build, study, or work in front of a screen for most of the day. ADHD builders who need help transitioning between focus states. Remote workers without a natural rhythm built into their environment. Anyone who has noticed that by 9pm their brain is still running at noon speed.

**FAQ:**

**Does it work without internet?**
Yes. After the first load, the app caches locally and runs fully offline. Your sound files, timer, and settings all work without a connection.

**Does it save my settings?**
Your preferences are saved in your browser's local storage. As long as you use the same browser on the same device, your settings persist between sessions. No account sync across devices.

**What is the binaural beat thing?**
Binaural beats work by playing two slightly different frequencies in each ear. Your brain perceives the difference as a third tone. The 40Hz range has been studied in cognitive research contexts. Lumina offers it as an optional sound mode. Use headphones for it to work properly.

**What if I already have a Pomodoro app?**
You can use Lumina's ambient sound and light filter without touching the timer. All modules are independent.

**Full description:**

Most people who lose money on prediction markets are not losing because of bad luck. They are losing because they are making two mistakes: they are treating it like betting, and they are not doing the research.

Polymarket is an information market. Prices are set by the collective estimate of thousands of people, each acting on whatever information they have. When you find information the market has not priced in yet, you have edge. When you do not, you are just paying the person who did.

This guide is about building that edge systematically.

The first section covers how to read Polymarket odds correctly. What the number actually means. How to translate probability percentages into expected value. Why a 70% market and a 70% true probability are two different things.

The second section covers information sources. Where prediction market traders actually find their edge. How to identify which data sources move prices before the mainstream narrative catches up. How to think about base rates and reference classes when forming a probability estimate.

The third section covers position sizing and risk management. How much of your balance to put on any single position. When to enter, when to wait, and when to walk away. The specific questions to ask before every trade.

The fourth section covers the decision framework. A repeatable process for evaluating any market from research to position to exit. The framework works whether you are looking at political markets, sports markets, or current event markets.

The fifth section covers psychological discipline. The mistakes that wipe out accounts even when the research is good. How overconfidence, recency bias, and loss aversion show up specifically in prediction market behavior.

This guide will not tell you which markets to bet on. It will give you a framework for thinking about any market more clearly than you do now.
