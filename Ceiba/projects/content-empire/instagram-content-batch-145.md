## Post 1 — The Weekly AI Stack

**Hook:** I run a content business solo. This is the AI workflow that makes that possible.

**Slide 1:** I run a content business solo. This is the AI workflow that makes that possible.

**Slide 2:** Monday: Use Claude to outline the week's content from one core idea.
Everything for the week comes from a single prompt session.

**Slide 3:** Tuesday-Thursday: Use the outlines to write drafts.
Claude handles first drafts. I handle editing and voice.

**Slide 4:** Friday: Batch-schedule everything through Buffer.
One session. Full week live. Done.

**Slide 5:** What this replaced: daily content scrambling, missed posting days, inconsistent output.
What it costs: about four focused hours per week total.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for the exact prompts and system breakdown.

**Caption:** The biggest lie in solopreneur content is that consistency requires massive time investment. It does not. It requires a system. The system I use starts on Monday with a single core idea, usually something I have been thinking about or a question someone asked me. I take that one idea into a single Claude session and generate outlines for five to seven pieces of content across different formats. That session takes about an hour. Then I write the drafts myself, using the outlines as scaffolding, over the next three days. Editing and adding my voice takes another hour total. Friday is scheduling. By the time the weekend arrives, the full week of content is live and I have not had to scramble once. AI did not replace my thinking. It replaced the part of the work that blocks thinking, the blank page, the structure problem, the "what do I even post today" friction. That friction is the time cost. Eliminate it and you get your week back.

## Post 2 — The One-Idea-to-Five-Platforms System

**Hook:** One idea. Five platforms. Twenty minutes. This is how it works.

**Slide 1:** One idea. Five platforms. Twenty minutes. This is how it works.

**Slide 2:** Start with one thesis: a single clear statement about something you know to be true.
Example: "Most creators fail because they optimize for reach before conversion."

**Slide 3:** Instagram carousel: 5 slides breaking the idea into steps.
Twitter thread: the idea expanded into 8 connected tweets.
LinkedIn post: the professional angle, more data-forward.

**Slide 4:** Newsletter section: the full argument with context.
Short-form video script: the hook-point-takeaway format.

**Slide 5:** AI handles the format translation.
You handle the original thesis and the final editing pass.
One brain session. Five pieces of content.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for the exact prompts that power this system.

**Caption:** Repurposing content is one of the most talked-about strategies in the creator space and one of the least-executed ones. Most people think repurposing means copying and pasting the same post across platforms. It does not. Each platform has a different format expectation and a different audience behavior. A LinkedIn post is not a Twitter thread. A newsletter section is not an Instagram caption. Repurposing done correctly means taking one idea and translating it for each platform's native format. AI makes this mechanical. You bring the original thinking, the core thesis, the insight. You give it to an AI with platform-specific formatting instructions. The AI produces the first draft of each format. You edit for voice. The whole process takes less time than writing one original post from scratch for each platform. One idea. Five touchpoints. One creative session. That is the leverage that makes solo content operations possible.

## Post 3 — The Research Compression System

**Hook:** I used to spend 3 hours on research. Now I spend 20 minutes. Here is the exact change I made.

**Slide 1:** I used to spend 3 hours on research. Now I spend 20 minutes. Here is the exact change I made.

**Slide 2:** Before: open browser, find ten tabs, read them all, try to synthesize.
Time: 2-3 hours. Result: usually overwhelmed, not clearer.

**Slide 3:** After: paste source material into Claude with a specific synthesis prompt.
"What are the three most important insights here and what do they imply for someone building a solo business?"

**Slide 4:** Time: 20 minutes. Result: a clear synthesis I can write from immediately.
No browser rabbit holes. No context collapse.

**Slide 5:** The key is the prompt quality.
Vague input produces vague output.
Specific constraints produce actionable synthesis.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for the prompt library that makes this work.

**Caption:** Research is one of the tasks that expands to fill whatever time you give it. Without a constraint, three hours of reading produces a vague sense of having learned something. With the right AI-assisted process, twenty minutes of focused input and synthesis produces an actual output you can act on. The shift is not about trusting AI to do the thinking. It is about using AI to handle the mechanical parts of research, scanning, categorizing, finding the connective tissue between sources, so that your thinking time is spent on the actual insight. The prompt matters more than most people realize. "Summarize this" produces a summary. "What are the three most non-obvious implications of this for someone in X situation" produces thinking you can use. Learn to write research prompts that compress information into actionable form. That skill alone saves multiple hours per week.

## Post 4 — The Email Draft Loop

**Hook:** Writing emails used to take me an hour. Now it takes ten minutes. The workflow is simple.

**Slide 1:** Writing emails used to take me an hour. Now it takes ten minutes. The workflow is simple.

**Slide 2:** Step 1: Write three bullet points. What do I want to say? What is the main point? What do I want the reader to do?

**Slide 3:** Step 2: Paste bullets into Claude with a voice prompt.
"Write this as a direct, no-fluff email. Dan Koe tone. Under 200 words. No greetings."

**Slide 4:** Step 3: Read the output. Edit for anything that does not sound like me.
Usually takes three minutes.

**Slide 5:** Step 4: Copy and send.
Total time: 10 minutes.
Previous total time: 45-60 minutes.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai. The tool is only as good as the process around it.

**Caption:** Email is one of the highest-leverage activities in a creator business. It is also one of the most time-intensive, which is why most people avoid it. The combination of blank-page anxiety and the pressure of writing directly to people who opted in creates friction that is easy to put off. The AI-assisted draft loop removes that friction without removing your voice. The key is understanding that you are still the author. The bullets are your thinking. The voice prompt is your editorial standard. The AI is handling sentence construction and structure. You are handling intent, point of view, and final edit. That division of labor is the right one. It preserves what makes the email valuable, which is that it sounds like you thinking about something real, while removing the part that blocks you from writing in the first place. Write better emails faster. Send them more consistently. The list compounds when you show up for it.

## Post 5 — The Idea Capture Protocol

**Hook:** Most good ideas are lost within four hours of arriving. This system catches them all.

**Slide 1:** Most good ideas are lost within four hours of arriving. This system catches them all.

**Slide 2:** The old way: think of an idea, plan to write it down later, forget it by dinner.

**Slide 3:** The new way: voice memo the moment the idea arrives.
Transcribe with AI (Whisper or any free tool).
AI converts the transcript into a structured brief: title, thesis, three supporting points.

**Slide 4:** The brief goes into a master ideas file.
Once a week, review the file and select content for the following week.
The Monday outline session draws from this file, not from a blank page.

**Slide 5:** The result: you never start a content week with nothing.
The creative energy that was wasted on "what do I post" now goes entirely into execution.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for the full system, including the exact voice memo to brief prompt.

**Caption:** The creative process has two phases and most people only optimize one of them. Generation, the arrival of ideas, gets treated as something you either have or you do not. Execution, the translation of ideas into content, gets all the attention. The real leverage is in the capture layer that connects the two. Ideas arrive at the wrong time. In the shower. During a run. In the middle of a conversation. If there is no frictionless capture mechanism, most of them evaporate. A voice memo takes four seconds. The AI transcription and structuring takes two minutes. The brief that results is the seed of a piece of content that might take an hour to write but would have taken three hours to start from scratch with no anchor. The capture system is not glamorous. It is four seconds of commitment every time an idea arrives. But over a year, it is the difference between a content calendar that is full and one that is empty.
