### Script 1 — Build a Gumroad Store From Scratch (My First Week, What Sold, What Flopped)

**Thumbnail concept:** Gumroad dashboard screenshot (blurred numbers) with a hand pointing at a sale notification. Bold text: "FIRST SALE IN 48 HRS" on a split background of before (empty store) and after (live store).

**Hook (0-30s):**
I launched a Gumroad store with 12 digital products in one week. I am going to show you exactly what I built, what sold in the first 48 hours, what flopped, and what I would do differently if I started over today. No theory. Real numbers, real products, real results.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
Gumroad is the fastest path to digital product sales if you are starting from scratch. No complicated tech setup. No Shopify fees. No payment processor headaches. You create an account, upload a file, write a description, set a price, and you are live. The hard part is not the platform. The hard part is knowing what to sell and how to position it. I made a lot of positioning mistakes in my first week. By the end of this video you will know how to avoid them. We are covering: how to set up your store in under an hour, what to sell and how to price it, how to write product descriptions that convert, and what I actually launched and what happened.

**Section 1 — Setting Up Your Gumroad Store (2-4min):**
Go to gumroad.com, create an account, and set your profile name. This becomes part of your store URL so make it clean and memorable. Add a profile photo and a short bio that explains who you are and who you help. Then create your first product. Click the plus button, choose "digital product," upload your file, which can be a PDF, a ZIP file, a video, or any downloadable format. Write your title first. The title should contain the main keyword someone would search for. "AI Employee Guide: Automate Your Work Week" is better than "My AI Guide." Write your description. I will cover this in detail. Set your price. Publish. You are live. The whole process takes 30 minutes if you already have the product file. If you do not have a product yet, start with a guide. A well-organized PDF on any topic you know well is enough to start. It does not need to be 50 pages. 10 to 20 dense, useful pages is enough.

**Section 2 — Writing Product Descriptions That Convert (4-7min):**
Most Gumroad product descriptions are terrible. They describe the product instead of the outcome. "This guide covers AI tools" tells me nothing. "After reading this guide you will know which five AI tools to use to cut your work week in half" tells me exactly what I get. The structure that works: open with the problem the buyer has right now. Be specific. "You are spending three hours a day on tasks that AI could handle in 10 minutes." Second paragraph: introduce the product and what it delivers. Not what it contains, what it delivers. Third paragraph: bullet list of specific outcomes or what is included. Keep each bullet under 15 words. Fourth paragraph: who it is for. Name them specifically. "This is for freelancers, consultants, and content creators who want to work less without earning less." Fifth: price justification. "The average person who applies this saves 10 hours a week. What is one hour of your time worth." Sixth: the CTA. "Download it now." No soft language. Direct. The whole description should be under 300 words.

**Section 3 — What I Launched and What Happened (7-10min):**
My first week I launched 12 products across four categories: AI productivity guides, automation templates, content creation systems, and finance tools. The ones that sold first were the AI productivity guides, specifically the AI Employee Guide and the n8n Automation Pack. The ones that did not sell in the first week were the more generic titles that did not have specific outcomes in the description. The main lesson: specificity sells. The more precisely you name the problem and the outcome, the faster the sale happens. The products that flopped had vague titles and vague descriptions. The ones that sold had titles that contained the buyer's problem and descriptions that promised a specific outcome. I also learned that pricing at $27 to $37 outperformed $9 to $15 for the same products. The higher price communicated more value and attracted buyers who were more serious about applying the content.

### Script 3 — The Fear Funnel Strategy: How to Turn Content Into Sales Without Hard Selling

**Thumbnail concept:** Funnel graphic with "FEAR" at the top (wide, lots of content) narrowing to "TRUST" in the middle and "SALE" at the bottom. A magnet pulling people in rather than a megaphone pushing them. Bold text: "NO ADS. NO HARD SELL."

**Hook (0-30s):**
There is a marketing system that requires zero ad spend, never asks you to hard sell anyone, and builds an audience that buys from you repeatedly over time. It is built on a simple psychological insight: people do not buy products. They buy relief from fear. Understanding that one thing changes how you create content, how you position products, and how much revenue you generate from the same audience. Here is how it works.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
I learned about the fear funnel concept from studying how the most effective solo creators and information product businesses market their work. It shows up in every case. Dan Koe writes about the fear of wasting your prime years on work that does not matter. Alex Hormozi talks about the fear of your business failing. Every successful content-to-sale system is built on an emotional foundation, and that foundation is almost always some variation of fear. Not manufactured fear. Real fear that the audience already has. Your job is to name it clearly, validate it, and then position your product as the path through it. This video breaks down the three-stage fear funnel and how to build it with content.

**Section 1 — Stage 1: Name the Fear (2-4min):**
The first stage of the funnel is content that names the fear your audience is living with. Not solutions yet. Not products. Just the recognition that the fear is real. "You are building a content strategy that will not pay off in two years because you are on the wrong platform." "You are pricing your digital products so low that buyers do not take them seriously." "You are automating the wrong tasks and missing the ones that would actually save you hours." When someone reads that and thinks "that is exactly what I am doing," you have their attention. You have not sold anything. You have earned trust by proving you understand their situation. This content belongs at the top of your funnel. It is high volume, high distribution. Tweets, Reels, short YouTube videos. The goal is recognition, not conversion.

**Section 2 — Stage 2: Build the Bridge (4-7min):**
Stage two is content that bridges from the fear to the possibility of relief. This is where you show what a better situation looks like. Not "buy my product," but "here is what it looks like when this fear is no longer running your decisions." "This is what a content strategy looks like when it compounds instead of flatlines." "This is what happens to conversion rates when you price for value instead of fear." "This is how your week looks when the right tasks are automated." This content is medium-depth. YouTube videos, newsletter issues, long-form threads. The audience at this stage is already somewhat qualified because they stayed through stage one. They are ready to be shown, not just told. Case studies, before-and-after comparisons, and walkthroughs work extremely well here.

**Section 3 — Stage 3: The Natural Close (7-10min):**
Stage three is where the product appears, but not as a pitch. As a logical next step. "I made a guide that walks you through exactly how to build this content strategy." "The template I use for this pricing framework is available at the link below." "The automation workflow I described is packaged in the n8n Automation Pack." The buyer is not being sold to. They are being offered something they were already looking for because stages one and two made them want it. The conversion rate on this type of CTA is significantly higher than cold ads or aggressive product pushes. The fear funnel works because it respects the buyer's intelligence. It earns the right to make an offer by first providing real value. When you do that consistently over time, your audience sells themselves.
