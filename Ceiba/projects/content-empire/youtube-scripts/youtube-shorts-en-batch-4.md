## Short 11: The Digital Product Idea Framework (Three Questions)
**Duration:** 70 sec
**Hook (0-3 sec):** I use three questions to decide if a digital product idea is worth building. Here they are.

**Body:**
[Text card: "Question 1"]
Question one: can I explain the outcome this product delivers in one sentence? If I cannot explain what someone has after buying it, the idea is not clear enough to build.

[Text card: "Question 2"]
Question two: is there evidence that people are already looking for this? Not just "would people want this" but are there forums, search results, Reddit threads, or conversations where someone is actively asking for help with this problem?

[Text card: "Question 3"]
Question three: can I build the first version in under a week? If the minimum version takes longer than a week, I am probably overbuilding. The first version is a test, not a final product.

[Camera]
Three questions. If the answer to all three is yes, I build it. If any answer is no, I adjust the idea until all three are yes, or I drop the idea.

[Text on screen: "The filter is not quality. It is clarity."]

**CTA:** Subscribe. I break down how to take an idea from answer to product in more detail on the channel.

## Short 14: How I Validate a Product Idea Before I Build It
**Duration:** 65 sec
**Hook (0-3 sec):** I validate every product idea before I spend time building it. The method takes about 20 minutes.

**Body:**
[Camera]
Step one: I search Reddit, Twitter, and relevant forums for the specific problem the product would solve. If I find at least ten posts from the past six months where someone is asking for help with that exact problem, the demand signal is there.

[Screen: Reddit search results for a niche topic]
Step two: I look at what people are linking to or recommending in those threads. If people are already recommending a solution, I look at what that solution is missing or who it is not serving.

[Camera]
Step three: I write a one-paragraph description of the product as if I were posting it for sale. Then I share it in one of those communities, as a question, asking if this is something people would find useful. The response tells me more than any amount of internal research.

[Text on screen: "Validation before production. Always."]
If I get five or more genuine positive responses, I build. If the community is quiet or skeptical, I adjust before I spend a week building something nobody asked for.
