### Script 1 — The Solopreneur Tech Stack: Every Tool I Use, What It Does, and What to Cut

**Thumbnail concept:** Clean grid of tool logos (Claude, n8n, Gumroad, Telegram, Notion, CapCut, Railway) with a dollar sign showing the monthly cost of each. Bold text: "THE REAL STACK" and a "FREE" badge on several tools.

**Intro (30s-2min):**

The right stack disappears into the background and makes work faster without requiring maintenance. I have tested dozens of tools over the past year. At the end I will give you the minimum viable stack for someone starting from zero.

**Section 1 — AI and Research Tools (2-4min):**
Claude from Anthropic is my primary AI tool. I use it for writing, strategy, research synthesis, and prompt engineering. The Pro plan runs $20 per month. Worth every dollar if you are using it daily for real work. For quick tasks and when I need GPT-4 specifically, I keep a ChatGPT account at the same price point. I do not use both simultaneously but I switch depending on the task. For research, I use Perplexity AI, which is better than Google for synthesizing current information on a topic. The free tier is enough for occasional use. The paid version at $20 per month is worth it if you are doing deep research regularly. For AI video and image generation, I use CapCut for video editing and Midjourney for images when I need them. CapCut is free. Midjourney is $10 to $30 per month depending on volume.

**Section 2 — Automation, Selling, and Infrastructure (4-7min):**
n8n is my automation backbone. I self-host it on Railway for approximately $5 to $10 per month depending on usage. This is where all my workflows live. Content distribution, lead capture, notifications, data pipelines. If you do not want to self-host, n8n Cloud starts at $20 per month. Gumroad is where I sell digital products. The fee structure is a transaction percentage rather than a monthly fee, which means I pay nothing when I am not selling. For a digital product business under $10K per month, this is the right choice. Notion is my knowledge base and project management. Free tier is sufficient for a one-person operation. GitHub is free for all the code and scripts I maintain. Railway for hosting, approximately $5 per month for light server usage. Telegram for community and bot infrastructure, free.

**Section 3 — The Minimum Viable Stack (7-10min):**
If you are starting today with limited budget, here is the minimum you need. Claude free tier or $20 per month. Gumroad free. Google Docs and Sheets free. n8n Cloud free tier or self-hosted on Railway. One social media platform where your audience lives, free. That is it. Total cost: zero to $20 per month. Everything else is optional and should only be added when you have a clear use case that the current stack cannot handle. The mistake most people make is building the full stack before they have traction. Every tool you add is a tool you have to learn, maintain, and pay for. Add tools one at a time and only when the need is proven. The stack should serve the work, not the other way around. I have seen people spend $200 a month on software and make $0 in revenue because they built the infrastructure before the business.

### Script 2 — How to Repurpose One Video Into 10 Posts (My Exact Workflow)

**Thumbnail concept:** A single YouTube video thumbnail in the center with arrows pointing out to ten different platform format mockups. Labels show each platform. Bold text: "1 VIDEO. 10 POSTS. 2 HOURS."

**Hook (0-30s):**
I produce content for seven platforms every week. I have one content creation session. The reason is a repurposing system that takes one YouTube script and turns it into 10 different pieces of content across every platform. I am going to show you the exact workflow, the tools, and the prompts so you can copy it.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
Content repurposing sounds like a content marketing buzzword. When it is done badly, it is. You end up with the same caption posted everywhere, which does not perform on any platform because every platform has a different format, audience expectation, and algorithm preference. Done well, repurposing means taking the core thinking from one piece of content and expressing it in the format native to each platform. The message stays consistent. The format adapts. This video covers how I turn one YouTube script into tweet threads, Instagram carousels, Pinterest pins, a newsletter section, a LinkedIn post, TikTok captions, and a short-form YouTube script. The whole process takes about two hours with AI assistance.

**Section 1 — The Source Content Framework (2-4min):**
The repurposing system only works if your source content is structured. A rambling video cannot be repurposed efficiently. A structured script can. My YouTube scripts follow a consistent format: hook, intro, three sections, CTA. Each section is a standalone argument with a clear point. This structure is intentional because every section can become a standalone piece of content. Section one of this video is one tweet thread. Section two is a different tweet thread. The intro plus hook is a 60-second YouTube short or TikTok. The three sections combined are a long LinkedIn post. The hook alone is a Pinterest pin headline. Build your source content with repurposing in mind from the start. The more specific and structured the original, the better every derivative becomes.

**Section 2 — The AI Repurposing Prompts That Work (4-7min):**
Here are the exact prompts I use. For tweet threads: "Here is a YouTube script section. Rewrite this as a 7-tweet thread. First tweet is a bold claim or question. Tweets 2 through 6 expand on the idea with one point each, short and punchy. Tweet 7 is a question or CTA. No hashtags in the thread itself." For Instagram carousels: "Here is a YouTube script section. Write a 7-slide Instagram carousel. Slide 1 is the hook, a claim that creates curiosity. Slides 2 through 6 each make one specific point in two sentences. Slide 7 is the CTA. Make each slide feel complete on its own." For newsletters: "Here is a YouTube script section. Rewrite this as a newsletter section in second person, conversational tone, 150 to 200 words. No bullet points. Paragraph format only." For Pinterest: "Here is a YouTube script section. Write 3 Pinterest pin descriptions based on this content. Each description is 100 to 150 words, keyword-dense, with a call to action at the end." These prompts run through Claude or GPT. The output needs editing but it is 80 percent ready.

**Section 3 — The Publishing System (7-10min):**
After the AI produces the derivatives, I review them in one batch. I do all 10 pieces at once, not one by one. This keeps the review focused. I edit for voice, catch any errors, and adjust anything that feels generic. Then I put them into a scheduling queue. Twitter posts go to Buffer or post natively. Instagram carousels get designed in Canva using my brand template. Newsletter sections go into my email platform draft. Pinterest descriptions get uploaded in batch. The whole review and scheduling step takes about 45 minutes. The AI production step takes about 30 minutes. Source content creation, the YouTube script, takes another 45 minutes to an hour. Total time for one week of content across seven platforms: under three hours. If you are spending more than that, the system is not working yet. Build the system, test it, improve it, and the time investment goes down as the quality goes up.

### Script 3 — AI Tools I Use Every Single Day (Honest Breakdown, No Sponsorships)

**Thumbnail concept:** Flat lay of phone and laptop showing AI tool interfaces. A sticky note on the laptop reads "daily stack." Bold text overlay: "WHAT I ACTUALLY USE" with a "no sponsors" badge in the corner.

**Hook (0-30s):**
Every AI tool review on YouTube is sponsored by the tool being reviewed. Mine is not. I do not have any AI tool sponsorships. What I am about to tell you is what I actually use every day to run a real business, what I have tried and stopped using, and why. Unfiltered, with the boring truth included.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
There are hundreds of AI tools available right now. Most of them are wrappers around OpenAI or Anthropic models with a different interface and a higher price tag. Knowing how to separate genuine utility from marketing is itself a skill. I have spent the past year testing tools heavily, some out of curiosity, some out of necessity. The tools I use every day are the ones that survived repeated real-world use. They are not always the most well-known or the most hyped. Some are free. Some have paid plans. All of them earn their place in my workflow.

**Section 1 — The Daily Anchors: Claude and Perplexity (2-4min):**
Claude is open every morning before anything else. First task every day: open the current project, paste the relevant context, and use Claude to plan the day's work. Not to create a to-do list. To think through the most important decision or problem I am facing and test my reasoning before I execute. This takes 10 to 15 minutes. It has saved me hours of going in wrong directions. Perplexity is my research tool. Anything I need to know about the current state of a topic, market, tool, or person, Perplexity gets me 80 percent of the answer faster than Google. I use the regular web search mode for current information. I paste the summary into Claude when I need to go deeper on strategy or synthesis. These two tools together handle probably 70 percent of my daily AI usage.

**Section 2 — Tools I Use Regularly But Not Daily (4-7min):**
n8n: I spend time in n8n two to three times per week maintaining workflows, building new ones, or debugging something that broke. This is not a daily tool but it is always running in the background doing daily work on my behalf. ChatGPT: I use GPT-4 specifically for tasks where I want a second opinion from a different model. Claude and GPT have different tendencies. GPT sometimes generates more creative variations. Claude is more careful and structured. For writing tasks where I want to compare outputs, I run both. CapCut: video editing for Reels and YouTube shorts. The AI features in CapCut specifically, the auto-caption, the background removal, and the beat-sync feature, save me 30 to 45 minutes per video. I do not use most of the AI effects but those three features are genuinely useful. Midjourney: image generation when I need something specific for a thumbnail or product cover that I cannot find as stock art. Not daily but regularly.

**Section 3 — Tools I Tried and Cut (7-10min):**
This is the part most review videos skip. Jasper AI: tried it for two weeks. It is a Claude or GPT wrapper with a content-specific UI. The output quality was identical to prompting Claude directly. Not worth the extra cost. Copy.ai: same issue. The prompting interface added steps without adding capability. Notion AI: I use Notion but the AI add-on did not improve my workflow meaningfully. Claude is better at every AI task Notion AI does. Not worth the monthly cost. Several "all-in-one AI business" tools I tried over the past year: none of them survived contact with real work. They are demos in product form. The pattern I have found is that dedicated tools built for one specific use case outperform generalist AI platforms every time. The tools that survive in my stack are the ones that do one thing extremely well, not 15 things adequately.
