**Short 10**
**Title:** How to Mix Bass in FL Studio
**Channel:** Music
**Hook (0-3s):** Your bass is muddy because of one mistake. Here is what it is.
**Content (3-55s):**
The mistake: your kick and your bass are competing in the same frequency range.
Both live between 60 and 120 hertz. If they are both loud in that range, they cancel each other out or create a low-end mess.
The fix has two parts.
Part one: sidechain compression. When the kick hits, the bass ducks slightly. Use Fruity Peak Controller or a compressor with sidechain input.
Part two: EQ carving. Put a high-pass filter on the bass at around 40 hertz. Cut the bass slightly around 80 to 90 hertz. Boost the kick in that same range.
Now they each own a frequency space.
The test: mono your mix and listen on a phone speaker. If the bass disappears, the mix is not working. Fix it there.
**End card (55-60s):** Follow for the full FL Studio mixing series.
**Caption:** Why your bass sounds muddy in FL Studio — and the fix.
