### Script 1 — The Content Repurposing Machine

**Thumbnail concept:** Split screen. Left side: one camera filming a YouTube video. Right side: a grid of 10 different platform posts all labeled (Reels, Twitter, TikTok, Newsletter, etc.) branching from that one source.

**Hook (0-30s):**
Most creators burn out. Not because they run out of ideas — because they treat every platform like a separate job. I used to post the same amount on five platforms and produce five times the work. Then I realized: I was doing it backwards. One piece of content should feed everything else. In this video I am going to show you the exact machine I built to turn a single recording session into 10 pieces of content.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
If you are posting daily to Instagram, Twitter, TikTok, YouTube, and a newsletter separately — you are running five businesses at once. That is not sustainable. The people who seem to be everywhere are not working five times harder. They are working smarter with a repurposing system. This video is for anyone who has a main content channel, whether that is YouTube, a podcast, or long-form writing, and wants to multiply their reach without multiplying their hours. By the end of this you will have a clear framework you can start using this week.

**Section 1 — Why Repurposing Fails for Most People (2-4min):**
The mistake most people make is repurposing as an afterthought. They film a YouTube video, post it, then a few days later think "maybe I should clip that." That lag kills momentum and the clips end up disconnected from what is trending.

The second mistake is copy-pasting. Pasting a YouTube transcript to Twitter does not work. Each platform has a native content format. Twitter wants punchy, atomic insight. Instagram wants a hook-first visual story. TikTok wants raw energy and pattern interrupts. A repurposing machine accounts for these differences from the beginning.

The third mistake is having no system for what gets repurposed. Not everything deserves repurposing. Your best 20 percent of content will drive 80 percent of repurposing results.

**Section 2 — The Three-Layer Content Architecture (4-7min):**
Here is the framework. Think of your content in three layers.

Layer 1 is your core content. One long-form piece per week. A YouTube video, a podcast episode, or a long-form blog post. This is where you go deep. 10 to 20 minutes. This is the engine.

Layer 2 is derivative content. These come directly from Layer 1. A single 15-minute YouTube video contains at minimum: three to five short clips for Reels and TikTok (pull the sharpest 60 seconds), one Twitter thread (take your main argument and make it a list), one Instagram carousel (take the numbered framework from the video and put it on slides), and one newsletter issue (add context and one thing you did not say on camera).

Layer 3 is reactive content. Replies, responses, comments. When your Layer 1 content gets engagement, the questions and reactions become Layer 3. Answer the top three comments in a short video. Quote tweet the best reply with your take. This is free content.

One 15-minute YouTube video, done with this system, produces 12 to 15 pieces of content. That is three weeks of daily posting from one recording session.

**Section 3 — The Weekly Production Process (7-10min):**
Here is what the actual week looks like.

Monday: Record your core video. Do not edit yet. After recording, write a one-sentence thesis of the video and list the three main points. That list is your Twitter thread skeleton.

Tuesday: Edit the video. While editing, mark timestamps for your three best 60-second clips. These are your Reels. Export those clips the same day.

Wednesday: Post the YouTube video. Write the newsletter based on the video. Add one insight you cut from the video for subscribers only. That incentivizes people to be on your list.

Thursday: Post the first Reel. Post the Twitter thread. Schedule the other two Reels for the next two days.

Friday: Write the Instagram carousel. Five slides. Slide one is the hook. Slides two through four are the framework. Slide five is the CTA. Post it.

That is five platforms covered from one recording session in one week. As you build the habit, the derivative content gets faster. Carousels that used to take two hours take 30 minutes once you have a template.

### Script 2 — How to Turn One Post Into 10

**Thumbnail concept:** One large piece of paper with "1 POST" written in bold on the left. An arrow pointing right to a grid of 10 thumbnails representing 10 different posts. Clean, high contrast.

**Hook (0-30s):**
What if every piece of content you created could feed your entire content calendar for the week. Not theoretically. Literally. I want to show you a repeatable method for taking a single post and multiplying it into 10 without losing quality or sounding repetitive. This is not about working harder. It is about extracting everything that is already there.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
The biggest lie in content creation is that you need more ideas. You do not. You need better extraction. Every solid idea you have contains at least 10 different pieces of content, written from different angles, for different platforms, in different formats. Once you understand how to extract those angles, you will never feel stuck for content again. This video is specifically for people who have ideas but feel like they can never produce enough to stay consistent. Let me show you the extraction method.

**Section 1 — The Six Angles Every Idea Has (2-4min):**
Take any idea. Let us use: "Most people waste money without realizing it."

Angle 1 is the statistic angle. "Americans waste an average of $1,500 per year on subscriptions they do not use." That is a tweet.

Angle 2 is the personal story angle. "I found three subscriptions I forgot about last month. Here is what I did." That is a Reel.

Angle 3 is the framework angle. "The five categories where people leak money without noticing." That is a carousel.

Angle 4 is the counterintuitive angle. "Spending more in this one area actually saves money overall." That is a YouTube short hook.

Angle 5 is the how-to angle. "How to audit your spending in 20 minutes." That is a tutorial video.

Angle 6 is the opinion angle. "The financial advice industry does not want you to see how simple this is." That is a Twitter thread.

One idea. Six angles. Six pieces of content. That is before you cross-post to multiple platforms.

**Section 2 — Format Multiplication (4-7min):**
Each of those six angles can then be produced in multiple formats. The framework angle (five categories where people leak money) can become: an Instagram carousel, a YouTube video outline, a newsletter section, a downloadable PDF checklist, and a podcast talking point.

That is not five times the work. The research is done once. You are just changing the container.

Here is how to do it without it feeling like you are recycling. Change the opening. Change the level of depth. The carousel gives the framework. The YouTube video explains the reasoning behind each category. The newsletter adds a personal story. Each version serves someone at a different depth of engagement with your content. New followers get the carousel. Long-term subscribers get the deep YouTube video. Buyers get the PDF checklist as a freebie.

The key principle: same information, different depth, different format.

**Section 3 — The Extraction Habit (7-10min):**
The system only works if it becomes a reflex. Here is how to build it.

When you finish writing or recording anything, run it through three questions before you close the file. One: what is the single sharpest insight in this piece? That becomes a tweet. Two: what is the step-by-step process hidden in this piece? That becomes a carousel or thread. Three: what is the story angle no one expects from this topic? That becomes a Reel hook.

You can do this in five minutes. Keep a running extraction doc. Every time you create something, add three rows to the doc: tweet, carousel idea, Reel hook. You will have a content backlog within two weeks.

**CTA + Outro (10-12min):**

### Script 3 — The Platform Distribution System

**Thumbnail concept:** A flow chart starting from "YOU" in the center, with five arrows pointing outward to five platform logos. Each arrow has a label like "adapted," "native format," "strategic timing." High contrast, clean design.

**Hook (0-30s):**
Posting the same thing everywhere is not a distribution strategy. It is noise. Algorithms punish it, audiences ignore it, and you get exhausted maintaining it. There is a smarter model. One where each platform gets content that was designed for it, timed right, and connected to a clear goal. That is what I am covering today.

**Intro (30s-2min):**
Distribution is the step that most creators skip entirely. They think about creation. They think about topics. They almost never think about the architecture of where things go and why. But your distribution decision is actually more important than most of your creative decisions. You can have a great piece of content fail because it went to the wrong platform, in the wrong format, at the wrong moment in your funnel. This video is the distribution map. By the end of this you will know which platform does what job, and how to feed each one without tripling your workload.

**Section 1 — Platform Roles, Not Platform Presence (2-4min):**
The first shift is from "I should be everywhere" to "every platform has a specific role in my system."

Here is the role map for the most common platforms.

YouTube is your authority builder. Long-form, searchable, evergreen. People come to YouTube to learn. When someone finds your YouTube channel, they treat you as a credible source. YouTube is slow to grow but has the longest content shelf life of any platform.

Instagram (Reels and carousels) is your reach engine. Short-form Reels get distributed to people who have never heard of you. Carousels go deep for people who already follow you. Instagram is your best acquisition tool right now.

Twitter is your credibility signal. When someone researches you before buying, they check your Twitter. Threads show thinking. Consistency shows professionalism. Twitter does not drive huge traffic but it closes deals.

TikTok is your testing ground. Post fast, see what resonates, take the winners to YouTube and Instagram. TikTok rewards volume and authenticity over polish.

Email (newsletter) is your owned asset. No algorithm. Direct line to your most engaged audience. Every other platform should funnel people toward your email list.

**Section 2 — The Funnel Mapping (4-7min):**
Once you understand the roles, you build a funnel map. Here is how it works.

Top of funnel: TikTok and Reels. Short-form content designed to reach new people. The CTA here is not "buy my product." It is "follow me" or "watch my full video." Keep it low friction.

Middle of funnel: YouTube and Twitter threads. Someone who watches a 15-minute YouTube video or reads a full Twitter thread is signaling real interest. Here you introduce your product or newsletter. Not aggressively. A soft mention. "If you want the full framework, I made a guide. Link in description."

Bottom of funnel: Email newsletter and direct DMs. These are the people who opted in. They gave you their attention deliberately. Now you can go deeper, make offers, build community. These are your buyers.

The system works when each platform is designed to move people to the next stage. TikTok moves people to YouTube. YouTube moves people to your email list. Email moves people to buy.

**Section 3 — The Minimum Distribution System (7-10min):**
Here is the lightest possible version of this system for someone starting out.

Pick two platforms. One for reach (Reels or TikTok) and one for depth (YouTube or newsletter). Build there first. Do not spread across five platforms before you have figured out how to produce consistently.

Set a rhythm. For reach: three to five posts per week. For depth: one per week. That is your baseline.

Connect them explicitly. Every reach post should mention your depth content. Every depth post should give people a reason to follow your reach platform. Create bridges between them intentionally.

Add your third platform (Twitter or email) once you have a consistent rhythm on the first two. The system scales in layers. You do not need all five platforms operating to make real progress.
