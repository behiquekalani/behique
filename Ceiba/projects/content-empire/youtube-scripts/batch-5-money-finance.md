## SCRIPT 4: "The One Financial Ratio That Changed How I Think About Money"
**Product:** Cash Flow Dashboard (soft sell)
**Channel:** Finance faceless or Main
**Format:** Educational talking head
**Length:** 4-5 minutes
**Hook type:** Single insight, educational

---

[HOOK]

One number changed how I think about money.

Not net worth. Not savings rate. Not income.

It is the ratio of fixed to variable expenses.

---

[THE RATIO]

Here is the ratio: Fixed Expenses divided by Total Expenses.

If the number is above 0.7, you have a problem. Most of your money is committed before the month starts. You have almost no flexibility. One unexpected expense breaks everything.

If the number is below 0.4, you have options. You can absorb shocks. You can deploy money toward investments or opportunities without rebuilding your entire life.

Most people in their twenties are above 0.7 without knowing it.

---

[WHY IT MATTERS]

Your income is variable. Life is variable. Your expenses should not be locked at 70% fixed.

Every time you sign a new subscription, you are adding a fixed cost. Every time you upgrade your apartment, you are adding a fixed cost. These decisions compound over time.

The people who build wealth quickly are not necessarily the ones who make the most money. They are the ones who keep their fixed expense ratio low while income grows.

---

[HOW TO CALCULATE YOURS]

Take last month's bank statement.

List every expense that was exactly the same as the month before: rent, subscriptions, loan payments, insurance. Add them up. That is your fixed expense total.

Divide by your total expenses for the month.

That is your ratio.
