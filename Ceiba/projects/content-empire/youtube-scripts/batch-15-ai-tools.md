"By day seven I noticed something uncomfortable.

AI was pointing out that I kept avoiding one specific task. It was in my list every single day. I kept rating my energy low when that task was at the top. The pattern was obvious once Claude reflected it back to me. I was not bad at mornings. I was avoiding one thing and blaming everything else.

"Day fourteen, I added a second layer.

I started logging my daily output at the end of each morning block. What I finished, what I did not, how I felt. Claude had context from the previous day and started noticing patterns in when I was most productive.

Turns out I do my best focused work between 9 AM and 11 AM. I already knew this loosely. But seeing it tracked and confirmed over two weeks made me take it seriously. I restructured my schedule completely around that window."

---

### [2:45 - 3:30] DAY 21 CHECK-IN
"Day twenty-one, the routine became automatic.

The conversation with Claude was taking about four minutes instead of fifteen. I had gotten faster at answering the questions honestly. I stopped being vague. Specific answers got specific plans.

Something else happened too. I stopped dreading mornings. That might sound like nothing. For me it was significant. I have ADHD. Mornings used to be twenty minutes of deciding whether to do anything at all. Now they have a shape."

---

### [3:30 - 4:30] DAY 30 RESULTS
"Day thirty. Here is what changed in one month.

Average focused work time per morning went from about ninety minutes to roughly two hours and forty minutes. Not because I worked harder. Because I stopped spending time figuring out what to do.

I finished twelve projects I had been dragging for weeks. Not because AI is magic. Because having a structured plan every single morning removes the decision fatigue that was killing my momentum.

The most unexpected result: I started waking up earlier voluntarily. Not because I set an earlier alarm. Because I was actually looking forward to the morning block."

### [4:30 - 5:15] WHAT I KEPT VS DROPPED
"Here is what I kept from the experiment.

The four-question prompt stays. I still run it every morning. The end-of-block log stays. Knowing what I finished gives me data to improve.

What I dropped: the rigid time blocks. AI was sometimes generating plans that did not account for energy fluctuation. I now use the plan as a guide, not a schedule.
