### [1:00] THE CORE IDEA: DEMAND VS SUPPLY GAP

Before anything else, understand this framework. You are not looking for popular products. You are looking for products with high demand and low supply on eBay specifically.

A product can be trending on TikTok and still be a bad eBay pick if 400 sellers are already listing it at the same price. The gap matters more than the trend.

Two numbers tell you everything:
- Sell-through rate: how many of the listed items actually sold
- Days on market: how long before it sold

High sell-through rate plus short days on market means buyers are outpacing sellers. That is your window.

---

### [2:00] HOW TO CHECK SELL-THROUGH RATE

Go to eBay. Search your keyword. Filter by "Sold Items" in the left sidebar. Count how many sold results come up. Then search the same keyword without that filter. Count the active listings. Divide sold by active. That ratio is your rough sell-through rate.

Above 50% is good. Above 80% is very good. Under 30% means the market is oversaturated.

This takes five minutes. Most people never do it.

---

### [3:00] PATTERN 1: SEASONAL ITEMS LISTED TOO EARLY

The first pattern is seasonal demand that sellers are not ready for. Think about products that spike before a holiday, a season, or an event. If you list them two to three weeks before the spike, you catch buyers before your competitors flood the market.

The signal: look at Google Trends for the keyword. Then check eBay sold listings for the same period last year. If you see a clear spike and the current supply on eBay is thin, you have a window.

---

### [4:00] PATTERN 2: ACCESSORIES FOR DISCONTINUED PRODUCTS

The second pattern is accessories and parts for products that are no longer manufactured. Big brands stop selling something. The aftermarket dries up. But people still own the product and still need accessories for it.

The signal: search for the accessory, then look at the parent product. If the parent product is no longer in production, there is no new supply chain for accessories. The few sellers who stock them hold the market.

---

### [5:00] PATTERN 3: NICHE B2B CONSUMABLES

The third pattern is consumables bought by small businesses. Think cleaning supplies for a specific machine, packaging materials for a niche category, or replacement parts for commercial equipment. These buyers are not shopping casually. They need it and they buy it repeatedly.

The signal: look at the buyer feedback on sold listings. If the same buyers are showing up multiple times across different sellers, they are repeat purchasers. That is predictable demand.

---

### [6:00] HOW TO RUN THIS PROCESS WEEKLY

This is not a one-time thing. The process is:

Step 1. Pick a category.
Step 2. Sort by sold listings. Look at the top 20 results.
Step 3. Calculate sell-through rate for 5 to 10 of them.
Step 4. Identify which pattern they fit.
Step 5. Source the top candidate and list it.

Do this every week for 30 minutes. Your product catalog builds itself over time.

### [0:15] THE FOUR TOOLS

Four tools. Each one answers a different question.

1. eBay Completed Listings: what actually sold and at what price
2. Terapeak (free inside Seller Hub): category-level demand data over 90 days
3. Google Trends: is interest rising, falling, or stable
4. Reddit: are real people talking about this problem or product

None of these cost anything. Most dropshippers use one of the four. Using all four together is where the edge comes from.

---

### [1:00] TOOL 1: EBAY COMPLETED LISTINGS

Go to eBay. Search a keyword. On the left sidebar under "Show only," check "Completed Items" and then "Sold Items."

What you are looking at: real transaction history. Green prices sold. Red prices did not. The spread between red and green tells you the market price ceiling.

Look for consistency. If 15 of 20 listings sold within 7 days at similar prices, that is a healthy market. If half are red and the sold prices are all over the place, the market is fragmented or overpriced by most sellers.

---

### [2:00] TOOL 2: TERAPEAK

Terapeak is eBay's own data tool. It is free if you have a Seller Hub account, which is free to create. Go to Seller Hub, then Research, then Terapeak Product Research.

Search your keyword. You will see average sold price, sell-through rate, total sales volume, and the number of sellers in the category over the last 90 days. This is the most reliable demand signal you have access to without paying for anything.

The number to focus on: sell-through rate above 50% and a total sales number that is worth your time. A product with 80% sell-through and only $200 in total monthly sales is not worth building inventory around.

---

### [3:00] TOOL 3: GOOGLE TRENDS

Go to trends.google.com. Search the same keyword. Set the time range to the last 5 years. You are looking for one of three patterns.

First: steady demand with no seasonal spike. Low volatility. Reliable.
Second: seasonal demand. You can anticipate the spike and list early.
Third: a recent upward trend. You may be early to a growing market.

Avoid: a product that peaked two years ago and is declining. The eBay data might still look okay because sellers are clearing old inventory. Trends shows you the direction.

---

### [3:45] TOOL 4: REDDIT

Search Reddit for the product category or the problem it solves. Use site:reddit.com in Google, or search directly on Reddit with the keyword plus words like "recommend," "looking for," or "help."

You are looking for two things. One: are people actively asking for this product. Two: are there complaints about existing options that create an opportunity.

Reddit shows you buyer intent in plain language. It is unfiltered market research.

---

### [4:15] THE WORKFLOW

Run these four tools in order for any product candidate:

Step 1. eBay Completed Listings: is it actually selling.
Step 2. Terapeak: what is the sell-through rate and volume.
Step 3. Google Trends: is demand stable or directional.
Step 4. Reddit: is there real buyer intent or frustration in the market.

If you get green signals from at least three of four, source and list. If two or fewer, move on.

This whole process takes 15 to 20 minutes per product candidate. Run three to five candidates per week.

### HOOK (0:00 - 0:15)

Most eBay dropshippers quit within 30 days. Not because the model does not work. Because they make the same four mistakes in the same order. I am going to show you what those are and how to avoid them.

---

### [0:15] FAILURE MODE 1: WRONG NICHE

The first mistake is picking a niche based on personal interest instead of demand data. Someone likes fitness, so they list fitness gear. Someone is into gaming, so they list gaming accessories. This feels logical. It is not.

Personal interest does not predict market fit. What matters is sell-through rate and competition density on eBay specifically. A niche you find boring can outperform a niche you are passionate about by 10x if the demand-supply gap is better.

The fix: run sell-through rate checks before you list anything. If the category does not hit at least 50% sell-through on Terapeak, move on. Your interest in the product is irrelevant.

---

### [1:30] FAILURE MODE 2: NO DIFFERENTIATION

The second mistake is listing the exact same product with the exact same title and description as 40 other sellers. eBay's algorithm has no reason to surface your listing over theirs. You become a price war.

Price wars are unwinnable for dropshippers. You have no warehouse, no volume discount, no logistics advantage. Competing on price alone means your margins compress to zero and then below.

The fix: differentiate on the listing itself. Write an original description. Use better photos. Target a more specific buyer keyword. You do not need to be the cheapest. You need to be the most relevant result for a specific search.

---

### [2:30] FAILURE MODE 3: NO SYSTEMS

The third mistake is treating eBay as a series of one-off tasks instead of building repeatable processes. New sellers spend 45 minutes listing a product. Then 45 minutes on the next one. Then they burn out.

The sellers who stay in it have templates. They have a research checklist. They have a supplier verification routine. They have a weekly schedule that makes the whole operation take 5 to 10 hours instead of 30.

The fix: after your first successful listing, document how you made it. That document becomes your template. Every listing after that is faster. Systems compound. Random effort does not.

---

### [3:30] FAILURE MODE 4: MARGINS IGNORED

The fourth mistake is calculating profit wrong. Most new dropshippers subtract COGS from sale price and call it profit. That is not profit. That is gross margin before fees.

eBay takes a final value fee. PayPal or managed payments takes a processing fee. If you offer free shipping, that comes out of your number too. Returns happen. On a $30 sale, the actual take-home after all of that might be $3 to $5.

The fix: build a margin calculator before you list. Know your floor. If a product cannot produce at least $6 to $8 net after fees at a competitive price, it is not worth listing. Some categories are structurally low-margin. Avoid them early on.

---

### [4:30] THE PATTERN

These four failures follow a predictable order. Wrong niche leads to no sales. No differentiation leads to price wars. No systems leads to burnout. Margins ignored leads to working for nothing. Each one compounds the previous one.

The flip side is also true. Get the niche right and the other three become easier to solve. Start with the research process.

## Script 5: "Product Research in 20 Minutes: The Exact System"

**Format:** Faceless (screen recording tutorial)

---

### HOOK (0:00 - 0:15)

I can evaluate a product opportunity in 20 minutes. Not guess. Not hope. Actually determine whether a product is worth listing based on real data. Here is the exact process, step by step.

---

### [0:15] WHY 20 MINUTES IS THE TARGET

If product research takes you two hours per product, you will never build a catalog. The goal is a system fast enough to run multiple candidates per session without burning out.

Twenty minutes is achievable once you know what you are looking for. This script is that training.

---

### [1:00] STEP 1: CATEGORY SELECTION (3 MINUTES)

Do not start with a specific product. Start with a category. Open eBay and go to the category browser. Pick a mid-level category, not too broad, not too narrow. "Home and Garden" is too broad. "Pond and Water Features" is a workable level.

The question you are answering here: is this category active enough to be worth researching. Check the number of sold listings in the last 30 days. If the category is generating consistent sales across multiple sellers, it is worth going deeper. If it is thin, move to the next one.

Time target: 3 minutes. Move fast.

---

### [1:45] STEP 2: KEYWORD RESEARCH (5 MINUTES)

Inside your chosen category, look at the titles of the top sold listings. What words are they using. Write down the 3 to 5 most common keywords.

Then take those keywords to Google Trends. Paste them in. Set the time range to 12 months. You are confirming two things. One: the search volume is real. Two: the trend is stable or rising, not declining.

If the keyword shows a declining trend but eBay sold listings look okay, that is a warning signal. The eBay data might be lagging. Avoid declining trend keywords at the start.

Time target: 5 minutes.

---

### [2:45] STEP 3: COMPETITOR ANALYSIS (5 MINUTES)

Search your top keyword on eBay with "Sold Items" filtered. Look at the top 10 results.

Three things to note:

One. Price range. What is the floor and ceiling.
Two. Listing quality. Are the descriptions thin and copied, or detailed and original.
Three. Seller history. Are the top sellers established stores with thousands of feedback, or smaller sellers.

Thin descriptions and smaller sellers mean there is room to enter with a better listing. Dominated by high-feedback power sellers with optimized listings, the barrier is higher.

Time target: 5 minutes.

---

### [3:45] STEP 4: MARGIN CHECK (4 MINUTES)

Take the average sold price. Open a margin calculator (free ones exist at salculator.com or just use a spreadsheet).

Input: sale price, COGS from your supplier, eBay final value fee (roughly 12-15% depending on category), shipping if you are covering it.

Output: net profit per sale.

If net profit is under $5, the product needs very high volume to be worthwhile. If it is $8 to $15, it is a good starting candidate. Above $15, it is worth prioritizing.

Write this number down. This is your go or no-go signal.

Time target: 4 minutes.

---

### [4:30] STEP 5: DECISION AND NEXT ACTION (3 MINUTES)

You now have four data points:
- Category activity: confirmed or not
- Keyword trend: stable, rising, or declining
- Competitive landscape: open or saturated
- Margin: viable or not

If three of four are positive, the product is a list candidate. Write it in your tracker with the source link and the target price. That is your next action.

If two or fewer are positive, move on. Do not try to rationalize a weak candidate into a listing.

Time target: 3 minutes.

---

### [5:00] THE WEEKLY TEMPLATE

Run this process on five candidates every week. That takes about 100 minutes total. 1.5 to 2 hours.

Of those five, typically one to two will pass. Over a month, that is four to eight new verified products added to your catalog. Over three months, you have a tested catalog built on real data.

The template is:
- Monday: run 5 candidates through the 20-minute process
- Tuesday: source and create listings for the ones that passed
- Thursday: check stock at suppliers, update any prices that have drifted
- Sunday: review which listings got views and sales, note what is working

That is the weekly system. 4 to 5 hours total.
