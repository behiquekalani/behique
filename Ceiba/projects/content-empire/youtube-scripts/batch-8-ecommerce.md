[HOOK . first 30 seconds]

Most people list 10 products and sell zero.

This video shows the exact method I use to find products with proven demand before I list a single item.

[INTRO CARD: "Find products people are already buying. The eBay sold listings method."]

---

[0:30 . THE CORE PROBLEM]

Here is what most beginner dropshippers do.

They browse AliExpress. They find something that looks interesting. They list it on eBay. They wait. Nothing sells.

They repeat this 10 times. Still nothing.

The mistake is using "looks interesting" as the research criteria. That is your opinion. It has nothing to do with buyer behavior.

The fix is simple. Stop guessing what people want. Go look at what they already bought.

---

[1:30 . THE METHOD . Sold Listings Filter]

[SCREEN: eBay search bar]

Open eBay. Search any category. I am going to use "USB-C hub" as the example.

You will see hundreds of active listings. That tells you nothing. Active listings just mean sellers are trying. It does not mean buyers are buying.

[SCREEN: left sidebar, filter panel]

On the left sidebar, scroll to "Show only." Check the box that says "Sold items."

Now the view changes completely. You are no longer looking at what sellers are listing. You are looking at what buyers already paid for.

[SCREEN: sort by "Price + Shipping: Highest first"]

Sort by price, highest first. You want to see the high-ticket items with actual sales. Not just the $3 items moving in bulk.

What you are looking for:

One. Multiple sales in the last 30 days at a consistent price. Not one lucky sale. A pattern.

Two. Sales across different sellers. If only one seller is moving this product, they might have a relationship or a brand advantage you do not have. You want to see the same item selling from multiple accounts.

Three. No brand requirement. If the sold listings are all from Nike or Apple, you cannot compete. If they are generic or unbranded, you can source it.

---

[3:00 . WHAT A GOOD PRODUCT LOOKS LIKE]

[SCREEN: example search results, highlight specific listings]

Here is an example of a product that passes the test.

Cable management clips. Non-branded. $8.99 to $14.99 price range. I can see 47 sold in the last month. Multiple sellers. The photos are basic. The listings are not optimized. That is your opening.

When you see a product with proven demand AND weak listings, that is the gap. You can come in with better photos, better titles, and the same or lower price.

Here is a product that fails.

Bluetooth speaker. Brand mentions in the listing. One seller has 90% of the sales. Price is inconsistent. This one you skip.

The filter does the work. You are just reading the data.

---

[4:30 . THE PROXY METHOD . Validate Before You List]

Never list a product you cannot verify demand for.

The sold history IS the validation. Before you touch a supplier, before you write a title, before you upload a single photo, run the sold listings check.

This is the proxy method. You are using someone else's sales history as proof that the market exists.

Here is the process in order.

Step one. Search the product on eBay. Apply the sold filter. Look for 15 or more sales in the last 30 days across at least 3 different sellers.

Step two. Check the price range. You need room to make money. Look at the low end and high end of what sold. That is your pricing corridor.

Step three. Check active listings count. If there are 800 active listings for a product with 20 sold, the market is saturated. If there are 40 active listings with 60 sold, that is a healthy ratio.

Step four. Source the product. Only now do you go find a supplier.

Reverse the order and you are guessing.

---

[6:00 . THREE CATEGORIES THAT CONSISTENTLY WORK]

Based on this method, three categories come up reliably.

Category one. Small electronics accessories. USB cables, phone mounts, charging docks, screen protectors. High search volume. Low weight. Easy to source. Non-branded versions sell consistently.

Category two. Specialty tools. Not hardware store tools. Specific tools for hobbies. Electronics repair kits. Watchmaker sets. Leather crafting tools. These have smaller audiences but almost no competition and buyers who know exactly what they want.

Category three. Fitness accessories. Resistance bands, foam rollers, lifting straps, pull-up bars. This category has been consistent for 4 years. The demand does not go away. The key is non-branded, non-Amazon-branded versions.

Run the sold listings filter on any of these and you will find products within 20 minutes.

## SCRIPT 2: "The Dropshipping Math Nobody Shows You"
**Product:** Ecommerce Playbook ($14.99)

[INTRO CARD: "Revenue vs. profit. The numbers most tutorials skip."]

---

[0:30 . REVENUE VS. PROFIT . THE GAP]

Revenue is the number people talk about.

Profit is the number that matters.

Here is the difference on a single eBay sale.

You sell a product for $50. That is your revenue. Fifty dollars comes into your account.

Now subtract the costs.

eBay final value fee: 12.9% of the sale price. That is $6.45.

Shipping cost to the buyer: $4.00, assuming a standard small parcel.

Product cost from your supplier: $25.00, assuming a 50% gross margin.

PayPal or payment processing: roughly $1.75 on a $50 transaction.

Total costs: $37.20.

Your profit on a $50 sale: $12.80.

That is not 50% margin. That is 25.6%.

---

[1:45 . THE REAL MATH ON LOWER-MARGIN PRODUCTS]

Most beginner dropshippers are not hitting 50% gross margin on product cost. They are hitting 30% or less.

Let us run it at 30%.

$50 item. Product cost: $35.

eBay fee: $6.45.

Shipping: $4.00.

Payment processing: $1.75.

Total costs: $47.20.

Profit: $2.80 per sale.

To hit $3,000 per month in profit at $2.80 per sale, you need 1,072 sales per month. That is 35 sales per day. Every day.

That is not impossible. But it is not passive either. And it is very fragile. One supplier delay, one negative feedback streak, one eBay policy change, and the math collapses.

---

[3:00 . HOW TO FIX THE MATH]

The math is not the problem. The parameters are the problem.

Here are four levers that actually move the number.

Lever one. Price. Higher ticket items absorb fixed costs better. A $150 item with the same $4 shipping and the same percentage eBay fee leaves much more room. The shipping cost does not scale with price. Your profit does.

Lever two. Supplier negotiation. Once you have 20 to 30 sales per month on a product, go back to your supplier and ask for a volume discount. Even 8% off your cost can double your margin on low-margin products.

Lever three. Volume discounts from the same supplier. If you are selling multiple products from one supplier, bundle your orders. Fewer shipments, lower per-unit cost.

Lever four. Your own store. eBay takes 12.9%. Shopify takes 2.9% plus 30 cents. That is roughly a 10% difference per sale. On a $50 item, that is $5 back in your pocket. On 200 sales a month, that is $1,000 in recovered margin.

---

[4:30 . WHAT GOOD MATH LOOKS LIKE]

Let us build a realistic profitable scenario.

You find a product that sells for $120. Product cost: $55. Gross margin: 54%.

eBay fee at 12.9%: $15.48.

Shipping: $6.00 for a heavier item.

Payment processing: $3.78.

Total costs: $80.26.

Profit per sale: $39.74.

To hit $3,000 per month, you need 76 sales. That is 2 to 3 sales per day.

That is a business. The first scenario with $2.80 margins is a trap.

The difference between those two scenarios is not hustle. It is product selection and pricing strategy.

## SCRIPT 4: "The Only 3 Suppliers You Need to Start eBay Dropshipping"
**Product:** eBay Dropshipping Guide ($9.99)
**Link:** https://behikeai.gumroad.com/l/ebay-dropshipping-guide
**Channel:** Ecommerce faceless
**Format:** Research reveal
**Length:** 6-7 minutes
**Hook type:** Shortcut reveal, decision simplifier

---

[HOOK . first 30 seconds]

There are over 4,000 suppliers on Alibaba alone.

Most beginner dropshippers spend two weeks reading comparison articles and never make a decision.

You need three suppliers. That is it.

This video covers which ones they are, why each one serves a different purpose, and how to evaluate any supplier before you commit to them.

[INTRO CARD: "3 suppliers. That is the whole starting stack. Here is why."]

---

[0:30 . WHY THREE IS THE RIGHT NUMBER]

One supplier is a single point of failure. If they have a stock issue or a shipping delay, your business stops.

Fifteen suppliers is noise. You cannot build relationships, negotiate pricing, or track reliability across fifteen sources.

Three gives you diversification without chaos. One primary. One backup. One option for a different product category or customer need.

Here is how the three break down.

---

[1:15 . SUPPLIER 1 . ALIEXPRESS]

AliExpress is the standard starting point. Here is why it makes sense for beginners and what its actual limitations are.

The advantages. No minimum order quantity. You can order one item to test before committing to volume. This matters when you are validating a new product.

US warehouse options. Many AliExpress sellers have US-based inventory. Filter for this specifically. "Ships from: United States" reduces delivery time from 3-4 weeks to 3-7 days. That is the difference between a happy customer and a dispute.

Category breadth. Almost any non-branded physical product is available. Small electronics, tools, home goods, fitness accessories, pet supplies.

The limitations. Variable quality across sellers. Shipping times from Chinese warehouses are too long for most eBay buyers. The US warehouse filter solves this for a subset of products.

How to use AliExpress effectively. Filter for US warehouse. Order samples before listing. Check seller feedback score. Look for 95% positive and at least 500 orders on the specific product, not the seller's overall store.

---

[2:30 . SUPPLIER 2 . CJ DROPSHIPPING]

CJ Dropshipping sits between AliExpress and a full logistics partner.

The advantages. Faster shipping than standard AliExpress. Their US and EU warehouses average 5 to 10 days. For eBay buyers who expect Amazon-like delivery, this is closer to acceptable.

Branded packaging options. You can add your own logo or packing slip at a small additional cost. This matters when you move to your own Shopify store. On eBay it is less critical but builds professionalism.

Agent support. CJ provides a dedicated sourcing agent once you have volume. They will find products for you, negotiate pricing, and manage quality checks. This is not available on day one but it is a meaningful advantage at 50-plus orders per month.

The limitations. Lower product selection than AliExpress. Not every product you find on AliExpress is available through CJ. You may end up using both for different categories.

How to use CJ. Start with AliExpress. Once you have a product selling consistently, check if CJ carries it. If yes, switch. Better shipping times at similar pricing is a margin-neutral upgrade with a customer experience improvement.

---

[3:45 . SUPPLIER 3 . WORLDWIDE BRANDS]

Worldwide Brands is different from the first two. It is a directory of vetted US-based wholesale suppliers, not a marketplace.

The advantages. All suppliers are pre-vetted. Worldwide Brands has been auditing suppliers since 1999. No fake manufacturers, no middlemen pretending to be wholesale. Every listing is a real US-based distributor.

Higher product quality. US-based wholesale means shorter supply chains, better quality control, and more reliable stock.

Better margins at volume. US wholesale suppliers charge more per unit than AliExpress on small orders. But at 30 to 50 units per month, the negotiated price plus the reduced return rate can produce better net margins than AliExpress.

The limitations. Annual membership fee. It is a one-time cost but it is a real barrier. And minimum order quantities exist on some suppliers. This is not a zero-MOQ solution like AliExpress.

How to use Worldwide Brands. Do not start here. Start with AliExpress to validate products with zero commitment. Use Worldwide Brands when you have a product with consistent sales and you want to improve quality and margins. It is the next level, not the first step.

---

[4:45 . HOW TO EVALUATE ANY SUPPLIER]

These three criteria apply to any supplier you consider, not just these three.

Criterion one. Response time. Send a test inquiry before you commit. A good supplier responds within 24 hours. A slow response before you are a customer means a slow response when you have an order problem.

Criterion two. Sample quality. Order one unit before listing. Check against the photos. Check build quality. Weigh it. Measure it. If it does not match the listing, it will not match what your customer expects.

Criterion three. Tracking info reliability. Ask specifically: how quickly is tracking information updated after shipment? Buyers open disputes when tracking does not move for 5 days. A supplier who uploads tracking within 24 hours of shipment protects your seller metrics.

These three criteria take 48 hours to evaluate. Do not skip them.

## SCRIPT 5: "Why 90% of Dropshippers Quit in Month 3 (and How to Be the 10%)"
**Product:** Ecommerce Playbook ($14.99)
**Link:** https://behikeai.gumroad.com/l/ecommerce-playbook
**Channel:** Ecommerce faceless
**Format:** Myth-busting / retention
**Length:** 5-7 minutes
**Hook type:** Failure analysis

---

[HOOK . first 30 seconds]

Month 3 is the graveyard.

Not month 1. Beginners expect month 1 to be hard.

Month 3 is where people have put in real time, seen some early results, and then watched growth stall. That stall is when most people quit.

This video is a failure analysis. Here are the four things that kill most dropshipping businesses by month 3, and the specific fix for each one.

[INTRO CARD: "Month 3 is where most people quit. Here is why, and how to not be one of them."]

---

[0:30 . FAILURE REASON 1 . SATURATED PRODUCTS]

The number one reason dropshippers stall in month 3: they listed products without verifying demand first.

Month 1 and 2, they might get lucky. A few sales on a product that happens to have some market. But by month 3, they have 30 or 40 listings and 80% of them have never sold. Revenue is not growing because the catalog is full of dead inventory.

The fix is the sold listings filter on eBay. Before listing anything, check the sold history. Look for at least 15 sales in the last 30 days across multiple sellers. If you cannot find that, do not list the product.

This sounds obvious. Most people skip it because it takes 10 extra minutes per product. Those 10 minutes are the difference between a $800/month business and a stalled catalog.

---

[1:30 . FAILURE REASON 2 . MANUAL REPRICING AT SCALE]

eBay is a competitive marketplace. Prices shift daily. If a competitor drops their price by $3 and you do not adjust, your listing moves to the bottom of search results.

In month 1 with 5 listings, you can reprice manually. In month 3 with 50 listings, you cannot. It takes too long and you will always be behind.

The fix is a repricer. Tools like Repricer Express or eBay's own automated pricing let you set a floor price and a target price. The tool handles adjustments. You stay competitive without checking prices every hour.

Without a repricer at scale, you are doing the work of a software tool by hand. That is time you cannot spend on research, supplier communication, or listing optimization.

---

[2:30 . FAILURE REASON 3 . TRACKING SALES AS A SINGLE NUMBER]

Month 3 revenue is $900. Month 4 revenue is $820. Why did it drop?

If you are only tracking total revenue, you cannot answer that question. You do not know if it was one bad supplier. A category that saturated. A group of listings with poor optimization. A supplier who started shipping slower.

The fix is to track by supplier, by product category, and by margin. Not just total revenue.

A simple spreadsheet works. Supplier A: 22 sales, $4.80 average margin. Supplier B: 8 sales, $9.20 average margin. Category: small electronics, 18 sales. Category: specialty tools, 12 sales.

When one number drops, you can find the source in 5 minutes instead of spending a week guessing.

---

[3:30 . FAILURE REASON 4 . NEGATIVE FEEDBACK AS MOTIVATION KILLER]

The first negative feedback hits hard. Someone is unhappy. They wrote it publicly. It sits on your account.

Most beginners treat this as a verdict on their business. Some of them quit shortly after.

Here is the reframe. Negative feedback is data.

A buyer left negative feedback saying the item took 3 weeks to arrive. That tells you the supplier's shipping estimate was wrong. Update the listing's estimated delivery or switch to a supplier with a US warehouse.

A buyer left negative feedback saying the item did not match the description. That tells you the photos or the product copy needs to be updated. Fix the listing.

Feedback is the most direct customer signal you have. Sellers who treat every negative review as a problem to solve instead of a judgment on their character build better businesses faster.

One negative review in 30 will not kill your account. Patterns of the same complaint will. Track the complaints. Fix the pattern.

---

[4:30 . WHAT THE 10% DO DIFFERENTLY]

The 10% who are still running a business in month 6 and beyond share one thing.

They build systems before they try to scale.

They do not list 50 products before they have a product research process. They do not take on 100 orders per month before they have a repricer. They do not try to manage 5 suppliers before they have a supplier evaluation system.

Scale is just more of what is already working. If what is working is a manual, inconsistent process, more of it makes more problems.

Systems first. Scale second.
