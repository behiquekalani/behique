They have a graveyard.

Here's how to build something that compounds instead of decays:

**Tweet 2**

"Marketing tips" is noise.

**Tweet 3**
2/ You need 3-5 pillars. Not more.

Specificity is the currency.

**Tweet 4**
3/ Build the pillar through depth, not breadth.

Don't cover every angle of a topic once.
Cover one angle from every direction.
The creator who goes deepest wins, not the one who covers the most ground.

**Tweet 5**
4/ Each pillar should connect to an offer.

Your content earns attention.
Your offer earns revenue.
If a pillar doesn't connect to something you sell, it's a hobby.

How many connect back to something you sell?

Orphan content is wasted effort. Pillar content is compound interest.

**Tweet 7**

30 posts on a specific topic from 3 different angles.
By day 90 you own that topic in the eyes of your audience.
Then you build the second pillar.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
Start today. Pick one pillar. Write the first post.

## Thread 92-3: The Repurposing System That Multiplies Your Library

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
You don't need more ideas.
You need to get more out of the ideas you already have.

Here's the repurposing system that turns one idea into 10 assets:

**Tweet 2**
1/ Start with the anchor piece.

Long-form is your foundation: a detailed thread, a newsletter issue, a YouTube video.
Everything else extracts from it.
Never start from short-form. You'll run out.

**Tweet 3**
2/ The extraction stack from one anchor:

- 1 long thread
- 3 short tweets (one idea each)
- 1 LinkedIn post (insight angle)
- 1 newsletter section
- 2-3 Instagram carousels or Reels scripts
- 1 short-form video hook

That's 10 assets from one anchor.

**Tweet 4**
3/ The 3-angle rule keeps it fresh.

Take one idea and write it from:
Angle 1: Personal experience
Angle 2: Data or research
Angle 3: Counterintuitive take

Same idea. Three completely different posts. None of them feel repetitive.

**Tweet 5**
4/ Build a swipe file as you create.

Every post you write that performs, save the framework.
Not the words. The structure.
Hook + problem + insight + example + CTA.
That structure works in every format.

**Tweet 6**
5/ Schedule the extraction, not just the creation.

Block time every week to repurpose.
"Creation week" and "distribution week" are better than doing both at once.
You create better when you're not also distributing.

**Tweet 7**
6/ Your library becomes self-referential over time.

Post about pricing. Post about content. Post about systems.
Then post that links back to all three.
The connective tissue is where authority lives.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
One idea, repurposed well, is worth more than 30 one-off posts.
Build the system. Let the library work.

## Thread 92-4: Why Most Content Libraries Fail

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
Thousands of creators have started a "content library."
Almost none of them finished building it.

Here's why they fail, and what you do differently:

**Tweet 2**
1/ They collect instead of connect.

Saving ideas is not a library.
A library is searchable, linked, and monetized.
If your folder of posts leads nowhere, it's not a library. It's an archive.

**Tweet 3**
2/ They create content for attention, not conversion.

Viral posts are empty calories unless they move people somewhere.
Where does your most popular post send people?
If the answer is "nowhere," you have a following, not a business.

**Tweet 4**
3/ They never define what the library is for.

Sales? Trust-building? Email list growth?
Pick one primary function. Build content that serves it.
A library without a function is just aesthetic.

**Tweet 5**
4/ They change pillars too often.

"I'll try this topic for two weeks."
Two weeks is not enough.
You need 90 days minimum on a pillar before you can judge whether it's working.
Patience is the unfair advantage.

**Tweet 6**
5/ They don't track what converts.

Most creators measure likes and follows.
7-figure creators measure what moves people from consumer to buyer.
Track the click-throughs. Track the sales per post. Optimize for those.

**Tweet 7**
6/ The fix is a system, not more discipline.

You don't need to be more consistent.
You need a structure that makes consistency easier than inconsistency.
Templates, scheduled creation blocks, a content calendar.
Make the right thing the easy thing.

**Tweet 8 (CTA)**
A content library that works is not complicated.
It's consistent, connected, and pointed at a clear outcome.
Build that. Everything else follows.
