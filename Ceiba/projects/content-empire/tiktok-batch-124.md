# TikTok EN Batch 124
**Topic: Why most solopreneurs plateau at $500/month**

## TikTok 1: The $500 Ceiling Is a Positioning Problem
**Hook (0-2s):**
You're not stuck at $500 a month because you work hard enough. You're stuck because of this.

**Script:**
The $500/month plateau is real and it has a specific cause. Not lack of effort. Not lack of content. Positioning.

Most solopreneurs at $500 a month are selling to a broad audience at a low price point. $7 ebooks. $17 templates. Content that appeals to everyone, which means it compels no one.

To break through, you need to narrow who you're for and raise what you charge.

This feels counterintuitive. Less people. More money. But here's the math: selling 100 units of a $5 product takes the same energy as selling 10 units of a $50 product. One of those is sustainable.

The $500 ceiling usually breaks when a creator picks a specific person with a specific problem and builds an offer priced at what that problem is actually worth to solve.

**CTA:**
Who is the most specific person you could serve? Name them. That's where your next $500 is.

---

## TikTok 2: Why More Content Won't Fix the Plateau
**Hook (0-2s):**
You've been told to post more. That's why you're still at $500 a month.

**Script:**
More content is the wrong solution to a positioning problem.

If your offer doesn't convert, doubling your posting frequency just means more people see an offer that doesn't convert. The number of eyeballs isn't the issue. The clarity of the offer is.

Here's the pattern I see constantly. Creator posts daily for 6 months. Gets 2,000 followers. Makes $500 a month. Thinks the answer is more posts.

The real answer: one week of no posting, all-in on fixing the offer. What is the specific transformation? Who is it specifically for? What's the specific evidence it works?

When those three questions have sharp answers, the offer converts at 5x the rate of a vague one.

Content gets eyeballs. Positioning gets sales. You need both, but you need positioning first.

**CTA:**
What's the specific transformation your product delivers? Not the features. The transformation. Say it out loud. If it sounds vague, you've found the problem.

## TikTok 3: The Actual Math Behind the $500 Plateau
**Hook (0-2s):**
Here's the math that explains exactly why most solopreneurs stay stuck at $500 a month.

**Script:**
Let's do the math on a $500 month.

At $27 per product, that's 18.5 sales. To get 18.5 sales at a 1% conversion rate, you need 1,850 people to see your offer.

At $97 per product, that's 5.15 sales. 516 people need to see your offer. Same effort, roughly speaking.

At $297 per product, that's 1.68 sales. You need under 200 people.

The creator stuck at $500 is usually selling a $27 product to 1,850 people every month. The creator at $2,500 is selling a $297 product to 840 people. The creator at $5,000 is selling a $97 product to 5,200 people OR a $497 product to 1,010 people.

The math changes when the price point changes. Most solopreneurs are undercharging because they're afraid. That fear is the ceiling.

**CTA:**
Run this math on your own business right now. What does your price point say about where you're stuck?

## TikTok 4: The Trap That Keeps You at $500
**Hook (0-2s):**
There's a specific trap that keeps digital product creators at $500 a month. Most people never see it.

**Script:**
The trap is called the beginner's market.

When you start, you sell to beginners because that's the audience that finds you first. Your content speaks to people who are just starting out. Your product prices reflect beginner psychology. Low price, low commitment.

The problem is that beginners have beginner budgets. They buy $7 courses. They consume endless free content. They rarely invest seriously.

To break $500, you have to shift your content, your positioning, and your audience toward people who are further along. People with an intermediate or advanced problem. A specific problem with a measurable cost to not solving it.

"I help beginners start an online business" keeps you at $500 forever.

"I help digital product creators who are already making sales but can't crack $3K/month" is a different market entirely. One that pays.

**CTA:**
Who's the more advanced version of your current customer? That's your next audience.

---

## TikTok 5: Breaking the Plateau in 30 Days
**Hook (0-2s):**
You can break the $500 plateau in 30 days. But you have to do the uncomfortable thing.

**Script:**
Here's the 30-day path off the plateau.

Week one: audit your current offer. What's the transformation? Who is it for? Is the price matched to the value? Most people discover in week one that their offer is either too vague, too cheap, or both.

Week two: rewrite the positioning. One sentence about who it's for. One sentence about what problem it solves. One sentence about what life looks like after. No more than three sentences. If you can't say it in three sentences, it's not clear enough.

Week three: build or update the sales page with the new positioning. Add one piece of social proof, even a quote from a beta tester.

Week four: one launch sequence to your email list. Four emails. Problem. Solution. Proof. Offer.

That's the whole path. Not a new product. Not more content. A sharper version of what you already have.

**CTA:**
Which week would trip you up? Be honest. That's where the real work is.
