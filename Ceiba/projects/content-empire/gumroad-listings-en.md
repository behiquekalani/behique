**Price:** $12.99

**URL slug:** `ai-security-guide`

**Tagline:** Build safely. Deploy confidently. Understand what you are protecting.

**Short description:**
The five real attack vectors in AI systems: prompt injection, data leaks, model manipulation, knowledge base poisoning, and agent impersonation. What to check before you deploy anything AI in a real business context.

**Full description:**

Most AI tutorials teach you how to build. Almost none teach you how to protect what you build.

If you are using AI to handle customer data, automate responses, or process internal documents, you have attack vectors you have probably not considered.

**The 5 real threats:**

1. **Prompt injection** — When an external user manipulates your AI's behavior with carefully crafted text. More common and more damaging than most people realize.
2. **Data leakage** — When your AI reveals information from the system prompt, other users, or internal documents. Happens through both direct queries and inference.
3. **Model manipulation** — When the output of your AI is influenced to produce incorrect or harmful results. Often invisible until damage is done.
4. **Knowledge base poisoning** — In RAG systems, when malicious documents contaminate the AI's responses. One bad document can corrupt your entire knowledge base.
5. **Agent impersonation** — In multi-agent systems, when a compromised agent influences others. The distributed AI attack you have not thought about yet.

**For each threat:** How the attack works (with real examples). How to detect it. How to prevent it. How to respond if it has already been exploited.

**Who this is for:** Developers building with AI, business owners using chatbots, and anyone handling customer data through an AI system.
