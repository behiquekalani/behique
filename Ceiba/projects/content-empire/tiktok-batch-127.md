Topic: The daily content habit (what to post when you have nothing to say)

## TikTok 1: The Blank Screen Problem
**Hook (0-2s):**
"You open the app to post. Your mind goes blank. This happens every single day."

**Script:**
The blank screen is not a creativity problem. It is a system problem.

Creators who post consistently do not have more ideas than you. They have a structure that tells them exactly what to do even when they do not feel inspired.

Here is the simplest version of that structure: one observation, one reaction, one lesson.

What did you notice today? How did you react to it? What does it teach you or someone like you?

That is a post. It is not perfect. It does not need to be. But it is real, it is specific, and it is yours.

The creators you follow who seem to always have something to say are not more interesting than you. They just stopped waiting to feel ready.

## TikTok 2: The 20-Minute Window
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Stop spending 3 hours on content. You only need 20 minutes."

**Script:**
Most creators spend more time planning content than actually making it.

Here is a structure that takes 20 minutes and produces enough content for a week.

Five minutes to write down every question your audience asked this week. Three minutes to pick the two that feel most interesting. Twelve minutes to write one short answer to each.

That is two posts. Do that four days a week and you have eight pieces of content by Friday.

The secret is not to create more. The secret is to reduce the decision-making time so that creation happens almost automatically.

Remove the choice. Follow the structure. Ship the work.

## TikTok 3: Steal From Your Own Conversations
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your best content is hiding in your DMs right now."

**Script:**
The most common reason creators say they have nothing to post is because they are looking in the wrong place.

They are staring at a blank document waiting for a great idea. Meanwhile their DMs are full of people asking them real questions about real problems.

Every conversation you have about your work is potential content. Every question someone asks you in person, every confusion someone expresses in the comments. That is your content calendar.

Your job is to formalize what you already know. Not to invent something new.

Go through your last ten messages. Find the most common thread. Post about that today.

## TikTok 4: The Reactive Post Method
**Hook (0-2s):**
"React to something you read today. That's your post."

**Script:**
Not every post needs to be a deep original thought.

Some of the highest-performing content follows this pattern: someone says something wrong, or incomplete, or that most people believe but should not. You respond to it.

You do not even need to name the original source. You just say "I keep seeing people say X" and then you explain why you think differently.

This works because it is specific, it creates contrast, and it positions you as someone with a clear point of view.

You have opinions. You read things every day that make you nod or shake your head. That reaction is your content.

Do not sit around waiting for a groundbreaking idea. React to the world and say what you actually think.

## TikTok 5: The Weekly Reset Ritual
**Hook (0-2s):**
"5 minutes every Sunday. You'll never run out of content again."

**Script:**
Every Sunday, do five things.

One: write down three things that happened this week that surprised you. Two: write one thing you learned that changed how you think. Three: write the question you got asked most. Four: write the mistake you made that you will not make again. Five: write one thing you are excited about for next week.

That is your content for the week. You do not need a content calendar, a content strategy, or a course on content creation.

You need five honest answers once a week and the willingness to say them out loud.

Most people skip this because they think they need to be interesting to do it. You do not. You just need to be specific.
