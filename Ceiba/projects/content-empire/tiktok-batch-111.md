## TikTok 1: Why Most Creators Stay Broke
**Hook (0-2s):**
You've been posting for a year. You have followers. You're still broke. Here's why.

**Script:**
Most creators think the problem is their content. It's not.

The problem is that they built an audience before they built an offer.

They spent 12 months optimizing hooks, trending sounds, posting schedules. And at the end of it, they have followers who expect free content forever.

The creator economy rewards two types of people: those who entertain at massive scale, and those who sell something specific to a specific person.

If you're not getting millions of views, you need to be the second type.

That means picking one problem you can solve. Building one product that solves it. And making sure every piece of content points to that product.

The creators who stay broke are the ones who create without a destination. Every post is a dead end. Watch this, enjoy this, maybe follow me.

The creators who make money create with direction. Every post is a step in a sequence that ends with a sale.

You don't need more content. You need a system behind the content you already have.

## TikTok 2: The One Skill Worth Learning in 2026
**Hook (0-2s):**
If you could only learn one skill this year, it should be this one.

**Script:**
Not coding. Not video editing. Not SEO.

Writing.

Specifically, the ability to take a complex idea and make it feel obvious to someone who's never thought about it before.

Every format you see online is just writing in disguise. The caption is writing. The script is writing. The email sequence is writing. The sales page is writing. The product itself is often just writing organized into a framework.

If you can write with clarity and conviction, you can build an audience, sell products, run an agency, consult for companies, and create content across every platform from one place.

The people who figured this out early are the ones who seem to be everywhere right now. They're not more talented. They just invested in the one skill that compounds across everything.

Clear writing is clear thinking. And clear thinking is the rarest skill in a world full of noise.

Start there.

## TikTok 3: Building a Product From Existing Content
**Hook (0-2s):**
You already have your first product. You just haven't packaged it yet.

**Script:**
Most people think building a product means starting from scratch.

It doesn't.

Look at the last 30 pieces of content you've made. What questions do people keep asking you? What topics generated the most comments, saves, shares?

That's your product outline.

Your audience has already voted on what they want from you. They've been telling you with every comment and every DM. The only thing you have to do is organize that content into a format someone can pay for.

Take 10 posts on the same topic. Write 200 more words per post. Add a framework. Call it a guide.

Boom. That's a $17 product.

Do it again. This time go deeper. Add templates, examples, a system. That's a $47 product.

The creators who make money fastest aren't the ones who create the most. They're the ones who package the best.

You've been creating for free long enough. Start organizing.

## TikTok 4: The Solopreneur's Approach to Branding
**Hook (0-2s):**
Stop trying to build a brand. Start building a point of view.

**Script:**
Brands need logos and color palettes and brand guidelines.

Solopreneurs need a perspective.

When you're one person building something, your brand is not your aesthetics. Your brand is what you believe and who you believe it for.

Pick a thesis. Something you'd argue in a room full of people who disagree. Something you think is true that most of your industry gets wrong.

That thesis is your brand.

Every post you make should either defend that thesis, give evidence for it, or apply it to a specific situation.

This is why some creators with worse production quality beat creators with massive budgets. One has a point of view. The other has a color scheme.

People don't follow aesthetics. They follow conviction.

Once you have a clear thesis, the visuals almost don't matter. Your audience will find you because you said something they couldn't stop thinking about.

That's branding for solopreneurs.

## TikTok 5: How to Grow Without Going Viral
**Hook (0-2s):**
Going viral once won't build your business. This will.

**Script:**
Everyone is chasing the viral moment. The one video that blows up and solves everything.

That's not how sustainable creator businesses work.

The creators who build real income don't rely on algorithms. They build assets.

An email list is an asset. Every person on it is someone you can reach without asking a platform for permission.

A product is an asset. Every sale compounds your reputation.

A methodology, a framework, a named system, that's an asset. It's what people reference when they tell someone else about you.

Viral content is rented attention. Assets are owned attention.

The strategy is simple. Post consistently. Build trust. Offer something worth buying. Collect emails. Repeat.

At 1,000 true fans who trust you, you make more money than creators with 100,000 followers who trust nobody.

Growth is not the goal. Depth is the goal. Depth converts.

Stop chasing reach. Start building relationships at scale.
