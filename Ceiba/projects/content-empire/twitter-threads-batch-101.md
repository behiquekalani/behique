### Thread 1: Why LinkedIn is the last platform with real organic reach

Most platforms are pay-to-play now.

Instagram reach is dead unless you pay.
TikTok is unpredictable.
X is noisy.

LinkedIn is the anomaly in 2026.

Here is how to actually use it:

1/ LinkedIn still rewards organic content with real distribution. A post from someone with 200 connections can reach 10,000 people in a week if it lands correctly. That does not happen on any other platform without paid reach or a major account sharing you.

2/ The intent on LinkedIn is different. People there are in builder mode. They are looking for tools, strategies, and people to work with. When your content finds them, they are already warm. That is the highest-value attention available.

3/ The creator population is tiny relative to the user base. 1 billion LinkedIn members. Under 1% post consistently. You are not competing with a billion people. You are competing with the small fraction who actually show up every week.

4/ The organic window is not permanent. Every platform eventually tightens it as ad revenue becomes the priority. LinkedIn is earlier in that cycle. The cost of not building now is missing the window while it exists.

5/ Your profile is not a resume. It is a landing page. Before you post a single thing, your headline, About section, and Featured section need to be built for conversion, not for employment.

6/ The hook formula is simple: the first line of every post is the only line most people will read. Master that one line and your reach doubles. The rest is just consistency.

Follow for the full LinkedIn growth system.

### Thread 2: Profile before posting. The 3 things that have to be right first.

Your profile is losing you clients right now.

Before you post a single thing on LinkedIn, fix these 3 sections.

1/ The headline. Most people write their job title. That is a label, not a hook. Your headline needs to say what you do and who you do it for.

Bad: "Marketing Manager at Company X"
Good: "I help SaaS founders build content systems that generate leads without paid ads"

2/ The About section. The first two lines are all most people see. Those lines need to earn the "see more" click. Start with a hook. End with a CTA. One specific instruction: follow, subscribe, DM me. Most profiles end with nothing. One sentence at the end changes everything.

3/ The Featured section. This is the real estate below your About that most people leave empty. Use it to pin your best post, your lead magnet, or your newsletter signup. Someone who reads your About is already warm. The Featured section is where you convert them.

These three fixes take one hour. They are the difference between a profile that works while you sleep and one that just exists.

### Thread 3: The LinkedIn algorithm in 2026. What actually determines who sees your posts.

The algorithm is simpler than people think.

Here is what is actually happening:

1/ When you post, LinkedIn shows it to a small sample of your connections first. It measures engagement rate in the first two hours. If that early signal is strong, it pushes the post wider. If not, it dies.

2/ Comments are worth more than likes. A post with 20 thoughtful comments outperforms a post with 200 likes. The algorithm weights comments because they signal real engagement, not passive scrolling.

3/ External links in the post body kill reach. LinkedIn does not want to send people off-platform. Put links in the comments, not the post itself. This is counterintuitive but consistent across every creator who tracks this.

4/ Native content wins. Video uploaded directly outperforms YouTube links. Documents (carousels) get saves, which are high-value signals. Write inside LinkedIn, not outside it.

5/ Consistency compounds. The algorithm rewards accounts with consistent posting history. Show up 3 times per week for 6 months and the baseline reach of your posts increases without you changing anything about the content.

The mechanics are not complicated. Most people just do not apply them consistently.

### Thread 4: The minimum viable LinkedIn content system for solopreneurs

You do not need to post every day.

Here is the system that works without burning out:

1/ Pick 3 content pillars. As a solopreneur: expertise (what you know), process (what you are building), and opinion (what you believe). Rotate through all three. Never post the same type twice in a row.

2/ Batch your content. One 2-hour session per week. Write all your posts for the next 7 days in one sitting. Do not try to come up with ideas daily. That is how creative paralysis kills consistency.

3/ Post 3 times per week. Not every day. Three solid posts per week outperform 7 mediocre ones. The algorithm cares about engagement rate, not raw frequency.

4/ Build a swipe file. Keep a running document of ideas, good hooks you see, observations from your week, questions you get from clients. When you sit down to batch, you are drawing from this file, not starting from zero.

5/ Engage for 20 minutes after you post. Respond to every comment. The algorithm boosts posts that continue generating conversation after the initial publish. Your own replies count.

3x per week. 2-hour batch session. Swipe file. That is the whole system.

### Thread 5: From followers to clients. The LinkedIn conversion path.

Followers are not the goal.

Here is what the actual funnel looks like:

1/ Post brings someone to your profile. Not everyone who reads your content will click through, but the ones who do are already warm. They have decided you might be worth paying attention to.

2/ Profile converts them. If your headline, About, and Featured section are doing their job, they either follow you or click your CTA. If the profile is empty or generic, you lose them here.

3/ CTA takes them to your email list. LinkedIn can change its algorithm tomorrow. Your reach can drop by 80%. The connections you built are not fully yours. The email list is. Every piece of content you produce should have a path to it.

4/ DM inquiry is the shortcut. Some people skip the whole funnel and reach out directly because a single post hit them at the right moment. When that happens, be ready. Know what you offer. Know your prices. Know how to qualify someone in 3 messages.

5/ The email list is where the business lives. The newsletter is what you control. LinkedIn is the top of the funnel, not the whole thing.

Post, profile, CTA, email. That is the path. Everything else is detail.
