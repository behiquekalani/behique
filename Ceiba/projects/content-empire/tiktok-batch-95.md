## TikTok 1: Why Your Content Isn't Converting
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Great content that doesn't convert is just entertainment. Here's the fix."

**Script:**
I spent six months posting content that got decent views but zero sales.

Here's what I was doing wrong.

I was teaching. Not positioning.

Teaching content says: here's how to do X.
Positioning content says: here's why X matters, here's what happens when you don't have X, and here's what it looks like to have X solved.

People don't buy information. They buy the feeling of their problem being understood.

When someone watches your content and thinks "that's exactly what I'm dealing with," you've done the hard work. The sale is almost automatic after that.

So here's the shift.

Lead with the problem, not the solution. Describe the problem in detail. The specific frustration. The moment it gets annoying. The thing they've already tried that didn't work.

Then present the solution as the logical next step.

Selling is not pushy when the buyer already believes the problem is real. Your content should do the work of making that problem undeniable.

**CTA:**
Look at your last 5 posts. Are they teaching or positioning? Tell me in the comments.

## TikTok 2: Build Wealth With What You Already Know
**Hook (0-2s):**
"You're sitting on knowledge that people would pay for. Here's proof."

**Script:**
Most people dramatically underestimate the value of what they know.

You've learned things through years of experience that would save someone else months of trial and error.

That shortcut has value. People pay for shortcuts constantly.

The question is not "do I know enough?" The question is "is there a specific person who is three years behind me who would pay to get there faster?"

The answer is almost always yes.

Here's how to find out.

Write down one skill you have that took you at least a year to learn. Then write down the five biggest mistakes you made while learning it.

That list of mistakes is your product. Turn it into a short guide titled "The [X] Mistakes That Slowed My [Skill] Progress and How to Avoid Them."

Price it at $19. Post one TikTok about mistake number one.

Watch whether people engage. If they do, they're validating the topic.

You don't need credentials. You need specificity and honesty.

**CTA:**
What took you the longest to learn in your career? That's your product. Start there.

## TikTok 3: The Passive Income Reality Check
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Passive income is real. But it's not what they're selling you."

**Script:**
The phrase "passive income" is used to sell dreams. You see the screenshots, the Stripe dashboards, the laptop on the beach.

Here's what those screenshots don't show.

The six months of active work before the passive income started.

True passive income requires an enormous upfront investment. Time, content, systems, trust. None of that is passive.

What you're actually building is a residual income system. You do intense work once. Then that work earns at a lower intensity over time.

The key word is "over time."

A digital product you build today might earn $50 in its first month. $200 in month three. $500 in month six. If you keep promoting it.

Most people quit in month one because it doesn't feel passive yet.

The ones who reach real income through digital products are the ones who treated the first year as planting season, not harvest season.

Plant first. Be patient. Harvest later.

**CTA:**
What are you planting this month? Tell me below.

## TikTok 4: The 80/20 Rule for Content Creation
**Hook (0-2s):**
"80 percent of your results will come from 20 percent of your content. Here's how to find it."

**Script:**
You don't need to go viral every day. You need two or three pieces of content that work so well they carry your whole account.

Here's how to find those pieces before you spend months guessing.

Look at the content that has already performed above average for you. It doesn't have to be huge. Just better than your average.

What did those pieces have in common?

Topic? Format? Length? Hook style?

That pattern is your signal.

For most creators, the pattern is one of three things: personal stories, strong counterintuitive opinions, or very specific how-to content.

Identify your pattern. Then deliberately create ten more pieces using that same structure.

Watch what happens.

Once you know your pattern, content creation stops feeling like gambling and starts feeling like a system. You're not hoping something works. You're replicating what already works.

Results compound when you stop experimenting randomly and start testing intentionally.

**CTA:**
Go back and look at your top three performing posts. What do they have in common?

## TikTok 5: How AI Is Changing Digital Products
**Hook (0-2s):**
"AI hasn't made digital products obsolete. It's made them more valuable."

**Script:**
When ChatGPT launched, a lot of people thought ebooks and courses were dead.

Anyone can ask AI for information now. Why would they pay for yours?

Here's the answer.

AI gives information. Your product gives context, curation, and applied experience.

The difference: I can ask ChatGPT how to build a Notion system for a solopreneur. It'll give me something generic.

Or I can buy your $27 Notion template that was built specifically for freelance designers who manage three clients at once, comes with tutorials, and was tested by 200 people.

One is information. The other is a ready-made solution.

The products that are losing value are the ones that only contained information. The products that are gaining value are the ones that save time and remove thinking.

Templates, systems, workflows, curated resources. These are worth more now because AI made generic information free.

Your product should do something AI cannot do out of a single prompt.

**CTA:**
The link in bio has my best-selling template packs. All tested and ready to use.
