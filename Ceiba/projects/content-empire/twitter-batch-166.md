**4. 3-tweet thread**

The compounding effect of a focused niche is real. Here's how it actually works:

/1

When you speak to one specific person, every piece of content you make gets more relevant to them. They keep coming back. Each post builds on the last. The algorithm rewards consistency in topic. Your authority compounds.

/2

Compare that to the generalist. New topic every week. No thread. No memory. Followers show up once and never return because there's nothing to stay for.

/3

The niche isn't permanent. It's a foundation. You earn the right to expand later. But first you have to own something. Pick a lane. Go deep. The money is always downstream of trust.

---

**5. Punchy single**

"My niche is too small."

The smaller the niche, the higher the price you can charge.
Specialists always get paid more than generalists.

---

**6. 6-tweet thread**

I was creating content for 6 months with no traction. Then I did one thing differently. Here's what changed everything:

/1

I stopped writing for everyone. I had been creating content about "online business." That's like saying you sell "food." It means nothing. Too broad. No signal.

/2

I picked one specific person: someone who works a 9-5 and wants to build a digital product on the side without quitting their job. That's a real human. I could picture them.

/3

I rewrote every post with that one person in mind. Same topics. Completely different framing. Instead of "how to make money online" it became "what to build when you only have 2 hours a day."

/4

Engagement went up. Not because the content was better written. Because it felt personal. The right people felt seen. The wrong people scrolled past. That's the filter working.

/5

Then something weird happened. People started tagging their friends. "This is literally you." Because specific content travels. Vague content disappears.

/6

The compounding kicks in around month 3 of staying in your lane. Posts reference older posts. Followers remember you. You're not starting over every week. You're building. Stay in the lane.

**7. Punchy single**

**8. 3-tweet thread**

Why focused niches compound faster than broad ones:

/1

Trust is the currency of online business. You build trust by being predictable. Predictable means: I know what this person stands for. You cannot be predictable if your content changes topics every week.

/2

When you stay in one lane, every post trains the algorithm and the audience simultaneously. Both start to expect you in that space. Both start to send you more of the right people.

/3

Six months of focused content creates an asset. A catalog that all points to the same idea. That catalog is a sales funnel before you ever build one. The niche is not a cage. It's a compound interest machine.
