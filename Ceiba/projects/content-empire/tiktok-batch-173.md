## Script 1: The $2K Ceiling Is Real
**Hook (0-3s):** "There's a specific reason most online businesses get to $2,000 a month and stop growing. It's not what you think."
**Body:** The $2K plateau is not a market problem. It's a model problem. At $2K, most people have maxed out what their current approach can produce. They're selling one-off products. They're trading time for money. They're relying on one traffic source. The business grew to match the model, then stopped. Breaking through requires a model change, not more effort on the same model. Doubling output on a broken model produces $4K once and then crashes. Fix the model first.

## Script 2: The One-Offer Trap
**Hook (0-3s):** "You're stuck at $2K because everything you sell depends on one thing working perfectly."
**Body:** Single offer. Single traffic source. Single price point. This is the structure of a $2K business. If the offer stops converting, revenue stops. If the traffic source changes its algorithm, revenue stops. There's no redundancy and no progression. The businesses that break past $2K have an entry offer, a middle offer, and a high-ticket offer. They have two or three traffic sources. They have a list. One thing failing doesn't collapse the system. You don't need more products. You need a structured offer ladder that captures customers at every level.
**CTA:** Save this. Offer ladder design coming next.
**On-screen text:**
- "One offer = one point of failure"
- "Entry > mid > high ticket = offer ladder"
- "Two traffic sources minimum"
- "Redundancy is not complexity"

## Script 3: You're Solving the Wrong Problem
**Hook (0-3s):** "You're trying to get more customers. You should be trying to get more money from existing customers."
**Body:** Getting a new customer costs five to seven times more than selling to an existing one. At the $2K plateau, most people focus obsessively on new customer acquisition while completely ignoring the people who already bought. Those buyers already trusted you enough to spend money. What else can you offer them? A next step. A related product. A deeper service. A community. An existing customer who spends $50 can become a $200 customer with one relevant offer. That shift alone can double revenue without acquiring a single new customer.
**CTA:** Follow to see what to offer existing buyers.
**On-screen text:**
- "New customers cost 5-7x more"
- "Your buyers are your best prospects"
- "What is the next step for someone who bought?"
- "Double revenue without new customers"

## Script 4: The Time-for-Money Ceiling
**Hook (0-3s):** "If your revenue stops when you stop working, you've built a ceiling into your business model."
**Body:** Service businesses and freelancers hit the $2K ceiling fastest. There are only so many hours in a day. You can raise rates, but there's still a cap. Breaking past this requires creating something that sells while you sleep. A course. A template. A system. A community. Not instead of services. In addition to them. The services fund the time to build the assets. The assets remove the ceiling. Most people know this but never prioritize building the asset because the service revenue feels safer. The safety is the trap.

## Script 5: The Distribution Problem Nobody Wants to Address
**Hook (0-3s):** "Your product is fine. Your distribution is broken. That's why you're stuck."
**Body:** Most people assume that if a product doesn't sell, the product is bad. Usually the product is fine. The distribution is broken. Distribution means: who sees your offer, how often, and in what context. If you're posting once a day on one platform, your distribution is weak. If nobody knows you exist outside your 400 followers, your distribution is weak. The $2K ceiling is often just a distribution ceiling. More platforms. More consistent content. Email list. Partnerships. Affiliates. The product doesn't need to change. The reach does.
**CTA:** Comment "DIST" for the distribution checklist.
**On-screen text:**
- "Product fine. Distribution broken."
- "Distribution = who sees your offer"
- "400 followers = small distribution"
- "Fix reach before fixing product"

## Script 6: The Pricing Problem at $2K
**Hook (0-3s):** "You might be underpricing yourself by 300% and not know it."
**Body:** At the $2K level, most people are selling low-ticket products because they're afraid higher prices won't convert. So they sell $20 products and need 100 sales a month to hit $2K. That's 100 people who said yes. With a $100 product, you need 20. With a $200 product, you need 10. The effort to sell 10 things is not five times easier than selling 100 things. But the targeting is different. Higher-ticket buyers exist. They buy based on outcome, not price. Your pricing is telling the market what you think your solution is worth. Price accordingly.

- "High-ticket buyers exist in your niche"
- "Pricing signals your belief in your product"
- "You may be 300% underpriced right now"

## Script 7: Why the Plateau Feels Like a Wall
**Hook (0-3s):** "The plateau doesn't happen gradually. One month you're growing, next month you're stuck. Here's why."
**Body:** Growth at the beginning is easy because you're filling demand that was already there. Early buyers are the most motivated ones. They find you without much effort. When that pool runs out, you have to build systems to generate consistent demand. That shift from reactive growth to proactive growth is the wall. It requires content strategy, SEO, email, partnerships. It requires building infrastructure you don't have yet. Most people interpret the plateau as evidence that the market is saturated. It's not. It's evidence that the low-hanging fruit is gone and real business building starts now.
**CTA:** Follow for what comes after the easy growth phase.
**On-screen text:**
- "Early growth = motivated buyers finding you"
- "After that pool: real work starts"
- "Plateau = infrastructure gap, not saturated market"
- "Reactive growth has a ceiling"

## Script 8: The Identity Shift Required to Break $2K
**Hook (0-3s):** "You won't break past $2K until you stop thinking like a freelancer and start thinking like a business owner."
**Body:** Freelancer mindset: sell time, deliver work, get paid, repeat. Business owner mindset: build systems that sell, create assets that compound, hire or automate what you do manually. The $2K ceiling is partly a mindset ceiling. If you still think your job is to do the work, you'll always be capped by how much work you can do. If you think your job is to build the system that does the work, the ceiling disappears. This shift is uncomfortable. It requires you to invest in things that don't pay off immediately. That discomfort is the price of the next level.
