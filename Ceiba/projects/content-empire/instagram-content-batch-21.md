## Caption 3 — What Clients Want vs. What Developers Think

**Hook:** Clients do not want AI. They want time.

## Caption 9 — How to Scope an Automation Project

**Hook:** Scope wrong, and the project never ends.

The most common mistake when building for clients: not defining what the automation does not do.

Every automation should have a clear boundary. It handles X. When Y happens, it flags a human. When Z happens, it stops and sends an alert.

Defining the edges before you build saves you three client calls after you deliver. Write it down. Get them to agree to it before you start.

## Caption 10 — The One-Hour Client Meeting

**Hook:** One hour. That is all you need.

The first client meeting has one job: identify the problem. Not sell the solution. Not talk about technology.

Ask one question: "What is the most repetitive thing your team does every week that does not require a human decision?"

Then listen. They will tell you everything. The problem, the scope, the value, and sometimes the price they would pay without you naming a number first.

The best client meetings feel like conversations, not pitches.

## Caption 11 — Building the Portfolio Before the Clients Come

**Hook:** Three free builds beat any certification.

No client wants to be your first. So do not tell them they are. Build three real automations for real businesses before you charge anyone.

Pick businesses you know. A family member's shop. A neighbor's service business. A local place you go to regularly. Build something useful for free. Document the results.

Now you have a portfolio. Not a course badge. Actual outcomes for actual businesses. That is what closes clients.
