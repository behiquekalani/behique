## TikTok 1: The Attention Economy Is Lying to You
**Hook (0-2s):**
"The attention economy is built to keep you broke. Here's how."

**Script:**
Social media platforms are not built for you to build an audience. They're built to keep people scrolling.

That means the content that performs best is content that provokes reaction, not content that builds trust.

Outrage, controversy, shock. That's what the algorithm rewards.

But that content builds the wrong audience. You get followers who came for the drama, not for the value.

Here's the reframe that changed how I post.

I'm not trying to go viral. I'm trying to be the first person someone thinks of when they have a specific problem.

That requires consistency over virality. It requires posting about the same topic from different angles, not chasing trending sounds.

The creators I see building real income are not the ones with the most views. They're the ones with the most trust in a specific niche.

Slow is sustainable. Fast is fragile.

**CTA:**
Pick your niche. Commit to 90 days. Watch what happens.

## TikTok 2: Build One Thing, Sell It Forever
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Most creators build new things every month. The wealthy ones don't."

**Script:**
Here's the difference between a content creator and a content business.

A creator makes new content constantly to maintain attention. If they stop posting, income stops.

A business creates an asset once and optimizes it over time. The asset keeps earning after the work is done.

The shift happens when you build something that sells while you sleep.

A course. A template pack. A newsletter with a paid tier. A guide that solves one expensive problem.

The key is traffic. You need a constant stream of people discovering the asset.

That's what short-form content does. It's not the product. It's the pipeline that feeds traffic to the product.

Every TikTok I make is a billboard for one of three products. Not ten products. Three.

I build the product once. I build the content forever. The content points to the product.

That's the model. One asset, sustained traffic, compounding income.

**CTA:**
What would you build once if you knew people would find it for years?

## TikTok 3: What AI Cannot Replace
**Hook (0-2s):**
"AI will replace most of what creators do. Not this."

**Script:**
AI can write copy. AI can edit video. AI can generate images, captions, emails, product descriptions.

I use AI for all of those things. It saves me hours every week.

But here's what AI cannot do.

It cannot have a point of view built from lived experience. It cannot have a real take that comes from years of doing the work, making mistakes, and seeing what no one else was paying attention to.

Your value as a creator is not your execution. It's your perspective.

The creators who are failing right now are the ones who were already competing on execution. Writing fast. Editing well. Formatting cleanly. AI does all of that better.

The creators who are winning are the ones who have something specific and true to say. Opinion over information. Experience over research.

Nobody can AI-generate your story. Your story is the only thing that cannot be commoditized.

Build your perspective before you build your system.

**CTA:**
What's one thing you believe that most people in your niche get wrong?

## TikTok 4: The Solopreneur's Unfair Advantage
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Being a solopreneur isn't a disadvantage. Here's why it's actually the edge."

**Script:**
Every large company has a bureaucracy problem. Decisions take weeks. Execution takes months.

You can decide and ship in 24 hours.

That speed is your unfair advantage and almost nobody is using it intentionally.

Here's what it means in practice.

A trend emerges in your niche. You see it, you create a product around it, and you post about it in the same week.

A large brand would need three approval cycles and a product roadmap update to do what you just did in two days.

Solopreneurs who win treat speed as a strategy. They don't wait for perfect. They ship a minimum viable product, get feedback, and improve while it's already making money.

The question to ask yourself is not "is this ready?" The question is "is this good enough to solve someone's problem today?"

If yes, ship it.

**CTA:**
What are you waiting to ship? Drop it in the comments. I'll tell you if it's ready.

## TikTok 5: The Evergreen Content Formula
**Hook (0-2s):**
"I posted this video two years ago. It still gets views today."

**Script:**
Most content has a lifespan of 24 to 72 hours. After that, the algorithm moves on and so does the audience.

Evergreen content is different. It answers questions people have permanently, not just this week.

Here's how to identify an evergreen topic.

Ask yourself: will someone search for this in two years?

"How to make money online with AI" — yes.
"My reaction to this week's viral moment" — no.

The evergreen framework I use:

One. Identify a problem your audience will always have.
Two. Teach the foundational principle, not the trending tactic.
Three. End with a timeless action step, not a platform-specific hack.

Foundational content compounds. Every new viewer who discovers it reinforces the algorithm signal.

I have three videos that drive 60 percent of my sales. All three were made in the first year. All three are evergreen.

Work once. Earn for years.

**CTA:**
Rewatch your best-performing video. Can you make an evergreen version of it today?
