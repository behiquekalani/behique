### Script 1 — 50 Lessons, Part 1: The Foundation (Lessons 1-10)
**Hook (0-3s):** 250 videos. 50 real lessons. Starting with the ones that changed everything.
**Setup (3-10s):** This is batch 50. That means 250 scripts written, 250 videos made, and a lot of things I wish I knew on day one.
**Content (10-55s):**
1. The hook is 90% of the result. Nobody hears the lesson if they swipe before it starts.
2. Your first 50 videos are practice. Post them anyway.
3. The algorithm rewards completion rate. Short videos that get watched fully beat long videos that get skipped.
4. Consistency compounds. 10 average videos a month beats 1 perfect video a month every time.
5. Your niche is not your topic. It's the specific problem you solve for a specific person.
6. Comments are more valuable than views. They mean something landed.
7. Never read your script word for word. Deliver it, don't recite it.
8. One strong hook tested across 10 videos teaches you more than 10 different hooks.
9. Your voice gets better by video 30. It sounds uncomfortable until then. Keep going.
10. The video you almost didn't post usually performs best.

20. Never chase virality. Chase usefulness. Virality follows.

### Script 4 — 50 Lessons, Part 4: Monetization and Business (Lessons 31-40)
**Hook (0-3s):** Nobody talks about the business side of content creation honestly. Here it is.
**Setup (3-10s):** Lessons 31 through 40. This is where the content becomes a real business.
**Content (10-55s):**
31. Build an email list from day one. The platform you're on is not yours.
32. Sell before you build. Validate ideas before investing weeks in them.
33. Digital products are the highest-leverage offer for a solo creator.
34. Free content builds the audience. Paid products serve that audience deeper.
35. One product that solves a real problem earns more than ten products that don't.
36. Your link in bio is prime real estate. Don't waste it on a social profile link.
37. Every piece of content should point toward a clear next step for the viewer.
38. Affiliate income is real but slow. Build your own product as soon as possible.
39. Price anchoring works. Show a higher-value bundle alongside your entry product.
40. The goal isn't to make one sale. The goal is to build a system that makes recurring sales.
