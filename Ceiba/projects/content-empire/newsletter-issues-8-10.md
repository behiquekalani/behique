## ISSUE 8

**Subject: I posted my first TikTok. 47 people watched it.**

---

Hey,

This week I posted the first five TikTok videos. No followers. No existing audience. Just screen recordings and a newly created account pointed at the same content I have been sitting on.

The first video got 47 views within the hour. That sounds low. But the account had zero followers. Nobody shared it. The algorithm found those 47 people on its own.

That's the part most people do not understand about new accounts.

---

### This Week's Build

Posted five TikToks as screen recording content. The account had zero followers when I started. Views came in within 60 minutes of the first post going live.

The algorithm on new accounts does not behave like the algorithm on established ones. It is actively testing your content against cold audiences to figure out where it belongs. Every view is the platform trying to categorize you. The faster you feed it content, the faster it figures it out.

Zero followers is not a disadvantage. It is a clean slate.

---

### Tool of the Week

**CapCut** - free desktop app for video editing. This is the actual tool I use to put together Reels and TikToks. Timeline editing, auto-captions, text overlays, and transitions all in one place without a subscription.

The desktop version is more capable than the mobile app. If you are making short-form video and using anything else, you are making it harder than it needs to be.

Free. Mac and Windows.

---

### The Number

**47** - views on the first TikTok, from an account with zero followers. The video was not viral. It was proof that the distribution channel works before you have an audience. That distinction matters.

---

### What I'm Thinking About

Zero followers is still distribution. The platform shows every video to some people. Those people are real. They watched, or they scrolled. Either way, that feedback loop exists from day one.

Most people wait until they have an audience to start posting. But you build the audience by posting. There is no other sequence that works.

## ISSUE 10

**Subject: 90 scripts. 0 videos recorded. The bottleneck is named.**

---

Hey,

Today is the 90-day check-in I announced back in Issue 4.

Here is the actual count: 90 scripts written, 45 Instagram posts drafted, 10 TikToks live, 0 YouTube videos recorded.

The gap between content and distribution is still the constraint. I have been building the pipeline. I have not been standing in front of a camera. Those are two different things and I kept confusing one for the other.

---

### This Week's Build

Reviewed everything from the past 90 days. 90 scripts produced, 45 Instagram posts drafted, 10 TikToks live on the account. YouTube: zero recorded.

The production capacity is not the problem. I can write scripts faster than I can post them. The bottleneck is recording, which is a physical act that requires showing up and pressing record. That does not happen inside a content calendar.

The gap between content and distribution is now clearly named. Naming it is the first step to fixing it.

---

---

### The Number

**90** - scripts written. 0 videos recorded from those scripts. The recording bottleneck is clear and named. The next 90 days have one focus: close the gap between production and publication.

---

### What I'm Thinking About

Production capacity is not the bottleneck. Publication is.

Building the system is the part that feels like work. Showing up to post is the part that actually moves the numbers. I spent 90 days getting very good at the first part.

The second part does not require more tools. It requires a different kind of consistency. Ship rate over quality rate. The lesson is in the data.
