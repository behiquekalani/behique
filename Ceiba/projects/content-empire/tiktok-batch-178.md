## Script 2: Why Knowledge Products Sell While You Sleep
**Hook (0-3s):** I made a sale at 3am last Tuesday. The product I built two years ago did it.
**Body:** A knowledge product is a one-time build, infinite sale. You write it once. You design it once. You price it once. Then you connect it to a payment processor and let people find it. You don't need to be online. You don't need to schedule anything. You don't trade your time for that revenue. That's the structural difference between a knowledge product and a service. A service pays you every time you show up. A product pays you whether you show up or not. The goal of building products isn't to stop working. It's to decouple your income from your hours. Start with one product and feel what that feels like.

## Script 3: The Self-Selling Product Formula
**Hook (0-3s):** A product that sells itself has these four elements. Most people skip number three.
**Body:** One: it solves a specific, felt problem. Not a vague aspiration. A real pain someone is actively trying to fix. Two: the title makes the outcome obvious. Not "The Ultimate Guide to Productivity." Something like "The 30-Minute Morning Routine That Ends Decision Fatigue." Three: social proof from someone exactly like the buyer. Not a generic testimonial. A story from someone the buyer can see themselves in. Four: a low enough price that the decision is easy. The product doesn't need a sales team when those four elements are right. The product sells itself because it already speaks the buyer's language.
**CTA:** Follow for the full product launch system.
**On-screen text:** ["4 elements of self-selling", "Specific felt problem first", "Social proof = someone like them", "Right price = easy decision"]

## Script 4: The Fastest Knowledge Product You Can Build
**Hook (0-3s):** You can build a sellable knowledge product this weekend. No course required.
**Body:** Open a Google Doc. Write the answer to the one question you get asked most often. Be specific. Be thorough. Give the real answer, not the sanitized version. Format it cleanly. Export it as a PDF. Price it between $9 and $27. Upload to Gumroad. Write one social post explaining what problem it solves and who it's for. That's it. You just built a knowledge product. Most people overthink this. They want it to be perfect before it ships. But a finished imperfect product that sells beats a perfect product that never launches. Ship the first draft and improve based on feedback.
**CTA:** Comment "doc" if you're going to build yours this weekend.
**On-screen text:** ["Google Doc to Gumroad this weekend", "Answer your most-asked question", "Price $9-$27 to start", "Ship first, improve second"]

## Script 5: Pricing Your Knowledge Product Right
**Hook (0-3s):** The price of your knowledge product tells buyers more than the description does.
**Body:** Price too low and people assume it's not worth serious attention. Price too high for a first product with no audience and no social proof and nobody buys. The sweet spot for a first knowledge product is $17 to $47. Cheap enough that the decision is frictionless. Expensive enough to signal real value. As you build social proof and an audience, you increase the price. The same PDF that sold for $17 to your first ten buyers can sell for $97 when you have fifty testimonials. Price is a signal. Start where the barrier is low. Build trust. Then price what you're actually worth.
**CTA:** Save this before pricing your first product.
**On-screen text:** ["Price = signal before content", "Sweet spot: $17-$47 to start", "Build proof then raise price", "Same PDF, different era, different price"]

Build the stack.

## Script 7: How Content Creates Product Demand
**Hook (0-3s):** The best sales pitch for your product is a TikTok that gives the idea away for free.
**Body:** When you post content that genuinely helps someone, two things happen. They trust you. And they want more. The content creates the demand for the product. You post a video about how to structure your week as a freelancer. Comments start asking "is there a template for this?" That's the market telling you what to build. You build the template. You link back to the video as proof it works. The video does the selling because it already demonstrated the value. This is the content-to-product loop. Content builds trust. Trust creates demand. Demand justifies the product. The product funds more content. Repeat.

## Script 8: Why Your Expertise Feels Obvious to You
**Hook (0-3s):** The reason you undervalue your knowledge is the curse of expertise.
**Body:** You've been living inside your knowledge for so long that it feels obvious. You can't imagine charging for it because you can't imagine not knowing it. But for someone who doesn't know what you know, your knowledge is genuinely transformative. They're stuck. You're not. That gap is the product. The copywriter who thinks writing clearly is obvious. The designer who thinks visual hierarchy is basic. The developer who thinks debugging is just pattern recognition. None of it is obvious to the person who needs it. Your curse of expertise is someone else's gap. Package the obvious. Price it honestly. Let it help people.
**CTA:** Save this if you've ever talked yourself out of building a product.
**On-screen text:** ["Curse of expertise is real", "Obvious to you = gold to them", "Your knowledge gap is the product", "Package the obvious"]
