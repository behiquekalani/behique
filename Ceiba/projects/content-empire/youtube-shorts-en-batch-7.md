Content batching is the only reason I'm able to stay consistent without content creation taking over my week.

[ON SCREEN: Batching = consistency without burnout]

Here's the two-hour process:

[ON SCREEN: The 2-hour process]

30 minutes: ideation. I review my notes from the week, conversations I had, things I read that surprised me. I pick the three ideas with the most energy.

[ON SCREEN: 0:00 - 0:30 — Ideation (3 ideas)]

30 minutes: outlines. I write a rough structure for each piece. Not the full content, just the skeleton. Hook, main point, supporting points, CTA.

[ON SCREEN: 0:30 - 1:00 — Outlines]

45 minutes: drafts. I write all three pieces back to back without editing. The goal is getting words out. Editing comes later.

[ON SCREEN: 1:00 - 1:45 — First drafts]

15 minutes: editing and scheduling. Quick edit pass, final review, schedule in Buffer or Typefully.

[ON SCREEN: 1:45 - 2:00 — Edit and schedule]

Two hours. One week of content. Done.

The key is batching like tasks together, not trying to do ideation, writing, and editing in the same mental state.
