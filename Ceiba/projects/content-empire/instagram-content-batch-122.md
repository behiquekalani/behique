## Post 2 — Attraction Content: What It Actually Is
**Hook:** Attraction content is not viral content. It is specific content.
**Slide 1:** Attraction content is not viral content. It is specific content.
**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Attraction content brings new people in by addressing a pain they are already searching for. It meets them where they are, not where you want them to be.
- Slide 3: The best attraction content makes someone stop and think "wait, this account understands something I have been dealing with." That is the mechanism.
- Slide 4: Viral is a distribution outcome, not a strategy. Specific is a strategy. You can be specific on purpose. You cannot be viral on purpose.
- Slide 5: Format options: problem-awareness posts, counter-intuitive takes, list posts that name a specific frustration.
**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai for a content system that builds the right audience.
**Caption:** Most creators confuse attraction content with entertainment content. Entertainment can attract but it attracts an audience that came for entertainment, not for what you sell. Attraction content that converts into buyers is content that addresses a specific, felt problem in the life of your ideal customer. It does not need to be clever or viral. It needs to be right. When the right person finds your attraction content, they do not scroll past. They save it, follow, and consume more. That is the beginning of the trust arc. The goal of attraction content is not reach. It is finding the specific people who have the specific problem your product solves. Volume of views is irrelevant if the people watching are not the people you serve.

## Post 3 — Trust Content: The Type Most People Skip
**Hook:** Trust content is the reason people buy from you instead of someone else.
**Slide 1:** Trust content is the reason people buy from you instead of someone else.
**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Trust content shows your thinking process, your behind-the-scenes, your mistakes, your worldview. It is the content that makes you a person instead of an account.
- Slide 3: People do not buy products from accounts. They buy from people they believe have solved or deeply understand the problem.
- Slide 4: Trust content formats: lessons learned from failures, case studies from your own experience, honest takes on your niche, process documentation.
- Slide 5: The creator who shows their thinking earns trust faster than the one who only shows polished results.
**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai. Be the person, not just the account.
**Caption:** There is a specific moment in a buyer's journey where they stop evaluating whether your product is good and start evaluating whether you are someone they want to learn from. That moment is created by trust content. Trust content is not behind-the-scenes for the sake of authenticity theater. It is intentional content that reveals your reasoning, your process, and your genuine perspective on the problems in your niche. It proves that you have actually lived inside the problem you are selling a solution to. The shortcut most creators take is skipping this step and going straight from attracting an audience to pitching them. The result is a large following that never converts because the trust gap was never closed.

## Post 4 — Conversion Content: The Ask That Does Not Feel Like One
**Hook:** The best conversion content does not feel like a pitch. It feels like a conclusion.
**Slide 1:** The best conversion content does not feel like a pitch. It feels like a conclusion.
**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: Conversion content works when it is the natural end of a journey you have already taken the reader through with your attraction and trust content.
- Slide 3: It names the problem clearly, describes who has it, explains the transformation your product creates, and makes the ask directly.
- Slide 4: Conversion content fails when it appears without the foundation. The same post that converts 3% of a warm audience converts 0.1% of a cold one.
- Slide 5: The fix: run conversion content to your warmest followers, not your broadest reach.
**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai. Build the foundation, then make the offer.
**Caption:** Conversion content gets a bad reputation because most people experience it as unwanted pressure. The pitch that feels pushy is usually a pitch that arrived before the trust was built. When conversion content works, it does not feel like being sold to. It feels like a solution appearing at exactly the right moment. The creator who has done the work of attraction and trust content first creates this experience naturally. The offer is not interrupting the journey, it is completing it. Every piece of conversion content you post should be built on the back of at least three to five pieces of trust content that addressed the same underlying problem. Without that foundation, the conversion post is just noise with a price tag attached.

## Post 5 — The Weekly Content Rotation That Works
**Hook:** Stop planning content by topic. Plan it by function.
**Slide 1:** Stop planning content by topic. Plan it by function.
**Slides 2-5:**
- Slide 2: A functional weekly content plan: Monday attraction post, Wednesday trust post, Friday conversion post. The three types, every week.
- Slide 3: You can vary the topics infinitely within this structure. The function stays constant. The subject rotates.
- Slide 4: This structure ensures you are never only attracting, never only building trust, and never only pitching.
- Slide 5: Consistency of function beats consistency of volume. Three intentional posts outperform seven random ones every time.
**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai. Build the system. Post with purpose.
**Caption:** The reason most content calendars fail is that they are organized around topics, not outcomes. Knowing what to post about is not the same as knowing what function each post should serve. When you organize your content plan around the three types, attract, trust, convert, and assign one function to each piece before you write a word, the strategy becomes clear and the execution becomes simple. You are never wondering what to post. You are asking "what type does the week need most right now?" and filling the gap. That shift from reactive posting to intentional rotation is the difference between a content strategy that builds a business and one that builds an audience you cannot monetize.
