# AI Installation Service - Discovery Call Script
# 30-minute discovery call. Qualifies leads, maps their problem, closes to next step.
# Not a demo call. Not a pitch call. A diagnostic call.

---

## PRE-CALL PREP (5 minutes before)

Pull up anything you know about them:
- Their business name, what they sell or offer
- How they found you (Instagram, referral, DM, etc.)
- Any notes from prior messages
- Have the three tier prices ready: $499 / $999 / $2,500

---

## SECTION 1: OPENING
### Time: 0:00 - 2:00

Goal: Make it feel like a conversation, not an interview. Set expectations clearly.

**Script:**

"Hey [Name], good to connect. Just so we're on the same page about the next 30 minutes: I'm going to ask you some questions about how your business runs today. Based on that, I'll tell you honestly whether AI automation makes sense for your situation and where the clearest opportunity is. If there's a fit, we talk about what that looks like. If there isn't, I'll tell you that too. Sound good?"

Wait for confirmation. Then:

"Before we get into the details, just a quick one: how did you hear about what I do?"

[Note their answer. This tells you what frame they already have.]

---

## SECTION 2: QUALIFICATION
### Time: 2:00 - 12:00

Goal: Understand their business, how they spend time, and what tools they already have. You're looking for repetition, volume, and pain.

### Questions to ask (not all of them, read the room):

**Business basics:**
- "What does your business do, and who do you serve?"
- "How many people are on your team, including yourself?"
- "Are you solo or do you have employees or contractors?"

**Time and tasks:**
- "Walk me through a typical work day. What does your morning look like before you get to the work you actually want to do?"
- "What tasks do you do every week that feel like they should just... happen on their own?"
- "If I asked you what the most time-consuming non-revenue task in your business is, what comes to mind first?"

**Volume indicators:**
- "How many customer messages or inquiries do you get per week, roughly?"
- "How often are you creating content, sending emails, or updating some kind of record?"

**Current tools:**
- "What tools are you already using? CRM, email platform, scheduling, anything?"
- "Have you tried to automate anything before? What happened?"

### What the answers mean:

QUALIFY if:
- They can name a specific repeating task that takes 3+ hours per week
- They have some existing tools (email, CRM, Notion, Airtable, Google Sheets) that data flows through
- They expressed frustration or said something like "I have to do this manually"
- Revenue is established (they're paying for tools, they have customers)

DISQUALIFY if:
- Their business is pre-revenue and they have no operational tasks yet (wrong timing, come back in 6 months)
- Every task they describe requires human judgment, creativity, or relationship (not a good automation candidate)
- They want something built that requires code and ongoing engineering, not automation installation
- They expect automation to fix a broken business model

**If they're not a fit:** "Honest answer: what you're describing is more of a [development project / operational redesign / different kind of problem]. That's not what I specialize in. I wouldn't want to take your money for the wrong thing. Here's what I'd suggest instead..."

---

## SECTION 3: PROBLEM DEFINITION
### Time: 12:00 - 20:00

Goal: Identify 2-3 specific automation opportunities in their situation. Be specific. Name the tool, name the task, describe the outcome.

**Script:**

"Based on what you've told me, I want to name a few things I'm seeing. Tell me if any of these feel accurate."

Then describe 2-3 specific opportunities. Examples:

- "Your customer FAQ is getting answered manually every time. That's a bot problem. An AI agent can handle 70-80% of those without you, and route the complex ones to you with context."
- "You're updating a spreadsheet manually after every sale. That's a webhook problem. We connect your payment tool to your sheet and it updates itself."
- "You're writing every follow-up email by hand. That's a sequence problem. We build a conditional email flow based on what each lead actually did."

After you name them: "Does that match how you experience it, or is the pain somewhere different?"

Let them correct you. This is how you show you listened.

**Go deeper on the biggest one:**
- "How long does [that specific task] take you each time you do it?"
- "How often per week?"
- [Do the math in your head: hours x weeks x hourly value = annual cost of the problem]
- "So roughly, that's about [X hours] a month on that one task. That's real time."

---

## SECTION 4: SOLUTION OVERVIEW
### Time: 20:00 - 25:00

Goal: Describe what Behike delivers. Not a demo, not a full pitch. Just clarity on what they're buying.

**Script:**

"Here's how I work. Three options depending on scope."

"The Starter is $499. One-time. I pick the single highest-impact automation in your business, build it, test it, and hand it to you with a walkthrough. You get 30 days of support. Most people are set up within 48 hours. Best for someone who wants to see it work before committing to more."

"The Growth package is $999. Same one-time structure. I build the full pipeline for your biggest operational bottleneck. Multiple automations connected together. Two to three weeks. Better fit if you've already identified a specific system that needs to run end-to-end."

"Full Stack Builder is $2,500. This is a complete AI buildout. I map your entire operation, identify everything automatable, and install it in sequence. Takes three to four weeks. Includes documentation, training, and support. This is what makes sense if you're ready to stop doing operational work yourself."

Then:

"Given what you've described, my honest read is that [specific tier] fits where you are. Here's why: [two sentences specific to their situation]."

Pause. Let them respond.

---

## SECTION 5: NEXT STEPS
### Time: 25:00 - 30:00

Goal: Close to a decision. Either they book the Starter or you agree on a follow-up date. Never leave this call without a defined next step.

**Script:**

"So: does this feel like the right fit for what you're trying to solve?"

---

### Objection: "I can probably figure this out myself."

"You probably can. Most of what I build uses free tools with good documentation. The question is whether your time is better spent learning the stack or just having it running next week. The Starter is $499 because you're buying the setup time, not the secret. If you want to build it yourself, I'd start with [specific tool]. Happy to point you in the right direction."

Why this works: removes the defensiveness, respects their capability, still anchors the value.

---

### Objection: "AI feels too complicated for my setup."

"That's actually the exact problem I solve. The complexity stays on my side. What you get is a system that works. The person who booked their coffee shop's AI FAQ bot doesn't know what a webhook is, and doesn't need to."

---

### Objection: "I need to think about it."

"That's fair. What would you need to feel confident about it? Is it the price, the outcome, something else?"

Wait for the real objection to surface. Then address that specifically.

If they genuinely need time: "Let's do this. I'll send you a one-page summary of what I'd build for you based on what we talked about today. Review it. If it makes sense, we start. If not, no pressure."

[This gives you a reason to follow up with something specific instead of "just checking in."]

---

### Close - if they want to move forward:

"The fastest next step is the Starter. I send you a short intake form, you fill it in, I start building. We can have something live within 48 to 72 hours of you completing the form."

Or if they need the intake form first: "I'll send the onboarding doc right after this call. It's five questions. Once I have that back, we're moving."

---

## POST-CALL CHECKLIST

- [ ] Send intake form or one-page proposal within 2 hours
- [ ] Note which tier they're interested in
- [ ] Note the specific tasks they mentioned by name
- [ ] Set a follow-up reminder for 48 hours if they didn't close
- [ ] Add to CRM / Notion client tracker

---

## CALL QUALITY SIGNALS

You ran a good call if:
- They named a specific task, not a vague problem
- You quoted their own words back to them in the solution overview
- You named a specific tier with a specific reason
- You left with a defined next step and a date

You need to improve if:
- You talked more than they did
- You described features instead of time saved
- You left without a clear next step
- You didn't address the real objection
