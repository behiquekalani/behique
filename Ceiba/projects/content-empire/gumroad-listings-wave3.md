Most focus timers are a circle that counts down.

Study Buddy is different in one specific way: it treats focus like a game. You earn XP for completing focus blocks. You lose nothing for logging a distraction, because logging is the habit. You level up. You see your streak. The system works with how an ADHD brain processes reward, not against it.

A 25-minute focus block timer with a 5-minute break timer. A task queue where you add what you are working on before you start. **How it works:**

1. Add your tasks to the queue before starting.
2. Select the task you are working on.
3. Start the 25-minute block.
4. If you get distracted, log it without stopping the timer.
5. At the end of the block, mark it complete and earn XP.
6. Take the 5-minute break.
7. Repeat.

At the end of a session, you see how many blocks you completed, what pulled your attention, and how your streak is building. That feedback is what makes the next session easier.

**Why the XP system matters:**

ADHD brains respond to immediate reward. A checked box does not do it. A number going up does. Study Buddy is designed around that specific neurological reality.
