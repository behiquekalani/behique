## POST 3 -- Carousel (Practical How-To)

**GOAL:** Saves + Trust

**HOOK:** "The 7-minute AI security audit anyone can do"

**CONTENT:**

- **Slide 1 (Cover):** "The 7-minute AI security audit. No tech background needed." Clean, professional design.
- **Slide 2:** "Minute 1: List every AI tool you use. ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, Jasper, Grammarly, whatever. Write them all down."
- **Slide 3:** "Minute 2: For each tool, check the permissions. What can it access? Files? Browser? Email? Camera? Turn off anything it doesn't absolutely need."
- **Slide 4:** "Minute 3: Search your computer for files named .env, credentials, or config. Open them. If any contain API keys or passwords in plain text, you have a problem."
- **Slide 5:** "Minute 4: Check your AI tool's privacy settings. Most tools let you opt out of training on your data. Find that toggle and turn it off."
- **Slide 6:** "Minute 5: Check your AI spending limits. If you use any paid AI API, set a monthly cap. Right now. Before a bug runs up your bill."
- **Slide 7:** "Minute 6: Think about what data you paste into AI. Client names? Financials? Strategy docs? Consider using a local AI (like Ollama) for sensitive work."
- **Slide 8:** "Minute 7: Ask yourself, 'If my AI tool got compromised right now, what's the worst thing that could happen?' If the answer scares you, you need better guardrails."
- **Slide 9 (CTA):** "Want the full version? Free AI Safety Checklist in bio. 15 checks, 10 minutes, peace of mind."

**CAPTION:**
You don't need a security degree to protect yourself. You just need 7 minutes and these steps.

Most AI security problems come from one thing: nobody checked the defaults.

AI tools ship with maximum permissions, no spending limits, and training enabled by default. Fixing those three things alone eliminates 80% of the risk.
