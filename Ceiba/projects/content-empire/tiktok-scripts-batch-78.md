## Script 1: How to Write a Product Description That Sells

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Most product descriptions describe the product. The ones that sell describe the life after the product."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "The mistake: features over outcomes"]
Nobody buys a planner. They buy the feeling of having their life under control. Lead with the outcome. Features are proof, not the pitch.

[on-screen: "The three-part structure"]
Line one: the transformation (what changes after they buy). Line two: the mechanism (how it works). Line three: the social proof or specificity (why they should believe you).

[on-screen: "The power of specificity"]
"Saves time" is weak. "Cuts your content planning from three hours to thirty minutes" is a reason to buy. Specifics create credibility. Vague language creates doubt.

[on-screen: "The last line"]
End with the thing they lose by not buying. Not manipulation. Clarity. Help them see the cost of inaction as clearly as the benefit of action.

## Script 3: The Solopreneur Content Stack

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Running a one-person business means your content has to do the work of a full marketing team. Here's the system."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "The problem with doing everything"]
Most solopreneurs either post randomly or burn out trying to be everywhere. The content stack solves both by building a system, not a schedule.

[on-screen: "The three-layer stack"]
Layer one: long-form anchor content (one piece per week). Layer two: social distribution (3-5 repurposed posts per week). Layer three: email to your list (one per week). That's it.

[on-screen: "One to many repurposing"]
One YouTube video or podcast episode becomes: three LinkedIn posts, five tweets, one email, and two short clips. You create once. You distribute everywhere.

[on-screen: "The non-negotiable"]
Every piece of content needs to do one of three things: attract a new audience, build trust with an existing one, or move someone closer to a purchase. If it doesn't do one of these, cut it.

## Script 5: The 5-Step Funnel

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Every dollar made online goes through a funnel. Most people skip steps one and two. That's why nothing converts."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: "Step 1: Awareness"]
Someone sees your content for the first time. They don't know you. They don't trust you yet. Your only job here is to earn the next scroll or click.

[on-screen: "Step 2: Interest"]
They consume more. They follow you. They join your email list. Interest means they're open to learning more, not buying yet.

[on-screen: "Step 3: Desire"]
This is where positioning matters. They see the outcome your product creates and start wanting it for themselves. This is built by consistent, specific, valuable content.

[on-screen: "Step 4: Action"]
The offer. One clear CTA. One product. One page. Remove friction. Make it easy to say yes.

[on-screen: "Step 5: Retention"]
They bought. Now what. This step is where the real money is. Repeat customers convert 60-70% easier than cold traffic.

**Caption:** The funnel is not a trick. It's a map of how trust turns into transactions. Skip steps and the whole thing collapses.
