**Tweet 4 (3-tweet thread)**
Your content library is either a leaky bucket or a compounding asset. Here's how to tell which one you have.

1/3

---

Leaky bucket: every post needs a new idea. Views spike, drop to zero, and you start over. You're on a treadmill you can never leave.

Compounding asset: old posts continue to bring in traffic, leads, and sales months after publication. The work accumulates.

2/3

---

How to shift from leaky to compounding:
- Post about principles, not news
- Answer questions people will have forever
- Build a body of work around one specific topic
- Repurpose when the algorithm serves it again

The goal is a library, not a feed.

3/3

**Tweet 6 (6-tweet thread)**
How I think about content that compounds. A framework for people who don't want to post forever just to stay relevant.

Thread.

1/6

---

There are three types of content:

1. Trend-dependent: only works right now. High reach, low shelf life.
2. Skill-based: teaches a repeatable skill. Medium reach, medium shelf life.
3. Principle-based: addresses fundamental human problems. Lower reach, unlimited shelf life.

You need all three, in that ratio: 20/30/50.

2/6

---

Principle-based content answers questions that humans have been asking forever.

"How do I make more money?"
"How do I find work that matters?"
"How do I stop wasting time?"

These questions don't expire. Content that answers them doesn't expire either.

3/6

---

Skill-based content is your proof of expertise. It shows you've done the thing, not just thought about it.

"Here's my exact process for X."
"Here's what I got wrong the first time."
"Here's the shortcut I wish someone told me."

This builds trust faster than anything else.

4/6

---

Trend-dependent content is your traffic lever. It brings people in.

The mistake: treating it as your whole strategy.

The move: use it to pull people toward your principle and skill content. Let the trend be the door. Make the principle content the room they stay in.

5/6

---

Summary of the compounding content framework:
- 50% principle-based (permanent problems)
- 30% skill-based (proof + process)
- 20% trend-dependent (traffic lever)

Your goal isn't to post more. It's to build a body of work that keeps working after you stop.

6/6

**Tweet 8 (3-tweet thread)**
Content that compounds has one property that most people ignore: it's searchable.

1/3

---

Searchable content answers a question someone is actively typing. It doesn't need a push notification. It doesn't need the algorithm. It surfaces when the person needs it.

That's the difference between interruption content and pull content.

2/3

---

To make your content searchable:
- Title it like a search query
- Solve one specific problem per post
- Use the language your audience actually uses
- Build at least one platform where SEO matters (blog, YouTube, Pinterest)

Posts that answer questions outlive posts that announce opinions.

3/3

---

**Tweet 9 (single)**
Consistency is overrated as a strategy. Compounding is underrated. Posting every day with low intent is just noise. Posting 3x a week with high specificity builds a catalog. Build a catalog.

---

**Tweet 10 (single)**
The best ROI in content: take your most useful thread from 18 months ago, update it with one new insight, and repost it. The compounding already happened. You're just collecting it.
