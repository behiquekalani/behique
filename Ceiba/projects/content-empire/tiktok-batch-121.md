**Topic: The only 3 metrics that matter for a digital product business**

## TikTok 1: The Metrics Lie You Were Sold
**Hook (0-2s):**
You're tracking the wrong numbers. That's why you're working hard and going nowhere.

**Script:**
Everyone wants to grow followers. More views. More reach. But none of that pays the bills.

There are only 3 numbers that actually matter in a digital product business.

Number one: conversion rate. Not how many people saw your page. How many bought. If 1000 people land on your page and 10 buy, that's a 1% conversion rate. The goal is to understand why 990 left.

Number two: average order value. One sale is not equal to another. A $7 sale and a $97 sale take the same amount of effort to market. Stack your offers so each customer is worth more.

Number three: email list growth rate. Social media is rented land. Your email list is the only audience you own. If your list is not growing, your business is not growing. Full stop.

Followers don't pay rent. These three numbers do.

## TikTok 2: Why Your Analytics Are Lying to You
**Hook (0-2s):**
10,000 views and zero sales. That's not a reach problem. That's a metrics problem.

**Script:**
Most creators are addicted to vanity metrics. Views, likes, comments, shares. These feel good. They tell you nothing about your business.

Here's the framework I use instead.

Metric one is conversion rate on your core offer. If you don't know this number, you can't improve your sales page. You're just guessing.

Metric two is traffic source quality. Not all traffic is equal. Someone who found you through a search for "how to build a digital product" converts way differently than someone who found you from a trending sound. Know where your buyers are coming from.

Metric three is customer lifetime value. How much does one customer spend with you over 6 months? If you're only tracking first purchase, you're missing half the picture.

Three numbers. That's it. Everything else is noise.

## TikTok 3: The $500/Month Creator's Metrics Problem
**Hook (0-2s):**
If you're stuck at $500 a month, I can almost guarantee it's because of this.

**Script:**
You're measuring the wrong things.

I've talked to dozens of digital product creators who are stuck. And the pattern is always the same. They know their follower count. They know their engagement rate. They have no idea what their conversion rate is.

Here's what to track instead.

One: email opt-in rate. Out of everyone who lands on your lead magnet page, what percent signs up? Under 30% means something is broken.

Two: sales page conversion. Out of everyone who sees your offer, what percent buys? Under 1% means your positioning is off. Over 3% means you're doing something right.

Three: email to purchase rate. Of every 100 people on your list, how many have bought anything? This tells you if your list is warm or dead.

Know these three numbers. Fix the lowest one first. That's the game.

## TikTok 4: The Only Dashboard a Solopreneur Needs
**Hook (0-2s):**
Stop building complex dashboards. You need three numbers. That's it.

**Script:**
I'm going to give you the only metrics dashboard a solo digital product creator needs.

First column: acquisition. How many new people are finding you per week? Pick one channel. Master it. Track weekly growth, not daily.

Second column: conversion. Of the people who land on your offer, how many buy? This is your most important number. A 1% improvement here doubles revenue faster than doubling traffic.

Third column: retention. How many customers come back for a second product? If this number is zero, you don't have a business. You have a one-time transaction machine.

Acquisition, conversion, retention. Print this on a sticky note. Check it once a week. Make one decision based on what you see.

That's the whole system.

## TikTok 5: The Metric That Predicts Revenue Before It Happens
**Hook (0-2s):**
There's one number that tells you what your revenue will be next month. Most people never track it.

**Script:**
Email list size times conversion rate times average order value equals predictable revenue.

That's the formula. Let me make it real.

Say you have 500 people on your email list. Your conversion rate on a launch email sequence is 2%. Your core product is $47.

500 times 0.02 is 10 sales. 10 times 47 is $470 per launch.

Now you know exactly what to change to hit $1,000. Either grow the list to 1,000. Or raise conversion to 4%. Or raise your price to $97.

Three levers. You can only pull one at a time effectively.

Most creators just create more content and hope. The ones building real businesses know which lever they're pulling and why.
