**Hook (0-3s):** Passive income exists. The version you've been sold is mostly fiction.
**Body (3-45s):**
Let's be specific about what passive income actually means.

It means that at some point in the future, work you do today continues to generate returns without additional effort. Keyword: at some point in the future. Not tomorrow. Not next week.

The version being sold online implies you set something up once and money flows. No maintenance, no updates, no customer questions. That's not how it works.

What actually happens: you build something for months with near-zero income. Then, if you did it right, that thing keeps producing while you build the next thing.

The passive part refers to the income stream, not the work required to create it. The work is very active, very real, and very front-loaded.

If you're okay with that trade, passive income is one of the best deals in business. If you're not, it will disappoint you every time.

**Script 4**
**Title:** Why most passive income attempts fail in month two
**Hook (0-3s):** Most passive income projects die in month two. Here's why.
**Body (3-45s):**
Month one is exciting. You build the product, set up the page, post about it. A few sales come in. You feel like it's working.

Month two is when reality hits. Growth slows. You're posting but the audience isn't compounding yet. The sales plateau. You start questioning whether it's worth it.

Most people quit here.

What they don't know is that this is the exact moment the work starts to accumulate. Content has a compounding effect that doesn't show up for three to six months. You're not failing. You're in the delay before the return.

The fix is to shift your metric. Stop measuring revenue in month two. Measure output. Number of posts. Email list growth. Product page views. These are leading indicators.

Revenue is a lagging indicator. If you're measuring the wrong thing, you'll quit at the worst time.
