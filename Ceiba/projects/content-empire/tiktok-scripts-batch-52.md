**Hook (0-3s):** The simplest digital product takes 2 hours to make and can sell forever.

**Script:**
The simplest digital product takes 2 hours to make and can sell forever.

It is a PDF guide on something you already know.

You do not need to be an expert. You need to know more than the person you are helping. If you know how to organize a budget, write the guide. If you know how to set up a home gym for cheap, write the guide. If you know how to get freelance clients on LinkedIn, write the guide.

Format it in Canva. Export as PDF. Upload to Gumroad. Set the price at $7.

Then tell 10 people about it.

The reason most people never make a digital product is they think it needs to be perfect before they sell it. It does not. It needs to solve one problem for one person.

Start there. Refine after.

The first $7 you make from something you made is a different feeling from any paycheck you have ever received.

## Script 2: Why Digital Products Beat Physical Products Every Time
**Hook (0-3s):** You make one digital product and it sells a thousand times. That math is hard to beat.

**Script:**
You make one digital product and it sells a thousand times. That math is hard to beat.

With physical products you have inventory, shipping, returns, storage. With digital products, you have none of that.

Someone buys your PDF at 3am while you are asleep. The file delivers automatically. You wake up with money in your account. Nothing broke. Nothing shipped. Nobody needed you awake.

The margins are also completely different. A $20 ebook costs you nothing to deliver. A $20 physical item might net you $4 after costs.

And you can update a digital product any time. Customers get better value. You get to charge more for the updated version.

For someone starting with limited time and money, digital products are the clearest path to building income that does not require you to show up for every dollar.

The barrier to entry is lower than it has ever been. The tools to make and sell them are free or near-free.

## Script 3: You Already Have a Digital Product Idea
**Hook (0-3s):** The question you just answered in a group chat is a digital product idea.

**Script:**
The question you just answered in a group chat is a digital product idea.

Think about the last time someone asked you for advice. On anything. Career, budgeting, fitness, software, cooking, side hustles. Whatever it was, you had an answer. That answer, structured and formatted, is a digital product.

Here is how to validate it before building.

Post a question on social media: "Does anyone struggle with X?" If you get responses, there is demand. Then tell them you are building a guide on it and ask if they would pay $10 for it. If even five people say yes, build it.

This is not about finding a niche you are passionate about. It is about finding a problem you can solve that other people have too.

Most people never start because they think their knowledge is too basic to sell. The people who need your guide are not where you are. They are where you were six months ago.

Sell the journey, not just the destination.

## Script 4: Gumroad vs. Shopify for Your First Digital Product
**Hook (0-3s):** Do not set up Shopify for your first digital product. Here is why.

**Script:**
Do not set up Shopify for your first digital product. Here is why.

Shopify is a full store platform. You need themes, apps, payment setup, domain, and hours of configuration before you make a single sale.

Gumroad takes 20 minutes. You create an account, upload your file, set a price, and get a link. That is your store. Share the link.

For a first product, your goal is not to have the best storefront. Your goal is to validate that people will pay for your thing. You need sales, not polish.

Once you have proof that people buy, you invest in the infrastructure. Not before.

I see people spend 3 weeks building a Shopify store and never actually launch. I also see people make their first $500 on a Gumroad page they built in an afternoon.

Start with the friction-free option. Upgrade when you have revenue to justify it.

The product matters more than the platform.

## Script 5: Price Your First Product Higher Than You Think You Should
**Hook (0-3s):** You are underpricing your digital product. Here is what that costs you.

**Script:**
You are underpricing your digital product. Here is what that costs you.

Most first-time creators price at $5 or $7 because they are scared of rejection. But low prices do not reduce rejection. They just mean you need 200 sales instead of 20 to hit your goal.

At $7, you need 143 sales to make $1000.
At $47, you need 22 sales.

Twenty-two people is a small audience. One hundred and forty-three is a grind.

Also, low prices signal low value. People expect $7 products to be surface-level. When you charge $37 or $47, buyers expect more and often get more value from the same content because they paid attention to it.

Price based on the outcome you are delivering, not the time it took you to create it.

If your guide saves someone 10 hours of research or helps them make their first $500, it is not a $7 product.
