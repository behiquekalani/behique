## Threads

**6. How to turn one idea into 30 pieces of content**

1/ One solid idea can generate 30+ pieces of content without being repetitive. Here's how to break it down:

2/ Start with the core thesis. Write a 1000-word essay. That's the anchor.

3/ From the essay: extract 5 standalone tweets (one per key point). Pull 3 quotes that stand alone. Rewrite the intro as a LinkedIn hook. Summarize the conclusion as a newsletter section.

4/ Record yourself explaining it. That's 3 short-form video scripts. Pull the most counterintuitive point as a hook and build a thread around just that.

5/ One idea. One essay. One month of content. The output isn't the bottleneck. The thinking is.

**7. Why solopreneurs stall at $1K/month**

1/ A lot of solopreneurs hit $1K/month and stop growing. It's not because the market dried up. It's because the system that got them to $1K can't get them to $5K.

2/ To get to $1K: you needed one thing that worked, sold to people you already knew, priced it low enough to remove risk.

3/ To get to $5K: you need a repeatable acquisition system, a product that stands without personal selling, and pricing that reflects real value.

4/ The shift isn't more effort. It's different structure. The $1K business is built on hustle. The $5K business is built on systems.

---

**8. On building an audience before a product**

1/ "Build an audience first" is good advice that's often applied wrong.

2/ Most people interpret it as: post content, get followers, then figure out what to sell them. That's backwards.

3/ Build an audience around a specific problem you understand deeply. The content is proof you understand it. The product is the solution.

4/ The audience doesn't come before the product idea. They develop in parallel. Your content clarifies your positioning. Your positioning shapes the product.

---

**9. The honest cost of building in public**

1/ Building in public has real costs that nobody talks about.

2/ You lose the ability to pivot quietly. Every public commitment creates social pressure. When you change direction, you have to explain it.

3/ You also attract noise: advice you didn't ask for, comparisons to others, people projecting their own fears onto your decisions.

4/ Whether it's worth it depends on your personality. For some people, public accountability is fuel. For others, it's distraction. Know which one you are before you start.

---

**10. What makes a digital product actually sell**

1/ Most digital products fail not because of quality but because of positioning.

2/ The question isn't "is this good?" The question is "does the title make someone feel like this was made specifically for their exact problem?"

3/ "How to Make Money Online" competes with everything. "How I Made $3K My First Month Selling Notion Templates" competes with almost nothing.

4/ Specificity in positioning is the difference between a product that needs to be pushed and one that gets found on its own.
