## Post 2 — Step 1: Clear the Inbox

**Hook:** The weekly review starts with one job: clear the inbox.

**Slide 1:** The weekly review starts with one job: clear the inbox.

**Slide 2:** Everything that came in during the week is sitting in the inbox. Links, ideas, tasks, observations, screenshots you moved over manually.

**Slide 3:** Your job: process each item. Is it actionable? Route it to a project. Is it reference? Move it to resources. Is it an idea? Move it to idea capture. Is it noise? Delete it.

**Slide 4:** You are not reading deeply or creating anything. You are making routing decisions. Each item gets five seconds.

**Slide 5:** When the inbox is empty, step 1 is done. The whole system now reflects what's real.

**Slide 6:** Save this. Post 3 covers project updates. Follow for the rest.

**Caption:**
Clearing the inbox is not a glamorous task. But it is the most important part of the weekly review.

Everything you captured during the week is sitting there waiting for a decision. Links you saved. Ideas that came up. Tasks you noted down. If you don't process them, they sit in the inbox and the inbox stops being trusted. When the inbox is not trusted, capture breaks down.

The processing rule is strict: every item gets a decision immediately. You don't go back. You don't skip items for later. You make the routing call and move on.

Noise gets deleted. Actionable items go to tasks or projects. Reference material goes to resources. Ideas go to idea capture. The inbox goes to zero.

This sounds tedious. The first time it is. After a few weeks it takes under 10 minutes because you haven't let the pile grow too large. The system rewards consistency.

## Post 3 — Step 2: Update Every Active Project

**Hook:** After the inbox: update every project that moved this week.

**Slide 1:** After the inbox: update every project that moved this week.

**Slide 2:** Open your project list. Scan every item with status Active.

**Slide 3:** For each one: what happened this week? What's the current status? What's the next concrete action?

**Slide 4:** If a project moved forward, note it. If it didn't move, decide why. Was it paused on purpose? Did it get deprioritized? Or did it just quietly stall?

**Slide 5:** Quietly stalled projects are the most important to catch. Name what happened. Make a decision. Restart with a clear next step or move it to Paused.

**Slide 6:** Follow @behikeai. Post 4 covers planning the week ahead.

**Caption:**
The project update step is where you hold yourself accountable without self-criticism.

Every project that's listed as Active should have moved in some direction this week. If it did, capture the progress and set the next action. If it didn't, that's information. Not a failure. Information.

Maybe the project was deprioritized by something more urgent. Maybe the next step was unclear. Maybe life happened. All of those are valid. The job is to name what happened and decide what's next.

The worst outcome is a project that quietly stalls without a decision. Those projects don't get done. They drain mental energy every time you see them. The weekly review forces a decision: either you're working on this or you're not.

Active means moving. If something isn't moving, change its status. That honesty is what keeps the system trustworthy.

## Post 4 — Step 3: Plan the Week Ahead

**Hook:** The last step of the review is the first step of the next week.

**Slide 1:** The last step of the review is the first step of the next week.

**Slide 2:** After clearing the inbox and updating projects, you look forward. What are the three most important outcomes for next week?

**Slide 3:** Not a task list. Three outcomes. The three things that, if they happen, will make the week a success.

**Slide 4:** Write them down in your dashboard. They go at the top. Every morning you look at them before you open anything else.

**Slide 5:** This is the bridge between maintenance and direction. The review keeps the system current. The three outcomes give the week a target.

**Slide 6:** The full weekly review template is inside the Solopreneur Second Brain. Link in bio.

**Caption:**
Most weekly reviews end with a cleaned-up system and no direction. You know what happened last week. You've updated what's in flight. But you haven't decided what matters next week.

The three outcomes step fixes this. Before you close the review, you pick three things. Not tasks. Outcomes. What does a successful week look like?

Maybe it's shipping a product. Maybe it's finishing a draft. Maybe it's having one sales conversation. Three concrete things that give the week shape.

These go at the top of your dashboard. Every morning you see them before you see anything else. They're the filter for how you spend your attention.

When you combine the review with the three outcomes, you get something rare: a system that is both current and directed. You know where you stand and where you're going.

That's the ritual. Build it once. Run it every week.
