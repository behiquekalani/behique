## Short 8: How to Create a Month of Content in One Day

**Hook (0-3s):**
What if you only had to think about content once a month? Here's the system that makes that possible.

**Body (4-50s):**
The reason content creation feels exhausting is because most people do it in fragments.
A post here, an idea there, scrambling to publish something today.

Batching solves this. Here's how the system works:

One day a month, set aside six hours.
Hour one: define four topics you want to cover this month. One per week.
Hours two through five: create all the content for each topic in one sitting.
One long-form piece per topic, then break it into shorter pieces for each platform.
Hour six: schedule everything.

When you create in fragments you spend 30% of your time just deciding what to create.
When you batch, all decisions happen in hour one and you execute for the rest of the day.

You also think better about each topic when you're fully in it rather than switching contexts every day.

The first time you do this it takes longer.
By the third month it's a six-hour day and a free rest of the month.

**Body (4-50s):**

Connect the insight to an action, a product, or a next step.
Not a hard sell. A natural direction.
"If you want to go deeper on this, I have a resource that walks through the whole system."

This three-part structure works for posts, emails, short videos, and long-form content.

## Short 13: The System Behind Every Productive Solopreneur

**Hook (0-3s):**
Every solopreneur who seems impossibly productive has the same thing running in the background. It's not a tool. It's a decision structure.

**Body (4-50s):**
Every morning they answer three questions before they open anything:
What is the one outcome that makes today a win?
What is the biggest obstacle between me and that outcome?
What is the first concrete action I need to take to clear that obstacle?

Everything else that comes in during the day gets evaluated against question one.
Does this move me toward today's win or away from it?

If away from it, it waits.
If toward it, it gets done.

This decision structure sounds simple.
It is simple.
But it removes the constant low-level anxiety of wondering if you're working on the right thing.
You already decided what the right thing is. Now you just execute.

The second component is a weekly review.
Every Friday, fifteen minutes.
What worked this week, what didn't, what's the most important outcome for next week.
That review is what keeps the decision structure from drifting.

Two practices. Total time: twenty minutes a day.
That's the system behind most productive people you admire.
