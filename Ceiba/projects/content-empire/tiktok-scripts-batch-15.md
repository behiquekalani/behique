### Script 1 — The Jobs AI Is Already Replacing Right Now

**Hook (0-3s):** These are not future predictions. These are things happening right now.

**Setup (3-10s):** AI is not replacing jobs in 10 years. It's replacing specific tasks today. And you need to know which ones.

**Content (10-55s):**
Data entry — Already automated. Any company still paying people to copy-paste data between systems is behind.
Basic customer support — Chatbots handle tier-one support at most tech companies. The human escalation tier is still needed. The frontline tier is not.
Basic copywriting — Not creative writing. Generic product descriptions, formulaic ad copy, template-based content. Gone.
Stock photography — AI image generation is faster, cheaper, and infinitely scalable. Generalist stock photographers are feeling it.
Basic translation — Not nuanced translation. Simple document translation. Tools like DeepL and GPT handle this at near-human quality.

What these have in common: they are predictable, repetitive, and don't require judgment.
The jobs being protected: the ones that require relationships, trust, context, and creative decision-making.

**CTA (55-60s):** Part 2 covers what to build instead. Follow.

### Script 2 — What AI Can't Replace (Protect Yourself With This)

**Hook (0-3s):** The people AI cannot replace have one thing in common.

**Setup (3-10s):** They make judgment calls. They build trust. They operate in ambiguity.

**Content (10-55s):**
What AI is actually bad at:
Building human relationships. Clients still want to feel heard by a person.
Making calls under genuine uncertainty. "Probably" is not enough when stakes are high.
Navigating politics, nuance, and cultural context in real organizations.
Creative direction. Not execution. Direction. Choosing what to make and why.
Integrating AI into other systems. The person who connects the tools is not replaced by the tools.

What this means for you:
The skill to build is meta-skill. The ability to work with AI tools, direct them, audit their output, and apply human judgment on top.
Not "can I be replaced by AI."
But "am I the person who uses AI to do what five people used to do."

If you are the person who deploys the tools, you are not at risk. You are the leverage.

This is the actual career strategy right now.

**CTA (55-60s):** Follow. The next video covers exactly which skills to build to stay ahead.

**On-screen text:** "What AI can't replace" / "Judgment / Relationships / Direction / Integration" / "Use the tools, don't be replaced by them"

**Trending sound direction:** Calm but forward-moving, slight uplift, no lyrics, focused energy

**Setup (3-10s):** Three moves that make you nearly impossible to automate.

**Content (10-55s):**

Don't just use AI. Be the person at your job, your school, or in your niche who knows which tool to use for what. That person has leverage others don't.

Generalist knowledge is getting commoditized fast. Deep, specific expertise in one field combined with AI fluency is a defensible position.

The formula: deep domain knowledge plus AI fluency plus proof of outcomes.

That stack is not replaceable because it requires lived experience to build.

### Script 4 — The One Skill That Gets More Valuable as AI Gets Stronger

**Hook (0-3s):** One skill becomes more valuable every single time AI gets better. Most people are not building it.

**Setup (3-10s):** The skill is prompting. But not just prompting. Thinking in systems.

**Content (10-55s):**
Here's what I mean.
As AI tools get more powerful, the bottleneck shifts.
The bottleneck used to be execution. Doing the thing.
Now the bottleneck is knowing what to ask for, how to evaluate the output, and how to string multiple AI tools together into a workflow.

That's systems thinking applied to AI.

What it looks like in practice:
You don't just use ChatGPT. You build a chain. You write, AI refines, a second tool formats it, a third tool schedules it.
You don't just use one model. You know which model is best for which task.
You test and iterate your prompts like a developer tests code.

This is not technical. It's structural. It requires you to think about what a system is supposed to do before you build it.

The person who thinks in systems and uses AI to execute will outproduce ten people who just "use AI."

**CTA (55-60s):** Follow. I post the actual workflows I use every week.

**On-screen text:** "The one skill AI makes MORE valuable" / "Systems thinking + AI fluency" / "Know what to ask, evaluate, chain"

**Trending sound direction:** Clean and intellectual, calm background music, slightly analytical energy

### Script 5 — From Job at Risk to Freelancer Using AI in 60 Days

**Hook (0-3s):** Your job might be at risk. Here's a 60-day plan to build something that isn't.

**Setup (3-10s):** Not a side hustle. A real income stream you control.

**Content (10-55s):**
Days 1-10 — Pick your skill.
What do you already know how to do. Writing, research, design, video, code, teaching. Pick the one that overlaps most with what AI can help you do faster.

Days 11-20 — Learn the AI tool stack for that skill.
If you write: ChatGPT, Claude, Grammarly.
If you design: Midjourney, Canva AI, Adobe Firefly.
If you do video: CapCut AI, Runway, ElevenLabs.

Days 21-30 — Build the offer.
Package your skill plus the AI stack into a service. "I create 10 short-form videos a month using AI. Here's the quality. Here's the price."

Days 31-45 — Get your first three clients.
LinkedIn outreach. Local Facebook Groups. Cold email. One message, one offer. Don't wait for perfection.

Days 46-60 — Productize it.
Turn the process into a template. Start selling the template to other freelancers.

60 days from job risk to owning your income.

**CTA (55-60s):** Which day are you starting. Comment it.

**On-screen text:** "60-day plan from job risk to freelancer" / "Days 1-60 breakdown" / "Skill + AI + offer = income you own"

**Trending sound direction:** Momentum-building, slightly cinematic, progressive energy, no lyrics
