## How It Works Technically

### Getting Your Link

1. Accept the Gumroad affiliate invitation
2. Log into Gumroad and go to Affiliates
3. Find the Behike store in your affiliates dashboard
4. Each product has its own affiliate link. Use the specific product link when promoting a specific product, or the store link for general promotion.

Your link looks like this:
`https://behikeai.gumroad.com/l/[product-slug]?affiliate=[your-id]`

### Tracking

Gumroad tracks clicks and purchases via browser cookie. The 60-day window starts from the click. If someone buys anything in the store within 60 days of clicking your link, you get credited.

One affiliate account. All products covered.

### Getting Paid

Gumroad pays affiliates via PayPal or direct bank transfer (US accounts). International affiliates use PayPal. Payout threshold is $10. You can request payout at any time once you hit the threshold. Gumroad processes payouts within 3-5 business days.

No invoicing required on your end. Gumroad handles the tax documentation (1099-K for US affiliates who exceed IRS thresholds).

## Content Guidelines

### What You Can Say

- Accurate descriptions of what's in the product
- Your honest opinion after purchasing or reviewing it
- Specific features listed in the product description
- The price (current listed price)
- That you earn a commission ("this is an affiliate link" or "I may earn a commission")

### What You Cannot Say

- Claims you cannot verify ("this will make you $X/month")
- Fake urgency ("only 3 copies left" when there is no such limit)
- Fake social proof ("thousands of students" if you don't know the actual number)
- Misleading comparisons to competitors without factual basis
- Anything that implies Behike guarantees specific results

**FTC disclosure is required.** Every post, email, or video that contains your affiliate link must include a clear disclosure that it is an affiliate link. "This is an affiliate link" or "I earn a commission if you buy" is sufficient. Put it near the link, not buried at the end.

If you're unsure whether something is okay to say, don't say it. Ask first.

## Top Performing Content Formats

Based on what converts for digital products in this category:

**1. Tutorial posts that reference the product naturally**
A post teaching a concept that the product covers in depth. End with "I learned this from [product name], here's the link if you want the full breakdown." Converts because the value is demonstrated before the ask.

**2. "What I'm using" or "tools in my stack" content**
A list of tools or resources you actually use. One of the items is a Behike product. Short description. Affiliate link. No pressure. Works because it's framed as recommendation, not promotion.

**3. Before/after or problem/solution posts**
The problem the product solves, stated plainly. The result after using it. The product as the bridge. Works best when the before/after is credible and specific.

**4. DM replies and comments**
When someone asks a question you know one of the products answers, reply with a short answer and offer the product as the deeper resource. These are the highest-conversion touchpoints because the person is already in need.

**5. Email newsletter mentions**
One-sentence mention in a longer email with full context around it. Not a dedicated promo email. A natural aside. Newsletter subscribers have higher intent and trust than cold social followers.

**Lowest converting:** Pure promotional posts with no value attached. "Check out this product" with a link gets ignored. Lead with something useful.

## FAQ

**Q: Do I need to buy the products to promote them?**
You don't have to, but you'll be more effective if you actually use what you're promoting. The best affiliate content comes from genuine experience. If you want a review copy, reach out and ask.

**Q: Is there a minimum traffic or follower requirement?**
No hard minimum. Quality of audience matters more than size. If your audience is relevant (solopreneurs, AI builders, creators, freelancers), a small engaged following outperforms a large irrelevant one.

**Q: Can I run paid ads to my affiliate links?**
No. Do not run paid ads directly to Behike affiliate links without prior written approval. Google and Meta ads policies around affiliate links are complicated, and unapproved campaigns can get the store's ad account flagged.

**Q: What if someone returns a product?**
Gumroad has a 30-day refund window on digital products. If a buyer you referred requests a refund, your commission is reversed. Refund rates on the current catalog are low, but this is the policy.

**Q: Can I promote on multiple platforms?**
Yes. Use the same affiliate link everywhere. There is no platform restriction. Instagram, YouTube, TikTok, email, newsletters, forums, Discord. If your audience is there and the content is honest, go for it.

**Q: When do I get paid?**
Once your balance hits $10, you can request payout from your Gumroad dashboard. It processes within 3-5 business days via PayPal or bank transfer.

**Q: Can I create my own landing page or funnel?**
Yes, but the product descriptions and copy must be accurate. You can write your own intro, tell your own story, add your own context. You cannot fabricate testimonials, invent features, or make income claims you can't support.

**Q: What if I want to promote a product bundle?**
The Builder's Bundle (Solopreneur OS + Behike Method v2, $29.99) is available in the store. Commission applies at 30% of the bundle price ($9.00). Promote it the same way as individual products.

**Q: Is this exclusive? Can I promote competing products?**
Not exclusive. Promote whatever makes sense for your audience. We only ask that Behike content is represented accurately.

**Q: What if I have a large audience and want custom content or a dedicated campaign?**
Email behikeai@gmail.com with "AFFILIATE COLLAB" in the subject. For affiliates generating meaningful volume, custom content, dedicated promo assets, and higher commission tiers are worth a conversation.
