# Twitter EN — Batch 242
## Topic: The 80/20 of content that actually drives sales

---

**Tweet 1**
80% of your content builds trust. 20% drives sales. Most creators get this backwards. They either sell too hard or never sell at all. The ratio is the strategy.

---

**Tweet 2**
Content that drives sales has one job: make the reader feel the cost of not acting. Not hype. Not pressure. Just clarity about what they lose by staying where they are.

---

**Tweet 3**
The posts that move product share a problem so precisely that readers think you wrote it about them. Specificity is the only sales mechanism that doesn't feel like selling.

---

**Tweet 4**
Four content types that convert: case studies, before/after, frameworks with gaps, and honest takes on what didn't work. Everything else is brand building, not pipeline.

---

**Tweet 5**
You don't need more content. You need to identify the 3-5 posts already in your archive that drove the most action, and write 10 variations of each. That's the 80/20 applied.
