## Tweet 752
**Type:** Thread opener
Getting your first testimonial without being awkward about it. (Thread)

1/ The chicken-and-egg problem: you need social proof to sell, you need sales to get social proof. The way to break the loop is to give your product to 3 to 5 people at a steep discount or free in exchange for honest feedback.

2/ Not a review. Not a rating. Feedback. Ask two questions: what was the most valuable part? And what did you accomplish or realize because of it? Most people will answer in a way that functions as a testimonial.

3/ Then ask: would you be okay if I shared this? Ninety percent say yes. The ones who say no have told you something useful about the product.

4/ The testimonials that convert: specific results with a number. "I raised my rate from $500 to $1,200 using the script in section 3." Not "really great, highly recommend." Specific beats general every time.

5/ If someone gives you a vague testimonial, ask a follow-up: can you tell me more about what specifically changed for you? Most people will give you the specificity if you ask.

## Tweet 755
**Type:** Thread opener
The anatomy of a hook that actually makes people stop. (Thread)

1/ A hook does not need to sound impressive. It needs to create a gap. The reader's brain recognizes there is something it does not know, and the only way to close the gap is to keep reading.

2/ Specificity creates gaps. "How to grow a newsletter" does nothing. "How a creator with 300 subscribers made $4,200 from one email" creates an immediate question: how? Specific numbers, results, and timeframes signal a real answer exists.

3/ Counterintuition creates gaps. When something violates what the reader already thinks is true, their brain needs to resolve it. "The worst time to launch a product is when you are ready." That breaks a model. The brain must continue.

4/ Stakes create gaps. "Most creators fix the wrong thing when their content stops growing." What are they fixing? What am I fixing? Urgency without panic is the tone.

5/ Test every hook with one question: would a stranger stop scrolling for this? If you cannot say yes with confidence, rewrite it before you post anything else.
