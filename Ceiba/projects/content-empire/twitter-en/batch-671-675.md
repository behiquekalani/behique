## Tweet 673
**Type:** Thread opener

The value-first selling framework that removes resistance before you even make the offer. (Thread)

1/ The best salespeople are demonstrators, not persuaders. They show so clearly what the outcome looks like that the buyer arrives at the purchase decision on their own. That is the whole framework.

2/ Your content is the demonstration. Every educational post and newsletter issue proves that you understand the problem you claim to solve. Sixty days of useful content builds more credibility than any sales copy.

3/ The offer confirms what the content already promised. You are not introducing a new idea. You are giving someone who already trusts your thinking a way to access more of it. Friction disappears.

4/ The sequence is non-negotiable. Publish value first. Build trust over time. Then make specific offers to specific people at specific moments. This order cannot be reversed.

5/ Value-first does not mean never asking for money. Once you have demonstrated value, asking for the sale is completing an honest exchange. The buyer feels good about it because they already know what they are getting.

People buy from creators they trust. Trust is built before the pitch, not during it.
