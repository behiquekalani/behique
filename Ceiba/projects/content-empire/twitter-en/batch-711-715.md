## Tweet 713
**Type:** Thread opener
The solopreneur day that actually works does not look like a corporate schedule.

Here is the sequence I use:

1/ Morning: creation. Deep work. Writing, building, recording. This block is protected. No email. No social. No exceptions until it is done.

2/ Midday: communication. Replies, client work, calls. This is reactive time by design. It does not bleed into the morning.

3/ Afternoon: distribution. Schedule content, update systems, handle logistics. Tasks that need execution, not creativity.

4/ Evening: 10-minute review. What moved today? What did not? One adjustment for tomorrow.

5/ The sequence matters more than the clock time. Guard the morning. Everything else follows.
