## Tweet 892
Thread: How to build a product that actually sells

1/ Most products don't fail because of bad information. They fail because of bad packaging.

2/ Packaging answers three questions immediately: What do I get? How does my life change? Why should I trust you?

3/ If your product page takes more than 30 seconds to answer those three questions, you have a packaging problem.

4/ People don't buy information. They buy a future version of themselves.

5/ "Learn email marketing" = information. "Build a 1,000-person list in 90 days" = transformation. Same content. Different pull.

6/ Specificity sells. Vague promises attract no one. Precise promises attract the exact right person.

7/ Build the transformation frame first. Then write the product around it.

## Tweet 895
Thread: What five years of consistent building actually looks like

1/ One year doesn't change your life. Five years does. Most people plan in one-year windows and quit after three months.

2/ Every product you build this year is either generating or not generating in two years. Seeds take time.

3/ If you had started a newsletter five years ago at 50 issues/year, you'd have 250 pieces building your authority right now.

4/ That library doesn't just accumulate. It compounds. Each new piece builds on the credibility of everything before it.

5/ The five-year thought experiment: project your current strategy forward honestly. Is the destination worth the path?

6/ Most people have the right strategy. They stop executing too early.

7/ Stay in the game long enough to collect the patience premium. It pays better than any shortcut.
