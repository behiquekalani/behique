## Tweet 647
**Type:** Thread opener

The content batching system that cut my creation time in half. (Thread)

1/ Most creators create daily. That means daily context-switching, daily blank pages, and daily decisions about what to post. It is the least efficient way to produce consistent content.

2/ Batching means picking one day per week and producing everything for the week in that single session. No other work happens on content day. Just creation, start to finish.

3/ The key to making it work: a 30-topic backlog that you maintain separately. When you sit down to create, you are never staring at a blank page. You are choosing from a pre-built list.

4/ Write in categories, not chronological order. All short-form first because the cognitive load is low. Then newsletters. Then longer pieces. Stay in the same mode for as long as possible.

5/ Schedule everything in the same session. When you stand up from content day, the next 7 days of posts are queued. You do not think about content again until next week.

Volume is not about working more. It is about working in the right order.

## Tweet 650
**Type:** Thread opener

Why I stopped trying to grow my audience and started trying to deepen the one I had. (Thread)

1/ Every creator is told the same thing: grow grow grow. More followers, more reach, more impressions. The growth obsession is real and it is mostly a distraction from building a business.

2/ The math that changed my thinking: an engaged list of 500 people who trust you will outspend an algorithm-dependent following of 50,000 by a significant margin. Trust converts. Reach does not.

3/ Deepening means creating content that goes further, not content that spreads wider. Longer form. More specific. More honest. This shrinks your general reach and grows your actual audience.

4/ Depth signals are different from reach signals. Not likes, but replies. Not impressions, but people who reference your content in their own work or messages. That is real influence.

5/ Revenue from 500 true fans is more sustainable than chasing the algorithm for temporary reach spikes that convert to nothing.

Build deep before you build wide. The depth is what makes the width worth something.
