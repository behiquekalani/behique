# Twitter Batch 1291

---

**Tweet 1**
A personal story is not about you.

It's a container for an insight the reader needs.

The reader should finish your story thinking about their own situation. Not yours.

---

**Tweet 2**
The personal story structure that works:

Setup. Mistake or struggle. Insight. Application.

Four parts. Keep it under 300 words. The story is the vehicle. Not the destination.

---

**Tweet 3**
Extraordinary events make poor teaching stories.

"I sold my company for $50M" is inspiring but inaccessible.

"I almost quit my newsletter after 18 months and here's what stopped me" is relatable and actionable.

Use ordinary struggles with specific insights.

---

**Tweet 4**
The reader sees themselves in your struggles, not your victories.

Share both. Lead with the struggle.

---

**Tweet 5**
One personal story per week builds more trust than seven advice posts.

Not because stories are better than advice.

Because stories prove you lived the advice.
