# Twitter Batch 1329

---

**Tweet 1**
The personal brand that resonates has a clear emotional signature.

You know how it feels to read before you finish the first sentence.

That feeling does not come from branding exercises.

It comes from writing authentically about what you actually believe. Repeatedly.

---

**Tweet 2**
Three beliefs that most people in your niche avoid saying publicly.

Those three beliefs are the foundation of your positioning.

Write them down. Post them this week.

See who agrees. See who pushes back.

Both responses are useful.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
The "value, value, value, then sell" format is dead.

Readers learned the rhythm and started skipping the end.

Better: make the content itself the demonstration.

If you teach writing, write so well people think "I want to write like that."

The product is already being sold.

---

**Tweet 4**
Specific audiences convert better than large audiences.

1,000 subscribers who chose you because your content described their exact situation outperform 20,000 general followers at 10x the rate.

Quality of the relationship over quantity of the reach.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
Why your email open rates are low (it's probably not your subject lines):

1. Your list has wrong-fit subscribers
2. Your content is too generic for the people on it
3. You email too infrequently and they forgot who you are
4. You email too frequently with low-value content

Subject lines are the last thing to fix. Start with list quality.
