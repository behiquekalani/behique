# Twitter Batch 1336

---

**Tweet 1**
The content strategy that compounds:

Year 1: build trust with specific, useful content.
Year 2: the trust becomes a product business.
Year 3: the product business becomes a distribution platform.

Most people quit in month 4.

---

**Tweet 2**
There is a version of "working on your business" that is actually avoidance.

Redesigning your website. Switching email platforms. Renaming your offer.

All legitimate tasks. None of them are the thing that actually moves revenue.

The revenue thing is always: make more people aware of a product that solves their problem.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
The idea that you need 10,000 followers before you can sell anything is a myth.

You need 100 people who deeply understand what you do.

Start there. Sell there. Grow from there.

Sequence matters more than numbers.

---

**Tweet 4**
Every post you write either builds trust, demonstrates expertise, or drives traffic to your offer.

If it does none of those three things, it is a cost, not an investment.

Audit your content calendar with that filter.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
The difference between a creator who grows and one who stalls:

Growing: writes what their audience needs to believe to become a buyer
Stalling: writes what their audience already believes and already knows

Your content job is to shift a perspective, not confirm one.

Shift, don't confirm.
