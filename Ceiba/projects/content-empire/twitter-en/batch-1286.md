# Twitter Batch 1286

---

**Tweet 1**
A good sales page is not manipulation.

It's a clear explanation of what you built, who it's for, what it does, and why the price is fair for the outcome.

If you built something real, describing it clearly is service. Not sleaze.

---

**Tweet 2**
Start your sales page with the problem.

Not the product.

The specific problem your reader would describe to a friend at 11pm. Name that. Then show them the way out.

---

**Tweet 3**
"This course changed my life" = worthless testimonial.

"I made $2,100 in the first week after using the pricing framework in module 3" = worth something.

Specificity in social proof is everything. Ask your buyers better questions.

---

**Tweet 4**
Sales pages fail in the first paragraph.

Three generic opening sentences before any value.

Write your best sentence first. Delete the warm-up.

---

**Tweet 5**
Countdown timers that aren't real. Fake scarcity. All-caps benefits.

These make people trust you less.

Write like you're explaining it to a smart friend who's skeptical. That's the whole framework.
