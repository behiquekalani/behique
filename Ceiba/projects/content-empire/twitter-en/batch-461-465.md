## Thread 464 — Why Most Online Businesses Die Before Year One

**Tweet 1 (Hook)**
90% of online businesses fail.
Not because the product was bad.
Not because the market was wrong.
Because the founder ran out of energy before the business ran out of potential.

**Tweet 2**
The problem is not strategy.
There is more free strategy online than any human can implement.
The problem is psychology.

**Tweet 3**
Most founders quit during the "valley of despair."
The phase between launch and traction where you are doing all the work and seeing almost no results.
This valley is where businesses are born or buried.

**Tweet 4**
What the valley looks like:
- You post. Nobody engages.
- You launch. Three people buy.
- You send emails. 30% open rate, 1% click rate.
- You work 10 hours and earn $0.

This is normal. This is the price of entry.

**Tweet 5**
The founders who survive have a different internal metric.
They measure output, not outcome.
"Did I execute the system today?" is the question.
Not "did I make money today?"

**Tweet 6**
You cannot control how many people buy.
You can control whether you published, whether you emailed, whether you improved the product.
Measure what you control.

**Tweet 7**
The other killer: shiny object syndrome.
You launch a product. It gets 3 sales.
You pivot to a new niche, new platform, new product.
You repeat this loop forever and wonder why nothing works.

**Tweet 8**
Real traction comes from repetition, not reinvention.
The same message, the same product, the same platform, for long enough to build compound interest.
Six months minimum before you evaluate whether to pivot.

**Tweet 9**
The businesses that survive year one are not smarter.
They are more stubborn.
They made a decision to outlast the discomfort.
That decision is available to everyone. Most do not make it.

**Tweet 10**
Specific advice:
Set a 90-day commitment to one product on one platform.
Do not change the product. Do not change the platform.
Optimize the message. Improve the offer. Stay the course.

**Tweet 11 (Close)**
The valley of despair is not a sign that you are failing.
It is a filter.
Most people turn back.
The ones who keep walking find what everyone else gave up looking for.
Keep walking.
