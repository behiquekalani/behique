# Twitter Batch 1331

---

**Tweet 1**
Most creators do not have a marketing problem.

They have a clarity problem.

Who is this for? What problem does it solve? Why does this person care today?

Answer those three questions and the marketing writes itself.

---

**Tweet 2**
The reason your first product is hard to sell:

You are describing what it is.

The reason someone buys: they see who they become after using it.

Rewrite your product description to start with the transformation. Everything else supports that.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
The best content I have ever read was not optimized for reach.

It was written by someone who had something real to say to a specific kind of person and did not care if anyone else understood it.

That is the content that spreads. Ironically.

---

**Tweet 4**
Digital products are not a get-rich-quick play.

They are a get-paid-repeatedly-for-work-you-do-once play.

The math is different. The timeline is different. The mindset has to match.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
The creator who quit too early all say the same things in hindsight:

"I stopped 2 months before it started working."
"I switched niches right when I was getting traction."
"I thought 3 months was enough time to judge."

The compounding in this game is real. But it takes 12 months minimum to see it.
