# Twitter Batch 1323

**Tweet 1**
Building in public only works if you are actually being honest.

Polished wins that look accidental are not building in public.

Specific failures with precise diagnoses are.

Know the difference before you start the series.

---

**Tweet 2**
The best productivity system for a solopreneur is not a task manager.

It is a decision filter.

One question: does this move my core metric this week?

No? Parking lot.
Yes? Short list.

Three things done beats thirty things started.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
Your niche is not too crowded.

You are too similar to everyone already in it.

Find what the top 3 voices in your niche consistently avoid saying.

Start there. That gap is your positioning.

---

**Tweet 4**
Motivation is the story before the work.

Systems are what keep you working when the story fades.

Every decision you automate is one less thing that drains your willpower.

Build systems. Not momentum.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
The "build in public" format that still works in 2026:

Not: "I made $X, here's how"

Yes: "I got this completely wrong, here is exactly what I misunderstood and what the data showed me"

Specific failures are more credible than generic wins. Every time.
