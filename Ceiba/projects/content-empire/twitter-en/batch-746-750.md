## Tweet 747
**Type:** Thread opener
The minimal tool stack that actually produces results for a one-person business. (Thread)

1/ Stage 1: zero to first sale. You need one email platform, one content platform, and one checkout tool. That is three tools. Substack, Gumroad, done. No CRM. No project management. No funnel builder. You do not have the volume to justify complexity.

2/ Stage 2: first sale to $1K per month. Add a social scheduling tool. That is it. Your inbox is still your CRM. Your email sequences are still your funnel. One new tool, max.

3/ Stage 3: $1K to $5K per month. Add lightweight project management. Add an analytics layer so you know where buyers come from. Maybe a booking tool if you are running calls. Still under 6 tools total.

4/ The rule: add a tool only when the pain of not having it is concrete and measurable. Not because a YouTube creator made it look useful. Not because you are procrastinating with setup instead of building.

5/ The creators earning the most with the least overhead delayed adding tools until revenue justified them. Start lean. Stay lean as long as you can.

## Tweet 750
**Type:** Thread opener
The trust pyramid. Why people know your work but still do not buy. (Thread)

1/ There are five layers to trust. Awareness: they know you exist. Credibility: they believe you know your topic. Likeability: they want to hear from you. Trust: they believe you have their interests in mind. Purchase: follows naturally when the rest are solid.

2/ Most creators try to sell from layer one or two. They optimize the sales page when the problem is layer three. People do not buy from sources they respect but do not connect with. Voice, personality, and real opinions build layer three.

3/ Layer four is built through honesty. Name what your product does not do. Mention alternatives when relevant. Acknowledge limitations. Counterintuitively, this closes more sales than a perfect pitch.

4/ If your conversion rates are low and your product is good, look at the pyramid. Which layer is incomplete? That is where to invest, not in the checkout page.
