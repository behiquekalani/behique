## Tweet 1057

THREAD: How pricing signals value (and why you should raise your price before you think you are ready)

1/ Your price is not just a number.

It is a message.

Low prices say: "I am not sure this is worth much."
High prices say: "I am confident this produces results."

Confidence converts. Hesitation does not.

2/ The mistake most new creators make: pricing based on how long something took to make.

Wrong signal.

Price based on the result it produces for the buyer.

A two-hour workshop that helps someone close a $10K client is worth far more than the two hours it runs.

3/ Price anchoring works in your favor when you let it.

Show the expensive option first.

The mid-tier option looks reasonable by comparison. Conversions on the mid-tier go up.

This is not manipulation. It is context.

4/ Raising your price does three things:

- Attracts better clients who respect the work
- Repels clients who would drain your energy
- Forces you to deliver at the level the price demands

That last one matters most. High prices make you better.

5/ The best time to raise your price is before you are fully booked.

Not after.

If you wait until demand forces it, you left months of revenue on the table.

6/ Test a higher price on your next offer before you decide it is too much.

One sale at the higher price tells you more than any amount of market research.

The market is not theoretical. It is the next person who reads your sales page.

## Tweet 1060

THREAD: How to platform-proof your income as a creator

1/ Every platform you depend on is a risk.

Instagram cuts reach overnight.
Twitter changes its API.
YouTube demonetizes.
TikTok gets banned.

If your business is built on any single platform, it can disappear in a day.

2/ Platform-proofing starts with one principle:

Own the relationship, not just the audience.

Email lists, SMS lists, Discord servers you control. Anything the platform cannot delete.

3/ The funnel is simple:

Platform (borrowed reach) -> Lead magnet (capture the subscriber) -> Email list (owned relationship) -> Offer (revenue that survives algorithm changes)

Most creators stop at step one and wonder why their business feels fragile.

4/ The fastest way to move people off-platform:

Give them a reason to come to you.

A free resource that solves an immediate problem. A waitlist for something valuable. A community that exists somewhere you control.

The content draws them in. The offer moves them over.

5/ Second layer of protection: sell directly.

Gumroad, a personal site, a Shopify store. Revenue that does not require the platform to send it to you.

Every dollar that flows through your own checkout is a dollar that cannot be withheld.

6/ The goal is not to leave every platform.

The goal is to build the business so that losing any one of them would not end it.

If losing Instagram tomorrow would be a minor inconvenience, you have built it right.

If it would be a catastrophe, you have not yet.
