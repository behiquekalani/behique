**Tweet 1**
The fear funnel: your audience is scared. Not of your product. Of the problem your product solves. Lead with the fear. Then offer the path out.

**Tweet 2**
Good copywriting names the thing people are afraid to say out loud. "You're watching others succeed while you're stuck" hits harder than "learn the strategy."

**Tweet 3**
Fear-based marketing isn't manipulation. It's empathy. People already feel the fear. Your job is to acknowledge it and show you understand before offering a solution.

**Tweet 4**
The funnel: fear the problem, desire the outcome, trust the guide, buy the solution. Most creators skip the fear step and wonder why nobody converts.

**Tweet 5**
If you want someone to take action, make the cost of inaction real. Not fake urgency. Real consequences. What happens if they don't change anything in 12 months?
