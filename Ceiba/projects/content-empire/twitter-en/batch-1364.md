# Twitter Batch 1364

---

**Tweet 1**
The first second of anything is the only second that matters.

Your headline. Your first sentence. The first frame of your video.

If that doesn't hook, the rest doesn't exist.

---

**Tweet 2**
Open with a situation, not a claim.

"You spent three hours on a post that got four likes" lands harder than "here's why your content isn't working."

The situation is specific. The reader sees themselves in it.

---

**Tweet 3**
Most writers do this backwards.

They write the insight first and bury the hook at the end.

Flip it. Put the most specific, relatable observation at the top. Let the insight come after the reader is already nodding.

---

**Tweet 4**
The hook is not decoration. It is the content.

Nobody gets to your insight without passing through your opening line first.

---

**Tweet 5**
Thread starter:

The reason most content gets ignored has nothing to do with quality.
