## Tweet 982

Thread: What you do in private determines everything. 1/

1/ Everything visible started as something invisible.

The public result is the final frame of a very long film.

Most people only see the final frame.

2/ The consistent creator has a private practice no one sees.

The high-performing athlete has thousands of unseen hours behind each visible moment.

The business that appears from nowhere has years of invisible iteration underneath it.

3/ The most important behaviors are the ones no one sees and no one praises.

The decision to read instead of watch.
The hour of work completed when nothing external required it.

4/ These choices do not feel significant in the moment.

There is no immediate feedback. No audience. No validation.

That invisibility is exactly what makes them powerful.

5/ Your private behavior is your real behavior.

Close the gap between who you are when no one is watching and who you want to be.

Not by performing better. By behaving in private the way you want to be seen behaving in public.

6/ Build something that runs in private for long enough and the public results become inevitable.

Not because success is guaranteed. Because you have become someone capable of producing it.

## Tweet 985

Thread: Build something that outlasts you. 1/

1/ There are two kinds of work.

Work that requires your continuous presence to exist.

And work that exists independently of you once it is created.

2/ A freelance hour sold requires you every time.

A product, a course, a useful body of writing, that works when you are sleeping.

The equation is different in kind, not just degree.

3/ Most people spend their career building the first type.

They trade time for money and the equation resets every billing cycle.

The work does not accumulate.

4/ Permanent work requires real effort to build.

But once built, it operates at a scale and persistence that hourly work cannot match.

5/ You do not need to think about legacy in a grand way.

The question is simple: what will exist because you built it?

Not because you were present. Because the thing itself continues.

6/ Start building for permanence.

Not because you will live forever.

Because the work worth doing is the work that does not need you to keep breathing to keep being useful.
