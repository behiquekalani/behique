## Tweet 931

Thread: The distraction economy and how to exit it 🧵

1/ Every platform you use has one goal: keep you on the platform. Not inform you. Not help you. Keep you there.

2/ The product is not the content. The product is your attention. They sell it to advertisers. You are the supply chain.

3/ Understanding this changes everything. You're not consuming media. You're being consumed by a system designed by behavioral engineers.

4/ Willpower won't fix this. The platforms have infinite A/B testing budgets. You will not out-discipline a behavioral loop.

5/ The answer is environment design. Remove the friction for deep work. Add friction to distraction. Phone in another room. Apps deleted. Notification defaults set to off.

6/ Reclaim attention and you reclaim thinking. These are the same resource.

## Tweet 934

Thread: Building in public without performing 🧵

1/ Build in public became a content strategy. Now most of it is indistinguishable from personal branding theater. Staged vulnerability. Convenient pivots.

2/ Real building in public requires actual uncertainty. Real wrong turns. Admissions that things didn't work before they turned into lessons.

3/ The audience can feel the difference. They can't always name it. But trust erodes quietly when the narrative is too clean.

4/ Real transparency is not oversharing. It's honesty about the actual state of things. Not retrospective honesty. Present-tense honesty.

5/ The creators with the deepest audiences almost always sacrificed short-term image management for long-term honesty.
