## Tweet 773
**Type:** Thread opener
I spent two years undercharging for work I was proud of. Here is the money mindset shift that changed everything. (Thread)

1/ Most pricing problems are not market problems. They are mindset problems. The market will pay what you ask if what you're asking matches the value of the outcome. The math isn't the issue. The story you're telling yourself is.

2/ Every creator has one of three broken money stories. "Charging more means I'm greedy." "If it were really good, people would find it." "I need to earn the right to charge real prices." None of these are true. All of them cost real money.

3/ The creator charging $297 for the same information someone else charges $19 for is not more skilled. They're more convicted. That conviction shows in the sales page, the community, the way they talk about the work.

4/ A low price is a signal. It says: "I'm not sure this is worth much." A real price says: "I've done the work. This delivers results. I stand behind it." Your buyer hears both signals whether you intend them or not.

5/ Charge what the outcome is worth. Build to the price if you have to. But decide on a real number first. The market can't confirm what you won't ask for.

## Tweet 775
**Type:** Thread opener
The five things that silently kill solopreneur momentum. And the fix for each. (Thread)

1/ Decision fatigue. Making the same recurring decisions daily drains your judgment by noon. Fix: make recurring decisions once and automate or batch them. What to post, when to work, which tool to use. Decide once. Follow the decision.

2/ Context switching. Jumping between creative work, client work, admin, and sales in one day is not productivity. Every switch costs 20 minutes of recovery. Fix: theme your days. Creation days. Admin days. Client days.

3/ Invisible progress. Nothing feels like it's moving. Then suddenly it compounds. Most solopreneurs quit before the compounding starts. Fix: track leading indicators. Content published. Subscribers gained. Proposals sent.

4/ Perfectionism as procrastination. "I'm not ready to launch yet" is one of the most expensive sentences in business. Fix: ship at 80%. The last 20% is almost never visible to the buyer.

5/ Isolation. Loneliness is demotivating in ways that are hard to name but easy to feel. Fix: one accountability relationship, deliberately maintained. Not a mastermind. One real person who knows what you're building.
