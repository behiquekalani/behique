**Tweet 1**
Stop pricing to your costs.

Price to the outcome.

What is the problem worth to the person who has it? Start there. Work backwards to your offer.

**Tweet 2**
The reason your prices feel high to you:

You know how long it took you to build the thing.

Your customer doesn't care about your time. They care about their result. Price the result.

**Tweet 3**
Low prices do not make selling easier.

They attract price-sensitive buyers who complain more, return more, and value your work less.

Raise the price. Attract the right buyer.

**Tweet 4**
Contrarian take: underpricing is just as dangerous as overpricing.

Underpricing erodes your positioning, attracts misaligned customers, and makes it harder to raise prices later.

Charge what it's worth. #Pricing
