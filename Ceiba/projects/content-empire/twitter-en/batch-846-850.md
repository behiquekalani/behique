## Tweet 847

1/ The pricing conversation nobody wants to have:

You are not underpriced because the market is unfair.

You are underpriced because you do not believe in what you built.

Here is how to fix that:

2/ Start with the outcome, not the format.

You did not build a PDF. You built the result the PDF delivers.

What is that result worth to the buyer?

3/ A $100 product that saves someone 3 months of trial and error is not a $100 product.

It is a product delivering 3 months of progress.

Price accordingly.

4/ Test your price by raising it.

Raise it once. Watch the conversion rate.

If it barely changes, raise it again.

Your market will tell you where the ceiling is.

5/ Low prices attract the wrong buyers.

Buyers who pay more implement more.

Buyers who pay more refer more.

The price you charge affects the quality of your customer base.

6/ You will never feel ready to raise your price.

Raise it before you feel ready.

The feeling follows the action.

## Tweet 849

1/ A creator's flywheel, explained simply:

2/ Write one long-form piece of content per week.

A newsletter issue. An essay. A deep thread.

This is the anchor. Everything else flows from it.

3/ Extract subheadings as tweet threads.

Extract the core insight as a one-liner.

Extract the examples as short-form posts.

4/ One newsletter issue becomes:
- 3 tweet threads
- 5 single tweets
- 2 short-form videos
- 1 carousel

That is 11 pieces of content from one block of thinking.

5/ Now take the ideas that resonate most.

The ones people share, save, reply to.

Those ideas go into your product outline next.

6/ Content is not the business.

It is market research for the business.

Every post you publish is a test. Read the results.

7/ The flywheel does not stop. It feeds itself.

Last year's best content becomes this year's framework.

This year's framework becomes next year's product.

Build in loops, not lines.
