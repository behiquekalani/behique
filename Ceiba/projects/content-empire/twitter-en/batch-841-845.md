Every niche feels saturated until you find your specific angle. Here's how to grow in a crowded space:

1/ The niche isn't the problem. Your version of the niche might be missing. Finance is saturated. Finance for first-generation college graduates with student debt and no family wealth is not.

2/ Look at the top 3 creators in your niche. Who do they implicitly exclude? Whose problems do they gloss over? That's your opening.

3/ Differentiation is rarely about being smarter. It's about being more specific and more honest. Take a real position. Not "here are 5 productivity tips." But "most productivity advice was built for people who don't have ADHD."

4/ Your personal story is the one thing no one can copy. Your angle, your experience, your genuine opinions. Build from those and you have a lane no one else can occupy.

5/ Specific content makes the right person feel seen. That response, that feeling of "this is exactly for me," drives shares, follows, and purchases better than any algorithm trick.

## Tweet 844
**Type:** Thread opener
One hour of focused marketing beats five hours of scattered effort. Here's how to run your full marketing operation in 60 minutes a week:

1/ Twenty minutes: write and schedule one piece of content. One idea, one format. A Twitter thread or a LinkedIn post. Done. Don't overthink it.

2/ Twenty minutes: engage meaningfully in your niche. Leave two or three real comments on posts by creators your audience follows. Add something to the conversation, don't just seek attention.

3/ Twenty minutes: send one email or nurture one warm lead with a personal check-in. Keep the relationship warm. One email a week to your list is better than silence followed by a pitch.

4/ Seven hundred words of content, two genuine conversations, one email. That is a real marketing operation for a solo operator. Done in sixty minutes.

5/ This only works if your content is focused and your niche is defined. Scattered topics need more time. A clear, specific brand makes one hour more than enough.
