## Tweet 992
1/ The pricing conversation most solopreneurs never have with themselves:

"Am I charging this because the market won't bear more, or because I'm afraid to find out?"

Most honest answer for most people: it's the second one.

Here's what happens when you test it:

2/ Fear tells you: "If you raise your price, nobody will buy."

Reality: a higher price filters for better clients, signals higher value, and often increases perceived quality before anyone experiences the product.

3/ The buyers who leave when you raise your price are the ones who were going to be the most work for the least result.

The buyers who stay are the ones who were always going to get the most from what you offer.

4/ You didn't lose buyers when you raised your price.

You lost the wrong buyers.

That's not a loss. That's an upgrade.

5/ The number that makes you nervous is usually closer to the right price than the number that feels "safe."

Test the nervous number. See what happens.

Most people are shocked by how little resistance they find.

## Tweet 995
1/ Three things I wish I knew before building my first digital product:

2/ The product you think your audience wants and the product they'll actually pay for are usually different.

Talk to 10 potential buyers before you build a single slide.

3/ "I'll launch when it's ready" is the most expensive sentence in the creator economy.

Ready is a myth. Ship the 80% version. Customers will tell you what the other 20% needs to be.

4/ Your first launch will underperform your expectations.

Not because the product is bad. Because you don't have enough trust built yet.

Build the trust first. Launch second.

Most people reverse this and wonder why it doesn't convert.
