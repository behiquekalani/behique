**Tweet 1**
Your price is a signal. Low prices tell people the work isn't serious. High prices tell people it is. Price is not just math. It's positioning.

**Tweet 2**
Most creators underprice because they're afraid of rejection. But the real rejection is when someone buys at $7 and expects $700 worth of value.

**Tweet 3**
Anchoring works. Show a $997 option first. Then show a $297 option. The $297 feels like a deal. Nothing about the product changed. The context did.

**Tweet 4**
Charm pricing ($97 vs $100) still works. But it works better when the product is already good. Psychology can't carry a weak offer.

**Tweet 5**
The creator who charges $500 for a course attracts buyers who finish it. The one who charges $17 attracts buyers who collect PDFs. Price filters behavior.
