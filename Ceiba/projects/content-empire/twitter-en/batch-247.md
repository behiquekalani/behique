## Topic: Why most landing pages don't convert

---

**Tweet 1**
Your landing page fails because it's about you. The visitor arrives asking "what's in it for me?" and they find a product description. That's the mismatch.

---

**Tweet 2**
Most landing pages list features. Buyers make decisions based on outcomes. "10 modules" means nothing. "You'll close your first client in 30 days" means everything.

---

**Tweet 3**
The average landing page has 3 problems: no clear thesis, no specific audience, no single call to action. Fix those three and you're already in the top 20%.

---

**Tweet 4**
Conversion is not a design problem. It's a clarity problem. If someone reads your page and can't describe the offer in one sentence, the page is broken.

---

**Tweet 5**
A landing page that converts has one job: take a skeptical stranger from "maybe" to "yes." That requires a specific promise, social proof that matches the skepticism, and one button. Most pages have none of these.
