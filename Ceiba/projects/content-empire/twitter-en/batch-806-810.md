## Tweet 807
**Type:** Thread opener
You do not have a content problem. You have a distribution problem.

Here is the full repurposing system I use to turn one piece into 20:

1/ One anchor piece per week. Newsletter, YouTube, long LinkedIn post. This is where the real thinking lives.

2/ Pull three quotes from it. Format as single tweets. No context needed. A good quote stands alone.

3/ Find one framework or list inside it. Turn it into a thread with a strong opener and one idea per tweet.

4/ Take the main argument and compress it to a 60-second video script. Caption-first. No face required.

5/ Send a condensed version to your email list. 150 words. One key takeaway. One question to reply to.

One thinking session. Five different formats. Twenty pieces over the month.

The content is not the bottleneck. Your distribution habits are.

## Tweet 809
**Type:** Single tweet
Cold outreach template that actually works:

1. Do something public for them first.
2. Send three sentences: who you are, the ask, an easy out.
3. Follow up once with new value attached.

That is the whole system. The rest is ego and noise.

## Tweet 810
**Type:** Thread opener
How to price your services without underselling yourself:

1/ Stop pricing by the hour. Nobody cares how long something takes. They care what it is worth to them.

2/ Ask yourself: what is one hour of my client's time worth in their business? Start there.

3/ Calculate how many hours your work saves or generates for them. That is your value floor.

4/ Your price should be a fraction of that number. If you save someone 40 hours at $50/hr, charging $400 is a discount.

5/ The guilt is not a signal that your price is wrong. It is a signal that you do not yet believe in your own results.

Charge for outcomes. Stop charging for time.
