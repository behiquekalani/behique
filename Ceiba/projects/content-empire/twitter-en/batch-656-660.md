## Tweet 656
**Type:** Thread opener

How to launch a digital product when you have zero audience. It is simpler than you think. (Thread)

1/ Stop waiting for an audience. You do not need one to make your first sales. You need the right 10 people. Ten people is not an audience. It is a contact list.

2/ Start with your network, not the internet. Write down every person you know who might need what you are selling or who knows someone who might. That is your first outreach list. Specific beats broad at this stage.

3/ Send personal messages, not broadcasts. Tell them who you are, what you built, why you built it, and why you thought of them specifically. Specificity gets responses. Blasts do not.

4/ Offer a beta price in exchange for honest feedback and a testimonial. You are buying social proof, which is the most valuable thing you are missing. Testimonials beat any marketing copy you can write.

5/ Use those first sales and testimonials to build a real sales page. Now you have proof. Now you can post publicly with actual results behind the claim.

The first sale is the hardest. Everything after it is pattern repetition.

## Tweet 659
**Type:** Thread opener

The content-to-cash pipeline. Most creators only have stage one. (Thread)

1/ Stage one is traffic. Your content on social platforms drives attention. This is where most creators spend all of their time and stop. Traffic alone does not pay you.

2/ Stage two is capture. A percentage of that traffic needs a reason to join your email list. A lead magnet, a free guide, a newsletter with a clear value proposition. Without this, you are building for the platform, not for yourself.

3/ Stage three is nurture. Your email sequence builds the relationship that social content cannot. It goes deeper, it is more personal, and trust compounds here faster than anywhere else.

4/ Stage four is conversion. One offer, positioned for the specific person you have been nurturing. One clear call to action. A price that reflects the value. No complexity.

5/ Stage five is retention. A buyer who has a good experience buys again. Build your second and third products for your existing buyers, not just for new audiences.

Most creators have stage one and nothing else. That is why they are busy and broke.
