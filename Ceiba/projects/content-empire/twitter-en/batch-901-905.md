## Tweet 902
Thread: The editing skill no one talks about in the creator economy

1/ The skill that separates good creators from great ones isn't content. It's editing.

2/ Not just editing writing. Editing decisions. Editing attention. Editing your list of what's worth building at all.

3/ Before publishing anything: what is the one thing this piece is supposed to do for the reader? If you can't answer in one sentence, it's not ready.

4/ Edit your schedule. Your calendar tells you what you actually value, not what you say you value.

5/ Edit your identity. The person you were when you started might not be who the next level requires.

6/ The best version of anything is always smaller than the first draft.

7/ Cut more. Publish less. Make each piece do more work.

## Tweet 905
Thread: What actually happens during the invisible period of building

1/ Before any breakthrough in the creator economy, there's a period of invisible work.

2/ Creating without feedback. Building without confirmation. Publishing without traction.

3/ This is not a sign that it's not working. It's a sign that compounding hasn't shown up yet.

4/ Compounding always shows up late relative to when the work started. That's the nature of compounding.

5/ The creators who quit during the invisible period never reach the threshold.

6/ The ones who treat it as mandatory tuition eventually cross it.

7/ You're probably closer to the threshold than you think. The question is whether you'll still be building when you get there.
