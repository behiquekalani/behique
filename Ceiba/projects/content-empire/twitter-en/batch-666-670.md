## Tweet 667
**Type:** Thread opener

The client red flags that cost solopreneurs more than money. (Thread)

1/ Red flag one: they negotiate hard on price before the project starts. Price-sensitive clients do not become generous ones. If they are trying to reduce your rate before seeing your work, that dynamic continues throughout the engagement.

2/ Red flag two: they cannot define what success looks like. Ask what a good outcome looks like in three months. If the answer is vague, you will spend the project chasing a moving target and never hit it.

3/ Red flag three: they have worked with multiple providers on the same project. Ask why those relationships ended. The answer reveals more about the client than any brief.

4/ Red flag four: urgency without flexibility. Real urgency exists. Manufactured urgency is a control tactic. It signals they do not respect your capacity or planning process.

5/ The cost of a bad client is not just the invoice amount. It is the creative bandwidth, the stress, and the better client you could not take because this one was occupying the space.

Screen harder than you sell.

## Tweet 670
**Type:** Thread opener

Content pillars are not a creative limitation. They are the infrastructure that makes consistent creation possible. (Thread)

1/ Most creators who struggle with consistency do not lack ideas. They lack constraints. When everything is a possible topic, nothing is an obvious next move. Pillars solve this.

2/ Three to five core themes define what your content is about. Within those pillars, specific topics, angles, and formats rotate freely. The pillars stay constant. The content inside them evolves.

3/ The blank page problem disappears. Instead of "what should I make today" you ask "which pillar am I working in this week." That is a smaller, answerable question.

4/ Pillars create brand coherence. An audience that follows you for one type of thinking recognizes your voice even as the specific topics change. That recognition compounds into trust.

5/ Find your pillars by looking at your last 20 pieces of content or the 20 you wish you had published. What categories do they cluster into naturally? Those are your pillars.

Three pillars you execute on beats fifteen you cycle through randomly every time.
