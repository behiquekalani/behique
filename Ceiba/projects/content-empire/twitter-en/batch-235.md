**Tweet 1**
You already know enough to sell something. The barrier is not knowledge. It is the belief that your knowledge needs to be extraordinary before it's worth packaging.

**Tweet 2**
A digital product is just your knowledge in a format someone can consume without your time. You've already done the hard part of acquiring the knowledge. Package it.

**Tweet 3**
The best digital products are not the most comprehensive. They are the most specific. Pick one problem you've solved and build a product around that solution.

**Tweet 4**
Curriculum design is not the hard part. Picking the specific person you're helping is. The more clearly you can describe who this is for, the faster it sells.

**Tweet 5**
You turn what you know into a product by writing it down, structuring it, pricing it, and telling people about it. None of those steps require a team or a platform deal. Start today.
