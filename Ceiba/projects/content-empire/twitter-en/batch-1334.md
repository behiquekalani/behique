# Twitter Batch 1334

---

**Tweet 1**
The one-person business does not require a team.

It requires one person who understands the whole system well enough to run each part sequentially.

You are not a freelancer. You are a company of one.

Operate accordingly.

---

**Tweet 2**
Solopreneur leverage checklist:

- Content built once, distributed many times
- Product sold once, delivered many times
- System built once, runs without you many times

Every hour you spend on something in only one of those categories is an hour that does not compound.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
The personal brand that tries to be liked by everyone ends up influencing nobody.

Pick your convictions. State them clearly. Defend them when challenged.

Conviction is what makes someone worth following.

Agreeableness is what makes someone forgettable.

---

**Tweet 4**
Your second product is more likely to succeed than your first.

Because the first one taught you what your buyers actually want next.

Ship the first product to earn the intelligence. Ship the second product to compound it.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
The weekly review that keeps a solopreneur on track (takes 20 minutes):

1. What did I ship this week?
2. What moved my core metric?
3. What got in the way?
4. What is the one thing I need to do next week above all else?

Four questions. Every Friday. No exceptions.
