## Tweet 1047

THREAD: How to build a newsletter that actually makes money (not just a big list)

1/ Most newsletters die not from lack of subscribers but from the wrong subscribers.

Your lead magnet is a filter. If it attracts everyone, it qualifies no one.

2/ The fix: your lead magnet should solve one specific problem for one specific person.

Not a "30 tools for productivity" checklist.

A "how to cut your admin work in half as a solo consultant" guide.

3/ The person who downloads that second one is pre-qualified.

They have a problem. You have the solution. That is the beginning of a sale.

4/ Second rule: your welcome sequence should feel like talking to a friend who just found out you exist.

Not a funnel. Not a pitch. An introduction.

What do you do. Who do you help. What can they expect.

5/ Third rule: make one ask per email. Not three.

One CTA. One link. One reason to click.

The more options you give, the fewer decisions people make.

6/ Result: a smaller, more engaged list that generates more revenue per subscriber than any bloated list you could buy or borrow.

Build less. Target more. Earn consistently.

## Tweet 1049

THREAD: The exact moment scope creep begins (and how to stop it before it starts)

1/ Scope creep does not start with a difficult client.

It starts with a vague proposal.

2/ "We will handle your social media" is not a scope.

How many posts? Which platforms? Does that include graphics? Video? Scheduling? Reporting? Responding to comments?

Every undefined thing becomes an assumption. Every assumption becomes an expectation. Every expectation becomes a demand.

3/ The fix: write scope in terms of deliverables, not activities.

"12 posts per month, 3 per week, Instagram only, graphics included, captions only, no video editing."

Now there is nothing to argue about.

4/ Add a "not included" section.

List every adjacent service that clients commonly assume is included but is not.

This is not aggressive. It is clarifying. Good clients appreciate it.

5/ Every out-of-scope request gets a change order.

Price attached. Timeline attached. Client signature required.

Most clients stop asking for extras the moment there is a process for billing them.

6/ The best client relationships are built on mutual clarity.

You protect your time. They get predictability. Everyone wins.

Write the scope. Add the exclusions. Send the change order.
