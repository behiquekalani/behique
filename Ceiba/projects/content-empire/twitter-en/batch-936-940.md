## Tweet 937

Thread: The long game explained 🧵

1/ Most people play the short game by default. Not because they prefer it. Because short games give immediate feedback, and brains are wired for immediate feedback loops.

2/ The long game operates on different math. The returns are delayed but structural. The compounding curve is invisible until it isn't.

3/ What the long game requires isn't patience alone. It requires clarity. You need to know what you're building toward to evaluate whether a sacrifice is an investment.

4/ Almost everyone abandons the long game before the compounding becomes visible. This means reduced competition for the people who stay.

5/ Short-term attention is crowded. Long-term trust is rare. Rare things are valuable. Play accordingly.

## Tweet 940

Thread: How to actually change a habit 🧵

1/ Motivation precedes action in theory. In reality, action precedes motivation. The feeling that makes it easier to show up comes from having shown up.

2/ You will rarely feel like doing the important thing. This is normal and permanent. It is not a problem to solve. It is a condition to work within.

3/ The goal is not to make the habit feel good at the start. The goal is to make not doing it feel worse than doing it.

4/ Environment design beats willpower. Make the right action easier to do than not doing it. Remove the friction. Add it to the alternatives.

5/ Start so small that resistance cannot justify itself. One paragraph. One push-up. One minute. The smallness bypasses the ego.

6/ What you are actually building is identity. Each repetition is a vote. The votes accumulate. The identity shifts. The behavior follows.
