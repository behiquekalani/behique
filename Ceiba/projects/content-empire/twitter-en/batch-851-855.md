## Tweet 852

1/ How I think about content when I have nothing to say:

(this is not writer's block. this is the wrong question.)

2/ You always have something to say.

You just have not been capturing the thoughts that pass through your day.

The fix is a capture habit, not a brainstorming session.

3/ Keep a note open at all times.

Not for writing. For flagging.

When something surprises you, frustrates you, or teaches you something, flag it.

4/ One week of consistent capturing produces 30+ content seeds.

You are not manufacturing ideas.

You are noticing the ones you are already having.

5/ Now look at your captures.

Which ones share a theme?

That theme is a newsletter issue. The individual captures are the supporting sections.

6/ Which capture feels most uncomfortable to share?

That is your best post.

The resistance is a signal, not a stop sign.

7/ Ideas are never the bottleneck.

Courage is.

Publish the thing that makes you nervous. That is the one that connects.
