## Tweet 972

Thread: What compounding actually looks like from the inside. 1/

1/ From the outside, compounding looks like sudden results.

From the inside, it looks like six months of nothing.

2/ You write every day. Nothing grows.
You train every week. The mirror looks the same.
You build. Revenue stays near zero.

This is not failure. This is how compounding works.

3/ The results are accumulating in a form you cannot yet see.

Skills. Habits. Reputation. Relationships.

All building underground before anything breaks the surface.

4/ The most common mistake is quitting during this invisible phase.

You conclude the thing does not work. You start something new. The clock resets.

5/ Serial starting is the most expensive habit.

Every new beginning is a withdrawal from the account you were building.

6/ Pick the right direction. Give it a full cycle, longer than feels reasonable.

Let it actually run before you evaluate it.

The boring middle is not waste. It is the foundation.

## Tweet 975

Thread: The identity you need before the goal will work. 1/

1/ Goals fail at the identity level before they fail at the behavior level.

You will not consistently do things that feel like they belong to someone else.

2/ The smoker who wants to quit but still sees themselves as a smoker keeps finding their way back.

The statement "I am trying to quit" keeps failing because the identity is still intact.

3/ The shift is from "I am trying to be" to "I am."

Not a declaration. An accumulation.

Every action that matches the desired identity casts a vote for that version of you.

4/ You do not need to be the writer. You need to write today.

That is one vote. Tomorrow is another.

Enough votes and the identity becomes real, not claimed.

5/ Start with one action small enough that failure is nearly impossible.

Then escalate.

The goal is not the first action. It is the pattern that builds the identity around it.
