## Tweet 653
**Type:** Thread opener

Five client red flags that always predict a painful engagement. Learn them before you sign anything. (Thread)

1/ They negotiate your price aggressively before the work starts. Price-sensitive clients do not become generous ones. If someone is already trying to reduce your rate, that dynamic will continue throughout the project.

2/ They cannot define what success looks like. Vague success criteria guarantee dissatisfaction. If a client cannot tell you specifically what a win looks like, they will always feel like it was a loss.

3/ They have already worked with multiple providers on the same project. Ask why those relationships ended. The answer reveals more than any brief ever will.

4/ Everything is urgent with no flexibility. Real urgency exists. Manufactured urgency is a control tactic. It signals the client does not respect your schedule or capacity.

5/ They respond slowly during the proposal stage. Communication before the contract reflects communication after it. Slow and inconsistent stays slow and inconsistent.

Bad clients cost more than money. They cost creative energy and the opportunity to work with better clients.
