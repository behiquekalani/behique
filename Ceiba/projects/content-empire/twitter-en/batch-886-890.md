## Tweet 887
Thread: Why your prices are too low and what to do about it

1/ Low prices don't attract more buyers. They attract more skeptical buyers. Price is a signal before it's a number.

2/ The math: 100 units at $20 = $2,000. 20 units at $200 = $4,000. Premium earns double with 80% fewer customers.

3/ Fewer customers means fewer support issues, fewer refunds, fewer people who didn't take your product seriously.

4/ The reason you're undercharging is fear. Fear of rejection. Fear of "that's too expensive." One no doesn't mean the price is wrong.

5/ It means that person isn't your buyer. Your buyer exists and is looking for something worth paying for.

6/ Fix: double one price for 30 days. Track conversion rate, not just total sales. Most people discover the higher price performs better.

## Tweet 889
Thread: The one-project rule that changed how I work

1/ Most solopreneurs are running 3-5 projects simultaneously and making real progress on none of them.

2/ The mental overhead of juggling projects kills the deep work each one needs to succeed.

3/ Concentration isn't a personality trait. It's a resource. Protect it like money.

4/ The one-project rule: one primary project gets full focus until it hits the next meaningful milestone.

5/ Everything else goes on hold. Not cancelled. On hold. There's a difference.

6/ Try it for 30 days. The clarity alone is worth the experiment.

7/ Most people discover that their "important" secondary projects were just escape hatches from doing hard work on the main one.
