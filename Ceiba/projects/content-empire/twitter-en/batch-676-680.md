## Tweet 676
**Type:** Thread opener

The anti-niche approach and who it actually works for. Spoiler: it is not most people. (Thread)

1/ The standard advice is to niche down. Pick one audience, one problem, go deep. That advice is correct for most creators. There is one real exception.

2/ The anti-niche approach works when your perspective is strong enough to be the product. Not your expertise in one domain, but your lens. The way you think about any problem. The insight you generate that most people could not.

3/ Creators like this do not sell expertise in one narrow field. They sell a way of thinking. The audience comes for the lens and then follows it wherever it points. The perspective is the niche.

4/ The mistake is treating "I have not figured out my niche yet" as a strategic decision to go broad. That is not anti-niche. That is diffuse. It produces content that attracts no one specifically.

5/ Most creators should niche by domain first. Build credibility with one audience about one problem. Once that audience trusts your judgment, expand the lens. The sequence matters.

Going broad before you have something worth going broad with just means everyone ignores you.

## Tweet 679
**Type:** Thread opener

The three types of leverage available to every solopreneur and how to use them. (Thread)

1/ Leverage is any mechanism that multiplies your output without multiplying your input proportionally. There is a ceiling on what more hours produces. There is no ceiling on what more leverage produces.

2/ Asset leverage: build once, sell repeatedly. A digital product has a front-loaded effort curve and an extending return curve. Every sale after the first generates revenue without additional creation time. This is the most accessible form of leverage for solopreneurs.

3/ System leverage: document what is repetitive and automate it. Content scheduling, email sequences, client onboarding. Time freed from repeating the same tasks goes into creation, which produces more assets. The system funds itself.

4/ Audience leverage: an audience that amplifies your work multiplies reach without multiplying effort. One post shared by 200 engaged followers reaches 10,000 people. Building the audience is slow. Using it is fast.

5/ Most solopreneurs focus on creating more. The highest-leverage move is usually to systematize and distribute what already exists more effectively. Audit your work: where are you doing the same thing repeatedly?

That is where your leverage lives.
