**Tweet 1**
A job trades your time for money. A business builds systems that trade value for money. One scales with hours. The other scales with reach. Pick one and build accordingly.

**Tweet 2**
Employment gives you certainty. Business gives you optionality. Most people choose certainty and wonder why they never feel free.

**Tweet 3**
A job is renting your skills to someone else's vision. A business is building infrastructure around your own. Neither is wrong. But only one compounds.

**Tweet 4**
The difference between a job and a business is not income. It is whether you can leave for a month and still get paid. Build toward that threshold.

**Tweet 5**
You do not escape a job by getting a better job. You escape it by building something that runs without you present. That is the actual goal.
