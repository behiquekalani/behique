## Tweet 927

Thread: Why your self-concept is killing your results 🧵

1/ You don't have a strategy problem. You have an identity problem. Every time your actions conflict with who you believe you are, the belief wins.

2/ The belief doesn't feel like a belief. It feels like a fact. "I'm not a morning person." "I'm not disciplined." "I'm not a writer." These are installed software, not hardware.

3/ The fastest way to change behavior is not to fight the identity. It's to cast votes for a new one. Small actions. Repeated. Each one says: this is who I am now.

4/ You don't announce the new identity. You act from it. The announcement comes later, when the evidence is undeniable.

5/ Start with one behavior. One vote per day. That's how identities shift. Not through revelation. Through accumulation.

## Tweet 929

Thread: How to price yourself correctly 🧵

1/ The most common pricing mistake is charging based on how long something took you to make. That's cost-based pricing. It's the wrong variable.

2/ Price based on the outcome the buyer receives, not the input you provided. What is the transformation worth to them? Price should be a fraction of that.

3/ Low prices don't attract more buyers. They attract the wrong buyers. People who buy on price make decisions on price. They are harder to satisfy and less committed.

4/ If no one has ever told you your price is too high, you haven't found the ceiling yet. The ceiling tells you something about perceived value.

5/ Raise prices. Improve delivery. Repeat. This is the cycle that builds a real business.
