## Tweet 722
**Type:** Thread opener
The minimum viable audience is not a ceiling. It is a foundation.

Here is the math most creators ignore:

1/ 1,000 true fans spending $100/year with you is $100,000. That is not a massive audience. That is a focused one.

2/ A creator with 500 deeply engaged subscribers consistently outperforms a creator with 50,000 passive followers who never buy.

3/ Depth of connection is the metric that matters. Not follower count. Not impressions. How many people genuinely care what you put out next?

4/ Narrow topics attract smaller audiences and higher conversion rates. Broad topics attract large audiences that browse and leave.

5/ Your first goal: 100 people who open every email, share your work unprompted, and buy when you launch. Build for those 100. They will bring the next 1,000.

## Tweet 725
**Type:** Thread opener
The weekly content sprint is how you post daily without creating daily.

Here is the system:

1/ Block 2-3 hours on Sunday or Monday. This is your only creation session for the week. Everything else that week is just distribution.

2/ Map the week with your content pillars. Three to five topics you consistently cover. One per day. Now you have a plan, not a blank page.

3/ Draft everything before editing anything. Writing mode and editing mode are different. Switching between them kills speed and quality. Draft all five posts, then edit all five posts.

4/ Schedule everything before you close the session. Buffer, Hypefury, native platform schedulers. Do not leave drafts for "later." Later does not come.

5/ The hidden benefit: when something timely happens mid-week, you have creative capacity to respond because you are not carrying the weight of unfinished daily content.
