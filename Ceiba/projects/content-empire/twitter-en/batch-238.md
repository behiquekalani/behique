**Tweet 1**
The minimum viable personal brand is one clear thesis, one consistent platform, and one content format you can sustain for a year. That's it. Everything else is optional.

**Tweet 2**
You don't need a logo, a website, a niche document, or a brand guide to build a personal brand. You need a point of view you publish consistently. The rest is decoration.

**Tweet 3**
Most people over-architect their brand before they've tested whether anyone cares about what they have to say. Publish first. Refine the packaging after you see what lands.

**Tweet 4**
Your personal brand is not what you say it is. It's what people say about you when you're not in the room. You build that through consistent behavior, not through branding documents.

**Tweet 5**
Pick one thing you know well, one person you want to help, and one platform where they spend time. Show up every week with something useful. That is a personal brand. Start there.
