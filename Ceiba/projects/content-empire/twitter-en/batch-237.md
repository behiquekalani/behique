**Tweet 1**
Your landing page isn't converting because you're describing features, not the moment after the problem goes away. People don't buy products. They buy the relief of a specific frustration.

**Tweet 2**
If your headline doesn't tell me who this is for and what problem it solves in under 8 words, you've already lost most visitors. Clarity converts. Cleverness doesn't.

**Tweet 3**
The biggest conversion killer is not design. It is vague positioning. "For anyone who wants to grow" means nothing. "For freelancers who want to charge more without losing clients" means something.

**Tweet 4**
A landing page that tries to speak to everyone ends up resonating with no one. The more specifically you describe the person you're helping, the more that person trusts you understand them.

**Tweet 5**
Testimonials that say "this was amazing" don't convert. Testimonials that describe a before and after, with a specific result and a specific timeline, do. The format matters more than the sentiment.
