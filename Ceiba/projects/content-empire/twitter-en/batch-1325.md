# Twitter Batch 1325

---

**Tweet 1**
The creator economy does not need more content machines.

It needs more operators.

Content machine: measures reach.
Operator: measures revenue per subscriber.

Know which one you are building.

---

**Tweet 2**
Revenue per subscriber is the metric most email newsletter writers ignore.

Divide your monthly revenue by your subscriber count.

Under $1? Your focus is conversion, not growth.

Over $3? Your focus is growth.

Know your number first.

---

**Tweet 3 (Contrarian)**
Unpopular take:

The gap between $0 and $1K/month is a content problem.

The gap between $1K and $5K/month is a conversion problem.

Most people try to solve a conversion problem by growing their audience.

It does not work. Fix the rate before you scale the reach.

---

**Tweet 4**
Every platform shift in the last decade taught the same lesson.

The creators who built on rented platforms lost.
The creators who built email lists survived.

The lesson is still available. Most people are still ignoring it.

---

**Tweet 5 (Thread starter)**
The pricing error that kills solopreneur revenue before it starts:

Pricing based on how long it took to build.

Try this instead:

What would your buyer pay a consultant or agency to get the same result?

Your product price = 10-20% of that number.

You are not cheaper because you are worse. You removed the middleman.
