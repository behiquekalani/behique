## Post 357 — Thread Opener (4 tweets)

**Tweet 1/4**
The creator economy is not broken. Your business model is.

Here is what actually separates the 5% who make real income from the 95% who are stuck at zero:

**Tweet 2/4**
The 95% treat content like a job.
Post. Wait. Hope. Repeat.

The 5% treat content like a pipeline.
Each piece moves someone closer to a product, a list, or a sale.

**Tweet 3/4**
The difference is not posting frequency.
It is intentionality.

Every post should answer: what action does this create?
If you cannot answer that, you are creating noise, not leverage.

**Tweet 4/4**
You do not need more followers.
You need more clarity on what you are building.

Clarity converts. Followers are just a number.

## Post 359 — Thread Opener (3 tweets)

**Tweet 1/3**
Digital products have the best margins of any business model.
No inventory. No shipping. No employees.

But 90% of people who try it fail in the first 90 days. Here is the reason:

**Tweet 2/3**
They build the product before validating the idea.
They obsess over design before talking to a single buyer.
They write 10,000 words before posting one piece of content.

All of that is avoidance disguised as productivity.

**Tweet 3/3**
Validation takes three posts and a DM.
Build the audience first. Then build the product.
That order changes everything.
