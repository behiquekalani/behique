## Tweet 782
**Type:** Thread opener
Most creators are marketing when they should be positioning. Here is how to tell the difference. (Thread)

1/ Marketing is what you say. Positioning is where you stand. Marketing can be taught, replicated, automated. Positioning cannot. It is the unique intersection of who you are, what you know, who you serve, and what result you deliver.

2/ The test: if you swapped your name for any other creator in your niche and the content still made sense, you are not positioned. You are participating. Participation does not build a business. Position does.

3/ Positioning answers four questions distinctly. Who is it for, specifically? What result does it deliver, measurably? How is your approach different, not just better? Why does that difference matter to your specific buyer?

4/ "I help people grow on social media" is not positioning. It is a category. "I help B2B consultants use LinkedIn to generate three to five qualified leads per week without cold outreach" is a position. One filters 95% of the market and attracts the right 5% hard.

5/ Marketing amplifies positioning. It cannot replace it. If your marketing is working hard without producing great results, stop adding more marketing. Sharpen the position. The results will follow.

## Tweet 785
**Type:** Thread opener
When to pivot vs when to push through. A framework for the hardest decision in building a creator business. (Thread)

1/ A pivot is justified when the data tells you something fundamental has changed. The market doesn't want what you built. The positioning is wrong despite real effort. You've learned enough to know there's a better version of this.

2/ A quit is when the discomfort of doing the hard work feels like a signal that the work is wrong. It almost never is. Most creators quit two weeks before the inflection point they couldn't see from where they were standing.

3/ The timeline test: have you given it six months of consistent effort with real output? Two months with sporadic effort is not data. It's a draft. The minimum meaningful evaluation period is six months of actual output.

4/ The feedback test: what does the feedback actually say? People don't understand it? Positioning problem. They understand but don't buy? Conversion problem. They buy but don't get value? Product problem. All three are fixable. Know which one you have.

5/ The direction test: are you pivoting toward something specific or away from something uncomfortable? Pivoting toward a clear insight is strategy. Pivoting away from discomfort is avoidance with a business plan attached. Be honest with yourself about which it is.
