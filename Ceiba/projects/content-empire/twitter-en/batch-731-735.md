## Tweet 731
**Type:** Thread opener
Niching vs broadening. The real answer:

1/
"Go niche or die" is half-true.

Niche in the beginning. It is how you build trust, attract the right people, and stop being noise.

But there is a ceiling. And most creators hit it without realizing it.

2/
The transition from niche to broader is a strategy question, not a personality question.

You are not selling out. You are expanding the aperture of your usefulness.

3/
The move works when you go from narrow topic to adjacent problems your same audience already faces.

You are not changing your audience. You are going deeper into their life.

4/
Go narrow to build trust.
Go broad to build loyalty.

The mistake is doing it in the wrong order.

## Tweet 734
**Type:** Thread opener
How to build a waitlist that actually converts:

1/
A waitlist is not a form and a waiting room.

Done right, it is a market validation engine, a pre-built community, and a conversion amplifier.

2/
Four elements of a real waitlist:

Clear promise. Reason to join now. Regular updates. Referral mechanism.

Remove any one of these and conversion drops.

3/
The most important: weekly updates.

People who join and hear nothing for 60 days forget they joined.

Your progress emails are some of the highest-open emails you will ever send. Use them.

4/
The conversion insight people miss:

Waitlist members who engage with your updates are not just leads. They are buyers waiting for permission to pay you.

Give them the button.
