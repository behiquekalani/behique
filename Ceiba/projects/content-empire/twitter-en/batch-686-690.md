## Tweet 687
**Type:** Thread opener
Your offer isn't the problem. Your description of it is.

1/ Most solopreneurs describe what they do, not what the client gets. Here's the fix.
2/ Weak: "I offer brand strategy sessions." Strong: "You leave with a clear positioning statement and a 30-day content plan."
3/ Buyers purchase outcomes, not activities. Rewrite your offer around the transformation.
4/ Add a container: time, deliverables, process. "A 5-day sprint that delivers X" is tangible. "Consulting" is not.
5/ Specificity builds trust before the sale. The more specific your promise, the more believable it is.

## Tweet 690
**Type:** Thread opener
Pricing anchoring is the most underused pricing strategy in the creator economy.

1/ The first number your buyer sees sets the mental reference for everything else.
2/ This is why premium tiers exist. You're not supposed to buy the $500 option. It makes $200 feel like the smart choice.
3/ Never start with your lowest price. Start high, come down if needed. Going up feels like bait-and-switch.
4/ Bundle placement matters. A $97 guide looks different next to $297 than next to $27. Nothing changed except the anchor.
5/ Price is a frame. You control the frame. Use it intentionally.
