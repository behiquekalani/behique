# Twitter Batch 1362

---

**Tweet 1**
The reason your digital product isn't selling isn't your audience size.

It's your specificity.

"Productivity system" sells to nobody. "Daily planning system for ADHD creatives who can't stick to routines" sells to exactly the right person.

---

**Tweet 2**
A buyer needs to read your product description and think "that's exactly my problem."

If they think "that's interesting," you lose the sale.

---

**Tweet 3**
Change this:
"Grow your brand"

To this:
"Instagram content system for coaches who hate showing up on camera"

One sentence. One person. One outcome.

That's the whole job of your product description.

---

**Tweet 4**
Specificity is the cheapest form of marketing.

You don't need ads. You need to describe one person's situation so accurately they feel seen.

---

**Tweet 5**
Most digital products are built around a vague outcome.

Vague outcomes don't trigger purchases. Specific situations do.

Fix the description before you blame the audience.
