## Tweet 642
**Type:** Thread opener
**Hook:** The real reason most digital products don't sell has nothing to do with the product. (Thread)

1/ The product is rarely the problem. Most digital products contain real value. The gap is almost always in positioning and distribution.

2/ Positioning: if your product title and description don't immediately tell the buyer who this is for and what specific result it delivers, they won't buy. Vague products feel risky. Specific products feel like exactly what someone was already searching for.

3/ Distribution: a product with no traffic is a product that doesn't exist. Where are you sending people to the sales page? One blog post? One social media bio link? That is not distribution. That is hope.

4/ Social proof: people don't buy first. They buy after someone else bought. Before you launch to a general audience, get three to five beta users who will give you honest testimonials. Lead with their results, not your features.

5/ Frequency: most creators launch once and then move on when it doesn't instantly sell out. Products need repeated exposure. Most buyers need to see an offer multiple times before they act.

Fix positioning, build distribution, collect proof, repeat the offer. The product was probably fine.

## Tweet 645
**Type:** Thread opener
**Hook:** Here is how I think about building a one-person business that does not require me to be online every day. (Thread)

1/ Sell things that do not require your time to deliver. Digital products, templates, guides, pre-recorded courses. Every service you sell trades time for money. Every product you sell does not.

2/ Automate the distribution. Your content should be scheduled. Your email sequences should be automated. Your checkout should handle itself. You should not be manually involved in a single transaction.

3/ Build a content library that works without you. A year of content published and indexed creates a permanent search presence. That content generates traffic and revenue while you sleep.

4/ Create a simple funnel that runs on repeat. Traffic source, email opt-in, nurture sequence, product offer. That four-step loop, once built, does not need daily attention.

5/ The goal is not passive income. Passive is a myth. The goal is asynchronous income. You build it actively, then it operates without your constant presence.

Build systems, not routines. Systems continue. Routines require you.
