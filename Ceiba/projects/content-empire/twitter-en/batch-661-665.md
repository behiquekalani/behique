## Tweet 662
**Type:** Thread opener

The newsletter monetization timeline that most creators get wrong. (Thread)

1/ Most newsletter creators wait too long to monetize. They tell themselves they need more subscribers, more polish, a better product. The real threshold is not a number. It is trust.

2/ Months one through three: give genuine value with zero pitches. Solve one specific problem for one specific type of person every issue. That consistent, unrequested value is what builds the trust foundation.

3/ Month three to four: introduce one low-risk recommendation. Something you have used and benefited from personally. Readers can tell the difference between authentic recommendation and an affiliate grab.

4/ Month four onward: launch your own product directly to the list. By this point, your readers know your voice, your perspective, and your judgment. A warm list converts at a multiple of any cold channel.

5/ The question is never whether you have enough subscribers. It is whether your current subscribers trust you enough to take action on your recommendation. Build that. Revenue follows.

An engaged list of 500 beats a disengaged list of 5,000 every time.

## Tweet 665
**Type:** Thread opener

The four stages of a creator career. Most people think they are failing when they are just in stage one. (Thread)

1/ Stage one is exploration. You are finding your voice, your format, and your niche. Content is inconsistent because you are experimenting. Revenue is near zero. This stage is supposed to look messy. Do not quit here.

2/ Stage two is foundation. You have found something that resonates. You publish consistently for one audience in one format. A small but real following forms. First revenue appears. This is where most creators plateau if they do not push through.

3/ Stage three is traction. Your list is growing. Products sell with regularity. Early content is still producing results. Revenue is real but not yet fully sustainable. Patience is the main skill required here.

4/ Stage four is leverage. Products fund distribution. You delegate repeating tasks. The flywheel runs without constant manual effort. You work on the business, not just in it.

Each stage has its own job. Doing stage four work while you are in stage one is how people burn out and quit before anything compounds.

What stage are you in right now?
