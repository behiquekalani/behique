## Tweet 912

Thread: How to get good at anything, actually.

1/ Most people plateau early. Not because they lack talent. Because they practice in the zone where they are already competent.

2/ Getting good requires operating at the edge of your ability. Slightly harder than comfortable. With accurate feedback on what failed specifically.

3/ The feedback piece is critical. Without it you get better at doing the wrong thing consistently. Practice without feedback is just repetition.

4/ Find the specific part of your craft you are worst at. Not vaguely weak. The single skill you avoid because it makes you feel incompetent.

5/ Drill that. Not everything. That one thing. Twenty percent of your time on your biggest weakness for ninety days.

6/ Your ceiling will move somewhere you cannot currently see from where you are standing.

## Tweet 915

Thread: Why most people never build real wealth as creators.

1/ They confuse income with wealth. Income is what you earn. Wealth is what remains and compounds when you stop working.

2/ Most creator income stops when output stops. No new posts, no new revenue. That is a job with a better aesthetic.

3/ Wealth as a creator means building things that work while you sleep. Digital products, evergreen content, email lists, owned audiences.

4/ The shift is from creator to operator. You stop selling your time and start building systems that sell for you.

5/ This takes longer to set up. The first year feels slower than just selling services. Then the math changes permanently.

6/ Most people never make the shift because the transition period is uncomfortable and slow. That discomfort is the filter that creates the moat.

7/ Build the asset layer. Parallel to your income layer. Slow and unsexy. Compounding and permanent.
