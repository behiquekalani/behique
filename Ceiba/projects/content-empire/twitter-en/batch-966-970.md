## Tweet 967

Thread: Why clarity is not found, it is built. 1/

1/ Everyone says "I just need to figure out what I want."

But you cannot figure it out in your head. You figure it out by doing things.

2/ The person who waits for clarity before acting is like someone who waits to feel warm before starting a fire.

Motion is what generates the heat.

3/ Every action gives you data the imagination cannot.

You learn what actually energizes you. What drains you. What problems you are willing to sit with.

4/ That data is clarity. But you only get it through movement.

Start with a hypothesis. Test it. Update. Move again.

5/ The people who seem certain did not start certain.

They started uncertain and built certainty through iteration.

That option is available to you right now.

## Tweet 969

Thread: The thing that is stealing your best hours. 1/

1/ You do not need more time.

You need hours where your attention is actually yours.

Most people have neither.

2/ The average knowledge worker is interrupted every 11 minutes.

It takes 25 minutes to recover full focus after each interruption.

Do the math. Most of the workday is recovery, not work.

3/ This is not a willpower problem.

The apps on your phone were built by teams whose only job is to make you unable to stop.

You are not fighting a habit. You are fighting a billion-dollar machine.

4/ The solution is not discipline. It is architecture.

Delete the apps. Use separate devices. Build friction into the path between you and distraction.

Make focus the default, not the exception.

5/ Two protected hours of real attention is worth more than a full day of fragmented half-presence.

Start there.
