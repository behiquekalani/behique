## Tweet 633
**Type:** Thread opener
**Hook:** Most solopreneurs are underpaid not because they lack skill but because they lack this. (Thread)

1/ They don't know what they are actually selling. Skills are not the product. The outcome the skill produces is the product. Until you can name the outcome clearly, you will always underprice.

2/ They pitch effort instead of results. "I will spend 10 hours on your website" is a cost frame. "You will have a site that converts visitors into clients" is a value frame. Buyers pay for value, not hours.

3/ They follow the market rate instead of setting their own. Market rate is the average of what everyone charges. If you have above-average results, you should charge above the average rate.

4/ They discount under pressure. Every time you lower your price when someone pushes back, you teach them that your stated price was not real. Then they push back every time.

5/ They don't have case studies. Data beats persuasion. "My last three clients saw a 40% increase in..." is more powerful than any sales page.

Know your outcome. Price your outcome. Defend your price.

## Tweet 635
**Type:** Thread opener
**Hook:** The creator economy is full of people who look successful and aren't. Here is how to tell the difference. (Thread)

1/ Vanity metrics vs. revenue metrics. Follower count, views, and likes cost nothing and prove nothing. Ask what their monthly revenue is. Most won't answer.

2/ Launch noise vs. sustainable income. Many creators have one big launch a year and call it success. Real success is a business that produces without a launch event.

3/ Content volume vs. business infrastructure. Posting every day is not a business. It is a content habit. The business is what happens when someone clicks your link.

4/ Audience size vs. audience depth. A 500-person email list that opens at 60% is more valuable than a 50K following that ignores your product announcements.

5/ The tell: ask how they make money. If the answer is vague or requires future events to work, they are not as successful as they appear.

Judge by the infrastructure. Not the follower count.
