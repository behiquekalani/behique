## Tweet 942

Thread: Why you're undercharging and what to do about it 🧵

1/ Underpricing feels safer. Lower prices mean fewer reasons to say no. Less rejection risk. It makes emotional sense.

2/ What it actually does: signals low value. Attracts buyers who optimize for cost. Makes your economics unsustainable.

3/ Price is communication before the product is. A $10 offer and a $1000 offer on the same content produce different results, partly because price changes how seriously the buyer engages.

4/ The right question isn't "what will people pay?" It's "what is the outcome I deliver actually worth?" Price is a fraction of that number.

5/ If raising your prices loses buyers, you're losing the wrong buyers. Your real customers are still there.

## Tweet 945

Thread: The work no one sees 🧵

1/ Every outcome you want exists on the other side of work that feels invisible. There is no shortcut around the invisible period.

2/ The invisible period is where most people quit. Not because the work is too hard. Because it doesn't seem to be working.

3/ It is working. The lag between input and output in compounding work is structural, not a sign of failure.

4/ The people who make it through the invisible period share one thing: they find meaning in the work itself, not only in the outcome.

5/ If your motivation is entirely outcome-dependent, you will stop when outcomes are slow. Build a floor. A reason that survives the silence.

6/ The accounting catches up eventually. But you have to be there when it does.
