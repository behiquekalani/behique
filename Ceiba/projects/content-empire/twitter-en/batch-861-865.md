## Tweet 862

1/ One offer done right beats ten offers done halfway.

Here is the math:

2/ Creator A has 7 products.

Each gets 20% of their attention.

None of them convert well enough to be a primary revenue source.

They are busy and broke.

3/ Creator B has 1 product.

All content points to it.

All conversations lead to it.

All credibility supports it.

They are focused and profitable.

4/ Early in the build: one offer wins every time.

Not because variety is bad.

Because clarity is essential when trust is still being built.

5/ Your first 100 customers should all buy the same thing.

That gives you:
- Clean data on what works
- A reason to improve one thing deeply
- An audience that knows exactly what you do

6/ Expand after you have proven the first thing.

Not before.

The shelf with seven half-finished products looks full and produces nothing.

7/ Ship one thing. Sell it. Improve it.

Then add the second thing.

The sequence matters.

## Tweet 865

1/ How to build in public without it feeling like performance:

2/ Building in public fails when it becomes a highlight reel.

Only the wins. Only the growth numbers. Only the before-and-after.

Nobody trusts a highlight reel.

3/ Real building in public is documenting the process while it is still uncertain.

"I launched this yesterday and here is what happened."

"I made this decision and here is the logic. I might be wrong."

4/ The uncertainty is the value.

Readers are not looking for proof you succeeded.

They are looking for proof you are human and still going.

5/ Share the iteration, not just the result.

What changed from version 1 to version 2? Why?

What did you build that did not work and what did you learn?

6/ The creator who documents the messy middle builds more trust than the one who only shares the clean conclusion.

Because the messy middle is where your reader lives right now.

7/ Build in public like you are writing a letter to yourself from a year ago.

Honest. Specific. Useful.

That is the only version worth reading.
