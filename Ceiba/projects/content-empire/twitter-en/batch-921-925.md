## Tweet 922

Thread: What changing your mind publicly actually does to your credibility.

1/ Most people fear it. You said something. New evidence arrives. You update. But you stay quiet about the update because changing your mind feels like losing.

2/ The fear is backwards. The person who defends an outdated position to protect their consistency is the untrustworthy one. They are choosing appearance over truth.

3/ The person who says "I was wrong about this, here is why I changed my view" is demonstrating the exact quality you should want in someone you follow.

4/ They are still thinking. Still honest. Still willing to be corrected. That is rare. It builds more real trust than any consistent position.

5/ Your audience does not need you to be right. They need you to be honest. Those overlap most of the time but not always.

6/ Change your mind when you have reason to. Say so when it matters. Watch what happens to the people who were actually paying attention.

## Tweet 925

Thread: How to stop second-guessing every decision.

1/ Second-guessing is not a thinking problem. It is a values problem. When you know what matters most to you, most decisions become obvious.

2/ The decisions that paralyze you are usually the ones where two real values are in conflict. Safety vs growth. Loyalty vs ambition. Speed vs quality.

3/ You cannot resolve that conflict with more information. You resolve it by deciding which value wins in this category for this season of your life.

4/ That decision made in advance is what allows you to stop relitigating things every time they come up. The meta-decision reduces the cognitive load of every downstream decision.

5/ Write down your value hierarchy for the domain where you second-guess most. Which matters more when they conflict. Be honest, not aspirational.

6/ Then when the next decision arrives, you already have an answer. You are not deciding. You are executing a prior decision.

7/ Most indecision is not about the choice in front of you. It is about the deeper question you have been avoiding answering.
