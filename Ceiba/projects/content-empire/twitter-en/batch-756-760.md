## Tweet 758
**Type:** Thread opener
How to build a product bundle that actually increases revenue. (Thread)

1/ Bundles work because of perceived value math. Three products at $27 each bundled for $57 feels like $81 of value at a $24 discount. The buyer wins. Your margin per transaction also improves. Both things are true at once.

2/ The positioning shift is the real power. A bundle says: this is a complete system. That changes the buyer's question from "which one should I get" to "how do I get all of this." That is a fundamentally better buying conversation.

3/ Bundle by outcome, not by topic. "The freelancer income growth bundle" (pricing guide plus client retention system plus consulting launch guide) sells better than "three of my best guides." Outcome-first packaging builds a story.

4/ Add one connecting piece. An intro doc, a roadmap, a sequence that shows how to use all three together. This raises perceived value without creating an entirely new product. Takes 2 hours max.

5/ Price at 30 to 40 percent below the sum of individual prices. Launch as a limited-time offer. Urgency is real when you mean it.

## Tweet 760
**Type:** Thread opener
Creator energy management. Why your schedule is not the problem. (Thread)

1/ Every creator has experienced sitting down to write for three hours and producing almost nothing. Then with 45 minutes before a call, writing something that resonates with thousands. The difference is almost never time. It is energy state.

2/ Identify your actual high-energy windows. Not when you think you should be productive. When you actually are. For most people it is a 2 to 3 hour window, usually in the morning. That window is for creation only.

3/ Design the conditions that reliably get you into that state. Temperature. Music or silence. Where you sit. What you did in the hour before. These variables are real. Experiment with them seriously.

4/ Protect your recovery. Creators who burn out are often failing to recover, not overworking. One full day per week with no creative output obligations is not laziness. It is maintenance.

5/ Energy is the asset. Time is the container. Managing your calendar without managing your energy is like optimizing a car's route without checking if it has fuel.
