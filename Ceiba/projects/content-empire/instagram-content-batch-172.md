## Post 2 — The Specificity Test

**Hook:** The more specific your lead magnet, the more people download it.

**Slide 1:** The more specific your lead magnet, the more people download it.

**Slide 2:** "Free guide to social media marketing"
vs.
"5 Instagram captions that get DMs from ideal clients"

One is vague. One is specific.
The specific one wins every time.

**Slide 3:** Why specificity works:
- Specific problems feel urgent
- Specific solutions feel achievable
- Specific titles feel relevant to the right people

Vague lead magnets attract nobody because they promise everything.

**Slide 4:** The specificity framework:
1. Who is struggling?
2. With what exact problem?
3. What is the fastest path to solving it?
4. What tangible result can they expect?

**Slide 5:** Test your lead magnet title with this question:
"Would someone text this to a friend who has exactly this problem?"

If yes, the title is specific enough.
If maybe, make it more specific.
If no, start over.

**Slide 6:** Build a lead magnet so specific people share it.

Link in bio.

**Caption:** Generic lead magnets get generic results. The more you try to appeal to everyone, the more you end up resonating with nobody.

The counterintuitive truth about lead magnets is that narrowing the promise increases conversions. When you say "free guide to Instagram marketing," you are competing with a thousand other free guides. When you say "the exact 3-step process I used to go from 200 to 2,000 followers in 60 days," you are giving someone a reason to choose yours specifically.

The goal is not to attract the most downloads. It is to attract the right downloads. People who download because your lead magnet solved their exact problem are the same people who buy when you launch a product.

Specificity is not just a copywriting trick. It is how you pre-qualify your list.

## Post 4 — The Opt-In Page That Does Not Convert

**Hook:** Your lead magnet is not the problem. Your opt-in page is.

**Slide 1:** Your lead magnet is not the problem. Your opt-in page is.

**Slide 2:** The average opt-in page that does not convert has:
- A vague headline with no promise
- A long description nobody reads
- A form that asks for too much information
- A CTA button that says "Subscribe"

**Slide 3:** The opt-in page that converts has:
- A headline that states the exact benefit
- Three bullet points that back up the promise
- A form that only asks for email (maybe first name)
- A CTA button that says what they get

**Slide 4:** The headline is 80% of the conversion.
If your headline does not make someone think
"yes, that is exactly what I need right now,"
nothing else on the page matters.

**Slide 5:** High-converting CTA button copy:
- "Send me the checklist" (better than "Subscribe")
- "Get the free template" (better than "Sign up")
- "Yes, I want the guide" (better than "Download")

The button copy should complete the sentence:
"I want to..."

**Slide 6:** Fix your opt-in page before you drive any more traffic.
Email List Accelerator at behike.gumroad.com
Link in bio.

**Caption:** Most people who struggle to build an email list think they have a traffic problem. They do not. They have a conversion problem. Traffic is hitting their opt-in page and leaving because nothing on the page gives them a reason to stay.

The opt-in page is not where you explain everything about your business. It is where you make one clear promise, back it up with three bullet points, and make it as easy as possible to say yes.

The simplest opt-in pages convert the best. A headline, a subheadline, three bullets, an email field, and a button. That is it. Every additional element is a potential reason for someone to get distracted and leave.

Strip it down to the promise and the form.

## Post 5 — The Distribution Problem Nobody Talks About

**Hook:** You built the lead magnet. Nobody is finding it.

**Slide 1:** You built the lead magnet. Nobody is finding it.

**Slide 2:** The silent killer of email lists is not a bad lead magnet.
It is invisible distribution.

You cannot build a list if nobody knows the opt-in page exists.

**Slide 3:** The 5 places your lead magnet should live:
1. Instagram bio link
2. Pinned post on every platform
3. Every piece of content you create (mention it in captions)
4. Your email signature
5. As the CTA at the end of every long-form piece

**Slide 4:** The content-to-lead-magnet connection:
Every post, video, or thread you create should have a logical path to your lead magnet.

Content creates curiosity.
The lead magnet solves the specific problem the content raised.

**Slide 5:** The promotion schedule that works:
- Mention your lead magnet in 1 of every 3 posts
- Pin it at the top of your profile
- Include it in every welcome email (let them refer friends)
- Add it to your bio across every platform you use
