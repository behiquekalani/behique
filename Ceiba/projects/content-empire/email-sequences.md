### Email 2 - Day 2

**Subject:** everyone makes this mistake with AI

**Body:**

Most people use AI to skip the thinking part.

They paste a vague prompt, get a vague answer, and then blame the tool. I did this for months. I would ask ChatGPT to "write me a business plan" and get back something that sounded smart but meant nothing.

The shift happened when I stopped using AI to replace my thinking and started using it to sharpen it.

Instead of "write me a business plan," I started with "here are 3 revenue models I'm considering. Poke holes in each one." Completely different output. Because I did the thinking first, then let AI stress-test it.

One quick thing you can try today: before your next AI prompt, write down what you actually think the answer is. Then ask the AI. Compare the two. You'll learn more in that gap than from any tutorial.

I wrote a full chapter about this in The Behike Method. The whole framework for using AI as a thinking partner instead of a replacement. If you're curious, it's here: [LINK]

But honestly, just try the exercise above first. It costs nothing and takes 2 minutes.
