# TikTok Batch 158 — The Minimum Viable Product

## Script 1: Stop Building, Start Shipping
**Hook (0-3s):** The product you keep perfecting is losing money every day.
**Body:** There is a version of your product that is good enough to sell right now. Not perfect. Good enough. The gap between good enough and perfect costs you weeks or months of revenue you never get back. The MVP is not about shipping garbage. It is about testing assumptions before you invest more. Does anyone actually want this? Will they pay for it? You cannot answer those questions by building more. You answer them by selling.
**CTA:** Follow if you have been building longer than selling.
**On-screen text:** ["Good enough ships. Perfect doesn't.", "Every day delayed = lost revenue", "Test assumptions first", "Stop building. Start selling."]

---

## Script 2: What MVP Actually Means
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people get the MVP concept completely wrong.
**Body:** Minimum viable product does not mean the worst version of your idea. It means the smallest version that tests the core assumption. If your idea is "people will pay to learn X from me," the MVP is not a 40-lesson course. It is a one-hour workshop or a PDF that teaches one outcome. You are testing whether people pay, not whether you can build a lot. Minimum is about scope. Viable is about whether it works.
**CTA:** Save this before you build your next product.
**On-screen text:** ["MVP ≠ worst version", "Smallest test of core assumption", "Scope down, not quality down", "Viable = does it work"]

---

## Script 3: The $27 PDF That Taught Everything
**Hook (0-3s):** A $27 PDF taught me more about my audience than six months of content.
**Body:** I posted consistently for months. Got followers. Got engagement. Made zero sales. Then I wrote a 15-page PDF, priced it at $27, and put it in my bio. First week: three sales. That told me more than any analytics dashboard. Who bought it. What language they used in their confirmation emails. What they were actually trying to solve. The product revealed the market. You learn more from one sale than from a hundred likes.
**CTA:** Follow if you want to build things people actually buy.
**On-screen text:** ["One sale > 100 likes", "Product reveals the market", "$27 PDF as market research", "Build it. Sell it. Learn."]

---

## Script 4: Validation Before Creation
**Hook (0-3s):** Sell it before you build it. This is not a trick.
**Body:** Pre-selling is a legitimate business strategy used by every major company. You describe the outcome. You set a price. You see if people buy. If they do, you build it. If they do not, you saved yourself weeks of work on something nobody wanted. This is not deceptive if you are transparent about the timeline. A waitlist, a presale, a founding member offer. These are all ways to validate before you invest. Build what people already want.
**CTA:** Comment "presale" if you want a breakdown of how to do this.
**On-screen text:** ["Sell first. Build second.", "Pre-selling = validation", "Build what people already want", "Waitlist → presale → product"]

---

## Script 5: One Outcome, One Product
**Hook (0-3s):** Your product is too big. That is why it does not sell.
**Body:** The more outcomes you promise, the less credible each one becomes. A product that does everything converts worse than a product that does one thing extremely well. Pick the most specific, most painful problem your audience has. Solve that one problem completely. Price it accordingly. A guide titled "How to Get Your First 100 Email Subscribers in 7 Days" sells better than "The Complete Guide to Email Marketing." Specificity is trust.
**CTA:** Follow for the product framework that actually converts.
**On-screen text:** ["One problem, one product", "Everything = nothing", "Specificity = trust", "What's the ONE outcome?"]

---

## Script 6: Speed Is the Strategy
**Hook (0-3s):** The creator who ships faster wins. Every time.
**Body:** Two people have the same idea. One spends three months building the perfect version. One ships a rough version in two weeks and starts collecting feedback. Six months later, the second person has a refined product, real testimonials, and revenue. The first person still has not launched. The market does not reward the best product. It rewards the first good product. Speed is not recklessness. It is competitive advantage.
**CTA:** Follow and stop letting perfect be the enemy of shipped.
**On-screen text:** ["Ship faster, win faster", "Market rewards first good product", "Rough + shipped > perfect + delayed", "Speed is competitive advantage"]

---

## Script 7: The Feedback Loop Is the Product
**Hook (0-3s):** Your first customers are building your second product for you.
**Body:** Every early buyer tells you exactly what was missing, what was confusing, and what they needed more of. That feedback is the brief for version two. Most creators ignore this because they are embarrassed the first version was not perfect. That embarrassment costs them the most valuable data they could collect. Lean into early feedback. Ask directly. What did you need that was not here? The answer builds your next offer.
**CTA:** Save this. You will need it when you launch.
**On-screen text:** ["Early buyers = free consultants", "V2 comes from V1 feedback", "Ask: what was missing?", "Feedback IS the product roadmap"]

---

## Script 8: No Audience Required for Your First Sale
**Hook (0-3s):** You do not need 10,000 followers to make your first sale.
**Body:** Most people wait until they have an audience before they build a product. This is backwards. The product teaches you who your audience is. You can make your first sale with 200 followers if those 200 people are the right people and you solved a real problem. Stop using "I do not have an audience yet" as a reason not to build. Build first. The audience finds the product. Or the product finds the audience.
**CTA:** Follow if you are waiting to start.
**On-screen text:** ["200 right followers > 10K wrong ones", "Product teaches you your audience", "Stop waiting for permission", "Build first. Audience follows."]
