## Script 72.1 — The YouTube Short That Gets Views

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Most YouTube Shorts fail in the first three seconds. Here is what the ones that do not have in common."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: THE RETENTION CURVE DECIDES EVERYTHING]
YouTube's algorithm measures one thing above all others: average view duration. If people leave early, the video dies. So the first three seconds have one job: make leaving feel like a mistake.

[on-screen: WHAT WORKS IN THE FIRST 3 SECONDS]
The hook has to create a gap. A question the viewer cannot answer without watching. Not "here is what I will teach you." More like "this is the thing you are doing wrong right now."

[on-screen: CREATE AN INFORMATION GAP]
The body has to deliver on that tension immediately. One idea, not five. The more you compress the value, the higher the retention.

[on-screen: ONE IDEA, COMPRESSED]
The last two seconds close the loop and plant a new question. That is what gets replays. And replays are the metric YouTube cannot ignore.

[on-screen: CLOSE THE LOOP, PLANT THE NEXT QUESTION]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Test one Short this week using this structure. Comment your niche below and I will give you a hook."

**Caption:** YouTube Shorts retention is won in the first 3 seconds. Here is what that actually looks like. (96 chars)

## Script 72.2 — How to Write Hooks for Any Platform

**Hook (0-3s):**
"One hook formula works across TikTok, Twitter, YouTube, and newsletters. Let me show you it."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: THE UNIVERSAL HOOK FORMULA]
Every high-performing hook does one of four things. It challenges a belief. It promises a specific outcome. It opens an information gap. Or it triggers identity.

[on-screen: 4 HOOK TYPES]
Belief challenge: "Everything you learned about X is wrong."
Specific outcome: "How I got X result in Y days without Z."
Information gap: "The thing nobody tells you about X."
Identity trigger: "If you do X, this video is for you."

[on-screen: BELIEF / OUTCOME / GAP / IDENTITY]
The mistake most creators make is writing hooks about themselves. "I tried X for 30 days." That works sometimes, but identity and gap hooks outperform it consistently.

[on-screen: HOOKS ABOUT THE AUDIENCE OUTPERFORM HOOKS ABOUT YOU]
The platform changes. The psychology behind the hook does not. These four types work because human attention has not changed.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Save this. Write one hook from each category today. Your next viral post is somewhere in those four."

**Caption:** Platform changes. Hook psychology does not. Four types. One formula. Use all of them. (88 chars)

## Script 72.3 — The 3-2-1 Newsletter Format

**Hook (0-3s):**
"The reason most newsletters fail has nothing to do with the content. It is the format."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: FORMAT IS THE PRODUCT]
Your newsletter is not failing because your ideas are bad. It is failing because you are asking your readers to do too much cognitive work.

[on-screen: THE 3-2-1 FORMAT]
Three ideas worth knowing. Two tools or resources. One question to sit with.

[on-screen: 3 IDEAS + 2 TOOLS + 1 QUESTION]
That is it. Under five hundred words. Scannable in ninety seconds. The constraint forces you to curate instead of dump.

[on-screen: CONSTRAINT CREATES CURATION]
James Clear built one of the largest newsletters in the world on this structure. It is not the only format, but it is the one that removes the friction of the blank page and the friction of the long read simultaneously.

[on-screen: REMOVE FRICTION ON BOTH SIDES]
Your reader does not have time. Give them three good things, two useful things, and one thing to think about. They will come back for that.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Try the 3-2-1 format for your next issue. Drop your newsletter topic below. I will help you fill the first one."

**Caption:** Most newsletters fail because of format, not content. 3 ideas. 2 tools. 1 question. That is it. (95 chars)

## Script 72.4 — Building on Borrowed Platforms vs Owning Your List

**Hook (0-3s):**
"TikTok can ban your account tomorrow and there is nothing you can do about it. Here is what that means for your business."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: RENTED LAND VS OWNED LAND]
Every platform you post on is rented land. Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, LinkedIn. None of them owe you your audience. The algorithm changes, the reach drops, the account gets flagged.

[on-screen: THE PLATFORM IS NOT YOUR BUSINESS]
Your email list is different. You own it. Nobody can turn it off. No algorithm decides who sees it. You pay to send, you get the send.

[on-screen: EMAIL = OWNED AUDIENCE]
The strategy is not to abandon platforms. Platforms are how you get discovered. But every platform post should have one goal: move people one step closer to your list.

[on-screen: PLATFORMS = DISCOVERY. EMAIL = OWNERSHIP]
One link in bio. One reason to subscribe. One thing they get when they do. That is the whole migration strategy.

[on-screen: ONE LINK. ONE REASON. ONE REWARD]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"If you do not have an email list yet, this is your sign. Start with one landing page. That is all you need."

**Caption:** Platforms are rented land. Your email list is owned. Everything you post should feed the list. (95 chars)

## Script 72.5 — The Content Calendar Myth

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Your content calendar is not keeping you consistent. It is keeping you busy."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: PLANNING IS NOT THE SAME AS EXECUTING]
Most creators spend more time building content calendars than creating content. The calendar becomes the productivity theater that replaces the work.

[on-screen: THE CALENDAR IS THEATER]
The real constraint is not having a plan. It is having a system that produces content faster than your idea backlog drains.

[on-screen: SYSTEM > SCHEDULE]
What works instead: a topic bank and a weekly sprint. Write down every question your audience asks. Every comment that sparked an idea. Every observation you had this week. That is your calendar.

[on-screen: TOPIC BANK + WEEKLY SPRINT]
Monday: pick five topics from the bank. Tuesday through Friday: one piece per day. Saturday: schedule. Sunday: refill the bank.

[on-screen: THE ACTUAL SYSTEM]
That is less planning. More output. And the content is better because it comes from real observations, not a spreadsheet from three weeks ago.

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Start a topic bank today. One note. Any app. Write every question you get asked this week. That is your next thirty pieces of content."

**Caption:** The content calendar is theater. A topic bank and a weekly sprint is the actual system. (90 chars)
