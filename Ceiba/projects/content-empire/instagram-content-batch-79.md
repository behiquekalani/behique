**Post 1**
**Hook:** The first 90 days of your solopreneur business should not look like a business at all.
**Caption:** No branding. No logo. No perfect website. The first 90 days are a research project. Your job is to talk to as many potential customers as possible, figure out what problem they actually have, and build the smallest possible thing that solves it. Most people spend 90 days building infrastructure for a business they have not validated yet. Do the validation first. Infrastructure is cheap after you know what you are building. It is expensive before.

**Post 2**
**Hook:** Picking a niche in day one is the most important decision you will make.
**Caption:** You cannot market to everyone and you cannot build for everyone. The first decision in the first 90 days is: who is the specific person I am building for. Not a demographic. A person. What do they struggle with. What do they search for at 11pm. What would they pay to fix. The more specific your answer, the clearer your content, the tighter your product, the easier your sales. The niche does not trap you. It launches you. Everything else gets easier after you answer this one question.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #nichedown #behikeai #startingout #onlinebusiness #buildinpublic #creatoreconomy #businessstrategy

**Post 3**
**Hook:** The first product you build should take no more than two weeks.
**Caption:** If your first digital product takes three months to build, you are building the wrong product. The first product is not meant to be your best work. It is meant to be your first test. A PDF guide, a short course, a template, a checklist. Something that solves a specific problem for a specific person, priced between $15 and $47, and shippable in two weeks. You learn more from one real sale than from months of planning. Build fast, ship early, improve based on who actually buys.
**Hashtags:** #digitalproducts #solopreneur #behikeai #onlinebusiness #buildinpublic #firstproduct #creatoreconomy #entrepreneurship

**Post 4**
**Hook:** Content before product is not a strategy. It is a prerequisite.
**Caption:** The 90-day window is when you should be creating content before you have anything to sell. Not because content is the business, but because content is how you validate demand without spending money. If you write 30 posts about a specific problem and nobody engages, you have learned something valuable before you invested in building a product. If posts consistently get saves, shares, and DMs asking for more, you have just been handed your product roadmap for free. The content is the cheapest research tool you have.
**Hashtags:** #contentcreator #solopreneur #behikeai #contentmarketing #audiencebuilding #buildinpublic #onlinebusiness #creatoreconomy

**Post 5**
**Hook:** In 90 days, your email list matters more than your follower count.
**Caption:** Followers are rented. Email subscribers are owned. In the first 90 days, your primary goal on every platform is to move people from a platform you do not control onto a list you do. A free resource, a short guide, a useful checklist. Give it away in exchange for an email address. 500 email subscribers who opted in for a specific reason are worth more than 10,000 Instagram followers who stumbled onto your page. When the algorithm changes, and it will, your list is the only thing that survives.

**Post 6**
**Hook:** The biggest 90-day mistake is trying to build everything at once.
**Caption:** Platform on Instagram. Build the website. Start the newsletter. Launch the podcast. Build the product. Film the YouTube video. All in month one. The result is six things at 30 percent instead of one thing at 100 percent. The first 90 days have one job: find your person, find their problem, and build the first thing that solves it. Pick one platform. Pick one product. Build one audience. The rest comes later, after you have proof of concept and something real to expand from.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #focus #behikeai #buildinpublic #startingout #onlinebusiness #creatoreconomy #entrepreneurship

**Post 7**
**Hook:** The first 30 days are for listening, not talking.
**Caption:** Before you have a following, the most valuable thing you can do is consume with intent. Follow the people your target audience follows. Read the comments on posts in your niche. Watch what questions people ask in forums and communities. Search for your topic on Reddit, YouTube, and Twitter. The patterns you find in those first 30 days will shape everything. The language people use to describe their problem is the language you should use in your content. You are not researching the competition. You are studying the customer.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #marketresearch #behikeai #onlinebusiness #startingout #contentcreator #buildinpublic #businessstrategy

**Post 8**
**Hook:** One offer done well is worth more than five offers done poorly.
**Caption:** The temptation in year one is to create as many revenue streams as possible as fast as possible. One product, then two, then a membership, then a course, then a coaching tier. The result is a scattered business that converts poorly because nothing is built out well enough to do its job. One offer, with a clear page, clear messaging, and a clear reason to buy, will outperform five mediocre offers every time. The 90-day goal is depth, not breadth. Get one thing working before you build the next.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #onlinebusiness #behikeai #buildinpublic #productlaunch #creatoreconomy #digitalproducts #businessstrategy

**Post 9**
**Hook:** The best investment in your first 90 days costs nothing but time.
**Caption:** In the early stage of a solopreneur business, capital is rarely the constraint. Time and attention are. The best investments you can make are free: learn in public, document your process, build genuine relationships with other creators in your space, and study what is working for people one or two levels ahead of you. The pattern matching you do in the first 90 days pays dividends for years. Most of what you need to know is already visible if you pay close enough attention.

**Post 10**
**Hook:** By day 90, you should have proof, not polish.
**Caption:** The goal by the end of 90 days is not a beautiful business. It is evidence. Evidence that your niche has a real problem. Evidence that your content resonates. Evidence that at least one person will pay for your solution. That evidence, however small, is the foundation everything else is built on. A business with proof and no polish can grow. A business with polish and no proof is just an expensive hobby. Chase the proof first. The polish comes after you know what you are polishing. Follow @behikeai for the 90-day build in real time.
