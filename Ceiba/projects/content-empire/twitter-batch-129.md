## Tweet 3 (thread)
1/ The four layers of a solopreneur operating system:

Most people only build one of them and wonder why nothing scales.

2/ Layer 1: Capture.
Every idea, task, and commitment goes into one place immediately.

Not your memory. Not your "I'll get to it later." One system.

3/ Layer 2: Process.
Once a week, everything in capture gets sorted.
What gets done? What gets delegated? What gets deleted?

Weekly review is not optional. It's the maintenance protocol.

4/ Layer 3: Execute.
Daily, you work from a list of 1-3 things that move the main needle.

Not a list of 20 things. Not everything in the backlog. Three things.

5/ Layer 4: Review.
Monthly, you evaluate if the operating system itself is working.

Where are you losing time? Where are you making decisions you've already made?

This layer is what most solopreneurs never build.

## Tweet 4
The operating system for one person is not a stripped-down version of what a company uses.

It's built for a completely different constraint: you cannot lose context between sessions.

Your system must remember what you decided so your brain doesn't have to.

## Tweet 5

## Tweet 6

## Tweet 7 (thread)
1/ Why solopreneur operating systems fail in month 2:

Not because the system was bad. Because it was built for who you were in month 1.

2/ A good operating system evolves.

The task management you needed when you had one project is not the task management you need when you have four.

The capture method that worked when you had 3 hours a day is not the method that works when you have 1.

3/ Build a review cycle into the system itself.

Every 30 days: what's working, what's not, what's changed about your constraints.

The system should upgrade with you.

## Tweet 8
One rule for a solopreneur operating system:

Never hold the same thought twice.

If you've decided something, document it. If you've solved a problem, write the solution down.

The second time you hold the same thought, you're wasting your clearest thinking on administration.

## Tweet 9
The bottleneck in a one-person business is almost never skills.

It's decision fatigue.

Every undocumented process, every re-decided decision, every unorganized task list costs you hours of judgment per week.

## Tweet 10 (thread)
1/ The minimum viable solopreneur OS:

2/ Inbox: one place where everything lands (I use a single notes app). Nothing lives anywhere else until it's processed.

3/ Weekly list: every Sunday, 3 outcomes for the week. Not tasks. Outcomes.

"Finish the product page" is an outcome. "Work on website" is not.

4/ Daily 3: every morning, the 3 things from the weekly list that happen today.

If a task isn't on the daily 3, it doesn't get touched. Full stop.

5/ End-of-week log: 5 minutes. What shipped? What didn't? What do I carry forward?

This is the only metric that matters: did the work move forward or not?

The rest is commentary.
