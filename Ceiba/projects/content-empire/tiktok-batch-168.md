## Script 3: The Difference Between a Niche and an Audience

**Hook (0-3s):** Your niche and your audience are not the same thing. Confusing them is costing you.

**Body:**
A niche is a problem space. An audience is the people who have that problem.

Creators who say "my niche is productivity" have defined a problem space so wide it's useless.

Creators who say "my audience is first-year freelancers who can't manage client workloads" have something specific enough to actually build around.

The niche framework that works: problem plus person plus context.

What is the problem? Who has it? When and where does it show up?

"Time management" is a niche. "Time management for new nurses in their first six months on the floor" is an audience.

Building content for an audience converts. Building content for a niche topic does not.

Stop thinking in topics. Start thinking in people.

## Script 5: The 3-Layer Niche Test

**Hook (0-3s):** Run this test before you commit to any niche.

**Body:**
Layer one: can you describe your ideal customer without using a job title or age range? If the only way you can describe them is "25-35 year olds interested in business," your niche is still too vague.

Layer two: can you name a specific problem they experience on a Tuesday at 2pm? Not a general struggle. A specific moment of pain. If you can't, you don't know them well enough yet.

Layer three: would your customer be embarrassed to admit they have this problem, or are they actively searching for solutions to it? Active searchers are buyers. Passive sufferers are not.

Pass all three layers and you have a real niche.

Fail any one of them and you're still in topic territory.

Do the test on your niche right now. I'll wait.

## Script 6: Why Broad Niches Die on TikTok

**Hook (0-3s):** The algorithm cannot amplify what it cannot categorize.

**Body:**
TikTok's algorithm pushes content to people who have shown interest in similar content.

If your niche is "personal development," the algorithm doesn't know where to send you. That category is too large. There's no clear cluster of people who want "general self-improvement."

But if your content is consistently about "ADHD productivity for college students," the algorithm finds the cluster. It sends your content to people who've watched other ADHD productivity content. Your first hundred views are from exactly the right people.

Their engagement signals more reach into the same cluster.

Broad content gets broad distribution that converts nobody. Specific content gets specific distribution that converts buyers.

The algorithm is not the enemy. A vague niche is.

Give the algorithm a category it can work with.

## Script 8: From Broad to Specific: A Real Example

**Hook (0-3s):** Let me show you how to niche down in real time.

**Body:**

That is the broadest possible niche. It helps no one.

Ending point: "I help 9-to-5 workers build a repeatable product research system for drop-shipping so they stop burning money on untested inventory."

That is a niche. It speaks to one person. That person knows it's for them the second they read it.

The same exercise works for any starting point. Keep asking "who specifically" and "what exactly" until you can't go more specific.
