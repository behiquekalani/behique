# YouTube Shorts EN — batch-1257

**Topic:** How to price your first digital product
**Duration:** 55-70 seconds

---

Pricing your first digital product correctly changes everything. Here's the framework.

**The wrong approach:**
Pricing by how long it took to make. "I spent three hours, so it should be $30." That's not how value works. Value is determined by the result the buyer gets, not the time the creator spent.

**The right approach:**
Price based on the transformation. Ask yourself: what is this product worth to the person who uses it? How much time does it save? How much money could it help them make? How much pain does it eliminate?

If your product helps someone avoid a $500 mistake, $47 is cheap. If it saves someone ten hours of research, $37 is a bargain.

**The practical pricing tiers:**
- Simple templates and checklists: $7 to $27
- Guides and frameworks: $27 to $67
- Mini courses with video: $47 to $197
- Full courses with community: $97 to $497

Start in the middle of your tier. Test. Raise the price if it converts well.

Most creators leave money on the table by pricing too low.
