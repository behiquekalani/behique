**Topic:** Productivity — the weekly review
**Duration:** ~62 seconds

One habit separates people who drift from people who compound. The weekly review.

Most people end the week without knowing what they actually accomplished. They're busy but not intentional. They start Monday without a clear direction from the previous week.

Here's the weekly review in three parts. It takes 20 minutes.

Part one: review. What did you complete this week? What moved forward? What got stuck? Spend five minutes just looking at what happened without judgment.

Part two: assess. Of the things that got stuck, why? Was it a priority problem, an energy problem, or an external block? This is where you learn from the week instead of just repeating it.

Part three: plan. What are the three most important things for next week? Which day will you do each? Put them on the calendar now, not Monday morning.

Done. Twenty minutes. That's the whole system.

The people who do this consistently don't just get more done. They get the right things done, which is a completely different outcome.
