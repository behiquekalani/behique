## Short 1236: How AI Is Changing What Skills Are Worth Learning
**Hook (0-3s):** AI is not replacing your skills. It is changing which ones matter.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:** The skills that AI can fully replace are the ones that involve repetitive execution without judgment. Basic data entry, template filling, simple summaries. The skills that AI multiplies are the ones that involve taste, judgment, and direction. Knowing what to build. Knowing what the audience needs. Knowing which direction to take a piece of content. AI is a tool that executes fast. You still have to decide what to execute. The most valuable person in an AI world is not the best coder or the best writer. It is the best thinker who knows how to direct execution tools.
**Thumbnail text:** Which skills actually matter in an AI world
**Description:** The conversation about AI replacing jobs misses the more important point. AI changes the value of different skills. Execution skills are being commoditized. Judgment, taste, and direction skills are becoming more valuable. This short breaks down exactly which skills are worth developing in a world where AI handles more and more execution work. If you are learning new skills right now, this video will help you prioritize the right ones. #AI #skills #futureofwork #behike #artificialintelligence #learning #creatoreconomy

## Short 1238: The Harsh Truth About Growing Slowly
**Hook (0-3s):** You are growing slowly because you are making safe content.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** Safe content does not get shared. People share things that surprise them, change how they think, or say something they have been feeling but could not articulate. Safe content checks a box. It does not create a feeling. The creators who grow fast are the ones willing to take a real position. Not controversial for the sake of it. But genuinely specific, genuinely opinionated, genuinely theirs. The more you water down your perspective to not offend anyone, the less your content is worth sharing. Growth requires a point of view that makes someone say: finally, someone said it.
**Thumbnail text:** Why safe content keeps you stuck small
**Description:** If your content is growing slowly despite consistent posting, safe content is probably the reason. Safe content avoids controversy, takes no real position, and speaks to everyone. But speaking to everyone means resonating with no one. The creators who see fast, organic growth are the ones willing to say something specific, something opinionated, something that makes a certain type of person feel seen. ## Short 1239: How to Price Your Work Without Underselling
**Hook (0-3s):** You are probably charging less than half of what the market will pay.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:** Most creators and freelancers price based on what they feel comfortable asking, not based on the value they deliver. Those are very different numbers. If your work saves a client ten hours a week, the value is in the hundreds of hours saved per year. Your price should reflect a fraction of that value, not your hourly discomfort level. The fix is simple. Stop anchoring your price to your time. Anchor it to the outcome. What does the client get? What is that worth to them? Price at a fraction of that value. You will be higher than what you were charging. And you will be fair.
**Thumbnail text:** You are charging too little for your work
**Description:** Underpricing is the most common mistake freelancers, consultants, and digital product creators make. It comes from pricing based on what feels comfortable rather than what the market actually values. This short breaks down a simple framework for shifting from time-based pricing to value-based pricing. When you anchor your price to the outcome you deliver instead of the hours you spend, your rates go up and your clients get a better deal too. The math actually works better for everyone. #pricing #freelancing #consulting #onlinebusiness #behike #creatoreconomy #valuebased
