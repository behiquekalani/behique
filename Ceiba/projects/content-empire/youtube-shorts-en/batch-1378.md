# YouTube Shorts EN — batch-1378

**Topic:** What separates people who reach their goals

**Script:**

What separates people who reach their goals from those who don't? Three things.

Thing one: they write the goal down with a specific date. "Get fit" is not a goal. "Drop 15 pounds by June 1st" is. The specificity tells your brain what to focus on.

Thing two: they track progress weekly. Without data, you don't know if what you're doing is working. A weekly check-in takes five minutes and catches problems early.

Thing three: they have a bias toward action. They don't wait for perfect conditions. They start with what they have and adjust as they go.

Goals aren't achieved by people with more talent. They are achieved by people with better habits around them. Subscribe for the system that makes goals stick.
