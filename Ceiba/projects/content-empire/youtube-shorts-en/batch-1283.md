**Topic:** Solopreneur — pricing your services right
**Duration:** ~62 seconds

Most solopreneurs undercharge. Here's how to price your services correctly.

The mistake: pricing based on what feels comfortable to ask for. That number is almost always too low because it's filtered through your own relationship with money, not the actual value you deliver.

Point one: price based on outcomes, not hours. If your work helps a client make $10,000, charging $500 is leaving money on the table. Price at 10 to 20 percent of the value created.

Point two: do a market research check. Find three to five competitors. Look at their pricing. Your rate should be in that range unless you have a specific reason to be lower (just starting) or higher (proven results).

Point three: raise your prices every six months. If you're fully booked, that's a sign your price is too low. Demand is high, supply is you, increase the price.

Point four: always anchor with your highest offer first. When you show a client three options at $2,000, $3,500, and $5,000, the $2,000 suddenly seems affordable. Anchoring works.

Charge what the work is worth. Not what you're comfortable asking for.
