## Script 710

**Title:** Why You're Not Lazy, You're Misaligned

**Hook:** Laziness is rare. Misalignment is everywhere. And most people can't tell the difference.

**Key Points:**
1. When work is misaligned with what you actually care about, your brain treats avoidance as a survival mechanism. You're not broken. You're protecting yourself from something that doesn't make sense to you.
2. The fix is not more discipline applied to the wrong direction. The fix is honesty about what you actually want to build.
3. Get aligned first. Choose the work that connects to something real for you. Then watch the energy show up without forcing it.

**End Card CTA:** Follow for more on building work that pulls instead of drains.

## Script 711

**Title:** Stop Chasing Viral. Chase Resonance.

**Hook:** One video that changes one person's life matters more than a million views from people who forgot you in ten seconds.

**Key Points:**
1. The creators who last are not the ones who went viral once. They're the ones who built a body of work that means something to a specific type of person.
2. Depth creates loyalty. Noise creates traffic. Only one of those builds an actual audience.
3. Create for the person who is exactly where you were before you figured something out. Speak directly to that person. Everything else is decoration.

**End Card CTA:** Follow for content strategy that builds something lasting, not just something big.

## Script 712

**Title:** Two Hours That Beat Eight

**Hook:** Eight hours of scattered work produces less than two hours of focused work. Most people refuse to believe this until they test it.

**Key Points:**
1. The modern workday is designed for the appearance of productivity. Meetings, notifications, and open tabs create busyness without output.
2. Deep work is the ability to focus on one cognitively demanding task without distraction. It's rare, it's uncomfortable, and it produces your best output.
3. Block two to three hours in the morning before the noise starts. No phone. No email. One task. Do this for thirty days and measure what changes.

**End Card CTA:** Follow for more on building a work structure that actually produces results.

## Script 713

**Title:** Nobody Gives You Permission

**Hook:** The moment you stop waiting for someone to tell you it's your time, your work changes permanently.

**Key Points:**
1. Permission is a concept designed for institutions. Nobody in a system of self-directed creation is handing out validation.
2. Every creator, entrepreneur, or builder who made it did so by starting without permission and improving in public over months and years.
3. The person who gives you a shot is almost always the person you become after you started without one.

**End Card CTA:** Follow if you're done waiting and ready to start.

## Script 714

**Title:** What Compounding Actually Feels Like

**Hook:** Nobody tells you that compounding feels like failure for the first six months. That's the part that filters out most people.

**Key Points:**
1. Compounding is not linear. It looks flat for a long time and then it curves sharply. The problem is most people quit during the flat part.
2. The work you're doing today is not producing results today. It's building the foundation that produces results in month twelve and month twenty-four.
3. The most important skill in any long-term project is the ability to stay engaged when the feedback loop is slow. Learn to measure inputs, not just outputs.

**End Card CTA:** Follow for content that keeps you building through the flat parts.
