## Short 930: Stop Making Content for the Algorithm
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: creator staring at analytics, overwhelmed] You're making content for the algorithm. That's why you're burning out.
**Content (5-50s):**
The algorithm rewards what the audience rewards.
So if you chase the algorithm, you're playing a game that changes every three months.
If you serve the audience, you're building something that compounds.
Here's the difference in practice.
Algorithm-first thinking: "What's trending? What's the ideal post length? What time do I post?"
Audience-first thinking: "What does my person need to hear today? What question do they ask all the time? What would genuinely change their week?"
Make that. Make it consistently. The algorithm will find it.
But the audience will stay.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for audience-first content strategy. What does your audience ask you most?

**Description:** Chasing the algorithm is exhausting and short-lived. In this Short, I explain why audience-first thinking is the only sustainable content strategy for creators and solopreneurs. When you consistently solve real problems for real people, the algorithm catches up eventually. But more importantly, you build an audience that stays, trusts you, and buys from you. This is the mindset shift that separates creators who burn out from those who build long-term businesses. ## Short 931: The Two Numbers That Actually Matter in Your Creator Business
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: close-up of two numbers on a whiteboard] Two numbers. That's all you need to track.
**Content (5-50s):**
Forget vanity metrics. Followers, likes, views — those feel good. They don't pay bills.
The two numbers that matter:
Number one: how many people clicked from your content to your product page this week?
Number two: how many of those people bought?
The first number tells you if your content is doing its job. The second number tells you if your offer is doing its job.
If the first number is low, your content needs a stronger call to action or better alignment with what you're selling.
If the second number is low, your offer, pricing, or product page needs work.
You can fix both. But only if you're tracking both.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for creator business tactics that focus on income, not vanity metrics.
**Title:** The Only 2 Numbers That Matter in Your Creator Business
**Description:** Most creators obsess over followers and views while ignoring the metrics that actually generate income. In this Short, I break down the two numbers every solopreneur and digital product creator needs to track weekly: traffic to product and conversion rate. Understanding these two numbers will tell you exactly what to fix in your business and show you where the money is being left on the table. ## Short 932: What "Building in Public" Actually Means
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: messy desk, open laptop, half-written document] Building in public doesn't mean showing your highlight reel.
**Content (5-50s):**
Building in public means showing the messy middle.
The draft that isn't finished. The week where nothing worked. The product that got zero sales at launch.
Why does this work?
Because it's honest. And honest content builds trust faster than anything.
When you share the struggle alongside the win, people believe the win is real.
And they believe you when you say your product helped you.
Most creators only show success. You show the journey.
The journey is what people connect to. The journey is what makes someone feel like they know you.
That relationship is worth more than any ad budget.

## Short 933: How to Write a Hook That Makes People Stop Scrolling
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: thumb hovering over a phone screen, pausing] This is the hook formula that stops the scroll.
**Content (5-50s):**
There are three kinds of hooks that work every time.
One: the counterintuitive statement. Say the opposite of what people expect. "I stopped planning my week and became more productive." Instant curiosity.
Two: the specific result. Numbers and specifics grab attention. "How I made $1,200 from a Google Doc in 48 hours."
Three: the direct challenge. Call out the viewer's situation. "You're not broke. You just haven't charged for your skills yet."
Every great hook does one thing: it makes the viewer feel like they'll miss something important if they scroll past.
Create that feeling. The views will follow.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for content strategy delivered every week. What hook type do you use most?
**Title:** 3 Hook Formulas That Stop the Scroll (Works on Any Platform)
**Description:** A strong hook is the most valuable skill a content creator can develop. In this Short, I share three proven hook formulas that work across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels: the counterintuitive statement, the specific result, and the direct challenge. Each formula creates curiosity or urgency that makes the viewer feel compelled to keep watching. Use these in your next video and watch your retention numbers improve immediately. Keywords: content hooks, YouTube hooks, scroll stopping content, TikTok hooks, content strategy, creator tips, video marketing.

## Short 934: Why Cheap Wins in the Digital Product World
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: price tag reading $27] Price your first product under $30. Here's the specific reason why.
**Content (5-50s):**
A high-priced product requires trust that you haven't built yet.
A low-priced product removes the trust barrier entirely.
When someone buys a $27 product from you, you get three things.
One: proof of concept. Real humans paid real money for your idea.
Two: an email address. You can now market to them forever.
Three: an opportunity to over-deliver. If you blow them away at $27, they'll buy your $97 and $297 products.
The $27 product is not a revenue goal. It's a relationship tool.
Price it to say yes to. Deliver like it's worth $270.
That gap between expectation and delivery is where loyalty is made.

**Title:** Why You Should Price Your First Digital Product Under $30
**Description:** Low-ticket digital products are not a compromise. They're a strategy. The first sale is never about the money. It's about establishing the relationship and over-delivering on the promise.
