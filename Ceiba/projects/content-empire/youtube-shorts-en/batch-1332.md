Digital products are the best business model for solo builders.

Here's the case.

Point one: No inventory, no shipping, no customer service at 2 AM. You build it once and sell it an unlimited number of times. The margin is essentially 100 percent after costs.

Point two: It forces you to package your knowledge. When you create a product, you have to organize what you know into something clear and teachable. That process alone makes you sharper.

Point three: It creates proof. A product on a page is more credible than a bio claim. "I made this system that helps people do X" is a better credential than "I know about X."

Templates. Guides. Courses. Notion dashboards. Prompt libraries.

If you know how to do something useful, you have a product waiting.

The only question is whether you package it.
