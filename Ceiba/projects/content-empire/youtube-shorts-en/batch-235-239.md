## Short 235
**Title:** How to Write Copy That Actually Converts (Simple Framework)
**Hook (0-3s):** [TEXT ON SCREEN: "Your sales page is losing buyers at the first sentence."]
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** [TALKING HEAD] Most sales pages open with a description of the product. Wrong. Open with the pain.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Pain. Agitation. Solution."] The PAS framework is the simplest conversion structure that exists. Pain: name the exact problem your buyer has right now. Agitation: describe how it feels to live with that problem. Solution: present your product as the specific escape.

[B-ROLL: sales page mockup, cursor scrolling through clean copy] One sentence on pain. Two sentences on what it costs them. Three sentences on what life looks like after your product. That's 90% of a working sales page. Everything else is just more words.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Problem first. Solution after. Always."] Write pain first. Buyers will self-select into everything that comes after.
**Description:** The most effective copywriting framework for digital product sales pages is also the simplest. Pain, agitation, solution guides the reader from their current problem to your specific solution without requiring advanced writing skills or marketing expertise. This short breaks down how to open a sales page, product description, or email sequence using this three-step structure. Apply it to your next product launch and compare your conversion rate before and after.
**Tags:** copywriting, sales page tips, how to write copy, conversion copywriting, digital product launch, creator economy, email marketing, solopreneur, online business, copywriting framework, PAS framework, sell digital products

## Short 236
**Title:** The Productivity System Used by High-Output Solopreneurs
**Hook (0-3s):** [CLOSE-UP of clean workspace] "Every high-output person I know uses some version of this system."
**Duration:** 57s
**Script:** [TALKING HEAD] The system is simple. It has three parts.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Weekly outcome. Daily focus. Protected time."] Every Sunday you write three outcomes for the week. Not a task list. Three results that would make the week a win. Every morning you pick the one outcome from that list that matters most today. Every day you block two hours where your phone is off and nothing else exists but that outcome.

[B-ROLL: clean notebook with three lines written, phone face down, timer running] Weekly outcomes stop you from drifting. Daily focus stops you from multitasking. Protected time stops you from being reactive. Three layers. That's the whole system.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Weekly. Daily. Protected. That's it."] It takes five minutes to set up. It changes what you get done by Friday.
**Description:** High-output solopreneurs don't use complicated productivity apps or 12-step morning routines. Most use a simple three-layer system built around weekly outcomes, daily focus, and protected work time. This short explains each layer and how to implement them starting this week. Whether you're a creator, freelancer, or online business owner, this framework helps you stop feeling busy while actually moving your most important projects forward.
