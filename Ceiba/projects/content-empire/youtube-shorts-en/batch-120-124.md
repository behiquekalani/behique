## Batch 120

### Short 1
**Title:** Why your digital product isn't selling (it's not the algorithm)

**5-sec Hook:** Your product isn't selling because your message isn't clear. Not because of the algorithm.

**Body (50 sec):** The algorithm distributes content. It doesn't write your message. If people see your product and don't buy, the conversion problem lives in the message, not the distribution. Here's how to diagnose it: read your product description out loud. If you can't say in one sentence exactly who it's for and what problem it solves, neither can your buyer. That ambiguity is the conversion killer. Fix the description before you run ads, before you post more content, before you blame the platform. When the message is specific, people recognize themselves in it. When they recognize themselves, they buy. That recognition is what you're actually selling. Before the product, before the price, before anything else: the message has to land.

### Short 2
**Title:** The solopreneur math nobody talks about

**5-sec Hook:** Selling a hundred products at ten dollars is harder than selling ten products at a hundred.

**Body (48 sec):** The math looks the same. A thousand dollars either way. But the operational reality is different. A hundred customers means a hundred potential support emails, a hundred refund requests, a hundred people who need to be satisfied. Ten customers at a higher price point tend to be more invested, get better results, and require less hand-holding. Higher price filters for commitment. That's not elitism. It's market dynamics. When someone invests a hundred dollars versus ten, their psychology changes. They pay attention. They implement. They get results. Results generate testimonials. Testimonials fuel the next round of sales. Raising your price isn't just financial strategy. It's product quality strategy.

Others become your second hundred. You don't need to be famous. You need to be undeniably useful to a specific group of people who know exactly what you do.

### Short 4
**Title:** The two types of work every solopreneur has to balance

**5-sec Hook:** There's work that pays now and work that pays later. Most people only do one.

**Body (46 sec):** Work that pays now includes client work, consulting, done-for-you services. It generates immediate cash but doesn't compound. Work that pays later includes content creation, product development, email list building, audience growth. It's slower but compounds over time. The trap is doing only present-tense work because it pays immediately and feels necessary. But if you spend all your time on present-tense work, you never build the infrastructure for future-tense income. The balance is protection: enough present-tense work to cover your costs while allocating consistent time to future-tense work. Even three hours per week on future-tense work compounds significantly over two years.

### Short 2
**Title:** The mental model that changed how I price my products

**5-sec Hook:** Stop pricing based on what it cost to make. Price based on what it costs the buyer NOT to have it.

**Body (48 sec):** Cost-based pricing is a supplier's framework. Value-based pricing is a buyer's framework. If your product saves someone forty hours of trial and error, the value isn't the hours you spent building it. The value is those forty hours of their time multiplied by what their time is worth. If they charge clients a hundred dollars an hour, those forty hours are worth four thousand dollars. Your product at two hundred dollars is a remarkable deal. Most creators price at what feels comfortable to charge. Value-based pricing asks what's the transformation worth to the buyer and prices accordingly. That number is almost always higher than comfort suggests.

**CTA:** Save this before your next pricing decision.
