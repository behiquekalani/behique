## Short 536: The Three-Post Rule That Built My Audience
**Hook (0-5s):** [Post calendar filling up] One rule. Three types of posts. That's the whole audience growth system.
**Content (5-50s):**
Every content strategy can be simplified into three types of posts.
Post type one: the teach post. Share one specific thing you know that your audience doesn't. Practical, actionable, complete.
Post type two: the story post. Share something that happened to you that your audience can see themselves in. Personal, honest, specific.
Post type three: the opinion post. Take a clear stance on something in your niche. Not for everyone. For your people.
Rotate through all three. Teach builds credibility. Story builds connection. Opinion builds loyalty.
Miss one type and your content starts to feel flat. Use all three and it feels dimensional.

## Short 537: Build Faster by Doing Less
**Hook (0-5s):** [Busy creator vs calm creator, same output] The calm creator ships more. Here's why.
**Content (5-50s):**
Counterintuitive productivity truth: doing more things leads to doing each thing worse.
When you have 14 projects open, your brain splits focus across all 14. None of them gets your full capacity.
The constraint principle: limit your active projects to two. One primary. One secondary.
Everything else goes on a list labeled "not yet." Not abandoned. Not forgotten. Not yet.
The creator who ships one excellent thing per month outperforms the creator who starts ten things per month but finishes none.
Focus isn't a personality trait. It's a design choice. Reduce the number of open loops and you'll build faster.

## Short 538: The $100 Product vs the $1,000 Product
**Hook (0-5s):** [Two price tags] Same amount of effort. 10x the revenue. Here's the only difference.
**Content (5-50s):**
The difference between a $100 product and a $1,000 product is usually not the content. It's the promise.
A $100 product promises to teach something. A $1,000 product promises a specific transformation.
"How to write better emails" is a $100 promise. "Write emails that convert cold leads into paying clients in under 30 days" is a $1,000 promise.
The buyer of the $1,000 product isn't paying for more information. They're paying for a specific, believable outcome with high stakes.
Ask yourself: what is the result someone gets from buying my product? If you can quantify it, specify it, or give it a timeline, you can price it higher.

## Short 539: Content That Compounds
**Hook (0-5s):** [Compound interest graph applied to content] Your old content is still working right now. Are you making the right kind?
**Content (5-50s):**
Not all content is equal in terms of shelf life. Some content expires. Some compounds.
Trending content: spikes fast, dies in a week. Tutorial content: builds slowly, earns for years. Opinion content: builds community over time and keeps attracting the same type of person.
The compounding content types to prioritize: tutorials that answer timeless questions, frameworks that apply to enduring problems, and contrarian takes that stake out permanent positions.
A video answering "how do I price my first digital product" will earn views for two years. A video about a TikTok trend will earn views for two weeks.
Build for the long shelf. Most creators don't. That's the opportunity.
