You don't have a money problem. You have a pricing problem.

Here's how I figured that out.

Point one: Cheap clients are expensive. They question every invoice, change their minds three times, and ghost on payment. Higher prices attract people who respect boundaries.

Point two: Price signals value. If your price is low, your perceived quality is low. This is not about what you're worth. It's about market psychology.

Point three: The same offer at a higher price often converts better because it reads as premium. Test it. Raise by 30 percent and see what happens to close rate.

I raised my prices twice in six months. Revenue went up. Headaches went down.

The raise is not greedy. It's a filter.
