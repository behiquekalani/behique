## Short 881: The 3-Step Formula for a Perfect Hook
**Hook (0-5s):** [Punchy] Your hook is the whole game. Get it wrong and nobody watches. Here are the three parts that make it work.
**Content (5-50s):**
A hook has three jobs in three seconds.
Job one: Identify the audience. Your hook should make the right person say "that's me." "If you're a freelancer who can't close clients" is ten times better than "if you want to make more money."
Job two: Name the tension. There's a gap between where they are and where they want to be. Your hook lives in that gap. Don't tease a topic. Tease a transformation or a problem.
Job three: Create forward momentum. The viewer should need to watch to resolve what you started. Open a loop. Ask a question. Make a claim that demands proof.
Example of a weak hook: "Today I'm sharing 5 productivity tips."
Example of a strong hook: "I used to lose 3 hours a day to nothing. These 5 changes fixed it in a week."
Write your hook last. Once you know what you're saying, you'll know the best way to open.

## Short 883: The Fastest Way to Validate a Digital Product Idea
**Hook (0-5s):** [Urgent] Most people spend months building a product nobody wants. Here's how to know in 48 hours if yours will sell.
**Content (5-50s):**
Step one: Post a piece of content about the problem your product solves. Watch what happens. If it gets saved and shared, that's demand signal.
Step two: Before you build anything, ask your audience directly. "I'm thinking about creating a guide on X. Would you pay $17 for it?" Run a poll or post the question. Five yes answers from real people is enough to start.
Step three: Sell it before you finish it. List the product on Gumroad with a note that says "pre-order." If people pay, build it. If they don't, you saved weeks of work.
Validation is a two-sentence description and a payment link.
The product can be built after you know it's wanted.
Build it for the audience you have. Not the one you imagine.
