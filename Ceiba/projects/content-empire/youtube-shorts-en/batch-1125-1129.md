## Short 1126: The Uncomfortable Truth About Going Viral
**Hook (0-3s):** Viral is not a strategy. This is.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:**
Nobody consistently goes viral on purpose. Viral is the exception. Consistent reach is the goal. One viral post gets you a spike. Consistent posting gets you an audience. The people who seem to always go viral are actually people who post so consistently that something goes viral eventually. The math is simple. Post 300 times. Maybe 10 hit. Those 10 look like luck. They're not. They're the result of 290 posts that didn't. Stop chasing the spike. Build the habit. The hits come to creators who show up every day, not the ones who wait for the perfect post.
**Thumbnail text:** Viral Is Not the Goal. This Is.
**Description:** Going viral feels like the dream. But viral without an audience strategy is just a spike that fades. This short explains the real math behind audience growth: consistent posting over time produces outlier results that look like luck but are really just volume. If you post 300 times, something will hit. The key is staying in the game long enough for it to happen. Consistency is the strategy that virality hides behind. Stop optimizing for the lucky break and start building the daily habit. That is where lasting audiences come from.

## Short 1128: Why Your Product Launch Will Probably Fail
**Hook (0-3s):** Most product launches fail before launch day.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:**
The mistake is launching to a cold audience. You create a product. You post about it once. Nobody buys. You blame the product. The product might be fine. The problem is there was no warmup. A launch is not an event. It's a process. Weeks before you launch, you should be posting content directly related to the problem your product solves. You're building demand without mentioning the product. By the time you drop the link, your audience already knows they need it. They just need to know you made it. Warm up your audience before you open the cart. That's the whole secret.
**Thumbnail text:** Why No One Bought Your Product
**Description:** Product launches fail not because of bad products but because of cold audiences. If your audience hasn't been hearing about the problem your product solves for weeks before launch, you're asking them to go from cold to paying in one post. That almost never works. The fix is a warmup sequence. Create content that educates your audience on the problem. Build anticipation. Let them identify with the pain before you present the solution. By launch day, they already want what you're offering. This short walks through the pre-launch framework that makes launch day feel like a natural conclusion instead of a surprise ask.
