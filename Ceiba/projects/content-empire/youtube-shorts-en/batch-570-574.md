## Short 571
**Title:** What Successful People Do Differently Every Single Day
**Hook:** It's not the big decisions that separate people. It's the small ones.

The person who checks their phone before getting out of bed versus the one who doesn't.

The person who writes down three priorities every morning versus the one who just starts responding to things.

The person who spends 30 minutes learning something new every day versus the one who watches another hour of television.

These are small choices.
Each one, maybe 1% better or 1% worse.

Over a year, the gap is enormous.

Successful people don't have more willpower.
They have better defaults.

Default: phone charges in another room.
Default: journal before coffee.
Default: block on calendar for deep work.

You don't rely on motivation to do things when they're the default.
You just do them.

Audit your defaults.

What happens automatically in your day that you didn't choose?
What would you choose if you designed it intentionally?

Build the better defaults.
Let them run.

## Short 572
**Title:** How to Price Your Skills and Stop Undercharging
**Hook:** You are worth more than you're charging. Here's how to fix it.

Most people underprice for one of three reasons.

One. They price based on their cost of time, not the value they deliver.
Two. They fear rejection and price low to avoid it.
Three. They compare to the cheapest option in the market instead of the best.

Value-based pricing is the fix.

Instead of asking "how long will this take me," ask "what is the result worth to the client?"

A landing page that takes you five hours and earns your client $50,000 is not worth $500.
It's worth several thousand.

Start by documenting the outcomes of your work.
Then price based on a percentage of that outcome.

If you can't document outcomes yet, raise your rate by 20% and test whether clients push back.
Most won't.

The market will tell you when your price is too high.
You will know because clients stop hiring you.
Until that happens, raise the rate.

You are not too expensive. You are just not charging your real price yet.

## Short 573
**Title:** Why Your Goals Feel Impossible (And How to Fix It Fast)
**Hook:** Your goals aren't too big. Your timeline is too short.

Most people set a one-year goal that should take three years.
When they're not there by month six, they conclude they're failing.

They're not failing. They miscalculated the timeline.

Here's the fix.

Take your goal. Assume it will take twice as long as you think.
Now plan the first 90 days of that longer timeline.

What can you realistically move in 90 days?
Not "complete." Move.

The problem with annual goals is they're too abstract.
They create a false urgency and then a false failure.

90-day cycles work because they're close enough to feel real.
Far enough to make real progress.

Pick one goal. Break it into milestones.
What does month one look like? Month two? Month three?

Progress, not completion.
Movement, not arrival.

The person who makes steady progress on a big goal for three years accomplishes more than the person who burns out on five annual goals.

Slow is smooth. Smooth is fast.
