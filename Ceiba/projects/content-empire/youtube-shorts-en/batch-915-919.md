## Short 917: Why Your Side Hustle Isn't Growing
**Hook (0-5s):** [Creator crosses arms.] Your side hustle isn't growing because you're treating it like a hobby.
**Content (5-50s):**
Hobbies happen when you feel like it.
Businesses happen on a schedule.
Here's what separates a side hustle that grows from one that stays flat:
Consistency over inspiration. You post on days you don't want to. You work when the motivation is gone.
Clear offer over vague positioning. "I help [specific person] do [specific thing] in [specific timeframe]" beats "I'm a coach" every time.
One traffic source over five. Pick one platform. Dominate it. Then add another.
Revenue tracking over vanity metrics. Followers don't pay rent. Know your monthly revenue number and obsess over it.
The side hustle that works is the one you treat like the job you're trying to escape.

## Short 919: How to Research Any Market in 30 Minutes
**Hook (0-5s):** [Creator opens laptop.] Before you build anything, do this 30-minute research process.
**Content (5-50s):**
Minute 0-10: Search Reddit for the niche. Find the top posts in the last year. What questions keep coming up? What problems are recurring?
Minute 10-20: Search Gumroad and Etsy for products in the niche. What's selling? At what price? Read the reviews. What do buyers praise? What do they complain about?
Minute 20-25: Search TikTok and YouTube for the niche. What content gets the most engagement? What comments are people leaving?
Minute 25-30: Write down the #1 problem you found repeated across all three sources.
That problem is your product idea.
You don't need a survey. You don't need to build an audience first. You need 30 minutes and three platforms.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for the full product research framework. What market are you researching right now?
**Title:** How to Research Any Market in 30 Minutes (Before You Build Anything)
**Description:** Most creators build products nobody wants because they skip market research. This short gives you a 30-minute research process using Reddit, Gumroad, and TikTok to validate your product idea before you spend a single hour building it.
