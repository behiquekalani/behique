## Short 380
**Title:** How to Find Your Content Voice in 30 Days
**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator speaks quietly, leaning in] "Your content sounds like everyone else's. Here's how to fix it."

**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: FINDING YOUR CONTENT VOICE]

[B-ROLL: creator writing in notebook]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: DAY 1-30: WRITE DAILY, DON'T PUBLISH]

This Short introduces the 30-day writing exercise that helps creators discover their authentic content voice by building a private writing practice before going public. The patterns that emerge from 30 days of unedited daily writing reveal your natural perspective, recurring themes, and genuine language, all the building blocks of a voice that audiences can't find anywhere else. Your voice is already there. This process finds it.

## Short 383
**Title:** How to Write a Sales Page That Converts Without Hype
**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator looks at blank document] "Most sales pages either bore people or scare them. Here's the middle path."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE NO-HYPE SALES PAGE]
Hype kills trust. But boring sells nothing. Here's the formula for a page that converts honestly.
[B-ROLL: clean sales page design on screen]
Section one: name the problem clearly. Not dramatically. Just specifically.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: NAME THE PROBLEM CLEARLY]
Section two: introduce the solution and who it's for. One paragraph. Simple language.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: SOLUTION + WHO IT'S FOR]
Section three: show what's inside. Be specific. Bullets work better than paragraphs here.
[B-ROLL: product contents list on screen]
Section four: the price, one call to action, and a simple guarantee.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: PRICE + CTA + GUARANTEE]
No fake urgency. No inflated claims. Just clear value for the right person.
**Description:** Sales pages that rely on hype, inflated claims, and artificial urgency have a trust problem. They may convert some buyers, but they destroy long-term brand credibility. This Short presents a four-section no-hype sales page framework that converts by being specific, clear, and honest. Learn how to name the problem without dramatizing it, introduce your solution in plain language, present product contents with specificity, and close with a simple price and guarantee. This approach works better for digital products because it attracts buyers who actually understand and want what you're selling.
**Tags:** how to write a sales page, sales page that converts, digital product sales page, no hype marketing, sales page tips, landing page, creator sales, product page, online business, solopreneur, digital products, conversion copywriting
