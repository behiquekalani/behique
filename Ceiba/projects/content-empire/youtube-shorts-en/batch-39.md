### Short 1
**Title:** The Content-to-Income Pipeline Nobody Talks About

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Most creators have content. Zero have a pipeline. That's why the income doesn't come."

**Body:**
- Content without a destination is entertainment. You need a funnel behind it.
- Short-form attracts. Long-form builds trust. Email converts. Product closes.
- Each piece of content should point somewhere with an economic purpose
- Map your pipeline: where does a new viewer go, and how do they eventually pay you?

**CTA:**
"Draw your pipeline on paper right now. If it ends at 'post,' you're leaving money behind."

### Short 2
**Title:** 4 Stages of the Solopreneur Content Pipeline

**Hook (0-5s):**
"There are exactly four stages between a stranger watching your content and money in your account."

**Body:**
- Stage 1: Discovery — short-form content, problem-aware audience
- Stage 2: Trust — newsletter, long-form, consistent value
- Stage 3: Offer — a clear product or service with a page behind it
- Stage 4: Conversion — email sequence, DM, or sales call that closes
- Most creators never build stages 3 and 4. That's the entire problem.

**CTA:**
"Which stage are you missing? Comment the number. I'll point you to the fix."

### Short 5
**Title:** The Solopreneur's Exit Strategy

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Most solopreneurs build a prison and call it a business. Here's how you build an exit instead."

**Body:**
- An exit isn't about selling. It's about not being required to show up every day.
- Build systems that run without your direct time: email automations, evergreen products, templates
- Document every process so someone else could execute it
- The goal: a business that generates revenue whether you post today or not

**CTA:**
"If your business stops the moment you stop working, you don't have a business yet. Fix that."

### Short 6
**Title:** The $500 Digital Product That Funds Your Freedom

**Hook (0-5s):**
"You don't need a $2,000 course to start. A $500 product can change everything."

**Body:**
- A focused template, system, or toolkit priced at $500 is not an impulse buy — it's a signal
- It filters serious buyers from window shoppers
- At 20 sales, you've cleared $10,000. That's one month of real income.
- Most people underprice to get volume. You need margin, not mass.

**CTA:**
"What knowledge do you have that's worth $500 to the right person? That's your product."

### Short 7
**Title:** Why Most Digital Products Fail Before Launch

**Hook (0-5s):**
"The product wasn't the problem. The positioning was. Here's the pattern."

**Body:**
- Created before validating: built for themselves, not for a specific buyer
- Named vaguely: titles that describe topics, not outcomes
- Priced for confidence they don't have yet
- Sold once to launch, then never promoted again
- Fix all four of these before you hit publish

**CTA:**
"Audit your existing product against these four. Comment which one you need to fix."

### Short 8
**Title:** The Solopreneur Flywheel: How the System Feeds Itself

**Hook (0-5s):**
"At some point your content brings in clients who become case studies that improve your content. That's the flywheel."

**Body:**
- Publish content that attracts the exact person your product serves
- Close them into a product or service
- Deliver results, collect the story
- Turn that story into more content that closes more clients
- The flywheel takes 90 days to feel. But it never stops once it starts.

**CTA:**
"You're one closed client away from having a case study. Go get that first one."

### Short 9
**Title:** How to Know When to Add a New Product

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Adding products too early is as dangerous as adding them too late. Here's when to move."

**Body:**
- Only add a new product when your current one converts consistently, not when you're bored
- Boredom is not a business signal. Demand is.
- Signs it's time: you keep getting asked for more, your current product has a clear next step, you have testimonials
- Never build to escape the current offer. Build to extend it.

**CTA:**
"How many products do you have right now? Comment the number. I'll tell you if it's too many or too few."
