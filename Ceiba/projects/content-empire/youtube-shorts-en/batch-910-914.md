## Short 912: The Attention Economy Is a Game. Here's How to Win.
**Hook (0-5s):** [Creator looks at phone, then at camera.] Every app you open is competing for the most valuable resource you have. Your attention.
**Content (5-50s):**
The attention economy has two types of players.
Consumers. People who scroll, watch, react. Their attention is the product being sold.
Producers. People who create content, build products, build audiences. Their attention produces assets.
Most people are consumers by default. Being a producer is a deliberate choice.
Here's how you make the switch:
- Before you open any app, create something first. Even one paragraph. One 60-second video. One tweet.
- Set a creation block before a consumption block. Never the reverse.
- Every platform you use, ask: am I building here or just being built for?
The game doesn't require you to quit social media.
It requires you to change which side of the screen you're on.

## Short 914: The Worst Financial Advice People Keep Following
**Hook (0-5s):** [Creator shakes head.] The financial advice most people follow is designed to keep you average.
**Content (5-50s):**
"Save 10% of your income." If you earn $35,000, that's $3,500 a year. After 40 years with 7% returns, you have $750,000. You retire at 65. That's not wealth. That's survival.
"Get a stable job with benefits." Benefits are not compensation. They're compensation shaped like security.
"Don't invest until you're debt-free." Meanwhile inflation eats the purchasing power of your savings while you wait.
The advice that actually changes trajectories:
Increase your income faster than your lifestyle. The gap is where wealth is built.
Build one income stream that doesn't require your hours.
Start investing early, even small, because compounding is patient.
The system was not designed to make you wealthy. It was designed to make you productive.
You have to design your own system.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for counter-conventional money thinking. What's the worst financial advice you've received?
**Title:** The Worst Financial Advice People Keep Following
**Description:** Most mainstream financial advice is calibrated for average outcomes. If you want above-average results, you need to question the defaults. This short breaks down three pieces of common financial advice that keep people stuck and what to replace them with. Subscribe for daily content on money, business, and building your own path.
