## Short 630

Start showing up as the person you're becoming. Not the person you think you currently are.

## Short 632
**Title:** How to Price Your Digital Products for Maximum Sales
**Hook:** Most people price themselves out of sales.

Pricing is one of the most misunderstood parts of selling digital products. Let me give you the framework.

The mistake most people make is pricing based on effort. How long did it take me to make this? That's the wrong question.

The right question is what is this outcome worth to the buyer? A guide that saves someone 20 hours is worth far more than a guide that took you 2 hours to write. Price the outcome.

For entry products, sweet spots are $17, $27, and $37. Low enough that it's an easy yes. High enough that it signals real value.

For core products, $97 to $197 works for most audiences. This is your main offer where most of your revenue comes from.

For premium, $497 and up. Reserved for your most comprehensive offer or direct access to you.

Always test prices. Launch at one price. Adjust based on conversion data. Never permanently discount.

The price also sets expectations. Price too low and people don't take it seriously.

Price for the outcome. Not the effort.

## Short 633
**Title:** Build Your Audience Before You Need It
**Hook:** Plant the tree before you're thirsty.

The worst time to start building an audience is when you need one.

When you have a product to launch and no audience, you scramble. You panic. You launch to silence. This is avoidable but only if you start early.

The best time to start was a year ago. The second best time is today.

Here's the compounding truth. An audience built over 12 months of consistent content does not grow linearly. It grows exponentially. Month one you have 50 followers. Month six you have 800. Month twelve you have 5,000. Month eighteen you have 20,000.

The growth accelerates. But only after you put in the early, slow months.

Your first hundred posts will get almost no engagement. That's normal. That's the dues every creator pays. Most quit before the algorithm figures out who to show their content to.

Don't quit before the compound effect kicks in.

Plant the audience now. Water it daily. When you need it, it will be there.

## Short 634
**Title:** The Creator Economy Is Still Early. Here's Your Window.
**Hook:** Most niches are still uncrowded.

People say the creator economy is saturated. They're wrong.

The overall space is crowded at the generic level. General finance content. Generic motivation. Broad productivity advice. Those are crowded.

But specific, niche content for specific audiences is still massively underserved.

Who is creating content specifically for immigrant entrepreneurs learning to navigate US business structures? Who is teaching SEO specifically for e-commerce stores under $50,000 revenue? Who is creating finance content specifically for freelance designers?

Niches that specific are wide open.

The creators who will dominate the next five years are the ones who go narrow now. Who own a specific audience. Who become the definitive resource for one type of person.

You do not need to appeal to everyone. You need to be indispensable to someone.

Pick the niche where you have real knowledge and real experience. Go there. Stay there.

The window is open. It will not stay open forever.
