## Short 856: How to Validate a Digital Product Before You Build It
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text on screen: "Validate first, build second"] Stop building products nobody asked for. Do this first.
**Content (5-50s):**
Before you spend 40 hours building a course, spend 4 hours validating the idea.

Step one: post about the problem your product solves. Count how many people comment saying they have that problem.

Step two: create a waitlist. Tell your audience you're building a solution. See who signs up.

Step three: offer a beta version at half price to 10 people. If nobody buys, the product needs a different angle.

Step four: create only after you have confirmed demand.

This saves you months of building the wrong thing for the wrong people.

Your product should be the answer to a question your audience is already asking out loud.

Listen before you build.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for more on digital product strategy and how to build offers that actually sell.
**Title:** How to Validate Your Digital Product Before Building It (Avoid Wasting Months)
**Description:** Most digital products fail because they were built before demand was confirmed. Validate the problem, create a waitlist, run a beta, then build the full version. This is how serious solopreneurs avoid building things nobody wants.
