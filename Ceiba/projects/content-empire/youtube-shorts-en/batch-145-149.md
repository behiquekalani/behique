## Short 149 — Digital Products: Price your knowledge like a professional, not an amateur

**HOOK (0-8 sec):**
The number one pricing mistake digital creators make is pricing by effort instead of outcome.

**POINT 1 (8-22 sec):**
You spent fifty hours building a course. So you charge fifty dollars because that feels proportional. That's amateur pricing. Professional pricing asks: what is the outcome worth to the buyer?

**POINT 2 (22-40 sec):**
If your product helps someone land a client worth five thousand dollars, your product is worth five hundred. If it saves someone twenty hours a month, price it against the value of those twenty hours.

**POINT 3 (40-55 sec):**
Higher prices also filter buyers. Someone who pays two hundred dollars for your product is more likely to implement it and get results than someone who paid ten. Better results mean better testimonials and better marketing.
