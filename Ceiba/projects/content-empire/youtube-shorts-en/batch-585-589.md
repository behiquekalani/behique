## Short 587
**Title:** How to Price Your Digital Products for Maximum Sales
**Hook:** Low prices kill conversions.

This is the counterintuitive truth most new creators never learn until they've been burned by it.

When you price too low, buyers don't think they're getting a deal. They think the product isn't worth much. Price is a signal. And a weak price sends a weak signal.

The psychology of pricing works like this. Buyers associate high prices with high quality. They approach expensive products with more commitment. They implement more. They get better results.

The result? Higher-priced products often generate better testimonials, better referrals, and more loyal customers than cheap ones.

So what's the right price?

Find the transformation your product creates. Put a dollar value on that transformation. Price slightly below that value.

A guide that helps someone make $1,000 more per month should not be $17. Price it at $97 and you'll get better buyers who actually use it and get results.

Price for the outcome. Not the page count.

## Short 589
**Title:** How to Find Your Content Niche as a Creator in 2025
**Hook:** The right niche is the intersection of three things.

Most creators pick a niche based on what's trending. Wrong move.

Trending niches flood fast. You enter a crowded room with no real reason to be there.

The right niche comes from three intersecting circles.

What you know deeply. What you've actually lived through, built, or solved. Not what you studied once. What you know in your bones.

What a specific person desperately needs right now. Not a general topic, a specific problem a specific person has today.

What you can talk about consistently for three years without burning out.

Where those three circles overlap, that's your niche.

And here's the key most people miss. The niche isn't a topic. It's a person. Define the exact human you're speaking to. Age, situation, specific problem, specific aspiration.

When you know exactly who you're talking to, your content stops feeling generic. It becomes magnetic.

The right person reads it and thinks, "this is made for me."

That's the goal.
