## Short 330
**Title:** How to Price Your First Digital Product Correctly
**Hook (0-3s):** [Direct to camera] "You are undercharging and it is hurting your business."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE PRICING TRAP]
New creators default to low prices out of fear. The fear makes sense. The math does not.

[B-ROLL: product listing on screen]
A $9 product attracts people who buy impulsively and rarely implement. A $47 product attracts people who are serious about solving the problem.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: PRICE SIGNALS VALUE]
Your price is a signal. Low price signals low value even when the content is excellent.

[B-ROLL: calculator, numbers]
The correct pricing formula for beginners is simple. Calculate how much time your product saves someone. Charge a small fraction of that value.

A content calendar template that saves 5 hours a week is worth far more than $15 to a busy creator. Price it at $37. See what happens.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: TEST HIGHER PRICES]
The risk of overcharging is lower than the cost of undercharging long-term.

**Description:** Pricing your first digital product is one of the most consequential decisions in your creator business, and most new creators get it wrong by defaulting to the lowest possible number. This short explains why underpricing hurts your business in ways that go beyond simple math, including attracting the wrong buyers and signaling low value. Learn how to use the time-savings formula to set a price that reflects real value. Digital product pricing. Creator economy. Solopreneur tips. How to price an ebook. Gumroad pricing strategy.

The metric that tells you your content is actually working is saves.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: WHY SAVES WIN]
When someone saves your video, they are saying: I want to come back to this. That is intent. Intent precedes action. Action precedes conversion.

[B-ROLL: someone tapping save on a video]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE SAVE RATE TARGET]
Aim for a save rate above 3%. If you hit 5%, you are making content people genuinely value.

Focus your energy on making content worth saving and the views will follow.

**Description:** Most content creators track the wrong metrics, focusing on views and likes instead of the signals that predict actual business outcomes. Save rate is the most underrated metric in short-form content, indicating genuine value perception and intent to return. This short explains why saves beat views as a performance indicator and gives you a target save rate to aim for. Content analytics. YouTube Shorts metrics. TikTok analytics. Creator economy. **Description:** Multiple income streams do not require multiple businesses. The sustainable path to diverse revenue as a solopreneur is building one audience and creating layered offers at different price points and commitment levels. This short explains the three-layer income model used by successful solopreneurs: a service, a digital product, and affiliate recommendations, all flowing from the same content system and email list.
