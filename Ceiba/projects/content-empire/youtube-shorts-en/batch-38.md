# YouTube Shorts EN — Batch 38
## Topic: How to Write an Offer That Converts

---

### Short 1
**Title:** Why Your Offer Doesn't Convert (It's Not the Price)

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Your price isn't the problem. Your offer is. Here's what's actually broken."

**Body:**
- Most offers describe what you do, not what the buyer gets
- People don't buy services — they buy outcomes
- Weak offer: "I'll build your website." Strong offer: "A conversion-ready site in 14 days."
- The shift is specificity. Specificity signals confidence.

**CTA:**
"Rewrite your offer with an outcome and a timeline. Post it below. I'll give you feedback."

### Short 2
**Title:** The 3-Part Offer Formula That Closes

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Every offer that converts has three parts. Most people only write one."

**Body:**
- The problem: name the exact pain your buyer feels right now
- The outcome: describe the specific state they'll be in after working with you
- The proof: one data point, result, or case study that makes it believable
- That's the whole formula. Problem. Outcome. Proof.

**CTA:**
"Save this. Use it to rewrite your offer tonight and tell me what changed."

### Short 3
**Title:** Stop Leading With Your Method. Lead With the Result.

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Nobody cares how you do it. They care what happens when you do."

**Body:**
- Builders lead with process. Buyers think in outcomes.
- "I use a proprietary framework" — no one cares
- "You'll have a working system in 30 days" — now they're listening
- Lead with the destination. Explain the road only if they ask.

**CTA:**
"This single reframe can double your conversion rate. Follow for more offer writing principles."

### Short 4
**Title:** How to Make Your Offer Feel Risk-Free

**Hook (0-5s):**
"The reason people don't buy isn't doubt in you. It's fear of wasting money."

**Body:**
- Remove the risk from the buyer's side. Put it on yours.
- A guarantee doesn't weaken your position. It signals confidence.
- Examples: results-based refunds, free first session, milestone-linked payments
- When you absorb the risk, you close faster

**CTA:**
"What guarantee could you add to your current offer? Think about it. Comment it below."

### Short 5
**Title:** The Specificity Test: Does Your Offer Pass?

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Read your offer out loud. If it could describe anyone else's service, rewrite it."

**Body:**
- Generic offers lose to specific ones at every price point
- "I help entrepreneurs grow" — vague, forgettable
- "I help coaches get 3 client calls booked per week using short-form content" — specific, hirable
- Specificity is not limiting. It's magnetic.

**CTA:**
"Run your offer through the specificity test. Post the before and after in the comments."

### Short 6
**Title:** Why Long Sales Pages Lose to Short Clear Offers

**Hook (0-5s):**
"A confused buyer doesn't buy. They leave. Short wins."

**Body:**
- Your buyer decides in the first three sentences
- Every word after that either confirms or kills the decision
- One sentence on the problem. One on the outcome. One on the next step.
- That's your offer. Everything else is noise.

**CTA:**
"Can you say your offer in three sentences? Try it right now in the comments."

### Short 7
**Title:** The Biggest Word That Kills Offers

**Hook (0-5s):**
"One word destroys more offers than bad pricing or weak positioning. The word is 'help.'"

**Body:**
- "I help you with your marketing" — passive, vague, forgettable
- "I build your content system in 21 days" — active, specific, hirable
- Replace 'help' with a direct verb and a measurable output
- Buyers don't want help. They want a specific result delivered.

**CTA:**
"Go to your bio or sales page right now and find every 'help.' Replace them. Watch what changes."

### Short 8
**Title:** Offer vs. Service: What's the Difference

**Hook (0-5s):**
"Most freelancers sell services. Smart solopreneurs sell offers. Here's the gap."

**Body:**
- A service is what you do. An offer is what the buyer gets.
- Services are interchangeable. Offers are specific.
- "Social media management" is a service. "30 posts per month that grow your audience by 500 followers" is an offer.
- Package the service as an offer. Charge accordingly.

**CTA:**
"Turn your top service into an offer using this format. Follow for the next framework."

### Short 9
**Title:** How to Price an Offer You've Never Sold Before

**Hook (0-5s):**
"You have a new offer and no idea what to charge. Here's the fastest way to find the number."

**Body:**
- Calculate the value of the outcome you deliver, not your time
- If your client earns $10K from your work, $1,500 to $2,500 is a reasonable ask
- Start at the price that feels slightly uncomfortable to say out loud
- Test it three times before lowering. Most people underprice at the first hesitation.

**CTA:**
"What's your offer and what are you charging? Drop it below. I'll tell you if it's right."

### Short 10
**Title:** The One-Sentence Offer Test

**Hook (0-5s):**
"If you can't say your offer in one sentence, your buyer can't either. That's a sales problem."

**Body:**
- Format: I help [specific person] achieve [specific outcome] in [specific timeframe]
- Fill in each blank with something concrete — not categories, not adjectives
- "I help fitness coaches book 5 discovery calls per month from Instagram" is clear
- "I help health professionals grow their online presence" is not

**CTA:**
"Write your one-sentence offer in the comments. I'll tell you if it passes."
