One product. That's all you need to start.

Most new creators make the mistake of trying to build a product line before they've validated anything. Five products that nobody knows exist is worse than one product with a real marketing system behind it.

**The one-product model works like this:**

**Step 1: Build one product that solves one specific problem.**
Keep the scope tight. One outcome. One person. One path to get there.

**Step 2: Build the content system that drives traffic to it.**
Every piece of content you create answers a question your target buyer has. Every post ends with a path to the product.

**Step 3: Improve based on buyer feedback.**
Your first buyers will tell you exactly what's missing. Version two is always better because of what version one's buyers taught you.

**Step 4: Add a second product only after the first is proven.**
Once you're generating consistent revenue from product one, the second product has a warm audience already. That's when scaling becomes straightforward.

One product done right beats ten products done badly.
