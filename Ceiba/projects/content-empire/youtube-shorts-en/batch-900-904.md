## Short 901: How to Say More With Less Words
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text flashing on screen: "Less words. More impact."] The best writers don't write more. They cut more.
**Content (5-50s):**
Here is the most effective editing rule I know.
After you write anything, cut the first sentence. It's almost always a warm-up that adds nothing.
Then cut every adverb. "Very," "really," "extremely." Delete them.
Then cut every passive sentence. "It was decided" becomes "I decided."
Then read it aloud. Any sentence you stumble on, rewrite it simpler.
What's left is tighter, faster, stronger writing.
The goal is never to impress with vocabulary. The goal is to be understood instantly.
If a reader has to work to understand you, you've already lost them.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for more writing frameworks. Like and save this for your next editing session.
**Title:** How to Write With Less Words and More Impact (The Editing Rule That Changes Everything)
**Description:** Great writing is not about length. It's about clarity. The best communicators cut ruthlessly, and this short teaches you the exact editing rules used by professional writers to tighten their work. Cut first sentences, remove adverbs, eliminate passive voice, and read aloud. Apply these four steps to any piece of writing and it instantly becomes more powerful.
