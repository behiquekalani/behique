Time blocking is the single most effective productivity system for solopreneurs. Here's the version that actually works.

The mistake most people make: they block time but don't protect it. The blocks get eaten by meetings, pings, and small requests. Then they wonder why they never get to the real work.

Here's the system in three layers.

Layer one: deep work blocks. Two to three hours every morning, usually before 11 AM. Phone off. Door closed. Single task. This is where you build products, write content, and do the work that moves your business forward.

Layer two: communication windows. Instead of checking email and messages constantly, check twice a day. Once at noon, once at 4 PM. Batch all your responses in those windows.

Layer three: admin blocks. One afternoon per week handles all the administrative work. Invoicing, organizing, planning, filing. It doesn't spread across your week and interrupt your building time.

Three layers. Everything has a home. Nothing bleeds into everything else.

The result: your best hours are protected. Your output goes up. Your stress goes down.
