**Topic:** Your phone is destroying your deep work

**Script:**

Your phone is destroying your deep work. Here's the proof. Every time you check your phone without purpose, your brain needs 23 minutes to fully refocus. That means three random checks eat over an hour of real productivity. Here's what to do instead.

Point one: put your phone in another room before you start a work session. Not face-down. Not on silent. Another room.

Point two: set one specific time window to check messages. Noon works well. Everything else waits.

Point three: turn off all app notifications permanently. The important stuff will find you another way.

You don't need willpower to beat your phone. You need distance. Create the distance. Protect your focus. Follow for more systems that actually work.
