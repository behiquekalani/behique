## Short 1110: Content That Converts vs. Content That Gets Views
**Hook (0-3s):** A million views with zero sales is a hobby, not a business.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:**
View counts are vanity. Conversions are the metric that matters. Most viral content does not convert because it attracts the wrong audience. Entertainment content brings in scrollers. Educational content brings in learners. Neither buys unless you give them a specific next step with a specific reason to take it. Here's what converts. Content that identifies a painful problem, presents a clear solution, and points to something they can buy to get that solution faster. Every piece of content should move someone one step closer to the product. Not every post needs to sell. Every post needs to point.
**Thumbnail text:** Views vs. sales (the real difference)
**Description:**
Going viral does not mean going profitable. This is one of the most important lessons for any creator trying to build a real business. In this Short, I break down the difference between content that gets views and content that converts. Viral content attracts the wrong audience for most businesses. Converting content attracts people with specific pain points and gives them a clear next step. The key is understanding that not every post needs to sell, but every post needs to point toward the solution you offer. Reframe your content strategy around conversion, not entertainment.

## Short 1111: How to Price Your First Digital Product
**Hook (0-3s):** Pricing low doesn't make it easier to sell. It makes it harder.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:**
First-time creators always underprice. They think a lower price removes friction and makes buying easier. It doesn't. Low prices signal low value. If your product costs three dollars, people wonder what's wrong with it. Here's a better framework. What problem does your product solve? How much does that problem cost the buyer in time, money, or pain? Price your product at a fraction of that cost. A product that saves someone 20 hours of research is worth at least 50 dollars, probably more. Price based on transformation, not production cost. Start at 27, 47, or 97. Test. Adjust. But never underprice from fear.
**Thumbnail text:** Price your product right (not low)
**Description:**
Pricing is one of the most misunderstood parts of launching a digital product. In this Short, I explain why pricing low is actually counterproductive. Low prices signal low value and attract buyers who will ask for refunds. The right approach is to price based on the transformation your product delivers. Calculate how much the problem costs your buyer in time, money, or frustration. Price your product at a fraction of that. Starting price points of $27, $47, or $97 are proven entry levels for digital products. This Short gives you the mental model to price with confidence instead of fear.

## Short 1112: The Compounding Effect of Daily Content
**Hook (0-3s):** One post does almost nothing. One thousand posts does everything.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:**
Content does not compound in a straight line. It looks like nothing for a long time, then everything at once. Day one of posting, almost no one sees it. Day 30, still minimal traction. Day 90, a few pieces start performing. Day 180, one piece takes off and points people to your previous content. Day 365, you have a catalog of work that keeps pulling in new people every day. Most creators quit between day 30 and day 90. That's exactly where the compounding starts. The ones who stay win. Not because they're more talented. Because they're still there.
**Thumbnail text:** 365 days changes everything
**Description:**
Content compounding is real, but it doesn't look linear. This Short breaks down what actually happens when you commit to daily or consistent content creation over a year. The first 90 days feel like shouting into the void. But by month six, old content starts driving new traffic. By month twelve, you have a catalog that works continuously. The creators who win are not always the most talented. They're the ones still creating when others quit. This Short is the reminder you need if you're somewhere in that early grind phase. Stay in it.

## Short 1113: Build Your Business Around Your Life, Not the Other Way Around
**Hook (0-3s):** Most entrepreneurs build a business that owns them. Do the opposite.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:**
Before you build anything, decide what your life needs to look like. What hours do you want to work? Where do you want to live? How much do you need to earn? Now build a business that fits those constraints, not a business that you'll try to fit your life around later. That flip is everything. A business built around your life is a different machine than a business you sacrifice your life for. It means building automations. Setting hard work windows. Saying no to revenue that requires too much of you. The goal is not to build the biggest business. It's to build one that serves your life.
**Thumbnail text:** Design life first, business second
**Description:**
Most people build a business and then wonder why they're miserable. The better approach is to design your ideal life first and then build a business that fits inside it. In this Short, I walk through the framework: decide your hours, your location, your income floor, and your non-negotiables. Then build a business model that lives inside those parameters. This requires saying no to some revenue. It requires automation and systems. But the result is a business that serves you rather than consumes you. That's the real definition of freedom.

## Short 1114: The Fastest Path to Your First Online Sale
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people overthink their first sale. Here's the shortcut.
**Duration:** 50 seconds
**Script:**
The fastest path to your first online sale is not a perfect product, a website, or a big audience. It's a conversation. Find one person with the problem you solve. Tell them what you know. Offer to help them in a structured way for a specific price. That's a sale. Do it again. Then package what you learned from those conversations into a product. The mistake most people make is skipping the conversation step and going straight to building. The conversation gives you the language your buyers use, the real problem they have, and proof that someone will pay. Start there.
**Thumbnail text:** Your first sale starts with one talk
**Description:**
Your first online sale doesn't require a website, a perfect product, or a big following. It starts with one conversation. In this Short, I walk through the direct path: find one person with the problem you solve, have a real conversation, offer structured help at a specific price, and close the sale. Then repeat. Package those conversations into a product. Most people skip the conversation phase and go straight to building something nobody asked for. The conversation gives you real buyer language, real insight into the problem, and real proof of concept. This is the fastest path to your first sale.
