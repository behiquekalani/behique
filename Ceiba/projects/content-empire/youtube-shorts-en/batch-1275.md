# YouTube Shorts EN — batch-1275

**Topic:** Productivity — eliminate decision fatigue
**Duration:** ~60 seconds

---

Decision fatigue is real and it's draining your best work. Here's how to eliminate it.

The problem: every decision you make, big or small, drains mental energy. By the afternoon, you're making worse choices, procrastinating, and doing shallow work because your decision-making capacity is depleted.

Here's what to standardize to preserve mental energy for the work that matters.

Point one: what you wear. Pick a small rotation. Decision eliminated.

Point two: what you eat on work days. Meal prep or a simple weekly rotation. Decision eliminated.

Point three: when you work on what. Use a fixed weekly schedule. Monday is content day, Tuesday is product work, Wednesday is outreach. No deciding every morning. The schedule decides for you.

Point four: which tools you use. Stop trying new apps every week. Lock in your stack and stick to it until a clear problem forces a change.

Every eliminated routine decision is decision-making capacity redirected toward your business.

Steve Jobs wore the same thing every day for this reason. Not because he lacked style. Because he knew the math.
