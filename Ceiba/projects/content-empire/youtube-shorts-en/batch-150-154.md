## Short 150 — Creator Economy
**Title:** Why the creator economy rewards the specific over the general

**HOOK (0-5 sec):**
"Generalists get scrolled past. Specialists get followed."

**3 QUICK POINTS (5-50 sec):**

Point 1: The algorithm doesn't know what to do with vague creators. If you post about "life, business, and mindset," you compete with everyone. If you post about "building a solo digital business as a student in Latin America," you own that lane.

Point 2: Specificity is not a limitation. It's a magnet. The more specific your content, the more a specific person feels like you made it exactly for them. That's what drives follows, saves, and shares.

Point 3: You can always expand later. But you have to earn the right to expand by first dominating something small. Start narrow. Go deep. Let the audience tell you when to broaden.

## Short 151 — Digital Products
**Title:** The fastest path from zero to your first $100 online

**HOOK (0-5 sec):**
"Your first $100 online is just a packaged answer to a question someone already has."

**3 QUICK POINTS (5-50 sec):**

Point 1: Think about the last problem you solved that someone else would benefit from. That's your product idea. Not a course, not a coaching program. Just a clear, documented answer.

Point 2: Package it simply. A PDF, a template, a checklist. Keep the scope tight. The narrower the promise, the clearer the value.

Point 3: Price it at $7-$27 for your first product. Low price removes buying friction. Your goal isn't revenue. It's proof of concept and your first real customer.

## Short 152 — Solo Business
**Title:** The solopreneur advantage nobody talks about

**HOOK (0-5 sec):**
"Running a business alone is not a limitation. It's a structural advantage."

**3 QUICK POINTS (5-50 sec):**

Point 1: Speed. You can make a decision and execute it in the same hour. No meetings, no alignment, no approval chains. When the market changes, you adapt immediately.

Point 2: Margin. No payroll, no office lease, no benefits package. A solo business with $10K monthly revenue keeps most of that revenue. A 10-person startup with the same revenue often runs at zero or negative.

Point 3: Alignment. Every decision you make serves your goals directly. There's no internal politics, no mission drift from investors, no compromise between your vision and someone else's.

## Short 153 — Creator Economy
**Title:** Why most creators burn out and what to do instead

**HOOK (0-5 sec):**
"Burnout doesn't come from working too hard. It comes from working on the wrong things for too long."

**3 QUICK POINTS (5-50 sec):**

Point 1: Chasing virality is the fast path to burnout. When your motivation depends on external metrics, every low-performance video feels like failure. That's unsustainable.

Point 2: The creators who last build from intrinsic motivation first. They create because the topic genuinely interests them, because the process of articulating their thinking is valuable in itself, not because they need a certain view count.

Point 3: Systems prevent the feelings of chaos that cause burnout. Batch your content. Schedule your posting. Separate creation from distribution. When the process is structured, the emotional swings flatten.

## Short 154 — Digital Products
**Title:** Three types of digital products and which one to build first

**HOOK (0-5 sec):**
"Not all digital products take the same time to build. Start with the fastest one."

**3 QUICK POINTS (5-50 sec):**

Point 1: Templates and tools. The fastest to create. If you have a spreadsheet, a Notion template, a prompt library, or a file format that saves time, that's a product. Build time: one afternoon.

Point 2: Guides and playbooks. A documented process you've already figured out. Step by step, written clearly. Builds in a weekend. Priced $17-$47. This is where most people should start.

Point 3: Courses and workshops. Higher value, longer build time. Save this for after you've validated demand with a cheaper product. Build the $27 PDF first. The $197 course comes after you know people want the result.
