**Topic:** How to Build Something Real With ADHD

**Duration:** 60-65 sec

---

Building something real when your brain works differently. What actually helps.

**Point 1: Work with your hyperfocus, not against it.**
ADHD brains get locked in on things they care about. The goal is not to fight this. It is to point it at the right target. Pick one project. When the hyperfocus hits, let it run. Build systems to capture what comes out of those sessions.

**Point 2: Make starting frictionless.**
The hardest part is beginning. Reduce every barrier to zero. Your work should be open on your screen when you sit down. Your task list should already be written from the night before. The fewer decisions before starting, the better.

**Point 3: Use external accountability.**
Deadlines work better than self-imposed ones for most ADHD builders. Post publicly about what you are building. Tell someone a completion date. External pressure creates urgency that internal motivation often cannot.

ADHD is not a reason you cannot build. It is a different operating system that works extremely well once you understand it.

Build your system around how you actually work.
