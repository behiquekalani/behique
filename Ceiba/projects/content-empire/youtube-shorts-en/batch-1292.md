# YouTube Shorts EN — batch-1292

**Hook:** Deep work is dying and most people are letting it happen.

**Point 1 — The problem**
The average knowledge worker checks their messages every six minutes. That means they never enter deep work. Deep work is the state where your best thinking happens. Without it, you produce shallow work at full-time hours.

**Point 2 — Why it matters**
Deep work is where value is created. Shallow work is where it's consumed. The people who can focus deeply for two to four hours a day will outproduce everyone working eight hours of fragmented time.

**Point 3 — The practice**
Start with one 90-minute block. No phone. No tabs. One task. Do this daily. Your capacity will expand. Your output will compound. Your competitors won't catch up because they're still checking Slack.

**CTA:** Follow for deep work protocols that actually stick in a distracted world.
