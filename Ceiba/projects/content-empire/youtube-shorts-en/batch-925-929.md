## Short 927: Why You Keep Stopping at 80%
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: progress bar stuck at 80%] You get 80% done and then you stop. Every single time.
**Content (5-50s):**
The last 20% is where the resistance lives.
80% feels safe. It's still a draft. It can still be perfect. Once it's 100%, it's out in the world and people can judge it.
That fear of judgment is the real reason you never finish.
The fix isn't more discipline. It's shrinking the stakes.
Tell yourself: the goal is not for this to be great. The goal is for this to be done and shipped.
Done and shipped teaches you more than anything you're still perfecting.
Ship the thing. Learn from what happens. Improve the next one.
The only way to get good is to have a lot of bad ones behind you.

## Short 928: The Fastest Way to Validate a Digital Product
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: DM conversation] Before you build it, validate it. Here's the fastest way.
**Content (5-50s):**
Most people build the product first and then try to find buyers.
Flip it.
Step one: post a piece of content about the topic your product would cover.
Step two: watch what happens. Saves, shares, comments saying "this was helpful" are green lights.
Step three: in your next post, ask directly. "I'm thinking about making a guide on this. Would you buy it for $27?"
Step four: if you get 10 or more yeses in the comments, start building.
You've just pre-validated your product with real people before spending a single hour creating it.
Now you're not guessing. You're building what people already said they want.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for more no-fluff product strategy. What topic would you want to validate?

## Short 929: One Habit That Separates Six-Figure Creators From Everyone Else
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: notebook with "WHAT WORKED" written on the cover] The habit that separates six-figure creators from everyone else is this.
**Content (5-50s):**
They review their content weekly.
Every week they look at what performed and ask three questions.
One: what format did the content use?
Two: what specific angle or insight drove the engagement?
Three: can I repeat this in a different context next week?
That's a review. Takes 15 minutes.
Most creators post and forget. They get lucky sometimes and never know why.
The ones who grow intentionally study their wins.
They know why something worked and they build a system around repeating it.
That's not luck. That's a skill you can learn.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for weekly creator growth tactics. Do you review your content? Be honest.
**Title:** The One Weekly Habit of Six-Figure Creators
