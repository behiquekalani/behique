**Hook (0-5s):** [Timer graphic on screen] You have 48 hours. Here's how to build and sell a digital product by Sunday night.
**Content (5-50s):**

Sunday morning: Clean it up. Add a title, a table of contents, and an intro that says exactly who this is for and what they'll get.
Sunday afternoon: Upload to Gumroad. Free account. Set your price. Write a two-sentence description. Publish.
Sunday evening: Post one piece of content mentioning the product.
That's it. You've built a digital product.
The only way to make it better is to sell the imperfect version first.

## Short 871: The Real Reason You're Not Growing on Social Media
**Hook (0-5s):** [Straight to camera, no intro] Your content isn't bad. It's not specific enough. Here's the fix.
**Content (5-50s):**
Broad content gets scrolled past. Specific content stops thumbs.
Example: "How to be more productive" gets scrolled past.
"How I stopped losing 2 hours a day to email and started finishing my actual work" makes someone stop.
The second headline describes a specific person with a specific problem. That person recognizes themselves.
Fix your content specificity in three steps:
Step one: Name the exact person your content is for. Not "entrepreneurs." Try "freelancers who can't get off their phone during work hours."
Step two: Name the exact problem. Not "productivity." Try "losing the first hour of every morning to inbox."
Step three: Name the exact outcome. Not "be more focused." Try "finish your deep work before noon every day."
Specificity is the shortcut that looks like extra work.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe for more growth-focused creator content.
**Title:** The Real Reason Your Social Media Content Isn't Growing
**Description:** If your content isn't growing, the problem is almost never the platform or the algorithm. It's specificity. Broad content doesn't stop the scroll. Specific content that describes a real person with a real problem does. This Short gives you a three-step framework for rewriting any piece of content to be more specific and more scroll-stopping. ## Short 872: Why I Post Content Every Single Day
**Hook (0-5s):** [Opening on the creator at desk, direct] People ask me why I post every day. The answer isn't what they expect.
**Content (5-50s):**
It's not for the algorithm.
It's not to go viral.
It's because daily posting is the fastest feedback loop available to a creator.
Every piece of content is a test. What hook worked? What format got saved? What topic got the most comments?
When you post once a week, you run 52 experiments a year.
When you post every day, you run 365.
At 365 experiments a year, you understand your audience in ways that weekly posters won't reach for years.
Now, daily posting isn't about quantity. You can post daily and still batch your content. Record 5 videos in one session. Schedule them out.
The point is consistent output builds consistent feedback which builds a real audience faster than anything else.
**End screen (50-60s):** Subscribe. I post creator strategy every week, sometimes more.
**Title:** Why I Post Content Every Single Day (It's Not What You Think)
**Description:** Posting daily isn't about hustle culture or grinding yourself down. It's about running the maximum number of experiments with your audience in the shortest time. More posts means more data. More data means faster clarity on what your audience wants, what format works, and what topics convert. This Short explains the strategic logic behind daily content creation. Subscribe for creator strategy and audience growth content.
