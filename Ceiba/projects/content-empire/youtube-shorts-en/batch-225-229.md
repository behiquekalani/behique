## Short 228

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Content System = Batch + Repurpose + Schedule"] Here's the shift. One day per week, you write five pieces of content. You break each one into three formats. Long post. Short clip. Carousel. That's 15 posts from one focused day.

**Description:** Content creator burnout happens when you treat posting like a daily obligation rather than a leveraged system. This short introduces the batch, repurpose, and schedule method used by consistent creators to produce two weeks of content in a single day. Stop the treadmill. Build a system instead. Whether you're on YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, or LinkedIn, this approach lets you show up consistently without exhausting your creative energy every single day.

## Short 229
**Title:** How to Price Your First Digital Product (Most Get This Wrong)
**Hook (0-3s):** [TEXT ON SCREEN: "If you priced your product under $30, read this."]
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** [TALKING HEAD] Pricing too low is the most common mistake new creators make. They think it increases sales. It usually does the opposite.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Low price = low perceived value"] Here's what happens when you price at $7. Buyers assume it's a $7 result. Buyers put less effort in. They get worse outcomes. They leave bad reviews. You make less money.

[B-ROLL: product page showing $47 and $97 price points] Most entry-level digital products should sit between $27 and $97. That range creates commitment without being a barrier. The buyer shows up. The buyer does the work. The buyer gets results.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Price for the outcome. Not the file size."] Price for the transformation you deliver. Not for how long it took to make.
**Description:** Pricing your first digital product is one of the most important decisions you'll make as a creator. Most beginners price too low and repel buyers rather than attract them. This short explains the psychology behind pricing, why low prices signal low value, and what price range works best for entry-level digital products. Learn how to price for the transformation you deliver instead of the effort it took to create. Apply this before your next product launch.
