[TEXT ON SCREEN: 90-DAY GOAL]

## Short 378

**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator holds up phone] "Don't build a product nobody wants. Test it first."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: VALIDATE BEFORE YOU BUILD]
The fastest path to a failed product is spending months creating something nobody asked for.
[B-ROLL: creator typing, then deleting file]
Here's a 48-hour validation method. Post one piece of content about the problem your product solves.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: STEP 1: POST ABOUT THE PROBLEM]
Watch the comments. If people respond with "I have this exact problem," you have demand.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: STEP 2: LISTEN TO COMMENTS]
Ask directly. "If I made a guide on X, would that be useful?" Count the responses.
[B-ROLL: comments section with engagement]
Pre-sell before building. Offer a discounted early access price. If people pay, build it. If they don't, pivot.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: PRE-SELL FIRST, BUILD AFTER]
Validation before creation saves months of wasted effort.
**Description:** One of the most costly mistakes creators make is spending months building a digital product that doesn't sell. The solution is validation before creation. This Short teaches a simple 48-hour validation method that uses content, direct questions, and pre-selling to confirm demand before you invest serious time. Learn how to test product ideas through content performance and audience feedback, how to ask for pre-orders that confirm real demand, and how to pivot quickly if the idea doesn't resonate. Build products people already want to buy.

## Short 379
**Title:** Consistency Over Perfection: The Creator's Real Edge

**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: CONSISTENCY BEATS PERFECTION]
Your best competitors are not the most talented people in your niche. They're the most consistent ones.
[B-ROLL: creator recording casually, no professional setup]
The algorithm rewards volume and frequency. Not production quality. Not perfect editing.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: VOLUME + FREQUENCY = VISIBILITY]
A creator who posts imperfect content three times a week outperforms one who posts perfect content once a month.
[B-ROLL: analytics chart showing consistent upward trend]
Consistency builds trust. Trust builds audience. Audience builds income. Perfection delays all three.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: DONE IS BETTER THAN PERFECT]
Publish it. Learn from the response. Improve next time. That's the real creative process. Not the polished one.
**Description:** Perfectionism is the most common reason creators stay invisible. While you're polishing, others are publishing. The algorithm, your audience, and your own skill development all reward consistency over perfection. This Short makes the case for publishing imperfect content regularly rather than perfect content rarely. Learn how posting frequency impacts algorithm performance, how consistency builds audience trust faster than quality alone, and why the iterative public process leads to better content over time than private polishing ever will.
