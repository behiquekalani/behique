**Topic:** The reading habit that changes how you think

**Script:**

Reading for 20 minutes a day compounds into something serious. Here is what it does.

Point one: vocabulary and writing improve automatically. You absorb sentence structure, argument framing, and word choice from great writers without trying. Your own writing gets better as a side effect.

Point two: you borrow mental models. Every book gives you new frameworks for understanding problems. A framework from a business book can solve a personal challenge. The more models you have, the better your decisions.

Point three: focused reading builds attention span. Your ability to concentrate is a muscle. Screens weaken it. Deep reading strengthens it.

Twenty minutes. One book at a time. No skimming. The return on this habit is compounding and it starts immediately. Subscribe for book recommendations that are actually worth your time.
