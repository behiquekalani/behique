## Short 950: How to Write a Hook That Stops the Scroll
**Hook (0-5s):** [Red text flashing: "STOP SCROLLING"] "Three words that stopped my scroll rate from dying."
**Content (5-50s):**
- Your hook is everything. If they scroll past second two, your content does not exist.
- The five best hook formulas: a number ("5 reasons why"), a challenge ("most creators get this wrong"), a secret ("here's what nobody tells you"), a result ("how I made $X in Y days"), a question ("are you doing this?").
- The hook must create a gap. Something the viewer does not know yet but immediately wants to.
- It should be specific. "How to grow" is weak. "How I gained 500 followers in 7 days with zero ads" is a hook.
- Say your hook in the first two seconds. Not after a slow intro.
- Test three different hooks on the same topic. The one with the highest watch time wins.
- Apply it everywhere: your title, your first sentence, your thumbnail text.

## Short 951: The Email That Sells Without Being Salesy
**Hook (0-5s):** [Open laptop, email draft visible] "The highest-converting email I ever sent did not mention the product until the last line."
**Content (5-50s):**
- Most email marketing fails because it leads with the offer before building the desire.
- The formula: story, lesson, offer.
- Open with a relatable story. Make your reader say "that's exactly my situation."
- Deliver one clear lesson from that story. Something genuinely useful.
- At the end, mention your product as the natural next step.
- The reader has already received value. The product feels like a logical extension.
- No hard sell. No desperation. Just a well-structured sequence that earns the sale.
- One email following this format can outperform a three-email promo sequence.
