**Topic:** How to price your first digital product
**Duration:** ~60 sec

**HOOK:**
Pricing your first product is easier than you think.

**POINT 1 — Start With the Problem:**
How much time does your product save? How much money does it help someone make or avoid losing? What is that worth?

If your template saves someone 5 hours of work and their time is worth $40/hour, your template is worth $200 to them. Price it at $25 and you're leaving value on the table.

**POINT 2 — The $27 Sweet Spot:**
For entry-level digital products, $17 to $37 is the sweet spot. Low enough to be an impulse buy. High enough to signal value and cover platform fees.

Don't price at $1. It signals "not worth your time."

**POINT 3 — Test and Adjust:**
Post at $27. If it sells easily, raise to $37. If it stalls, test at $17. Pricing is data collection, not a permanent commitment.
