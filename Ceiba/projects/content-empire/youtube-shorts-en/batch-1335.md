Validate before you build. Always.

This one rule would save most builders months of wasted work.

Point one: The question is not "can I build this?" The question is "will people pay for this?" Those are two completely different questions. Most builders answer the first and ignore the second.

Point two: Pre-selling is not a hack. It's good discipline. If you can get 10 people to pay for something before it exists, you have a product worth making. If you can't, the market is telling you something.

Point three: Validation takes a week. Building takes months. Do the cheap test first. A landing page, a DM campaign, a pre-order. Get a signal before you invest deeply.

The graveyard of products nobody bought is full of things that were technically well-built.

Build what the market wants, not what you wish it wanted.
