**Topic:** Creator economy — the audience trust ladder
**Duration:** ~63 seconds

---

Building an audience that buys is not just about getting followers. It's about climbing the trust ladder. Here's how it works.

Rung one: awareness. Someone sees your content for the first time. They don't know you. They're not buying anything. Your only job here is to give them a reason to come back.

Rung two: familiarity. They've seen you three, five, ten times. They know your style. They start to trust your perspective. This is where most creators stay forever.

Rung three: authority. They turn to you when they have a question in your topic area. They share your content. They recommend you to others. This takes consistent, specific, valuable content.

Rung four: buyer. They trust you enough to pay. This happens naturally when rungs one through three are solid. You don't push. They pull.

Most creators try to sell to people on rung one. It fails every time. You can't skip the ladder.

Post content that builds rungs. Patient, specific, consistent value. The buyers show up when the trust is built.
