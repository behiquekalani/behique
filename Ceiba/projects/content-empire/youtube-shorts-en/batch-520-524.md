## Short 521: Why Most Courses Fail
**Hook (0-5s):** [Laptop closing] "Most online courses fail not because of the content, but because of this one thing."
**Content (5-50s):**
The course isn't the problem.
The positioning is.
Most course creators make the same mistake:
They sell what they want to teach, not what their audience wants to learn.
Here's the gap:
You want to teach "content strategy."
Your audience wants to learn "how to get 10,000 followers in 90 days."
Same knowledge. Completely different framing.
Before building anything, ask these questions:
What is the specific result my audience wants?
What's the fastest path I can give them to that result?
What would they pay to get there faster?
Build the course around the answer.
Not around what you enjoy teaching.

## Short 522: The Referral Loop
**Hook (0-5s):** [Circle graphic] "The cheapest client acquisition system costs you nothing. Most businesses never build it."
**Content (5-50s):**
It's a referral loop.
Here's how it works:
Deliver results that exceed expectations.
Wait 2-3 weeks. Then ask: did this help you?
If yes: do you know one person who needs the same thing?
If they say yes, introduce yourself with a short message they can forward.
That's it.
No ads. No cold outreach. No content calendar required.
Most service businesses get their best clients through referrals accidentally.
The ones who grow intentionally build the referral loop on purpose.
Set a reminder for every client 3 weeks post-delivery.
One question. One conversation. Compounding results.

## Short 523: The 80-20 of Content Creation
**Hook (0-5s):** [Bold numbers: 80/20] "20% of your content will drive 80% of your results. Here's how to find it."
**Content (5-50s):**
The Pareto Principle shows up everywhere in content.
A handful of your posts will drive almost all your growth.
The problem: you don't know which ones until you post them.
So what do you do?
Post consistently for 30 days.
At day 30, look at your analytics.
Find the top 3 performing pieces.
Ask: what did they have in common?
Topic? Format? Hook style? Posting time?
Double down on those patterns.
Cut everything that didn't move.
This is not about guessing what works.
It's about running experiments and reading the results honestly.
Your data is smarter than your intuition.
