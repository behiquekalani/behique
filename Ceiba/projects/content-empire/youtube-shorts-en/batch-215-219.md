## Short 215
**Title:** How to Write Hooks That Stop the Scroll Every Time
**Hook (0-3s):** [Finger snapping] "You have three seconds. Here is how to use them."
**Duration:** 55s

**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: The 4 Hook Formulas That Work]
Every hook that stops a scroll does one of four things.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Formula 1 — Specific Result + Time Frame]
One: promises a specific result in a specific time. "How I made $3K in 30 days with one product."
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Formula 2 — Challenge a Belief]
Two: challenges a belief the audience holds. "Posting daily is killing your growth."
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Formula 3 — Counterintuitive Reveal]
Three: reveals something surprising. "I raised my prices and sold more."
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Formula 4 — Name Their Problem]
Four: names a specific person's exact problem. "If you're a freelancer stuck at $3K a month, watch this."
[B-ROLL: Analytics showing high retention videos]
[Direct to camera]
Pick one formula. Use it for the next 30 videos. Watch your average view duration change. The hook is the whole game.

**Description:**
The hook is the single most important element of any short-form video. If you lose your viewer in the first three seconds, no amount of quality content will save the video. This Short breaks down the four hook formulas that consistently stop the scroll and earn viewers across TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels. Whether you challenge a belief, promise a specific outcome, reveal something counterintuitive, or name a viewer's exact problem, these formulas work across any niche. Practice writing ten hooks for every video and test the ones that feel most like a punch. Your retention metrics will shift within weeks.

## Short 218
**Title:** The Simple Formula for Writing a Product Description That Sells
**Hook (0-3s):** [Split screen: boring vs compelling product page] "One of these sold 47 copies this week. The other sold zero."
**Duration:** 55s

**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: The Product Description Formula]
Most product descriptions describe the product. Good ones sell the transformation.
[B-ROLL: Two product pages side by side]
Weak description: "A 30-page guide with five chapters covering email marketing basics."
Strong description: "The exact email system that took my list from zero to 2,000 subscribers in 60 days, with copy-paste templates included."
[TEXT ON SCREEN: The Formula]
Line one: Who this is for.
Line two: What problem it solves.
Line three: The specific result they will get.
Line four: What is included.
Line five: Why trust you.
[Direct to camera]
[TEXT ON SCREEN: Describe the after, not the what]
Every line should answer the buyer's question: "What is in this for me?" Answer that question in five lines and your conversion rate goes up immediately.

**Description:**
Product descriptions are one of the most overlooked conversion levers for digital product creators. Most descriptions list features. Effective descriptions sell outcomes. The difference between a product page that converts and one that does not almost always comes down to how clearly the transformation is communicated. This Short gives you the exact five-line product description formula that answers every buyer's core question before they even have to ask it. Apply this formula to every product you sell on Gumroad, Shopify, or any other platform and watch your conversion rate improve immediately.

**Tags:** digital products, product description, copywriting, Gumroad, solopreneur, content creator, conversion rate, sales page, make money online, creator economy, product launch, online business
