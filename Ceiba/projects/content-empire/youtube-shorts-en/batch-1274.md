**Topic:** Creator economy — monetization stages
**Duration:** ~63 seconds

---

Monetization doesn't happen all at once. It happens in stages. Here's the order that actually works.

Stage one: digital products. Start here. No audience required for the first sale. Build something, price it, sell it directly. This proves the concept and gives you something to point your content toward.

Stage two: affiliate income. Once you're creating consistent content, add affiliate links for tools you already use. This earns in the background without creating anything new. Passive and low effort.

Stage three: services. Your audience trusts you now. Offer a done-for-you version of what you teach. This is active income but high per-client value. Good for building cash while you scale passive.

Stage four: community or membership. Once you have a real audience, a paid community can generate consistent monthly revenue. This is the hardest to build but the most stable once it's running.

Stage five: brand deals and sponsorships. This comes last, not first. Brands pay for audiences, not follower counts alone. Build the audience first.

Each stage funds the next one. Start at stage one.
