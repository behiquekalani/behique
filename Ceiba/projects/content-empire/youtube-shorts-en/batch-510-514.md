## Short 511: The Freelancer's Pricing Trap
**Hook (0-5s):** [Visual: price tag flipping] "Charging hourly is the fastest way to cap your income as a freelancer."
**Content (5-50s):**
Here's the trap:
You charge $50/hour.
You get faster at the work.
Now the same task takes you two hours instead of four.
You just cut your own income in half.
Hourly pricing punishes expertise.
The fix: charge for outcomes, not time.
A logo that took you two hours is not worth $100.
It's worth what the brand means to the business.
A sales page that took you four hours is not worth $200.
It's worth a percentage of the revenue it generates.
Stop trading time for money.
Start pricing based on the result you deliver.
Your skill should make you richer, not cheaper.

## Short 512: You Don't Need a Big Audience to Make Money
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text: "1,000 true fans"] "You don't need 100,000 followers. You need 100 people who trust you completely."
**Content (5-50s):**
Here's the math most creators ignore:
100 people who trust you and buy everything you create.
At $100 per product, that's $10,000.
At $500 per coaching program, that's $50,000.
You don't need virality. You need depth.
The creators chasing millions of followers are often making less than the ones serving a tight, loyal 500.
How to build a small, high-trust audience:
1. Go deep on one specific problem, not wide on a general topic.
2. Respond to every comment and DM, especially early on.
3. Give your best ideas away for free. Save the system for the product.
Small and trusted beats large and ignored.
