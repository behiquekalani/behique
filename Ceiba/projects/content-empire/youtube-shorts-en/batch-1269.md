**Topic:** Productivity — the 3-task rule
**Duration:** ~58 seconds

Stop writing 20-item to-do lists. Here's why and what to do instead.

Long task lists create the illusion of productivity. You spend more time managing the list than doing the work. And when you end the day with 14 items unchecked, you feel like a failure even if you actually did the three most important things.

The 3-task rule: every morning, pick exactly three tasks. One big one, one medium one, one quick one. That's your day. Everything else is optional if there's time.

Why three works. It forces prioritization. You can't put 20 things on a list of three, so you have to decide what actually matters. And three is achievable, which means you end the day in a win state.

The key is ranking them. Your number one task should be the hardest, highest-value thing. Do that one first, before email, before social media, before anything else.

One done right beats ten done halfway.

Try it tomorrow. Three tasks. Ranked. Do number one first.
