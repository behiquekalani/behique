**Duration:** ~65 seconds

The difference between good and bad AI output is usually the prompt. Here's a simple framework for getting better results every time.

Point one: be specific about who you are and what you want. Instead of "write a caption for my product," try "write a TikTok caption for a $27 Notion template aimed at college students who want to organize their semester. Tone: casual and direct. Under 100 words."

Point two: give context about the audience. The AI doesn't know who you're talking to unless you tell it. Include the audience's pain point, their situation, and what they care about.

Point three: specify the format. Tell it how many words, what structure, what tone. "Three bullet points, each under 20 words" gives you something useful. "Write some tips" gives you something generic.

Point four: iterate fast. If the first output isn't right, don't start over. Say "make it shorter" or "make it more direct" or "cut the fluff in the second paragraph." AI responds well to specific edits.

Better prompts, better output, less editing time.
