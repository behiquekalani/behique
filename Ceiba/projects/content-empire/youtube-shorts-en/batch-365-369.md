## Short 365
**Title:** How to Build a Content System That Never Burns Out
**Hook (0-3s):** [Cut to creator at desk, coffee in hand] "This one system killed my burnout for good."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE CONTENT SYSTEM]
Most creators burn out because they treat every post as a new decision. That's exhausting.
[B-ROLL: calendar app with color blocks]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: PILLAR, SHORT, WRITTEN]

[B-ROLL: phone on tripod, recording]

[TEXT ON SCREEN: BATCH ONCE, PUBLISH ALL WEEK]
Build this system and you'll never stare at a blank page again.
**Description:** Creator burnout is real, but it's not caused by working too much. It's caused by making too many decisions without a system. In this Short, I break down the content batching system that removes decision fatigue and lets you create more with less stress. Learn how pillar content, three formats, and a weekly production day can transform your workflow. Perfect for digital creators, content marketers, and solopreneurs building an online presence.

## Short 367
**Title:** The 3-Second Hook Formula That Stops the Scroll
**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator snaps fingers at camera] "Your first 3 seconds are costing you views."
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE HOOK FORMULA]
The algorithm doesn't care how good your video is. It cares how many people stay past three seconds.
[B-ROLL: video analytics showing drop-off graph]
Here's the formula that works. Open with a provocation, a number, or a counterintuitive statement.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: PROVOCATION — NUMBER — COUNTERINTUITIVE]
"Most people think X. But actually Y." "I made $10K doing one thing differently." "Stop doing Z immediately."
[B-ROLL: creator filming on phone]
Don't introduce yourself. Don't explain the video. Hook first. Context second.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: HOOK FIRST. ALWAYS.]
Write your hook before your script. Then reverse-engineer the content to deliver on it.
**Description:** The first three seconds of your video determine whether anyone watches the rest. If you're losing viewers immediately, your hook is the problem. This Short teaches the 3-second hook formula used by top creators to stop the scroll and maximize watch time. Learn the three hook types, provocation, number-based, and counterintuitive, and how to apply them to every video you create. Whether you're on TikTok, YouTube Shorts, or Instagram Reels, mastering the hook is the highest-leverage skill in short-form video.

## Short 368
**Title:** How to Price Your Digital Product Without Guessing
**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator holds up price tag] "You're underpricing and it's hurting your sales."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: DIGITAL PRODUCT PRICING FRAMEWORK]
Most new creators price on fear. They go low because they think that's what sells.
[B-ROLL: price comparison on screen]
It's the opposite. Low prices signal low quality before anyone opens the product.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: PRICE IS A SIGNAL]
Here's a simple framework. Price based on the outcome you deliver, not the hours it took.
[B-ROLL: calculator app]
If your guide helps someone earn an extra $500 a month, $97 is nothing. $197 is still cheap.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: VALUE-BASED PRICING WINS]
Anchor your price to the result. Then test. The right price attracts the right buyers.
**Description:** Pricing a digital product is one of the biggest challenges for new creators and solopreneurs. Most people default to low prices out of fear, but this strategy backfires. Low prices attract difficult buyers and signal low quality. This Short introduces value-based pricing, a framework that anchors your price to the outcome your product delivers rather than the time it took to create. Learn why $97 can feel cheaper than $27 in the right context and how to set prices that attract confident, committed buyers.

## Short 369
**Title:** Why Your Niche Is Too Broad and How to Fix It
**Hook (0-3s):** [Creator shakes head slowly] "Talking to everyone is the reason no one buys from you."
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE NICHE PROBLEM]
"Fitness creator." "Business coach." "Marketing tips." These aren't niches. They're categories.
[B-ROLL: crowd scene, then single person]
A niche is a person, not a topic. The more specific the person, the stronger the content.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: NICHE = PERSON, NOT TOPIC]
Compare: "productivity content" versus "productivity for ADHD entrepreneurs building an online business."
[B-ROLL: engagement analytics spiking]
The second one has fewer viewers and more buyers. Buyers are the only metric that pays.
[TEXT ON SCREEN: FEWER VIEWERS. MORE BUYERS.]
Pick your person. Name their problem. Speak only to them. Watch your conversion rate shift.
**Description:** Most creators stay stuck because their niche is too broad. When you create content for everyone, you connect with no one. A niche isn't a topic. It's a person with a specific problem in a specific context. This Short walks you through the difference between a category and a real niche, and shows how narrowing your focus leads to higher conversions, better engagement, and stronger audience loyalty. If you're getting views but no sales, a vague niche is likely the cause. This video shows you how to fix it in one step.
