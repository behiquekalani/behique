**Topic:** How to charge what you are worth

**Script:**

Charging what you are worth starts with one mindset shift. You are not trading time. You are delivering an outcome.

Point one: reframe your offer around the result, not the process. The client does not care how many hours you work. They care what their business looks like after you are done.

Point two: understand the value of the outcome. If your work helps a client make an extra $10,000, charging $2,000 is a bargain. Price against the value you create.

Point three: test higher prices on new inquiries. You will be surprised how rarely people push back when the offer is framed correctly. The pushback usually means the value proposition needs clarity, not a lower price.

Your rate reflects your belief in the outcome you deliver. Raise both. Subscribe for more on pricing strategy.
