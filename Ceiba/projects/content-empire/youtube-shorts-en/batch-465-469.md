## Short 466: Why You're Overthinking Your Niche
**Hook (0-5s):** [Pointing to camera] Stop overthinking your niche. You already know enough to start.
**Content (5-50s):**
The niche isn't the decision. The consistency is.

Here's the real framework:

Who has a painful problem? Find that person.
Can you solve one part of that problem? Build that solution.
Can you talk about it 3 times a week for 6 months? That's your niche.

Most people overthink the niche because it feels permanent. It's not. Your niche is a starting point, not a life sentence.

Pick the most specific version of what you know. Not "business." Not even "online business." Try "online business for first-generation college graduates." That's findable. That's a niche.

Start specific. You can expand later. You can't expand from zero.

## Short 469: The Content Calendar You'll Actually Use
**Hook (0-5s):** [Tossing a planner away] Every content calendar fails for the same reason. Not this one.
**Content (5-50s):**
Most content calendars are built around dates. Post on Monday. Post on Wednesday. Post on Friday.

The problem: you don't always have something worth saying on those days.

Build your calendar around ideas instead.

Step 1: Keep an idea dump. One note where you add ideas as they hit you. No editing.
Step 2: Once a week, pull three ideas from the dump. These become your posts.
Step 3: Write all three in one sitting. Schedule them. Done.

The output is the same. But you're working with ideas when they're hot, not forcing content on a cold Tuesday morning.

Batch your creation. Spread your distribution. That's a calendar you'll actually stick to.
