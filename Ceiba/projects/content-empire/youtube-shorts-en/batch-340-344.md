## Short 342
**Title:** How to Validate a Digital Product Before You Build It
**Hook (0-3s):** [Close-up, direct] "Build after you sell. Not before."
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE VALIDATION FIRST RULE]
Most creators spend weeks building a product nobody asked for. Then they launch. Then silence.

[B-ROLL: creator looking at screen disappointed]
The fix is to validate demand before you invest the time.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE 3-STEP VALIDATION METHOD]
Step one: post content about the topic for one week. Count how many people ask for more.

Step two: post a poll or ask directly. "Would you buy a template for this?" Ten genuine yes votes is enough to build.

[B-ROLL: poll on phone screen]
Step three: pre-sell. Announce the product before it is done. Accept payment. Then build it with confidence knowing someone actually wants it.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: SELL FIRST, BUILD SECOND]
This approach eliminates the risk of building in silence. It also gives you real buyer feedback before the final version is done.

**Description:** The most common mistake new digital product creators make is building extensively before confirming anyone wants what they are making. This short teaches the three-step validation method: content testing, direct polling, and pre-selling. By confirming demand before investing build time, you eliminate the risk of launching to silence. Digital product validation. How to validate a product idea. Pre-sell digital products. Creator economy. Solopreneur product strategy. First digital product. Online business for beginners.

**Duration:** 60s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: WHY CALENDARS FAIL]
Most content calendars fail because they plan too far ahead without accounting for energy levels.

[B-ROLL: overly complex content calendar]
You plan 30 days of content on Sunday. By Tuesday, three things have changed and the whole calendar feels irrelevant.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: THE WEEKLY SPRINT MODEL]
The system that works is a weekly sprint. Not a monthly plan.

Every Sunday, plan only the coming week. Five posts. Two batching sessions. One writing session.

[B-ROLL: simple weekly calendar on screen]
The plan is short enough to be realistic and long enough to give structure.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: BATCH BY ENERGY, NOT BY DATE]
Batch filming on your high-energy days. Write captions on your medium-energy days. Schedule on your low-energy days.

Match the task to the energy available and the calendar stops feeling like a punishment.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: DONE EVERY WEEK]
This system accounts for real life. That is why it gets done week after week.

**Description:** The reason most content calendars fail is not lack of discipline. It is a planning system that does not account for how energy and motivation actually fluctuate. The weekly sprint model solves this by shrinking the planning horizon and matching tasks to energy levels rather than dates. This short gives you the exact weekly content system used by consistent creators who publish without burnout. Content calendar system. Creator productivity. Weekly content plan. [TEXT ON SCREEN: THE FORMULA]
Define your audience by: who they are, what specific problem they have, and what makes their version of the problem unique. That is your niche. That is your business.

**Description:** The specificity paradox says that targeting a smaller, more defined audience generates more revenue than targeting broad categories. This is because specificity creates resonance, resonance creates trust, and trust drives purchases. This short explains how to define your ideal audience using the three-part formula: who they are, their specific problem, and what makes their situation unique. Niche audience strategy.
