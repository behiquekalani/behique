## Short 1
**Title:** Why Cheap Prices Attract the Worst Clients

**Hook (0-5s):**
The moment you lower your price to get more buyers, you filter out the serious ones.

**Body:**
- Price is a filter, not just a number.
- Low prices attract people who want a deal, not a transformation.
- High prices signal that what you built has real weight.
- The right buyer never complains about price when the outcome is clear.

**CTA:** If you're pricing yourself low and still getting ghost requests, the problem isn't your audience. Raise the price. Watch who stays.

## Short 2
**Title:** The One Pricing Move Most Creators Miss

**Hook (0-5s):**
You're not charging for your time. You're charging for the result your buyer stops needing to figure out.

**Body:**
- Hourly pricing punishes expertise. The faster you work, the less you earn.
- Outcome-based pricing flips the model.
- Price the destination, not the drive.
- What is the cost of your buyer NOT having this knowledge?

**CTA:** Reframe your next product as a solution to a specific pain. Then price the pain away.

## Short 3
**Title:** You're Not Overpriced. You're Under-Positioned.

**Hook (0-5s):**
Nobody pays $5 for a Rolex. But they pay $12,000 without blinking. The watch didn't change. The framing did.

**Body:**
- Positioning is the work that makes pricing make sense.
- Vague offers get compared on price. Specific offers get bought on trust.
- One clear promise beats a list of ten features.
- Your price tells people how seriously you take your own work.

**CTA:** Audit your offer page today. If someone can compare you to someone else on price, your positioning needs work.

## Short 4
**Title:** The Attention Economy Has One Rule

**Hook (0-5s):**
Your content doesn't compete with other creators. It competes with every other decision your viewer is about to make.

**Body:**
- You are competing with dopamine, not just other videos.
- The first three seconds either buy attention or lose it.
- Relevance beats production quality every time.
- Attention is currency. Spend it on what creates trust.

**CTA:** Before you post, ask: would I stop scrolling for this? If the answer isn't immediate, rewrite the hook.

## Short 5
**Title:** Content That Converts vs. Content That Gets Liked

**Hook (0-5s):**
Likes don't pay rent. Conversions do.

**Body:**
- Entertaining content builds an audience. Converting content builds a business.
- You need both, but they serve different purposes in your funnel.
- Viral posts raise awareness. Specific posts create buyers.
- One direct post about who you help and what you sell beats 20 relatable memes.

**CTA:** Post one direct offer this week. No metaphors. No fluff. Just who it's for, what it does, and where to get it.

## Short 6
**Title:** How to Price Your First Knowledge Product

**Hook (0-5s):**
Most first-time creators price their product at $9 because they're scared. That fear costs them everything.

**Body:**
- Fear-based pricing creates a low-margin, high-volume trap.
- Your first product is proof of concept. Price it at what it's worth.
- $97 to $197 is the sweet spot for entry digital products.
- Research what your buyer already spends to solve this problem.

**CTA:** Find three competing products in your niche. Price yours at the midpoint with a stronger promise.

## Short 7
**Title:** Your Personal Brand Is Not Your Personality

**Hook (0-5s):**
Stop trying to be likable. Start being credible.

**Body:**
- A personal brand is a signal about what you stand for and who you serve.
- Personality attracts followers. Point of view attracts buyers.
- Pick one niche and go deep. Depth builds authority faster than breadth.
- People follow those who teach them to see something differently.

**CTA:** Write down the one belief about your niche that most people get wrong. That's your brand thesis.

## Short 8
**Title:** The Solopreneur Income Stack Explained in 60 Seconds

**Hook (0-5s):**
One income stream isn't a business. It's a fragile bet.

**Body:**
- Layer one: active service income. Solves cash flow.
- Layer two: productized service or course. Scales your time.
- Layer three: passive digital products. Earns while you sleep.
- The goal is to reduce dependency on any single stream.

**CTA:** Identify which layer you're missing right now. That's where to focus the next 90 days.

## Short 9
**Title:** Why Niche Content Grows Faster Than General Content

**Hook (0-5s):**
A channel about "productivity" is forgettable. A channel about productivity for solo developers is a category.

**Body:**
- Niche content attracts people looking for exactly that thing.
- The algorithm rewards specificity because retention rates go up.
- Specific audiences buy. General audiences browse.
- Narrow your topic. Expand your depth.

**CTA:** Take your current topic and add one specific qualifier. That qualifier is your niche.

**Title:** The Moment Your Content Starts Working

**Hook (0-5s):**
Most creators quit three months before their content compounds.

**Body:**
- Content has a lag effect. What you post today pays in 60-90 days.
