Most people are busy all day and produce nothing.

Here's the difference between busy and productive.

Point one: Busy fills time. Productive moves the needle. You can spend 8 hours in meetings, emails, and shallow tasks and have nothing to show for it. Real output requires deep work, and deep work requires protection.

Point two: The One Thing question changes everything: What is the single task that, if done today, makes everything else easier or unnecessary? That question cuts through the noise and finds the actual priority.

Point three: Work in offense/defense blocks. The first block of your day is offense. Deep work, creative output, the hard stuff. Afternoon is defense. Emails, meetings, admin. This structure is not perfect but it's far better than reactive all day.

Try this for one week.

You'll get more done in five days than you did in the last two weeks.
