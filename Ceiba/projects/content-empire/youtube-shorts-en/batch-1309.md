**Topic:** What AI tools are actually good for
**Duration:** ~60 sec

---

**HOOK:**
AI tools don't make you smarter. They make you faster.

**POINT 1 — The Right Use Case:**
AI is excellent at first drafts, research summaries, brainstorming, code scaffolding, and repetitive formatting tasks. It is bad at nuance, relationships, and anything requiring real judgment.

Use it for the 20% of your work that requires speed, not the 80% that requires thought.

**POINT 2 — The Prompt Is the Skill:**
Getting useful output from AI is a skill. Vague prompts get vague answers. Specific prompts with context get actionable results.

Learning to prompt well is one of the highest-leverage skills right now.

**POINT 3 — Stack It, Don't Replace With It:**
The best AI users combine their expertise with AI speed. They edit, refine, and direct rather than just accepting the first output.

Your judgment on top of AI output is the actual product.
