## Short 893: How to Write a Hook That Stops the Scroll
**Hook (0-5s):** [Fast cut to text: "Most hooks fail in the first word."] Your first word determines whether anyone reads the second.
**Content (5-50s):**
Strong hooks do one of four things.
One: make a bold claim. "You're doing email wrong."
Two: create a knowledge gap. "The thing every successful creator does that nobody talks about."
Three: use numbers. "3 reasons your content gets no views."
Four: speak directly to pain. "If you're tired of posting and getting nothing back..."
Weak hooks describe. Strong hooks provoke.
"Here are some tips" is a description. Nobody cares.
"You're making this mistake and it's killing your reach" is a provocation. Now they have to read.
Write your hook last. It's the hardest part and deserves the most time.
