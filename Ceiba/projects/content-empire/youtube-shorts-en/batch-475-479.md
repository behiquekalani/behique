Input, output, teach. That's the loop. Thirty days on any practical skill.

## Short 476: The Freelancer's Escape Plan
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text flash: "Trading hours for dollars is a trap"] Every freelancer hits the income ceiling. Here's the exit.
**Content (5-50s):**
The trap: you get good, you get busy, you raise rates, you're still trading time for money. The ceiling is your hours.

The exit has three moves:

Move one: productize your service. Turn what you do for clients into a guide, template, or course. Sell it without your time attached.

Move two: build a small audience around your expertise. The audience becomes your sales team.

Move three: add one recurring revenue stream. Retainer client, subscription product, membership. Predictable base income that lets you take bigger swings.

You don't quit freelancing overnight. You replace one income stream at a time until the product income exceeds the client income.

Then you choose how much client work to keep.

## Short 479: The Solopreneur Income Stack
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text layers stacking on screen] One income stream is fragile. Here's how to stack them.
**Content (5-50s):**
Most solopreneurs start with one income source. Freelance work, one product, one client. That's not a business. That's a dependency.

The income stack that creates real freedom:

Layer one: service. Active income. Trades time but funds everything else.

Layer two: digital product. One-time effort. Sells 24 hours a day. Built from what you do for clients.

Layer three: content. Free acquisition channel. Brings people to layers one and two.

Layer four: recurring. Newsletter paid tier, membership, retainer. Predictable base that stabilizes everything.

Each layer funds the next one. Service funds the product. Product funds the time to create content. Content funds the membership.

You don't build all four at once. You stack them in order. One layer at a time.
