## Short 290
**Title:** How to Write a Hook That Stops the Scroll
**Hook (0-3s):** [Camera on face, fast cut] "Three seconds. That's all you get."
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[TEXT ON SCREEN: "The 3-second rule"]
The first three seconds of your video decide everything. If you don't create immediate curiosity, the algorithm has no reason to distribute it further.

[B-ROLL: Split screen comparing weak vs strong hook openings]
Weak hook: "Today I want to talk about content strategy." Strong hook: "Most creators are killing their own reach and don't know it."

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Three hook formulas"]
Bold claim. Counter-intuitive statement. Specific number. Pick one. Write it. Rewrite it twice.

[B-ROLL: Creator reviewing script notes]
Your hook is the most important sentence in your video. Treat it like the headline of the most important article you'll ever write. If the hook fails, the rest doesn't matter.
**Description:** Learning how to write a strong video hook is the single highest-leverage skill for short-form content creators. Without a compelling hook, even the best content goes unseen. This Short breaks down the three-second rule, explains why weak hooks kill algorithmic reach, and gives you three proven hook formulas you can apply immediately to your next TikTok, YouTube Short, or Reel. Includes real examples comparing weak and strong openings and a simple process for writing and rewriting hooks before you publish. Content creation, TikTok hooks, YouTube Shorts strategy.
