## Short 530: The Niche-Down Formula
**Hook (0-5s):** [Whiteboard diagram] Most creators pick a niche that's 10x too broad. Here's the formula to fix it.
**Content (5-50s):**
The formula is: [who] plus [specific problem] plus [desired outcome].
Bad niche: personal finance. Good niche: personal finance for freelancers who want to stop living paycheck to paycheck.
Bad niche: fitness. Good niche: home workouts for busy parents who have 20 minutes and no equipment.
The narrower your niche, the stronger your hook, the clearer your content, the easier it is for the right people to find you.
You're not reducing your audience. You're increasing your relevance to the audience that matters.
A thousand people who know you're exactly for them is worth more than a million who kind of relate to you.

## Short 531: Digital Products 101 in 60 Seconds
**Hook (0-5s):** [Products appearing on screen one by one] You could build any of these in a week. Here's what they are.
**Content (5-50s):**
Digital products are things you make once and sell forever. No inventory. No shipping. No overhead.
The most accessible types to start with: templates, which solve a specific workflow problem instantly. Guides, which walk someone through a process step by step. Swipe files, which are collections of proven examples in a specific category.
How to pick which one: find the question you get asked most often. Answer it thoroughly. Package it. Price it.
The person who already trusts you from your content is the most likely buyer. You don't need a huge audience. You need a specific audience and a product that solves their exact problem.

## Short 533: How to Come Up With Content Ideas Every Day
**Hook (0-5s):** [Screen shows empty content calendar] Never stare at a blank content calendar again after this.
**Content (5-50s):**
The problem isn't that you have no ideas. It's that you don't have a capture system.
Ideas come while you're in the shower, driving, or half-asleep. If you don't capture them immediately, they're gone.
Step one: create a single capture channel. One note on your phone. Nothing fancy.
Step two: every time you notice yourself thinking "I wonder why this works," or "I wish someone had told me this," that's a content idea. Capture it immediately.
Step three: every week, review your captures and pick three to develop into full posts or videos.
You already have more ideas than you think. You just haven't been catching them.

## Short 534: The Real Cost of Cheap Tools
**Hook (0-5s):** [Two side-by-side setups] Cheap setup on the left. Pro setup on the right. Which one earns more? The answer isn't what you think.
**Content (5-50s):**
The expensive setup doesn't always win. But the wrong cheap tools cost you more than money.
The real cost of cheap tools: time. A clunky editing tool that takes twice as long costs you hours every week. Multiply that by 52 weeks and you've lost weeks of your year.
The right question to ask: does this tool speed me up or slow me down? Is the time cost worth the money saved?
Where to go cheap: storage, hosting, design. Where to invest: your primary creation tool, your distribution tool, your email platform.
Your tools should remove friction from your process, not add it.
