## Short 271
**Title:** How to Write Content That Sells Without Feeling Salesy
**Hook (0-3s):** [TEXT ON SCREEN: "STOP selling. Start doing this instead."] [Spoken: "Content that sells never looks like selling."]
**Duration:** 50s
**Script:**
[B-ROLL: Person scrolling social media, then stopping at a post]

The content that converts best doesn't feel like an ad. It feels like a conversation with someone who gets you.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "The formula: Problem + Empathy + Solution + Proof + Offer"]

Start by naming the exact problem your reader is experiencing right now. Use their language, not yours. Then show that you understand it because you lived it. Offer the solution briefly. Back it up with a result. Then make the offer.

[B-ROLL: Sales page converting on phone screen]

When someone reads that and thinks, "This is exactly my situation," they're already sold before you finish. The sale happens in the empathy, not the pitch. Write to be understood, not to be impressive.

[TEXT ON SCREEN: "Empathy converts. Impressiveness doesn't."]
**Description:** The creators and solopreneurs who make the most money with content never feel like they're selling. This video teaches you the exact content formula that drives conversions without the pushy energy. Learn how to use empathy, specificity, and social proof to write content that makes the right person feel deeply understood and ready to buy. This framework applies to social media posts, email, landing pages, and video scripts. Stop selling. Start connecting.
