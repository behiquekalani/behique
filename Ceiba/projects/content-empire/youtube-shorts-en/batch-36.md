## Short 1
**Title:** The Attention Economy Punishes Average

**Hook (0-5s):**
In a world with infinite content, average gets ignored by default.

**Body:**
- The cost of mediocre content is not just low views. It's a damaged reputation.
- Remarkable does not mean perfect. It means worth remarking on.
- One strong, specific post outperforms ten generic ones every week.
- Your signal needs to cut through or it becomes noise.

**CTA:** Delete your weakest post this week. Replace it with something you're actually proud to have your name on.

## Short 2
**Title:** How to Make Content That Sells Without Being Salesy

**Hook (0-5s):**
The best sales copy never feels like sales copy. Here's how to write it.

**Body:**
- Describe the problem so accurately that readers assume you have the solution.
- Show the before state. Let the reader feel it.
- Introduce the result. Don't oversell it. Just be specific.
- Add one clear next step. One. Not five.

**CTA:** Write one post today that describes your ideal buyer's current pain better than they could describe it themselves. That post will sell without trying.

## Short 3

The product isn't the problem. The audience temperature is.

**Body:**

## Short 7
**Title:** Pricing Psychology: Why Round Numbers Feel Wrong

**Hook (0-5s):**
$100 feels more expensive than $97. And $97 feels more expensive than $94. Here's the science.

**Body:**
- Prices ending in 7 or 9 have measurably higher conversion rates.
- Round numbers trigger scrutiny. Odd numbers feel calculated, not arbitrary.
- The difference between $99 and $100 is one dollar. The perception gap is far larger.
- Small pricing adjustments can shift conversions by 10-20 percent.

**CTA:** If your price is a round number, test a version ending in 7. Measure for 30 days. The data will surprise you.

## Short 8
**Title:** The Platform You Post On Changes the Content You Should Make

**Hook (0-5s):**
The same idea fails on Twitter and wins on YouTube. Platform shapes format.

**Body:**
- YouTube rewards depth and watch time. Go long, go detailed.
- Twitter rewards compression. One idea, maximum tension.
- Instagram rewards visual proof and aspirational positioning.
- Cross-posting without reformatting is leaving results on the table.

**CTA:** Take your best-performing piece of content and adapt it natively for one other platform this week. Different format, same thesis.

It's not consistency. It's not aesthetics. It's specificity.
