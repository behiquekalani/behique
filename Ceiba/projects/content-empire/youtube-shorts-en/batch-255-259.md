## Short 257
**Title:** How to Validate a Digital Product Before You Build It
**Hook (0-3s):** [CREATOR holds up laptop] "I wasted three weeks building a product nobody wanted. Then I learned this."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** [TEXT ON SCREEN: Validate before you build.] Before writing a single word of your product, ask your audience directly. Post a poll. Send an email. Ask: "Would you buy a guide on X for $27?" If 10% of your audience says yes, build it. [B-ROLL: Twitter poll, email reply] Even simpler: presell it. Announce the product before it exists. Take payments. Then build what you've already sold. [TEXT ON SCREEN: Pre-sell first. Build second.] This eliminates the risk of building something nobody wants. You only build what people have already proven they'll pay for. That's how you ship with confidence.
**Description:** Building a digital product nobody wants is one of the most common and costly mistakes in the creator economy. This short teaches you the exact validation method used by smart solopreneurs before writing a single word of their product. From running audience polls to sending direct emails to preselling before the product exists, these techniques confirm real demand before you invest your time. Validate first. Build second. Ship with confidence.

## Short 259
**Title:** The Newsletter Strategy That Turns Readers Into Buyers
**Hook (0-3s):** [EMAIL notification sound plays] "My newsletter makes more money per subscriber than any of my social accounts."
**Duration:** 55s
**Script:** [TEXT ON SCREEN: Email converts 5x better than social.] Here's why most newsletters fail to make money. They inform but never sell. They share but never offer. [B-ROLL: newsletter open, product link] Every newsletter issue should have one of three jobs: educate, entertain, or sell. Usually two of three. Never zero. [TEXT ON SCREEN: The 80/20 newsletter formula.] 80% value. 20% offer. In a newsletter of 400 words, 80 words should point toward something the reader can buy or do. Keep the ratio. Never send an email that has no direction. Your subscribers trusted you with their inbox. Use that trust wisely.
**Description:** Most newsletters fail to generate revenue because they deliver value without ever making an offer. This short breaks down the 80/20 newsletter formula that consistently converts readers into buyers. Learn how to structure each issue to educate, entertain, and include a clear, non-pushy offer that drives digital product sales. If you have an email list and it's not generating consistent revenue, this is the one strategy adjustment that can change your results immediately.
**Tags:** newsletter monetization, email marketing, creator economy, digital products, solopreneur, email list, newsletter strategy, content creator, passive income, email marketing tips, make money with email, online business
