## Short 502: The 20-Minute Rule for Any Hard Project
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text: "20 MINUTES"] If you can not start, use this rule. It works every time.
**Content (5-50s):** Commit to twenty minutes. Tell yourself you will stop after twenty minutes if you want. Set a timer. Start. Here is what happens: after twenty minutes you are already in flow. Starting is the hardest part. Your brain resists beginnings, not middles. Once you are in the work, you do not want to stop. The twenty-minute rule removes the starting resistance without the pressure of a full session.
**Outro (50-60s):** Subscribe for more anti-procrastination techniques that actually hold up.
**Title:** The 20-Minute Rule That Cures Procrastination Every Time
**Description:** Your brain resists starting, not working. The 20-minute rule removes the friction of beginning without the pressure of commitment. Use this on any project that feels impossible to start. #productivity #procrastination #focus #habits #timemanagement

## Short 504: Why Most Advice Online Is Wrong for You
**Hook (0-5s):** [Blunt opener] Most advice you read online does not apply to you. Here's why.
**Content (5-50s):** Advice comes from context. "Post three times a day" works for someone with a team. "Go all in" works for someone with savings. "Network at events" works for someone in a city with events. Most advice skips the context it requires. Before you take any advice, ask: what resources did this person have that I do not? What is their situation versus mine? Good advice is only good when the context matches.
**Outro (50-60s):** Subscribe for advice that actually tells you the context it came from.
**Title:** Why Most Online Advice Is Wrong For You
**Description:** Every piece of advice comes with hidden context. Before taking advice, ask what resources and circumstances the advisor had that you might not. Here's how to filter what actually applies to your situation. #advice #entrepreneur #mindset #business #realtalk
