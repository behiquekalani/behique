A lead magnet is the free thing you give away to build your email list. Most people build them wrong. Here's how to do it right.

The mistake: giving away something generic. "Free productivity guide" gets ignored. Nobody opts in because there are a thousand of those already.

Point one: your lead magnet should solve one very specific problem completely. Not a general overview. A complete solution to a narrow problem. "The exact morning routine checklist I used to go from zero to $3K/month while working part-time" is specific. It tells a story. It converts.

Point two: it should be immediately usable. A checklist, a template, a swipe file. Not a 40-page ebook they'll never read. The faster the value, the higher the perceived quality.

Point three: it should point toward your paid product. The free thing should solve part of the problem. Your paid product solves the whole thing. The lead magnet is the entry point, not the destination.

Put this lead magnet on every platform. Drive traffic to a simple signup page. Build the list.

That list is your most valuable business asset.
