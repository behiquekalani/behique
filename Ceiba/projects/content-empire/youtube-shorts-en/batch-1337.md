Ship fast. Fix in public. Repeat.

That's the only product development cycle that works for solo builders.

Point one: Waiting for perfect means waiting forever. Version one exists to get feedback, not to impress people. The people who buy first are not expecting perfection. They're buying into the direction.

Point two: Public feedback is the fastest improvement loop you have. Better than your own judgment. Better than a focus group. Real people using your real product in the real world will tell you exactly what to fix.

Point three: Speed builds credibility. When people see you shipping regularly, they trust that you're serious. A builder who ships beats a planner who thinks every time.

Your first version will not be your best.

That's the point.

Ship it so you can build the second.
