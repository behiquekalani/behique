Systems are what discipline actually looks like.

Not willpower. Systems.

Point one: Motivation is weather. It changes. A system runs whether or not you feel like it. The goal is not to get yourself hyped every day. The goal is to build a process that makes the work happen regardless of how you feel.

Point two: Good systems remove decisions. Decision fatigue is real. Every time you decide whether to do something, you spend energy. A system precommits you. Monday is always posting day. No decision required.

Point three: Start with one system. Not five. Pick the thing you struggle most to do consistently and build the smallest possible version of a system around it. A checklist. A time block. A trigger. Keep it simple enough to actually use.

You don't need to be more disciplined.

You need a better system.
