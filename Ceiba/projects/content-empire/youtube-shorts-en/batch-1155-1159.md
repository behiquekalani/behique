## Short 1155: The Two Types of Work

**Hook (0-3s):** There's work that pays you now. And work that pays you forever.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** Trading time for money pays you now. Writing a newsletter, recording a course, building a digital product. Those pay you forever. Most people spend all their time on the first type because it feels safer. The check comes on Friday. But the second type is where the leverage is. It takes longer to set up. The payoff is delayed. But once it's running, it doesn't require your presence. The goal is to gradually shift your ratio. More of your time on the work that compounds. Less on the work that stops when you stop.
**Thumbnail text:** Two Types of Work Exist
**Description:** Not all work is equal. Some work pays you once. Some work pays you for years. Most people spend their entire career in the first category. ## Short 1156: Why Small Audiences Convert Better

**Hook (0-3s):** 1,000 true fans beats 1 million passive followers every time.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** A small, loyal audience that trusts you will buy from you at a rate that a massive passive audience never will. Conversion rates on a tight community are 10 to 20 times higher than on a viral account with no relationship. The goal is not fame. The goal is trust. You build trust through consistency, specificity, and honesty. Not reach. A creator with 5,000 followers who all came for the same reason can make more money than an account with 500,000 who wandered in from a trending sound. Build for trust. The revenue follows.
**Thumbnail text:** Small Audience, Big Revenue
**Description:** The conventional wisdom is to chase big numbers. The reality is that small, loyal audiences convert at dramatically higher rates than large passive ones. In this Short I explain why the 1,000 true fans model is not outdated, it's more relevant than ever. Focus on trust, not reach. The money follows the relationship, not the follower count. #audiencebuilding #smallaudience #truefans #creatormonetization #digitalproducts #contentcreation #buildingonline #communitybuilding #creatoreconomy #niche

## Short 1157: What I'd Do With $0 and High Skills

**Hook (0-3s):** Zero dollars to start. Here's what I'd do.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:** Pick one skill people already pay for. Writing, design, video editing, coding, research. Build one free thing that shows what I can do. Post it publicly. Start documenting everything I learn in public. Reach out to five people per week who might want the service. Land the first client. Use that money to build the first digital product. Use the product revenue to run ads. That's the loop. No funding needed. No connections needed. Just skill, consistency, and the willingness to start before you're ready.

## Short 1158: The Founder Mindset vs the Employee Mindset

**Hook (0-3s):** Two people doing the same job see it completely differently.
**Duration:** 55 seconds
**Script:** The employee mindset asks: "What's expected of me?" The founder mindset asks: "What could this become?" One is waiting for instructions. One is looking for opportunities inside every constraint. You don't have to own a company to think like a founder. The student with a founder mindset doesn't just do the assignment. They ask why it matters. They improve it beyond the rubric. They build relationships with professors and peers. The mindset travels. It works in a job, in school, in a side project. Adopt it wherever you are.
**Thumbnail text:** Founder vs Employee: Same Job, Different Mind
**Description:** The difference between people who build things and people who work for things is rarely about resources. It's about mindset.
