## Short 958: The Anti-Niche Strategy for Fast Growth
**Hook (0-5s):** [Text: "You're niching wrong"] "Everyone told me to niche down. I did something different and grew faster."
**Content (5-50s):**
- Standard advice: pick one niche and stay in it.
- The problem: most beginners do not know which niche fits them yet.
- The anti-niche phase: post broadly for 30 days. Cover multiple topics you care about.
- Watch what resonates. Where do people engage? What gets saved and shared?
- The data tells you your real niche. Not theory. Not guessing. Actual signal.
- Then niche down into the intersection of what you enjoy and what performs.
- Your niche should not just be a market. It should be a match between your knowledge and your audience's need.
- Let the data find your niche instead of forcing it from day one.

## Short 959: How to Price Your First Digital Product
**Hook (0-5s):** [Price tags with question marks] "Pricing your first product is not guesswork. Here's the framework."
**Content (5-50s):**
- Most beginners underprice because they undervalue their knowledge. Stop.
- The right price depends on three things: the problem's urgency, the transformation delivered, and the buyer's purchasing power.
- Urgent problem plus big transformation plus professional audience means you can charge $97 to $197.
- Lower urgency or beginner audience means $17 to $47.
- Never price below $17. Below that, people question the quality.
- A quick exercise: what would it cost your buyer to solve this problem without your guide? Coach, course, consultant?
- Your guide should be priced at a fraction of that, which still feels like a massive deal.
- Start at $47. Test. Raise once you have 10 sales.
**End screen (50-60s):** "Subscribe for more digital product strategy. Drop your product idea below and I'll suggest a starting price."

You'll learn why underpricing hurts more than it helps, how to anchor your price against the cost of alternative solutions, and a simple starting price strategy with room to scale.
