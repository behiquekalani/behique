Put in the active work now to earn the passive income later. It's not magic. It's delayed gratification.
**Thumbnail text:** Passive income requires active work first
**Description:**
Passive income is one of the most misunderstood concepts in the creator space. It's real, but the "passive" label is misleading. In this Short, I explain what passive income actually is: front-loaded work that pays out over time. Every digital product, course, or piece of content that earns passively required serious active work to create and distribute. The passive phase comes after the active phase compounds. This reframe changes how you approach building. You're not trying to avoid work. You're trying to do concentrated work now so future income requires less of you.

## Short 1119: How to Turn One Idea Into a Week of Content
**Hook (0-3s):** One idea can fuel an entire week of content. Here's how.
**Duration:** 60 seconds
**Script:**
Stop searching for new ideas every day. Take one insight and break it into formats. The core idea becomes a Twitter thread. That thread becomes a short-form video script. The video becomes a LinkedIn post. The post becomes a newsletter section. The newsletter section becomes a quote card. That's five pieces of content from one original thought. The idea doesn't change. The format does. Each platform gets a version tuned to how its audience consumes content. This is how you stay consistent without burning out. You're not creating five new ideas. You're distributing one good idea to five different audiences.
**Thumbnail text:** 1 idea, 5 pieces of content
**Description:**
Content repurposing is the most underused strategy for staying consistent online. In this Short, I walk through exactly how to turn one core idea into a full week of content across multiple platforms. The idea becomes a thread, a video, a post, a newsletter section, and a quote card. Each piece is formatted for its platform. You're not creating more ideas. You're distributing one good idea to different audiences. This strategy kills two problems at once: idea fatigue and inconsistency. One strong insight per week is enough to run a full content calendar.
