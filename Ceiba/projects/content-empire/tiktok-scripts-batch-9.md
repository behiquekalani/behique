## Script 2: "I have ADHD and I run a business. Here is what actually helps."

**Caption:** ADHD and running a business. Here is what actually works for me.
**Hashtags:** #adhd #solopreneur #adhdentrepreneur #buildinpublic #neurodivergent #adhdtools #onepersonbusiness

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
I have ADHD and I run a business. Not in spite of it. With it. But it took me a long time to stop fighting my own brain and start building around it.

[B-roll: person at desk, focused, clean workspace with minimal distractions]

[0:08] **WHAT DOES NOT WORK**
Long to-do lists. They paralyze me. Big open-ended projects with no clear next step. I spiral. Willpower-based systems. When the motivation dips, the system collapses. I spent months trying to force myself into productivity frameworks built for neurotypical people. None of them stuck.

[B-roll: long Notion task list with too many items, getting closed]

[0:28] **WHAT ACTUALLY WORKS**
One thing at a time, written down somewhere I can see it. Not a list. One item. The next physical action, not the project name. "Write three hooks" not "finish content calendar."

[B-roll: single sticky note on desk with one task written on it]

[0:42]
Time blocking with real appointments. I schedule my focus blocks on a calendar like meetings. If it is not on the calendar, it is optional. My brain respects deadlines more than intentions.

[B-roll: calendar showing focus block labeled "Writing — 2 hours" as an event]

[0:55]
Capture systems. I use a Telegram bot to capture ideas immediately so my brain can let go of them. The fear of forgetting something is one of the biggest focus killers for me. Once it is logged, I can concentrate.

[B-roll: voice message being sent to Telegram bot, bot reply showing idea saved]

[1:10] **CTA**
Your brain is not broken. It just needs a different interface. Start with one change, not a whole system overhaul.

[B-roll: solopreneur at desk, calm, one focused task on screen]

---

[0:00] **HOOK**

I am not telling you to quit your job or drop out of school. I am telling you the decision was made by evidence, not rebellion. I found something that fit how I think. Most people never look.

## Script 4: "The real cost of building solo (it is not what people tell you)"

**Caption:** The real cost of building solo. Not what the motivational posts say.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #buildinpublic #solofounder #onepersonbusiness #entrepreneurreality #buildingonline #honestbusiness

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
Everyone talks about freedom when they pitch the solopreneur life. Nobody talks about the tax that comes with it.

[B-roll: solopreneur at laptop, late at night, alone]

[0:06] **WHAT PEOPLE EXPECT**
Set your own hours. Work from anywhere. No boss. No office. All of that is real. I am not going to pretend otherwise.

[B-roll: laptop on a café table, nice view, morning coffee]

[0:15] **WHAT PEOPLE DO NOT EXPECT**
Decision fatigue. When you work alone, every decision is yours. What to build. What to price it. What to post. Where to spend the next hour. There is no one to delegate the small calls to. That weight is constant.

[B-roll: person staring at too many open tabs, decision paralysis visible]

[0:30]
The isolation is real too. Not loneliness, exactly. More like missing the friction of other people. A team pushes back on bad ideas. Solo, your bad ideas can survive for weeks before reality catches up.

[B-roll: solopreneur alone at desk, thinking, no one else around]

[0:45]
And the pace. Building solo means every bottleneck is you. When you are sick, the business slows. When you are stuck creatively, nothing ships. There is no one to cover for you.

[B-roll: person trying to work while clearly not feeling well]

[0:58]
The cost is real. So is the upside. I am not leaving. But I went in with the wrong expectations and it made the first six months harder than they needed to be.

[B-roll: the same solopreneur, smiling now, building something]

[1:10] **CTA**
Build the systems before you need them. That is the only advice I would give my past self.

[B-roll: n8n canvas, Notion dashboard, automation running in background]

## Script 5: "6 months of building online. What changed. What did not."

**Caption:** 6 months building online. What actually changed. What did not.
**Hashtags:** #buildinpublic #solopreneur #6months #onlinebusiness #solofounder #entrepreneurreality #digitalproducts

---

[0:00] **HOOK**
Six months ago I had zero products live, zero followers, and zero sales. Here is a real update, not a highlight reel.

[B-roll: blank Gumroad page from month one, then current dashboard]

[0:07] **WHAT CHANGED: THE OBVIOUS**
I have products live now. I have content that gets views. I have a small but real audience. The numbers are not impressive by most metrics. They are real by mine, which means they are built on something.

[B-roll: Gumroad products listed, TikTok or Instagram stats showing growth]

[0:22] **WHAT CHANGED: THE LESS OBVIOUS**
How I think about work. Six months ago I treated every task like it needed to be perfect before I posted it. Now I know that done and out in the world teaches you ten times faster than polished and sitting in drafts.

[B-roll: rough first post, then iterating, improvement visible over time]

[0:40] **WHAT DID NOT CHANGE**
The uncertainty. I still do not know which product will click next. I still post content without knowing if it will land. I was expecting clarity to arrive after a certain amount of effort. It has not. You just get more comfortable operating without it.

[B-roll: analytics with some good spikes, some flat periods, no clear pattern]

[0:58]
The doubt still shows up. So does the motivation. What changed is that I stopped waiting for one to go away before listening to the other.

[B-roll: solopreneur at laptop, clearly mid-build, not waiting]

[1:08] **CTA**
If you are in month one or two and it feels slow, that is what it actually feels like. Keep building. The compound effect is invisible until it is not.

[B-roll: simple growth chart starting flat then curving up]
