## Post 5 — Why 1 Post a Day Beats 7 Posts on Sunday

**Hook:** Posting 7 times on Sunday and nothing the rest of the week is not consistency. It's accumulation.

**Slide 1:** Posting 7 times on Sunday and nothing the rest of the week is not consistency. It's accumulation.

**Slide 2:** The algorithm difference:
Daily posting = platform learns to distribute your content daily.
Weekly dumping = platform learns to distribute once a week, then nothing.

**Slide 3:** The audience difference:
Daily content builds a daily habit in your followers.
They start expecting your posts.
They look for them.
Weekly dumping builds no habit.
Your audience has forgotten you by Tuesday.

**Slide 4:** The cognitive difference:
7 posts on Sunday requires a 4-hour batch session.
1 post a day requires a 20-minute writing session.
The daily session is sustainable for years.
The Sunday dump is sustainable for weeks.

**Slide 5:** The compound difference:
Daily posting is 365 posts per year.
Weekly Sunday posting is 52 sessions, which might produce 52-100 posts.
365 posts compound 3-7x harder than 52 posts.

**Slide 6:** One post a day. Every day.
The Ghost Writer Kit makes this sustainable.
behike.gumroad.com | Link in bio.

**Caption:** The "batching everything on one day" strategy sounds efficient until you see the data.

Platforms don't reward volume in a single day. They reward consistent signal. When you post once a day, every day, you tell the platform's algorithm: "This account is active and reliable. Show its content regularly."

When you post seven times on Sunday and nothing on Monday through Saturday, you train the algorithm differently. It learns to show your content on Sunday and ignore you the rest of the week.

Beyond the algorithm, there's the audience psychology. A creator who shows up in your feed every day becomes part of your routine. A creator who disappears for six days and dumps content on the seventh is not someone you follow with any intention.

The 20-minute daily writing session beats the 4-hour Sunday marathon. It's more sustainable, it trains better habits, and it produces a compounding distribution advantage that accumulates over months and years.

One post a day. Build the habit.
