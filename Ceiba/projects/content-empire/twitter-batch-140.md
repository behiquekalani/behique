# Twitter/X Batch 140
## Topic: Milestone — 140 batches: what works on Twitter in 2026

## Tweet 1
140 tweet batches. Here's what I've learned about what actually works on this platform in 2026.

Thread.

## Tweet 2
1/
What works in 2026: specificity over volume.

2/
A tweet that says "solopreneurs should do X" performs worse than "solopreneurs who sell digital products under $50 should do X."

3/
The sharper the targeting in the content itself, the better it travels. Broad posts are invisible. Specific posts get forwarded.

## Tweet 3
Hooks that still work:
- The contrarian take that's actually correct
- The reframe of common advice
- The observation most people feel but nobody says

Hooks that are dying:
- "Hot take:"
- "Unpopular opinion:"
- "This might get me canceled but..."

## Tweet 4
1/
Threads in 2026: still the best format for long-form ideas.

2/
But the game has changed. Tweet 1 needs to function as a standalone post, not just a teaser.

3/
Write every thread opener as if the rest doesn't exist. If it earns a click, great. If not, it still delivered value and built reach.

## Tweet 5
The accounts that grew fastest this year shared one thing: they had a point of view on their niche that nobody else held.

Not a unique topic. A unique angle on a common topic. That's a real differentiator.

## Tweet 6
1/
What I learned about engagement vs. reach:

2/
Engagement tells you what resonates with your current audience.
Reach tells you what new people are finding.

3/
Optimize for reach early. Optimize for engagement once the audience exists. Most people do this backwards and wonder why they're stuck.

## Tweet 7
Posting frequency that works: enough to stay in people's feeds, not so much that each post gets less consideration before you ship it.

For most people that's 1 focused post per day, not 5 rushed ones.

## Tweet 8
1/
The format nobody uses enough: the observation.

2/
Not advice. Not a list. Just: "I noticed that X happens when Y, and I think it means Z."

3/
Observations feel real. They feel like someone thinking out loud rather than teaching.
That's why they spread. It triggers "yes, I've seen this too" responses.

## Tweet 9
1/
What doesn't work in 2026 that used to:

2/
Engagement bait questions ("What's your favorite tool for X?")
Reposting other people's threads with "this is gold"
Posting productivity takes that have no unique angle

3/
The platform has trained people to scroll past templates. Only original thinking earns attention now.

## Tweet 10
1/
140 batches in. One honest conclusion:

2/
Twitter rewards people who think clearly in public. Not people who perform thinking. Not people who copy formats. People who actually have something to say and learn to say it precisely.

3/
That skill is worth building. It carries everywhere: sales pages, pitches, email, product naming.

4/
The platform isn't the point. The clarity is.
