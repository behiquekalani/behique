### Short 14 — Polymarket for Beginners: the 60-Second Version

**Content (3-50s):**

Example: "Will X happen by December 31?" If you think yes, you buy yes shares. If yes happens, your shares pay out at $1 each. If no happens, they pay zero.

The current price of a share reflects the market's estimated probability. If yes shares are trading at $0.65, the market thinks there's a 65% chance of yes.

You're not betting against a casino. You're taking positions against other traders who have different information or different opinions than you.

The edge comes from having better information or better judgment than the market consensus. If you know something the market doesn't, or you think the market is wrong about the probability, that's where the profit is.

It's not gambling if you're making informed probability assessments. It's closer to a very specific form of research-based trading.

It requires USDC to trade. You need a crypto wallet to onboard. Coinbase to Polymarket is the standard path.
