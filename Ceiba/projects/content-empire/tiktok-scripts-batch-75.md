## Script 75.1 — 375 Scripts: What Actually Worked

**Hook (0-3s):**
"I have written over three hundred scripts for this brand. Here is the honest breakdown of what worked and what did not."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: 375 SCRIPTS. REAL DATA.]
The scripts that performed best had three things in common. They started with a contrarian thesis. They delivered the core point in the first ten seconds, not after a preamble. And they ended with a specific, low-friction CTA.

[on-screen: CONTRARIAN + FAST DELIVERY + SPECIFIC CTA]
The scripts that flopped were too broad. "How to grow on social media" performed worse than "The one thing I changed that doubled my reach in thirty days." Specific always beats general.

[on-screen: SPECIFIC > GENERAL, EVERY TIME]
The other pattern: the best scripts were the most personal. The ones where I said something I actually believed, not something I thought the algorithm would reward.

[on-screen: PERSONAL BEATS ALGORITHMIC]
Lesson one from three hundred and seventy-five scripts: write the thing you would say to one person in a room, not to an audience of thousands.

[on-screen: WRITE FOR ONE PERSON]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Follow for the next seventy-five batches. The system is getting sharper. The content is getting better."

**Caption:** 375 scripts in. What worked: contrarian thesis, speed, specificity, and saying what I actually believe. (103 chars)

## Script 75.2 — What Flopped and Why

**Hook (0-3s):**
"Here are the content formats that looked promising and did not work. I am telling you so you do not waste months on them."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: WHAT FLOPPED]
Trend-chasing. Every time I made a video because a format was popular rather than because I had something to say, the performance was average and I felt nothing making it.

[on-screen: TREND CHASING = AVERAGE PERFORMANCE]
Long explanations with no punchline. If the script took more than three seconds to get to the point, it dropped in retention every time. Preamble is a penalty.

[on-screen: PREAMBLE IS A RETENTION PENALTY]
Product-first content. Scripts that led with "here is what I am selling" instead of "here is the problem I can help you solve" consistently underperformed by a wide margin.

[on-screen: PROBLEM-FIRST > PRODUCT-FIRST]
Generic motivation. "You can do it" content without a specific framework or insight gets saves almost never and follows almost never. People save what they can use. They follow who they trust.

[on-screen: FRAMEWORKS GET SAVED. GENERIC GETS SCROLLED]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Tell me a content format you have been using that is not working. Let us figure out why."

**Caption:** Trend chasing, preamble, product-first scripts, generic motivation. These flopped. Here is what I learned. (107 chars)

## Script 75.3 — The Hook Formula That Consistently Wins

**Hook (0-3s):**
"After three hundred and seventy-five scripts, one hook formula outperforms everything else. Here it is."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: THE WINNING HOOK FORMULA]
The formula: [Belief most people hold] + [why that belief is costing them] + [the one thing they need instead].

[on-screen: BELIEF + COST + ALTERNATIVE]
Example: "Most creators think consistency means posting every day. That belief is burning them out. Here is what consistency actually means."

[on-screen: EXAMPLE IN ACTION]
This works because it does three things in one sentence. It calls out an audience that already holds the belief, so they feel seen. It creates tension by naming the cost. And it plants a curiosity gap that only gets closed by watching.

[on-screen: SEEN + TENSION + CURIOSITY GAP]
The other formula that came close: the number-based specific outcome. "How I [specific result] in [specific time] without [common sacrifice]." But the belief-challenge hook edges it out because it activates identity, not just interest.

[on-screen: BELIEF CHALLENGE ACTIVATES IDENTITY]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Write one hook using that formula today. Belief, cost, alternative. Drop it in the comments."

**Caption:** One hook formula won across 375 scripts. Belief most people hold. The cost. The alternative. (93 chars)

## Script 75.4 — The Pattern That Surprised Me Most

**Hook (0-3s):**
"The thing that surprised me most about building this content brand was not the algorithm. It was this."

**Body (3-50s):**
[on-screen: THE UNEXPECTED PATTERN]
The posts I almost did not publish, the ones that felt too honest or too specific or too niche, consistently outperformed the ones I was confident about.

[on-screen: POSTS YOU DOUBT TEND TO WIN]
The reason: confidence in a post usually means you are playing it safe. Doubt means you said something real, something that might not land but might also hit hard.

[on-screen: CONFIDENCE = SAFE. DOUBT = REAL]
The audience you actually want does not want polished and safe. They want true and useful. Those are not always the same thing.

[on-screen: TRUE AND USEFUL > POLISHED AND SAFE]
The other surprise: the compounding effect is real and it is not linear. Nothing happened for a long time, and then things happened faster than I could process. The system does not give you feedback on your timeline.

[on-screen: COMPOUNDING IS REAL. THE TIMELINE IS NOT YOURS]

**CTA (50-60s):**
"Publish the post you have been sitting on. The one that feels too honest. That one."

**Caption:** The posts I almost did not publish outperformed the ones I was confident about. Trust the doubt. (96 chars)
