Topic: Building a digital product from your most popular content

## TikTok 1: Your Best Post Is Already a Product
**Hook (0-2s):**
"That post that went viral two months ago? It's a product you haven't built yet."

**Script:**
Every piece of content that performed well is a signal. Your audience is telling you: more of this, please.

Most creators see that signal and make more content. Smart creators see it and build a product.

If you wrote a thread that got saved five hundred times, you have a guide. If you made a video that got a thousand comments asking follow-up questions, you have a course. If people keep sharing one specific post about your process, you have a template.

Your most popular content is already doing the market research for you. You just need to stop making more of it for free and start packaging it.

## TikTok 2: The Expansion Method
**Hook (0-2s):**
"One post. 10 pages. Real product."

**Script:**
Here is the simplest framework for turning content into a product.

Take your most popular post. Write down the three things you assumed when you wrote it. Those assumptions are now three chapters.

Write down the three objections someone might have after reading it. Those objections become three more sections.

Write down what someone needs to do after reading it to actually get the result you described. That is your implementation section.

You now have a seven-part guide. That is a real product. You can sell it for fifteen to fifty dollars and deliver instant value.

Most people overthink this. The product is already there. You just need to expand it.

## TikTok 3: From Comments to Product
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your comments section is a product brief you're ignoring."

**Script:**
When someone leaves a comment asking how you did something, that is a buying signal.

When the same type of question appears across multiple posts, that is a product brief.

Go into your last 30 posts right now. Look only at the comments that ask follow-up questions or ask for more detail. Group those questions by theme.

The theme with the most questions is your next product.

You are not building a product from scratch. You are building a product from what your audience already told you they want. That is the difference between guessing and knowing.

## TikTok 4: The Minimum Viable Product Rule
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your product doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist."

**Script:**
Creators stall out on product creation because they want to build something comprehensive before they sell anything.

The truth is your first product does not need to be comprehensive. It needs to solve one specific problem better than anything free on the internet.

One hundred percent of a perfect product that does not exist makes zero dollars.

Fifty percent of an imperfect product that solves a real problem makes real money.

Start with the smallest version that delivers a clear result. Sell it. Use the revenue and the customer feedback to build version two.

The goal is not a perfect product. The goal is a first product that opens a relationship with a paying customer.

## TikTok 5: Package Before You Create
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Write the sales page before you build the product. Every time."

**Script:**
Here is a habit that will save you months of wasted effort.

Before you build anything, write the sales page. Write the title. Write what it does. Write who it is for. Write what someone can do after they buy it that they could not do before.

If you can not write that page clearly, you do not have a product idea. You have a topic.

Topics do not sell. Products that solve specific problems for specific people sell.

The sales page exercise forces you to think like a buyer before you think like a creator. It is the fastest way to know whether your idea is real before you invest hours building it.
