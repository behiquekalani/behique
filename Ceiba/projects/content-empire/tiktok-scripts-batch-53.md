## Script 1: The Money System Every Solopreneur Needs
**Hook (0-3s):** If every dollar you make goes into one account, you will always feel broke.

**Script:**
If every dollar you make goes into one account, you will always feel broke.

Here is the system that fixes that.

Split your income into four buckets the moment it hits.

Operations: the money you need to run the business. Tools, hosting, ads, whatever keeps things moving. Cap this at 30 percent.

Taxes: set aside 25 percent the moment money comes in. Not later. Now. You do not want to owe what you do not have.

Pay yourself: 30 percent goes to you as personal income. Non-negotiable. If you never pay yourself, you will burn out or quit.

Growth: 15 percent goes back into the business to scale. Courses, tools, better equipment, outsourcing.

The percentages shift over time. But having the system means money never disappears into vague spending.

Most solopreneurs fail financially not because they do not make money, but because they never built a container for it.

## Script 2: Stop Waiting for Consistent Income. Build a Floor Instead.
**Hook (0-3s):** Solopreneur income is not consistent. But your expenses are.

**Script:**
Solopreneur income is not consistent. But your expenses are.

The goal is not to make the same amount every month. The goal is to build a floor. A minimum amount your business generates no matter what.

Here is how you build a floor.

First, know your number. What is the bare minimum you need to cover your personal bills and business costs. That is your floor. Everything above it is leverage.

Second, build recurring revenue. Even $200/month in subscriptions or retainer work changes how the whole business feels. Unpredictable income with a $200 base feels safer than pure feast-or-famine.

Third, stack low-maintenance income. Digital products, affiliate links, content that generates traffic over time. These do not require you to show up every day to generate revenue.

The anxiety of variable income comes from not knowing what the bottom looks like. Once you know your floor and you are above it, the variability becomes upside, not risk.

## Script 3: The Invoice Trap That Keeps Solopreneurs Broke
**Hook (0-3s):** Getting paid is not the same as making money. Here is the difference.

**Script:**
Getting paid is not the same as making money. Here is the difference.

When you invoice a client, that is revenue on paper. But if the invoice is not paid for 30 or 60 days, your bank account does not know that.

This is the invoice trap. You have revenue. You do not have cash. And cash is what pays your rent.

Here is how to fix it.

Require deposits. For any project over $200, ask for 50 percent upfront before you start work. Most clients who are serious will pay it. The ones who refuse are a signal.

Net 15 not Net 30. Change your payment terms. Net 30 means you wait a month. Net 15 cuts that in half. Some clients will push back. The ones who matter usually accept it.

Use Stripe or PayPal links instead of manual invoices. When payment is one click, it gets done faster.

Cash flow kills more small businesses than lack of revenue. Fix the collection system before the business grows and the problem gets bigger.

## Script 4: How I Think About Pricing as a Solopreneur
**Hook (0-3s):** Pricing is not a math problem. It is a positioning decision.

**Script:**
Pricing is not a math problem. It is a positioning decision.

Most solopreneurs price based on what they think clients can afford, or what competitors charge, or what feels safe.

None of those are the right inputs.

Price based on the value delivered. If you help a business generate $10,000 in new revenue, charging $500 for that service is not confident pricing. It is leaving money on the table.

Price based on what you want your client to look like. Low prices attract price-sensitive clients who negotiate, demand more, and pay late. Higher prices attract clients who trust the process and respect your time.

Price based on your capacity. If you can only take 5 clients, each one needs to pay enough that 5 is sustainable. You cannot discount your way to a full roster.

The mindset shift: you are not asking for money. You are offering a trade. Your expertise for their budget. Make the trade fair for both sides.

Then stop apologizing for what you charge.

**CTA:** What would you charge if you knew the client would say yes.

## Script 5: Build the Business That Pays You Even When You Take a Week Off
**Hook (0-3s):** If you stop working and the money stops too, you have a job, not a business.

**Script:**
If you stop working and the money stops too, you have a job, not a business.

That is not a judgment. It is just the starting condition. And you can change it.

Here is what a real business looks like at the solopreneur level.

You have at least one income stream that does not require you to show up in real time. Digital products, content, affiliate income, a productized service that runs on templates.

You have systems and processes, not just skills. Anyone could follow your process even if they are not you. That means you could hire, or outsource, or just take time off without everything breaking.

You have a customer base, not just clients. People who buy from you more than once and who come back without being chased.

Most solopreneurs stay in the trading-time-for-money trap because they never build the asset layer. The product, the content, the system that works while they rest.

Start building the asset layer now, even if it only takes 30 minutes a week.
