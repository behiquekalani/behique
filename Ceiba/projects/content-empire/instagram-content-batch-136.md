## Post 4 — How Customers Think vs. How Followers Think

**Hook:** A follower asks: "Is this interesting?" A customer asks: "Does this solve my problem?"

**Slide 1:** A follower asks: "Is this interesting?"
A customer asks: "Does this solve my problem?"

**Slide 2:** Followers engage with content.
Customers engage with outcomes.

If your content is interesting but does not solve a named problem, you will get followers, not buyers.

**Slide 3:** The shift in content:
Follower content: "Here is a cool thing I learned."
Customer content: "Here is why you are stuck and what to do about it."

**Slide 4:** Customers do not buy because they like you.
They buy because they believe you understand their situation better than they do.

That belief is built through specific, accurate content.

**Slide 5:** Write for the person who has a problem.
Not for the person who wants entertainment.
One of them has a credit card out.

**Slide 6:** Solve problems. Build buyers.
Start with the Behike Method. behike.gumroad.com
@behikeai

**Caption:** There is a simple test for any piece of content you create: does this move someone toward a solution, or does it just make them feel something?

Both have value. But only one of them converts.

Followers are drawn to personality, humor, aesthetics, relatability. All valid. But customers are drawn to understanding. When they read your content and think "this person gets exactly what I am dealing with," that is when they start considering whether you have something they should pay for.

This does not mean your content has to be boring and transactional. The best content does both. It is interesting and it is useful. Dan Koe, Alex Hormozi, Sahil Bloom -- the ones who earn consistently write content that names the exact situation their audience is in, then offers a shift.

## Post 5 — The Conversion Content Formula

**Hook:** Every piece of content either builds toward a sale or wastes both your time and theirs.

**Slide 1:** Every piece of content either builds toward a sale or wastes both your time and theirs.

**Slide 2:** The formula for content that converts followers to customers:

Problem named + Mechanism explained + Result shown = Buyer intent created

**Slide 3:** Problem named:
You have to say exactly what is wrong.
Not vaguely. Specifically.
"You post daily and still have no sales" is better than "content creation is hard."

**Slide 4:** Mechanism explained:
Tell them why the problem exists.
Not because they are lazy. Because they are missing a specific piece.
Name the piece.

**Slide 5:** Result shown:
Show what life looks like after the mechanism is applied.
Not hype. A specific, believable outcome.
"You go from posting randomly to having a 30-day content system" is a result.

**Slide 6:** Apply this formula to your next post.
Then apply it to a product. That is how sales happen.
@behikeai | behike.gumroad.com

**Caption:** Content without a conversion architecture is just entertainment. And entertainment does not build a business.

The most effective solopreneur content follows a simple but underused structure: name the problem precisely, explain the mechanism behind it (why it keeps happening), and then show the result that comes from fixing it. This sequence moves people from passive scrollers to people who think "I need to know more."

The reason most creators miss this is they jump straight to the result without doing the work of the problem and mechanism first. People do not buy results they do not believe. They believe results after they see that you understand their situation.

Write the problem first. Every time. Even when you already know the answer.

This is the framework behind every piece of content we publish at Behike.
