**Thread 1**
**Topic:** What "the creator economy is dead" actually means

Tweet 1: The creator economy is not dead. The era of low-effort content monetizing on vibes alone is dead. Those are different things. One is a crisis. The other is a correction.

Tweet 2: For the past 4 years, a lot of people made money from content by being early, not by being good. Platforms were expanding, algorithms were generous, audiences were easy to grow. That phase is over.

Tweet 3: What's happening now is a filter. The people who built real audience trust are keeping their income. The people who built follower counts on trends are watching those follower counts go silent.

Tweet 4: The filter is actually good news if you're starting now. It means the competition at the top isn't people with 5-year head starts. It's people willing to go deeper than everyone else.

Tweet 5: You don't need more followers. You need a smaller group of people who actually trust you enough to buy what you recommend. That is still very achievable. It just requires substance.

Tweet 6: Follow @behikeai. I write about building real audience and income as a one-person operation, not the surface-level version.

**Thread 2**
**Topic:** Why most creators are in the wrong business

Tweet 1: Most creators are not in the content business. They're in the attention business. Those require completely different strategies. The attention business is getting harder. The content business is getting better.

Tweet 2: Attention is rented. You borrow it from platforms that can change their algorithm tomorrow and instantly change your income. You have no control over the asset. You're a tenant, not an owner.

Tweet 3: Content that creates trust converts. Attention without trust does not. A creator with 50,000 followers who has earned real trust will outsell a creator with 500,000 followers who chased viral moments.

Tweet 4: The shift is from reach to resonance. From impressions to impact. From posting to publishing. Most creators resist this shift because it requires actually having something to say.

Tweet 5: Start with your perspective. Not your niche, your perspective. What do you believe that most people in your space would push back on? That's where real content starts.

Tweet 6: Follow @behikeai. I write about building a business where your thinking is the product, not your posting volume.

**Thread 3**
**Topic:** The filtering is creating a massive gap you can walk into

Tweet 1: Here's what nobody is saying about the creator economy right now: the people quitting are mostly the people who should quit. That's leaving real space for people with actual substance.

Tweet 2: When noise leaves, signal gets amplified. When low-effort accounts stop posting, the algorithm starts rewarding people who stayed. This is not the worst time to start. It might be the best.

Tweet 3: The people who win in a filtered market are not the people with the best production value. They're the people who understand their audience's specific problem better than their audience does.

Tweet 4: That's a research problem, not a talent problem. You can get there by reading deeply, testing content, paying attention to what questions people keep asking. None of that requires equipment.

Tweet 5: The practical move: pick one specific person you're trying to help and make every piece of content so accurate to their situation that they feel like you're reading their mind.

Tweet 6: Follow @behikeai for more on building an audience that actually converts. No trend-chasing, no algorithm hacks.

**Thread 4**
**Topic:** The real skill that separates creators who survive

Tweet 1: The creators who survive every platform shift, every algorithm change, every market correction have one thing in common. They built something the platform cannot take away: a point of view.

Tweet 2: Point of view is not opinion. It's a consistent framework for how you see a specific problem. When you have it, every piece of content becomes a piece of the same larger argument. Audiences follow arguments.

Tweet 3: Without point of view, you're just a curator. And curation gets commoditized immediately when everyone has access to the same AI tools and the same trending topic feeds.

Tweet 4: Building your point of view takes about 6 months of consistently writing out what you think about your topic, what you disagree with, and what you believe is underrated. It's not a fast process.

Tweet 5: But once you have it, it compounds. Every new piece of content reinforces the framework. New readers get indoctrinated faster. The audience self-selects. Conversion rates go up.

Tweet 6: This is what I'm building with @behikeai. If you want to follow the process in real time, follow the account.

**Thread 5**
**Topic:** What the next wave of the creator economy actually looks like

Tweet 1: The next phase of the creator economy will not be dominated by full-time content creators. It will be dominated by experts who happen to create content. That's a completely different category.

Tweet 2: The full-time creator model requires you to manufacture content at volume. The expert model requires you to have real things to say and say them clearly. Volume is optional. Substance is not.

Tweet 3: We're moving from content quantity to content depth. From podcast interviews with everyone to specific, researched long-form. From daily posts to weekly essays that actually change how someone thinks.

Tweet 4: The business model follows the same shift. Less brand deals, more direct monetization. Less ad revenue, more digital products and services. Less platform dependence, more email list ownership.

Tweet 5: If you're starting now, start with depth. Pick the thing you understand better than most people. Write about it honestly. Build the email list from day one. Sell your own thing before you get big.

Tweet 6: Follow @behikeai. This is exactly the model I'm building and documenting publicly.
