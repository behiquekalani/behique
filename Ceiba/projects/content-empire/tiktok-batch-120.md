## TikTok 2: The One Metric That Actually Matters

**Hook (0-2s):**
"Forget followers. This is the number that tells you if your content is working."

**Script:**
Profile visits.

Not likes. Not shares. Not comments.

When someone watches your video and then goes to your profile, that's a buying signal.

They're evaluating you. They want to know if you're consistent, if you make sense, if you're someone they want to follow and eventually buy from.

Your job in every video is not to entertain. It's to make that person think "I want to see more of this."

That thought drives profile visits. Profile visits drive follows. Follows drive sales.

A video with 100,000 views and zero profile visits did nothing for your business.

A video with 5,000 views and 800 profile visits built your audience.

Start tracking profile visits after every post. Optimize for that number. Everything else follows.

## TikTok 3: Why Consistency Beats Virality Every Time

**Hook (0-2s):**
"One viral video will not build your business. But 300 consistent ones might."

**Script:**
Here's what nobody tells you about viral content.

It attracts the wrong people.

When a video goes viral, it reaches an audience that is not your core audience. They followed you for the moment, not for the topic.

They inflate your follower count. They dilute your engagement rate. They make your metrics look better and your business worse.

The people who build real businesses from TikTok are not the ones who went viral once.

They're the ones who posted three times a week on one topic for eighteen months.

Their audience is smaller. But every person in it is there because they chose the topic, not because an algorithm fed them a random video.

Small, aligned, intentional. That's the audience that buys.

**CTA:**
Quality over virality. Follow if you're building for the long game.

## TikTok 4: The Content Mistake That Wastes the Most Time

**Hook (0-2s):**
"The biggest content mistake is not low quality or low frequency. It's switching topics."

**Script:**
Switching your niche is the fastest way to destroy the trust you built.

Every time you post off-topic, you're sending a signal to your audience that they can't rely on you to solve their specific problem.

They followed you for one thing. The moment you give them something else, they start skipping your videos. The algorithm notices. Your reach drops.

This doesn't mean you can never evolve.

It means every piece of content, even if the format is different, should connect back to the core problem you solve.

You can tell a personal story. You can post a hot take. You can share a reaction.

But the throughline should always be: "This is relevant to the person I'm trying to help."

Stay on topic. The algorithm rewards it. Your audience rewards it. Your business rewards it.
