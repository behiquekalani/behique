**Title:** How I automated my content pipeline without coding
**Hook (0-3s):** I built a content pipeline that runs itself. No code, no developers.
**Body (3-45s):**
The pipeline works like this.

I record a voice note on my phone. It uploads automatically to Google Drive via a folder-watching trigger. n8n detects the new file, sends it to Whisper for transcription. The text comes back into n8n.

Then Claude receives the transcript with a prompt that says: rewrite this as a Twitter thread, an Instagram caption, and a short email. Three formats, one source.

n8n routes each format to the right place: Twitter draft queue, Notion content calendar, email draft folder.

Total setup time: about three hours the first time. Time saved every week: four to six hours.

The tools used: n8n for orchestration, Whisper for transcription, Claude API for rewriting, Google Drive as the trigger point. No code required. All visual logic flows.

**Script 4**
**Title:** The automation mistake that wastes solopreneurs' time
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people automate the wrong things first. This is what that looks like.
**Body (3-45s):**
I see solopreneurs spend ten hours automating their social media scheduling and zero hours automating their lead capture.

The order is backward. Automate what makes you money first.

Ask yourself: what is the single most repetitive task between a lead and a sale? Automate that. What happens between a sale and a happy customer? Automate that.

Social media scheduling is nice. But if your delivery is manual, your lead follow-up is manual, and your customer onboarding is manual, automating your tweets is rearranging furniture while the plumbing is broken.

The order of automation priority: delivery first, lead nurture second, customer follow-up third, content distribution fourth, reporting and analytics last.

Work backward from the transaction. Everything that touches money gets automated before anything that touches visibility.

**Script 5**
**Title:** n8n vs Zapier for solopreneurs. The honest comparison.
**Hook (0-3s):** n8n or Zapier. Here's the honest answer for a solo operator.
**Body (3-45s):**
Both tools connect apps and automate workflows. The real difference is cost model and control.

Zapier charges per task. As your automations run more, your bill grows. At scale, it can cost hundreds per month for a solo operation. It's polished and beginner-friendly.

n8n is self-hosted. You run it on a $5 VPS, you own the instance, you pay a flat rate with no per-task billing. The interface is slightly steeper to learn but not complicated after a few hours.

For someone just starting: Zapier's free tier gets you going without setup friction. Learn the logic there.

For someone running more than a few hundred automations a month: n8n self-hosted pays for itself within the first month compared to Zapier's paid tiers.

The recommendation: start on Zapier free. When the bill hits $30 a month, migrate to n8n. The logic you learned transfers directly.
