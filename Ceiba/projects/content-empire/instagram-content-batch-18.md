**Title:** Most people build automations backwards. Here is the right order.
**Recommended posting time:** Tuesday, 9:00 AM EST
**Hashtags:** #n8n #workflowautomation #nocode #automationtools #solopreneur #buildinpublic #aitools #productivityhacks #worksmarter #techfounder #notionworkflow #zapieralternative #selfhosted #digitalbuilder #solopreneurlife

**Slide 1 (Cover):**
Headline: Most people build automations backwards.
Body: Here is the correct order. (Swipe)

**Slide 2:**
Headline: Step 1 — Map the manual process first.
Body: Before you touch n8n, write out every step you do by hand. If you skip this, you automate broken behavior.

**Slide 3:**
Headline: Step 2 — Identify the trigger.
Body: Every automation starts with a signal. A form submission. A new row. A webhook. Define it before you build anything else.

**Slide 4:**
Headline: Step 3 — Define the output.
Body: What should exist that didn't before? A Notion entry. A Telegram message. An email. Get specific.

**Slide 5:**
Headline: Step 4 — Connect the middle.
Body: Now build the n8n flow. The middle is just the path from trigger to output. Keep it short.

**Slide 6:**
Headline: Step 5 — Test with bad data.
Body: Most workflows fail on edge cases, not the happy path. Feed it weird inputs before you call it done.

**Slide 7:**
Headline: Step 6 — Document it in one sentence.
Body: "When X happens, do Y." If you can't say it that simply, the workflow is too complex.

**Slide 8:**
Headline: Step 7 — Schedule a monthly review.
Body: Automations break silently. APIs change. Services go down. Set a reminder to check it every 30 days.

**Slide 9 (CTA):**
Headline: Save this before you build your next workflow.
Body: Which step do you usually skip? Comment below.

**Title:** The n8n mistake that wastes hours.
**Recommended posting time:** Friday, 12:00 PM EST
**Hashtags:** #n8n #workflowautomation #nocode #automationmistakes #solopreneur #buildinpublic #techfounder #selfhosted #zapier #productivitytools #aiautomation #workflowbuilder #digitalbuilder #worksmarter #techtools

**Caption:**
The biggest mistake I see in n8n workflows: skipping the error handler.

You build the happy path. Everything works in testing. You deploy it. Three days later, an API times out, the workflow silently fails, and you never know.

The fix is a single node. Add an "Error Trigger" workflow that catches failed executions and sends you a Telegram or email alert. It takes five minutes to set up.

Most workflow failures are not failures of logic. They are failures of visibility. If you don't know it broke, you can't fix it.

Save this and add error handling to your next build before you deploy.
