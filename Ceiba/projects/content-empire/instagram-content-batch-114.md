## Post 2 — The 4-Part Second Brain Setup for Business Builders

**Hook:** Most people build a second brain for capturing notes. Build one for building a business.

**Slide 1:** Most people build a second brain for capturing notes. Build one for building a business.

**Slide 2:** Part 1: Inbox. Everything goes here first. Voice memos, screenshots, links, raw thoughts. No organization required at capture.

**Slide 3:** Part 2: Projects. Active work with a deadline or outcome. Your current product, your launch plan, your content calendar.

**Slide 4:** Part 3: Resources. Reference material organized by topic. What you know about marketing, your niche, your audience research.

**Slide 5:** Part 4: Archive. Completed projects and ideas that are not active. Nothing gets deleted. It just moves here.

**Slide 6:** This is the PARA method. It takes one hour to set up in Notion. Follow @behikeai for the template.

**Caption:** The PARA system, developed by Tiago Forte, is the most practical organizational framework for a business-focused second brain.

The reason it works is that it organizes by actionability, not by topic. Your active projects are always visible. Your reference material is retrievable. Your completed work is archived, not deleted.

Most people organize by category, which creates the problem of not knowing where to look when you need something. PARA organizes by what you are doing with the information, which is a much more useful lens.

The tool does not matter much. Notion is popular. Obsidian is powerful. Logseq is free. Pick one and stay with it long enough for the compound effect to kick in.

The system is only as good as your capture habit. Build that first.

## Post 5 — The 15-Minute Daily Second Brain Habit

**Hook:** A second brain you use for 15 minutes a day is worth more than one you build perfectly and abandon.

**Slide 1:** A second brain you use for 15 minutes a day is worth more than one you build perfectly and abandon.

**Slide 2:** Morning, 5 minutes: review your active projects. What is the one thing that moves each forward today?

**Slide 3:** Throughout the day, 5 minutes: capture everything. Ideas, insights, links, quotes. Do not organize yet.

**Slide 4:** Evening, 5 minutes: process the inbox. Move items to the right project or resource. Delete the noise.

**Slide 5:** That is it. 15 minutes. No elaborate ritual. No color-coded system. Just the habit of capturing and reviewing.

**Slide 6:** The system compounds if you show up daily. Follow @behikeai to see how it connects to everything else.

**Caption:** The biggest failure mode for second brain systems is over-engineering them at setup and then abandoning them when life gets busy.

The system that works is not the most beautiful one. It is the one you actually use every day.

15 minutes is enough. Five minutes in the morning to orient yourself. Five minutes during the day to capture what comes up. Five minutes in the evening to process and clear the inbox.

Over time, that habit builds something irreplaceable. A knowledge base that reflects your actual thinking, your real research, your genuine experience. Something no one else can replicate, and that AI tools can query and build on.

Start with the habit, not the perfect system. The system evolves from the habit.
