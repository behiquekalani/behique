# Instagram Batch 39: What I'd Do Differently if Starting Over
# Account: @behikeai
# Topic: Year 1 mistakes and what they teach

**Post 1**
**Hook:** If I started over, the first thing I'd build is an email list. Not a product.
**Caption:** I spent months building before I had anyone to sell to. The product was ready before the audience was. That's the most common mistake in this space and it's fully avoidable. An email list of 500 people who trust you is worth more than a product launched to silence. Before you write a single page of a guide, start publishing content and collect emails. The list is the asset. Everything you sell goes to that list first.

**Post 2**
**Hook:** I wasted the first two months chasing the perfect product idea.
**Caption:** Perfect killed more builds than bad ideas ever did. I spent weeks researching, mapping, rethinking. Meanwhile nothing was shipping and no one was seeing anything. The first product doesn't have to be the best product. It has to exist. You learn more from one product that's live and collecting real feedback than from six months of planning one that never ships. Done beats perfect in the early phase, every time, no exceptions.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #digitalproducts #buildingonline #onlinebusiness #startuplessons #contentcreator #behikeai #creatoreconomy

**Post 3**
**Hook:** I tried to be everywhere on social media at once. That was the wrong call.
**Caption:** Twitter, Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, all at the same time, all in year one. The result was mediocre presence on every platform and strong presence on none. The right approach is one platform, mastered, with consistent output, until it's working. Then you expand. Platform-specific content performs better than content repurposed across everything. Pick the platform where your buyers actually are and go deep before you go wide.
**Hashtags:** #contentcreator #solopreneur #socialmediastrategy #onlinebusiness #buildingonline #behikeai #contentmarketing #personalbranding

**Post 4**
**Hook:** I underpriced everything because I didn't believe the value was real yet.
**Caption:** The product was good. The price said otherwise. Low prices don't signal value. They signal uncertainty. And buyers feel that uncertainty. When you price something at $4 because you're not sure anyone will pay $19, you're communicating doubt before the buyer even downloads anything. Price based on the outcome the product delivers, not based on how confident you feel. Confidence in pricing comes after the price, not before. Set the real price and let the market respond.
**Hashtags:** #digitalproducts #solopreneur #pricingstrategy #onlinebusiness #contentcreator #buildingonline #behikeai #creatoreconomy

**Post 5**
**Hook:** I built products I thought were interesting instead of products people were already searching for.
**Caption:** The difference between a product that sells on day one and a product that needs months of education is whether demand already exists. People searching for "how to automate my eBay store" are ready to buy. People who've never considered eBay automation need to be convinced the problem is real before they'll pay for a solution. Start where demand already exists. You can build toward interest later. In year one, match the product to the search, not the other way around.
**Hashtags:** #digitalproducts #solopreneur #productresearch #onlinebusiness #buildingonline #contentmarketing #behikeai #creatoreconomy

**Post 6**
**Hook:** I ignored comments and DMs for the first four months. That was a significant error.
**Caption:** The people who comment on your posts are your most engaged potential buyers. They took an action. They're warm. Every ignored comment is a missed conversation and a missed signal about what your audience actually cares about. I responded to maybe 30% of early comments and I can look at the engagement data now and see exactly where I lost momentum. Respond to everything in the first year. The algorithm rewards it and the relationship compound builds from those interactions.
**Hashtags:** #contentcreator #solopreneur #communitybuilding #onlinebusiness #buildingonline #behikeai #personalbranding #creatoreconomy

**Post 7**
**Hook:** I confused being busy with making progress.
**Caption:** In year one I was producing constantly. Content, products, research, systems. All of it felt like forward motion. But a lot of it was motion without direction. Busy is not the same as productive and in a solo business they can look identical from the inside. The diagnostic I use now: at the end of a week, can I name one thing that moved revenue or audience forward. If I can't, the week was full but not productive. That test is simple enough to run every Friday.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #buildingonline #productivity #onlinebusiness #contentcreator #behikeai #creatoreconomy #personalbranding

**Post 8**
**Hook:** I didn't document anything in year one. That was expensive.
**Caption:** Every mistake I made, every thing I figured out, every workflow that worked. None of it was captured. By month six I was relearning things I'd already learned. By month nine I was making the same mistakes from month three. Documentation isn't a productivity habit. It's a compound interest account. Every insight you capture is available to your future self and, if you share it, available to an audience. The most valuable content I post now came from things I started writing down last year.
**Hashtags:** #solopreneur #buildingonline #personalbranding #contentcreator #onlinebusiness #behikeai #contentmarketing #creatoreconomy
---

**Post 9**
**Hook:** I treated every launch like a one-time event instead of a repeating system.
**Caption:** A product launch shouldn't require a completely new strategy every time. The framework: build the product, create three pieces of content that explain the problem it solves, send one email to the list, and let the content run. That's the system. After a few launches you refine it. But in year one I reinvented the wheel every single time, which meant every launch was exhausting and inconsistent. Build the launch system once. Run it repeatedly. Improve it on the margins.
**Hashtags:** #digitalproducts #solopreneur #productlaunch #onlinebusiness #contentmarketing #buildingonline #behikeai #creatoreconomy
---

**Post 10**
**Hook:** The biggest mistake of year one was trying to figure it all out before starting.
**Caption:** I needed to know the full strategy before I took the first step. That's an ADHD brain thing and also just a perfectionism thing. The problem is the strategy only becomes clear through doing. You don't know which platform works for you until you post. You don't know which product sells until it's live. You don't know your voice until you've written a hundred posts. Information before action feels responsible. In practice it's just a different name for avoidance. The clarity is on the other side of the work.
