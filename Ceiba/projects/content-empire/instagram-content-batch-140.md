## Post 2 — The Night Before: How to Prepare for Batch Day

**Hook:** Your batch day fails or succeeds based on what you do the night before.

**Slide 1:** Your batch day fails or succeeds based on what you do the night before.

**Slide 2:** The night before batch day, do these four things:

1. Pull your content pipeline (Notion or any system) and pick 30 topics
2. Choose the format for each (carousel, reel, thread, email)
3. Group topics by theme so related content gets shot together
4. Clear your schedule for the next day completely

**Slide 3:** Topic selection rule:
Every topic should map to one of your core pillars.
If you have three pillars (productivity, monetization, tools), pull 10 topics per pillar.
Balanced content calendar without thinking about it.

**Slide 4:** Group by format, not by topic.
All carousels together. All reel scripts together. All captions together.
Context switching between formats kills momentum. Batching within format keeps it.

**Slide 5:** Set up your workspace the night before too.
Camera angles. Lighting. Script template open.
No setup friction in the morning. Just execution.

**Slide 6:** Prepare tonight. Produce tomorrow.

**Caption:** Batch day is not a spontaneous creative session. It is an execution day. And execution days only work when the thinking has already been done.

The night before is where the content strategy happens. You decide what to create, in what order, for which platform, in which format. By the time you sit down the next morning, every creative decision is already made. You are not generating. You are producing.

This is the shift that separates people who batch successfully from people who sit down for a "batch day" and spend four hours figuring out what to make.

Treat the night before as seriously as the batch day itself. The 30-minute prep session the night before is what makes the 8-hour production session the next day actually work.

## Post 3 — The Batch Day Schedule Hour by Hour

**Hook:** Here is the exact 8-hour schedule that produces a month of content in one day.

**Slide 1:** Here is the exact 8-hour schedule that produces a month of content in one day.

**Slide 2:** Hours 1-2: Script and outline block
Write all scripts and carousel outlines.
Do not film yet. Do not edit yet.
Just words. 30 pieces of written content.

**Slide 3:** Hours 3-5: Recording block
Record all reels and talking-head content in one sitting.
Same backdrop, same lighting, different scripts.
Record multiple takes per script. Do not edit between takes.

**Slide 4:** Hour 6: Caption and copy block
Write all captions, hashtag sets, and email body copy.
Your scripts are already done. This is just adaptation.

**Slide 5:** Hours 7-8: Edit and schedule block
Edit footage. Export carousels. Schedule everything in your tool of choice.
By the end of hour 8, nothing is in draft. Everything is queued.

**Slide 6:** Block it on your calendar once a month.
The Reel Factory System gives you the templates. behike.gumroad.com
@behikeai

**Caption:** A batch day without a schedule becomes a long day of doing a little bit of everything and finishing nothing.

The hour-by-hour structure exists to protect single-mode focus. Writing hours only write. Recording hours only record. Editing hours only edit. When you force context switches between modes, you lose the momentum that makes batch days actually efficient.

This schedule is aggressive but achievable. The key is protecting the blocks. If hour 3 starts slipping into hour 4 because of indecision during recording, everything downstream shifts. Move through each block with a predetermined output goal, not a time goal.

Scripts: 30. Recordings: 30. Captions: 30. Scheduled: 30. Those are the targets. The hours are the structure that makes them reachable.

## Post 4 — The Repurposing System That Multiplies Your Output

**Hook:** You do not need more ideas. You need to use each idea on five platforms instead of one.

**Slide 1:** You do not need more ideas. You need to use each idea on five platforms instead of one.

**Slide 2:** One batch day core idea becomes:
- A 60-second reel (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube Shorts)
- A carousel (Instagram, LinkedIn)
- A Twitter/X thread
- An email newsletter section
- A blog post intro

One idea. Five platforms. 30 minutes of repurposing.

**Slide 3:** The repurposing stack by format:
Reel: record once, cut to 60s for Instagram, 45s for TikTok, 30s for Shorts
Carousel: export as PNG, post natively on both IG and LinkedIn
Thread: pull the five slides. Each slide = one tweet.

**Slide 4:** The time math:
Without repurposing: 5 platforms x 8 minutes of creation = 40 minutes per idea.
With repurposing: 8 minutes to create the core + 10 minutes to adapt = 18 minutes.
That is a 2.2x output improvement per idea. Over 30 ideas per month, that compounds significantly.

**Slide 5:** Content repurposing is not lazy. It is efficient.
The best idea deserves to be seen on every platform.
Different people live in different places.

**Caption:** The most productive content creators are not the ones with the most ideas. They are the ones who extract the most value from each idea they have.
