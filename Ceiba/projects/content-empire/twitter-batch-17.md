## Thread 2 | What 40Hz Binaural Beats Actually Do (Not the Hype Version)

**Tweet 1 (Hook):**
Binaural beats have a reputation as wellness pseudoscience.

Some of that reputation is deserved. But the 40Hz research is different.

Here's what the studies actually show.

---

**Tweet 2:**
First, how binaural beats work.

Your left ear hears one frequency, say 200Hz. Your right ear hears a slightly different one, say 240Hz. The difference is 40Hz.

Your brain processes that difference and produces a third signal at 40Hz. That's the binaural beat.

It requires headphones. Speakers cancel the effect.

---

**Tweet 3:**
The 40Hz frequency specifically is associated with gamma brain activity.

Gamma waves appear during high-level cognitive processing, focused attention, and working memory tasks.

The question researchers are testing: can you induce more gamma activity by playing 40Hz binaural stimulation?

---

**Tweet 4:**
The ADHD connection is specific.

ADHD involves underactivation in the prefrontal cortex during attention tasks. Gamma activity in that region correlates with better executive function.

A 2020 study found that 40Hz binaural beats improved attention task scores in ADHD subjects compared to control.

One study. Small sample. But the direction is consistent with the theory.

---

**Tweet 5:**
The effect is not the same for everyone.

Individual differences in baseline brain activity, sleep quality, caffeine level, and ambient noise all affect the result.

Nobody is promising it turns on focus like a switch. The research suggests it creates a more favorable state for focus. That's different.

---

**Tweet 6:**
Why I built it into Focus DJ specifically.

If the mechanism requires headphones and specific frequency separation, the implementation matters.

A generic "binaural beats playlist" on YouTube may not be set up correctly. Focus DJ calculates the exact carrier and offset to produce a clean 40Hz difference signal.

---

**Tweet 7:**
The practical setup.

Headphones on. 40Hz mode selected. Sit down to work.

That's it. No meditation ritual required. No specific breathing pattern. Just the audio running while you work.

The goal is not to feel the effect. The goal is better output at the end of the session.
