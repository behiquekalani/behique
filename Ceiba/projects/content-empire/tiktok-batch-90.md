## TikTok 3: The Five Content Pillars Framework
**Hook:**
If you don't have content pillars, you're making content harder than it needs to be.

**Script:**
Content pillars are the five topics you always return to. They connect to your offer, your audience's problems, and your own experience. For a solopreneur selling a digital product business course, the pillars might be: mindset, tools and tech, content creation, monetization, and productivity. Every piece of content you make fits into one of those five buckets. When you sit down to create, you don't stare at a blank page. You pick a pillar, pick an angle, and write the hook. The pillars also train your audience. Over time they know what to expect from you, and that predictability builds trust. Set your five pillars once. Use them forever.

## TikTok 6: The Repurposing System
**Hook:**
One piece of content can live in ten different places. Here's how.

**Script:**
Start with a long-form piece. A newsletter issue, a YouTube video, a detailed Twitter thread. That one piece becomes: five TikTok scripts, three Instagram carousels, ten short tweets, one LinkedIn post, and a section in your next email newsletter. The ideas aren't created fresh for each platform. They're extracted from the original and reformatted. This is what professional content teams do. As a solopreneur, you can replicate it with a simple extraction process. After you write the long-form piece, ask: what are the five most quotable statements here. Turn each into a short-form script. The thinking is already done. You're just adapting the packaging.

**CTA:**
Save this framework. Apply it to your next newsletter or long video.

## TikTok 7: The Problem With Business Advice Online
**Hook:**
Most business advice online is reverse-engineered from success and doesn't apply to you yet.

**Script:**
Someone who has a hundred thousand followers and ten products tells you how they grew their business. They're describing what worked for them at a scale you haven't reached yet. Their advice isn't wrong. It's just not where you are. The advice for someone at zero is completely different from the advice for someone at ten thousand followers. At zero, you need volume and experimentation. At ten thousand, you need conversion and product. At a hundred thousand, you need systems and delegation. When you hear advice online, ask: what stage is this person at, and does this apply to where I am right now. Applying late-stage advice at an early stage is one of the most common mistakes in online business.

## TikTok 8: The Specific Person Strategy
**Hook:**
Write your content for one specific person and watch what happens to your engagement.

**Script:**
When you write for everyone, you reach no one. When you write for one person, you reach everyone who is like that person. The specific person strategy means you have a real or imagined person in your head when you create. You know their age, their specific situation, the exact thing keeping them up at night, and the language they use to describe their problem. When your content speaks that precisely, the people who match that profile feel seen in a way that general content never achieves. And that feeling of being seen is what drives the comment, the save, the follow. Narrow your target. Sharpen the message. The more specific you get, the better your content performs.

## TikTok 9: Income Stacking for Beginners
**Hook:**
Here's how to go from one income stream to five in twelve months.

**Script:**
Month one to three: digital product, single item, low ticket. Month four to six: email list with a weekly newsletter. Monetize with an affiliate link and your own product. Month seven to nine: add a mid-ticket offer. Could be a small course, a template pack, or a done-with-you service. Month ten to twelve: add a paid community or a consulting offering for your best audience members. Five streams: low-ticket product, mid-ticket product, affiliate income, paid community, and consulting. All built on the same audience, the same brand, the same content. The key is not rushing the stack. Each stream needs to be working before you add the next one.

## TikTok 10: The Daily Habit That Builds Everything
**Hook:**
One daily habit separates creators who grow from creators who plateau.

**Script:**
The habit is writing. Not for an audience. Just writing to think. Spend fifteen minutes each morning writing down what you know about your niche that other people don't. What you've tried that didn't work. What surprised you. What you'd tell yourself two years ago. This daily writing practice does two things. It clarifies your thinking, which makes every piece of content you create sharper. And it builds a bank of ideas you can pull from when you sit down to create. Most people who say they don't know what to create simply haven't been doing the thinking in writing. Make the thinking visible. The content ideas come from the thinking, not from inspiration.

**CTA:**
Start tomorrow. Fifteen minutes. Write what you know. Report back.
