**Topic: What to focus on in your first 90 days online**

## TikTok 1: The 90-Day Plan Nobody Gives You
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Your first 90 days online determine everything. Here's the actual plan."

**Script:**
Most people start their online business by doing a little bit of everything and making progress on nothing.

Here is what the first 90 days should actually look like.

Days 1 to 30: pick one platform and one content format. Only one. Post every single day. Do not launch a product. Do not build a website. Just post. The goal is to develop your voice and get reps in before an audience shows up to judge you.

Days 31 to 60: start capturing emails. Put up a simple landing page with one free resource. Every piece of content you make should point people toward that list. Your content channel feeds the list. The list is the asset.

Days 61 to 90: build and launch your first product. Small. Simple. Priced between $17 and $47. Sell it to your list. Sell it to your followers. Get your first paying customer. That customer changes how you see yourself and this entire business.

After 90 days you have a voice, an audience, a list, and at least one sale. That is more than most people have after a year.

What day are you on? Drop it in the comments.

## TikTok 2: The One Metric That Matters in Month One
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Stop tracking followers in month one. Track this instead."

**Script:**
In your first 30 days online, the metric that matters is not followers. It is not views. It is not likes.

The metric is: did you show up and publish today?

Consistency in month one is not about performance. It is about building the habit and learning the craft. You are going to make bad content in month one. That is not a problem. That is the curriculum.

Every creator you admire had a month one. Their month one was bad too. The difference is they kept going past month three when the content started getting better.

If you are tracking vanity metrics in month one, you will quit when the numbers are small. And they will be small. They are always small at the start.

Track publish rate. Track how many posts you completed. Track whether you showed up. That is the data that builds the business.

**CTA:**
How many days this month have you actually posted? Be honest.

## TikTok 3: The Niche Mistake That Wastes Your First 90 Days
**Hook (0-2s):**
"The niche confusion that kills most beginners before they even start."

**Script:**
I see people spend weeks trying to "find their niche" before they post a single piece of content.

Here is the truth. You do not find your niche. You develop it through posting.

Start with a broad area you have real experience or genuine interest in. Post about what you know. Post about what you're learning. Post about problems you've personally solved.

After 30 days of daily posting, patterns will emerge. Some topics will get more response. Some angles will resonate. Your audience will tell you what they want more of through their engagement.

The niche finds you if you stay consistent long enough for the feedback loop to form.

The creator who waits for the perfect niche before starting is still waiting. The one who starts messy and adjusts gets specific in 60 days through actual data.

Start now. Adjust as you go. The niche is downstream of action, not upstream of it.

**CTA:**
If you've been waiting to "nail your niche" before posting, this is your sign to start.

## TikTok 4: What to Ignore in Your First 90 Days
**Hook (0-2s):**
"Here's the list of things you should not do in your first 90 days online."

**Script:**
Just as important as what you should focus on is what you should ignore completely.

Do not build a course in month one. You don't understand your audience well enough yet.

Do not obsess over your website. Nobody is going to your website in month one. They're finding you on social.

Do not spend hours designing a logo. Ship a simple, clean image and move on. The brand develops over time.

Do not follow multiple gurus with conflicting advice. Pick one framework and commit to it for 90 days before evaluating.

Do not compare your month one to someone else's year three. That is a trap that only leads to quitting.

Do not start a podcast, a YouTube channel, a newsletter, and a TikTok at the same time. Pick one distribution channel and go deep before expanding.

Simplicity is a competitive advantage in the beginning. The more variables you eliminate, the faster you learn what's actually working.

## TikTok 5: The 90-Day Mindset That Changes Everything
**Hook (0-2s):**
"The mental frame that makes the first 90 days actually survivable."

**Script:**
The biggest reason people quit before 90 days is expectations.

They expected faster results. More followers. More sales. More validation.

Here's the frame that keeps you going. The first 90 days are not about getting results. They are about becoming the type of person who shows up consistently. That's it.

When you hit 90 days, something shifts. You have evidence that you can stay consistent. You have data on what works. You have a few early wins, even small ones. And you have left behind the 95 percent of people who quit in the first 30 days.

The competition disappears early. Most people who start alongside you will be gone by week four.

If you can frame the first 90 days as a test of character rather than a test of performance, you will make it. And after 90 days, the game changes entirely.

**CTA:**
Save this for the next time you feel like quitting before 90 days.
