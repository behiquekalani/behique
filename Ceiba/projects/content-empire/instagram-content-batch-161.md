## Post 5 — The Notion Mistake That Keeps Most People Stuck

**Hook:** Most people use Notion as a graveyard for ideas they never act on.

**Slide 1:** Most people use Notion as a graveyard for ideas they never act on.

**Slide 2:** The mistake: building a beautiful workspace instead of a functional one. Every hour spent tweaking icons and adding cover images is an hour not working.

**Slide 3:** The second mistake: no hierarchy. Everything is a page. Nothing connects to anything. It is just digital clutter in a nicer font.

**Slide 4:** The third mistake: no review ritual. You add things in. Nothing comes out. Notion becomes an inbox with no output. Ideas die.

**Slide 5:** The fix: build for function first. Three databases. One dashboard. A weekly review that clears the backlog. That is a working system.

**Slide 6:** Less design. More system. @behikeai has the templates to get you there.

**Caption:** Notion is not the problem. How most people use it is the problem.

They spend hours building a workspace that looks like a productivity influencer's setup. Custom icons, gradient covers, nested databases three levels deep. And then they open it every morning and still feel lost.

A workspace that looks good but does not reduce friction is useless. The best Notion setups are boring to look at and frictionless to use. Simple databases. Clear views. A dashboard that answers "what do I do today" without making you click through five pages.

The weekly review is the piece most people skip. Without it, Notion fills up. Ideas pile in. Nothing gets processed. It becomes another thing to feel guilty about.

One weekly session, 30 minutes, to close the loop on what happened and set the priorities for what comes next. That single habit turns Notion from a digital junk drawer into an actual business operating system.

Build simple. Review weekly. Let function drive the design.
