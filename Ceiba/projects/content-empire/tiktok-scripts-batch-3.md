## Script 3: eBay Research in 10 Minutes
**Duration:** ~45 seconds

**Hook (first 3 seconds):**
Visual: eBay sold listings filter, items with green "Sold" tags
Text overlay: "This filter changes everything."
Spoken: "Most people look at what's for sale on eBay. Look at what actually sold."

**Body:**
Go to eBay. Search any product category. Filter by "sold listings." Now you're not looking at what people hope to sell. You're looking at what people paid real money for.

From there, three numbers matter. Sell-through rate: what percentage of listings actually sold. Velocity: how many units moved in 30 days. And margin: what you can buy it for versus what it sold for.

If sell-through is above 60% and you can hit 30% margin after fees, that product is worth testing.

Buy one unit. List it. See if it sells in two weeks. If it does, buy five more. That's the whole system.

You don't need a course. You need the filter and three numbers. The rest is just repeating what works.

**CTA:**
Text overlay: "Sold listings. Sell-through. Margin."
Spoken: "That's the loop. Go run it on one category today."

Spoken: "Same idea, two languages, posted the same day. Here's how I actually do that."

**Body:**

What I do is adaptation. One idea, written from scratch in both languages, for two different audiences with different contexts.

The English version for @behikeai talks to builders in North America and Europe who have access to all the tools, all the resources, and still aren't executing.

The Spanish version talks to someone in Monterrey or San Juan who has half the tools, twice the hustle, and no roadmap written for their situation.

Same insight. Different framing. Completely different resonance.

Publish both the same day. Over time you're building two audiences simultaneously with one creative session.
