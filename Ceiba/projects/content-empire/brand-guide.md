# Behike Brand Guide
# The source of truth for all brand decisions

---

## BRAND NAME

**Behike** (pronounced: beh-HEE-keh)

Named after the Behique, the spiritual healer and advisor of the Taino people, the indigenous civilization of Puerto Rico and the Caribbean. The Behique was the bridge between knowledge and action, between the spiritual and the practical.

That's what this brand is: bridging AI knowledge with practical application.

---

## BRAND IDENTITY

### Mission
Make AI accessible, practical, and safe for builders who want to do more with less.

### Voice
- **Direct.** Say what needs to be said. No corporate fluff.
- **Calm authority.** Confident without being arrogant.
- **Philosophical-practical hybrid.** Dan Koe energy: depth meets actionability.
- **Real.** Written by a person, not a brand committee.
- **Anti-hype.** No "revolutionary." No "game-changing." No exclamation marks unless earned.

### Audience
- Young entrepreneurs (18-35) who are building something
- Small business owners overwhelmed by AI but curious
- Side hustlers looking for leverage
- Developers who want to deploy AI beyond tutorials
- Anyone who feels left behind by the AI wave but ready to catch up

### Personality
If Behike were a person: a calm, technical friend who actually knows what they're talking about. Not trying to sell you anything. Just showing you what they built and how. If you want in, the door is open.

### Design Principles
1. **Apple minimalism.** White space is a feature, not waste.
2. **Dark-first.** Default to dark backgrounds with light text.
3. **Data is beautiful.** Numbers, charts, and stats are visual elements.
4. **No stock photo people.** Use abstract graphics, code screenshots, or AI-generated imagery.
5. **Consistency.** Same colors, same fonts, same tone across all platforms.

## CONTENT VOICE GUIDELINES

### DO:
- Use short sentences and paragraphs
- Lead with the insight, not the setup
- Include specific numbers and examples
- Be honest about limitations and failures
- Use periods and commas for pauses (never em dashes)
- Write like you're talking to one person, not a crowd

### DON'T:
- Use emojis in product copy or guides (social media captions are an exception)
- Use "game-changing," "revolutionary," "unlock your potential," or similar hype words
- Use exclamation marks more than once per piece of content
- Promise guaranteed results
- Compare negatively to competitors (focus on what we offer, not what others lack)
- Use AI-sounding language ("certainly," "absolutely," "as an AI")

### TONE BY PLATFORM:

| Platform | Tone | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| Instagram | Confident, visual, slightly casual | "5 AI tools. Zero monthly cost. Here's the stack." |
| TikTok | Energetic, hook-heavy, conversational | "Watch me hack an AI agent in 10 seconds" |
| X/Twitter | Sharp, opinionated, thread-worthy | "Most people using AI are wasting 90% of its potential." |
| YouTube | Calm, authoritative, educational | "Let me show you exactly how this works." |
| Newsletter | Personal, reflective, friend-to-friend | "This week I learned something about..." |
| Products | Professional, clear, value-focused | "This guide shows you exactly how to..." |

## BRAND STORY (for About pages, bios, press)

Behike was born from a simple observation: most people know AI is powerful, but they don't know how to actually use it.

Founded by Kalani Andre, a computer engineering student in Puerto Rico, Behike started as a personal project. Three computers networked in a bedroom. Six AI agents handling research, writing, coding, and content. Zero employees. Zero monthly AI costs.

What began as a way to build faster became a system that runs a business. And when people started asking "how did you do that," Behike became the answer.

Today, Behike creates AI tools, guides, and automation services for builders who want to do more with less. Everything we teach, we've tested. Everything we sell, we use. The tools are free or open-source. The knowledge is the product.

Built in Puerto Rico. Powered by AI. Run by one person with a lot of agents.
