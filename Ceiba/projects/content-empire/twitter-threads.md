## THREAD 5: "The real reason you're not using AI effectively"

**Hook tweet:**
You've tried AI. The results were underwhelming.

The problem isn't the AI. It's how you're talking to it. (thread)

**Tweet 2:**
Most people type one sentence and expect magic.

"Write me a blog post."

That's like telling a contractor "build me a house" with no blueprints, no budget, and no timeline. You'll get something. It won't be what you wanted.

**Tweet 3:**
The fix is embarrassingly simple: be specific.

Bad: "Write me a blog post about AI."
Good: "Write a 1,200-word blog post for small business owners about 3 AI tools that save time on data entry. Tone: professional but conversational. Include one real example per tool."

**Tweet 4:**
Second fix: give context.

Tell the AI who you are, who the audience is, and what outcome you want.

"I run a digital marketing agency. My clients are local restaurants. Write an email that convinces them to try AI-powered menu recommendations."

Context changes everything.

**Tweet 5:**
Third fix: show format.

Don't describe what you want. Show an example of what good looks like.

Paste a piece of content you love and say "write something in this style about [topic]."

AI is great at pattern matching. Give it a pattern.

**Tweet 6:**
Fourth fix: set constraints.

"Keep it under 500 words."
"Use only simple language, no jargon."
"Structure it as: hook, 3 points, CTA."
"Do NOT use emojis or exclamation marks."

Constraints make AI sharper. Unlimited freedom makes it generic.
