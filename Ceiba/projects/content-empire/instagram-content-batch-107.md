The output is yours. The thinking is yours. The speed is the AI's contribution.

This is the workflow that actually produces content worth reading.

## Post 2 — The 5-Prompt System for Writing a Month of Content in a Day

**Hook:** I wrote 30 days of content in one afternoon. Here's the exact 5-prompt system.

**Slide 1:** 30 days of content in one afternoon. Here's the 5-prompt system I used with Claude.

**Slide 2:** Prompt 1 — Brain dump. Tell Claude everything you know about your topic without editing. Let it find the angles you missed.

**Slide 3:** Prompt 2 — Topic list. Ask for 20 post ideas based on your brain dump. Delete the generic ones. Keep what resonates.

**Slide 4:** Prompt 3 — Outline first. For each post, ask Claude to outline before writing. Fix the structure before filling in content.

**Slide 5:** Prompt 4 — Your voice. Paste a sample of your writing. Ask Claude to match tone, not generate content.

**Slide 6:** Prompt 5 — Edit mode. Ask Claude to make each post shorter, sharper, and more specific. Then post it.

**Caption:** Writing is not the bottleneck. Thinking is the bottleneck.

The 5-prompt system works because it separates the thinking from the writing. You do the thinking in prompt 1 and 2. You do the structuring in prompt 3. You preserve your voice in prompt 4. You sharpen the output in prompt 5.

Most people sit down to write and try to do all five of those things simultaneously. That's why it takes four hours to write one post.

When you hand each stage to a workflow, you move faster and end up with better work. Not because the AI is smarter than you. Because the process is smarter than starting from nothing every single time.

The bottleneck in your content is not talent. It's process.

## Post 3 — How to Keep Your Voice When Using AI

**Hook:** AI-written content all sounds the same. Here's how to keep your voice intact.

**Slide 1:** The biggest fear with AI writing is sounding like everyone else. Here's how to prevent it.

**Slide 2:** The problem: most people ask Claude to write from scratch. AI fills the gaps with generic patterns. Your voice disappears.

**Slide 3:** The fix: always write the first sentence yourself. Even one sentence establishes the voice AI will follow.

**Slide 4:** Better fix: paste 3 examples of your best writing and say "Match this tone and sentence structure." Claude is very good at this.

**Slide 5:** Best fix: use AI to edit your drafts, not generate them. Your messy draft, Claude's clean structure. You keep your perspective. AI removes the clutter.

**Slide 6:** Your voice is your competitive advantage. Protect it. Follow @behikeai for tools that keep you you.

**Caption:** The homogenization problem is real. If you ask AI to write content from a topic keyword, you get content that could belong to anyone. It has correct information and no soul.

The solution is process, not avoidance.

Write your own first sentence. Paste your own writing samples as voice references. Draft the core idea yourself and let AI clean it up. Use AI for structure, transitions, and editing. Keep the opinion and the experience.

The people who sound the most human online while using AI are the ones who understood that AI is a tool for refinement, not a replacement for perspective.

Your audience follows you because of how you see things, not because you produce content faster than everyone else. Protect the perspective. Let AI handle the polish.

## Post 4 — Claude for Research: The Workflow Most People Skip

**Hook:** Most people use AI to write. The smarter use is research.

**Slide 1:** Most creators use Claude for writing. The higher-leverage use is research.

**Slide 2:** Use case 1: Competitive analysis. Paste a competitor's content and ask Claude to identify the gaps, angles they missed, and what their audience probably wants next.

**Slide 3:** Use case 2: Counterargument prep. Before publishing, ask Claude to argue against your post. Find the holes before your audience does.

**Slide 4:** Use case 3: Audience psychology. Describe your niche in detail and ask Claude what that audience is afraid of, what they want to prove, and what words they use.

**Slide 5:** Use case 4: Topic depth. Pick one topic and ask Claude for 10 layers of depth — from beginner to advanced. Build 10 posts from one conversation.

**Slide 6:** AI is a research partner, not just a writer. Follow @behikeai.

**Caption:** The content that performs best is not well-written content. It's well-researched content that happens to be well-written.

Most creators spend 90% of their AI budget on writing and 10% on thinking. The highest-leverage creators flip that ratio.

Use Claude to stress-test your ideas before you ship them. Use it to find what your audience actually cares about, in the language they use. Use it to identify what everyone in your niche is saying and then figure out what none of them are saying.

The writing is the last step. The thinking is the hard part. AI is genuinely useful for the thinking step in a way most people haven't tried yet.

Research first. Write second. This is the workflow change that makes everything else faster.

## Post 5 — The 3 Claude Prompts Every Creator Should Have Saved

**Hook:** 3 Claude prompts I keep in my notes and use every week.

**Slide 1:** 3 Claude prompts that are permanently saved in my workflow.

**Slide 2:** Prompt 1 — The Sharpener. "Here is my draft. Make it 30% shorter. Make every sentence more specific. Do not change my tone or main argument."

**Slide 3:** Prompt 2 — The Hook Machine. "Here is my main point. Write 10 different first sentences for a carousel post. No fluff. No questions. Make each one land on its own."

**Slide 4:** Prompt 3 — The Caption Builder. "Here is my carousel content. Write a caption that states the thesis in sentence 1, explains the importance in sentences 2-3, and ends with one clear action."

**Slide 5:** These three prompts handle the parts of content creation that most people find hardest: editing, hooks, and captions. All three can be done in under 15 minutes.

**Slide 6:** Steal these. Test them. Make them yours. Follow @behikeai for more.

**Caption:** Prompts are reusable tools. Most people treat every AI conversation as starting from scratch. The better approach is building a small library of prompts that work for your specific content and voice, and using them every week.

These three cover the highest-friction moments in my content workflow. Editing is hard because cutting your own writing feels like losing. The Sharpener makes cutting mechanical. Hooks are hard because the first sentence determines whether anyone reads the rest. The Hook Machine gives you 10 options so you're choosing, not guessing. Captions are hard because most people don't know what they're trying to say in 200 words. The Caption Builder gives you a structure.

Three prompts. Three problems solved. That's the whole playbook.
