# Psychologist Framework — Kalani's Maintenance Plan

Source: Real therapeutic plan from Kalani's psychologist. This is the backbone of BehiqueBot and Ceiba's accountability logic.

---

## For Attention / ADHD (Inatención)

### Day Structuring
- Visual calendars and daily planning tools
- Break large tasks into small, concrete subtasks — never vague
- Pomodoro technique: 25 min focus + 5 min rest
- Frequent alarms and reminders, daily checklists

### Cognitive & Environmental Hygiene
- Minimize distractions (headphones, clean workspace)
- Fixed routines: same time to sleep, eat, and work
- Single-tasking — one thing at a time, consciously

### Mental Impulsivity Management
- Brief mindfulness (2-5 min) at start and end of day
- Identify distracting thoughts and consciously redirect
- "Post-it mental" technique: write down impulses/ideas without acting on them immediately

### Visual & Sensory Support
- Color coding for task prioritization
- Visual organization tools (tables, mind maps, bullet journals)
- Sensory regulation materials when needed

---

## For Emotional Accountability

### Self-Observation & Emotional Monitoring
- Weekly emotional state check-in: "How are you actually doing?"
- Identify early warning signs of emotional/behavioral shifts
- Periodic internal checks: "How am I? What do I need?"

### Self-Care Routines
- Diaphragmatic breathing, grounding, tapping, safe place visualization
- Regular physical exercise, adequate rest, balanced nutrition
- Meaningful leisure (art, nature, reading, spirituality)

### Support Network
- Identify trusted people to talk to when needed
- Agree on a signal/mechanism for emotional relapse support
- Participate in social activities that strengthen belonging

### Relapse Prevention
- List of personal risk factors (stressors, distorted thoughts, destabilizing environments)
- List of effective tools already used in the past
- Action plan for relapse:
  1. Use a regulation technique
  2. Contact support network
  3. Consider contacting therapist if needed

---

## Self-Compassion & Cognitive Reframing

- Reframe self-criticism: "I'm not lazy, I'm learning to organize myself"
- Validate effort over result: "I focused for 15 minutes today — that's a win"
- Celebrate small weekly advances (personal achievement log)
- Daily affirmations: "I am enough", "I am in control of my emotions"

---

## Ceiba Application Rules

When using this framework:
1. Ask the right questions — don't just log answers
2. Notice what Kalani DOESN'T say as much as what he does
3. Validate effort before pushing for more
4. Reduce scope when stuck: what's the smallest possible next action?
5. If avoidance persists: gently suggest reconnecting with support network or psychologist
