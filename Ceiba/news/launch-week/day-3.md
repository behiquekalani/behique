The Behike Method doesn't fight your brain. It works with it.

Rule 1: Never plan more than 3 tasks per day.
If you finish all 3, that's a perfect day. Not a slow one.

Rule 2: The first task of the day is always the smallest.
Momentum beats motivation every time.

### Slide 4:
Rule 3: Capture everything. Execute nothing immediately.
Every idea goes into a capture system (mine is a Telegram bot I built).
Ideas sit for 24 hours before you decide if they're real.

Rule 4: Time-block in 25-minute chunks with forced breaks.
Not because Pomodoro is magic. Because your brain needs transitions.

### Slide 5:
Rule 5: End every work session by writing what's next.
Not "work on project." That's too vague. Write: "Open the pricing page and change the $9.99 to $12.99."

Specific next actions kill decision fatigue. Decision fatigue is what actually stops you, not laziness.
