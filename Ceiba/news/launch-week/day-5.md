## POST 1 -- @kalaniandrez -- 7:00 PM EST
**Format:** Carousel (4 slides)

### Slide 1:
I have 47 unfinished projects on my laptop.

Here's what ADHD entrepreneurship actually looks like.

### Slide 2:
The fantasy: "ADHD gives you hyperfocus superpowers."

The reality: Hyperfocus is random. You can't aim it. One day you code for 12 hours and build something incredible. The next day you can't open a file.

The 47 projects aren't failures. They're evidence of a brain that gets excited faster than it can execute.

### Slide 3:
What I changed:

I stopped fighting it. Instead of forcing myself to finish everything, I built a system:

1. Every idea goes into a capture bot (I literally built one for this)
2. Ideas sit for 24 hours before I decide
3. Max 3 active projects at any time
4. Everything else goes on a backlog with no guilt attached
5. If I haven't touched a project in 2 weeks, it moves to archive

### Slide 4:
47 unfinished projects. 3 active ones that are actually moving.

That's not a failure rate. That's a filter.

If this sounds familiar, follow. I post the real version of building with ADHD. Not the motivational one.

### Caption (copy-paste):
I used to feel guilty about every unfinished project. Like each one was proof I couldn't follow through.

Then I realized something. The projects weren't the problem. The system was.

When everything is "active," nothing moves. When you pick 3 and let the rest rest, things actually ship.

I still have 47 folders. But I only open 3 of them. And those 3 are further along than all 47 ever were.

If you have ADHD and you're trying to build something, follow this page. I'm documenting all of it.
