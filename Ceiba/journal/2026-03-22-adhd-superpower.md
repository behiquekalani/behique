Someone once told me ADHD makes you bad at business. I just built 25 products in a week.

Not outlines. Not "coming soon" pages. Real products with real content. Some are 15,000 words. Some are interactive apps. One has a playable Minecraft game inside it.

The trick is not fighting the hyperfocus. It's aiming it. My psychologist calls it the Post-It Mental principle. You capture everything that comes to mind immediately, without needing to execute it right now. Then you pick ONE thing and aim the full force of your brain at it.

That's what BehiqueBot was supposed to be. A capture system. So the ideas don't get lost between the shower and the desk.

The productivity books say "plan your day in advance" and "stick to the schedule." Mira, my brain doesn't work like that. Some days I can't start until 11pm. And then I build until sunrise. The productivity system has to flex around the brain, not the other way around.

Built an ADHD-specific budget app today. Five categories instead of fifty. Impulse cooldown timer. Visual jars that fill up. Because every budget app I've tried made me close it after 5 seconds. Too many inputs. Too many decisions. My app has ONE number pad and ONE tap to categorize.

Also wrote a chapter called "The ADHD Builder's Edge" for the book. 4,000 words about why our brains are actually built for entrepreneurship. Hyperfocus is the engine. The problem is never intensity. It's direction.

I don't want to "manage" my ADHD. I want to aim it.
