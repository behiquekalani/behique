# Issue 2610: Build in Public, Sell in Private

**The Idea:** Building in public is one of the most effective growth strategies available to a solopreneur. You share the journey, the failures, the behind-the-scenes. You make the audience feel like they're part of something. They root for you. They tell their friends. Trust compounds faster than any ad spend.

But there's a misunderstanding about what "build in public" means for selling. Some creators think it means turning their sales process into public content. The pitch, the objection handling, the urgency. That's not building in public. That's spam in a timeline.

The actual model works like this. Build in public to grow trust. Sell in private to close. The public content answers the question "who is this person and do I trust them?" The private conversation, the email, the direct offer, answers the question "is this right for me?"

When you conflate the two, you either undersell to people who were ready to buy, or you come across as performative to people who just wanted to watch the journey. The audience has different members at different stages. Most of them are not ready to buy. Build for the majority in public. Sell to the minority in private.

**Why It Matters:** Trust is the fuel. Transactions are the output. Treating them as the same thing burns both.

**One Action:** Audit your last ten pieces of content. Label each one as either "trust-building" or "selling." If more than 30% are selling, you're burning trust faster than you're building it.
