# Output More Than You Consume

Most people consume ten units of information for every one unit of output. Then they wonder why they feel busy but stuck.

## The Consumption Trap
Learning feels like progress. Reading another book, watching another course, saving another thread feels productive. It isn't. Information without application is just stimulation. It keeps your brain busy without moving anything forward.

The person who reads less and ships more will, over time, outpace the person who consumes voraciously but produces rarely. The gap between knowing and doing is where most potential dies.

## What Output Actually Does
Every piece of work you put into the world gives you feedback you can't get from consuming. You learn what resonates. You learn where your thinking has holes. You discover what you actually believe versus what you think you believe.

Output is the laboratory. Consumption is just reading someone else's lab notes. Both have a role. The ratio matters.

## Rebalancing the Ratio
A healthy output-to-input ratio isn't 50/50. For most builders, the right target is closer to 70% output, 30% input. That means more creating than consuming on any given day. It feels uncomfortable at first because most people have the opposite habit baked in.

Discomfort here is a signal you're moving in the right direction.

## This Week's Action
Track your time for three days. Split it into "input" (reading, watching, listening) and "output" (writing, building, creating). If input exceeds output, flip the ratio for the following three days.
