# Issue 2390: The Niche Is Not a Prison

**The One Thing:** Niching down expands your reach. Trying to appeal to everyone reaches no one.

---

The fear of niching is the fear of exclusion. If I narrow my focus, I lose potential customers. This is backwards. Narrowing your focus makes you the obvious choice for a specific person. Being the obvious choice for a specific person is worth more than being a vague option for everyone.

## How Niche Creates Authority

When you speak to everyone, you sound like no one. When you speak to one person's exact problem, you sound like a genius. That specificity is mistaken for expertise. And over time, it becomes expertise, because you go deeper on fewer problems than anyone else in your space. Depth creates authority. Breadth creates noise.

## The Test for a Good Niche

Ask three questions. First: is there an existing conversation happening in this niche? If people are already searching, talking, buying in this space, demand exists. Second: can you speak to it with genuine credibility? Not perfection. Credibility. Third: is there a product or service you can build around it? If all three are yes, you have a niche worth pursuing.

## Evolving Without Losing Your Core

Niches can evolve. Most great creators start specific and expand naturally as their authority grows. The mistake is expanding before the authority is there. Build the specific audience first. Then earn the right to go broader. The niche is a launching pad, not a life sentence.
