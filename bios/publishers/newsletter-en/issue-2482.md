# Issue 2482: The Specific Knowledge Trap

**The Idea:** Everyone tells you to develop specific knowledge. That is good advice. But most people misapply it.

They go deep on a skill. They become genuinely good. Then they try to sell "I am good at X" and wonder why nobody buys.

Specific knowledge is not the product. It is the input. The product is the outcome your specific knowledge produces for someone else.

A programmer who can build Stripe integrations fast is not selling programming. They are selling "your checkout works and you stop losing customers at the payment step." A writer who understands persuasion is not selling writing. They are selling "your readers do the thing you want them to do."

The gap between skill and sellable product is translation. You have to take what you know and reframe it entirely around what the buyer gains. This sounds obvious. It almost never gets done.

Most creators pitch their effort and expertise. Buyers do not care about your effort. They care about their problem going away.

**Why It Matters:** Specific knowledge with no translation to buyer outcomes is a hobby. Translated, it becomes a business. The translation step is where almost all the money is.

**One Action:** Write down your core skill in one sentence. Then rewrite it starting with "So that you can." That second sentence is your actual offer.
