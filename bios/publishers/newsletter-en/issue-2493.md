# Issue 2493: The Problem With Passive Income as a Goal

**The Idea:** "Passive income" is one of the most misused phrases in the online business world.

Nothing is fully passive. Every income stream requires either ongoing creation, ongoing marketing, or ongoing maintenance. What people actually mean when they say passive income is income that is decoupled from their time in close-to-real-time.

A digital product does not require you to be present for every sale. But it required you to create it. It requires you to market it. It requires you to keep the sales infrastructure running. The passivity is partial and delayed.

The more useful frame is leverage. Can you create something once that generates returns over a longer time horizon? Yes. Is that valuable? Absolutely. But framing it as passive leads to magical thinking about effort and timeline.

The creators who build durable "passive" income treat it like an investment. You put in significant work upfront. You maintain and market. You reinvest returns into better products and wider distribution. Over years, the ratio of income to active effort improves dramatically.

That is the real prize, not zero effort, but decreasing effort per dollar over time.

**Why It Matters:** Expecting truly passive income leads to product launches with no follow-through, abandoned funnels, and confusion about why things are not working. The work does not stop. It shifts.

**One Action:** For each digital product you have, spend 30 minutes this week improving one distribution channel. Not creating new products. Improving reach for what already exists.
