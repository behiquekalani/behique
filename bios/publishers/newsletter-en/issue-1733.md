# Issue 1733: Ship Ugly, Refine Later

**One insight. No fluff.**

The thing you are waiting to finish before you release it is not going to be ready. Not because you are not capable, but because "ready" is a moving target that perfectionism keeps pushing forward. The refining process needs real feedback from real users, and real users only exist after you ship.

Version one is supposed to be imperfect. It is the beginning of a conversation with the market, not the final statement. The people who buy version one are the most forgiving customers you will ever have. They want the thing to exist. They are buying a promise as much as a product. Ship the promise. Build toward it afterward.

The cost of not shipping is higher than the cost of shipping something rough. Not shipping means no feedback, no revenue, no iteration, no audience. Shipping something imperfect means all of those things start happening immediately. Done beats perfect. Ugly and out beats polished and hidden. Ship ugly. Refine in public. That is how good products are actually built.
