# The Price You Set Is a Signal

Underpricing is not humility. It is a miscommunication about value.

## The Idea
Pricing is one of the least understood levers in a creator business. Most creators default to low prices because they are afraid of rejection. They think a lower price removes the barrier to purchase. Often it creates a different barrier: doubt about quality.

Buyers do not just evaluate the product. They evaluate the price as information. A $7 product signals something. A $70 product signals something different. A $700 product signals something else entirely. None of these signals are automatically right or wrong, but they all shape the buyer's expectations and the buyer's trust.

When you charge less than the value you provide, you are not being generous. You are sending a signal that the product is not worth much, or that you do not believe in it fully. Buyers pick up on this.

The right price is one that communicates the level of transformation being offered, covers your costs, and creates a margin that makes the business sustainable. It should be high enough that the buyer takes the product seriously.

Raising prices is also a filter. Higher prices attract buyers who are ready to act and who value what you are offering. Lower prices attract buyers who are price-sensitive, which often correlates with buyers who engage less, implement less, and ask for refunds more.

## Why It Matters
Your pricing tells a story about your work before anyone reads a single word of the product. Make sure the story is accurate.

## One Action
Pick your lowest-priced product and calculate what you would need to charge to feel genuinely confident that the price matches the value. If it is higher than your current price, raise it today.
