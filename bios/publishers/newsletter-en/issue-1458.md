# Solopreneur Systems: What to Automate First

The biggest time sink in any solo operation is not the work itself. It is the recurring overhead around the work. Scheduling, follow-ups, delivery, formatting. Automate those first.

## The Automation Audit

Before you add any new automation, run an audit of your week. Log every recurring task that happens more than twice. These are your automation candidates. Anything that is the same process every time should not require your attention.

The goal is to protect your high-judgment hours. Anything that does not require your judgment specifically should be handled by a system.

## The Three Categories

Automations fall into three types: content delivery (sending the newsletter, posting the content), operational (invoicing, onboarding, follow-ups), and research (monitoring, aggregating, summarizing information).

Start with delivery and operational. These are high-frequency, low-judgment tasks that drain real time. Research automation comes later, once you have built the infrastructure for what you do with the research.

## The Trap of Over-Automation

Not everything should be automated. The personal touch in a reply to a longtime reader matters. The specific call-out in a newsletter to a recent event matters. Over-automation strips out the signals of genuine human presence.

Automate the mechanical. Keep the personal manual. The distinction is not always obvious, which is why you should audit for it intentionally.

## This Week's Action

List every task you did this week that you have also done identically in at least two previous weeks. Pick the one that takes the most time. Research one tool that could automate or semi-automate it. Implement it before next Monday.
