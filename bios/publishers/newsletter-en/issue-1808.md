# Issue 1808: The Problem With Waiting to Feel Ready

**One insight. No fluff.**

Readiness is a feeling that comes after you start, not before. You will not wake up one morning feeling prepared to do the hard thing. That feeling is earned through action, not granted by time.

The person waiting to feel confident before starting is asking for the reward before doing the work. Confidence is a result. It comes from doing something uncertain and surviving it. Then doing it again. Then doing it enough times that uncertainty stops being threatening.

Every skill you currently have felt impossible before it felt natural. You learned to read, to drive, to hold a conversation. None of those felt ready at the start. They became natural through repetition. The hard thing you're avoiding is no different. The gap between you and the person who has done it is just reps. Stop waiting for a feeling that can only arrive after you begin.
