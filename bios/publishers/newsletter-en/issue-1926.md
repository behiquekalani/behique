# Issue 1926: Attention Is the First Product

**One insight. No fluff.**

Before you can sell anything, you have to earn attention. Most people reverse the order. They build the product first, then try to figure out the audience. This is why so many good products die in obscurity. The product is sound. The audience was never built.

Attention is itself a product. It has to be manufactured with the same intentionality as anything you sell. You build it through consistent output, a clear point of view, and content that is genuinely useful or genuinely interesting. Not useful-in-a-vague-way. Useful to a specific person with a specific problem who is actively looking for an answer.

The writer who posts every day for two years has built something real. Not a following in the vanity sense, but a durable asset. When they launch a product, there are people who already trust their judgment. The sale is almost a formality. The work was the two years before the launch. Build the audience before you need it. By the time you need it, it is too late to start.

*Build something worth owning.*
