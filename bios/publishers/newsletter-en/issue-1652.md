# Content That Compounds vs Content That Spikes

A viral post gives you a week. A well-built catalog gives you a career. Most people optimize for the week.

## The Spike Illusion
Viral content feels like success because the metrics are immediate and visible. Thousands of views. Hundreds of shares. It's measurable, shareable, and exciting. It also disappears. Three weeks later, nobody remembers it, and you're back to zero building the next spike.

The spike strategy exhausts creators because it requires constant novelty. You can't rest on a spike. The moment you stop producing, the numbers go flat.

## How Compounding Works in Content
Compounding content earns attention over time rather than all at once. A how-to guide written in 2022 is still getting discovered in 2026. A framework explained clearly is still being shared years after publication. These pieces work because they address durable problems.

Each new piece you add to this library makes the whole library more findable and more valuable. That's compounding. It's slow to start and hard to stop.

## Building Both Without Burning Out
The best strategy isn't to choose. It's to build a compounding base first, then let occasional spikes drive new people into a catalog worth staying for. The spike is the entrance. The catalog is the reason they stay.

Without the catalog, spikes are just traffic with nowhere to go.

## This Week's Action
Write one piece this week designed to still be useful in three years. Focus on a fundamental principle, a process, or a framework. Make it as clear and thorough as you can.
