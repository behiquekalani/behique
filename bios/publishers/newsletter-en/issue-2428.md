# Issue 2428: Multiple Income Streams Without Losing Focus

**The One Thing:** Add income streams in sequence, not in parallel. Master one before building the next.

---

The advice to diversify your income is correct. The execution most people attempt is wrong. They try to run three different businesses simultaneously and end up with three half-built things that earn nothing. The answer is sequencing.

## One at a Time

Pick the income stream closest to working. Put full effort there until it's generating consistent revenue. Then and only then do you add the next layer. This is not slow. This is how you actually build multiple streams without destroying all of them.

The solopreneur who has one stream producing $3,000 a month is in a better position than the one running five streams producing $200 each. Concentration produces results. Diversification is for protecting results you already have.

## The Natural Progression

Digital products pair well with a newsletter. The newsletter builds trust. Trust converts to product sales. Product sales fund paid traffic. Paid traffic grows the list. The list buys more products. This flywheel compounds without requiring you to start something entirely new each time.

Services can fund the time it takes to build a product. A product can reduce your dependency on service income. These aren't competing. They're complementary when sequenced right.

## When to Add the Next One

Add the next income stream when the current one requires less than ten hours a week to maintain. That's the signal. Not when you're bored. Not when you saw someone else do it. When the current one is running without constant input.
