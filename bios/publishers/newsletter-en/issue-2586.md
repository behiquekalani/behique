# Issue 2586: Why Most Digital Products Fail Before Launch

**The Idea:** The most common reason digital products fail is not quality. It is validation sequence. People build first, then find out if anyone wants it.

The correct order is reversed. You find a real problem, confirm people feel it, describe your solution in plain language, and gauge response. Then you build. By the time you launch, you already know someone will buy.

This sounds obvious. Almost no one does it. Building feels like progress. Talking to potential customers feels like delay. So creators spend weeks on design, curriculum, and sales copy for something no one asked for.

Pre-selling fixes this. You offer a product before it exists, take payment, and build it for the people who bought in. If no one buys the pitch, you saved weeks. If people buy, you have validated the concept and funded the creation.

The other common failure mode is solving the wrong problem. You solve the problem you found interesting, not the problem your audience is actively searching for. The most successful digital products solve an urgent, specific problem that people are already trying to fix on their own.

**Why It Matters:** Validation before creation converts product development from a gamble into a calculated build. It shortens the loop between idea and income and reduces the cost of being wrong.

**One Action:** Before your next product, write a one-paragraph pitch describing the problem and outcome, and share it with five people who match your target audience. Watch their reaction.
