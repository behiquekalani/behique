# The Price You Set Signals the Value You Believe In

Underpricing is not humility. It is a statement about how much you believe your work is worth. Buyers read that statement.

## The Idea

Price is communication. When you price something low, you are telling the buyer that either the value is low or your confidence is low. Either interpretation reduces their willingness to invest attention in what you made. This is the paradox of underpricing: you think low prices reduce the barrier to entry. They actually reduce perceived value and attract buyers who are less committed.

The right price is not the highest price you can get away with. It is the price that accurately reflects the transformation your product or service delivers. If your product saves someone ten hours a week, price it against the value of those ten hours, not against the cost of your time making it.

Most creators underprice because they are afraid of rejection. A $97 price tag feels aggressive. But a customer who says no to $97 would have said no at $47 too and gotten less value. The buyers who are right for your work will pay the right price when they understand what they are getting.

Raising your prices also changes your relationship to the work. When you charge more, you care more. You put in more. The quality of your attention goes up because the stakes went up. Clients who pay more also behave differently. They follow through. They get results. They refer others.

Pricing is a confidence exercise before it is a business decision.

## Why It Matters

Your price is a reflection of your self-concept. Fix the self-concept first. Then set the price.

## One Action

Look at your current pricing. Ask: if I doubled this, would the product still deliver more value than the price? If yes, you have room to raise. Raise it on the next sale.
