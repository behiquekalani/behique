# Issue 1722: Price Is a Signal

**One insight. No fluff.**

When you underprice your work, you do not attract more customers. You attract the wrong ones. Low prices signal low confidence, and people buy confidence before they buy product. The moment you apologize for your price, you have already lost the sale psychologically.

Pricing is positioning. A $9 product and a $97 product can contain identical content. One says "I made this quick." The other says "I built something worth your attention." The buyer's experience changes based on what they paid. Higher investment produces higher engagement. That is not exploitation. That is how humans work.

Set a price that makes you slightly uncomfortable. If it feels too easy to say out loud, it is probably too low. The right price filters out tire-kickers, funds reinvestment, and communicates that you take your own work seriously. Raise the price. Watch the quality of buyer improve.
