# Build Once, Earn Repeatedly

The freelancer trades time for money. The digital product creator breaks that equation.

## The Idea
There is a hard ceiling on service-based income. You can raise your rates and you can work more hours, but eventually you run out of both. The income is linear because the delivery is linear. Every dollar earned requires an equivalent unit of time and effort.

Digital products break this structure. You build something once and it can be purchased indefinitely. A PDF guide, a template pack, an audio course, a notion system. The build cost is paid once. Every subsequent sale is near-pure margin.

This does not mean digital products are easy. The build requires real skill and the marketing requires real effort. But the economics are structurally different. You are no longer selling your time. You are selling packaged knowledge, and packaged knowledge does not clock out.

The more products you build, the more income streams you have running simultaneously. Each new product is not just revenue. It is also content, it is authority, and it is proof that you understand your audience well enough to solve their problems at scale.

The transition from service provider to product creator is not just a business model shift. It is a shift in how you think about your time, your knowledge, and your potential income ceiling.

## Why It Matters
One product generating $500 per month is not impressive in isolation. But five products each generating $500 per month is a $30,000 annual income stream that does not require your daily attention.

Stack the products. Stack the income.

## One Action
Identify one recurring question people ask you. That question is a product brief. Spend this week outlining the answer as a structured guide or template.
