# The Bottleneck Is Almost Never the Work

You keep saying you need more time to work on your thing. The real delay is a decision you haven't made yet.

## The Idea

Most creators think their bottleneck is execution. They need to write more, build more, post more. So they try to carve out time, protect blocks in their calendar, and stay up late to ship.

But when you trace back the actual reason nothing is moving, it's rarely the work. It's a choice that hasn't been made. A commitment that hasn't been declared. A fear that's been dressed up as "not ready yet."

Not ready to launch? The bottleneck is a decision about whether the product is good enough. Not growing the audience? The bottleneck is a decision about what the channel strategy is. Revenue stuck? The bottleneck is a decision about pricing, offers, or who you're actually selling to.

The work can't start until the decision is made. And most people procrastinate on decisions because decisions create accountability. If you decide, you can be wrong. If you never decide, you can always say you were still figuring it out.

The cost of an unclear decision compounds over time. Every week you spend "almost ready" is a week of data, feedback, and revenue you never get. You learn faster by shipping than by planning.

Make the decision. Write it down. Then do the work that follows from it.

## Why It Matters

Bottleneck clarity is a superpower. When you know that execution is not the problem, you stop wasting energy optimizing the wrong thing. One clear decision made today is worth more than a month of vague effort.

## One Action

Name one thing that has been "almost ready" for more than two weeks. Write down the single decision that would unblock it. Make that decision today. Imperfect and shipped beats perfect and waiting.
