# Issue 2437: The One Metric That Matters

**The One Thing:** Pick one number that tells you if your business is working. Ignore everything else until that number moves.

---

Most solopreneurs track too many things and change nothing. Vanity metrics, engagement rates, follower counts, open rates. They watch them obsessively and make zero decisions from the data. The problem is not too little data. It's no single number they're trying to move.

## What Counts as a Real Metric

A real metric connects directly to revenue or to the thing that precedes revenue. Email list size matters if you sell through email. Conversion rate matters if you have traffic. Revenue per subscriber matters if you're optimizing monetization. Pick the one number that, if it improved, would materially change your outcome.

For most people starting out, the only number that matters is: how many people have heard my offer this week? That's it. Not likes. Not impressions. Not profile visits. Offer reach.

## Vanity vs. Traction

Impressions tell you your content was visible. They do not tell you if anyone cared enough to do anything. Comments and saves tell you something resonated. Email sign-ups tell you someone trusted you enough to invite you into their inbox. Revenue tells you someone trusted you enough to pay.

Each of these is a step closer to the metric that matters. Track the one closest to money and work backward from there.

## The Weekly Scoreboard

At the end of each week, write down your one number. If it went up, understand why. If it went down, understand why. Do more of what moved it up. Stop doing what didn't move it. This loop is the entire science of building a business.

---

**This Week's Action:** Choose one metric to track for the next 30 days. Write it down. Track it every Friday. Make one change based on the trend.
