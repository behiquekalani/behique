# The Business Model Most Creators Overlook

Everyone chases the course launch. Almost nobody builds the thing that generates revenue every single month without launching anything.

## Recurring Revenue Changes Everything
A single purchase is an event. Recurring revenue is a foundation. The creator with 200 subscribers paying $29 per month has more business stability than the one who launches to 10,000 followers twice a year. The math is simple. The psychology is what trips people up.

Building a subscription requires you to deliver consistent value every month. That constraint is a forcing function for quality. You can't hide behind a one-time launch. You have to show up.

## What You Can Charge For Monthly
Access to a community with real engagement. A software tool that solves a specific problem. Ongoing templates, resources, or curated research. Coaching calls on a recurring cadence. Any of these can be productized into a monthly subscription starting at $9 and scaling as value increases.

The mistake is trying to build the premium version first. Start simple. Charge for the thing you're already doing. Make it official.

## The Hybrid Model
The most durable creator businesses mix one-time products with recurring revenue. Products bring in bursts of cash. Subscriptions provide the floor. Services on top handle the high-end custom work. Together they create a business that can absorb slow months without panic.

Don't pick one model. Stack them in the right order.

## This Week's Action
Write down what you already do monthly that delivers value to someone. A newsletter, a research report, a template pack, a community thread. That's your subscription product in rough form. Price it and describe it in two sentences.
