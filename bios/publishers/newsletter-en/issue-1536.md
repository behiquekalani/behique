# Time Design: Why Your Schedule Is Your Strategy

Show me how you spend your time and I'll tell you what you actually value. Not what you say you value. What you value.

## The Default Schedule Lies
Most people have a schedule that was built by their obligations and other people's priorities. Meetings got placed where they were convenient for others. Emails get checked when they arrive. The day gets eaten by the reactive before the proactive ever has a chance.

Designing your time means starting from zero. What would the ideal day look like if you were in charge? Then reverse-engineering your current schedule toward that vision. Incrementally. Each week, reclaim one hour.

## Time Blocks as Commitments
The difference between "I'll write this week" and "I write Monday, Wednesday, Friday from 7 to 9am" is the difference between a wish and a commitment. Time blocks are appointments with yourself. They deserve the same respect as appointments with other people.

When you block time, you also need to block what fills that time. Vague blocks get filled with vague activities. "Write newsletter" is a block. "Check things" is not.

## The Energy Audit
Not all hours are equal. Map your energy across the day. Where are you sharp? Where do you fade? Schedule creative and strategic work in your peak energy windows. Schedule administrative and communication tasks in the troughs.

This single adjustment, matching task type to energy level, can double your useful output without working more hours.

## This Week's Action
Do a time audit. Track every 30-minute block for three days. Categorize each as creative, administrative, reactive, or social. Then identify the single biggest time drain and cut it by 50%.
