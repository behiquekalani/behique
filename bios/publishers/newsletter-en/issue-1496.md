# Content Strategy Is Just Frequency Plus Focus

Strip away all the jargon and a content strategy is two variables: how often you publish, and what you publish about. Most creators fail at one or both.

## The Frequency Problem

Inconsistency is the silent killer of creator momentum. One great post a month gets less compounding than four decent posts a week. The algorithm rewards consistency, but more importantly, your own skill rewards it. You get better faster when you are publishing more often. The feedback loop tightens.

The trap is waiting until you have something great to say. You will not know what great sounds like for your audience until you have published enough to see what resonates.

## The Focus Problem

Posting about everything is posting about nothing. Your audience follows you for a reason. When you drift from that reason without warning, you lose people who were there for the original topic. Focus does not mean repetition. It means staying within a domain your audience understands and trusts you to own.

You can have variety within a focus. The danger is variety outside of it.

## This Week's Action
Look at your last 10 posts. Could a stranger identify your core topic from them? If not, you have a focus problem. Write your next three posts on the same topic.
