# Issue 1889: Speed Is a Skill You Can Practice

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people think they move slowly because they're being thorough. In most cases they're being afraid. Thoroughness has a natural limit. After that limit, extra time is not producing better decisions. It's producing more comfort with the idea of eventually deciding.

Speed compounds. The person who ships ten things while you're still refining one has ten data points, ten lessons, and ten chances for something to break through. You have one highly polished attempt with no market signal. The asymmetry is brutal. Perfectionism is not a quality standard. It is a fear response dressed up as professionalism.

The way to get faster is to practice deciding with incomplete information. Set a time limit. Make the call. Observe the outcome. Adjust. The muscle builds through repetition, not through waiting until you're ready. You will never feel ready. Ship anyway.
