# The Mental Model Behind Every Good Decision

You do not need more information. You need better filters.

## The Idea
A mental model is a simplified representation of how something works. The right mental model lets you cut through ambiguity and make decisions faster without sacrificing quality. Most high-output people are not smarter than average. They have better frameworks.

One of the most useful is inversion. Instead of asking "how do I succeed at this," ask "what would guarantee failure?" Then avoid those things. This sounds simple. It is remarkably effective because the human brain is better at recognizing what is wrong than constructing what is right.

Another is second-order thinking. Most people think one step ahead. They consider the immediate outcome of an action. Second-order thinkers ask what happens next, and then what happens after that. Decisions that look good in the short term often look terrible from three steps out. Decisions that look costly now sometimes compound into massive advantages.

First principles thinking is another. Strip away every assumption about how something should be done and rebuild from the ground up. This is uncomfortable and slow, but it produces strategies that genuinely differ from the competition.

You do not need all three all the time. Picking one and applying it deliberately is enough to separate your decisions from default thinking.

## Why It Matters
Default thinking produces default results. The builder who has internalized two or three solid mental models will consistently outperform someone relying on intuition or trends alone.

Models are tools. Collect the ones that fit your work and use them constantly.

## One Action
Apply inversion to your biggest current project. Write down five things that would guarantee it fails. Now check: are you currently doing any of them?
