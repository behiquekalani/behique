# Decision Fatigue Is a Revenue Problem

The more decisions you have to make before noon, the worse your judgment is by the time the important decisions arrive. This is not philosophy. It is cognitive resource management.

## Where the Drain Happens

Most solopreneurs start the day with inbox, messages, and feed scrolling. They spend their freshest hours reacting to other people's priorities. By the time they get to the work that actually moves revenue, they are already depleted. Then they wonder why the creative work feels hard.

The morning is the most valuable cognitive resource you have. Every notification you check before doing focused work is spending that resource on someone else's agenda.

## Reduce the Load

The goal is not to make no decisions. It is to reserve your best decision-making capacity for the work that matters. Standard operating procedures, automated tools, pre-committed calendars, and pre-decided workflows all reduce the decision load without sacrificing control.

What can you decide once so you never have to decide again? What can be automated? What can be delegated? Each answer gives you a little more of your best thinking back.

## This Week's Action
Identify the three decisions you make every day that do not need to be made fresh each time. Write a default for each. Apply it starting tomorrow.
