# Sell Before You Build

The most expensive mistake in product creation is building something no one asked for.

## The Idea

The traditional product development model goes: idea, build, launch, hope. Most products fail at the hope stage because there was never any validation before the build stage. You spend months creating something, then find out the market does not care.

The smarter model flips two steps: idea, sell, build, deliver. You describe the product, put an offer in front of people, and measure whether they will actually pay. Not whether they say they like it, but whether they put money down.

This is not about being unethical or promising things you cannot deliver. It is about testing the idea before investing the time. A pre-sale with a clear delivery timeline is honest. It is also the fastest way to find out if you have a real product or just a real idea.

The psychological benefit is also significant. When you have customers before you finish building, you have an obligation. Obligation produces output. The blank page with no deadline never gets filled. The blank page with five people waiting for the file gets filled by Thursday.

## Why It Matters

Time is the only resource you cannot recover. Testing before building is the most respectful thing you can do with your own time. One pre-sale attempt takes a day. One failed launch after three months of building costs you everything.

## One Action

Write a one-paragraph description of a product you want to create. Post it to your audience or send it to ten people who fit your target. Ask if they would pay for it. Use their responses to decide whether to build.
