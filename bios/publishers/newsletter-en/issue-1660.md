# Consistency Beats Intensity Every Time

Bursts of extreme effort feel heroic. They produce dramatic short-term output. They also produce burnout, inconsistency, and the kind of recovery periods that wipe out whatever you gained.

## The Intensity Fantasy
The 18-hour day is celebrated in certain builder circles. So is the all-nighter, the sprint, the crunch. These moments exist and sometimes they're necessary. But as a default operating mode, intensity is a losing strategy.

High-intensity work requires proportional recovery. Most people underestimate the recovery required and overestimate their ability to sustain the pace. The result is cycles of heroic output followed by weeks of low productivity.

## What Consistency Actually Builds
Two hours of focused work every day for a year is 730 hours. Most people never come close to that. They do 10-hour days occasionally and one-hour days the rest of the time. The math doesn't work out.

Consistency builds skill because it keeps you close to the work. The insights come not from the marathon sessions but from showing up repeatedly over time. The compounding is in the showing up.

## Designing for Sustainability
The question isn't "how hard can I work?" It's "how hard can I work indefinitely?" Those are different questions with very different answers. The sustainable pace is the fastest pace.

A 50% effort maintained for two years outperforms a 100% effort maintained for three months.

## This Week's Action
Pick one creative or business activity you want to build as a core practice. Commit to 45 minutes on it every day for the next 30 days. No exceptions. No heroics. Just consistent showing up.
