# Issue 2593: The Real Reason People Do Not Finish What They Start

**The Idea:** Most unfinished projects are not abandoned because people are lazy. They are abandoned because the feedback loop stopped.

When you start something new, the gap between effort and evidence of progress is narrow. You build the first version, it works, people respond, you feel momentum. Then the loop extends. The work becomes harder, the results take longer to show up, and the dopamine that kept you going disappears.

Without a system for generating internal feedback, most people default to seeking external validation. They post about what they are building and wait for reactions. When reactions are small, they interpret it as the project not mattering. They pivot or quit.

The fix is creating your own feedback loop. Metrics you check daily that are within your control. Words written. Subscribers gained. Products finished. Not likes, not follower counts, not revenue projections.

You also need a completion framework. The reason unfinished projects pile up is that people never defined "done." What does a finished version of this look like? What is the minimum that makes it shippable? Vague projects die. Defined projects ship.

**Why It Matters:** Shipping consistently is the skill that separates people who build reputations from people who have good ideas. Completion is a practice, not a trait. You can develop it with better systems.

**One Action:** Pick one unfinished project. Define in writing what the minimum shippable version looks like and set a date by which you will ship that version.
