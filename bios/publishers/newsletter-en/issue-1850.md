# Issue 1850: Networks Are Not Relationships
**One insight. No fluff.**

Networking culture turned human connection into a transaction. You collect contacts, track follower counts, and show up to events where everyone is simultaneously trying to extract value from everyone else. That process produces weak ties and a lot of business cards.

Real relationships are built differently. They start with genuine interest in a specific person, not in what they can do for you. They develop through repeated contact over time. They survive periods where nothing is being exchanged. That last part is the real test.

The practical move is to invest deeply in fewer people rather than shallowly in many. One person who trusts you completely is worth more than fifty who vaguely know your name. Depth is harder to build but impossible to replicate. It also compounds. The person who trusts you introduces you to the next person who trusts you, and the whole thing builds without effort.
