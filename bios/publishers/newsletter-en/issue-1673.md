# Specificity Is the Shortcut Nobody Takes

The more specific you are, the more magnetic you become to the exact person who needs you.

## Broad Is Invisible
When you try to speak to everyone, you speak to no one. Broad positioning is comfortable because it feels inclusive. It is actually just noise. The person who writes for "entrepreneurs" competes with millions. The person who writes for "early-stage founders trying to close their first B2B customer" competes with almost nobody.

The narrower your focus, the clearer your message. The clearer your message, the faster people self-select into your audience.

## Specificity in Every Layer
Specificity is not just about your niche. It applies to every piece of content you create. A vague headline gets scrolled past. A specific one stops the person it is meant for. "How to be more productive" is noise. "Why I stopped using task lists and started using time blocks" is a signal. The second one is written for a real person with a real problem.

Train yourself to replace every general word with a specific one. Watch your engagement change.

## The Fear Behind Vagueness
Most people are vague because specificity feels like exclusion. If you write for a narrow audience, you will not reach the wide one. That is true. But the wide audience was never going to buy from you anyway. The narrow audience is actually looking for you. Specificity is not about reducing your audience. It is about finding the ones who will actually pay.

## This Week's Action
Rewrite your bio or your positioning statement to include a specific person, a specific problem, and a specific outcome. Remove all general words. Read it out loud. If it could describe anyone, rewrite it.
