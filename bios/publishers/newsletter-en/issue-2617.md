# Issue 2617: Content Strategy Is Distribution Strategy

**The Idea:** Most people think content strategy is about what to create. Topic selection. Formats. Calendars. That's the easy part. The hard part, and the part that actually determines growth, is distribution. Where does your content go after you publish it? Who sees it? How does it reach new people?

The creator who writes brilliant content and publishes it to 80 followers will grow slower than the creator who writes decent content and publishes it everywhere, repurposes aggressively, and collaborates with people who already have the audience they want.

Distribution means knowing which platform your target audience lives on and building there first. It means understanding the algorithm well enough to reach new people, not just existing followers. It means getting in front of audiences that are not yours yet, through collaborations, features, and partnerships.

The best content strategy for a new creator is 20% creation and 80% distribution. As your audience grows, that ratio shifts. But early on, distribution is survival. The audience does not come to you. You go to the audience.

A clear distribution plan asks: after I publish this, how many people outside my current audience will see it? If the answer is near zero, your strategy needs work.

**Why It Matters:** Content without distribution is a journal. It might be valuable to you, but it doesn't build a business. Growth requires getting in front of people who don't know you yet.

**One Action:** Pick one distribution channel you've been neglecting. Spend two hours this week getting one piece of content in front of a new audience through that channel.
