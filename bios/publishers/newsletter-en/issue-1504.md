# The One-Person Business Model That Works

You do not need a team. You need a clear model.

## Products, Not Services, Build Freedom

A service ties your income to your presence. A product does not. This is not a new idea. But most people never act on it because products feel harder to start.

They are not harder. They are just different. A service starts with a client. A product starts with an insight. The insight is cheaper to build on than the client is to find.

## The Stack That Scales

One lead magnet. One email list. One core product. One premium offer. That is a complete business. Four things. Most people are trying to manage forty.

Complexity kills solopreneur businesses. Not competition. Not the market. The builder gets distracted building and forgets to sell. Keep the model simple enough that you can hold it in your head while you are at the grocery store.

## The Distribution Problem Is Upstream

The product is rarely the problem. The distribution is. Your product does not sell itself because no one knows it exists. Solve the distribution problem before you build another product.

One channel, done well, is enough. Most successful solopreneurs built their entire business on one primary channel. They went deep before they went wide.

## This Week's Action

Draw your business model on a napkin. If it takes more than three boxes, simplify it. The goal is clarity, not comprehensiveness.
