# Issue 2521: The Smallest Viable Audience

**The Idea:** Everyone chases reach. Follower counts, impressions, monthly views. The metrics that feel like proof but rarely convert to income.

The real question is simpler: how few people do you actually need?

If your product costs $200 and you want to make $4,000 a month, you need 20 buyers. Not 20,000 followers. Twenty buyers. That number should change how you think about content, positioning, and outreach.

A solopreneur with 500 engaged readers who trust them completely will always out-earn the one with 50,000 passive scrollers. The difference is specificity. Specific problem. Specific person. Specific offer.

Most creators never reach this clarity because they optimize for growth instead of depth. They want to be bigger before they decide to be useful. But depth comes first. You build a business on people who feel like you wrote directly to them, not people who found you by accident and forgot about you by Tuesday.

Smallest viable audience is not a consolation prize. It is the actual path.

**Why It Matters:** Trying to reach everyone is the fastest way to reach no one. When you get precise about who you serve and what they need, you stop competing on volume and start competing on relevance. Relevance converts.

**One Action:** Write down the exact number of buyers you need this month to hit your income goal. Work backward from that number, not forward from your follower count.
