# Build in Public or Build Alone Forever

The builders who share the process attract the audience before the product is ready.

## What Building in Public Actually Is
Building in public is not showing off. It is documenting the real process, including the hard parts, the setbacks, and the decisions you are not sure about. The audience that forms around this kind of transparency is different from the one that forms around polished final products. They are invested in your success because they watched it happen.

That investment is not just emotional. It converts to customers, advocates, and referrals.

## The Accountability Effect
There is a secondary benefit nobody talks about enough. When you announce publicly what you are building, you become accountable to an audience. Most people work faster and more consistently when they have committed to an external timeline. Build in public and you essentially turn your audience into a soft accountability system.

The pressure is real and useful. Use it.

## What to Share and When
Share decisions, not just milestones. Share the reasoning behind a pivot. Share the result of an experiment, including the failed ones. Share the question you are currently trying to answer. People do not follow builders for the finished product. They follow for the thinking. The thinking is the content.

Every choice you make during the build is a piece of content that costs you nothing extra to produce.

## This Week's Action
Post one update about something you are currently building, even if it is unfinished. Include the current state, the next decision you need to make, and an honest question. Tag it as a build-in-public update.
