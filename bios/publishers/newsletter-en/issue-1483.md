# Price Is a Message, Not a Number

Your price tells people what to expect before they read a single word of your sales page.

## What Low Pricing Communicates

A $9 product says: this is a quick read, expect a framework, don't expect transformation. That is fine if it is true. The problem is when founders underprice work they intend to be taken seriously. They attract buyers who are not serious, get mediocre results, and wonder why their reputation is not growing. The price set the expectation. The expectation drove the experience.

Pricing low as a strategy to build volume makes sense only if you have the infrastructure to support volume. Most solopreneurs do not. They just underprice out of fear and call it strategy.

## Charge for the Outcome, Not the Hours

The person who pays $500 for a framework that saves them six months of confusion is getting a deal. The person who pays $15 for the same information formatted as a PDF is getting a PDF. Same knowledge, different framing, completely different result. Your price signals how much weight you put on the outcome. Own that signal.

## This Week's Action
Look at your lowest-priced product. Ask if the price matches the real value of the outcome. If it does not, raise it 30% this week.
