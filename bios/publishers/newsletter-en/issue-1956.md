# Issue 1956: Your Business Is a Hypothesis

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most businesses fail not because the founders lacked effort but because they treated their assumptions as facts. They assumed people wanted what they were building. They assumed the price was right. They assumed the channel would work. None of those assumptions got tested until after significant time and money had been committed.

A business is a set of hypotheses. The job of the early stage is not to build, it is to prove. Can you find ten people who will pay for this before you build it? Can you describe the problem so accurately that the person who has it says "exactly"? Can you acquire a customer before you have a product? If yes, you have something worth building. If no, you have a project.

This reframe changes everything about how you move. You stop building features and start running tests. You stop polishing and start asking. You stop waiting until it is ready and start putting incomplete versions in front of real people to watch what they do. The goal is to be wrong as fast and as cheaply as possible. Every wrong assumption you find early is an assumption you did not pay for later.
