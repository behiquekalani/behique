# Issue 2403: Digital Products Are Not Passive Income

**The One Thing:** A digital product requires active marketing, or it earns nothing regardless of how good it is.

---

The phrase "passive income" has done more damage to solopreneurs than almost any other idea. It implies you build something once and money flows forever. That's not how it works. What's passive is the delivery. The marketing is never passive.

Your PDF, your course, your template, your guide, none of it sells itself. Every sale comes from attention. And attention requires effort.

## The Distribution Problem

Most people spend 80% of their time building the product and 20% telling people about it. The ratio should be flipped, especially when you're starting out. The product needs to be good enough. Not perfect. Marketing needs to be constant. Always.

Think about it this way: you can write the best guide on productivity systems ever made. If the only place it exists is a Gumroad page with no traffic, it earns zero. Meanwhile, someone with a worse guide and a newsletter of 5,000 people earns $3,000 in a weekend because they have distribution.

Distribution is the actual product. Everything else is the thing distribution sells.

## Build the Shelf

One product is a lottery ticket. Five products is a catalog. Ten products is a store. When someone buys from you and loves it, they should immediately see four other things they want. That upsell moment is free. You already have their trust.

Think in categories. One product for beginners. One for intermediate. One for systems. One for productivity. One for mindset. The buyer who loved your productivity template is a natural customer for your content calendar.

Build the shelf, then fill it over time.

## Price for Profit, Not Comfort

$9 products require hundreds of sales to matter. $97 products require dozens. $297 products require a handful. Pick your pricing based on the business you want to run.
