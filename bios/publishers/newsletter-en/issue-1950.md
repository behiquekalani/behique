# Issue 1950: The Value of Being Hard to Understand

**One insight. No fluff.**

Clarity is a virtue. But there is a version of clarity that flattens you into something simple enough for a stranger to dismiss in five seconds. The most interesting people are not fully legible. They hold contradictions. They resist the easy category. That resistance is not a communication failure. It is what makes them worth paying attention to.

Your work does not need to be immediately understood to be immediately felt. Some of the most resonant writing, art, and products are the ones where the audience thinks: I am not sure what this is, but I know I need it. That pull is created by depth, not simplicity. It comes from the creator having thought about something long enough that the thinking itself becomes visible in the work.

Do not sand down the edges of your ideas to make them easier to share. The sharp edges are what catches people. Make your work clear enough to enter, but deep enough to stay in. If someone fully understands your work on first contact, you may not have put enough of yourself into it yet.
