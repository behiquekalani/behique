# One Platform First

Spreading across five platforms before mastering one is not a strategy. It's dilution.

## The Idea

The creator economy rewards depth before breadth. The people who grow fastest don't start everywhere. They start somewhere specific, develop real signal about what works, and then expand from a position of strength.

When you're on five platforms simultaneously with no traction on any of them, you have no feedback loop. You don't know what content works because results are thin across all channels. You feel busy but you're not compounding.

One platform done well teaches you more than five platforms done poorly. You learn the format. You learn the audience. You learn what kind of content they engage with and what they ignore. That learning is real capital. You can take it to the next platform.

The rule is simple: stay on one platform until you have consistent traction. Consistent means 90 days of publishing without results being the decision. At 90 days you have enough data to evaluate. Before that, you don't.

Most people quit at day 30 because it "isn't working" and move to another platform. Then they quit that one at day 30. The cycle repeats. They spend a year being a beginner on five platforms instead of becoming intermediate on one.

Pick the one where your audience actually lives. Show up there first. Build something real before you add complexity.

## Why It Matters

Shallow presence everywhere creates no momentum. One strong platform becomes the engine for everything else. The newsletter, the product sales, the community, the referrals. All of it flows from one place where people actually trust you.

## One Action

Write down your top three platforms. Circle the one where your best potential buyers spend the most time. Pause activity on the other two for 30 days. Go deep on the one that matters.
