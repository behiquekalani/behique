# Issue 1944: The Trap of Optimizing Too Early

**One insight. No fluff.**

There is a stage in every project where the builder stops building and starts optimizing. The product is not finished, the audience is not built, the revenue does not exist yet. But they are A/B testing button colors. This is not productivity. It is sophisticated avoidance dressed up as craft.

Optimization before validation is a category error. You are improving something you do not yet know people want. Every hour spent refining a system that has not proven itself is an hour not spent proving it. The feedback loop you need requires something to exist in the world, not something to exist perfectly in your head.

Ship the ugly version. Get ten people to use it. Let them tell you what is broken. The things they complain about are the things worth fixing. The things they do not mention are probably not problems. Build the habit of validation before optimization and you will waste years less time building things that do not need to exist.
