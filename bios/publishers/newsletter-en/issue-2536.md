# Issue 2536: What a Personal Monopoly Actually Means

**The Idea:** A personal monopoly is not about being the only person who can do what you do. At some level of abstraction, there are always others who do similar things.

A personal monopoly is about being the only person who does it the way you do it, from where you sit, with your particular story and perspective.

It is the intersection of your specific knowledge, your personality, your aesthetic, and your chosen audience. No one else occupies that exact intersection because no one else is the same combination of all four variables.

The practical implication is that you stop competing and start creating your own category. When someone is looking for what you specifically offer, expressed the way you specifically express it, you are not in a competitive pool. You are the only option.

Getting there requires clarity on all four elements. What do you know that others do not from experience. How do you think and communicate distinctively. What does your work look and feel like. Who specifically are you building it for.

Most creators spend their energy on the what and neglect the other three. The what is where competition lives. The intersection of all four is where the monopoly forms.

You cannot build a personal monopoly by copying someone else's approach and adding your face to it. You build it by going deeper into what makes your perspective genuinely distinct.

**Why It Matters:** Competing in a crowded space is exhausting and rarely profitable. Building a personal monopoly means operating where you have no competition because no one else can be you. That is the most defensible position in an online business.

**One Action:** Write down what makes your approach to your topic different from the three most visible people in your space. If you cannot name three clear differences, that is the work to do this week.
