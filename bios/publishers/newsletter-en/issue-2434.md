# Issue 2434: Systems for Thinking, Not Just Working

**The One Thing:** Your thinking process is a system too. Design it like you design everything else.

---

Most people design their work systems but leave their thinking process to chance. They have project management tools and calendars but no structured way to generate ideas, make decisions, or process what they're learning. The thinking side of the business is left to random inspiration.

## The Idea Capture Problem

Good ideas appear at bad times. In the shower. On a walk. Right before sleep. Without a system to catch them, they disappear. The best builders have a single place where every idea lands. Not organized. Not filtered. Just captured.

Review that inbox once a week. What's worth keeping? What connects to something you're already building? The value isn't in every idea. It's in the one that keeps showing up.

## The Decision Framework

Most decisions don't need deep analysis. They need a framework. For low-stakes choices, use a simple test: which option aligns with where I want to be in one year? For high-stakes choices, add inversion. Which option has the worst possible failure mode?

Write down your decisions before you make them. Write down why. Review them three months later. This builds a track record of your own thinking that you can learn from.

## Weekly Review as Thinking Maintenance

A weekly review is not just a task management check-in. It is a thinking maintenance session. What did I learn? What surprised me? What should I do differently? These questions take ten minutes. The clarity they produce is worth hours of confused execution later.

---

**This Week's Action:** Set a 10-minute weekly review for the same day each week. Ask three questions: what worked, what didn't, what's the most important thing next week.
