# Issue 2622: Specific Knowledge Is Not a Niche

**The Idea:** People confuse specific knowledge with niche selection. They are not the same thing.

A niche is a market category. Specific knowledge is what you know that almost nobody else knows in the same combination. Niche is external. Specific knowledge is internal.

You can pick a niche and still be replaceable. If anyone else with the same credential could do your job, you have a niche but no specific knowledge. Specific knowledge is what makes you the only person who could have built that exact thing, written that exact piece, or solved that exact problem.

It often comes from the edges of your experience. The years you spent doing something most people quit. The obsession you had that embarrassed you. The field you studied and the separate field you applied it to. The combination is what matters. Nobody else walked the same path in the same order with the same context.

The people who build real leverage are not the ones who chose the right niche. They are the ones who figured out what they actually know and then found the people who need it.

**Why It Matters:** Niche selection is a content strategy. Specific knowledge is a moat. One gets you traffic. The other gets you indefensible positioning.

**One Action:** List three things you know that most people in your audience do not. Find the intersection. That intersection is the beginning of your specific knowledge.
