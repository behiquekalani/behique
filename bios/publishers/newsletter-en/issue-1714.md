# Issue 1714: The Reader You Are Writing For

**One insight. No fluff.**

The best writing is written for one specific person. Not a demographic, not an audience segment, not a persona built from survey data. One person whose problem you understand well enough to describe it better than they can themselves. That specificity is what makes writing feel personal even when thousands of people read it.

Most people write for everyone, which means they write for no one. They hedge their claims, soften their opinions, and avoid anything that might exclude someone. The result is writing that feels safe and forgettable. The writing that actually builds an audience is the writing that makes a specific kind of person think the writer understands them completely.

Think about the person whose problem you solved before you knew anyone would pay for the solution. Write for them. Not for the imaginary reader who will be offended by an opinion. For the real person whose situation you know. Specificity in writing is not a limitation. It is the mechanism by which the writing finds the right people and keeps them.
