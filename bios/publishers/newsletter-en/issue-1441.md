# You Don't Need More Ideas. You Need a System.

Most builders have too many ideas and too little output. The gap is not creativity. It is infrastructure.

## The Idea Debt Problem

Every idea you collect without executing becomes debt. It sits in your notes app, your voice memos, your back-of-napkin sketches. Debt compounds. The pile grows. The guilt follows.

The solution is not to stop generating ideas. It is to build a triage system that forces every idea into one of three buckets: do now, schedule, or delete. No middle ground. The middle is where ambition goes to rot.

## What a System Actually Looks Like

A working system is not complicated. It is a single place where ideas land, a weekly review where you assign them or kill them, and a daily output target that is embarrassingly small. One page. One post. One product feature.

Small targets done consistently beat large ambitions done sporadically. Every time. The compounding is quiet, but it shows up.

## Execution Is the Filter

Most people treat ideas as the scarce resource. They are not. Execution is. When you treat execution as the filter, your idea list shrinks to what actually matters.

The builders shipping consistently are not more creative. They are more systematic. They built the infrastructure first and let the ideas fill it.

## This Week's Action

Pick one idea from your backlog that has been sitting for more than two weeks. Either commit to a ship date this week or delete it permanently. No compromising. Moving on with clarity beats holding on with guilt.
