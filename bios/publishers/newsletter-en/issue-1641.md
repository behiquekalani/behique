# The Inner Game Nobody Talks About

Most creators burn out not from overwork. They burn out from building someone else's version of their life.

## The Performance You're Running
Every time you post, write, or ship, there's a quiet question underneath it: is this me, or is this the character I think will get results? That split is expensive. You can't sustain a performance indefinitely. The mask gets heavy.

The creators who last aren't the ones with the best strategy. They're the ones who stopped pretending their work was separate from who they are. The work became the person. That's when it stops feeling like effort.

## What Alignment Actually Means
Alignment isn't a buzzword. It's the feeling when you sit down to work and there's no internal resistance. No negotiation. Just motion. Most people never get there because they picked their niche, their voice, and their goals based on what they saw working for others.

You can't reverse-engineer someone else's alignment. You can only find your own by paying attention to when you feel sharp versus when you feel hollow.

## The Work That Survives
The work that compounds over years is almost always the work that felt most personal at the time. Not the polished piece built for clicks. The honest one you almost didn't publish.

That's not an accident. Specificity is the engine of connection. Generic is forgettable. Personal is permanent.

## This Week's Action
Write one piece of content this week that you've been avoiding because it feels too personal. Publish it without editing out the truth. Notice what happens.
