# Decisions That Compound

Not all decisions are equal. Some are reversible. Some change everything.

## The Category Error in Decision-Making

Most people treat all decisions the same. Whether to check email now or later. Whether to quit their job. Whether to write today. These are not the same category of decision. Treating them equally wastes the mental energy that should go to the ones that actually matter.

Trivial decisions should be automated or eliminated. Important decisions should get disproportionate time and energy. Most people do it backwards.

## Asymmetric Decisions

Look for decisions where the upside is large and the downside is bounded. Starting a newsletter: the upside is potentially thousands of readers. The downside is a few hours spent. That is asymmetric. Take more of those.

Decisions where the downside is large and the upside is small are the ones to avoid. Optimizing the logo before you have a product. Redesigning the website before you have traffic. Small upside. Real cost of time and attention.

## The Long Game

Every decision you make is also a vote for the kind of person you are becoming. Choosing to publish today is not just about today's post. It is a vote for the identity of someone who publishes consistently.

The compounding is in the identity, not just the outcomes. Make more decisions that vote for the person you want to become.

## This Week's Action

Find one decision you have been postponing because it feels too big. Break it into its smallest possible first step. Take that step today. The momentum from one decisive action is worth more than weeks of planning.
