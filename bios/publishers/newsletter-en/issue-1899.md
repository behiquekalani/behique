# Issue 1899: When to Stop Optimizing and Start Shipping

**One insight. No fluff.**

Optimization is the last refuge of the person who is afraid to ship. You can always find one more thing to improve. One more edge case to handle. One more word to tighten. The product is never quite ready and somehow never will be. This is not a craft standard. It is a fear response with a professional-sounding name.

The version that exists in the world does more than the perfect version that doesn't. It generates feedback, creates customers, builds your reputation, and gives you real information about what to fix next. The unreleased version generates nothing except the temporary comfort of not having been judged yet.

Set a specific ship condition: what does "done enough" look like? Write it down before you start building so you can't move the goalposts later. When you hit that condition, ship it. Optimization after launch is improvement. Optimization before launch is usually avoidance.
