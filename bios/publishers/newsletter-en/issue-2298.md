# Niche Down or Get Lost

The market does not reward the generalist. It rewards the specialist who speaks directly to someone.

## The Idea

"I help people improve their lives" is not a niche. It is a category so broad that no one feels spoken to. The goal of positioning is to make a specific person read your work and think, "this is for me." That cannot happen when you are speaking to everyone.

Niche selection is not about limiting yourself. It is about concentrating signal. When you speak to a specific person with a specific problem at a specific moment in their journey, your content lands differently. It does not just resonate. It converts.

The formula is simple. Who is your person? What is their exact problem? What stage are they in? A creator who helps beginner freelancers get their first three clients is not the same as one who helps seasoned freelancers scale to six figures. Both are valid. Both need entirely different content, products, and messaging. Pretending you serve both waters down everything.

The fear is that going narrow means fewer potential buyers. The reality is the opposite. The more specific your positioning, the more certain buyers feel that you are the right person to help them. Specificity is trust at scale.

## Why It Matters

Broad positioning creates indifference. Specific positioning creates resonance. When someone feels genuinely seen by your content, they follow, subscribe, and buy. The narrower your niche, the stronger the signal. Strong signal attracts the right people faster than broad signal attracts everyone weakly.

## One Action

Write a single sentence that describes exactly who you help, with what specific problem, and what result they get. If you cannot do it in one sentence, your positioning needs work. Return to this sentence every time you create content or design an offer.
