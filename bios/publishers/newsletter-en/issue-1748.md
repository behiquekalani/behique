# Issue 1748: Creativity Is a Practice, Not a Mood

**One insight. No fluff.**

Waiting for inspiration is a luxury that professionals cannot afford. Amateurs create when they feel like it. Professionals create on schedule and trust that the feeling will follow. It usually does, but only after you have already started.

The mind produces ideas through action, not preparation. You cannot think your way to a good idea while sitting still. You find the idea by writing the bad version first, shipping the rough draft, building the ugly prototype. The good version emerges from contact with the real.

Creativity is not a gift distributed unevenly. It is a muscle that atrophies without use and strengthens with repetition. Show up daily. The quality is downstream of the volume.
