# Issue 1976: When Patience Is Just Avoidance

**One insight. No fluff.**

Patience is a virtue until it becomes a strategy for not deciding. There is a version of patience that is active: you are working, learning, accumulating, and the timing is genuinely not right yet. And there is a version that is passive: you are waiting for the situation to improve on its own so you do not have to take a risk.

Most people cannot tell which version they are practicing. They use the vocabulary of patience to describe what is functionally avoidance. "I am waiting for the right moment" often means "I am afraid to act before certainty arrives." The moment never comes because the certainty never comes. The wait becomes permanent.

The test is simple. Ask what specifically changes if you wait three more months. If you can name something concrete that improves the odds, the wait is strategic. If the answer is vague, "things will be clearer," or absent, you are stalling. Real patience is tactical. It has a condition that triggers action. Avoidance masquerading as patience has no condition. It just continues.
