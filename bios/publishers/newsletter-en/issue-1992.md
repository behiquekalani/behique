# Issue 1992: Opinions Are an Asset

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people share information. Facts, links, summaries, things that already exist somewhere and now exist in your feed too. Information is cheap because it is abundant. The thing that is actually scarce is interpretation. Someone who has looked at what everyone else has looked at and arrived at a different, clearly argued, specific conclusion. That is rare. That is what people actually pay attention to.

Having opinions is not the same as being contrarian. Contrarianism is performing disagreement for attention. An opinion is a conclusion you arrived at through thought, which you will defend with reasoning, and which you are willing to revise if better reasoning appears. The difference is that contrarianism is about identity and opinions are about truth-seeking. People can tell the difference.

The practical point is this. If you want to be worth reading, stop being a relay station for other people's information and start being a place where ideas get processed. Take a thing you know well, something you have experience with directly, and write what you actually think about it. Not what you were told to think. Not what is safe to think. What you actually think, with the reasoning behind it. That is an asset. Information without interpretation is noise.
