# Issue 2562: Specific Knowledge Is Not a Niche

**The Idea:** People confuse niche with specific knowledge. A niche is a category. Specific knowledge is what only you can teach, because it came from your own path, your failures, your obsessions.

"Social media marketing" is a niche. "How I rebuilt my freelance income after losing my biggest client in 72 hours" is specific knowledge. One is a commodity. The other is irreplaceable.

Specific knowledge is built by doing the thing, not by studying the thing. The person who actually ran five product launches knows something no course can teach. They know how it felt to have zero sales on day one, how they adjusted, and what actually worked. That texture is the value.

Your job is not to find an untapped niche. Your job is to articulate what you already know at a level of specificity that makes people feel you are reading their mind. When your writing sounds like it could only come from you, you have found your specific knowledge.

**Why It Matters:** Commoditized content is skipped. Specific, experience-born knowledge stops people mid-scroll. It also becomes impossible to replicate, which means you stop competing on price and start competing on clarity.

**One Action:** Write one paragraph today about a failure you had in your work, what you learned, and what you would do differently. That paragraph is the seed of your specific knowledge.
