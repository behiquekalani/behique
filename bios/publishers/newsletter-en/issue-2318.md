# The Offer Stack: How One-Person Businesses Scale Revenue

One offer is a start. A stack of offers at different price points is a business.

## The Idea

The mistake most solopreneurs make is having one product and wondering why revenue is inconsistent. One product means one price point. One price point means you are accessible to exactly one type of buyer. Everyone else, whether they want more or want to start smaller, has no place to go.

An offer stack is a tiered set of products or services that serve the same audience at different levels of depth and investment. The entry product is low-cost and low-commitment. It brings in first-time buyers and builds trust. The mid-tier product solves the problem more completely and delivers more transformation. The premium product offers the deepest access, most personalization, or most comprehensive outcome.

A buyer who trusts you after a $17 guide is a warm lead for a $97 course. A buyer who completes the course and sees results is a natural fit for a $500 consulting engagement. The relationship deepens. The revenue per customer grows. The acquisition cost per customer drops because you are monetizing people you already have, not only hunting for new ones.

The stack also creates optionality. When someone can't afford your premium offer, they have somewhere to start. When someone wants more than your entry product, they have somewhere to go. You meet buyers where they are instead of losing them because the fit wasn't exact.

## Why It Matters

Customer lifetime value is the metric that separates businesses from income sources. A customer who buys from you three times is worth more than three separate customers, because the acquisition cost for the second and third purchases is nearly zero.

Build the stack. Then serve people across it over time.

## One Action

Map your current offer stack, even if it only has one item. Identify the missing tier, either an entry product below your current lowest price or a premium product above your current highest. Define what that tier would include. Give yourself 30 days to build and list it.
