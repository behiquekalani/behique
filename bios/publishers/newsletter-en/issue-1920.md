# Issue 1920: Build for the Person You're Becoming

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most decisions are made by the person you are right now, optimized for your current constraints, preferences, and fears. This is reasonable. It's also a ceiling. If you only build what your current self can see, you'll only reach where your current self can imagine. The interesting outcomes live past the edge of your current imagination, which means they require you to make decisions on behalf of a person you haven't fully become yet.

This reframes how you evaluate opportunities. Not "does this make sense for who I am today?" but "does this make sense for who I'm trying to become?" A skill that feels unnecessary now might be foundational at the next level. A relationship that doesn't seem obviously valuable today might be the connection that changes everything in two years. The person you're becoming can see what you can't. Act from that perspective.

Write down who you intend to be in three years. Not what you'll have. Who you'll be. What you'll know, how you'll think, what you'll be capable of. Now ask whether your current decisions are consistent with that person's life. Most of the time, they aren't. Not because you're doing something wrong, but because you haven't made the future version real enough to act from. Make it real. Then let it make decisions for you.
