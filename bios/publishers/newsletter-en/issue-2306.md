# Sell the Outcome, Not the Process

Nobody buys a gym membership for the treadmill. They buy the version of themselves who fits in last year's jeans.

## The Idea

The most common mistake in positioning a product or service is describing what it is instead of what it does. Features, methods, and processes are internal information. Your buyer does not care about them. They care about the specific, concrete transformation they will experience if they use what you're selling.

This is not a copywriting trick. It is an alignment between what you're building and what actually motivates a human to pay for something. People do not buy things. They buy futures. They are paying to skip from where they are now to a specific better state. Your job is to name that future as clearly as possible.

The formula is simple: who they are now, plus what they want to avoid or achieve, plus what life looks like after they use your product. Every piece of your marketing is just filling in those blanks with honesty and precision.

When you nail this, something useful happens. The wrong buyers self-select out. The right buyers recognize themselves immediately and feel like you built this specifically for them. Conversion stops being a hard sell and starts being a recognition. The buyer feels found, not persuaded.

## Why It Matters

Weak positioning creates a hard sales job. Strong positioning creates an inevitable purchase. When someone reads your offer and thinks "this is exactly what I need," you didn't persuade them, you just showed them clearly what was already true.

Most creators undersell their work by describing it too accurately and not aspirationally enough. The product is real. The outcome is also real. Describe the outcome.

## One Action

Take your current offer and write two sentences: one that describes what it is, one that describes what someone's life looks like after they use it. Delete the first sentence from your public-facing copy. Keep the second. Test it for one week.
