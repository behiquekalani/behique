# What Most Creators Get Wrong About Niches

Going narrow does not shrink your audience. It grows it.

## The Idea

New creators fear the niche. They want to talk about everything: fitness, business, mindset, travel, tech. They think narrowing down means leaving money on the table.

The opposite is true.

A broad creator speaks to everyone and resonates with no one. A narrow creator speaks to a specific person and becomes indispensable to them. The specific person tells others like themselves. The audience self-replicates.

The math works differently than intuition suggests. If you try to reach one hundred million people with a generic message, you get a fraction of a percent. If you reach ten million people with a message built exactly for them, you capture a much larger share. Specificity improves conversion at every stage.

But the niche mistake is thinking it is only about topic. It is really about perspective. Two creators can both talk about solopreneurship. But one brings a philosophy degree and a meditation practice. The other brings a decade in corporate finance and a startup exit. Same topic, completely different audience. The perspective is the differentiation.

Your niche is not just what you talk about. It is how you see the world and why that worldview matters to your reader.

## Why It Matters

When your niche is clear, content creation gets faster. You know who you are writing for. You know what they struggle with. You know what they want to believe.

That clarity is the engine of resonance.

## One Action

Write down your niche in this format: "I help [specific person] do [specific thing] through [specific lens or method]." Keep refining until it is uncomfortable how specific it is.
