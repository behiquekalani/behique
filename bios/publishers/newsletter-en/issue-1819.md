# Issue 1819: The Invisible Tax of Context Switching

**One insight. No fluff.**

Every time you switch tasks, there's a cost that doesn't show up anywhere. Not in your calendar, not in your to-do list. It shows up in your quality of thinking. The work you do in the first ten minutes after switching tasks isn't the same quality as the work you do after ninety minutes of focused effort on one thing.

Attention isn't a switch you flip. It's a state you build.

Deep work produces things shallow work can't. Not because you try harder, but because you sustain focus long enough for non-obvious connections to form. That's where real insight comes from, not from more information, but from staying with a single problem long enough to see it from all sides.

Protect your deep work time like it's your most productive asset, because it is. Batch communication. Kill notifications during focused sessions. Fewer context switches in a day means more high-quality output in total. You don't need more time. You need longer uninterrupted stretches of the time you have.
