# Issue 1900: The Long Game Is the Only Game Worth Playing

**One insight. No fluff.**

Short-term thinking produces short-term results. Everyone knows this and almost no one actually operates by it. The person who chases the trend, copies what's working, and optimizes for this month's numbers will always be running behind whoever set the trend. Following is always slower than building something original.

The long game requires tolerating a long time of building without recognition. It means shipping things that don't get traction yet. It means developing a point of view before anyone cares about your point of view. It means doing the same work, in the same direction, long after the initial excitement has worn off. This is what most people are unwilling to do.

The good news is that the bar for long-game thinking is very low because almost nobody does it. If you can stay consistent in one direction for two years while others pivot every three months, you will be in a completely different position. The patience is the strategy.
