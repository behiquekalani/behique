# Issue 1952: The Problem with Waiting for Permission

**One insight. No fluff.**

Somewhere along the way, most people learned to wait for someone to tell them they are ready. A degree, a certification, a follower count, a certain number of years of experience. The credential becomes the permission slip. Without it, the assumption is that starting is presumptuous. That publishing before you are qualified is arrogant.

But the people granting those credentials rarely know more than you do about the specific thing you want to build. A university does not know your idea. An employer does not know your ceiling. The audience you want does not care about your qualifications. They care about whether the thing you made is useful to them. That judgment happens after you ship, not before.

Permission is a bottleneck you invented. The internet removed the gatekeepers decades ago and most people are still queuing at the gate. You do not need to be the best. You need to be specific, useful, and real. Start from where you are with what you have. The credential you are waiting for will not arrive before the work. It comes after.
