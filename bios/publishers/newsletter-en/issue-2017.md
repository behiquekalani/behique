# Issue 2017: Selling as Translation, Not Persuasion

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people who are uncomfortable with selling have a mental model of it as persuasion. Getting someone to do something they would not otherwise do. Convincing them. That model makes selling feel manipulative, which is why capable builders avoid it and then wonder why their work does not reach anyone.

The better model is translation. You have built something that solves a real problem. The buyer has that problem. Selling is the act of connecting those two facts in language the buyer understands. You are not creating desire. You are helping someone recognize that what they already want exists and that you made it. If you do not sell, you are not sparing them from persuasion. You are just withholding information they would benefit from having.

The failure mode of this model is when there is no real match. When the product does not solve the problem, or when the problem is not real, translation stops working and you have to fall back on pressure. That is where selling becomes uncomfortable. The fix is upstream: build something that solves something real for someone specific. Then selling is just accurate communication, and it requires very little aggression to work.
