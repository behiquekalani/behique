# Brand Partnerships That Don't Wreck Your Reputation

One bad sponsorship deal can undo a year of trust-building. The money is rarely worth it when the fit is wrong.

## The Trust Equation
Your audience follows you because they believe in your judgment. Every brand you associate with either reinforces that belief or chips away at it. There is no neutral. When you promote something you don't actually use or believe in, your audience can feel it. Not always immediately. But over time.

The creators who build lasting careers from partnerships treat them like hires. You wouldn't let just anyone join your team for money. Apply that same filter.

## What Good Fit Actually Looks Like
A brand worth partnering with should pass three questions. Do you use or believe in this product? Would you recommend it if there were no payment involved? Does it connect naturally to the problems your audience is trying to solve?

If the answer to all three is yes, the partnership will feel organic. The content will perform better. The audience trust stays intact.

## Structuring Deals That Last
Don't chase one-off deals. Push for long-term arrangements. Ongoing partnerships are easier to integrate, more valuable to the brand, and more credible to your audience. A six-month deal with one brand is worth more than six one-month deals with six different ones.

## This Week's Action
List every brand you've promoted or are considering in the next quarter. Apply the three questions above. Cut any that fail more than one. Reach out to the ones that pass and propose a longer-term arrangement instead of a single post.
