# Issue 1802: The Audience You Don't Have Yet

**One insight. No fluff.**

Everyone wants an audience before they build anything. They stall because they don't have followers, traction, or proof that anyone cares. So they wait. And while they wait, they fall further behind the person who just started posting.

The audience comes after the work. Not before.

You are not building for an existing crowd. You are creating a signal that attracts the people who will care once they find you. That signal doesn't exist until you make it. Waiting for permission to start is waiting for something that will never arrive.

Post the thing. Write the issue. Ship the product. Do it again. The consistency itself is the proof of concept. People don't follow potential. They follow momentum. So build it in public, even when nobody is watching. Especially then.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
