# Digital Products Are Not Passive. They Are Leveraged.

The "passive income" framing does more damage than good. It sets the wrong expectation and attracts the wrong effort.

## What You Are Actually Building

A digital product is not a vending machine you set up and forget. It is a system that does the heavy lifting once you have done the upfront work. You write the guide once. You build the course once. You record the framework once.

The distribution, the sales page, the delivery, all automated. But the creation was anything but passive. It was some of the most concentrated work you will ever do.

## The Leverage Is in Replication

One hour of focused creation can generate value for thousands of people simultaneously. That is the actual unlock. Not "doing nothing." Doing something valuable once and letting it replicate without your presence.

This mindset shift matters because it changes how you approach the work. You stop chasing quick content and start building things worth buying. The depth you put in determines the residual value you get out.

## Pricing Is Positioning

Most first-time product creators underprice. They think low price means low barrier means more sales. That logic collapses fast.

Low prices signal low confidence. They attract bargain hunters, not committed buyers. Price your product at what it would cost someone to learn that same thing through any other method, then price accordingly. Confidence in your price is confidence in your work.

## This Week's Action

If you have an existing digital product, raise the price by 20%. If you do not have one yet, write a one-paragraph description of the problem you solve and who you solve it for. That is your product brief.
