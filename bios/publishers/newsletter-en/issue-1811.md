# Issue 1811: Opinions Are a Product

**One insight. No fluff.**

Generic content disappears. Not because it's bad, but because it's interchangeable. When you say what everyone else is saying, there's no reason to follow you specifically. Your perspective is what makes you irreplaceable.

An opinion is not a hot take. It's a specific stance on a specific thing, backed by your own reasoning and experience.

Most creators are afraid to take positions. They hedge, qualify, and soften everything. They want to appeal to everyone, which means they connect with no one. The person willing to say "I think this widely accepted idea is wrong, and here's why" builds a following that a neutral content machine never could. Not because controversy is good, but because conviction is magnetic.

Your strongest piece of content is never a summary of what others have said. It's the thing you genuinely believe that most people haven't said clearly yet. Find that thing. Say it directly. Repeat it in many forms. That's a brand.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
