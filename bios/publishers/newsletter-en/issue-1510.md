# Focus Is a Competitive Advantage

Attention is the most contested resource in history. The person who protects theirs will outbuild everyone else.

## The Distraction Economy

Every platform, app, and notification is designed to steal your attention. Not partially. Completely. The goal is to keep you scrolling, not building.

Most people give their best hours to consumption and their worst hours to creation. The math does not work. You cannot build something significant on the hours that are left over after everything else takes its cut.

## What Deep Work Actually Means

It means four uninterrupted hours on one thing. No tabs. No phone. No switching. Most people have not experienced this in months.

The output from four hours of real focus is not four times better than one hour. It is ten times better. The compound returns on focused work are nonlinear. This is the thing productivity gurus understate.

## How to Protect Attention in a World That Attacks It

The defaults are set against you. You have to actively opt out. This means turning off notifications. It means not starting with social. It means having a clear answer to the question "what is the one thing I am doing today?" before the day starts.

Not three things. One. The others wait.

## This Week's Action

Identify the single biggest attention leak in your day. Is it your phone? Email? Multitasking? Eliminate it for one week. Track what you build in the time you recover.
