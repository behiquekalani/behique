# Issue 2422: The Content Pyramid Nobody Talks About

**The One Thing:** One long piece of content should generate at least ten shorter pieces. Stop starting from scratch every day.

---

Content creators burn out because they treat every post like a fresh project. That's not content creation. That's content suffering. The most productive creators online aren't creating more. They're repurposing smarter.

## Long to Short Always Wins

Start with one anchor piece. A long essay, a newsletter, a YouTube video, a podcast episode. Something with depth. Then strip it down. Pull the five best sentences for Twitter. Extract the main argument for a short video script. Turn a section into a carousel. That one deep piece becomes a week of content.

This is called the content pyramid. Long-form at the top. Short-form distributed below. Most people build the pyramid upside down and wonder why they run out of ideas every Tuesday.

## The Archive Is Your Asset

Every piece of content you produce is raw material for future content. An essay from six months ago is a thread waiting to happen. A thread from last year is a short video waiting to be filmed. Your archive is not dead. It's a library.

Search your old content before you start something new. Odds are you've already had the idea. You just need to say it in a different format.

## The 50/30/20 Rule

Fifty percent of your content should be educational. Thirty percent should be personal, opinion-based, or perspective-driven. Twenty percent should be promotional. This mix builds trust before it asks for anything.

---

**This Week's Action:** Take one old piece of content and break it into three shorter formats. Post them across three days.
