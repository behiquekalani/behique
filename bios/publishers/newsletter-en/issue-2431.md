# Issue 2431: Build in Public or Build in Private

**The One Thing:** Building in public earns trust before you launch. Building in private produces polish. Pick based on your stage.

---

There is no wrong answer here. Building in public and building in private both work. They serve different purposes at different stages. Most people pick one based on personality and never question whether it's the right choice for where they actually are.

## The Case for Public

Building in public creates an audience before you have a product. Every post about what you're building is a trust deposit. By the time you launch, some people are already bought in. They watched it get built. They gave feedback. They feel ownership.

This works best early in your journey or when you're entering a new niche. The transparency creates connection. The vulnerability creates relatability. The consistency creates trust.

## The Case for Private

Some projects get killed by early feedback. A rough prototype shown to the wrong audience gets criticized in ways that drain motivation before the idea has had time to develop. Not every idea survives public scrutiny at the concept stage.

Build privately when you need momentum before exposure. Get the first version done. Then bring it out. The feedback at that point is more useful because you have something real to iterate on.

## When to Switch

Start public when you need an audience. Start private when you need confidence. Switch to public once the core is built and you're ready for feedback that will actually improve the thing rather than kill it.

Both approaches end at the same place. A product in front of people. The path matters less than the destination.
