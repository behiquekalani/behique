# Issue 2383: Sell Before You Build

**The One Thing:** Validate demand with a sale before you spend a single hour building the product.

---

Most creators build first and hope. They spend weeks, sometimes months, crafting something nobody asked for. Then they wonder why it does not sell. The sequence is backwards. Selling before building is not a trick. It is the only honest test of whether your idea has legs.

## The Pre-Sale Is Not Dishonest

Pre-selling is a promise. You collect payment, then you deliver. This is how software companies raise Series A rounds. This is how book authors get advances. This is how smart solopreneurs avoid building ghost products. If someone gives you money for something that does not exist yet, you have proof. Without that proof, you have a guess.

## How to Validate in 48 Hours

Write a sales page. Not a polished one. A clear one. Describe the problem, describe the solution, describe who it is for. Set a price. Share it to your existing audience. If ten people pay within 48 hours, build it. If zero people pay, you learned something valuable for free. That lesson would have cost you months otherwise.

## The Rule That Changes Everything

Never spend more than one week on a product before someone pays for it. One week of effort is a reasonable bet. Six months is a gamble that rarely pays off. Build fast. Validate first. Refine after you know it sells.

---

**This Week's Action:** Write a one-paragraph description of your next product idea. Share it to five people and ask: "Would you pay $X for this?" Record every response.
