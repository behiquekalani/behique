# Creative Blocks Are Information, Not Failure

When you can't create, it's not laziness. It's a signal. The work is trying to tell you something.

## What the Block Is Usually About
Creative blocks almost always come from one of three places. Perfectionism disguised as preparation. Unclear direction on what the work should actually accomplish. Or genuine depletion that needs rest, not more effort.

None of these are fixed by forcing output. They're fixed by diagnosing which one you're dealing with.

## The Perfectionism Version
You have the idea. You know roughly what you want to say. But you won't start because it might not be good enough. This block is broken by lowering the bar on the first draft. Intentionally write badly. Give yourself permission to produce something ugly and revise it. The blank page is the only enemy. A bad draft is already progress.

## The Direction Version
Sometimes the block is honest. You don't know what you're making or why. That confusion is worth sitting with. Ask: who is this for? What do they need to know? What do I want them to do after? Answer those three questions and the block usually dissolves because now there's a real purpose to work toward.

## This Week's Action
The next time you sit down to create and nothing comes, write one sentence: "This piece is for [specific person] who needs to understand [specific thing] so they can [specific result]." Use that as your brief. Start from there.
