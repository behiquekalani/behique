# Issue 2393: How to Write a Product Description That Converts

**The One Thing:** Your product description should make the reader feel understood before it explains what the product is.

---

Most product descriptions start with features. Page count, format, delivery method, bonus included. Buyers do not care about features first. They care about whether you understand their problem first. Flip the order. Start with pain, then bridge to solution, then describe the product.

## The PAS Framework in Practice

Problem. Agitate. Solution. This is the oldest copywriting structure because it matches how humans make decisions. State the problem they recognize. Deepen the pain of living with that problem unsolved. Then present your product as the exit. The reader does not buy the product. They buy relief from the problem.

## Words That Kill Conversions

"Comprehensive." "In-depth." "Ultimate guide." These words are used so often they have become invisible. Instead, be specific. Not "comprehensive guide to email marketing" but "the 3-email sequence that converted 17% of my list on launch day." Specific beats general in every test, every market, every category.

## The Trust Signal You Are Missing

Add one sentence that tells the reader exactly who this is NOT for. "This is not for people who are still figuring out their niche." That sentence does two things: it builds trust by showing honesty, and it makes the target reader feel even more certain it is for them. Exclusion creates desire.

---

**This Week's Action:** Rewrite the description of one of your existing products using PAS. Start with the problem. Get specific. End with one sentence about who it is not for. Then test it for 30 days.
