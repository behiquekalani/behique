# Identity Before Strategy

You can have the best strategy in the world and still fail. If your identity does not match who the strategy requires you to be, you will self-sabotage every time you get close.

## The Hidden Block
Most people think their problem is tactical. Wrong funnel, wrong offer, wrong platform. But the real block is identity. They do not see themselves as someone who earns that much, publishes that often, or commands that price. So they unconsciously avoid the actions that would get them there.

The tactics are fine. The belief system is broken.

## How to Audit Your Identity
Ask yourself: what would a person who has what I want do this week? Then ask why you are not doing that. The gap between your answer and your actions is your identity gap. That gap does not close with more information. It closes with repeated action that contradicts the old story.

Start small. Do the thing the new version of you would do. Then do it again.

## The Compound Effect of Self-Concept
Every time you act in alignment with the identity you want, you build evidence. Evidence becomes belief. Belief becomes default behavior. This is how you become the type of person who builds a real business, not just someone who reads about it.

You cannot think your way into a new identity. You act your way in.

## This Week's Action
Write one sentence that describes who you are becoming. Post it somewhere visible. Read it before starting work each morning this week.
