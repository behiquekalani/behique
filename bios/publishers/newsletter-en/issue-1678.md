# Client Red Flags You Learn to See Early

The clients who waste your time and drain your energy all showed signs before you said yes.

## The Red Flag Inventory
Every difficult client relationship has a pattern if you look back honestly. They asked for a discount before you even sent a proposal. They questioned your process before understanding your results. They wanted to start without a contract. They moved slowly on decisions but urgently on complaints. These are not surprises. They are signals that arrived early and were ignored.

The faster you learn to read these signals, the less time you spend in work that costs more than it pays.

## Why You Ignore the Signs
You ignore red flags when you need the money or when the project sounds interesting or when you tell yourself you can manage it. Sometimes you can. More often, the difficult dynamic you sensed in the discovery call is exactly what shows up during the project. The client who questions your pricing will question your judgment. The client who is slow to sign will be slow to pay.

The discovery call is the data. Use it.

## Protecting Your Pipeline
You do not have to take every client. As your work gets better and your reputation builds, the right clients become more available. But even before that point, saying no to a bad fit protects your time for better opportunities. One difficult client can consume the energy you need to find three good ones.

The best clients refer more best clients. Build around them.

## This Week's Action
Write down two or three non-negotiable client criteria that, if violated, mean you decline the work. Put them in your discovery process so you are asking the right questions before you say yes.
