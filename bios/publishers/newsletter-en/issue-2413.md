# Issue 2413: Niche Down or Get Ignored

**The One Thing:** A specific offer to a specific person always outperforms a general offer to everyone.

---

"I help people get better" is not an offer. "I help burned-out software engineers reclaim their evenings without quitting their job" is an offer. One of those makes someone say "that's me." The other makes them scroll.

Specificity is not a constraint. It is what makes you findable.

## The Paradox of Broad Offers

When you try to help everyone, you resonate with no one. The person reading your content is looking for someone who understands their specific situation. When your message is broad, they assume you don't understand it.

The specific creator wins even with a smaller audience because their message lands deeper. A 1,000-person audience who all feel "this person gets me" is worth more than a 50,000-person audience who thinks you're vaguely interesting.

Broad audiences scroll. Specific audiences buy.

## How to Find Your Specific

Start with a problem you've personally solved. Not a problem you've read about. A problem you lived inside. That lived experience is what makes your perspective credible and your solutions concrete.

Then narrow the audience. Not "people who want to be productive" but "parents of toddlers who have 45 minutes a day and want to build a side project." The narrower the definition, the more the right person feels seen.

## Niching Is Not Forever

You don't have to stay in your niche forever. Start specific to build momentum. Once you have an audience and proof of earnings, you can expand. But expanding a successful specific thing is much easier than rescuing a failed general one.

Get known for something. Expand later.
