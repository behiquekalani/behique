# Issue 1957: The Attention Economy Has One Rule

**One insight. No fluff.**

Every platform is built on the same premise: capture attention and monetize it. The platforms win when you stay longer. Your interests are not aligned. They want your time. You want your time back. The only winning move is to use the platform without letting the platform use you.

Most people are passive consumers in the attention economy. They scroll, react, absorb. A small number are active producers. The difference is not just financial, though that matters. The difference is psychological. Consumers leave sessions feeling emptied. Producers leave sessions having made something. The same platforms that drain passive users can compound active ones.

Use platforms as distribution, not as home. Create something, post it, leave. Do not stay to watch the metrics in real time. Do not scroll as a reward for creating. The feed is designed to pull you in every direction except toward your own work. Post with intention and exit with the same intention. The attention economy is a game you can win, but only by playing it deliberately.
