# Issue 1801: You Don't Have a Productivity Problem

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people think they need a better system. A tighter schedule. The right app. They spend weeks optimizing their workflow and wonder why nothing ships.

The problem isn't how you work. It's what you've decided is worth working on.

Productivity without direction is just efficient wandering. You can be disciplined, consistent, and completely off track. The person who works six hours on the right thing beats the person who works twelve hours on the wrong one. Always.

Before you add another habit or restructure your calendar, ask one question: Is the thing I'm working on the most important thing I could be working on right now? Not eventually. Right now. If the answer is no, stop. Fix the direction first. Then optimize the engine.
