# Time Design: You Don't Have a Productivity Problem

Most people who feel unproductive are not lazy. They are reactive. Their day is designed by other people's urgency, and they mistake staying busy for making progress.

## The Difference Between Reactive and Designed Time

Reactive time is responding to what arrives. Email, notifications, requests, conversations. It feels productive because it involves action. But most of it does not build anything.

Designed time is carved out in advance for work that only you can do. Deep work, creative output, strategic decisions. This is where business gets built. If your calendar does not protect it, it will not happen.

## The Two-Block System

Split your day into two kinds of blocks. Creation blocks happen first, when your mind is fresh. These are for your most important output: writing, building, creating. Nothing interrupts these.

Reactive blocks happen after. Email, messages, admin, calls. They need to happen. They do not need to happen first.

Most people do this backwards. They start the day with email and wonder why they never have time for the work that matters.

## Why Designers of Time Outperform

It is not talent. It is constraint. When you decide in advance what this hour is for, you remove the decision overhead that drains energy throughout the day. The scheduled creator ships more than the inspired one.

Inspiration is not a schedule. Output is. Design your time around output, and inspiration will find you there.

## This Week's Action

Block two hours tomorrow morning before you check any messages. Label it "creation block" in your calendar. Use it for the one thing you have been putting off because you "never have time."
