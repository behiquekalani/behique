# Issue 1730: Creativity Is a Muscle

**One insight. No fluff.**

Creativity is not a personality trait reserved for artists. It is a practice available to anyone willing to do the repetitive, unglamorous work of generating ideas consistently. The people who seem naturally creative are usually just people who have been producing at volume for a long time. Output produces output.

The creative muscle atrophies when you wait for inspiration. Inspiration is not a cause. It is a side effect of doing the work. The writer who writes daily produces breakthrough ideas as a statistical outcome of consistent volume. The one who waits for the muse produces nothing except a growing sense that they have lost their edge.

Set a quota. Not a quality standard, a quantity standard. Write ten ideas every day even when they are bad. Make something small every week even when it is not ready. The standard for the practice is consistency, not brilliance. Brilliance is what happens after you have been consistent long enough to stumble into it. Show up first. The good work will come.
