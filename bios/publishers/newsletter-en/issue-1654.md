# How to Become a Category of One

The most dangerous place to be in any market is in a category with others. Competition destroys margins. Comparison destroys differentiation. The goal is to create a category where you're the only example.

## Categories Are Invented, Not Found
Nobody stumbles into being a category of one. It's a deliberate act of positioning. You take your actual perspective, your specific experience, your unusual combination of skills, and you name it in a way nobody else has.

The naming matters. When you can describe what you do in a way that makes people say "I've never thought about it that way," you're building a category.

## What Makes the Category Defensible
A category of one is defensible when it's rooted in your actual identity. Not a persona. Not a positioning exercise. The real thing. Your history, your failures, your specific way of seeing, these are things nobody can copy.

Skills can be replicated. Experiences can be approximated. But the specific synthesis of who you are, what you've lived, and how you think cannot be faked sustainably.

## The Patience Required
Building a category takes longer than following an existing one. There's no playbook to copy. No successful competitor to model. You're making the map as you move. That's uncomfortable and that's the advantage.

The people willing to do that work end up with something nobody else has.

## This Week's Action
Write a one-paragraph description of your category that doesn't use any existing industry terms. Describe what you do in entirely your own words. Share it with someone who doesn't know your field and ask if it makes sense.
