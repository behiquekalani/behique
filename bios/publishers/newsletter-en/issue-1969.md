# Issue 1969: The Only Question That Cuts Through Noise

**One insight. No fluff.**

There is one question that dissolves most confusion about what to do next: what would I do if I could not tell anyone about it? Strip away the social signal. No post, no story, no mention in a conversation. If you still want to do it, the desire is real. If the desire evaporates, you were chasing the report, not the thing.

This filters out a surprising amount of what people call goals. The startup that sounds impressive in conversation but would never be attempted in secret. The fitness transformation started because someone needs to see the change. The book written for the idea of being an author, not for the ideas themselves. None of these are bad, but none are clean motivations. Mixed motives produce inconsistent behavior.

The activities that survive the question tend to be the ones worth building around. The work you would do anyway, the problem you think about in the shower, the skill you practice even when you are behind on everything else. That is the thread to pull. Everything else is negotiable.
