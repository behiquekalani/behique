# Issue 2044: You Do Not Need More Information
**One insight. No fluff.**

There is a moment in any serious project where information stops helping and starts replacing action. You have enough. You know enough. The next step is obvious, but uncertainty feels dangerous, so you research one more thing. Read one more thread. Watch one more breakdown.

More information at that point is not strategy. It is delay with a justification attached. The truth is that most decisions do not require certainty. They require a direction and a commitment to adjust based on what happens next.

The bias toward action is not recklessness. It is an acknowledgment that reality teaches better than theory. Every action you take generates data that no amount of research could give you. You learn where you were wrong. You discover what actually matters. You find the real obstacles, not the imagined ones. Information has diminishing returns. Action has compounding returns. The moment you notice yourself researching something you already understand, that is the signal to close the tab and start the thing.
