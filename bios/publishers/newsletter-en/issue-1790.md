# Issue 1790: Creativity Is Not Inspiration. It Is a Practice With a Schedule.

**One insight. No fluff.**

Waiting for inspiration is a strategy for people who do not need to produce anything. For everyone else, it is procrastination with a romantic framing. The writers and designers and builders who consistently output quality work are not visited by inspiration more often. They sit down at the same time, do the work regardless of how they feel, and often produce their best ideas mid-session, not before it.

The act of starting, even badly, is what generates the creative state. It does not precede it. You cannot think your way into wanting to write. You write your way into thinking clearly. This is why so many creators describe their best work as something that surprised them. It came out during the doing, not during the planning.

Build a creative practice the same way you would build a fitness practice. Same time, same place, same trigger. The quality of any individual session matters far less than the consistency of showing up. A body of work is not made of your best days. It is made of all the days you showed up, including the mediocre ones that taught you what the good ones needed.
