# Your Attention Is the Product

Every platform you use was designed by a team of engineers whose only job was to keep you on it longer. That is not a conspiracy. That is just the business model.

## The Economics of Distraction

Advertisers pay for eyeballs. Platforms sell eyeballs. You are not the customer. You are the inventory. Once you understand this, every notification, every infinite scroll, every autoplay feature looks different. They are not features. They are extraction mechanisms.

The average person touches their phone 2,600 times a day. That is not accident. That is architecture. The platforms are winning. Most people never question whether they want to play.

## What You Lose When You Don't Notice

Attention is not just time. It is the raw material of thought. Every hour you spend reacting to other people's content is an hour you did not spend creating your own. Every dopamine hit from a like is borrowed energy you will pay back later with fog and fatigue.

The creators who build real audiences are not the ones who post the most. They are the ones who protect their focus long enough to say something worth hearing.

## The Only Defense That Works

You cannot out-willpower a billion-dollar algorithm. Rules beat resolve every time. Turn off notifications. Schedule your consumption window. Build before you browse. These are not productivity hacks. They are acts of self-preservation.

Your output quality is a direct function of your input quality. Guard your attention like it is your most valuable asset, because it is.

## This Week's Action

Audit every app on your phone. Delete or disable notifications for anything that does not directly support your work or relationships. Do it now, not at the end of the week.
