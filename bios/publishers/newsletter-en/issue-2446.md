# Issue 2446: The One-Person Business Model

**The Idea:** The one-person business is not a compromise. It is a deliberate structure that maximizes freedom while keeping overhead near zero.

Traditional business thinking says you need a team to scale. But scale is not the goal for everyone. For some, the goal is a business that funds a great life without becoming a second job. That business looks very different from a venture-backed startup.

The one-person model works because digital products have zero marginal cost. You write the guide once. You sell it a thousand times. You record the course once. You enroll students for years. The economics are fundamentally different from service businesses where your time is the product.

When you combine specific knowledge with digital packaging and a direct audience, you have a machine that generates income without requiring you to trade hours for dollars. The business becomes a system you build, not a job you work.

The tradeoff is that it takes longer to build than a job. But the outcome is different. You own it.

**Why It Matters:** Most people build businesses that own them instead of the other way around. The one-person model forces clarity about what you actually want and then optimizes for that, not for conventional metrics.

**One Action:** List the three things you already know well enough to teach. Pick the one with the clearest market demand. That is the foundation of your one-person business.
