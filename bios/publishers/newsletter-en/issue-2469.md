# Issue 2469: Building in Public Is a Distribution Strategy

**The Idea:** Building in public is often framed as vulnerability or authenticity, and there's truth in that. But the more practical reason to do it is distribution.

When you share your process, your setbacks, your experiments and results, you create a category of content that nobody else can copy. The person building something similar can't post your journey. It's yours. The specifics are unique to you.

This content works because it does two things simultaneously. It provides useful information to people at earlier stages of the same path, and it documents your credibility to people who might one day buy from you. Every update is a trust deposit.

Most people keep their process private out of fear of looking foolish or being wrong in public. But the willingness to be seen working, including being seen struggling, is exactly what makes it interesting. The polished finished product teaches. The messy process connects.

The audience you build by sharing your real journey is more loyal than the audience you build by sharing highlights. They've watched you work. They know what you've put in. When you eventually sell something, they already trust you.

**Why It Matters:** Building in public turns your work process into marketing. You don't need a separate content strategy because the work is the content. Every step forward is a post.

**One Action:** Share one real update about something you're currently building. Not a finished result. The current state: what you're trying to do, what's working, what isn't.
