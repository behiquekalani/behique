# Issue 1928: The Niche Is a Starting Point, Not a Cage

**One insight. No fluff.**

Every creator is told to pick a niche. Most follow this advice too literally. They find a category, plant their flag, and spend years producing the same kind of content for the same kind of person. The audience grows but so does the boredom. Eventually they burn out, pivot, and lose the following they spent years building.

The niche is not a permanent address. It is a starting point that gives your early work coherence. It signals to the right people that you are for them. It creates the initial trust. But trust is portable. Once you have built it, you can take it in new directions as your thinking evolves. The audience follows the person, not the topic, if you have done the work of making the person worth following.

The goal is to become someone whose mind is worth tracking, regardless of what they are currently working on. That level of trust is not built through topic consistency. It is built through consistency of depth, honesty, and quality of insight. What you write about can change. How you write should not. Your voice is the niche.

*Build something worth owning.*
