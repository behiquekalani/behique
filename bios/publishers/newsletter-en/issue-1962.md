# Issue 1962: Comfort Is a Delayed Tax

**One insight. No fluff.**

Comfort feels like a reward. You earn it, you settle into it, and for a while it feels like you made it. The problem is comfort does not stay neutral. It slowly degrades the skills, the hunger, and the tolerance for difficulty that got you there in the first place.

Every month you avoid the hard conversation, the risky project, the skill you have not built yet. You are not resting. You are paying a tax that will come due later. The longer you wait, the higher the rate. People who spent years in safe jobs and then tried to build something discovered this the hard way. Atrophy is quiet.

The solution is not to live in constant chaos. It is to deliberately choose difficulty at a regular interval. One hard thing per week. One skill that makes you feel incompetent. One project with real stakes. Not because suffering is noble, but because it keeps the machine calibrated. You stay hard to beat.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
