# Issue 2070: Build the Distribution Before the Product

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most product failures are not product failures. They are distribution failures. The product was fine. Nobody saw it. The builder assumed that quality would generate its own audience, then discovered that quality is table stakes and visibility is the actual game. This mistake is expensive and common enough to be considered a default outcome for people who skip distribution planning.

The correct sequence is: build an audience, then build a product for that audience. The wrong sequence is: build a product, then search for an audience. The first sequence means you launch to people who already want what you make. The second sequence means you are both launching and recruiting simultaneously, which is twice the work with half the clarity.

You do not need a massive audience. You need a real one. A hundred people who trust you and want what you are building is enough to validate, launch, and iterate. The mistake is thinking distribution is a marketing problem you solve after the product is ready. It is an asset you build before the product exists, and it determines whether the product succeeds before you ship a single line.
