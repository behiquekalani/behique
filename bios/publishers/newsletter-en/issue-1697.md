# Build Work That Outlasts You

There's a version of your work that lives on a hard drive and dies with a computer. Then there's a version that spreads, compounds, and keeps helping people years after you wrote it.

## The Difference Between Ephemeral and Enduring
A social media post lives for 48 hours. A well-crafted article indexed by search engines can serve people for a decade. A course purchased once can be completed years after its creation date. A book can outlive its author.

The medium matters. Short-form content builds awareness. Long-form, searchable, downloadable work builds a legacy.

## What Enduring Work Requires
It requires you to think about the reader not just in this moment but in five years. It requires enough depth that the work still holds up after the original context fades. It requires a topic with staying power. Not a trend. A fundamental human challenge: how to focus, how to earn, how to make something meaningful.

Write about those things and you're writing content with a long shelf life.

## Your Catalog Is the Asset
One piece of deep work is a start. Fifty pieces is a catalog. A catalog of deep, specific, well-indexed work is an asset that generates discovery, trust, and sales without requiring your daily attention.

Think about building the catalog, not just publishing the post.

## This Week's Action
Write the most thorough, useful piece of content you know how to write on a topic your audience cares about deeply. 1,000 words minimum. Optimize it for search. Publish it somewhere that indexes it. Add internal links. This is a long-term asset you're creating.
