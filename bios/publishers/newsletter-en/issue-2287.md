# Pricing Is a Positioning Statement

Your price does not just determine who buys. It determines who you are in the market.

## The Idea

Low prices feel safe. They feel accessible. They feel like less risk. But they signal something to the buyer that you may not intend: that the thing you made is not worth much. Price is not just a number. It is a message. And the message travels before anyone reads your sales page.

When you underprice, you attract buyers who are price-sensitive. These buyers compare you to competitors, ask for discounts, and leave when something cheaper appears. They are not bad people. They are just responding to the signal you sent. You told them price was the point.

Premium pricing attracts a different buyer. Someone who is investing, not gambling. Someone who wants a result, not a bargain. These buyers tend to be easier to work with, more likely to implement, and more likely to return. Premium pricing also creates margin for you to improve the product, serve buyers better, and market more effectively.

Most solopreneurs underprice because they are afraid no one will pay more. The cure is testing. Raise your price by thirty percent and watch what happens. In most cases, sales do not drop. Sometimes they go up, because the higher price signals higher value. You will never know until you test.

## Why It Matters

Chronically underpricing is a slow drain on your business and your confidence. It keeps you on the volume treadmill, needing hundreds of sales to make real money instead of tens. Raising prices is one of the highest-leverage moves available to any solopreneur. It is also one of the most delayed.

## One Action

Look at your lowest-selling product. Raise the price by twenty-five to fifty percent. Update the sales page to justify the new price with better framing. Leave it for thirty days before evaluating. Price is a variable worth experimenting with.
