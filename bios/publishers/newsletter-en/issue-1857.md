# Issue 1857: The Attention You Give to Failure
**One insight. No fluff.**

Failure analysis is useful exactly once. You look at what happened, identify what was in your control, extract the lesson, and move forward. What most people do instead is loop through it repeatedly without extracting anything new. That is not reflection. That is rumination dressed up as accountability.

The difference is direction. Reflection asks "what can I do differently?" Rumination asks "why did this happen to me?" One is forward-facing and produces adjustments. The other is backward-facing and produces only more of the same feeling.

The practical test is simple. After thinking about a failure, do you have a concrete thing to do differently? If yes, reflection worked. If not, you are ruminating. Stop thinking and start doing something, even something small. Action breaks the loop where thought cannot.
