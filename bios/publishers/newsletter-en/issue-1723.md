# Issue 1723: Write to Think, Not to Impress

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people write to look smart. That is backwards. The best writing happens when you write to figure out what you actually think. The sentence that surprises you mid-paragraph, the argument you did not expect to make, the conclusion that arrives before you planned it. That is real writing.

Trying to impress an audience before you have clarity makes everything worse. You perform instead of communicate. You use complicated words to cover simple confusion. The reader feels the gap between what you know and what you are pretending to know. They leave. They forget you.

Write the bad draft first. Do not share it. Write until you find the thing underneath the obvious thing. That buried idea is what your readers actually need. The final version almost writes itself once you have been honest with yourself in the first draft. Clarity is the product. Writing is how you mine it.
