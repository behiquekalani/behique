# Issue 2508: Price as a Signal

**The Idea:** Price does not just represent the value of your product. It communicates your positioning, your target customer, and how seriously you take your own work. Most new creators underprice because they are afraid of rejection. What they miss is that a low price attracts a different kind of buyer, one who is harder to work with, more likely to complain, and less likely to get results.

Premium pricing is not about charging more for the same thing. It is about signaling that your work is for people who are serious. People who pay $97 for a guide are more invested in using it than people who pay $7. That investment changes their behavior. They read it. They apply it. They get results. Those results become testimonials.

There is also a math problem with low pricing. If your product costs $9 and you want to make $5,000 per month, you need 556 customers. If your product costs $97, you need 52. The sales process for 52 customers is manageable. The sales process for 556 is a different business entirely.

Underpricing is not modesty. It is a strategic error. Price signals quality. Quality attracts the right buyers. The right buyers make your business easier and your testimonials stronger.

**Why It Matters:** The price you set is a filter. It selects the customer you end up working with. Set it too low and you attract volume you cannot handle without burning out.

**One Action:** Look at your current pricing. If it is under $29 for a standalone guide, raise it. Test $47 or $97. Watch whether the quality of buyer changes. It will.
