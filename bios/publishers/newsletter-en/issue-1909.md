# Issue 1909: Ship Before You're Ready

**One insight. No fluff.**

Ready is a feeling, not a state. You feel ready when you've eliminated all the uncertainty, answered all the questions, and reduced the risk of being wrong to zero. That moment never comes. The people waiting for it are still waiting. The people who shipped anyway are three iterations ahead, with real feedback, real users, and a concrete understanding of what actually matters.

The version of your product that exists in your head is always better than the one you'll build. That's not a bug in the process. It's a feature. The gap between the imagined and the real is where learning happens. The polished thing you never release teaches you nothing. The rough thing you put in front of people immediately tells you what's working, what's confusing, and what needs to change. You cannot get that information any other way.

Set a ship date and treat it like a hard deadline, not a soft target. Something will be unfinished. Let it be unfinished. Launch with the note that says "more coming." The feedback you get in week one will reshape what "more" even means. Most of what you thought was critical won't be. Most of what your users care about, you hadn't thought of. The only way to find out is to ship.
