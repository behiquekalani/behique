# Issue 2032: Price Is a Positioning Decision

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most beginners underprice everything. They think low prices remove objections. They do not. Low prices create different objections. The customer starts wondering why it is so cheap, what is wrong with it, whether the person behind it knows what they are doing. Price communicates value before the product gets to prove itself.

Pricing is not just economics. It is positioning. It tells people what category your work lives in and what kind of person it is for. A $10 course and a $500 course are not competing. They attract different buyers with different problems and different levels of commitment. The $500 buyer is more likely to do the work and get the result.

Charge more than feels comfortable. Then make the product worth it. That sequence, price high then deliver, forces you to grow in ways that underpricing never will. The discipline of justifying a real price tag is one of the fastest ways to improve what you make.
