# Issue 2487: The Mental Model That Changes How You Price

**The Idea:** Most solopreneurs price based on inputs. How many hours did this take? What did it cost to produce? What do competitors charge?

All of these are the wrong reference points.

The right reference point is outcome value. What is the outcome worth to the buyer?

A template that saves a marketing consultant four hours per client project is worth at least four hours of that consultant's billing rate, every time they use it. If they bill $200 an hour, that template is worth $800 per use. A $47 price tag is not bold. It is cheap.

This mental model shifts how you see your products. A PDF is not a PDF. It is a shortcut to a result. A course is not a course. It is a compressed path through experience the buyer would otherwise have to acquire slowly and expensively.

When you price against the outcome, your products become easier to sell because you stop apologizing for the number. You explain the math. The buyer sees the value before the objection forms.

Low prices signal low confidence in outcomes. High prices, backed by clear outcome framing, signal belief in your own work.

**Why It Matters:** Underpricing does not make you more accessible. It makes you less trusted. Price signals quality in a world where buyers cannot inspect the product before purchasing.

**One Action:** Take your lowest-priced product. Calculate the outcome value it delivers. If the value exceeds the price by 10x or more, raise the price today.
