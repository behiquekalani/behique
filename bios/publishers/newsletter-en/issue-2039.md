# Issue 2039: The Second Project Is Harder

**One insight. No fluff.**

Your first project had the advantage of low expectations. Nobody was watching, including you. You could make it ugly and ship it anyway because you had not yet formed the belief that your work should be better than this. The bar was on the floor and you cleared it.

The second project is harder because you brought baggage from the first. Now you know what good looks like. Now you have something to prove. The standards you absorbed from finishing once make it harder to start again because the gap between where you are and where you want to be is suddenly visible in a way it was not before.

The solution is the same as the first project. Ship before it is ready. Let the standard raise over time rather than demanding the finished standard before you begin. Your second thing does not have to be better than your first. It just has to exist. Growth happens in the series, not the single attempt.
