# Your Price Is a Signal, Not Just a Number

Cheap prices attract cheap clients. This is not a coincidence.

## What Price Communicates
Before anyone buys, your price tells a story. A $47 product signals one thing. A $4,700 service signals something different. Not just about value, but about the kind of person who engages with you.

Lower prices attract people who are price-sensitive by default. They'll negotiate, ask for refunds at higher rates, and require more support per dollar generated. Higher prices attract people who are outcome-focused. They invest, implement, and tend to get better results. Better results generate better testimonials.

## The Undercharging Trap
Most creators underprice because they're afraid. Afraid of rejection, of looking arrogant, of asking for more than they think they're worth. That fear has a cost. It keeps you busy with the wrong clients and unable to invest time in the right work.

Raising prices is often the fastest way to improve your client quality and your own commitment to delivering results.

## How to Price With Confidence
Price to the outcome, not the effort. If your offer saves someone $50,000 in mistakes, charging $5,000 for it is a fair trade. If your course takes ten hours to complete but transforms someone's income, price the transformation, not the hours.

Test higher prices before assuming they won't work. Most people find they convert better than expected.

## This Week's Action
Look at your lowest-priced offer. Calculate what outcome it delivers if the buyer takes action. Now ask whether the price reflects that outcome. If not, raise it by 20% and watch what happens to your buyer quality.
