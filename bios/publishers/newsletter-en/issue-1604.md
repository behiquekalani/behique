# Build It to Sell It: The Exit Mindset From Day One

Most builders build for themselves. They make decisions based on what is fun or interesting or familiar. The builder who keeps a future buyer in mind makes different, better decisions.

## What Changes When You Think About Exit

You start documenting. You build processes instead of relying on your own head. You keep finances clean. You reduce single points of failure, including yourself.

A business that only works because you are in it every day is not a business. It is a job you own. Buyers pay for systems, recurring revenue, and clean operations. Not for the founder's presence.

## The Types of Exits Available

You do not have to dream of a billion-dollar acquisition. A content site that earns $3,000 per month can sell for $100,000. A productized service with documented processes can sell for two to three times annual profit. Small exits are real exits.

Understanding the multiples your type of business commands changes how you build from week one. You make different bets when you know what the endgame math looks like.

## Building Transferability

The test is simple. Could someone else run this without you explaining everything? If the answer is no, that is the work. Document your processes. Automate the repetitive parts. Build an audience that follows the brand, not just you personally.

Transferability is also what gives you negotiating power. It signals that the asset is durable.

## This Week's Action

Write down the three most critical processes in your business that only exist in your head right now. Document one of them in full this week, even if it is rough.
