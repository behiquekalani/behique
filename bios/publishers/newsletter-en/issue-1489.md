# Writing Is Thinking Made Public

Most people avoid writing because it exposes how unclear their thinking actually is. That exposure is the point.

## The Clarity Test

If you cannot write a clear paragraph about your business model, you do not understand it yet. If you cannot write a compelling argument for why your product is worth buying, you have not solved the sales problem in your own mind first. Writing forces the vagueness out into the open where you can deal with it.

This is why founders who write tend to build tighter products. They have already stress-tested the logic in public. The product is an extension of thinking that has already been refined.

## Writing as a Competitive Signal

When you publish your thinking consistently, you attract people who think the same way. Those people become your best customers, collaborators, and referrers. The quality of your writing filters your audience more precisely than any targeting algorithm. You are broadcasting your mind. The people who resonate with it will find you.

Start messy. Write the unclear version. The act of writing will sharpen it. You cannot edit a blank page.

## This Week's Action
Write 300 words on the problem your work solves. Do not edit as you go. Finish the draft, then clean it up. Publish the result somewhere.
