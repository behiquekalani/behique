# Issue 2001: The Skill Nobody Teaches in School

**One insight. No fluff.**

School teaches you to answer questions. Nobody teaches you to ask the right ones. That asymmetry follows you into adulthood and most people never notice it.

The person who frames the problem controls the conversation. They decide what matters, what gets ignored, and what the solution looks like before anyone starts working. That is not a small power. It is the whole game. Engineers solve problems. Architects define them. The pay gap between those two roles is not about difficulty. It is about who holds the frame.

Most people spend their lives answering questions other people wrote. That is a fine life if you are comfortable. But if you want to build something that did not exist before, you need to get good at standing upstream of the question itself. Ask: why is this a problem? Who decided it was? What would have to be true for this not to be a problem at all? That kind of thinking will make you uncomfortable at first. It will also make you dangerous in the best way.
