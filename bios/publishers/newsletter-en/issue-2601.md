# Issue 2601: Your Niche Is a Person, Not a Topic

**The Idea:** Most people pick a niche the wrong way. They choose a topic. "Productivity." "Finance." "Marketing." Topics are broad categories. They attract everyone and no one. The better move is to pick a person. Not a demographic. A specific human being with a specific problem at a specific stage of life.

When you write for a topic, you write vague content. When you write for a person, every sentence has weight. You know what they're afraid of. You know what they've already tried. You know the exact words they use when they talk to themselves at 2am.

The niche is not "solopreneurs." The niche is "the engineer who has been freelancing for two years, makes decent money, but can't figure out why it doesn't feel like a real business yet." That specificity doesn't shrink your audience. It magnetizes the right part of it.

Most creator businesses fail because the founder tried to serve everyone. The ones that win draw a tight circle around one type of person and go deep.

**Why It Matters:** Specificity converts. Vague content gets consumed and forgotten. Content written for one specific person gets saved, shared, and acted on. A tight niche also makes your offers easier to sell because the reader thinks "this was made for me."

**One Action:** Write a one-paragraph description of the exact person you create for. Include their stage, their problem, and what they've already tried. Post it somewhere you see it daily.
