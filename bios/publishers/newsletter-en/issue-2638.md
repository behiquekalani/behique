# Issue 2638: The Difference Between Content and Commodity

**The Idea:** Most content online is commodity. Same advice, same format, same examples, slightly different packaging. When you scroll past it, nothing is lost. You have seen a version of it before.

The opposite of commodity content is content that reflects a specific point of view formed by specific experience. Nobody else could have written it the same way. Not because of credentials, but because the angle is genuinely yours.

The frame you use to think about a problem. The counterintuitive thing you believe that most people in your space disagree with. The story from your own path that illustrates something the conventional wisdom misses. These are not tricks. They are outputs of actually having a perspective.

Perspective is developed through practice. You have to write enough to find out what you actually think, not what you have absorbed from the people you've been reading. The first fifty posts are usually the borrowed ones. After that, something more original starts to emerge.

The creators who build durable audiences are not the ones who produced the most content. They are the ones whose content has a recognizable worldview running through it. Readers can identify the perspective even before they see the name.

**Why It Matters:** Commodity content does not build loyalty. It adds to noise. The path to a real audience runs through a real point of view.

**One Action:** Write one post this week that argues for something most people in your niche would push back on. Defend it clearly. See what happens.
