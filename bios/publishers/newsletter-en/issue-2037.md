# Issue 2037: Niche Is Not a Cage

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people resist choosing a niche because it feels like closing doors. If you specialize, you cannot talk about everything. If you serve one person clearly, you lose the vague appeal to everyone else. That logic sounds reasonable and it is completely backwards.

Specificity is not a cage. It is a signal. When someone who has the exact problem you solve finds your work, they do not feel like they found something generic. They feel like you made this for them. That feeling is worth more than all the reach you get from being vague. One buyer who feels understood is worth fifty confused visitors.

Go narrower than feels comfortable. The more precisely you can describe the person you are building for and the problem you are solving, the more powerfully your work will land for that person. You are not excluding everyone else. You are being clear enough that the right people can find you. That is not a limitation. It is the whole point.

*Build something worth owning.*
