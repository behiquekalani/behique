# Niche Down or Get Ignored

The fear of going too narrow is almost always worse than the reality. Specificity is not a constraint. It is a magnet.

## Why Broad Does Not Work

When your message speaks to everyone, it resonates with no one. The general productivity newsletter competes with thousands of others. The productivity newsletter for solo developers who work from home competes with almost none. Same category, completely different audience density.

The narrow position also signals expertise. When you specialize, people assume you are deep in the subject. That assumption has value. It shortens the trust-building phase and makes the sale easier.

## The Right Way to Niche

Start with who you already understand. Not who you want to understand eventually. The best niche is the intersection of who you are, what you know, and who you can reach. A 24-year-old student who builds AI tools and lives in Puerto Rico has a niche built in. The story is the niche.

Going narrow does not mean staying narrow forever. It means starting with enough precision to build an actual audience, then expanding from a position of strength.

## This Week's Action
Write out your current positioning in one sentence. Then rewrite it with one more layer of specificity. Compare the two. The more specific one is almost always stronger.
