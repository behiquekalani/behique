# Issue 1799: The Market Does Not Reward Effort. It Rewards Outcomes.

**One insight. No fluff.**

This is one of the hardest truths for people coming from an employment mindset. In a job, effort is often rewarded directly. You show up, do the work, put in the hours, and get paid regardless of whether the output creates value for anyone. The market does not work this way. The market pays for results that someone else values enough to exchange money for.

This is not cruel. It is clarifying. It means your energy is best spent understanding what outcomes people actually want, and then building your skills around delivering those outcomes reliably. Not working harder at things the market does not care about. Not getting better at something nobody is paying for.

The reframe that changes behavior: stop asking "how hard am I working?" and start asking "who is better off because of what I made?" If the answer is nobody yet, that is not a failure of effort. It is information about direction. Find who benefits from what you do best, communicate that benefit clearly, and charge for it honestly. Effort fills in the details after that question is answered.
