# How to Network Without Performing

Most networking advice tells you to be strategic. Who can help you? Who should you know? That framing turns every conversation into a transaction. People feel it. It doesn't work.

## Real Connections Start With Genuine Interest

The best professional relationships in your life probably started without an agenda. You talked to someone because what they were doing was interesting. You stayed in touch because the conversation was worth having.

That is still how it works. The "strategic networking" version produces shallow connections that go nowhere. The genuine version produces relationships where people think of you naturally when something relevant comes up.

The question is not "how can this person help me?" The question is "is this person doing something I find genuinely interesting?" If yes, engage. If no, move on. Forced connection is obvious.

## Build in Public, Connect Through Work

The most efficient networking is not networking at all. It is sharing your work publicly and letting people self-select into your orbit. People who resonate with what you're building will reach out. They already know what you're about. The conversation starts from a real place.

This compounds in a way that event-based networking never does. A tweet thread about a problem you solved reaches thousands of people. A room of fifty professionals reaches fifty. The math is not close.

Put your thinking online. Document your process. Show what you're building. The right people find you.

## Follow Up Like a Human

When you do make a connection, follow up with something specific. Not "great to meet you." Something that shows you were actually listening. A link to something related to what they mentioned. A thought about the problem they described.

Specificity is the proof of attention. It separates the people who were present from the people who were performing presence. And it makes follow-up feel natural rather than obligatory.

## This Week's Action

Find one person in your space whose work you genuinely respect. Send them a specific message about something they made. Not a pitch. Not a request. Just a real response to their work. See what happens.
