# Time Blocks Are Not Optional

A schedule without protected blocks is just a wish list.

## The Idea
Most solopreneurs manage time reactively. They respond to what appears in front of them. They work on what feels urgent. They fill gaps with shallow tasks and wonder why nothing important progresses.

Time blocking is the practice of assigning specific work to specific windows of time in advance. It removes the decision of what to work on in the moment, which is where willpower gets wasted. The decision was already made. The block is the commitment.

The most important blocks are the ones protecting your highest-value work. Deep work, creative production, strategy. These require uninterrupted focus. They cannot be done in fifteen-minute windows between notifications. If you do not protect them explicitly, everything else will fill them.

The system also requires a buffer category. Tasks that appear unexpectedly, quick decisions, admin. If you try to run zero-slack scheduling you will fail every day and abandon the system. Build in space for reality.

Weekly planning is what makes daily blocking sustainable. A Sunday review, fifteen minutes, where you look at the week ahead and assign blocks before the week starts. Without this, daily scheduling becomes reactive again.

The resistance most people feel toward time blocking is discomfort with constraint. But constraint is not the enemy of creativity or freedom. It is the condition for them.

## Why It Matters
Unprotected time gets filled by other people's priorities. Blocked time stays yours. That difference, compounded over a year, separates the builders who ship from the ones who stay perpetually busy.

## One Action
Block tomorrow's calendar before you sleep tonight. Assign your most important task to the highest-energy window. Honor it when tomorrow arrives.
