# Issue 2085: Opinions Are Underpriced

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most content is informational. Here is how this works. Here are five steps. Here is what happened. Informational content is useful, but it is abundant. Anyone can aggregate information. Search engines do it better. The thing that is actually scarce, and actually builds an audience, is a clear, specific, defended opinion.

An opinion says: here is what I think is true, here is why, and here is what that means for how you should act. That structure requires you to take a position. Most people avoid that because positions can be wrong. They can be challenged. They invite disagreement. So instead people hedge. They present both sides. They say "it depends." They stay safe and they stay invisible.

The people who build real audiences are willing to be specifically right about specific things. Not contrarian for its own sake. Not provocative to generate heat. Actually thinking something, and saying it clearly enough that people can agree or disagree. That clarity is what makes someone worth following. You do not follow people who tell you what everyone already thinks. You follow people who tell you what they actually think, and make you think harder as a result.

*Build something worth owning.*
