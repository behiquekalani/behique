# Issue 1834: The Long Game Requires Short Feedback Loops

**One insight. No fluff.**

Playing the long game does not mean deferring all feedback until the end. It means pursuing something that compounds over years while building short feedback loops that tell you if you are moving in the right direction. The people who disappear for two years building something in silence often emerge with the wrong thing. Feedback is not a distraction from the work. It is the work.

Short feedback loops look like shipping small versions early, getting a real person to pay for something before you build the full product, posting content regularly and watching what resonates. They are not about chasing validation. They are about calibrating. Without feedback, you are building based on assumptions, and assumptions compound errors just as readily as good decisions compound results.

The distinction that matters is the type of feedback you seek. Feedback from people who have skin in the game, who would actually pay or use what you are building, is worth pursuing. Feedback from friends who want to be supportive, or from strangers trying to seem smart, is not. Identify your real test audience, get your work in front of them quickly, and let their behavior tell you the truth.
