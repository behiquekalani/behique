# Issue 1863: Your Niche Is Not a Category. It's a Perspective.

**One insight. No fluff.**

Everyone tells you to find your niche. Pick a topic. Stick to it. What they actually mean is pick a category, which is the wrong unit of analysis. Categories are crowded. Personal finance, fitness, productivity. Thousands of people already own those. You cannot compete in a category. You can only compete with a perspective.

Your perspective is the intersection of your specific experiences, the problems you have actually solved, and the worldview you have developed from solving them. Nobody has your exact combination. The mistake is trying to erase that specificity to appeal to everyone. Generic appeal is no appeal. The more you sound like a category, the less you sound like a person, and people follow people, not categories.

Write from your actual life. The unconventional path you took. The failure that taught you something textbooks skipped. The contrarian belief you hold that most of your field disagrees with. That is your niche. It cannot be copied because it is not a topic. It is you, made specific enough to be useful.

*Build something worth owning.*
