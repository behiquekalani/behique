# Digital Products Are Leverage. Most People Just Don't Use Them Right.

You create it once. It sells while you sleep. That's the pitch. Here's what nobody tells you about what comes before.

## The Mistake Everyone Makes First
They build the product, then look for buyers. The market doesn't work that way. You find the pain first. Then you build the solution. A digital product that solves a real, urgent problem sells itself. A digital product built in isolation needs a full marketing department to move.

Before you write a single word or record a single video, talk to ten people in your target market. Not to pitch them. To understand them. What keeps them up at night? What have they already tried? Where did those solutions fail?

## The Three Product Types That Actually Sell
Templates save time. Courses teach skills. Communities provide belonging. Everything else is a variation of one of these three. Pick the category that matches your knowledge, then go deep instead of wide.

A focused $97 template that solves one specific problem will outsell a $297 course that tries to cover everything. Specificity wins. The more narrowly you define the problem, the more the right buyer feels like you read their mind.

## Distribution Is the Real Product
The best digital product with no distribution is a hobby. Build your distribution first. An email list, a Twitter following, a YouTube channel. When you have an audience, launching a product is an event. Without one, it's a whisper into a void.

## This Week's Action
Write down the one specific problem you know better than most people. Not a category. A specific problem with a specific cause and a specific outcome. That's your product idea.
