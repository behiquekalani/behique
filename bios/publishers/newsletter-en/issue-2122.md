# Issue 2122: The Cost of Keeping Options Open

**One insight. No fluff.**

There is a hidden tax on indecision that nobody talks about. Every time you delay a choice, you pay it. The cost is not visible on a balance sheet. It lives in your attention, your energy, and the slow erosion of momentum that comes from holding too many possibilities at once.

Keeping your options open feels like freedom. It is actually paralysis with better branding. Real freedom comes from commitment. When you choose one direction and close the others, your mind stops allocating resources to managing alternatives. The cognitive load drops. Suddenly you can think clearly about the one thing that actually matters.

Most people fail not because they chose wrong, but because they never chose at all. They spent years optimizing for optionality and ended up with a perfectly maintained set of unopened doors. Pick one. Walk through it. The door being open means nothing. Walking through it means everything.
