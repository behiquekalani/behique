# Systems Are How You Clone Yourself Without Hiring Anyone

Every task you do more than once is a candidate for a system. If you are not systematizing, you are manually rebuilding the same thing every time.

## The Idea

A system is a defined process that produces a predictable outcome. It captures your best thinking on how to do something and removes the need to re-think it each time. Systems are how one person produces the output of three people without working more hours.

The easiest systems to build are the ones around your most repeated tasks. Onboarding a new client. Publishing a piece of content. Responding to a type of inquiry. Reviewing your weekly progress. These happen over and over. Every time you do them without a system, you are spending cognitive resources that could go elsewhere.

The process of building a system is straightforward. Do the task once with full attention. Write down every step in sequence. Identify the steps that could be automated or templated. Identify the steps that require your judgment and document the criteria for that judgment. Run the system and refine it.

The goal is not to remove yourself from the work entirely. The goal is to remove yourself from the parts of the work that do not require you. You are not being replaced by your system. Your system is handling the parts that were wasting you.

A builder with ten good systems is far more productive than a builder with a team of three and no systems. Because the ten systems never call in sick, never need feedback, and never require motivation.

## Why It Matters

Time is the constraint. Systems expand what you can do within that constraint without expanding the constraint itself.

Build once. Run forever.

## One Action

Choose one task you repeat weekly. Set a timer for 20 minutes. Document every step of that task as if training someone else to do it. You now have the first draft of a system.
