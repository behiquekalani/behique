# Issue 2464: Why Most Solopreneurs Stay Broke

**The Idea:** The most common reason solopreneurs don't make money is not lack of talent. It's avoidance of the offer.

They create content, build an audience, develop skills, read books about business, and optimize their systems. But they never actually put something up for sale. There's always one more thing to do before the offer is ready.

This is a protection strategy. If you never launch, you never get rejected. The product that exists only in your head is still perfect. The moment you publish it, it becomes something that can be ignored or criticized.

The creator who ships an imperfect product and iterates has real data. They know what worked, what didn't, what people actually wanted. The one still preparing has theory. Theory doesn't pay.

Revenue requires an offer. An offer requires a decision to ship. That decision is the hardest part of building online, not the technical work, not the writing, not the skills. The act of putting a price on your work and letting the market respond.

Most solopreneurs are one published offer away from their first sale. The gap is the decision to ship.

**Why It Matters:** A published offer that earns $50 teaches you more than a year of preparation. The market gives you feedback that no amount of internal planning can replicate. Ship first, refine based on reality.

**One Action:** Set a date. Not "someday I'll launch," but "by [specific date] I will have something for sale." Put it in your calendar today.
