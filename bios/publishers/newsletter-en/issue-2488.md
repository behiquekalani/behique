# Issue 2488: Niche Is Not a Box. It Is a Base.

**The Idea:** "Pick a niche" is common advice. Most people hear it as a restriction. They resist because they have multiple interests and do not want to be trapped.

This is a category error.

A niche is not a cage. It is a starting position. You dominate a specific space first, then expand from a position of authority. The mistake is thinking you have to stay in the box forever rather than understanding that the box gives you the credibility to break out of it meaningfully.

James Clear started with habit science. He now writes about almost anything. Naval Ravikant started with startup advice. He now talks about philosophy and health and happiness. The niche created the platform. The platform enabled the expansion.

If you skip the niche step and try to talk about everything from the start, you are asking people to follow a generalist with no track record. That is a hard sell.

But if you plant a flag, build an audience around it, and then expand, your audience follows because they trust your thinking. Your history of being right about one thing makes them want to hear you on others.

**Why It Matters:** A niche is how you build the trust required to eventually not need one. Skip it and you spend years trying to build trust in a vacuum.

**One Action:** Write out the single clearest sentence describing what you are specifically known for today. If you cannot write it, you have not picked a base yet.
