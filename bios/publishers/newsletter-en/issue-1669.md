# Authority Is Not a Title. It Is a Track Record.

Nobody gives you authority. You demonstrate it, repeatedly, until people stop questioning it.

## The Monetization Gap
Most creators know their subject. Few know how to demonstrate that knowledge in a way that makes people reach for their wallets. The gap between knowing something and getting paid for it is not certification. It is demonstrated results. A portfolio of outcomes, even small ones, beats a list of credentials every time.

If you want to monetize your expertise, start producing public evidence of it. Write the analysis. Share the experiment. Document the result.

## Teaching Builds the Proof
Every time you teach what you know in public, you create a reference point. Your audience does not just learn from it. They form an opinion about your level of understanding. Over time, those reference points accumulate into a body of work that no resume can replicate. The person who has written 50 pieces on one topic is recognized as an authority whether or not they have a degree in it.

The writing is not just marketing. It is the credential.

## Price From the Track Record
When you have a clear body of work, pricing becomes easier. You are not charging for your time. You are charging for the concentration of understanding that took years to develop. The track record is what justifies a premium price. Without it, you are competing on rate. With it, you are competing on results.

## This Week's Action
Identify one specific outcome you have produced, either for yourself or someone else, that proves your expertise. Write about it publicly. Include the numbers, the process, and what you would do differently. Make it a reference piece.
