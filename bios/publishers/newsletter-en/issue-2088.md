# Issue 2088: Audience Before Product

**One insight. No fluff.**

The traditional sequence is build a product, then find an audience for it. This sequence is backward and expensive. You spend months building something, launch it, and discover that your assumptions about who wants it and why were wrong. Then you either pivot or shut down. Most people repeat this several times before they figure out what people actually need.

The better sequence is build an audience around a specific problem, then create the product that solves it. When you do this, you ship into a group of people who already trust you, have told you what they need, and are waiting for the solution. The launch is not a gamble. It is a delivery.

This is why writing publicly, documenting your thinking, and engaging with a specific niche before you have a product to sell is not wasted time. It is market research that also builds distribution. Every conversation with your audience is a data point. Every question they ask tells you what they are willing to pay to solve. By the time you build, you already know the answer. Most builders skip this phase because it feels slow. But building without an audience is slower. You just do not feel the slowness until after you have shipped.
