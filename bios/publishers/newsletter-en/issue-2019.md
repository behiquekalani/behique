# Issue 2019: Building in Public Is Not About Attention

**One insight. No fluff.**

The popular version of building in public is about audience growth. Share your journey, document your process, attract followers. That is a real effect but it is the wrong reason to do it. People who build in public for attention produce content about building. People who build in public for accountability and clarity produce work.

The real value of making your work visible is what it does to your thinking. When you know you are going to explain what you built and why, you make better decisions during the build. You think about the reasoning, not just the output. You ask whether you could defend the choices you are making. That reflective loop produces better work independent of whether anyone sees it.

The secondary value is the network effect that happens slowly over years. When you share genuine work consistently, without performing, without polishing reality into a highlight reel, you attract people who are doing real things. Those connections are more valuable than a large audience of passive consumers. The goal is depth of connection with a small group of people who are doing serious work, not width of reach across a large group who are not. That outcome requires that you be more interested in the work than in being seen doing it.
