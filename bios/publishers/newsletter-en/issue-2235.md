# The 80/20 of Getting Your First 100 Customers

Most of what you think you need to get customers is wrong. Here's what actually works.

## The Idea

The first 100 customers are the hardest to get because you have no social proof, no algorithm working in your favor, and no referral flywheel. You're operating on trust signals alone.

The 20 percent of actions that drive 80 percent of results at this stage are: a clear offer, a small warm audience, and direct outreach or explicit asks.

A clear offer means one product, one promise, one price, one CTA. Not a catalog. Not a sales funnel with five steps. A single clear answer to: what do I get, and how do I buy it?

A small warm audience means people who already follow you, even loosely. Your newsletter list. Your existing followers. Your network. You don't need a million people. You need 200 engaged ones who trust you enough to give you a chance.

Direct outreach means you actually tell people about the product. Not just post about it once and hope. You email your list. You DM people who've engaged with relevant content. You post about it explicitly, more than once, in multiple formats.

The 80 percent of activities that don't move the needle at this stage: redesigning your site, optimizing your SEO, building more products, adding integrations. These feel productive. They're not.

## Why It Matters

First 100 customers is a momentum milestone. After that, social proof, word of mouth, and algorithmic distribution start helping. Before that, you're doing it manually.

## One Action

If you have a product ready, count the people currently in your orbit who might buy it. Email or message 10 of them this week. Tell them what you built, why you built it, and how to get it. That's all.
