# Focus Is a Decision, Not a Feeling

You don't wait to feel focused. You decide to focus, then create the conditions that make it possible.

## The Attention Economy Is Real
Your attention is being harvested. Every app, every notification, every autoplay is designed by teams of engineers to capture as much of your time as possible. This is not paranoia. It's a business model. The question is whether you're a willing participant or someone who has decided to opt out.

Opting out doesn't mean destroying your phone. It means designing your environment so the default behavior is deep work, not distraction. Notifications off. Phone in another room. No social media until the creative work is done.

## What Deep Focus Produces
Four hours of genuine focus produces more than eight hours of fragmented attention. Not because of magic. Because the best ideas come at the end of a long, uninterrupted thinking session. The first hour clears the surface. The second hour goes deeper. By hour three, you're in territory most people never reach.

Most people never get there because they interrupt themselves at hour one.

## The Schedule That Protects Focus
Morning is for creation. Afternoon is for communication. Evening is for reflection. This is a framework, not a rule. The point is to protect the highest-quality hours for the highest-leverage work. Your brain at 8am is not the same brain as your brain at 3pm.

Stop scheduling meetings over your best thinking hours.

## This Week's Action
Audit your calendar for next week. Identify the two hours where you're most mentally sharp. Block them as "deep work" and defend them. Cancel or reschedule anything that has claimed that space.
