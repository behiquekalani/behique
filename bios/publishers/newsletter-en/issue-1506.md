# Time Design Is a Skill You Were Never Taught

You were taught time management. That is not the same thing.

## The Management Trap

Managing time assumes you have a fixed block and you need to fit tasks into it. Design assumes you choose what goes in the block. Most people optimize for fitting. They should be optimizing for choosing.

The question is not how to be more productive. The question is what is worth being productive at. That is a different question with a different answer.

## Energy, Not Hours

There are high-energy hours and low-energy hours in every day. High-energy hours are for creative work and hard decisions. Low-energy hours are for administrative tasks and execution.

Most people invert this by accident. They check email first. They attend meetings in the morning. They save the creative work for when they are already spent. The result is mediocre output and constant fatigue.

## What a Designed Week Looks Like

Mondays for direction. Tuesdays and Wednesdays for deep creative work. Thursdays for calls and collaboration. Fridays for review and planning.

That is a framework, not a rule. The point is intention. You decide what kind of day each day is before external requests fill it in.

## This Week's Action

Block two hours on your calendar tomorrow, in the morning, labeled "deep work." Turn off notifications. Do the one thing that would actually move your business. Protect it as if it is a meeting with your most important client.
