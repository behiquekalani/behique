# Issue 2041: The Skill Nobody Teaches You
**One insight. No fluff.**

Every school teaches you how to think inside a system. Math problems with one answer. Essays with a rubric. Tests where the goal is to guess what the teacher wants.

Nobody teaches you how to think when there is no rubric. When the problem is undefined. When you have to decide what the goal even is before you can chase it.

That skill is the one that separates people who build things from people who wait for instructions. It is called problem framing. And it is learned by doing the uncomfortable thing: starting without permission. You pick a problem that matters to you, you define what solved looks like, and you iterate. No teacher. No rubric. No safety net. Just you and the feedback loop of reality. Most people avoid this their whole life. The ones who do not tend to become the ones worth listening to.
