# Issue 2054: Subtraction Is a Strategy
**One insight. No fluff.**

Everyone asks what they should add. What new tool, what new platform, what new system, what new habit. The question almost nobody asks is what they should remove. Subtraction is uncomfortable because it feels like giving up. It looks like less. But doing fewer things with more focus almost always produces better results than doing many things with fragmented attention.

The high performers in most fields are not doing more than everyone else. They are doing less, but they have chosen that less with unusual care and they protect it aggressively. They have said no to good opportunities in order to stay available for the rare excellent ones. They have cut systems that were working moderately well to free up the focus required to make something work exceptionally.

Your current constraints are probably not what you think they are. They are not a lack of tools, platforms, or strategies. They are a dispersion of energy across too many directions. The question is not what to add. The question is what, if removed, would allow everything else to compound.
