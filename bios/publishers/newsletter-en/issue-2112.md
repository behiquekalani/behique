# Issue 2112: Price Is a Signal You Are Sending

**One insight. No fluff.**

Low prices do not attract better customers. They attract more customers, which is a different thing. The client who haggles on a $50 product will make your life difficult at $50. The client who pays $500 without flinching has already decided you are worth it. Price sets the frame before you say a single word about what you do.

Underpricing is not modesty. It is a signal to the market that you do not believe in your own work. Buyers pick up on that signal faster than you think. Premium pricing communicates something that no marketing copy can fake: confidence in the value you deliver. When the price is too low, the buyer wonders what you are hiding.

Raise your prices. Not gradually, not apologetically. Just raise them. You will lose some customers. The ones you keep will be better clients, better collaborators, and more satisfied because they paid for something they valued. The discomfort of raising prices is not a sign you are wrong. It is a sign you are renegotiating your relationship with your own worth. That renegotiation is overdue.
