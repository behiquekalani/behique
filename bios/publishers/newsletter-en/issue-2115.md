# Issue 2115: The Power of Finishing Small Things

**One insight. No fluff.**

Finishing is a skill and most people are out of practice. They start projects, hit the first hard stretch, and move on to the next exciting beginning. The cycle repeats. The habit of not finishing goes unnoticed because starting always feels like progress. It is not. A hundred beginnings with no completions is not a productive life. It is a collection of warm-ups.

The fix is deliberate practice in finishing. Not big projects. Small ones. Write the essay and publish it. Build the small tool and ship it. Have the conversation you have been postponing. These small completions rebuild the neural pathway that connects effort to outcome. They train the part of your brain that knows what it feels like to be done.

Each small finished thing also compounds. The essay teaches you something the draft never could. The shipped product gets feedback that the planned product never receives. The completed conversation clears space that the avoided one was occupying. Finishing is not just about the artifact. It is about the information you collect by going all the way through. Start fewer things. Finish more of them. The returns are immediate.
