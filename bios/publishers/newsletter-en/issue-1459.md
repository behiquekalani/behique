# The Mindset Tax on Every Decision You Avoid

Avoided decisions do not go away. They become background load. Every unresolved choice you are carrying is drawing energy you could be spending on creation.

## Decision Debt

Decision debt works like financial debt. It accrues interest. The longer you hold an unresolved decision, the heavier it gets. The business name you have not committed to. The pricing you have not set. The niche you have not chosen.

Each one occupies mental space that your best work needs. Clean decisions, even imperfect ones, release that space back to you.

## The Cost of Perfectionism in Decisions

Perfectionism sounds like high standards. In decisions, it functions as avoidance with a respectable cover story. You are not waiting for more information. You are waiting to feel certain, which never comes.

Most decisions are reversible. Most information you are waiting for will not meaningfully change the outcome. Make the call with what you have. Adjust when you learn more.

## The 80% Rule

When you have 80% of the information and no decision, you are in the expensive zone. The last 20% of certainty costs disproportionate time and energy to acquire, and rarely changes the decision.

Get to 80%, make the call, and use the saved energy to ship something. The market will give you better feedback than any additional research will.

## This Week's Action

Write down the three decisions you have been avoiding for the longest time. For each one, set a deadline. Not "when I have more info." A specific date. Make the decision by that date or delegate it to someone else. Clear the debt.
