# Issue 1858: Meetings Are a Tax on Thinking
**One insight. No fluff.**

A one-hour meeting does not cost one hour. It costs the hour plus the setup time, the recovery time, and the broken context that takes 20 minutes to rebuild afterward. For creative or deep work, a single meeting in the middle of the day can effectively cancel the whole afternoon.

The default in most organizations is to schedule meetings until work time is what remains, not the other way around. This is backwards. Deep work should be the protected default, and synchronous communication should be the exception that requires justification.

If you have control over your schedule, block your best hours for your most important work before anything else gets scheduled. If you do not have full control, start by declining the meetings where you are on the invite list but not a decision-maker. You will rarely be missed. The people who need you will find you.
