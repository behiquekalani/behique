# Online Income Requires a Distribution Strategy

Building something good is not enough. The internet rewards reach as much as quality. If nobody sees your work, it doesn't matter how good it is.

## The Idea

Most creators focus 90% of their effort on making the thing and 10% on getting it in front of people. The successful ones flip that ratio, or at least balance it.

Distribution is a system, not a tactic. It's not posting randomly and hoping the algorithm rewards you. It's choosing one or two channels where your audience lives, understanding how those channels work, and showing up consistently enough to build momentum.

The fastest distribution strategy for a solo operator is borrowed audience, then owned audience. Borrowed audience means showing up where people already gather. Guest posts, podcast appearances, collaborations, engaging with larger accounts in your space. You're borrowing their attention. The goal is to convert some of that attention into people who follow you directly.

Owned audience is your email list. It's the only distribution channel you actually control. Social platforms change their algorithms, restrict reach, ban accounts. Your email list is yours. Every subscriber is someone who gave you direct access to their inbox. That access is worth more than ten times the follower count on any platform.

Build the borrowed audience to grow the owned one. That's the full loop.

## Why It Matters

Distribution strategy turns a creator into a business. Without it, you're making content and hoping. With it, you're building a repeatable system for getting your work in front of the right people at the right time.

## One Action

Pick one borrowed audience channel to focus on this month. Not three. One. Commit to showing up there five times per week for 30 days. Track what happens.
