# Issue 2458: Write to One Person

**The Idea:** The most common writing mistake in content marketing is trying to speak to everyone. The result is content that feels like a public announcement: technically addressed to all, emotionally resonant with none.

The fix is simple. Write to one specific person. Not a demographic. Not a buyer persona. A real person you can picture, whose specific situation you understand, whose frustrations you can name without guessing.

When you write to one person, your language becomes specific. You stop using vague phrases and start saying the exact thing they have been thinking. That precision creates the feeling that you are reading their mind, and that feeling is what drives engagement, shares, and sales.

The paradox is that the more specific you write, the more people recognize themselves in what you write. Humans are more alike than they appear. A precise description of one person's situation resonates with thousands of others in similar situations.

Generic content competes with everything else in the feed. Specific content stops the scroll because it feels personal. The reader thinks you wrote it for them. That is the goal.

**Why It Matters:** Specificity in writing is what separates content that gets ignored from content that gets shared. The discipline of writing to one person is one of the fastest ways to improve the performance of everything you publish.

**One Action:** Before writing your next piece of content, write three sentences describing the exact person it is for. Name their situation, their frustration, and what they want. Let those sentences guide every word that follows.
