# Issue 2619: The Offer Hierarchy

**The Idea:** A mature knowledge business does not have one product. It has an offer hierarchy. A range of products at different price points that serve the same customer at different stages of their relationship with you.

The entry point is usually free or very low cost. An email list opt-in, a $9 PDF, a free resource. Its job is to build trust, demonstrate value, and get someone into your world.

The mid-tier offer is typically $47 to $197. It goes deeper on one specific problem. It's structured. It produces a clear outcome. This is where most of your volume should come from.

The high-ticket offer is $500 and above. Coaching, consulting, a high-touch program. Not everyone needs this, but it should exist because some people want more access and are willing to pay for it.

The hierarchy works because different buyers are at different readiness levels. Some people find you and want to buy immediately. Some need six months of free content first. Some will never buy without a direct conversation. A single product misses most of these buyers. An offer hierarchy catches them all.

Your customer moves through the hierarchy as trust builds. That progression is your business model.

**Why It Matters:** An offer hierarchy multiplies your revenue per customer. One buyer can move from a $9 purchase to a $197 product to a $500 coaching engagement, all based on trust that compounds over time.

**One Action:** Map out what you sell at each price tier. Identify the gaps. If you have no entry product, create one. If you have no high-ticket offer, price one.
