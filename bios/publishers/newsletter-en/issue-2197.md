# Stop Building Features. Start Solving Problems.

Nobody buys features. They buy outcomes.

## The Idea

The builder's trap is falling in love with what you are making instead of what it does for someone.

You spend weeks perfecting the design, adding features, refining the onboarding, improving the copy. And none of it matters if it does not clearly solve a real problem someone has right now.

The problem-first mindset starts with a different question. Not "what should I build?" but "what does this specific person need, and what is the simplest thing that delivers it?"

Simple is harder than complex. Complex lets you feel like you are adding value. Simple forces you to confront whether you are actually creating value.

The product that solves one problem clearly outperforms the product that solves five problems vaguely. Because the customer looking for that one solution can find it, understand it, and trust that it works.

When you start with the problem, your marketing becomes easier too. You already know the pain. You already know the language. The customer already knows what they want. Your job is to show them you have the answer, clearly and quickly.

Features are how you describe what something does. Problems are why someone buys it.

## Why It Matters

Every hour spent on features that do not directly reduce the customer's problem is waste. Reorienting around the problem sharpens every decision: what to build, what to remove, what to say.

## One Action

Describe your main product or service in one sentence that contains no features. Only the problem it solves and the outcome it creates. If you cannot do that, you do not know what you are selling yet.
