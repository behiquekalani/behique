# Issue 1773: The Work Is the Point, Not the Outcome
**One insight. No fluff.**

Outcome dependence makes every day a referendum on your worth. If the post performs, you are valid. If it flops, you question everything. This is a fragile way to live. It makes your mood and your decisions dependent on signals outside your control.

The alternative is process attachment. You care about doing the work well, consistently, at a standard you set for yourself. The outcome is feedback, not verdict. A post that flops tells you something about the message, the timing, the angle. It does not tell you whether to continue.

The builders who last are almost always process-attached people. They have a standard. They hold it. They adjust based on feedback but do not collapse under it. The outcome becomes a side effect of the process, not the other way around.
