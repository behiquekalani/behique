# Issue 2433: Pricing Is a Message

**The One Thing:** Low prices signal low value. Price based on the transformation you deliver, not the time it took to create it.

---

Most creators underprice because they're afraid. Afraid no one will pay. Afraid they're not worth it. The price they set ends up being a reflection of their self-doubt, not the actual value of what they built. That's a solvable problem.

## What Price Communicates

A $7 product says "this is a small thing." A $97 product says "this will change how you work." A $497 product says "this is an investment in your future." The price is a signal before the buyer even reads the description. Price low and you attract buyers who want the cheapest option, not the best result.

The buyers you want are not looking for the cheapest option. They're looking for the most reliable path to the result. Price accordingly.

## The Cost of Underpricing

When you price too low, you need more sales to hit the same revenue target. More buyers means more support, more questions, more refund requests from people who bought something cheap expecting to get lucky. High prices attract committed buyers. Committed buyers get results. Results generate testimonials.

The economics of high-ticket digital products are clear. Ten sales at $97 is the same revenue as 97 sales at $10, with ten times less complexity.

## How to Find the Right Price

Ask what the transformation is worth. Not the product, the transformation. If your template saves someone five hours a week, that's 260 hours a year. At even a modest hourly rate, that's a significant number. Price based on the outcome delivered.

---

**This Week's Action:** Take your lowest-priced product and double the price. Watch if conversion rate changes enough to matter. It probably won't.

**Quote:** "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get." — Warren Buffett
