# The Audience Before the Product

Building a product for an audience you do not have is building in the dark.

## The Idea
The traditional product development model is: build, then find customers. This works when you have capital for distribution and can absorb high failure rates. As a solopreneur, you have neither.

The creator model inverts it. Build an audience around a specific set of ideas. Document what resonates. When something resonates consistently, that is your product. Your audience has already told you what they want to pay for. You are not guessing. You are fulfilling.

This does not mean waiting years to ship. It means starting to share your thinking publicly before your product exists. A newsletter, a Twitter thread series, a short-form video channel. Each piece of content is a market research tool that doubles as audience building.

Pay attention to what people respond to. Comments, shares, replies, saves. These are votes. They are telling you what solves a real problem for a real person. When you see the same response pattern three or four times, you have found a product.

The audience also provides launch distribution. When you launch to a list of people who already trust you, you start with conversions, not from zero. The first revenue validates the product. Validation unlocks the next product.

## Why It Matters
Products fail most often because of distribution, not quality. An audience solves the distribution problem before the product exists. Build the audience while building the product, not after.

## One Action
Identify the one platform where your target audience currently spends time. Commit to publishing one piece of genuine value there every week for the next ninety days. Track which topics generate real engagement.
