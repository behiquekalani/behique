# Digital Products Are Stored Work

You can only trade time for money so many times before you hit a ceiling.

## The Idea

A digital product is a different kind of asset. You build it once. You sell it a thousand times. The marginal cost of each new sale approaches zero. This is the economic model that most people never access because they never leave the time-for-money trap.

The trap feels safe. You work, you get paid. Clear transaction. But the ceiling is low. There are only so many hours. There is only so much you can charge per hour before the market pushes back.

A digital product breaks the ceiling. An ebook, a template, a course, a system. These are containers for your thinking. You pour your knowledge into a format, and that format does the selling for you at 2am on a Tuesday while you sleep.

The hard part is not the product. It is the patience to build the distribution before you need the revenue. Most people want the sale before they build the audience. The order matters. Build the trust first. The sale follows trust naturally.

## Why It Matters

You already have knowledge worth packaging. The question is whether you will package it or watch someone else package it and profit. Digital products do not require a degree or a team. They require clarity about what you know and who needs to know it.

## One Action

Pick one thing you know how to do that takes others months to figure out. Outline a five-step guide. That outline is the skeleton of your first product.
