# Issue 1948: Sell the Transformation, Not the Tool

**One insight. No fluff.**

No one buys a drill because they want a drill. They buy it because they want a hole in the wall. That logic extends to every product you build. The feature list is not the sell. The before-and-after is. People do not buy what a thing is, they buy what it does to their situation.

This trips up builders constantly because they are in love with the thing they made. They understand every detail of the mechanism. They want to explain how it works because they are proud of how it works. But the customer is not there for the mechanism. They are there for the result. They want to know: will my life be different after this? Better, faster, easier?

Rewrite every product description with this test: does this sentence describe the tool or the transformation? Cut every sentence that only describes the tool. Replace it with what the customer gains, avoids, or becomes. The best copy does not explain the product at all. It describes the customer after they have used it.

*Build something worth owning.*
