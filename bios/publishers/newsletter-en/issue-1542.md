# Digital Products Are Not Passive Income. They Are Scalable Labor.

The phrase "passive income" is doing serious damage to how people build. Nothing about building a digital product is passive. The labor just happens upfront.

## What You Actually Build

A digital product is a system that delivers your knowledge without your presence. You still have to understand the problem deeply. You still have to structure a solution that works. You still have to write, record, or design something that earns trust. The passivity comes after real work.

Calling it passive income tricks people into expecting money before doing the hard part. Then they quit when the hard part shows up.

## The Product That Works

The best digital products solve one specific problem for one specific person. Not a broad course on productivity. A checklist that helps a freelance designer close clients faster. Specificity is what makes something worth paying for.

Most creators go too broad because they are afraid of excluding potential buyers. The opposite is true. Specificity is what makes someone feel seen enough to buy.

## Pricing Is a Signal

Cheap products attract people who do not want to implement. Higher prices attract buyers who are serious. This is not gatekeeping. It is filtering for the right customer.

Your price tells the buyer how much you believe in what you built. If you undercharge, you are undermining your own offer before anyone reads the description.

## This Week's Action

Pick one digital product idea you have been sitting on. Write the single sentence that explains who it is for and what problem it solves. No further action required this week. Just get the sentence right.
