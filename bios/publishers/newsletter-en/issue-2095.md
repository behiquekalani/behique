# Issue 2095: Price Is Communication

**One insight. No fluff.**

Price is not just a number. It is a signal. It tells people what category your product lives in, what quality to expect, what kind of person buys it, and how seriously they should take it. Most creators underprice their work not because the market demands it, but because they are uncomfortable with the discomfort of charging what it is worth.

Low prices attract high-friction customers. People who pay little tend to ask more, demand more, and value what they received less. This is not a character flaw. It is a psychological reality. What costs nothing gets treated accordingly. When you charge appropriately, you attract people who are serious, who follow through, and who actually get the result your product promises. The price filters for commitment.

Raising your price is also a way of improving your product without changing a single feature. The perception shifts. The customer shows up differently. The outcome improves because the person receiving it invested enough to take it seriously. This does not mean price gouging or charging more than value justifies. It means recognizing that the number you put on your work is part of the work. Getting it right is a design decision, not just an accounting one.
