# Issue 2395: Stop Optimizing What Is Not Working

**The One Thing:** Optimization only helps what already works. Fix the broken thing first.

---

There is a version of productivity that is actually sophisticated procrastination. You spend time making a funnel 3% more efficient when the real issue is the offer is wrong. You A/B test subject lines when the real issue is the list is cold. Optimization feels productive. Sometimes it is just avoidance with a spreadsheet.

## The Diagnostic Before the Fix

Before you optimize anything, run the diagnostic. Is the problem in the traffic, the conversion, or the product? If nobody is seeing the offer, fixing the conversion rate is irrelevant. If people are seeing the offer and not buying, the product pitch is broken, not the headline. Diagnose before you prescribe.

## The Honest Question You Are Avoiding

Most creators know what the broken thing is. They do not want to face it because fixing it is harder than optimizing around it. Rebuilding the offer is hard. Raising prices feels scary. Accepting that a product has no market hurts. So instead, they test thumbnails. Build the courage to work on the hard thing.

## When Optimization Is the Right Move

Optimization is the right move when you have evidence that the core works. If you have proven the offer converts, and you want to scale it, then yes. Test channels. Test formats. Optimize the funnel. But you need the baseline proof first. No baseline proof means no optimization phase. Go back to basics.

---

**This Week's Action:** Identify the one thing in your business that is not working that you have been avoiding. Write one honest sentence about why it is broken. Then write the first step to fix the actual problem.
