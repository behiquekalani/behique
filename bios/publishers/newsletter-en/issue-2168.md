# The Mental Model That Separates Builders from Consumers

Every piece of information you encounter is either raw material or entertainment. Most people treat everything as entertainment.

## The Idea

There's a fundamental difference in how builders and consumers process information. Consumers ask "is this interesting?" Builders ask "what can I make with this?"

This is not about working more hours. It's about developing a production orientation toward your own thinking. When a builder reads an essay, they're not just absorbing. They're asking: does this challenge something I believe? Does it add a dimension to an idea I'm developing? Can this become a product, a piece of content, a conversation?

The consumption trap is easy to fall into because the internet is optimized to keep you consuming. Every platform rewards time-on-app, not output-per-session. The algorithm benefits when you scroll. It doesn't care whether you ship anything.

Builders have to impose their own structure on top of this. The simplest version: a capture system. Anywhere you consume information, you have a way to extract and store what's useful. Not to archive it but to use it. An insight that sits in your notes unseen is the same as an insight you never had.

The next level is a review habit. You look at your captures once a week and ask: what can I do with this? Some become tweets. Some become newsletter issues. Some become product ideas. The raw material is already there. The question is whether you're treating it as input or just as content.

## Why It Matters

The gap between someone who reads a lot and someone who builds a lot is not intelligence. It's process. One collects. The other transforms. You can shift from one to the other in an afternoon with the right capture and review system.

## One Action

Set up a single inbox for ideas. It can be a note, a file, a voice memo folder. Anything. For the next week, every time you encounter something that sparks a thought, capture it immediately. Review at the end of the week and turn one capture into something you publish.
