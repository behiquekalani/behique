# A Content Schedule You Can Actually Keep

The best content strategy is one that doesn't burn you out by month two. Sustainability beats volume every time.

## Why Ambitious Schedules Fail
You set a goal to post every day. It works for two weeks. Then a project runs long, a personal thing comes up, and you miss a day. Then two. Then the guilt of falling behind stops you entirely.

This is not a discipline problem. It's a design problem. The schedule was built for a best-case version of your life, not the real one.

## Design Around Your Actual Capacity
Look at the last month honestly. How many pieces of content did you actually produce? Not how many you planned to. How many you actually did. That number is your real baseline. Build a sustainable schedule slightly above that. Not double it. Not triple it.

Growth in output comes from consistency over time, not ambition in week one.

## Batching as a System
One of the most effective shifts is moving from daily creation to weekly batching. Set aside one or two dedicated blocks to create everything for the week. This removes the daily decision fatigue. It lets you get into a flow state instead of constantly context-switching between creating and distributing.

Three hours on Monday can produce a full week of content.

## This Week's Action
Look at next week's calendar. Find a two or three hour block with no hard commitments. Schedule it as a content creation session. Prepare five pieces in that session. Do nothing else during that block.
