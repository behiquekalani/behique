# Decision-Making Is a Skill You Can Train

Most people treat big decisions like events. Something that happens to them. Better decision-makers treat it like a practice, something they do daily at small scale to build the muscle.

## The Cost of Decision Avoidance

Delayed decisions are not neutral. Every day you postpone a real choice, you pay a cognitive tax. The open loop stays active in the background, consuming attention that should be going toward execution.

Most stuck feeling is just deferred decisions. The project that stalls, the product that never launches, the conversation that never happens. Every one of those is a decision that got avoided.

## The Regret Minimization Frame

Jeff Bezos talked about imagining himself at 80 looking back. Would he regret taking the risk or not taking it? The question is not which choice is safer. It is which choice his future self would wish he had made.

This frame is useful because it shifts you out of present-fear mode and into long-view mode. Most fears are short-term. Most regrets are long-term. That asymmetry should change how you decide.

## Making Decisions Faster

Speed of decision is a competitive edge. The person who decides fast and adjusts based on feedback will always outperform the person who delays for certainty that never comes.

Perfect information is not available. Enough information to make a reasonable decision usually arrives quickly. Learn to recognize "enough." Make the call. Then pay attention to what the results teach you.

## This Week's Action

Identify one decision you have been avoiding for more than two weeks. Set a timer for ten minutes. Make the decision before the timer goes off. Write down the reasoning in two sentences. Then move.
