# Issue 1975: Depth Is the New Scarcity

**One insight. No fluff.**

Attention is cheap and abundant. Everyone is producing content, commentary, opinions, and noise at an unprecedented rate. What is genuinely rare is depth. The person who has actually thought about something carefully, gone past the obvious take, sat with the contradictions, and arrived at something non-obvious. That person is increasingly hard to find.

This creates an opportunity that most people overlook because it requires patience. Depth cannot be manufactured at scale. You cannot prompt your way into genuine understanding or batch-produce real expertise. It accumulates through time, repeated exposure, and the willingness to be confused long enough to understand. Speed works against it.

The practical implication: go deeper on fewer things. Most people spread attention across too many topics and develop shallow competence everywhere. Pick two or three areas and go further than anyone else in your orbit. Read the primary sources. Talk to practitioners. Build the actual thing. When you eventually write or speak from that depth, people feel the difference. It reads like something earned, because it was. That is what resonates in a world full of surface-level takes.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
