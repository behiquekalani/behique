# The Digital Product That Keeps Teaching

Most digital products are static. You build them once, sell them, and they age. The best digital products are designed to keep delivering insight on the second and third read.

## What Makes a Product Evergreen

Evergreen products are built on principles, not tactics. A product on "how to get more followers with current trends" expires in six months. A product on "how to build trust with an audience" will still be relevant in a decade. The underlying human behavior does not change. Platforms do.

This matters economically. An evergreen product does not need constant updates to stay valuable. It does not lose relevance after an algorithm change. It earns over a longer window with less maintenance.

## Depth Over Volume

Most creators chase product volume. More products means more revenue, right? Sometimes. But one deep, well-built product that solves a real problem is more valuable than five thin ones that each solve part of a problem. The deep product gets referred. It builds your reputation. It justifies a real price.

Before you build your next product, ask: is this adding depth or just volume?

## This Week's Action
Review your current product lineup. Identify the one that would survive a three-year time window. Double down on marketing that one before building another.
