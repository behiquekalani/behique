# Issue 2416: The Courage to Charge Real Prices

**The One Thing:** Underpricing is a confidence problem disguised as a generosity problem.

---

Charging low prices feels humble. It doesn't feel like fear. But fear is almost always the root. Fear of rejection. Fear that the product isn't good enough. Fear of someone thinking you're greedy. Fear that if you charge more, no one will buy.

That fear costs you money every single day you let it run the pricing decision.

## What Low Prices Communicate

Your price is a signal before anyone reads your description. A $9 product says: I'm not sure about this. A $97 product says: I know exactly what this does and I stand behind it.

Customers infer quality from price. Not always accurately. But consistently. When you price too low, some customers don't buy because they assume something must be wrong with it. Your low price is filtering out the buyers you actually want.

The buyers who haggle and ask for discounts are almost never your best customers. They're looking for cheap, not for value. Let your price filter for value seekers.

## The Pricing Experiment

Take your current price. Double it. Sell the next ten units at the doubled price. Watch what happens.

In most cases, the conversion rate does not drop in half. Often it barely moves. But your revenue doubles. That experiment, run once, changes how you think about pricing permanently.

## Increase Incrementally

If doubling feels impossible, raise by 20% each month. By month six, you've nearly tripled your price and done it slowly enough to see that the buyers didn't disappear.

Price like you believe in what you made.
