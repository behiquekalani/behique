# Design Work Around Your Biology

Every person has a biological rhythm that either supports or undermines their best work. Most people never figure out theirs because they're too busy following someone else's schedule.

## The Calendar Isn't Neutral
A 9-to-5 schedule was designed for factory output, not creative work. When you adopt it by default, you're accepting a framework built for a different kind of work, a different century, and a different person.

Your brain has natural peaks and valleys throughout the day. The question is whether your hardest work aligns with your peaks or fights against them. Most people do their deepest work during their recovery time and their shallow tasks during their peak time.

## Knowing Your Biology
Your peak cognitive window is the time when abstract thinking, problem-solving, and creative work feel effortless compared to the rest of the day. For most people it's 2-4 hours long. It happens at different times depending on your chronotype.

Find your window first. Protect it second. Everything else schedules around it.

## What Designing Your Day Actually Takes
It takes experimentation and honest self-observation. Test different schedules for two weeks at a time. Track when you produce your best work and when you produce your worst. The data is in your own patterns.

It also takes the willingness to say no to meetings, requests, and social norms that violate your peak window. This is a harder skill than it sounds.

## This Week's Action
For five days, track every working hour. Note the quality of your output each hour on a simple 1-3 scale. At the end of the week, identify your two best hours. Rearrange next week around them.
