# Your Attention Is the Product. Protect It.

Every platform you use is in the business of selling your focus to advertisers. You are not the customer. You are the inventory.

## The Economy Nobody Warns You About
There is an entire industry built around capturing and holding human attention. Billions of dollars in engineering talent optimized to keep you scrolling, watching, reacting. Not because it helps you. Because it helps them.

The solopreneur who doesn't understand this will spend most of their day reacting to other people's agendas. The one who does understand it treats attention like cash. You spend it deliberately or you waste it.

## What Deep Work Actually Requires
It's not a quiet room. It's a closed browser. It's a phone in another space. It's a 90-minute block where nothing interrupts the single task in front of you. Deep work feels uncomfortable at first because we've been trained to expect constant stimulation.

That discomfort is the signal you're doing it right.

## Build the Habit Before You Need It
Don't wait until you're behind on a project to start protecting your focus. Build the structure now. Set the hours. Kill the notifications. Tell the people around you what those hours mean. Consistency in the small things creates the capacity for the big ones.

## This Week's Action
Block two 90-minute focus sessions on your calendar for this week. No email, no social, no messages. Work on one output during each block. Track what you produce versus a normal two-hour stretch and compare.
