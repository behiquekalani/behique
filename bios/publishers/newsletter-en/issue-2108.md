# Issue 2108: Specificity Is a Superpower

**One insight. No fluff.**

Vague goals produce vague results. "I want to be healthier" produces nothing. "I will walk 30 minutes after dinner on weekdays" produces a habit. The specificity is not a detail. It is the entire mechanism. Generality lets your brain stay comfortable without committing. Specificity forces a decision.

This works in reverse too. Specific problems are easier to solve than vague ones. "My business is struggling" is unsolvable. "My conversion rate is 0.3% and the industry average is 2%" is a problem with a solution. The more precisely you can name the gap, the closer you already are to closing it. Naming is half the work.

Apply this to your thinking, your goals, and especially your content. The creator who speaks to one specific person in one specific situation outperforms the one who broadcasts to everyone. Specificity builds trust because it proves you actually understand. Anyone can make a general claim. Only someone who has been there can get the detail right. Get specific. It is the clearest sign that you know what you are talking about.
