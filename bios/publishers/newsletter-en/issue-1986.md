# Issue 1986: The Real Cost of Optionality

**One insight. No fluff.**

Keeping your options open sounds like wisdom. Do not close doors. Stay flexible. Maintain freedom. It is advice designed to protect you from commitment, which is ironic because commitment is the only mechanism that produces real results. Optionality feels like freedom. Most of the time, it is paralysis with better branding.

The cost of keeping options open is attention. Every open option requires maintenance. You have to keep it alive in your mind, revisit it occasionally, justify not choosing it. Multiply that across ten or twenty open decisions and you have a mind running mostly on overhead. Nothing gets the full attention it needs because everything is getting partial attention.

The high performers you admire are not more talented. They are more committed. They chose a direction early, often before it was certain, and they put everything into it. The choosing itself created the clarity. The closed door stopped the mental drain. If you are waiting to know enough before you commit, you will wait forever. Commit first. Clarity is what commitment produces, not what it requires.
