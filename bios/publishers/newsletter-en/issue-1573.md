# Consistency Is Not Discipline. It's Design.

Willpower runs out. Good systems don't require it.

## The Discipline Myth
Consistency is usually framed as a character trait. You either have it or you don't. You're either disciplined or you're not. This framing is wrong and it's why most people fail to maintain it.

Discipline is a finite resource. It depletes across the day, across the week, under stress. Designing your environment and schedule so the right behavior is the default requires almost no discipline at all.

## What Design Actually Looks Like
It's writing at the same time every day so you don't have to decide when to write. It's batching content creation into one focused session instead of fighting yourself daily. It's setting up your workspace so that starting is easy. Removing the friction from the behavior you want to repeat is more effective than motivating yourself to push through friction.

The most consistent creators are not the most disciplined. They've made the right behavior the path of least resistance.

## Building Consistency That Holds
Start smaller than you think you need to. Consistency is about showing up, not about volume. Publishing once a week for a year beats publishing daily for a month and burning out.

The metric is: can you sustain this indefinitely? If the answer is no, you've set the bar too high. Lower it until the answer is yes.

## This Week's Action
Pick one creative output you want to be consistent about. Define the minimum viable version of it. The smallest thing you'd be willing to do every week without exception. Schedule it. Do that version first, then build from there.
