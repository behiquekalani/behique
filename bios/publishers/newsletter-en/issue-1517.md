# The Minimum Viable Routine

You do not need a perfect morning routine. You need a consistent minimum.

## Why Routines Collapse

Most routines fail because they require everything to go right. They are 90-minute affairs that only work when the schedule is clear and the energy is high. Then one bad morning breaks the streak. The streak was the goal. Now it is gone.

The minimum viable routine survives bad days. It is five minutes on the worst day and ninety minutes on the best. The floor is what matters, not the ceiling.

## What Goes in the Minimum

One grounding act. For some people it is writing. For others it is movement or reading. One act that signals to your brain that the day belongs to you before it belongs to everyone else.

Then one high-leverage action before you check anything reactive. Email is reactive. Slack is reactive. Building is proactive. Start with the proactive work. Let the reactive work wait.

## The Asymmetry of Mornings

The first two hours of the day disproportionately determine the quality of the rest. Not because of productivity math. Because of identity reinforcement.

If you spend the first two hours consuming and reacting, you build the identity of a consumer and reactor. If you spend them creating, you build the identity of a creator. What you do first shapes who you are for the rest of the day.

## This Week's Action

Design your minimum viable morning. Three things. Maximum 30 minutes. Do it every day this week, even when circumstances are bad. The minimum is the system.
