# The Niche Is Not a Cage

Specificity is not limitation. It is the fastest path to being found.

## The Idea
Creators resist niching down because it feels like shrinking. If you narrow your focus, you are excluding potential customers. The logic seems sound. It is wrong.

The internet is a scale machine. Narrow content reaches fewer people per post but attracts a far higher percentage of exactly the right people. A general fitness account competes with millions of creators for general attention. A fitness account specifically for parents with 30 minutes a day has a much smaller audience, but that audience is far more likely to buy, share, and stay.

The niche is not where you stay forever. It is the entry point. Once you have earned trust and built a foundation in a specific space, you can expand from a position of authority rather than desperation.

The other thing most creators miss: being specific about your niche does not mean only talking about one topic. It means filtering everything through a specific lens. The lens makes your perspective distinct. Distinct perspectives are what people follow, share, and pay for.

Generic content gets consumed and forgotten. Specific content gets saved, recommended, and purchased.

## Why It Matters
If your content could have been written by anyone, it will be remembered by no one. The goal is to create content where readers think "this was made for me." That feeling comes from specificity, not from breadth.

## One Action
Write one sentence that defines your niche in three layers: who it serves, what problem it solves, and who it is different from. Post it somewhere visible. Every piece of content should pass through that filter.
