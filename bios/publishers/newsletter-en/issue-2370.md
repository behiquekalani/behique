# Mental Models Are Competitive Advantages

How you think determines what you see. What you see determines what you build.

## The Idea
A mental model is a simplified framework for understanding how something works. The person who has more accurate models makes better decisions faster and with less information.

Most people operate on inherited mental models. They think about money, work, and success the way they were taught by default. These defaults are rarely optimized for building something independent. They are optimized for employment, stability, and waiting for permission.

The builder's advantage comes from deliberately replacing these defaults. First principles thinking: break problems down to their base components instead of copying existing solutions. Inversion: identify what would guarantee failure and avoid those things. Second-order thinking: ask what happens after the obvious consequence.

These are not academic exercises. They change the quality of questions you ask. A person with second-order thinking does not just ask "will this product sell?" They ask "what does it mean for my positioning if this sells to people who are not my target customer?" That question prevents expensive mistakes.

Mental models compound. Each new framework adds to a library you draw on continuously. The people who seem to make decisions faster and better are usually drawing on a richer model library, not raw intelligence.

The good news is that models are learnable. Reading broadly, thinking slowly, and testing ideas against reality are the only curriculum required.

## Why It Matters
Business decisions made with weak models are expensive. Decisions made with strong models are fast, confident, and more often right. Your model library is the most portable competitive advantage you have.

## One Action
Pick one mental model you have not used recently. Inversion, opportunity cost, or Pareto. Apply it to your biggest current business question and write down what it reveals. Add the insight to a notes file you keep.
