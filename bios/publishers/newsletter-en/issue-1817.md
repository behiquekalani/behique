# Issue 1817: The Myth of Passive Income

**One insight. No fluff.**

Every income stream called "passive" was built with intense active effort first. The digital product that earns while you sleep took weeks to research, write, and package. The course that generates recurring revenue took months to build and longer to market. The newsletter that prints subscriptions took years of consistent writing.

Passive income is deferred active income. The passivity comes later, if at all.

This matters because the framing sets wrong expectations. People build one thing, it earns nothing in week one, and they call it a failure. They didn't fail. They stopped too early. The passive phase of a revenue stream requires patience, iteration, and often more work than the initial build. Distribution, discovery, trust. These take time and consistent attention.

Call it what it is: recurring income from prior work. That's still worth building. Just don't expect it to be easy or fast. Build it anyway.
