# Issue 2476: The Digital Product That Doesn't Require Perfection

**The Idea:** Digital products are the most forgiving business model that exists. A physical product has to be right before it ships. A software product needs to work. A digital guide, a template, a framework document, it can ship at 70 percent and improve from there.

This matters because the enemy of shipping is perfectionism. The creator who waits until everything is ready ships once a year if they're lucky. The one who ships good enough and iterates ships twelve times and has real feedback data before the first one finishes their prep work.

The reader who buys your guide doesn't need it to be the definitive work. They need it to help them solve a specific problem. If it does that, it's complete. Everything else is polish.

The willingness to ship something functional instead of waiting for something perfect is a competitive advantage. It means you're in the market learning while others are still at the whiteboard planning.

Version one is not the final product. It's the first conversation with your audience. The feedback from real buyers will tell you what to improve, what to add, what was actually valuable. You can't get that feedback from planning.

Done is not the enemy of good. Done is the prerequisite for good.

**Why It Matters:** A digital product at 70 percent shipped earns money and generates feedback. A product at 100 percent that hasn't shipped earns nothing and generates no learning. The market only responds to what exists.

**One Action:** Pick one digital product idea you've been thinking about. Define the minimum version: what does it need to include to genuinely solve the problem? Build that version only. Ship it.
