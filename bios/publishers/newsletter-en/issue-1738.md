# Issue 1738: Specificity Sells

**One insight. No fluff.**

Generic advice does not sell. Generic products do not sell. Generic positioning does not sell. Everything generic competes on price, which is a race to the bottom that nobody wins except the person with the lowest costs. Specificity is the exit. The more precisely you define who your product is for and what specific outcome it delivers, the less competition you face and the higher you can charge.

"I help people with productivity" is generic. "I help early-career engineers build a second-brain system that gets them promoted in eighteen months" is specific. Same core skill. Completely different market position. The second version has a clearer buyer, a clearer problem, and a clearer outcome. It commands a premium and attracts people who are ready to buy.

Go back to your product, your offer, or your content and ask: what specific person, with what specific problem, gets what specific outcome? If you cannot answer that in one sentence, the positioning is still too vague. Specificity is not limiting. It is clarifying. And clarity is what makes people reach for their wallet.
