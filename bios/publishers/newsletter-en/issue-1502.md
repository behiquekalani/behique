# Charge More. Apologize Less.

Underpricing is not humility. It is fear dressed up as fairness.

## The Price Is a Signal

When you charge $10 for something worth $500, you are not being generous. You are telling the market you do not believe in what you built. Price communicates value before the buyer reads a single word of your sales page.

High prices attract serious buyers. Serious buyers get results. Results become testimonials. Testimonials justify the price. The cycle reinforces itself.

## Why Creators Stay Cheap

Most creators price based on how long something took to make. That is the wrong variable. Price based on the value delivered, not the hours invested.

A PDF that saves someone $10,000 is worth more than $47. A framework that saves someone 200 hours is worth more than a monthly subscription fee. Stop pricing your work like an hourly contractor.

## The Upgrade Happens in Your Head First

You cannot charge premium prices while holding a discount-store identity. The internal shift has to come first. Decide you are the kind of person who creates premium work. Then price it that way.

Nobody is going to give you permission to charge what you are worth. You take it.

## This Week's Action

Look at your lowest-priced offer. Double it. Run it for two weeks and track whether demand actually drops. Most of the time, it does not.
