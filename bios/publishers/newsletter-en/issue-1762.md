# Issue 1762: Price Is a Signal You Send Yourself
**One insight. No fluff.**

Pricing your work low is not humility. It is a statement about what you believe your work is worth. And that belief shapes how you build, how you sell, and who you attract. Cheap prices pull cheap buyers. Cheap buyers drain energy. Drained energy produces worse work.

The cycle is self-reinforcing. You price low, attract difficult clients, hate the work, produce less, and conclude you are not ready to charge more. But the sequence started with the price, not the quality.

Raise the price before you feel ready. You will build toward it. The customer who pays twice as much asks better questions, respects your time, and tells better people. That feedback loop compounds. Pricing is not just strategy. It is the first sentence of the story you tell about your work.
