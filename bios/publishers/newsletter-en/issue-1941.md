# Issue 1941: The Work Before the Work

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people wait until they feel ready to start. They mistake preparation for procrastination and call it being thorough. The problem is that readiness is a feeling, not a state. It never arrives on its own.

The work before the work is unglamorous. It is the first draft no one reads, the script you write before the camera turns on, the code you delete because it taught you what not to do. This phase has no audience and no reward. That is exactly why most people skip it.

But the builders who compound fastest are the ones who love the ugly draft. They know that output quality is determined before the public-facing work begins. The research, the failed tests, the rough outline. That is where the actual craft happens. Everything after is just execution on a foundation you either built or skipped.
