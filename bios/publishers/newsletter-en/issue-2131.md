# Issue 2131: Reading People Is a Skill You Can Build

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people think understanding others is intuitive. Either you have it or you do not. This is wrong. Reading people is a learnable skill, and like all learnable skills, it improves with deliberate practice and honest feedback. The gap between intuitive readers and analytical readers is mostly attention. One group pays close attention. The other group pays attention to themselves.

The foundation is behavioral consistency. People reveal themselves through patterns, not single moments. Someone who is late once is human. Someone who is late to everything that benefits you but on time to everything that benefits them is showing you a consistent data point. Patterns require time and patience to see, which is why most people miss them.

The deeper layer is what people do when they think you are not watching. In public, behavior is managed. In unguarded moments, it is not. Who someone is at dinner is different from who they are when they think their behavior has no audience. Pay attention to both. The delta between the two is the most useful information you will collect.
