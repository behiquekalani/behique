# Issue 1837: You Are Building an Asset or a Job

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people who work for themselves have not escaped employment. They have just changed employers from a company to their clients. If your income stops the moment you stop working, you have a job, not a business. The distinction is not about how hard you work. It is about what you own.

An asset is something that produces value without requiring your direct labor every time. A product, a system, a body of content, a piece of software. These things work while you sleep, while you are sick, while you are on vacation. Building toward ownership of assets is a different project than being good at a skill that clients pay for directly.

You can start with the skill. That is often the right move. Use the income from the skill to fund the time required to build the asset. But the mistake is getting comfortable with the skill-income and never making the transition. Skills plateau. Assets compound. The person who figures out how to package their skill into something others can buy without booking their time has escaped the trap. That is the actual goal.

*Build something worth owning.*
