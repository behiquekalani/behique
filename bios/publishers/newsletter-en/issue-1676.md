# Ship It Imperfect. Fix It Live.

The perfect version of your product that does not exist yet cannot help anyone.

## Perfectionism Is a Defense Mechanism
Perfectionism feels like high standards. It is usually a way to avoid the discomfort of being judged. If you never ship, you can never fail publicly. But you also cannot learn, cannot earn, and cannot help the people who need what you are building.

The market does not reward the best product. It rewards the one that shows up first with something good enough. Good enough today beats perfect in six months.

## What Launching Imperfect Teaches You
The first version of anything reveals information that no amount of planning can surface. Your first product launch tells you what people actually value, not what you assumed they would. Your first course cohort tells you which modules they skip and which ones they replay. That information is the product of shipping, not planning. You have to be in the market to learn from the market.

## The Iteration Model
Think of your launch as version one, not the final version. Communicate that to your audience. Tell them you are iterating based on their feedback. This framing turns an imperfect product into a collaborative one. It also gives you permission to improve it over time, which is what all good products do anyway.

The creators who ship fast and improve often outperform the ones who perfect and release once.

## This Week's Action
Identify one thing you have been perfecting instead of shipping. Set a deadline for a version one release. Write down the three things that must be in it and accept that everything else ships in version two.
