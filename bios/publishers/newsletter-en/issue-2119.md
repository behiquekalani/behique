# Issue 2119: Most Advice Is Wrong for You

**One insight. No fluff.**

Advice is contextual. It comes from someone else's situation, applied to their skills, their risk tolerance, their resources, and their timing. When you take advice without filtering for context, you are borrowing a solution to a problem that is probably not the same as yours. Most advice fails in transfer, not in quality.

The person who tells you to post daily built an audience in a different era. The person who tells you to niche down maximally solved a different problem than the one you have. The person who says to ignore revenue until you have product-market fit has a different financial situation than you. These are not bad advisors. They are accurate historians of their own experience. That is different from a prescription for yours.

The better move is to extract principles instead of tactics. Why did posting daily work? Because it built consistency and forced iteration. Can you get those benefits another way? Probably. Why did niching down work? Because specificity builds trust with a specific person. How specific do you need to be for your goal? Maybe less extreme. Filter advice for the principle underneath it, then adapt the principle to your context. That is how you use other people's experience without becoming a copy of them.
