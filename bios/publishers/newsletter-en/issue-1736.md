# Issue 1736: Know What You Are Optimizing For

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people are optimizing for things they never consciously chose. They are optimizing for approval because their environment rewarded that. For safety because fear is a loud signal. For busyness because it looks like productivity from the outside. None of these are wrong per se, but optimizing for them unconsciously means your life moves in a direction you did not pick.

The question to ask once a quarter: what am I actually optimizing for right now? Not what you say you want. What your calendar, your decisions, and your spending reveal you want. There is usually a gap between those two lists. That gap is the problem.

Once you identify what you are actually optimizing for, you can decide if it is still what you want. Sometimes it is. Sometimes you realize you have been building toward someone else's definition of success for years. Course correction starts with honesty. Name what you are building toward. Make sure it is yours. Then build toward it deliberately, not by default.
