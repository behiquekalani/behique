# Issue 2002: Why Most People Never Finish Anything

**One insight. No fluff.**

Starting feels like progress. The blank doc, the new repo, the fresh notebook. That feeling of momentum at the beginning is real, but it is also borrowed. You are spending future energy in the present. When the borrowed energy runs out, most people quit and start something new to get the feeling back.

Finishing is a different skill than starting. It requires sitting with ugly, incomplete work and caring about it anyway. It means returning to a project when it is no longer exciting, when the problems are harder than expected, and when nothing external is pushing you forward. Most creative and technical work lives in that middle zone. That is where most people disappear.

The builders who ship consistently are not more talented. They just have a different relationship with the ugly middle. They do not expect it to feel good. They treat momentum as something you manufacture rather than wait for. Show up on a schedule, do the smallest useful thing, and get out. Do it again tomorrow. The finished product does not care how you felt while making it.
