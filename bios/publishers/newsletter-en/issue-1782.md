# Issue 1782: Price Is a Signal You Send Before Anyone Buys

**One insight. No fluff.**

Pricing is not math. It is communication. When you price something low because you are afraid nobody will pay more, you are not being humble. You are telling every potential buyer that you do not believe in what you built. That signal travels. People pick up on it before they even read your sales page.

High prices do not repel buyers. They filter them. A $200 product attracts people who have already decided to invest in the problem you solve. A $19 product attracts people who are still on the fence about whether the problem is worth solving at all. Those are different customers with different commitment levels, different results, and different relationships with you.

Raise your price before you feel ready. You will attract fewer leads and more buyers. The quality of feedback improves. The refund rate drops. Most people discover this after years of undercharging. You do not have to wait that long.
