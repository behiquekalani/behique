# Issue 1813: Solving the Right Problem

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most failed products didn't fail because of poor execution. They failed because they solved a problem nobody had, or solved the wrong version of a real problem. The builder worked hard on something that didn't matter.

Working hard on the wrong problem is still the wrong problem.

Before building, the most important question isn't how. It's what, for whom, and why now. What pain are you solving? Who specifically feels it? Why is the current solution not good enough? If you can answer those three questions with specifics, not hypotheticals, you're probably onto something real. If your answers are vague, the product will be too.

Talk to the people you think you're building for. Not to validate your idea. To understand their actual frustration in their actual words. Then build the thing they described, not the thing you imagined. It's a harder creative constraint. It produces better products.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
