# Issue 1876: How to Think When Everyone Is Reacting

**One insight. No fluff.**

When something big happens, most people react within minutes. The take is hot, the emotion is high, and the post goes out before any real thinking has occurred. This is not opinion. It is reflex dressed as opinion. The crowded end of any news cycle is full of people performing their reaction to other people's reactions. Nothing original lives there.

The opportunity is in the delay. Wait 48 hours. Let the first wave of reaction clear. Ask what most people missed, what the obvious take ignored, and what the event actually reveals about something deeper and more permanent. That is the essay worth writing. That is the analysis worth reading. The slow take wins on quality even when it loses on timing.

This applies beyond current events. Most conversations, most trends, most business moments reward whoever slows down enough to think past the surface. The contrarian is not always right, but the reflexive taker is almost always shallow. Develop the habit of pausing before forming a position. Ask what you actually believe after the noise settles. Then write that.
