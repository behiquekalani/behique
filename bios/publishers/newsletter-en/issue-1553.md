# Niche Authority: The Narrower You Go, The Bigger You Get

Going broad feels safe. Wider appeal, larger potential audience. It is also a guarantee of being forgettable. The market has no shortage of generalists.

## Why Narrow Wins

A specific person with a specific problem wants a specific solution. Not a general guide. Not broad principles. They want someone who understands their exact situation and speaks directly to it.

When you are specific, your content makes people feel seen. That feeling is the foundation of loyalty. Broad content makes people feel informed. Specific content makes them feel understood. One of those converts. One does not.

## The Fear of Niching Down

"What if my audience is too small?" This question is based on a false assumption: that you need a massive audience to build a viable business. You do not. You need a few hundred people who trust you enough to buy.

One thousand true fans is not a metaphor. It is a real number. One thousand people who buy something from you once a year at a hundred dollars is a six-figure business. That audience is achievable in almost any niche.

## How to Find Your Niche Intersection

Your niche is at the intersection of a topic you understand deeply, an audience you can speak to authentically, and a problem that costs people something to leave unsolved.

Find a real problem. Talk to the people who have it. Build the solution. The audience is already there. They are just waiting for someone specific enough to find them.

## This Week's Action

Write a one-paragraph description of the most specific version of your ideal audience. Include their situation, their problem, and what they have already tried. The more specific the paragraph, the more useful it will be.
