# Issue 1964: Opinions Are Infrastructure

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people treat opinions like accessories. Something you wear when it is convenient and take off when the room disagrees with you. That approach feels safe, but it costs you something real: the respect of people who actually know things.

Strong, reasoned opinions are infrastructure. They are the load-bearing walls of how you think. When you have thought something through and arrived at a conclusion, that conclusion becomes the foundation for the next one. Your thinking compounds. People who have no real opinions have no base to build from, so they rebuild from zero every time the conversation changes.

The risk is not that you will be wrong. You will be wrong. Everyone is. The risk is that you will develop the habit of having no real view on anything. That habit produces a particular kind of person: agreeable, forgettable, and incapable of the kind of conviction that builds anything lasting. Have a take. Defend it. Update it when better evidence arrives. That cycle is how real thinking works.
