# Issue 1963: You Are the Product of Your Information Diet

**One insight. No fluff.**

Every idea you have ever had came from somewhere. A book, a conversation, a video, a problem you sat with long enough to solve. The quality of your thinking is inseparable from the quality of what you consume. You cannot think better thoughts on worse inputs.

Most people treat their information diet like they treat their actual diet: they know what would be good for them, but they default to whatever is easiest and most stimulating. The result is the same. Intellectual junk food produces mental fog, borrowed opinions, and shallow takes. You scroll for hours and produce nothing original.

The fix is curation, not abstinence. You do not need to stop consuming. You need to stop consuming passively. Read things that disagree with you. Read primary sources instead of summaries. Read books that require a second pass. The goal is friction. Friction is what forces the brain to actually process instead of just absorb. You become more useful when you are harder to fill up.
