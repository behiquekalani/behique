# The 100-Day Rule for Anyone Building Something New

Every new skill, project, or platform feels broken for the first hundred days. That feeling is not feedback. It is friction.

## The First Hundred Days Are Not Representative

People quit new pursuits based on feedback that is not yet meaningful. Ten posts with low engagement. A newsletter with thirty subscribers after two months. A product with no sales in the first week.

These data points are real. They are not useful. The sample size is too small, your skills are too early, and your audience is too new. Making strategy decisions from this data is like reading a book's first page and deciding you understand the theme.

The first hundred days are about skill building, not results. You are not yet good enough at the thing to know what good performance looks like.

## What Changes After Hundred Days

Two things happen around the hundred-day mark. Your skills improve enough that the work gets easier. And you accumulate enough output to actually see patterns.

You can see which content resonates. You can see what the audience responds to. You have a baseline. Before that baseline exists, you are operating without real information. After it exists, you can make legitimate strategic decisions.

Quitting at day thirty is not pivoting. It is abandoning the experiment before the results come in.

## The Commitment Changes Your Behavior

Knowing you are committed to a hundred days changes how you approach each day. You are not auditioning. You are practicing. The pressure of judgment lifts. You do the work because the work is the point, not because today's post might be the breakthrough.

That shift in orientation is itself what produces the breakthrough. The people who treat every post as a lottery ticket get worse results than the people who treat every post as practice.

## This Week's Action

Identify a project or platform you started and abandoned before hitting the hundred-day mark. Decide honestly: did you quit because the strategy was genuinely flawed, or because the early feedback was discouraging? If the latter, consider restarting with a hundred-day commitment before evaluating.
