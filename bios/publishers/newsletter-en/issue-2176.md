# Stop Waiting to Be Discovered

Discovery is not a passive event. It's the result of deliberate work done consistently over a long time.

## The Idea

There's a fantasy version of creator success that most people carry without realizing it. In that version, you do good work, eventually the right person notices, and things take off. It's the discovery myth. It's mostly fiction.

The creators who seem to be discovered overnight usually have ten years of invisible work behind them. The "overnight" part is when the compounding became visible to the public. The work was happening long before anyone was paying attention.

This matters because the discovery myth produces a specific kind of passivity. You make things, post them, and wait. You check metrics looking for signs of takeoff. When they don't come, you wonder what's wrong with your content, your niche, your timing. You never consider that the problem might be the expectation itself.

Active distribution is the alternative. You don't just make content. You put it in front of people. You comment in communities where your audience already is. You send cold emails to people who need to know about your work. You build relationships with adjacent creators. You treat distribution as a skill to develop, not a thing that happens to you.

The creators who scale fastest combine quality content with aggressive distribution. They don't wait for the algorithm. They stack the distribution manually until the algorithm has enough signal to take over.

## Why It Matters

You can't control virality. You can control where you show up and how often. Consistent presence in the right spaces is more reliable than any viral strategy.

## One Action

Identify three communities where your ideal buyer spends time online. This week, add genuine value in each one. Not promotion. Just answers, perspective, and presence. Do this for four weeks and track what changes.
