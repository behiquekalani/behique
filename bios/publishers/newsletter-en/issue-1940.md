# Issue 1940: The Return on Invested Time

**One insight. No fluff.**

Return on invested capital is a standard financial metric. Return on invested time is not tracked by most people, but it is the more important number. Capital can be replenished. Time cannot. The allocation of your hours is the most consequential financial decision you make every day, even if it does not feel like one.

Most people spend their time on activities that generate no compounding return. Passive consumption, low-leverage busywork, meetings that could have been a message. These are caloric activities. They burn time without building anything. The hours are gone and nothing exists that was not there before.

High-ROI time looks different. Writing that builds an audience. Building a skill that increases earning capacity. Creating a product that generates recurring revenue. Making a connection that opens new options. These activities compound. The same hour spent on them becomes more valuable over time because the output accumulates. The discipline is identifying which category each activity falls into and deliberately shifting hours from the first to the second. You already have all the time you need. It is an allocation problem.
