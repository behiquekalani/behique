# Issue 1882: Depth Is the New Scarcity

**One insight. No fluff.**

Attention is everywhere. Depth is rare. Every platform is engineered to produce scrollers, not thinkers. The result is a world full of people who have consumed millions of words and retained almost none of them. Surface-level engagement has never been more abundant or more worthless.

The person who reads one book slowly, takes notes, and connects the ideas to their actual work will outcompete the person who reads fifty books and moves on. The person who builds one skill past the point of comfort will outcompete the person who samples ten. Not because the quantity was wrong, but because depth compounds and breadth alone does not.

Your competitive advantage is not how much you can consume. It is how far you are willing to go on the things that matter. Choose one thing. Go deeper than anyone expects. That is where the value is.
