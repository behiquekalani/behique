# Issue 2123: Depth Is the New Scarcity

**One insight. No fluff.**

Everything is surface level now. Feeds are full of takes. Hot, fast, frictionless takes that vanish in four hours. Nobody goes deep because depth takes time and time does not perform well in a scroll environment. This is not a complaint. It is an opportunity.

When everyone goes wide, depth becomes rare. The person willing to spend forty hours understanding one topic thoroughly will produce something the person who skims ten articles cannot. The output is qualitatively different. It answers the question behind the question. It earns trust in a way that aggregated surface content never will.

You do not need to know everything. You need to know something deeply enough to lead people through it. Depth is not about credentials or years of experience. It is about the willingness to sit with a hard problem until you actually understand it. That willingness is increasingly uncommon. Which means it is increasingly valuable.
