# Issue 1939: Specificity Is the Differentiator

**One insight. No fluff.**

Generic content is everywhere. Generic advice, generic products, generic positioning. The market is flooded with solutions for "everyone" that are actually useful for no one in particular. The way to cut through is not to be louder. It is to be more specific than anyone else is willing to be.

Specificity is uncomfortable because it feels like it shrinks your audience. If you say your product is for freelance designers who work alone and charge under $100 an hour, you have eliminated most potential buyers. What you have also done is become unmistakably relevant to the people who remain. They will feel seen. Feeling seen is rare. Rare things command attention.

The path to a broad audience almost always runs through a narrow one first. You build deep trust with a specific group. They tell others. The specificity becomes the origin story rather than the limitation. The creator who starts by speaking to one thousand people very precisely ends up speaking to one hundred thousand people very credibly. The creator who starts by speaking to everyone usually ends up speaking to no one effectively.
