# Everything You Know Is a Product Waiting to Exist

You already have more sellable knowledge than you think. The problem is you've lived with it so long it doesn't feel valuable anymore.

## The Curse of Expertise
When you know something deeply, it stops feeling special. You forget that most people don't know what you know. The skill that took you three years to develop could save someone else three years of trial and error. That time savings has real monetary value.

The curse of expertise is thinking your knowledge is obvious. It isn't. Not to the person who needs it.

## What Productizing Knowledge Actually Means
It means taking what you know and packaging it in a format that transfers that knowledge without requiring your direct presence. A written guide. A course. A template. A process document. A done-for-you framework.

The goal is to answer the question: what would someone need to know to get the result I can get? Write that down. That document is a product.

## Starting Small and Specific
The biggest mistake is trying to productize everything you know at once. Start narrow. Pick one specific outcome you can help someone achieve. Create the minimum product that delivers that outcome. Sell it to ten people. Improve it based on their feedback. Then think about expanding.

A $27 PDF that solves one specific problem is a real business.

## This Week's Action
Write down three specific things you know how to do that others in your circle regularly ask you about. Pick the one that takes the least time to teach. Outline a simple guide that covers it. You have the start of a product.
