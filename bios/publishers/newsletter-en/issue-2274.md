# The Repurposing Engine

Most creators create from scratch every time. Smart ones create once and distribute everywhere.

## The Idea

Content creation is expensive in time and energy. Every piece you make from scratch is a fixed cost. Repurposing turns one creation event into multiple distribution events. It's the closest thing to a content multiplier that actually works.

The engine works like this. You start with a long-form piece. A newsletter, a video, a long essay. That piece is your source document. It contains ideas, frameworks, examples, and conclusions. Each of those is a potential standalone piece of shorter content.

From one newsletter issue, you can extract five tweets, two LinkedIn posts, a quote graphic, a thread, and a short-form video script. The ideas are already formed. The structure is already there. You're not creating. You're packaging.

Most people don't do this because it feels like recycling. It's not. Different platforms serve different audiences at different attention levels. The person who reads your full newsletter is different from the person who sees your tweet. The short-form version introduces your thinking to people who would never have found the long-form.

The practical system is simple. Write long-form once per week. Spend two hours breaking it into platform-specific formats. Schedule or post everything. Repeat.

You get more output with less creation effort. The quality stays high because you're working from your best thinking, not rushing to generate filler.

## Why It Matters

Volume of content distributed is directly correlated with audience growth. Repurposing lets you maintain that volume without burning yourself out creating from zero every single day.

## One Action

Take your last long-form piece. Set a timer for 30 minutes. Pull out five ideas, quotes, or frameworks that could stand alone as tweets or short posts. Write them. That's your next batch of content done.
