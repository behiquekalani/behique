# Issue 2417: How to Create Content When You Have No Ideas

**The One Thing:** Ideas don't come to idle minds, they come to minds that are already working.

---

Waiting for inspiration is the wrong strategy. The creator who waits for a great idea before starting will post less than the one who starts writing and lets the idea emerge through the process.

Writing is not the output of thinking. It is the process of thinking.

## The Idea Generation System

Document everything. When you read something that makes you pause, write down why. When you hear something in a conversation that surprises you, note it. When you have a thought at 11pm that feels important, capture it.

Most great content ideas start as a reaction to something else. A book you disagreed with. An experience that went differently than expected. A question someone asked you that you didn't have a clean answer for. Those friction points are where your best ideas live.

Keep a running list. When you sit down to create content, pull from the list instead of waiting for the muse.

## The Angle Shift

You do not need new topics. You need new angles on existing topics. Take one subject you've covered before. Now look at it from the beginner's perspective, the advanced practitioner's perspective, a counterintuitive angle, a personal story angle, a common mistake angle.

One topic, five angles, five pieces of content. Your list of topics is probably already large enough to post for a year if you work the angles.

## Constraints Produce Creativity

Give yourself a constraint: write a post in under 150 words. Or write it in exactly three sentences. Or write it as a list of five things. Constraints force your brain to find new shapes for the same ideas.

Unlimited options produce blank pages. Constraints produce output.

---

**This Week's Action:** Capture every idea that passes through your mind this week, no matter how small. Write it down immediately. Review the list on Sunday.
