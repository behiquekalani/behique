# Issue 2067: Price Is a Signal, Not a Number

**One insight. No fluff.**

Low prices do not attract better customers. They attract customers who need convincing. The customer who argues about price before buying will argue about deliverables after. The customer who pays without negotiating has already decided you are worth it. These are different relationships from the start, and they create different outcomes for your energy, your work, and your reputation.

Price communicates positioning. A $10 product says: this is disposable, try it without thinking. A $500 product says: this requires commitment, and commitment changes behavior. People who invest seriously engage seriously. They implement, report back, leave reviews, refer others. The economics of premium pricing are not just about margin. They are about client quality.

The fear of charging more is really a fear of rejection made financial. If you raise your price and someone says no, it feels like they are rejecting you, not your offer. That fear keeps more people underpriced than any market condition. The real question is not "what will the market bear?" It is: "what price selects for the clients I actually want to work with?"
