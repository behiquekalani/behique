# Pricing Psychology: You Are Not Charging Too Much

Almost every first-time creator undercharges. Not because they researched the market and found competitive pricing. Because they are scared to find out if their work is worth more.

## The Fear Inside Underpricing

When you charge less than your work is worth, you are making a decision for the buyer before they can make it themselves. You are saying: "I already decided you would not pay more, so I will save you the trouble of rejecting me."

That is not generosity. That is fear wearing the mask of humility.

## What Your Price Communicates

Price is information. A $9 product signals something different than a $97 product, even if the content is identical. Buyers use price to estimate value, quality, and the seriousness of the seller.

Underpricing does not attract more buyers. It attracts price-sensitive buyers who are less likely to implement, less likely to get results, and more likely to ask for refunds. Raise the price. The right customers will self-select.

## How to Test Your Ceiling

Double your current price on the next offer you launch. Watch what happens. If conversion drops a little, you may have found a better margin point. If conversion holds, you were leaving significant money behind.

Most people never run this test because they are afraid of the feedback. The test itself is valuable regardless of outcome. You are gathering data about how the market sees your offer.

## This Week's Action

Review your current pricing on any product or service you offer. Identify the number you have been afraid to charge. Write a paragraph justifying why that number is actually fair. Then seriously consider using it.
