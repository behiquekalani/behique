# Distribution Is the Skill Nobody Teaches

Building is taught. Getting people to actually see what you built is not.

## The Idea
Most creators over-invest in product quality and under-invest in distribution. They spend months building something excellent, launch it, and then wonder why nothing happens. The problem is not the product. The problem is that no one knows it exists.

Distribution is the set of skills and systems that determine how many people see your work. It includes understanding platforms and their algorithms, building an audience before you need it, creating content that is inherently shareable, and knowing how to use email, social media, and communities as channels.

The hard truth is that an average product with great distribution will outsell an excellent product with poor distribution. Every time. This is uncomfortable because most creators tie their identity to the quality of what they make. Distribution feels like marketing, and marketing feels like manipulation.

It is not. Distribution is just the work of matching your solution with the people who need it. It is a service to your audience, not a trick.

The creators who figure this out early stop thinking about distribution as a separate activity that happens after building. They design distribution into the product and the content from the start. They ask "how will this spread?" before they ask "what will this contain?"

## Why It Matters
Your work does not speak for itself. Your distribution speaks for your work. Treat distribution as a core skill, not an afterthought.

## One Action
Before building anything new, write a distribution plan first. Name the three specific channels you will use, the posting frequency, and the first 10 people you will personally share it with.
