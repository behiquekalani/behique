# Skill Stacking: The Solopreneur's Real Advantage

You don't need to be the best at anything. You need to be competent at several things that, combined, make you rare.

## Why Depth Alone Isn't Enough
Being world-class at one skill is a viable career path if you want to work for others. For a solopreneur, it's limiting. The market pays for solutions, not skills. A solution usually requires more than one capability.

The developer who understands marketing can build things that sell. The writer who understands systems can produce at scale. The designer who understands psychology can create work that converts. The combination is the advantage.

## Building a Stack That Works
Skill stacking isn't random. The most powerful stacks are built around a primary skill, supplemented by adjacent skills that amplify its value. Pick your primary first. Then identify what would make that skill 2x more valuable in the market.

Writing is amplified by psychology. Design is amplified by copywriting. Engineering is amplified by product thinking. Start there. Add deliberately.

## The Rare Person at the Intersection
At the intersection of two or three developed skills, there are very few people. That's not a problem. That's the position. When you're rare, you set terms. You choose clients. You price based on value rather than market rate.

Most people compete in crowded lanes. The skill stacker creates their own lane.

## This Week's Action
List your top three skills. Pick one adjacent skill that would make your primary skill significantly more valuable. Spend 30 minutes a day for the next two weeks building that adjacent skill.
