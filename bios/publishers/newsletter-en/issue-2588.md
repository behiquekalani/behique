# Issue 2588: The Problem With Chasing Multiple Income Streams at Once

**The Idea:** Income diversification is the goal. Splitting your attention across five income streams at the beginning is the mistake.

Most solopreneurs who burn out do not burn out from working too hard on one thing. They burn out from working at half-speed on five things simultaneously. Each stream gets enough effort to exist, not enough to grow. Everything stalls. Nothing compounds.

The smarter sequence is depth before breadth. Pick one income stream that matches your current assets. Build it until it runs with minimal daily input. Then add the next.

A newsletter that takes eight hours a week to run should not coexist with a podcast, three social platforms, a course, and a consulting offer in month two. That is a system designed to fail. It distributes your energy so thin that every piece underperforms.

The question to ask is: what is the one stream that, if it worked, would change my situation? Start there. Go deep. Build the systems. Then layer.

Diversification is a reward for depth, not a substitute for it.

**Why It Matters:** Premature diversification is one of the leading causes of solopreneur stagnation. Choosing depth first compounds faster and creates the stable foundation that makes multiple streams sustainable later.

**One Action:** Audit your current income efforts. Identify which single stream has the most momentum. Put everything else on pause and concentrate resources there for 30 days.
