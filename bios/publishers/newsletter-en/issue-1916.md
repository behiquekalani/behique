# Issue 1916: Price Is a Signal You're Sending

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people underprice because they fear rejection. Lower price means fewer objections, and fewer objections feel safer. But price doesn't just determine revenue. It determines perception, buyer quality, and how seriously people engage with what you've built. A cheap product attracts cheap behavior. People who didn't invest much don't implement much. Your transformation rate drops. They complain more. The relationship is worse.

Higher prices filter for commitment. The person who pays a real amount for something has already decided it's worth their attention. They show up differently. They do the work. They get the result. That result becomes the testimonial that justifies the price for the next person. The product didn't change. The price changed who bought it, which changed the outcome, which changed the evidence you have to sell with. Price is upstream of all of that.

You also need to understand that buyers use price as a proxy for quality. When they can't evaluate your work directly, they use price as a heuristic. A low price signals low confidence in your own work, not affordability for your customer. Raise the price before you feel ready. Lower it only with evidence that the price is the actual objection, not just a convenient excuse for buyers who weren't right for you anyway.
