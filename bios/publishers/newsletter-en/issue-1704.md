# Issue 1704: The Cost of Waiting to Be Ready

**One insight. No fluff.**

There is no moment when you will feel ready. Readiness is not a state that arrives. It is a story you tell yourself to avoid the discomfort of starting before you know how things will turn out. Every person you admire started before they were ready. The gap between them and you is not knowledge. It is reps.

Skills do not transfer from theory to reality through more preparation. They transfer through embarrassing first attempts, incremental correction, and the compounding effect of doing something imperfect for long enough to make it good. You cannot think your way to competence. You perform your way there.

The cost of waiting is not wasted time. It is wasted momentum. Every week you spend preparing instead of starting is a week someone else is collecting real feedback and building real skill. Start now. Ship something rough. Learn from the contact with reality. Refine. Repeat.
