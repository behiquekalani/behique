# Issue 1712: Identity Before Strategy

**One insight. No fluff.**

Strategy fails when identity does not support it. You can have the right plan and the wrong belief about yourself, and the plan will fall apart under pressure. Most people try to solve execution problems with better tactics when the real issue is that they have not decided who they are yet.

Identity is not a feeling. It is a decision you make and then act on until the acting makes it true. You do not wait to feel like a writer before you write. You decide you are a writer and then write until the decision feels obvious. The behavior comes first. The identity follows. That sequence is backwards from how most people think about it.

When you hit resistance, when the work feels hard and you want to quit, what pulls you through is not willpower. It is clarity about who you are. Willpower is finite. Identity is structural. Build the identity first. Then build the strategy on top of it. One will hold when the other runs out.
