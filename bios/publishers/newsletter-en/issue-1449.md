# Business Models Are Not Equal. Pick the Right Architecture.

Not all revenue is the same. Chasing the wrong business model can build a prison that looks like a business from the outside.

## The Four Models That Matter for Solopreneurs

Services trade time for money. High margin, low scale. Good for cash flow, terrible for freedom unless you are very selective. Products trade upfront work for recurring sales. High scale potential, lower immediate income. Subscriptions create recurring revenue with high retention pressure. Communities charge for access and belonging, the most durable model when trust is high.

Most solopreneurs need to start with services, then migrate. The mistake is staying in services too long because it is comfortable and predictable.

## The Architecture Question

The right question is not "what makes the most money right now." It is "what architecture matches the life I want in three years."

If you want freedom, you build products and automate delivery. If you want community, you build around relationships. If you want stability, you build subscriptions. These are not the same systems. Building the wrong one is building a ladder against the wrong wall.

## The Transition Problem

Most creators never leave services because they never create the runway to do so. The only way out is intentional overlap. You build the product while you still have service income. You grow the audience while you still have client work.

It requires short-term constraint for long-term architecture. That is uncomfortable. It is also the only path.

## This Week's Action

Map your current revenue sources on a simple two-axis grid: high/low effort on one axis, high/low scale on the other. Whatever sits in the high-effort, low-scale quadrant is the first thing you should be migrating away from.
