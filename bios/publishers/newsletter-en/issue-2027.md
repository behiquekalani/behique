# Issue 2027: Opinions Are a Skill

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people have no opinion. They have a collection of other people's opinions they have assembled into a persona. Ask them what they think about something real and they will tell you what is popular or what will not get pushback. They mistake agreeableness for humility and vagueness for open-mindedness.

Having a genuine opinion is a skill. It requires actually thinking, which means following an idea past the comfortable part, past where most people stop, into the territory where you might be wrong. It requires being willing to say something that somebody disagrees with and being okay sitting in that tension.

Builders who create interesting work have opinions. About what matters, what does not, what is overrated, what is underrated. Those opinions show up in the work and make it specific. Specific work resonates. Vague work disappears. The blandest thing you can do is try to appeal to everyone. Pick a position. Hold it. Refine it with evidence. That is what thinking actually looks like.
