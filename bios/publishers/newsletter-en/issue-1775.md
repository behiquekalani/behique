# Issue 1775: Creativity Is a Practice, Not a Gift
**One insight. No fluff.**

The idea that creativity is something you have or do not have is one of the most damaging beliefs in a builder's life. It turns a skill into a trait and a practice into luck. People who say "I am not creative" have decided something that cannot be untrue. But it was never true to begin with.

Creativity is the output of a practiced input process. It comes from exposure to many domains, from asking questions other people do not ask, from making connections across things that seem unrelated. That process can be trained. It responds to repetition. The more you practice generating ideas, the more ideas arrive.

The constraint is not talent. The constraint is output anxiety. Most people filter ideas before writing them down because they fear bad ideas. Remove the filter. Write everything. Bad ideas lead to average ideas which lead to occasionally brilliant ones. The pipeline requires volume before it produces quality.
