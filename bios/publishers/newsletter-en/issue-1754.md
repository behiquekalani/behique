# Issue 1754: Most Goals Are Just Fantasies With Deadlines

**One insight. No fluff.**

A goal without a system attached is just a wish formatted as a plan. People write goals every January with total confidence and total vagueness. "Make more money." "Get in shape." "Start the business." None of these are plans. They are directions with no roads.

The difference between a goal that gets achieved and one that gets abandoned is granularity. What specifically will you do tomorrow? Not eventually. Tomorrow. The brain cannot execute vague instructions. It needs concrete actions in a specific context.

Shrink the goal to the next action. Not the vision, but the next move. The vision stays on the wall for motivation. The next action goes on the calendar. One is decoration. The other is a system.
