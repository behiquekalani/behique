# Issue 2389: The Digital Product That Sells While You Sleep

**The One Thing:** A digital product built once can sell indefinitely. Your time does not scale. Information does.

---

Time-for-money is the oldest trade. It is also the most limiting. There is a ceiling. You can work more hours up to a point, then you hit a wall. Digital products break that ceiling. You create once, distribute infinitely, and sleep while the transactions happen.

## Why Information Products Work

People pay for clarity. The internet is full of free information. What people cannot find for free is organized, specific, actionable clarity on the exact problem they have right now. That specificity is what you are selling. Not information. Access to your organized thinking about a very particular problem.

## The Product Types That Perform Best

Templates and systems: high perceived value, low time to consume. Guides and playbooks: specific problem, clear solution, fast delivery. Courses: deeper transformation, higher price point, more support needed. Start with templates. They are the fastest to build, easiest to price, and hardest to return. A buyer uses a template the day they buy it.

## The Distribution Mistake

Most creators build a product and wait. Waiting is not a strategy. The product needs a path: from attention source to email list to offer. If someone discovers your work and there is nowhere to go, the sale never happens. Build the path before the product or at the same time. Never after.

---

**This Week's Action:** Identify one skill you use regularly that someone else would pay to learn faster. Draft a one-page outline for a template or guide around that skill. Set a publish date within two weeks.

**Quote:** "Create once. Sell forever." — Paul Jarvis
