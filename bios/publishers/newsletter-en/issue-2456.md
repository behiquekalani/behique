# Issue 2456: Personal Monopoly Is the Goal

**The Idea:** A personal monopoly is a position so specific that you are the only obvious choice for a particular type of person with a particular type of problem. It is not about being the best in the world. It is about being the best fit for one specific audience.

The internet makes personal monopolies possible at scale. Before, geography limited specialization. Now, someone who serves a very narrow niche can find their exact audience anywhere in the world.

You build a personal monopoly by combining your specific knowledge with a clear point of view and consistent communication. Over time, your name becomes synonymous with a specific problem space. When someone in your target audience encounters that problem, you are who they think of.

This is the end state of good positioning. You stop competing because you have defined a category where you operate alone. Not because no one else exists, but because your combination of perspective, knowledge, and communication style is uniquely yours.

The path to personal monopoly is through narrowing, not broadening. Every time you try to serve more people, you dilute the specificity that makes you irreplaceable to the few.

**Why It Matters:** Monopoly positions command premium prices, attract highly aligned customers, and require less marketing because the positioning does the selling. It is the most durable business advantage available to a solo creator.

**One Action:** Write one sentence that describes your unique position in your niche. Make it specific enough that someone reading it knows immediately whether or not they are your exact person.
