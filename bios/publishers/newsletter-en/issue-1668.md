# Evergreen Content Does Not Get Tired. You Do.

The problem with most content strategies is not consistency. It is chasing what is current instead of what is permanent.

## What Evergreen Actually Means
Evergreen content answers questions that do not expire. How to focus when you are distracted. How to price your first offer. How to write a cold email that gets a reply. These questions will be asked five years from now by a different person with the same problem. The content you write today to answer them will still be doing its job then.

Trending content is a treadmill. Evergreen content is a flywheel. One requires constant effort to stay relevant. The other builds on itself over time.

## The Evergreen Test
Before you write anything, ask one question: will this matter in two years? If the answer depends on a current event, a platform update, or a viral moment, it is trending. Write it if you want attention now. But evergreen content is what builds the asset base that works long after you stop posting.

The goal is to own a library of answers, not a feed of reactions.

## Repurposing Without Re-Writing
Evergreen content repurposes without losing value. A 1000-word article on pricing becomes a Twitter thread, an email, a short-form video, and a podcast segment. The core idea does not change. The format does. One strong evergreen piece can generate six months of content if you slice it deliberately.

## This Week's Action
Write one piece of content that answers a timeless question in your niche. No references to current events. Just a clean, useful answer. Post it, then note which formats you could repurpose it into next month.
