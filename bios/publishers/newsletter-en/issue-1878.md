# Issue 1878: Build for the Person You Were Two Years Ago

**One insight. No fluff.**

You do not need to invent your target audience. You already know them. They are you, two years earlier, before the thing you learned, the mistake you made, the realization that changed how you work. That version of you had a specific problem, a real gap in understanding, and no clear path to closing it. You closed it. That is the product you should build.

The reason most products feel generic is that they are built for an imagined customer. They are built for "someone interested in productivity" or "aspiring entrepreneurs." These are categories, not people. When you build for the person you were, you know the exact moment of confusion, the specific language you used when you did not yet understand, the bad advice you tried first and why it failed.

That specificity is your competitive advantage. You write from inside the problem, not above it. Your reader feels understood instead of lectured. The most useful thing you can do for someone at the beginning is be honest about what it actually took you to get to where you are. Not the polished version. The real sequence, including the wrong turns.
