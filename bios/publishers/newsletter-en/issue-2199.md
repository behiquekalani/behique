# What It Means to Build Different

Different is not a style. It is a commitment to thinking for yourself.

## The Idea

Most businesses copy each other. Same funnels, same offers, same language, same pricing models. They converge on what works until nothing is differentiated and everyone is competing on price.

Building different is not about being contrarian for its own sake. It is about taking the time to think from first principles instead of copying what exists.

First principles thinking means starting from the actual problem and the actual person, not from the template of what a business in your category usually looks like. It means asking: what would this look like if I designed it from scratch for exactly this audience, with no assumptions carried over from how it is typically done?

When you think this way, you find gaps. You find underserved audiences who have been given solutions designed for someone else. You find formats no one has tried. You find pricing structures that align differently. You find distribution channels that others have ignored.

The work is harder. Copying is faster. But copying creates a business that is one of many. First principles creates a business that is one of one.

And one of one is where pricing power, loyalty, and real differentiation live.

## Why It Matters

In a crowded market, the copycat competes on price. The original competes on uniqueness. Uniqueness is more defensible, more profitable, and more interesting to build.

## One Action

Pick one element of your business (your offer, pricing, format, distribution, or positioning). Throw out any assumption about how it should work. Redesign it from scratch based only on your audience's actual need.
