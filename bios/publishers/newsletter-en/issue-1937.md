# Issue 1937: The Comparison Trap Has a Cure

**One insight. No fluff.**

Comparison is not the problem. Comparison to the wrong reference point is. When you compare your current self to someone else's current self, especially someone with different resources, different starting conditions, and years more experience, you are generating a number that means nothing. It tells you nothing useful about what to do next.

The only comparison that produces useful information is comparison to your past self. Are you better than you were six months ago? Do you know more, produce more, think more clearly? That comparison has traction. It points toward specific improvements. It measures effort rather than circumstances. It is the only benchmark fully within your control.

The cure for destructive comparison is a rigorous personal log. What did you know a year ago versus now. What did you build last quarter versus this one. The trajectory becomes visible and the trajectory is what matters. A person improving at ten percent per quarter on a long time horizon will surpass almost anyone who started ahead of them. The question is never where you are. It is always which direction you are pointing and how fast you are moving.
