# Issue 1816: How to Find What You're Actually Good At

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people don't know what they're good at because they've never pushed hard enough in any one direction to find out. They dabble, get comfortable with mediocrity, and assume that mild competence is their ceiling.

It isn't. But the ceiling is only visible from the edge of your effort.

The things you're genuinely good at share a few signals. They feel effortless compared to how hard they seem for others. You lose track of time doing them. You get curious about depth instead of bored by repetition. You produce results others notice without feeling like you did anything special. Those signals matter. They're pointing at something real.

The trap is thinking your strengths have to be rare or impressive-sounding. They don't. A boring strength applied consistently to a real problem is worth more than a glamorous skill applied lazily. Find the intersection of what you do naturally and what others need urgently. That intersection is where your work becomes irreplaceable.
