# Mental Models Are Shortcuts That Actually Work

You make hundreds of decisions every week. Most of them use the same cognitive patterns. Better patterns produce better decisions. That's what mental models are.

## The Idea

A mental model is a framework for understanding how something works. Not a hack. Not a trick. A repeatable way of looking at a category of problem that helps you see clearly and decide well.

The most useful ones for builders: opportunity cost, the 80/20 principle, second-order thinking, and the minimum effective dose.

Opportunity cost means every choice is a trade. When you say yes to one thing, you say no to everything else you could have done with that time. Most people only evaluate the upside of what they choose, not the cost of what they skip.

The 80/20 principle means roughly 20% of your inputs produce 80% of your outputs. The work is identifying which 20%. Most solopreneurs spend the majority of their time on the 80% that produces 20% of results. This is not laziness. It's lack of measurement.

Second-order thinking means asking not just what will happen, but what will happen as a result of what happens. A decision that looks good in the first order often looks different in the second.

Minimum effective dose means finding the smallest action that produces the desired result. Not the most impressive action. The most efficient one.

## Why It Matters

Mental models reduce the cognitive load of decision-making. You don't have to think from scratch every time. You apply a lens and it sharpens what you see. Over time, applying better lenses consistently produces compounding results.

## One Action

Pick one mental model from this issue. Apply it to one real decision you're facing this week. Write down what you see differently when you use the lens.
