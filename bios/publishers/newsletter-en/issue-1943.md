# Issue 1943: Niche Down Until It Hurts

**One insight. No fluff.**

Broad appeal is the enemy of actual connection. When you try to speak to everyone, you write copy that sounds like it was generated by committee. Generic, safe, forgettable. The audience you want is not looking for something that vaguely applies to them. They want something that reads like you wrote it for them specifically.

Niching down is uncomfortable because it feels like rejection. If you say "this is for freelance developers who hate agency work," you are actively excluding everyone else. That exclusion feels like lost opportunity. But what you are actually doing is making a promise. You are telling a specific person: I see you. That promise is what builds loyalty.

The fear is that your audience will be too small to matter. The reality is that a tiny, passionate audience outperforms a massive indifferent one at every level. Revenue, retention, referrals. Go specific enough that your ideal reader feels a little seen and a little called out. That tension is not a warning sign. It is proof you found the right lane.
