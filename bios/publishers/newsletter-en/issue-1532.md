# How to Make Decisions Without Overthinking Them

Most bad decisions come from too little information. Most non-decisions come from too much.

## The Overthinking Loop
Overthinking is not analysis. It's anxiety with a productivity costume on. You already know enough to decide. What you're really doing is avoiding the responsibility of being wrong. The solution is not more research. It's raising your tolerance for being wrong.

Every good decision-maker has made expensive mistakes. The difference between them and the chronic overthinker is they made the mistake faster, learned from it, and moved forward.

## A Framework That Actually Works
For small decisions, use five seconds. Pick the option that moves you toward your goal and act. For medium decisions, use the 10/10/10 rule. How will you feel about this decision in 10 minutes, 10 months, and 10 years? For large decisions, set a deadline. If you don't have enough information by Friday, you go with your best guess on Friday.

Deadlines force clarity. Open-ended analysis never does.

## The Cost of Not Deciding
Indecision is a decision. It's a choice to stay where you are. Every day you don't launch is a day the product doesn't exist. Every day you don't reach out is a day the relationship doesn't start. The cost of inaction is invisible but real, and it compounds.

Done imperfectly is worth more than planned perfectly. Ship, then improve.

## This Week's Action
Identify one decision you've been avoiding for more than two weeks. Give yourself 24 hours to make it. Write down the two options, pick the one that moves you forward, and execute one first step before the 24 hours is up.
