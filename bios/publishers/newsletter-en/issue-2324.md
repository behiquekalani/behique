# The Creator Economy Rewards Specificity

Broad appeal is a race to the bottom. Specific depth is a moat.

## The Idea

The biggest mistake new creators make is trying to reach everyone. They post generic motivation. They write surface-level tips. They copy what worked for someone with ten times the audience and wonder why it fails for them.

The creator economy works differently than broadcast media. You do not need millions of viewers. You need the right hundreds. A tight, specific audience that trusts you is worth more than a large, passive audience that barely notices you exist.

Specificity does two things. First, it signals expertise. When you speak directly to a narrow problem, you sound like someone who has lived it. Generic content sounds like someone who read about it. Second, it filters. The wrong people leave. The right people lean in. Your conversion rate on anything you offer improves because your audience self-selects.

The fear is that you will lose people by narrowing. The truth is you will lose the people who would never buy anyway. The ones who stay are warmer, more engaged, and more likely to share your work with others exactly like them.

## Why It Matters

You are not a media company trying to maximize impressions. You are a builder trying to build trust with a specific group of people. The more specific you are about who you serve and what problem you solve, the faster that trust forms.

## One Action

Review your last five pieces of content. Ask who each one is written for. If the answer is "everyone," rewrite the opening line to speak to one specific type of person.
