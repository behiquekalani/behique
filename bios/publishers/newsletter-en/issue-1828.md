# Issue 1828: Specificity Is the New Currency

**One insight. No fluff.**

Generalist content is free and abundant. Specific, hard-won insight is scarce. The more specific you are about who you help, what problem you solve, and why your approach works, the more valuable your work becomes. Trying to appeal to everyone is a losing strategy because you end up meaning nothing to anyone.

This applies to products, content, services, and even conversations. The creator who writes for "ambitious people" competes with everyone. The creator who writes for "first-generation immigrants building online businesses while working full-time" competes with almost no one. The specificity feels limiting from the inside. It does not look limiting from the outside.

When you niche down, something counterintuitive happens. You attract more people, not fewer, because the people who match your specifics feel seen in a way that generic content never achieves. They share it. They buy. They return. The path to scale is almost always through a specific person in a specific situation. Start there. Serve them exceptionally well. The broader audience follows from depth, not from breadth.
