# Issue 1710: Sell the Transformation, Not the Features

**One insight. No fluff.**

People do not buy products. They buy a version of themselves they want to become. When you describe what your product does, you are selling features. When you describe what your buyer's life looks like after they use it, you are selling the thing they actually want.

This distinction sounds like marketing advice. It is actually product design advice. If you cannot describe the transformation clearly, you do not understand your product well enough yet. The transformation is the product. The features are just the mechanism that delivers it.

Before you write a single sales page, answer this: what does a person believe, do, or feel after they buy this that they could not before? If you can answer that in one sentence without jargon, you understand your product. If you cannot, build more clarity before you build more features. Clarity about transformation converts. Feature lists do not.
