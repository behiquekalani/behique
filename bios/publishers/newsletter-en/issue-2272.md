# Niche Down or Stay Invisible

The more specific you are, the easier you are to find.

## The Idea

Generic content reaches no one in particular. Specific content reaches exactly who needs it, and those people feel like you're talking directly to them.

This is the central paradox of growing an audience as a solo creator. The instinct is to be broad so you can appeal to more people. The reality is that being broad means you're slightly relevant to everyone and deeply relevant to no one. That's not a position anyone recommends to their friends.

The narrow focus does something specific. It filters. The people who aren't your audience self-select out, and the people who are your audience feel understood. Feeling understood creates trust. Trust creates subscribers, customers, and referrals.

A creator helping "people who want to make money online" competes with millions. A creator helping "burned-out teachers who want to build digital products they can sell on the side" has almost no competition and an audience with a shared, specific pain.

You can always expand later. The mistake is trying to be broad before you've built any gravity. Get tight first. Build a real base of people who would miss you if you stopped. Then expand into adjacent territory from a position of strength.

Niching down is not a limitation. It's a targeting system.

## Why It Matters

Specificity accelerates every part of your creator business. Your content is more shareable because it speaks to a specific person. Your offers convert better because they address a specific problem. Your word-of-mouth grows faster because people know exactly who to send to you.

## One Action

Write a one-sentence description of who you help, what problem you solve, and what result they get. Make it so specific that a stranger could picture the exact person. Share that sentence in your next piece of content as a clear positioning statement.
