# The Solopreneur's Guide to Managing Risk Without a Safety Net

Going solo means no HR department, no safety net, no paid sick days. Risk management isn't optional. It's how you stay in business.

## What Risk Actually Looks Like at Your Scale
For a solopreneur, risk is not a market crash or a supply chain failure. It's three clients leaving in the same month. It's a platform ban with no notice. It's a health issue that takes you offline for four weeks. These are not unlikely scenarios. Every solopreneur who has been at it long enough has one of these stories.

The question isn't whether something will go wrong. It's whether you've prepared for it.

## Diversification as Protection
If 80% of your income comes from one client or one platform, you are not running a business. You are running a dependency. One decision by one person can end everything you've built.

Spread your income across clients, products, and platforms before you need to. Not as a hedge but as a foundation. A business with three income streams at 33% each is far more resilient than one at 80/10/10.

## The Unsexy Preparations That Matter Most
Emergency fund for three to six months. Contracts that protect your payment terms. Backups of every asset and client communication. A simple health insurance situation. These don't feel like business moves. They feel like adult admin. Do them anyway.

## This Week's Action
List every income source you have and the percentage each represents. Identify the biggest single point of failure. Write down one thing you can do this month to reduce that concentration. Start that one thing this week.
