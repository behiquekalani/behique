# The Niche Is a Starting Point, Not a Prison

Go specific to build authority. Expand once the authority exists.

## The Idea

The resistance to niching down usually sounds like this: "I don't want to limit myself." The fear is that going specific will cut off opportunities. The reality is the opposite. Generalists are hard to find. Specialists are sought out.

When you pick a specific niche, you are not permanently restricting what you can talk about or sell. You are building a reputation in a place where you can actually be known. A generalist who covers everything for everyone is memorable to no one. A specialist who solves one specific problem for one specific type of person is the first person that type of person calls.

The path is always specific first. Build enough trust and recognition in a niche that people consider you a reference point. Then expand. The audience you built respects your perspective because you earned it in a specific domain. That trust transfers when you expand, because you have demonstrated the quality of your thinking.

This is how every durable personal brand gets built. They start with something narrow: personal finance for teachers, productivity for founders, content strategy for B2B companies. They own that territory. Then they expand the conversation because their audience follows the person, not just the topic.

## Why It Matters

In an attention economy, being findable matters more than being broad. Specificity makes you findable. It also makes your content better, because you are solving a real problem for a real person instead of writing for everyone and resonating with no one.

## One Action

Write the most specific version of your niche in one sentence: "I help [specific person] do [specific thing] so they can [specific outcome]." If that sentence feels too narrow, you are probably on the right track. Post content aimed at that exact person for the next 30 days.
