# Issue 2657: Pricing Is Communication

**The Idea:** Your price is a message before it is a number.

A low price communicates low confidence and low stakes. A high price communicates value, seriousness, and selectivity. Buyers read these signals before they read anything else on your page. The price sets the frame for everything that follows.

This is why underpricing is so damaging. You think you are making it easier for people to say yes. Often you are making them more skeptical. If it is that cheap, what is wrong with it? That is the real thought behind a buyer who hesitates at a low price.

Pricing at the level your product deserves requires two things: belief in what you built, and clarity about who you built it for. If you believe the outcome is real and you know exactly who needs it, the price is easy to set. If you are vague on either of those, you will default to low prices as a hedge against rejection.

The right price is one where a small percentage of your audience thinks it is too expensive, a large percentage thinks it is fair, and a percentage thinks it is clearly worth more. That spread tells you the price is in the right zone.

Charge what the transformation is worth. Not what the information costs to produce.

**Why It Matters:** Price signals quality in the buyer's mind before any sales copy does its job. Correct pricing attracts serious buyers, repels the wrong ones, and improves your margins without requiring more volume.

**One Action:** Review one of your products and ask: does this price reflect the outcome, or the cost of production? If it reflects cost, raise it by 30% this week and observe the impact.
