# Digital Products: The Asset Class Nobody Talks About

Real estate requires capital. Stocks require patience. Digital products require knowledge you already have.

## Why Digital Products Win

Zero marginal cost. You make it once. You sell it a thousand times. The economics are unlike anything else available to a single person with a laptop and an internet connection.

The barrier is not technical anymore. The barrier is starting. Most people know enough right now to create something worth selling. They are just waiting for permission that will never come.

## The Myth of the Perfect Product

The first version does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist. A $27 PDF that helps 100 people is more valuable than a $297 course you never finish building.

Shipping teaches you things about your audience that no amount of planning can. The market tells you what it wants when you give it something to react to.

## What to Build First

Build the smallest, most specific thing you can teach. Not a comprehensive guide. A specific solution to a specific problem.

The title should make someone say "I need that." If the title sounds like a Wikipedia article, rework it. If it sounds like the answer to a question someone typed at 2am, you are on the right track.

## This Week's Action

Write the title and one-sentence description for a digital product you could create in the next seven days. Do not build it yet. Just define it. See if the description makes you excited. If it does not excite you, it will not excite a buyer.
