# Issue 2647: Identity Before Strategy

**The Idea:** Most business advice starts with tactics. That is the wrong place to start.

Before you pick a platform, a niche, or a product type, you need to know who you are in this space. What do you stand for? What do you refuse to do? What does success look like to you specifically, not to the person whose course you just bought?

Identity questions sound soft. They are not. They are the most practical questions in business.

When you are clear on identity, decisions get fast. Should you take that brand deal? Does it fit the identity? Yes or no. Should you create a course or a membership? Which one aligns with how you want to work? Clear identity answers that question without an hour of deliberation.

Without identity, you will follow every trend, copy every competitor, and constantly feel like you are behind someone else's version of success. That is not a strategy problem. It is an identity problem.

The solopreneurs who build durable businesses are not the ones with the best tactics. They are the ones who know exactly who they are and build everything from that center. Tactics come and go. Identity is the anchor.

**Why It Matters:** Identity-driven businesses attract loyal audiences. People follow people, not strategies. When your work is an authentic expression of who you are, it cannot be copied. It can only be yours.

**One Action:** Write three sentences about who you are as a creator or solopreneur. What you believe, what you build, who you build it for. This becomes the filter for every decision you make this week.
