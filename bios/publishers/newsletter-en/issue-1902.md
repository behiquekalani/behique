# Issue 1902: Clarity Is a Competitive Advantage

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people are vague on purpose. Vague goals can't fail. "I want to grow" is immune to judgment. "I want 1,000 customers by December" is not. So they stay vague, call it flexibility, and wonder why nothing compounds.

Clarity is uncomfortable because it creates the possibility of being wrong. But being wrong with a clear target teaches you something. Being vague teaches you nothing. You can't adjust a direction you never set. You can't diagnose a failure you never defined. The people who make rapid progress aren't the ones who figure out the perfect path. They're the ones who pick a specific target, get feedback fast, and correct with precision.

Write down your goal in one sentence. No hedging. No "kind of" or "trying to." A single sentence that someone else could evaluate in six months and say yes or no. That sentence alone will do more for your output than any framework, productivity system, or motivational video. Clarity is not a personality trait. It's a skill. And it's available to anyone willing to tolerate the discomfort of being specific.
