# Issue 2519: The Offer Stack Strategy

**The Idea:** Most solopreneurs have one offer. One price point. One product. When that product does not convert, they have nothing else to catch the buyer. When a buyer wants more, there is nothing to give them. Both problems point to the same solution: an offer stack.

An offer stack is not three random products. It is a deliberate progression. Each level solves a different depth of the same problem for the same customer. The entry product solves a piece of the problem at a low price and low commitment. The core product solves the main problem at a mid-range price. The premium product provides comprehensive transformation or your direct involvement at a high price.

The architecture serves multiple functions. It reduces risk for the buyer. They can test you at $19 before spending $197. It maximizes revenue per customer. The buyer who gets results from your entry product is predisposed to buy the next level. It creates a journey, not a transaction.

The content strategy aligns with the offer stack. Different levels of content speak to buyers at different stages of awareness. Someone discovering you gets entry-level content. Someone who has bought from you gets content that points toward the next level.

Build the stack intentionally. Price the levels with clear logic. Then design your content to move people through it.

**Why It Matters:** A single offer is a binary decision. The customer either buys or they do not. A stack gives every buyer a way in and a path to more value.

**One Action:** Map your current offers. If you only have one, write down what an entry-level product and a premium product could look like on either side of it. You do not need to build them this week. You need to have the architecture planned.
