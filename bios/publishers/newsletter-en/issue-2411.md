# Issue 2411: Income Streams Are Not Built in Parallel

**The One Thing:** Build one income stream to $3,000 a month before adding a second one.

---

Everyone wants multiple income streams. The mistake is trying to build them all at once. You end up with six streams that produce nothing instead of one that produces reliably.

Focus compounds. Dilution doesn't.

## The Single Stream Rule

Pick the income stream closest to working. Pour your time there. Ignore every other opportunity until that one hits a number you committed to in advance. Three thousand a month is a reasonable target. It proves the model. It proves you can sell. It gives you capital to build the next thing.

The mistake most people make is switching streams when one gets hard. Hard means you're close. That's when you push, not pivot.

## What to Build First

The fastest income stream for a solopreneur is usually the one that requires the least infrastructure. Digital products beat courses. Services beat products when you're starting. Consulting beats a SaaS when you have no audience.

Start with the thing that can earn money this week. Not this year. If you built something today and sent it to the ten most relevant people you know, could someone buy it? If yes, that's your first stream.

## Build the Foundation

Once your first stream is stable, the second one is much easier. You have proof you can sell. You have cash to invest. You have confidence that changes how you approach new builds.

The second stream also benefits from the first one's audience. Every new product you launch goes to people who already trust you. That changes everything about the economics.

## Stack Deliberately

After two streams, look for the third that serves the same customer as the first two. One customer, multiple purchases, more lifetime value.

---

**This Week's Action:** Name your primary income stream and set a monthly revenue target. Work only on that stream until you hit the number.

**Quote:** "Chase the vision, not the money. The money will end up following you." - Tony Hsieh
