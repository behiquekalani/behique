# Issue 2097: The Network You Ignore

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people think about networking as meeting new people. They attend events, send cold messages, try to get in front of impressive strangers. This is the least efficient version of network building. The most valuable network you have is the one you are already in and largely ignoring.

Former classmates, early collaborators, people you worked alongside years ago. These relationships have something cold outreach never does: established trust. They already know how you operate. They have context for your work. When an opportunity fits you, they think of you first because you are a real person to them, not a name in an inbox.

The maintenance cost of these relationships is low. A short message when something relevant comes up. Sharing their work when you find it useful. Following their progress and occasionally saying something specific about it. None of this requires a system. It requires genuine attention. The people who build strong networks over time are usually just the ones who stay interested in what the people around them are doing. That interest, consistently expressed, is what keeps old connections alive and turns them into the kind of relationships that actually move things.
