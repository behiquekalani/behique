# Issue 2087: Identity Before Strategy

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people approach change backward. They try to adopt a new strategy before they have adopted a new identity. They want the system without becoming the person who runs it. So they follow the diet but do not think of themselves as someone who eats well. They use the productivity framework but still fundamentally see themselves as disorganized. The system collapses because the identity rejects it.

Identity change is not something that happens after results. It is what makes results possible. You do not lose the weight and then become a healthy person. You start acting like a healthy person, and the weight follows. The sequence matters more than the effort. Most effort fails not because of insufficient willpower but because it is pointed in a direction the identity does not support.

The practical version of this is casting votes for your new identity before you feel like it is real. Show up to write before you call yourself a writer. Build the product before you call yourself a founder. The identity is not something you earn. It is something you practice until the evidence accumulates and the label fits. That process is uncomfortable, because you are claiming something you have not yet proven. That discomfort is the transition itself.
