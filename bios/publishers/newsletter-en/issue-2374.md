# The High-Ticket Trap

Chasing premium prices before you have premium positioning is a fast way to burn trust.

## The Idea
The online business world is obsessed with high-ticket offers. Coaching at five thousand dollars. Masterminds at ten thousand. The logic is that fewer sales at higher prices is easier than many sales at lower prices.

The math checks out. The positioning requirement does not.

High-ticket prices require high-trust positioning. A buyer paying five thousand dollars for coaching needs to believe, with confidence, that you have solved the exact problem they are trying to solve. That belief comes from a track record, documented results, testimonials, and a body of work. It does not come from a sales page alone.

Solopreneurs who jump to high-ticket before building that foundation either underdeliver or struggle to close. Both outcomes damage the brand they are trying to build.

The path is: low-price products first. They build buyer data, testimonials, and confidence. Mid-price products next, with more scope and more support. High-ticket eventually, when the track record justifies it.

There is no shame in twenty-dollar products. They compound. They build lists of buyers. Buyers buy again. The arithmetic of many small transactions is underrated.

Start at the price the market can say yes to easily. Earn the right to higher prices by delivering obvious value at each tier.

## Why It Matters
Mismatched price and positioning creates friction that stops sales. Matched price and positioning removes it. Build the trust infrastructure first, then price accordingly.

## One Action
Map your current offer stack by price tier. Identify which tier has the weakest testimonial base. Focus your next three sales on that tier and collect documented results.
