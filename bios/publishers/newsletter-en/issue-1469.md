# The Business Model You Choose Shapes the Life You Live

Most people optimize for revenue first. They should be optimizing for the life the business creates.

## The Model Defines the Constraints
A service business that scales with more clients means more calls, more deliverables, more management. You build a job, not a business. A product business can scale without your time, but it requires upfront investment in building something worth selling repeatedly. Neither is wrong. But they produce very different lives.

Pick the model before you build. Reverse-engineer from the life you want.

## The Solopreneur Math
One high-ticket client at $3,000 a month is less leverage than 100 customers buying a $30 product. Same revenue, completely different ceilings. The solopreneur who builds around products retains optionality. They can take a week off. They can experiment without client approval. They can build without a calendar full of calls.

Leverage is freedom. Your model either creates it or destroys it.

## What Most Builders Miss
They copy the model of the successful person they admire without copying the context. That person has an audience, a team, or a reputation that makes their model work. Without those inputs, the same model produces different outputs. Understand what the model requires, not just what it produces.

The inputs matter as much as the design.

## This Week's Action
Map your current or planned business model on paper. Mark every piece that requires your direct time. Then ask: which of these could be replaced with a system, a product, or a hire? That is your leverage audit.
