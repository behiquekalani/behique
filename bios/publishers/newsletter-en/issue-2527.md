# Issue 2527: Building in Public Is Not About Transparency

**The Idea:** Building in public gets misunderstood constantly. People think it means sharing your revenue numbers, your failures, your exact process. That is one version. But it is not the core of what makes it work.

Building in public works because it collapses the distance between you and the people you want to reach. It makes the journey visible, and people connect to journeys more than destinations. When someone watches you figure something out, they root for you. When they root for you, they trust you. When they trust you, they buy from you.

The transparency is not the product. The connection is.

What you share matters less than how you share it. You can build in public without revealing your exact numbers. You can share the thinking behind a decision, the lesson from a mistake, the reasoning that led to a pivot. All of that is public building without requiring you to expose every metric.

The question to ask before each post is not "is this transparent enough?" It is "does this give my audience something real to connect to?" A thought, a struggle, a small win, a shift in your understanding. That is the material.

People do not follow you for the destination. They follow you because you are going somewhere and they want to see if you make it.

**Why It Matters:** Audiences built around a journey convert better than audiences built around information. The person who followed your process feels invested in your success and is far more likely to support it financially.

**One Action:** Share one thing you learned this week that changed how you think about your work. Not a tip. A lesson from your actual experience.
