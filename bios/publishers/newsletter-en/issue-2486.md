# Issue 2486: What Building in Public Actually Requires

**The Idea:** Building in public has become a content strategy. That is a problem.

When people treat it as a strategy, they share wins with false modesty, manufacture vulnerability, and turn setbacks into tidy narratives with lessons learned. The audience can feel it. It reads like performance, not reality.

Actual building in public requires sharing things before you know how they turn out. That is the part most people skip.

Posting "here is the thing I just launched and it did 10x" is not building in public. That is celebrating in public. Building in public is "here is the thing I am unsure about, here is what I am trying, here is what I do not know yet."

That version is uncomfortable because you do not control the narrative. Something might fail publicly. Your uncertainty might make you look less than expert.

But that discomfort is exactly what makes it valuable. People trust you more when you stop performing certainty you do not have. Real documentation of real process, including doubt, is rare and magnetic.

**Why It Matters:** Performed building in public blends into noise. Authentic building in public creates the kind of trust that no amount of polished content can manufacture.

**One Action:** Share one thing you are working on today that you do not yet know will succeed. Include the uncertainty. Do not wrap it in a lesson.
