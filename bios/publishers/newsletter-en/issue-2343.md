# Digital Products Don't Sleep

You need income that doesn't require your presence. That's not passive income. That's product income. There's a difference.

## The Idea

Passive income is a marketing term. Product income is a business model. The distinction is in what you actually build.

A digital product is a packaged solution. You solve a problem once, wrap it in a format someone can consume without you, and sell it repeatedly. The work happens upfront. The distribution happens while you do other things.

This isn't magic. It requires building something worth buying, a way to find buyers, and a reason for them to trust you before they pay. But once those three pieces are in place, the model works at any hour, in any time zone, without a meeting.

The best digital products come from your own solved problems. Not from market research spreadsheets. If you figured out how to do something that others struggle with, you already have a product. You just haven't written it down yet.

Start small. A 15-page PDF that solves one specific problem is worth more to the right buyer than a 200-page course that covers everything loosely. Value density matters more than volume. People don't want more content. They want faster results.

## Why It Matters

Digital products give you the most important thing a solo operator can have: asymmetric returns on your time. You spend 20 hours building a product. That product can sell 2 copies or 2,000. The time cost is the same either way.

That asymmetry is why product income changes how you think about work.

## One Action

Identify one problem you've solved in the last six months that someone else would pay to avoid solving themselves. Write a one-paragraph description of what the solution looks like. That's your product brief.
