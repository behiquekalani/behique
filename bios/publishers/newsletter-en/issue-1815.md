# Issue 1815: Speed Is a Competitive Advantage

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most people think quality and speed are in tension. Build fast and the work is shallow. Take your time and the work is good. That trade-off is real at the extreme ends, but in the middle, where most work actually lives, speed is quality.

The builder who ships in a week and iterates learns faster than the one who perfects for a month.

Velocity creates data. Data creates better decisions. Better decisions create better products. The person who ships imperfect things frequently knows more about what works than the person who ships one perfect thing per quarter. Reality is a better teacher than theory, and the only way to access reality is to put things into it.

Speed also forces prioritization. When you have to ship something today, you cut the unnecessary. When you have unlimited time, everything feels necessary. Constraints clarify. Urgency is not the enemy of quality. Overthinking is.
