# Issue 2509: The Mental Model That Changes How You See Competition

**The Idea:** Most people treat competition as a zero-sum game. If someone else is successful in your niche, that means less room for you. This is wrong in almost every market and catastrophically wrong in the creator and digital products space.

The real insight is abundance framing. There are more people who need what you teach than you could ever reach in your lifetime. Other creators in your space are not taking your customers. They are expanding the overall market by bringing people into the conversation who then discover you.

The more useful mental model is differentiation, not competition. No two people have the same combination of background, communication style, personality, and specific experience. You are not competing with other solopreneurs. You are occupying a unique position in the market that no one else can replicate exactly.

The practical implication is that studying competitors should be about learning, not copying. What are they doing well? What gap are they leaving? What problem are they not solving for a subset of their audience? That gap is your opportunity.

When you stop seeing the market as a pie you have to grab a slice of and start seeing it as a space you get to define, everything changes. You go from reactive to creative.

**Why It Matters:** Fear of competition causes people to niche down until they are invisible or copy others until they are indistinguishable. Your job is to be more specifically you, not more like someone else.

**One Action:** Name one creator in your space. Write down three things they do well and one gap in their content or product that you could fill. That gap is a signal.
