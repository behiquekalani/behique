# Issue 2107: The First Draft Is Not the Work

**One insight. No fluff.**

The first draft is a quarry, not a cathedral. Its job is to exist. To give you raw material to work with. Expecting the first draft to be good is like expecting a pile of stone to look like a sculpture. The carving happens after. Most people quit at the quarry stage because they confused the material with the finished thing.

This matters beyond writing. The first version of your product, your system, your strategy: all of it is first draft. It is meant to be rough. It is meant to have problems you did not anticipate. The purpose of version one is to reveal what version two should fix. You cannot skip this step. You can only go through it faster or slower.

The edit is where the real thinking happens. The rewrite is where ideas get sharp. The second pass on anything is usually worth three times what the first pass produced. So the goal is not to get the first version right. The goal is to finish the first version quickly enough that you have time to do the actual work, which is everything that comes after.
