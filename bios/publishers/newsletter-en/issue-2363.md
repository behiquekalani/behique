# Digital Products Are Stored Effort

Every dollar a digital product earns after launch is a return on effort you already spent.

## The Idea
When you create a digital product, you are converting time into an asset. A guide, a template, a course, a tool. The work happens once. The value can be extracted indefinitely.

This is fundamentally different from service work. Services require your time on every delivery. Products deliver without you. The math compounds quickly. If a $30 template sells ten times a month, that is $300 from something you built once. If you have twenty products, the numbers change significantly.

The barrier most people hit is not creation. It is specificity. Generic products do not sell because the internet already has a version of everything generic. The products that sell are the ones built around a specific problem for a specific type of person.

Think about the last problem you actually solved. A workflow you figured out, a skill you developed, a process you made faster. That solution, packaged clearly, is a product. You already have the knowledge. What you are missing is the packaging.

The second barrier is distribution. A product without an audience is just a file on a server. Building an audience first, even a small one, changes the economics dramatically. The audience comes before the product catalog.

## Why It Matters
Stored effort is the closest thing a solopreneur has to passive income done right. It still requires maintenance and marketing. But the ratio of output to input improves every month a product stays live.

Start with one product. One problem. One audience. Ship it. Learn what converts.

## One Action
Identify one thing you know how to do that took you time to figure out. Write the title of a product that teaches exactly that. Keep the scope narrow enough to finish in a weekend.
