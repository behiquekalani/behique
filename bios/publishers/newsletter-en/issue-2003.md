# Issue 2003: The Compounding Nobody Talks About

**One insight. No fluff.**

Everyone knows about financial compounding. Fewer people think about skill compounding. Even fewer think about perspective compounding, which is the most powerful of the three.

Perspective is what you use to decide which skills to build, which bets to take, and which problems are worth solving. A better perspective makes every other resource more effective. The person who sees the situation more clearly does not just outperform. They outperform by more each year because the advantage feeds itself. Every good decision teaches you something that makes the next decision slightly better.

The problem is that perspective does not compound automatically. It requires exposure to friction. Reading is necessary but not sufficient. You need to make decisions with real stakes, get the result, and sit with what you got wrong. Most people avoid the stakes part. They study, they plan, they optimize their environment. But they do not ship, do not price, do not publish, do not commit. The compounding of perspective requires that you put something on the line. Without skin in the game, the lessons stay theoretical and the compounding never starts.
