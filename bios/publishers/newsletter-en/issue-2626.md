# Issue 2626: The Product Is the Curriculum

**The Idea:** Most digital products fail not because the information is bad but because the sequence is wrong.

People download a guide or a course and get lost. Not because they can't follow instructions but because the order doesn't map to how the problem actually unfolds. They need step three before step one makes sense. Or they need a mental model before the tactics have anywhere to land.

The best digital products are built backwards. Start with the outcome the buyer wants. Work backwards to the last thing they need to know before that outcome becomes achievable. Then the thing before that. Keep going until you hit the starting point that matches where they actually are when they buy.

This is curriculum design. It is a separate skill from expertise. You can know everything about a subject and still build a terrible product because you can't remember what it was like not to know it.

The curse of knowledge is the biggest threat to your product quality. The fix is to teach in beta. Watch what confuses people. Listen to the questions they ask. Those questions are the gaps in your sequence, and filling them is what turns a good product into a great one.

**Why It Matters:** A well-sequenced product produces results. Results produce reviews, referrals, and repeat buyers. The curriculum is the marketing.

**One Action:** Take your current main product and identify the one step where most buyers get stuck. Fix that step before you build anything new.
