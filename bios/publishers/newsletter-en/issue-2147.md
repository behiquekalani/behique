# Content Strategy Without an Audience Is Just Writing in a Diary

Most content strategies fail before they start because they focus on creation without addressing distribution. Making things is the easy part.

## The Idea

Distribution is the skill most creators underinvest in. They spend 90% of their time on production and 10% on getting it seen. The math should be closer to 50/50, especially in the early stages when your audience is small.

The fastest way to grow is not better content. It is better placement. Getting one piece of content in front of the right five hundred people is worth more than a hundred pieces seen by nobody. This requires understanding where your specific audience already spends time and meeting them there.

There are two types of distribution. Owned and borrowed. Owned distribution is your email list, your website, your direct channel. Borrowed distribution is posting on platforms you do not control. Platforms change their algorithms. They ban accounts. They die. Your email list does not.

A content strategy that does not build owned distribution is a house on rented land. You are one policy change away from losing everything you built. The right sequence is: use borrowed distribution to grow, convert that audience to owned channels, and use owned channels to launch.

Every piece of content should serve one of two purposes. Attract new people, or deepen the relationship with existing ones. Know which one you are doing before you publish.

## Why It Matters

You could write the best content on the internet and still fail if nobody sees it. Distribution is not an afterthought. It is the product.

Build the channel before you need it.

## One Action

Identify one place your ideal audience already congregates online. Spend 20 minutes there today studying what resonates with them. Then create one post designed specifically for that context.
