# Issue 1844: Why Most Advice Fails You
**One insight. No fluff.**

Advice is a snapshot. It captures what worked for one person, in one context, at one moment. When you apply it to a different context, the results change. Most advice fails because people forget this.

The deeper problem is that advice travels without its conditions. "Wake up at 5am" strips out the person's job, their kids' sleep schedule, their energy type, their goals. You get the rule but not the reason. Rules without reasons are cargo cult thinking.

What you need is not advice but principles. Principles explain why something works, which lets you adapt them to your situation. Instead of copying what someone did, understand what problem they were solving. Then solve your version of that problem. You will get better results and actually understand what you are doing.
