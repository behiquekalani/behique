# Issue 1737: Digital Products Do Not Sleep

**One insight. No fluff.**

A digital product is a piece of your thinking made permanent and sellable. You build it once. It sells while you are asleep, on a walk, in a conversation with someone you love. The economics of this are fundamentally different from trading time for money, and most people still have not fully absorbed what that means for how they should be spending their days.

The ceiling on time-traded-for-money is hard. There are only so many hours. The ceiling on a well-positioned digital product is not set by your hours. It is set by how many people have the problem it solves and how well you explain to them that it solves it. That is a radically different constraint.

The work is in the front. Research, creation, positioning, first sales. After that, the main job is driving traffic to something that already works. One good digital product, properly positioned, gives you options that no hourly rate can replicate. Build the thing. Put it in front of the right people. Let it work while you build the next one.
