# Cash Flow Is Not Profit. Know the Difference.

You can be profitable on paper and broke in your bank account at the same time. Most solopreneurs learn this the hard way.

## Why Solopreneurs Confuse the Two
Profit is what's left after all expenses are subtracted from revenue. Cash flow is the actual movement of money in and out of your accounts right now. A business can show profit on a spreadsheet while the owner can't pay rent because invoices haven't cleared.

If you're running on one-time sales and irregular income, this gap becomes a real problem. Plan for it or suffer from it.

## Building a Cash Flow Buffer
The first financial goal for any solopreneur isn't investment. It's a buffer. Three months of operating expenses sitting in a separate account. It removes desperation from your business decisions. You stop taking bad clients because you need the money. You stop underpricing because rent is due.

That buffer is not lazy money. It's the cost of thinking clearly.

## Simple Habits That Stabilize Income
Invoice immediately. Track receivables weekly. Separate your business and personal accounts. Know your monthly fixed costs by memory. These aren't complicated accounting skills. They're basic discipline that most solopreneurs skip until something goes wrong.

## This Week's Action
Add up your last three months of business expenses. Divide by three. That's your monthly baseline. Set a savings goal to build three times that number in a dedicated account. Start transferring a fixed percentage from every payment you receive.
