# Mental Models Are Shortcuts for Wise People

A mental model is a compressed understanding of how something works. The person with better models makes better decisions with less effort. That asymmetry compounds over time.

## Why Models Beat Information

Information overload is not a storage problem. It is a retrieval problem. You cannot act on information you cannot access when you need it. A mental model is pre-digested information you can apply instantly.

Reading everything about marketing is less useful than deeply understanding one model: people buy based on how they see themselves, not on features. That one model changes how you write copy, structure offers, and talk to customers.

## The Models Worth Learning First

First principles thinking: break the problem down to its true components and reason up from there. Inversion: instead of asking what will make this succeed, ask what will make it fail and avoid those things. Compounding: small consistent actions produce outsized results over time, in money, skill, and relationships equally.

These three alone will change most decisions you make in a day.

## Building Your Own Models

The best models come from your own experience. When something works or fails badly, extract the principle. Write it down. Test whether it applies in other contexts.

Your personal model library is a competitive edge that cannot be copied. It was built from your specific experiences and failures. No one else has that exact database.

## This Week's Action

Write down one principle you have learned from personal experience this year. Make it a sentence that could apply outside its original context. Share it with someone who would benefit from it.
