# Solve Your Own Problem. Then Sell the Solution.

The best product idea you have is probably the one you wish had existed when you were stuck.

## The Idea

Most people search for product ideas by looking outward. They analyze markets, study competitors, look for gaps. That research can work. But the cleanest product ideas come from the inside: what problem did you personally solve that took you far too long?

When you have lived the problem, you know the confusion, the failed attempts, the moment the solution finally clicked. You understand the emotional journey, not just the technical steps. That understanding is what makes great teaching and great products.

The person who built a system to stay consistent with client work while managing ADHD knows what it felt like to fail at that before succeeding. They can speak to the frustration in a way that resonates. A creator who researched that market from the outside never can.

This approach also eliminates the validation step for the simplest reason. You already know the problem is real. You already know the solution works. You have one living case study: yourself.

The objection is "my problem is too specific." That objection is usually wrong. If you encountered the problem, others did too. If you searched for a solution and found nothing satisfying, that is evidence of a gap. Your experience points to a market.

Document how you solved it. Write it clearly enough that someone earlier in your journey could follow it. Package it. That is a product.

## Why It Matters

Products built from genuine experience carry authenticity that manufactured solutions never match. That authenticity is felt by buyers and produces better outcomes, which drives word-of-mouth and repeat purchases.

## One Action

Write down the three hardest problems you solved in the last two years. For each one, write one sentence on how you solved it. Pick the one where the solution is most transferable. That is your next product brief.
