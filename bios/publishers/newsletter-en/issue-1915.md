# Issue 1915: Boredom Is a Signal, Not a Problem

**One insight. No fluff.**

The first instinct when boredom arrives is to eliminate it. Reach for the phone. Open another tab. Start a new project. This response treats boredom as an error in the system, something to be fixed immediately. But boredom is one of the most useful signals your mind produces. It tells you that the current stimulus level is below your capacity. That gap, if you sit in it, generates something valuable.

Most original thinking doesn't happen during active work. It happens in the spaces between. The shower. The walk. The quiet moment when you're not trying to produce anything. These spaces are where the mind makes connections that focused attention prevents. Focused attention is good at following known paths. Boredom sends the mind off the paths to see what's there. That's where unexpected ideas come from.

Build deliberate boredom into your day. Not as punishment or spiritual practice. As a creative tool. Ten minutes with no input, no screen, no podcast. Just you and whatever your mind produces when there's nothing to react to. The discomfort fades quickly. What emerges after it fades is often more useful than anything you'd have produced by filling the time with content. The mind wants to work. Give it nothing to react to, and it will start creating.
