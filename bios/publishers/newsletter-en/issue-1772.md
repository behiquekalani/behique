# Issue 1772: Money Follows Clarity, Not Hustle
**One insight. No fluff.**

Working harder on the wrong thing produces nothing except exhaustion. The person who is busy all day but unclear on what they are actually building will not reach a financial goal by adding more hours. The hours are not the constraint. The clarity is.

Clarity means knowing exactly who has the problem you solve, exactly what that problem costs them, and exactly how your thing makes it better. Most people can answer one of those. Few can answer all three without vagueness. That vagueness is the revenue leak.

Before the next sprint, test your clarity. Say out loud: "I help [specific person] do [specific thing] by [specific mechanism]." If it sounds generic, the positioning needs work before the hustle does. Clear positioning sells. Busy positioning just looks productive.
