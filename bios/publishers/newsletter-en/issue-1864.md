# Issue 1864: The Problem With Planning Everything

**One insight. No fluff.**

Planning feels like progress. It has all the emotional rewards of action without the risk of failure. You move things around on a spreadsheet. You build the roadmap. You color-code the timeline. Hours pass and nothing exists in the world that did not exist before you sat down. Planning is the most socially acceptable form of procrastination we have invented.

The plan is not the work. It is a proxy for starting the work, and proxies accumulate. You plan the plan, then revise the plan, then share the plan for feedback. At each stage you feel like you are moving, but you are standing still. Real progress is messy and unplanned. The best version of your project exists in a future you cannot map from here.

Start with a direction, not a plan. Write the first sentence, ship the rough draft, build the ugly prototype. The feedback from doing one hour of real work is worth more than a week of planning. The path clarifies only after you take the first steps. You cannot think your way to clarity. You have to build your way there.
