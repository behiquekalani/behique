# What Most People Get Wrong About Niche

A niche is not a cage. It's a launchpad.

## The Idea

The fear of niching down is real. You worry that picking a specific audience means cutting off everyone else. So instead, you stay broad. "I help people improve their lives." "I create content for entrepreneurs." Technically true. Completely invisible.

Here's what the fear gets wrong. Specificity doesn't shrink your audience. It sharpens your signal. When you speak directly to one type of person with one type of problem, everyone with that problem recognizes themselves immediately. The message lands. The people who aren't that person move on. And that's fine.

The mistake is thinking a niche means only that niche forever. It doesn't. The most followed creators started in a narrow lane. A specific problem, a specific person, a specific point of view. Once they built a core audience that trusted them deeply, they expanded. The expansion worked because the foundation was solid.

Also worth noting: "creator" is not a niche. "Solopreneur" is barely a niche. "Solopreneur who builds digital product businesses without a social media following" is a niche. The difference is specificity of problem and person.

If you're struggling to grow, the answer is almost never to get broader. It's to get more specific. Find the sharper version of your message. Speak to a smaller group with more precision. Let the signal compound before you widen it.

## Why It Matters

Niche clarity reduces content decision fatigue, improves conversion on your offers, and builds a reputation faster than a broad approach ever will.

## One Action

Rewrite your bio or content positioning in one sentence that includes who specifically you help, what specific problem you solve, and what specific outcome they get. Test it for 30 days before changing it.
