# Issue 2566: Building in Public Is Not Oversharing

**The Idea:** Building in public gets misunderstood. People think it means posting every thought, every revenue number, every failure in real time. That is not what it is. That is a performance of transparency.

Real building in public is showing the thinking behind the decisions. Not just "I made $3,200 this month" but "here is why I priced it at $97, what happened when I raised it to $147, and what I learned." The numbers are context. The thinking is the value.

People follow builders they can learn from. What makes someone worth following is not how much they make. It is how they think. Show the framework behind the choice, the question you asked yourself before acting, the thing you would do differently.

Building in public also builds trust in a way no ad can. When someone watches you work through a real problem in real time, they feel like they know you. That trust transfers to your products and services. People buy from those they trust. They trust those they have watched earn it.

**Why It Matters:** Transparent process is one of the most underused marketing strategies available to solopreneurs. It requires no budget, builds deep trust, and creates content that your competitors cannot copy because it comes from your actual experience.

**One Action:** Share one real decision you made this week in your business. Not the outcome. The thinking. The question you wrestled with and what you decided. Post it wherever your audience lives.
