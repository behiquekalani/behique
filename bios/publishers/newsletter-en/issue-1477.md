# The Niche Is Not a Box, It's an Entry Point

Most people resist niching because they think it limits them. It does the opposite. It gives you a clear entry point into someone's mind, and once you are in, you can go anywhere.

## Why Specificity Grows Audiences Faster
The internet rewards specificity because search, algorithm, and word of mouth all operate on categories. "I help solopreneurs build digital product businesses" cuts through faster than "I help people improve their lives." The first creates an instant filter. The right people self-select. The wrong people scroll past.

Broader positioning does not catch more fish. It catches fewer, because no one knows it is meant for them.

## The Expansion Strategy
Start narrow, earn trust, then expand. Tim Ferriss started with productivity for overwhelmed professionals. Now he covers everything. James Clear started with habits. Naval started with startups. They earned the right to expand by first becoming trusted in one domain. The audience follows the person, not the category.

You cannot skip the narrow phase and get the broad audience.

## How to Pick the Entry Point
Your niche should be at the intersection of three things: a problem you understand deeply, an audience you can find and speak to, and a market willing to pay for solutions. All three need to be present. One or two is not enough. If you cannot find the third, the niche is a hobby, not a business.

Be honest about which one you are missing.

## This Week's Action
Write your positioning in one sentence using this format: "I help [specific person] do [specific outcome] without [specific pain or obstacle]." Test it on five people outside your industry and see if they immediately understand it.
