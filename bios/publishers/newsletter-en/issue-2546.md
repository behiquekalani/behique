# Issue 2546: Build in Public, Not for Vanity

**The Idea:** Building in public has become a strategy, which means it has also become a performance. People share milestones for attention. Progress updates for follower growth. The transparency is real but the purpose is validation.

That version works short-term. But it burns out because the audience becomes the point, and when engagement dips, so does the motivation to build.

The version that actually compounds is different. You build in public because it forces clarity. Explaining what you are building to an audience forces you to understand it better yourself. The act of articulation sharpens your thinking.

You also build in public because it creates accountability without needing a boss. When you tell people what you are working on, you are more likely to finish it. That external commitment functions as a productivity system.

And you build in public because it attracts the right collaborators, customers, and critics. The people who follow the process are already invested before the product launches. They are your warmest audience when it is time to sell.

The goal is not to be the most transparent person on the internet. The goal is to use transparency as a tool for building better and selling more effectively. Vanity metrics are a distraction. Real outcomes are the signal.

**Why It Matters:** Done right, building in public is a business development strategy, not a content strategy. It creates an engaged audience that wants you to succeed.

**One Action:** Share one thing you are currently working on, why you are building it, and what problem it solves. Do it today, without waiting for it to be finished.
