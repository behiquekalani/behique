# Issue 2635: Niche Clarity Is an Ongoing Project

**The Idea:** People treat niche selection as a one-time decision. Pick the niche, build the audience, run the business. Done.

That is not how it works.

Your niche is a living hypothesis. You start with a guess about who you serve and what they need. Evidence comes in through sales, conversations, content performance, and the questions people ask you. That evidence should update the hypothesis.

The danger of locking in a niche too early is that you optimize for a category before you have enough signal. You build a brand around a problem that turns out to be less painful than you thought, or you attract an audience that cannot afford your solution, or you realize three years in that the work makes you miserable.

The danger of never locking in is that you stay too broad to be remembered. Generic positioning is invisible. The market is not paying attention to everyone. It pays attention to people who say something specific to someone specific.

The working approach is to commit to your current best guess while staying open to updates. Run it for six months. Collect the signal. Refine. The niche that sustains a long-term business is usually discovered through iteration, not by reading the right article about positioning.

**Why It Matters:** The right niche is the one where your specific knowledge meets a painful problem held by people who can and will pay. Finding that intersection takes time and experimentation.

**One Action:** Write down your current niche hypothesis in one sentence. Then list three things that would make you revise it. Are any of those things happening now?
