# Issue 2396: The Attention Economy Has a Simple Rule

**The One Thing:** The creator who teaches the most useful thing wins the most attention.

---

Attention is finite. Every person on your platform has 24 hours. They allocate some of those hours to content consumption. You are competing for a slot. The only sustainable way to win that slot is to be genuinely useful, more consistently than everyone else trying to reach the same person.

## Entertainment Versus Education

Both work. Both capture attention. But education converts. Someone entertained by a video moves on. Someone who learned something from a video subscribes, saves the post, and comes back. Your content goal is not to be watched. It is to be remembered and returned to. That requires teaching, not just performing.

## The Usefulness Test

Before publishing anything, ask: does this make my reader's life, work, or thinking better in a specific and measurable way? If the answer is vague, rewrite it until the answer is clear. "This helps people feel motivated" is not a useful outcome. "This gives someone a concrete template for their Monday planning session" is.

## The Consistency That Builds Trust

One incredible piece of content does not build an audience. A reliable stream of useful content does. Trust is built through repetition. When someone reads your newsletter and finds something actionable three weeks in a row, they start to depend on you. That dependency is the foundation of a real creator business.

---

**This Week's Action:** Look at your last five pieces of content. For each one, write down the single most actionable thing a reader learned. If you cannot write it, your content is not useful enough yet. Revise your approach for next week.

**Quote:** "Be so good they cannot ignore you." — Steve Martin
