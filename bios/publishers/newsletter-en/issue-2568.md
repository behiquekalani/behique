# Issue 2568: The Digital Product Model Nobody Talks About

**The Idea:** Everyone knows about courses and ebooks. What fewer people discuss is the digital product model that does not require you to teach everything.

The most profitable digital products solve one specific problem at a specific moment. Not "everything you need to know about freelancing." Just "the exact email template that closes a discovery call." One page. One outcome. $27.

These micro-products are fast to build, easy to understand, and carry low friction to buy. The customer does not need to commit to a course. They see the problem they have right now, they see your solution, and the price is low enough that deciding feels easy.

Micro-products also serve as the top of a value ladder. Someone who buys a $27 template trusts you. They know you deliver. When you offer a $297 course or a $2,000 service package, they have already experienced your quality. The conversion rate on a warm buyer is five to ten times higher than on a cold one.

The model is not a race to build the biggest product. It is a system of small, specific, useful things that build trust incrementally and generate recurring revenue from a growing body of buyers.

**Why It Matters:** A library of ten $27 products that sell consistently beats one $97 course that needs constant promotion. Small products compound. They also teach you what your audience actually values before you invest months building something big.

**One Action:** Name one specific problem your ideal customer has in the next 48 hours. Write a one-sentence description of a product that solves only that problem. That is your next micro-product.
