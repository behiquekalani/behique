# Issue 2589: Building in Public Is Not a Marketing Strategy

**The Idea:** Building in public gets talked about as a growth tactic. Post your revenue numbers, share your failures, document the journey. People will follow, trust you, and eventually buy.

That framing is technically accurate and completely misses the point.

The creators who build in public effectively are not doing it for the algorithm. They are doing it because processing things publicly sharpens their thinking. Writing about a problem forces clarity. Explaining a decision creates accountability. Sharing a failure extracts the lesson.

When you treat building in public as a performance, the audience can tell. The content is technically transparent but emotionally hollow. You are narrating, not sharing. The difference is visible.

The version that works starts from genuine reflection. You share what you are actually working through, including uncertainty and contradiction. That kind of honesty is rare enough online that people pay attention.

The side effect is growth. But the root is not growth-seeking. It is a commitment to thinking out loud about real things.

If you can only build in public when things are going well, you are performing. If you can do it during the hard months too, you are building something real.

**Why It Matters:** Authentic public building creates trust that cannot be manufactured. It compounds into a reputation for honesty, which is worth more than any growth hack over a multi-year timeline.

**One Action:** Share one honest reflection about something in your business that is not going as planned. Not as a complaint. As a genuine observation.
