# Creator Economics: The Numbers That Matter

Most creators track vanity metrics. The ones who build real businesses track these.

## Revenue Per Subscriber

Follower counts tell you nothing. Revenue per subscriber tells you whether your audience is engaged enough to buy.

A creator with 1,000 email subscribers and $5 average revenue per subscriber earns $5,000 from that list. A creator with 50,000 followers and no product earns nothing. Build the smaller, more engaged audience before the large, passive one.

## The Conversion Math

If 1% of your email list buys a $100 product, a list of 1,000 generates $1,000 per launch. A list of 5,000 generates $5,000. The math is simple. The execution is consistent content plus clear offers.

Most creators skip the clear offer part. They build the audience and then feel uncomfortable selling to it. The audience expects an offer eventually. Give them one.

## Lifetime Value Thinking

Stop thinking per transaction. Start thinking per person. If a customer buys your $47 PDF, then your $197 course, then your $997 program, the lifetime value is over $1,200.

The first sale is just the beginning of the relationship. The economics of a creator business only become clear when you track what happens after the first sale.

## This Week's Action

Calculate your current revenue per email subscriber. If you do not have one, that is the answer. Set a target: $2 per subscriber per month. Everything you build should move you toward that number.
