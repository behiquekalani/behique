# The Anti-Niche Strategy

Everyone tells you to niche down. Pick one topic. One audience. One problem. That advice works great if you want to be a vendor. It fails if you want to build a brand.

## Why Niches Become Cages
A niche is a positioning tool, not an identity. When you define yourself entirely by what you talk about, you limit what you can become. The market shifts. Interests evolve. The creator locked inside a niche has to choose between staying relevant and staying "on brand."

The anti-niche approach is different. Instead of picking a topic, you pick a perspective. Your worldview is the through-line. Every subject you touch carries the same lens. That's what makes a creator irreplaceable.

## The Intersection Is the Strategy
Your actual value isn't in knowing one thing deeply. It's in connecting things most people don't connect. The engineer who reads philosophy. The marketer who studies military history. The developer who thinks about psychology.

That intersection is yours alone. Nobody can replicate it. The broader your input, the more unique your output. Wide reading is competitive advantage.

## What Category of One Looks Like
When your perspective is the product, you don't compete on topic. You compete on clarity of thought. People don't follow you because you cover a subject. They follow you because you make them see that subject differently.

That's a harder thing to build. It's also a harder thing to replace.

## This Week's Action
Write down three subjects you know well that most people in your primary field don't study. Find one connection between them and write a short post about it this week.
