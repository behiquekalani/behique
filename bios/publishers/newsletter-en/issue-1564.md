# Niche Domination Is a Patience Game

The creators who own a niche didn't get there fast. They just didn't stop.

## What Domination Actually Looks Like
It's not about being the most famous. It's about being the most associated. When someone in your niche hears a specific problem, they think of you. That association doesn't happen from one viral post. It happens from consistent, specific, useful output over months and years.

You don't need to win the internet. You need to win one small corner of it. A niche that's profitable, specific enough to own, and broad enough to sustain a business.

## The Niching Down Fear
Most people resist going narrow because it feels like closing doors. It does the opposite. Specificity is what makes you memorable. "I help freelancers price their services" is more powerful than "I help people with business." Narrow niches have less competition and more loyal audiences.

The goal is to be the obvious choice for your specific person. Not a decent option for everyone.

## How You Actually Own It
Show up consistently with useful content. Take public positions on things in your niche. Build a framework or system that has your name on it. That's the playbook. There's no secret beyond repetition and specificity.

The person who publishes 200 specific pieces of content in one niche owns that niche. Most people publish 20 and give up.

## This Week's Action
Write down your niche in one sentence. Then make it more specific. Then more specific again. Keep going until it feels almost too narrow. That's usually about right. Post one piece of content with that hyper-specific angle.
