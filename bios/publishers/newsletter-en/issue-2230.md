# The Mental Model That Changes How You Work

Working harder inside a bad model just gets you to the wrong destination faster.

## The Idea

A mental model is a simplified representation of how something works. You already have them. You have a model for how attention works, how markets work, how people make decisions. The question is whether those models are accurate.

One of the most useful mental models for builders is first-principles thinking. Instead of reasoning by analogy, which is "this worked for someone else so I'll copy it," you break the problem down to its base elements and reason up from there.

Applied to your business: what is the core problem your audience has? What does solving that problem actually require? What's the simplest possible way to deliver that solution? Most businesses are built by copying adjacent models. First-principles builders ask whether the model makes sense at all.

Another high-leverage model: second-order thinking. Most people think one step ahead. Second-order thinking asks: and then what? If I price low, I get more buyers. And then what? Low-commitment buyers who don't use the product and generate no testimonials. And then what? I'm stuck rebuilding from scratch with no social proof.

The quality of your decisions is largely determined by the quality of your models. Bad models make you work hard in the wrong direction. Good models show you leverage points: small inputs that produce large outputs.

## Why It Matters

You can't upgrade your results without upgrading your thinking. Tactics change. Mental models, when accurate, apply across contexts and compound over time.

## One Action

Pick one decision you're currently wrestling with in your business. Apply second-order thinking: list the first-order consequence, then the second-order consequence for each option. Choose based on what the chain of effects looks like, not just the immediate outcome.
