# The Mental Model That Changes Everything

Second-order thinking. Most people only see what happens next. The ones who build lasting things see what happens after that.

## The Idea

First-order thinking asks: what is the immediate result of this action? Second-order thinking asks: what is the result of that result? Third-order thinking goes one step further.

This is not complexity for its own sake. It is the difference between decisions that feel good in the moment and decisions that hold up over time.

Example. You lower your price to get more customers. First-order result: more sales. Second-order result: you attract price-sensitive customers who are harder to retain. Third-order result: your business becomes a grind of high volume and low margins with customers who leave the moment someone cheaper appears.

A creator who posts controversial content for engagement gets followers fast. But those followers are there for controversy, not for their ideas. When the creator wants to sell something, the audience does not convert. The first-order success created a second-order trap.

Second-order thinking is a discipline. It requires slowing down before acting. It requires asking uncomfortable questions about where a path actually leads, not just where it starts. Most people skip this because the answer sometimes means saying no to something that feels good right now.

## Why It Matters

The decisions that shape your life most significantly are almost never the obvious ones. They are the ones where you had a feeling something was off but proceeded anyway because the first step looked good. Train yourself to look two steps ahead.

## One Action

Take one major decision you are currently considering. Write the first-order result, then the second-order result, then the third. Make the decision based on the third, not the first.
