# You Don't Have a Productivity Problem

You have a clarity problem. Productivity tools won't fix that.

## The Idea

Most people trying to "get more done" are optimizing the wrong variable. They add apps, systems, and routines on top of a foundation that was never solid to begin with. The result is a faster hamster wheel.

The real question isn't how to do more. It's what deserves to exist on your calendar at all.

Clarity precedes execution. Before you optimize how you work, you need a ruthless answer to why. Not a mission statement. A concrete answer to: if I did this one thing well, everything else becomes easier or irrelevant.

Warren Buffett's 25-5 rule captures this. Write 25 goals. Circle the top 5. The other 20 are your avoid-at-all-costs list. Not because they're bad. Because they compete with what matters.

This isn't about doing less. It's about doing less of the wrong things with total commitment so the right things get your best energy. Your best energy is finite. It runs out by afternoon for most people. You don't have a time shortage. You have a priority leak.

## Why It Matters

Every system you build on top of fuzzy priorities will eventually collapse. The inbox zero habit, the morning routine, the Notion dashboard. They all depend on knowing what you're pointed at.

When the direction is clear, execution becomes obvious. You stop deliberating and start building.

## One Action

Write down the three things that, if done consistently for 90 days, would change your business or creative output the most. Put them at the top of every day's plan. Everything else competes for what's left.
