# Issue 1887: The Cost of Keeping Options Open

**One insight. No fluff.**

Optionality feels like freedom. It is often the opposite. When you keep every door open, you walk through none of them. The person who has not committed to a direction spends energy managing possibilities instead of building outcomes. The options themselves become the project.

Every major thing worth building required narrowing. You can't write a book while hedging whether you want to be a writer. You can't build a real audience while wondering if you should pivot to a different topic. Commitment is not the same as certainty. You will never have certainty. Commitment means acting as if the direction is right until the evidence says otherwise.

The people who build something meaningful are not the ones with the most options. They are the ones who picked a direction and went all the way down it. The optionality mindset is a comfort mechanism. It costs you the compounding that only comes from going deep.
