# Issue 1758: Build the Minimum Viable Belief

**One insight. No fluff.**

You do not need full confidence to start. You need just enough belief to take the next action. People wait for certainty before beginning, but certainty only comes from doing. This is a trap with no exit unless you break the condition.

The minimum viable belief is: "This might work, and not trying guarantees failure." That is enough. You do not need to believe you will succeed. You need to believe that attempting is better than abstaining.

Most successful builders did not have complete faith at the start. They had enough to take one step. The next step became possible only after the first one was taken. Belief builds on evidence. You cannot get the evidence without the action. Start with less certainty than you think you need.
