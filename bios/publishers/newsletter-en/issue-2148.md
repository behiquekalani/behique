# Mental Models Are the Actual Return on Reading

Most people read to feel informed. Few people read to change how they think. The difference in outcome is enormous.

## The Idea

A mental model is a framework for understanding how something works. When you internalize a good one, it changes what you notice, what questions you ask, and what decisions you make. It is the difference between seeing patterns and just seeing events.

Most people accumulate information without building models. They read a book, retain a few interesting facts, and move on. The information decays. The model would have lasted.

The builders who make consistently good decisions are not smarter. They have better filters. When they encounter a new problem, they have a library of proven frameworks to apply. First principles thinking. Inversion. The 80/20 principle. Second-order consequences. These are not just concepts. They are tools.

The way to extract models from what you read is simple. After every book, ask one question: what is the core mechanism here? Not the examples. Not the stories. The underlying principle. Write it in one sentence. That sentence is the model. The model is portable. It works in contexts the author never imagined.

Most books contain one great idea surrounded by 250 pages of examples. Find the idea. Discard the packaging.

## Why It Matters

Reading without extraction is entertainment. There is nothing wrong with entertainment. But if you want reading to compound, you need to process it differently.

Build a model file. Review it monthly. Watch your thinking change.

## One Action

Take the last book you finished. Write down the single most useful idea in it as a one-sentence principle. That is the model. File it somewhere you will actually see again.
