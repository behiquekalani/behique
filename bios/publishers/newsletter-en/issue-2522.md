# Issue 2522: Specific Knowledge Cannot Be Taught

**The Idea:** There is a category of knowledge that cannot be found in a course, a book, or a YouTube tutorial. It is built through years of doing a thing you were obsessed with before anyone paid you for it.

Naval Ravikant called it specific knowledge. It is the combination of your unusual interests, your lived experiences, and the patterns you noticed while everyone else was looking elsewhere. It sounds like personality. It feels like an accident. But it is actually the most defensible asset you can build.

The reason it cannot be taught is the same reason it cannot be copied. The path that created your specific knowledge involved your exact history. Someone else cannot replicate that. They can learn the framework you extracted, but they will never have the foundation that made the framework possible.

Most people undervalue this. They think their insights are obvious because the insights feel natural to them. That feeling of naturalness is the signal, not the problem. What feels obvious to you after a decade of obsession is genuinely invisible to people who never walked that path.

Your job is not to discover your specific knowledge. It already exists. Your job is to package it and put a price on it.

**Why It Matters:** Commodities compete on price. Specific knowledge competes on authenticity. When your offer is built from something no one else can replicate, pricing becomes easier and competition becomes irrelevant.

**One Action:** List three things you know from experience that most people only know from theory. That gap is where your product lives.
