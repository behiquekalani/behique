# Bootstrap or Die: Why Funding Makes You Fragile

Every startup story you read about raises millions before shipping anything. That is not the model. That is the exception dressed up as the rule.

## The Pressure That Focuses You

Bootstrapping forces constraints. You cannot hire your way out of a bad product. You cannot spend your way into customers who do not want what you built. The money limit forces you to talk to real people, solve real problems, and ship fast.

That pressure is a feature. Founders who raise money early lose it. They build for investors, not users. They optimize for the pitch deck, not the product.

## What Bootstrapping Actually Looks Like

It looks like charging before you build. It looks like a landing page and a Stripe link before a single line of code. It looks like one customer paying fifty dollars, then another, then ten more.

It is not glamorous. It is also not risky. You validate the idea with the market before you invest months of your life in it. That is rationality, not limitation.

## When to Bring in Money

There is a time for outside capital. It is after you have product-market fit. After you know the unit economics work. After growth is the only constraint. That is a completely different conversation than raising money to figure out if anyone wants what you are building.

Bootstrap until you cannot scale fast enough. That is the honest sequence.

## This Week's Action

Take your current idea or project and write down how you would generate the first $500 from it without building anything new. If you cannot answer that, the idea needs more clarity before more work.
