# The Problem With Multiple Income Streams

Everyone wants five income streams. Few people can sustain one good one.

## The Idea

The advice to diversify income is correct at the right stage. At the wrong stage, it is a distraction that masquerades as strategy. When you try to build five income streams at once, you are not building five streams. You are building five underfunded attempts that each receive a fraction of the attention they need to succeed.

A single income stream, properly developed, can support a one-person business. That stream needs a good offer, consistent traffic, a clear conversion path, and reliable delivery. Those four things alone take months to get right. If you divide your attention across five streams simultaneously, none of them get the focus required to cross from "working on it" to "working."

The pattern is predictable. Someone starts freelancing, launches a course, starts an affiliate page, opens an Etsy shop, and begins a newsletter all in the same quarter. Six months later, none of them have reached escape velocity. They blame the market or the algorithm. The real problem was resource allocation.

One stream, taken to profitability, gives you the cash and confidence to build the second. The second funds the third. This is how diversified income actually gets built: sequentially, not simultaneously.

## Why It Matters

The fantasy of multiple income streams is compelling because it feels like it reduces risk. In the early stages, it increases risk by diluting your ability to make any single stream work.

Real diversification comes after one stream is stable. Until then, focus is your greatest financial advantage.

## One Action

List every income stream or potential income stream you are currently working on. Circle the one closest to generating real revenue. Cut your active investment in the others to near zero. Run that one to profitability before you add anything.
