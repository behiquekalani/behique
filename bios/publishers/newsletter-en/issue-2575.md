# Issue 2575: Mental Models for Solopreneurs

**The Idea:** A mental model is a way of seeing that helps you make better decisions faster. Most solopreneurs operate on gut feel and borrowed advice. The ones who build quietly and consistently are usually running a few reliable mental models in the background.

The most useful one for solo business: the 80/20 principle applied to inputs. Twenty percent of your activities produce eighty percent of your results. The question you should ask every week is not "what do I need to do?" It is "what are the two or three things that actually move the needle, and am I protecting time for those?"

A second model: reversibility. Before you commit significant time to anything, ask whether it is reversible. Writing a blog post is reversible. Signing a year-long contract is not. Default to reversible decisions when uncertain. Commit fully to irreversible ones after careful thought.

A third model: the bottleneck. Every system has one constraint limiting its output. In a solo business, the bottleneck shifts. Sometimes it is traffic. Sometimes it is conversion. Sometimes it is delivery. Find the current bottleneck. Fix that one thing. The other problems will become visible once the current one is solved.

**Why It Matters:** Good mental models replace the need for constant research and advice. They give you a reliable filter for decisions that would otherwise consume hours of your week. Build a small library of them and decision-making becomes a superpower.

**One Action:** Apply the 80/20 model to your last week of work. List everything you did. Identify what actually moved something forward. Ask yourself honestly whether you spent most of your time on the 20 percent that matters.
