# Creator Economics: Why Most People Price Themselves Out of Success

Charging too little isn't humility. It's a death sentence for your business.

## The Psychology of Low Prices
When you undercharge, you attract the wrong customers. People who buy on price are the most demanding, the least loyal, and the quickest to ask for refunds. High prices filter for people who value outcomes, not discounts.

Pricing is a signal. It tells the market what kind of person you are and what kind of result they should expect. A $7 product says "this is casual." A $97 product says "this is serious." Same content, different buyer, different relationship.

## Value Is Not Cost-Plus
Most creators price based on what it cost them to make the product. Hours times a rough rate. That's the wrong framework. Price based on the value of the outcome. If your product saves someone 10 hours a week, it's worth 10 hours of their salary per week. That's your floor.

The gap between what something costs to create and what it's worth to the buyer is where profit lives. Creators who understand this stop competing on price entirely.

## The Ladder You Need
One free offer. One low-ticket product. One mid-tier. One high-end. This ladder is not about extracting money. It's about serving people at different levels of commitment. Some will start free. Some will go straight to high-end. Build all four and let the customer choose their entry point.

## This Week's Action
Pick one product you currently sell or plan to sell. Double the price. Write one paragraph explaining why it's worth that. If you can't write the paragraph, the product needs work. If you can, raise the price.
