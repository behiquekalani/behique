# Issue 2073: The Compounding Nobody Talks About

**One insight. No fluff.**

Everyone talks about financial compounding. Fewer people talk about the kind that matters more at the beginning: skill compounding. Every hour you spend becoming sharper at your craft makes the next hour more productive. Every project you complete gives you pattern recognition that shortens future projects. The output grows faster than the hours you put in, if you stay in the same domain long enough.

The opposite is also true. Switching between unrelated skills resets the compound curve every time. You trade the accelerating returns of depth for the slow early-curve returns of breadth. There are moments when breadth is strategic. But most people switch too often because the early part of a new skill feels like progress while the late part of an existing skill feels like diminishing returns. The feeling is wrong. The late stage is where the acceleration actually lives.

Pick the skill that intersects your interest and the market's need. Go further into it than feels comfortable. The people who seem disproportionately productive are usually not working more hours. They are working deep in a skill where their compounded experience makes each hour worth three of someone else's. That is the return nobody shows you on a chart.
