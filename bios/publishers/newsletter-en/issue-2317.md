# Build in Public, Not for Applause

Sharing your process is a distribution strategy. Performing your process is a trap.

## The Idea

Building in public has become popular enough that it now has two versions. The first version is useful: sharing the real process of building something, including the failures, the pivots, the lessons, and the small wins. This builds trust over time because it is honest and specific and demonstrates actual knowledge.

The second version is a performance. It shares the aesthetics of building. The workstation photo. The revenue screenshot. The "I just hit X milestone" post that is more about the number than the lesson. This version attracts followers, but not the kind that buy or care long-term. It optimizes for applause.

The distinction matters because they require different mindsets and produce different results. Genuine building in public is content created from the work. You are doing the work, then sharing what you learned. The content is a byproduct of the process.

Performed building in public is work done for the content. The actions are reverse-engineered to produce a shareable moment. This is not inherently dishonest, but it distorts the priorities and eventually shows. The audience that values authenticity detects the shift.

Share the lesson, not the moment. Share the decision, not the result. Share the framework you discovered, not the revenue that validated it. That version is actually useful to someone else.

## Why It Matters

The audience that trusts you, that really trust you, is built on honesty over time. It cannot be manufactured with highlights. Build the thing first. Let the content be real because the work is real.

## One Action

Write one "build in public" post this week based entirely on a decision you made, not a result you achieved. Describe the options you considered, what you chose, and why. No numbers required. Just the thinking. Post it and see what kind of response you get.
