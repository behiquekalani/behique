# You're Charging for Time. Start Charging for Outcomes.

Hourly pricing caps your income at the number of hours you can work. Outcome pricing is tied to the value you create, which is not limited by the clock.

## The Hourly Trap
When you charge by the hour, the client is always watching the meter. Every hour you bill is an expense in their mind. And when you get faster at the work, you earn less for the same result. The model punishes expertise.

Outcome pricing flips this. You charge for the result delivered, not the time spent. A client pays for a website that converts, not for forty hours of design work. The distinction changes everything.

## What Outcome Pricing Requires
You have to know your client's goals clearly enough to define what success looks like. You have to be confident in your ability to deliver it. And you have to be able to articulate the value of that outcome in terms they understand.

A logo isn't worth $500. A brand identity that attracts a premium client worth $10,000 to the business is worth a percentage of that $10,000.

## Transitioning From Hours to Outcomes
You don't have to change everything at once. Start with your next proposal. Instead of listing hours and rates, list deliverables and the outcome they produce. Give one price. Tie it to the result. Watch how the conversation changes.

## This Week's Action
Look at your current pricing or last proposal. Rewrite it in outcome language. Replace every mention of hours or rates with the specific result the client gets. Send the rewritten version to one prospect and compare the response.
