# Video Is the Fastest Path to Trust

Text builds credibility. Video builds trust. There's a difference.

## Why the Camera Changes Everything
When someone reads your writing, they know what you think. When they watch you talk, they know who you are. Voice, pace, energy, hesitation. All of it transmits. You can fake a persona in text for months. You can't fake it on camera for three minutes.

This is why video converts. The person who watches five of your videos feels like they know you. They're not evaluating whether to trust you. They've already decided.

## The Format Doesn't Matter as Much as You Think
Short form builds reach. Long form builds depth. Both work. The mistake is avoiding video entirely because you don't have the perfect setup. Your phone and a window with natural light is enough to start. Equipment is an excuse when you're starting. Execution is everything.

The first ten videos will feel uncomfortable. That's normal. Comfort comes from repetition, not from better gear or more preparation. Ship the imperfect video.

## The Compounding Effect
Every video you make teaches you something about how you communicate. Your presence improves. Your clarity sharpens. A library of videos works for you constantly, surfacing in search, getting shared, building context for new viewers.

One video a week for a year is 52 touchpoints with your audience. That's a lot of trust built while you slept.

## This Week's Action
Record one video this week. Keep it under five minutes. Teach one specific thing you know well. Don't over-edit. Publish it. The goal is to make starting normal, not perfect.
