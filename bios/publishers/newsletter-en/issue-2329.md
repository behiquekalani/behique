# The Power of the Narrow Niche

The riches are in the niches. Not because it is a cute saying, but because of how trust works.

## The Idea

When you try to be relevant to everyone, you are relevant to no one. The human brain is wired to trust specialists. The doctor who only treats one condition. The lawyer who only handles one type of case. The consultant who only works with one type of company. Specialization signals mastery.

Online, this effect is amplified. Algorithms reward content that performs well with specific audiences. Communities form around specific interests. People follow creators who speak directly to their situation, not creators who speak generally to "people trying to improve."

The fear of niching down is real. You worry about cutting off potential customers. But the narrower your niche, the clearer your message. The clearer your message, the more the right people trust you. The more they trust you, the easier it is to sell anything.

You can always expand later. The creator who builds a loyal audience of 5,000 dedicated followers can pivot and bring those followers along. The creator with 50,000 disengaged followers cannot do anything with that number. Depth before breadth. Always.

## Why It Matters

A narrow niche is not a limitation. It is a foundation. You build something real with a small, trusting audience and then grow from that foundation. Generic appeals build nothing. Specific depth builds everything.

## One Action

Name the most specific version of your audience. Not "entrepreneurs" but "first-generation college grads trying to build their first online business." The more specific, the better your content will perform.
