# Identity Comes Before Strategy

Most business advice skips a step. It tells you what to do before it tells you who to be. That order produces inconsistent results.

## The Idea

Strategy is the plan. Identity is the operating system. A good strategy in the hands of someone without a clear identity produces confused execution. A clear identity makes strategy obvious.

When you know who you are as a builder, most decisions make themselves. You know what to say yes to because you know what you stand for. You know what to decline because you know what dilutes you. The clarity is not about being rigid. It is about being intentional.

Most people build their identity from their résumé: what they have done, what they have been paid for, what others have called them. That is backwards. Your identity should come from your values and your vision, not your history.

Ask: what kind of work do I want to be known for in ten years? What problems do I care about enough to become an expert in? What standards do I want to hold myself to even when no one is watching? The answers to those questions form your working identity.

Once the identity is clear, your content, your products, your pricing, and your client selection all align around it. You stop making one-off decisions and start executing from character.

## Why It Matters

Builders who are confused about who they are take longer to get anywhere. Not because they lack skill, but because they keep changing directions.

Clarity of identity is the unfair advantage no one talks about.

## One Action

Write three sentences. Who you are, what you do, and why it matters to you personally. Not for marketing. For yourself. Read it before you start work tomorrow.
