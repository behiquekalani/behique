# Consistency Beats Creativity When You Are Starting Out

Early-stage creators obsess over making every piece of content exceptional. That obsession is the enemy of growth.

## The Idea

Consistency is the skill that compounds. Creativity is the skill that peaks. Early on, you do not know which of your ideas will land. The only way to find out is to test many of them. Testing requires output. Output requires consistency. Consistency requires releasing the pressure to be exceptional on every rep.

The creator who posts three times a week for a year has 156 attempts to find what works. The creator who posts twice a month when inspiration strikes has 24. The second creator is not more creative. They are less practiced. Creativity develops through repetition, not through waiting.

The first hundred pieces of content you produce are tuning your instrument. You are learning what your voice sounds like. You are learning what your audience responds to. You are building the skill of finishing things. None of that happens without volume.

This does not mean produce garbage. It means release work that is 80% of what you are capable of, consistently, rather than 100% of what you are capable of, rarely. The 80% you publish compounds. The 100% you are still editing does not.

The algorithm rewards consistency. Your audience rewards consistency. Your own development rewards consistency. There is no argument for waiting.

## Why It Matters

You cannot optimize something you have not published. Perfection before publishing is the most expensive form of procrastination.

Ship the work. Then improve the next one.

## One Action

Set a publishing cadence you can maintain even on your worst week. Not the ideal cadence. The floor. Commit to that floor for 30 days without breaking it.
