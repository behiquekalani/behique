# How to Build a Product Without an Audience Yet

The common advice is "build your audience first, then create a product." That is one path. It is not the only path. And for many builders, it is the wrong one to start with.

## The Problem with Audience-First

Audience-first requires sustained public output for months before any revenue feedback. That is a hard psychological ask, especially early on. You are producing in the dark, trusting that something will eventually click.

Product-first gives you a feedback loop sooner. You build something, you try to sell it, and the market tells you immediately whether you understood the problem.

## Starting With the Problem, Not the Platform

The first question is not "where should I build an audience." It is "what problem do I understand better than most people." The answer to that question tells you what product to build. The platform follows.

Find ten people who have that problem. Talk to them. Build the minimum version of the solution. Sell it before it is perfect. The feedback from real buyers is worth more than months of free content to an unqualified audience.

## The Cold Start Strategy

You do not need followers to find early customers. You need forums, communities, and direct outreach. Post in the places where your target person already spends time. Offer to solve their specific problem. The first sale from a stranger is the real validation.

After you have sales, you have a story. That story becomes content. The content builds the audience that accelerates future sales. The sequence starts with the problem, not the platform.

## This Week's Action

Identify one specific problem you have solved in your own life or work. Write a one-sentence description of the solution. Find one online community where people with that problem gather. Post something genuinely useful there. Do it this week.
