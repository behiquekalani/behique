# Build for the Person You Were Two Years Ago

The best products come from personal experience. The best content speaks to someone who is behind you on a path you have already walked.

## The Idea

Your ideal customer is not an abstraction. They are you, earlier. Two years ago, three years ago, before you learned what you now know. That version of you had specific problems, specific fears, and specific questions. You remember what it felt like to not know the answer. That memory is your competitive advantage.

When you build for that person, your communication is automatically authentic. You are not performing empathy. You are reporting lived experience. You know exactly what they are afraid to ask because you were afraid to ask it. You know what solutions they have already tried because you tried them too. You know what finally worked.

This framing also solves the "I am not an expert enough to teach" problem. You do not need to be at the top of the mountain. You need to be one step ahead on the trail. The person behind you does not need the view from the summit. They need help getting past the part where you just were.

Most people get this wrong by trying to serve everyone. They dilute their message trying to reach beginners and intermediates and advanced practitioners simultaneously. The result is content that lands for nobody.

Serve one person. Serve them completely. Let the product speak.

## Why It Matters

The most resonant products in the world are made by people solving their own old problems. The specificity of personal experience cannot be faked.

## One Action

Write down three problems you had two years ago that you have since solved. Pick the one that still frustrates others. Build something around that solution.
