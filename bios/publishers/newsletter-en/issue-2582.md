# Issue 2582: Why Your Niche Is Probably Too Wide

**The Idea:** When people say "pick a niche," they usually mean something like "content creators" or "freelancers." That is not a niche. That is a category. Categories have no gravity.

A niche is specific enough that the right person reads your first sentence and thinks: "This is for me."

The fear of going narrow is real. You worry you will run out of people to reach. But the math works in reverse. A narrower focus means every person who finds you is more qualified. Conversion rates go up. Word of mouth gets sharper. Products become easier to define.

Consider the difference between writing for "entrepreneurs" versus writing for "freelance designers who want to productize their services." The second group knows immediately if they belong. They share your content with other people exactly like them.

You do not need millions of readers. You need thousands who are the right fit. Niche clarity is how you find them without wasting years on the wrong audience.

Most people stay vague because specificity feels like risk. It is actually the opposite. Vagueness is the risk. It means you blend into the noise and convert no one.

**Why It Matters:** Niche clarity is the foundation of every system that comes after it. Product design, content strategy, and pricing all get easier when you know exactly who you serve.

**One Action:** Write a one-sentence description of your ideal reader that is specific enough to exclude the majority of people online.
