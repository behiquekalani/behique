# Stop Waiting to Feel Ready

Readiness is a feeling. It rarely arrives before you start.

## The Preparation Trap

Preparation feels productive. It looks like work. It has all the markers of progress: research, planning, organizing, learning. But none of it is the thing itself.

The gap between preparation and execution is where most ideas die. Not from bad ideas. From indefinite refinement. The project that is 80% ready and never shipped contributes nothing.

## What Actually Makes You Ready

Shipping something makes you ready to ship the next thing. Not studying the craft. Not watching others do it. Doing it badly, learning, and doing it again.

The people who are good at creating are not better because they waited longer. They are better because they shipped earlier and collected more feedback cycles. Experience is the teacher. You cannot substitute preparation for it.

## The Cost of Waiting

Every week you wait to launch the product, you lose a week of data. You lose a week of audience feedback. You lose a week of revenue. The opportunity cost of waiting is always higher than the cost of shipping imperfectly.

Done and imperfect beats perfect and waiting. Every time.

## This Week's Action

Pick the thing you have been preparing to do. Ship the smallest version of it this week. Not the full vision. The smallest testable version. Learn from what happens. Iterate. That is the process.
