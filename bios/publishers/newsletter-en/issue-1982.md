# Issue 1982: Clarity Is a Practice, Not a State

**One insight. No fluff.**

People talk about clarity like it is something you find once and keep. Like a destination. You do the journaling, you do the meditation, and eventually clarity arrives and stays. That is not how it works. Clarity is a daily recalibration. You lose it constantly. The work is returning to it.

What actually steals clarity is not confusion. It is accumulation. Too many inputs, too many obligations, too many open loops running in the background. The mind that is trying to hold everything simultaneously cannot see anything sharply. Clarity is not added. It is recovered by subtracting what should not be there.

The practice is simple but uncomfortable. Every few days, ask what you are doing that has no clear reason. Not "no good reason," just no reason you can state in a sentence. If you cannot explain why something belongs in your life or your schedule, it is probably filling space that belongs to something better. Remove it. See what sharpens.
