# Issue 1870: The Real Reason Most Ideas Never Get Built

**One insight. No fluff.**

Ideas are abundant. The shortage is not ideas. It is the tolerance for the uncomfortable middle, the phase after the excitement of a new idea and before the satisfaction of a finished thing. That middle phase has no glamour. It is debugging code that breaks for unclear reasons. Rewriting a chapter that still does not say what you mean. Building a feature nobody asked for. This is where most ideas die.

The people who actually finish things have one advantage over the people who do not. They have been disappointed enough times that they no longer expect the process to feel good. They build anyway, not because it is enjoyable, but because they have decided the outcome matters more than the comfort of stopping. That decision is the entire difference.

You have to make a similar decision before you start. Not "this will be fun" but "this is worth finishing even when it is not." The excitement of an idea is not the work. It is the invitation to do the work. Take it seriously only if you are willing to show up on the days when the idea feels stupid and the progress feels slow. Those days are most of them.
