# Issue 1942: You Are Not Blocked, You Are Bored

**One insight. No fluff.**

When a builder says they are blocked, what they usually mean is that the next step is unclear and uncomfortable. The mind reframes discomfort as impossibility to protect itself from the risk of trying and failing. It is not a technical problem. It is a threat response.

The tell is this: if you are blocked but you can still scroll, watch videos, or have conversations, you are not blocked. You are avoiding. The difference matters because the solution is completely different. A real blocker requires external input. Avoidance requires internal honesty.

The fix is not motivation. It is friction reduction. Break the task down until one step takes less than two minutes. Do only that. The brain does not resist starting, it resists starting something it cannot see the end of. Give it a small win and it will ask for another. You are not stuck. You just haven't made the next step small enough yet.
