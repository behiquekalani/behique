# Niche Down to Scale Up

The counterintuitive truth of online income: the more specific you are, the more money you make.

## The Idea
Most people start broad because they are afraid of missing someone. They want to appeal to everyone. The result is content and products that appeal to no one strongly enough to pay.

The internet rewards specificity. When your offer, your content, and your positioning speak directly to a specific person with a specific problem, they stop scrolling. They pay attention. They share because it feels like it was written for them.

A narrow niche is not a small market. It is a clear market. "Productivity for freelancers" serves a smaller apparent audience than "productivity" but converts at a much higher rate because the relevance is undeniable. The person who needs it knows they need it immediately.

Specificity also compounds. When you are known for something precise, referrals become automatic. "You should follow this person, they are exactly about X and you need X" is a sentence your audience can say. That sentence scales you for free.

The fear is that you will box yourself in. The reality is the opposite. Specificity builds a foundation. Once you have an audience built around one clear thing, you can expand the scope. But you cannot expand from nowhere.

Start specific. Earn the right to expand later.

## Why It Matters
Broad positioning creates competition with everyone. Specific positioning creates authority in a corner of the market. Authority in a small space compounds into authority in larger spaces.

The builders who win long-term started by being the best at something narrow enough to dominate.

## One Action
Write one sentence that describes exactly who you help and what result they get. Test it with someone outside your industry. If they cannot immediately say whether it applies to them, it is not specific enough yet.
