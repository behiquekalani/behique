# Issue 2386: Why Your Prices Are Wrong

**The One Thing:** Price for the transformation you deliver, not the time it took you to create it.

---

Most creators underprice because they think about cost. How long did it take? How many words is it? How many pages, videos, modules? Buyers do not think this way. They think: what is this worth to me if it works? Price for that answer, not yours.

## The Cost-Based Pricing Trap

A 10-page PDF that saves someone $10,000 in consulting fees is worth more than a 200-page book that teaches the same thing slowly. Hours spent is irrelevant to the buyer. Outcome delivered is everything. When you price based on effort, you are solving for your ego, not the market. The market only cares about the result.

## What Transformation-Based Pricing Looks Like

Find the problem your product solves. Quantify what solving that problem is worth. A template that saves 5 hours per week is worth months of time savings annually. A guide that helps someone land their first client is worth their first client fee. Your price should be a fraction of what the transformation delivers, not a reflection of what it cost you to make.

## Raising Prices Is Not Greed

It is positioning. Higher prices attract buyers who are serious. They buy, implement, and get results. Lower prices attract window shoppers who never open the file. Raise your price. Watch your customer quality improve. Watch your refund rate drop. This is not a theory.

---

**This Week's Action:** Pick your lowest-priced product. Write down the single biggest transformation it delivers. Then research what that transformation is worth in the real world. Adjust your price accordingly.

**Quote:** "Price is what you pay. Value is what you get." — Warren Buffett
