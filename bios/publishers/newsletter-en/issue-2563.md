# Issue 2563: Price Is a Signal, Not a Number

**The Idea:** Most people underprice because they are afraid. They think a lower price means more buyers. What it actually signals is low confidence and low value.

Price is information. A $7 ebook says "this is casual content." A $197 course says "this will change something for you." The content could be identical. The price shapes the perception before anyone opens a single page.

Buyers do not evaluate price in isolation. They compare it to the outcome they expect. If your $97 guide helps someone close one freelance client, it cost them nothing. It made them money. The question is not "is this too expensive?" The question is "does this person believe the outcome is worth it?"

Your job is to make the outcome credible before the price is seen. That means testimonials, specificity, a clear before-and-after, and a tone that says you have done this yourself. When those elements are in place, raising your price increases conversions, not decreases them.

**Why It Matters:** Chronic underpricing keeps solopreneurs on a treadmill. You work more, earn less, and attract buyers who are price-sensitive and high-maintenance. Higher prices attract committed buyers who implement and get results.

**One Action:** Find one thing you sell or plan to sell. Double the price in your head. Write one sentence explaining why that outcome is worth the higher number. If you can write it convincingly, the price is probably right.
