# The One-Person Media Company Is Not Optional Anymore

Distribution used to be a competitive advantage. Now it is table stakes. If you are not building an audience, you are renting one from someone who is.

## What Changed

Three years ago you could build a solid business on referrals and word of mouth without touching social media. That still works. But the ceiling is lower, and the vulnerability is higher. One bad quarter with no inbound and you are scrambling. The creator who has been publishing for two years never scrambles the same way.

Content is not marketing. It is infrastructure. A newsletter with 3,000 readers is a distribution channel you own. A Twitter presence with sharp writing is a hiring funnel, a sales funnel, and a proof of expertise all in one.

## Start Small, Stay Consistent

The mistake is trying to be everywhere at once. Pick one format you can sustain, one platform where your audience exists, and one topic you can write about for three years. That narrowness is what makes the compounding work. Broad and sporadic beats nothing, but narrow and consistent beats everything.

You do not need to go viral. You need to be findable by the right people at the right moment.

## This Week's Action
If you do not have a newsletter, set one up this week. If you do, write the next issue. One thing. Consistent.
