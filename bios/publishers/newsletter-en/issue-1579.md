# Distribution Is the Skill Most Creators Skip

You can write the best content of your life and have nobody read it. Distribution is why.

## The Creation-Distribution Imbalance
Most creators spend 90% of their time creating and 10% distributing. The most successful creators flip that ratio, or at minimum get to 50-50. Good content with poor distribution dies. Decent content with excellent distribution thrives.

This isn't a cynical observation. It's a reminder that building an audience requires actively getting your work in front of people who don't know you yet.

## What Distribution Actually Means
It means knowing the specific channels where your audience spends time. It means having a system for cross-posting, repurposing, and submitting your content to aggregators, newsletters, and communities. It means building relationships with other creators who reach the same audience. It means showing up consistently so the algorithm has something to work with.

Distribution is not spamming. It's strategic placement.

## Building a Distribution System
Define your primary distribution channel. Where does your audience already gather? Be there consistently. Then add one secondary channel that feeds into the first. A thread that links to a newsletter. A short video that promotes a podcast. Everything points somewhere that you own or control.

Over time, you build multiple on-ramps to the same destination.

## This Week's Action
Identify one community, newsletter, or publication in your niche where your ideal audience spends time. Submit one piece of content or request a feature. Most creators never do this. Doing it once puts you ahead of most of your competition.
