# The Compounding You're Ignoring

Everyone knows about financial compounding. Very few apply the same logic to skills, reputation, and content.

## Skills Compound
The writer who writes every day doesn't just get incrementally better. At some point, the quality jumps. The gaps close. The voice becomes distinct. This is compounding. Each piece you write makes the next one faster and better. The work done in year one pays dividends in year three.

Most people quit before the compounding begins. They expect linear returns and get discouraged when early results are small. Early results are always small. That's not a warning. It's how compounding works.

## Content Compounds
A piece of content published today will still be discovered next year, in five years, in ten. The essay, the video, the newsletter issue. Unlike a service hour that is sold once, content can keep delivering value indefinitely. This is the fundamental economic advantage of the creator.

The more you publish, the larger the surface area for discovery. Each new piece is also a door into your entire back catalog. Build the catalog.

## Reputation Compounds
Every person you help, every promise you keep, every quality piece you publish is a deposit into your reputation. Reputation is slow to build and fast to destroy. But a deep reputation becomes a near-impossible competitive advantage. People do business with people they trust.

You can't buy reputation. You can only earn it. Start earning it now.

## This Week's Action
Identify one thing you've been doing for less than six months and feeling frustrated by slow results. Calculate what it would look like at month 24 if you maintained the same pace. Often the math is more encouraging than the feeling.
