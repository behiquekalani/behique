# Issue 1886: Most Problems Are Upstream

**One insight. No fluff.**

When something keeps breaking in your life, the instinct is to fix the symptom. You manage the stress instead of eliminating the stressor. You optimize the workflow instead of questioning whether the work is right. You refine the pitch instead of reconsidering the product. The fixes feel productive. The problem persists.

Most recurring problems have a cause two or three levels upstream. The fatigue is not about sleep. It is about how you spend your energy. The relationship tension is not about communication style. It is about misaligned values. The business stalling is not about marketing. It is about who you are selling to.

The discipline is to trace problems backward before solving them forward. Ask why three times before you start building a solution. Most of the time you'll find that the real fix is simpler and more fundamental than whatever you were about to build.
