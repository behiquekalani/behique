# Creator Economics: Where the Money Actually Lives

Follower count is not a business. Ad revenue is not a business. Both are outputs of a business, but neither is the thing itself.

## The Misunderstanding About Monetization

Most new creators think the path is: build audience, then figure out how to make money. The sequence is wrong. The monetization model should exist before the audience is large, because the model shapes what audience you need.

A creator selling high-ticket consulting needs a small, highly qualified audience. A creator selling a $10 template pack needs a large, broad one. Building the wrong audience for your model creates a mismatch that is hard to recover from.

## The Three Revenue Layers

The most resilient creator businesses have three layers: low-price products for volume, mid-price products for momentum, and high-price offers for leverage. Each layer serves a different segment of trust.

New readers buy the low-price thing to test. Engaged readers buy the mid-tier when they want to go deeper. Committed readers buy the premium offer when they are ready to fully solve the problem. Build all three layers or you leave most of your revenue on the table.

## Ownership Versus Renting

Platform revenue rents your audience. The platform changes the algorithm, the revenue changes. Your own products, your own list, your own community, those you own.

The goal is to move as much of your business as possible to owned infrastructure without abandoning the rented platforms that feed it new people.

## This Week's Action

Audit your current revenue streams. Identify what percentage comes from platforms you do not control versus products and lists you own. Set a target to shift that ratio 10% toward owned in the next 90 days.
