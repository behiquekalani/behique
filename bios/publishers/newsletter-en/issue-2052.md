# Issue 2052: Niche Down or Get Ignored
**One insight. No fluff.**

The instinct when building an audience or a product is to appeal to everyone. Broader feels safer. If you target a niche, you might exclude people. If you go specific, the market seems smaller. So people stay vague, stay generic, and wonder why nothing sticks.

The reality is the opposite. Specificity creates resonance. When someone reads your work and thinks "this was written for me," they pay attention. They share it. They buy. The person who writes for everyone writes for no one in particular, and no one in particular shows up.

The niche you fear is too small is almost always bigger than you think, and far less crowded than the generic middle. The builder who owns a specific corner of a category earns more trust, more loyalty, and more referrals than the generalist who tries to serve everyone moderately well. You do not need a massive audience. You need the right audience. Specificity is the fastest path to both finding them and making them care.
