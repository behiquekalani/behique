# Issue 1997: The Network That Actually Works

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most networking advice is optimized for collecting, not connecting. Attend events. Collect cards. Follow up. Build the spreadsheet of contacts. It produces a wide, shallow network that nobody benefits from much, including you. The people who actually open doors for each other operate differently. Their network is narrow and deep, built around genuine intellectual exchange rather than mutual resume padding.

A useful network is not built by attending events. It is built by producing things worth noticing. When you publish specific, useful, honest work, the people who find it valuable are already self-selected to be the right people. They reach out because they care about the same things you care about. That shared interest is a real foundation. The connection is already qualified before you have your first conversation.

The practical implication is to stop spending time managing contacts and start spending time on work that attracts the kind of people you want to know. Write the thing. Build the thing. Publish the thinking. The network that forms around genuine work is smaller than a LinkedIn database and a hundred times more useful. Quality of connection beats quantity every time. One right person changes your trajectory. A thousand surface contacts rarely do.
