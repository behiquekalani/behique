# Issue 1770: Your Writing Voice Is Your Most Defensible Asset
**One insight. No fluff.**

Any information you share can be Googled. Any framework you teach has been taught before. Any tool you recommend exists in someone else's article. The only thing that cannot be replicated is the specific combination of how you think, what you have lived, and how you put it into words. That combination is your voice.

Voice is not style. Style is surface. Voice is perspective. It is the particular angle you bring to a subject based on who you are, what you have failed at, and what you believe. Two people can write about the same topic and produce completely different things. The difference is voice.

Most writers spend years trying to sound like someone else. The shortcut is to stop editing out the parts that sound most like you. The instinct to soften an opinion, to add a qualifier, to make it more palatable — that instinct is usually the enemy of voice. Write the real version first.
