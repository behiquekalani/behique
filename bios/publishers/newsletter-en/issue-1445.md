# Your Brain Is a Bad Project Manager

You are not lazy. You are just using the wrong tool for tracking work. Your brain was not built for project management. It was built for pattern recognition and survival.

## The Cost of Keeping It in Your Head

Every task you are holding mentally instead of externally is a background process. It drains energy even when you are not actively thinking about it. The mental load of "I need to remember to do X" compounds across dozens of tasks.

The solution is not discipline. It is externalizing. Get it out of your head and into a system, any system, that you review reliably. A simple list beats a sophisticated system you never use.

## Capture First, Organize Later

The most important habit is capture, not organization. When something needs to get done, write it down immediately. The specific location matters less than the consistency of capture.

Later, you review what you captured and assign it to a date, a project, or delete it. But capture is the first gate. Most productivity failures happen there, before organization is even relevant.

## The Weekly Review Is Not Optional

Everything falls apart without a weekly review. It is the moment where captured items get processed, priorities get reset, and the week ahead gets shaped.

Fifteen minutes on Sunday beats two hours of scrambling on Monday. The review is not busywork. It is the infrastructure your execution runs on.

## This Week's Action

Set a 15-minute calendar block this Sunday labeled "Weekly Review." Use it to clear your inbox, process your task list, and write the three most important things you want to accomplish next week.
