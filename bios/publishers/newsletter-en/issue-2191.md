# Why Most People Never Launch

Perfectionism is procrastination wearing a professional costume.

## The Idea

The product is almost ready. Just a few more tweaks. One more revision. Wait until the design is better. Wait until you have more followers. Wait until the timing is right.

This is not caution. It is fear, dressed up as standards.

The launch threshold creeps up to match your comfort zone. Every time you almost ship, your brain finds one more thing to improve. This is not a work ethic problem. It is a risk tolerance problem.

What most people are actually afraid of is not failure. It is data. A finished product that underperforms gives you information. It tells you what to fix, what resonates, what the market actually wants. Unfinished work tells you nothing, and it lets you stay in the comfortable story that it would have worked if only you had shipped it.

The market does not reward the polished product that never launches. It rewards the imperfect product that exists.

Version one does not need to be perfect. It needs to be honest. Honest about what it does, clear about who it helps, complete enough to deliver on its promise. Everything else is iteration.

Done beats perfect every single time.

## Why It Matters

Every week you do not launch is a week of market feedback you do not get. That feedback is the input your product needs to improve. You are not waiting to launch when it is good. You are preventing it from becoming good by waiting.

## One Action

Name something you have been building for more than thirty days. Set a launch date for it that is no more than seven days from now. Tell one person about that date.
