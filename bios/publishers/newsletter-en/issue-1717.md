# Issue 1717: The Niche Is Not a Trap

**One insight. No fluff.**

People resist niching down because they think it limits them. It does the opposite. A broad focus divides your attention across too many audiences to serve any of them well. A specific focus lets you go deep enough on one problem that you become the obvious choice for the people who have it. Depth of relevance beats breadth of reach for building anything real.

The niche is not permanent. It is a starting point. You do not stay small forever because you start specific. You build credibility, trust, and audience with one group, then expand from a position of strength rather than trying to grow from a position of anonymity. Every large audience started as a small one.

Start narrow enough that you can be the best available resource for a specific person with a specific problem. That clarity will attract your first hundred buyers faster than any broad positioning ever will. When you have those hundred people, you will understand what they need next. Build that. The niche opens up when you earn it.
