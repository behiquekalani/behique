# Issue 2391: The Decision Framework That Saves 10 Hours a Week

**The One Thing:** Most decisions do not deserve the time you give them. Build a filter and use it every day.

---

Decision fatigue is not a myth. Every choice you make draws from the same cognitive budget. Spend that budget on small decisions, and you have nothing left for the ones that matter. The highest-leverage thing you can build in your solopreneur life is a decision framework.

## The Four-Category Sort

Every task or decision falls into one of four buckets. Does it grow revenue? Does it protect revenue? Does it free up your time? Does it do none of these? The last category gets deleted or delegated. No exceptions. Founders stay poor because they spend hours in the fourth bucket without noticing.

## The 5-Minute Rule for Small Decisions

If a decision takes less than 5 minutes to reverse, make it in 30 seconds. Stop researching fonts, platform choices, pricing tiers, and email subject lines beyond the first reasonable option. Good enough and shipped beats perfect and delayed in 95% of cases. Save your deliberation for irreversible decisions.

## Your Repeating Decisions Are Already Made

You should not be deciding what to work on each morning. You should be executing a plan that was made the night before. You should not be deciding what to post. You should have a content calendar. Every decision you can make in advance is cognitive energy freed up for real thinking. Build the calendar. Write the SOP. Make the recurring decisions once.

---

**This Week's Action:** List every recurring decision you make in a typical week. Pick three and eliminate the decision by creating a rule: "I always do X in situation Y." Write the rules down.
