# Issue 2388: Build in Public Without Oversharing

**The One Thing:** Share your process and thinking, not your anxiety and doubt.

---

Building in public is one of the highest-leverage growth strategies available to a solo creator. It is also one of the most misunderstood. Vague vulnerability posts are not building in public. Numbers without context are not building in public. Here is what actually works.

## The Difference Between Process and Performance

Performance is posting your revenue to impress. Process is posting your decision-making to teach. One attracts followers looking for shortcuts. The other attracts builders who want to learn. The audience you want is the second group. They buy products. They become long-term readers. They share your work with their networks.

## What to Share and What to Keep

Share: your reasoning, your experiments, your failures with a lesson attached, your wins with a system attached. Keep: the doubt spiral at 2am, the client conflict you have not resolved, the financial stress that makes you question everything. Those belong in a journal, not a tweet. Your audience does not need your anxiety. They need your perspective.

## The Practical Content Formula

One post per week: what you tried, what happened, what you learned, what you are doing next. That is it. Four sentences of genuine transparency beats ten posts of vague hustle content. Real process shared honestly is magnetic. People follow builders who teach while they build.

---

**This Week's Action:** Write one honest behind-the-scenes post this week. Include one specific metric, one mistake, and one thing you learned. No fluff. Publish it.

**Quote:** "Transparency is not about removing all privacy. It's about removing deception." — Naval Ravikant
