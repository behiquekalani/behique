# Issue 2075: Who You Spend Time With Is a Decision

**One insight. No fluff.**

The people around you set the ceiling on your thinking without you noticing. Spend enough time with people who see certain outcomes as impossible and you will stop imagining those outcomes. Spend time with people for whom those outcomes are normal and the belief infrastructure shifts. This is not about toxic positivity or cutting off everyone who is not rich. It is about recognizing that proximity is one of the strongest inputs to your operating assumptions.

You cannot fully choose your environment, especially early. But you have more choice than you exercise. The podcasts you listen to, the accounts you follow, the communities you join, the events you attend: all of these shape the reference class you compare yourself to. If your reference class is people ten years ahead of where you want to be, your baseline expectation for yourself adjusts upward. That adjustment changes your daily decisions in ways you never explicitly calculate.

This is not a call to be strategic about friendship in a cold way. It is a call to be honest about whose company you are keeping and what it is costing you. Comfort is expensive if it keeps you thinking small. The people who challenge you, who are further along, who take you seriously enough to tell you the truth. More of them is worth more than it costs.
