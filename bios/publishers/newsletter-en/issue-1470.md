# Decision Fatigue Is Killing Your Output

You are not lazy. You are exhausted from making too many small decisions before you even start the important ones.

## How Decision Fatigue Works
Every choice you make depletes a finite cognitive resource. What to eat, what to wear, what to check first, what to respond to. By the time you sit down for creative or strategic work, the best part of your mental capacity is already spent. You make worse decisions. You avoid hard tasks. You reach for distractions.

This is not a motivation problem. It is an energy management problem.

## The Solution Is Standardization
Successful creators and builders reduce the number of daily decisions by designing defaults. Same morning routine. Same work blocks. Same content format. Same response templates. This is not rigidity. It is protecting your best cognitive hours for the work that actually matters.

Every system you build is a decision you no longer have to make.

## What to Automate First
Start with the decisions you make every day that do not require creativity. When you start work. What you work on first. What your content format looks like. Where you save files. When you check email. Each one of these, if standardized, returns cognitive bandwidth to what actually produces results.

The person who designs their day in advance never has to decide what to do next.

## This Week's Action
Identify three daily decisions you make repeatedly that could be standardized. Write them down as defaults. Commit to those defaults for two weeks and track whether your output quality improves.
