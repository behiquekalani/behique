# Digital Products Are Leverage You Already Have

You do not need a new skill to build a digital product. You need to document a skill you already use.

## The Trap of Preparation
Creators delay launching because they think they need more expertise first. But the person who needs your product is three to five years behind you. They are not looking for a PhD. They are looking for a shortcut built by someone who has already been where they are. You already qualify.

The preparation trap is a form of fear dressed up as responsibility. Recognize it.

## What You Actually Need to Launch
One specific problem. One audience who has it. One documented solution. That is the formula. The product can be a PDF, a short video series, a Notion template, or a simple checklist. The medium matters less than the clarity. If someone reads it and says "this is exactly what I needed," you have a product.

Stop optimizing what does not exist yet. Ship the rough version.

## The Economics of Selling Once, Earning Repeatedly
A $47 PDF sold three times a week is $564 a month. That is not life-changing, but it proves the model. You stack products. You build an audience. You raise prices as proof accumulates. The first product is not the masterpiece. It is the proof of concept that changes how you see yourself.

Most people never sell the first one. That is why most people stay stuck.

## This Week's Action
Name the one skill or process you know best. Write it as a product title. Then write five bullet points of what that product would cover. That is your product outline.
