# Work That Compounds

Not all work is equal. Some work produces once. Some work produces forever.

## The Idea
There are two categories of work a solopreneur does. Perishable work produces value once and expires. Answering an email. Doing a client call. Writing a custom proposal. The effort is real. The return is single-use.

Compounding work produces value repeatedly. A piece of content that keeps getting discovered. A product that keeps selling. A system that keeps running. A skill that makes every future project faster.

Most people fill their days with perishable work because it feels productive and provides immediate feedback. The response arrives. The task closes. The dopamine hits. Compounding work often has no immediate feedback. You write a post and see nothing happen. You build a system and it takes weeks to pay off. The delay makes it feel less real.

But the arithmetic is decisive. An hour spent on perishable work returns one unit of value. An hour spent on compounding work can return a hundred units over twelve months. The choice of which work to prioritize is the most important financial decision a solopreneur makes, made daily.

Audit where your hours go. The ratio of perishable to compounding work determines your ceiling.

## Why It Matters
Compounding is the only mechanism that creates the gap between effort and income. That gap is what financial freedom actually is. Without compounding work, income stays proportional to hours. That is employment, not ownership.

Design your days around compounding first.

## One Action
Categorize every task you worked on yesterday as perishable or compounding. Count the hours in each category. If perishable hours exceed compounding hours, identify one compounding task you can add to tomorrow's schedule.
