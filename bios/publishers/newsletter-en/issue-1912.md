# Issue 1912: The Audience You Ignore Is the One That Pays

**One insight. No fluff.**

Most creators chase the broadest possible audience because it feels like the safest strategy. More people means more potential. But breadth kills specificity, and specificity is what makes someone buy. When you're talking to everyone, you're resonating with no one. The person who reads your content thinks "this could have been written for anyone." They're right. It could. So they move on.

Specificity is the counterintuitive shortcut to scale. When you write for one kind of person with one specific problem in one specific context, that person feels seen in a way that generic content never achieves. They share it. They come back. They buy, because you're not just another voice making general observations. You're the person who clearly understands their situation. That precision of understanding is perceived as expertise, and expertise justifies the purchase.

Define your audience by the problem they have, not the demographic they fit. Not "25-35 year old professionals." Something like: "people who have tried to build an online business twice, made some money, but can't seem to get it to replace their income." That's a person. You can write directly to that person. And that person, when they find you, will feel like you're speaking specifically to them, because you are.
