# Issue 1767: The Best Products Are Compressed Obsessions
**One insight. No fluff.**

Every product worth buying came from someone who could not stop thinking about a problem. They thought about it in the shower, in the car, while talking to other people about other things. That obsession produced depth. Depth produces insight that a casual interest never reaches.

The creator who built a product after spending two months thinking about it will always lose to the person who spent two years living with it. Not because the second person is more disciplined. Because they had time to find the non-obvious angles, to test their own assumptions, to understand the problem from the inside.

If you are trying to build something and it feels thin, the answer is usually more obsession, not more polish. Go deeper into the problem. Talk to more people who have it. Live with the frustration yourself. The product will appear once you understand the pain well enough to map it precisely.

*Build something worth owning.*
