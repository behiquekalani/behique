# Issue 1776: The Trap of Optimizing Too Early
**One insight. No fluff.**

Optimizing a system that has not proven itself is one of the most productive-feeling ways to waste time. You are refining something before you know if it works. You are polishing a house that may be built on the wrong street.

This happens with content pipelines, with product pricing pages, with onboarding flows. People spend weeks perfecting something that would have taught them everything useful in its first rough version. The optimization comes after the learning. Not before.

Ship the rough version. Get ten real data points. Then optimize the parts that actually matter to the people who actually use it. Premature optimization is not a technical problem. It is a psychological one. It feels like diligence but functions as avoidance of the real test, which is contact with reality.
