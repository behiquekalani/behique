# Your Old Content Is Sitting on Revenue

The content you wrote two years ago still has value. Most creators abandon it instead of activating it.

## The Archive as Asset
Every piece of content you have ever published is a potential source of new attention. Old posts that performed well can be updated, re-published, and re-promoted to a new audience. Old threads can become articles. Old articles can become email campaigns. The content already exists. The distribution channel is what changes.

Most creators spend 100% of their energy on new content and 0% on activating what they already built. That ratio is backwards.

## Finding the Evergreen Gems
Go through your last two years of content. Find the five pieces that got the most engagement or the most helpful comments. These are your anchors. They told you what your audience genuinely values. Build your next content sprint around deepening those topics.

You are not recycling. You are building on proof of concept.

## Repurposing Is Not Lazy
Turning a newsletter issue into a Twitter thread is not laziness. It is efficient distribution. A well-written idea deserves to reach every person who would benefit from it, regardless of which platform they prefer. The idea is the work. The format is just the container.

Build the idea well once. Then ship it in every container your audience uses.

## This Week's Action
Pick one piece of content from more than one year ago that performed well or that you believe deserved more reach. Update it, expand it by 20%, and re-publish or repurpose it for a new platform this week.
