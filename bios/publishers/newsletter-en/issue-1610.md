# The Problem with Building Around Passion

"Follow your passion" is the worst career advice. Not because passion is bad, but because passion alone does not make a viable business. Markets do not care about your enthusiasm.

## Passion Fades Under Pressure

When the thing you love becomes the thing you must do to pay rent, the relationship changes. Deadlines, difficult clients, revenue pressure, and repetition strip the joy out of almost any subject. The people who survive this are not the most passionate. They are the most resilient.

Passion is great fuel for starting. It is a poor foundation for sustaining. You need a reason that holds up when passion has gone quiet.

## What Actually Works

Mastery creates passion more reliably than passion creates mastery. When you get good at something, when you can solve problems others cannot and get paid well for it, you start to care about it deeply. The causation runs in both directions, but the mastery side is more reliable.

Build around a genuine problem you can solve. If you also happen to love the domain, great. But the problem-solving capacity is what the market pays for.

## The Sustainable Version

The sustainable version of passion-driven work is purpose-driven work. Purpose is less emotional than passion. It is quieter, more durable. It says: this is important, people need it, I am the right person to do it. That holds up on the hard days when passion has nothing to say.

## This Week's Action

Write down the business or project you are building, then ask: would you still do this if you felt no excitement about it, purely because people needed it and you were good at it? If yes, you have a foundation.
