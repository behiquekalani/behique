# The Productized Service Nobody Is Talking About

Custom work is exhausting. Every client is different, every scope creeps, every deadline moves. Productized services solve that. They are still services, but sold like products.

## What Makes a Service a Product

A productized service has a fixed scope, a fixed price, and a fixed timeline. You do not negotiate. You do not customize. The client knows exactly what they are buying before the conversation starts. That clarity speeds up sales, reduces friction, and lets you build a repeatable delivery process.

The first time you run it, you are building it. The fifth time, you are running a system. By the twentieth, it is close to passive. The knowledge is yours, the process is defined, and the price is set.

## Where to Start

Look at the custom work you have already done. What did most clients actually need? Strip away everything else and package that core. Give it a name, a price, and a one-page description. Put it on your site. You now have a productized service.

The trap is over-engineering it before you sell it. Sell it once with a rough process, then refine. The market will tell you what to keep.

## This Week's Action
Identify one recurring type of work you do for clients. Write a one-paragraph scope description and set a fixed price. That is your first productized service.
