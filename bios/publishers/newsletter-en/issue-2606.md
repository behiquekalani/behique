# Issue 2606: The Price Is the Message

**The Idea:** Your price doesn't just reflect value. It creates it. When you underprice your work, you attract buyers who are optimizing for the lowest cost. Those buyers are the hardest to serve, the least committed, and the most likely to ask for refunds.

When you price higher, you filter for buyers who are serious. They've already decided they want the transformation. The price is a signal that you believe in what you built. Low prices signal doubt. High prices signal confidence.

This is uncomfortable because most creators undercharge out of fear. Fear that no one will buy at the real price. That fear is almost always wrong. The $47 offer that sells 10 copies and the $197 offer that sells 3 copies generate nearly the same revenue. But the $197 buyer expects more, invests more, and gets more from the product. The feedback loop improves your product faster.

There is also a practical reality. You cannot build a sustainable solopreneur business on $10 products. You need fewer sales, not more sales. The math of a $97 product with 30 sales per month is cleaner and more sustainable than 300 sales of a $9 product.

Raise your prices. See what happens. The answer will probably surprise you.

**Why It Matters:** Pricing strategy determines who you attract and how much energy you spend per dollar earned. A higher price with the same conversion rate is just better economics.

**One Action:** Find one product or service you offer. Double the price for 30 days. Track whether conversions drop. In most cases, they don't.
