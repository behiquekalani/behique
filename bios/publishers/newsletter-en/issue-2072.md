# Issue 2072: Momentum Is a Resource

**One insight. No fluff.**

Momentum is real and it is exhaustible. It takes energy to start something. Once moving, that same thing requires far less energy to continue. This is not motivational framing. It is how work actually operates. The first hour of a project costs more cognitive effort than the third hour. The first day of a habit costs more willpower than the thirtieth day. Starting is expensive. Continuing is cheap.

This is why stopping matters more than most people account for. Every interruption is a restart cost. Every day you skip requires you to rebuild the starting energy from zero. Protecting momentum means protecting continuity. Not pushing harder. Stopping less. The builder who works four consistent hours daily for a month will outproduce the one who works ten-hour bursts followed by recovery days, even if the raw hour counts are similar.

The practical application is to make stopping harder than continuing. Ship a small thing every day so the streak has value worth protecting. Keep the project visible so picking it back up takes no activation energy. Define done criteria narrowly enough that you can complete something in a single session. Momentum compounds quietly. Most people never experience it because they keep interrupting themselves before it can build.
