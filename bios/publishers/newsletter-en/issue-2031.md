# Issue 2031: Boredom Is the Signal, Not the Problem

**One insight. No fluff.**

Boredom used to be a message. When something bored you, it meant you had outgrown it, or it was not aligned with what you actually cared about. That signal was useful. It pointed you toward better use of your time.

Now boredom lasts about eight seconds before the phone fixes it. Which means the signal never gets delivered. You never sit long enough with the discomfort to understand what it is telling you. You just eliminate it and move on, which means you also eliminate the information it was carrying.

The work worth doing usually feels boring at some point. Not because it is bad work, but because all deep work has a dull middle that separates the committed from the curious. If you have trained yourself to escape boredom the moment it arrives, you will never get through that middle. Sit with it. Ask what it is telling you. Sometimes the answer is to quit. More often the answer is to keep going.
