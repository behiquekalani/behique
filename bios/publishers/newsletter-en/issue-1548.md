# The Business Model Is the Strategy

Most creators think about content strategy, marketing strategy, growth strategy. They spend the least time on business model strategy. That is the backwards order.

## Why the Model Matters First

How you make money determines what content you need to make. It determines your ideal customer, your pricing, your volume requirements. Working backwards from a clear business model makes every other decision easier.

A newsletter that makes money from sponsorships needs a different content strategy than one that sells digital products. The platform looks similar. The logic is completely different.

## The Models That Work for Solo Operators

Digital products scale without your time. Services trade time for higher margin and faster cash. Subscriptions create predictable revenue but require consistent delivery. Community combines all three with the highest conversion path.

Most solo operators should start with one model and get it working before adding another. Mixing models before you have traction in one is how people stay busy and broke simultaneously.

## The Underrated Model: Teaching What You Learn

You do not need to be the world's top expert to build a teaching business. You need to be one step ahead of the person you are teaching. And you need to communicate clearly.

Most experts are bad teachers because they cannot remember what it felt like not to know. Recent learners make great teachers. Their proximity to the confusion is the value.

## This Week's Action

Write down your current business model in one sentence. Include who pays you, what they pay for, and how the money arrives. If you cannot write that sentence clearly, that is the first problem to solve.
