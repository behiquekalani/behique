# Issue 2473: The Mechanics of Online Income

**The Idea:** Online income looks mysterious from the outside. People see someone making money from a newsletter or a course and assume there's a secret they don't have access to. There isn't. The mechanics are simple and learnable.

Someone has a problem. You have a solution. You get in front of them in a format they trust. You make an offer. They buy. That's it.

Every successful online income source follows this structure. The variables are the type of problem, the format of the solution, the channel of distribution, and the price point. The underlying logic is always the same.

The complexity people add around this is mostly self-generated. They overthink the format before understanding the problem. They build the distribution channel before they know what they're distributing. They price before they've tested whether anyone wants the thing.

Start with the problem. Find it by paying attention to what questions people ask you, what you've had to figure out the hard way, what gaps exist between what people want and what they can find for free. The answer to that question is your product direction.

Then build the minimum version. Test whether people will pay for it. Improve based on what you learn. Add distribution once you know what you're distributing.

**Why It Matters:** When you understand the mechanics, there's nothing to figure out. There's only execution. The mystery dissolves and what's left is work.

**One Action:** Identify one problem you've solved that other people are still stuck on. Write one sentence describing who has the problem and what the solution is. That's your product hypothesis.
