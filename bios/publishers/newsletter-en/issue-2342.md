# The Creator Economy Rewards Specificity

The internet has too much content for general to win. The only creators getting paid consistently are the ones who go narrow and deep.

## The Idea

There's a myth that a broad audience means more money. That logic made sense when media was scarce. Now the opposite is true. The more specific your niche, the more valuable your audience, and the more your content is worth to the right people.

Think about it from the reader's side. If you have a problem, you don't want a general answer. You want someone who has solved exactly your problem, in exactly your situation. That person feels rare. That person gets paid.

Specificity is not about limiting yourself. It's about making your signal stronger in a world drowning in noise. When you write for everyone, you write for no one. When you write for the 28-year-old bootstrapped founder trying to get their first 100 customers, you write something that person forwards to five friends.

This applies to products too. The more specific the problem your digital product solves, the less price resistance you face. "A productivity course" competes with a thousand others. "A workflow system for solo developers shipping client work" is a category of one.

## Why It Matters

Specificity compounds. Every piece of content you make for a specific audience builds credibility with that audience. You become the person they think of first. Algorithms reward resonance over reach. Resonance comes from specificity.

The fastest path to monetization is not growing big. It's growing precise.

## One Action

Write down the exact type of person you're building for. Not a demographic. A person with a specific problem, a specific goal, and a specific reason they haven't solved it yet. Post one piece of content this week written directly to that person.
