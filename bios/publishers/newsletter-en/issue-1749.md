# Issue 1749: The Product Is Not the Point

**One insight. No fluff.**

The product is a vehicle. What people are buying is a future version of themselves. They are not buying the PDF, the course, the template. They are buying the belief that this is the thing that finally gets them there.

This means the most important work in selling is not describing features. It is painting the outcome with enough detail that the buyer can feel it before they purchase. The product earns the sale. The story opens the door.

Build the product honestly and describe the transformation accurately. Overpromise and the refund requests arrive. Underpromise and no one buys. The skill is matching the real outcome to language that makes the buyer feel the gap between where they are and where they want to be.

---
*Build something worth owning.*
