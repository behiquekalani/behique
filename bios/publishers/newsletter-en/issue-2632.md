# Issue 2632: Income Streams Are Not Created Equal

**The Idea:** Not all income is the same. The dollar you earn from a productized service is different from the dollar you earn from a digital product, even if the amount is identical.

Service income requires your time every time. You deliver, you get paid. Stop delivering, stop getting paid. That is not inherently bad. Services can be high-margin and fulfilling. But they do not compound.

Digital product income can compound. A product you built once can sell tomorrow, next month, and three years from now, without additional work from you. The unit economics change entirely. The first sale covers your time investment. Every sale after that is increasingly pure margin.

The income diversification that matters is not spreading across many sources. It is building income that compounds alongside income that does not. One active stream that pays well, funding the time to build passive infrastructure. Over time, the passive streams catch up and eventually exceed the active ones.

The mistake is trying to build passive income before the active income is stable. Passive income takes time to build and provides no short-term certainty. Optimize active income first. Use the stability to fund the patient work of building products that sell without you.

**Why It Matters:** The end state most solopreneurs are trying to reach is a business that does not require their constant presence. You get there by building the right types of income in the right order.

**One Action:** Map your current income by type. How much requires your time this week? How much comes in regardless? Make that ratio visible.
