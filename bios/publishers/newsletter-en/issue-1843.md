# Issue 1843: Comfort Is the Enemy of Range
**One insight. No fluff.**

The person who stays in one domain gets deep but narrow. They become very good at a specific thing inside a specific context. Then the context changes, and everything they built stops working.

Range is the ability to transfer. To take a mental model from one field and apply it somewhere it has never been applied. The best ideas come from collisions between disciplines, not from going deeper into one.

You build range by deliberately working outside your area. You read history when you are a programmer. You study biology when you are a marketer. You do not do this to become an expert in everything. You do it to carry patterns that nobody around you has seen. The most valuable people in any room are the ones who bring in what the room does not already know.
