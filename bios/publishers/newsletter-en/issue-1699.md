# Portfolio vs Single Product: Which Game Are You Playing?

These are two different business models with different timelines, risks, and ceilings. Knowing which one you're building changes every decision.

## The Single Product Model
All energy on one offer. Deep positioning. One audience. One transformation. One price point. This model scales vertically. You get better at delivering the one thing, build a referral engine around it, and potentially raise the price as demand grows.

The risk is concentration. If the product stops working or the market shifts, you have one thing to adjust. The reward is clarity. You know exactly what you're building every day.

## The Portfolio Model
Multiple products at different price points serving the same audience. An ecosystem instead of a single offer. This spreads risk across products. A slow month for one can be offset by another. It also creates natural upsell paths and keeps customers in your world longer.

The risk is dilution. Splitting attention across five products can mean none of them get the focus they need to become great.

## How to Know Which One You Are
Early stage: single product, always. You need to prove one thing works before you expand. Once one product generates consistent income, evaluate whether a second product serves the same audience more deeply or just adds complexity.

Growth should be vertical before it is horizontal.

## This Week's Action
Write down every product or offer you currently have. Count them. If you have more than two and none are generating consistent income, collapse your focus to one. Pick the one closest to working. Put everything there for 60 days.
