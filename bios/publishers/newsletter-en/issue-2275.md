# Skin in the Game Makes You Sharper

Advice without exposure to consequences is noise. The clearest thinking comes from people who have something to lose.

## The Idea

Nassim Taleb's most important idea is also one of the most widely ignored. People who don't bear the consequences of their advice give bad advice. Not because they're dishonest. Because the feedback loop is broken.

When you put real money into a trade, you think differently than when you're commenting on someone else's trade. When your income depends on whether your product actually solves the problem, you think differently about the product. Skin in the game removes the buffer between opinion and reality.

This applies to how you build your creator business. When you sell something and have to answer to buyers who are disappointed, you fix the product. When you post with your real name and real face, you say things you actually believe. When you invest in your own tools and systems, you use them more seriously.

The instinct to avoid skin in the game is a form of risk aversion. It feels safe to stay theoretical. But the cost is that you never develop the calibration that only comes from being wrong in real situations with real stakes.

Taking risks is not the same as being reckless. It means having your own interests genuinely aligned with the outcomes you're working toward. That alignment sharpens your thinking, forces real decisions, and produces better results over time.

## Why It Matters

The best operators have their own interests on the line. They work harder, think more clearly, and course-correct faster. If you want to develop real judgment in any domain, you have to accept real exposure to the consequences of your decisions.

## One Action

Identify one area of your business where you've been operating without real stakes. A product you haven't launched, an investment you haven't made, a commitment you haven't declared. Put something real on the line this week. Notice how it changes how you think.
