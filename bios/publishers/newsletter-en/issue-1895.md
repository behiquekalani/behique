# Issue 1895: Energy Management Beats Time Management

**One insight. No fluff.**

You cannot manufacture more hours. You can manufacture better hours. Time management assumes all hours are equal. They are not. An hour at peak focus is worth five hours of fragmented, distracted effort. The math is uncomfortable for people who track time as a proxy for productivity.

Most people spend their best cognitive hours on low-stakes tasks: checking messages, attending meetings that could be emails, organizing systems instead of using them. Then they try to do deep work when their energy is depleted and wonder why it feels so hard. The schedule is not the problem. The energy allocation is.

Identify the two to three hours in your day when your mind is sharpest. Protect them completely. Do only the work that requires full capacity in that window. Move everything else to lower-energy slots. That single change will produce more output than any time-tracking system you could build.
