# Build the Business That Fits the Life, Not the Other Way Around

Most people build a business and then try to fit their life around it. That produces a very specific kind of misery: successful by the numbers, hollow in the experience.

## The Design Question Nobody Asks
Before deciding what to build, ask: what does my ideal week look like? How many hours do I want to work? Where do I want to work from? Who do I want to work with? What kind of problems do I want to solve? These are not fantasy questions. They are the design specs for the business you should build.

The business should serve the life. Always.

## When the Metrics Win and the Life Loses
Revenue is a lagging indicator of alignment. A business generating $20,000 a month that requires 80 hours a week and creates constant anxiety is not a success. It is a different version of the problem you were trying to escape. The goal was never just money. The goal was freedom, meaning, and optionality.

Do not mistake revenue for winning if the life does not match.

## The Realignment Process
If your current business does not fit the life you want, the path is not always to burn it down. Audit what you do. Remove the parts that do not fit. Systematize the rest. Add the pieces that align better. This takes time but it is possible. The business is a construction project. You can renovate it.

You built it. You can redesign it.

## This Week's Action
Write down what your ideal week looks like in detail: hours, location, activities, energy. Then compare it to your actual week. Where are the biggest gaps? Pick one gap and make one structural change to close it.
