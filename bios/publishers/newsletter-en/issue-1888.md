# Issue 1888: Uncomfortable Feedback Is the Only Useful Kind

**One insight. No fluff.**

The feedback that agrees with you is not feedback. It is validation. People who tell you your work is great, your idea is brilliant, and your plan is solid are not helping you. They are being polite, or they are not paying close enough attention. Either way, you leave the conversation no more capable than when you entered.

Uncomfortable feedback is different. It points at the thing you hoped they wouldn't notice. It names the assumption you built the whole structure around. It asks the question that makes the easy version of your plan fall apart. That feedback is rare because most people won't give it unless they trust that you can handle it.

Make it easier for people to be honest with you. Ask specific questions instead of inviting general praise. Thank people who tell you hard things. The builder who seeks out discomfort in feedback will outpace the one who collects compliments.
