# Packaging Is Half the Product

Two identical products with different presentations produce completely different sales results.

## The Idea
Most builders focus ninety percent of their effort on creation and ten percent on presentation. The market does not reward effort. It rewards clarity.

Packaging is everything a buyer experiences before they decide to purchase. The title, the description, the visual design, the price point, the format. Each element is either working for you or against you. A great product with a confusing title will not sell. A mediocre product with a specific, benefit-driven title and clear formatting will outperform it.

This is not manipulation. It is communication. Your job is to make it immediately obvious who the product is for, what problem it solves, and why this version is better than figuring it out themselves. If any of those three things is unclear, you have a packaging problem, not a product problem.

The title is where most people lose. Generic titles signal generic products. Specific titles with a clear outcome in the name, like "The Two-Hour Client Intake System for Freelancers," tell the right person exactly that this is theirs. They do not have to wonder.

Price is also packaging. Too low signals low value. Too high with no trust signals risk. Anchor your price to the transformation, not the time it took to create.

## Why It Matters
A product that does not sell cannot help anyone. Packaging is what bridges the gap between value you created and value a buyer receives.

Treat presentation as part of the product, not an afterthought.

## One Action
Rewrite the title of your current or next product using this formula: [Specific Outcome] for [Specific Person] in [Timeframe or Constraint]. Test which version converts better.
