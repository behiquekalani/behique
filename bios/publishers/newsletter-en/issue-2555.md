# Issue 2555: The Personal Monopoly

**The Idea:** A personal monopoly is what you have when no one else can offer exactly what you offer. Not because you have a patent or a legal advantage, but because the combination of your skills, perspective, and experience is genuinely unique.

Most people never achieve this because they try to compete on the same terms as everyone else. They learn the same skills, create similar content, and offer comparable services. In that space, the only competition is price. And competing on price is a race toward zero.

The alternative is to become the only person who sees the intersection you see. Your technical background combined with your communication style. Your niche expertise combined with your specific audience. Your lived experience combined with the framework you built from it.

When you occupy that intersection, comparison shopping becomes almost impossible. People do not ask "who else does this?" because the answer is no one. The question becomes "do I want to work with this person?" That is a much better question to be answering.

Building a personal monopoly takes time. You have to go deep on your specific knowledge, be explicit about your perspective, and keep showing up long enough for the market to recognize the pattern. But once it clicks, the competitive pressure mostly disappears.

**Why It Matters:** Competing on skill alone is exhausting because someone younger and cheaper is always behind you. Competing on your unique intersection is sustainable because it cannot be replicated.

**One Action:** Write the intersection that is yours alone. Three elements, combined: your background, your specific knowledge, your perspective on the topic. That is your monopoly.
