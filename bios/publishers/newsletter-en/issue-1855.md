# Issue 1855: The Person You Argue With Online
**One insight. No fluff.**

Every minute spent in an online argument is a minute not spent building. This is not a moral statement. It is a resource allocation observation. Attention is finite and arguing online is one of the lowest-return uses of it.

The feeling that drives you to reply is usually ego, not reason. Someone was wrong. You want them to know you noticed. You want the audience to see your correction. None of that produces anything except more of the same exchange. The other person does not change. You do not feel better. You just spent energy on it.

The alternative is not silence from fear. It is indifference from strength. When you are building something real, the opinions of strangers carry very little weight. Not because you are above it, but because you are busy. The best response to a bad argument is a better body of work. That is the only rebuttal that actually scales.
