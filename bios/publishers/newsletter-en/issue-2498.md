# Issue 2498: How to Write Copy That Does Not Feel Like Copy

**The Idea:** The best sales copy does not feel like sales copy. It feels like a conversation with someone who understands your exact situation.

Most people write sales copy by describing their product. Features, benefits, price. This reads like every other page on the internet and your brain automatically treats it as advertising noise.

The alternative is specificity. Vague copy is generic. Specific copy feels personal.

"Help you grow your business" is vague. "Wake up Monday with two new client inquiries in your inbox without spending a dollar on ads" is specific. The specificity creates a mental image. The mental image creates desire.

Specificity also requires that you deeply know your buyer. Not demographics. Psychology. What does your buyer say to themselves at 11pm when they cannot sleep? What did they search for before they found you? What have they tried that almost worked?

The creator who can name their buyer's internal experience in the buyer's own words will outconvert every polished sales page built by someone who does not know the customer.

Research is the writing. Everything else is just arrangement.

**Why It Matters:** Copy that feels like copy gets skipped. Copy that feels like recognition gets read, shared, and acted on.

**One Action:** Find five real testimonials or comments from buyers or followers. Extract the exact words they used to describe their problem. Use three of those exact phrases in your next sales page rewrite.
