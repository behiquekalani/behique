# Financial Minimalism for Builders

The goal isn't maximum income. The goal is minimum income required to maintain maximum freedom. Most people have this backwards.

## The Revenue Trap
More revenue often means more complexity. More clients. More tools. More team. More overhead. At some point, the business requires so much maintenance that the freedom it was supposed to create is gone. You built a job with more stress and no boss.

Financial minimalism means designing your income around what you actually need rather than what's available. It means saying no to growth that adds complexity without adding freedom.

## What Enough Actually Looks Like
This is a personal calculation, not a universal standard. For some people, enough is $5K a month. For others, it's $30K. The number matters less than the clarity. When you know your number, you can build to it deliberately and stop when you get there.

Most people never define their enough. So they keep optimizing for more without knowing why or what it's for.

## The Freedom Equation
Freedom has two components: money and time. A business that makes you rich but owns your schedule isn't free. A business that gives you flexible time but barely covers expenses isn't free either. The design goal is the overlap.

Build toward the intersection of enough income and enough autonomy. That intersection is where the good life lives.

## This Week's Action
Calculate your actual monthly number. Not what would be nice. What would cover your life with some margin. Write that number down and build a concrete path to it.
