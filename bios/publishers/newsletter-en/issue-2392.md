# Issue 2392: Evergreen Content Is a Compounding Asset

**The One Thing:** Write content that is as useful two years from now as it is today.

---

Trending content gets clicks today. Evergreen content gets clicks for years. Most creators optimize for spikes. The ones who build lasting businesses optimize for compounding. A post that attracts one reader a day adds up to 365 new people a year, every year, without additional effort.

## The Anatomy of Evergreen Content

Evergreen content answers a question that does not expire. How do I price a digital product? How do I find my first 100 email subscribers? How do I deal with imposter syndrome as a new creator? These questions were relevant five years ago. They will be relevant five years from now. Trending content answers: what happened this week? That has a shelf life measured in days.

## The Long Game Math

One well-optimized evergreen post, found via search, can bring in 50 to 500 new readers per month indefinitely. Do that 20 times, and you have a traffic machine that costs nothing to maintain. That is the math behind why some blogs outlast their creators. The content keeps working after the creator stops.

## How to Build Evergreen Into Your Routine

For every three posts you publish, make one of them evergreen. Not trending. Not reactive. Foundational. Answer a question your audience will always have. Write it with enough depth that it deserves to be found. Optimize the title for a search someone will still be running in 2030.

---

**This Week's Action:** Write one piece of content this week that answers a question your audience will still be asking in three years. No trending angles. No current events. Just a durable answer to a durable question.
