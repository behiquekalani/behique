# Issue 2611: The Attention Economy Is Changing

**The Idea:** The rules of online attention are shifting. For years, the playbook was volume. Post every day. Stay consistent. Be everywhere. The platforms rewarded frequency and the creators who could produce the most content won the distribution game.

That model is collapsing under its own weight. Feeds are saturated. Engagement rates are declining across most platforms for most creators. The audience is becoming more selective, not less. They are following fewer people more deeply, not more people more shallowly.

This shift rewards a different kind of creator. Not the one who posts the most, but the one who is worth coming back to. The one who makes you feel like you learned something real, not just that you consumed something entertaining. Depth is becoming the competitive edge.

This is good news for creators who have something to say. The content treadmill was always a game you couldn't win forever. The new game is about becoming genuinely valuable to a smaller number of people. That game is sustainable. It compounds. And it rewards the people with actual ideas, not just the people with the most time to post.

The window to reposition is open right now. The creators who figure out depth before it becomes mainstream will own their audiences completely.

**Why It Matters:** A depth-first strategy builds an audience that stays. Volume-first builds an audience that scrolls through. The business outcomes are completely different.

**One Action:** Identify your single best piece of content, the one that got the most meaningful responses. Study why it resonated. Write more like that, less of everything else.
