# Issue 1847: The Problem with Passion-First Thinking
**One insight. No fluff.**

"Follow your passion" is one of the most dangerous pieces of career advice ever popularized. It tells people to search for a feeling before building any skill, which leads them in circles.

Passion is a result, not a starting point. You become passionate about things you are good at. You get good at things by doing them seriously for long enough to see real results. The sequence matters. Skill comes first. Interest deepens with competence.

The question is not "what am I passionate about?" The question is "what am I willing to do badly for a long time while I get good at it?" That is the real filter. Not the emotional draw, but the willingness to endure the awkward early phase. What you can endure is more reliable than what excites you today.
