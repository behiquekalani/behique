# Issue 2410: The Problem With Perfection

**The One Thing:** Perfectionism is procrastination with better branding.

---

Perfectionists don't fail because they aim too high. They fail because they never ship. Every hour spent polishing something that isn't live is an hour not spent learning from real feedback from real people.

The version you ship is always better than the version you're still editing.

## What Feedback Actually Does

When you keep something in draft, you're operating on assumptions. You assume what people want. You assume what problems they have. You assume what will resonate. Most of those assumptions are wrong in small ways. Some are wrong in large ways.

Real feedback from a real audience corrects those assumptions fast. The post you thought was mediocre gets 300 shares. The post you labored over for a week gets twelve likes. The product you thought was obvious was the one customers actually wanted.

You cannot learn these things without shipping. The data only exists on the other side of publish.

## The 80% Rule

Ship when it's 80% ready. That last 20% is almost never what customers notice or care about. What they notice: does this solve my problem? Is the promise clear? Does the creator seem like someone I trust?

Spending a week perfecting formatting does not answer any of those questions. Shipping a clear, direct, 80% product does.

## Reps Beat Perfection

A creator who ships 100 decent products learns more than one who spent the same time perfecting five. More reps means more data, more feedback, more opportunities to find what works, and more practice at the actual skill of building and selling things.

Perfection is a single bet. Volume is a portfolio.

---

**This Week's Action:** Find one thing you've been sitting on and publish it today. Not tomorrow. Today.
