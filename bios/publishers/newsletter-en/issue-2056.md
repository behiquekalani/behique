# Issue 2056: Teach What You Are Learning
**One insight. No fluff.**

There is a myth that you must be an expert before you can teach. That you need years of credentials and a comprehensive grasp of a subject before you are allowed to share what you know. This myth keeps most people silent, and the silence is a loss for everyone.

The truth is that the best position to teach from is one step ahead. Not ten steps, not a hundred. One. Because when you are one step ahead, you remember exactly what confusion felt like. You remember which explanations did not work and which questions you had that nobody answered. That proximity to the learning makes your teaching more useful, not less.

Teaching also accelerates your own learning. The act of explaining forces you to find the gaps in your understanding. You cannot fake clarity when you are trying to make something clear for someone else. Teach publicly and you get feedback. Teach consistently and you build a reputation for knowing the thing. Two years of teaching while learning compounds faster than two years of waiting until you know enough to start.

*Build something worth owning.*
