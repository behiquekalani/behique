# Issue 1919: The Difference Between Goals and Directions

**One insight. No fluff.**

Goals have end dates. Directions don't. Most people set goals, hit them or miss them, and then experience a gap. The runner who trained for a marathon and doesn't know what to do the week after crossing the finish line. The founder who hit the revenue target and feels unexpectedly empty. Goals give you a destination. They don't give you a reason to keep moving after arrival.

Directions are different. A direction is a vector, not a point. Getting better at building things. Creating more clarity in how I think. Developing deeper relationships with a smaller number of people. These don't end. You can't check them off. They inform every decision without expiring. When a goal is in service of a direction, hitting the goal becomes a waypoint, not a finish line. The energy keeps flowing past it.

Set goals inside directions. The goal answers "what am I building toward right now?" The direction answers "why does any of this matter?" Without the direction, goals feel arbitrary after a while. Without the goal, direction feels abstract and hard to execute. You need both. One gives you traction. The other gives you meaning. Neither one is useful without the other, and most people only have one.
