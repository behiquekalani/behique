# Issue 1883: You Don't Need More Information

**One insight. No fluff.**

The research phase has an expiration date. Past it, you're not learning, you're delaying. Every additional article, course, or thread you consume is a negotiation with yourself, a way to feel like you're moving without actually committing to the thing. It feels responsible. It is not.

At some point the information you need is only available through doing. The market will tell you things no course can. The friction of building will reveal problems no research predicted. The customers who don't buy will teach you more than the ones who do. All of this is inaccessible until you ship something real.

The rule is simple: if you've researched the same topic more than three times, you already know enough to start. What's missing is not information. It's the decision to act on what you already have.
