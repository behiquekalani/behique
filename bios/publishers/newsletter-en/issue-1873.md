# Issue 1873: Why Comparison Feels Worse Than It Used To

**One insight. No fluff.**

Comparison is not new. What is new is the density of it. Every time you open a feed you are exposed to the curated highlights of thousands of people, selected specifically for their ability to provoke a reaction. You are not comparing yourself to your neighbors the way past generations did. You are comparing yourself to the global top performers in every category simultaneously, all day, in your pocket.

The result is a distorted baseline. Things that would have seemed remarkable ten years ago now feel inadequate because you can always find someone doing it better online. This warps your ability to recognize real progress. You dismiss genuine wins because they do not match the external benchmark. The gap between where you are and where you see others feels permanent and personal.

The fix is not to stop caring about quality. It is to change your reference point. Compare yourself to who you were six months ago, not to who someone else is today. The question is not "am I better than them?" It is "am I better than I was?" That comparison is the only one that actually measures your growth. The other one just measures the internet's ability to make you feel small.
