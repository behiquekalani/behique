# Issue 2577: Productize Before You Automate

**The Idea:** Many solopreneurs rush to automate their business before they understand it. Automation makes a bad process faster. It does not fix the process.

Productization comes first. That means turning what you do from a custom, one-off engagement into a repeatable, defined thing. Same inputs, same process, same outputs. When you can describe your service as a product, you understand what it actually is. When you understand what it is, you can automate parts of it.

The sequence: do the work manually first. Do it the same way three times. Write down exactly what you do each time. Then look at what is mechanical and repetitive. That is what you automate. The judgment-dependent parts stay human. The repeatable parts become systems.

Skipping productization and jumping to automation is how you end up with a complex system that does the wrong thing consistently and efficiently. It looks like progress. It is not.

Get the process right first. Make it boring in its consistency. Then let automation remove the friction.

**Why It Matters:** A productized business is a scalable business. You can sell it, delegate it, systematize it. A custom service business is always contingent on your personal presence. Productization is what creates options.

**One Action:** Pick one thing you do repeatedly in your business. Write the steps as if explaining it to a new hire. Notice which steps require judgment and which are mechanical. That list tells you exactly what to systematize next.
