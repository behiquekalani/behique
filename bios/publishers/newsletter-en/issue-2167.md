# The Price You Set Is a Signal, Not a Number

Underpricing is not humility. It's a statement about what you believe your work is worth. The market believes you.

## The Idea

Price is communication. Before a buyer reads your sales page, before they watch your demo, before they see a single testimonial, they see your price. That number tells them everything about how you perceive your own work.

Low prices attract price-sensitive buyers. Price-sensitive buyers are the hardest to serve. They ask the most questions, request the most refunds, and tell their friends who are also price-sensitive. You build your whole audience out of people who will leave the moment someone undercuts you by five dollars.

The psychology of pricing runs counter to what most new creators expect. Higher prices often generate more trust, not less. A $7 product feels like a gamble you can afford to lose. A $197 product feels like something the creator believes in enough to stand behind.

This doesn't mean you should arbitrarily raise prices. It means your price should reflect the outcome you deliver, not the hours you spent or the pages you wrote. A product that helps someone earn an extra $500/month is worth significantly more than $37, regardless of how long it took to build.

Your pricing also sets a signal for how you see yourself. Creators who consistently underprice eventually become resentful. They work hard and wonder why it's not clicking. The math is clear: low price, high volume required, high burnout probability.

## Why It Matters

You need fewer customers to hit your income targets when your price is right. Fewer customers means better service, better relationships, and more energy to improve your product. Raise your price, serve fewer people better.

## One Action

Take your best product and look at the outcome it delivers. Find three competitors charging at least 2x what you're charging for similar outcomes. Adjust your price this week. Track the effect on conversion quality, not just conversion rate.
