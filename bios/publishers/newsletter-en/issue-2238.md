# Repurposing Is a Superpower, Not a Shortcut

One idea, many formats. That's leverage, not laziness.

## The Idea

There's a stigma around repurposing content. It feels like recycling. Like you're cheating the audience by not giving them something new every time. That framing is wrong and expensive.

The reality is that most of your audience missed your original post. Twitter has a 30-minute half-life for most tweets. Instagram Reels surface once and then fade. Your newsletter competes with 200 others in an inbox. Any given piece of content reaches, at best, a fraction of the people who would benefit from it.

Repurposing is not about being lazy. It's about not leaving value on the table. A long-form newsletter becomes a Twitter thread. The Twitter thread becomes a carousel. The carousel gets turned into a TikTok script. The best comments on the TikTok become the next newsletter's intro. One idea, five pieces, three platforms, three months of content.

The key is not mechanical copying. You adapt the idea to the format and the platform's native language. A tweet is a punchy take. A newsletter is an explanation. A short video is a story. Same idea, different delivery, different access points for different people.

This also means your best ideas get the distribution they deserve. A single tweet with a great insight posted once is a waste. That insight should live in your newsletter, your bio, your product copy, your profile. Build systems to systematically resurface your strongest ideas.

## Why It Matters

Content leverage is the difference between a creator who burns out and one who builds sustainably. You don't need infinite new ideas. You need to fully develop the ideas you already have.

## One Action

Take your single best-performing piece of content from the past 90 days. Turn it into one new format this week. If it was a tweet, write it as a newsletter section. If it was a newsletter, break it into five tweets. Ship it.
