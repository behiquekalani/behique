# How to Build a Micro-Brand That Sticks

A micro-brand isn't a small version of a big brand. It's a different category entirely. It runs on trust, specificity, and a defined point of view. Most companies can't build one. An individual can.

## What Makes a Brand Stick
Brands stick when they stand for something beyond their product. Apple isn't about computers. It's about a particular relationship between technology and humanity. You don't need Apple's budget to apply this logic. You need clarity.

What do you actually believe about your field? What's the thing most people get wrong? What would you say even if it cost you followers? That's your brand. It lives in the gap between what the market says and what you know to be true.

## The Micro Advantage
Big brands have to appeal to everyone. You don't. Your micro-brand can repel the wrong people and attract the right ones with unusual precision. That specificity creates loyalty that scale can't manufacture.

The person who finds your brand and thinks "this is exactly what I was looking for" is worth ten casual followers. Build for them. Repel the rest without apology.

## Consistency Over Time Is the Whole Game
A micro-brand isn't built in a campaign. It's built in the accumulation of decisions. Every post, every response, every product either reinforces your identity or dilutes it. The ones who build lasting brands are the ones who stay recognizable across years of change.

You don't need more content. You need more consistent content.

## This Week's Action
Write three sentences that define your brand's point of view. Not what you sell. What you believe. Post those three sentences publicly this week and see who responds.
