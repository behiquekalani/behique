# Issue 1836: Price Is a Signal You Send

**One insight. No fluff.**

Low prices do not attract more serious buyers. They attract more skeptical ones. When you charge too little, you signal that you are not confident in what you built, and people pick up on that signal even if they cannot name it. Price is communication before the sale happens. It tells people what category to put you in.

This is one of the most counterintuitive things about selling. Raising your price often reduces objections rather than increasing them. The buyer at a higher price point is typically more committed, less likely to demand refunds, and more likely to get results because they are invested. The buyer at a low price treats the purchase as low-stakes and acts accordingly.

There is a practical exercise here. Take whatever you currently charge or plan to charge, and double it. Then ask: what would I need to add, prove, or communicate to justify that number? Usually the answer is not much. A better guarantee, a clearer outcome statement, a stronger case study. Build those things and raise the price. You will find that the work required to justify a higher price almost always produces a better product too.
