# Price Is a Signal, Not Just a Number

Low prices attract price-sensitive buyers. High prices attract outcome-sensitive buyers. The type of customer you get is largely determined by the price you set.

## The Psychology of Pricing
When something is cheap, people assume it reflects quality. When something is priced confidently, people assume competence. This is irrational but true. A $9 course and a $497 course on the same topic attract completely different buyers, and the higher-priced buyer often gets more value, not because the content is better, but because they are more committed.

Price affects buyer behavior, not just your revenue.

## What Your Price Communicates
Every price tells a story. A $7 product says "low-risk test." A $200 product says "I believe this will change something for you." A $2,000 product says "this is a serious investment and I will make it worth it." None of these are wrong. But you need to know which story you are telling and whether it matches your positioning and your audience's expectations.

Misaligned pricing destroys conversions more than bad copy.

## How to Find the Right Price
Test upward. Most digital product creators underprice by 2x to 5x. Start with what feels uncomfortable, then add 20%. If conversions are reasonable and feedback is positive, you priced correctly. If buyers are complaining about price but buying anyway, raise it again. Price resistance from non-buyers is not data. Price acceptance from buyers is.

The market will tell you. Let it.

## This Week's Action
If you have an existing product, raise the price by 30% this week. Leave it for 30 days and track both conversion rate and total revenue. The result will likely surprise you.
