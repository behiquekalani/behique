# Issue 1859: Build for One Person First
**One insight. No fluff.**

The instinct when building something new is to design for everyone. Broad appeal, no sharp edges, nothing that might exclude a potential customer. This produces things that connect with nobody. Generic by design.

The counterintuitive move is to build for one specific person. Not a demographic. A person. Someone whose problem you understand deeply, whose language you know, whose specific frustration you have felt. When you solve for that person precisely, others with the same problem recognize themselves in what you made.

Specificity is not a limit. It is how you create resonance. The narrower the problem you solve, the more powerful the solution feels to the people who have it. Broad messaging is everywhere. Specific messaging cuts through. Start with one person, solve their problem completely, then let them tell the others.
