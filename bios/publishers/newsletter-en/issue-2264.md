# Price Higher Than You're Comfortable With

If your price feels comfortable, it's probably wrong.

## The Idea

Underpricing is not humility. It's fear wearing the mask of fairness.

Most first-time digital product creators price based on what they would pay, not what the transformation is worth. They think about their own financial position, assume everyone is like them, and set a price that feels "reasonable." Then they wonder why their revenue is stuck.

Here's what pricing actually signals: value, seriousness, and who the product is for. A $7 ebook attracts people looking for cheap information. A $97 guide attracts people who are serious about changing something. The buyer is different. The commitment is different. The results they get are often different too.

High prices also create financial leverage. If you need 1,000 sales at $10 to reach $10K, you'll burn out creating volume. At $100, you need 100 buyers. At $500, you need 20. The number of people you need to reach shrinks dramatically when you price for transformation instead of information.

You are not selling a PDF. You are selling a result. Price the result. If someone implements what you teach and earns $5,000, a $200 product was absurdly cheap. Price relative to the outcome, not relative to your cost of production.

## Why It Matters

Revenue per sale determines how sustainable your business is. Low prices require high volume. High volume requires massive reach most solo operators don't have. One pricing decision can change your entire business model.

## One Action

Take your most valuable product and double the price for one week. Track conversion. You may lose some buyers. You will almost certainly earn more total revenue, and the buyers who remain will be better.
