# Issue 2645: Specific Knowledge Cannot Be Taught

**The Idea:** There is knowledge you can find in a textbook. Then there is knowledge that only exists because of your specific path through life.

The textbook stuff is a commodity. Specific knowledge is not.

Your specific knowledge is the intersection of your unusual interests, your hard experiences, and the problems you have solved in ways nobody else would have thought to try. It is not necessarily rare in the abstract. It is rare in the combination.

Two people can both understand marketing and psychology. But only one of them grew up broke in a country with no financial infrastructure and figured out how to build income from scratch. That lived context changes everything they know about money, risk, and hustle. That is not marketable information. It is a worldview. And worldviews are the most valuable thing you can sell.

The trap is thinking your knowledge is too ordinary to matter. Most people undersell their specific knowledge because they are too close to it. What feels obvious to you took years to learn. What feels simple to you is someone else's unsolved problem.

Find that knowledge. Name it. Sell it.

**Why It Matters:** Specific knowledge creates genuine differentiation. Nobody can undercut you on something only you have. It is the foundation of a personal monopoly.

**One Action:** Write down three things you know deeply that came from your specific life path, not from any course or book. Those are the seeds of your most valuable products.
