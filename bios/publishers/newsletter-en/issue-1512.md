# The Mental Models That Run Your Business

You do not have a business problem. You have a thinking problem.

## First Principles vs Analogy

Most decisions get made by analogy. Someone else did it this way, so you do it this way. This works until the context changes. First principles thinking ignores how others do it and asks what is actually true.

When you price by analogy, you copy what others charge. When you price by first principles, you ask what value you actually create and work backward from there. The second approach almost always leads to higher prices and better positioning.

## Inversion

Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how to fail. List every way your business could collapse. Then eliminate those failure modes one by one.

Most business problems are obvious when you invert them. The creator who burns out did not protect their creative energy. The solopreneur who ran out of clients did not build an audience first. Inversion makes the solution visible before the problem becomes a crisis.

## The Pareto Filter

Twenty percent of your actions drive eighty percent of your results. Most people know this. Almost nobody acts on it.

Apply it ruthlessly. Twenty percent of your content drives eighty percent of your audience growth. Twenty percent of your products drives eighty percent of your revenue. Find the twenty percent. Do more of that. Stop doing the rest.

## This Week's Action

List ten things you did last week to grow your business. Circle the two that actually moved the needle. Schedule more time for those two next week. Cancel one thing that produced nothing.
