# The Mental Model That Changes How You Work

Most people optimize tasks. The best builders optimize constraints.

## The Idea

A constraint is anything that limits your throughput. Time, energy, skills, resources, distribution. Every system has one. Find the binding constraint and you find where your effort matters most.

This is the theory of constraints applied to a solo business. Instead of trying to improve everything at once, you identify the single bottleneck that limits your growth and fix only that.

If you have great products but no audience, distribution is your constraint. Improving your products further does nothing. Building your audience does everything.

If you have an audience but nothing to sell, monetization is your constraint. More content does nothing until you create an offer.

If you have an offer and an audience but a broken sales page, conversion is your constraint. More traffic into a broken funnel is waste.

Most people work on what they are comfortable with. Writers write more. Builders build more. This feels productive and accomplishes nothing if it is not the constraint.

The discipline is to honestly audit where work actually gets stuck, then direct all effort there. It is uncomfortable because it often means working on the thing you are worst at or most afraid of.

## Why It Matters

Removing one constraint can double output without adding hours. It is the highest-leverage move available to any builder at any stage.

Ask the question weekly: "What is slowing me down the most right now?"

## One Action

Draw your business as a pipeline with five stages: awareness, interest, offer, purchase, delivery. Mark where things get stuck. That stuck point is your constraint. Address it this week.
