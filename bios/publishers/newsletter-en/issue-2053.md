# Issue 2053: The Real Reason People Do Not Ship
**One insight. No fluff.**

Shipping is not a technical problem. The code runs. The design is done. The product exists. The delay at the end is almost always psychological. The thing you built is a representation of what you believe, what you can do, what you think is worth existing in the world. Releasing it means finding out if you were right.

That finding-out is the frightening part. If nobody cares, it is not just that the product failed. It feels like you failed. So you delay. You refine. You add one more feature. You wait until it is ready, which means until you feel safe, which means never.

The solution is not to care less. It is to change the unit of evaluation. You are not shipping to be judged. You are shipping to gather information. A product that nobody buys tells you something specific and useful. A product that a few people love tells you where to go deeper. Neither outcome is failure. Both are data. The only way to get the data is to release the thing, and every day you do not, you are choosing imagination over reality.
