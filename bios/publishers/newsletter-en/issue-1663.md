# Teach What You Know Right Now

The biggest lie in the creator space is that you need to be an expert before you can teach. You do not.

## The Gap Is the Product
The most useful teacher is not the one who knows everything. It is the one who crossed a gap recently enough to remember what it felt like. If you struggled with something six months ago and solved it, you are exactly qualified to help the person struggling with it today. Your proximity to the problem is the value.

Experts often make the worst teachers because they have forgotten the confusion. Your recent experience is not a liability. It is the curriculum.

## Teaching Accelerates Learning
There is a compounding effect to teaching what you know. When you explain a concept to someone else, your own understanding sharpens. You discover the gaps in your knowledge faster than any course or book could surface them. And the process of creating the explanation produces the content that builds your audience.

You do not wait until you are ready. You get ready by teaching.

## Start With the Transformation You Just Made
Think about one thing you did in the last 90 days that changed how you work, think, or earn. Write down the before state, the decision you made, and the after state. That is your first piece of teaching content. It requires no credentials. It only requires honesty.

## This Week's Action
Identify one skill, habit, or process you learned in the last three months. Write a 300-word post explaining how you learned it and what changed. Publish it without editing it for tone.
