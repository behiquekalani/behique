# What Your Niche Actually Is

Your niche is not a topic. It is an intersection.

## The Intersection Model

Everyone who tells you to "pick a niche" means well. Most of them give bad advice. They say pick one topic and stay in it. That is how you build a commodity.

Your niche is the intersection of three things: what you know, who you are, and who you want to help. That intersection is specific enough to stand out and broad enough to build on.

## Why Broad Niches Fail

"Productivity" is not a niche. "Productivity for ADHD creators who want to build a business without burning out" is. The first one has infinite competition. The second one has very little.

The narrower the audience, the more resonant the message. The more resonant the message, the faster the trust builds. Build for the specific reader before you build for the broad one.

## Evolving Without Losing Coherence

Your niche can evolve. It should. But it should evolve with you as you learn, not as a pivot away from what was not working after two weeks.

Give the niche time to gather an audience. Then refine it based on who shows up and what they need. The audience self-selects when you are specific enough. Let them tell you who they are.

## This Week's Action

Write your current niche in one sentence. Then rewrite it with a more specific audience and a more specific outcome. Compare the two. The second version should make you slightly uncomfortable with how narrow it is. That means it is working.
