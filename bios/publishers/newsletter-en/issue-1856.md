# Issue 1856: How Taste Develops
**One insight. No fluff.**

Taste is not a gift. It is an accumulation. You develop it by consuming a lot, thinking critically about what you consumed, and producing something with what you learned. All three steps matter. Most people do only the first.

The gap between taste and ability is where most creators get stuck. You can see what is good before you can make something good. That gap is uncomfortable. It feels like failure. It is actually the signal that your taste is ahead of your skill, which means you are developing correctly.

The only way through is to keep producing. Not waiting for the skill to match the taste before you publish, but publishing while the gap exists and watching it close over time. The people who skip this part, who wait until they feel ready, are usually waiting forever. Production is the training. There is no other version of it.
