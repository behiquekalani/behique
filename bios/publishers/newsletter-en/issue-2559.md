# Issue 2559: The Goal Is Not Passive Income

**The Idea:** Passive income is the wrong goal. Not because it is impossible, but because framing it as passive misrepresents what it actually requires to build and maintain.

The income from a digital product is passive once the systems are in place. But building those systems is active. Writing the product is active. Building the audience that buys it is active. Setting up the funnel that converts is active. The passive phase is downstream of a significant amount of active work.

The better goal is scalable income. Income that grows without a proportional increase in your effort. That framing is honest. It acknowledges the upfront work while still capturing the upside of leveraged output.

The solopreneur who builds toward scalable income makes better decisions. They invest time in building assets instead of chasing revenue by trading hours. They create systems instead of just doing tasks. They treat everything they build as something that should keep working after they step away from it.

Passive income as a fantasy attracts people who want the result without the construction. Scalable income as a goal attracts people who are willing to do the upfront work to change how their effort compounds over time.

Same destination. Very different path. Very different people who arrive.

**Why It Matters:** The framing you use shapes the decisions you make. Building for scalable income produces a different business than building for passive income. The former requires strategy. The latter invites shortcuts.

**One Action:** Audit one hour of your working week. Ask whether that hour is building an asset that will keep working, or just producing output that stops the moment you stop. Adjust accordingly.
