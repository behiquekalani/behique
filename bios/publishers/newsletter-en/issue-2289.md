# Build in Public, But Be Intentional About It

Transparency is a strategy. Use it like one.

## The Idea

Build in public became a trend because it worked. Sharing your journey, your numbers, your failures, and your wins creates trust at a speed that polished content rarely matches. People connect with process. They follow along. They root for you. And when you release something, they buy, because they were there for the story.

But build in public done wrong becomes performance. You share for likes instead of value. You post revenue screenshots to impress rather than to teach. You mistake visibility for depth. The audience you attract with performance content is not the same as the audience you attract with genuine transparency.

The difference is intention. Performative build-in-public says "look what I made." Genuine build-in-public says "here is what I learned, here is what failed, here is what I am figuring out." One entertains. The other builds a tribe.

The most powerful version is teaching through your own experience. Not "here is the theory." But "here is what I tried, here is what happened, here is what I would do differently." That kind of content compounds. It positions you as someone who actually builds things and tells the truth about what it is like.

## Why It Matters

People are skeptical of polished online brands. They have been sold to too many times by people who made success look easier than it was. Authentic build-in-public content cuts through that noise. It earns trust faster than any ad campaign and creates an audience that stays through the difficult seasons.

## One Action

Share one real thing about your current build this week. Not a win. A lesson. Something you tried, something that did not work, and what you learned. Keep it short, make it specific, and do not dress it up. Raw honesty builds faster than polished positioning.
