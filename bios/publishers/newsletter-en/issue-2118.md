# Issue 2118: Build Your Own Curriculum

**One insight. No fluff.**

School taught you how to learn on someone else's schedule, for someone else's goals, measured by someone else's metrics. That is a useful introduction and a terrible long-term strategy. The most capable people in any field are self-directed learners who designed their own curriculum based on what they actually needed to become good at the thing they were actually building.

The self-directed curriculum starts with a goal, not a subject. What do you want to be able to do? Work backwards from that. What skills does that require? What knowledge gaps are currently blocking you? That gap is your curriculum. Not a degree. Not a course catalog. The specific gap between where you are and what you need to do next.

The resources are everywhere. Books, papers, people, projects, experiments. The constraint is not access. It is the discipline to follow a self-designed path without external accountability. That discipline is itself a meta-skill worth building. The person who can identify what they need to learn and then actually learn it on their own terms is never at the mercy of a gatekeeper. Design your own curriculum. Then finish it.
