# Issue 2069: Niche Down Until It Feels Too Small

**One insight. No fluff.**

The instinct is to stay broad because broad feels safer. If you market to everyone, surely someone will buy. In practice the opposite is true. When you speak to everyone, no one feels spoken to. The writer who addresses "busy professionals" loses to the one who writes for "first-generation college grads building a side income while working full-time." The specificity is not limiting. It is targeting.

There is a moment when narrowing your focus starts to feel uncomfortable. Like you are cutting off too much of the market. That feeling is usually the signal that you have hit the right level of specificity. Not before the discomfort. At the discomfort. Most people stop narrowing one step earlier because it feels safe, and they end up in a competitive middle ground where nothing distinguishes them.

The narrow niche does not trap you forever. It gives you a foothold. You earn credibility with one specific group. You understand their language, their fears, their objections. Then you can expand from a position of trust rather than a position of hoping someone notices you. Start smaller than feels right. The market will pull you broader once you have proven yourself in the corner.
