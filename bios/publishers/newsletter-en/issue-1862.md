# Issue 1862: One Skill Compounds Faster Than Ten

**One insight. No fluff.**

There is a fantasy that breadth protects you. Learn coding and design and marketing and finance. Cover all the bases. What actually happens is you become competent at everything and exceptional at nothing. You get stuck in the middle of every field, too shallow to do real work, too scattered to build momentum.

Depth is the anomaly. Most people quit learning a skill at the point where it gets hard. That is exactly where the compounding starts. The first 80 percent of any skill is crowded. Everyone is there. The last 20 percent is almost empty because it requires patience, discomfort, and delayed feedback. That is where your work starts to look different from everyone else's.

Pick the one skill that connects to everything you want to build. Get unreasonably good at it before chasing the next thing. A writer who understands human psychology earns more than a decent writer who also knows Photoshop. The compound return on depth dwarfs the shallow return on breadth. Narrow down. Go further.
