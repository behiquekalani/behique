# Issue 1830: The Problem With Goals

**One insight. No fluff.**

Goals are overrated. Not because ambition is bad, but because goals are outcomes, and outcomes are largely outside your control. You can set a goal to earn $10,000 a month. You cannot control whether that happens. What you can control is the system of behaviors that makes it increasingly likely. Focusing on the outcome instead of the system is how people end up demoralized.

There is also a structural problem with goals. They imply an endpoint. You work toward the goal, you reach it or you do not, and then what? The people who succeed long-term are not goal-obsessed. They are process-obsessed. They love the work enough that the goal becomes a byproduct rather than a destination.

The reframe is this. Set goals to give yourself direction, then immediately forget them and build the system. Design your daily structure so that if you repeat it consistently, the goal becomes almost inevitable. Check on the goal quarterly to see if the system is working. Adjust the system, not the effort level. More effort applied to a broken system just produces faster failure. Fix the system first.
