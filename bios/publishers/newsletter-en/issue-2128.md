# Issue 2128: Boredom Is a Signal, Not a Problem

**One insight. No fluff.**

When you are bored, your first instinct is to escape it. Open a new tab. Check your phone. Find something stimulating. This is trained behavior. It treats boredom as discomfort to eliminate rather than information to read. The result is that you never actually hear what boredom is trying to tell you.

Boredom is the mind surfacing dissatisfaction with your current direction. It is not laziness. It is your internal compass tapping you on the shoulder. When you keep getting bored by the same thing, the thing is wrong, not your ability to focus. High engagement is natural when the work is right. Constant distraction is a symptom of misalignment.

The practice is to sit in the boredom long enough to get the message. What specifically are you bored by? Is it the task itself or just the framing? Is it the project or just the stage you are in? Boredom has answers. But you have to stop running from it long enough to hear them.
