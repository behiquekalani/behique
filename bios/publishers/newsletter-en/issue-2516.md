# Issue 2516: The Problem with Selling Information

**The Idea:** Information is not worth what it used to be. Anything that can be googled, YouTubed, or found on Reddit has near-zero perceived value. Selling raw information in 2024 is a losing strategy. What sells is not information. It is transformation, implementation, and certainty.

Transformation means the person becomes different after consuming your product. Not just informed. Changed. They see their problem differently. They have a new capability. They have gone from point A to point B.

Implementation means you reduce the gap between knowing and doing. Most people already know what they should do. They do not do it because the path from knowledge to action is unclear or overwhelming. A product that removes that friction is worth ten times more than one that adds more knowledge to the pile.

Certainty means your customer feels confident that following your system will work for them. This comes from specificity. The more precisely your product targets their exact situation, the more certain they feel that it applies to them.

Stop asking "what information can I share?" Start asking "what does this person need to be able to do, and how do I make it easy for them to do it?"

**Why It Matters:** The information market is saturated. The transformation market is not. Products that produce real, documented change are rare and command real prices.

**One Action:** Look at your product or offer. Write down one specific before-and-after transformation it delivers. If you cannot write it in two sentences, your product positioning needs work before your marketing does.
