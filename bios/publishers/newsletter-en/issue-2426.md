# Issue 2426: Digital Products Are the Best Business for One Person

**The One Thing:** A digital product sells while you sleep, scales without headcount, and costs almost nothing to deliver.

---

If you are a one-person operation, digital products are the closest thing to an unfair advantage. You create something once. You sell it infinite times. The margin is close to 100%. The delivery is automated. There is no inventory, no shipping, no returns that cost you money.

## Why the Model Works

Physical products have a ceiling built into them. More sales means more fulfillment, more costs, more logistics. Digital products invert this. More sales means more profit, same product, same delivery infrastructure. One person can run this without employees.

The biggest mistake is waiting to build something perfect before selling. Launch the rough version. Sell it. Improve it based on real buyer feedback. Version two is always better because real customers told you what was missing.

## What Sells

The digital products that sell consistently are the ones that save time or teach a skill people already want. Templates, frameworks, guides, and toolkits perform well because the value is immediate. The buyer gets it, opens it, and gets something useful in the first five minutes.

Stay close to your experience. Sell what you've already figured out. Your shortcuts are someone else's starting point.

## The Distribution Problem

A great product with no audience earns nothing. Build the distribution before or alongside the product. Newsletter, social account, community. Pick one channel and show up consistently. Then the product has somewhere to land.
