# Issue 2086: The First Version Is Not the Product

**One insight. No fluff.**

Shipping your first version is not the goal. It is the prerequisite for finding out what the goal actually is. Before you have a real product in front of real people, everything is speculation. Your assumptions about what they want, what confuses them, what they will pay for, what makes them return. All of it is guesswork. The first version converts guesswork into data.

This is why perfectionism before launch is one of the most expensive mistakes a builder can make. Every week spent polishing is a week without signal. You are optimizing a theory. The market is going to tell you which parts of that theory were wrong, and it will do so immediately. The faster you get to that conversation, the faster you can build something that actually works.

The first version's job is not to be good. Its job is to exist, reach people, and generate real feedback. The product that ships six months later, after you have iterated on real feedback, will be almost unrecognizable compared to what you initially planned. That is not failure. That is the process. The builders who understand this ship faster, waste less, and end up with better products than the ones who spent the same period of time planning.
