# TikTok Batch 1804

**Script 1: Specific Knowledge Cannot Be Automated**
Hook (0-3s): What you know from lived experience cannot be Googled.
Body (3-45s): There is a difference between information and specific knowledge. Information is available to everyone. Specific knowledge comes from years of doing one thing in one context with real stakes. The mechanic who has fixed every variation of one engine model. The designer who has worked with 50 SaaS companies. The parent who homeschooled three kids through different learning styles. That knowledge cannot be automated. It cannot be outsourced. It is your moat. Build products around what only you know.
CTA (45-60s): What is your specific knowledge? Write it in the comments. One sentence.

**Script 2: Identity Drives Consistency**
Hook (0-3s): You do not need motivation. You need identity.
Body (3-45s): Motivation runs out. It is a feeling. Feelings change with sleep, food, and circumstances. Identity does not. When you are a person who publishes every week, you publish even when it is bad. When you are a person who ships products, you ship even when the launch is quiet. Motivation says I will do it when I feel ready. Identity says this is what I do. The creators who last do not have more motivation. They have made it part of who they are. Decide who you are. The behavior follows.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for daily content on the identity side of building. This is what most creators skip.

**Script 3: Energy Over Hustle**
Hook (0-3s): Working 14 hours a day is a rookie mistake.
Body (3-45s): Not because hard work is bad. Because unmanaged energy is inefficient. You have four hours of peak cognition per day. Everything beyond that is diminishing returns. The best builders protect those four hours like they are sacred. Deep work in the morning. Admin in the afternoon. Rest is not laziness. It is maintenance. A burnt-out creator stops shipping. A rested creator ships for years. You are the asset. Treat yourself like one.
CTA (45-60s): Drop a fire emoji if you protect your peak hours. Follow for more on sustainable building.
