# TikTok Batch 1806

**Script 1: The Niche Within the Niche**
Hook (0-3s): If your niche has a Reddit community, it is still too broad.
Body (3-45s): Go one level deeper. Not content creators, but content creators who do not show their face. Not fitness, but fitness for people who hate gyms. Not personal finance, but personal finance for freelancers who get paid irregularly. The deeper you go, the less competition. The deeper you go, the more the right person feels you are speaking directly to them. Broad niches have big audiences and weak trust. Deep niches have small audiences and fanatical buyers. Choose your model.
CTA (45-60s): Tell me your deep niche in the comments. Let us see who went furthest.

**Script 2: Writing Is the Foundational Skill**
Hook (0-3s): Every business skill runs through writing.
Body (3-45s): Your sales page is writing. Your emails are writing. Your social posts are writing. Your product itself is writing. If you can write clearly, you can sell without a sales team. You can market without an agency. You can build trust with strangers who have never met you. Writing is not a nice-to-have for creators. It is the engine. Every hour spent improving your writing compounds across every channel you ever use. Start with writing. Everything else gets easier.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for writing frameworks that translate directly to revenue. First one drops tomorrow.

**Script 3: AI Amplification Is Not Automation**
Hook (0-3s): Stop trying to automate your business. Amplify it.
Body (3-45s): Automation removes the human. Amplification scales the human. When you use AI to amplify, you write the outline and AI expands it. You record the idea and AI transcribes and formats. You draft the email and AI sharpens it. The result still sounds like you because it starts with you. The creators who try to fully automate produce content that feels empty. The ones who use AI to amplify produce more without losing their voice. Know the difference. Keep the voice.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for AI workflows built for solo creators. Real ones, not generic advice.
