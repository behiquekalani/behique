# TikTok Batch 1790

**Script 1: Your Content Is Your Resume**
Hook (0-3s): Every post you publish is a public sample of your thinking. Act accordingly.
Body (3-45s): Clients, partners, future employers, collaborators. They will Google you. They will find your content. What they see will determine whether they reach out. Your content is not just for followers. It's a permanent record of how you think, what you know, and how you communicate. Bad content hurts you. No content is invisible. Great content opens doors you never knocked on. Think of every post as a job application for opportunities you haven't imagined yet.
CTA (45-60s): Has a post ever brought you an unexpected opportunity? Client, collab, media feature? Tell me.

**Script 2: Stop Optimizing for Viral. Optimize for Trust.**
Hook (0-3s): Viral is a lottery. Trust is a skill.
Body (3-45s): You can't engineer a viral post. You can engineer a trustworthy one. Trust comes from specificity. From showing up consistently. From saying things that are actually true, not just things that sound good. From admitting what you don't know. The irony: trusted creators go viral more often. Because their content is so good, so real, so useful that people share it. Optimize for the trust. The viral moments are a byproduct.
CTA (45-60s): What's one thing you've said in your content that was true but uncomfortable? Share below.

**Script 3: The Rule of 100**
Hook (0-3s): Before you judge your results, publish 100 pieces of content. Then we talk.
Body (3-45s): Most creators quit around post 20. The algorithm hasn't found them yet. The content is still rough. The voice isn't fully developed. But at post 100, something shifts. You've got data. You know what works. Your style is sharper. Your confidence is real. 100 posts is the minimum sample size for anything meaningful. Most people never get there. The ones who do have a completely different perspective on what's possible. Commit to 100 before you evaluate anything.
CTA (45-60s): What post number are you on right now? Be honest. Let's count together.
