# TikTok Batch 1839

**Script 1: The Worst Advice About Growing an Audience**
Hook (0-3s): "Post more" is the worst growth advice anyone can give you.
Body (3-45s): Posting more without posting better is how you burn out and see no results. The lever is not frequency. The lever is relevance. One deeply relevant post that hits exactly what your audience is thinking about will outperform ten average posts every time. Before you add more volume, improve the quality of what you're already making. Study your best posts. Understand why they worked. Replicate the structure. Volume comes later, once you know what to make more of. Start with relevance, then scale frequency.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the content quality framework I use before I ever think about frequency.

**Script 2: Build the Business, Not the Persona**
Hook (0-3s): The business built on a persona has to maintain the persona forever.
Body (3-45s): When your brand is you, the real version, it scales naturally. You don't have to perform. You just have to show up and be honest. The persona requires constant maintenance. The real person just has to grow. Your opinions will change. Your knowledge will deepen. Your business will evolve. All of that is fine and even compelling when the brand is you. A persona can't evolve without confusing the audience. The real person can because growth is expected. Build the business around who you actually are.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see a business built on real identity, not performance.

**Script 3: Why Free Products Are the Best Marketing**
Hook (0-3s): The best thing you can put in front of a stranger is a free product that's genuinely good.
Body (3-45s): A free resource that delivers real value does three things. It attracts the right people. It filters out the wrong people. And it establishes trust before you've ever had a real conversation. A free guide, a template, a checklist, a mini-course. Whatever format fits your niche. When someone experiences your free product and thinks "this is better than the paid stuff I've bought elsewhere," they are ready to pay. Free products are not charity. They're the most efficient trust-building mechanism available.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see how I use free products to fill my email list with ready-to-buy subscribers.
