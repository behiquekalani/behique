# TikTok Batch 1812

**Script 1: The Real Reason You Have Not Started**
Hook (0-3s): You know enough to start. The reason you have not is not knowledge.
Body (3-45s): It is judgment. The fear that people you know will see you trying and fail. The fear that your first attempt will not match your taste. The fear that the people already doing this will think you are late. All of that is noise. Nobody is watching as closely as you think. The people you fear judgment from are busy worrying about their own work. The audience you want does not exist yet. They will not exist until you show up. Start ugly. Start anyway.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the honest version of building online. No polished highlight reels here.

**Script 2: Monthly Recurring Beats One-Time Sales**
Hook (0-3s): One-time sales are exciting. Monthly revenue is peace of mind.
Body (3-45s): When your income resets to zero every month, the pressure is constant. You are always chasing the next sale. Recurring revenue changes the math. Even a modest subscription product at $29 per month with 50 subscribers is $1,450 coming in every month before you do anything. That changes how you think. You stop sprinting and start building. Subscriptions, memberships, communities, retainers. If you have a digital product that works once, ask how you could make it ongoing.
CTA (45-60s): Are you building recurring revenue yet? Yes or no in the comments.

**Script 3: The Platform You Own**
Hook (0-3s): The algorithm is not your friend. It is a landlord.
Body (3-45s): You do not own your followers. You rent access to them. The landlord can raise the rent. They can change the lease. They can demolish the building. The platform owns the relationship. You pay with your content. When you move people to your email list, your podcast, your private community, you stop renting. You own the relationship directly. Social platforms are billboards. Your owned platform is the store. Do not confuse them.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the full owned-platform playbook. First email drops this week.
