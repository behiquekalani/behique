# TikTok EN Batch 1751

**Video 1 — Habits**
Hook (0-3s): One habit that adds an hour of productive time to every day.
Body (3-45s): Time blocking the night before. Every Sunday night and every evening I spend 10 minutes writing down the three things that must happen tomorrow. Not a wishlist. Three. Ordered by priority. When I wake up I know exactly what to do. I don't spend the first hour of my morning figuring out what to work on. That decision is already made. That one shift saved me an hour a day minimum. Decision fatigue is real and it starts the moment you open your eyes and don't know what to do. Remove that problem the night before.
CTA (45-60s): Try it tonight. Tell me tomorrow how it went.
Caption: Time blocking the night before eliminates morning decision fatigue and adds an hour to your day. #habits #productivity #solopreneur #morningroutine #timemanagement

**Video 2 — Platform**
Hook (0-3s): Which platform should you start with? Here's the honest answer.
Body (3-45s): The one you'll actually use consistently. That's it. Not the one with the best algorithm right now. Not the one the gurus say is blowing up. The one you like using. Because the best strategy fails if you hate doing it. If you love writing, Twitter or a newsletter. If you love talking, TikTok or YouTube. If you love visuals, Instagram. The platform is the vehicle. Your ideas and consistency are the engine. A vehicle you hate drives slower than one you love. Start where you feel most natural.
CTA (45-60s): What's your main platform and why? Comment below.
Caption: The best platform is the one you'll actually use. Consistency beats algorithm chasing. #platformstrategy #contentcreator #solopreneur #socialmedia #creatorlife

**Video 3 — Failure**
Hook (0-3s): My product flopped. Here's what I actually learned.
Body (3-45s): I spent three weeks building a template pack. Launched it. Sold four copies. I was embarrassed. But then I looked at who bought those four. They had a specific problem I hadn't fully addressed. So I asked them questions. I found the gap. Rebuilt with that knowledge. The second version sold 40 copies in the first week. The flop wasn't a failure. It was expensive market research. Every product that doesn't sell tells you something specific if you're willing to ask. Don't delete your failures. Interrogate them.
CTA (45-60s): What did your last failed product teach you?
Caption: A flopped product is expensive market research. Interrogate it before you delete it. #failure #digitalproducts #solopreneur #productlaunch #growthmindset
