# TikTok Batch 1714

## Script 1 — Patience With Process
**Hook (0-3s):** The process is not the obstacle. The process IS the point.
**Body (3-45s):** People chase outcomes and tolerate the process. Flip this and everything changes. Fall in love with the daily reps. The writing sessions that feel flat. The workouts that aren't peak performance. The builds that feel slow. These unglamorous sessions are the foundation every result eventually rests on. The outcome is just the visible marker of the process. When you love the process, consistency becomes natural. You stop needing motivation because you actually enjoy the work. That's the state where compounding accelerates.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's one part of your process that you've started to genuinely enjoy? Tell me.

## Script 2 — Pricing Psychology
**Hook (0-3s):** Low prices don't attract more customers. They attract worse ones.
**Body (3-45s):** When you undercharge, you signal low value. People make quality assumptions based on price. The $10 course and the $1,000 course can contain identical information. The $1,000 student implements and gets results. The $10 student doesn't engage because the investment isn't high enough to force commitment. Price is a filter, not just a revenue decision. Premium pricing attracts buyers who are serious, who take action, who get results, who become case studies. Raise your prices. Deliver more value. Watch the quality of your customers improve.
**CTA (45-60s):** Are you undercharging right now? Be honest. What would you charge if you had full confidence?

## Script 3 — The Curiosity Advantage
**Hook (0-3s):** Curious people will always outperform talented people who've stopped wondering.
**Body (3-45s):** Talent without curiosity plateaus. The talented person relies on what they already know. The curious person keeps asking why, keeps exploring adjacent fields, keeps making unexpected connections. Most major innovations are just curiosity from one field applied to a problem in another field. The more widely you explore, the more unique your perspective becomes. Nobody can compete with your specific combination of experiences and interests. Nurture curiosity like it's your edge. Because it is.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's the most random thing you've been curious about lately? No judgment. Share it.
