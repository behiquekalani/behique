# TikTok Batch 1794

**Script 1: The Fear Funnel**
Hook (0-3s): Your fear is pointing you directly at your next growth opportunity.
Body (3-45s): Every time you feel resistance around creating a piece of content, launching a product, or raising your prices, that's data. The resistance usually means: this matters to you. This is real. This could actually change something. The safe content performs flat. The content you almost didn't post is usually the one that resonates deepest. The product you were scared to launch is often the one that sells. Fear is a signal, not a stop sign. It means you're close to something true.
CTA (45-60s): What piece of content have you been afraid to post? What's stopping you? Tell me.

**Script 2: Your Worst Posts Teach the Most**
Hook (0-3s): The post that flopped taught me more than the one that went viral.
Body (3-45s): A viral post tells you: this resonated. But you don't always know why. A flop is more instructive. Wrong hook. Wrong angle. Wrong format. Mismatched audience expectation. When a post dies, you have something to examine. Why didn't this work? What would have made it land? The creators who grow fastest are the ones who treat every underperforming post as a case study, not a failure. The data is there. You just have to be willing to look at it without ego.
CTA (45-60s): What's a post that flopped but taught you something? What did you learn?

**Script 3: Launching with Zero Followers**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need an audience to validate your first product. You need one sale.
Body (3-45s): One person paying you real money for something you made is worth more than 10,000 followers who've never spent a dollar. That first sale proves the idea is real. It proves someone trusts you enough to exchange value. You don't need a big audience to get that. You need to find one person with the problem, offer them a solution, and ask for money. Tell someone. Post once. DM five people. The path to your first sale is almost always shorter than you think. You're overthinking the distribution. Just make the ask.
CTA (45-60s): Have you made your first digital product sale? Tell me the product and the price. Let's celebrate.
