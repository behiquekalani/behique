# TikTok Batch 1783

**Script 1: First Principles Thinking for Creators**
Hook (0-3s): Stop copying what other creators do. Ask why it works instead.
Body (3-45s): Most creators copy tactics. The top 1% copies principles. A tactic is: post at 6pm on Tuesdays. A principle is: post when your audience is most active and has mental bandwidth. Tactics expire. Platforms change, algorithms shift. Principles don't. If you understand why something works, you can adapt it to any platform, any format, any audience. Learn the roots, not the branches.
CTA (45-60s): What's one principle you've figured out about creating content that actually holds? Drop it below.

**Script 2: Build in Public, Win in Private**
Hook (0-3s): Building in public isn't about showing your wins. It's about documenting your process.
Body (3-45s): The mistake most people make when building in public: they only share the highlights. Big launch. New product. Milestone hit. But the content that actually builds trust is the messy middle. The product that flopped. The post that got no views. The week you didn't post because life happened. People don't follow you for your success. They follow you because you make them feel less alone in their own struggles. Show the real work.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the unfiltered version of building a digital product business. Real numbers included.

**Script 3: Passive Income Has an Active Phase**
Hook (0-3s): Passive income isn't passive at the start. Not even close.
Body (3-45s): Every passive income stream you've ever seen had a brutal active phase first. The ebook didn't write itself. The course didn't build itself. The newsletter audience didn't appear overnight. The word "passive" refers to the income stage, not the setup stage. You put in heavy work upfront. Then the systems carry you. Most people quit during the setup phase and conclude passive income is a myth. It's not a myth. They just didn't finish the active part.
CTA (45-60s): What passive income stream are you building right now? Tell me in the comments.
