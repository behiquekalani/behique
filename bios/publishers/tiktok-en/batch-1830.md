# TikTok Batch 1830

**Script 1: The Compounding Content Strategy**
Hook (0-3s): Your content from two years ago is still working for you right now.
Body (3-45s): That's the compounding effect of consistent content. Every post, video, and email you put out is a searchable, shareable asset. It keeps generating attention long after you published it. This is why consistency beats virality. One viral post spikes and fades. Two hundred consistent posts build a library that keeps working. When someone discovers you, they don't see your latest post. They see everything you've ever made. That library is your credibility. Build it one post at a time and never stop.
CTA (45-60s): Follow so you don't miss a single post in the library I'm building.

**Script 2: The Problem with Chasing Every New Platform**
Hook (0-3s): Every time a new platform launches, creators abandon what was working.
Body (3-45s): New platform, new algorithm, new format, new audience. The temptation to start fresh somewhere untapped is real. But here's what happens: you fragment your effort across too many places and go deep on none of them. The strategy that works is to own one platform first. Get consistent, get good, understand the audience. Then expand. Repurpose to other platforms, not start from scratch. The one-person business can't afford to be everywhere at 20%. It needs to be somewhere at 100%.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the platform-first strategy I used to stop spreading thin.

**Script 3: The Sales Page Mindset Shift**
Hook (0-3s): A sales page is not a pitch. It's a mirror.
Body (3-45s): The best sales pages don't tell the reader how great the product is. They describe the reader's exact situation back to them so clearly that the reader thinks you read their mind. When someone feels understood, they trust you. When they trust you, they buy. The copy that converts is not clever. It's accurate. It names the exact problem, the exact frustration, and the exact outcome the reader wants. If you do that well, the product almost sells itself. Get the reader to think "this is exactly for me" in the first paragraph.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for copywriting frameworks that actually convert readers to buyers.
