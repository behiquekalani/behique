# TikTok Batch 1796

**Script 1: The Algorithm Is Not Your Enemy**
Hook (0-3s): The algorithm doesn't hate you. It just doesn't know you yet.
Body (3-45s): The algorithm has one job: show people content they want to see. If your content isn't reaching people, there are two possibilities. One, the algorithm hasn't collected enough data on your content yet. Two, your content isn't performing well enough on the early signals. Watch time, saves, shares. It's almost never personal. It's just math. The solution is the same in both cases: post more, pay attention to what performs, and stop creating content for the algorithm. Create for the human sitting on the other side of the screen.
CTA (45-60s): What metric do you actually track when it comes to your content performance?

**Script 2: What Niching Down Actually Means**
Hook (0-3s): Niching down doesn't mean shrinking. It means getting more specific about who you help.
Body (3-45s): "Fitness creator" is a category. "Strength training for women over 40 who hate the gym" is a niche. The niche isn't smaller. It's more precise. And precision converts. When someone in your exact target audience finds your content, they feel like you're reading their mind. That feeling is rare. It's also why they follow, buy, and refer friends. The irony: the more specific you get, the more people feel seen. Niching down is not limiting your reach. It's concentrating your signal.
CTA (45-60s): What's your niche right now? Say it as specifically as possible in the comments.

**Script 3: First Principles for Pricing**
Hook (0-3s): Strip away everything and ask: what is the customer actually paying for?
Body (3-45s): Not the PDF. Not the video course. Not the coaching call. They're paying for a transformation. From where they are to where they want to be. Once you understand that, pricing becomes clearer. How much is that transformation worth? How long would it take them to figure it out alone? What's the cost of staying stuck? Those are your pricing anchors, not the number of pages in your ebook or the hours in your course. Value is the metric. Everything else is a guess.
CTA (45-60s): What transformation does your product or service provide? Say it in one sentence.
