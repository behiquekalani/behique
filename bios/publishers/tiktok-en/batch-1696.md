# TikTok Batch 1696

## Script 1 — The Energy Audit
**Hook (0-3s):** Not all hours are equal. Your peak energy window is your most valuable real estate.
**Body (3-45s):** Most people do their most important work when they have energy left over, not when they're at their best. That's backwards. Figure out when your brain is sharpest. Most people peak in the morning, hit a dip midday, then recover slightly in the afternoon. Schedule your hardest, most creative work during your peak. Admin, email, and routine tasks in the valley. This single shift, no extra hours needed, can double your actual output. Protect your peak like it's the only time you have.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for performance scheduling. Comment your peak energy window.

## Script 2 — Start With Why Every Time
**Hook (0-3s):** If you don't know why you're doing something, you'll stop the moment it gets hard.
**Body (3-45s):** The why is load-bearing. When you have a clear, personal, non-negotiable reason for what you're building, temporary obstacles become details rather than dealbreakers. Without the why, every hard day is a reason to quit. With it, hard days are just the cost of the thing that matters. Write down your why. Not the polished version. The real one. The one that involves your family, your fear, your faith, or your purpose. Then read it when everything tells you to stop.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for purpose-driven content. Comment your why in one sentence.

## Script 3 — The Leverage of Standards
**Hook (0-3s):** Raise your standards and watch what you automatically stop tolerating.
**Body (3-45s):** Standards are invisible boundaries that filter what you accept into your life. Low standards mean you accept mediocre work, draining relationships, and poor results as normal. Raise the standard and everything that was previously acceptable suddenly becomes intolerable. This is not arrogance. It's clarity. You don't have to fight to remove the wrong things. You just have to decide what's acceptable and hold the line. Most people never raise their standards because it would require them to change.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for standards-based growth. Comment one standard you recently raised for yourself.
