# TikTok Batch 1711

## Script 1 — Reading as Research
**Hook (0-3s):** You're reading the wrong books for the wrong reasons.
**Body (3-45s):** Reading for entertainment is fine. Reading to feel smart is a trap. Reading as research is a superpower. The difference is intentionality. Before you open a book, know what problem you're trying to solve. Read to extract what's applicable to your current situation. Take notes in your own words. Apply at least one thing before picking up another book. The person who reads 50 books a year and applies nothing is less advanced than the person who reads 5 books and implements everything. Reading without output is just consumption with extra steps.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's the last book that actually changed how you work? Drop the title below.

## Script 2 — Speed as Strategy
**Hook (0-3s):** Speed is not recklessness. Speed is a competitive moat.
**Body (3-45s):** The builder who ships fast learns fast. Every version in market teaches you something no amount of planning can reveal. Slow builders wait until it's right. Fast builders make it right through iteration. The market does not reward the most prepared launch. It rewards the one that got there first with something good enough. Then iterated. The companies and creators who move fast are not cutting corners on quality. They're cutting corners on fear. They ship, learn, improve. Repeat indefinitely.
**CTA (45-60s):** What would you ship today if you weren't waiting for it to be perfect?

## Script 3 — The Value of Specificity
**Hook (0-3s):** Vague goals produce vague results.
**Body (3-45s):** "I want to get fit" is not a goal. "I want to do 10 pull-ups by June 1st" is a goal. "I want to make money online" is not a goal. "I want to earn $3,000 a month from digital products by September" is a goal. Specificity creates clarity. Clarity creates action. Action creates results. When your goal is vague, your brain can't run toward it because it doesn't know what it looks like. Define exactly what winning looks like. Set a date. Make the target measurable. Then your efforts have direction.
**CTA (45-60s):** Rewrite one vague goal into a specific, measurable one. Post it below.
