# TikTok Batch 1792

**Script 1: The Real Cost of a Bad Hook**
Hook (0-3s): Nobody sees your great content if your first three seconds lose them.
Body (3-45s): The hook is not a nice-to-have. It's the whole game on short-form. If you don't grab attention in three seconds, the person is gone. And they weren't lazy. You just didn't give them a reason to stay. A great hook creates a pattern interrupt. It makes a bold claim, asks a provocative question, or shows something unexpected. The rest of your video can be amazing. But without a hook, nobody sees it. Practice writing hooks. It's the highest-leverage skill in short-form content.
CTA (45-60s): What's the best hook you've ever written? Drop it in the comments. I'll give feedback.

**Script 2: Identity and the Creator Path**
Hook (0-3s): You'll never consistently create until you stop thinking of yourself as someone who's trying to be a creator.
Body (3-45s): There's a version of this that's subtle. You do the work, but you still say things like "I'm trying to build an audience." Or "I'm working on becoming a creator." Notice the gap. "Trying" and "working on becoming" signal that you haven't fully claimed the identity. The moment you say "I'm a creator. I make content about X for Y people" without apology, without hedging, something changes. You start making decisions from that identity. Posting becomes natural. Quitting becomes inconsistent with who you are.
CTA (45-60s): Say it out loud right now. "I am a creator who..." Finish it in the comments.

**Script 3: Revenue Diversification for Solopreneurs**
Hook (0-3s): One income stream for a solopreneur is a job. Three is a business.
Body (3-45s): If 100% of your income comes from one source, you're one bad month away from crisis. Digital products. A service. A newsletter with sponsorships. Affiliate income. One-on-one coaching. These aren't separate businesses. They're branches of the same tree. You build them on top of your audience and expertise. Each one reinforces the others. A product buyer might become a coaching client. A coaching client becomes a case study for your product. Build the first stream. Then add the second. Don't wait until stream one is perfect.
CTA (45-60s): How many income streams do you have right now? Drop your number below.
