# TikTok Batch 1789

**Script 1: Build What You Needed Two Years Ago**
Hook (0-3s): The best digital product idea is the thing you wished existed when you were starting.
Body (3-45s): You have more expertise than you think, because expertise isn't about knowing everything. It's about knowing more than the person behind you. The you from two years ago had problems. You solved them. Write that down. The specific process you developed. The framework that helped you. The shortcut you figured out. Someone who's two years behind you needs exactly that. Not the expert version. The relatable version from someone who recently struggled with the same thing.
CTA (45-60s): What do you know now that you desperately needed two years ago? Drop it in the comments.

**Script 2: Systems Beat Motivation**
Hook (0-3s): Motivation is unreliable. Systems are not.
Body (3-45s): Motivation shows up when you feel good, when the content is performing, when the comments are positive. It disappears when life hits, when the algorithm tanks your reach, when you're tired. A system doesn't care how you feel. It runs anyway. Batch content on Mondays. Schedule posts in advance. Have a template for every format. The creators who publish consistently aren't more motivated than you. They've just removed motivation from the equation entirely.
CTA (45-60s): What's one system you've built that keeps you creating even when motivation is gone?

**Script 3: The Compounding Post**
Hook (0-3s): One post from 18 months ago is still bringing people to my page today.
Body (3-45s): Not every post has a 48-hour lifespan. Some content has legs. A detailed tutorial. A hot take that aged well. A case study with real numbers. This content gets shared, saved, and found through search long after you posted it. The problem: you don't know which post will be that one in advance. So you ship everything. Some dies fast. Some compounds for years. This is why consistency matters more than any individual post going viral. You're playing the long game, whether you intend to or not.
CTA (45-60s): Do you have a post that's still bringing you followers or sales from months ago? Tell me what it was.
