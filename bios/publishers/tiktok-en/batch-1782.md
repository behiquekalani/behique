# TikTok Batch 1782

**Script 1: Your Price Is Your Signal**
Hook (0-3s): If no one's complaining your price is too high, it's too low.
Body (3-45s): Price is not just about money. It's a signal. A $7 ebook says one thing. A $97 guide says something completely different, even if the content is identical. People use price to decide how seriously to take your work. Underpricing doesn't attract better customers. It attracts more people who don't value what you make. Raise the price. You'll lose some browsers. You'll keep the buyers. And buyers are who you need.
CTA (45-60s): What's the price of your first digital product? Drop it below. Judgment-free zone.

**Script 2: One Niche, One Year**
Hook (0-3s): The fastest way to build authority online: stay in one lane for one full year.
Body (3-45s): Most creators switch niches every three months. The topic feels stale, the views slow down, they panic and pivot. But authority compounds. Every time you post in the same niche, the algorithm learns. Your audience trains to expect you. Your expertise deepens. The creators who dominate their space didn't get there by being interesting. They got there by being consistent and specific. One niche. One year. See what happens.
CTA (45-60s): Comment your niche below. If you've been in it for over a year, add how long. Let's see the veterans.

**Script 3: Small Bets, Big Returns**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need to go all in. You need to start small and iterate.
Body (3-45s): The "all in" narrative is a lie. Most creators who succeeded didn't bet everything on one launch. They made small bets. Posted consistently. Tested what worked. Doubled down on what resonated. Small bets teach you faster than big swings because the cost of failure is low. You learn, adjust, and go again. The creators who burned out fast were the ones who treated every post like a make-or-break moment. Treat it like a data point instead.
CTA (45-60s): What's the smallest bet you're making right now in your creative work? Tell me below.
