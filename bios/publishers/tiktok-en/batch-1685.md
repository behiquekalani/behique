# TikTok Batch 1685

## Script 1 — Solve Real Problems
**Hook (0-3s):** Nobody cares about your product. They care about their problem.
**Body (3-45s):** This is the single most common mistake new builders make. They fall in love with their solution before they understand the problem. Then they build something technically impressive that nobody buys. The customer doesn't want your features. They want relief from pain. They want to feel smarter, faster, safer, or free. Your job is to understand their problem so deeply that your solution feels inevitable. Stop selling what you built. Start solving what they feel.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for builder mindset content. Comment the problem you're currently solving.

## Script 2 — The Morning Ritual
**Hook (0-3s):** Win the first hour and you've already won the day.
**Body (3-45s):** The first thing most people do in the morning is check their phone. That immediately puts them in reactive mode. Responding to other people's priorities. Absorbing other people's news. That state follows them all day. Instead: no phone for the first hour. Move your body. Write or think on paper. Set your three most important tasks. Now you're in charge of your day instead of just reacting to it. This single change produces more output than most productivity systems combined.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for daily upgrades. Comment what your first hour looks like right now.

## Script 3 — Long-Term Thinking Is Rare
**Hook (0-3s):** If you can think five years ahead, you have almost no competition.
**Body (3-45s):** Most people think in weeks. They make decisions based on how they feel today. They optimize for immediate reward. Businesses, careers, and relationships built on short-term thinking collapse. The people who win long-term are willing to make decisions that look wrong in the short term. Delay gratification. Build the foundation. Stay the course when it's uncomfortable. Long-term thinking is a competitive advantage most people voluntarily give up. Use it.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for long-game thinking. What decision are you making now that your future self will thank you for?
