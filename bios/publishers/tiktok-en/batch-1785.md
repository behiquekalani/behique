# TikTok Batch 1785

**Script 1: Creator Burnout Is a Systems Problem**
Hook (0-3s): Creator burnout isn't a mindset problem. It's a systems problem.
Body (3-45s): If creating feels exhausting, the problem usually isn't your passion. It's your process. You're creating from scratch every time. No templates. No batching. No repurposing. You're reinventing the wheel for every post. Build a content system instead. One core idea becomes five pieces of content. One long-form piece becomes a week of short-form posts. Your workflow should feel boring and efficient, not chaotic and draining. Systematize the production. Save the energy for the ideas.
CTA (45-60s): What's one thing in your content workflow you've systematized? Drop it below.

**Script 2: Platform Risk Is Real**
Hook (0-3s): You don't own your TikTok audience. You're renting it.
Body (3-45s): Every follower you have on a platform you don't control is a follower you could lose overnight. TikTok bans. Algorithm shifts. Account suspensions. Platform shutdowns. They've all happened before and will happen again. The creators who are truly stable aren't the ones with the most followers. They're the ones with an email list. With a newsletter. With owned distribution. Build on social. But plant your flag somewhere you control. Email is boring. It's also irreplaceable.
CTA (45-60s): Do you have an email list? Yes or no. If yes, how big?

**Script 3: Minimum Viable Habits**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need a 5am routine. You need one habit that makes you feel like yourself.
Body (3-45s): The productivity content industrial complex has convinced you that success requires perfect mornings, 4 journals, and 90 minutes of deep work before anyone else wakes up. None of that is required. What's required: one minimum viable habit. One thing you do every day that keeps you moving. For creators it might be writing one sentence. Recording one idea. Reading one thing in your niche. Make it so small it's embarrassing to skip. Then let it grow naturally.
CTA (45-60s): What's your one minimum viable habit? The tiny thing you do no matter what?
