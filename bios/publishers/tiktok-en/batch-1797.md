# TikTok Batch 1797

**Script 1: The Minimum Viable Product for Creators**
Hook (0-3s): Your first digital product doesn't need to be good. It needs to exist.
Body (3-45s): The MVP for creators is embarrassingly simple. A 10-page PDF that solves one specific problem. A 30-minute workshop on one skill. A template pack for one workflow. Not a 12-module course. Not a 200-page guide. One problem. One solution. Done. You'll learn more from selling your first ugly product than from planning your perfect one for another six months. The market will tell you what to improve. Your imagined customer won't.
CTA (45-60s): What's the smallest possible product you could ship in the next seven days? Name it.

**Script 2: The Trust Deficit**
Hook (0-3s): Most creators don't have a content problem. They have a trust problem.
Body (3-45s): You can post great content and still not sell anything. Because content attracts. Trust converts. Trust is built over time through consistency. Through being right. Through admitting when you're wrong. Through showing your process, not just your results. Through keeping promises to your audience, even small ones. If people aren't buying, look at the trust signals before the product. Is your face visible? Do people know your story? Have you been consistent for long enough to have a track record? Fix the trust first.
CTA (45-60s): What's one thing you do consistently that builds trust with your audience?

**Script 3: Short-Form Is Discovery. Long-Form Is Depth.**
Hook (0-3s): Short-form gets people in the door. Long-form makes them stay.
Body (3-45s): TikTok and Shorts are for discovery. People find you there. But they stay for the long-form. The podcast. The newsletter. The YouTube video that goes deep. If you're only doing short-form, you're constantly finding new people but never converting them into actual community. If you're only doing long-form, you're not being discovered. The combination is what builds a real business. Short-form drives traffic. Long-form builds depth. Both together build something lasting.
CTA (45-60s): Are you a short-form creator, long-form, or both? What's your main platform?
