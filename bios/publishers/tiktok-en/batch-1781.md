# TikTok Batch 1781

**Script 1: You Are Not Behind**
Hook (0-3s): Stop telling yourself you're behind. You're not.
Body (3-45s): There's no schedule you're supposed to be on. The person you're comparing yourself to started earlier, sure. But they also had different circumstances, different information, different everything. The only race that matters is the one you're running. And the only question worth asking: did you move today? One post. One page. One product. One conversation. That's it. Compound interest doesn't care if you started late. It cares if you stop.
CTA (45-60s): Drop a number in the comments. Days since you started your creative business. Let's see some real timelines.

**Script 2: The Boring Middle Is Real**
Hook (0-3s): Nobody talks about months 2 through 18. That's the part that kills most creators.
Body (3-45s): Month one you're fired up. You post every day, you're building, you feel it. Then month two hits. The views are flat. Nobody's buying. Your friends don't get it. You start to wonder if it's working. This is the boring middle. It's not a sign to quit. It's a sign you're doing it right. Every creator you follow went through this exact phase. They just didn't post about it. The ones who made it through didn't have more talent. They just refused to interpret silence as failure.
CTA (45-60s): If you're in the boring middle right now, type "boring middle" below. You're not alone.

**Script 3: Attention Is the New Currency**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need money to start. You need attention.
Body (3-45s): Money follows attention. Always has. A creator with 5,000 engaged followers can outperform a brand with a $50,000 ad budget. Why? Because trust is built through consistency and honesty, not through reach. Every video you post is a deposit in an attention account. You don't see the interest right away. But it compounds. The creators making real income today are the ones who treated attention like an asset three years ago. Start treating it that way now.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for more on building audience before product. That's the sequence that actually works.
