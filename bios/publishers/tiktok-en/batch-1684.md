# TikTok Batch 1684

## Script 1 — Wealth Is a Mindset First
**Hook (0-3s):** You will never build wealth with a scarcity mind.
**Body (3-45s):** Scarcity thinking says: there's not enough, protect what you have, play small, don't risk it. Abundance thinking says: value can be created, more is possible, invest in yourself, take the shot. Most people grew up with scarcity programming. School, news, family conversations, all of it trained you to be cautious. That conditioning is the ceiling. Not your income. Not your connections. Not your opportunities. The work is internal before it's external. Change the operating system.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for mindset work that actually leads somewhere. Comment one scarcity belief you're actively unlearning.

## Script 2 — Consistency Is a Skill
**Hook (0-3s):** Consistency isn't a character trait. It's a skill you train.
**Body (3-45s):** People treat consistency like it's something you either have or don't. That belief is the problem. Consistency is built through reps. Small commitments kept. Promises to yourself honored. Over time, your brain learns that you follow through. The trust you build with yourself compounds exactly like compound interest. Miss a day, fine. Come back. The identity of "someone who shows up" is built through the pattern, not through perfection. Start small. Stay long.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for the long game. What's one commitment you've kept for 30 days or more? Comment it.

## Script 3 — Reading as a Superpower
**Hook (0-3s):** One book a month puts you in the top 1% of people on earth for knowledge growth.
**Body (3-45s):** The average adult reads zero books per year after school. Zero. If you read one book a month, you'll read 12 a year. Over ten years, that's 120 books. Each one is a compressed lifetime of someone's best thinking. You get access to frameworks, mistakes, and insights that took them decades to build. For the price of a meal and a few hours. Reading is the highest ROI activity available to any human being. Most people just won't do it.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for book-driven insights. Comment the last book that actually changed how you think.
