# TikTok Batch 1815

**Script 1: Systems Replace Willpower**
Hook (0-3s): Willpower is the worst system for consistent output.
Body (3-45s): Willpower depletes. It is the first thing that runs out when life gets hard. A system does not deplete. You sit down at 8am on Tuesday and the system runs. You open the template. You follow the process. You publish. No motivation required. The creators with the most consistent output are not the most disciplined people. They have the simplest systems. A publishing calendar. A content template. A writing ritual. Remove the decision from the process and the process survives without willpower.
CTA (45-60s): Tell me your current system for creating in the comments. Or your lack of one.

**Script 2: Solve Pain, Not Interest**
Hook (0-3s): Products about passion get interest. Products about pain get bought.
Body (3-45s): Someone interested in productivity will watch your video. Someone who is drowning in missed deadlines will buy your system. Interest is casual. Pain is urgent. The closer your product is to solving acute pain, the less marketing you need. People who are hurting buy fast. People who are curious browse, save, and never return. Find the pain. Name it exactly. Show the transformation. Price it at what relief is worth, not what an hour of your time costs.
CTA (45-60s): Follow. I cover offer design and pain-based positioning every week.

**Script 3: The Audience Before the Product**
Hook (0-3s): Build the audience before you build the product. Not the other way.
Body (3-45s): Most people spend months building a product and then go looking for buyers. Backwards. Spend 90 days creating content and building an email list first. By the time you launch, you know exactly what your audience needs because they have told you in the comments, in the replies, in the DMs. Your product answers the questions your audience already asked. That is not a launch. That is delivery. And delivery converts at a different rate than guessing.
CTA (45-60s): Are you building the audience first or the product first? Where are you?
