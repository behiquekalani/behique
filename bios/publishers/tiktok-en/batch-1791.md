# TikTok Batch 1791

**Script 1: The Problem with "Finding Your Passion"**
Hook (0-3s): Don't find your passion. Find a problem you care about solving.
Body (3-45s): Passion is vague. Problems are specific. "I'm passionate about fitness" won't build a business. "I help remote workers build consistent workout habits without a gym" will. The more specific the problem, the more direct the solution, the more obvious the business. Your passion might be the fuel. But the problem is the engine. Ask yourself: what problem do I have a unique perspective on? What problem have I solved that others are still stuck on? Build there.
CTA (45-60s): What specific problem do you help solve? Say it in one sentence in the comments.

**Script 2: Content Repurposing Is Not Lazy**
Hook (0-3s): Posting the same idea in five different formats is not lazy. It's smart.
Body (3-45s): Your audience doesn't all consume content the same way. Some read threads. Some watch short videos. Some prefer long-form. Some are on LinkedIn, some on TikTok, some open newsletters. One idea, formatted five ways, reaches five different people. This isn't repetition. It's reach. The creators doing this well are not creating five times as much. They're creating once and distributing intelligently. The work is in the original idea. The formats are just packaging.
CTA (45-60s): What platform do you repurpose content to most? And which one do you ignore but probably shouldn't?

**Script 3: The Difference Between Busy and Productive**
Hook (0-3s): Most creator "busy" is procrastination with better aesthetics.
Body (3-45s): Reorganizing your content calendar is not content creation. Redesigning your logo for the third time is not branding. Recording a video, editing it for four hours, and deciding not to post it is not shipping. Busy feels like progress. Productive IS progress. The metric is simple: did you publish something today? Did someone see your work? Did you move a product closer to done? If not, you were busy. And busy is the enemy of the creator you're trying to become.
CTA (45-60s): What's one "busy" task you keep doing that isn't actually moving anything forward?
