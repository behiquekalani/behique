# TikTok Batch 1728

## Script 1 — The Internet Gives You Access, Not Results
**Hook (0-3s):** You have access to the same information as every billionaire. Why haven't you built what they built?
**Body (3-45s):** Access is the least valuable part of the equation. Execution is the variable that separates outcomes. The problem isn't information. It's decision-making under uncertainty, the willingness to act without knowing the outcome, and the persistence to stay after the first failure. Everyone has access. Almost nobody executes consistently. That gap is where your opportunity lives. Stop looking for more information. Start doing something with what you already know. You have enough to start.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. Less theory. More building. Every day.

## Script 2 — How to Price Yourself Correctly
**Hook (0-3s):** If nobody's told you your price is too high, it's too low.
**Body (3-45s):** Low prices attract the worst clients and signal low value. High prices attract serious clients and create expectation of serious results. Price is positioning, not math. When you charge more, you deliver more. Not because the work changed. Because you take it more seriously and so does the client. The fear around raising prices is about self-worth, not market reality. Test a higher price. See who says yes. The people who say yes without negotiating are your real clients. Build for them.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this. Your pricing mindset is the ceiling on your business.

## Script 3 — Why Creators Burn Out
**Hook (0-3s):** Most creators don't burn out from working too hard. They burn out from creating without a purpose.
**Body (3-45s):** If you're creating to get followers, you'll burn out when growth slows. If you're creating to make money, you'll burn out when the money isn't fast enough. But if you're creating because you have something real to say, burnout becomes much harder to reach. The fuel is the meaning. Every session of building, every post, every product needs to connect back to why you started. If you've lost the why, that's the real problem. Find it again before you fix the content strategy.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I build from purpose, not metrics. Come see what that looks like.
