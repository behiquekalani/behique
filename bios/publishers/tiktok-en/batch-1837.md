# TikTok Batch 1837

**Script 1: The Value Stack Every Digital Product Needs**
Hook (0-3s): Your digital product needs more than good content. It needs a value stack.
Body (3-45s): A value stack is everything the buyer gets plus the way it's packaged. The core product plus the bonus resources. The main guide plus the templates. The course plus the community access or the quick-start checklist. The stack doesn't have to be huge. But it needs to make the buyer feel like they got more than they paid for. That feeling is what creates word-of-mouth. That's what generates the unsolicited review or the share in a group. Over-deliver on value and the marketing takes care of itself.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the value stack template I use when building every new product.

**Script 2: Authenticity Is Not a Strategy. It's a Filter.**
Hook (0-3s): Authentic content doesn't attract everyone. That's why it works.
Body (3-45s): When you share your real opinions, your real process, your real personality, you repel the people who aren't your people. That's not a problem. That's the feature. The audience built around your authentic voice is the most valuable audience you can have. They're there because of who you actually are. Not a persona. Not a character. You. That alignment means they trust you more, engage more, and buy more. The diluted version of yourself tries to appeal to everyone and ends up resonating with no one. Be the real version. The right people find you.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see how I run this whole brand as myself, not a character.

**Script 3: The Problem with One-Time Launches**
Hook (0-3s): Launch once and forget is not a product strategy. It's a lottery ticket.
Body (3-45s): The creator who launches a product once, gets some sales, and moves on is leaving most of their revenue on the table. The people who didn't buy during the launch didn't say no forever. They said not yet. Your job after the launch is to keep the product visible. Feature it in content. Reference it in the newsletter. Run a promo when it makes sense. The product that stays in front of the right audience keeps selling. Treat your product catalog like a store, not a series of events. The store is always open.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the evergreen product strategy that keeps sales coming in consistently.
