**Script 1: Pricing Psychology Nobody Taught You**
Hook (0-3s): $97 sells better than $100. Here's why that's not the full story.
Body (3-45s): Yes, charm pricing works. But the bigger insight is this: price signals quality. If your digital product is $7, people assume it's worth $7. When you price at $97 or $147, people show up ready to use it. They take it seriously. Low prices don't create loyal customers. They attract browsers. Price your work based on the transformation it delivers, not the hours you spent making it. A guide that saves someone 40 hours is worth more than your hourly rate times the time it took to write.

**Script 2: AI Tools Don't Replace You. They Amplify You.**
Hook (0-3s): The creators who win with AI aren't using it to replace their voice.
Body (3-45s): They're using it to move faster while keeping the voice human. AI is a production tool, not a thinking tool. You still need the specific knowledge. The lived experience. The perspective that makes someone stop scrolling. What AI removes is the friction between the idea and the output. You spend less time formatting, editing, repurposing. More time on the actual work that only you can do. The creators who ignore AI fall behind on speed. The ones who outsource their thinking to AI lose the one thing that made them worth following.

**Script 3: Distribution Beats Production Every Time**
Hook (0-3s): You don't have a content problem. You have a distribution problem.
Body (3-45s): Most creators spend 80% of their time making content and 20% getting it seen. That ratio should be flipped. One great piece of content shared in five places beats five pieces of content nobody finds. Your blog post becomes a Twitter thread, a TikTok script, an email, a LinkedIn post. The work is done once. The distribution is ongoing. The creators who grow fastest aren't making more content. They're putting the same content in front of more eyes, in more formats, on more platforms.
