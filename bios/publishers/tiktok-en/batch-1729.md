# TikTok Batch 1729

## Script 1 — The Trap of Infinite Learning
**Hook (0-3s):** You've been learning for years. When does the building start?
**Body (3-45s):** Learning is valuable until it becomes a hiding place. At some point, "I need to learn more before I start" becomes avoidance in disguise. The real education happens in the building. You learn more from shipping one product than from 50 hours of courses about how to ship products. Set a deadline. Pick a thing. Build it imperfectly. Ship it. The feedback you get from a real person using your real product is worth more than any course on the internet.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I build in public. You'll see the mistakes and the wins in real time.

## Script 2 — Social Media as a Business Tool
**Hook (0-3s):** Social media isn't entertainment. For builders, it's infrastructure.
**Body (3-45s):** Every post is a distribution channel. Every follower is a potential customer. Every piece of content is a long-term asset. Treating it as entertainment is fine if you want entertainment. Treating it as business infrastructure changes how you plan, create, and measure. The creators who win financially are the ones who treat every post like a product. Clear message. Clear audience. Clear value. Stop posting what feels good. Start posting what serves your future customer.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this. Your feed is either an asset or a distraction. You decide.

## Script 3 — The Art of Saying No
**Hook (0-3s):** Every yes you give to the wrong thing is a no to the right one.
**Body (3-45s):** High performers aren't productive because they do more. They're productive because they protect their time aggressively. Every new commitment costs focus. Focus is finite. The discipline to say no to good opportunities in service of great ones is what separates the busy from the effective. Build a short list of what you're actually optimizing for this year. Use it to filter every request. If the request doesn't serve the list, the answer is no. Simple, uncomfortable, and necessary.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. This page is about building intentional, not just busy.
