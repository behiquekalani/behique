**Script 1: The Attention Economy Has a Flaw You Can Exploit**
Hook (0-3s): Everyone is chasing attention. Almost nobody is converting it.
Body (3-45s): Attention is not a business. Attention that leads to a relationship is a business. Most creators are so focused on views and followers that they never build the thing that turns attention into income. Here's the gap: when someone finds you valuable enough to follow, give them somewhere to go deeper. A free resource. An email list. A low-cost product. Each step deepens the relationship and earns more trust. The creators making real money aren't the ones with the most attention. They're the ones who built the best path from attention to trust to income.

**Script 2: Why Small Audiences Buy More**
Hook (0-3s): 500 true fans will buy more than 5,000 casual followers.
Body (3-45s): Kevin Kelly said you need 1,000 true fans to make a living. That was in 2008. With digital products and global reach, the number is probably closer to 500. A true fan buys everything you make. They share your work. They send you the message that keeps you going at 11pm when you want to quit. You don't need to go viral to build a real business. You need to go deep with a small group of people who genuinely care about what you're building. Small and specific beats big and general every time.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the strategy I use to find and serve my true fans.

**Script 3: What Nobody Tells You About Building in Public**
Hook (0-3s): Building in public will make you uncomfortable. That's the point.
Body (3-45s): When you commit to sharing the real numbers, the real failures, and the real questions you're sitting with, something shifts. You stop performing. You start connecting. The people who resonate with your honesty become your best audience. Not because they feel sorry for you. Because they see themselves in you. Building in public is not a strategy for attention. It's a strategy for alignment. The people who find you through your honest process are already pre-qualified to be customers, collaborators, and community members.
