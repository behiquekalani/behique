# TikTok Batch 1683

## Script 1 — The Attention Economy
**Hook (0-3s):** Every app on your phone was engineered to steal your future.
**Body (3-45s):** This is not metaphor. There are teams of engineers whose only job is to make you spend one more minute scrolling. They study behavioral psychology. They run A/B tests. They optimize for dopamine. And they're very good at it. The average person gives 7 hours a day to screens. That's 2,555 hours a year. Over a decade, that's over 70,000 hours. More than enough to become world-class at anything. Your attention is the battleground. Fight for it.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow if you're reclaiming your focus. Comment what you'd do with 7 more hours a day.

## Script 2 — Be Useful First
**Hook (0-3s):** Stop trying to be interesting. Start being useful.
**Body (3-45s):** Interesting gets attention once. Useful keeps people coming back. The creators and builders who last long-term aren't the flashiest. They're the ones who solve a real problem, answer a real question, or help someone move forward. Interesting is entertainment. Useful is service. And service compounds. Every piece of content that actually helps someone is an asset that works for you indefinitely. Build useful things. Write useful words. Be the person people come back to.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for content that's actually useful. What's one thing you've learned here that helped you? Comment it.

## Script 3 — The Deep Work Window
**Hook (0-3s):** Two focused hours beats eight distracted ones every single time.
**Body (3-45s):** Most people's workday is a series of interruptions. Email, Slack, notifications, context switching. They stay busy for eight hours and produce maybe ninety minutes of actual output. Deep work is different. Close everything. Set a timer. One task. Full focus. The quality of output in a two-hour deep work block will consistently outperform a full day of scattered effort. It's not about working more. It's about working in a state where your brain can actually build something.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for focus principles. Comment your best deep work start time.
