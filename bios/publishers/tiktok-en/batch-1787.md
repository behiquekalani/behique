# TikTok Batch 1787

**Script 1: The Cost of Waiting Until Ready**
Hook (0-3s): Waiting until you're ready is costing you more than shipping imperfect work.
Body (3-45s): There's a version of you that's been "almost ready to launch" for six months. Perfect logo, waiting. Website needs tweaking, waiting. Not enough followers yet, waiting. Here's the problem: the market doesn't care about your readiness. It cares about showing up. The creator who shipped something imperfect three months ago already has data, feedback, and revenue. You're still polishing. Ship the thing. The feedback you get from real customers is worth more than any pre-launch preparation.
CTA (45-60s): What have you been almost ready to launch? Name it. Publicly. That's the first step.

**Script 2: How AI Changes the Creator Math**
Hook (0-3s): A creator with AI now does what used to take a team.
Body (3-45s): Think about what used to require multiple people: writing, editing, research, design direction, repurposing content across platforms. Now one person with the right AI workflow handles all of it. This doesn't mean your content becomes generic. It means you spend your time on the parts only you can do. Your ideas. Your voice. Your audience relationships. AI handles the scaffolding. You provide the substance. The solo creator who understands this is the most powerful entity in content right now.
CTA (45-60s): What part of your creator workflow have you offloaded to AI? Tell me below.

**Script 3: The Pricing Mindset Shift**
Hook (0-3s): You're not charging what you're worth. You're charging what you think people will pay.
Body (3-45s): Most creators underprice because they're afraid of rejection. "What if nobody buys at $97?" But here's what they don't ask: "What if 10 people buy at $97 instead of 50 buying at $19?" The math changes. And so does the customer quality. People who pay more take the work more seriously. They implement. They get results. They leave better testimonials. They refer others. Your price is not just a number. It's a filter for who you work with and how seriously they take your work.
CTA (45-60s): Have you raised your prices in the last six months? What happened? Tell me.
