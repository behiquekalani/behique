**Script 1: Specific Knowledge Is Your Moat**
Hook (0-3s): Nobody can copy your exact combination of skills and experience.
Body (3-45s): That's specific knowledge. It's not a degree. It's not a certification. It's the thing you know from years of doing something specific that nobody else has lived the same way. Most people undervalue it because it came naturally to them. If something feels obvious to you but confusing to others, that gap is a business. Write about that. Teach that. Build products around that. The market pays for clarity on things people are confused about. You already have the clarity. You just haven't packaged it yet.
CTA (45-60s): Follow if you're figuring out what your specific knowledge actually is.

**Script 2: Building in Public Is Not Oversharing**
Hook (0-3s): Building in public doesn't mean posting your bank account every week.
Body (3-45s): It means being honest about the process. The real wins and the actual setbacks. People follow you for the journey, not just the destination. When you share a real mistake you made, that post will outperform any polished tip you give. Authenticity creates trust. Trust creates buyers. The audience that watches you figure things out will be more loyal than the audience that only sees your highlight reel. The key is sharing with intention. Not trauma dumping. Not fake vulnerability. Real moments that have a lesson attached.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see how I build this business in real time.

**Script 3: Long Game Thinking Changes Everything**
Hook (0-3s): The average overnight success took seven years.
Body (3-45s): Long game thinking is not patience. It's a different way of making decisions. Short game thinking asks: what gets me results this week? Long game thinking asks: what compounding over three years looks like today? Email list grows by 10 people this week. That's nothing. But 10 per week for three years is 1,500 people who chose to hear from you. Every long game player looked like they were going nowhere for a long time. Then the compounding kicked in. The work you do today is not for today. It's for the version of you two years from now.
CTA (45-60s): Follow if you're playing the long game and need someone in your corner.
