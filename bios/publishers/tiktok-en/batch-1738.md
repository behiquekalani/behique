# TikTok Batch 1738

## Script 1 — The Market Doesn't Care About Your Effort
**Hook (0-3s):** Nobody buys hard work. They buy outcomes. Know the difference.
**Body (3-45s):** You can work 80 hours a week on something nobody wants and make nothing. You can work 10 hours on something the market desperately needs and build a business. Effort is necessary. It is not sufficient. The market rewards value delivery, not hours logged. The builder who spends time understanding what the market needs before building is more efficient than the one who builds first and hopes. Research before you build. Validate before you scale. Let the market guide the effort.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. Market-first thinking. Every post.

## Script 2 — Your Network Is Your Ceiling
**Hook (0-3s):** Show me your five closest people and I'll show you your trajectory.
**Body (3-45s):** This isn't about cutting off old friends. It's about adding new ones. The people around you set the standard for what's normal. If nobody in your circle is building a business, building feels abnormal. If everyone in your circle is building, it becomes the baseline. You don't rise above your environment without a fight. The easiest upgrade is to find communities where the thing you want is already ordinary. Online or in person. Proximity to the right people accelerates growth faster than almost any skill or strategy.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this. Your environment is your ceiling or your floor. You choose.

## Script 3 — Ship Before Perfect
**Hook (0-3s):** Every day you wait for perfect is a day your competitor gets feedback from real users.
**Body (3-45s):** Perfection is a feedback-avoidance mechanism. You can't be rejected by users if they never see the product. Shipping forces you into the real world where real feedback lives. That feedback is brutal and useful and exactly what you need to build something people actually want. The first version of every great product was embarrassingly bad. The founders shipped it anyway. They used the feedback to build something better. You cannot iterate on something that hasn't shipped. Ship it. Fix it. Ship again.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I ship in public and improve in public. Come watch.
