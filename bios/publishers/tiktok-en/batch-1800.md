# TikTok Batch 1800

**Script 1: The Courage to Publish**
Hook (0-3s): The content graveyard is full of posts that were almost finished.
Body (3-45s): Ideas that got researched but never written. Videos that got recorded but never edited. Posts that got written but never scheduled. The creative process has a final hurdle that most people underestimate: the courage to actually publish. To let it be seen. To accept that someone might criticize it. That it might flop. That it might not be perfect. None of that matters as much as the practice of shipping. Every published piece makes the next one easier. Every draft in your camera roll is a debt you owe yourself.
CTA (45-60s): What's sitting in your drafts right now? Name it. Then go post it.

**Script 2: The Power of a Specific Audience**
Hook (0-3s): A creator who speaks to one person speaks to millions. A creator who speaks to everyone speaks to no one.
Body (3-45s): Write for one reader. Film for one viewer. Build for one person with one specific problem in one specific situation. When your content is that targeted, the people it's made for feel it immediately. They feel seen. They share it with people exactly like them. The specificity acts as a filter that actually increases reach. Because people share content that says what they couldn't articulate themselves. And that kind of content is always specific, never generic. Write to your most specific reader and watch what happens.
CTA (45-60s): Describe your ideal viewer in one sentence. Not demographics. Who are they and what do they want?

**Script 3: Start Small, Stay Real**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need a big idea to start. You need an honest one.
Body (3-45s): The biggest ideas in content are the smallest truths. The thing you figured out that felt obvious but wasn't. The mistake you made that most people make too. The shift in perspective that changed everything for you. You don't need a revolutionary framework. You need to say something true in a way that lands. Honesty is the original content strategy. Before niches, before algorithms, before formats. People follow creators they believe. Start by saying what you actually think. The audience finds you from there.
CTA (45-60s): What's one honest thing about your creator journey you haven't said publicly yet? Say it now.
