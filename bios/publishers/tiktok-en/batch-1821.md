# TikTok Batch 1821

**Script 1: You Don't Need an Audience to Start**
Hook (0-3s): You don't need 10,000 followers to make money online.
Body (3-45s): Here's what actually matters. One specific person. One clear problem. One product that solves it. Most creators wait until they have an audience to build something. That's backwards. Build the product first. Then the audience has a reason to follow you. The one-person business model works because you're not trying to be everything to everyone. You pick a niche, get specific, and sell something real. The follower count follows the clarity.
CTA (45-60s): If you're building a one-person business, follow for daily breakdowns on how it actually works.

**Script 2: The Email List Is the Only Asset You Own**
Hook (0-3s): Every platform can ban you tomorrow. One thing can't be taken away.
Body (3-45s): Your email list. Instagram can change its algorithm. TikTok can get banned. Twitter can throttle your reach. None of that touches your email list. It's the only distribution channel you fully own. Even 500 engaged email subscribers will outperform 50,000 passive followers on any platform. Start building it today. Not when you're big enough. Not when you have something good enough. Today. The tool doesn't matter. A free plan on any email platform works fine. What matters is the habit of collecting and writing.
CTA (45-60s): I'm sharing how I grew mine from zero. Follow so you don't miss it.

**Script 3: The Boring Middle Is Where Businesses Are Built**
Hook (0-3s): Nobody talks about month four. Month four is where most people quit.
Body (3-45s): Month one feels exciting. You're posting, building, learning. Month two you see some traction. Month three you expect results. Month four, nothing has blown up yet. That's where the real work starts. The boring middle is not failure. It's the phase every successful creator had to survive. The ones who make it aren't more talented. They just kept showing up when it stopped feeling exciting. Consistency in the boring middle is the skill nobody teaches. But it's the only one that actually pays off.
CTA (45-60s): Follow if you're in the boring middle and need someone who gets it.
