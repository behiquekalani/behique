# TikTok Batch 1832

**Script 1: The Only Business That Scales Without Burning Out**
Hook (0-3s): The businesses that scale without burning the owner out have one thing in common.
Body (3-45s): They separate income from time. A service business trades your hours for money. It caps out when you run out of hours. A digital product business makes money from what you've already built. The more you build, the more you can earn without adding more time. This is not about working less. It's about building once and earning repeatedly. The one-person business that scales is one where the builder has shifted from being the product to owning the products. That shift takes time but it compounds in ways that are hard to overstate.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the roadmap from time-for-money to product-for-money.

**Script 2: Niche Down Until It Feels Too Narrow**
Hook (0-3s): The niche that feels too narrow is usually exactly right.
Body (3-45s): "Content for creators" is too broad. "Content for freelance graphic designers who want to land premium clients" is a niche. The more specific you get, the more your ideal reader feels like you wrote this for them specifically. That feeling converts. General content attracts everyone and sells to no one. Specific content attracts fewer people and sells to most of them. The irony of niching down is that your potential reach doesn't shrink. It just becomes more relevant. And relevant beats broad every single time.
CTA (45-60s): Follow if you're working through what your niche actually is.

**Script 3: Stop Optimizing for Likes**
Hook (0-3s): Likes don't pay bills. Buyers do.
Body (3-45s): The most liked post you ever make might never sell a single product. The least liked post you ever make might bring in ten buyers. The metrics that matter for a business are different from the metrics platforms use to make you feel engaged. Track email signups. Track product sales. Track how many people clicked through to your landing page. Those are business metrics. Likes and views are vanity metrics. They feel good but they don't tell you whether your content is actually working for your business. Build a dashboard for what matters.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see the metrics I actually track to grow this business.
