## Script 1 — The Minimum Viable Habit
**Hook (0-3s):** The goal isn't a perfect habit. It's a habit you never break.
**Body (3-45s):** Perfect habits fail because life is imperfect. The writer who commits to 2000 words misses a day and stops entirely. The one who commits to one paragraph never breaks the chain. Start with the minimum viable version of every habit you want to build. So small it's almost embarrassing. The point isn't the output. The point is the identity. Every day you do the thing, you vote for the identity. That vote eventually becomes who you are. Then you can increase the output. Start small. Stay consistent. Scale up.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this. Minimum viable habit. Maximum long-term impact.

## Script 2 — What Most Creators Get Wrong About Monetization
**Hook (0-3s):** Most creators wait too long to monetize. Then rush it. Both mistakes kill the business.
**Body (3-45s):** Waiting too long means you miss the compounding of early revenue. Rushing it before you've built trust means you sell to people who don't know you yet. The sweet spot: start selling when you have 100 to 200 people who actively engage with your content. That's a small enough group to talk to directly. Large enough to validate an offer. The first product doesn't need to be your best. It needs to be real. Build from the real feedback you get from real buyers. Iterate from there.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I'm building the monetization phase in public right now.

## Script 3 — Resilience Is a Skill, Not a Trait
**Hook (0-3s):** Some people don't bounce back faster because they're tougher. They've just failed more times.
**Body (3-45s):** Resilience is trained. Every time you fail and recover, you build a reference point for the next failure. The first time something goes wrong feels like the end. The tenth time feels like a bump. The people you see handling chaos with grace aren't calm by nature. They've just been through enough that they know chaos doesn't last. You can't skip the failures. But you can log them. Every difficult thing you get through is data. Evidence that you recover. Stack enough evidence and you become someone who doesn't fear failure anymore.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I document the failures along with the wins. Both matter.
