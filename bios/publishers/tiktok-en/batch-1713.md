# TikTok Batch 1713

## Script 1 — The Ownership Era
**Hook (0-3s):** The internet made it possible to own your income. Most people still don't.
**Body (3-45s):** Every hour you trade for money is an hour you can only sell once. That's the employment trap. Assets scale. A digital product can sell a hundred times while you sleep. A newsletter reaches ten thousand people at no extra cost after you write it once. Content compounds for years after you post it. The shift from trading time for money to building assets that generate money is the most important financial decision of our era. You don't need a company to do this. You need internet access and something to say.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's one asset you could start building this week? Think digital, think scalable.

## Script 2 — Authenticity as Strategy
**Hook (0-3s):** Being yourself online is not just a value. It's a growth strategy.
**Body (3-45s):** The internet has unlimited generic content. It has a shortage of specific, real, opinionated voices. When you share your actual perspective, including the parts that are incomplete and uncertain, you attract people who genuinely connect with how you think. Those people become your most loyal audience, customers, and community. Trying to be universally liked is a recipe for being universally forgettable. The more specific and real you are, the smaller the crowd but the deeper the loyalty. Depth beats breadth.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's one opinion you have that's slightly controversial in your space? Share it below.

## Script 3 — The Problem with Multitasking
**Hook (0-3s):** Multitasking is not a skill. It's a cognitive tax.
**Body (3-45s):** Every time you switch tasks, your brain pays a switching cost. You lose context. You have to rebuild mental models. Research shows multitaskers actually perform worse on individual tasks than single-taskers. They're also less able to filter out irrelevant information. The illusion of multitasking efficiency comes from the busyness it creates. Busy feels productive. But deep, single-threaded focus produces results that scattered attention never will. Do one thing until it's done. Then do the next thing. Linear is faster than parallel.
**CTA (45-60s):** How many tabs do you have open right now? That number is your problem size.
