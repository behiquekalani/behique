## Script 1

**Hook:** Charging more is often the kindest thing you can do for your customer.

**Body:** When something is cheap, people don't take it seriously. They buy it and never use it. They don't implement it. They don't get the result. When something costs real money, people show up for it. A higher price can be the thing that actually gets your customer across the finish line. Price for commitment, not just conversion.

**CTA:** Has a low price ever made you take something less seriously? Comment below.

## Script 2

**Hook:** Nobody remembers the creator who played it safe.

**Body:** The most followed people online took a clear stance. They said something that some people disagreed with. They built something that could fail. They put a version of themselves online that was real enough to be criticized. Playing it safe makes you forgettable. Being specific and honest makes you memorable.

**CTA:** Follow for content that says something, not just fills a slot.

## Script 3

**Hook:** The solo business path rewards consistency more than talent.

**Body:** Talented people quit all the time. Consistent people build audiences, products, and income. Talent without consistency is potential. Consistency without talent is still a business. If you show up and ship, you will outlast most of the people who started with more than you.

**CTA:** Share this with someone talented who keeps stopping and starting.
