# TikTok Batch 1813

**Script 1: The Right Buyer Needs No Convincing**
Hook (0-3s): If you have to convince someone hard, they are the wrong buyer.
Body (3-45s): The right customer reads your sales page and feels seen. They think you wrote it for them. They buy before they finish reading because the problem is so specific and the solution is so clear that hesitation feels unnecessary. The wrong customer needs to be persuaded, reassured, and discounted into buying. Then they ask for refunds. Your marketing job is not persuasion. It is recognition. Make the right person feel found. The wrong person will self-select out.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for more on writing that attracts the right buyer instead of everyone.

**Script 2: Volume Creates Luck**
Hook (0-3s): The viral hit is not planned. The volume is.
Body (3-45s): Nobody knows which post will break out. What they do know is that zero posts produce zero breakouts. The creators who post every day for a year will have more luck than the creator who posts once a week and spends four days editing it. Not because luck is random. Because volume increases the number of attempts. More attempts, more swings, more chances for one to land. The luck surface area is proportional to output. Create more. Ship faster. Edit less.
CTA (45-60s): How many posts a week are you publishing right now? Drop the number.

**Script 3: Build for the 1000, Not the Million**
Hook (0-3s): You do not need a million followers to make a living.
Body (3-45s): One thousand true fans who buy everything you create is a business. Do the math. A thousand people paying $100 a year is $100,000. A thousand people paying $10 a month is $120,000. You do not need virality. You need loyalty. And loyalty comes from depth, not breadth. The creator with 50,000 disengaged followers and no offer makes less than the creator with 2,000 readers who open every email. Build for depth. The revenue follows.
CTA (45-60s): Follow. I talk about building depth before scale. Fewer followers, more income.
