# TikTok Batch 1688

## Script 1 — Build Assets Not Income
**Hook (0-3s):** A job pays you once. An asset pays you forever.
**Body (3-45s):** The difference between people who build wealth and people who stay broke isn't income, it's asset allocation. A job gives you money for time. An asset gives you money for value. Content, code, products, systems, ownership. These are assets. They work when you sleep. They compound over time. The goal isn't to earn more per hour. The goal is to build things that generate value independent of your hours. Start thinking like an owner, not an employee.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for asset-building frameworks. Comment the first asset you're building or have built.

## Script 2 — Ship Ugly First
**Hook (0-3s):** Done and ugly beats perfect and invisible every single time.
**Body (3-45s):** Perfectionism is fear wearing a productivity mask. You keep polishing because shipping feels dangerous. What if it's not good enough? What if people judge it? Here's the truth: the market doesn't reward perfection. It rewards speed and iteration. Ship version one. Get feedback. Improve. Ship version two. The people who are ahead of you got there by shipping ugly things fast and learning from real feedback. You can't optimize something that doesn't exist yet.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for execution principles. Comment something you've been polishing too long.

## Script 3 — The Journaling Edge
**Hook (0-3s):** Ten minutes of journaling will tell you more about yourself than a year of therapy.
**Body (3-45s):** That's not an exaggeration. When you write without editing, your brain reveals what's actually happening beneath the surface. The anxiety you've been ignoring. The desire you've been suppressing. The belief that's been running your decisions without your permission. Journaling is debugging your operating system. Most people live their entire lives reacting to programs they never consciously installed. Writing is how you find them and rewrite them. Ten minutes. Every morning. No excuses.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow for inner game tools. Comment your current journaling habit, or lack of one.
