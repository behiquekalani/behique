# TikTok Batch 1831

**Script 1: The Difference Between Content and Marketing**
Hook (0-3s): Most people think they're doing marketing. They're just making content.
Body (3-45s): Content without a destination is entertainment. Marketing is content with a purpose and a path. Every piece of content you make should lead somewhere. A free resource. An email signup. A product. A next step. This is not manipulation. It's service. If your content genuinely helps someone, giving them a way to go deeper is the right thing to do. Content that helps but offers no path forward is wasted potential. Build the path. Be clear about where it leads. The creator who does this will always outperform the one who just makes great content with nowhere to go.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the content-to-conversion path I use in my own business.

**Script 2: Why "I Don't Have Time" Is a Prioritization Lie**
Hook (0-3s): You have time. You have choices. And right now you're choosing other things.
Body (3-45s): That's not a criticism. It's just true. Everyone has 24 hours. The solo creators building businesses while working full-time jobs are not superhuman. They found the 30-minute window and protected it. They said no to something else. They stopped consuming content about building and started actually building. Even 30 minutes a day, applied consistently over a year, is more than 180 hours of focused work. What would you build with 180 hours? The time is there. The question is whether you're ready to protect it.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the system that helps you build in the windows you already have.

**Script 3: Teach the Method, Sell the Implementation**
Hook (0-3s): The most effective content strategy: give away the how, sell the done-for-you.
Body (3-45s): Teach everything you know for free. The framework, the method, the principles. A percentage of the people who consume your free content will want to implement it but won't want to do it themselves. They'll pay for the template, the system, the guide, or the service that does the heavy lifting. This is not giving away too much. It's building trust at scale. The creators who hoard their knowledge lose. The ones who share generously and then offer implementation support win every time.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see how I teach everything and still build a product business around it.
