# TikTok Batch 1795

**Script 1: The Identity Tax**
Hook (0-3s): Calling yourself a "creator" costs you nothing. Becoming one costs you comfort.
Body (3-45s): The label is free. The work is not. To become the creator you say you want to be, you'll have to post when you don't feel like it. You'll have to publish things that don't perform. You'll have to keep going when it looks like nothing is working. You'll have to say no to things that feel safe and yes to things that feel exposed. That's the identity tax. Not money. Comfort. The ones who pay it consistently are the ones who eventually look back and realize they became the thing they were working toward.
CTA (45-60s): What's one comfort you've had to sacrifice on your creator journey? Real answer.

**Script 2: The Attention Scarcity Myth**
Hook (0-3s): Attention is not scarce. Bad content is just easy to scroll past.
Body (3-45s): People will spend hours watching content. They'll binge entire series. They'll read 3,000 word posts in full. The attention is there. What's scarce is content good enough to earn it. The competition isn't other creators. It's low-quality content that's flooded every feed. The gap is quality, specificity, and trust. If your content genuinely helps someone, they will stop scrolling. Every time. The excuse that "nobody has attention anymore" is just a way of avoiding the harder truth: your content isn't yet good enough. Make it better.
CTA (45-60s): What's the last piece of content that made you stop scrolling? What was it?

**Script 3: The Long Game Advantage**
Hook (0-3s): The best business strategy is the one most people are too impatient to execute.
Body (3-45s): Show up consistently for two years in any niche and you will be ahead of 95% of people who started at the same time as you. Not because you're more talented. Because most of them quit. The long game is not complicated. Post consistently. Build genuine value. Talk to your audience. Iterate on feedback. But it requires patience most people refuse to develop. Every time you post while others take breaks, every time you ship while others plan, the gap widens. The long game is yours if you want it.
CTA (45-60s): What year did you start your creator journey? Let's see who's been playing the long game.
