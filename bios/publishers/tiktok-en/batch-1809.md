# TikTok Batch 1809

**Script 1: You Already Have Enough to Start**
Hook (0-3s): You do not need more knowledge to start. You need a decision.
Body (3-45s): The person who needs one more course before starting is avoiding. They have enough. The fear of being wrong is louder than the desire to be helpful. But here is the truth: you know more than someone. Probably a lot of someones. The gap between what you know and what they know is the product. You do not need a PhD. You do not need ten years. You need to know more than the person you are helping and be willing to teach it. That is the whole bar.
CTA (45-60s): What do you know that you have not taught yet? Drop it in the comments.

**Script 2: Short-Form Is the Funnel**
Hook (0-3s): Short-form content is not the destination. It is the door.
Body (3-45s): Sixty seconds gets them curious. Then they go to your profile. They see your bio, they click your link, they sign up for your list or buy your product. Short-form content does not have to close the sale. It just has to open the relationship. Stop trying to explain everything in one video. Tease. Intrigue. Direct. The goal is not to inform them fully. The goal is to make them want more. Make them click. Then close on the other side.
CTA (45-60s): Link in bio. That is where I explain the full system.

**Script 3: Consistency Is the Differentiator**
Hook (0-3s): Your competition will outspend you. They will not outwork you for a decade.
Body (3-45s): Big brands have budgets. Agencies have teams. Influencers have head starts. You have something none of them have. The willingness to show up every day without corporate approval. Without a committee. Without quarterly reviews. Just you, a decision, and the work. Most competitors quit in year one. Most of the rest quit in year two. The creator who is still publishing in year five inherits an audience. Not because they were the best. Because they stayed.
CTA (45-60s): Follow. I have been doing this for a while and I am just getting started.
