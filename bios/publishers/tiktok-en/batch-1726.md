## Script 1 — The Two Types of Work
**Hook (0-3s):** There are two types of work. One builds your future. One maintains your present. Most people only do the second.
**Body (3-45s):** Maintenance work keeps things running. Emails, meetings, daily tasks. It's necessary. But it doesn't compound. Creative work, building, learning, creating systems, that's what builds the future. The tragedy is most people fill their days with maintenance and wonder why nothing changes. You need both. But the ratio matters. If you're spending 90% on maintenance and 10% on building, your future is being mortgaged by your present. Flip the ratio. Even 60/40 changes the trajectory.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I post about building the future while managing the present.

## Script 2 — Solve Problems, Don't Just Study Them
**Hook (0-3s):** Knowing about a problem and solving it are completely different skills.
**Body (3-45s):** Most people in the education system are trained to analyze problems. Very few are trained to fix them. Builders are fixers. They move from understanding to action faster than anyone else. The gap between knowing and doing is where most potential dies. Stop consuming content about what you want to build. Start building the thing you've been consuming content about. Mistakes in execution teach more than perfect consumption. The lesson only lands when you're in it.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this. The next time you find yourself consuming instead of building, come back here.
