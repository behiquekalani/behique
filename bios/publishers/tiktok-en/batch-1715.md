# TikTok Batch 1715

## Script 1 — Clarity Over Cleverness
**Hook (0-3s):** The smartest builders I know write like a 10-year-old could understand them.
**Body (3-45s):** Jargon is insecurity wearing intellectual clothing. When you truly understand something, you can explain it simply. When you can't, you use complexity to hide the gap. The best communicators, the ones who build massive audiences and command premium prices, are the ones who make hard ideas feel obvious. Clear writing equals clear thinking. And clear thinking is the actual skill. Before you write anything, ask: could someone who knows nothing about this understand it? If not, you haven't understood it well enough yet.
**CTA (45-60s):** Take one complex concept you know and explain it in 3 sentences in the comments.

## Script 2 — The Second Brain
**Hook (0-3s):** Your brain is for thinking, not storing. Stop using it as a hard drive.
**Body (3-45s):** Every idea you try to hold in your head is competing for processing space. Every task you're mentally tracking adds cognitive load. External systems, notes, task managers, structured files, free your brain to do what it's actually good at. Connecting ideas. Solving problems. Creating. Build a second brain. Capture everything that matters. Organize it so you can find it. Review it regularly. Let your external system hold the information so your internal system can do the thinking.
**CTA (45-60s):** What tool do you use to capture and organize your thinking? Drop it below.

## Script 3 — Momentum Physics
**Hook (0-3s):** Starting is the hardest part. Momentum is the reward for starting.
**Body (3-45s):** Newton's first law applies to builders. Objects at rest stay at rest. Objects in motion stay in motion. The creative session you've been avoiding for a week will feel almost effortless once you sit down and start. The workout that felt impossible becomes normal by minute 10. The first sentence of the essay is the hardest one. Momentum is a gift you give yourself by starting before you feel ready. The feeling of readiness follows action. It does not precede it. Start small. Start now. Let momentum carry you.
**CTA (45-60s):** What's one thing you've been avoiding starting? Commit to 10 minutes on it today.
