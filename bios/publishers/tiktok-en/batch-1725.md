# TikTok Batch 1725

## Script 1 — The Hidden Cost of Playing It Safe
**Hook (0-3s):** Playing it safe is the riskiest thing you can do with your life.
**Body (3-45s):** Safe choices feel comfortable now. They compound into regret later. The 70-year-old who never took the risk doesn't regret the risk. They regret the life they didn't build. Safety is an illusion anyway. Jobs get cut. Industries collapse. The "stable" path isn't stable. It's just familiar. The builder who takes calculated risks in their 20s has options in their 40s. The one who plays it safe has a pension they hope will be enough. Choose the uncertainty you control over the uncertainty you don't.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I'm building out loud so you can see what calculated risk actually looks like.

## Script 2 — Why Your Ideas Die in Your Head
**Hook (0-3s):** You've had the idea. You know what to build. You haven't started. Why?
**Body (3-45s):** Ideas die in the gap between thinking and doing. The gap is filled with three things: fear of failure, perfectionism, and the lie that conditions need to be better before you begin. None of those are real blockers. They're beliefs dressed up as logic. The fix is embarrassingly simple: start before you're ready, in the smallest possible form. Write the first sentence. Build the first screen. Make the first post. The momentum you need doesn't exist before you start. It's created by starting.
**CTA (45-60s):** Save this and go do the one thing you've been putting off.

## Script 3 — Reading as a Competitive Advantage
**Hook (0-3s):** The average person reads zero books a year. One book a month puts you in the top 1%.
**Body (3-45s):** Reading is compressed experience. Every business book is the author's decades distilled into hours of your time. You get to skip their mistakes. Non-readers wonder why certain people make better decisions, see patterns faster, and navigate complexity with ease. It's not intelligence. It's inputs. The quality of your thinking depends on the quality of what you feed it. Read one chapter a day. That's it. In a year you'll have processed more ideas than most people encounter in a decade.
**CTA (45-60s):** Follow. I post what I'm reading and what it's actually changing.
