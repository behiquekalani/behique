# TikTok Batch 1833

**Script 1: The Problem with Multi-Platform from Day One**
Hook (0-3s): Trying to be on every platform at once is how you go nowhere on all of them.
Body (3-45s): The creator who tries to master TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, and LinkedIn all at once spreads their energy so thin that none of it works. Pick one. Master the format. Build the audience. Understand what that platform rewards. Then repurpose your best-performing content to a second platform. The order is: go deep, then go wide. Going wide first is the content creator version of trying to run before you've learned to walk. Depth creates a foundation. Breadth extends it. Don't skip the foundation.

**Script 2: What a Real Content Audit Looks Like**
Hook (0-3s): Before you make more content, find out what's already working.
Body (3-45s): Go back through your last 30 posts. Which ones got the most saves? Which ones got the most DMs? Which ones led to someone clicking your link? Those are the posts that resonated. Make more of those. Not because you're chasing an algorithm. Because the audience told you what they wanted to see more of. A content audit is not about deleting what didn't work. It's about understanding the pattern in what did. You already have the data. Most creators just don't use it.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the simple content audit template I use every month.

**Script 3: The Psychology Behind a Product That Sells Itself**
Hook (0-3s): The best-selling digital products don't need a hard sell. Here's why.
Body (3-45s): When a product is positioned correctly, the right person sees it and immediately thinks "I need this." That happens when the product name is specific, the outcome is clear, and the price matches the perceived value. No hype. No fake urgency. No countdown timers. Just a clear offer for a clear person. The hard sell exists because the product is unclear. When the product is right, the marketing is just pointing the right people toward it. Design the product with positioning in mind from the start.
