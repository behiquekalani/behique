# TikTok Batch 1784

**Script 1: Identity Before Habits**
Hook (0-3s): Don't set a goal to post every day. Decide you're a creator. Then act like one.
Body (3-45s): Behavior follows identity. If you tell yourself "I'm trying to post consistently," every day is a battle of motivation. If you tell yourself "I'm a creator who ships content," posting becomes the default. You don't negotiate with yourself about whether to do it. You just do it, because that's who you are. Identity-based habits don't require willpower. They require deciding who you are first, then letting behavior follow naturally.
CTA (45-60s): In the comments: finish this sentence. "I am a creator who..."

**Script 2: AI Tools Won't Replace You. They'll Replace the Creator Who Refuses to Use Them.**
Hook (0-3s): AI doesn't replace creators. It replaces creators who treat it like a threat.
Body (3-45s): Here's what AI actually does: it eliminates the time-wasting parts of your workflow. Research, formatting, first drafts, title testing, repurposing. The creative judgment, the unique angle, the voice that makes people stop scrolling. That's still you. Creators who are leaning into AI right now are publishing more, testing more, iterating faster. The ones ignoring it are falling behind. Not because AI is smarter. Because speed matters, and AI gives you more of it.
CTA (45-60s): What's one AI tool you've built into your creator workflow? Drop it below.

**Script 3: Audience Before Product**
Hook (0-3s): The biggest mistake new creators make: building the product before the audience.
Body (3-45s): If you build a product and then try to find an audience, you're starting from scratch every single time. If you build an audience first, every product launch has a built-in customer base. Document your journey. Teach what you know. Be specific about who you're talking to. By the time you launch your first product, some of those people already trust you. That trust converts. An audience of 500 people who know, like, and trust you will out-buy an audience of 50,000 strangers every time.
CTA (45-60s): Are you building audience first or product first? Tell me where you are in the sequence.
