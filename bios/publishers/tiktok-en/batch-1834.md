**Script 1: The Difference One Year Makes**
Hook (0-3s): One year of consistent daily action looks unrecognizable compared to today.
Body (3-45s): Not because of any single thing you did. Because of compounding. Every post you write teaches you something. Every product you build teaches you more. Every email you send builds the relationship deeper. The creator who looks back from a year of consistent work doesn't think "that one viral post changed everything." They think "I can't believe how much I learned just by showing up every day." The year is going to pass either way. The only question is what you build while it passes.
CTA (45-60s): Follow and let's make this next year count together.

**Script 2: How to Price Your First Digital Product**
Hook (0-3s): Pricing your first product is less complicated than you think.
Body (3-45s): Start with the outcome. What specific result does this product deliver? Put a dollar value on that result for your buyer. If it saves them 10 hours of work at $50 an hour, the result is worth $500. You don't need to charge $500. But you shouldn't charge $5. A price around 10-20% of the value delivered is usually the sweet spot for entry-level products. For a $500 outcome, that's $47-97. Not $7. Not $297 for your first product. Find the number that feels fair but not cheap, and test it. You can always adjust.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the full pricing guide I use across all my digital products.

**Script 3: Trust Is the Real Product**
Hook (0-3s): You're not selling a guide. You're selling trust in a product form.
Body (3-45s): When someone buys from you, they're betting that your guide, your course, or your template will deliver what you promised. That bet is based on trust. The trust you built through free content, through honest writing, through showing up consistently before asking for anything. This is why the sales cycle for a one-person business is different. You're not cold-selling strangers. You're warm-selling people who already believe in you. The sale is the last step, not the first. Build the trust first. The sale follows naturally.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see how I build trust before I ever ask for a sale.
