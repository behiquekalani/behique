**Script 1: The One-Person Business Has No Ceiling**
Hook (0-3s): One person. No employees. Multiple income streams. No ceiling.
Body (3-45s): The one-person business model flips everything you were taught. You don't need a team to scale. You need leverage. Digital products are leverage. Content is leverage. An email list is leverage. Systems are leverage. One person with the right combination of these can out-earn a company of twenty people with none of them. The ceiling is not your capacity to work. It's your creativity in building systems that work for you. Most people don't hit that ceiling because they stop before the systems click.
CTA (45-60s): Follow for the exact systems I'm using to build this.

**Script 2: Creator Burnout Is a Systems Problem**
Hook (0-3s): Creator burnout isn't about posting too much. It's about having no system.
Body (3-45s): When every piece of content feels like you're starting from scratch, you burn out. When every product launch requires reinventing the wheel, you burn out. The solution isn't to create less. It's to build systems that reduce the decision fatigue. A content system means you never stare at a blank page. A product system means you know exactly how to build and launch. A repurposing system means one idea becomes ten pieces of content automatically. Burnout is a symptom of a missing infrastructure. The creators who last are the ones who built the infrastructure.
CTA (45-60s): Follow to see the systems I built to stop burning out.

**Script 3: Your First Digital Product Doesn't Need to Be Perfect**
Hook (0-3s): The best digital product you can build right now is an imperfect one that ships.
Body (3-45s): Perfectionism is a delay strategy. It feels productive because you're still "working on it." But a $27 guide that exists and helps ten people is worth infinitely more than the perfect $97 course that never gets finished. Start with what you know right now. A checklist. A template. A short guide. Put it out. Get feedback. Improve it. Version two is always better than version one. But version two only exists if version one shipped. Done beats perfect. It always has and it always will.
CTA (45-60s): Follow if you've been sitting on a product idea and need someone to push you.
