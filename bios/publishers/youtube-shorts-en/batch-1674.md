## Short 1 — Maker's Schedule
**Title:** Why Meetings Are Killing Your Creativity (The Maker vs Manager Schedule)
**Hook (0-3s):** Meetings are the enemy of creation.
**Body (3-55s):** Paul Graham coined the distinction between the maker's schedule and the manager's schedule and it's one of the most useful mental models for builders. Managers work in hourly blocks. One meeting, next meeting. Their schedule is already fragmented. One more meeting costs one hour. Makers are different. Deep creative work requires long, uninterrupted blocks of time to reach flow state. A single meeting in the middle of a creative day doesn't just cost that hour. It fractures the entire day. Before and after the meeting, your brain is partially occupied with the meeting context. The only protection is ruthless calendar design. Large morning blocks. Communication batched to afternoons. Meetings only when truly necessary.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. How much uninterrupted deep work time do you actually get daily? Be honest below.

## Short 2 — Network Is What You're Known For
**Title:** Your Network Isn't Who You Know — It's Who Knows What You Do
**Hook (0-3s):** Your network is not who you know. It's who knows what you can do.
**Body (3-55s):** Collecting business cards and LinkedIn connections is not building a network. Building a network is creating a reputation specific enough that the right people think of you when the right problem appears. Generic networkers are interchangeable. They show up at every event and add everyone. They're easily forgotten. Specific builders stand out because they're associated with a problem they visibly solve. The way you build this is not through networking events. It's through producing work that demonstrates your thinking. Writing, content, projects, case studies. Every public piece of work is a standing advertisement for what you can do. Build the work first. The network follows.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What is the one thing you want your network to know you for? Comment below.

## Short 3 — The Mentor Shortcut
**Title:** The Fastest Path to Anywhere Is Someone Who's Already Been There
**Hook (0-3s):** The fastest path to anywhere is someone who's already been there.
**Body (3-55s):** There is a version of your learning journey that takes 10 years and a version that takes 3. The difference is often the presence of a great mentor. Not a life coach. Not a motivational speaker. Someone who has specifically done the thing you're trying to do, failed at the things you'll fail at, and found the paths that actually work. Their value is mostly in what they tell you not to do. The dead ends they prevent you from entering. Finding a mentor isn't about cold outreach with long requests. It's about making your work visible enough that people worth learning from can find you. Start producing. Start publishing. Mentors find students who are already in motion.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. Who has been the most impactful teacher or mentor in your journey? Give them a shout.
