## Script 1

**Hook:** How to turn one idea into a week of content. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
One core idea can generate five pieces of content with zero repetition.

Day one: the big picture post. State the idea and why it matters. This is the thesis.

Day two: the breakdown post. Divide the idea into three to five components and explain each one briefly.

Day three: the example post. Share a specific real-world example that proves the idea. Story-based, personal when possible.

Day four: the counterargument post. Steel-man the opposing view and then explain why the original idea still holds. This builds credibility because it shows intellectual honesty.

Day five: the action post. Turn the idea into something the reader can do today. Tactical, specific, immediately useful.

One idea, five formats, five days of content. That's a content battery.

## Script 2

**Hook:** Why writing online is the highest-return activity for solo business owners. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three reasons writing is the foundation of every successful solo business.

Reason one: writing clarifies your thinking. The act of translating ideas into words forces precision. Vague ideas survive in your head. They collapse when you try to write them clearly. Better writing means better thinking means better decisions.

Reason two: writing creates permanent value. A post published today can attract readers three years from now. A conversation happens once. Writing compounds over time.

Reason three: writing is the input to everything else. Your sales page, your emails, your DMs, your pitch, your product. It all starts with writing. The better your writing, the better every other output in your business. There is no higher-leverage skill for a solo creator.

**CTA:** Save this and commit to writing something every single day. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** The four stages every solo business goes through, and where most people get stuck. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Stage one: ideation. Finding your niche, your audience, your offer. The risk here is staying in ideation forever. Clarity comes from action, not from more planning.

Stage two: initial creation. Building the first version of your product and your content foundation. The risk here is perfectionism. Ship imperfect. Gather data. Improve.

Stage three: audience building. Growing the community of people who trust your work. The risk here is inconsistency. Show up on a schedule, not based on motivation.

Stage four: optimization. Refining your offer, scaling your distribution, building more sophisticated products. The risk here is distraction. Don't optimize what isn't working. Build what's already working into a system.

Most people get stuck in stage one or quit in stage two. Stage three is where the business becomes real.

**CTA:** Follow and figure out which stage you're in right now. (5 sec)
