## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** How to build your first digital product from scratch with no audience.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: No audience required at the start. You need three things. A specific problem you know how to solve, a format that delivers the solution clearly, and a place to sell it. That's the whole starting kit. The audience grows as a byproduct of the product being good.

Idea 2: Pick the simplest format that fully delivers the result. A checklist works for process-based knowledge. A template works for repeatable tasks. A short guide works for frameworks and concepts. Keep the scope tight. One problem, one solution, one outcome. Tight products sell better than bloated ones.

Idea 3: For distribution without an audience, lean on platforms that have built-in search. Etsy, Gumroad, Notion marketplaces, and similar platforms already have buyers looking for what you're building. Optimize your listing for the exact search terms your buyer would use. Your first sales often come from organic search, not from your own following.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** This is why most online businesses stall out after the first few months.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The initial energy is not sustainable. You start with excitement, you launch, you get some traction, and then the daily work of consistency sets in. The motivation that got you started doesn't last, because motivation is an emotion and emotions fluctuate.

Idea 2: What replaces motivation is systems. A content calendar that removes the daily decision of what to post. A production workflow that makes batching content faster. A weekly review that keeps you focused on what's working. Systems create output without requiring the same energy level every day.

Idea 3: The other thing that keeps people going is a clear reason. Not a vague reason like "I want to be my own boss." A specific reason. By this date, I want this outcome for this reason that matters to me. That specificity gives you something to return to when the work feels hard and the results feel slow.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and build the systems that keep you going when motivation runs out.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The identity shift you need before your business can grow.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Most people approach business as something they're trying. Trying to sell a product. Trying to build an audience. Trying to freelance. The word "trying" carries the assumption that you might stop. It's temporary language for a temporary commitment.

Idea 2: The shift is from trying to being. You're not trying to be a writer. You're a writer who is still developing their audience. You're not trying to sell digital products. You're a digital product creator who is still growing their reach. The identity comes before the results.

Idea 3: This isn't self-delusion. It's directional clarity. When you define yourself by what you're building, your daily decisions align with that identity. You write because writers write. You build because builders build. The identity creates the behavior, and the behavior creates the results. The sequence matters.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and let's build the identity that makes the results inevitable.
