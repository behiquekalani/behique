## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here are three reasons why solo businesses are outperforming traditional startups right now.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Overhead is nearly zero. A solo creator with a laptop and a clear offer has costs that are a fraction of what a funded startup carries. Lower overhead means faster profitability and fewer survival pressures.

Idea 2 - Decision speed. In a solo business, you see a problem and you fix it the same day. No approval chains, no department politics. Speed of execution is a real competitive advantage.

Idea 3 - Customer relationships are direct. When you run solo, every client interaction is with you. That builds trust faster than any enterprise account team can. Trust converts to loyalty and referrals.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for weekly one-person business education.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The three types of digital products and which one you should build first.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Templates and tools. These are the fastest to build and the easiest to sell because the value is immediately obvious. A buyer can see exactly what they're getting before they purchase.

Idea 2 - Guides and frameworks. These teach a process. More effort to create but higher perceived value. Works best when you have a clear, repeatable system you can document.

Idea 3 - Courses and workshops. Highest price point, most effort to produce. Build these only after you've validated the problem with a cheaper product. Use sales data, not assumptions.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for more digital product strategy.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that separate writers who get paid from writers who don't.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They write for a specific person. Not "general audience." One person with one problem. The more specific your mental target, the more the real reader feels seen.

Idea 2 - They publish before they're confident. Confidence comes from publishing, not from waiting. The first 50 posts are tuition. Pay it quickly.

Idea 3 - They connect writing to an offer. Content without a product attached is a hobby. The goal is to turn attention into revenue. Every good piece of writing should have somewhere to send the reader next.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe if you're building a writing-based business.
