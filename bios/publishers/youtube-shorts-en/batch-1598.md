## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three signs your business model is wrong for you, and what to do instead.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Sign one is dread. If you feel consistent dread before doing the main activity in your business, the model doesn't fit. Occasional resistance is normal. Persistent dread is a signal. The activity you do most often should feel at least neutral, ideally engaging. If it doesn't, the model needs to change.

Idea 2: Sign two is avoiding the core work. If you're always busy but the revenue-generating part of your business keeps getting pushed back, you're avoiding something. That avoidance is information. What are you doing instead? That might be the real model.

Idea 3: Sign three is declining quality over time. If your early work was better than your recent work, the model is draining you. Good work requires some level of engagement. When the work gets worse as you get more experienced, it usually means the work itself stopped being interesting. Following the engagement is how you find the right model.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why you don't need confidence to start. You need action.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Confidence is not a prerequisite. It's a byproduct. You don't become confident and then start. You start, do the thing imperfectly, survive the imperfection, and develop confidence from the survival. The sequence is action, then confidence, not the other way around.

Idea 2: Waiting to feel confident is waiting for something that requires the thing you're waiting to do. It's circular. Every person who looks confident in their business looked uncertain at the start. The confidence you see in others was earned through the exact uncomfortable actions you're avoiding.

Idea 3: The practical application is this. Identify the one action you keep delaying because you don't feel ready. Do it this week. Not perfectly. Not completely. Just do a version of it. You will discover that the fear was bigger than the reality. That discovery is one unit of confidence. Repeat until you have enough.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and take the action you've been avoiding.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Here's how to repurpose one idea into a week of content across multiple platforms.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Start with the long-form version. Write a newsletter or a blog post that covers one idea in full. This is the source of truth. Include context, nuance, examples, and a clear takeaway. The full version might take an hour to produce. Everything else is derived from it.

Idea 2: From the full piece, extract three short takes. A strong sentence becomes a tweet or short thread. A key insight becomes a TikTok hook and three-sentence body. A specific example becomes a Shorts script. Each platform gets a version calibrated to its format, all from the same source material.

Idea 3: Finally, turn the core idea into an image or graphic for Pinterest or Instagram. A visual representation of the main concept drives search traffic and reach on platforms that favor imagery. One idea, one hour of original writing, five to seven pieces of distributable content. Efficiency is the creator's greatest asset.
