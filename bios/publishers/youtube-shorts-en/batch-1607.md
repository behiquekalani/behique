## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here is how to validate a digital product before you spend a week building it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Step one: Post a piece of content about the core problem your product solves. Watch the engagement carefully. If people comment questions, share personal stories, or save the post, that is a signal that the pain is real.

Step two: Write a one-paragraph description of the product and its outcome. Share it directly with five to ten people in your target audience and ask if they would pay for it. Not "is this interesting?" Ask specifically "would you buy this for X amount?"

Step three: Pre-sell before you build. Announce the product, set a launch date, and accept payment now for delivery later. If nobody buys, you saved yourself weeks of work. If people buy, you just funded the creation.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why most online businesses plateau and what breaks the ceiling.

**Main Point (45s):**
The plateau happens for a predictable reason. Your initial audience was your warmest people, the ones who already trusted you. Once you sell to them, growth slows because you have not built a reliable way to reach new people continuously.

What breaks the plateau is usually one of three things. First, a repeatable top-of-funnel content system that consistently attracts new people. Second, a referral mechanism built into your product that spreads through your customers' networks. Third, paid traffic that amplifies what is already converting organically.

The mistake most people make at the plateau is creating new products instead of fixing distribution. More products do not help if the discovery problem is not solved first.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three writing habits that will make your content 10 times more effective.

**Main Point (45s):**
Habit one: Write the last line first. Know your conclusion before you start. When you know where you are going, every sentence works toward that destination and nothing is wasted.

Habit two: Cut your first paragraph. Most writers use the first paragraph to warm up. It is almost always unnecessary. Your second paragraph is usually where the actual content begins. Delete the warmup.

Habit three: Read it aloud before you post. Your ear catches awkward rhythm that your eye misses. If you stumble over a sentence, your reader will too. Reading aloud is the fastest editing tool you have.
