## Script 1

**Hook:** Three pricing mistakes that kill digital product sales. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Mistake one: pricing by effort. The hours you put in are irrelevant to the buyer. They care about the result. A product that saves someone 10 hours of frustration is worth more than a product that took you 10 hours to build. Price the outcome, not the labor.

Mistake two: copying competitors without context. If someone else charges $27, that doesn't mean you should. Their audience, their trust level, and their positioning are all different from yours.

Mistake three: discounting by default. Slashing your price communicates that it was never worth the original amount. Instead of discounting, add bonuses. Maintain the price. Let the perceived value go up, not down.

**CTA:** Save this before you price your next product. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** Why consistency beats talent every single time in the creator economy. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Talented people who post twice a month get passed by average writers who post every day. Here's why.

The algorithm rewards consistency. More posts means more surface area for discovery. Each post is a chance to be found.

Your audience rewards consistency. People follow creators they can count on. Sporadic posting feels unreliable. Daily or weekly posting builds anticipation.

You reward yourself with consistency. Writing every day makes you a better writer faster than writing occasionally ever could. The reps matter.

Talent is the starting point. Consistency is the multiplier. You cannot out-talent someone who shows up every single day.

**CTA:** Follow and start your consistent posting streak today. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Three things you need before you build your first digital product. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
One: a real conversation with your target audience. Not assumptions. Actual conversations. Ask what they're struggling with. Listen for the exact words they use. Those words will become your product description.

Two: a clear outcome promise. What is the person's life like after they use your product? Define it precisely. "You'll feel better" is not an outcome. "You'll close your first client within 30 days" is an outcome.

Three: a minimum viable version. Don't spend three months building before you validate. Create the smallest version that delivers the core value. Sell that first. Improve based on real feedback from real buyers.

**CTA:** Follow for step-by-step digital product strategy. (5 sec)
