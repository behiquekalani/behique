## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Why your social media following isn't turning into income, and how to fix it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The problem is usually a missing offer. You have an audience and they trust you, but there's nothing to buy. Followers convert when there's a clear, specific product that solves the exact problem your content addresses. If the offer is missing, the income is missing.

Idea 2: The second issue is a broken bridge. Even with a product, if your content never explicitly points to it, most people won't look for it. Every piece of content should have a clear next step. A direct mention of what you sell and how it helps them specifically.

Idea 3: The third issue is audience misalignment. If you've built an audience around entertainment but your product is educational, the conversion will be low. Your audience needs to be composed of the same people who would benefit from your product. Build the audience with content that mirrors what you sell.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** This is what it actually looks like to build a business without a team.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The first year looks like a lot of experimenting. You try different content formats, different offer structures, different audiences. Most of it won't work immediately. You're gathering data. The goal of year one is to find one thing that consistently works, not to build a full system.

Idea 2: The second phase is doubling down on what worked. You drop everything that didn't and put all your energy into the thing that showed traction. This is where most solo operators make a mistake. They keep the things that didn't work because they're comfortable, instead of aggressively expanding what is working.

Idea 3: The third phase is systematizing the thing that works. Write down the process. Create templates. Batch content production. Automate what can be automated. This is how one person creates the output of a small team without burning out. Systems replace bandwidth.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Here's how to price a digital product if you have no idea where to start.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Start with the outcome. What does the buyer achieve after using your product? If they save time, quantify the time. If they make money, estimate the amount. If they solve a frustrating problem, estimate what the frustration costs them. Your price should be a fraction of the value they receive.

Idea 2: Then look at comparable products. Not to copy the price, but to understand the market's expectations. If similar products are between fifty and two hundred dollars, starting at twenty signals you're worse than your competitors. Starting at the middle of the range and delivering above-average value is the safe starting point.

Idea 3: Finally, test. Launch at a price. Measure the conversion rate. If you're converting well and have no complaints, test a higher price. Price is not permanent. Adjusting it based on real data is how you find the right number. Most people never test. They set a price and assume it's correct.
