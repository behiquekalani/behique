## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that make a written piece of content actually sell.

**Main Point (45s):**
First, specificity. Vague claims do not move people. "This will change your life" does not convert. "This will save you three hours every Monday morning" does. The more specific the promise, the more believable it is, and the easier it is for the right person to recognize themselves in it. Second, a single clear action. Every piece of writing should have one thing it wants the reader to do. Follow, buy, save, share, comment. One thing. When you give multiple options, decision fatigue sets in and people do nothing. Third, emotional resonance. Logic explains the purchase. Emotion triggers it. Before you write anything, decide what feeling you want the reader to have at the end. Then build toward that feeling from the first sentence.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for writing and content strategy that converts.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** What separates a one-person business that lasts from one that burns out in year one.

**Main Point (45s):**
The difference usually comes down to three things. First, sustainability of energy. A business model that requires you to be on at all times will eventually break you. The businesses that last are built around systems that work without your constant presence. Email sequences, automated delivery, scheduled content. Your energy is preserved for high-value decisions, not repetitive tasks. Second, alignment between your natural strengths and your revenue model. If you hate video, do not build a YouTube channel as your primary distribution. Build in the medium you can sustain. Misalignment kills consistency. And without consistency, nothing compounds. Third, a genuine interest in the problem you are solving. The markets where you stay curious are the ones where you naturally publish, improve, and serve. Curiosity is the fuel that outlasts motivation.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for sustainable business strategy built for the long run.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Here is why most people never build a second product after their first.

**Main Point (45s):**
It is not lack of ideas. Most people have more ideas than they can execute. The real barrier is what I call the perfection trap. The first product took so long to build and market and refine that the idea of doing it again feels exhausting. So they avoid it. The fix is to build your second product faster by using everything the first one taught you. You already know your audience. You already know the format they prefer. You already know which topics they care about. Use that data. The second product should take half the time of the first. The third product should take half the time of the second. The system compounds if you let it. But you have to ship product two to discover that.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe to learn how to build a digital product catalog efficiently.
