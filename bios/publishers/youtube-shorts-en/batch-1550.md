## Script 1

**Hook:** How to build an audience that actually buys from you. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three principles for building a buying audience.

First: attract buyers, not browsers. Content that teaches specific, tactical things attracts people who want to implement. Content that entertains or inspires broadly attracts people who want to be entertained. Both are valid. Only one builds a buying audience.

Second: solve a complete problem, not a partial one. Don't just identify the issue. Walk people through the solution. When your free content solves real problems, readers trust that your paid content will solve bigger ones.

Third: talk about your offer regularly. Not aggressively. Naturally. Mention what you sell in relevant contexts. "By the way, I built a guide on exactly this" is not pushy. It's useful. Your audience can't buy what they don't know exists.

## Script 2

**Hook:** Three things most solo builders underestimate about building online. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
One: how long the early phase takes. Most people expect results in 30 days. The realistic timeline for meaningful traction is six to twelve months of consistent effort. Not because it's slow. Because trust takes time to build and compounding takes time to kick in. Plan for the long phase and don't quit during it.

Two: how important positioning is. Two people can write about the same topic with wildly different results based purely on how they position themselves. "I write about business" is a category. "I help design-school dropouts build profitable solo businesses" is a position. Position first.

Three: how simple the business model can be. One product. One landing page. One way to drive traffic. That's a complete business. Complexity is not a sign of sophistication. Simplicity that converts is.

**CTA:** Save this and simplify one thing in your current setup. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** The writing framework that makes every piece of content shareable. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three elements make content shareable.

Element one: the counterintuitive idea. Something that challenges what people already believe. Not for shock value. Because challenging an assumption makes people think. Thinking leads to sharing. "You don't need a big audience to make big money" is counterintuitive. It makes you stop scrolling.

Element two: the specific proof. Back up the counterintuitive idea with a concrete example or number. "A creator with 800 subscribers made $12,000 in a month" is specific proof. It makes the claim believable.

Element three: the actionable takeaway. End with something readers can do in the next 24 hours. Theory without action is just philosophy. Readers share content that helps them do something.

Lead with counterintuitive. Prove with specific. Close with action.

**CTA:** Follow to study more frameworks like this one. (5 sec)
