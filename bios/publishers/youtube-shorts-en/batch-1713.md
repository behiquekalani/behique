This is how you maintain consistency without creative burnout. You're not running out of ideas. You're under-repurposing the ones you have.

**Short 2: The First $1,000 Sequence**
Hook (0-5s): [Dollar bill visual building up] The exact sequence I'd follow to make my first $1,000 online from scratch.
Content (5-50s): Step one: freelance a skill I already have. Writing, editing, design, spreadsheets. Anything. Find five people who need it. Charge $200 each. That's $1,000. No product needed. No audience needed. Just a skill and five conversations. Step two: use the client work to fund the time to build a product. Document the process you're doing for clients. That documentation becomes your product. Step three: sell the product to the same types of people you did the service for. They're already proven buyers. The service validates the offer. The product automates the income. This is the right sequence. Most people skip to step three and wonder why nobody buys.
Close (50-60s): What skill could you freelance starting this week? Drop it below and subscribe.
Description: The path to first $1,000 online doesn't start with a digital product. It starts with a service. Here's why the sequence matters and how to transition from service to scalable product income.
Tags: make money online, solopreneur, freelancing, digital products, first $1000
