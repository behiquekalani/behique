## Short 1
**Title:** The Skill Stack That Makes You Uncopyable as a Solopreneur
**Hook (0-3s):** One rare skill makes you good. Two combined makes you dangerous.
**Script (3-55s):** Most people compete in one skill and wonder why they're replaceable. The solopreneurs who build sustainable businesses combine rare skills into a unique combination that nobody else has in exactly the same way. Writing plus design. Coding plus marketing. Teaching plus systems. Each skill alone is common. Together, they're a stack that's nearly impossible to replicate. Your career isn't about being the best at one thing. It's about owning an intersection that serves a specific market better than anyone else can. Build toward that intersection.

## Short 2
**Title:** Why Your Opinion Is a Business Asset (And You Should Stop Hiding It)
**Hook (0-3s):** Having a clear point of view is the most underused business strategy.
**Script (3-55s):** Creators who stay neutral on everything are easy to scroll past. They have no friction, and friction is what makes content memorable. Your specific perspective on your industry, on the conventional wisdom, on what most people get wrong, is more valuable than any information you can share. Anyone can share information. Only you can share your particular take on it. The creators who build real audiences take real positions. They make someone think "finally, someone said it." Be that creator. Say the true things, even when they're uncomfortable.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for content and positioning strategy that builds a real following.

## Short 3
**Title:** How to Beat Perfectionism and Actually Ship Your Work (A Simple System)
**Hook (0-3s):** The creator who ships imperfect work monthly beats the one who ships perfect work never.
**Script (3-55s):** Perfectionism is not a quality standard. It's a delay mechanism. The standard for shipping should be: is this useful and honest? Not: is this the best thing I've ever made? Your audience doesn't need your magnum opus. They need a consistent stream of genuine value. Set a minimum viable version of whatever you're making. Commit to publishing it by a specific date. Then publish it, flaws and all. The response from your actual audience will tell you more about what to improve than any amount of pre-publication agonizing will.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for shipping habits and systems that overcome the perfectionism trap.
**Tags:** [perfectionism, solopreneur, shipping, contentcreator, creatorlife, productivity, justship]
