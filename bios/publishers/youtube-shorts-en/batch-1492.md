## Short 1
**Title:** How to Write Content That Makes People Stop Scrolling
**Hook (0-3s):** The hook is everything. If the first sentence doesn't stop the scroll, nothing else matters.
**Script (3-55s):** A great hook does one of three things: challenges a belief, promises a specific result, or creates a curiosity gap. "5 productivity tips" does none of those. "You're working hard because you've been lied to about productivity" does all three. Study the first line of every piece of content you consume that makes you stop. Reverse-engineer what made you stop. Apply that pattern to your own content. Your first sentence should make the reader feel like they'd be missing something important if they scrolled past.

## Short 2
**Title:** The Difference Between a Hobby and a Business (Most Creators Miss This)
**Hook (0-3s):** If you don't have an offer, you have a hobby. Not a business.
**Script (3-55s):** Content without an offer is content for free. That's fine if you're building an audience for the long game. But at some point, a business requires revenue. The shift from creator to business owner is simple: make an offer. A product. A service. A course. A call. Something people can pay for. You don't need 100k followers to make your first sale. You need one person who trusts you and has the problem your offer solves. Make the offer before you feel ready. The first sale will tell you more than 6 months of planning.

## Short 3
**Title:** The Focus Skill Nobody Teaches But Everyone Needs
**Hook (0-3s):** Focus is not a personality trait. It's a skill. And you can build it in 30 days.
**Script (3-55s):** Most people treat focus like a fixed resource. Either you have it or you don't. That's wrong. Focus is trained through deliberate practice just like any other skill. Start with 25-minute focused work blocks. One task. No switches. No checking anything. Then increase to 45 minutes. Then 90. In 30 days, you'll find you can sustain attention for longer periods than you thought possible. The brain adapts to what you train it to do. Train it to focus and it will. Train it to switch constantly and it will do that instead.
