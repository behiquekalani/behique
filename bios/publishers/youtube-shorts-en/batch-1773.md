**Short 1:**
SEO Title: The Real Reason Most Digital Products Don't Sell
Hook: Your digital product might not be selling because you built what you wanted, not what the market needs.
Script: Before you build anything, do the research. Find the places where your target customer is expressing frustration in their own words. Forums, comment sections, social media threads. Look for the specific complaint that comes up repeatedly. The problem people are paying to solve right now. Build the solution to that specific complaint. Name it using their language. Position it against their exact frustration. When a product speaks directly to a real, active pain in the words the buyer already uses to describe that pain, it doesn't need a hard sell. The positioning does the work. Research before you build.

**Short 2:**
SEO Title: Stop Optimizing for Likes, Start Tracking Business Metrics
Hook: The most liked post you've ever made might have sold zero products.
Script: Likes are a platform metric. Saves, click-throughs, email signups, and product sales are business metrics. They're different things. A highly liked post might entertain the audience but trigger no action. A lower-liked post that clearly articulates a problem and offers a solution might generate ten product sales. Business metrics tell you whether your content is doing its actual job. Vanity metrics tell you whether the platform algorithm liked it today. Build a simple dashboard. Track email signups per week. Track sales per week. Track link clicks per week. Those numbers tell the truth about whether the content is working.

**Short 3:**
SEO Title: How Free Products Build a Paid Customer Base
Hook: The best marketing tool you have is a free product that genuinely over-delivers.
Script: A free resource that delivers real value does three things at once. It attracts the exact right people by being specific about who it's for. It filters out the wrong people who won't benefit and therefore won't buy. And it establishes trust before any financial transaction has happened. When someone experiences your free product and thinks this is better than the paid things I've bought elsewhere, they're ready to be a paying customer. Free products are not charity. They're the most efficient trust-building mechanism available to solo creators. Build a great free product. Put it in front of the right people. Watch what happens.
