## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that no business school teaches about running a solo business.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Your energy is your primary resource, not your time. Time management is taught everywhere. Energy management is not. A low-energy hour is nearly worthless. A high-energy hour can produce days worth of value. Protect your peak state.

Idea 2 - Trust is a currency with higher value than credentials. Nobody asks for your degree before they buy your template. But if they've read your work for six months and feel like you understand them, they'll pay without hesitation.

Idea 3 - The first sale is the hardest. Not the tenth, not the hundredth. The first. Every sale after that involves a person who's easier to reach because you've already proven the concept to yourself. Get to that first sale fast.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for real-world solo business education.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three things you should do with every piece of content you create.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Repurpose it into at least two formats. A written post becomes a video script. A video becomes a newsletter section. A newsletter section becomes three short-form posts. One idea, multiple formats, multiplied reach.

Idea 2 - Link it to something you sell. Not aggressively. But every piece of content should have a natural connection to your offer. If someone finds your content useful, they should be able to take the next step immediately.

Idea 3 - Archive it. Keep a simple folder of your best work. Over time, this archive becomes a content library you can reference, update, and republish. Old content that performed well will perform again for new audiences.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for content operations and repurposing strategy.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons most online courses fail to deliver real results for buyers.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They're too long. Most courses contain 80% filler content that exists to justify the price. The best courses are ruthlessly edited to only what the student needs to get the result. Shorter and more useful always beats longer and padded.

Idea 2 - They don't create implementation moments. Watching a video is not learning. Learning happens when students apply something. The best course creators build action steps into every module, not at the end.

Idea 3 - They teach what the creator knows, not what the student needs. These are sometimes very different things. Build your curriculum starting from the student's desired outcome and work backward to what they need to learn.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for course creation and digital product education.
