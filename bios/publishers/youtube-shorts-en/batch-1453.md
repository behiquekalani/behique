## Short 2
**Title:** How to Use Social Media Without Letting It Use You (The Creator's Framework)
**Hook (0-3s):** Every minute you scroll without purpose is a minute your competitor is building.
**Script (3-55s):** Social media platforms are engineered to extract your attention. Your job as a creator is to go in, publish, engage with your audience, collect what you need, and leave. Not to browse. Not to consume passively. In and out. Give yourself a specific purpose before opening any app: respond to comments, post the content, research one thing, then close it. The creators building real businesses are deliberate about this. They use the platforms as distribution tools, not as entertainment. Your attention is your most finite resource. Guard it.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for intentional creator strategies that protect your most valuable resource.

## Short 3
**Title:** Why Your Side Project Stays a Hobby (And the Fix Is Simpler Than You Think)
**Hook (0-3s):** A project without a deadline is a dream. Deadlines make things real.
**Script (3-55s):** The single biggest difference between a hobby project and a business is that a business has targets and timelines. A hobby gets your leftover time and energy. A business gets scheduled blocks and non-negotiable deadlines. If your side project doesn't have a launch date, a revenue goal, and a weekly schedule, your brain treats it as optional. Make it non-optional. Put the deadline on the calendar. Tell someone about it. Create external accountability. Then show up for that block like you would for a client. That's the whole shift.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for accountability frameworks that turn your project into a business.
**Tags:** [sidehustle, solopreneur, entrepreneurmindset, productivity, goals, businessbuilding, accountability]
