## Script 1

**Hook:** How to write content that attracts clients without running a single ad. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three types of content that attract clients organically.

Type one: the problem-aware post. Describe the exact symptoms of a problem your ideal client has. When they read it, they recognize themselves. Recognition creates connection. Connection leads to DMs.

Type two: the process reveal. Walk through how you approach a specific challenge. Don't hide your method. Sharing your process proves competence better than any claim could. Clients hire people whose thinking they've already seen work.

Type three: the result post. Share a real outcome you achieved for yourself or a client. Be specific. Not "increased traffic." "Increased search traffic by 40 percent over 90 days using this exact method." Specific results attract people who want that specific result.

These three content types are a complete organic client acquisition system.

**CTA:** Save this and write one of each type this week. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** The one-page business plan that actually works for solo operators. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
The one page has five sections.

Section one: the customer. One sentence describing the exact person you serve. Age, situation, specific problem.

Section two: the offer. One sentence describing what you sell and what it does for them.

Section three: the price. Your pricing structure. What you charge and for what.

Section four: the distribution. How people find you. Which platform, which content type, which frequency.

Section five: the revenue goal. A specific monthly number and how many sales it requires to hit it.

That's it. If you can't fill in all five sections clearly, you don't have a business plan. You have an idea. The clarity that comes from filling in each section is where the strategy begins.

**CTA:** Follow for the full breakdown of each section. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Three habits of creators who consistently grow their income month over month. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Habit one: they review their numbers weekly. Revenue, email subscribers, content engagement. Not obsessively. Strategically. You can't improve what you don't measure. Weekly reviews catch trends before they become problems.

Habit two: they test something new every month. A new format, a new offer, a new price. Controlled experimentation is how successful businesses evolve. If every month looks exactly the same, growth has plateaued.

Habit three: they talk to their customers regularly. Not surveys. Conversations. What did you find most useful? What's still frustrating you? What would you pay more for? This real-time feedback shapes the product roadmap better than any data dashboard.

**CTA:** Follow and implement all three habits starting this week. (5 sec)
