## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to grow your digital product business without paid advertising.

**Main Point (45s):**
Method one: consistent organic content. Pick one platform and publish three to five times per week. This builds an audience slowly but it builds one you own. Organic followers who found you through content are more engaged than paid traffic because the algorithm matched them to your topic. Method two: collaborations and cross-promotion. Partner with one creator in an adjacent niche per month. Swap audience introductions. Write for their newsletter, appear in their content, contribute to their community. Shared audiences grow both of you faster than either could grow alone. Method three: referral systems. Your existing buyers are your best marketing channel. Add a simple referral ask to every delivery email. Give buyers a reason to share, whether that is a discount for their referral, a bonus, or simply a genuine ask. Word-of-mouth is free and it carries social proof built in.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The difference between writing that informs and writing that sells, explained clearly.

**Main Point (45s):**
Informative writing shares facts, concepts, and frameworks. It answers the question "what is this." Educational writing in this mode builds credibility and earns trust but rarely directly drives purchases. Persuasive writing shares all of that plus emotional context, personal stakes, and a specific desired outcome. It answers the question "why does this matter to you right now." The difference in results between the two is significant. Great business writers switch between these modes deliberately. They inform to establish authority. They persuade to drive action. A post that is purely informational is a credibility deposit. A post with persuasive structure at the end is a withdrawal. Your content strategy should have both. Most solo business owners write almost entirely in the informational mode and then wonder why content does not convert. Balance the modes.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that happen when you stop trying to be everything to everyone online.

**Main Point (45s):**
First, your content gets sharper. When you write for one person with one problem, every word earns its place. There is no filler because you know exactly what your reader needs to hear and nothing else is relevant. Sharp writing is what gets saved, shared, and remembered. Second, your offer converts better. A product that serves everyone is trusted by no one. A product designed for one specific person with one specific problem is immediately recognizable as the solution when the right person encounters it. Specificity is the engine of conversion. Third, your growth accelerates. Counterintuitively, narrowing your focus attracts more people, not fewer. The specific person who sees themselves in your content does not just buy. They tell other people exactly like them. Referral traffic from a specific audience is the most targeted marketing there is.
