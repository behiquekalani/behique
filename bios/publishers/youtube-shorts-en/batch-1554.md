## Script 1

**Hook:** How to use your writing to build trust before you ever make an offer. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Trust is built in three layers through writing.

Layer one: competence. You demonstrate that you know what you're talking about through specific, detailed information. Anyone can have opinions. Opinions backed by specifics signal expertise.

Layer two: character. You show who you are through your opinions, your values, and your willingness to take a stance. Neutral content doesn't build relationships. Opinionated, principled content does.

Layer three: consistency. Someone who has been showing up with valuable content for six months has earned more trust than someone who published one great piece. Recency and regularity both matter.

When your audience experiences all three layers, the sale becomes much easier. You're not convincing a stranger. You're making an offer to someone who already respects your thinking.

**CTA:** Follow and start building each layer intentionally. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** What no one tells you about the first three months of a solo business. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three truths about the beginning that most people don't prepare for.

Truth one: you will produce work you're not proud of. That's not a problem. That's the cost of developing your voice. The only way out is through. Publish anyway.

Truth two: engagement will be low even when your content is good. Early distribution is limited because you haven't built the relationships or the algorithm trust to spread your work widely. Low engagement early means nothing about your future potential.

Truth three: the business model will change at least once. What you plan to sell in month one is often not what you end up selling in month six. Stay flexible. The market will show you what it wants if you pay attention.

**CTA:** Save this and revisit it when the early phase feels discouraging. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Three questions to ask yourself before creating any piece of content. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Question one: who is this for? Not a demographic. A person. Name them in your head. What problem are they trying to solve right now? If you can't answer that clearly, you don't know who you're creating for and the content will be unfocused.

Question two: what do I want them to think, feel, or do after reading this? One outcome. Not three. What's the single most important thing you want them to walk away with? Design the whole piece around delivering that one thing.

Question three: why would they share this? Content that gets shared either teaches something useful, challenges a belief, or validates an experience the reader has had but never seen articulated. Design your content to hit at least one of those three.

**CTA:** Follow for a complete content creation framework. (5 sec)
