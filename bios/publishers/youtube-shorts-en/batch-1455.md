## Short 1
**Title:** Why "I'll Start When I'm Ready" Is the Most Expensive Phrase in Business
**Hook (0-3s):** Waiting to feel ready has a real dollar cost. Here's what it is.
**Script (3-55s):** Every month you delay starting costs you the compounding that month would have started. Content compounds. Skills compound. Audiences compound. Product revenue compounds. A person who starts one year before you, everything else being equal, will have a twelve-month head start on all of that compounding. The readiness you're waiting for doesn't come from preparation. It comes from reps. You get ready by doing the thing, not by getting ready to do the thing. The cost of waiting isn't just time. It's all the compounding that time would have started.

## Short 2
**Title:** What Every Solopreneur Gets Wrong About Passive Income (The Real Version)
**Hook (0-3s):** Passive income isn't really passive. It's deferred active income.
**Script (3-55s):** Every digital product that "sells while you sleep" was built during a lot of hours you were very much awake. The passive part is the revenue once the system is running. The active part is everything that comes before. The product creation, the audience building, the marketing system, the sales page optimization. None of that is passive. But here's the payoff: you do the work once and it pays you repeatedly. The goal isn't to avoid work. It's to front-load the work so future-you benefits from what present-you built. That's the real model.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for the honest breakdown of how digital income actually works.
**Tags:** [passiveincome, solopreneur, digitalproducts, onlineincome, creatoreconomy, realisticadvice, entrepreneurship]
