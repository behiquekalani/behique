**Short 1:**
SEO Title: How to Validate a Digital Product Idea Before Building It
Hook: Do not spend months building something nobody will buy.
Script: Before you build, validate. The fastest validation method: post the concept as a question to your existing audience. Ask if they would pay for a solution to this specific problem. If ten or more people say yes enthusiastically, you have signal. If people ask follow-up questions about how it works, you have demand. If nobody responds, adjust the problem. Better validation: pre-sell it. Write a description. Set a price. Tell people if you get ten pre-orders you will build it this month. Pre-sales are the highest form of validation because people put money behind the answer. Build after the proof.

**Short 2:**
SEO Title: The Psychology Behind Why People Buy Digital Products
Hook: People do not buy information. They buy certainty.
Script: The buyer of a digital product is not buying the PDF. They are buying the belief that their problem will be solved. They are buying confidence that they picked the right solution. They are buying belonging to a group of people who figured this out. Your marketing job is to provide all three forms of certainty before they buy. Certainty about the outcome. Certainty that they can implement it. Certainty that you know what you are talking about. Every testimonial you show, every specific claim you make, every clear framework you share is reducing doubt and building the certainty they need to buy.

**Short 3:**
SEO Title: From Zero to First Digital Product Sale Step by Step
Hook: Here is the exact path from zero to your first digital product sale.
Script: Week one. Pick the problem. Find it in the questions you get asked or the problems you solved for yourself. Week two. Write the solution. A guide, a checklist, a template. Something a person could open and use immediately. Week three. Put it on Gumroad or Lemon Squeezy. Free account. Five minutes to set up. Week four. Tell 100 people directly. Post it on social media. Send it to your email list if you have one. DM it to people who have asked you about this topic. The first sale comes from the people who already know you. After the first sale, you have proof of concept. Then you scale.
