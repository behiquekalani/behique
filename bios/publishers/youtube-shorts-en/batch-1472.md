## Short 1
**Title:** The Morning Routine Nobody Talks About (Because It Is Too Simple)
**Hook (0-3s):** Your morning routine does not need to be two hours. It needs to be intentional.
**Script (3-55s):** The productivity world has turned morning routines into a performance. Ice baths, journaling, meditation, exercise, visualization, reading. If that works for you, great. But most people need something simpler: start the day on your terms before the world demands something from you. Twenty minutes. One thing that moves your body. One thing that focuses your mind. Then open your most important task, not your email, not your phone, your most important task. Do that first. That is a morning routine. The simplicity is the point.

## Short 2
**Title:** Niching Down Feels Like Losing. It Is Actually How You Win.
**Hook (0-3s):** The more specific you get, the bigger your impact. That sounds backwards. It is not.
**Script (3-55s):** When you talk to everyone, you resonate with no one. When you talk to one specific person in one specific situation with one specific problem, that person feels seen. That feeling is rare. That feeling converts. Most creators stay broad because they are afraid of leaving people out. But by trying to include everyone, they connect with no one. Go narrow. Define your ideal person so specifically it feels uncomfortable. Then speak directly to them. The broader market will still find you. But your core audience will love you.

## Short 3
**Title:** Why Useful Content Beats Interesting Content Every Single Time
**Hook (0-3s):** Interesting gets one view. Useful gets subscribers.
**Script (3-55s):** The most followed creators are not the most entertaining ones. They are the most useful ones. They teach something. They solve something. They demonstrate something. Every time you watch them, you walk away knowing something new or doing something differently. That is usefulness. And usefulness is the foundation of loyalty. Entertainment can keep someone watching for a moment. Utility keeps them coming back for years. Before you post, ask: does this make someone better at something? If yes, post it. If not, rethink it.
