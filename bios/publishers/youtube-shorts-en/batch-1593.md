## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Why templates outperform courses as a product, and what to do about it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Courses require commitment. The buyer has to set aside time, follow a curriculum, and do the work. Most buyers don't. Average course completion rates hover around ten to fifteen percent. That means most of your customers never get the result, which leads to buyer's remorse and lower word-of-mouth.

Idea 2: Templates require almost nothing. The buyer opens the file, fills in their information, and they're done. They get the outcome in minutes instead of hours. That fast win creates satisfaction, which creates referrals, which creates more sales without more marketing.

Idea 3: The smartest move is to bundle. Offer the template as the core product and add a short video walkthrough as a bonus. The template does the heavy lifting. The video handles the questions. You keep the simplicity that makes templates convert while adding the depth that justifies a higher price point.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The business model that fits your personality is worth more than the optimal one.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: There are dozens of ways to make money online. Courses, coaching, freelancing, newsletters, affiliate marketing, agency, SaaS. They all work for someone. The question is which one you will actually sustain. If you hate being on camera, YouTube is a bad fit regardless of the income potential.

Idea 2: Fit matters more than potential. A business model you love at a hundred dollars a day is better than one you hate at five hundred. Because the one you love you'll keep building, improving, and scaling. The one you hate you'll abandon when it gets hard, which it always does.

Idea 3: The way to find your fit is to test small. Try the format for sixty days. Not to get rich, just to see if you enjoy the daily work. Pay attention to which tasks you do before everything else and which ones you avoid. Your natural behavior is your best business model signal.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Here's the three-step process for validating a business idea in a weekend.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Step one is demand research. Spend two hours on Reddit, YouTube comments, and Amazon reviews in your target niche. Write down every problem that comes up more than once. These are real problems people are already articulating. You don't have to guess what the market needs. They're already telling you.

Idea 2: Step two is competition research. Find three to five products or services already solving the problem you identified. Note what they charge, what they include, and what the reviews say is missing. The gap in what's available is your product opportunity.

Idea 3: Step three is a minimum viable offer. Create a simple landing page describing the product and put it in front of your target audience. You can use social media posts, community posts, or direct messages to people with the problem. If ten people express genuine interest in two days, the idea is real. If nobody responds, pivot before you build.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and validate your next idea before you build a single thing.
