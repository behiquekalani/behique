## Script 1

**Hook:** Why most online businesses fail in the first year and how to avoid it. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three failure patterns repeat themselves constantly.

Pattern one: building before validating. Spending months creating a product no one asked for and then wondering why no one buys. Validate with conversations and presales before building anything significant.

Pattern two: inconsistent effort. Two weeks on, three weeks off, then a big push, then silence. Audiences don't grow on inconsistent schedules. The algorithm doesn't reward intermittent effort. Steady and boring beats sporadic and intense.

Pattern three: optimizing instead of creating. Spending more time tweaking the website than writing content. More time analyzing metrics than making offers. The highest-leverage activity is almost always creation and distribution. Everything else is management.

Most failures come from avoidance, not from lack of skill. Stay in the arena.

**CTA:** Save this and identify which pattern you're most at risk for. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** How to create content that sells your product without ever feeling salesy. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three content types that sell without feeling like a pitch.

Type one: the transformation story. Share your own journey from the problem to the solution. At the end, mention that your product teaches the method. You're not pitching. You're telling the truth.

Type two: the problem breakdown. Deeply diagnose a problem your audience has. Walk through why it happens, what it costs them, and how it can be solved. Then mention that your product is the systematic solution. You've already demonstrated value before the mention.

Type three: the client result. Share what happened when someone used your method or product. Their result is your proof. You don't have to argue for your product. The outcome argues for itself.

All three build context before the mention. Context makes mentions feel helpful, not pushy.

## Script 3

**Hook:** The truth about passive income that no one says out loud. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three things that are actually true about passive income.

Truth one: passive income requires active income first. The systems, products, and audiences that generate passive income require significant upfront work. You're not escaping work. You're shifting when the work happens.

Truth two: passive income is not zero maintenance. Products need updating. Customers need support. Traffic needs sustaining. "Set and forget" is a marketing line, not a reality. The real benefit is that maintenance is far less than creation.

Truth three: passive income rewards people who can tolerate delayed gratification. The revenue from work you did six months ago feels amazing. Getting there requires six months of work with minimal immediate reward. Most people quit before the delay ends.

Know the truth before you pursue the goal. It makes the path sustainable.
