## Short 1 — Ownership Era
**Title:** The Internet Made Ownership Possible — Most People Still Work For Others
**Hook (0-3s):** The internet made it possible to own your income. Most people still don't.
**Body (3-55s):** We're living in the most unprecedented era for individual economic ownership in human history. For the first time, you don't need a company, a publisher, a network, or a record label. You need an internet connection and something worth saying. The shift from labor to assets is available to almost anyone. An email list is an asset. A YouTube channel is an asset. A digital product catalog is an asset. An audience with trust is an asset. These things generate income while you sleep, while you're sick, while you're on vacation. The ceiling on assets is your creative output. The ceiling on your time is 24 hours. One of these scales. The other doesn't.

## Short 2 — Pricing as Filter
**Title:** Low Prices Don't Help Your Customers — They Hurt Them (The Psychology)
**Hook (0-3s):** Low prices don't attract more customers. They attract worse ones.
**Body (3-55s):** This sounds counterintuitive until you understand price psychology. Price signals commitment. When a customer pays $10 for a course, their brain categorizes it as low stakes. They don't engage deeply. They don't implement. They don't get results. When they pay $1,000, the investment creates psychological pressure to act. The same information produces completely different outcomes based solely on price. Beyond commitment, price filters customers by seriousness. Serious buyers who get results become testimonials, referrals, and case studies. Unserious buyers who don't implement create refund requests and bad reviews. Raising your prices and delivering real value is not just better revenue. It's better customers who actually win.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What's your current price point? What would you charge with full confidence?

## Short 3 — First Principles Thinking
**Title:** Think From First Principles — The Mental Model That Breaks Every Limit
**Hook (0-3s):** Most problems aren't solved because people think with borrowed assumptions.
**Body (3-55s):** First principles thinking is the practice of stripping a problem down to its fundamental truths before building solutions. Instead of asking "how does everyone else do this?" you ask "what is actually true about this problem, and what logically follows from that?" The example that makes this concrete is Elon Musk and battery costs. Instead of accepting that batteries cost what they cost, he asked what they're made of and what those materials actually cost. He found the accepted price was twenty times the materials cost. By rejecting the conventional price as a given, he could design a path to a dramatically cheaper battery. Apply this everywhere. What assumption is everyone accepting that you should question?
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What industry assumption do you think is wrong? Tell me below.
