## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three distribution channels every one-person business should understand.

**Main Point (45s):**
Channel one: social media for discovery. Platforms like TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube Shorts are discovery engines. They put your content in front of people who do not know you yet. The goal on these platforms is not to sell. It is to earn attention and direct people to a place where a deeper relationship can form. Channel two: email for conversion. Email is where discovery turns into sales. You can sell effectively on social media, but email gives you a direct line to your most interested audience. Conversion rates from email are consistently higher than from social platforms. Treat email as your primary sales channel, not social. Channel three: search for compounding. SEO-optimized content on YouTube, a blog, or a podcast builds an audience over time from people actively searching for answers. It is slower to build but it compounds indefinitely. A good search-optimized post can bring traffic for years without any additional effort.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** What writing for money actually looks like in practice.

**Main Point (45s):**
Writing for money is not a single skill. It is a set of overlapping skills that compound together. The first is copywriting. The ability to write words that drive a specific action. A purchase, a click, a reply. Copywriting is learned by studying great sales pages and practicing short-form persuasion daily. The second is content writing. The ability to write things that attract and retain an audience over time. This is not about selling in every post. It is about providing consistent value that earns the right to occasionally sell. The third is storytelling. The ability to make information feel personal and emotionally relevant. Facts inform but stories persuade. The most effective digital product marketers are strong storytellers first. All three skills reinforce each other. Working on one improves all three. You do not need to master them simultaneously. Pick one and start there.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three metrics that actually matter for a solo business, and three that do not.

**Main Point (45s):**
The metrics that matter: revenue per audience member, email open rate, and conversion rate on your offers. These tell you whether your audience is engaged and whether your offers are working. Low revenue per audience member usually means your offers are weak or your audience alignment is off. Low email open rate means your list is getting cold. Low conversion rate means your messaging is unclear. The metrics that do not matter: follower count, likes, and video views. These feel good and occasionally lead to real business outcomes. But chasing them as goals leads you to optimize for entertainment instead of trust. An account with ten thousand highly engaged followers in a specific niche will outperform an account with five hundred thousand passive followers almost every time. Measure the things that connect to revenue, not the things that feel like status.
