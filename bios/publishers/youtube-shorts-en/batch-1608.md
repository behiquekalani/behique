## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** How to go from zero followers to your first digital product sale in 90 days.

**Main Point (45s):**
Month one: Pick your niche and start posting daily. Do not create a product yet. The goal is to understand what resonates with the people you want to serve. Post 30 pieces of content and track what gets attention.

Month two: Build your lead magnet based on what your audience responded to most. Something free, specific, and immediately useful. Start building your email list. This is your distribution channel that you own.

Month three: Create a simple paid product based on the most common question your audience asks. Price it between 27 and 97 dollars. Write a clear sales page. Email your list and make the offer. Your first sale will come from people who already know and trust you.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The identity shift that changes everything about how you build online.

**Main Point (45s):**
Most people build online while still thinking of themselves as an employee. They wait for instructions. They look for the right template. They want someone to tell them what to do. That mindset produces inconsistent, timid content and businesses that never fully launch.

The shift is this: start thinking of yourself as a publisher. Publishers have opinions. Publishers ship on schedule. Publishers are not waiting for inspiration, they are operating a system that produces output on a reliable cadence.

The second you make that shift, you stop asking permission and start making decisions. Your content gets sharper. Your products get clearer. Your business starts to feel real because you are treating it like one.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three pricing mistakes that are costing solo creators thousands of dollars.

**Main Point (45s):**
Mistake one: Pricing based on your time instead of the outcome you create. If your product saves someone $5,000 in mistakes, pricing it at $47 because it took you two hours to make is leaving enormous money on the table.

Mistake two: Offering too many price points. A menu of options triggers comparison and indecision. One clear offer converts better than three tiered ones in almost every case for solo creators just starting out.

Mistake three: Discounting instead of adding value. When someone pushes back on price, most creators drop the price. A better response is to add something that makes the original price feel like an even better deal. Protect your pricing anchors.
