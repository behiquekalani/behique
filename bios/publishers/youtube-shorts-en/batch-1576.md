## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three signs you need to raise your prices as soon as possible.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - You're resentful of the work. When you feel underpaid for a project, resentment creeps into the quality of the work. That's not fair to the client or to you. Raise the price until you genuinely look forward to delivering on it.

Idea 2 - You attract clients who constantly push back. Budget clients attract other budget clients through referrals. Raising your prices changes the client type you attract. Better clients, better projects, better referrals.

Idea 3 - You haven't raised prices in over a year. Inflation, skill growth, and market rates all move over time. If your price is the same as last year, you're effectively earning less. Review and raise annually at minimum.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three content strategies that produce buyers instead of just followers.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Problem-focused content. Don't write about solutions first. Write about problems in detail. When someone reads a post that describes their exact problem, they'll look at whatever you recommend to solve it.

Idea 2 - Demonstration content. Show the product working. Show the process in action. Show a transformation happening in real time. Watching someone use a thing builds more desire than reading about what it does.

Idea 3 - Objection-addressing content. Identify the five reasons people don't buy your offer and make individual pieces of content addressing each one. By the time they reach your sales page, the hesitation is already handled.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things every beginner writer gets wrong in their first six months.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They try to sound impressive instead of clear. The goal of good writing is not to demonstrate vocabulary or complexity. It's to make the reader understand something quickly. Clarity is the skill. Impressiveness is a trap.

Idea 2 - They skip editing. First drafts are always worse than they feel in the moment. Every good writer edits aggressively. Read it aloud. Cut what doesn't need to be there. The edit is where writing actually gets made.

Idea 3 - They don't publish consistently enough. Writing improves from publishing, not from drafting. The feedback loop of putting work out and seeing what resonates is irreplaceable. Publish before you're confident. That's how confidence forms.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for writing education from someone building in public.
