## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that change when you stop thinking like an employee and start thinking like a business owner.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - You stop trading time for money and start building assets. An employee's value resets to zero at the end of each pay period. A business owner's assets, content, products, and relationships, compound over time.

Idea 2 - You take full responsibility for outcomes. There's nobody to blame for a bad month. That's uncomfortable at first. Then it becomes empowering, because it also means you get full credit for good months.

Idea 3 - You invest before you earn. Employees expect a paycheck before they give their best work. Business owners invest time, energy, and sometimes money into building something before any return comes.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for business ownership mindset and strategy.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to use your existing knowledge to generate revenue this month.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Offer a paid consultation. If you have 45 minutes and a skill someone needs, you can sell that time. Platforms like Calendly and Stripe make this setup a one-hour project. One consultation validates the market and pays you immediately.

Idea 2 - Write a paid guide. Take one process you execute regularly and write it up step by step. Sell it as a PDF. This takes a weekend to produce and can sell indefinitely.

Idea 3 - Teach a live workshop. Host a 60-minute session on a specific topic you know well. Charge for attendance. Even a small group at $50 to $100 each produces meaningful revenue and creates content and testimonials you can use afterward.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for practical revenue generation education.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three copywriting rules that will improve every piece of content you write.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Write to one person. Not a group. Pick one real person you know who represents your reader and write directly to them. This changes the tone immediately. It becomes warmer, more specific, and more persuasive.

Idea 2 - Use the word "you" more than "I." People care about their own situation first. Make the content about them. "You'll be able to do X" converts better than "I show people how to do X."

Idea 3 - Remove every word that doesn't add meaning. After you write something, read it aloud and remove every word that slows you down without adding clarity. Tighter writing is almost always better writing.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for copywriting education.
