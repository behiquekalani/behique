## Script 1

**Hook:** How to validate a digital product idea before building it. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Step one: write a one-paragraph description of the product. What does someone get? What problem does it solve? Who is it for? If you can't write that paragraph clearly, the idea isn't developed enough yet.

Step two: share that description with five people in your target audience. Not friends and family. People who actually have the problem. Ask them: "Would this solve a real problem for you? Would you pay for it?" Their exact words will tell you whether you're on the right track.

Step three: offer a pre-sale. Tell ten people you're building it and offer them early access at a discount in exchange for payment now. If no one buys, the idea needs refinement. If even two people pay, you have a signal worth building toward.

**CTA:** Follow for the full validation framework. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** Freedom in business is built on three specific skills. Here they are. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Skill one: writing. Everything in business comes down to communication. Proposals, sales pages, emails, content, all of it is writing. The person who writes clearly and persuasively controls every conversation and every transaction.

Skill two: distribution. You can build the best product in the world and it means nothing if no one sees it. Distribution is how you get your work in front of the right people, consistently. This includes content creation, SEO, outreach, and referrals.

Skill three: offer construction. Building an offer that communicates value clearly and makes buying feel obvious. Not manipulation. Just clarity. Clarity is the thing that turns traffic into revenue.

Master writing, distribution, and offer construction and you can build a business in almost any niche.

**CTA:** Save this and rate yourself on each skill right now. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** What separates a $1K month from a $10K month in a solo business. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
At $1K a month, most people are selling time, doing freelance work, and trading hours for income. The ceiling is your available hours.

The jump to $10K requires three things.

One: a productized service or digital product. Something where the delivery doesn't scale directly with your time.

Two: an audience that trusts you. Not a huge one. A focused one. A few hundred people who read everything you publish will out-earn tens of thousands of passive followers.

Three: a consistent offer. Not launching once and hoping. Making your offer visible in your content regularly so new people always have a clear path to buying.

The $10K ceiling breaks when your income is tied to value delivered rather than hours worked.

**CTA:** Follow for the full path from freelancer to solo business owner. (5 sec)
