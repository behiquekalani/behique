## Script 1

**Hook:** How to use social proof before you have any customers yet. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three ways to build credibility without a testimonial.

One: document your own results. Show the outcome you achieved using the method you're selling. Before and after. Numbers when possible. Your own story is proof of concept.

Two: show the process in public. Share your thinking, your work, your drafts. When people see the depth of your approach, they trust the product without needing someone else to validate it first.

Three: use specificity as a signal of expertise. General claims are cheap. Specific claims require knowledge. "I help freelancers raise their rates" is general. "I help B2B freelancers restructure their proposals to justify a 40 percent rate increase" is specific. Specific = expert.

You can build trust before you have a single customer. Use these three methods until the testimonials start coming in.

## Script 2

**Hook:** Three questions to ask before you commit to a business idea. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Question one: is there a clear, specific person who has this problem? Not a demographic. A real individual with a frustration you can name. If you can't picture one specific person, the market isn't defined yet.

Question two: is there evidence that people are already spending money on this problem? Books, courses, services, consultants. An existing market is a good sign. It means people consider the problem serious enough to pay for solutions. You're not trying to create demand. You're finding existing demand.

Question three: can you reach this person? Where do they spend time? What do they read? What communities are they in? Reachability is what turns a good idea into a real business. Ideas without distribution are just ideas.

**CTA:** Save this and run your current idea through all three questions. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why your content bio is one of the highest-leverage things you can fix today. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three elements every creator bio needs.

Element one: who you help. Not your job title. Not "passionate about business." The specific person you create for. "I help first-generation college grads build freelance income while finishing school." That person knows immediately whether they should follow.

Element two: what you help them do. One clear outcome or transformation. What changes for them because of your content? Make it specific and desirable.

Element three: a call to action. One link. Not five links. One. Where do you want someone to go when they visit your profile? Your email list, your product, your best piece of content. One place. One decision.

A bio that converts is the cheapest traffic improvement you'll ever make.

**CTA:** Follow and fix your bio today using these three elements. (5 sec)
