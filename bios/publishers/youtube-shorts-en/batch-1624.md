## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** The exact steps to take when you have an idea for a digital product.

**Main Point (45s):**
Step one: Write a one-sentence description of the outcome. Not the product, the outcome. If you cannot do this in one clear sentence, the product is not clear enough yet.

Step two: Find five to ten people who match your target buyer and ask them this specific question: "How much would you pay to get that result today?" Not "would you buy this?" People say yes to that question and then do not buy. Ask about money directly.

Step three: If at least three of them name a number that works for you, build a minimum version and pre-sell it. If nobody names a workable number, rethink either the product or the audience.

The goal of this process is to find out if real people will pay real money before you spend real time.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** How to make your content do more work without creating more content.

**Main Point (45s):**
Strategy one: Repurpose aggressively. One long-form post becomes a thread, a short video script, a newsletter section, and three standalone social posts. You are not creating more, you are distributing existing ideas across more surfaces.

Strategy two: Optimize your top performers. Find your five best-performing posts from the last 90 days and update them, repost them, or build sequels from them. Your best ideas deserve more than one exposure.

Strategy three: Build internal links across your content. Mention related posts, direct people to other resources you have made, create a reading path for new followers. This turns isolated pieces into a system that keeps people engaged longer and builds trust faster.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things experienced solo creators know that beginners usually learn the hard way.

**Main Point (45s):**
First: distribution is harder than creation. Making a good product or writing a great post is manageable. Getting it in front of people who need it is the actual hard part. Plan your distribution before you create, not after.

Second: your second launch will almost always outperform your first. You know more about your audience, your offer is sharper, your copy is tighter, and you are less nervous. Do not judge your potential from your first attempt.

Third: relationships are infrastructure. The creators who grow faster are usually the ones who invest in genuine connections with other builders in their space. Collaborations, cross-promotions, and referrals are how distribution compounds. Build relationships before you need them.
