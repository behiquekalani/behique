## Short 1
**Title:** The Solopreneur's Framework for Making Better Decisions Faster
**Hook (0-3s):** Slow decisions are expensive. Here's how to get faster without getting reckless.
**Script (3-55s):** Most decisions in a solo business don't need a week of consideration. They need a clear framework applied in ten minutes. Here's the one I use: is this reversible or irreversible? If it's reversible, make the decision in 24 hours and move. If it's irreversible, take the full time you need. Most decisions are reversible. You can unpublish a post. You can refund a customer. You can pause a service. The decision paralysis most solopreneurs experience comes from treating reversible decisions like permanent ones. Speed up on the reversible ones. Everything else flows from there.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for decision-making frameworks that keep your business moving.

## Short 2
**Title:** Why Most Solopreneurs Plateau and How to Break Through to the Next Level
**Hook (0-3s):** The plateau isn't a sign you've hit your ceiling. It's a sign you need new skills.
**Script (3-55s):** Every solopreneur hits a point where what got them here stops working. The strategy that took them from zero to $2K a month doesn't take them to $5K. The skills that built the first audience don't build the next ten thousand followers. The plateau is actually useful information. It tells you precisely what skill or system you haven't built yet. The path through a plateau is always the same: identify the next skill required, invest in building it, apply it consistently until the growth resumes. The plateau is not a wall. It's a signal.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for plateau-breaking strategies and the skills that unlock each next level.

## Short 3
**Title:** What Every Solopreneur Should Know About Selling Before They Try to Sell
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people are bad at selling because they think selling is convincing. It isn't.
**Script (3-55s):** Great selling is diagnosing. Before you ever make an offer, you're finding out whether this person actually has the problem your product solves. If they do, and your product solves it, the sale is almost already made. The pitch is just confirming what they already feel. Bad selling tries to persuade someone who doesn't need the thing. Good selling finds the person who already wants the outcome and shows them that your product is the path. The shift: stop trying to convince and start asking the questions that reveal whether there's a fit. Then let the fit do the selling.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for sales mindset and strategy that makes selling feel natural.
**Tags:** [sales, solopreneur, selling, entrepreneurship, digitalproducts, businessgrowth, creatoreconomy]
