## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here are three things every successful one-person business has in common.

**Main Point (45s):**
First, a clear offer. Not ten services or five products. One clear thing that solves one clear problem. The person who buys from you needs to understand what they are getting in thirty seconds or less. Second, consistent content. Not viral content. Consistent content. Showing up every week builds the trust that eventually turns an audience into customers. Third, a direct revenue model. They are not waiting for brand deals or sponsorships. They have a product, a service, or a membership they own and sell directly. No middleman, no platform dependency. Those three things. Clear offer, consistent content, direct revenue. That is the whole model.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Writing online is a business skill. Here is why most people underestimate it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Writing is how you think in public. When you write a post and publish it, you are training yourself to clarify your ideas. And clarity is what sells. The first thing writing teaches you is your own positioning. When you struggle to explain what you do in one sentence, the writing process forces you to find the sentence. The second thing it teaches you is what your audience cares about. The posts that get engagement tell you what problems people want solved. That is market research you never had to pay for. The third thing is distribution. A consistent writing habit builds an audience before you have a product to sell. When the product is ready, the buyers are already there.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons digital products beat services for solo business owners.

**Main Point (45s):**
Reason one: no time cap. A service business trades hours for money. When your hours are full, income stops. A digital product can sell ten times in one day without any extra work from you. That is a fundamentally different relationship with time. Reason two: margin. When you sell a service, you have costs in the form of your time, tools, and overhead. When you sell a digital product, your cost of delivery after the first sale is nearly zero. The margin on a well-priced digital product is almost always higher. Reason three: leverage. One product can reach a global market. You are not limited to clients in your city or your network. Anyone with a problem your product solves is a potential buyer. That scalability is what makes digital products worth building.
