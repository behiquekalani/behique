**Short 1: The Imposter Trap**
Hook (0-5s): [Text: "Who am I to teach this?"] If you've asked yourself this question, this is for you.
Content (5-50s): Here's the thing about imposter syndrome that nobody explains properly. You're not comparing yourself to your ideal standard. You're comparing yourself to the most experienced people in your field. That's an unfair fight. You don't need to be the top expert to teach something. You need to be ahead of the person you're teaching. If you figured out how to do something that someone else is currently stuck on, you have something worth sharing. Your perspective is the differentiator. Your specific path to the solution. The way you explain it. The framework you developed. None of that exists anywhere else. That's your moat.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe for more on building confidence and a creator business at the same time.
Description: Imposter syndrome isn't a sign you're not qualified. It's a sign you're comparing yourself to the wrong standard. Here's the reframe.

**Short 2: Sell Before You Build**
Hook (0-5s): [Visual: blank page with price tag already on it] The most counterintuitive move in digital products is the most profitable one.
Content (5-50s): Sell the product before it exists. I know that sounds backwards. Here's why it works. Writing a sales page forces you to articulate exactly what the product does and who it's for. Showing it to people gives you real market feedback before you invest time building. Pre-orders fund the build. And the people who buy before it exists become your most engaged early customers. They give you feedback while you build. They become your testimonials at launch. They share it because they're invested. The pre-sell is not a hack. It's the most honest way to validate that your idea has real demand.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe if you want to build products that sell before they're done.
Description: Selling before you build isn't a trick. It's the most reliable validation strategy for digital products. Here's exactly how.
Tags: pre-sell, validation, digital products, solopreneur, product launch
