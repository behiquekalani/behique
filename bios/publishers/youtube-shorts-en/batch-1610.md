## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** How to write a sales page that converts even if you hate selling.

**Main Point (45s):**
Section one is the headline. It should name the specific outcome the buyer gets. Not what the product is, but what changes for them. Lead with transformation.

Section two is the problem section. Describe the pain your buyer is feeling right now, in the exact words they would use to describe it themselves. When someone reads this and thinks "how did you know?", you have done this right.

Section three is the offer. What is it, what do they get, and what happens after they buy. Keep this extremely clear. No jargon, no filler. Then give them one button to click with no friction and no confusion about what happens next.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The content types that consistently build the most loyal audiences.

**Main Point (45s):**
Type one: Contrarian takes. Saying something that challenges a commonly held belief in your niche immediately separates you. People either strongly agree or strongly disagree, both of which drive engagement and conversation.

Type two: Personal frameworks. When you share the specific way you think about or approach a problem, you give people a mental model they can adopt. Frameworks are highly shareable because they provide structure, not just ideas.

Type three: Progress documentation. Sharing where you are in your journey, honestly and regularly, creates a relationship with your audience. They feel invested in your outcome. When you win, they celebrate with you. That emotional investment converts to loyalty.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Why your niche is not too crowded and how to find your angle in it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Every niche feels crowded from the outside. But most of the visible content in any niche is surface-level and generic. The opportunity is always in going deeper, being more specific, and having a perspective that is genuinely yours.

Your angle is the intersection of three things. What the niche talks about. What you actually know and have experienced. And what the audience within that niche is not getting from current creators.

Find that gap and own it. The personal finance niche is crowded. Personal finance for first-generation immigrants navigating student loans while sending money home is not crowded. That is an angle. That specificity is your competitive advantage.
