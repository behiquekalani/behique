## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Why your content isn't growing even though you're posting every day.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Frequency without quality improvement is just noise. If you're posting daily but not getting better at the craft, the output plateaus. Growth requires improvement, not just repetition. Review your analytics weekly. What got more saves? More shares? More comments? Do more of that intentionally.

Idea 2: The second issue is posting without engaging. Social platforms reward accounts that create conversations, not just accounts that broadcast. After you post, spend twenty minutes engaging with comments, replying to people in your niche, and contributing to conversations already happening. Activity drives reach.

Idea 3: The third issue might be the wrong platform for your content type. Not every format works everywhere. If you're a writer, written platforms will serve you better than video. If you think visually, image-heavy platforms will outperform text-only ones. Match your natural content style to the platform that rewards it.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The three building blocks of a sustainable solo business income.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The first building block is a core product. Something that delivers a specific result, priced to reflect the value of that result, and optimized for delivery without your constant presence. This is the engine. Everything else is built around it.

Idea 2: The second building block is consistent traffic. A content channel, an email list, or both. Something that reliably brings new people into your world every week. Traffic without a product is wasted. A product without traffic is invisible. Both have to run simultaneously.

Idea 3: The third building block is trust infrastructure. Testimonials, case studies, a body of public content, transparent communication. Buyers don't buy from strangers. They buy from people they've observed being helpful over time. Your trust infrastructure is what converts traffic into revenue. Build all three and the business sustains itself.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** How to write a sales page for a digital product with no writing experience.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: A sales page has five elements. A headline that describes the exact outcome. A description of the problem the buyer is currently experiencing. A description of what life looks like after the product solves it. A breakdown of what's included and the transformation each piece enables. And a clear call to action with the price.

Idea 2: The language should match how your buyer actually speaks. Not polished marketing language. The words they use when they're frustrated with the problem. If they say "I can never stick to a schedule," your copy should include those exact words. Mirroring their language makes them feel understood, and feeling understood precedes buying.

Idea 3: The strongest single addition to any sales page is a specific testimonial. Not "great product" but "Before this template, I was spending three hours every Monday planning my week. Now it takes me twenty minutes and I actually follow through." Specificity in testimonials gives future buyers a precise vision of what's possible.
