## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things every solo business owner should audit every quarter.

**Main Point (45s):**
Audit one: your offer suite. Are your products still solving the right problems for your current audience? Markets shift and so do needs. A product that was your best seller twelve months ago might be outdated. Review each product and ask: is this still the best solution I can offer? Audit two: your content performance. Which posts generated the most engagement, saves, and sales? Double down on those topics and formats. Which generated the least? Stop doing them. Let data guide your creative decisions, not assumptions. Audit three: your time allocation. Track one week of your working hours honestly. Are you spending most of your time on tasks that generate revenue or on tasks that feel productive but do not? Identify the highest-return activity in your business and find ways to do more of it. These three audits, done every ninety days, will keep your business aligned with what is actually working.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why the one-person business is actually a lower-risk model than most people think.

**Main Point (45s):**
The conventional view is that solo business is risky because there is no guaranteed income. But consider what happens in a traditional job when the market shifts. Your employer makes a business decision and you lose your income entirely, with no asset to show for the years you contributed. In a solo business, if one product stops selling, you still have your audience, your skills, your relationships, and your body of work. You pivot. You build another product. Your income might dip but your assets remain. The risk in traditional employment is concentrated and binary. The risk in solo business is distributed and recoverable. Most people compare the security of employment at its best to solo business at its worst. A fairer comparison is employment at its worst to solo business at its realistic middle, and that comparison looks very different.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three tools every solo business owner should know how to use before paying for ads.

**Main Point (45s):**
Tool one: a simple email marketing platform. Mailchimp, ConvertKit, Beehiiv. Pick one and learn it fully before moving to anything else. The ability to collect emails, send sequences, and segment your list is foundational to every other revenue-generating strategy you will ever run. Tool two: a basic sales page builder. Gumroad, Carrd, or any landing page tool that lets you write a compelling offer and collect payment without needing a developer. Remove the technical barrier between your idea and your customer's ability to buy. Tool three: a content scheduling tool. Buffer, Later, or any tool that lets you write posts in advance and schedule them to go out automatically. The time you save by batching content creation is time you put back into product development and business strategy. These three tools together cover distribution, sales, and audience building. Master them before adding anything else.
