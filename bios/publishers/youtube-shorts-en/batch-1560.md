## Script 1

**Hook:** How to build a simple content system that keeps you consistent without burning out. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three components of a sustainable content system.

Component one: the idea bank. Keep a running document of every interesting thought, observation, or question you have. Don't wait for inspiration on publishing day. Fill the bank daily so you always have raw material.

Component two: the writing block. A fixed time each day or week where you convert ideas into finished content. Thirty minutes is enough. The block should be protected, consistent, and free of distractions. Same time, same place builds a habit.

Component three: the scheduling buffer. Maintain at least a week's worth of content ahead of your publishing schedule. This removes the pressure of "I have to post today." A buffer creates calm. Calm creates better work.

Systems beat motivation. Motivation is unpredictable. Systems are reliable.

**CTA:** Follow and build your first version of this system this week. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** The three-part formula for an offer that sells itself. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Part one: the specific person. Name exactly who this is for. Not "entrepreneurs." Not "business owners." "Freelancers who have been in business for less than two years and are struggling to raise their rates." When the right person reads that, they immediately know it's for them.

Part two: the concrete outcome. What does their situation look like 30 days after buying? Describe it in their words, not yours. Use the language from your customer conversations. The more precisely you reflect their desired outcome, the stronger the desire to buy.

Part three: the reason to act now. Not a fake countdown timer. A real reason. Limited access, a founding member price, a cohort that closes. Give people a genuine reason why now is better than later and your conversion rate goes up without manipulation.

**CTA:** Save this and apply all three parts to your current offer. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** What every successful solo creator has in common. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three things show up in every solo creator success story.

One: they picked one channel and mastered it before expanding. Spreading across five platforms at once builds nothing. Going deep on one builds an audience. Then you expand from strength.

Two: they treated it like a business from day one. Not a hobby, not a side project. A business. That means tracking metrics, setting revenue goals, building systems, and making decisions based on data, not feelings.

Three: they stayed visible through the uncomfortable middle phase. When engagement was low, they kept publishing. When sales were slow, they kept improving the offer. The middle phase is where most people quit and where the successful ones separate themselves.

Pick your channel. Run it like a business. Stay visible through the middle.

**CTA:** Follow for the systems that keep you visible even when it's hard. (5 sec)
