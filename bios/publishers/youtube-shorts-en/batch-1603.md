## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here's the fastest way to find out what digital product to build next.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Ask your existing audience directly. Not "what do you want me to make?" but "What's the one thing you're still stuck on after following my content?" The gap between what your content provides and what they still need is your next product. You're not guessing. You're asking.

Idea 2: If you don't have an audience yet, go to the comment sections of your largest competitors. Look for patterns in what people ask about in the comments. "Do you have a template for this?" "Is there a step-by-step guide?" These are people telling you exactly what to build. Go build it.

Idea 3: The third source is your own purchase history. What have you bought recently that helped you? Can you create a version of that for your audience? Buying something that helped you is strong validation that the category works. Your personal experience as a buyer is often your best product research.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why building for the long term actually produces faster results in the short term.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Long-term thinking forces you to build real assets. A genuine audience, a real product, an authentic brand. These take time to build but they compound. Short-term thinking produces tactics that work once and need to be replaced constantly. The time spent building a tactic is lost. The time spent building an asset returns.

Idea 2: The counterintuitive part is that long-term investments show results sooner because they align incentives. When you're building something real, you naturally stop doing things that don't work and double down on things that do. The feedback loop is cleaner. The decisions are simpler.

Idea 3: The creators who burn out are usually the ones chasing short-term metrics. Every algorithm change devastates them. Every slow week feels like failure. Long-term builders don't panic at a slow week because they know the asset is growing even when the metrics aren't moving visibly. Perspective is its own performance strategy.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to get your first ten customers without any following.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Direct outreach is the most reliable method. Find ten people who have the problem your product solves. Message them personally. Not with a pitch but with a useful observation related to their specific situation. Offer to help. If they engage, introduce the product. One in ten usually converts. Ten outreach messages equals one customer.

Idea 2: Community participation is the second method. Join three online communities where your ideal customer hangs out. Spend two weeks being genuinely helpful. Answer questions. Share useful information. Don't mention your product. When people know you as helpful, they'll ask what you offer. That natural interest converts at a much higher rate than a cold pitch.

Idea 3: Partner with one person who has your audience. Offer them something in exchange for introducing you to their followers. A free version of your product, a guest post, a collab piece of content. One introduction from a trusted voice to their existing audience is worth fifty cold outreach messages. Borrowed credibility converts.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for practical first-customer strategies that don't require an audience.
