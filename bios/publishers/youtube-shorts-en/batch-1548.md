## Script 1

**Hook:** Why your freelance rate is too low and how to fix it. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Most freelancers underprice for three reasons.

One: they compare themselves to the cheapest competitor. The cheapest competitor is chasing a race to the bottom. Don't compete there.

Two: they price based on time instead of value. If it takes you two hours and it's worth $2,000 to the client, charging $200 because it "only took two hours" is a mistake that punishes your skill.

Three: they're afraid of rejection. Charging more means some people say no. That's healthy. A high no-rate means your price is right. You want to be out of budget for the clients who don't value the outcome.

Raise your rate by 30 percent. You'll lose some prospects. The ones who stay are the ones who actually value what you do.

## Script 2

**Hook:** How content compounds into a business over time, explained clearly. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Content compounding works in three stages.

Stage one, months one through three: you're building the archive. Every post you publish is a data point. You're learning what resonates, what doesn't, and who shows up for your content. Very few people see this. It feels like you're shouting into silence.

Stage two, months four through eight: you start seeing patterns. Certain topics and styles outperform others. Followers begin to accumulate. Some posts get found weeks after they're published. The archive starts working for you.

Stage three, month nine and beyond: compounding kicks in. Old content brings in new readers. New readers discover old content. Your offer starts converting because trust has built up over time. The library is the asset.

**CTA:** Save this and revisit it when month three feels hard. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Three types of digital products you can create with nothing but your knowledge. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Type one: the written guide. A PDF or document that walks someone through a process you've already figured out. Best for tactical, step-by-step information. Fast to create, easy to distribute, and buyers understand the value immediately.

Type two: the template. A pre-built framework someone can customize and use. This could be a spreadsheet, a document, a Notion system, a content calendar. Templates sell because they save time. The value is the starting point, not the information.

Type three: the email course. A sequence of emails delivered over days or weeks that teach a specific topic. Lower perceived cost for the buyer, higher conversion than a static PDF, and it builds your email list simultaneously.

Start with whichever format matches the type of knowledge you have.

**CTA:** Follow to learn how to build and sell each format. (5 sec)
