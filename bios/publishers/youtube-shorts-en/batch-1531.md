## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three questions to ask before building any digital product.

**Main Point (45s):**
Question one: who is this for, exactly? Not a demographic. A specific person with a specific problem in a specific situation. The sharper your answer, the better your product will be and the easier it will be to market. Question two: what does this person do right before they need my product? Understanding the trigger moment helps you place your marketing where the need is freshest. If someone searches a specific phrase or faces a specific situation right before they would buy, that is where you show up. Question three: what does success look like for the buyer three months after purchase? If you cannot describe the transformation, you do not know your product well enough yet. The transformation is the product. The PDF, the video, the spreadsheet are just the delivery vehicle. Know the destination first, then build the map.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why your first sale is worth more than money.

**Main Point (45s):**
When you make your first sale, three things happen that are more valuable than the dollar amount. First, your belief system updates. Before the first sale, part of you is waiting for evidence that this can actually work. The first sale provides that evidence. It shifts you from hoping to knowing. That psychological shift is foundational to everything that comes after. Second, you get a real customer to learn from. Not a hypothetical buyer. An actual person who paid and is now using your product. Their experience, their feedback, and their questions are gold. They will tell you exactly what to build next. Third, you prove the model is viable. One sale means the chain works. The content attracted someone, the offer converted them, the product delivered value. Now your job is to do it more systematically. The first sale validates the entire system.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to write content that generates sales without ever feeling pushy.

**Main Point (45s):**
Method one: teach the why behind the what. When you explain why something works, not just what to do, you demonstrate expertise. Expertise creates desire for more of your knowledge. That desire converts to sales without any direct pitch. Method two: share the result before the method. Lead with transformation. Show the outcome that someone achieved, then explain how the product contributed. Results-first framing is the most natural sales vehicle there is. Method three: use contrast. Describe the before state with enough detail that the reader recognizes themselves in it. Then describe the after state with equal clarity. The gap between before and after is where the purchase decision lives. When someone feels the before deeply and sees the after clearly, the product in between becomes the obvious solution.
