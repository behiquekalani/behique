## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** How to build a content library that works for you 24 hours a day.

**Main Point (45s):**
The concept is simple but most people execute it wrong. A content library is not just a folder of posts. It is a collection of interconnected pieces where each one points to others and all of them eventually point to your offer.

Step one: Create pillar content. These are your most comprehensive, most useful pieces on your core topic. These live permanently and rank in search or get reshared over time.

Step two: Create derivative content from each pillar. One pillar post can become five shorter pieces, a thread, a video script, and three social posts. You are not creating from scratch every time. You are redistributing depth across formats.

Step three: Make sure every piece has a clear path to your offer. A link, a mention, a call to action. Content without a destination is a dead end.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three things that separate a $100 product from a $1,000 product.

**Main Point (45s):**
First: The specificity of the outcome. A $100 product promises general improvement. A $1,000 product promises a specific measurable result within a defined timeframe. The buyer of the higher-priced product knows exactly what they are getting and when.

Second: The level of access and support. Higher-priced products often include community, feedback, accountability, or direct access. The price is not just for content, it is for the relationship and the structure around the content.

Third: The perceived risk reduction. Higher-priced products tend to have stronger social proof, clearer guarantees, and more detailed explanations of what happens after purchase. The more expensive the decision, the more confidence the buyer needs before clicking buy.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for more on positioning and pricing knowledge products.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Why consistency beats strategy for creators just starting out.

**Main Point (45s):**
Strategy matters. But strategy without execution is just planning. And most creators spend more time planning than actually creating.

Here is what consistency actually does that strategy alone cannot. It generates data. You learn what works by posting, not by thinking about posting. Consistent output creates a real feedback loop where your audience tells you with their attention what resonates.

It also builds discipline that outlasts motivation. Motivation peaks and crashes. The creator who has built a daily habit of creating does not need to feel motivated. They have made the decision in advance and the habit carries them through the uninspired days.

Start with consistency. Let strategy grow from the data you collect by showing up.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and commit to one piece of content per day for the next 30 days.
