## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** What to do in your first 30 days building a solo online business.

**Main Point (45s):**
Week one: Do not build anything yet. Spend the entire week studying your target audience. Read what they write. Watch what they watch. Understand the specific language they use to describe their problems. This research will make every future decision easier.

Week two: Pick one platform and post every single day. The platform does not matter as much as the commitment. You are training yourself to create consistently and you are starting to understand what resonates.

Weeks three and four: Based on what got the most engagement, design your first offer. It does not need to be big. A focused guide or template around the topic your audience responded to most is enough. Price it, write a clear description, and make it available.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for the week-by-week breakdown of building from zero.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons the creator economy is the best opportunity for young people right now.

**Main Point (45s):**
Reason one: The barrier to entry is lower than it has ever been. Free tools, free distribution, and global reach are available to anyone with a phone. The cost to start is essentially zero.

Reason two: The skills you build are transferable. Writing, video, sales, marketing, product development. Every skill you develop running an online business is valuable in any other context. You are building a skill set while building an income.

Reason three: Your personal brand is cumulative. Every piece of content you publish adds to a growing body of work. Unlike a job where your work belongs to someone else, your online presence compounds and belongs entirely to you. Starting young gives you the longest possible compounding runway.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and start building your runway today.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The biggest difference between creators who make money and those who do not.

**Main Point (45s):**
It is not talent. It is not following size. It is not the platform they chose or the niche they picked.

The difference is this: the ones who make money have made an explicit decision to treat their online presence as a business. They set income goals. They build systems. They make offers. They measure what works.

The ones who do not make money are still in content mode. They are focused on expression, on building an audience, on improving their craft. All of those things have value. But until you treat it like a business by making offers and tracking results, the money will not follow. The mindset shift is the unlock.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe if you are ready to make the shift from creator to business owner.
