## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here's the difference between building an audience and building a business.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: An audience is a group of people who find you interesting. A business is a system that generates income. You can have a large audience and a tiny business. You can also have a small audience and a healthy business. The two are related but not the same.

Idea 2: What converts an audience into a business is an offer. Specifically, a product or service that solves a real problem for your audience at a price they'll pay. Without that, you have influence without income. The business only starts when something is for sale.

Idea 3: The order that works is: define your ideal customer, create content that speaks directly to them, build the product they need, and sell it through the content. This order means you're building the audience for the product, not building a product for the audience you happen to have. That alignment is what makes the conversion work.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three content types every solo business needs to grow and convert.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The first type is educational. This is what builds trust. You teach something useful, people learn, they come back for more. Educational content positions you as someone who knows what they're talking about. It's the majority of what you should create. Roughly sixty percent.

Idea 2: The second type is relatable. This is what builds connection. You share your process, your failures, your honest take on something. People see themselves in your story. Relatable content is what makes followers feel like they know you. Without it, you're informative but not compelling.

Idea 3: The third type is promotional. This is what generates revenue. You tell people what you sell, who it's for, and why they should get it. This should be roughly twenty percent of your output. Most creators either skip this entirely out of fear or do it too much and alienate their audience. The balance of all three is what builds a sustainable content business.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** If you want to build income online, this is the skill you need first.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Before strategy, before platforms, before products. You need to be able to communicate value clearly. If you can't explain in plain language why someone should buy what you offer, no strategy will save you. Copywriting is the meta-skill that everything else depends on.

Idea 2: Copywriting is not manipulation. It's clarity. It's the ability to describe a problem so accurately that the person with that problem feels understood, and then describe a solution so specifically that they can picture the result. That combination is what drives purchases.

Idea 3: You can develop this skill for free. Study the sales pages of products you've bought. Analyze why the language worked on you. Read the best newsletters in your industry. Notice what makes you click. Practice writing one sales message a week. In three months, you'll be a different communicator and a different business owner.
