**Short 1: The Myth of the Big Audience**
Hook (0-5s): [Text on screen: "You don't need 10,000 followers"] Nobody told you this. You don't need a big audience to make money with digital products.
Content (5-50s): Here's the math. If you sell a $37 product and you need $1,000 a month, that's 27 sales. 27 sales. Not 10,000 followers. A product that solves a specific problem for a specific person converts at 2 to 5 percent. So you need 540 to 1,350 targeted people to see it. Not a million followers. The mistake most new creators make is waiting until they have scale before they try to monetize. The truth is: a small, focused audience with a specific pain will outperform a large, general audience every single time. Build the right audience, not the big one.

**Short 2: Your Morning Decides Your Day**
Hook (0-5s): [Visual: blank notebook, coffee] The first 30 minutes of your morning set the trajectory of your entire day.
Content (5-50s): Most people start their morning by checking their phone. That means the first information their brain receives is other people's priorities, news, notifications, stress. Your brain is most plastic, most creative, and most focused in the first 90 minutes after waking. If you give that to a scroll session, you've handed your peak performance window to an algorithm. The swap is simple. Before you touch any screen: write three things. What you're grateful for. What must happen today. And one sentence describing your goal this week. Takes five minutes. Changes everything.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe for daily tactics on building a focused, high-output life.
Description: Your morning routine is the highest-leverage habit you can build. Here's the five-minute version that works.
Tags: morning routine, productivity, solopreneur, habits, deep work
