## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here's how to stop trading time for money without quitting your job first.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: You don't have to quit to start. You need ten hours a week. Two hours on weekdays after work, more on weekends. In those ten hours, you build the product, create the content, and grow the audience. The job funds the operation while the business develops. This is the most sustainable transition model.

Idea 2: The goal for the first six months is not to replace your income. It's to prove the model. Make the first ten sales. Write the first hundred posts. Build the first thousand subscribers. These are milestones that tell you the business is real, not just revenue targets that create pressure to rush.

Idea 3: The transition happens when one of two things is true. Either the business income matches your expenses and you can afford to leave, or you have enough momentum and data to project when that will happen. Don't quit on hope. Quit on numbers. The patience required to wait for those numbers is what separates successful transitions from failed ones.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** What I wish I knew before building my first online product.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: I wish I had validated it before building it. I spent weeks on a product that I thought people needed. I launched it and almost nobody bought. The problem wasn't the execution. The problem was that I assumed demand instead of confirming it. One week of validation would have saved me a month of wasted work.

Idea 2: I also wish I had priced it higher from the start. I underpriced out of fear and attracted the wrong buyers. The people who bought at the low price didn't implement the product, didn't get results, and some asked for refunds. The first time I raised the price significantly, the buyers were more engaged and the outcomes were better.

Idea 3: The third thing I wish I knew is that the first product is not the final product. It's a learning tool. The first version tells you what to add, what to cut, and who it actually serves. Treating the launch as an experiment rather than a definitive statement removes the pressure and makes the feedback easier to receive.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The content format that builds the deepest trust the fastest.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Case studies. Not testimonials, not general advice, but detailed case studies that walk through a specific situation, the actions taken, and the measurable results. Case studies are trust at scale. They show that your methods work in the real world, not just in theory.

Idea 2: A good case study follows a simple structure. Who was the person before? What problem were they facing? What specific actions did they take using your method? What were the results? How long did it take? The more numbers and specifics you can include, the more persuasive it becomes.

Idea 3: You don't need a large client base to produce case studies. One good case study from a real customer who got a real result is enough to start. Reach out to anyone who has used your product and gotten value. Ask if they'd be willing to share their story in exchange for a feature. Most people say yes. That story becomes your most powerful piece of sales content.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for content strategy built around proof, not just advice.
