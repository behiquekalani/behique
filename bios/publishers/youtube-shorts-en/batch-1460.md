## Short 1
**Title:** How to Turn Your Knowledge Into a Digital Product This Week (Step by Step)
**Hook (0-3s):** You already know enough to build a product someone will pay for.
**Script (3-55s):** Start by answering three questions. What do you know how to do that someone else struggles with? What result can you reliably help someone achieve? And what's the fastest way to deliver that result? The answers give you your product. Package it simply: a short course, a template, a guide, a workshop. Price it between $27 and $97 to start. Write one page explaining who it's for, what problem it solves, and what they'll be able to do after. Put it on Gumroad or a similar platform. Share it with your audience. You have a digital product now.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for step-by-step product creation frameworks built for beginners.

## Short 2
**Title:** The Daily Habit That Made Me a Better Thinker and a Better Creator
**Hook (0-3s):** The one daily habit that compounds faster than any other: deliberate reading.
**Script (3-55s):** Not doom scrolling. Not news feeds. Actual books, essays, or long-form articles read with full attention and a pen in hand. Thirty minutes of deliberate reading per day is 180 hours a year. That's equivalent to roughly 45 full books, or a deep education in almost any subject you choose. The compounding isn't just in the knowledge. It's in the thinking patterns that reading instills. The solopreneurs who build the most interesting businesses and create the most compelling content are almost universally voracious readers. It's not a coincidence.

## Short 3
**Title:** Why Solo Businesses Fail in Year One (The Mistake Everyone Makes)
**Hook (0-3s):** Most solo businesses fail in year one for the same reason. Here it is.
**Script (3-55s):** They run out of motivation before they build momentum. Motivation is a terrible fuel for a long-term build. It's inconsistent, weather-dependent, and dries up the moment something gets hard. The builders who make it past year one aren't more motivated. They've built systems that make showing up the default even when the feeling isn't there. A locked calendar block. A weekly accountability partner. A clear metric to track. These things don't require motivation. They require setup. Build the system in year one and the system carries you through the hard parts of year two.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for year-one survival frameworks that build momentum before motivation runs out.
**Tags:** [businessfailure, solopreneur, entrepreneurship, systemsthinking, accountability, habits, businessgrowth]
