## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that make a digital product feel premium without a premium production budget.

**Main Point (45s):**
First: an extremely clear structure. Premium products guide the user through a logical progression where each section builds on the last. Confusion makes people feel like they wasted money. Clarity makes them feel smart for buying.

Second: specificity in every recommendation. "Exercise more" feels cheap. "Do 15 minutes of zone-two cardio three times a week for the first four weeks before adding resistance training" feels premium. The specificity signals expertise and makes the advice actionable.

Third: a confident, direct voice. Uncertainty is contagious. If your product hedges every recommendation with "it depends" and "results may vary," it feels like a disclaimer, not a guide. Write as someone who knows what works and is telling you clearly.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for more on creating knowledge products that people rave about.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** How to turn one idea into six months of content.

**Main Point (45s):**
Start with your central idea. Let us say it is "solo creators should build products before services." That is your thesis.

Expand it into sub-topics: why services cap income, what the ideal first product looks like, how to validate before building, pricing strategies, common mistakes, real examples from people who did it, the mindset shift required, and what happens after the first product sells.

That is eight sub-topics. Each one can become a long post, a short video, a thread, or a newsletter issue. If you create three pieces per sub-topic, that is 24 pieces from one idea. Cross-post each piece in three formats and you have 72 pieces of content from a single central thesis.

That is a full quarter of content from one idea if you post every day.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and start mapping out your central idea today.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The difference between building an audience and building a business.

**Main Point (45s):**
An audience is people who consume your content. A business is an audience that buys from you. These are not the same thing and confusing them is one of the most common and expensive mistakes creators make.

You can have 50,000 followers and make nothing because you have never made an offer. You can have 500 subscribers and make $10,000 a month because every part of your system is designed to convert.

The shift from audience to business requires three things: a clear offer, a reliable way to present that offer to new people, and a consistent follow-up system for people who did not buy the first time. Followers are the beginning, not the finish line.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe if you are ready to build a business, not just an audience.
