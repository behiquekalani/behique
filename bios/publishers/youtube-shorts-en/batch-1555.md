## Script 1

**Hook:** How to go from zero to first sale with a digital product, step by step. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Step one: identify the problem. Talk to five people in your target audience. Ask what they're struggling with. Write down their exact words.

Step two: build the minimum product. Not the full version. The smallest version that solves the core problem. A PDF, a template, a guide. Done in a week, not a quarter.

Step three: write a simple sales page. Problem, solution, outcome, price, buy button. Four sections. No design degree needed.

Step four: share it. Send the link to three people you know who have the problem. Post about it once. Tell people directly what it is and what it does.

Step five: close the loop. Whatever feedback you get, use it. Your first sale is data, not just revenue.

The whole process from zero to first sale can happen in two weeks if you stop waiting for it to be perfect.

**CTA:** Follow for the full digital product playbook. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** The three types of online content and which one builds the best business. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Type one: entertainment content. High reach, low buying intent. Great for awareness and growth but rarely converts to revenue directly. Think memes, trends, relatable humor.

Type two: inspirational content. Medium reach, medium buying intent. Builds emotional connection but needs to be backed by practical content to create actual results.

Type three: educational and opinionated content. Lower initial reach, highest buying intent. The person who teaches you something specific and useful becomes someone you trust. Trust converts.

The best strategy uses all three, but foundations on educational content. Your core content should teach. Your supplementary content can inspire or entertain. The teacher becomes the trusted advisor. The trusted advisor makes the sale.

**CTA:** Save this and audit your last ten posts using these categories. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Three reasons solo businesses scale better than most people think. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Reason one: digital products don't require proportional effort to deliver. Build once, sell many times. The marginal cost of your hundredth sale is near zero. That's a fundamentally different math than service businesses where every delivery requires your time.

Reason two: content is a scalable distribution channel. A post published today keeps working tomorrow, next month, and next year. Your archive of content becomes an evergreen marketing engine that grows without additional effort.

Reason three: the one-person structure eliminates most overhead. No payroll, no office, no hierarchy. Every dollar of revenue converts to profit at a much higher rate than a traditional business. Lower costs with scalable products equals a model that scales quickly.

**CTA:** Follow to build the systems that make solo scaling possible. (5 sec)
