## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** How to write about what you know and turn it into a product people will pay for.

**Main Point (45s):**
Step one: List every skill you have that you use regularly. This includes professional skills, hobbies, things you have figured out through trial and error, and knowledge you have that most people in your circle do not.

Step two: For each item, ask who else needs this and would pay to skip learning it the hard way. Often the most valuable knowledge is not what you studied, it is what you discovered through your own experience.

Step three: Package the knowledge in the format that matches the urgency of the problem. A quick checklist or template works for simple processes. A guide or course works for complex skill-building. A community works for ongoing challenges that require accountability.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The system for building a solo business that runs without you being online all day.

**Main Point (45s):**
The foundation is asynchronous delivery. Your products should deliver value without you having to be present. Video courses, written guides, templates. these work while you sleep.

The second layer is automated communication. A welcome email sequence, a product delivery system, a FAQ that answers the most common questions before they become support tickets. Build these once and they run on repeat.

The third layer is scheduled content. You do not post in real time, you batch create and schedule in advance. A productive three-hour block on Monday can fuel your entire week of posts. You are designing a machine, not living inside of one.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for the full breakdown of building your asynchronous business system.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Why most people fail at building an online business and exactly what to do differently.

**Main Point (45s):**
The most common failure pattern looks like this. Someone gets excited, creates content for six weeks, sees slow growth, gets discouraged, pivots to a different niche, repeats. Nothing ever compounds because they keep starting over.

The fix is committing to one niche, one platform, and one core offer for a minimum of six months before evaluating whether to pivot. Most businesses that eventually succeed look like failures at the three-month mark.

The second thing to do differently is separate your output from your results. Your job in the first six months is to produce and learn. Results are a lagging indicator. They reflect work you did 90 days ago, not work you did this week. Keep producing.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for honest, realistic advice on what it actually takes to build this.
