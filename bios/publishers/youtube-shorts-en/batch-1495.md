## Short 1
**Title:** How to Price Your Offer So People Actually Buy
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people price based on what they think they're worth. You should price based on what the result is worth to the buyer.
**Script (3-55s):** Your price should be a fraction of the value you deliver. If your product saves someone $5,000 in mistakes, $297 is a no-brainer. If your service saves someone 10 hours a week and their time is worth $100/hour, $1,000/month is cheap. Stop pricing based on cost or effort. Start pricing based on the outcome you deliver. Then communicate that outcome clearly in your offer. When the buyer can see the math, the price becomes obvious. Value-based pricing always outperforms cost-plus pricing.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for pricing strategy. What outcome does your offer create? Comment the value in dollars or hours saved.
**Tags:** pricing strategy, solopreneur, digital products, online business, value

## Short 3
**Title:** The Mindset Shift That Turns Freelancers Into Business Owners
**Hook (0-3s):** Freelancers sell time. Business owners sell outcomes. The shift is mental before it's financial.
**Script (3-55s):** When you're a freelancer, you quote hourly rates and defend your hours. When you're a business owner, you quote project prices and defend your outcomes. The client doesn't want hours. They want the result. Package your skills into an outcome. "I'll build you a landing page that converts" is a business offer. "I charge $75 an hour for web design" is a freelance offer. Same skill. Different positioning. Different revenue. The shift requires you to stop thinking about what you do and start thinking about what the client gets. That's the whole transition.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for business owner strategy. Are you selling time or outcomes right now? Comment honestly.
**Tags:** freelance, solopreneur, business owner, positioning, online business
