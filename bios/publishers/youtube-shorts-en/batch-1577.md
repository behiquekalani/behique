## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that are different about building a business online in your twenties versus waiting until later.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - The compounding starts earlier. Content, audience, skills, and revenue all compound over time. Starting at 22 and building to 30 gives you eight years of compound growth. Starting at 35 gives you fewer runway years before major life responsibilities increase.

Idea 2 - You can afford to be wrong more often. Failing at 24 has lower stakes than failing at 40. The risk tolerance available in your twenties is a genuine asset. Use it.

Idea 3 - You develop business literacy early. Someone who's tried to sell something, attract an audience, and handle clients by 25 thinks differently than someone who hasn't. That business fluency affects every career and investment decision you'll ever make.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe if you're building early and want to do it right.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons why personal brand and business brand should be the same thing for most creators.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Trust travels with people, not logos. People buy from people they know and trust. If your audience trusts you personally, that trust extends to your products. A faceless brand has to build that trust from scratch.

Idea 2 - Personal brands are harder to commoditize. Two agencies selling the same service compete on price. Two individuals selling the same service compete on who the client wants to work with. You always win a market of one.

Idea 3 - Personal brand content is more sustainable to create. Writing as yourself about what you genuinely know and believe is much easier to maintain for years than maintaining a manufactured brand voice.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for personal brand strategy.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that happen to your business when you niche down more specifically.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Your content resonates more deeply. Specific content gets saved and shared more than broad content. A person who fits your niche exactly will share your post with everyone they know who also fits your niche.

Idea 2 - Pricing becomes easier to defend. Specialists charge more than generalists because specialization implies depth of expertise. The narrower your focus, the higher the perceived expertise, the more defensible your rate.

Idea 3 - You attract better referrals. Clients in a specific niche tend to know other people in the same niche. When you serve one well, they send you people exactly like them. Your ideal client base self-replicates.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for niche strategy and positioning education.
