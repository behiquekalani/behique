## Script 1

**Hook:** Three signs your content strategy is working, even when it feels like it isn't. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Sign one: strangers are saving your posts. Saves are a stronger signal than likes. When someone saves a post, they're telling the algorithm "I want to come back to this." That's a buying signal in disguise. Watch your save rate, not just your like count.

Sign two: you're getting DMs with questions. When people ask you follow-up questions about your content, it means they're engaged enough to want more. Those people are your warmest prospects. Respond to every single one.

Sign three: people are sharing without tagging you. They're sending your content to friends. That's organic word-of-mouth distribution. You won't always see it but it's happening if you're creating genuinely useful content.

Traction doesn't always look like traction. Look for these signals.

## Script 2

**Hook:** How to write a sales page that actually converts, in three steps. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Step one: start with the problem, not the product. Your buyer doesn't land on your page and immediately trust you. They land there with a problem. Lead with that problem. Describe it in their exact language. When they think "this person gets it," they keep reading.

Step two: make the outcome concrete. Not "you'll feel more confident." Confident doing what, exactly? "You'll be able to walk into a salary negotiation and ask for 20 percent more without second-guessing yourself." That's concrete. Concrete outcomes build desire.

Step three: remove the risk. The biggest barrier to buying is the fear of wasting money. A clear refund policy, testimonials from real users, or a sample of the content removes that fear. Make the decision feel safe and the conversion rate goes up.

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why building in public is the smartest growth strategy for a solo creator. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Building in public works because of three dynamics.

First, it creates a narrative. People don't just follow products. They follow stories. Sharing your process, your wins, your setbacks, and your decisions turns your business into a story people can follow.

Second, it builds trust before the sale. By the time you launch a product, your audience has watched you build it. They know your work ethic. They've seen your thinking. Trust is already there. The launch converts because the audience is warm.

Third, it attracts collaborators and customers simultaneously. The right post can bring in a partner, a client, and a future customer all at once. Visibility compounds over time.

The people who build in public out-grow the people who build in secret every single time.
