## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three revenue models for solo business owners, ranked by simplicity.

**Main Point (45s):**
Model one, the simplest: sell a single product. One thing, one price, sold directly. No complexity, no subscriptions, no services. Build the product, write the sales page, send traffic. This model teaches you the fundamentals of marketing and conversion without operational complexity. Model two: sell a product suite. Three to five products at different price points that serve the same audience at different stages. This model increases revenue per customer without requiring new customers. The upgrade path does the work. Model three: a combination of products and a recurring element. A core product plus a monthly membership, a newsletter subscription, or a retainer service. This model adds predictable monthly revenue that smooths out the volatility of product launch income. Start with model one. Move to model two once your first product is selling consistently. Layer in model three once you understand your customer well enough to serve them on an ongoing basis.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why clarity of audience beats clarity of product when you are just starting.

**Main Point (45s):**
Most new solo business owners start with a product idea and then try to find buyers. The sharper approach is to start with a clear picture of the buyer and then ask what they need. Here is the practical difference. If you know your audience is freelance graphic designers trying to get their first retainer client, you can build ten different products for them. A proposal template, a pricing guide, a client email script, a portfolio guide. The audience clarity is the business foundation. The products are secondary. If you start with a product but have no clear picture of who it is for, you will struggle with every marketing decision because you do not know who you are talking to. Audience first, product second. The product will be better, the marketing will be sharper, and the sales will come faster.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that make a solo business feel sustainable long term.

**Main Point (45s):**
First, the work aligns with your natural strengths. A business that requires you to constantly operate outside your strengths is exhausting. If you are a writer, build a business around writing. If you are better in conversation, build around coaching or community. The most sustainable businesses are the ones where the core activity feels natural, not forced. Second, the revenue model has recurring elements. Pure product launch income is a feast-or-famine cycle. Adding a recurring element, even small, like a newsletter subscription or a monthly resource, creates a baseline income that reduces financial stress. Third, the niche is one you have genuine curiosity about. Niches you find interesting sustain content creation. Niches you chose for strategic reasons alone run dry within months. The combination of strong strengths, recurring revenue, and genuine curiosity is what creates a business you can build for a decade.
