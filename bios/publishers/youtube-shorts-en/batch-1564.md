## Script 1

**Hook:** How to write an offer that converts cold readers into buyers. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Cold readers have three questions before they'll consider buying.

Question one: is this for me? Answer it in your first sentence. Name the person. Name the problem. Make it instantly recognizable.

Question two: can I trust this person? Answer it with specifics. Real results, real examples, real language that only someone with experience would use. Vague claims don't build trust. Specific details do.

Question three: is it worth the risk? Answer it with your guarantee or your social proof. Make the decision feel safe. A refund policy communicates confidence in your product. Testimonials communicate that others have already taken the risk and won.

Answer all three and the offer converts. Miss one and you lose readers at that point.

**CTA:** Save this and check your current offer against all three questions. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** Three things to do before you launch a digital product. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Thing one: email your warmest audience first. Before any public launch, send a personal email or DM to the people who have engaged with your content most. Give them early access. They're the most likely to buy and to give you honest feedback quickly.

Thing two: prepare three pieces of supporting content. A breakdown of the problem your product solves. A post about who it's specifically for. A post about the result it delivers. Publish these in the week before launch to warm up your audience.

Thing three: write a follow-up sequence. What do you send to someone who clicks but doesn't buy? A reminder email with added context, a FAQ answer, a customer story. The money is in the follow-up, not just the launch day.

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why your audience size matters less than you've been told. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three reasons a small audience outperforms a large one in a solo business.

Reason one: engagement rate. A small, targeted audience engages at 10 to 20 percent. A large, general audience engages at one to two percent. The smaller audience produces more responses, more DMs, and more sales per post.

Reason two: trust depth. People who have been following you for months have higher trust than someone who just found you. Depth of relationship, not breadth of reach, is what drives conversions.

Reason three: cost of service. A hundred customers you know deeply produce better outcomes and better testimonials than a thousand customers you know superficially. Your business is stronger with fewer, better customers.

Stop chasing followers. Chase the right relationships.

**CTA:** Follow for the strategy that builds a small but highly profitable audience. (5 sec)
