## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** The creator economy rewards specific people, not versatile people.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Trying to cover every topic attracts no one specific. When your content could be for anyone, it's actually for no one. The algorithm can't categorize it. Potential followers can't decide if it's for them. Specificity is the thing that makes people feel like you're speaking directly to them.

Idea 2: Going specific doesn't mean staying small. It means going deep on one topic until you are the authority on it. Then you can expand. The largest creators in any niche started by owning a very specific corner of it. The broad coverage came after the authority was established.

Idea 3: The test for specificity is simple. Can you describe your ideal audience in one sentence? Can you name the exact problem your content solves for them? If the answers are vague, the content will be too. Sharpen the who before you produce more of the what.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and let's get specific about who you're building for.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Here's how to turn your knowledge into a repeatable income stream.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The first step is identifying your teachable skill. Not everything you know is a product. Focus on the things you know that produce a clear, measurable outcome for someone else. Can you help someone get a job, save time, learn a software, or solve a specific problem? That's the foundation.

Idea 2: The second step is packaging it so it doesn't require your ongoing presence. A PDF guide, a recorded course, a template library, a prompt pack. The format should deliver the outcome without you being on a call every time. Scalability is the point.

Idea 3: The third step is placing it where buyers already are. Gumroad, Etsy, your own website, a landing page linked from your content. Distribution is what activates the product. The best product with no distribution earns nothing. Put it somewhere and point traffic to it consistently.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for more on building your first knowledge income stream.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Most people give up right before their content starts to compound.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Content growth is not linear. It looks flat for a long time and then it doesn't. The early months feel unrewarding because the feedback is minimal. But every piece of content is building the foundation. The algorithm learns what you make. New viewers discover old content. The library grows.

Idea 2: The compound effect in content is real. A video you made six months ago can start performing today. A post you wrote last year can go semi-viral this quarter. The older content doesn't expire. It accumulates. This is completely different from a salary where yesterday's work isn't still paying you.

Idea 3: The creators who make it are the ones who stayed consistent through the flat period. They didn't optimize for the first hundred views. They built the library. And when the compound effect kicked in, they had a body of work that captured the new attention and converted it. The library is the asset.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and keep building. The compound effect is real.
