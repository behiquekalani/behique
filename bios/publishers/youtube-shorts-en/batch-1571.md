## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things you must understand about your audience before you sell them anything.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Their specific problem, not the general category. "I want to make more money" is a category. "I've been freelancing for two years but I can't break $3,000 a month because I only have one client" is a specific problem. The second one is sellable.

Idea 2 - What they've already tried. If you know what solutions they've tried and why those failed, you can position your product as the thing that solves what the others missed. That positioning is extremely persuasive.

Idea 3 - The cost of not solving it. Financial, emotional, or in time. Understanding the real cost of the problem tells you how much the solution is worth. Price accordingly.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for audience research and product strategy.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons most content creators plateau at the same follower count for months.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They're posting but not improving. Volume alone doesn't produce growth. After every post, review what worked and what didn't. Deliberate practice applied to content creation produces compounding results.

Idea 2 - They're not collaborating. The fastest way to reach new audiences is to appear in front of existing ones. Guest posts, collaborations, podcast interviews, and cross-promotions all give you access to pre-warmed audiences.

Idea 3 - Their profile doesn't convert visitors to followers. If someone lands on your profile and doesn't understand what you do in five seconds, they leave. Your bio, pinned posts, and first impression matter more than most creators realize.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for growth strategy that actually moves the number.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three systems every solo creator should set up before they get busy.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - A content capture system. Ideas are fragile. When you have a good one, you need to capture it immediately in a trusted place. A simple notes app, a voice memo habit, or a weekly idea dump prevents good content from disappearing.

Idea 2 - A client onboarding template. Every new client should receive the same clear document that outlines expectations, deliverables, timelines, and payment terms. Do this once and reuse it forever.

Idea 3 - A weekly review habit. A 30-minute block at the end of each week to review what got done, what didn't, and what the priority is next week. Without this, reactive work replaces strategic work every time.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for solo creator systems and workflow education.
