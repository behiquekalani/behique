## Short 1 — Long Game Thinking
**Title:** Short-Term Thinkers Compete — Long-Term Thinkers Dominate
**Hook (0-3s):** Short-term thinkers compete. Long-term thinkers dominate.
**Body (3-55s):** The long game changes every decision you make. When you're playing a 10-year game, you stop optimizing for this month's income and start optimizing for relationships, skills, and assets that compound. You share knowledge freely because you know the returns arrive years later. You invest in people who can't help you right now because you know the network effects compound. You do the work even when nobody is watching because you understand that the audience comes after the body of work exists, not before. The people who seem to win easily are usually playing a decade game while their competitors are playing a quarter game. Extend your timeline and your strategy changes completely.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. Are you playing a long game or short game right now? Be honest below.

## Short 2 — Digital Products Basics
**Title:** You Already Have a Digital Product — You Just Haven't Packaged It
**Hook (0-3s):** You already have a digital product inside you. You just haven't packaged it yet.
**Body (3-55s):** A digital product is any knowledge, skill, or framework you possess, packaged in a format someone can buy and consume without your time being present. That means templates, guides, courses, toolkits, frameworks. Here's what most people miss. The gap between you and a digital product is not knowledge. It's packaging. You know things right now that someone else would pay to learn. The problem is you've known it long enough that it feels obvious. That's a feature, not a bug. Your obvious is someone else's breakthrough. Start with the question: what's the most common problem people ask me about? Package the answer. That's your first product.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What do you know that someone would pay $20 to learn? Think carefully and share below.

## Short 3 — The Failure Data Mindset
**Title:** Failure Isn't the Opposite of Success — It's the Path (Here's How)
**Hook (0-3s):** The people winning right now failed more than you've tried.
**Body (3-55s):** We see results and reverse-engineer a story of talent and smooth progression. It's almost always wrong. Every successful builder has a long list of failed projects, wrong bets, embarrassing outputs, and costly lessons. The difference between builders who make it and those who don't is not how often they fail. It's how they process failure. Failure is data. It tells you specifically what doesn't work, which is often more valuable than knowing what does. The faster you collect failure data, the faster you converge on what works. Treat every attempt as an experiment. Remove the shame. Keep the lesson. Ship the next version.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What's one failure that taught you something you couldn't have learned any other way?
