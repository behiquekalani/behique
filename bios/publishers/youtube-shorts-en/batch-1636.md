## Short 1 — The Reinvestment Rule
**Title:** Why Reinvesting Early Revenue Separates Good Businesses From Great Ones
**Hook (0-3s):** The temptation when first money comes in is to spend it. Resist that.
**Body (3-55s):**
- Early revenue is not profit. It is fuel.
- Every dollar reinvested in tools, education, and systems accelerates the flywheel.
- The businesses that grow fastest are the ones that reinvest the most aggressively early.
- Your lifestyle upgrade can wait. The business needs the capital more than you need the comfort.
- Six to twelve months of aggressive reinvestment changes the trajectory of the entire business.
- Delay gratification at the start. Amplify it later.

## Short 2 — The Proximity Effect
**Title:** Why Who You Spend Time With Matters More Than What You Study
**Hook (0-3s):** The people closest to you are setting your ceiling and your floor right now.
**Body (3-55s):**
- Your income tends to average toward the people you spend the most time with.
- Your ambition tends to match the room you are most comfortable in.
- This is not about abandoning people. It is about adding new rooms.
- Online communities, masterminds, and events expose you to new ceilings.
- New ceilings reset your sense of what is possible.
- Upgrade the rooms you spend time in.

## Short 3 — The Daily Output Minimum
**Title:** Set a Daily Output Minimum (Not a Maximum) for Creative Work
**Hook (0-3s):** The daily minimum is the most powerful creative commitment you can make.
**Body (3-55s):**
- A minimum is not a limit. It is a floor.
- On your worst day, you hit the minimum. On your best day, you far exceed it.
- The minimum keeps the streak alive. The streak is everything.
- Consistency is built in the bad days, not the inspired ones.
- Set a minimum so low it is embarrassing. Two hundred words. Five minutes of recording.
- Then show up every day. The compound effect does the rest.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe and tell me your minimum in the comments.
