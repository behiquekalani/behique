**Short 1: The True Fan Economy**
Hook (0-5s): [Text: "You need 100, not 100,000"] Kevin Kelly was right in 2008 and he's still right now.
Content (5-50s): 100 true fans who buy everything you release equals a sustainable creator business. Run the math with me. 100 fans at $100 a year in purchases is $10,000 in annual revenue. 100 fans at $300 a year is $30,000. The key word is true. Not casual followers. Not drive-by viewers. People who genuinely connect with what you create and look forward to every new thing you release. The path to 100 true fans is not mass appeal. It's deep specificity. Pick a narrow topic. Go deeper than anyone else. Attract fewer people who care more. That's the math that builds a real business.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe if you're building depth, not just chasing reach.
Description: 100 true fans is enough to build a real creator business. Here's the math and the path to get there.
Tags: true fans, creator economy, solopreneur, audience building, digital products

**Short 2: The Fear of Selling**
Hook (0-5s): [Text: "Not selling is more selfish than selling"] This reframe changed how I talk about my work.
Content (5-50s): If you have a product that solves a real problem, and you're too worried about seeming pushy to tell people about it, you're doing them a disservice. There is someone right now Googling the exact problem your product solves. They're spending time, energy, and probably money trying to figure it out. You already figured it out. You packaged the solution. And you won't tell them it exists because you're worried about their perception of you. That's backwards. Selling is communication. It's saying: I solved a problem you have. Here's how. Here's what it costs. The alternative is letting them keep struggling while your solution sits unannounced.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe for more honest takes on building and selling creative work.
Description: The fear of selling hurts your potential customers more than it helps them. Here's the reframe that makes promotion feel right.
Tags: selling, marketing, solopreneur, digital products, mindset
