## Short 1
**Title:** How to Write a Compelling Offer in 5 Minutes (The One-Sentence Test)
**Hook (0-3s):** If you can't describe your offer in one sentence, it's not ready to sell.
**Script (3-55s):** The one-sentence offer test: I help [specific person] achieve [specific result] in [specific timeframe or context]. Fill that in and read it out loud. If it sounds vague or generic, you're not done. "I help overwhelmed solopreneurs build a $3K/month digital product business without an existing audience" is specific. "I help entrepreneurs succeed online" is not. Specificity is respect for your customer's time. They're scanning dozens of offers. Give them a reason to stop and say "that's exactly what I need."
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for offer clarity frameworks that make selling feel natural.

## Short 2
**Title:** Why You Should Be Writing Every Day as a Solopreneur (Even If You Hate It)
**Hook (0-3s):** Writing is the one skill that makes every other skill better.
**Script (3-55s):** Writing forces clarity. Unclear thoughts become clear when you try to write them down. And clarity is the foundation of every other thing you do as a builder: your marketing, your offers, your content, your emails. You don't have to write publicly to get the benefits. Even a private journal of ideas compounds fast. But if you do write publicly, even a short thread or a quick post, you're building an asset that attracts the right people over time. Start with 200 words a day. See where it goes in 60 days.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for writing systems and prompts that build the habit without the friction.

## Short 3
**Title:** The Real Difference Between a Creator and a Content Machine (Be This One)
**Hook (0-3s):** A content machine outputs. A creator thinks. Pick which one you want to be.
**Script (3-55s):** The creator economy rewards depth, not volume. You don't need to post every day if what you post on Tuesday changes how someone thinks all week. The content machines pump out generic advice at high velocity. The real creators share the uncomfortable insights, the counterintuitive takes, the specific lessons from real experience. Those pieces get shared. They build real relationships. They convert to loyal buyers. Focus on saying one true, useful, specific thing rather than saying ten vague ones. Quality over volume, always.
**CTA (55-60s):** Subscribe for depth-first content strategy that builds real traction.
