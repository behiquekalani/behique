## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to build recurring revenue as a solo creator without a subscription model.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Retainer clients. Offer a monthly package with a defined scope. A set number of hours, a defined deliverable, or ongoing access. Retainers are predictable revenue that require one sale to start a multi-month relationship.

Idea 2 - Ongoing consulting. Some clients don't need a one-time project. They need a trusted advisor they can access regularly. Monthly calls, async feedback, or quarterly strategy sessions. This is high-value recurring work that clients gladly renew.

Idea 3 - Evergreen product funnels. A low-cost entry product that automatically leads to a higher-cost product. If the funnel is set up right, new buyers enter the sequence and upgrade without your direct involvement. Revenue that runs in the background.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for recurring revenue models and solo business education.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three pieces of content you should create right now if you want to attract paying clients.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - A detailed case study. Show a real result you produced for someone. Include the starting point, the method, and the outcome. This is the most persuasive content type for converting readers into clients.

Idea 2 - A process walkthrough. Explain how you approach your craft step by step. This demonstrates competence in a way that a general post about your skills cannot. It shows rather than tells.

Idea 3 - A specific opinion post. Take a position on something people debate in your industry. This attracts people who share your worldview and repels those who don't. You want clients who already agree with how you think.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for client attraction content strategy.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three reasons most people fail to monetize their knowledge even when they know a lot.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They don't believe their knowledge is valuable. What took you years to figure out is genuinely novel to someone who just started. You've normalized your expertise. The market hasn't.

Idea 2 - They wait for perfect packaging. The knowledge is solid but the course isn't done, the website isn't ready, the branding isn't right. These are completion-blocking excuses. Imperfect packaging that exists beats perfect packaging that doesn't.

Idea 3 - They don't talk about what they know. If you never tell anyone what you know, they'll never know to buy it from you. Visibility of expertise is a prerequisite to selling it. Start talking.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for knowledge monetization and creator education.
