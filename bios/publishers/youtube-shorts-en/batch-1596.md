## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here's why building in public is the fastest way to grow a solo business.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Building in public means sharing your process openly. The wins, the failures, the experiments, the numbers. Most people hide this because they're afraid of being judged for being small. But small and transparent is more compelling than polished and opaque.

Idea 2: When you share openly, people invest emotionally in your journey. They root for you. They comment. They share your content with people who have similar goals. This organic word-of-mouth is worth more than any paid promotion because it comes with a personal recommendation attached.

Idea 3: Building in public also holds you accountable. When you publicly commit to a goal, you're more likely to follow through. And the community that forms around your journey becomes your most loyal first buyers when you eventually launch. They were there for the whole story. They want to see you win.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The most underrated distribution channel for digital products right now.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: SEO-optimized content on platforms with built-in search is still massively underused by independent creators. Pinterest, YouTube, Etsy, and even Reddit drive consistent organic traffic to digital products without requiring a large following.

Idea 2: The key is intent-based traffic. Someone searching "email template for cold outreach" on Etsy is already a buyer. They have the problem, they know they want a solution, and they're on a purchasing platform. Showing up in those results puts you in front of buyers instead of browsers.

Idea 3: To use this effectively, study what your ideal buyer actually searches for. Not what they would search if they knew your product existed, but what they search when they first have the problem. Create content or listings that match that language exactly. Organic intent-based traffic is free and it's higher quality than most paid traffic.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** How to figure out what digital product to build if you have no idea where to start.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Start with your own life. What did you figure out that took longer than it should have? What process did you develop that you now wish you had when you started? What mistake cost you time or money that you could have avoided with better information? Your past struggles are product ideas.

Idea 2: Then validate the assumption that others have the same problem. Search for it on Google, Reddit, and YouTube. Look at the volume and tone of the conversation. If people are asking about it frequently and with urgency, the market is real. If the search results are thin, either the market is small or the problem is niche enough to be an advantage.

Idea 3: Finally, build the minimum version first. Not the full product you imagine. The smallest thing that fully solves the core problem. Launch that, get feedback, and let real buyer behavior tell you what to add. Most products get better faster by launching early than by planning longer.
