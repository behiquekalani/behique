## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to get your first ten paying customers without a big audience.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Direct messaging in communities. Find online communities where your target customer spends time. Contribute genuinely for a few weeks. Then mention your product in context when relevant. Warm community relationships convert far better than cold ads.

Idea 2 - The warm network offer. Tell the 50 closest people in your professional network what you've built. Ask them to share it with anyone who might benefit. You don't need to reach strangers when your network can make introductions.

Idea 3 - The beta customer offer. Launch at a reduced price in exchange for a testimonial and feedback. The lower barrier helps you get early buyers. The testimonials and case studies become your most powerful marketing asset for every sale after.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three habits of creators who consistently produce quality content without burning out.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - They consume intentionally. They read and watch things outside their niche, they study good writing across different fields, and they take notes. The creative input directly feeds the output quality.

Idea 2 - They separate creation from editing. Writing and editing at the same time is cognitively inefficient. Create freely in one session, then edit in a separate session with fresh eyes. This produces both speed and quality.

Idea 3 - They have a done standard, not a perfect standard. They know what "good enough to publish" looks like and they publish at that standard. The compulsion to perfect indefinitely is the most common creativity killer among skilled writers.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for sustainable content creation habits.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that will make your next product launch perform better than your last.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Build an audience before the launch, not during it. The launch day should be a moment of concentrated visibility, not the starting point of awareness. Spend four to six weeks warming up the audience before you announce the product.

Idea 2 - Use a waitlist. A waitlist creates urgency, signals demand, and gives you a ready-made list to email on launch day. Even a small waitlist converts at a much higher rate than cold traffic.

Idea 3 - Collect and use social proof before you launch. Testimonials from beta users, screenshots of results, or even enthusiastic comments from your audience. Social proof dramatically reduces the perceived risk of buying something new.
