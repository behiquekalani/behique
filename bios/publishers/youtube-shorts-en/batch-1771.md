**Short 1:**
SEO Title: How to Stop Spreading Thin Across Every Platform
Hook: Being everywhere at 20% is worse than being somewhere at 100%.
Script: The creator who tries to master TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, and LinkedIn simultaneously goes nowhere on all of them. Each platform has its own algorithm, its own format, its own culture. Learning one takes months. Learning five at once produces mediocre results on all of them. The strategy that works is to pick one platform, go deep, get consistent, understand what resonates with that specific audience, and build the habit. Then, once you've found your footing, repurpose your best content to a second platform. Not repost. Adapt. That's the sequence. Depth first. Breadth second. Never skip depth.

SEO Title: Building a Content Archive That Keeps Working
Hook: The content you posted two years ago can still bring in subscribers today.
Script: Every piece of content you put out is a permanent, searchable, discoverable asset. Unlike a social post that disappears in the feed within 48 hours, a YouTube video or blog post can surface in search results indefinitely. The creator who has been publishing consistently for two years has a deep archive that keeps attracting new people. The creator who just started has nothing yet. This is why starting now matters more than starting perfectly. Every piece adds to the archive. The archive compounds. After a year, the compounding is noticeable. After three years, the archive becomes one of your most valuable business assets.
