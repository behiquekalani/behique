## Script 1

**Hook:** How to write an email to your list that actually gets opened and clicked. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three principles for effective email writing.

Principle one: the subject line is everything. It's the only thing that determines whether the email gets read. The best subject lines are specific, curious, or personally relevant. "How I doubled my reply rate" beats "Newsletter issue 47" every single time.

Principle two: write like you're talking to one person. Not your list. One person. Use "you" constantly. Reference specific situations. The reader should feel like you wrote it just for them, not broadcast it to a crowd.

Principle three: have one clear point and one clear action. Not three topics and four links. One idea, explained clearly, with one thing to do at the end. Simple emails get clicked. Complex ones get closed.

Every email you send either builds the relationship or erodes it. Write accordingly.

**CTA:** Save this and revise your next email before sending. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** Three frameworks for pricing your digital product at the right number. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Framework one: outcome-based pricing. Calculate what the outcome of your product is worth to the buyer. If your product helps them save $1,000, it should be priced between $100 and $300. Ten to thirty percent of the value created is a reasonable starting point.

Framework two: market positioning pricing. Look at the range of prices in your market. The bottom fifth is competing on price. The top fifth is competing on brand. Price in the top third from the start to signal quality without claiming a brand you haven't built yet.

Framework three: the test-and-adjust method. Launch at a price you're slightly uncomfortable with. If your first ten buyers convert without hesitation, raise the price. Friction-free early sales often mean the price is too low.

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why your mindset about money is secretly limiting your business growth. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three money beliefs that block growth.

Belief one: "People in my niche don't have money." This is almost never true. People in every niche spend money on solutions to problems they care deeply about. The question is whether your offer is positioned as a serious solution.

Belief two: "Charging a lot feels greedy." Undercharging creates resentment, burnout, and a lower quality of work. You can't do your best work when you're financially stressed. Charging well is what sustains the quality that actually helps people.

Belief three: "I need more experience before I can charge real prices." You need enough experience to deliver on your promise. That's it. You don't need to be the best in the world. You need to be better than your buyer at the specific thing you're selling.

**CTA:** Save this and examine which belief is costing you the most. (5 sec)
