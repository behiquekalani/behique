**Short 1: The Asset Mindset**
Hook (0-5s): [Seed growing into tree visual] The difference between spending time and investing time.
Content (5-50s): Spending time: scrolling, consuming, doing tasks that end when you stop. Investing time: creating content that stays live. Building a product that sells on repeat. Writing an email sequence that nurtures buyers automatically. Growing an audience that compounds. The solopreneur who spends time gets paid once per hour. The solopreneur who invests time creates assets that pay indefinitely. The question to ask yourself at the end of every day is simple: did I build anything today that will still be working when I'm not? If the answer is consistently no, you're spending time. Not investing it.

Description: The distinction between spending time and investing time is the core mindset shift required to build sustainable solopreneur income. Here's how to audit your time against this standard.

**Short 2: The Overnight Success Myth**
Hook (0-5s): [Iceberg visual] Every overnight success has a very long story you didn't see.
Content (5-50s): The creator who blew up in three months spent two years on a previous account that went nowhere. The solopreneur who launched a product to $10,000 in day one had spent eight months building an email list and posting daily. The "overnight" part is the last chapter. The book before it is long and not interesting to post about. This matters because when you're in month three with no traction, you're comparing your chapter two to someone else's chapter ten. You don't have the same context. You're not behind. You're building the part that nobody films. Keep writing the early chapters. The climax comes.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe if you're writing your early chapters and need proof that the story continues.
Description: The overnight success narrative in the creator economy hides years of quiet, unglamorous work. Understanding this myth is essential for maintaining momentum through the early phases.
Tags: creator economy, solopreneur, consistency, building online, entrepreneur mindset
