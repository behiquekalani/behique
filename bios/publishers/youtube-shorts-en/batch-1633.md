# YouTube Shorts Batch 1633

## Short 1 — The Problem-First Business
**Title:** Start With the Problem, Not the Product (This Is Why Most Businesses Fail)
**Hook (0-3s):** Most businesses fail before they launch because they start with a product instead of a problem.
**Body (3-55s):**
- A product is a solution. It is useless without a problem it is solving.
- Find a painful, specific, recurring problem in a market you understand.
- Talk to ten people experiencing that problem before building anything.
- The insights from those conversations will shape a product people actually want.
- Most founders skip this phase because building feels more productive than listening.
- Listening is building. It is the most important phase.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for the problem-first business framework.

## Short 2 — The Trust Flywheel
**Title:** How Trust Compounds Into Revenue Over Time (The Trust Flywheel)
**Hook (0-3s):** Trust is the most valuable currency on the internet. Here is how it compounds.
**Body (3-55s):**
- Every piece of valuable content you publish adds to your trust account.
- Trust makes people open your emails, click your links, and buy your products.
- Trust reduces the friction in every transaction.
- High trust creators convert at rates that low trust creators cannot approach.
- Trust is built through consistency, accuracy, generosity, and time.
- You cannot shortcut it. But you can accelerate it by showing up every day.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe and build trust with me daily.

## Short 3 — The Speed Principle
**Title:** Why Moving Fast Is More Important Than Moving Right
**Hook (0-3s):** In most situations, a fast wrong move beats a slow right one.
**Body (3-55s):**
- Speed creates data. Slowness creates assumptions.
- Every iteration of your product, content, or strategy teaches you something.
- The tenth version is always better than the first.
- But you cannot reach the tenth without going through the first.
- The cost of delay is always higher than the cost of a fixable mistake.
- Move. Correct course. Move again.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for the iteration mindset applied to real building.
