## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three things that changed when I started treating my online presence like a business.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Content became intentional. Every post had a purpose. Either building trust, demonstrating expertise, or pointing toward an offer. Random posting stopped. Strategic publishing started.

Idea 2 - I started tracking what worked. I stopped guessing which content resonated and started looking at the data. The posts with the most saves and shares told me exactly what my audience valued most.

Idea 3 - I added monetization earlier. The habit of waiting until the audience was "big enough" to sell to disappeared. I made a small offer early, learned from the response, and improved from there.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three frameworks for coming up with content ideas every single week without running dry.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - The question framework. Look at every question you get from clients, followers, or people in your network. Each question is a content idea. Write down every question you've answered in the last month. That's a content calendar.

Idea 2 - The mistake framework. Think of every mistake you or someone you know made related to your topic. Write about what went wrong, why, and what to do instead. Mistake content is highly relatable and shareable.

Idea 3 - The contrast framework. "Most people think X. Here's why Y actually works better." This format produces educational content that challenges assumptions, which is the content type most likely to get saved and shared.

**CTA (5s):** Like and subscribe for content idea generation education.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that are worth paying for in a solo business that most people cut.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Good software that saves time. Recurring costs are worth it when the hours saved exceed the subscription cost. A $30 tool that saves you two hours a week is a $30 monthly investment with a significant hourly return.

Idea 2 - Education from practitioners. A course or book written by someone actively doing the thing you're trying to do is worth multiples of its cost in time saved and mistakes avoided.

Idea 3 - A second set of eyes. A trusted peer, a coach, or a community of builders at your level will see things you can't see about your own work. Feedback is one of the most underrated investment categories for solo creators.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for solo business investment and operations education.
