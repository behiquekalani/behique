## Script 1

**Hook:** The difference between content that builds authority and content that just gets views. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Views are reach. Authority is trust. They're different currencies.

Content that gets views is often broad, entertaining, or trend-based. It attracts a wide audience quickly. But wide audiences don't buy at the same rate as focused ones.

Content that builds authority is specific, opinionated, and backed by evidence. It attracts fewer people but attracts the right ones. People who have the exact problem you solve.

The creators who build lasting businesses optimize for authority, not reach. They write the same core ideas in multiple formats, consistently, until they become synonymous with that topic in their niche.

You can have both, but decide which one is the foundation. Authority should be. Reach is a byproduct.

## Script 2

**Hook:** Three systems every solo business owner needs to stop trading time for money. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
System one: content creation. A repeatable weekly or daily routine for producing content. Not improvised. Scheduled. A specific time, a specific format, a specific topic rotation. Consistency requires systems, not willpower.

System two: product delivery. Whatever you sell needs to be delivered without you involved in every transaction. Digital downloads, automated email sequences, course platforms. If your delivery requires your active presence, you don't have a product. You have a service.

System three: lead generation. A predictable way to bring new people into your world every week. This could be organic content, a referral process, or a small paid strategy. Unpredictable lead flow creates unpredictable income.

Build these three systems and your business becomes something that works whether or not you're working.

**CTA:** Save this and identify which system is missing in your business. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why niche is not a limitation. It's a superpower. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Generalists think niching down means excluding potential customers. They have it backwards.

When you're specific, you're easier to find. Someone searching for "freelance copywriter for SaaS companies" will hire you over the person who describes themselves as a "writer for businesses." Specificity wins searches.

When you're specific, you build deeper trust. Your audience believes that someone who speaks directly to their industry understands their actual problems. General content feels like it could apply to anyone, which means it resonates with no one strongly.

When you're specific, your referrals improve. Happy customers refer people exactly like themselves. A niche specialist gets referred to exactly the right people, not random leads who aren't a fit.

**CTA:** Follow to learn how to find and own your niche. (5 sec)
