## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here's the fastest way to start making money from what you already know.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Knowledge products are the lowest-barrier business you can start. You don't need funding, a team, or an office. You need a skill, a clear result, and somewhere to sell it.

Idea 2: The process is three steps. Identify the specific problem you know how to solve. Package the solution into a format someone can consume without you on a call. Set a price and put it somewhere people can buy.

Idea 3: The first version doesn't have to be polished. A Google Doc, a recorded video walkthrough, a Notion template. The format matters less than the clarity of the outcome. What does the buyer walk away knowing or having that they didn't before? Answer that clearly, and you have a product.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and I'll show you how to build your first knowledge product this week.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** The reason most people never build an audience is not effort. It's this.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Most people try to grow an audience before they know why someone should follow them. They post content without a clear reason for the audience to stay. Generic content attracts generic attention, which converts to nothing.

Idea 2: Audience growth starts with specificity. Pick one problem. Serve one type of person. Say one thing that distinguishes you from everyone else in your space. That specificity is what makes people click follow instead of scroll past.

Idea 3: The fastest way to grow is to be the most specific person talking about a specific topic for a specific audience. You won't appeal to everyone. You're not supposed to. You're supposed to deeply resonate with the right people, and those people will share you.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for the audience-building strategies that actually work.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Most pricing mistakes happen before you even talk to a customer.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The most common pricing mistake is anchoring your price to your time. You charge what you think an hour of your work is worth, rather than what the outcome is worth to the buyer.

Idea 2: Outcome-based pricing changes the math entirely. If your product saves someone ten hours a week, it's worth hundreds of dollars a month to them. If your course helps someone get a promotion, it's worth thousands. Price the result, not the effort.

Idea 3: A second common mistake is underpricing to avoid rejection. But a too-low price signals low confidence in the result. Buyers sense it. They delay purchase. They don't implement. Charge what the result is worth and attract the buyers who believe it too.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe for more on pricing, products, and building solo income.
