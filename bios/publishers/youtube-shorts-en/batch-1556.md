## Script 1

**Hook:** How to find a profitable niche in less than one hour. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three questions will get you there.

Question one: what have I figured out that took me longer than it should have? This points to a knowledge gap you bridged. Others are still on the wrong side of that gap. That's your product.

Question two: what do people ask me for advice about? In your personal and professional life, what topics come up repeatedly? When people seek you out for guidance, you're already positioned as an authority.

Question three: what problems do I see in my current or former industry that no one is solving well? Industry insiders often see the gaps most clearly. That insider perspective becomes your positioning.

Sit with those three questions for 30 minutes. Write every answer down without filtering. Then look for the intersection. That intersection is your niche.

**CTA:** Follow and share your answer to question one in the comments. (5 sec)

## Script 2

**Hook:** The compounding asset most online creators ignore. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
The asset is your back catalog. Most creators focus on what they're publishing today. The ones building real businesses focus on making old content evergreen.

Every post you publish has three lifespans. The immediate reach on publication day. The algorithm-driven reach over the next few weeks as the platform tests it. And the search-driven reach that continues indefinitely if the topic is timeless.

Content about your opinions on current events has only the first lifespan. Content about fundamental skills, timeless frameworks, and universal problems has all three.

Build your content strategy around timeless topics and your archive becomes a distribution engine that grows without you publishing new content every day.

**CTA:** Save this and review your last month of content for timelessness. (5 sec)

## Script 3

**Hook:** Why most freelancers stay stuck at the same income for years. (5 sec)

**Main Point:** (45 sec)
Three patterns keep freelancers from growing.

Pattern one: taking any client who pays. Working with clients who aren't a fit wastes energy and time that could be spent finding better clients. Selectivity feels risky early. It's actually protective.

Pattern two: never raising rates. If you've been charging the same amount for two years, you've been giving your clients a pay raise while your cost of living increased. Rates must move with your skill and the market.

Pattern three: no productized offering. Pure service income is capped by hours. Even one digital product, one template, one guide, adds a revenue stream that isn't capped by your schedule.

Breaking through those patterns requires one decision at a time. Start with the one that's costing you the most right now.

**CTA:** Follow for the freelancer-to-business-owner transition roadmap. (5 sec)
