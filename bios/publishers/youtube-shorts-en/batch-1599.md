## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** The overlooked reason why most creators never build a real income.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: The reason is a missing product. They create content for years, build a loyal audience, and have nothing to sell. When they finally add a product, they wonder why the conversion is low. The product should be built alongside the audience, not after. Every piece of content is a data point about what the audience needs. Use that data to build before the audience asks.

Idea 2: The second part is commitment to selling. Most creators are uncomfortable promoting their own work. They mention the product once and move on. Selling requires repetition. The same offer needs to be in front of the audience at different times, in different formats, with different angles. People need to hear about it multiple times before they buy.

Idea 3: The third part is making the offer specific. "Buy my course" is weak. "Get the system I used to go from zero to ten thousand subscribers in six months, step by step, in under two hours" is specific. Specific offers sell. Vague offers don't. The more precisely you describe the outcome, the more people self-select into buyers.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three things that will make your digital product better before you launch it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: First, give it to five people before launch. Not to review it but to use it. Watch where they get confused. Watch which parts they skip. Watch what questions they ask. That observation will reveal the gaps in your product that you can't see because you made it. Real use always reveals what documentation can't.

Idea 2: Second, rewrite your product description from the buyer's perspective. Most product descriptions are written from the creator's perspective. They describe what's in the product. Buyers want to know what they'll be able to do after using it. Reframe every feature as a benefit. Not "thirty-page guide" but "thirty-page guide that walks you through exactly how to do X in one afternoon."

Idea 3: Third, add one strong testimonial to the sales page before you go live. Even if you gave the product for free to get it, one specific quote from a real person dramatically increases conversion. "This saved me three hours a week" from a named person with a photo converts better than the most polished sales copy without social proof.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The pricing psychology that makes buyers say yes faster.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1: Anchoring is one of the most powerful pricing tools available. When buyers see a higher price first, a lower price looks like a deal in comparison. This is why showing a crossed-out original price next to a sale price converts so well. The anchor creates the reference point.

Idea 2: Value stacking works the same way. Instead of listing one product at fifty dollars, list everything the buyer receives and assign individual values to each element. The template is worth thirty. The video walkthrough is worth twenty. The bonus checklist is worth ten. Total value sixty dollars. Your price: forty-seven. The stack makes the price feel like a bargain.

Idea 3: Urgency is the third element. Not fake urgency, but real scarcity or deadlines. A launch discount that expires, a limited cohort size, a bonus that's removed after a specific date. Real urgency creates real action. People delay decisions by default. A genuine reason to act now moves them off the fence.
