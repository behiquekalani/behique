## Short 1 — Bet on Yourself
**Title:** The Best Investment You'll Ever Make Is in Your Own Capabilities
**Hook (0-3s):** Markets crash. Skills don't.
**Body (3-55s):** Every external investment carries risk you can't control. Market conditions. Economic cycles. Regulatory changes. But the investment in your own capability is different. You control the work you put in. You control which skills you build. You control how you apply them. A skill set that produces real value is infinitely portable, recession-resistant, and compounding. The person who spends their 20s building genuine capabilities in areas that matter comes out in their 30s with options that no stock portfolio can match. Invest externally if you can. But never sacrifice the internal investment. That one is always first.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for skill-first wealth content.

## Short 2 — Single Tasking Wins
**Title:** Multitasking Is a Myth — Single Tasking Is a Superpower
**Hook (0-3s):** You are not multitasking. You are rapidly failing at several things simultaneously.
**Body (3-55s):** The research is clear. The brain doesn't process two complex tasks simultaneously. It switches between them, and every switch carries a cognitive cost. Context switching degrades performance by as much as 40 percent. Yet we wear busyness as a badge. We check email while on calls. We scroll while watching videos. We split focus between the meeting and the Slack thread. The single tasking practice is simple: one task, one window, one focus until completion or scheduled end. The quality improvement is immediate and significant. Try it once. You won't go back.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for focus and performance content.

## Short 3 — The Decade Bet
**Title:** Stop Thinking in Years — Think in Decades and Win by Default
**Hook (0-3s):** Most people overestimate what one year can do and underestimate what ten can.
**Body (3-55s):** One year of consistent writing rarely produces a breakthrough. Ten years produces a body of work that defines a career. One year of investing rarely changes your financial picture. Ten years of consistent investing can transform it. One year of building an audience produces modest results. Ten years builds a platform. The reason to care about the decade view is not just strategy. It's patience calibration. When you're thinking in decades, a bad quarter doesn't feel catastrophic. It's just a data point in a longer story. Zoom out. Extend your timeline. The compounding that looks impossible over one year looks inevitable over ten.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for decade-view thinking content.
