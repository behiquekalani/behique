**Short 1: The Asset vs Content Trap**
Hook (0-5s): [Text overlay: "Content disappears. Assets compound."] Most creators are building the wrong thing.
Content (5-50s): A TikTok video has a shelf life of 48 to 72 hours. An Instagram post, maybe a week. But a YouTube video? Three to five years. A digital product? Forever. A newsletter? Compounds with every issue. When you spend all your time creating short-form content without building something that lasts, you're on a treadmill. You have to keep running just to maintain your position. The shift is simple but powerful: every piece of short-form content you create should point to a long-form asset. A product. A guide. A community. A course. Short form drives traffic. Assets capture it. Build both, but know which one pays you while you sleep.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe if you want to build assets, not just rack up views.
Description: There's a difference between content and assets. Most creators only build one. Here's why that keeps them stuck.

**Short 2: Price High or Price Yourself Out**
Hook (0-5s): [Visual: two price tags, $9 and $47] The price you set tells your buyer something before they even read the page.
Content (5-50s): Low prices signal low value. It's psychology, not fairness. When someone sees a $9 product, they assume it's not that serious. When they see a $47 product, they assume it requires commitment. Commitment means better buyers. Better buyers get results. Buyers who get results leave reviews and come back. I raised one of my products from $9 to $47 and my refund rate dropped from 11 percent to under 2 percent. Same product. Different buyer type, filtered by price. The rule of thumb: price your product at what a transformation is worth, not what the content weighs. One better outcome is worth more than 100 pages.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe for more honest takes on building a real digital product business.
Description: Pricing strategy isn't about fairness. It's about filtering for the right buyer. Here's what changed when I 5x'd my price.
Tags: pricing, digital products, solopreneur, online business, copywriting
