## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Three mistakes that keep solo creators stuck at the same income level for years.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Not raising prices. If your price hasn't changed in a year, you've given yourself a pay cut. Skills improve over time. Market rates shift. Your pricing needs to reflect your current value, not your value when you started.

Idea 2 - Doing everything themselves. Some tasks don't require your expertise. Spending hours on admin, formatting, or repetitive tasks is expensive because it consumes time that could produce revenue. Outsource or automate the tasks that don't require your brain.

Idea 3 - Not building a list. Every month without an email list is a month of potential audience members lost to algorithm changes and platform uncertainty. The list is the most important asset a solo creator can build.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Three ways to know if you're making progress even when it doesn't feel like it.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Your content is improving. Compare something you wrote or recorded six months ago to something recent. If there's a visible quality gap, you're getting better. Improvement precedes results by weeks or months.

Idea 2 - Your clarity on your offer is sharper. If you can describe what you do and who it's for more concisely than you could before, you're making real progress. Clarity of positioning is the engine of growth.

Idea 3 - People are engaging differently. More questions, more specific comments, more people tagging others. When your audience starts treating you like an authority, it's a leading indicator before the numbers catch up.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that make a sales page convert well without feeling pushy.

**Main Point (45s):**
Idea 1 - Specificity over superlatives. "This will help you get more clients" is weak. "This helped a freelance designer add $1,800 to their monthly income in 45 days" is specific and credible. Remove every vague adjective and replace it with a concrete number or example.

Idea 2 - Clear objection handling. Identify the top three reasons someone wouldn't buy and address each one directly on the page. When you name the objection out loud and respond to it honestly, resistance drops.

Idea 3 - A risk reversal. A refund policy, a guarantee, or a free trial removes the fear of a bad purchase. The more confident you are in your product, the stronger the guarantee you can offer. Strong guarantees increase sales more than almost any other change.
