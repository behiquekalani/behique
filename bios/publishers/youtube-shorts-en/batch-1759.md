**Short 1:**
SEO Title: How to Create Evergreen Content That Drives Sales for Years
Hook: Some content expires in a week. Some earns for years. Here is how to make the second kind.
Script: Evergreen content answers questions that do not expire. How do I get my first client is still relevant in five years. What is trending this week is not. Write about fundamentals. Write about psychology. Write about process. Write about mindset. These topics compound because the question keeps being asked by new people entering your niche. A YouTube video answering how to price a digital product will get found every week by someone new asking that exact question. Archive your best answers in searchable formats. Long-form videos. Blog posts. Guides. The work you do once keeps paying.
End screen CTA: Subscribe for evergreen content strategy. Build once, earn for years.

**Short 2:**
SEO Title: Why Digital Products Beat Freelancing for Long-Term Income
Hook: Freelancing trades time for money. Digital products trade knowledge for money.
Script: Both work. But they scale differently. Freelancing has a ceiling defined by your hours. Digital products do not. A freelancer can only take on so many clients. A product can be sold to an unlimited number of buyers. The challenge with digital products is the upfront build. You invest time before you see income. Freelancing pays immediately. The smart path for most creators: freelance to build skills and income, then build a product around what clients keep paying you to do. Use the freelancing income to fund the time to build the product. Then let the product reduce the need for client work.
End screen CTA: Subscribe for the freelance-to-product transition strategy. Full breakdown coming.

**Short 3:**
SEO Title: How to Build a Content Calendar You Will Actually Use
Hook: Content calendars that nobody follows are not calendars. They are guilt lists.
Script: Build a content calendar you will actually use in four steps. Step one. Decide your publishing frequency. Not what you aspire to. What you have sustained for at least two weeks. Step two. Pick your content categories. Two or three topics that rotate. Step three. Plan one week ahead. Not one month. Planning too far breaks when life changes. Step four. Use a tool that takes under five minutes to update. Notion, a Google Sheet, a notes app. The calendar should create clarity, not more work. If maintaining the calendar feels like a job, simplify it until it does not.
End screen CTA: Subscribe. I share creator systems and schedules every week. Simple and practical.
