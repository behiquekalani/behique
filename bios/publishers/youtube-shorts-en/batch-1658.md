## Short 1 — The Rejection Filter
**Title:** Every No Is Just a Filter — Your Yes Is Still in the Pool
**Hook (0-3s):** Rejection isn't judgment. It's filtration.
**Body (3-55s):** When you reframe rejection as filtration, the emotional weight changes completely. The client who passed wasn't your client. The audience that didn't respond isn't your audience. The opportunity that fell through wasn't your opportunity. Every no clears the pool closer to the right fit. The people who fear rejection treat every no as a verdict about their worth or their work. The people who understand filtration treat every no as progress. You're one step closer to the yes that's right. Keep showing up. Keep putting work into the world. The right fit exists. Rejection is just the path to finding it.

## Short 2 — The Flywheel Effect
**Title:** Success Looks Like Overnight — It's Actually a Flywheel
**Hook (0-3s):** Every overnight success has an invisible backstory of years of pushing.
**Body (3-55s):** The flywheel metaphor is useful because it captures how momentum actually works. Early pushes produce almost no visible result. You push again. Still slow. You push again. Barely moving. This phase is where most people quit. They can't see the momentum building because it's too slow to register. But each push adds to the last. Then something shifts. The flywheel starts turning on its own. Results arrive. To outside observers, it looks sudden. To you, it was a hundred quiet pushes nobody saw. The only way to get to the spin is through the slow phase. Most people quit in the slow phase. Don't.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for flywheel and compounding content.

## Short 3 — Minimalism as Performance
**Title:** Fewer Decisions = More Energy for What Actually Matters
**Hook (0-3s):** Every decision you make depletes the same finite resource. Stop wasting it on small things.
**Body (3-55s):** Decision fatigue is real and it accumulates throughout the day. By afternoon, your decision quality degrades because you've spent the resource on dozens of small choices. The fix is elimination. Simplify your wardrobe. Simplify your meals. Simplify your schedule. Automate recurring decisions. Each category you simplify frees up cognitive bandwidth for the decisions that actually shape your life. The strategic choices. The creative work. The leadership decisions. Minimalism isn't about aesthetics. It's about resource allocation. Clear the small decisions and you show up fully for the big ones.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe for minimalist performance and decision design content.
