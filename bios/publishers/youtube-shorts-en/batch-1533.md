## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Here is the simplest possible framework for launching a digital product.

**Main Point (45s):**
Step one: define the problem. Write one sentence that describes the exact pain your customer is feeling right now. Not a vague category. A specific, felt frustration. That sentence becomes the headline of your product page. Step two: define the transformation. Write one sentence that describes what their life looks like after your product solves the problem. That sentence becomes your core promise. Step three: build the bridge. The product is the content that takes someone from the pain sentence to the transformation sentence. It does not need to be long. It needs to be complete. Step four: write the page. Use the pain sentence and transformation sentence you already wrote, add bullet points of what is included, add a price, and add a buy button. You have a product page. Step five: send traffic. One post per day on the platform your audience uses. Link to the page. Adjust based on what gets clicks.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** Why most one-person businesses plateau and how to break through it.

**Main Point (45s):**
The plateau usually happens for one of three reasons. The first is offer saturation. You have been selling the same product to the same audience for so long that most of the people who would buy it already have. The fix is to either expand your audience or introduce a new product at a different price point. The second is distribution stagnation. You are on the same platforms, using the same content formats, talking to the same people. Growth requires reaching new people. Test one new channel or format per quarter. The third is price ceiling. Your product prices have not grown with your reputation. If your prices are the same as they were two years ago, you are probably leaving money on the table. Audit your pricing against your current level of expertise and market positioning. Raising prices alone can break through a revenue plateau.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** Three things that make a digital product worth buying and worth recommending.

**Main Point (45s):**
First, it has to deliver a complete solution. Partial solutions create partial satisfaction. If your product gets the customer seventy percent of the way to the result and leaves them hanging on the last thirty, they will not recommend it. Build the full bridge, not just the first half. Second, it has to be easy to consume. A 200-page ebook that requires weeks to finish is not a great product experience even if the content is excellent. Optimize for completion. Short, clear, organized. When customers finish your product, they feel accomplished and that feeling is what drives word-of-mouth. Third, it has to over-deliver on the promise. Whatever you say the product will do, it should do more. Set a modest promise and deliver a significant result. Underpromise and overdeliver is the oldest customer satisfaction principle in business, and it still works every time.
