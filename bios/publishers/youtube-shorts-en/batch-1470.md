## Short 1
**Title:** Why Your Business Breaks Without You (And How to Fix It)
**Hook (0-3s):** If your business stops when you do, you do not have a business. You have a job.
**Script (3-55s):** The solo operator trap is building a business that is entirely dependent on your presence. Every task only you can do. Every decision only you can make. That is a ceiling, not a business. The fix is documentation. Write down every process. Build templates for recurring tasks. Identify what can be automated and automate it. When you document your work, you can delegate it. When you can delegate it, you can grow past your own bandwidth. The goal is to build a machine that can run without you for at least a week.

## Short 2
**Title:** 3 Hours of Deep Work vs 8 Hours of Busy Work: A Real Comparison
**Hook (0-3s):** Three focused hours will outproduce eight scattered ones. Every single time.
**Script (3-55s):** Deep work is the kind of work that moves your business forward. Creating content, building products, making strategic decisions, writing copy. Shallow work is the noise around it. Email, Slack, administrative tasks, reformatting documents. Most people fill their day with shallow work and wonder why they are not making progress. Block three hours. No phone. No notifications. One task. Just once. I promise you will produce more in that block than in two full days of scattered work. Then make it a daily habit.
