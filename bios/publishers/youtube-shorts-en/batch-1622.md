## Script 1

**Hook (5s):** Why your business model matters more than your marketing strategy.

**Main Point (45s):**
A bad business model with great marketing just generates more unhappy customers faster. Before you pour effort into growing your audience or improving your content, make sure the underlying model is sound.

A sound model means: your product delivers real value, your price covers your needs with room to grow, your delivery is not dependent on you being present for every transaction, and acquiring a new customer costs less than what they pay you.

Most solo creator struggles are model problems disguised as marketing problems. They think they need more visibility when what they need is a better offer at a better price delivered in a more scalable way. Fix the model first.

## Script 2

**Hook (5s):** How to use free content to pre-sell paid products before they even exist.

**Main Point (45s):**
Every piece of free content you create is either building demand for a future product or it is not. The creators who do this intentionally are building product waitlists before they have even started building.

Here is how it works deliberately. Pick the topic of your next product. Create five to ten pieces of free content that go deep on the problem that product solves. At the end of each piece, mention that you are building something that takes this further and ask people to comment if they are interested.

Count the comments and DMs. If the numbers are strong, you have validated demand. If they are weak, you have saved yourself from building something that would not sell. Either way, you win from the research.

**CTA (5s):** Subscribe and start pre-selling your next product with free content today.

## Script 3

**Hook (5s):** The compound effect of daily writing that most creators underestimate.

**Main Point (45s):**
When you write one piece per day, it does not feel like much. But look at the math. In one year of daily writing, you have 365 pieces of content. You have published more than most people will publish in a lifetime.

More importantly, you have improved 365 times. The gap between your post on day one and your post on day 365 is enormous. Not because you got smarter, but because you practiced, got feedback, iterated, and practiced again.

The writers, creators, and builders who are visibly good at what they do are almost always people who have been doing it quietly and consistently for longer than you realize. The compound effect is invisible until it is undeniable.
