**Short 2:**
SEO Title: Creator Burnout: What Causes It and How to Prevent It
Hook: Most creator burnout is not about too much work. It's about the wrong kind of work.
Script: Doing creative work you care about for an audience that values it is energizing, not draining. Burnout happens when you're doing performative work for an algorithm that doesn't reward it, with no system to reduce the constant decisions, and no visible progress to sustain the motivation. The fix is not working less. It's fixing the three root causes. Build systems so decisions are made at the system level. Find alignment between what you care about and what your audience needs. Track meaningful progress metrics, not platform vanity metrics. Burnout is a signal that one of these three things is broken. Diagnose which one and fix it.
End screen CTA: Subscribe for the burnout prevention framework I use to keep creating consistently for the long term.

**Short 3:**
SEO Title: How to Find Your Niche When You're Good at Multiple Things
Hook: Being good at multiple things is an advantage, not a problem, if you frame it right.
Script: The person who knows marketing, writing, and business strategy doesn't have to pick one. They have a unique intersection. Their niche is the person who needs all three applied together. The combination of your skills is often more specific and valuable than any individual skill. The question is not which of my skills should I focus on. It's who benefits most from the combination of everything I know. That intersection is your actual niche. It's not crowded because nobody else has your exact combination. Identify it. Name it specifically. Build content around it. The right people will find you because nobody else is speaking to exactly what they need.
End screen CTA: Subscribe for the niche identification workshop I share with new creators getting started.
