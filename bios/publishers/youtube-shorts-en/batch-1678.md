# YouTube Shorts Batch 1678

## Short 1 — Teaching to Master
**Title:** Teach It Before You Master It — The Fastest Learning Loop That Exists
**Hook (0-3s):** The fastest way to master something is to teach it.
**Body (3-55s):** This is the Feynman technique in practice. Richard Feynman believed you truly understood something only when you could explain it to a child without jargon or abstractions. If you stumbled during the explanation, the stumble pointed exactly to the gap in your understanding. This is why creating content about what you're learning accelerates your mastery so dramatically. When you explain publicly, you can't hide behind vague understanding. Your audience's questions reveal what you don't know yet. Their confusion becomes your curriculum. You don't need to be an expert to start. You just need to be one step ahead and willing to explain clearly. The teaching is the learning.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. Teach me one concept you've recently learned in the comments. Right now.

## Short 2 — Comparison Reframe
**Title:** You're Not Behind — You're At The Start of Your Own Timeline
**Hook (0-3s):** Comparing your chapter 1 to someone else's chapter 20 is making you miserable.
**Body (3-55s):** Social media has created a comparison environment unlike any that's existed before. Everyone is broadcasting their highlights. Their wins, their best moments, their polished results. Nobody broadcasts their year three when nothing was working. Nobody shows you the embarrassing first attempts. So you compare your unedited reality to everyone else's highlight reel. And you feel behind. The correction is simple but requires discipline. The only useful comparison is you versus you at a previous point in time. Are you better than you were six months ago? More capable? Clearer? More experienced? If yes, you're winning. The timeline is yours alone. Other people's progress tells you nothing about yours.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What's one way you're better now than 6 months ago? Tell me in the comments.

## Short 3 — Momentum Law
**Title:** Starting Is The Hardest Part — Momentum Is What Waits on the Other Side
**Hook (0-3s):** Starting is the hardest part. Momentum is the reward for starting.
**Body (3-55s):** Newton's first law has a direct application to creative work. Objects at rest stay at rest. Objects in motion stay in motion. The creative session you've been avoiding doesn't get easier with more avoidance. It gets harder. The resistance grows. But the moment you sit down and start, even poorly, even reluctantly, the physics shift. You're in motion now. The session that felt impossible from the outside often becomes the most productive one you have. This is because the barrier is almost never the work itself. It's the transition from rest to motion. Make the transition small. Two minutes. One sentence. One small action. Cross the threshold. Everything changes after that.
**End screen (55-60s):** Subscribe. What creative thing have you been avoiding? Start it in the next hour. Tell me what happened.
