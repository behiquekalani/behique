No experimenting with a second platform until the 90 days are done. Every piece of content leads to the same landing page. Every call to action points to the same product. The compounding effect of this focus is dramatic. Your content builds. Your audience learns what you sell. The algorithm figures out your niche. Day 90 looks nothing like day one. Most creators never find out because they quit on day 20 to try something different.
Close (50-60s): Are you in for the 90-day challenge? Subscribe and I'll check in with you monthly.
Description: Focus is the underrated advantage in creator business. This 90-day challenge framework has produced consistent results for solopreneurs building their first digital product income stream.

**Short 2: Content Is a Sales Team**
Hook (0-5s): [Team of people visual becoming documents] Every piece of content you create is a salesperson that never clocks out.
Content (5-50s): Think about it this way. A video you post today will still be discoverable three years from now. Still building trust. Still directing people to your offer. If you have 200 pieces of content pointing to your product, that's 200 salespeople working around the clock for free. The more you produce, the bigger the team. And unlike human salespeople, your content doesn't get tired. Doesn't ask for a raise. Doesn't call in sick. The only thing it needs from you is to keep adding to the team. Every day you don't post is a day you didn't hire a salesperson. Keep hiring.
Close (50-60s): Subscribe for the content creation system I use to build this team efficiently.
Description: Reframing your content as a permanent sales team changes how you think about consistency and volume. Here's the mental model that keeps the most productive solopreneurs posting.
Tags: content creation, solopreneur, digital products, creator economy, content marketing
