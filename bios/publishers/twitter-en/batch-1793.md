1. Your first sale closes a trust gap. Every sale after that closes a relevance gap. The second is much smaller.

2. The customers who already bought from you are not past customers. They are your warmest future customers.

3. Map the next step your customer takes after buying. Then build that product before you run out of momentum from the first launch.

4. Repeat buyers do not need to be convinced you can deliver. They just need to know what you are offering next.

5. Build a business where the second product is an obvious choice for everyone who bought the first. That is retention as a product strategy.
