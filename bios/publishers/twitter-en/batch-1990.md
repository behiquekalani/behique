1. Identity precedes behavior. Decide who you are before you design what you do. The behavior follows the identity, not the other way around.

2. Compounding is patient. It does not reward the people who check daily. It rewards the people who build and wait and trust the math.

3. The simplest test for whether something belongs in your schedule: does it produce a result you care about, or does it just feel like work?

4. Most creators stop right before the inflection. The accumulation phase is invisible, and quitting during it looks identical to quitting after enough effort.

5. You do not need to reinvent anything. Applying an old principle to a new context is innovation. Most great ideas are remixes of things that already worked.
