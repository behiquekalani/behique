1. Every yes is a no to something else. That is not metaphorical. It is arithmetic.

2. Saying no requires knowing what you are building toward. Without that, everything looks potentially relevant and you never close a door.

3. The social cost of no is usually smaller than it feels. People adjust. They find other solutions. The internal cost of not saying no is higher and accumulates.

4. Learn to notice when your yes came from unclear values rather than genuine alignment. That distinction is where the practice actually happens.

5. Being always available is not a virtue. It is what happens when you have not decided what matters most to you yet.
