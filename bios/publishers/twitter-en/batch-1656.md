# Twitter Batch 1656

## Tweet 1
Testimonials are borrowed credibility.

Showing your work is earned credibility.

One tells people what others think of you. The other lets them decide for themselves.

## Tweet 2
Testimonials are easy to fake, easy to curate, and increasingly easy to distrust.

Readers know you only show the best ones.

The system is eroding its own credibility.

## Tweet 3
Show your thinking.

Show your failures and what you learned.

Give people enough to evaluate your judgment themselves.

That conclusion, reached independently, is worth more than any five-star review.

## Tweet 4
When someone reads your work and thinks "this person knows what they're talking about," that's not a testimonial.

That's demonstration-based trust.

It's much harder to dislodge.

## Tweet 5
Write one piece this week that shows your process rather than your results.

Walk through how you approached a real problem.

Include what didn't work before you found what did.
