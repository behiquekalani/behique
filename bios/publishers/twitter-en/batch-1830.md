1. Goals tell you where to aim. Systems determine whether you get there. Focus on the system. The goal is just a calibration device.

2. Goals imply an endpoint. Process obsession does not. The people who succeed long-term love the work enough that the goal is a byproduct.

3. Check on your goals quarterly. Adjust the system, not the effort level. More effort in a broken system produces faster failure.

4. Set the goal to get direction. Then forget it and build the system. Design your days so the goal becomes almost inevitable if repeated.

5. The person who is obsessed with the outcome is constantly disappointed. The person obsessed with the process is rarely bored.
