1. Discipline is not intensity. Intensity is unsustainable. Consistency over years requires something closer to acceptance than aggression.

2. If your work environment requires you to fight yourself every time you sit down, that is a design problem, not a character problem.

3. Set up your environment so the right action is the path of least resistance. Reduce friction for what you want to do. Add friction for what you want to avoid.

4. The person who can do unremarkable work on an unremarkable day, without needing it to feel significant, outlasts almost everyone.

5. Extraordinary results are built entirely out of ordinary sessions. There is no other mechanism.
