1. The market does not care how long it took you to build something. It cares whether the thing solves a real problem for a real person. Ship accordingly.

2. Being good at one thing is more valuable than being okay at ten. Depth is the anomaly. Breadth is the default. Choose the harder path.

3. Most people are one year of consistent output away from being recognized as an expert in their field. Most people quit at month three.

4. Your best skill is probably the one you use so naturally that you forgot it counts. What do people ask you about without you asking them to?

5. The gap between amateur and professional is not talent. It is the number of times the professional kept going when the amateur stopped.
