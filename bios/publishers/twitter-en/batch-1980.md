1. Ownership starts with: if this fails, it is on me. That accountability changes every decision that follows.

2. You are rewarded by most systems for completing defined tasks, not for outcomes. That shapes how you think.

3. An owner notices the thing about to break before it breaks. An operator waits to be told.

4. The legal structure matters less than the habit of thinking in terms of outcomes instead of inputs.

5. Ownership means you cannot blame the structure. That discomfort is the price of the upside.
