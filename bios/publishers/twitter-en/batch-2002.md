1. Starting feels like progress. It is borrowed energy. When it runs out, most people start something new instead of finishing what they started.

2. Finishing is a different skill than starting. It requires showing up when the project is ugly, behind, and no longer exciting. That is where most people disappear.

3. The finished product does not care how you felt while making it.

4. Momentum is not something you wait for. It is something you manufacture. Show up, do the smallest useful thing, leave. Repeat tomorrow.

5. The ugly middle of a project is where talent gets separated from commitment. Most talented people quit there. Committed people ship from there.
