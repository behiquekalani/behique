Safe is not neutral. Safe has a cost. When you choose predictable, you trade upside for comfort and call it responsibility.

Fear is immediate. Regret is eventual. Most people optimize against the immediate feeling and end up with the eventual one.

Safe is often the most expensive choice on the table. You are risking your time, your potential, and the story you tell yourself at the end.

The question is not whether risk is real. It is whether the risk of not trying is worth less than the risk of trying and failing.

Playing it safe is still playing. You are still betting. You just chose the option with the smallest upside.
