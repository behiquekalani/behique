# Twitter Batch 2486

**Tweet 1:** Building in public has become a content strategy. That's the problem. When it's a strategy, it becomes performance.

**Tweet 2:** Posting your wins after you know how they turned out is not building in public. That's celebrating in public. Building in public is sharing before you know.

**Tweet 3:** Real building in public requires sharing things while you're still unsure. That's the part most people skip.

**Tweet 4:** People trust you more when you stop performing certainty you don't have. Real documentation of real doubt is rare and magnetic.

**Tweet 5:** You don't control the narrative when you build in public authentically. Something might fail publicly. That discomfort is exactly what makes it valuable.
