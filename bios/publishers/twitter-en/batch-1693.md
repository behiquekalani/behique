# Twitter Batch 1693

## Tweet 1
Hourly pricing punishes expertise.

The better you get, the faster you work. The faster you work, the less you earn.

Charge for outcomes, not time.

## Tweet 2
A logo isn't worth $500.

A brand identity that attracts a $10K client is worth a percentage of $10K.

Know what your work produces. Price accordingly.

## Tweet 3
The moment you switch from "here's my rate" to "here's the outcome and here's the price" the whole conversation changes.

Clients stop looking at cost and start looking at value.

## Tweet 4
How do you move from hourly to outcome pricing?

Start with your next proposal.

Replace hours and rates with deliverables and results. Give one number. Watch what happens.

## Tweet 5
Charging by the hour means the client is always watching the meter.

Charging by the outcome means they're watching the result.

Which one do you want them focused on?
