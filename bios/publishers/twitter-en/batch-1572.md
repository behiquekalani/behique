# Twitter Batch 1572

## Tweet 1
Trust is built in private.

In the DM you actually replied to. The promise you kept. The thing you delivered when nobody was watching.

That's what converts, not the public performance of being helpful.

## Tweet 2
Overpromising on an offer destroys trust faster than almost anything else.

Underpromise. Overdeliver. This is not new advice. Very few people follow it.

## Tweet 3
People can smell inconsistency before they can name it.

They feel when your content stopped being honest and started being calculated.

That's when the unsubscribes start.

## Tweet 4
Thread: 5 ways trust gets destroyed quietly

1/ Recommending things for commission you don't believe in
2/ Disappearing after a launch
3/ Changing your public position for applause
4/ Saying you'll deliver something and not following through
5/ Optimizing for engagement over accuracy

None of these are dramatic. All of them compound.
