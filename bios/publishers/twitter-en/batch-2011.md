1. Optimization is only useful after validation. Optimizing the wrong thing efficiently is not a win.

2. Does it work? Does anyone care? Does it work reliably at scale? Most people skip to question three because it is more interesting than question two.

3. Do the ugly version first. Get one real result. Then decide if it is worth making clean.

4. A well-structured system for a product nobody wants is not an achievement. It is a monument to premature optimization.

5. The sequence matters more than the quality of each step. Right sequence, average execution beats wrong sequence, brilliant execution.
