1. The thing preventing most people from building a real audience is that they keep trying to appeal to everyone. Pick one person. Write for them.

2. Finishing a thing badly is a prerequisite to finishing the next thing well. Most people never get to the next thing because they cannot stomach finishing this one.

3. A strong reputation is built in small interactions over long stretches of time. It is not built in announcements. Show up. Keep showing up.

4. The business you build on a deep personal interest is sustainable in a way that market-driven businesses rarely are. Interests do not expire.

5. If you had to bet on yourself with real money, would you? If not, that is the work. Not the strategy. Not the product. That belief.
