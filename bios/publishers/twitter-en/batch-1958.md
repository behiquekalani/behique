A little of everything produces nothing marketable. Pick one skill. Learn it until it earns. Then learn it past that point.

---

The first level of skill gets you a job. The next level gets you clients who seek you out. The level after that gives you pricing power.

---

Most people quit a skill right before the curve turns. They swap to the next shiny thing and reset the clock. Stay in one lane longer.

---

General capability is invisible in most markets. Specific depth is what gets recommended, referred, and remembered.

---

Learn one thing until it earns. Not until you feel ready. Until someone pays. Then keep going.
