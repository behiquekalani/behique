1. Your network is not the people you know. It is the people who trust you. Size is irrelevant without that foundation.

2. Depth beats breadth in relationships every time. One person who genuinely knows your work is worth a hundred LinkedIn connections.

3. The easiest way to build a powerful network is to be useful before you need anything. The sequence matters more than the volume.

4. People remember who showed up when they had nothing to gain. Be that person more often than feels necessary.

5. A reputation for generosity with your time and knowledge is one of the most durable assets you can build. It compounds quietly.
