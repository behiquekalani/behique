# Twitter Batch 1618

## Tweet 1
Every social platform you build on is rented land.

The algorithm changes. The terms change. The platform shuts down.

Your audience was never yours.

## Tweet 2
Creator equity:

Social followers: rented.
Email subscribers: owned.
Search traffic from your domain: owned.
A brand people search for by name: owned.

One policy change can erase the first. None of them touch the rest.

## Tweet 3
The tweet that performs well should become an email.

The reel that goes viral should grow the list.

Platform success feeds the owned infrastructure. That is the move.

## Tweet 4
The assets worth building regardless of whether you plan to sell:

An email list.
A searchable content archive on your own domain.
A methodology people associate with your name specifically.

## Tweet 5
Most creators build almost entirely on rented platforms.

Then act surprised when the platform changes something and their income changes with it.

Build what you own. Everything else is borrowed time.
