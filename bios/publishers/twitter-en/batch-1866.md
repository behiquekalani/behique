1. Speed is not a quality problem. It is a feedback mechanism. Ship faster and you learn faster. Learn faster and the quality catches up quickly.

2. Perfectionism is not a high standard. It is fear with a good cover story. The real standard is whether it helps someone, not whether it impresses you.

3. The people shipping consistently are not more talented. They just stopped waiting for the right time, which never arrives, and started on a good enough day instead.

4. Shipping ugly work in public is more valuable than polishing it in private. The market corrects your assumptions faster than your imagination does.

5. Every day you do not ship is a day the competitor who does not overthink is getting ahead of you. Done is a competitive advantage.
