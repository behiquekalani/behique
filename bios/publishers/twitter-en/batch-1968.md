1. Advice is context-stripped. Someone's conclusion without the situation that produced it is just noise.

2. "Follow your passion" worked for someone whose passion the market happened to need. That is not a law.

3. The skill is not finding better advice. It is knowing when advice applies and when it does not.

4. Rejecting counsel from people you respect, when the context does not transfer, is a sign of judgment.

5. Most people are looking for a shortcut. The shortcut is actually the long way around.
