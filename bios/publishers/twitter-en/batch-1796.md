1. The bottleneck has never been information. It has always been application.

2. Reading about negotiation makes you more informed. Negotiating makes you better at negotiating. Not the same outcome.

3. New rule: you cannot consume the next thing until you have tried to apply the last thing once.

4. Consuming more is not the same as learning more. Learning requires a feedback loop. Consumption does not.

5. The growth lives in the application loop, not the consumption loop. Most people never switch loops.
