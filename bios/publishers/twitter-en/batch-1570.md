# Twitter Batch 1570

## Tweet 1
Your personal OS is not a productivity app.

It's the set of principles and filters that guide how you operate. What gets your attention. What you say no to. How you recover when things go wrong.

Without it, you're just reacting to whatever is loudest.

## Tweet 2
Most people have values vaguely defined and priorities completely undefined.

Which means every week starts with the same ambiguity.

Write down your top 3 priorities for this season. Post them somewhere visible. Watch your decisions improve.

## Tweet 3
You don't design a personal OS in a weekend.

You build it from watching yourself for a month.

When were you most effective? What killed your focus? Which decisions drained you? That observation becomes your framework.

## Tweet 4
Thread: the 4 components of a functional personal OS

1/ Values: what you won't compromise on
2/ Priorities: what gets your best energy
3/ Principles: the rules you operate by so you stop re-deciding
4/ Rhythms: the recurring behaviors that compound

Most people only have #1. Vaguely.

## Tweet 5
What's one decision you make repeatedly that you could just turn into a rule?

Rules eliminate decision fatigue. The best operators have more rules and fewer daily decisions than most people realize.
