1. Most people read passively. The book happens to them. Three weeks later they remember almost nothing and feel like they learned something.

2. The best time to read a book is when the problem it solves is alive in your life right now.

3. A concept you explain to someone else is retained ten times better than one you highlighted and moved past.

4. Reading only earns its value when it changes what you do next. Otherwise it is a pleasant way to feel like you are growing without actually growing.

5. Do not read widely. Read precisely. Pick the book that answers the specific question you are currently carrying. Everything else can wait.
