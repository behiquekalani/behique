1. Money is a signal, not a goal. It tells you whether the value you created actually landed. Chase the signal and you miss the point.

2. The people obsessed with the income number make worse decisions than the people obsessed with the problem they are solving.

3. Get obsessed with serving someone so specifically that payment is the natural consequence. Work backwards from value.

4. Quick money and right-direction money are different things. Mixing them up costs you years of position you cannot buy back.

5. When money does not show up, it is telling you something. Listen before you try harder at the same wrong thing.
