1. Polishing an unvalidated product is optimization without a target. You don't know what to polish because you haven't let real users tell you what matters.

2. The version in your head is perfect because no one has used it yet. Release it. The reality will teach you more than another month of building.

3. Ship with confidence, not with certainty. Confidence is "I built something worth testing." Certainty is "I know this will work." Only one of these is available before launch.

4. Early adopters don't need a finished product. They need a clear solution to a real problem. Polish comes after problem confirmation, not before.

5. What your first users ignore is as valuable as what they love. Silence tells you what you thought was important actually isn't. That's worth knowing.
