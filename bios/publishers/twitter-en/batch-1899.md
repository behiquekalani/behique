1. Optimization is the last refuge of the person afraid to ship. You can always find one more thing to improve. Ship it anyway.

2. Decide what "done enough" looks like before you start building. Write it down. When you hit it, stop and ship. Don't move the line.

3. The version that exists in the world does more than the perfect version that doesn't. Existing is already a massive advantage.

4. Optimization after launch is improvement. Optimization before launch is usually avoidance dressed up as craft.

5. The product that ships imperfect and iterates will outperform the product that never launches. Every time.
