# Twitter Batch 2478

**Tweet 1:** Most people price based on what it cost them to make. The buyer doesn't care about that. They care what the outcome is worth to them. Price accordingly.

**Tweet 2:** If your product saves someone ten hours a week for a year, the math is not "hours I spent times my rate." The math is the value of those hours saved.

**Tweet 3:** Higher prices filter toward people who take the work seriously. Someone who pays $200 is more likely to implement than someone who paid $10. Good for your reputation.

**Tweet 4:** Underpricing is not humility. It's a mismatch between the value you create and what you're willing to claim.

**Tweet 5:** Check your current price against the outcome it produces, not the cost of producing it. Adjust the gap. Your business model depends on it.
