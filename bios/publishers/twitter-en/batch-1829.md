1. "I am not a morning person" is not a description. It is an instruction your brain follows to stay consistent with a story you decided was true.

2. Identity-level change is the most powerful and the most resisted. Habits built on an unchanged identity collapse under pressure.

3. You do not wait for the evidence to accumulate before claiming a new identity. You construct the story in parallel with constructing the behavior.

4. Call yourself what you want to become before you fully are it. Not as an affirmation. As a hypothesis you are actively testing.

5. The cage most people live in is not a circumstance. It is a self-concept they stopped questioning.
