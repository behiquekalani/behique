# Twitter Batch 2308

## Tweet 1
You do not need a million followers.

You need a thousand people who actually care.

Those two things are very different. Most creators chase the first and are confused when the second never arrives.

## Tweet 2
A newsletter with 800 engaged subscribers will outperform a social account with 80,000 passive ones.

Attention is not the metric. Relationship is.

## Tweet 3
The math of a one-person business is much smaller than people think.

$50 product x 200 buyers a year = $10,000.

You don't need to go viral. You need to be consistently useful to a small group of people with a specific problem.

## Tweet 4
Build depth before breadth.

The audience that forms around depth is loyal, buys from you, and refers others.

The audience that forms around entertainment or trends is fragile and expensive to maintain.

## Tweet 5
Your real audience is not your follower count.

It is the number of people who reply, share, or return voluntarily.

Count those people. Build your next product for them specifically. You already have everything you need.
