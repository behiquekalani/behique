1. Playing it safe is not risk management. It's choosing a predictable bad outcome over an uncertain good one. The cost is just harder to see.

2. Every "safe" choice has a price. You just pay it slowly over years instead of all at once. The slow price is usually higher.

3. Risk compounds in both directions. Consistent action builds confidence and options. Consistent avoidance builds neither.

4. The question is never whether to take risks. You're always taking a risk. The only question is whether the risk has upside.

5. What's the actual downside of the thing you've been avoiding? Write it out. Most scary risks look much smaller written down.
