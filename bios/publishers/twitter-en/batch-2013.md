1. Probability is only one input. The other input is asymmetry. A 20% chance at a life-changing outcome with low downside is often the better bet than a 90% chance at a forgettable one.

2. Writing online is an asymmetric bet. It can reach thousands and cost you a few hours. Staying silent has no downside and no upside.

3. Most people consistently avoid asymmetric bets because the vocabulary that describes them sounds reckless. So they take safe bets and get safe outcomes.

4. The asymmetric bet is not always about money. It is about maximizing what is possible relative to what you are risking.

5. Learn to evaluate the shape of an outcome, not just its likelihood. That single shift changes most of your decisions.
