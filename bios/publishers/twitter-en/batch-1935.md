1. Safety is a short-term metric. Over a long time horizon, the risk of never taking risk is the highest risk available.

2. Each safe choice makes the next risky choice harder. You build a life that requires stability to maintain. The exit cost grows.

3. Risk taken at 22 is cheap. You have nothing to protect. The same gamble at 42 is a different calculation entirely.

4. The people with the most freedom later are almost always the ones who took concentrated risk early, not the ones who waited.

5. Comfort does not eventually feel like enough. It just gets more expensive to leave the longer you wait.
