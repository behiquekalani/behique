1. The story you tell yourself about who you are shapes every action you take. Update the story if the story is outdated.

2. Most self-narratives were written by a younger, less capable version of you. You're allowed to revise them.

3. You don't change a story with affirmations. You change it with contradictory evidence. Do the thing the old story says you can't.

4. The internal narrative runs in the background filtering what's possible. If the filter is wrong, the outputs will be too.

5. Small actions that contradict the old story are how new stories begin. Finish the thing. Show up. Do it again.
