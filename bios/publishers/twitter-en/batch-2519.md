# Twitter Batch 2519

**Tweet 1:** Most content gets written for the creator, not the reader. Ask before you post: what does this change for the person reading it? If the answer is nothing, rewrite it.

**Tweet 2:** The creator who commits to one platform for a year will outgrow the one who was present on five platforms for a month. Depth on one beats shallow on many every time.

**Tweet 3:** Selling a $97 product to 100 people is easier than selling a $9 product to 1,000. Fewer transactions, fewer customers to serve, same revenue. Price up before you scale volume.

**Tweet 4:** Every good product starts with an honest answer to one question: what is the most painful thing about this person's situation that I know how to fix? Build from that answer.

**Tweet 5:** Identity shifts are the real value of education. When your buyer finishes your product and sees themselves differently, that is the transformation worth paying for. Teach identity, not just tactics.
