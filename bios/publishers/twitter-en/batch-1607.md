# Twitter Batch 1607

## Tweet 1
Work expands to fill the time available for its completion.

Give yourself a week to write a post. It takes a week.

Give yourself ninety minutes. It takes ninety minutes and is often better.

## Tweet 2
The extra time you give yourself does not go to quality.

It goes to doubt.

Shorter deadlines remove the doubt loop.

## Tweet 3
Thread: Parkinson's Law for creators.

1/ The best writers have deadlines. The best musicians have recording windows. The best builders set artificial launch dates.

Constraints are not unfortunate. They are the mechanism that forces completion.

2/ Without a hard stop, the project lives in permanent almost-done.

Almost done generates no revenue. Almost done grows no audience.

3/ Set timelines shorter than feel comfortable. Finish before it feels ready. Publish before you have second-guessed every sentence.
