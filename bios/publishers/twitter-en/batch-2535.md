# Twitter Batch 2535

**Tweet 1:** The calendar is a scheduling tool. The strategy is the idea at the center of everything you create. Most people use the calendar as a substitute for strategy. That is why the content feels scattered.

**Tweet 2:** Income streams that do not reinforce each other are just jobs. Income streams built from one area of expertise compound. Every product makes the others more credible.

**Tweet 3:** An email subscriber is worth more than ten social media followers because the relationship is direct. No algorithm decides if they see your message. You send it. They receive it.

**Tweet 4:** The reason your audience feels generic is usually that you were afraid to go specific enough. What felt too narrow is often the exact thing that makes someone forward your content.

**Tweet 5:** Building a one-person business is mostly a clarity problem. Clear on who you serve, what you sell, and why it matters. Revenue follows clarity. It almost never precedes it.
