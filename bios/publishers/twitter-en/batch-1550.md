# Twitter Batch 1550

## Tweet 1
The best productivity advice is not a system.

It's a question.

"What is the one thing that, if done today, makes everything else easier or unnecessary?"

Do that first.

## Tweet 2
Thread: Why context switching is destroying your output.

1/ Every switch costs a re-entry fee.
2/ You pay it even when the tasks feel small.
3/ Half your energy on two projects is not equal to full energy on one.
4/ One project at a time is not a limitation. It's a multiplier.

Protect the state that produces your best work.

## Tweet 3
The most underused productivity tool is a clearly defined stopping point.

Not "work until I'm done."

"Work until 11am. Stop at 11am."

Constraints make you move faster.

## Tweet 4
Two hours of genuine deep work will produce more than six hours of reactive busyness.

Most people have never actually tested this.

Try it tomorrow. First two hours. No phone. No email. See what happens.

## Tweet 5
Your calendar is a mirror.

It shows you what you actually value, not what you say you value.

Look at it honestly before you make any changes to your system.
