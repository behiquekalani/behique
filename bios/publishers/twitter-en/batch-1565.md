## Tweet 1
Short form builds reach. Long form builds authority.

You need both eventually. But if you have to choose where to start, choose the one that outlasts the algorithm.

Long form doesn't expire.

## Tweet 2
A 2,000-word essay that genuinely changes how someone thinks about their industry will do more for your authority than 100 viral tweets.

One lasts. The other is forgotten in 48 hours.

## Tweet 3
The best long-form content sounds like someone working something out, not someone performing expertise.

Write from curiosity. Not from "let me show you what I know."

## Tweet 4
Thread: what long-form content does that short-form can't

1/ Gets indexed and discovered in search for years
2/ Gets shared in private channels where real decisions get made
3/ Builds the depth of trust required for high-ticket purchases
4/ Forces you to actually understand what you're talking about

The compression of ideas into tweets is valuable. But don't skip the depth.
