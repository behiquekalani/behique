1. Building in public for attention produces content about building. Building in public for accountability produces work.

2. When you know you are going to explain what you built and why, you make better decisions during the build. The audience improves the process whether or not they show up.

3. The goal is not a large audience of passive consumers. It is a small group of people doing serious work who find your work useful.

4. Share genuine work consistently, without performing, without polishing reality into a highlight reel. The connections that form around that are more valuable than follower counts.

5. You have to be more interested in the work than in being seen doing it. The moment those priorities flip, the work suffers and the audience can tell.
