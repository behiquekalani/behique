1. Writing forces clarity. You cannot write something coherent from an incoherent idea.

2. Thinking feels faster than writing. It is. The output is proportionally worse.

3. Externalizing your thoughts lets you argue with them. You cannot argue with what is still inside the argument.

4. The founders who write before they build spend more time upfront and save more time downstream.

5. Writing is not documentation. It is cognition made visible.
