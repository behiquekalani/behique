1. Not all work is equal. Some work produces output. Some produces output and compounds your capability at the same time. Chase the second kind.

2. If today's work is well within your current ability, you're maintaining. Maintenance has its place, but it shouldn't be most of your time.

3. The difficult phase of a project is not a signal to quit. It's the filter that removes everyone who wasn't serious. Stay past it.

4. Most people abandon things right before the breakthrough. Not because they failed. Because discomfort felt like a warning instead of a signal to continue.

5. Depth is built by staying. Not by accumulating experiences. Not by pivoting fast. By committing long enough to see things most people never see.
