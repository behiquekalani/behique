1. The perfect moment is a myth your brain invented to protect you from the risk of failure. The conditions you are waiting for will not arrive.

2. Every skilled person you admire started producing terrible work in conditions that were not ideal. The work improved because they started.

3. The feedback loop that creates skill only activates when you produce something real and put it in front of real consequences. You cannot shortcut that.

4. The willingness to begin badly, in public, without guarantees, is one of the rarest things a person can develop. It compounds into an entirely different life.

5. You practice starting under imperfect conditions enough times and you become someone who moves. That character trait is worth more than most skills.
