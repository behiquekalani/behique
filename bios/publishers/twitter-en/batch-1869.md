1. You do not have a time problem. You have an attention problem. Two focused hours produce more than eight distracted ones every time.

2. Every notification is a small withdrawal from your attention balance. The fee is invisible but the total is enormous. Guard the account carefully.

3. Your best hours deserve your best work, not your easiest tasks. Stop scheduling meetings during the window when your thinking is sharpest.

4. Context switching is not multitasking. It is paying a cognitive tax every time you switch. The cost is not the switch itself. It is the recovery time after.

5. The single most reliable way to produce more is to protect a block of time where nothing else is allowed. Not scheduling. Not email. Just the work.
