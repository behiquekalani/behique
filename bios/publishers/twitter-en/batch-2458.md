# Twitter Batch 2458

**Tweet 1:** Write to one person. Not a demographic. Not an avatar. One specific human whose situation you understand without guessing. The specificity will resonate with thousands.

**Tweet 2:** The more precisely you describe one person's situation, the more people recognize themselves in it. Specific is not limiting. Specific is magnetic.

**Tweet 3:** Generic content competes with everything. Specific content stops the scroll because the reader thinks you wrote it for them. You basically did.

**Tweet 4:** Before you write, answer this: who is the one person this is for, what situation are they in right now, and what do they need to hear? Then write.

**Tweet 5:** The paradox of writing for one person is that it reaches more people than writing for everyone. Narrow your target. Widen your reach.
