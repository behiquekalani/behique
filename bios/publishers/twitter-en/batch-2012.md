1. Reputation is built in moments when no one important is watching. That is also when it is most accurately built.

2. The colleague who performs on high-stakes tasks and cuts corners otherwise has a reputation. So does the person who does the same quality work regardless of audience. Those reputations are not the same.

3. Trust is built in invisible moments. Most of the valuable opportunities in life come through people who trust you, not people who are impressed by you.

4. The habits you build in private shape your baseline output in public. The gap between those two is smaller than most people want to admit.

5. What you do when you are tired and behind and no one will notice is exactly the work that builds the reputation that opens the next door.
