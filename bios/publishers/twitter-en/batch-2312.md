# Twitter Batch 2312

## Tweet 1
The most useful feedback you can get is from someone who almost bought and didn't.

They had the problem. They found you. Something in the gap didn't close.

Find out what it was.

## Tweet 2
"Why didn't you buy?" is the wrong question.

"What would have made this feel like an obvious yes?" is the right one.

One puts the person on the defensive. The other gives you a product roadmap.

## Tweet 3
Your existing customers will tell you how to improve what you have.

Your almost-buyers will tell you what's stopping you from growing.

Most creators only talk to the first group.

## Tweet 4
Three DMs this week to people who expressed interest but didn't convert.

One question: "What would have made this a clear yes for you?"

Listen without defending. Write down what you hear. Build the answer into your next iteration.

## Tweet 5
Almost-buyers are your most valuable research.

They are closer to buying than a cold lead. They understand the problem. They found your solution.

Something small is in the way. Find it. Remove it. The conversion lifts without more traffic.
