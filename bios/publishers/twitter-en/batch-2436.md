# Twitter Batch 2436

1. Consistency is a system problem, not a motivation problem. Fix the system. Stop waiting to feel like it.

2. Design a version of your workday you can execute even on your worst day. That's your floor. Hold it.

3. Batching tasks pays the startup cost once instead of five times. Same output, less mental overhead.

4. "I am a person who writes every day" is harder to abandon than "I'm trying to post more." Identity sticks.

5. Motivation fluctuates. The creators who last 10 years built systems that don't depend on how they feel.
