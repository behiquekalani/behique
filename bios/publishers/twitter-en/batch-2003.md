1. Perspective compounds faster than money. A better view of the situation makes every other resource more effective.

2. Perspective does not compound automatically. You have to make real decisions, get real results, and sit with what you got wrong. Study alone is not enough.

3. Without skin in the game, lessons stay theoretical. The compounding of judgment only starts when something is actually at stake.

4. Reading without acting on it is expensive entertainment. The book you read while solving a real problem is worth ten you read abstractly.

5. The person who sees the situation more clearly does not just outperform. They outperform by more each year. That is what compounding looks like in practice.
