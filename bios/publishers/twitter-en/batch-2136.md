Every original thinker you admire was a student first. They imitated deeply until the imitation broke down and something new emerged.

Imitation is how you understand the decisions behind a finished thing. Copy something closely enough and you stop seeing the surface. You see the structure.

The key is what you do after the imitation. If you stop there, that is copying. If you extract the principles and apply them to your own material, that is synthesis.

Synthesis produces original work. All synthesis is built on a foundation of understanding. Understanding is built on careful imitation.

Study what works. Do not be embarrassed about it. Every master had a period of disciplined copying before they had a voice of their own.
