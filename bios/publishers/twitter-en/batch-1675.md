# Twitter Batch 1675

## Tweet 1
1% better every day sounds trivial. Most people quit before the slope changes. The ones who stay past the flat part of the curve are the ones who get to see compounding work.

## Tweet 2
One percent improvement across ten skills is noise. One percent improvement in one specific skill, repeated for 6 months, is mastery. The focus is what makes the math work.

## Tweet 3
You cannot improve what you do not measure. Define the one thing you are trying to get better at. Track one data point weekly. That is the whole system.

## Tweet 4
The compound curve is invisible for months. Then it becomes obvious. Most people quit during the invisible phase and tell themselves it was not working. It was working. They just left before the payout.

## Tweet 5
What is one skill you use every week that, if you got 10% better at it over the next year, would change everything? Start there. Not with the exciting new skill. With the foundational one.
