1. Skill compounds non-linearly. Three years of daily practice does not make you three times better. It makes you exponentially better.

2. Reputation has gravity. Build it long enough and opportunities start finding you instead of you chasing them.

3. The curve stays flat for a long time before it bends. Most people quit during the flat part and never see the bend.

4. Two years of consistent skill-building beats four years of scattered effort every time. Direction matters more than intensity.

5. The gap between early results and late results is so large most people assume something is wrong. Nothing is wrong. Stay.
