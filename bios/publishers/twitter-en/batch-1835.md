1. Boredom is a compass, not a problem. It points directly at the gap between where you are and where you want to be. Tolerate it. Listen to it.

2. The ideas that feel genuinely new rarely arrive during consumption. They arrive in the quiet after you have resisted the pull of distraction.

3. Filling every gap with input prevents your brain from doing the consolidation work that produces real insight. Idle time is not wasted time.

4. If you have never trained yourself to tolerate low stimulation without panic, the boring phases of building will derail you. That capacity is rarer than talent.

5. The person who can be bored without catastrophizing it can finish things. That is the whole edge.
