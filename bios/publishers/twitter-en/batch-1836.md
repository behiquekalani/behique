1. Low prices do not attract more serious buyers. They attract more skeptical ones. Price is communication before the sale happens.

2. Raising your price often reduces objections rather than increasing them. The buyer at a higher price point is more committed and more likely to get results.

3. Take whatever you plan to charge and double it. Ask what you would need to add or prove to justify that number. Build those things.

4. The work required to justify a higher price almost always produces a better product. The pricing exercise is secretly a product development exercise.

5. You signal how much you believe in what you built through what you charge for it. Buyers pick up on that signal even when they cannot name it.
