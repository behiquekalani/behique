# Twitter Batch 1566

## Tweet 1
The thing nobody tells you about going solo:

The business skills are learnable. The mental game is what breaks you.

Most solopreneurs quit not because their business failed. They quit because they couldn't handle the weight of uncertainty.

## Tweet 2
Slow months aren't evidence your business is dying.

They're part of every solo operation's rhythm.

The panic is the problem. Not the slowness.

## Tweet 3
Consistency without emotional dependency on outcomes is the most underrated business skill.

Show up regardless of results. Do the work that compounds slowly. Trust the process enough to not blow it up.

## Tweet 4
What's the story you tell yourself on a slow week?

Write it down. Then write a counter-statement based on actual evidence.

Keep that document somewhere visible. Your future self will need it.

## Tweet 5
The solopreneurs who last the longest treat their business like a practice, not a performance.

You're not going on stage. You're building something. That requires showing up the same way on Tuesday at 9am as you do after a week.
