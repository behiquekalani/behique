# Twitter Batch 1632

## Tweet 1
The first 100 days of any new pursuit are not representative.

Your skills are too early. Your audience is too new. The sample size is too small.

Making strategy decisions from that data is like reading a book's first page and deciding you understand the theme.

## Tweet 2
Two things happen around day 100:

Your skills improve enough that the work gets easier.

You have enough output to see actual patterns.

Before that, you're operating without real information. After it, you can make legitimate decisions.

## Tweet 3
Quitting at day 30 is not pivoting.

It's abandoning the experiment before the results come in.

## Tweet 4
People who treat every post as a lottery ticket get worse results than people who treat every post as practice.

The streak matters more than any single piece.

This is the part nobody wants to hear.

## Tweet 5
Name a project you started and abandoned before hitting 100 days.

Was the strategy genuinely flawed?

Or was the early feedback just discouraging?

Those are not the same reason to quit.
