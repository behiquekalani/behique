# Twitter Batch 1678

## Tweet 1
The difficult clients all showed signs before you said yes. You just chose to ignore them because you needed the money or the project sounded interesting.

## Tweet 2
The client who questions your pricing will question your judgment. The client who is slow to sign will be slow to pay. The discovery call is data. Use it.

## Tweet 3
You do not have to take every client. Saying no to a bad fit protects the time and energy you need to find a good one. One difficult client can consume the capacity to find three good ones.

## Tweet 4
Thread: Client red flags most people miss.

1. Asks for a discount before seeing the proposal
2. Questions your process before understanding your results
3. Wants to start without a contract
4. Slow on decisions but fast on complaints

Any one of these is a conversation to have before you start.

## Tweet 5
The best clients refer more best clients. Build your business around them. The difficult ones cost more than they pay when you count all the time and energy.
