# Twitter Batch 2267

## Tweet 1
Being on five platforms with no traction on any of them is not a strategy.

It's dilution.

Pick one. Go deep. Build real signal before you expand.

## Tweet 2
Stay on one platform until you have consistent traction.

90 days minimum.

Before that you don't have data. You have impatience disguised as evaluation.

## Tweet 3
Most people quit at day 30 because "it isn't working."

Then they quit the next platform at day 30.

Then the next.

They spend a year being a beginner at five things instead of getting good at one.

## Tweet 4
One platform done well teaches you:

- What content your audience actually engages with
- What format works for you
- What topics convert attention to trust

That learning transfers everywhere. Spread too thin and you learn nothing.

## Tweet 5
The audience that trusts you is built in one place first.

Then it travels with you.

Build the base before you build the branches.
