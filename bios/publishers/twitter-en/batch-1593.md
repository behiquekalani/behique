# Twitter Batch 1593

## Tweet 1
Most creators operate in reactive mode.

When it is time to post, they sit down and try to produce something from scratch.

This is the worst possible workflow.

## Tweet 2
One day of focused writing can produce a week's worth of content.

This is not theoretical. It requires a backlog of ideas, clear formats, and protection of the creation window.

## Tweet 3
The hidden benefit of batching is psychological.

When your content is planned in advance, you stop posting from anxiety.

You start posting from abundance.

## Tweet 4
Context switching has a cost.

Moving from writing to email to editing to publishing in the same morning fragments your attention and lowers the quality of every task.

Batch by task type.

## Tweet 5
What would your week look like if Monday was for writing, Tuesday for editing, and Wednesday for publishing?

Most people never try it. Most people who do never go back.
