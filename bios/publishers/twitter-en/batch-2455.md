# Twitter Batch 2455

**Tweet 1:** Low prices do not attract more buyers. They attract less committed ones. Price signals who your product is for.

**Tweet 2:** The buyer who pays $500 for your course shows up, does the work, and sends referrals. The $50 buyer often doesn't open it. Price changes behavior.

**Tweet 3:** Competing on price means competing with everyone. Competing on value means competing with almost no one. Choose your battlefield.

**Tweet 4:** If your price feels too low to you, it probably looks suspicious to your buyer. Confidence in your price is part of the product.

**Tweet 5:** What is the outcome your product delivers worth to someone who achieves it? Price relative to that answer. Not relative to what feels safe to charge.
