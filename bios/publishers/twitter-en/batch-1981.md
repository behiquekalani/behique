1. The person with one skill is competing against everyone. The person with three complementary skills is competing against almost no one.

2. You do not need more information. You need to act on what you already know. The gap is never knowledge. It is execution.

3. Most people optimize for looking busy. The rare move is optimizing for output. These are not the same thing and they rarely overlap.

4. Imposter syndrome is just unfamiliarity with the territory you are entering. It dissolves with repetition. The cure is more reps, not more reassurance.

5. A decision made in discomfort and followed through is worth ten decisions made in comfort and abandoned. Commitment is the skill.
