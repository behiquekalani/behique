1. "Everyone" is not a target market. "Everyone" is where good offers go to become invisible.

2. The moment narrowing your niche feels too small is usually the moment you've hit the right level of specificity. Stop one step earlier and you're average.

3. Specific audiences feel spoken to. Broad audiences feel addressed. Only one of those converts.

4. You don't pick a narrow niche forever. You earn a foothold, then expand from trust rather than from hoping someone notices you.

5. Start smaller than feels right. The market will pull you broader once you've proven yourself in the corner.
