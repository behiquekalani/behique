# Twitter Batch 2577

**Tweet 1:** Automation makes a bad process faster. It does not fix the process. Productize first. Automate second.

**Tweet 2:** Do the work manually three times. Write down exactly what you do. Then look at what is mechanical. That is what you automate.

**Tweet 3:** The judgment-dependent parts stay human. The repeatable parts become systems. Know the difference before you touch an automation tool.

**Tweet 4:** Skipping productization and jumping to automation is how you build a complex system that does the wrong thing consistently and efficiently.

**Tweet 5:** Get the process right first. Make it boring in its consistency. Then let automation remove the friction. That sequence matters.
