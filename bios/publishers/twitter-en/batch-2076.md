1. Goals tell you where to go. Systems determine whether you arrive. Most people invest heavily in the first and almost nothing in the second.

2. Willpower is not a reliable engine. It depletes, fluctuates with sleep and mood, and fails in predictable ways. Systems don't have bad days.

3. A goal without a system is a wish with a deadline. The deadline passes. The goal gets pushed. The pattern repeats.

4. Goals are events. Systems are infrastructure. The best builders care more about the machine than the individual output.

5. The writing system you build to launch one newsletter keeps producing after launch. The goal ends. The system compounds.
