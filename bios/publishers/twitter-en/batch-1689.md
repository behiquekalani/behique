# Twitter Batch 1689

## Tweet 1
500 subscribers who open your emails is a business.

50,000 who don't isn't.

Stop chasing the number. Build the relationship.

## Tweet 2
The email list isn't valuable because of its size.

It's valuable because it's the only channel you own that doesn't require an algorithm to say yes before your message reaches someone.

## Tweet 3
Quick math: 500 subscribers, 5% conversion, $97 product = $2,425 from one email.

Small list. Real relationship. Specific offer.

The numbers work when the trust is real.

## Tweet 4
Most email newsletters fail not because they lack subscribers.

They fail because the creator never built a real relationship with the ones they had.

Send value. Not content. Value.

## Tweet 5
Write your next email as if it's going to one specific person.

Name them in your head. Address their actual problem. Give something useful.

Then send it to everyone.

The specific always performs better than the general.
