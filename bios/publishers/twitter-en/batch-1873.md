1. You are not comparing yourself to your peers. You are comparing yourself to the global top performers in every category at once, all day, on your phone. That is not a fair benchmark.

2. The feed is not reality. It is the highlight reel of thousands of people, curated to provoke a reaction. Stop using it to measure your progress.

3. The only comparison that tells you anything useful is you six months ago versus you today. That one measures actual growth. Every other comparison measures the internet's ability to shrink you.

4. Progress that does not match someone else's public output is still progress. You are building your thing, not a faster version of their thing.

5. Most people who look ahead of you are also overwhelmed, uncertain, and wondering if they are doing enough. The confident exterior is the product, not the reality.
