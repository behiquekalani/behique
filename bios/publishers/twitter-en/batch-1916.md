1. Build deliberate gaps into your day. No input. No content. Let your mind do what it does when it's not reacting to anything. Most people never find out what that is.

2. The shower produces ideas that focused work doesn't. Not because showers are magic. Because your mind is off the path and making connections it can't make under direction.

3. Willpower is scarce. Friction is adjustable. Put the phone in another room. Make the bad option inconvenient. Let the environment do the deciding.

4. What's visible is what gets done. Put your one important thing in your line of sight when you sit down. Remove everything else that competes for attention.

5. A distraction-free environment isn't a luxury for monks. It's a performance tool. The people who protect theirs produce more than those who don't, every time.
