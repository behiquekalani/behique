# Twitter Batch 1603

## Tweet 1
A business that only works because you are there every day is not a business.

It is a job you own.

Buyers pay for systems and recurring revenue. Not for the founder's presence.

## Tweet 2
Building to sell from day one makes you better at building.

You document. You systematize. You reduce yourself as a single point of failure.

Those habits make the business better whether you sell it or not.

## Tweet 3
Small exits are real exits.

A content site earning $3,000/month can sell for $100,000.

You do not need a billion-dollar acquisition to win.

## Tweet 4
The test of a transferable business:

Could someone else run this without you explaining everything from scratch?

If the answer is no, that is the work.

## Tweet 5
Most solopreneurs build themselves into their business so deeply that they cannot leave even if they want to.

That is not success. That is a different kind of trap.

Build the exit option even if you never use it.
