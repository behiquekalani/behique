# Twitter Batch 1480

## Tweet 1
Most people build a business and try to fit their life around it.

That produces a very specific kind of misery: successful by the numbers, hollow in the experience.

Build the business that fits the life. Not the other way around.

## Tweet 2
Before deciding what to build, ask: what does my ideal week look like?

Work backwards from that answer.

The business is a construction project. Design it before you build it.

## Tweet 3
Revenue is a lagging indicator of alignment.

$20K a month with 80-hour weeks and constant anxiety is not the goal.

Do not mistake revenue for winning if the life does not match.

## Tweet 4
What is one part of your current work that you would remove tomorrow if you could?

That thing is worth examining. It might be closer to removable than you think.

## Tweet 5
Thread: how to realign a business that no longer fits your life

1. Audit everything you do
2. Mark what requires your direct time
3. Remove what does not fit the life you want
4. Systematize what can be systematized
5. Add only what aligns

You built it. You can redesign it.
