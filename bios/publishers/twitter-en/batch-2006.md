1. You can be extremely productive on the wrong thing and end up further from where you want to be than if you had done nothing.

2. Busyness is a defense mechanism. It gives you the feeling of progress without requiring you to confront whether you are moving toward the right thing.

3. The output per hour is not the right metric. The question is whether the hours you are optimizing are connected to the outcome you actually want.

4. Most people answer the "what am I building toward" question once, at the start, and never revisit it. That is how you end up optimizing for a goal you outgrew three years ago.

5. Work expands to fill the time available. It also drifts toward what is comfortable. Both of those need periodic correction.
