1. The problem with learning too much before doing is that you collect theory without the experience to make it stick. Do first. Study second. Absorb it at depth.

2. Your first 100 pieces of content are practice. The goal is not reach. The goal is finding your voice. Publish anyway. The audience is secondary.

3. A calendar that reflects your priorities is a rare document. Most calendars reflect other people's priorities. Audit yours.

4. The business model you can explain to your grandmother is the business model you actually understand. If you need jargon, keep simplifying.

5. Momentum is fragile at the start and durable later. Protect the early momentum like nothing else matters. Because at that stage, it does not.
