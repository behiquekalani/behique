# Twitter Batch 1510

## Tweet 1
Four uninterrupted hours on one thing produces more than eight distracted hours on everything.

Focus is a multiplier, not just a preference.

## Tweet 2
You cannot build something significant on the hours that are left over after everything else takes its cut.

Protect the first hours. Give the last ones to the world.

## Tweet 3
What is the one thing you are working on today?

Not three. Not five. One. The rest waits.

## Tweet 4
The compound returns on focused work are nonlinear.

One deep session does not produce twice the output of two shallow ones. It produces ten times. This is the thing most productivity advice misses.

## Tweet 5
Contrarian take: Most people do not need more time.

They need fewer things competing for the time they already have.
