# Twitter Batch 1639

## Tweet 1
Pivoting can be strategy.

It can also be quitting with better framing.

The difference matters enormously and most people can't tell which one they're doing in the moment.

## Tweet 2
Staying too long on something that doesn't work: stubbornness disguised as persistence.

Leaving too early on something that needs more time: avoidance disguised as strategy.

Both feel like good decisions. That's what makes them hard.

## Tweet 3
Real pivot signal is specific.

It's not "this isn't growing fast enough."

It's: I tested this specific offer with this specific audience for this specific period and they didn't respond despite genuine value.

## Tweet 4
Weak metrics plus growing engagement = audience problem. Keep going, fix distribution.

Weak metrics plus zero engagement = message problem. That's worth changing.

These require different responses. Most people treat them the same.

## Tweet 5
Before you pivot, answer this:

Have you actually tested it, or have you just published it?

Testing means putting it in front of the right people repeatedly. Publishing once is not a test.
