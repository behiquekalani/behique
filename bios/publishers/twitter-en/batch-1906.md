1. Price is a signal about confidence. Low prices don't attract more customers. They attract customers who aren't committed to the outcome.

2. The person who pays a real price shows up differently. They do the work. They get the result. That result becomes the next testimonial.

3. Buyers use price as a proxy for quality when they can't evaluate the work directly. Underpricing signals doubt in your own product.

4. Your first version is research, not a product. Its only job is to produce the information that makes version two worth building.

5. Test with ten people before a public launch. What they ignore, what confuses them, and what they love will rewrite your entire roadmap.
