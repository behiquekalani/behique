# Twitter Batch 2257

## Tweet 1
Broad targeting feels like safety.

It's a trap.

If you speak to everyone, nobody feels spoken to.

Pick a specific person with a specific problem.

## Tweet 2
"Productivity tips for professionals."

Vague. Forgotten in seconds.

"Time systems for freelance designers with ADHD."

That person just stopped scrolling.

## Tweet 3
Specificity creates recognition.

The reader thinks "this is for me."

That feeling is what drives follows, purchases, and word-of-mouth.

## Tweet 4
A 200-person list of people who relate to everything you say outperforms a 2,000-person list of people who sometimes agree.

Niche down.

The right people convert at a completely different rate.

## Tweet 5
Niche down one more level past where you think is too specific.

That's usually where the real audience lives.

The fear of narrowing is almost always wrong.
