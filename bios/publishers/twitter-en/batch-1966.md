1. Vague goals let you want things without feeling the friction of pursuing them.

2. Name exactly what done looks like. The obstacle usually becomes obvious immediately.

3. "Get better at writing" is not a plan. "Write 300 words every morning for 30 days" is a plan.

4. Specificity removes the escape hatch that lets you feel like you are working without moving.
