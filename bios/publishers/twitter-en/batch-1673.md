# Twitter Batch 1673

## Tweet 1
The more specific you are, the more magnetic you become to the exact person who needs you. Broad positioning is comfortable. Specific positioning converts.

## Tweet 2
"How to be more productive" is noise. "Why I stopped using task lists and started using time blocks" is a signal. Train yourself to replace every general word with a specific one.

## Tweet 3
Narrow positioning is not about reducing your audience. It is about finding the ones who will actually pay. The wide audience was never going to buy anyway.

## Tweet 4
Thread: The specificity test for your content.

Can the right person read the first line and immediately think "this is for me"?

If not, rewrite the first line. The hook is not about being clever. It is about being precise.

## Tweet 5
Your positioning should be specific enough to repel the wrong people. If everyone can see themselves in it, it is not specific enough.
