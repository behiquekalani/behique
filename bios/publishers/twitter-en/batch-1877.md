1. You are not the person you were at 19. Stop letting the beliefs you formed then limit the actions you take now. Test them. Most will not survive contact with who you have become.

2. "I am not the type to" is usually "I have not tried yet and the uncertainty feels safer to avoid." It is not identity. It is a habit of avoidance.

3. Self-concept calcifies when you stop testing it. The person who keeps experimenting stays flexible. The person who stops narrows slowly into the story they told about themselves.

4. The beliefs that limit you most are the ones that feel the most obviously true. They are obvious because you have never challenged them. Try.

5. Who you are is less fixed than you think. Most of it is just accumulated behavior. Change the behavior long enough and the identity follows.
