# Twitter Batch 1571

## Tweet 1
Most creators quit before the compounding starts.

That's the entire story.

The curve is flat for six months, then it tilts. Almost nobody is patient enough to see the tilt.

## Tweet 2
A library of content works for you constantly.

Surfacing in search. Getting shared. Building context for new readers who arrive knowing nothing about you.

That library requires consistent output. There's no shortcut.

## Tweet 3
The biggest threat to content compounding is inconsistency followed by rebranding.

When you disappear for two months and come back as a different version of yourself, you reset the momentum.

Pivots are fine. Extended breaks are expensive.

## Tweet 4
Your best-performing content from six months ago is still your best asset.

Repurpose it. Update it. Put it in front of new eyes.

Working smarter with what already worked is the fastest way to grow.

## Tweet 5
Contrarian take: going viral is not the goal.

Going consistently is.

One viral post gives you a spike. Consistent output over two years gives you a business. They're not the same thing.
