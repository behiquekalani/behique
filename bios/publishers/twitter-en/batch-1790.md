1. Waiting for inspiration is procrastination with a creative excuse attached to it.

2. You cannot think your way into wanting to write. You write your way into thinking clearly.

3. The creative state does not precede the work. It arrives during it.

4. A body of work is not made of your best days. It is made of all the days you showed up, especially the mediocre ones.

5. Same time. Same place. Same trigger. You are not building motivation. You are building a reflex.
