1. Trying to appeal to everyone means meaning nothing to anyone. Specificity is not a constraint. It is the strategy.

2. The creator who writes for a specific person in a specific situation competes with almost no one. The creator who writes for everyone competes with everyone.

3. Deep, specific relief generates word of mouth. Mild usefulness generates nothing. You want people to feel seen, not vaguely addressed.

4. The narrower the problem and the more complete your solution, the more you can charge and the less competition you face. That is not a coincidence.

5. Start with one person. Serve them better than anyone else on the planet. The broader audience follows from depth, not from reach.
