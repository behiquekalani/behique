1. You are not building a product or a brand. You are building a body of work. That framing changes what you make, how often, and why.

2. Every decision you defer is a decision you are paying attention to without a return. Make the decision. The mental space it frees is worth more than the deliberation.

3. The founder who understands their customer better than their competitor does will win even with an inferior product. Understanding is the edge.

4. The goal is not to stop having bad days. The goal is to make your bad days more productive than most people's good days. Systems do that.

5. You have enough. You know enough. You are ready enough. The question was never preparation. It was whether you were willing to start.
