1. Planning is the most socially acceptable form of procrastination. The plan is not the work. The work is the work.

2. You cannot think your way to the right direction. You have to build your way there. The path only becomes clear after you take the first steps.

3. The question is not whether your idea is ready. The question is whether you are willing to start before it is.

4. Every hour spent optimizing a strategy before testing it is an hour you chose a feeling of progress over actual progress.

5. Finished beats perfect. A done thing that exists in the world teaches you more in one week than a perfect thing still in your head teaches you in a year.
