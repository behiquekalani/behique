1. Time is not your scarcest resource. Attention is. You can have empty hours and waste them all because your attention was already spent before you sat down.

2. Every app and platform is built on the assumption that your focus is available for interruption. Treat your attention like it is finite, because it is.

3. One hour of full attention outperforms four hours of fragmented effort. This is not motivational. It is structural.

4. Your most important work should happen before the world has had a chance to make demands on you. Protect that window like your output depends on it, because it does.

5. Full presence is not a mood. It is a resource you schedule and defend. Everything else is maintenance.
