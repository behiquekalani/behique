1. Having ten viable paths forward is not freedom. It is paralysis wearing a suit.

2. Options feel like power. Past a threshold, they are a trap. You cannot commit fully to any direction while keeping one foot in all of them.

3. The counterintuitive move is to close doors. Not because the other options are bad, but because open doors bleed focus slowly enough that you never notice the bleeding.

4. Depth compounds. Breadth stays flat. Both feel like progress from the inside. The difference shows up over five years.

5. Constraints force commitment. Commitment is where the interesting work happens. The person with no constraints is usually the person producing nothing.
