# Twitter Batch 1619

## Tweet 1
Discipline is overrated.

Not because it does not matter, but because relying on it as your primary operating mechanism is unstable.

Willpower depletes. Systems do not.

## Tweet 2
A system is a predefined answer to a recurring question.

Which day do I post? The system decided.
How long before I check messages? The system decided.

Good systems are boring. They remove improvisation and replace it with output.

## Tweet 3
The systems that stick are simple enough to follow when you are tired and distracted.

Complexity is why most systems fail.

One field per decision. One rule per situation. Nothing more.

## Tweet 4
Thread: Why willpower is a poor foundation for creative work.

1/ Every decision costs energy. The more decisions you face, the less reliable your judgment by end of day.

2/ Creators who rely on willpower for consistency are betting their output on a resource that runs out.

3/ The fix is not more willpower. It is fewer decisions. A system makes the decision once so you never have to make it again.

## Tweet 5
The one question that cuts through productivity noise:

What recurring decision costs you the most mental energy each week?

Design a system around that. Everything else can wait.
