1. Everyone asks what to add. Almost nobody asks what to remove. Subtraction is usually the higher-leverage question.

2. Doing fewer things with more focus produces better results than doing many things with fragmented attention. Every time.

3. A good no protects a better yes. The discipline of saying no to good opportunities is what makes space for excellent ones.

4. Your constraints are probably not what you think they are. The real constraint is usually scattered energy, not missing tools.

5. What one thing, if removed, would make everything else compound faster? That is the question worth sitting with.
