# Twitter Batch 1628

## Tweet 1
Content that teaches is not about sharing information.

It's about giving someone the framework so they can apply it in situations you didn't cover.

That's the difference between making someone smarter and making them dependent.

## Tweet 2
Generic advice is free everywhere.

Your value is in the specific thing that worked for you in your exact situation with your exact constraints.

Stop filtering that out in favor of sounding more authoritative.

## Tweet 3
Thread: why your content isn't teaching as much as you think it is.

1/ Giving the conclusion without the reasoning is not teaching. It's asserting. "Do this" tells someone what to do. "Here's why this works" makes them capable of doing it in new contexts.

## Tweet 4
Clear structure is not a style choice.

It's an accessibility choice.

A clear sequence from problem to insight to application means the reader follows the logic instead of working to find it.

## Tweet 5
The reader who finishes your content and feels more capable is the reader who shares it, comes back for more, and eventually buys.

What you're really selling is the feeling of getting smarter.
