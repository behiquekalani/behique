1. Have a take. Defend it. Update it when you are wrong. That cycle is how thinking actually works.

2. Opinions you drop the moment the room disagrees were never really yours.

3. The most forgettable people in any room are the ones who agree with whoever spoke last.

4. Being wrong out loud is more useful than being vague on purpose.

5. Conviction is not stubbornness. It is a foundation. You can rebuild on it. You cannot build on nothing.
