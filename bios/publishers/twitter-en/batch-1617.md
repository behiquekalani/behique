# Twitter Batch 1617

## Tweet 1
If every dollar you earn requires an hour of your time, you have a job.

You just happen to own it.

The work of building a real business is breaking that link.

## Tweet 2
Asymmetric income: your earnings are not proportional to your hours.

A product you built once and sell repeatedly.
A newsletter that earns sponsorships whether or not you were productive that week.

That is the architecture worth building toward.

## Tweet 3
Thread: How to transition from trading time for money.

1/ You do not quit services overnight. You run services to fund asset-building.

2/ Every dollar of service margin that goes to lifestyle is a dollar not building the infrastructure that frees you.

3/ The sequence: service pays bills, margin builds assets, assets eventually replace service income.

## Tweet 4
Most people spend all their service income on lifestyle and save none for asset creation.

The timeline never starts.

## Tweet 5
What percentage of your current income requires your active presence to generate?

If the answer is above 70%, that number is the problem worth solving.
