# Twitter Batch 2489

**Tweet 1:** Most content decays in 72 hours. Some content compounds for years. The ratio in most creators' output is inverted.

**Tweet 2:** Short-form drives discovery. Long-form converts and retains. A tweet sparks curiosity. The essay satisfies it. The email list captures the relationship.

**Tweet 3:** Ask about every piece you create: will this still be valuable in two years? If yes, it deserves more time than content with a 72-hour shelf life.

**Tweet 4:** Content that compounds builds your asset base over time. Short-form alone builds activity, not assets. Assets generate returns without continuous input.

**Tweet 5:** The creator who writes 50 evergreen essays has a different business than the one who posts 500 times and disappears. Archive is infrastructure.
