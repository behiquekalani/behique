# Twitter Batch 1644

## Tweet 1
Writing for a thousand readers means writing for none of them.

Pick one person. Write to that person. The specificity is what makes everyone else feel seen.

## Tweet 2
Your best reader is a past version of yourself.

The version stuck on the exact problem you've since solved.

Write to that person. They're everywhere.

## Tweet 3
The difference between content that gets bookmarked and content that gets acted on:

Bookmarked = sounds true
Acted on = feels personally addressed

Write for the second.

## Tweet 4
Narrow your audience to deepen your reach.

This sounds like a paradox. It's actually just how human attention works.

## Tweet 5
Before your next piece of content, write one sentence:

"I'm writing this for [specific person] who is struggling with [specific problem]."

Keep that sentence visible. Cut everything that person wouldn't care about.
