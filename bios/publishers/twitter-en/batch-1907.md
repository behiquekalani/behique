1. Goals have end dates. Directions don't. Build goals inside directions so hitting one becomes a waypoint, not a finish line.

2. The runner who doesn't know what to do the week after the marathon trained for an event, not a direction. Don't let your purpose expire.

3. Most decisions are optimized for who you are now. The interesting outcomes require decisions made on behalf of who you're becoming.

4. Write down who you intend to be in three years. Not what you'll have. Who you'll be. Then ask whether your current decisions match that person.

5. The future version of you can see what you can't. Give that version decision-making authority now. It's not delusional. It's directional.
