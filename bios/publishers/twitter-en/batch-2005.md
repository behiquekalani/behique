1. The path forward is usually hidden behind the first step, not visible from where you are standing.

2. A year of waiting for the right moment is a year of compounding that never started.

3. Run cheap experiments. Treat them as information-gathering. Use what you learn to sharpen the next move. That is strategy, not recklessness.

4. You can research indefinitely and never reach the place where the next step is obvious. That place does not exist at your current level of information.

5. The cost of waiting for clarity is invisible because nothing breaks. You just stay in the same place while the world keeps moving.
