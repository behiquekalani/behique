What you tolerate defines what you get. The standard you hold consistently is the floor of your output. Everything below it is a choice you are making.

Perfectionism is fear. Standards are integrity. One prevents you from shipping. The other makes the next version better.

Raising your standards applies to you first. It is easy to demand quality from others. Harder to demand it from yourself when no one is watching.

The work you do when no one is watching is exactly what determines what you are capable of when they are.

A small tolerance for low quality becomes a culture of low quality. Noise compounds. So do standards.
