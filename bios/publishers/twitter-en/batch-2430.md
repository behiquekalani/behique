# Twitter Batch 2430

1. "I need to think about it" usually means "I'm not convinced this will work for me." Speak to the fear.

2. Make the buyer's fear explicit in your content. Then answer it honestly. That transparency converts better than any testimonial.

3. A strong guarantee is a trust signal, not just a policy. It tells buyers you believe in what you sell.

4. Generic promises raise the fear. Specific outcomes lower it. Be specific about what your product does.

5. Every objection has a fear underneath it. Address the fear. The objection dissolves.
