# Twitter Batch 1684

## Tweet 1
Cash flow and profit are not the same thing.

You can be profitable on paper and unable to pay rent.

Learn the difference before the difference teaches you the hard way.

## Tweet 2
The first financial goal for a solopreneur is not investment. It's a buffer.

3 months of operating expenses in a separate account.

That money buys you clarity, options, and the ability to say no to bad clients.

## Tweet 3
What does your business cost to run each month if you made zero sales?

If you don't know that number by heart, you don't have a business. You have a hobby with invoices.

## Tweet 4
Solopreneur financial habits worth building:

- Invoice immediately, not when you remember
- Track receivables weekly
- Separate business and personal accounts
- Know your monthly fixed costs cold

Not complicated. Just uncommon.

## Tweet 5
The worst business decisions come from financial desperation.

You take the bad client. You underprice. You can't say no.

A cash buffer removes desperation from your decision-making. It's not lazy money. It's clear-thinking money.
