You do not rise to the occasion. You fall to your preparation. The crisis reveals what you built in the calm.

---

The habit of clear thinking is built during low-stakes moments. Journaling. Solving problems you did not have to solve. Revisiting past decisions honestly.

---

Deliberate thinking is a skill. Practice it on small decisions. The more you use it on trivial questions, the more accessible it is on important ones.

---

Think carefully about what you eat. Think carefully about your first hour. Small stakes, high frequency. That is the formula.

---

Waiting for a crisis to start thinking clearly is like training for a marathon on race day. Build the infrastructure before you need it.
