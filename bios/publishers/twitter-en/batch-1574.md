# Twitter Batch 1574

## Tweet 1
People don't buy products. They buy versions of themselves.

The course is just the vehicle. What they're really buying is the feeling of becoming the person who figured this out.

Sell the transformation, not the format.

## Tweet 2
Most purchases are motivated by fear of loss before they're motivated by desire for gain.

Fear of staying stuck. Fear of being left behind. Fear of wasting more time on the wrong approach.

Speak to that fear with empathy. Not manipulation.

## Tweet 3
Vague promises don't convert.

"Change your life" means nothing. "Go from no clients to booked out in 60 days" means something.

Specificity creates believability. The more precise you are, the more trusted you become.

## Tweet 4
Thread: why your sales copy doesn't convert

1/ You're describing features instead of outcomes
2/ You haven't mirrored your buyer's current situation back to them
3/ Your promise is too vague to be believed
4/ You're writing for everyone instead of one specific person

Fix one of these and watch conversion improve.

## Tweet 5
What words does your ideal customer use to describe their problem?

Not the words you use. Their words.

The sales copy that converts best sounds like it was written by the buyer, not the seller.
