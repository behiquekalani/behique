Building something worth owning means accepting that the process of building it is not always enjoyable. That's fine. Do it anyway.

---

The market is not the audience for your potential. It is the audience for your output. Stop pitching potential. Ship output.

---

Most people stop at the point where the compounding starts. They quit when it looks like nothing is happening, right before everything starts to accelerate.

---

You already know what you need to do. The issue is not information. The issue is that doing it requires something you're not sure you're ready to give.

---

The builder who ships bad work honestly is more respected in the long run than the one who ships polished work inconsistently. Consistency signals commitment. Commitment builds trust.
