1. You do not have an information problem. You have an execution problem. Every book you finish without applying anything is just expensive entertainment.

2. The first version is not supposed to be good. It is supposed to exist. You cannot edit a blank page and you cannot improve something you never shipped.

3. Most "productivity" is just organized avoidance. The real work is the uncomfortable thing you keep putting at the bottom of the list.

4. Reading about how to build something is not the same as building it. At some point the research becomes the procrastination.

5. People do not remember your intentions. They remember what you shipped. Start something, finish it, ship it. Repeat until the body of work speaks for itself.
