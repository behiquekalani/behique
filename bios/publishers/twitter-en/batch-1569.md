# Twitter Batch 1569

## Tweet 1
The creators chasing money the hardest are usually making the least.

Money follows value. It doesn't lead it.

Start by asking what you know that would change someone's situation. The revenue conversation comes after.

## Tweet 2
Give away so much that people feel weird not paying you.

When you finally offer something, the sale is easy. The trust is already built.

This is not a trick. It's the actual mechanic.

## Tweet 3
Most creators undercharge because they haven't articulated the outcome clearly.

Price is not about the product. It's about the transformation the product delivers.

Define the transformation first. Then set the price.

## Tweet 4
What's the before and after for your most important offer?

Before: [specific situation your buyer is in]
After: [specific situation they're in after]

If you can't write that in two sentences, the offer isn't clear enough yet.

## Tweet 5
You can charge for saved time. You can charge for organized information. You can charge for accountability. You can charge for results.

"I don't know what to charge for" usually means "I haven't identified what outcome I'm delivering."

Identify the outcome. The price follows.
