1. Comparing yourself to someone else tells you nothing useful. Comparing yourself to six months ago tells you everything.

2. The only benchmark fully within your control is your own trajectory. Are you moving faster or slower than last quarter?

3. Someone else's current position has nothing to do with your next step. Stop using it as a measuring stick.

4. Keep a log. What did you know a year ago versus now. The gap is visible and the gap is motivating.

5. A person improving ten percent per quarter on a long timeline will pass almost anyone who started ahead of them.
