1. Every creator you admire spent years copying before they created anything original. They just do not talk about it.

2. Originality is not the starting point. It is what emerges after you have absorbed enough inputs that your synthesis becomes unrepeatable.

3. Copying is not cheating. It is the fastest path to understanding structure, so that when you depart from it, you know exactly what you are departing from.

4. Study what a good hook actually does. Figure out why that paragraph earns its place. Dissect the work you admire before trying to produce something similar.

5. You cannot develop a voice without spending serious time inside voices you respect. That is not imitation as a goal. It is imitation as a method.
