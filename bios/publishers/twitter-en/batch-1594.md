# Twitter Batch 1594

## Tweet 1
A business plan you cannot recite from memory is not guiding your decisions.

One page. Five questions. That is the whole plan.

## Tweet 2
The five questions that fit on one page:

Who do you help? What problem do you solve? What do you sell? How do people find you? How does money move from them to you?

If you cannot answer all five clearly, you do not have a business yet.

## Tweet 3
Complexity kills execution.

A fifty-page business plan sits in a folder. A one-page plan sits in your head and guides every decision.

## Tweet 4
Your one-page plan is not a contract. It is a working document.

Revisit it every quarter. Cross things out. Rewrite what changed.

A plan that never changes is a sign of avoidance.

## Tweet 5
Most solopreneurs skip the planning step entirely and wonder why their days feel scattered.

The plan is not about predicting the future. It is about making your decisions faster and more consistent.
