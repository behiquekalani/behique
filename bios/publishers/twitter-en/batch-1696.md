# Twitter Batch 1696

## Tweet 1
Build it, then see if people want it?

That's backwards.

See if people want it, then build it.

Save yourself months of wasted effort.

## Tweet 2
A real validation test is not asking your friends if they like the idea.

It's asking a stranger to pay before the product exists.

If they will, build it. If they won't, adjust.

## Tweet 3
The instinct to build immediately is often avoidance disguised as progress.

Testing requires accepting that the idea might not work.

Building lets you blame execution. Testing forces honesty.

## Tweet 4
How to validate your next idea in one week:

Write a one-paragraph description of what it does.
Share it with 5 potential buyers.
Ask if they'd pay.
If 3 say yes, ask for a deposit.

That's your green light.

## Tweet 5
The minimum viable version of your product is:

Enough to deliver the promised outcome for one person.

Not a full course. Not a polished guide. Just enough to prove the value exists.

Start there.
