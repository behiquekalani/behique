1. Mastery is not a prerequisite. It is a result. You get there by shipping, not by preparing to ship.

2. The audience you are trying to find is waiting for the version of you that is specific enough to be worth following. Generic content attracts nobody worth keeping.

3. Most criticism comes from people who are not doing what you are doing. Weight the feedback accordingly.

4. Systems thinking is just asking "and then what?" until the second and third order effects become visible. Most problems live there.

5. The goal is not passive income. The goal is building something that works when you are not working on it. The first is a lifestyle fantasy. The second is an architecture problem.
