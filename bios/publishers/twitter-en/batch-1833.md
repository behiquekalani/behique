1. Find one person with a specific problem and solve it so completely they would pay almost anything. Everything else follows from getting that right.

2. Mild usefulness does not generate word of mouth. Deep, specific relief does. You want people telling others, not mildly satisfied and silent.

3. Before you build anything: who is the exact person this is for, what is the exact problem it solves, and does it solve it completely or partially? Partial is worth less.

4. The narrower the problem and the more complete your solution, the more you can charge and the less competition you will face. Start narrow.

5. Most people build the product first and then search for someone who wants it. The reliable path runs in the opposite direction.
