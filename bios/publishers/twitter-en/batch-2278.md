# Twitter Batch 2278

## Tweet 1
A product is a thing you made.

An offer is what makes someone say yes.

Most creators spend 95% on the product and five minutes on the offer. The ratio should be closer to even.

## Tweet 2
A great offer answers four questions before the buyer has to ask:

What is it?
What does it do for me specifically?
Why should I trust that it works?
What happens if it doesn't?

## Tweet 3
"A 30-page PDF about productivity" is a product.

"The system I used to go from scattered and overwhelmed to shipping three products in 90 days" is an offer.

Same document. Different conversion rates.

## Tweet 4
A strong guarantee removes risk from the buyer and transfers it to you.

That transfer signals confidence.

It tells the buyer you believe in what you're selling enough to absorb the downside.

## Tweet 5
Rewrite your best product's offer from scratch.

Include: what it is, who it's for, what result they get, and a simple guarantee.

Compare it to your current page. You'll see the gap immediately.
