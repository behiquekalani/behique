1. Clarity is not the prerequisite to action. It is the output of action. You do not think your way to knowing what to build. You build your way there.

2. Waiting for complete information before starting is a strategy for never starting. Partial information is enough for the next step. Take it.

3. The person who acts with 60% certainty learns faster than the person who waits for 100%. The remaining 40% arrives after you move, not before.

4. Act your way into clarity. Write something and see what you actually think. Ship something and see what the market actually wants. Iterate from there.

5. You already know enough to begin. The rest will come. It always does, but only after you stopped waiting for it and started moving.
