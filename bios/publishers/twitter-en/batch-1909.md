1. The constraint you're trying to remove might be the thing keeping your work interesting. Unlimited resources produce bloated products. Work with what you have.

2. Constraints eliminate option paralysis. When you can't afford a large team, you build something one person can own. That focus becomes a feature.

3. Decide what you're not building. The no's are the structure that makes your yes's worth something. Scope creep is a values problem disguised as a planning problem.

4. Systems don't require willpower. Design a morning that runs on rails and your tired afternoon self doesn't have to make decisions your rested self already answered.

5. Pick one chaotic area of your work. Define the trigger, the steps, and what done looks like. Run it until it's automatic. That's the whole system design process.
