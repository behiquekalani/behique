1. The version of your product that exists in your head is always better than the one you'll build. The gap between them is where the learning happens. Stop living in the gap.

2. Ship with the note that says "more coming." Week one feedback will reshape what "more" even means. Build after you know, not before.

3. Motivation follows action. It does not precede it. Start for thirty seconds. That's the entire strategy.

4. Work that stretches you compounds. Work that's comfortable maintains. Both have a place. The ratio is what most people get wrong.

5. Boredom is the mind signaling that its capacity exceeds its current stimulus. Sit in it. Don't eliminate it. That's where original thinking comes from.
