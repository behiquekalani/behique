1. Low prices attract people who are not sure they have a problem. High prices attract people who are done waiting to solve it.

2. Your price is a headline. It tells buyers what category of solution you are before they read a single word of your copy.

3. Undercharging is not modesty. It is a statement that you do not fully believe in what you built.

4. The customer who haggles on price will argue about every deliverable. The customer who pays without negotiating wants results.

5. Price your work for the outcome it creates, not the hours it took you to create it.
