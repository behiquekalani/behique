# Twitter Batch 1575

## Tweet 1
Cheap prices attract cheap clients.

Not because those clients are bad people. Because price signals what kind of relationship they're entering.

Your price tells a story before the sale even starts.

## Tweet 2
Lower prices attract people who are price-sensitive. They negotiate, refund at higher rates, and require more support per dollar.

Higher prices attract outcome-focused people. They invest, implement, and get results.

Better results generate better testimonials. The cycle compounds.

## Tweet 3
The real reason most creators underprice: fear.

Fear of rejection. Fear of looking arrogant. Fear of asking for more than they think they're worth.

That fear has a cost. It keeps you busy with the wrong clients.

## Tweet 4
Price to the outcome, not the effort.

If your offer saves someone $50K in mistakes, $5K is a fair price.

Hours of work are irrelevant. The transformation is the metric.

## Tweet 5
Raise your price by 20% this week and see what happens.

Most people find conversion stays the same or improves.

The fear of charging more is almost always bigger than the actual consequence.
