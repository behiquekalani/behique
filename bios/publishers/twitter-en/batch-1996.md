1. Most anxiety about the future dissolves when you shift from "what might happen" to "what will I do when it does." One is rumination. The other is preparation.

2. The best marketing is a product so specific to a real problem that the person with that problem tells everyone they know about it.

3. You cannot optimize your way to meaning. Meaning comes from deciding what matters and doing it repeatedly, not from running a more efficient schedule.

4. The hardest part of building anything is the middle. The beginning has excitement. The end has completion. The middle is just work. Show up for the middle.

5. When you make something honest and specific, the people it is for will find it. The people it is not for will not care. Both outcomes are correct.
