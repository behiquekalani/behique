1. Finishing builds the belief that finishing is possible for you specifically. That belief is worth more than any individual project.

2. Unfinished projects occupy mental space indefinitely. That background load is real and it compounds across everything you attempt.

3. A launched product at 80% of what you imagined is infinitely more valuable than a perfect product in permanent draft.

4. You cannot iterate on something that isn't out. You cannot learn from something that isn't tested. Ship it.

5. Make finishing the priority over quality when quality is what's blocking completion. Refinement is a second phase. First phase has to close.
