The thing you have been meaning to do for months is almost always the highest-leverage thing you need to do. The avoidance is proportional to the importance.

The things that matter most carry the most psychological weight. Real stakes. Real visibility. Real judgment. That is why they get avoided.

You stay busy with lower-stakes tasks that deliver the comfort of motion without the risk of exposure. Name this pattern when you see it in yourself.

"I am avoiding this because it matters and I am afraid of what it will show me." That naming alone removes the unconscious quality from the avoidance.

Once you name the avoidance clearly, doing the thing becomes easier than continuing to avoid it. Most of the time.
