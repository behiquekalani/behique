1. Return on invested time is more important than return on invested capital. Capital can be replenished. Hours cannot.

2. Most daily activities are caloric. They burn time without building anything that persists when you stop.

3. Writing that builds an audience. Skills that raise earning capacity. Products that generate recurring revenue. These compound. Track time spent on each.

4. You already have enough hours. It is an allocation problem, not a scarcity problem.

5. The same hour spent building something that compounds becomes more valuable over time. The same hour spent consuming does not.
