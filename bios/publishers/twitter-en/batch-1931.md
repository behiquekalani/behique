1. Approval is feedback, not direction. It tells you what lands. It does not tell you what is worth building.

2. When you optimize for what gets likes, you stop writing what is true and start writing what is comfortable.

3. The work that lasts is almost always ahead of the audience's current appetite. Build for what should exist, not what already performs.

4. You need a private quality standard that exists before external validation. That is how you know what good looks like.

5. Trust your own read. The crowd is usually right about what it likes and wrong about what it needs.
