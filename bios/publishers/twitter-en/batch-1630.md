# Twitter Batch 1630

## Tweet 1
Working alone is not the same as working in isolation.

Working alone means no coordination overhead and decisions in seconds.

Working in isolation means no one challenges your thinking and blind spots accumulate.

You want the first. Actively avoid the second.

## Tweet 2
Solo operators can pivot completely in a day.

A team of five needs a week of discussions to reach the same conclusion.

Coordination costs are invisible until you've built a business without them.

## Tweet 3
The best structure for most solopreneurs isn't a team.

It's one high-quality collaborator with complementary skills.

Two people with clear domains out-executes five with overlapping roles almost every time.

## Tweet 4
Hiring should solve a skill gap, not an energy problem.

If you're hiring because you're tired, you have a capacity issue.

If you're hiring because you genuinely cannot do the thing, you have a skill gap.

These require different responses.

## Tweet 5
Lone genius or team synergy?

Neither. The real answer is the smallest possible structure that covers your actual needs.

Complexity scales with headcount. Keep the model simple until it genuinely can't stay that way.
