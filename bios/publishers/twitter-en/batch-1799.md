1. The market does not pay for effort. It pays for outcomes someone else values enough to exchange money for.

2. Working hard on something nobody wants is not noble. It is a misdirection problem dressed as a work ethic problem.

3. Stop asking how hard you are working. Start asking who is better off because of what you made.

4. The employment mindset says show up and get paid. The market says deliver value and get paid. These are different contracts.

5. If effort is not converting to results, the problem is usually direction, not amount. More effort in the wrong direction is not the fix.
