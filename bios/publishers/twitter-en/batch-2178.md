# Twitter Batch 2178

## Tweet 1
Hard work in the wrong direction doesn't move you forward.

It moves you deeper into the wrong direction.

Direction matters more than effort.

## Tweet 2
Two types of work:

Maintenance: keeps the lights on.
Compounding: builds assets.

Most people's days are 80% maintenance. That's the problem.

## Tweet 3
Maintenance expands to fill available time if you don't protect compounding work.

You start with a "quick task" and surface three hours later.

Nothing compounding happened.

## Tweet 4
Compounding work goes first.

Before the inbox opens. Before anything asks something of you.

Protect the first hours. Run maintenance second.

## Tweet 5
Track last week's work. Sort every task: maintenance or compounding.

If compounding is under 50%, that's the number to fix.

Not your strategy. Not your niche. That ratio.
