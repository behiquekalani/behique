1. Owning something changes how you think about its problems. They become yours to solve, not someone else's to manage.

2. The standard you hold yourself to when you own something is higher than any manager will ever set for you.

3. Start owning something this year. It does not need to be large. It needs to be yours.

4. You cannot learn certain things from a course. Ownership teaches through consequences in a way that observation never can.

5. A life you control is not the result of freedom from obligations. It is the result of owning the right ones.
