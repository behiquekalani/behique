1. Curiosity is not a personality trait. It's a practice that strengthens or atrophies depending on whether you exercise it.

2. Most people at your skill level are drawing from the same pool of ideas. The person who imports a framework from a different field produces ideas nobody in the room has seen.

3. Staying in one domain forever makes you efficient. Reading across domains makes you original. Both matter. Neither is sufficient alone.

4. The cross-pollination of fields produces original thinking that can't be generated from within a single domain, no matter how deep you go.

5. Read about fields you don't work in. Ask people in different industries how they solve problems you recognize. That's where the unexpected angles come from.
