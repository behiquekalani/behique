# Twitter Batch 2260

## Tweet 1
Two kinds of work:

Compounding work. Produces returns long after effort ends.

Evaporating work. Must be repeated constantly to maintain effect.

The ratio you maintain determines your ceiling.

## Tweet 2
A replied Slack message is evaporating work.

A published article driving traffic for three years is compounding work.

One fades. One grows.

Choose what to build your week around carefully.

## Tweet 3
Before every task, ask:

"Will this matter in a year?"

If yes, do it properly.
If no, do it faster, delegate it, or delete it.

Most of what feels urgent fails that test.

## Tweet 4
Digital products are pure compounding work.

A PDF built in one day can sell for years.

Content posted today keeps attracting people after you've moved on.

Build things that outlast the moment.

## Tweet 5
Look at last week.

List every task. Categorize as compounding or evaporating.

Calculate the ratio.

If it's less than 30% compounding, you know what to change.
