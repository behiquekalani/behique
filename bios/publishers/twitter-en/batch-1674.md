# Twitter Batch 1674

## Tweet 1
The builders who share the process attract an audience before the product is ready. Build in public and your launch starts the day you begin, not the day you ship.

## Tweet 2
Building in public is not showing off. It is documenting the real process, including the setbacks and the decisions you are not sure about. That honesty is what builds a loyal audience.

## Tweet 3
When you commit to building something publicly, your audience becomes a soft accountability system. The pressure is real and useful. Most people avoid it and wonder why they procrastinate.

## Tweet 4
Every decision you make during the build is a piece of content that costs you nothing extra to produce. Share the reasoning, not just the result.

## Tweet 5
What are you currently building that you have not shared publicly yet? The update you are sitting on is probably more interesting to your audience than you think it is.
