# Twitter Batch 2307

## Tweet 1
Motivation is a feeling. Identity is a belief.

Feelings come and go. Beliefs shape behavior every day.

Stop relying on motivation. Update your identity.

## Tweet 2
"I'm trying to build a business" and "I am building a business" produce different behaviors.

One is tentative. The other is committed.

The language you use about yourself is not just words. It is the architecture of your actions.

## Tweet 3
You cannot wait for results to change your identity.

The results come from the behavior. The behavior comes from who you believe you are.

Update the belief first. The results follow.

## Tweet 4
Identity-based action:

Not "should I post today?" but "I am someone who publishes consistently, so I post today."

The question disappears when the identity is set. That's the whole trick.

## Tweet 5
Write three identity statements right now.

"I am a person who ___."
"I am a person who ___."
"I am a person who ___."

Make them present tense. Make them specific to the builder you are becoming, not the one you already are.

Read them every morning for two weeks. Watch what shifts.
