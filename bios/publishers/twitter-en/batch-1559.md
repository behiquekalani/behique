# Twitter Batch 1559

## Tweet 1
Deep work is not working a lot.

It's working on hard things without switching.

Two hours of that will outproduce six hours of the other kind every single time.

## Tweet 2
Thread: Why deep work keeps failing for most people.

1/ The phone is in the same room.
2/ The work is not defined before the session starts.
3/ They decide what to work on during the session instead of the night before.
4/ The block is not protected on the calendar.

Fix the environment. The focus follows.

## Tweet 3
Five hundred deep work sessions a year.

Two hours each.

That's a book, a course, a product suite, a body of writing.

All done during hours that most people spend in their inbox.

## Tweet 4
Deep work is an environmental design problem, not a willpower problem.

Remove the distractions before you sit down.

Do not rely on discipline to resist what you left in the room.

## Tweet 5
The most valuable thing about a deep work session is not what you produce.

It's the pattern recognition that builds during sustained focus.

That pattern recognition is what separates experts from people who just have experience.
