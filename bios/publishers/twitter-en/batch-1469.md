# Twitter Batch 1469

## Tweet 1
Before deciding what to build, decide what life you want.

Then build the business that fits it.

Most people do this backwards and end up successful and miserable.

## Tweet 2
One high-ticket client at $3K/mo vs 100 customers at $30 each.

Same revenue. Completely different ceilings.

The product model is not just more scalable. It is more free.

## Tweet 3
The business model you choose shapes the life you live.

Clients = calendar full of calls. Products = calendar you control.

Neither is wrong. Be intentional about which one you pick.

## Tweet 4
What does your business require from you that you wish it didn't?

That friction point is worth examining. It might be removable.

## Tweet 5
Hot take: copying someone else's business model without copying their context is a guaranteed path to confusion.

Their audience, reputation, and infrastructure made the model work.

Understand the inputs before you copy the design.
