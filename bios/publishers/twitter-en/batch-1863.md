1. Your niche is not a topic. It is your specific perspective on a topic. Nobody can copy your exact combination of experience, failure, and hard-won understanding.

2. Generic content for everyone reaches no one. The more specific you get about who you are writing for, the more that person feels like you wrote it just for them.

3. The creators people actually follow are not the most informed. They are the most honest. Specificity and honesty together are nearly impossible to replicate.

4. Stop trying to appeal to a wider audience. Go deeper with a narrower one. Depth of connection beats breadth of reach every time.

5. The version of you that is most interesting online is the version closest to who you actually are. Performing a persona is exhausting and eventually collapses.
