# Twitter Batch 2259

## Tweet 1
The best product idea you have is probably the one you wish had existed when you were stuck.

You don't need market research for that.

You lived the market.

## Tweet 2
When you've lived the problem, you know:
- The confusion
- The failed attempts
- The moment it finally clicked

That emotional depth is what makes great products.

## Tweet 3
"My problem is too specific."

Usually false.

If you encountered the problem, others did too.
If you searched for a solution and found nothing satisfying, that's evidence of a gap.

## Tweet 4
You don't need external validation for your own experience.

Document how you solved it.
Write it clearly enough that someone earlier in the journey could follow it.
Package it.

That's a product.

## Tweet 5
Three hardest problems you solved in the last two years.

Write them down.

One sentence each on how you solved it.

The most transferable one is your next product.
