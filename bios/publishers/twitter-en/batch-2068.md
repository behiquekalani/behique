1. The task you've been avoiding for two weeks isn't hard. It's important. Those are different problems with different solutions.

2. You don't avoid hard things. You avoid things where failure is visible. Recognize the actual fear and it loses half its power.

3. Do the avoided task first. Not after you warm up. Not second. First. Before anything that feels natural or productive.

4. The avoided task doesn't disappear. It waits, quietly costing you momentum every day you route around it.

5. Starting the dreaded task almost always reveals it was manageable. The imagination inflates difficulty. Reality corrects it.
