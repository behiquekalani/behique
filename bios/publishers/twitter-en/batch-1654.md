# Twitter Batch 1654

## Tweet 1
The most dangerous place to be in any market is in a category with others.

Competition destroys margins.

Comparison destroys differentiation.

## Tweet 2
Categories are invented, not found.

When you can describe what you do in a way that makes people say "I've never thought about it that way," you're building a category.

## Tweet 3
A category of one is defensible when it's rooted in your actual identity.

Not a persona. Not a positioning exercise.

Your history. Your failures. Your specific way of seeing.

## Tweet 4
Skills can be replicated.

Experiences can be approximated.

But the specific synthesis of who you are and how you think cannot be faked sustainably.

## Tweet 5
Write a one-paragraph description of what you do that doesn't use any existing industry terms.

No jargon. No borrowed language.

Just your own words.

That's your category.
