Distraction is not just lost time. It is lost depth. Every interruption resets your cognitive state to shallow.

---

A two-minute distraction costs 25 minutes of depth. Three interruptions in a morning and you have done no real thinking at all.

---

Deep work vs shallow work compounded over 30 years produces outputs so different they are almost incomparable.

---

Platforms are designed to interrupt. Open offices are designed to interrupt. Protecting your focus is not antisocial. It is strategic.

---

The person who builds a reputation for responsiveness and the person who builds a reputation for great work are not on the same path.
