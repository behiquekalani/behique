1. Reading is not learning. You have not learned something until you can use it, explain it, or build something with it.

2. Consumption at high volume with no application is just comfortable procrastination. You feel like you are preparing. You are actually avoiding.

3. After reading anything worth reading, write one paragraph about what it means for your actual work. Not a summary. An application. That is the difference.

4. The real advantage is not knowing more things. It is being one of the few people who does something with what they know.

5. Your reading list is not an asset. Your applied insights are. Stop collecting, start converting.
