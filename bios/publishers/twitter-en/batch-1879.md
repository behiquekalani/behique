1. Busy and building feel identical in the moment. They look completely different six months later. One is a treadmill. The other is a staircase.

2. At the end of each week ask what exists that did not exist at the start. If the answer is nothing, you were busy. Something, and you were building.

3. Busyness is protection. It keeps you occupied enough to feel useful without exposing you to the risk of building something that might fail.

4. Most shallow work expands to fill the time you give it. Compress it. Protect the time where you build things that last longer than the day you made them.

5. You can stay busy your entire career and end up in the same place you started. Building is the only version of work that does not reset every Monday.
