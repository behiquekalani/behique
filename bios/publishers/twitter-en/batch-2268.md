# Twitter Batch 2268

## Tweet 1
The loudest person in the room is rarely the most productive one.

Ship first. Share second.

Build a reputation on what's real.

## Tweet 2
Talking about a goal before achieving it gives your brain a small version of the reward for achieving it.

Research backs this.

Pre-announcing goals makes you less likely to follow through. Stop pre-announcing.

## Tweet 3
The quiet operator doesn't post about what they're going to do.

They post about what they already did.

Different energy. Different results.

## Tweet 4
Attention is a tool, not a goal.

You use attention to grow distribution.

You don't use attention as a substitute for real progress.

## Tweet 5
This week: complete one project before telling anyone about it.

Notice how different the energy feels when you share something finished.

That difference is worth protecting.
