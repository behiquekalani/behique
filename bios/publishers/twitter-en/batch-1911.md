1. You don't control your reputation. You control the inputs. The outputs belong to everyone who knows you. Act accordingly in every interaction.

2. Do the thing no one asked you to do. Solve the problem they didn't mention. That's the action that creates the kind of trust no ad spend can replicate.

3. Every interaction is a data point in someone's mental model of you. The model updates constantly. You're always either building or eroding it.

4. The most dangerous belief in business is "they'll figure it out." Clarity is your job. Confusion is your fault. Make everything obvious.

5. Follow up after the work is done. It costs ten minutes. It communicates that you think in terms of outcomes, not deliverables. That's rare enough to be memorable.
