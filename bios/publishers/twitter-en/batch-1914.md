1. Lower prices don't reduce objections. They attract people who weren't committed in the first place. The objections just change form.

2. A cheap product attracts cheap behavior. Low investment, low implementation, worse outcome. Your transformation rate tanks. Then the reviews reflect that.

3. Raise the price before you feel ready. Lower it only when you have evidence that price is the actual objection, not a proxy for doubt.

4. The specialist is findable. The generalist is invisible. You can always become more general later. You can't become known for nothing.

5. Breadth feels safe. Specificity feels risky. Specificity wins because it's the only thing that makes a stranger feel like you're talking directly to them.
