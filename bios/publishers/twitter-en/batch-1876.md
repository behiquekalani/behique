1. Hot takes are reflexes dressed as opinions. Wait 48 hours. Let the first wave clear. Then say the thing that most people missed.

2. The slow take wins on quality even when it loses on timing. One sharp insight published late is worth more than ten fast reactions that say nothing new.

3. Most online discourse is just people performing reactions to other people's reactions. Getting out of that loop entirely is a competitive advantage.

4. The contrarian is not always right. The reflexive taker is almost always shallow. Develop the habit of pausing before forming a position.

5. Thinking in public means showing your actual reasoning, not just your conclusions. The people who do that build trust faster than the people who just assert things.
