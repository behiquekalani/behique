# Twitter Batch 1648

## Tweet 1
Consuming information feels like progress.

It isn't.

Information without application is just stimulation. It keeps your brain busy without moving anything forward.

## Tweet 2
The person who reads less and ships more will, over time, outpace the person who consumes voraciously but produces rarely.

The gap between knowing and doing is where most potential dies.

## Tweet 3
Every piece of work you put into the world gives you feedback you can't get from consuming.

Output is the laboratory.

Consumption is just reading someone else's lab notes.

## Tweet 4
A healthy output-to-input ratio for builders:

70% output, 30% input.

Most people have it exactly backwards.

## Tweet 5
Track your time for three days.

Split it into input (reading, watching, listening) and output (writing, building, creating).

What does your ratio look like?
