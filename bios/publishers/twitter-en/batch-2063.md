1. A one-person business is not a startup waiting to happen. It's an entirely different game with different physics and different wins.

2. Small means fast. Fast means you find out before your competition does. Stop apologizing for being lean.

3. The goal isn't to look like the companies you admire. The goal is to build systems so refined that one person can do what used to require a team.

4. You don't need employees to be legitimate. You need revenue, product, and a reputation. Two of those you control entirely.

5. Overhead is not a sign of success. It's a sign that your margins need to support more than your work. Keep it lean until it earns complexity.
