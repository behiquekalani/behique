Failure is a data point, not a verdict. The story you tell about it determines whether you learn or hide.

---

Every failed launch contains information. The offer was wrong, the audience was wrong, the timing was wrong. These are all solvable.

---

If failure means you are not capable, you will stop trying. If failure means the approach did not work, you will adjust the approach. Choose the second story.

---

The builders who compound over time are curious about failure, not just resilient to it. They debrief, extract, adjust, go again.

---

The failure is not the end of the story. It is a chapter that tells you how to write the next one better.
