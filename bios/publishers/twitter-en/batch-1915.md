1. You cannot get feedback from a product that doesn't exist yet. Every week spent building in isolation is a week of delayed learning.

2. The person who ships and iterates seven times in a year beats the person who perfects for twelve months every time. Not because speed beats quality. Because iteration beats assumption.

3. Most of what you think is critical won't be. Most of what your users care about, you hadn't thought of. Ship to find the actual priorities.

4. Set a ship date. Treat it like a hard deadline. Something will be unfinished. Let it be unfinished. The feedback is worth more than the missing feature.

5. Done creates feedback. Feedback is the only teacher that actually changes you. Polish creates satisfaction. Satisfaction doesn't compound.
