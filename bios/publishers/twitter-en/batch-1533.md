# Twitter Batch 1533

## Tweet 1
The first price a buyer sees becomes the anchor.

Show your highest-tier first. Let the comparison do the work.

## Tweet 2
$97 vs $100: the difference isn't $3.

$97 signals "I priced this carefully." $100 signals "I rounded."

For most digital products, that signal matters.

## Tweet 3
If you raised your price and sales went up, you were underpriced and sending the wrong signal.

If sales stayed the same, you just gave yourself a raise.

Either outcome is a win.

## Tweet 4
Price is a story.

It tells the market what kind of person you are, what kind of result they should expect, and who this is for. Get the story right before you worry about the number.

## Tweet 5
Test your price before you commit to it.

Run the same offer at two price points. The market will tell you what you need to know faster than any competitor analysis will.
