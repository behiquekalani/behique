1. The people around you set the ceiling on your thinking without you noticing. That's not philosophy. That's how reference classes work.

2. Proximity is one of the strongest inputs to your operating assumptions. Be intentional about whose orbit you're in.

3. If your reference class is people ten years ahead of where you want to be, your baseline expectation for yourself adjusts upward. That changes your daily decisions.

4. Comfort is expensive when it keeps you thinking small. Name that cost before deciding it's worth it.

5. You don't need to be strategic about friendship in a cold way. You need to be honest about whose company you're keeping and what it's costing you.
