# Twitter Batch 2253

## Tweet 1
Low prices don't attract more buyers.

They attract worse ones.

Price is communication. A $7 guide says "this might help a little."

A $97 guide says "this solves a real problem."

## Tweet 2
Price based on the value of the outcome. Not the hours you spent building it.

If your product helps someone land a $5,000 client, charging $50 is giving away value.

## Tweet 3
High prices attract better buyers.

They implement. They get results. They leave better testimonials. They're less likely to refund.

Better buyers = better business.

## Tweet 4
The fear of high prices is really the fear of being told no.

Being told no by a stranger on the internet is not a problem.

It's information.

## Tweet 5
Find out what the problem costs your buyer in money, time, or stress.

Price your solution at 10-20% of that.

A solution to a $500 problem is worth $50-100.

That's the formula.
