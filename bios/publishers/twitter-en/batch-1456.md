# Twitter Batch 1456

## Tweet 1
You don't need an audience to build a product.

You need a problem you understand better than most people. Start there.

## Tweet 2
Audience-first requires months of output before any revenue feedback.

Product-first gives you market feedback in days. One approach is slower and more uncertain than most people admit.

## Tweet 3
The first sale from a stranger is the real validation.

Not a friend. Not a follower. Someone who found you through a problem and decided your solution was worth paying for.

## Tweet 4
How to find early customers without an audience:

Post in the communities where your target person already spends time.
Offer to solve their specific problem.
Sell it before it's perfect.

## Tweet 5
After you have sales, you have a story. The story becomes content. The content builds the audience that accelerates future sales.

The sequence starts with the problem. Not the platform.
