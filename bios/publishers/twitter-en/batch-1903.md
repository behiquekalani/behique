1. Identity before achievement. You can't perform your way into a belief about yourself. Decide who you are first. The performance follows.

2. The person who believes they're a builder doesn't negotiate whether to build today. The belief removes the debate. That's the advantage.

3. Three hours of full attention produces more than eight hours of distracted work. The variable isn't time. It's where your mind actually is.

4. One interruption doesn't cost one minute. It costs the twenty minutes it takes to rebuild the concentration you had before the ping.

5. Block the deep hours before anyone can fill them. Protect them like they're the only hours that matter. Because for output purposes, they are.
