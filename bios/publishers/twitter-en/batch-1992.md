1. The shortcut most people ignore: doing fewer things much better. The instinct is always to add. The result is usually dilution.

2. You will not figure out your purpose by thinking about it. You will figure it out by making things and noticing what feels true while you are making them.

3. Discomfort is information. Your first instinct is usually to eliminate it. Better instinct: read it first, then decide what to do.

4. Good enough and shipped beats perfect and in your head. The market can only respond to what exists. Give it something to respond to.

5. The real moat is not your product. It is your point of view. Products can be copied. Perspective cannot. Build the perspective first.
