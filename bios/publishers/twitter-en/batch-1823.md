1. Generalists are free and abundant. People who go uncomfortably deep in one domain are scarce. Scarcity is where value lives.

2. The creator who writes for "ambitious people" competes with everyone. The creator who writes for a specific person in a specific situation competes with almost no one.

3. Surface-level content has a short shelf life. Depth compounds. The work you do to earn a genuine opinion pays dividends for years.

4. Breadth that has no foundation underneath it is just noise. Go deep first. The breadth becomes meaningful once there is something beneath it.

5. Most people perform interest rather than develop it. Performed interest is visible quickly. Developed interest is what actually builds something.
