# Twitter Batch 1621

## Tweet 1
Short-form writing is not about fewer words.

It's about zero wasted words.

The difference is everything.

## Tweet 2
Most hooks fail because they describe instead of commit.

"I want to talk about productivity" = description.

"You're not lazy. You're bored." = commitment.

One pulls. One doesn't.

## Tweet 3
What if the reason your content isn't landing has nothing to do with distribution?

What if the thinking just isn't clear yet?

## Tweet 4
The sentence you're most attached to is usually the one that needs to go.

Cut it. Save it somewhere. But cut it.

The reader never knew it existed. They only feel whether the piece moves.

## Tweet 5
Write ten opening lines before you choose one.

Most writers treat the hook as an afterthought.

The best treat it as the whole game.
