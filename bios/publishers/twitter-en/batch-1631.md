# Twitter Batch 1631

## Tweet 1
Unlimited options produce paralysis.

Constraints produce output.

This is not a creativity tip. It's a cognitive fact.

## Tweet 2
Twitter's character limit created a new genre of writing.

Six-word stories are a genre.

The constraint is not the limitation. The constraint is the creative pressure that makes something good possible.

## Tweet 3
Try this: write one 300-word piece every morning before you check anything else.

The constraint removes the decision about when and how to start.

The decision energy goes into the writing instead of the resistance.

## Tweet 4
The most stylistically free creators have deep technical foundations.

They know the rules so well they can break them on purpose.

You can only subvert a structure you understand.

## Tweet 5
Pick a tight format. Commit to it for 30 days.

Not because it's the best format. Because the creative problem-solving the constraint forces will teach you more than 30 days of unconstrained work.
