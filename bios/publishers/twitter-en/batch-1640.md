# Twitter Batch 1640

## Tweet 1
Discipline is finite. It depletes.

Obsession is self-replenishing.

The most productive people are not the most disciplined. They're the most obsessed.

## Tweet 2
The person obsessed with a problem for two years accumulates more real knowledge than someone who studies it casually for ten.

Depth of attention matters more than duration.

Every hour of obsessive focus produces more compound understanding than ten hours of obligatory engagement.

## Tweet 3
You cannot manufacture obsession.

But you can ask whether your current work is aligned with what you're actually obsessed about.

If the answer is no, the work will always feel hard. That's information.

## Tweet 4
Hot take: most productivity problems are not discipline problems.

They're alignment problems.

You're trying to force yourself to care about something you don't actually care about.

Fix the alignment and the productivity problem disappears.

## Tweet 5
Write down the last three things you stayed up too late thinking about without any external pressure to do so.

That list is a map of your real obsessions.

How much of your current work is in that territory?
