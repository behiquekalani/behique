1. A product that promises everything to everyone promises nothing to anyone.

2. The most specific product in a category is usually the best-selling one. Specificity earns trust before the sale.

3. Who is the one person this is for? If you cannot name them, the product is not finished.

4. "This is for anyone who wants to improve" is not a target audience. It is a wish.

5. Narrow the promise. The right buyer recognizes themselves and buys. The wrong buyer self-selects out. Both outcomes are good.
