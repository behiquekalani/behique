# Twitter Batch 2314

## Tweet 1
"I don't want to limit myself by niching down."

Generalists are hard to find. Specialists are sought out.

The niche is not a cage. It is a starting point with a clear address.

## Tweet 2
A generalist who covers everything for everyone is memorable to no one.

A specialist who solves one specific problem for one specific person is the first person that person calls.

Be the first call, not an option.

## Tweet 3
Specific first. Expand later.

Build authority in a narrow space. Then your audience follows you when you expand, because they trust the quality of your thinking.

Authority transfers. Vagueness doesn't.

## Tweet 4
The most specific version of your niche:

"I help [specific person] do [specific thing] so they can [specific outcome]."

If that sentence feels too narrow, you're probably on the right track.

## Tweet 5
Specificity makes you findable.

In an attention economy, being findable matters more than being broad.

The more precisely you name the person and their pain, the less competition you face and the more naturally the right buyer arrives.
