# Twitter Batch 1629

## Tweet 1
Finding your voice is not a branding exercise.

Voice is the set of convictions that shows up consistently across everything you make.

You can't study your way to it. You write your way to it.

## Tweet 2
You can copy someone's style perfectly and still produce hollow content.

Because style is surface. Conviction is what makes the writing matter.

Copied style with no conviction underneath it reads exactly like that.

## Tweet 3
The single most common voice killer is self-censorship.

You have the actual thought. Then you sand it down before publishing because you're worried about how it lands.

The sanded version is the one nobody shares.

## Tweet 4
Resonance is better than approval.

Approval is generic. It means you said something inoffensive.

Resonance means you said something true and the right people felt it.

## Tweet 5
How many posts have you published where you softened the real opinion before hitting publish?

That number is roughly the amount of distinctiveness you've filtered out of your brand.
