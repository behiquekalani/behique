1. The noisiest builders are rarely the most dangerous ones. The people who disappear for months and emerge with something real are running on a different engine.

2. Performing effort says "look how hard I am working." Sharing output says "here is what I built." One builds an audience. The other entertains one.

3. External validation is fuel that runs out. Build in focused silence, then emerge with the thing. The results become impossible to ignore.

4. The gap between promise and delivery is where most people's credibility lives or dies. Deliver the thing first. Talk about it second.

5. The most leveraged use of public attention is sharing things that are already complete and already valuable. Not the process in real time. The finished work.
