1. Specificity is the most underused tool in content. The more precise you are, the more clearly a specific person sees themselves in your work.

2. The difference between an idea and a product is a deadline. Ideas are free. Products cost you the discomfort of completion.

3. Most people want the result but not the process. The people who want the process get both. The people who only want the result usually get neither.

4. If you could only work three hours today, what would you do? Now ask why you are not doing that first on days when you have eight hours.

5. Every piece of content you publish is a permanent asset. Email subscribers come and go. Search results are rented. Owned content compounds.
