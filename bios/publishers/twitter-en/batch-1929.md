1. Keeping every option open is not strategy. It is a way of avoiding the discomfort of commitment.

2. You learn more in thirty days of execution than in six months of research. Start earlier than feels comfortable.

3. Every open loop has a maintenance cost. Twenty half-explored options is a full-time mental job that produces nothing.

4. Commit early. Gather information through doing. The market will tell you what you need to know after you start.

5. Flexibility is earned through commitment, not preserved by avoiding it. You cannot pivot something you never built.
