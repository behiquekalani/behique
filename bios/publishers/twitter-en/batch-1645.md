# Twitter Batch 1645

## Tweet 1
Every yes is a no to something else.

Most people track their yeses.

Almost nobody tracks the cost of them.

## Tweet 2
A no given clearly and without apology is more honest than a yes that drains you.

The people worth working with understand this.

The ones who don't also tell you something useful.

## Tweet 3
People with strong boundaries command more respect, not less.

Their yes means something because it's rare.

Scarcity applies to time and attention, not just products.

## Tweet 4
The filter for every new opportunity:

Does this pull me toward what I'm building, or does it pull me away from it?

That's the only question that matters.

## Tweet 5
What's one commitment you're currently holding that isn't serving your actual goals?

What would happen if you ended it cleanly this week?
