1. The most valuable people in your network are the ones you already know and rarely message. Cold outreach can't buy that trust.

2. Saying less, said well, lands harder than saying more. Most people keep adding words when they should stop.

3. The visible output you admire was preceded by invisible work you never saw. Stop comparing your process to their result.

4. The standard set by your context is not your standard. It's the average of the people around you. Choose your benchmark deliberately.

5. First versions don't need to be good. They need to exist. Reality gives better feedback than imagination.
