# Twitter Batch 2475

**Tweet 1:** You don't have 24 equivalent hours. An hour when you're sharp is worth three when you're depleted. Manage energy, not time.

**Tweet 2:** Do the hardest work in your best hours. Not email. Not social. The work that requires depth first, then everything else.

**Tweet 3:** The discipline of stopping is as important as the discipline of starting. Grinding past the point of diminishing returns makes the next session harder.

**Tweet 4:** The solopreneur who works four focused hours produces more than the one who works twelve scattered ones. Depth per hour, not hours per day.

**Tweet 5:** Identify the two-hour window when you're most mentally sharp. Block it tomorrow exclusively for your most important creative work. No exceptions.
