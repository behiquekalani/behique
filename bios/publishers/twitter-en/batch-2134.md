Time management is a lie. You do not manage time. You manage energy. The quality of the hour matters more than the count of hours.

High-energy output in three hours beats low-energy output in ten. Your calendar does not capture this. Your results do.

Past a threshold, more hours produce less. The exhausted version of you is not working. It is performing work while damaging tomorrow's capacity.

Sleep, movement, and mental boundaries are not lifestyle preferences. They are performance variables. Treat them that way.

The person who manages energy, not just time, consistently outperforms the one who grinds through depletion.
