# Twitter Batch 2492

**Tweet 1:** Repurposing burns people out because they adapt content to every platform manually. The better model: extract, don't adapt.

**Tweet 2:** One 1,500-word essay contains 5-8 distinct ideas. Each is a tweet. The tweets become a thread. The thread becomes a carousel. You didn't create new things. You extracted what was already there.

**Tweet 3:** Strong ideas are platform-agnostic. The same insight that lands in long form also lands in a single sentence. Isolate it and remove everything around it.

**Tweet 4:** Repurposing is not copying yourself. It's ensuring your best thinking reaches people in the format they prefer to consume.

**Tweet 5:** Most of your best ideas are buried in long-form pieces your audience never saw because they don't consume long-form. Extraction fixes that.
