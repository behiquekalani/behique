# Twitter Batch 2166

## Tweet 1
You're not building a feed. You're building a library.

Feeds disappear by Tuesday. Libraries grow in value.

Every piece you create should earn its place in the library.

## Tweet 2
One piece that solves a real problem beats five pieces that entertain your existing audience.

Think about discovery. How does someone who doesn't know you find this?

## Tweet 3
Two types of content:

Content that performs (today's likes, gone tomorrow)
Content that compounds (found via search 6 months from now, converts a reader into a buyer)

Build more of the second one.

## Tweet 4
Most content fails because it's written for existing followers.

It assumes context. Uses inside language.

The piece that scales is the one written for the person who hasn't found you yet.

## Tweet 5
Depth over volume.

One piece that genuinely shifts someone's thinking will circulate longer than 30 pieces that don't.

The goal is resonance, not frequency.
