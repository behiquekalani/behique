# Twitter Batch 1615

## Tweet 1
Most email sequences fail because they were written to impress rather than to serve.

Stop showing off your product. Start solving the reader's problem first.

## Tweet 2
The five-email sequence that actually works:

1: Deliver immediate value
2: Deepen the problem
3: Introduce the conceptual solution
4: Show proof
5: Make the offer

By email five, they are not being sold. They are being invited.

## Tweet 3
If you start selling in email one, trust is broken before it was built.

The goal is to be so useful that buying feels like a logical continuation, not a pivot.

## Tweet 4
Each email should do one thing.

Not three things. One.

If you cannot describe the purpose of an email in a single sentence, it needs to be rewritten.

## Tweet 5
Short emails that land one idea outperform long emails that make five points.

Nobody asked for the five-point essay.

They asked for the specific thing you promised when they signed up.
