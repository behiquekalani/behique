# Twitter Batch 1647

## Tweet 1
A micro-brand isn't a small version of a big brand.

It's a different category. It runs on trust, specificity, and a point of view.

Most companies can't build one. An individual can.

## Tweet 2
Big brands have to appeal to everyone.

You don't.

Your job is to repel the wrong people and attract the right ones with unusual precision.

## Tweet 3
What do you believe about your field that most people in it get wrong?

That belief is your brand.

Not your logo. Not your color palette.

## Tweet 4
A micro-brand isn't built in a campaign.

It's built in the accumulation of decisions.

Every post either reinforces your identity or dilutes it.

## Tweet 5
Write three sentences that define your brand's point of view.

Not what you sell. What you believe.

Post those three sentences publicly this week and see who shows up.
