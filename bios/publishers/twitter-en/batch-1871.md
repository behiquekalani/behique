1. People do not buy information. They buy the person they become after using it. Sell the after state. The content is just the path.

2. If you have to explain the value of your product, the positioning is wrong. The outcome should be obvious before the customer reads the details.

3. Every feature you add that does not move the customer toward the promised outcome is noise. Strip the impressive stuff that does not serve the transformation.

4. The best products do less, more clearly. The worst ones try to cover everything and deliver nothing specific enough to change anyone.

5. Lead with who your reader becomes, not what you teach. "Learn X" is weak. "Stop doing Y and start doing Z" is a transformation. One sells. The other informs.
