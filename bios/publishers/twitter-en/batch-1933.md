1. Character is not what you do in public. It is the aggregate of small decisions you make when no one would know.

2. The standard you hold in private is the one that shows up in public when pressure is highest. Train it when stakes are low.

3. Cutting corners in private is skill training. You are teaching yourself a habit. Eventually it becomes automatic.

4. Reputation takes years to build and is mostly made of what you chose to do when no one was watching.

5. Hold the standard alone first. Then holding it with an audience is easy. The order matters.
