# Twitter Batch 2306

## Tweet 1
Nobody buys a gym membership for the treadmill.

They buy the version of themselves who fits in last year's jeans.

Sell the outcome. Not the process.

## Tweet 2
Weak positioning: "I offer social media management."
Strong positioning: "I help e-commerce brands turn Instagram into their second-best sales channel."

Same service. Completely different conversation.

## Tweet 3
Your marketing should answer one question before anything else:

"What does my life look like after I use this?"

If you can't answer that in two sentences, your positioning isn't done yet.

## Tweet 4
The right buyer reads your offer and thinks: "this is exactly for me."

They don't need to be convinced. They recognize themselves in the description.

That recognition is what specificity creates. It's not magic. It's just accurate targeting.

## Tweet 5
Describing your product accurately is not the same as describing it compellingly.

Accurate: what it is.
Compelling: what it does for the person who uses it.

Your sales page should be heavy on the second. Light on the first.
