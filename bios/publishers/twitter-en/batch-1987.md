1. Saying no to most things is what makes room for the one thing worth saying yes to. A full schedule is usually an avoidance strategy.

2. The reason most projects fail is not lack of ideas. It is the gap between the last rush of excitement and the first sign of real results.

3. A product built on a problem you personally experienced is already better than most. You understand the user because you were the user.

4. Writing clarifies thinking. If you cannot explain your idea clearly in writing, you do not understand the idea yet. Write to find out.

5. The most underrated business move is making fewer things better. Most people keep adding. The better move is usually subtracting.
