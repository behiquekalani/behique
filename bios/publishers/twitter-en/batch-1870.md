1. The excitement about a new idea is the invitation to do the work, not the work itself. Most ideas die when the excitement wears off and the difficulty arrives.

2. If you only work when you feel like it, you will not build anything worth having. Feelings are unreliable inputs. Commitment is the only consistent one.

3. The uncomfortable middle of every project is where the real building happens. The people who finish things have learned to work through it, not around it.

4. An idea without a finish date is a wish. An idea with a deadline and a specific next action is a project. Know which one you are holding.

5. Deciding a project matters more than the comfort of stopping is the entire difference between the people who ship and the people who start and abandon.
