# Twitter Batch 1646

## Tweet 1
Chasing trends is exhausting because you're always running to catch something that's already moving.

By the time you publish your take, the feed has moved on.

## Tweet 2
A post about a trending topic has a shelf life of days.

A post about a durable problem has a shelf life of years.

The math for which to build is obvious. Most people still chase trends.

## Tweet 3
Evergreen content isn't boring content.

It answers questions people will still be asking in three years.

Those questions don't expire.

## Tweet 4
A post written two years ago that still drives traffic is doing work while you sleep.

That's the entire case for building a content library instead of chasing a feed.

## Tweet 5
Look at your last ten pieces of content.

How many will still be relevant in two years?

That ratio tells you everything about your content strategy.
