# Twitter Batch 2309

## Tweet 1
A system is any set of repeatable steps that produces a result without you figuring it out fresh each time.

You probably have zero of these and wonder why your output is inconsistent.

Build the system. Then run it.

## Tweet 2
One hour building a system that saves 30 minutes a week pays for itself in two weeks.

Then it keeps paying. Indefinitely.

The ROI on systems is infinite if the system works. Build them before you feel ready.

## Tweet 3
Talented people without systems produce inconsistently.

Less talented people with good systems outperform them over time.

Talent is a starting point. Systems are the compounding layer.

## Tweet 4
Pick one task you do more than 3 times a week.

Write every step you take to complete it, in order.

That document is your first SOP. You just built a system. It took 20 minutes. Do this for everything.

## Tweet 5
The resistance to building systems sounds like: "I'm not big enough to need them yet."

You are. Even at day one, doing things the same way each time saves cognitive load and produces consistent output.

The system is not overhead. It is the real work.
