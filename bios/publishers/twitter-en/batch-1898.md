1. Good intentions don't create demand. The market responds to what you actually deliver, not what you meant to deliver.

2. "Nobody bought it" is not a judgment on your character. It's information about a gap between what you built and what people needed. That gap is fixable.

3. The builders who improve fastest separate self-worth from work performance. The work failing doesn't mean you failed.

4. Your product can be wrong and your thinking can still be sharp. Iterate from that position and you'll get somewhere.

5. The market is an honest teacher. Engage with it directly. Update quickly. The feedback is always more useful than the comfort of not knowing.
