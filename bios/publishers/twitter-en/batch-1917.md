1. Goals give you a destination. Directions give you a reason to keep moving after you arrive. You need both. Most people only have one.

2. "Get better at building things" doesn't expire. "Hit $10k by December" does. Let the goal serve the direction, not replace it.

3. The empty feeling after hitting a big goal is the absence of direction. You got somewhere. You just don't know why or where to go next.

4. Build goals inside directions. The goal answers what you're building toward now. The direction answers why any of it matters. Neither works without the other.

5. A three-year identity question cuts through short-term noise: "Is this consistent with who I'm becoming?" Run decisions through that filter.
