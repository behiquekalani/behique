1. The people who improve fastest do not work the hardest. They have the shortest, tightest feedback loops.

2. 1,000 hours of practice with good feedback beats 10,000 hours in a closed room. The time is not the variable. The quality of feedback on each repetition is.

3. Every week you spend in a closed loop is a week of slow improvement. Every week you spend in an open loop with real feedback is worth ten closed ones.

4. If you are building a product, find ten real users as fast as possible. Their resistance will teach you more than six months of internal iteration.

5. The gap between where you are and where you want to be is mostly a feedback problem, not a talent problem.
