1. The second layer of every problem is where the fix lives. Surface explanations are what you tell people first. The real explanation takes honesty to reach.

2. Ask why once. Then ask why again. The root of most productivity problems is a belief, not a schedule. Beliefs are changeable. Start there.

3. Problems that look complex at the surface are often simple and uncomfortable underneath. The discomfort is what makes people stop at the first layer.

4. Name the actual block out loud. The moment you can say it clearly, the path forward usually becomes obvious. Vagueness protects avoidance.

5. "I don't have time" is never the real answer. Time is downstream of priority. Priority is downstream of belief. Find the belief.
