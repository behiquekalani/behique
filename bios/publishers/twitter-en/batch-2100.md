1. The standard you set for yourself is the only one worth caring about. Context sets the floor. You set the ceiling.

2. Say what you think. Then stop. Adding more words after you've made your point is not thoroughness. It's insecurity wearing the costume of effort.

3. The work that compounds was usually boring to produce. That's the trade. Boring to make, interesting over time.

4. Every person you admire for their output is inside an ongoing process of invisible work. You see the result. You don't see the current version of them still doing it.

5. Build one thing at a time long enough to know if it works. Most things fail from insufficient time, not insufficient quality.
