1. The version of you watching your daily decisions is the one who inherits the results. Make choices they'll thank you for.

2. Private consistency is the foundation of everything public. Nobody sees the work you do when no one is watching. It still counts.

3. Ask before each small decision: is this what the person I'm becoming would do? Apply it for sixty days. Your life will be unrecognizable.

4. You're not performing for your future self. You're building them. Every choice is construction material.

5. The gap between who you are and who you want to be closes one small decision at a time. Not dramatically. Just consistently.
