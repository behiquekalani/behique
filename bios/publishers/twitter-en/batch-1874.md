1. Stay solo longer than feels comfortable. The clarity you develop by doing everything yourself is something no team can give you before you have it.

2. Adding people before you have product clarity distributes responsibility for a direction that has not been found yet. That is not hiring. That is alibi building.

3. The best team is built by someone who has already done every function well enough to know what good looks like. Get there first.

4. Two uncertain people do not equal one certain direction. They equal two months of alignment meetings before any building happens.

5. Your solo output will teach you more about your business model than any co-founder conversation will. Build first. Hire when the model is proven.
