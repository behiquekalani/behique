# Twitter Batch 1668

## Tweet 1
Trending content is a treadmill. Evergreen content is a flywheel. One requires constant effort to stay relevant. The other builds on itself indefinitely.

## Tweet 2
Before you write anything, ask: will this matter in two years? If the answer depends on a current event or platform update, it is trending. Both have value. Know which one you are building.

## Tweet 3
The evergreen test: can someone who finds this article two years from now get full value from it without any context about what was happening when you wrote it? If yes, it is evergreen. Ship it.

## Tweet 4
One strong evergreen piece can generate 6 months of content if you repurpose it deliberately. Article becomes thread. Thread becomes email. Email becomes short-form video. The idea does not change. The format does.

## Tweet 5
What is a question your audience asks you regularly that will still be relevant in 5 years? Write the definitive answer to that question this week. That is your highest-leverage content move.
