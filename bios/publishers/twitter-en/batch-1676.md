# Twitter Batch 1676

## Tweet 1
Perfectionism is a defense mechanism. If you never ship, you can never fail publicly. But you also cannot learn, earn, or help anyone.

## Tweet 2
The perfect version of your product that does not exist yet cannot help anyone. The imperfect version you ship today can.

## Tweet 3
The market does not reward the best product. It rewards the one that shows up first with something good enough. Good enough today beats perfect in six months.

## Tweet 4
Thread: What launching imperfect teaches you.

Your first product tells you what people actually value, not what you assumed.
Your first cohort tells you which modules they skip.
Your first sale tells you who your real buyer is.

None of that information exists until you ship. Ship.

## Tweet 5
Version one is not the final version. It is the first draft of a product you will improve based on real feedback. The creators who ship fast and iterate often outperform the ones who perfect and release once.
