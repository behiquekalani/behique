1. Selling as persuasion feels manipulative. Selling as translation feels like service. The difference is whether there is a real match between what you built and what they need.

2. If you do not sell, you are not sparing people from pressure. You are withholding information they would benefit from having.

3. When the product actually solves the problem, selling requires very little aggression. Mostly it just requires accurate communication.

4. The fix for uncomfortable selling is upstream. Build something that solves something real for someone specific. The rest gets easier.

5. You have built something. Someone has the problem it solves. Connecting those two facts is not manipulation. It is translation. Do not confuse the two.
