1. Playing the long game does not mean deferring all feedback. It means pursuing something that compounds while building short loops that tell you if you are on track.

2. The people who disappear for two years building in silence often emerge with the wrong thing. Feedback is not a distraction. It is the work.

3. Feedback from people with skin in the game is worth pursuing. Feedback from people who want to seem smart is noise.

4. Ship small versions early. Get a real person to pay before you build the full thing. Let their behavior tell you the truth.

5. Assumptions compound errors just as readily as good decisions compound results. Short feedback loops correct assumptions before they become catastrophic.
