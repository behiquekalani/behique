# Twitter Batch 1610

## Tweet 1
Your best customer research tool is not a survey.

It is forums, reviews, and comment sections where people describe their problems without knowing anyone is watching.

That language is honest. Use it.

## Tweet 2
The difference between good copy and bad copy:

Bad copy: "improve your productivity"
Good copy: "I feel like I am spinning my wheels and getting nowhere"

One is a category. One is a feeling. Write for the feeling.

## Tweet 3
What does your ideal customer say in private when they are frustrated with the problem you solve?

Find those exact words. Use them in your marketing.

That is not manipulation. That is proof you were listening.

## Tweet 4
Distance from your customer is the first sign of a business about to get disrupted.

The builders who stay relevant do ongoing research. They read forums. They answer comments. They stay close.

## Tweet 5
Most founders know their product deeply and their customer shallowly.

The ones who flip that ratio consistently win.

Your product is replaceable. Your understanding of a specific person's specific pain is not.
