1. Every meaningful attempt costs something. The question is not whether you pay the failure tax. It is whether you also pay the far more expensive failure-to-attempt tax.

2. Failed attempts are tuition for a specific type of knowledge that cannot be acquired any other way. You do not get a refund, but you do get the lesson.

3. Stop asking "what if this fails?" as a reason to wait. Start asking "what will I learn if this fails?" The second question has a real answer.

4. The information from a failed attempt is precise, personal, and actionable. The information from not trying is nothing.

5. The person who calculates the cost of failure and decides it is worth paying is fundamentally different from the person who finds a reason to wait. That difference accumulates into a different life.
