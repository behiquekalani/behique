1. If you are apologizing before quoting your price, you do not believe in what you built. Fix the product first.

2. Selling is not manipulation. It is communicating clearly that you built something worth someone's money.

3. The discomfort around selling is usually about the product, not the pitch. When you trust what you made, the conversation is easy.

4. Describe the problem. Show the solution. State the price. Anyone who needs it will say yes. That is the whole process.

5. You are not imposing when you tell someone about a thing that could genuinely help them. You are doing them a service.
