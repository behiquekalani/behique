# Twitter Batch 1577

## Tweet 1
Confused people don't buy.

Every piece of confusion in your communication costs you a reader, a subscriber, a buyer.

Clarity removes friction. Friction kills conversion. Simple.

## Tweet 2
Can you explain what you do in one sentence that a stranger understands immediately?

Not a clever tagline. A plain sentence.

If it takes three sentences and they still look uncertain, your positioning isn't clear yet.

## Tweet 3
Template: I help [specific person] do [specific thing] so they can [specific outcome].

Fill it in precisely.

That single sentence does more for your marketing than any content strategy.

## Tweet 4
Your sales page should answer four questions immediately:
- Who is this for?
- What problem does it solve?
- What does the buyer get?
- What happens after they buy?

If any of these require scrolling to find, the clarity isn't there yet.

## Tweet 5
Read your bio or offer page out loud.

Does it sound like something you'd actually say to a real person?

If not, that's the clarity problem. Fix the language before you fix the marketing.
