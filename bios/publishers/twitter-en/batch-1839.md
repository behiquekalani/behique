1. Unlimited resources do not produce the best work. Constraints do. The startup with no money figures out distribution channels that funded competitors never tried.

2. You are not at a disadvantage because you have limits. You are in the conditions that historically produce the most interesting solutions.

3. Give yourself half the time you think you need. Set a budget that forces prioritization. Write the essay in 300 words instead of 3,000. Watch what sharpens.

4. Constraints force the question: what is actually essential here? Applied consistently, that question separates clear work from bloated work.

5. Constraints are not obstacles to creativity. They are the mechanism. Abundance lets you spend your way past the thinking. The thinking was the whole point.
