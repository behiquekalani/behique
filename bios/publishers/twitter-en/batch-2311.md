# Twitter Batch 2311

## Tweet 1
Underpricing is not humility.

It is a failure of positioning.

Your price communicates your belief in your own product. Price from evidence, not from fear.

## Tweet 2
Low prices attract low-commitment buyers.

They are harder to serve, less likely to implement, and more likely to ask for refunds.

Higher prices attract serious buyers. Price accordingly.

## Tweet 3
A $10 guide and a $100 guide on the same topic create completely different expectations before anyone reads a word.

Price is a signal. Make sure it is sending the right message.

## Tweet 4
How to price a digital product:

Calculate the value of the outcome it delivers. Time saved, money made, problem avoided.

Charge 30-50% of that value.

Most people charge 5% and wonder why sales feel like pulling teeth.

## Tweet 5
Raise your price. Then work to deserve it.

That order sounds backwards. It isn't.

The commitment to deserve a higher price produces the product improvements that justify it. The external pressure creates internal growth.
