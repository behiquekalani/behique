1. The opinions you keep private are the ones that could build your audience. Saying what you actually think is the entry fee.

2. Neutrality is not professional. It is invisible. And invisible does not build anything.

3. Your point of view is your only differentiator in a world full of people saying similar things with different words.

4. Most people are afraid to be wrong in public. The people who are willing to be wrong in public learn faster and build more trust.

5. Conviction is more valuable than credentials for building an audience. People follow people who actually believe something.
