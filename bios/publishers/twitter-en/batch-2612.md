# Twitter Batch 2612

**Tweet 1:** The gap between a $9 product and a $97 product is rarely the information. It's almost always the packaging.

**Tweet 2:** A clean, structured product makes value legible. A messy one undersells the knowledge even when the content is excellent.

**Tweet 3:** Packaging includes the name, the promise, the sequence, and the sense that someone thought about how you would use this.

**Tweet 4:** Better packaging produces better outcomes. People follow through more when the information is structured logically.

**Tweet 5:** Take knowledge you already have. Structure it as a five-step process with a clear before and after. That's 80% of a sellable product.
