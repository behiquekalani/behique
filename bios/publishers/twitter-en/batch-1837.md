1. If your income stops the moment you stop working, you have a job. You just changed employers from a company to your clients.

2. Skills plateau. Assets compound. The person who packages their skill into something others can buy without booking their time has escaped the trap.

3. Use skill income to fund the time required to build the asset. Do not get so comfortable with the skill income that you never make the transition.

4. An asset is anything that produces value without requiring your direct labor every time. Build toward ownership of those things.

5. The goal is not to be good at a skill that clients pay for. The goal is to own something that works while you are not working.
