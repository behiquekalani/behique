1. Your best product idea is already inside you. Think about who you were two years ago and what you desperately needed. Build that. You know the problem from the inside.

2. The most useful content is written from inside the problem, not above it. Your reader feels understood instead of lectured. That difference is everything.

3. Do not build for an imagined customer. Build for the version of you that was stuck six months ago. You know exactly what they needed. Give them that.

4. The value of your experience is not in the wins. It is in the specific sequence of mistakes you made and corrected. That sequence is what someone else needs.

5. Write the thing you wish someone had sent you three years ago. That is the most honest brief you will ever have. Use it.
