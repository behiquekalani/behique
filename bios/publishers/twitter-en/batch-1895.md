1. Not all hours are equal. An hour of peak focus beats five hours of distracted effort. Stop tracking hours. Start protecting your best ones.

2. Most people spend their sharpest mental hours on low-stakes tasks. Then they wonder why deep work feels impossible. It's an allocation problem.

3. Identify when your mind is sharpest. Block that time. Use it only for work that requires full capacity. Everything else can wait.

4. Energy management is the productivity skill nobody talks about. Manage energy first. Time will follow.

5. You can have a full calendar and an empty output. Busyness consumes energy. Meaningful work requires it. Protect the difference.
