# Twitter Batch 1598

## Tweet 1
A lot of people build elaborate note-taking systems and never produce anything with them.

That is not a second brain. That is an organized distraction.

## Tweet 2
The collection trap: reading, capturing, tagging, linking.

These feel like progress because they involve effort.

But they are upstream of the actual work. The actual work is writing, building, shipping.

## Tweet 3
Design your knowledge system around your outputs, not your inputs.

Ask not what you want to store. Ask what you want to produce.

## Tweet 4
A real personal knowledge system does three things:

Captures ideas so they are not lost. Connects ideas so new insights emerge. Generates content when you need to write or build.

Most systems only do the first one.

## Tweet 5
Open your notes from three months ago.

Find one idea. Connect it to something you are working on now.

Write one paragraph about what that connection means.

That is the system working correctly.
