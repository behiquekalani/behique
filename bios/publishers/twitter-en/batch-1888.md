1. The feedback that agrees with you is not feedback. It's validation. Find someone who will tell you the thing you were hoping they wouldn't notice.

2. Ask specific questions if you want honest answers. "What did you think?" gets politeness. "What would make you not buy this?" gets information.

3. The builders who improve fastest separate their self-worth from their work's performance. The work can fail while you remain capable.

4. Thank people who give you hard feedback. They spent real attention on your work and told you the truth. That's rare.

5. Comfortable feedback leaves you where you are. Uncomfortable feedback moves you. Seek the second kind.
