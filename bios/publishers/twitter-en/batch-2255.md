# Twitter Batch 2255

## Tweet 1
A perfect system that produces nothing is just organized procrastination.

Systems exist to reduce friction.

If your system adds friction, it's broken.

## Tweet 2
The test for any system: does it increase output?

Not whether it feels organized.
Not whether it looks impressive in a screenshot.

Whether it produces more and better work. That's it.

## Tweet 3
Good systems are invisible.

They run in the background.
They make the next action obvious and easy.

A 30-second checklist beats a 20-minute complex workflow every time.

## Tweet 4
The best builders have surprisingly simple systems.

A text file for ideas.
A ten-minute weekly review.
A daily writing habit with no tracking.

Simple because simple gets done.

## Tweet 5
Your audience doesn't see your systems.

They see your work.

Output is the thing. Systems only matter if they produce more of it.
