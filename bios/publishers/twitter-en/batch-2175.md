# Twitter Batch 2175

## Tweet 1
A great product is inherently easier to market.

A weak product requires extraordinary marketing to compensate for what it lacks.

Build the product first. Market it second.

## Tweet 2
A product sells itself when your buyer can explain what it does for them in one sentence.

If they can't explain it, they can't refer it.

Referrals are the most powerful distribution channel you have.

## Tweet 3
The best sales page is a headline your buyer reads and thinks: "This is exactly what I've been looking for."

That doesn't come from copywriting skill. It comes from talking to buyers before you build.

## Tweet 4
Use the exact words your buyer uses to describe their frustration.

In your headline. In your product name.

Recognition is the fastest path to trust.

## Tweet 5
Find three people who represent your ideal buyer.

Ask them: "How would you describe this problem to a friend?"

Record their exact words. Use them. Compare conversion before and after.
