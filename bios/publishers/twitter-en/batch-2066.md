1. Every skill has a surface and a second layer. Most people master the surface and stop. The second layer is where the real gap lives.

2. Writing is the surface. Knowing what to leave out is the second layer. The second layer is almost always about restraint.

3. Selling is the surface. Knowing who not to sell to is the second layer. The second layer is almost always about judgment.

4. Good performers execute reliably. Great performers understand the underlying logic well enough to adapt when conditions change.

5. Find the meta-skill in whatever you're building. What do the best know that the good ones don't? Start there.
