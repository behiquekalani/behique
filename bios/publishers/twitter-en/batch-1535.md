# Twitter Batch 1535

## Tweet 1
Most people reason by analogy: "this worked for someone else."

First principles thinking asks: "what is actually true here, ignoring how it's been done before?"

One is fast and often wrong. The other is slow and often right.

## Tweet 2
Inversion: instead of asking "how do I succeed?" ask "how would I definitely fail?"

Then remove those failure modes. You'll find risks you would have never seen by thinking forward.

## Tweet 3
First-order thinking: if I lower my price, sales go up.

Second-order thinking: but profit per unit drops, customer quality declines, and I need 3x volume to sustain the business.

Always think two steps ahead before committing.

## Tweet 4
The mental model that changed how I make decisions:

Expected value. Not "will this work?" but "what's the probability times the payoff?"

A 20% chance at 10x beats a 90% chance at 1.2x almost every time.

## Tweet 5
What mental models are you actually using to make decisions?

If you can't name them, you're probably using whatever felt right in the moment. That's intuition, not strategy.
