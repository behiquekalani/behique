1. You don't need to feel like a writer to write today. The feeling of being a writer comes from the accumulated evidence of having written.

2. Identity follows behavior. Not the other way around. Stop waiting for the permission that only repetition can issue.

3. Define yourself by what you do daily, not what you feel you are. Those two things should match. If they don't, one of them is lying.

4. "I am a builder" when you haven't built anything in months is a story. "I build every day" is a statement that either describes reality or demands you change it.

5. The gap between who you say you are and what you do daily is where most people live permanently. Close it through action. The identity follows without effort.
