# Twitter Batch 2524

**Tweet 1:** Price based on outcomes, not inputs. What did you put in is the wrong question. What does the buyer get out is the question that unlocks real pricing.

**Tweet 2:** Content strategy starts with one idea at the center, not a calendar. When the central idea is clear, the calendar fills itself with variations on that theme.

**Tweet 3:** Building in public works because it collapses the distance between you and the people who might buy from you. Connection, not transparency, is the mechanism.

**Tweet 4:** A personal monopoly forms at the intersection of your knowledge, your personality, your aesthetic, and your chosen audience. No one else occupies that exact intersection.

**Tweet 5:** Most digital products fail before launch because the audience was never built first. Content about the problem should always precede the product that solves it.
