Unlimited options produce paralysis. Constraints force a decision. Decisions create momentum. Momentum creates work. Use more constraints.

---

A 200-word limit makes you ruthless about what stays. A 24-hour deadline forces the version that exists over the version that could.

---

The blank canvas is terrifying. The half-finished draft is easy to continue. Start with a constraint, not a blank page.

---

When you feel stuck, the instinct is to add options. The thing that usually works: remove one. Narrow the scope. Ship the simpler version first.

---

The constraint is not the problem. The constraint is what makes the thing real.
