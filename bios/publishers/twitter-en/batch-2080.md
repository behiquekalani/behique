1. Short-term thinking is the brain's default. The wiring optimizes for now. Building anything that compounds requires overriding it deliberately.

2. Every client you take shapes your reputation. Every skill you avoid changes your capability in a year. Daily decisions are long-game decisions viewed up close.

3. The people who end up with something real are usually not the ones who wanted it most. They're the ones who refused to trade it for something easier at the moments when trading felt rational.

4. Ask one question about every significant decision: what does this look like in five years? Most short-term temptations don't survive that question.

5. Long-game thinking doesn't mean ignoring the present. It means evaluating present decisions by their future consequences. That's not passive. It's the sharpest filter you have.
