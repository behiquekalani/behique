1. Momentum is a resource. Starting costs energy. Continuing is cheap. Protect continuity more than intensity.

2. Every interruption is a restart cost. Stopping less matters more than pushing harder.

3. Four consistent hours daily beats ten-hour bursts followed by recovery days. Consistency compounds. Sprints don't.

4. Make stopping harder than continuing. The streak has value worth protecting. Use that.

5. Most people never experience momentum because they interrupt themselves before it builds. Momentum requires an unbroken run long enough to feel it.
