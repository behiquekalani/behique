# Twitter Batch 1690

## Tweet 1
Creative blocks are information, not failure.

They're telling you something: perfectionism, unclear direction, or genuine depletion.

Diagnose first. Push through second.

## Tweet 2
The most effective cure for perfectionism-based creative block:

Write something bad on purpose.

Give yourself explicit permission to produce something ugly. The blank page is the only real enemy.

## Tweet 3
When you're stuck creatively, ask:

Who is this for?
What do they need to know?
What do I want them to do after?

Answer those three questions and the block usually lifts because now the work has a purpose.

## Tweet 4
Forcing output when you're genuinely depleted doesn't work and produces low quality results.

Sometimes the most productive thing is rest.

Know the difference between avoidance and actual depletion.

## Tweet 5
Most creative blocks aren't about creativity at all.

They're about clarity.

You don't know what you're making or why. That uncertainty stops the hands before they start.

Get clear on the purpose. The words follow.
