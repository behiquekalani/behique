1. Clarity is a competitive advantage. A vague goal can't fail, which means it can't teach you anything either. Be specific enough to be wrong.

2. The blank canvas is not freedom. It's paralysis. Constraints force decisions. Decisions produce action. Start by choosing what you won't do.

3. Every open option costs attention. The person "keeping options alive" is paying a tax on every one of them. Close doors on purpose.

4. Commitment accelerates everything. When the decision is made, energy stops going into the debate and starts going into the work.

5. Most people solve surface problems. The real problem lives one "why" deeper. Ask it. The answer is almost always a belief, not a circumstance.
