1. Clarity about what you want is rare because it requires commitment, and commitment means accepting trade-offs.

2. Vagueness keeps all options open and guarantees none of them get built.

3. Write down what you actually want. Not what sounds good. Not what your parents would approve of. What you want.

4. A real want generates real decisions. A fuzzy want generates activity that feels productive and builds toward nothing.

5. The discomfort of being specific about what you want is not a warning. It is confirmation that you are getting honest.
