# Twitter Batch 2573

**Tweet 1:** The gap between what you know and what you earn is usually a packaging problem. Not a knowledge problem.

**Tweet 2:** Good packaging answers three questions before the buyer asks them: what is this, what does it do for me, and why should I trust you.

**Tweet 3:** A poorly packaged offer makes people work to understand what they are buying. They hesitate. Good packaging makes the decision feel obvious.

**Tweet 4:** Study how the best offers in your space are presented. Notice the naming. The specificity. The before-and-after structure. Apply that discipline to what you know.

**Tweet 5:** Can you describe the outcome of your product in one clear sentence? If not, the packaging is the problem, not the product.
