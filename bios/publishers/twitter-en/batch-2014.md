1. Identity helps you stay consistent. It also helps you stay wrong when new information contradicts your method.

2. Once you are deeply invested in being a certain kind of thinker, ideas that challenge your approach feel like personal attacks. That is the trap.

3. The fix is to hold your identities slightly loosely. Think of yourself as someone trying to build well, not someone who is a particular kind of builder.

4. The goal is the outcome, not the self-concept. Methods should change as you learn. Identity should not be the reason they cannot.

5. The people who improve the longest stay curious about their own assumptions. That requires being more interested in being right than in being consistent.
