# Twitter Batch 1476

## Tweet 1
Low prices attract price-sensitive buyers.

High prices attract outcome-sensitive buyers.

You do not want more buyers. You want better ones.

## Tweet 2
A $9 course and a $497 course on the same topic attract completely different buyers.

The higher-priced buyer often gets more value, not because the content is different, but because they are more committed.

Price affects buyer behavior.

## Tweet 3
Most digital product creators underprice by 2x to 5x.

Raise your price. See what happens.

Price resistance from non-buyers is not data. Acceptance from buyers is.

## Tweet 4
What would you charge for your product if you were confident it delivered the result?

Charge that.

## Tweet 5
Thread: signs you are underpricing

1. Buyers never complain about price
2. You feel embarrassed telling people the price
3. You are attracting a lot of tire-kickers
4. You are working hard and still not profitable
5. Your best customers say it was "worth every penny"

Raise the price.
