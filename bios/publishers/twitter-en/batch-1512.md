# Twitter Batch 1512

## Tweet 1
Most decisions get made by analogy.

Someone else did it this way so you do too. This works until the context changes. Learn to think from first principles.

## Tweet 2
Instead of asking how to succeed, ask how to fail.

List every way your business could collapse. Eliminate those failure modes. That is strategy.

## Tweet 3
20% of your actions drive 80% of your results.

Most people know this. Almost nobody acts on it. Which 20% is doing the work in your business?

## Tweet 4
A thread on the three mental models that changed how I work:

1. First principles: ignore what others do, start from what is true
2. Inversion: ask how to fail, eliminate those paths
3. Pareto: find the 20% and do more of it, ruthlessly

## Tweet 5
The creator who makes decisions by analogy is always one market shift away from being obsolete.

The one who makes decisions from first principles adapts.
