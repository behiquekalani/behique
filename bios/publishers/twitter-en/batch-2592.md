# Twitter Batch 2592

**Tweet 1:** Going slow at the front end compresses the back end. A product built on deep problem understanding requires far less pivoting.

**Tweet 2:** The cost of going slow early is measured in weeks. The cost of going fast and wrong is measured in months or years.

**Tweet 3:** Writing a strategy before opening your laptop is not passive. Talking to ten potential customers before building is not passive. These are the highest-leverage actions available.

**Tweet 4:** Understand the problem more deeply before you build the solution. This sounds obvious. Almost nobody does it.

**Tweet 5:** The founders who take time to understand before they build are the ones who move fastest over a two-year horizon. Front-end slowness produces back-end speed.
