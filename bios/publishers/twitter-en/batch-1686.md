# Twitter Batch 1686

## Tweet 1
The best content schedule is the one you can keep without burning out.

Not the most ambitious one. The most sustainable one.

Consistency over intensity, every time.

## Tweet 2
Posting every day sounds powerful until you do it for three weeks and run out of ideas, then disappear for a month.

Pick a pace you can hold for a year.

That's the one that builds an audience.

## Tweet 3
Content batching changed my output more than any other habit.

3 hours on Monday. Everything for the week done.

No daily scramble. No decision fatigue. Just create once, distribute all week.

## Tweet 4
What's your actual baseline for content production?

Not what you plan. What you actually produce.

Build your schedule slightly above that floor, not double it.

## Tweet 5
The gap between "I'll post every day" and "I haven't posted in two weeks" is almost always a systems problem, not a discipline problem.

Design the system. Remove the friction. Show up.
