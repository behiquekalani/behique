# Twitter Batch 1477

## Tweet 1
Niching does not limit you.

It gives you an entry point into someone's mind.

Once you are in, you can go anywhere.

## Tweet 2
"I help solopreneurs build digital product businesses" cuts through faster than "I help people improve their lives."

The first creates an instant filter. The right people self-select.

Specificity is not a box. It is a door.

## Tweet 3
Tim Ferriss started with productivity. James Clear started with habits. Naval started with startups.

They earned the right to expand by first becoming trusted in one domain.

You cannot skip the narrow phase.

## Tweet 4
Your positioning in one sentence: "I help [specific person] do [specific outcome] without [specific obstacle]."

If you cannot write that sentence clearly, the niche work is not done yet.

## Tweet 5
The audience that buys is not the broadest one.

It is the most specific one that trusts you enough to spend money.

Stop trying to appeal to everyone. It makes you invisible to the people who would actually pay.
