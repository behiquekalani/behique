1. The accumulation trap: you feel productive collecting knowledge. Your brain can't tell the difference between learning and doing. But the world only rewards the doing.

2. You don't have a motivation problem. You have a specificity problem. Name the exact task you're avoiding, down to the sentence or button. Then it becomes small enough to do.

3. Willpower depletes by afternoon. Environment doesn't. Design your space so the right thing is the easy thing. Stop relying on a tired brain to make good decisions.

4. Your environment is running a system. The question is whether you built it or drifted into it. Most people drift, then wonder why they feel busy but stuck.

5. Ready is a feeling. It never arrives on schedule. The people three iterations ahead of you aren't smarter. They just shipped before they felt ready.
