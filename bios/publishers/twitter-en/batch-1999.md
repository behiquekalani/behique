1. The biggest thing holding most creators back is not skill or audience size. It is the refusal to say something specific enough to be disagreed with.

2. Deep work produces asymmetric output. One hour of focused, uninterrupted work beats four hours of distracted effort. Guard the hour.

3. The value of a daily practice is not any single day. It is the identity it builds over time. You become the person who does the thing, and that person produces differently.

4. Most overnight successes are ten years in the making. The overnight part is just when other people finally noticed. Do not compare their visibility to your timeline.

5. The most honest thing you can put in your work is something you genuinely believe that most people in your space are afraid to say out loud.
