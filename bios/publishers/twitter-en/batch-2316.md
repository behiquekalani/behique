# Twitter Batch 2316

## Tweet 1
Competition is not your enemy. It is market validation.

If no one else is in your space, one of two things is true: untapped opportunity, or no market.

The second is far more common. Competition means demand exists.

## Tweet 2
You don't need to compete with everyone in a category.

You need to win for a specific subset of that market.

That's a winnable game. Competing for everyone isn't.

## Tweet 3
When you see someone succeeding in your space, the useful question is not "how do I beat them?"

It is "who are they not reaching? What are they not saying? What gap exists that I could fill?"

Your position lives in their blind spots.

## Tweet 4
The relevant question is never: "Is anyone else doing this?"

It is always: "Am I doing this better, for a more specific person, or with a different perspective?"

Those are the only three differentiators that actually matter.

## Tweet 5
Study your space this week.

For each person operating in it, write: who they don't speak to, what angle they don't take, what format they avoid.

Find the gap across all three. Build your next piece of content directly into it.
