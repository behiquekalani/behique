Advice is contextual. It comes from someone else's situation applied to their skills, their risk tolerance, their timing. Filter for that before you follow it.

---

The person who says post daily built an audience in a different era. The principle underneath it is iteration. You can get that differently.

---

Bad advisors are rare. Accurate historians of their own experience who are bad matches for your situation are extremely common.

---

Extract principles, not tactics. Why did it work? Can you get those benefits another way? That is how you use other people's experience without copying it.

---

Most advice fails in transfer, not in quality. The advice was right for them. Your job is to figure out what is right for you.
