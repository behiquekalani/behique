1. Reading great thinkers makes you feel smart. Writing your own version of what they said tells you how smart you actually are.

2. Close the article. Open a blank document. Write what you remember. What you can reconstruct is what you learned.

3. Most content consumption is the simulation of progress. You feel like you are working. Nothing is actually being built.

4. Borrowed clarity is not clarity. It is scaffolding that disappears when the source closes.

5. The test for understanding is not recognition. It is generation. Can you produce the idea from scratch? That is the bar.
