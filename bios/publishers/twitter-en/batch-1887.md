1. Keeping all your options open is not a strategy. It's a way to avoid the discomfort of choosing. Close some doors. The rest will matter more.

2. You can't go all the way down any path while hedging the direction. At some point, commitment is the strategy.

3. Commitment is not certainty. You will never be certain. Commitment is acting as if the direction is right until the evidence says otherwise.

4. Optionality feels like freedom. Most of the time it's just expensive indecision.

5. The people who build something lasting picked a direction and went all the way down it. Not because they had no doubts. Because they decided anyway.
