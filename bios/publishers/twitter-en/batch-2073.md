1. Skill compounds. Every project makes the next one faster. Stay in the same domain long enough to feel the acceleration.

2. Switching skills resets the compound curve. Most people do it too often because early-stage novelty feels like progress.

3. The late stage of an existing skill feels like diminishing returns. It isn't. That's where the compounding actually lives.

4. People who seem disproportionately productive are usually just working deep in a domain where their experience makes each hour worth three of a beginner's.

5. Pick the skill that sits at the intersection of your genuine interest and what the market actually needs. Then go further into it than feels comfortable.
