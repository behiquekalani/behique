# Twitter Batch 1522

## Tweet 1
Charging too little isn't humility. It's a signal problem.

Low prices attract buyers who optimize for price. High prices attract buyers who optimize for results.

Know who you want in the room.

## Tweet 2
Hot take: most creators are bad at pricing because they price based on what it cost them, not what it's worth to the buyer.

Those are very different numbers.

## Tweet 3
The $97 product that solves one specific problem will outsell the $297 course that tries to teach everything.

Specificity > comprehensiveness.

## Tweet 4
If you raised your prices and nothing happened, one of two things is true:

1. Your audience trusts you
2. You were severely underpriced

Usually it's both.

## Tweet 5
Thread: 3 pricing mistakes that kill creator businesses

1. Pricing by hours worked
2. Matching the cheapest competitor
3. Waiting until the product is "done" to set a price

The market doesn't care how long it took. It cares what it's worth.
