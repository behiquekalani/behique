1. Unlimited options produce paralysis, not freedom. The constraint is often what makes the work happen at all.

2. Give yourself a deadline that isn't comfortable. You'll find out exactly what you actually think. Comfort deadlines produce comfortable work.

3. The tight format forces clarity. The word limit finds the real idea. The restriction is not the enemy of quality. It creates it.

4. Build with three tools instead of thirty and you'll build something instead of configuring indefinitely.

5. Artificially constrain the next project. Remove one tool. Cut the timeline in half. See what you produce when you have to decide instead of deliberate.
