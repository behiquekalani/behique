1. A day full of small task switches is a day where you never go deep on anything. You pay the context-loading cost over and over without reaching depth.

2. Four hours of uninterrupted focus produces more useful output than eight hours of fragmented attention. Most knowledge workers have this inverted.

3. Protect blocks long enough to matter. Not 25 minutes. Ninety minutes minimum, by design, with your hardest problem inside.

4. Schedule everything else around your focus block, not the other way around. It feels selfish. It is the condition under which good work is actually possible.

5. Fragmented attention is easier to schedule and easier to justify. It is also where most ambitious work quietly goes to die.
