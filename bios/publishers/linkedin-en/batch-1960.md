# LinkedIn Batch 1960

**Post:** Most people fail at building an online business because they treat it like a project instead of a practice.

Projects have deadlines. Practices have no end date.

When you treat your business like a project, you expect results within a defined timeline. When those results do not appear on schedule, the project feels like a failure.

But building a business is a practice. There is no finish line. There is only the quality of what you do today and the compounding effect of doing it over time.

The shift in framing changes everything.

A practice does not fail. It evolves. A slow week is not a problem. It is data.

The person who approaches business as a practice does not panic when growth plateaus. They ask what needs refining. They adjust and continue.

The person who approaches it as a project either hits the goal and stops, or misses the goal and quits.

Neither leads to something durable.

What would it look like to show up for your work the way you show up for something you genuinely care about, without needing a specific outcome by a specific date?

That is the energy that builds something real.

Not urgency. Not hustle. Steady, intentional practice over a long enough timeline.

The time will pass anyway. Might as well be building.
