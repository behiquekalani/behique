Digital products are the closest thing to passive income that actually works.

You build something once. A guide, a template, a course, a system. Then you sell it to a hundred people without doing a hundred hours of work.

The key word is build. Most people skip that part. They want the sales without the asset.

Creating a digital product forces you to package what you know. That process alone is valuable. You figure out what you actually understand deeply versus what you only think you understand. You find the gaps in your own thinking.

Then when you sell it, the market gives you real feedback. Not the polite feedback of friends. The feedback of someone deciding whether to spend money.

Start small. A 10-page guide that solves one specific problem is worth more than a 40-hour course nobody finishes.

The builder who ships something imperfect today will learn more in 30 days than the one who spends 6 months perfecting something that never launches.

What knowledge do you have right now that someone would pay $20 to get in organized form?
