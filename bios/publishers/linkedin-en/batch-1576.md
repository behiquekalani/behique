Building in public is one of the most underused growth strategies for solo operators.

Not because it attracts attention. Because it creates accountability, builds trust, and turns your process into content.

When you share what you're building as you build it, a few things happen.

People root for you. They're invested in the outcome because they've watched the journey. When you launch, they're not strangers evaluating a product. They're supporters who've been waiting.

Your thinking sharpens. Writing about what you're doing forces you to articulate it clearly. You find the gaps. You clarify the direction. The act of explaining it makes the work better.

You build an audience before you have something to sell. By the time you launch, the audience is warm.

The objection is usually vulnerability. "What if it fails? What if people see me figure things out?" That's the point. The imperfect process is more relatable than the polished final product.

People don't follow perfect brands. They follow real people solving real problems.

Document the build. Share the thinking. Ship the work.

Then document the results. The compounding starts early and it never stops if you keep going.
