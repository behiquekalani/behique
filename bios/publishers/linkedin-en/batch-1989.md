# LinkedIn Batch 1989

**Post:** Your personal brand is not your logo or your color palette.

It is the answer to one question: "What do people say about you when you leave the room?"

Most people are so focused on the visual elements of their brand that they never build the actual thing. They have a consistent font and no consistent message. They have a beautiful website and no clear point of view.

Your brand is the residue of your consistency over time. Every post you publish, every conversation you have, every product you ship adds to it or subtracts from it.

The creators with strong personal brands did not have a brand strategy. They had a perspective they kept showing up with, day after day, until people started associating them with it.

You build a personal brand by having opinions and sharing them clearly. By solving a specific type of problem for a specific type of person. By showing up often enough that people start recognizing your name before they finish reading your post.

Pick your lane. Own it. Show up in it consistently.

The brand builds itself from the repetition.
