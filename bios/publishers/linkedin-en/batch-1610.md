# LinkedIn Post — batch-1610

You do not need a team to build a serious business.

This is not a manifesto against hiring. It is a reframe on what is actually required.

Most solo builders hire too early because they confuse busyness with business. They feel overwhelmed, assume they need help, and bring on a person before the model is solid enough to support the cost.

Hiring adds complexity. Management time, communication overhead, accountability in both directions. None of that is bad. But it is wrong at the wrong stage.

Before you hire, ask: have I removed everything that should not exist? Have I automated what should be automated? Have I built systems that make the recurring work fast?

Most solo operators who feel like they need help actually need better systems and sharper focus.

The goal of the one-person business is not to stay one person forever. It is to prove the model works, build real cash flow, and then add capacity intentionally, not reactively.

Know what stage you are in. Build for the stage you are in.
