# LinkedIn Batch 1507

## Post 1
**QUITTING THE WRONG THING IS NOT FAILURE.**

Knowing when to stop is a skill. Most people either quit too early, before results compound, or too late, after sunk cost takes over.

The question is not whether to quit. It is whether you are quitting because the thing is wrong or because it is hard.

Hard is expected. Wrong is information. Learn the difference.

How do you tell the difference in your own work?

## Post 2
**Every expert was once a beginner who decided to stay.**

Nobody starts with skill. Skill is the product of repeated, intentional practice over time.

The people you look up to in your field did not arrive knowing what they know. They showed up consistently, made a lot of mistakes in public, and kept going anyway.

You are not behind them. You are earlier in the same process.

What is one thing you are committed to getting good at this year?

---

## Post 3
**You cannot optimize what you do not measure.**

Feelings are not metrics. "I feel like I was productive" is not data. Data is what actually happened.

Track the things that matter. Revenue, output, audience growth, hours of deep work. When you see the numbers, you stop lying to yourself.

The truth is always in the data. And the data will show you exactly where to focus.

What are you measuring in your business or creative work right now?
