# LinkedIn Batch 1983

**Post:** Burnout is not caused by working too hard. It is caused by working without meaning.

You can work 12-hour days on something you love and feel energized. You can work 6-hour days on something you resent and feel completely empty.

The problem is not hours. It is alignment.

When your work is misaligned from what you actually value, every task feels heavy. You are spending your most limited resource, your time and attention, on outcomes you do not care about.

Solopreneurs are not immune to this. In fact, they are more vulnerable. Because you built the thing yourself, you feel responsible for every version of it, even the ones you no longer believe in.

The solution is not a vacation. It is a recalibration.

What did you originally want this business to give you? Time, freedom, impact, income? Is what you are doing right now producing any of those things?

If not, the work is not the problem. The direction is.

Fix the direction before adding more output. More output in the wrong direction is just faster burnout.
