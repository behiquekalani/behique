Most people price their services based on what they feel comfortable charging.

That number is almost always too low.

Pricing is not math. It is positioning. When you charge $500 for something, you attract a certain client. When you charge $5,000 for the same thing, you attract a different one. The work does not change much. The perceived value does.

The uncomfortable truth: your price is a signal. Low prices signal low confidence. They also signal that you will negotiate, overdeliver out of anxiety, and take on clients who drain you.

Raise your price before you feel ready. You will never feel ready.

The right price is the one that makes you slightly uncomfortable to say out loud. Once it stops making you uncomfortable, raise it again.

Solo business is not about doing more. It is about charging correctly for what you already do well.

Your skills are worth more than your insecurities will let you believe.

Price accordingly.
