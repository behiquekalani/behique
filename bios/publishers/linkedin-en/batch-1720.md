Attention is the first currency of a solo business.

Before anyone buys from you, they notice you. Before they notice you, you have to show up somewhere they are, consistently, with something worth stopping for.

Most people wait until they have something to sell before they start showing up. That's backwards.

You build the attention before you need it. The time to plant the tree is before you're hungry.

Showing up consistently does not mean posting every day forever. It means committing to a cadence you can hold for years, not weeks.

Three posts a week for two years beats seven posts a week for three months, followed by silence.

The algorithm rewards consistency. More importantly, the human brain rewards it. Familiarity builds trust. Trust precedes buying.

The solo operator who understands this doesn't chase viral moments. He builds a daily or weekly habit of showing up, saying something real, and inviting the right people to follow along.

Slowly, the audience forms. The trust builds. The offers land.

None of it happens overnight. All of it compounds.

Show up before you need anything from the audience. By the time you make the offer, they've already decided to say yes.
