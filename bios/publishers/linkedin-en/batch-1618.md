# LinkedIn Post — batch-1618

The income ceiling in a service business is real. The path around it is products.

Service income is linear. You trade hours, you get paid. Trade more hours, get paid more. But hours are finite. So income has a hard ceiling built in.

Product income is non-linear. You build the thing once and the distribution compounds. A course sold in January is still selling in November. A template you made on a Tuesday earns while you are on vacation.

The transition from service to product is not a pivot. It is an addition. Keep the service while you build the product. Use client work to fund the time to build. Use client problems to identify what the product should solve.

The product does not need to be a course. It can be a template, a framework, a swipe file, a system, a community. Anything that delivers value without requiring your direct time every single time.

One product that sells 200 times a year at $97 is $19,400 without a single call.

The math is clear. Build the thing.
