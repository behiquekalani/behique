# LinkedIn Post — batch-1600

Everything about your business communicates something. Your price, your writing, your website, your response time.

The question is whether those signals are intentional.

Most solopreneurs let the signals happen by default. They underprice out of habit. They write vague copy out of discomfort. They have a messy website because they never got around to fixing it.

Every one of those defaults costs you something.

The clients you attract, the price they expect to pay, the level of trust they bring to the relationship, all of it is shaped by the signals you put out.

Audit your signals. Start with your pricing page and your sales copy. Does the language match the level of client you want to work with? Does your price communicate quality or desperation?

You do not need a rebrand. You need intentional signals.

Decide what you want to communicate. Then check every surface of your business against that decision.

What you project is what you attract. Adjust accordingly.
