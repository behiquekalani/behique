Knowledge monetization starts with one honest question.

What do you know that someone else would pay to learn faster?

Not what you're passionate about. Not what you think sounds impressive. What have you figured out, through real experience, that would save someone else months of confusion?

That answer is the foundation of a product.

The mistake is thinking your knowledge needs to be groundbreaking. It doesn't. It needs to be specific, practical, and faster than the alternative.

If you figured out how to get your first freelance client using only LinkedIn, that's a product. If you built a tracking system that helped you stay consistent for a year, that's a product. If you learned how to run a profitable market stall on weekends, that's a product.

The expertise gap between you and the beginner is enormous. They cannot see it because they don't know what they don't know. You can see it because you already crossed it.

Bridge that gap. That's the product.

Package it clearly. Price it honestly. Sell it to the person who is where you were 12 months ago.

Your past struggle is someone else's shortcut. Charge for the shortcut.
