# LinkedIn Batch 1932

**Post:** Nobody tells you this about digital products.

The hard part is not building them. The hard part is deciding what problem they actually solve.

I see creators launch products that are really just information dumps. Long PDFs with no clear outcome. The buyer reads it, feels vaguely informed, and forgets it by Friday.

A product that sells is a product that promises a specific result to a specific person.

Not "everything about Instagram." But "how to write a first comment that stops the scroll."

Not "email marketing fundamentals." But "the five-email welcome sequence that converts subscribers to buyers in their first week."

Specificity is the whole game.

When you try to help everyone, you help no one enough to pay you.

The solopreneurs I watch build real income in their first year all have one thing in common: they picked a lane narrow enough to feel uncomfortable, then went deep.

Your niche is probably not too narrow. It is almost certainly too wide.

Pick a real person. Write to them. Solve one actual problem for them completely. Then price it at what it is worth.

The market rewards precision. Vagueness gets ignored.
