# LinkedIn Batch 1958

**Post:** You do not need a team to build a real business. But you do need to stop doing everything yourself.

Those sound contradictory. They are not.

The solopreneur who insists on doing everything personally is not being thorough. They are being a bottleneck.

Every hour you spend on a $20/hour task is an hour you did not spend on your $200/hour work.

The goal is not to hire a team. The goal is to remove yourself from the low-value work so you can do more high-value work.

That starts with automations. Then templates. Then contractors for specific tasks. Then systems that run without you.

Most of this does not require full-time employees or complex org charts.

It requires being honest about what you are actually worth per hour and refusing to spend your time below that rate.

If you can pay someone $30 to do something that would take you two hours, and that frees you to create something worth $300, that is not an expense. That is a return on investment.

The hardest part is giving up control. The second hardest part is believing your time is worth protecting.

Both are worth working through.

Your job is not to be the best at everything. It is to build a system that produces the best outcomes.
