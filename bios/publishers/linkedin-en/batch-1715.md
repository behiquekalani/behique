The best business decisions come from clear thinking, not more information.

Most solo operators don't have a research problem. They have a decision problem.

They consume content, collect frameworks, read books, take notes. They have more inputs than they can process. And they still can't decide.

More information doesn't solve indecision. It often makes it worse.

The cure is a forcing function. Give yourself a deadline and make the call. The imperfect decision made today beats the perfect decision made next quarter.

The second cure is writing. When you can't decide, write out what you're actually afraid of. Not what the options are. What you're afraid will happen if you choose wrong.

Most decisions look different once the real fear is visible. Half the time, the fear is irrational. The other half, naming the fear tells you exactly what to address before deciding.

You already have enough information. You've had enough information for weeks.

The question isn't what to decide. The question is why you haven't decided yet.

Answer that honestly. Then make the call.

Clear thinking is a skill. Practice it like one. Write to think. Decide to learn. Act to adjust.
