Your price is a signal before the client even reads your proposal.

Low price says: I am not sure this is worth much. High price says: I have done this before, I know what I deliver, and I am not desperate for your business.

Most solo builders underprice because they are afraid. Afraid the client will say no. Afraid they are not experienced enough. Afraid of the silence after sending the number.

But here is what actually happens when you raise your prices. The clients who say yes take you more seriously. They show up to calls prepared. They implement what you tell them. They get better results. Those results become case studies. The cycle compounds.

The clients who say no were never going to be good clients anyway. They would have negotiated every invoice and questioned every decision.

Pricing is not just math. It is a filter.

Set your price at the number that makes you slightly nervous to say out loud. That is usually the right number.
