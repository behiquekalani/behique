# LinkedIn Batch 1918

**Post:** Pricing your work too low is not humility. It is a failure of self-awareness.

When you undercharge, you attract clients who haggle. You work more hours than the project deserves. You resent the work. You tell yourself you will raise prices when you are more experienced.

But experience does not automatically change your pricing. Courage does.

The fear behind low prices is usually this: if I charge more, they might say no.

True. Some will. But the ones who say yes will treat you differently. They respect the investment. They show up prepared. They value your time because you made it clear yours was worth valuing.

Premium pricing changes the quality of your work relationships more than any contract or policy ever will.

And here is the thing nobody says out loud: your price is a signal. When you price low, you tell the market you are unsure of your own value. The market believes you.

When you price confidently, you tell the market you know exactly what this is worth. The market believes that too.

Pick a number that makes you slightly uncomfortable. Start there.

Not because you are trying to trick anyone. Because that discomfort is usually closer to what your work is actually worth.
