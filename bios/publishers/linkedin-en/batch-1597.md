# LinkedIn Post — batch-1597

The most dangerous stage of a solo business is when it starts working.

When money comes in, most people immediately complicate the thing that worked. They add services. They hire too fast. They change what was already converting.

Simple things that work should not be changed until there is a clear reason to change them.

Your job when something works is to document it, repeat it, and protect it from your own impulse to improve it before you understand why it works.

Scaling a solo business does not mean adding complexity. It means doing the same thing for more people or charging more for the same thing.

That is it.

Most solopreneurs fail not because they cannot find product-market fit. They fail because they find it and then dismantle it.

When something works, resist the urge to build the next thing immediately. Milk it. Systematize it. Understand every part of why it converts.

Then, only then, think about what is next.
