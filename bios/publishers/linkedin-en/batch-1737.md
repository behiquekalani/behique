# LinkedIn Post 1737

The version of you that plays it safe is not protecting you. It's just keeping you comfortable in a situation you already know you need to leave.

Safety is a good heuristic for survival. It's a terrible strategy for building something. The comfortable path is usually the crowded one, with the lowest margins, the highest competition, and the least interesting work.

Doing something uncertain is not recklessness. It's the price of operating in a space where the upside is still unclaimed.

You already know what you need to build. The resistance isn't confusion. It's fear dressed up as analysis. The plan is good enough. The next step is clear enough.

Start. Adjust when you have data, not before.
