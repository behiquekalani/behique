Your identity shapes your business more than your strategy does.

If you think of yourself as a freelancer, you'll price like a freelancer, market like a freelancer, and cap your income like a freelancer. Even if you technically sell products.

The shift happens when you start identifying as a builder. Not someone who trades time for money. Someone who creates assets that work independently.

That identity change isn't instant. It happens in small moments. The first time you wake up to a sale you made while sleeping. The first time someone pays for something that took you two hours to make but solves their problem completely.

You start to see time differently. Tasks that don't build assets feel wasteful. Work that compounds feels energizing.

Identity is upstream of action. If you want to change what you do, start by deciding what kind of person you are.

Builders build. That's the whole identity. The rest follows.
