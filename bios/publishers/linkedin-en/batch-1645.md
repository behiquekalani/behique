Digital products are not passive income. They're upfront work that pays repeatedly.

This distinction matters. If you expect passive income, you'll quit when the first week brings no sales. If you understand it's upfront work, you'll build the distribution, the audience, the trust first, and then watch the product follow.

The people selling courses, templates, guides, and toolkits are not sitting on beaches refreshing Stripe. They spent months building an audience that wanted the thing before the thing existed.

The business model is simple:
1. Pick a problem your audience has
2. Solve it once in a product
3. Drive traffic to that product repeatedly
4. Use revenue to build more products or better distribution

The hard part is step 3. Traffic is not automatic. You earn it through content, search, community, or paid ads. Usually some combination of all four.

But once it works, one product can fund the next. The catalog compounds. The audience grows.

That's the actual model. Not passive. Compounding.

Build something once. Improve it. Distribute it widely. Repeat.
