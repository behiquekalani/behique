Your specific background is not a limitation. It is your positioning.

The engineer who teaches business writing. The teacher who builds SaaS tools. The designer who writes about pricing strategy.

These combinations are rare. Rare is valuable.

Most people try to erase the parts of their background that do not fit the narrow category they are entering. They try to sound like everyone else in the space.

That is the opposite of what works.

The intersection of your specific experiences is where you have the least competition and the most credibility. No one else has exactly your combination.

You do not need to be the best in any single field. You need to be the only one at the intersection of your specific fields.

That intersection is your niche. Your background is the proof that you belong there.

Write about the weird combination. Build products for the overlap audience. Position yourself as the person who understands both worlds.

The narrower and more specific that intersection, the more obvious your value becomes to the people who need exactly what you offer.

Stop hiding the unusual parts. Lead with them.

Your specificity is what will make people say, "Finally. Someone who gets it."
