# LinkedIn Batch 1955

**Post:** Nobody talks about the period between starting and gaining traction.

That gap is where most people quit.

You are putting out content, building products, showing up consistently. The results are minimal. Your analytics look like a flatline.

This is not failure. This is the foundation phase.

Every person who built something real went through a version of this. They just do not post about it as often as they post about the wins.

The foundation phase is where you develop taste. Where you learn what your audience actually responds to versus what you assumed they would respond to.

It is also where you develop resilience. If you only stay in it when results are coming, you will always be a fair-weather builder.

The builders who make it through the foundation phase are not the most talented. They are the ones who decided to treat the lack of results as data, not as a verdict.

Every piece of content that does not land teaches you something. Every product that does not sell tells you something about your positioning.

The question is whether you are paying attention or whether you are letting the silence discourage you.

Build with intention during the quiet period. The people who do that are the ones who are still building five years later.
