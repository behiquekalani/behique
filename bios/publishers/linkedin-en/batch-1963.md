# LinkedIn Batch 1963

**Post:** The people who build the most successful personal brands are not the most credentialed. They are the most transparent.

Credentials signal authority to institutions. Transparency builds authority with real people.

When you share what you are actually working through, what is working, what is not, what you are learning in real time, you become someone people trust.

Not because you have all the answers. Because you are honest about the questions.

Most personal brands fail because they present a finished version of the person. Polished, certain, above struggle.

That version is impressive for about five seconds. Then it becomes distant. People do not see themselves in it.

The brand that builds a real audience lets people see the process.

Not manufactured vulnerability, not performative struggle. Just honest documentation of the work.

"Here is what I tried. Here is what happened. Here is what I learned."

That format works because it is useful. People can apply it to their own situation. They walk away with something.

Your path is the product. You do not need a perfect outcome to share it. You just need to share it clearly.

Build in public. The audience you attract will be the ones who are on a similar path and glad to have company.

Those are the best kind of followers. They become customers, collaborators, and friends.
