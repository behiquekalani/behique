Ownership is the thing most employees will never experience until they leave.

When you work for someone else, you build their asset. Your best ideas, your systems, your client relationships, your reputation at scale, all of it flows upward. You get a salary. They get equity.

That's not a judgment. It's a structure. And if you understand the structure, you can decide whether it works for you.

Ownership means your work accumulates in your name. The content you create is yours. The audience you build is yours. The systems you develop are yours. The products you ship belong to you.

This doesn't require quitting your job tomorrow. It requires starting something small that you own. A newsletter. A product. A service under your name. One hour a day, compounding over years.

The gap between employee and owner is not talent. It's not intelligence. It's whether the value you create belongs to you or someone else.

Most people never think about this directly. They should.

What do you own today that will still be generating value in five years?
