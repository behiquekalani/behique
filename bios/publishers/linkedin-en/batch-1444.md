# LinkedIn Batch 1444

## Post 1
**The best business model is one you would run for free.**

That sounds naive. It is not. When your work aligns with what you would do anyway, the cost of entry disappears. You get 10,000 hours of practice that feels like leisure. Everyone else gets 10,000 hours of grind.

Find the intersection of what you know deeply, what others pay for, and what you can do without a clock telling you to stop. That is the business.

Where does your natural obsession overlap with a real market need?

---

## Post 2
**Accountability is not about discipline. It is about design.**

If willpower is the only thing standing between you and your goals, the design is wrong. Make the right action the default. Remove the friction from what you want to do. Add friction to what you want to avoid.

Your environment shapes your behavior more than your intentions do. Stop trying to be more disciplined. Start designing a better environment.

How does your workspace currently support or undermine your best work?

---

## Post 3
**The hardest part of building is not the building. It is the shipping.**

Most projects die in the last 10%. The product is done but not published. The post is written but not sent. The offer is ready but not launched. That gap between done and shipped is where most potential dies.

Set a deadline. Tell someone about it. Launch anyway. Perfection is the enemy of impact.

What project are you holding at 90% that needs to ship today?
