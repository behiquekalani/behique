Building in public is not a marketing strategy. It's an accountability structure.

When you share your progress publicly, you create a social contract with your audience. They're watching. That pressure is useful.

Most people work in private, waiting until everything is perfect before showing it. The problem is that perfect never arrives. So they never show anything. The audience never grows. The business never starts.

Building in public forces the thing out into the world while it's still imperfect.

And imperfect, shipped, is infinitely more useful than perfect, waiting.

The side effect of building in public is that the audience forms around the process, not just the outcome. By the time you have something to sell, you already have people who watched you build it. They trust the work because they watched it happen.

It's not about sharing every detail. It's about sharing the real parts. The insight that unlocked something. The mistake that cost two weeks. The decision that changed the direction.

Real beats polished. Process beats highlight reel.

Build in public. Let people follow the work, not just the result.
