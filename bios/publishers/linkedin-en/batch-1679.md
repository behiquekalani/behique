The best business advice I've ignored the longest: charge for your expertise, not your deliverables.

When you charge for deliverables, the client owns the conversation. They ask for changes. They compare prices with competitors. They evaluate whether the thing you made is "worth it."

When you charge for expertise, the dynamic flips. You're not delivering a file. You're delivering a decision, a result, a direction that only you can provide given what you know.

The same applies to digital products. Don't sell a template. Sell the ability to skip the six months it took you to develop that template. Don't sell a guide. Sell the clarity you gained from two years of trial and error.

Buyers don't pay for information. They pay for transformation. They pay for shortcuts. They pay to feel like someone who knows what they're doing handled it.

Position yourself as that person.
