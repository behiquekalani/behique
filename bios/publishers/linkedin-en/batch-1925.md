# LinkedIn Batch 1925

**Post:** The most underrated skill in the creator economy is editing your own work.

Not spell-checking. Not grammar. Knowing what to cut.

Most creators write too much. They include every thought, every caveat, every hedge. The result is posts that take three paragraphs to say what could have been said in three sentences.

Readers are not lazy. They are busy. They are scanning for signal. The moment they sense filler, they leave.

The edit is where the value gets revealed.

When you write something, ask: what is the one thing I want this person to walk away believing or doing? Now find every sentence that does not serve that one thing. Cut it.

What remains is usually better. Tighter. More confident. More worth reading.

This is hard because cutting your own words feels like losing work. You spent time on those sentences. They felt important when you wrote them.

The reader does not care how long it took you. They care whether reading it was worth their time.

Make it worth their time by respecting it enough to cut.

The best writers are not the ones who write the most. They are the ones who know when to stop.
