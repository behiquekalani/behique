# LinkedIn Batch 1985

**Post:** Most people launch a product and wait for results.

The smarter move is to launch a product and immediately start collecting information.

What did people say when they bought? What questions did they ask before buying? What did they try before they found you? What made them hesitate?

That information is worth more than any marketing course. It tells you exactly what to say in your next piece of content, what objections to address in your copy, and what to build next.

The first version of a product is a research tool. The second version is the actual product.

But most people treat the first version like it has to be perfect before launch. They spend months refining before they have a single real customer. Then they launch and discover that the assumptions they spent months refining were wrong.

Ship early. Talk to customers. Let the market teach you.

The product that sells well six months from now will look nothing like the product you are imagining today. That is fine. That is how it is supposed to work.

Build. Learn. Improve. Repeat.
