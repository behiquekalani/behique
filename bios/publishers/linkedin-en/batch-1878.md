**Post:**
Pricing anxiety is real. And it keeps good products from reaching the people who need them.

Here's the thing about underpricing: it doesn't just hurt your revenue. It signals low value. People assume cheap means low quality. It's counterintuitive, but charging more often converts better.

The right price isn't about what feels fair to you. It's about what your buyer believes the outcome is worth.

If your product saves someone 10 hours a week, price it against that value. Not against the time it took you to build it.

Most creators price based on effort. Smart ones price based on result.

Double your current price on your next offer. See what happens. You might lose some buyers. But the buyers you keep will be better, more committed, and less likely to ask for refunds.

Good pricing filters for serious customers. That makes everything downstream easier.

Charge what you're worth. Then work to make it undeniably worth it.
