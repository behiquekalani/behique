# LinkedIn Batch 1952

**Post:** The best digital product you can build is the one that answers the question you get asked most.

Not the most creative idea. Not the biggest market. The question people are already asking you.

If three different people asked you the same thing this month, that is a product waiting to exist.

Most solopreneurs skip this step. They build what they think people want, then wonder why nobody buys.

The simplest research method: look at your inbox, your DMs, your comments. Find the pattern. Build the answer.

A 20-page PDF that solves a real problem sells better than a 100-page course that solves a hypothetical one.

People do not pay for information. They pay for the removal of a specific friction they are currently feeling.

Your job is not to educate them broadly. It is to solve the thing that is bothering them right now.

When you find the right problem, selling feels easy. Not because you are manipulating anyone, but because the fit is obvious.

The product explains itself. The customer already knows they need it before you finish the pitch.

That is the difference between a product you push and a product that pulls.

Start with the question. Build backwards from there.
