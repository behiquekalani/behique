# LinkedIn Post 1724

Digital products don't have shipping costs, inventory risk, or a warehouse. Yet most people still think you need a physical product to build a real business.

A well-made PDF, course, or template can outperform a physical product on every margin metric. The cost of goods is your time, once. The profit on sale two hundred is identical to sale one.

That's not a hack. That's a fundamental shift in how value gets created and transferred. Information has always been valuable. Digital infrastructure just made it cheaper to distribute.

The barrier isn't technology. Gumroad, Notion, a simple checkout. You can be live in a day. The barrier is believing your knowledge is worth packaging.

It is. Someone out there is paying for what you know. The only question is whether they're paying you or a competitor.
