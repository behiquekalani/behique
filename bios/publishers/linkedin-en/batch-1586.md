Most solopreneurs underprice because they are afraid.

Not because their work is not worth more. Because they are afraid the customer will say no, and hearing no feels like proof they were right to doubt themselves.

Pricing is not math. It is psychology.

When you charge low prices, you attract clients who question everything, respect nothing, and drain your time. When you charge higher prices, clients tend to show up prepared, implement your work, and come back.

Price is a signal. Low price signals low confidence. High price signals that you know exactly what you are doing.

The fix is not to raise prices randomly. It is to build conviction in the value you deliver, and then price that value accurately.

Ask yourself: what is this worth to the person buying it? Not what it cost you to make. What it is worth to them.

Price from that answer. Not from your insecurity.
