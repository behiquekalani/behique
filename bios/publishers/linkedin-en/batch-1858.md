**Post:**
Most solo creators quit six months before it would have worked.

I am not guessing. I watched it happen in real time, multiple times, in communities I was part of.

Someone builds something solid. Posts consistently for four or five months. Gets some traction but not enough. Feels like they are going nowhere.

They stop.

Then a month later, I see their content pick up. An algorithm shift. A piece that finally hits. A compound effect from all those months of work.

But they are not there to benefit from it.

The internet rewards patience more than talent.

You cannot control when the tipping point comes. You can control whether you are still there when it does.

Most strategies work if you run them long enough.

Most creators do not run them long enough.

Keep going. Not forever. But definitely past the point where it feels pointless, because that feeling usually precedes the breakthrough.

What stopped you the last time you quit something too early?
