**Post:**
The creator who ships ugly work consistently beats the perfectionist every time.

Not because ugly work is good. Because consistency builds audience, builds skill, builds feedback loops.

Perfectionism is a tax. You pay it with time, momentum, and opportunities.

I've posted content I thought was weak. Those posts sometimes performed best. Because the algorithm doesn't care about your internal critic. The audience connects with honesty, not polish.

Here's what I've learned: done beats perfect. Always. But done-and-improving beats both.

Set a deadline for every piece of content. When the deadline hits, you ship. Then you look at what worked and you do more of it.

Your 100th post will be better than your first. But only if you actually write the first 99.

Stop waiting until it's ready. Ship it. Learn from it. Repeat.

That's the whole game.
