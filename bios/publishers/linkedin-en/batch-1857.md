**Post:**
Here is an unpopular opinion about content strategy: most creators are overthinking it.

The algorithm changes constantly. Trends shift. What worked six months ago stops working.

But one thing has not changed in ten years: people want to feel understood.

Not entertained. Not impressed. Understood.

The creators who build lasting audiences are the ones who make their readers feel seen. Who articulate things the reader already felt but could not put into words.

That is not a strategy. That is a skill. And it is learnable.

Start by asking: who is my reader at 11pm? What are they thinking about that they would not say out loud at work?

Write to that person.

Not to the person who wants to be inspired. To the person who is grinding through something hard and needs someone who gets it.

That is your content strategy. Everything else is distribution.

Who is your reader at 11pm?
