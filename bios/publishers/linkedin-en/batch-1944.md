# LinkedIn Batch 1944

**Post:** The best business skill most people ignore: getting clear on what you actually want.

Not what looks impressive. Not what your peers are building. What you actually want your days to look like.

Most solopreneurs build toward a vague goal of financial freedom and end up in a business that creates a different kind of trapped.

If you want to travel, build something location-independent from day one. If you want deep creative work, do not build a model that requires constant client calls. If you want your evenings free, stop building audience-dependent revenue that requires daily publishing.

The business should be designed backward from the life.

This sounds obvious. Almost nobody does it.

They start with a revenue model that looked good on someone else's Twitter feed and then try to retrofit a life around it.

The fit is always off. The result is a job with more responsibility than the one you left.

Before the next sprint, ask one question: if this business works exactly as planned, what does a Tuesday look like?

If that Tuesday does not appeal to you, you are building the wrong thing.

Build the life first on paper. Then build the business that produces it.

That sequence changes every decision you make.
