# LinkedIn Batch 1933

**Post:** The biggest trap in online business is optimizing before you have traction.

You spend three months perfecting a landing page for a product that has never sold once. You A/B test headlines for an email list of 200 people. You redesign your website before you have figured out what you are actually selling.

This is not strategy. This is fear wearing a productivity costume.

The real work at the start is simple and uncomfortable: talk to people, ship fast, get feedback.

The product does not need to be perfect. It needs to exist and reach real hands.

A rough offer sold to ten people teaches you more than a polished funnel nobody sees.

Most online business failure is not a marketing problem. It is a sequencing problem. People optimize phase three before they have survived phase one.

Phase one is: does anyone care? Phase two is: can I deliver this reliably? Phase three is: can I scale it?

Most people jump to phase three on day seven.

Ship the thing. Sell it manually. Talk to your buyers. Then, and only then, build systems around what is working.

The order matters more than the execution.
