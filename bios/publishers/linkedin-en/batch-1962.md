# LinkedIn Batch 1962

**Post:** Digital products are not passive income. They are upfront work that can generate ongoing returns.

The distinction matters because one sets realistic expectations and the other sets you up for disappointment.

You will spend real time creating a quality product. More time building an audience who trusts you enough to buy it. More time positioning it correctly so the right people find it.

None of that is passive.

But once that foundation is in place, a product can sell without requiring your time on each transaction. That is the payoff.

The mistake is expecting the payoff before the foundation exists.

People build a product in a weekend, list it without an audience, and conclude that digital products are a myth.

The product was not the problem. The sequence was.

Build the audience first. Earn trust. Understand what they actually want to buy. Then build the product for that specific group.

In that order, digital products work. Out of that order, they mostly do not.

The upfront work is real. The ongoing returns are real too.

You are not buying passive income. You are building it, slowly, through the quality of what you make and the trust of the people you serve.

That is a better deal than most people realize. It just takes longer than most people want to wait.
