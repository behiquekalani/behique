Your personal brand is not your logo. It's your point of view.

Logos are forgettable. Points of view are not.

The reason people follow certain creators for years is not because the branding is beautiful. It's because the way that person thinks about the world is useful or interesting or different enough to come back to.

A point of view is the collection of things you believe are true that most people haven't thought through. It comes from your specific experience, your mistakes, your obsessions, your work.

Building it is simple: write down 10 things you believe about your field that most people get wrong. Then write about those beliefs. Repeatedly. From different angles. With evidence from your own life.

Over time, people know what you stand for before they read a word. Your name becomes an indicator of a specific way of thinking.

That's a brand. That's also an audience. That's also a business, if you build products and services that extend the thinking.

Most people never do this because it requires taking a position. Positions can be challenged. But a business built on "I agree with everyone" goes nowhere fast.

Take a position. Defend it. Update it when you learn. Repeat.
