# LinkedIn Post 1740

The most underrated skill in the creator economy is knowing when to stop building and start selling.

Builders love building. It's comfortable. It's controlled. It's always something to do. Selling is uncomfortable because it requires putting your work in front of people and letting them decide.

The pattern is familiar: one more feature, one more revision, one more thing before it's ready. That's not perfectionism. That's fear wearing a productivity costume.

Your product is ready when it solves the problem it was built to solve. Not when it's perfect. Not when you feel proud of every corner. When it works for the person it's for.

Stop building the thing. Start talking about it. The feedback will show you what to build next.
