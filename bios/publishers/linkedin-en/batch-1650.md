The solo business model works because it removes the inefficiency of unnecessary people.

Not because people are bad. Because most organizational structures add coordination overhead without adding output. Meetings to plan meetings. Approvals to approve approvals. Reports that no one reads.

A solo operator removes all of that. Decisions happen instantly. Execution follows immediately. Pivots don't require consensus.

This is not about being anti-collaboration. It's about being honest that small, focused, and autonomous beats bloated and slow for a specific type of work.

Digital products, consulting, content, code, writing. These all have one thing in common. One skilled person can produce the full output. The value doesn't require a team.

The model looks like this: deep expertise in one area, documented and productized, sold to people who need that expertise, without the overhead of management, HR, or office politics.

You keep more of what you earn. You work when you want. You build what matters to you.

It's not easy. But it's honest. And for the right person, it's the only model that makes sense.
