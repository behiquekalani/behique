Audience building is not a vanity project.

It's the distribution layer of your business.

Without an audience, every product launch is a cold start. You spend as much time figuring out who to tell as you spend building the thing. That ratio kills most solo projects before they have a chance.

With an audience, launches get easier. Feedback is faster. Validation happens before you build instead of after.

The audience doesn't need to be large. It needs to be specific and trusted. 500 people who read everything you write is a better asset than 50,000 who followed you for a meme.

Build the audience with consistency and honesty. Pick a topic. Show up on a schedule. Share what you actually think, not what you think will perform.

The right people will stay. The wrong people will leave. That's the filter working correctly.
