# LinkedIn Batch 1558

## Post 1
**MONEY IS A BYPRODUCT OF VALUE DELIVERED, NOT A GOAL IN ITSELF.**

Chase money and you make decisions that compromise your work. Chase value and money follows the work.

This is not idealism. It is mechanics. The market rewards genuine utility. The more specifically useful you are to a defined group of people, the more the money makes sense.

Focus on the value. Keep score with money.

What is the most valuable thing you could offer someone this week?

---

## Post 2
**The difference between a hobby and a business is a paying customer.**

You can call it a business all you want. Until someone hands you money, it is an experiment.

That is not a criticism. Experiments are necessary. But be honest about which stage you are in.

The experiment becomes a business the moment someone pays. Optimize for that moment.

---

## Post 3
**Burnout does not come from working too hard. It comes from working without meaning.**

You can work extremely hard on something you care about for years without burning out.

But work on something misaligned with who you are, even moderately, and you exhaust yourself fast.

The question is not how to work less. It is whether what you are working on is actually yours.

Are you building what you want or what you think you should want?
