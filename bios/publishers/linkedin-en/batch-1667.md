The niche question isn't "what's profitable." It's "what problem have I lived through?"

Lived experience creates the kind of insight no amount of research can replicate. You know the exact moment of frustration. You know the wrong turns. You know what almost works but doesn't quite.

That knowledge is rare. Most people sit on it because they assume everyone already knows it.

They don't.

The person three steps behind you would pay to skip those wrong turns. Your job is to package what you know into something they can use.

The more specific you are, the better. "Productivity for software engineers who work remote" beats "productivity tips" every time. Not because the audience is bigger, but because the message lands harder.

Pick a lane narrow enough that you can actually own it. Go deep instead of broad. The riches aren't in the widest niche. They're in the one where you're the obvious choice.
