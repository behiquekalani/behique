The trap of the solo business is thinking you need more before you can start.

More knowledge. More credibility. More followers. More time. More tools.

You don't. You need a clear enough offer and one person willing to pay for it.

Everything else is infrastructure you build after you've confirmed the model works.

The waiting is not preparation. It's avoidance wearing the costume of preparation. And the longer you wait, the more the idea ages in your head without ever testing against reality.

Reality is brutal and fast. You ship. You get data. The data tells you what to fix. You fix it. You ship again.

That cycle is where the learning lives. You cannot get that learning from more reading or more planning.

The plan that doesn't exist yet will be better than the one you've been perfecting for three months. Because the one that doesn't exist yet will be informed by actual feedback.

Start incomplete. You'll complete it through doing.

Pick the smallest version of the offer. Tell three people. See if anyone buys.

That's not a business plan. That's a better business plan than most people execute in a year of "getting ready."
