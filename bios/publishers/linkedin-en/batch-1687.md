Your niche is not a cage. It is a launchpad.

The fear of niching down is the fear of exclusion. If I focus on one type of person, I will miss everyone else.

The reality: when you speak to everyone, you reach no one.

Specificity is what makes your content feel like it was written for someone. Generic content gets scrolled past. Specific content gets saved, shared, and bought.

The tighter your niche, the more likely you are to become the obvious choice for that group. And the obvious choice does not have to compete on price.

Finding your niche is not a one-time decision. It is a process of narrowing until something clicks. Start with what you know, who you enjoy helping, and what problem you can solve better than most.

You will know your niche is right when strangers tag other strangers in your content and say "this is you."

That is the moment your audience starts growing itself.

Broad appeals to everyone. Specific builds a business.

Pick a lane. Own it. Expand from a position of strength later.
