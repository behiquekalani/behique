# LinkedIn Batch 1893

**Post:** Digital products have one advantage that services never will.

You build them once.

A coaching call requires your time every single time. A digital product doesn't. The PDF you write this week can sell while you're asleep, on vacation, or working on something else entirely.

This isn't passive income in the fantasy sense. It requires real work upfront. Research, writing, editing, testing, positioning. You put in real hours before the first sale.

But after that? The economics flip.

Every additional sale costs you almost nothing. Your margin improves as you scale. Your time stays the same while your revenue grows.

Most solopreneurs discover this too late. They spend years trading time for money in a service model, then wonder why they can't get ahead. The calendar is always full but the bank account never grows fast enough.

The shift happens when you start thinking like a publisher, not just a practitioner.

What do you know that others need? What problems have you already solved? What frameworks have you built through years of experience?

That knowledge already exists. It just isn't packaged yet.

Package it once. Sell it repeatedly. That's the model.
