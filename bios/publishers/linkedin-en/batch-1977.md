# LinkedIn Batch 1977

**Post:** The creator economy rewards specificity more than any other quality.

Not perfection. Not production value. Not even frequency.

Specificity.

When you write about a specific problem for a specific person in a specific situation, something clicks for that reader. They feel like you wrote it for them. Because you basically did.

Generic content reaches everyone and connects with no one. Specific content reaches fewer people and converts at a much higher rate.

The fear is always the same: "If I get too specific, I will lose people." But the opposite is true. You lose people by being vague. They scroll past because nothing stuck.

When someone reads your content and thinks "this is exactly my situation," they follow, subscribe, and eventually buy. That moment of recognition is the most valuable thing you can create.

You do not need a million impressions. You need a thousand people who feel like you get them.

Specificity is the shortcut to that.
