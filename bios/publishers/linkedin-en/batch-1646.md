Your audience is not followers. Your audience is people who think the way you think.

The goal of content is not reach. It's resonance. You want the right 500 people to feel like you're writing directly to them, not 50,000 people to scroll past.

This changes how you write. You stop trying to appeal to everyone. You start writing with specific convictions. You share opinions that some people will disagree with, because that's what filters in the people who agree deeply.

Generalist content attracts no one. Specific content attracts your people.

The creator with 3,000 highly engaged followers can outperform someone with 100,000 passive ones. Engagement converts. Passive attention does not.

Build your audience by being more specific, not less. Go deeper into your niche. Use the exact language your ideal reader uses. Reference the problems they actually have.

When someone reads your post and thinks "this is exactly what I needed," you've found them. That's the goal. That's the whole game.

Stop chasing reach. Start earning trust.
