# LinkedIn Batch 1919

**Post:** The thing holding most creators back is not skill. It is distribution.

You can write better than most people in your space. You can think more clearly. You can produce work that is genuinely valuable.

None of it matters if the right people never see it.

Distribution is the unsexy half of building an audience. Creating is the fun part. Getting your work in front of people who do not already know you is the work most people skip.

So they post consistently to an audience of a few hundred, wonder why nothing grows, and eventually quit.

Here is what actually builds reach: putting your content where attention already lives, then giving people a reason to follow you back to your platform.

Comment on larger accounts with something worth saying. Guest write for publications in your space. Cross-post to every platform that makes sense. Build relationships with people who have overlapping audiences.

Growth happens at the edges. Not in the center of your existing community.

This means doing things that do not scale at first. Real conversations. Direct outreach. Showing up in spaces where you are not already known.

Uncomfortable. Necessary. The price of building something real.
