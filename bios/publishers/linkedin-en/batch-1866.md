**Post:**
The most important thing I do every morning is not check email or Slack.

It is protect the first hour.

That hour is when my thinking is clearest. When I can work on things that require depth. When I produce the writing and decisions that actually move my business.

Everything that requires reacting to something else -- that can wait until 10am.

Most people spend their best cognitive hours processing other people's priorities. Then they wonder why they feel busy but unproductive.

The math is simple. One protected hour of deep work per day is five hours per week. Two hundred and sixty hours per year.

That is more than six full work weeks of your best thinking.

Versus spending it on notifications that felt urgent but were not.

You do not need to wake up at 5am. You need to protect whatever hour you wake up fresh.

The work you do in that hour will outlast anything you do in reactive mode.

What would you work on if you protected your first hour starting tomorrow?
