**Post:**
AI didn't make writing easier. It made the bar for real writing higher.

When anyone can generate a decent paragraph in seconds, the value of a genuinely human perspective goes up.

AI output is recognizable. Not always from the words. From the absence of something. There's no blood in it. No actual experience. No moment where you sense the person had to choose to say this specific thing.

That's the gap you should be writing into.

Write about the thing you're afraid to admit. The mistake you're still recovering from. The belief you hold that most people in your field would argue with.

That kind of writing can't be generated. It has to be lived.

AI is a tool. A good one. Use it. But don't let it replace the part of you that has something real to say.

The solopreneurs and creators who will win the next decade are the ones with strong, distinct voices. Not the ones who produce the most content fastest.

Develop your voice. Then protect it.
