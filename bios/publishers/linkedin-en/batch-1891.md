# LinkedIn Batch 1891

**Post:** Most people think they need to grow an audience before they can sell anything.

That's backwards.

You need to sell something to know what your audience actually wants.

Here's what nobody tells you when you're starting out: your first customers will give you better market research than a thousand followers who never buy.

A purchase is a commitment. A follow is not.

When someone gives you money, they're telling you exactly what problem matters enough to solve. That information is worth more than any analytics dashboard.

The solopreneur path starts when you stop building in the dark and start building for someone specific. One person with a real problem. One solution that actually works.

Then you document the result. Then you attract the next person with the same problem.

This is how you build an audience that buys, not one that just watches.

The sequence matters: sell first, validate second, scale third. Most people reverse it and wonder why their product launch flopped after months of work.

You don't need more content. You need your first real customer.

Start there.
