# LinkedIn Post 1722

The best time to build an audience was five years ago. The second best time is today, before your competition figures that out.

Most people treat content like a chore. Post something, hope it lands, forget about it. That's why they stay stuck at 200 followers for three years.

Content is infrastructure. Every post is a node in a network that compounds over time. One post that gets 50,000 views isn't luck. It's the result of 200 posts that trained you to write the one that hit.

The math is simple. Show up consistently, get better every week, and the audience arrives. What kills most creators isn't failure. It's stopping 30 days before the algorithm finally notices them.

Treat your content like a product line, not a diary.
