# LinkedIn Batch 1966

**Post:** The most dangerous phase in a solopreneur's journey is early success.

One good month. A post that goes viral. A product launch that exceeds expectations.

It feels like confirmation that everything is working. And then the next month is average, and the one after that is below average.

Early success creates a reference point that distorts your expectations going forward.

You start comparing every month to the outlier instead of the trend.

The people who build durable businesses understand that results are not linear. They have outlier months, average months, and difficult months.

The skill is staying consistent through all three.

When results are good: stay the course, do not dramatically expand or change everything at once.

When results are average: keep building, stay focused on the fundamentals.

When results are bad: diagnose, do not panic.

The business does not live or die on any single month. It lives or dies on the cumulative quality of your decisions over time.

Most people overreact to both good results and bad results. Both reactions pull them off course.

The most valuable thing you can develop is equanimity. The ability to process outcomes, learn from them, and continue without being destabilized.

That is not a personality trait. It is a practice. You build it by consciously choosing it, especially when the numbers are hard to look at.
