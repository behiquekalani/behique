# LinkedIn Batch 1476

## Post 1
**IDENTITY PRECEDES BEHAVIOR.**

You don't act your way into being a certain kind of person. You become that person and act accordingly.

The writer who publishes consistently first decided they were a writer. The disciplined person first adopted discipline as a core identity value.

Behavior change that doesn't connect to identity is temporary. It's willpower borrowing against a loan that comes due.

Who are you becoming? Not what do you want to achieve. Who are you becoming?

## Post 2
**The thing holding most solopreneurs back isn't the market. It's the ask.**

They create great work. They build real skills. They help people genuinely.

Then they whisper the offer. Apologize for the price. Hope someone notices without being asked.

The ask is part of the work. Selling your services clearly and confidently isn't arrogance. It's respect. For your work and for your audience's ability to make a decision.

When did you last make a direct, confident offer for your work?

## Post 3
**What gets tracked gets improved.**

Not because measurement is magic. Because measurement creates visibility.

You can't improve what you can't see clearly. Revenue, output, time allocation, content performance. Track the things that matter to your goals.

One week of honest tracking usually reveals exactly where the problem is.

What's one number in your business you should be tracking but aren't?
