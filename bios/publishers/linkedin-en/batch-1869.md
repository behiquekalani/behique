# LinkedIn EN Batch 1869

**Post:**
I do not hire for skills. I hire for how someone handles being wrong.

Every skill is learnable. The ability to receive feedback, update your thinking, and keep your ego out of the work -- that is rare.

In a team of one, you have to be honest with yourself about this.

How do you handle it when something you built does not work?

Do you look for reasons it was the market's fault? The algorithm? Bad timing?

Or do you sit with the discomfort and ask what you could have done differently?

The second response is what separates creators who improve fast from ones who plateau.

The market gives you feedback constantly. Every metric is information. Every low-converting offer is telling you something.

You can defend against that feedback or you can use it.

Your skill level six months from now depends almost entirely on which one you choose.

What was the last piece of feedback that you initially resisted but later accepted was right?
