# LinkedIn Batch 1526

## Post 1
**THE ENTREPRENEUR'S GREATEST ENEMY IS COMFORT.**

Once things are good enough, most people stop pushing.
Good enough becomes the ceiling.

The builders who keep growing are the ones who get comfortable being uncomfortable.
They choose the harder path not because it is easy, but because it is the one that leads somewhere.

Comfort is the slow death of potential.

Where have you gotten comfortable in a way that is limiting you?

---

## Post 2
**Digital products are not passive income. They are asynchronous income.**

There is a difference.
Passive implies no work. Asynchronous means the work happens before the sale.

You write the course. You build the template. You design the system.
Then it sells while you sleep, but only because you worked while others were sleeping first.

The "passive" part is the reward for front-loaded effort.

Are you willing to do the front-loaded work that makes the passive part real?

## Post 3
**Niche down until it feels too specific. Then niche down again.**

"I help people with productivity" is invisible.
"I help ADHD founders build systems that do not require willpower" is a magnet.

The narrower your focus, the louder your signal to the people who need you most.
Specificity is not a limitation. It is a precision tool.

If you speak to everyone, you reach no one.

How specific is your current positioning, really?
