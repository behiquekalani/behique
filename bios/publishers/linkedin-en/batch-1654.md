Saying no is a business strategy.

Every yes has a cost. It costs time, energy, attention. Say yes to the wrong thing and you've said no to the right thing without realizing it.

The solo operator who can say no clearly is the one who builds something focused. The one who can't say no ends up doing a little of everything for everyone, and building nothing that compounds.

The hardest no's: the client who pays well but wants everything. The opportunity that sounds good but doesn't fit. The collaboration that looks prestigious but pulls you off course.

The framework: does this move toward the business I'm building or away from it? If the answer is away from it, the answer is no. Even if the money is good. Especially if the money is good, because money is the most persuasive distraction.

Every no that aligns with your direction makes the next no easier. You get a reputation for focus. Focused people attract focused opportunities.

The people who built the solo businesses worth having were not the ones who said yes to everything. They were the ones who knew exactly what they were building and protected it.
