# LinkedIn Batch 1991

**Post:** Most solopreneurs undercharge because they price the product, not the transformation.

A $47 PDF sounds like a lot for a PDF. But a $47 system that saves you 10 hours a week? That's $4.70 per hour. That's nothing.

The product is just the container. The transformation is what people actually buy.

When I first started selling digital products, I described features. Page count. Modules. Templates. Sales were slow.

Then I switched to outcomes. What does this make possible? What stops being a problem? What becomes easier?

Everything changed.

Your customer does not care how long it took you to build the thing. They care about the 3 AM problem it solves. The embarrassment it removes. The goal it makes reachable.

Reprice based on outcome. Rewrite your copy around the after, not the what.

You are not selling a PDF. You are selling the version of someone's life where that problem no longer exists.

Price accordingly.
