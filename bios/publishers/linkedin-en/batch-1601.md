# LinkedIn Post — batch-1601

Most people price their work based on time.
That is the wrong metric.

If a client earns $50,000 from your advice, charging $500 because it "only took an hour" is not humility. It is math failure.

Price based on outcome, not effort. The hour you spent solving their problem represents years of experience compressed into a conversation. You are not selling time. You are selling the result of knowing what to do and when to do it.

Solo builders get this wrong early and it costs them years. They discount themselves into exhaustion, take on clients who do not respect their work, and wonder why the business feels so hard.

The fix is simple but uncomfortable. Raise the number. Test the reaction. If nobody flinches, raise it again.

Your pricing is a signal. A low price does not attract better clients. It attracts people who want more for less.

You built the skill. Charge for what it produces.
