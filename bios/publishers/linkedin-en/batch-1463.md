# LinkedIn Batch 1463

## Post 1
**YOUR PRICE IS A SIGNAL.**

Low prices attract people who want cheap. That's not a customer base. That's a trap.

Raise your price and something interesting happens. Better clients appear. They respect your time. They implement your advice. They refer other good clients.

The math of premium pricing isn't just financial. It's operational. You do better work when you work with better people.

What would change about your business if you doubled your price tomorrow?

---

## Post 2
**The gap between who you are and who you want to be is a skills gap. Not a luck gap.**

Most people wait for the right moment, the right opportunity, the right connection.

Builders close the gap by learning what's required. Then doing it. Repeatedly. Without waiting for permission.

Luck finds people who are already in motion.

What skill would change everything for you if you mastered it this year?

---

## Post 3
**Comparison is the default setting. It's also the thing that kills momentum.**

You look at someone three years ahead of you and feel behind. But you're not behind. You're just earlier.

The only useful comparison is to who you were last month. Is the work better? Is the thinking clearer? Are you solving harder problems?

That's the only scoreboard that matters.

What's one thing you can do today that your past self couldn't?
