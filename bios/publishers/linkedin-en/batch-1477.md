# LinkedIn Batch 1477

## Post 1
**MOST PIVOTS AREN'T STRATEGIC. THEY'RE IMPATIENCE.**

When results don't come fast enough, the instinct is to change direction. Try something new. Start over.

But sometimes the thing you're doing is right and you just haven't done it long enough.

The skill is learning to tell the difference between a real signal to change and impatience dressed as strategy.

A real signal: you've done the thing consistently for months with zero traction and honest feedback confirms the problem.

Impatience: you tried it for three weeks and it felt slow.

How long have you actually given your current approach?

## Post 2
**The best teachers aren't the ones who know the most. They're the ones who remember what it's like to not know.**

If you've been doing something for years, you have expertise. But you've also forgotten what confused you at the beginning.

The most valuable content often comes from the person who just figured something out. Because they remember every obstacle.

What did you figure out this year that confused you when you started?

## Post 3
**Revenue is a lagging indicator. Activity is the leading one.**

If you want to predict future revenue, don't look at this month's numbers. Look at this month's outreach, publishing, conversations, and offers made.

The work you do now shows up in income 30-90 days from now.

Plant now. Harvest later. Don't confuse the lag for failure.

What activity, if done consistently for 60 days, would most likely move your revenue?
