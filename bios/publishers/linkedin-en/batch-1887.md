# LinkedIn EN Batch 1887

**Post:**
The best content isn't about what's trending. It's about what's true.

And truth is specific.

"Work smarter not harder" is not true. It's a placeholder. It says nothing.

"I stopped checking email until 11am and my creative output doubled" is true. It's specific. You can test it.

Generic advice is everywhere. Specific, tested, personal insights are scarce.

When you write about something you've actually done, something you've failed at and learned from, something that changed your results, that's real content. People can feel the difference.

They share specifics. They screenshot specifics. They remember specifics.

You don't need to be famous. You don't need a huge audience. You just need to be specific and honest about your actual experience.

What happened this week that taught you something? Write about that. What did you try that didn't work the way you expected? Write about that.

Your real story is more valuable than borrowed wisdom.

Start there.
