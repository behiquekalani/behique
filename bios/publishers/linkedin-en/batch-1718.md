Most people overcomplicate their business to avoid the simple work.

The simple work is talking to potential customers. Writing clearly. Making offers. Following up.

Nobody wants to do that. So they redesign the website, rebuild the funnel, rename the product, and change the logo. Activity that feels productive but produces nothing.

The business that works looks boring from the outside. Same offer. Same audience. Same message. Repeated for months until the market responds.

Boring consistency beats clever novelty every time.

If your business isn't working, the answer is rarely a new strategy. The answer is usually more reps with the current strategy.

How many times have you made the offer this month? How many people did you follow up with? How many pieces of content did you actually ship?

Be honest with the numbers. They tell the real story.

Complexity is often avoidance dressed up in business language. The person who keeps reinventing their offer hasn't actually made enough offers yet.

Do the simple thing. Make the offer. Write the post. Send the email. Have the conversation.

Do it more times than feels comfortable.

That's the whole strategy.
