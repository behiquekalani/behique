# LinkedIn Batch 1992

**Post:** The biggest lie in online business is that you need a big audience to make money.

You need a specific audience. There is a difference.

10,000 followers who found you by accident will never buy anything. 300 people who follow you because you solve their exact problem will buy everything.

Niche down until it feels uncomfortable. Then niche down one more time.

I know creators with 500 followers doing $5K months. I know accounts with 100K followers making nothing. The math is not about volume. It is about relevance.

Ask yourself: does my audience know exactly what I help them do? Can they describe it to a friend in one sentence?

If the answer is no, the content is too broad. You are attracting everyone, which means you are attracting no one with a specific problem and money to solve it.

Specificity is not limiting. It is how you become irreplaceable.

Small and specific beats big and vague every single time.
