Digital products are not passive income.

They are upfront work that, if done right, generates income without proportional time input later.

That distinction matters. Passive implies no effort. What you're actually building is a system where the effort is front-loaded.

You spend 40 hours creating something once. Then it sells while you sleep, while you're building the next thing, while you're eating dinner with your family.

That's not passive. That's asymmetric.

The people who get this build differently. They're not trying to create a product for every customer. They're trying to create one product that serves many customers from the same file.

The economics change completely.

One template, one course, one guide. Built once. Sold to ten people, a hundred people, a thousand people. Same file, same delivery, zero marginal cost per unit.

This is why digital products are the most accessible business model for a solo operator. You don't need a team. You don't need inventory. You need a clear point of view and the discipline to package it.

Do the upfront work. Build the asset. Let it run.
