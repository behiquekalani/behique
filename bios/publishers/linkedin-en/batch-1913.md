# LinkedIn Batch 1913

**Post:** Your niche is not a box. It is a door.

People resist niching down because they think it limits them. If I focus on one thing, I lose everyone else.

The reality is the opposite.

Generalists are invisible online. You scroll past them because nothing about their message is for you specifically. It speaks to everyone, so it connects with no one.

When you go specific, you become magnetic to a specific type of person. That person tells others like them. You build a reputation in a focused space instead of a faint presence in a broad one.

And here is what most people miss: specificity does not trap you. It earns you the credibility to expand later.

Nobody knows Tim Ferriss as the random-topic guy. They know him for 4-hour workweeks, and that trust opened every other door.

Pick the one problem you understand deeply. The one transformation you have either lived or helped others achieve. Build around that.

When you own a corner of someone's mind, you have real estate that compounds over time.

Start specific. Earn trust. Expand when the audience asks you to.

That is the path. It is less sexy than staying broad but it actually works.
