# LinkedIn EN Batch 1859

**Post:**
Pricing your digital product at $7 is not humble. It is a mistake.

I priced my first product at $9 because I was scared. I thought lower price meant more buyers.

What I got was buyers who did not take the product seriously. Who downloaded it and never opened it. Who never got results and therefore never told anyone about it.

Higher prices do the opposite of what you think.

They filter for buyers who are serious.
They create investment that drives action.
They allow you to spend more on acquiring customers.
They signal quality before the customer even opens the product.

The customer who pays $97 will read every word. Will implement. Will get results. Will leave a review and recommend it to a friend.

The $7 customer is just collecting things.

Price for the transformation, not the document. What would it be worth if your buyer actually used it?

Start there and work backward.

What would getting a real result from your product be worth to your ideal customer?
