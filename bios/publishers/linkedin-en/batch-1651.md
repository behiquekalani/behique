Most people treat their niche like a constraint. It's actually a compass.

The fear of niching is the fear of saying no. If I focus on one thing, I'll miss everything else. That logic sounds reasonable and produces mediocre results.

The reality: the narrower your focus, the deeper your expertise. The deeper your expertise, the more valuable your work. The more valuable your work, the higher your prices. The higher your prices, the fewer clients you need.

A generalist needs 20 clients to make what a specialist makes with 5. Which one has more time to do excellent work?

Niching is not permanent. You can expand later. But expansion built on depth creates real leverage. Expansion built on nothing creates noise.

Pick the intersection of: what you're genuinely good at, what people will pay for, and what you don't mind doing repeatedly.

That intersection is your niche. It's not a box. It's a launchpad.

The solo operators who break through are almost always the ones who went deeper instead of broader when it mattered.

Go deep first. Width is a reward for depth, not a substitute for it.
