# LinkedIn Batch 1455

## Post 1
**CONSISTENCY IS THE MOAT MOST PEOPLE CANNOT CROSS.**

It is not expensive. It does not require talent. It just requires showing up when you do not feel like it. Most creators fail here. Not because they lack skill. Because they underestimate how long it takes.

One year of consistent output on one platform changes your trajectory. Two years is almost unfair. Three years puts you in a different category entirely.

How long have you been consistent on your chosen platform?

---

## Post 2
**Your content is a long game. Play it like one.**

Short-form metrics are addictive and misleading. Likes and views feel real but they are not the game. The game is: does this content build trust with the right person over a long period of time?

Optimize for depth of connection, not breadth of reach. One person who buys everything is worth more than 10,000 who scroll past.

Are you building for viral moments or for long-term trust?

---

## Post 3
**The creator who serves 1,000 people deeply will outlast the one who serves 1,000,000 people shallowly.**

Depth of relationship is the real moat. It is what keeps people subscribed when they could unsubscribe. What makes them recommend you when they could recommend anyone. What makes them buy without needing to be convinced.

Build a reputation for genuinely changing the lives of a small number of people. Scale is optional. Impact is not.

What would your most loyal reader or customer say about the difference you made?
