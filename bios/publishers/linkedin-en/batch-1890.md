**Post:**
Three years ago I thought I needed more time. Turns out I needed fewer priorities.

Time is fixed. Priorities are a choice.

The people who get more done in less time aren't working more efficiently across ten projects. They've eliminated eight of the projects and gone deep on two.

Focus is a form of subtraction.

Every yes to one thing is a no to everything else. Most people don't feel that trade-off. They say yes repeatedly and wonder why nothing moves.

I keep a "not doing" list. Things I've decided not to work on this quarter. It's as important as my task list. Because without it, everything feels urgent.

What are you doing right now that doesn't belong in this season of your business? Not eventually. Not someday. Right now, this quarter.

Make the list. Then actually stop doing those things.

Your output on the remaining priorities will surprise you.

Subtraction is a strategy. Use it.
