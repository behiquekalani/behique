# LinkedIn Batch 1559

## Post 1
**SPECIFICITY SELLS. GENERALITY BORES.**

"I help people improve their lives" is not a pitch. It is a statement so broad it means nothing.

"I help first-time freelancers land their first three clients in 90 days" is a pitch. It is specific, measurable, and relevant to a real person with a real problem.

The more specific you get, the more the right people lean in.

How specific is your current offer or message?

## Post 2
**The version of yourself six months from now is a direct result of what you do consistently today.**

Not occasionally. Not when motivated. Consistently.

The gap between who you are and who you want to be closes through daily action, not massive effort.

Small, consistent beats large and sporadic every time over a long enough timeline.

What small action, done daily, would have the biggest impact on your life in six months?

## Post 3
**Reading without applying is just entertainment with extra steps.**

Every book you finish and do not use is just a story you told yourself about being productive.

The useful thing is to read one chapter, extract one principle, and test it in your work this week.

Less input. More testing. More learning that actually sticks.

What is the last thing you read that you have actually applied?
