# LinkedIn Batch 1942

**Post:** The one-person business model is the best business model of this decade.

Zero overhead. No office. No payroll. No investors to answer to.

Just one person with a clear skill, a clear audience, and a clear offer.

This model was not viable fifteen years ago. Distribution was expensive. Reaching an audience required capital. Now, the only thing standing between you and a global audience is a decision and a laptop.

What makes it hard is not the mechanics. It is the psychology.

Most people were trained to work inside systems other people built. They know how to follow a process. They do not know how to design one.

The one-person business requires you to be the operator and the architect simultaneously. You build the system while running inside it.

This is uncomfortable. It is also what makes it scalable without employees.

When the systems are yours, improvements compound in your favor. Every efficiency you add stays yours. Every asset you build belongs to you.

You are not building equity for someone else. You are building it for yourself and your family.

The barriers to entry are low. The barriers to staying in are psychological. That gap is exactly where the opportunity lives.

Build for three years. Then look at what you have.
