**Post:** The niche question is the one that paralyzes more beginners than anything else.

"What should I focus on?"

Here's a way to think about it that actually works: don't pick a topic, pick a problem.

Topics are vague. Problems are specific. "Marketing" is a topic. "Getting your first 100 email subscribers without paid ads" is a problem. One of these can build a business. The other can't.

People pay to solve problems, not to learn topics.

When you orient your entire offer around a specific, painful problem that a specific kind of person has, the niche finds itself. The content writes itself. The product ideas come naturally.

The deeper you understand the problem, the less you need to think about strategy.

You don't need the perfect niche. You need a real problem that real people are willing to pay to solve, and you need to be someone who has either solved it or can credibly help them solve it.

That's it. That's the whole thing.

Stop optimizing the niche. Start obsessing over the problem. The rest follows.
