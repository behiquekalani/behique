# LinkedIn Batch 1972

**Post:** The hardest part of selling online is not writing copy.

It is believing the thing you built is worth paying for.

Most creators underprice because deep down they are not sure anyone will buy. They set a low price to soften the rejection. If nobody buys, at least it was cheap.

But here is what low prices actually communicate: "I made this and I'm not sure it's good."

Buyers read that signal. They pass.

When you raise your price and commit to it, something different happens. You stop apologizing for your offer. You explain it with clarity and conviction. You attract buyers who take it seriously because you took it seriously first.

Price is not just math. It is a statement about your own belief in what you made.

If you cannot defend your price to a skeptical buyer, the problem is not the price. It is that you have not convinced yourself yet.

Do the inner work. Then set the number.
