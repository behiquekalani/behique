# LinkedIn Batch 1472

## Post 1
**TRUST IS BUILT IN SMALL MOMENTS, NOT BIG GESTURES.**

Every time you deliver what you promised, on time, without excuses, you make a deposit.

Every time you over-explain a delay, every time you miss a deadline, every time expectations aren't met, you make a withdrawal.

Most people focus on big impressive moments. The real work is in the small consistent ones that nobody posts about.

Think about the last three things you promised someone. Did you deliver?

## Post 2
**Your first offer doesn't need to be perfect. It needs to exist.**

The feedback you get from a real customer is worth more than six months of planning.

Real customers tell you what they actually value. What confused them. What they would have paid more for. What they didn't need at all.

Build the minimum thing. Sell it. Listen hard. Improve.

That cycle beats any strategy doc written in isolation.

What would the smallest, most direct version of your current offer look like?

## Post 3
**The people who seem to have it all figured out usually just started earlier and failed more.**

Expertise isn't a personality trait. It's accumulated reps.

The difference between a beginner and an expert is mostly failed attempts and the willingness to keep going anyway.

There's no shortcut around the reps. But you can start them today.

What are you waiting to start because you don't feel ready yet?
