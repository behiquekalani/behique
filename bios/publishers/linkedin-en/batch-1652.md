The best time to start a newsletter was two years ago. The second best time is now.

Email is the only distribution channel you actually own. Social platforms can change the algorithm, limit reach, or disappear entirely. Your email list goes with you.

Every follower you have is rented. Every subscriber is owned.

The business case is simple. Email converts at 3-5x the rate of social media. A list of 1,000 engaged subscribers is more valuable than 10,000 passive followers on any platform.

You don't need a big list to start. You don't need a perfect format. You need to send something useful, consistently.

What to write about: whatever you know that your ideal reader doesn't. Insights from your work. Frameworks you use. Problems you solved. Mistakes you made and what you learned.

Send it weekly. Keep it under 500 words to start. Be honest. Be specific.

The list compounds. Year one might be slow. Year two, you have something real. Year three, you have an asset.

Most people never start because they don't think they have enough to say. Start anyway. You'll find your voice by using it.
