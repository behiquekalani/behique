Digital products are the closest thing to a perfect business model for a solo operator.

You build it once. You sell it repeatedly. You do not trade time for every dollar.

Compare that to consulting: you solve the same problem for different clients, billing hours, capped by how many hours exist in a week. The income ceiling is physical.

A digital product removes the ceiling. A $97 PDF, course, or template sold to 500 people this year earns the same as a single high-ticket client, without the calls, without the revisions, without the relationship management.

The objection is always "but there's so much competition." Yes. There is competition in every category. Competition means there is a market. Your job is not to eliminate competition but to be specific enough that you are the obvious choice for a particular person with a particular problem.

Niche down. Build something for someone specific. Price it honestly. Write about the problem it solves.

That is the whole model. Complicated execution, simple strategy.
