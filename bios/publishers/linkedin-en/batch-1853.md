**Post:**
Digital products are not passive income. They are upfront work that becomes mostly passive later.

The distinction matters because a lot of people quit during the upfront part thinking they are doing something wrong.

Building the product: active.
Writing the sales page: active.
Setting up the funnel: active.
Running traffic: active.
Answering early customer questions: active.

None of that is passive. All of it is necessary.

But once the machine is built and validated, you can sell the same PDF a thousand times without doing a thousand times more work.

That asymmetry is the point.

Most people never get there because they expect passivity too early. They build something, launch it, make three sales, and call it a failure.

Three sales is not failure. Three sales is proof of concept.

Build from there. Improve the copy. Expand the audience. Refine the offer.

The passive part comes after the grind part. Not instead of it.
