The best investment you can make is in skills that compound.

Not stocks. Not crypto. Not real estate. Those are good investments. But skills pay dividends that no market can take away.

A skill you develop at 22 still works at 42. Writing. Sales. Systems thinking. Code. Design. Each one increases your earning capacity permanently. Stack a few together and you become someone who can build from nothing.

The compounding works like this: the more you know, the faster you learn. The faster you learn, the quicker you ship. The quicker you ship, the more you iterate. The more you iterate, the better you get. There's no ceiling.

The skills worth investing in have a few things in common. They're hard to automate fully. They transfer across industries. They make every other skill more effective. Writing is the clearest example. Writing makes you better at sales, teaching, product design, and thinking.

Most people invest in credentials. Credentials are tickets to games that are already crowded. Skills are tickets to games you can create yourself.

Spend 30 minutes a day on one skill that you don't get paid for yet. In two years, you'll get paid for it.
