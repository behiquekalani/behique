**Post:**
Nobody talks about the mental cost of running a one-person business.

Not the money. Not the strategy. The mental cost.

Every decision lands on you. The pricing, the offers, the platform, the tech stack, the content calendar. There's no committee. No one to defer to. That freedom is real, but it's also heavy.

The builders who last learn to make decisions fast and move on. They don't revisit every choice a hundred times. They pick, act, and adjust.

The ones who burn out are usually the ones who can't stop second-guessing themselves.

Two things help me: writing decisions down before I make them, and setting a review date instead of re-evaluating constantly. I decide now, check results in 30 days, and don't touch it until then.

You cannot run a lean business with a bloated mind.

Protect your decision-making energy like it's your most valuable resource. Because it is.
