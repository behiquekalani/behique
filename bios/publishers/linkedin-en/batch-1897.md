**Post:** The best business model for a solopreneur is the one you'll actually run.

Not the one with the best margins on paper. Not the one your favorite influencer is promoting. The one that fits how you actually work, what you're genuinely good at, and what you can sustain without resenting.

I've seen people build email newsletters when they hate writing. Build coaching businesses when they hate calls. Build courses when they hate teaching.

They built what they thought they should, not what they were actually suited for.

The market rewards quality and consistency. Quality and consistency require that you enjoy the work enough to keep doing it when results are slow.

Before you pick a business model, ask: Can I do this every week for two years without burning out? If the answer is maybe or I'll push through it, keep looking.

Your best work comes from the intersection of what the market needs and what you can deliver repeatedly without it destroying you.

There's a version of this that feels like play. Find that version. Build that business.
