Everyone talks about finding your niche. Fewer people talk about going deep enough in it to actually matter.

Shallow niching looks like: I help entrepreneurs with marketing.

Deep niching looks like: I help B2B SaaS founders write email sequences that convert free trial users to paid plans.

The second version is uncomfortable to say out loud because it feels like it excludes too many people. That discomfort is the signal you are on the right track.

Specificity creates trust faster than any amount of social proof. When someone reads your positioning and thinks "this is exactly my situation," they do not shop around. They reach out.

The fear is that going narrow will shrink your market. The reality is that going narrow will shrink your competition and increase your conversion rate at the same time.

You can always expand later once you own a specific space. It is much harder to go narrow after you have been broad for years.

Depth beats breadth in the solo business world, almost every time.

How specific is your current positioning? Be honest about whether it actually filters in the right people.
