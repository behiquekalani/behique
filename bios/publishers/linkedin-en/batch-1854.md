**Post:**
Your niche is not a topic. It is a perspective.

Two people can write about productivity every day. One builds an audience of thousands. The other gets ignored.

Same topic. Different results.

The difference is almost never the topic. It is the angle.

The person building an audience is not writing about productivity. They are writing about productivity for ex-corporate employees who left their job at 35 and now have no structure.

Or productivity for creative people who hate rigid systems.

Or productivity for parents with three kids and exactly forty-five minutes per day to work on something that matters.

Specificity is not limiting. It is clarifying.

When you write to everyone, you resonate with no one. When you write to a specific person in a specific situation, that person feels seen.

And they share your content with five other people in the same situation.

Your niche is not what you talk about. It is who you are talking to, and why they need to hear it from someone who understands their exact context.

Narrow it down. Then narrow it down again.

What is one layer of specificity you could add to your current niche?
