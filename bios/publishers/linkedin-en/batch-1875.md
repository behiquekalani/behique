# LinkedIn EN Batch 1875

**Post:**
The biggest lie in creator content is that you need a niche before you start.

You don't. You need to start before you find your niche.

Most people find their angle through repetition. You post twenty things and notice three keep performing. That's your direction. But you couldn't have known that from the outside.

Waiting for clarity before you ship is like waiting to feel confident before you do the scary thing. Confidence comes after action, not before. Clarity comes after reps, not before.

The niche finds you if you're paying attention.

Start broad. Start messy. Start with what genuinely interests you today. Over time, the through-line becomes obvious.

The creators who've been around five-plus years all say the same thing: their current niche wasn't planned. It emerged.

Stop planning your creative identity. Go create things and let it emerge from real output.

The plan is the reps.
