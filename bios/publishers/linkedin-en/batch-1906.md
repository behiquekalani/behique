**Post:** The worst business advice I ever followed: build it and they will come.

They won't. You have to go to them.

Distribution is the part of business school that doesn't get enough attention. You can have the best product in your category and still lose to someone with a worse product and a better distribution strategy.

This is the lesson that separates the solopreneurs who grow from the ones who stay stuck wondering why quality isn't enough.

Quality is the price of entry. Distribution is the game.

Distribution means: who sees your work, how often, and through what channels? A great product nobody hears about is a failed product.

When you're starting out, your first distribution strategy is showing up in the same places your ideal customers already are. Not building a new platform. Going to the existing ones.

Comment on the posts they read. Write in the communities they're in. Partner with the newsletters they subscribe to.

Get in front of them before you worry about building something for them to come to.

Build distribution first. Then build the destination.
