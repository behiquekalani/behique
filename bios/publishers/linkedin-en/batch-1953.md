# LinkedIn Batch 1953

**Post:** Consistency is not about showing up every day. It is about showing up often enough that people remember you exist.

Most creators burn out chasing daily output. They set unsustainable schedules and then feel like failures when life interrupts.

The real goal is presence over time, not volume in a week.

A creator who publishes three times a week for two years will outperform someone who posts daily for three months and then disappears.

The math favors the sustainable pace, every time.

Here is what consistency actually requires:

A system that works when you are tired. A schedule that survives a difficult week. A process that does not depend on peak motivation.

If your content plan only works when you feel inspired, it is not a plan. It is a hope.

Design for your average day, not your best day.

What can you produce when you are not feeling it? Build your baseline around that. Let the good days be bonuses.

The creators who build real audiences are not the most talented ones. They are the ones who kept showing up after the novelty wore off.

Talent gets you started. Systems keep you going.
