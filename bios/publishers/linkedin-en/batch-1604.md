# LinkedIn Post — batch-1604

An audience is not a vanity metric. It is infrastructure.

People confuse followers with fame. They are not the same thing. A small, focused audience of the right people is worth more than a large audience that does not care about what you sell.

1,000 people who trust you will buy your product.
100,000 people who followed you for memes will not.

Build an audience around a specific problem you solve. Write about that problem consistently. Show your thinking, not just your results. People do not follow profiles. They follow perspectives.

When you have an audience that trusts you, distribution is solved. You do not need to pay for attention. You do not need to beg platforms for reach. You have a direct line to people who want what you build.

This takes longer than running ads. It compounds in ways ads never do.

The builder with 5,000 loyal readers and a $500 product has a better business than the one with 50,000 cold followers and a discount code.

Depth over reach. Always.
