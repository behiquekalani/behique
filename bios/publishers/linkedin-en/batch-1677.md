The most profitable thing you can do for your solo business is get clear on who you're not for.

Most people try to appeal to everyone. That's not inclusivity. That's cowardice.

When you try to reach everyone, your message reaches no one with force. It lands soft. It gets scrolled past. It doesn't make anyone feel like you're talking directly to them.

The narrower your audience definition, the harder your message lands. "Freelance designers who want to raise their rates" is ten times more powerful than "anyone who works for themselves."

The people outside your niche won't buy from you anyway. The people inside it will feel like you read their mind.

Write for one person. Describe their specific problem. Name what they've already tried that hasn't worked. Tell them what they should do instead.

That post will perform better than any generic "tips for success" post you've ever written.
