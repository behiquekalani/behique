# LinkedIn Post — batch-1595

An audience is not a vanity metric when you use it correctly.

Followers who trust you are a distribution channel for every product you build, every service you offer, every idea you want to test.

But most people build the wrong audience. They optimize for volume instead of alignment. They chase broad reach instead of deep resonance with the right people.

A smaller audience that is genuinely interested in your specific work is worth more than a large audience that follows you for entertainment.

Build for alignment. Speak directly to the person who has the problem you solve. Be specific enough that the right people feel like you are reading their mind.

Those people share your content. They buy your products. They refer others who are similar.

That is how an audience compounds. Not through viral moments, but through consistent relevance to a specific person over time.

Define your person. Write for them every day.

A thousand true readers are worth more than a hundred thousand passive ones.
