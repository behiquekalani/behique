# LinkedIn Post 1754

Validation does not come before the build. It comes after.

Every founder who says they are "validating" their idea before building is often just delaying. Real validation is a sale. Real validation is someone handing you money. Surveys, interest lists, and "would you buy this?" conversations are noise.

Build the smallest possible version of the thing. Put it in front of people who match your target. If they pay, it is validated. If they don't, you have real feedback. Either way you learn more than six months of pre-launch research.

Build small. Validate with cash.
