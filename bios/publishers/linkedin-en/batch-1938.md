# LinkedIn Batch 1938

**Post:** Pricing tells people what you think of your own work.

Low prices signal low confidence, not accessibility.

I have watched creators halve their price trying to get more buyers, then watch sales stay flat. Lower price did not fix the problem. The problem was unclear value.

When you cannot articulate exactly what someone gets and exactly how it changes their situation, no price feels justified to the buyer. So they say no. You lower the price. They still say no.

The fix is not the number. The fix is the clarity of the outcome.

Tell me what my life looks like after I buy this. Tell me specifically. Then charge what that outcome is worth.

Charging more also changes how buyers show up. A person who pays $200 for your course reads every page. A person who pays $7 does not even open it.

Premium pricing filters for serious buyers, which means better outcomes, better testimonials, and better retention.

The math also works differently. Fifteen sales at $200 beats 300 sales at $7 in every way that matters.

Price for the value delivered. Start higher than feels comfortable. Work down only if the offer genuinely needs refinement.

Most offers need better positioning, not a discount.
