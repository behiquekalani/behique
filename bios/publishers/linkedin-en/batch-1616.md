# LinkedIn Post — batch-1616

The best business advice I ignored for too long: stop solving problems you do not have yet.

Early stage builders spend enormous energy on scaling, on hiring, on systems for volume they do not have yet. While ignoring the actual problem: not enough people know the offer exists.

The sequence matters.

Step one: does someone want this? Step two: can you deliver it reliably? Step three: can you make it findable? Step four: can you make it scalable?

Most people jump to step four while ignoring step one.

Validation comes first. A landing page with ten sign-ups tells you more than a fully built product with zero buyers. Talking to five potential customers in a week tells you more than a hundred surveys.

The instinct to build before validating is understandable. Building feels productive. Validating feels uncertain. But building the wrong thing productively is not progress.

Talk to people first. Build what they actually say they need. Deliver it. Then systematize.

Do not automate chaos. Solve the problem first.
