# LinkedIn Batch 1974

**Post:** The best business advice I ever got was also the most boring.

Pick one person. Solve one problem. Sell one thing.

Not five revenue streams. Not a full product suite. Not a brand that speaks to everyone.

One.

The pressure to diversify early comes from fear, not strategy. Fear that one thing will not be enough. Fear that one audience is too small. Fear that one offer limits you.

But diversifying before you have depth is just spreading thin. You end up with five mediocre things instead of one great one.

The creators and solopreneurs who grow fast are almost always the ones who went uncomfortably narrow first. They built something specific for someone specific and sold it well. Then they expanded from that base of trust.

Narrow is not a constraint. Narrow is the strategy.

The question is not "what can I offer?" It is "what can I become known for first?"

Start there. Build depth. The rest comes later.
