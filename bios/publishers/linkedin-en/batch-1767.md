# LinkedIn Post 1767

**The people who say "I don't have time" usually have a prioritization problem, not a time problem.**

168 hours in a week. Same for everyone. The difference isn't the clock. It's what gets scheduled first.

When you say you don't have time to build, what you're actually saying is something else always wins the negotiation. That something else usually isn't more important. It's just louder.

Time blocks for your real work don't happen by accident. They get carved out deliberately, often at the expense of comfort or convenience.

One hour a day before the rest of the world wakes up. That's 365 hours a year. That's enough to build something real.

Your future self lives in the margins you create today.
