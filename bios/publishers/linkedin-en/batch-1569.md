Pricing is not a math problem. It's a positioning problem.

Most solo operators underprice because they're comparing themselves to the market. They look at what others charge and pick a number nearby.

That approach assumes you're interchangeable with every other option. You're not. If you are, that's the real problem.

The question is not "what will someone pay for this?" The question is "what outcome does this deliver and who cares enough about that outcome to pay well for it?"

When you charge low, you attract people who make decisions based on price. Those are the hardest customers to serve. They question everything. They ask for refunds. They don't value the result because they barely valued the investment.

When you charge appropriately, you attract people who care about the outcome. They're invested. They implement what you give them. They get results. They refer others.

Price is also a signal of your own belief in the work. If you don't charge enough to feel good about delivering it, that energy shows.

Raise the price. Improve the offer to match. Attract people who want the outcome, not the discount.

That's a better business and a better client relationship.
