# LinkedIn Batch 1987

**Post:** The biggest lie in online business is that you need a big audience to make money.

You do not.

You need a small audience that trusts you and has a specific problem you can solve.

Five hundred people who would buy everything you make is a better business than fifty thousand followers who will never spend a dollar.

The math works. Sell a $97 product to 1% of a five-hundred-person email list and you made $485. Do that once a month and you have a five-figure year.

Most people see those numbers and think small. But small and sustainable beats big and chaotic every time.

And here is the real truth: you are more likely to build the deep trust required for consistent sales with a small, specific audience than a broad, passive one.

Big audiences are hard to build and easy to lose. Small, loyal ones compound quietly.

Stop chasing followers. Start deepening relationships with the people already paying attention.

That is where the business actually lives.
