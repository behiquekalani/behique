# LinkedIn Post 1836

Most people never build the thing they imagine because they are waiting to feel ready.

Ready is a feeling that never arrives. It is the brain's way of avoiding the discomfort of being seen before you are polished. The problem is, polished only comes from being seen before you are ready.

The sequence is backwards for most people. They think: get ready, then ship. The actual sequence is: ship, learn, adjust, repeat. The feedback loop only starts when something is in the world.

Ship before ready. That is the job.
