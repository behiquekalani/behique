# LinkedIn Post 1738

One-person businesses are not a trend. They are a structural shift in how value gets created.

The cost of starting a business in 2026 is near zero. Tools that used to require a team of five now run on one laptop. Distribution that used to require a marketing department now runs through a personal account.

The companies that were built on the assumption that more headcount means more output are going to struggle explaining that logic to the next generation of builders.

The solo operator is not an anomaly. They're a preview of how work gets done when the tools finally catch up to the individual.

You don't need permission to build. You need a problem, a solution, and the will to ship.
