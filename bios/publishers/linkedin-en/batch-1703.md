Digital products are not passive income. They are deferred income from active work.

Someone has to write the guide. Build the template. Record the course. Package the knowledge. That work is real and takes real time.

But here's the difference: you do it once.

A client project pays once. A digital product pays every time someone buys it. Same knowledge. Completely different economics.

The mistake most people make is building the product without building the audience. So they launch to silence. Not because the product is bad. Because nobody knows it exists.

The sequence matters. Audience first. Trust second. Product third. That order is not optional.

You build an audience by sharing what you know before you charge for it. Not all of it. The insight, the framework, the point of view. The depth lives in the product.

If you have specific knowledge, you already have product material. The knowledge is already there. The question is whether you've packaged it in a way someone can buy.

Most haven't. That's the gap worth closing.
