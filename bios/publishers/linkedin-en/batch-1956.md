# LinkedIn Batch 1956

**Post:** Pricing is the most underleveraged tool in a solopreneur's business.

Most people treat it like a fixed variable. They pick a number that feels fair and leave it there for years.

But pricing is communication. It tells the market who you are, who you serve, and how much you value what you do.

Low prices do not attract more clients. They attract more skepticism.

When something is priced too low, people assume there is a catch. Or that the quality matches the price. Or that you are desperate.

None of those associations build trust.

Raising your prices is not about greed. It is about positioning yourself where the clients who value quality actually shop.

The client who haggles over a $200 service will be your most demanding and least satisfied. The client who pays $2,000 for the same outcome tends to trust you more, respect your time, and refer others.

Higher prices attract better clients. That is not a coincidence.

You do not have to make a massive leap. Raise by 20%, see what happens. If demand holds, raise again.

The ceiling is almost always higher than you think it is.

What you charge shapes how people perceive you before they ever talk to you.

Price accordingly.
