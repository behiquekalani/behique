# LinkedIn Post — batch-1599

The one-person business is not for everyone. It requires something most people are not willing to do.

Decide.

When you work for someone else, decisions are made for you. What to build, how to price it, who to target. You execute. Someone else decides.

In a solo business, you decide everything. And every wrong decision is yours to learn from.

Most people avoid this. The weight of full accountability is uncomfortable. It is easier to have a boss to blame, a team to diffuse responsibility across.

The solopreneur who succeeds is not the smartest or the most talented. It is the one who makes decisions quickly, executes on them, and updates when wrong.

Speed of learning depends on speed of deciding. You cannot learn from a decision you have not made.

Build the skill of deciding. Set a time limit for how long you sit with a choice. Make the call. Move.

Wrong decisions that you learn from are worth more than perfect decisions you never make.
