**Post:**
Building a business from a laptop in a place no one has heard of taught me something important about location.

Location is a constraint. Creativity is the response to constraints.

Being outside a major hub means you do not have the networking advantages, the casual industry connections, the serendipitous coffee shop encounters.

But it also means you are not distracted by the performance of being in the scene. You are forced to build something real because you have nothing else to rely on.

The internet dissolved most location disadvantages. What it did not dissolve is the mindset that location is the problem.

The problem is never location. The problem is waiting for better circumstances instead of building with what you have.

I have seen people in New York build nothing and people in small towns build six-figure businesses.

The common variable is not the city. It is the decision to start.

Where you are is fine. What matters is what you do from there.

What are you using as a reason to wait that you could flip into an advantage?
