# LinkedIn Batch 1907

**Post:** One underrated skill for solopreneurs: knowing when to quit something.

Not give up. Quit.

There's a difference. Giving up is abandoning something because it got hard. Quitting is making a deliberate decision that your time is better spent elsewhere based on evidence.

Most people in the online business world are too afraid to quit things publicly. They treat every pivot as a failure instead of a data point. So they drag along projects that stopped making sense months ago, burning time and energy that could go toward something better.

Your time is finite. Attention is the real resource. Every hour on something that isn't working is an hour you're not spending on something that could.

The smart move is to set clear evaluation criteria before you start anything. At three months, I expect X. If I don't see it, I'll reassess.

Then actually reassess.

Staying in a failing project because you're afraid of what quitting looks like is just pride wearing productivity as a costume.

Quit the things that aren't working. Double down on what is. Your business is better for it.
