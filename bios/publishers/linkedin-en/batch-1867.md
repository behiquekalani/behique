**Post:**
There is a version of productivity that keeps you very busy while changing nothing.

I lived in it for almost a year.

I was reading books about building businesses. Watching every video from creators I admired. Taking notes. Building systems for my systems.

I was doing everything except the one thing that would have actually helped: talking to potential customers and shipping products.

Preparation has a point of diminishing returns. Most people hit it in week two and keep preparing anyway because preparation feels safe.

Creating feels exposed.

The feedback loop that actually teaches you is not the book or the course or the framework. It is the market.

Your first ten customers will teach you more than a hundred hours of preparation.

You learn what problem you actually solve. Who actually values it. What language they use. What objections they have.

None of that exists in the preparation phase.

At some point you have to stop getting ready and start getting feedback.

What are you preparing for that you could start doing imperfectly right now?
