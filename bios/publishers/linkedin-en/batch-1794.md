# LinkedIn Post 1794

**Every expert was a beginner who kept going past the point where most people stop.**

The gap between beginner and expert isn't intelligence. It's tolerance for the awkward middle. That stage where you know enough to see your own mistakes but not enough to fix them quickly. That stage is where most people quit.

They quit because it feels like not making progress. But the discomfort of the middle stage is exactly what progress feels like. You're building the neural pathways, the intuitions, the pattern recognition that expertise requires.

Getting through it is the whole job. No shortcut exists.

Stay past the point where most people leave. That's where the skill lives.
