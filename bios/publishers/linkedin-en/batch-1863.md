**Post:**
The loneliest part of building something is that the people closest to you often do not understand what you are doing.

Not because they do not care. Because they have a different reference point for what "work" looks like.

When I told people I was building digital products and creating content, the responses ranged from supportive-but-confused to outright skeptical.

It is not malicious. They are applying the framework they know.

What helped me was finding one or two people who were on a similar path. Not mentors with decades of experience. Peers doing the same thing at the same stage.

Those conversations changed everything.

You need someone who understands why you are excited about a small win that looks irrelevant from the outside. Someone who can tell when you are rationalizing versus reasoning.

Build your work. But also build a small circle of people who get it.

If you do not have that yet, start putting your work in public. The right people will find you.

Who in your life actually gets what you are building?
