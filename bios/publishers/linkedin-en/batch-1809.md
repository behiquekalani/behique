# LinkedIn Post 1809

Nobody tells you that building in public is the fastest way to get real feedback.

You could spend three months building in private, launch, and hear nothing. Or you could share the process, show the ugly parts, document what is not working, and collect feedback in real time before you ever ship.

Building in public is not about ego. It is not about performing hustle. It is a testing strategy. The creator who shares their thinking attracts people who are already interested in the problem. Those people give better feedback than any survey.

Start before you are ready. Document what you are building. The audience finds the work.
