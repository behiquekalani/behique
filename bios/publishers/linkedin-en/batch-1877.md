**Post:**
One of the hardest skills to build as a solopreneur: knowing when to stop.

Not quit. Stop.

Stop tweaking the landing page that's already converting. Stop researching when you already have enough to act. Stop refining the offer nobody has seen yet.

There's a version of productivity that's actually avoidance. It feels like work. It looks like work. But it's really just staying busy so you don't have to face the result.

The result being: does this work? Do people want this? Will it sell?

Those questions are uncomfortable. So we keep building instead of shipping. Keep editing instead of publishing. Keep planning instead of executing.

Set a ship date. Put it on the calendar. Then hold it.

The feedback loop only starts when something is in front of real people. Everything before that is just rehearsal.

Ship the rehearsal. Start getting real data.

Real data beats strategy every time.
