Most people price their work based on how long it took to make.

That's the wrong number.

Price it based on what it's worth to the buyer. A one-page checklist that saves someone 10 hours of research is worth $50. Not $5.

The mental shift: stop charging for your time. Start charging for the outcome.

Freelancers charge hourly. Product builders charge for results. There's a reason product builders scale and freelancers plateau.

If you've been undercharging, it's not because your work isn't good. It's because you're still thinking like an employee. You're pricing based on effort instead of value.

Raise the number. Add more specificity to the promise. Watch what happens.

The market doesn't reward hard work. It rewards clarity about what you're solving and for whom. Get that right, and pricing becomes obvious.
