# LinkedIn Batch 1964

**Post:** There is a difference between being busy and making progress.

Most solopreneurs are very busy.

They answer messages, tweak their website, attend calls, research tools, brainstorm ideas, revise their positioning. They work long hours and feel productive.

But their revenue stays flat. Their audience does not grow. Nothing compounds.

The reason is usually that they are optimizing for activity rather than output.

Activity feels productive. Output produces results.

Output is the content that gets published, not the content that gets drafted and reconsidered. The product that launches, not the one that is "almost ready." The offer that goes out, not the one being perfected in a doc.

The shift requires accepting imperfection as the cost of progress.

Every hour spent polishing something that has not shipped yet is an hour of zero feedback. No data. No learning. No revenue.

Shipping something imperfect gives you information. That information makes the next version better.

Done and out in the world beats perfect and in a folder, every time.

Take stock of your last week. How much of it was activity, and how much of it was output?

The honest answer is usually uncomfortable. That discomfort is useful.

Do the work that ships. Everything else is preparation.
