The solo business model that most people overlook is the knowledge business.

You already know things that other people will pay to learn faster. The problem is you've lived with that knowledge so long it feels obvious.

It's not obvious to the person two years behind you on the same path.

Your specific combination of experience, mistakes, and frameworks is a curriculum. Packaged correctly, it's a product.

This is not about being an expert in the classical sense. It's about being further along than your customer on a path they want to take.

The format doesn't matter as much as people think. A $29 PDF that solves one specific problem outperforms a $500 course that tries to cover everything.

Pick the most painful problem. Solve it clearly. Package the solution. Sell it.

You can build this in a weekend. The bottleneck is not production. It's the willingness to believe your knowledge has value and to price it accordingly.

That mental barrier is what keeps most people from starting. They're waiting until they know more. Until they feel ready. Until the product is perfect.

The right time was two years ago. The next right time is now.
