# LinkedIn Batch 1903

**Post:** Most solopreneurs have a visibility problem, not a quality problem.

The work is good. The ideas are solid. The offer makes sense. But nobody knows it exists.

Marketing feels gross until you reframe it: sharing your work is a service. If your product genuinely helps people, hiding it from them is a disservice.

That one reframe changes everything.

You stop thinking about marketing as self-promotion and start thinking about it as information delivery. Someone out there right now has the exact problem your product solves. If you don't tell them your solution exists, they'll stay stuck or buy something worse.

Posting consistently, writing clearly about what you do, talking about your offer directly, these are all forms of service when the work behind them is real.

The uncomfortable truth is that most people don't buy because they never hear about you. Not because they heard about you and decided no.

Be louder about the real thing you do. The right people will find it, recognize it, and buy it.

Visibility is a skill. Practice it like one.
