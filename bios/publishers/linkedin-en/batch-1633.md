The best marketing for a solo business is documentation.

Not polished case studies written in third person. Real documentation of what you are building, what you are learning, what failed and why.

People do not follow brands. They follow builders. They want to see the thinking behind the work, not just the finished product.

When you share how you solved a problem, you demonstrate competence without claiming it. The reader draws their own conclusion, which is always more convincing than you stating how good you are.

Documentation also makes you a better practitioner. Writing about what you did forces you to understand it more clearly. The gaps in your explanation are the gaps in your understanding.

Over time, your body of documented work becomes a body of evidence. Before someone hires you or buys from you, they have already spent hours reading your thinking. The trust is built before the conversation starts.

This is what compounds. Not follower counts. Not ad spend. A consistent record of real thinking applied to real problems.

Start documenting one thing you built or solved this week. Publish it without waiting for it to be perfect.
