# LinkedIn Post — batch-1594

Raising your prices will lose you some clients. That is not a problem. That is the point.

The clients you lose when you raise prices are usually the ones taking 80% of your time and energy. Replacing them with one client who pays more and respects your work is not a downgrade.

There is a level of pricing where the client relationship shifts. Below it, clients feel they are doing you a favor. Above it, they feel they invested in an outcome and want it to work.

That shift changes everything. Better communication. More trust. Faster implementation.

You get there by raising your prices and standing behind them.

The fear is losing revenue in the transition period. That is real. But staying at low prices to avoid that discomfort means you are trading long-term growth for short-term comfort.

Know what your work produces for the person buying it. Price for that outcome.

Your price is part of your brand. Make it say something true.
