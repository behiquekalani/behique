# LinkedIn Batch 1904

**Post:** There's a moment in every solopreneur's journey where you have to stop consuming and start shipping.

Most people never get there.

There's always another course to take. Another podcast to listen to. Another strategy to study. Information becomes a substitute for action because it feels productive without carrying the risk of failure.

Here's the uncomfortable truth: everything you need to start, you already know. The next thing you need to learn will only become clear once you start.

You learn copywriting by writing copy, not by reading about it. You learn product development by launching something, not by studying frameworks. You learn sales by having conversations with real buyers.

The gap between where you are and where you want to be isn't filled with more information. It's filled with execution.

Set a deadline. Ship something. Then study the feedback.

That feedback, from real people interacting with real work, is worth ten courses. But you only access it by putting something into the world.

Stop studying the path. Walk it.
