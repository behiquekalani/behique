Learning is the job when you work for yourself.

There's no training program. No manager telling you what skills to build next. No HR department mapping your development.

You have to figure out what you don't know, then go get it.

The solopreneurs who stagnate are the ones who stop treating learning as a core part of the work. They get good at one thing and repeat it forever. Eventually, the market moves and they're left behind.

The ones who stay sharp treat every week as a study session. Not formal. Just intentional.

What are the gaps between where I am and where I want to be? What do I need to understand better? Who's three steps ahead of me in this area?

Spend five percent of your working hours learning. That's two hours in a forty-hour week.

Not consuming. Learning. There's a difference. Learning is active. It ends with something you can apply.

Stay a student. The builders who stay curious stay relevant.
