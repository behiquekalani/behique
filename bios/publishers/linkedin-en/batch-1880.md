The creator economy isn't oversaturated. Most niches are underserved by good content.

What's saturated is generic content. Recycled tips. Surface-level advice. The same five productivity hacks from a hundred different accounts.

That's the thing people are tired of. Not creators. Not content. Shallow content.

Go deeper than the obvious take. If you can find the insight behind the insight, you win.

Most people see a trend and explain it. The better play: explain why the trend exists, what it means long-term, and what most people are getting wrong about it.

That's a different level of thinking. It takes longer. But it's also what builds real audiences.

Your competition isn't every creator in your space. Your competition is the shallow version of your topic.

Beat that. Go one level deeper on everything you publish.

The smart readers will find you. And they'll tell their smart friends.

Depth compounds.
