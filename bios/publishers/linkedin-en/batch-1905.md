# LinkedIn Batch 1905

**Post:** Every creator I know who built something real went through a phase where they felt completely invisible.

Posting into the void. Sending newsletters nobody opened. Launching products that nobody bought.

That phase is not a sign you're failing. It's the tax you pay for starting.

The mistake is interpreting silence as rejection. Most of the time, silence just means you haven't found your people yet. They exist. You just haven't built up enough signal for them to find you.

The algorithm eventually rewards persistence because platforms need content. The market eventually rewards quality because buyers need solutions. But neither happens on your timeline.

The only way through the invisible phase is to stop caring about being seen and start caring about the work itself. Make the next post better than the last. Make the next product more useful. Make the next email more honest.

When the work is good and you keep showing up, the audience finds you. Not because of luck, but because quality and consistency are searchable over time.

You are not invisible because you're unworthy. You're invisible because you're early.

Keep building. It compounds.
