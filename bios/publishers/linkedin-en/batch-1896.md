# LinkedIn Batch 1896

**Post:** The creator economy doesn't reward the most talented people.

It rewards the most consistent ones.

I've watched incredibly skilled writers post once a month and wonder why nothing is growing. I've watched average writers post every day for two years and build audiences that pay them well.

Talent is the starting point. Consistency is the multiplier.

This isn't about grinding yourself into the ground. It's about understanding that trust is built through repeated exposure over time. Your audience needs to see you think, repeatedly, before they decide you're someone worth following seriously.

One great post doesn't build a business. A hundred ordinary posts, published consistently over months, does.

The bar is lower than most people think. You don't need to go viral. You don't need a perfect strategy. You need to show up with something useful on a schedule your audience can count on.

Predictability is underrated. People follow creators they trust to be there. Be that person.

Pick a frequency you can sustain for a year, not one that impresses you for two weeks. Then do it. The rest follows from that.
