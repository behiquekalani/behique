# LinkedIn Batch 1445

## Post 1
**STOP BUILDING PRODUCTS NOBODY ASKED FOR.**

The biggest waste of creative energy is building something in silence and hoping the world shows up. Validate first. One sentence pitch, one honest conversation, one pre-sale before you write line one.

If people will not pay for the idea, they will not use the free version either. Demand does not appear after you build. You find demand before you build.

What is the last thing you sold before you built it?

---

## Post 2
**The riches are in the niches everyone ignores.**

Not every niche is ignored because it is bad. Some are ignored because they require actual expertise. The creators willing to go deep into a specific, weird, or difficult topic often find the most loyal and highest-paying audiences.

Do not chase what seems popular. Chase what you actually understand better than most people. Depth of knowledge is a moat that most creators never build.

What topic do you know better than 90% of people on the internet?

---

## Post 3
**Small audiences can build big businesses.**

1,000 true fans is enough. Not trend-chasers, not passive followers. True fans who buy everything you make. The math is straightforward. 1,000 people paying $100 a year is $100K. Most creators never reach that because they chase reach instead of depth.

Serve a small audience extraordinarily well. The revenue follows the depth of relationship.

How deeply do you know the 100 most engaged people in your audience?
