# LinkedIn Batch 1898

**Post:** Most people think marketing is about getting attention.

It's actually about holding it.

Getting attention is cheap. A controversial take, a good hook, a share from a big account. These things happen. The hard part is what happens after someone lands on your page.

Do they stay? Do they subscribe? Do they come back?

That's where most solopreneurs fall apart. They optimize for the top of the funnel and ignore everything else. Big reach, low conversion, confused about why nothing is working.

The real work is in what you say after you've captured attention. Your welcome email. Your about page. Your second piece of content. Your offer.

These are the things that convert a curious stranger into someone who trusts you enough to buy.

Stop chasing reach. Start optimizing for depth. Go deeper with fewer people and let word of mouth handle the growth.

A hundred people who genuinely trust you will build a more durable business than ten thousand followers who barely remember your name.

Market for depth. Build for trust. The numbers take care of themselves eventually.
