**Post:**
Good systems don't require willpower.

That's the whole point of a system.

If your productivity depends on motivation, you don't have a system. You have a wish.

Every habit I've actually kept was frictionless by design. The things I had to fight for every day eventually fell apart. Because willpower is finite and motivation is unreliable.

The setup matters more than the intention.

Want to write daily? Open the doc before you go to bed. The barrier becomes "just start typing." Want to exercise? Lay out the clothes the night before. The barrier becomes "just put them on."

This applies to business too. Automate the recurring work. Template the repetitive content. Build the workflow once so execution is nearly automatic.

Your energy is too valuable to spend on friction.

Spend one hour this week redesigning something you do repeatedly. Remove every step you don't need.

Then watch how much easier it becomes to show up.
