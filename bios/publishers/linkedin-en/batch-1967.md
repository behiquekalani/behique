# LinkedIn Batch 1967

**Post:** One product with a clear buyer is worth more than ten products with vague audiences.

The solopreneur urge to diversify early is almost always a mistake.

You add a second product before the first one is selling well. Then a third. Then you are managing a catalog without mastering any part of it.

The result is a store full of things that each underperform because none of them have your full attention.

Mastery in a business context means knowing your product deeply: knowing who buys it, why they buy it, what words they use when they talk about their problem, what hesitations they have before purchasing.

That knowledge only comes from focus. You cannot develop it across ten products simultaneously.

Find the one product that solves a real problem for a real person. Sell it to the point where you understand it inside out.

Then, and only then, think about what comes next.

The second product should serve the same buyer or extend the transformation of the first one. It should feel like a natural next step, not a pivot.

Depth before breadth. Focus before scale.

The businesses built on one product done excellently outlast the businesses built on many products done adequately.

Resist the urge to add more. Add more depth instead.
