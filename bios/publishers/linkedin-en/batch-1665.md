Deep work is not a productivity hack. It's the only way difficult things get built.

Shallow work fills calendars. Deep work fills portfolios.

The difference between someone who ships one meaningful project per month and someone who ships nothing is not talent or time. It's the ability to sit with a hard problem for more than 20 minutes without reaching for distraction.

That ability is a skill. You train it like a muscle.

Start with one 90-minute block per day where nothing else exists. No notifications. No tabs open except what you need. No background music with lyrics. Just the work.

At first it feels slow. Then it starts to feel like the only state where you're actually alive.

Most serious builders will tell you the same thing: their best work happened in silence. Not in meetings. Not on calls. In blocks of uninterrupted time that other people thought was antisocial.

Protect those blocks like they're your most valuable asset. They are.
