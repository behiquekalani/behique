Burnout in a solo business is almost always a pricing problem.

When you are overworked and underpaid, the instinct is to work harder to make up the difference. That makes it worse.

The real fix is to charge enough that you have fewer clients and more capacity. Fewer clients means you can do exceptional work for each one. Exceptional work means better results. Better results mean better referrals and higher future prices.

The math works in your favor the moment you stop competing on price.

A solo consultant charging $500 per project with 20 clients is exhausted and undifferentiated. A solo consultant charging $5000 per project with 4 clients has time to think, time to deliver, and time to build assets on the side.

Same revenue. Completely different life.

If you are feeling the early signs of burnout right now, look at your pricing before you look at your workload. The workload is usually a symptom. The pricing is usually the cause.

What would change in your business if you doubled your current rate?
