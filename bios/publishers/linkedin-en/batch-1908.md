**Post:** The biggest unlock in the creator economy isn't a new platform or a viral moment.

It's specificity.

Vague content attracts vague attention. Specific content attracts the right people. And the right people buy things that are designed for exactly their situation.

"How to grow on social media" competes with thousands of posts. "How a freelance UX designer gets clients without cold email" competes with almost none.

The specific version is harder to write. It requires you to know your audience well enough to describe their situation more accurately than they can themselves.

When you hit that level of specificity, something shifts. People stop scrolling. They say "this is exactly what I needed." They share it with people who have the same problem.

The paradox of specificity: the narrower your focus, the broader your eventual reach. Because highly specific content gets shared within the community that needs it.

Pick the most specific version of your problem. Write about that. Sell something that solves exactly that.

Specificity is the competitive advantage most people are too afraid to use.
