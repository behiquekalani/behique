**Post:**
You don't need more followers. You need deeper ones.

1,000 people who trust you completely will always outperform 100,000 who barely know you exist.

The vanity metric trap is real. Follower count feels good. It's visible. People ask about it. But it correlates poorly with income for most creators.

What correlates with income: email list size, comment quality, DM volume, repeat buyers.

These are trust metrics. And they're built through depth, not volume.

Go deep on your best audience members. Reply to every comment. Respond to every DM. Know who keeps showing up. Serve those people first.

Trust at scale starts with trust at one.

If you can make one reader feel genuinely seen and helped, multiply that by a thousand. That's a business.

If you can get a million impressions but no one feels anything, that's a content factory. Not a business.

Choose which you're building. Then build accordingly.
