# LinkedIn Post 1783

**Selling feels wrong until you understand that the right product in the right hands changes something.**

Most builders hate selling. They built a thing, they know it's good, but asking people to pay for it feels transactional. Like they're reducing their work to a number.

Flip it. When you sell, you're transferring a solution to someone who has a problem. The money is just confirmation that the solution mattered. Avoiding sales doesn't make you more ethical. It just keeps your solution away from people who need it.

The best sellers believe completely in what they built. That belief is what converts.

Build something you'd pay for yourself. Selling becomes easy after that.
