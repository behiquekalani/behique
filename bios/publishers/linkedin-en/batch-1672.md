The real cost of underpricing is not just lost revenue.

It's the wrong customers.

When you charge too little, you attract people who optimize for cheap. They're harder to serve, more likely to request refunds, and less likely to get results because they don't have skin in the game.

Higher prices filter for committed buyers. People who pay $200 for a course treat it differently than people who paid $19. They show up. They implement. They get results. And results become testimonials.

This is counterintuitive when you're starting out. You want volume. You want people to be able to afford it. But the customers who paid the most are almost always your best customers.

Raise your price. Add more specificity to what's included and what result the buyer should expect. The number of sales might drop. The quality of your customers will go up.

And in most cases, the revenue goes up too.
