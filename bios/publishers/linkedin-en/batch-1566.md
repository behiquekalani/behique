The biggest mistake in building a solo business is optimizing for revenue before you've validated the model.

Validation is simple. Someone gives you money for the thing. Not a waitlist. Not a survey. Not "I'd totally buy that." Actual money.

Until that happens, you're in hypothesis mode. Everything you build before validation is a bet.

Bets are fine. Just know you're making one.

The mistake is building a full product, a full funnel, a full content engine, before you've confirmed that a single person will pay for the core offer.

Sell before you build. Or at minimum, build the smallest version that delivers the outcome and sell that first.

If it sells once, it can sell again. Now you have something to build on.

This is not about being scrappy or cutting corners. It's about sequencing work correctly. Build what's necessary for the next decision, not what's necessary for the dream version.

The dream version comes after the model is proven. In the right order: validate, iterate, systematize.

Most people do it backwards. Don't be most people.
