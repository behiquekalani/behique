# LinkedIn Post — batch-1609

Your niche is not a cage. It is a signal.

When you say you help one specific type of person with one specific problem, you are not limiting your reach. You are making it easier for the right people to find you and instantly know you are for them.

Generalists struggle with marketing because there is no hook. "I help businesses grow" is not a hook. It is a shrug.

"I help early-stage founders write launch copy that converts without hiring an agency" is a hook. Someone reads that and either self-selects in or out. The ones who self-select in are already warm. They already feel seen.

Specificity is the most misunderstood growth strategy online. Everyone wants a broad audience. Nobody wants to miss anyone. But by trying to speak to everyone, you end up resonating with no one.

Go narrow. Go deep. Build authority in one corner of the internet before expanding.

The people who build strong personal brands all went through a specific phase first. They owned a topic, a format, a perspective. Then they grew from there.

Start narrow. Scale from strength.
