**Post:**
Most people treat their calendar like a to-do list with time slots.

That's backwards.

Your calendar should reflect your energy, not just your tasks. There are times in the day when you think sharply. Times when you're good for communication. Times when you can only handle low-stakes work.

Build your schedule around that.

I stopped scheduling deep work in the afternoon two years ago. My output in the same number of hours doubled. Not because I worked harder. Because I stopped fighting my biology.

The solopreneur advantage is that no one tells you when to work. Most people don't use that. They default to a 9-5 structure out of habit.

Audit your last week. When did you do your best thinking? When did you feel like you were dragging?

Protect the first. Defer the second.

That's the whole system.
