# LinkedIn Batch 1980

**Post:** The people who read your content are not looking for information.

They have Google for information.

They are looking for someone who understands their situation, who has thought through what they are stuck on, and who can give them a clear direction.

That is very different from providing facts.

Facts are searchable. Perspective is not. Your take on why something works, what most people miss, and how to actually apply an idea is the thing that cannot be found elsewhere.

This is why the most popular creators are not the most informed. They are the most opinionated. They have a point of view and they defend it. That clarity is attractive because clarity is rare.

Most content is careful and neutral. Careful because the creator does not want to be wrong. Neutral because they are afraid to exclude anyone.

But careful and neutral is also forgettable.

Have the opinion. Share the take. Be specific about who you are talking to and what you believe.

That is what gets saved, shared, and purchased.
