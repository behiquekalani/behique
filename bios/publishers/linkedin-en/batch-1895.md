# LinkedIn Batch 1895

**Post:** Pricing is the most avoided conversation in the solopreneur world.

Everyone wants to charge more. Almost nobody does.

The fear is always the same: what if they say it's too expensive? What if I lose the sale? What if they think I'm not worth it?

Here's the reality: cheap prices attract clients who will question your value constantly. High prices attract clients who trust your expertise and let you do your best work.

Price is positioning, not math.

When you charge $50 for something, you're telling the market this is a commodity. When you charge $500, you're telling them it's expertise. The product might be identical. The perception is completely different.

Solopreneurs who undercharge usually do it because they're still trying to convince themselves they're worth it, not because the market won't pay.

Raise your prices. You'll lose some people who were never going to be good clients anyway. You'll attract people who take the work seriously because they've invested in it.

Your price is a statement about what you believe your work is worth. Make it honest.
