# LinkedIn Batch 1481

## Post 1
**MOST PEOPLE NEVER BUILD ANYTHING.**

They consume. They plan. They get ready to get ready.

The gap between someone who ships and someone who doesn't isn't talent. It's tolerance for imperfection. You have to be willing to put out work that isn't perfect yet.

Every creator you admire started with something embarrassing. That's not a bug in the system. It's the system.

What would you ship today if you stopped waiting to be ready?

## Post 2
**The business model most people ignore: sell what you already know.**

You've spent years learning things. Most of that knowledge is invisible to others. It looks obvious to you, but it's not obvious to someone two steps behind you.

Digital products are the closest thing to printing money with your mind. Write a guide. Build a template. Package a process. Charge for access.

The cost to create is nearly zero. The upside is not capped.

What do you know that someone would pay to learn?

---

## Post 3
**You don't need a team. You need a system.**

Solopreneurs who scale don't hire first. They build repeatable processes first. They document, automate, and eliminate before they delegate.

A team on top of chaos creates expensive chaos.

Get clear on what you do every day. Remove what doesn't drive output. Automate what repeats. Then, if you still need help, bring someone in.

Most founders skip this. It costs them years.

What system do you need to build before you hire?
