# LinkedIn Batch 1450

## Post 1
**Your network is not your net worth. Your thinking is.**

People overvalue who they know and undervalue how they think. The best connections in the world cannot save a bad idea or a weak offer. But sharp thinking finds a way even with a small network.

Invest more in your thinking than in your relationships. The relationships will follow the quality of your ideas. Not the other way around.

What book or framework changed how you think most recently?

---

## Post 2
**The easiest way to grow is to be genuinely useful.**

Not algorithm-friendly useful. Not technically helpful. Genuinely, specifically, practically useful to a person with a real problem. That kind of content gets shared because it has to. People cannot keep it to themselves.

Write the post you wish existed when you were three years behind where you are now. That is the content that builds a real audience.

What is the most useful single piece of content you could create this week?

---

## Post 3
**BUILD THE REPUTATION BEFORE YOU NEED IT.**

Most people start marketing when they need money. That is the worst time to start. The best time to build trust is before you have anything to sell. Show up, provide value, be consistent.

By the time you launch something, the audience should already know who you are and what you stand for. Reputation is the infrastructure that makes sales feel natural.

How long have you been building your reputation before asking for anything in return?
