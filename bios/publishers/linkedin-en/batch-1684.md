Building in public is not about transparency for its own sake.

It is a distribution strategy.

When you document your process, your wins, your failures, your thinking, you give people a reason to pay attention before you have anything to sell. You build trust over time, at scale, without cold outreach.

Most people wait until their product is perfect before talking about it. By then, they have zero audience and wonder why no one buys.

The builder who posts about their process while building has an audience ready when they launch. That is the actual advantage.

Sharing the messy middle is not weakness. It is relatability. Relatability converts.

What to share: what you are building and why, what is not working and what you learned, what surprised you, what you would do differently.

What not to share: fake positivity, manufactured drama, engagement bait with no substance.

People follow people who are genuinely figuring something out. They unfollow people who perform it.

Build in public because it makes you better, keeps you accountable, and compounds into an audience over time.

That audience becomes your most valuable business asset.
