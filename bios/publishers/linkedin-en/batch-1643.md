Building in public is the cheapest marketing strategy most people ignore.

You don't need a brand. You don't need a team. You don't need a polished product. You need to show your work while it's happening.

Sharing the process builds trust faster than sharing the result. People don't just buy the outcome. They buy the story of how you got there. They buy proof that you think the way they want to think.

Every post where you share what you're building, what failed, what you learned is a touchpoint. Multiply that by 100 posts and you've built an audience that already trusts you before you ever pitch anything.

The fear people have is judgment. What if I fail publicly? That fear is backwards. Failing publicly and recovering is more compelling than succeeding quietly.

The ones who win at building in public are not the ones with the best product. They're the ones who document with the most honesty.

Start today. Share what you built this week. Share what broke. Share what you'd do differently.

That's the content. That's the brand.
