**Post:**
The highest-value skill I have built as a creator is not writing. It is not design. It is not marketing.

It is the ability to sit with discomfort and keep working anyway.

Every creator I respect has this. Every one.

The discomfort shows up differently for everyone. For some it is the fear of being judged. For others it is the resistance before creating. For others it is the feeling that they are not ready yet.

But the pattern is the same: the work requires you to feel uncomfortable, and most people stop there.

The ones who keep going build something. The ones who wait for comfort build nothing.

Comfort is not a prerequisite for creation. It is a reward that comes after.

You do not need to feel ready. You need to feel willing.

Willing to ship something imperfect.
Willing to look stupid for a season.
Willing to be misunderstood while you figure out what you are actually saying.

Sit with it. Keep working.

What creates the most discomfort for you in your creative process?
