# LinkedIn Batch 1941

**Post:** Personal brand is not about being famous. It is about being the obvious choice.

When someone in your space has a problem you solve, do they think of you?

That is the only metric that matters in the early stages.

Fame is diffuse. Being the obvious choice is specific and profitable.

To become the obvious choice for a specific person with a specific problem, you need three things:

A clear topic. Not five topics. One. The person who is known for one thing gets recommended. The person who does everything gets forgotten.

A consistent voice. People need to recognize you across contexts. Your values, your perspective, your way of framing problems should be identifiable even without your name attached.

Repetition without apology. Say the same core ideas in different ways until they are associated with you. This feels repetitive from the inside. It is not. Most people only see a fraction of what you publish.

The mistake is trying to appeal to everyone. When you appeal to everyone, you become wallpaper.

When you go narrow and deep, the right people start to seek you out. Referrals happen without you asking. Opportunities arrive that are a fit rather than a reach.

Build a small reputation in a specific space. It compounds faster than you expect.
