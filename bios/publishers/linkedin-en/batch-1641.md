Most people price their services by copying competitors.

That's a race to the bottom. You end up exhausted, underpaid, and resentful of the work you used to love.

Pricing is not math. It's positioning.

When you charge more, you attract people who value results. When you charge less, you attract people who question every invoice. The clients who haggle the most are always the ones who drain the most energy.

Here's the shift: stop pricing based on time and start pricing based on outcomes.

What does the client gain if your work succeeds? Revenue, time, clarity, reputation. That's the number you should be working backward from.

A $500 project that saves someone $10,000 in mistakes is underpriced. A $5,000 project that produces the same result is reasonable.

Your price is a signal before a word is spoken. Set it deliberately.

The solo operator who understands pricing builds a business that sustains itself. Everyone else runs a very expensive hobby.
