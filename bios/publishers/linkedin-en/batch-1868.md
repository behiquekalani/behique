**Post:**
The creator economy has a visibility problem disguised as a talent problem.

Most people who are not getting traction are not less talented than the people who are. They are less visible.

Visibility is a skill. It is learnable. And it has almost nothing to do with the quality of your work.

It has to do with how consistently you show up. How well you understand the platform. How clearly you communicate what you do and who you help.

I have watched genuinely brilliant people go unnoticed because they post once every two weeks, use jargon their audience does not understand, and never explain what someone should do after reading their content.

And I have watched people with average ideas build huge audiences because they post every day, speak plainly, and always have a clear next step.

Visibility is not bragging. It is clarity.

Be clear about what you do. Be consistent about showing up. Give people a reason to follow you and a way to go deeper.

The talent is table stakes. The visibility is the work.

What is one thing you could do this week to be more visible to your ideal audience?
