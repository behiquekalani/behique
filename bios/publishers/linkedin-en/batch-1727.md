# LinkedIn Post 1727

Everyone talks about finding your niche. Nobody talks about the cost of staying in the wrong one for two years.

A niche is not just a topic. It's an audience with a specific problem and the willingness to pay to solve it. Most creators pick a topic they like and wonder why it doesn't convert. Liking something is not enough. You need to be solving something.

The fastest path to income is not the most interesting niche. It's the most painful one. Find where people are frustrated, embarrassed, stuck, or bleeding money. Build there.

Once the income is stable, you can add depth, nuance, and the topics you actually love. But start with pain, not passion.

Where does your audience hurt? Start there.
