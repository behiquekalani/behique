# LinkedIn Post 1792

**Price your products for the value they deliver, not the time they took you to make.**

Time-based pricing is a trap. If you write a guide in two hours that saves someone 40 hours of trial and error, pricing it at $20 because it took you two hours undervalues the outcome.

Value is about the buyer, not the seller. What does this result mean to them? What would they pay to avoid the problem you're solving? What's the cost of not having this?

The creator who prices on value earns more per hour than the one who prices on effort. Not because they're more talented, but because they understand what they're actually selling.

You're not selling hours. You're selling outcomes.
