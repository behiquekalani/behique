Your niche is not a box. It's a door.

Most people resist niching down because they think it limits them. That's backwards.

A specific niche gives people a reason to choose you over the generalist. When someone has a specific problem, they don't want a broad solution. They want the person who has seen that problem a hundred times.

The financial coach who helps tech workers manage equity compensation. The copywriter who writes specifically for SaaS founders. The designer who only does brand identity for solo service businesses.

Specific. Memorable. Easy to refer.

The generalist competes on price because he has no other differentiator. The specialist competes on fit. Completely different game.

You don't have to niche forever. Most people who go deep in one area eventually expand naturally, with an audience that already trusts them.

But you start specific. You earn attention by being the best answer to a specific question. Then you earn the right to go broader.

Pick the narrowest niche you can serve credibly. Show up there consistently. Watch referrals start coming without you asking.

The door is narrow on purpose. Walk through it first.
