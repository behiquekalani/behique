# LinkedIn Post — batch-1587

Building an audience is not about going viral. It is about becoming the most useful person in a specific room.

Pick a topic narrow enough that you can own it. Then show up consistently with genuine insight, not recycled takes.

Most creators are too broad too early. They want to appeal to everyone and end up resonating with no one. The narrower your focus, the faster you build a loyal audience.

Consistency beats quality in the short run. Quality beats quantity in the long run. Both beat showing up occasionally with something "perfect."

Your audience does not need you to be the most talented person in the space. They need you to be reliable, honest, and useful. Those three things are rarer than talent.

Build in public. Share your process, not just your results. Results are impressive. Process is relatable.

People follow people they trust. Trust is built through repetition, honesty, and specificity.

Show up. Say something real. Do it again.
