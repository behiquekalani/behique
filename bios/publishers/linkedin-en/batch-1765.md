# LinkedIn Post 1765

**Consistency beats intensity every single time. But nobody wants to hear that.**

Intensity is exciting. A 12-hour sprint feels like progress. The binge-work session has a story you can tell.

Consistency is boring. Thirty minutes a day, every day, for a year. No dramatic arc. No highlight reel.

But compounding doesn't care about your feelings. It rewards regularity. The person who shows up every day at 50% will outperform the person who shows up once a month at 100%.

The builder who publishes one post a day for a year will always beat the one who published fifty in a weekend and then disappeared.

Build the habit. Make it small enough that skipping feels weird.
