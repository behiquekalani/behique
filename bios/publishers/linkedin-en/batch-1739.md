# LinkedIn Post 1739

Your first version will be imperfect. Ship it anyway.

The product you've been perfecting in private for six months is not better than the one you could have shipped four months ago and improved based on actual feedback. Perfection is a delay tactic that feels like quality control.

Real quality comes from iteration. Version one teaches you what version two needs to be. You cannot learn that from a draft that never leaves your hard drive.

The market will tell you what's wrong. The people who buy it will show you what's missing. That feedback is more valuable than anything you can generate alone in a planning session.

Done is the strategy. Done gets feedback. Done makes money. Perfect never ships.
