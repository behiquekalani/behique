# LinkedIn Batch 1914

**Post:** Most digital products fail before they are ever built.

They fail in the planning stage because the creator builds what they want to make instead of what someone is already trying to buy.

This is the mistake. You spend weeks on something that feels important to you. You launch. Silence.

Not because your product was bad. Because you skipped the step where you verified that someone else has this problem badly enough to pay to solve it.

Here is a simple test before you build anything.

Go find five people in your target audience. Tell them you are thinking about building X. Ask if that is a problem they have. Ask how they currently deal with it. Ask what they would pay to solve it.

If you cannot find five people to have this conversation with, you do not have an audience yet. Solve that first.

If the five people tell you this is not a real problem, trust them. Not your gut.

If three of them say they would pay for something like this and ask when it is ready, you have your signal.

Build after validation, not before. It feels slower. It is actually faster because you skip the part where you launch to no one.
