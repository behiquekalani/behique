# LinkedIn Batch 1936

**Post:** Audience size is a vanity metric until you understand monetization.

Ten thousand followers who do not buy anything is just a big group chat.

Two hundred followers who trust you deeply and open every email you send is a real business foundation.

This is the mistake most early creators make. They optimize for reach before they understand what converting that reach into revenue actually looks like.

The better question early on is not "how do I get more followers?" It is "what do the followers I already have actually need, and am I giving it to them?"

Small, engaged audiences buy. Large, passive audiences scroll.

Every platform algorithm is designed to spread content widely. But wide does not mean deep. And depth is what produces revenue.

Pick a problem. Serve the people who have it. Do it so consistently that when they think of that problem, they think of you.

At 500 real readers, you can run a profitable business. Most people refuse to believe this because they have been trained to worship scale.

Scale comes after depth. Not before.

Build something people would pay for with 200 followers. Then grow. The order is not reversible.
