# LinkedIn Batch 1924

**Post:** Consistency beats intensity every time in the long run.

The sprint mentality kills more businesses than laziness does.

You go hard for three weeks. Post every day. Build all day. Run on caffeine and momentum. Then crash. Take a week off. Come back feeling behind and guilty.

The audience noticed. The algorithm noticed. The habit is broken.

Sustainable output at a moderate pace beats explosive output followed by burnout, every single time.

Four posts a week for two years beats seven posts a week for three months followed by silence.

One hour of focused work daily beats a 12-hour session every Saturday.

The math of consistency is not glamorous but it is reliable. You can plan around it. Your audience can expect it. Your body can sustain it.

Ask yourself not what is the most you can do but what is the most you can do every single day without needing recovery days.

Start there. Build from there.

The creator who posts reliably for three years without burning out will always outperform the one who sprints and collapses and sprints again.

Boring is sustainable. Sustainable wins.
