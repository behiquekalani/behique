Your personal brand is not your aesthetic. It's your point of view.

Colors, logos, and profile pictures are packaging. The point of view is the product.

When someone reads your posts for three months and can predict how you'll think about a new problem, you have a brand. When they can't predict anything because you write about everything equally, you have a feed.

A strong point of view means you have opinions. You take positions. You say things some people will disagree with.

That disagreement is not a problem. It's proof the brand is working.

Neutral content attracts neutral engagement. Strong perspective attracts strong alignment. You want the people who lean in, not the ones who scroll past without stopping.

Your point of view comes from your real experience. What you've tried. What failed. What changed your thinking. What you believe differently now than you did three years ago.

That's the material. You already have it.

The work is translating your real beliefs into clear, consistent public communication. That's what builds a brand that earns trust before you ever ask for a sale.

Be specific about what you believe and why.

The right people will find you. The wrong ones will filter out.
