# LinkedIn Post 1731

The internet doesn't reward the best. It rewards the most visible.

This is uncomfortable for people who were told talent is enough. It's not. A mediocre product with great distribution will beat an excellent product with no audience every single time.

This doesn't mean quality doesn't matter. It means quality is table stakes. The game is distribution. You need both.

The good news: distribution is learnable. It's a skill, not a birthright. You can study it, test it, and get better at it the same way you got better at your craft.

The people complaining that social media is unfair are often the ones who never treated content strategy as seriously as their core skill.

Treat distribution like a product. Build it with intention.
