**Post:**
The best business advice I ever got was from someone who had never run a business.

My grandfather. He said: do not make money the goal. Make being useful the goal. Money follows useful.

I thought it was simple and kind of naive when I heard it.

Now it is the thing I return to when something is not working.

Whenever a product flops, I ask: is this actually useful to someone, or am I just hoping it is useful?

Whenever content underperforms, I ask: did I make this to help someone or to feel like I made something?

Whenever an offer does not convert, I ask: am I solving a real problem or a perceived one?

Usefulness is not soft. It is the most rigorous standard there is.

The market is unforgiving about things that are not useful. It is also wildly generous about things that genuinely help.

Build useful things. Price them fairly. Tell people they exist.

That is the whole playbook.

What is the most useful thing you have built or are planning to build?
