# LinkedIn Batch 1978

**Post:** Most people wait until they feel ready to start.

Ready never comes. Ready is a feeling your brain manufactures to delay risk.

The founders, creators, and solopreneurs who built real things did not feel ready. They felt uncertain and launched anyway. The act of starting is what created the clarity they thought they needed beforehand.

You learn more from one week in the market than from months of planning before it. The audience tells you what they want. The sales data tells you what works. The silence tells you what to cut.

None of that information is available before you start. It only exists on the other side of beginning.

The question is not "am I ready?" The question is "what is the minimum I need to start learning?"

That minimum is almost always smaller than you think. A simple offer. A basic page. One honest piece of content explaining who you help and how.

Start with that. Refine as you go.

Ready is built, not waited for.
