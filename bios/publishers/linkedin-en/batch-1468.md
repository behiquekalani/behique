# LinkedIn Batch 1468

## Post 1
**DIGITAL PRODUCTS DON'T SLEEP. YOUR CLIENTS EXPECT YOU TO.**

One of the most underappreciated shifts in modern business: your work can be packaged.

A course, a template, a guide, a system. Work you do once that earns repeatedly while you focus on the next problem.

This isn't passive in the lazy sense. It requires real work upfront. But the ratio changes. More leverage per hour invested.

What knowledge do you have right now that could be packaged into something someone would pay for?

## Post 2
**The biggest lie in productivity culture: if you just work harder, everything will fall into place.**

Work ethic matters. But working hard in the wrong direction just gets you to the wrong destination faster.

Strategy first. Then execution.

Know exactly what you're building and why. Then work as hard as you want.

Without strategy, effort is just expensive motion.

What's the one strategic question about your business you've been avoiding answering?

---

## Post 3
**Niche down until it feels uncomfortable. Then go a little further.**

Most people resist niching because it feels like shrinking. It's actually expanding.

A specific audience knows you're talking to them. Specificity creates resonance. Resonance creates trust. Trust creates sales.

"I help people" reaches no one. "I help freelance designers raise their rates without losing clients" reaches exactly the right people.

How specific is your current positioning?
