# LinkedIn Batch 1965

**Post:** The biggest misconception about building online is that you need a large audience before you can make money.

You need a specific audience. Not a large one.

A list of 300 people who trust you and care about what you do will generate more revenue than a following of 30,000 passive scrollers who clicked follow once and never came back.

Depth beats breadth at every scale.

This is why some creators with 50,000 followers earn less than others with 5,000. The first built reach. The second built relationship.

Relationship is what drives purchases. People buy from people they trust.

The path to trust is not going viral. It is showing up with consistent, specific, useful content until a specific group of people thinks of you when they have a specific problem.

That takes time. It does not take a massive audience.

If you have 100 engaged followers, focus entirely on serving them well. Understand their problems deeply. Build something that solves the exact friction they have.

If you do that well, those 100 people will tell others. Growth will follow naturally.

The obsession with follower counts is a distraction from the actual work: building something people genuinely value.

Build the trust first. The scale will come as a result, not as a prerequisite.
