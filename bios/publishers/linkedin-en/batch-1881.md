**Post:**
The pivot people don't talk about: going from "building in public" to actually building.

At some point, the documentation has to stop and the work has to start.

I see a lot of builders who have a great following because of their process. And then they get stuck there. The narrative of building becomes the product. The audience wants the journey, not the destination.

That's fine if it's intentional. But if you want to actually ship things that make money, you have to close the laptop, turn off the camera, and go deep.

Real building requires silence. Not an audience watching.

Schedule blocks where you build with no documentation, no posting, no updating the community. Just work.

The best output comes from those sessions. Share it after. Not during.

Build in private. Ship in public. The order matters.
