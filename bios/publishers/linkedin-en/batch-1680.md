Solo business is not about working less. It's about working on the right things.

The average employee spends 70% of their time on work that could be done by almost anyone. Meetings. Status updates. Low-stakes emails. Coordination theater.

The solo builder can cut all of that. Every hour goes toward building something that matters or nothing at all.

That's the real advantage. Not the freedom to sleep in. The freedom to spend your cognitive resources entirely on things that compound.

You can build a product. Write something that teaches. Create a system that automates. Connect two ideas that hadn't been connected before.

None of that requires a team. It requires protected time and a clear output in mind.

Define what your most important output is this week. The one thing that, if done well, makes every other task irrelevant.

Then do that first, every day, until it's done.
