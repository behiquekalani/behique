Digital products are the closest thing to building once and getting paid forever.

You create something once. A template, a guide, a course, a system. You host it. You sell it to the first person, then the hundredth, then the thousandth. Your marginal cost per sale is essentially zero.

That is not magic. That is economics.

The mistake most people make: they try to build a $500 product before they have an audience. The product sits there unsold, and they conclude that digital products do not work.

What does not work is building before distributing.

The order is: build an audience around a specific problem, learn exactly what that audience needs, build the product that solves it, sell it to the people who already trust you.

That sequence works. The reverse rarely does.

Your first digital product does not need to be perfect. It needs to solve one problem completely. Price it at $27 to $97. Sell 10 copies. Learn. Improve. Price higher.

The builder who ships an imperfect product learns faster than the one who polishes forever.

Start small. Start now. The skills compound with every product you ship.
