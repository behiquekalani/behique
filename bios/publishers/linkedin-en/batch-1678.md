There are two kinds of business builders.

Those who build when they feel motivated. And those who build on a schedule.

The first group has great weeks and empty months. The second group ships consistently, builds compounding assets, and eventually laps the first group without trying harder.

Motivation follows action, not the other way around. Waiting to feel ready is a trap. The system needs to run whether the feeling shows up or not.

Design your schedule so that the default option is to work. Make it harder to skip than to start. A 30-minute protected block every morning is more valuable than a 4-hour session you only manage twice a month.

Show up. Do the minimum. Let the momentum build.

Most days you'll exceed the minimum anyway once you've started. But the commitment is to the schedule, not the inspiration.

Consistency is boring. It's also the only thing that compounds.
