# LinkedIn EN Batch 1852

**Post:**
The creator who posts consistently beats the creator with better ideas every single time.

This took me too long to accept.

I used to hold posts back. Wait for the perfect angle. Spend three days refining something that needed thirty minutes.

Meanwhile people with half my knowledge were publishing daily, building audiences, getting opportunities.

Consistency is not about lowering standards. It is about understanding what standards actually matter.

Your audience does not remember the post you spent a week on versus the one you wrote in an hour. They remember whether you showed up when they needed something.

Presence compounds. Polish does not.

Here is what changed for me: I stopped trying to write the definitive post on every topic and started treating each post as a single data point in a larger conversation.

One post will not build your brand. Two hundred posts over a year will.

The goal is not to go viral. The goal is to still be here in twelve months.

Show up. Ship the thing. Write the next one.

How long do you spend on average writing a LinkedIn post?
