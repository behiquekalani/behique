# LinkedIn Batch 1917

**Post:** You do not need a team to build a real business. You need a system.

The fantasy is that once you hire people, the work gets easier. Sometimes it does. Often it just means more communication, more management, and more overhead at a stage where you cannot afford it.

Most solopreneurs scale too fast because they mistake busyness for readiness.

Before you add complexity, ask one question: is this process documented well enough that someone else could follow it without me explaining it every time?

If the answer is no, hiring does not solve your problem. It amplifies it.

Start with systems. Map how you create content. Map how you handle customer inquiries. Map how you deliver your product. Write it down in a way that is clear enough for a stranger to execute.

Now you have something worth delegating.

Once you have that, you can bring in a contractor for specific tasks. Not a full-time hire. A specialized pair of hands for the parts that are clearly defined.

This is how lean businesses stay lean. Not by refusing to grow but by making sure the foundation holds before adding weight.

Build the system first. Delegate second. The order matters more than the speed.
