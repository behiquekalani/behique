Writing is the highest-leverage skill a solo builder can develop.

Every product description, every email, every post, every pitch is writing. If you can not write clearly, you can not communicate value clearly. If you can not communicate value, you can not sell.

Most people think writing is a talent. It is not. It is a practice.

You get better by writing badly for a long time, then gradually less badly. You develop a sense for what makes a sentence land. You learn to cut the unnecessary words. You stop hiding behind corporate language and start saying what you actually mean.

The ROI on writing is compounding. A post you wrote two years ago still reaches people today. An email sequence you built last year still converts leads this quarter. A product description you crafted once sells while you sleep.

No other skill has that kind of return on invested time.

Write something today. Publish it even if it feels incomplete. The gap between a bad post and no post is the entire gap between a growing audience and silence.

What is stopping you from writing more consistently?
