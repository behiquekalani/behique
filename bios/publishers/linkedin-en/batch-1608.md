# LinkedIn Post — batch-1608

The market does not care about your effort. It cares about your output.

This is hard for people raised in school systems where showing your work earns partial credit. In business, nobody gives you points for trying. They give you money for results.

This does not mean effort is irrelevant. It means effort is table stakes, not a differentiator. Everyone who stays in the game long enough is putting in effort.

What separates the ones who grow from the ones who grind in place is output quality. Are the words better? Is the product more useful? Is the process faster? Is the promise clearer?

If you have been working hard and not seeing results, the question is not whether you tried hard enough. The question is whether the output is solving a real problem clearly enough for the right person.

Hard work inside the wrong frame stays hard forever.

Audit the output, not the effort. Ask: would someone pay for this? Would they tell a friend? Would they come back?

That is the actual report card.
