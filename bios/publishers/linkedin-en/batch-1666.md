Building in public isn't about sharing your wins.

It's about creating a record.

When you document what you're building, you create a trail that compounds. People who find you six months from now see a person who was doing the work long before anyone was watching. That's credibility you can't buy.

The mistake most people make: they only post when things go well. That makes the whole feed feel performative. The posts that actually build trust are the ones where you say "this didn't work and here's why."

Honesty is the only differentiator in a world full of polished highlight reels.

You don't need to be successful to build in public. You need to be doing something real and documenting it honestly.

Your audience will grow from people who see themselves in where you are right now. Not from people who want to be where you'll be later. Serve them where they are and bring them with you.

That's how lasting audiences get built.
