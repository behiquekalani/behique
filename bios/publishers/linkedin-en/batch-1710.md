Identity precedes action. Not the other way around.

Most people wait until they have results before they call themselves something. "I'll call myself a designer when I have clients. I'll call myself a writer when someone pays me."

That reasoning keeps you stuck indefinitely.

The person who says "I am a writer" before anyone pays them will write every day. They will seek feedback. They will study the craft. They will send work out.

The person waiting to "become" a writer will wait for conditions that never arrive.

Your identity shapes your decisions before your results do.

Claim the identity. Then let the actions follow from it.

This isn't fake-it-till-you-make-it. That framing is dishonest. You don't pretend to have skills you don't have.

You commit to the identity of the person you are becoming. Then you act like that person would act. With consistency. With standards. With work.

The results come from the sustained action. The sustained action comes from the identity.

Get the identity right first. The work aligns behind it.
