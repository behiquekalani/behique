# LinkedIn Batch 1927

**Post:** The creator who makes $10k a month is not ten times better than the one making $1k.

They made one different decision at some point. Probably about price.

This is the uncomfortable truth most people avoid: revenue problems are often pricing problems dressed up as skill problems or audience problems.

You tell yourself you need more followers. More experience. A better portfolio. More testimonials.

Sometimes that is true. Often you just need to charge more.

The difference between a $47 product and a $197 product is not five times the quality. It is the confidence to believe your work is worth $197 and the willingness to test whether the market agrees.

Often it does.

People are not as price-sensitive as you think. They are value-sensitive. If you can clearly articulate the outcome your product delivers, the price matters much less than the confidence with which you present it.

Start by auditing what you currently charge. Is there anything you would feel embarrassed to admit you charge that little for?

If yes, that is your first pricing experiment.

Double it. Or triple it. Run the test for 30 days. See what happens.

You might be surprised how little changes except your bank account.
