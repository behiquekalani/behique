Your digital product doesn't have to be a course.

It can be a template. A checklist. A swipe file. A framework. A set of prompts. A reference guide.

Some of the best-selling digital products are simple. A one-page process document. A spreadsheet someone spent 20 hours perfecting. A Notion system that took a year to develop but can be duplicated in two minutes.

Simple doesn't mean cheap. Simple means focused. And focused products are easier to explain, easier to sell, and easier for the buyer to use.

The product that solves one problem clearly beats the 200-page course that covers everything vaguely.

If you've been putting off building a product because you don't have time for a course, build the simple version. The checklist. The template. The one clear thing.

Ship it. Learn from it. Build the bigger thing from that foundation.
