# LinkedIn Post — batch-1588

Deep work is not a productivity hack. It is the only way to produce work that actually matters.

Shallow work keeps you busy. Deep work moves things forward.

Most people fill their days with shallow work because it feels productive. Emails, meetings, quick tasks. All of it gives you the sensation of progress without the reality of it.

Real output comes from uninterrupted blocks of focused effort on your most important problem. That is it.

To protect deep work, you have to be willing to disappoint people in the short term. Say no to meetings. Turn off notifications. Guard your morning.

The interruptions feel urgent. They rarely are. The work that requires your full brain is what actually builds your business.

Start with one 90-minute block per day. No phone. No tabs. One problem. Full attention.

Do that for a week and compare your output to the previous week.

Deep work is not magic. It is just rare. Rare things have disproportionate rewards.
