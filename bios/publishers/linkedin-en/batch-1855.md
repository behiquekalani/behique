# LinkedIn EN Batch 1855

**Post:**
I used to think building in public was about sharing wins.

It is not. The wins are the least interesting part.

What people actually want to see is the thinking. The decisions. The wrong turns and how you corrected course.

Wins are easy to scroll past. They feel distant, aspirational, almost fictional.

But "here is the mistake I made last Tuesday and what I learned from it" -- that stops people mid-scroll.

Because everyone is making mistakes. Most people are hiding them.

When you share the real process, you do two things at once: you build trust with your audience and you process your own experience more deeply.

Writing about what went wrong forces you to understand it better. You cannot write clearly about something you do not understand.

So building in public is not marketing. It is thinking out loud while marketing happens as a side effect.

Start sharing the process. Not just the outcome.

What is one thing you got wrong this week that taught you something useful?
