The solo business model that actually works:

One niche. One audience. One core offer. One distribution channel.

Not five. Not two. One of each, done well.

Most people fail at solo business because they try to be everywhere at once. Four social platforms, three different offers, two different audiences, one person trying to manage all of it.

That's not a business. That's chaos with a logo.

Start by picking the one channel where your ideal customer already spends time. Show up there consistently. Build the one product that solves their most urgent problem. Price it fairly for the outcome it delivers.

Do that for six months before adding anything.

The urge to expand is usually fear disguised as ambition. Fear that one thing won't be enough. It usually is, if you actually commit to it.

Focus is the only strategy that scales from zero.
