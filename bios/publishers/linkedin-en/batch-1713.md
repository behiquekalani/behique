Specific skills are worth more than broad knowledge.

The market does not pay for "I know a little about a lot of things." It pays for "I am the best at this one thing for this one type of person."

The graphic designer who specializes in book covers for nonfiction authors earns more per project than the designer who will make anything for anyone. The market is smaller. The rate is higher. The clients are easier to close.

Specificity is a pricing strategy.

Most people avoid going specific because they fear missing out on clients outside their lane. The irony is that going broad makes you invisible. There is nothing to remember about you. Nothing to refer.

Specific gets remembered. Specific gets referred.

Pick one skill. Go deep. Become the most useful voice in that corner of the market. Write about it, teach it, show your work in that domain.

You will attract fewer people. The people you attract will be the right ones. They will pay more, stay longer, and tell others.

Depth beats breadth for solo operators. The market rewards specialists.

Go deep on one thing first. You can go broad later when they already trust you.
