Most people price their services to win the client.

That's the wrong goal.

When you price to win, you attract clients who are shopping for the lowest number. You end up overworked, resentful, and broke. Not because you lack skill. Because you aimed at the wrong target.

Price to filter instead.

A higher price does three things most people don't expect. It repels the wrong clients automatically. It signals quality before you say a word. And it gives you enough margin to actually do good work.

The person who pays $500 for your service and the person who pays $5,000 are completely different humans. Different expectations. Different respect. Different outcomes.

You don't raise your price after you feel confident enough.

You raise your price. Then the confidence follows.

Most solo operators undercharge for years, waiting for permission. The permission never comes from outside. You set the number and hold it.

Your price is a statement about who you are and who you're for. Make it honest.
