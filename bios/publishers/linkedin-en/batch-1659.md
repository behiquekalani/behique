A one-person business can print money if the margins are right.

Most businesses fail not because they lack revenue. They fail because costs grow faster than revenue. The solo model solves this structurally.

No payroll. No office. No inventory. No benefits. The margin on a digital product or a service delivered remotely is 70-90% after tools and platform fees. That's a number most traditional businesses can't reach.

The implication: you don't need to make millions. You need to make enough. What does "enough" actually look like for your life? For most people, it's a fraction of what they think.

$5,000 a month is enough to live well in most cities. $10,000 is enough to live well almost anywhere and invest the rest.

At 80% margins, $10,000/month requires $12,500 in gross revenue. At $500 per product or service unit, that's 25 sales per month. Less than one per day.

The math is not complicated. The execution is the hard part. But the goal is achievable without a large team, outside funding, or a decade of hustle.

Do the math for your own number. The target is probably smaller than you think.
