# LinkedIn EN Batch 1861

**Post:**
Content that performs well and content that builds your business are not always the same thing.

This distinction took me too long to figure out.

A post can go viral and attract zero buyers. A post can get fifty likes and generate five thousand dollars in sales.

Vanity metrics and business metrics are different scoreboards.

When I started tracking which posts led to email signups, product sales, or DM conversations -- the pattern was clear.

The posts that built my business were almost never the ones that performed best by platform metrics.

They were the posts that spoke directly to a specific problem. That named a pain people had. That offered a clear next step.

Not the most entertaining. The most useful.

Know your actual goal before you write. If it is reach, optimize for reach. If it is sales, optimize for conversion.

Trying to do both at once usually means you accomplish neither.

What metric actually matters for where you are right now?
