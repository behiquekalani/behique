# LinkedIn Batch 1894

**Post:** Nobody told me that building an online business would feel like therapy.

You write something honest and people either connect with it or they don't. You make an offer and the market either validates your thinking or ignores it. You put your ideas into the world and discover, fast, what you actually believe versus what you only thought you believed.

It strips away the comfortable lies quickly.

The lie that you're not ready. The lie that your ideas aren't good enough. The lie that you need more credentials, more followers, more time.

Every post, every product, every failed launch teaches you something about yourself that a degree never could.

The people who succeed online long-term aren't the most talented. They're the ones who stayed consistent long enough to stop being afraid of the feedback.

When a post flops, it's data. When a product doesn't sell, it's a message about positioning, not worth.

You get good at this by doing it when it's uncomfortable. That's not a motivational statement, it's mechanics. Skill requires repetition. Repetition requires showing up when you'd rather not.

The business teaches you the person. Build something honest and watch what you learn.
