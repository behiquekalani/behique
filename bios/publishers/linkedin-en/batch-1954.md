# LinkedIn Batch 1954

**Post:** The reason most solopreneurs stay small is not lack of skill. It is fear of specificity.

They want to help everyone. So they resonate with no one.

Broad positioning feels safe because rejection is diffuse. If you speak to everyone and nobody buys, you can tell yourself the market was not right.

Specific positioning feels risky because the rejection is personal. If you speak to one person and they do not buy, that means something.

But here is the truth: specific positioning does not shrink your market. It sharpens your message until the right people can find you.

You are not eliminating buyers. You are making yourself visible to the buyers who actually need what you have.

A freelance designer who helps e-commerce brands reduce cart abandonment through better checkout UX will earn more than one who "helps businesses with design."

Not because the market is bigger. Because the message is clearer.

Clarity creates trust. Trust creates buying decisions.

The more specific you are about who you help and what problem you solve, the easier every sale becomes.

Pick the smallest viable niche you can serve well. Go deep before you go wide.

Specificity is not a limitation. It is a competitive advantage most people are too afraid to use.
