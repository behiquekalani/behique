# LinkedIn Post 1768

**Charging more is not greed. Undercharging is fear wearing a mask.**

When you price low, you tell the market something. You tell them the work isn't that valuable. You attract clients who want the cheapest option, not the best result. You create a workload that doesn't leave room for quality.

Higher prices filter for people who are serious. Serious clients get better results because they're invested. Better results become your portfolio. Your portfolio justifies higher prices.

It's a loop. But you have to start it.

The first time you raise your rate feels dangerous. It almost never is. The market has more tolerance for your prices than your fear does.

Price based on the value you deliver, not the hours you work.
