# LinkedIn Batch 1926

**Post:** Your audience does not want more content. They want better answers to specific questions.

The content overload is real. Everyone is posting. Most of it blurs together.

What cuts through is not more output. It is more precision.

When someone reads your post and thinks "how did they know that was exactly my situation," you have done something rare. You have described their problem better than they could describe it themselves.

That recognition is the foundation of trust. It says: this person understands me. Maybe they can help me.

You get there by going narrow. Not writing about productivity but about how ADHD professionals lose entire mornings to decision paralysis. Not writing about business but about the specific anxiety a first-time digital product creator feels before hitting publish.

The more precisely you describe the person and the problem, the more powerfully the right person connects with your work.

Write for one person. Imagine them reading. Would they stop scrolling? Would they think you wrote it for them?

If yes, publish it.

If it feels too generic, too safe, too broadly applicable, cut it down until it is specific enough to sting.

Precision is the product. Everything else is just words.
