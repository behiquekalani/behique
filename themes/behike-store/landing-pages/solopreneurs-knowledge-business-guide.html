From the Guide

What the guide actually says

Economics

The cost to sell the thousandth copy is zero

The cost to create a digital product is fixed and paid once. There is no manufacturing cost, no shipping cost, no inventory risk. Once created, a digital product is an asset that generates revenue on demand. This is the structural advantage that separates knowledge businesses from every other model at the same revenue level.

Packaging

Buyers do not pay for information. They pay for transformation.

"A complete guide to email marketing, covering 22 different strategies" is information packaging. "How to write your first 100-person email list and send your first campaign in 7 days" is transformation packaging. Same knowledge. The second version outsells the first because it tells the buyer exactly where they will be after purchasing it.

Authority

Authority means demonstrated understanding, not credentials

You do not need a degree, a job title, or a media appearance to sell knowledge effectively. Authority in this context means one thing: when a potential buyer reads your writing and thinks "this person gets exactly what I am dealing with." That is earned through specificity, not a biography. Most people have more authority than they recognize.

Positioning

The market does not reward the most comprehensive. It rewards the most specific.

Strong positioning creates a self-selection filter. The right person reads your positioning and immediately recognizes themselves in it. The wrong person self-selects out. This increases conversion rates dramatically compared to broad positioning that tries to speak to everyone. Every knowledge business that underperforms is trying to serve too broad an audience.

6-Month Plan

What each month builds toward

The guide includes a full month-by-month execution sequence. Here is the summary.

Month 1

Foundation

Identify your knowledge category, write your positioning statement, choose one content platform, publish 12 pieces of content about your specific topic.

Month 2

Validation

Conduct 3-5 free conversations with your target audience. Take detailed notes. This is product research. What they say becomes your sales copy.

Month 3

First product

Build one digital product, narrow scope, $15-$50 price point. Create a simple sales page. Announce to your existing audience. Gather honest feedback.

Month 4

Distribution

Build a lead magnet, set up a welcome email sequence, reach out to relevant communities and newsletters. Target 100 email subscribers by end of month.

Month 5

Second product and consulting

Build a complementary second product. Offer consulting at an hourly rate to 2-3 clients. Use those conversations to learn what a course could cover.

Month 6

Course beta and review

Outline and beta-launch a course at a discounted price. Review total revenue across formats. Set targets for the next six months based on what is working.
