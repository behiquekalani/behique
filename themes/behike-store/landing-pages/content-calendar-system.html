The Real Problem

The calendar is not the problem.
The design is.

Most content calendars were built for marketing teams. They assume consistent output from multiple people. One creator trying to run a five-person publishing schedule breaks within a month.

The result: missed posts, accumulated guilt, bursts of output followed by silence. The algorithm penalizes you. Momentum disappears. You conclude you have a discipline problem. You do not.

The Three Pillars

Consistency is not about willpower.

01

Plan Themes, Not Posts

Themes generate dozens of posts. Planning at the post level is where strategy collapses into busywork.

02

Buffer Before You Publish

A two-week buffer means life does not break your schedule. Build the buffer first, then publish.

03

Work With Your Cycles

Produce aggressively in high-capacity periods. Publish from the buffer in low-capacity periods. Never force.
