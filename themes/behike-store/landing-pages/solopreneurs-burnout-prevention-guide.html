The Real Problem

Burnout is a misalignment problem, not a workload problem.

You can work 60 hours a week on something aligned with who you are and not burn out. You can work 30 hours a week on the wrong things and be finished in six months. The standard advice addresses hours. This guide addresses the system.

M

Meaning Erosion

The original reason you went solo was autonomy and meaningful work. Scope creep and client accommodation gradually rebuild the exact conditions you were trying to escape.

A

Agency Collapse

When every hour is already spoken for by someone else's needs, the fundamental freedom of solopreneur work disappears. A vacation does not fix a structural problem.

D

Chronic Dread

The persistent background resistance to the day is the early warning sign. It appears months before the crash. Most solopreneurs dismiss it until it becomes paralysis.

"Most burnout fixes operate at the wrong level. They tell you to take breaks within a business model that was never designed to be sustainable. This guide fixes the model."
