The Real Problem

Three reasons solo consultants stay broke.

None of them are about expertise. The knowledge is usually there. The infrastructure is not.

01

Hourly pricing kills the ceiling

When you charge by the hour, clients pay for your input. Your income is capped by the hours you can work. Consultants who bill $200/hour and work 40 hours per week earn $32,000 per month gross. Consultants who price on value bill the same 40 hours and earn $80,000. The difference is not talent. It is structure.

02

No positioning means no referrals

"I help businesses grow" is not a position. Nobody refers that consultant because nobody remembers what they do or who they do it for. The consultant who "builds post-purchase retention systems for Shopify brands between $1M and $10M" gets referred constantly, because the description is specific enough to match against a name in someone's contact list.

03

No sales process means inconsistent revenue

Most solo consultants have no structured way to move from a conversation to a signed agreement. They send proposals too late, follow up once or not at all, and handle pricing objections by discounting. The result is a revenue line that spikes and collapses with every project cycle. This guide installs the process that fixes it.

The 3 Models

Which consulting archetype are you?

Every solo consulting practice is built on one of three operational models. Knowing yours is the first structural decision you make.

◆

The Advisor

Brought in to think. Assesses, analyzes, and recommends without executing. The value is judgment. Typically works on monthly retainer. Common models: fractional executives, strategy advisors, board-level consultants.

$3K-$15K per client per month
▶

The Implementer

Advises and executes. Comes in with a diagnosis and a plan, then builds the thing. Sets up systems, trains teams, launches campaigns. Charges per project or retainer with hands-on deliverables included.

$5K-$25K per project
◇

The Trainer

Teaches teams how to do something. Runs workshops, builds internal capability, transfers methodology. Charges per day or per workshop. Productized content makes this model highly scalable once materials are built.

$3K-$15K per engagement
