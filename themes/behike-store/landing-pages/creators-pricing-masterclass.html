The Problem

Undercharging is not
the safe choice.

Most creators think low prices are the conservative option. Charge less, sell more, avoid rejection. The actual cost of undercharging is $12,000 per year in foregone revenue, a catalog full of difficult buyers, and a brand positioned at the bottom of its market.

01

Cheap means accessible means more buyers

Lowering your price does not proportionally increase volume. It signals low value, attracts price-sensitive buyers, and makes it harder to raise prices later. The $47 product usually outperforms the $7 product in total revenue and buyer quality.

02

I have not earned the right to charge more

This is a confidence problem disguised as a market assessment. Your price is not a reward for past performance. It is a statement about current value. Creators with less expertise but more confidence charge more and convert fine.

03

I should see what sells before committing to a price

Launching at low prices to "test the market" trains your audience to expect cheap and makes price increases feel like a betrayal. If a product does not convert at its correct price, the problem is the positioning or the copy, not the price.

04

High prices will scare people away

They will scare away price-sensitive buyers who were going to be high-maintenance and low-commitment. That is a filter, not a loss. The goal is not to convert everyone. It is to convert the right buyers at a price that reflects what you built.
