Chapter Previews

A sample of what you'll read

On the consulting ceiling

"The knowledge that makes you effective with clients did not come from a single project. It came from dozens of engagements, failures, iterations, and pattern recognition built over years. That pattern recognition is not visible to your clients. They see the output. You see the thinking behind it. The course packages that thinking."

On productization

"A good consulting framework has three properties: it is sequential, it is transferable, and it is falsifiable. If your process depends on you reading the room or making judgment calls you cannot explain, that is a coaching layer, not a course layer."

On pricing

"A $47 course on a topic worth thousands signals one of two things to a buyer: either the creator does not believe in the value, or the content is surface-level. Neither creates confidence. Pricing communicates quality before the buyer sees a single module."

On the transition

"This guide is specifically designed to eliminate the binary. You do not have to choose between consulting and courses. You build the course while keeping consulting income flowing, then shift the ratio deliberately over 90 days."

The Plan

Your 90-day migration at a glance

Days 1-15

Extract and validate

Extraction interviews on your last three engagements. Identify the one problem your course will solve. Validate with five former clients before writing a single module.

Days 16-30

Build the outline

Map the five-module structure to your framework. Create a detailed outline and a simple sales page. Set a launch date 45 days out.

Days 31-60

Create and pre-sell

Record the first two modules. Send your sales page to your ten warmest former clients. Open pre-sales at a launch discount.

Days 61-75

Launch and build

Open public sales. Complete remaining modules based on student pace. Run the first live Q&A. Collect testimonials from early buyers.

Days 76-90

Optimize and shift

Review completion rates. Update the weakest module. Calculate course revenue vs consulting hourly equivalent. Decide which consulting work continues.
