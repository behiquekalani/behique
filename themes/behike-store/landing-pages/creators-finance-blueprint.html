Core system

The three-account system

The most common creator financial disaster is simple: spending money that was already owed in taxes. Three accounts, three purposes, zero confusion about what is actually yours to spend.

1

Operating Account

All revenue enters here. All business expenses paid from here. Your salary transfers out from here. Maintain a 1-2 month buffer.

2

Tax Account

25-30% of every deposit, transferred the same day revenue arrives. Not accessible for anything else. Quarterly payments come from here.

3

Personal Savings

Your emergency fund, short-term goals, and investment staging area. Funded automatically through a fixed transfer from your personal account.

Build order

The sequence that protects you as you grow

Financial stability is not built all at once. It is built in a specific order. These are the priorities, in order.

First

One-month cash buffer

Before anything else. This prevents a slow month from creating debt. The most urgent priority for any creator without it.

Second

Three-account structure

The structural separation that prevents tax money from being spent on living expenses. Set it up before the tax account matters.

Third

6-9 month emergency fund

Built over 12-18 months via automated transfer. Based on essential expenses (rent, utilities, groceries, minimums), not total lifestyle spending.

Fourth

Retirement account

Roth IRA first for most creators. Solo 401(k) when income justifies it. Percentage-based, not fixed-amount. Scales with your income automatically.

When income crosses $30K

LLC formation + accountant

Liability protection becomes meaningful and a specialist CPA saves more than they cost. Do not rush this step; do not skip it indefinitely.
