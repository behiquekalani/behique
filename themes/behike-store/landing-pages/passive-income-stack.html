The problem

Three myths that keep people stuck

Before building any income stream, you need to clear out what you have been told. Most of it is wrong in ways that cost real time.

Myth 01

"Passive income means no work"

The delivery is passive. The build is not. Every durable income stream requires serious construction time upfront. The people earning $3,000 per month from digital products built and tested and listed products for 6 to 9 months before they got there.

The correct word is scalable, not passive.

Myth 02

"You need an audience before you can earn"

The first income stream in this stack requires zero audience. You need a product and a listing. Your first sale will likely come from organic search on Gumroad or Etsy, not from followers. Audience amplifies what is already working. It does not create it.

Start building. The audience follows the track record.

Myth 03

"Build all streams simultaneously for faster results"

This is the most expensive mistake. Six half-built systems produce zero revenue. One complete, optimized system with traction produces real cash flow that buys you time to build the second. Sequence determines outcome more than any other variable.

One stream at a time, every 60 days.
