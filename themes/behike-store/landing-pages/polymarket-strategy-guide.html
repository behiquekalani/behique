The Real Problem

Most people approach prediction markets like gambling. It is not gambling.

The gambler bets on preference. The investor holds indefinitely. The prediction market trader buys a probability assessment and profits if their estimate is more accurate than the market's. These are structurally different activities.

🎲

Trading sentiment instead of probability

Most new traders start from social media and general opinion. They are not trading mispriced probabilities. They are trading sentiment that has already been priced in by people who formed the same opinion earlier. That is negative expected value by definition.

📈

No position sizing framework

Betting too much destroys accounts even with a real edge. Betting too little wastes the edge you have. Without a mathematical framework for how much to risk on any position, even correct analysis produces poor returns.

👀

Not knowing when you have edge

The question is never "what do I think will happen." It is "is the market mispricing this event, and do I have a specific reason to believe it is?" If you cannot answer that specifically, you do not have identified edge. You have an opinion.
