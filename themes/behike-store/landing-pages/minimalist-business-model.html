The Problem

Three complexity traps
that stall solopreneurs

Most online businesses fail before they start because complexity is mistaken for progress. These are the three most common traps.

Trap 01

The 5x20 Trap

Running five projects at 20% effort each. No single thing reaches the compounding threshold. You're always building, never growing. Five partial businesses is zero real businesses.

Trap 02

Platform ADD

Spreading content across six platforms before mastering one. You develop a shallow presence everywhere and a deep relationship nowhere. Algorithms reward consistency. Audiences reward clarity.

Trap 03

The Complexity Tax

Every unnecessary tool, offer, and project drains attention silently. Most solopreneurs pay this tax on dozens of things at once and wonder why the fundamentals never compound.
