The Core Argument

Building infrastructure before you have clients is productive procrastination.

The solopreneur who waits for everything to be ready before selling will wait a long time. The one who builds the minimum viable service, sells it, delivers it, and iterates will have a real business inside of 90 days.

Most solopreneurs fail before they start because they are trying to build a finished product when what they need is a working draft. They design service packages, write intake forms, and build onboarding sequences before they have a single client. By the time they launch, they have burned their motivation on infrastructure that no client has ever tested.

The minimum viable service is the alternative. The smallest, most specific offer you can deliver well right now, to one specific person, in exchange for real money. This guide shows you how to build it, sell it, and evolve it into a signature offer.

The Offer Test

Three questions that validate any service offer before you pitch it.

If your offer fails any of these, it is not ready to sell. The questions are simple. Getting clear answers is the actual work.

Question 01

What is the specific problem?

Not a category. A named problem a specific person experiences. Could they hear it and immediately know whether it applies to them?

Question 02

Who is the specific person?

Not a demographic. A specific role, situation, or context. You should be able to picture them clearly enough to write a pitch directly to them.

Question 03

What is the specific outcome?

Not the process. The result. What does done look like for the client? Can they evaluate whether they want that before buying?
