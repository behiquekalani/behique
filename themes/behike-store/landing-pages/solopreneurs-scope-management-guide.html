What you will actually read

On scope documents

The excluded items section most solopreneurs skip

Explicitly list what is NOT included in the scope. "This scope does not include e-commerce functionality, email marketing integration, or post-launch technical support beyond two weeks." Explicit exclusions prevent the assumption that everything not mentioned is included.

On change requests

The response that preserves the relationship

"I would love to include that. Let me put together a change request so we can get it properly scoped and priced." This confirms you can do the work, signals that additional work comes with additional cost, buys time to evaluate the request, and keeps the conversation professional.

On limits

Professional limits build more respect than endless accommodation

Clients who respect you do not leave when you enforce professional limits. Clients who leave because you enforced your contract were not clients worth keeping. Limits signal that you run a real business with real processes. Most clients respond positively to that signal.

On pricing

Hourly pricing is the scope creep model

Every extra hour is a cost you absorb or a line item you have to justify. Value-based pricing ties your fee to the outcome, not the time. When scope expands, you adjust through a change request. The conversation becomes about value, not hours worked.

"Scope creep does not just eat hours. It eats quality, energy, and the capacity to serve other clients well. The solopreneur who absorbs every extra request eventually delivers mediocre work on everything."
