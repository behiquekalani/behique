The Trap

Custom consulting is a ceiling.
Productized consulting removes it.

When you sell time, your income is capped by hours. When you sell outcomes packaged into a repeatable process, your income is capped by demand.

⏰

Time-based billing

Every hour billed is an hour of your life exchanged. There is no leverage in that model. The ceiling is the number of hours you can work in a week.

🔄

Scope creep

Custom work has no natural boundaries. Clients ask for more. Engagements expand. Margins compress. You end up working more for the same price you quoted three weeks ago.

🔱

Starting from scratch

Every new custom client is a new problem, a new negotiation, a new process. Nothing compounds. The tenth client costs the same to onboard as the first.

The Framework

Three criteria before
you productize anything.

Not every service can or should be productized. These three tests tell you whether you have the raw material for a repeatable offer.

01

The problem is specific and recurring

The same core problem appears in five of your last ten clients. If every client arrives with a completely different situation, you cannot build a template around it. Repetition in the problem is the raw material for a productized solution.

02

The output is definable in one to three sentences

If your deliverable requires a phone call to explain, it is not scoped tightly enough. A productized service should be describable in plain language: what the client receives, in what format, by what date.

03

The delivery process can be templated

You can document the same intake form, the same production workflow, the same output structure for every engagement. The content changes. The process does not. If the process is different every time, the work cannot be productized.
