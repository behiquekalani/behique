The Real Problem

Most solopreneurs design their workspace by accident.

They set up wherever is convenient and tolerate the rest. The result is a workspace that looks like a workspace but functions like a distraction machine. They diagnose the problem as motivation or discipline. The real problem is environmental design.

■

Constant context switching

Interruptions that cost 10 to 25 minutes of recovery time each, adding up to hours of lost capacity per day.

■

Digital environment on default

Notifications, badges, and open tabs pulling attention away from the work that actually moves the business.

■

No deep work architecture

Working in shallow mode all day while believing you are doing serious work, because the environment makes deep work impossible.

■

Mixed-signal spaces

Working from spaces the brain associates with rest and distraction, so the work signal never fully loads.
