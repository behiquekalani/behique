The Problem

The hamster wheel is a systems problem, not a creativity problem.

Most creators conflate three distinct activities: thinking, creating, and publishing. When you try to do all three in the same session, you tax different parts of your brain simultaneously.

The result is mediocre work, creative exhaustion, and the persistent feeling that you are always behind.

Batching separates these activities into dedicated blocks. Each block gets your full attention. Nothing competes with anything else. The result is more output, better quality, and actual creative energy left at the end of the week.
