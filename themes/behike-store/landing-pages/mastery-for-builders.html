Robert Greene's Framework, Updated

Three phases. One path.

Greene mapped how every master got there. This book maps what that looks like when AI is in the room.

Phase 01
Apprenticeship

You submit to the learning process. You absorb more than you produce. The goal is not output. The goal is building the internal model that makes good output possible. With AI, you can compress this phase by weeks, not years. You get a sparring partner available at 2am who never gets tired of your questions.

Phase 02
Creative-Active

You start combining things. The internal model starts generating new ideas. This is where ADHD builders often crash, because the hyperfocus that got you through apprenticeship now pulls you in twenty directions. This book gives you a constraint system so the divergence becomes productive, not scattered.

Phase 03
Mastery

You see patterns others miss. Your intuition is calibrated. You are not faster than other people, you are deeper. AI does not replace this phase. It accelerates the path to it by collapsing the feedback loop between attempt and correction. The reps compound faster.

Chapter 03 Preview

The ADHD brain is not a broken normal brain. It is a different system.

Most productivity advice assumes you are neurotypical and just need discipline. This book does not. Here is what your brain actually does well, and how it maps directly to the mastery process.

⚡

Hyperfocus as a depth tool

Neurotypical people cannot spend 6 hours on one problem without losing steam. You can. The apprenticeship phase requires sustained deep immersion. You have a natural capacity for that. The skill is not creating it. It is directing it.

∞

Task-switching as cross-domain synthesis

Masters in Greene's research almost always drew from multiple fields. The connection between fields is where original insight lives. Your brain moves between domains faster than most. That is not distractibility. That is a synthesis engine.

△

Pattern recognition at scale

ADHD brains are often faster at detecting anomalies and underlying patterns than neurotypical brains. In mastery terms, this means you can see the system faster. You just need enough reps to trust what you are seeing.

◇

Big-picture orientation

You naturally ask why before how. In the creative-active phase, that orientation is what lets you see where a field is going instead of just where it is. The builders who ship things that feel ahead of their time are usually not smarter. They are just starting from a different angle.

Chapter 05 Preview

The 30-Day Skill Sprint

A protocol, not a schedule. The structure is fixed. The skill you apply it to is yours. Each week has a different objective and a different relationship with AI.

Week 1
Foundation
  • Map the skill to its core components
  • Identify the 20% that produces 80% of output
  • Build your AI critique prompt system
  • Set your daily minimum viable rep
  • Define what done looks like at day 30
Week 2
Deliberate Practice
  • Daily reps at the edge of your ability
  • AI critique after every session
  • Constraint drills: one variable at a time
  • Pattern log: what keeps breaking
  • Mid-sprint calibration check
Week 3
Ship
  • Publish a real output to a real audience
  • External feedback vs. AI feedback comparison
  • Identify the gap between practice and live performance
  • Iterate publicly, not in drafts
  • Ship at least three times
Week 4
Reflect and Stack
  • Document what changed and what did not
  • Write the gap analysis: where is the ceiling now
  • Design sprint 2 based on what you found
  • Build the next constraint set
  • Rest. The consolidation happens offline.
