On specialization

Exclusion is a feature, not a bug

When you niche down, you stop being background noise for the wrong people. You become signal for the right ones. The right people pay. The wrong people scroll. Every piece of content a specialist publishes compounds. A generalist's content does not. Specialists build portfolios. Generalists build archives.

On authority content

The gap between filling a feed and building authority

Good authority content says "most people think X, but the more accurate model is Y, and here is why." This positions you as someone who has thought past the conventional wisdom. You are not repeating what others say. You are processing information and producing insight.

On monetization

Pricing as an authority signal

Most creators underprice out of insecurity. Low prices send a signal that contradicts authority. An authority figure who prices at a discount implicitly says they are not sure they deserve full price. Price based on the outcome your audience gets, not the hours you spent creating the product.

On collaboration

The most efficient trust-building mechanism available

When someone your audience already trusts vouches for you, a portion of their authority transfers to you. This is not manipulation. It is how human social cognition works. Be specific in your pitches. Give before you ask. The most durable collaborations start with generosity.
