The single-product ceiling

One product means one type of customer.
That is a ceiling, not a business.

A single product captures one slice of demand. The customers who want less are not served. The customers who want more have nowhere to go. A product suite solves both problems at the same time.

💳

You only capture one willingness-to-pay

A $49 product is invisible to the person who will spend $499. It is also inaccessible to the person who only has $9.99 right now. A suite serves the entire demand curve, not one point on it.

👥

No natural path for buyers

A customer who buys your $27 product and loves it has nowhere to go next. Without a logical next step, they leave instead of staying in your ecosystem. You lose the relationship at the moment it peaks.

📈

Revenue ceiling without more launches

Growing a single-product business means constantly acquiring new customers. A suite means the same customer can generate 10x or 50x the initial purchase value through a designed progression.

Free to $499.
