The specificity spectrum

Five levels of positioning clarity

Generalist

"I help businesses with marketing."

Competes with everyone. Differentiates on price or personal relationship only.

Category Specialist

"I help small businesses with email marketing."

A defined service, but still competing in a crowded category. Price is still the primary differentiator.

Audience Specialist

"I help e-commerce businesses with email marketing."

The audience is defined. You can speak the specific language of the problem. Better conversion, stronger referrals.

Problem Specialist

"I help e-commerce businesses improve email deliverability."

The problem is defined. You are the person they call when the specific pain is acute. Expertise is concentrated and demonstrable.

Outcome Specialist

"I help e-commerce businesses using Klaviyo increase email revenue by 20% without increasing send frequency."

Audience, tool, problem, specific measurable outcome. Anyone with that exact problem knows immediately you are talking to them.

01

The referral loop mechanics

A generalist client may not know who else to refer. A client who hired "the person who specializes in email list growth for health coaches" will refer every health coach they know. Specificity makes referrals easy.

02

The one-sentence positioning test

"I help [specific audience] solve [specific problem] and get [specific outcome]." If you would put that sentence in your website headline right now, your niche is ready to test. If it sounds too narrow, you have found your resistance point.

03

The 10-conversation validation method

Contact 10 people who fit your audience description. Have problem conversations, not pitch conversations. If eight or more confirm the problem is real and costly, proceed. If fewer than six do, adjust the niche.

04

The three pivot types

Audience pivot, problem pivot, outcome pivot. Each one requires different actions and preserves different existing assets. Know which type of pivot you need before you start changing anything.

05

The risk-adjusted pricing argument

A specialist in email deliverability for e-commerce brands has solved the problem 20 times. A generalist has solved it twice. The risk-adjusted value of hiring the specialist is significantly higher. This is how you justify the premium, not with confidence, but with economic logic.

06

The exit signal pattern

Three consecutive quarters of declining inbound interest, rising client churn, and inability to find new buyers after active effort. That is a pattern. A single slow month is not. Know the difference before making a pivot decision.
