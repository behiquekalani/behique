The Problem

Underpricing does not just cost you money.
It costs you clients, respect, and time.

Most solopreneurs price based on what feels safe, not what the market will bear. The result is a business that works harder than it should for less than it should earn. There are three specific ways this shows up.

Cost 01

Wrong clients at scale

Low prices attract buyers who are optimizing for the lowest cost. These are the clients who scope-creep, push back on every invoice, and treat you like an expense rather than an asset. Higher prices filter for people with real skin in the game.

Cost 02

Revenue left on the table permanently

At $9.99 per digital product, you need 1,001 sales to earn $10,000. At $97, you need 103. The math compounds over time. Every month you spend underpriced is a month of revenue that cannot be recovered.

Cost 03

Positioning that works against you

Price is a signal. A $9 ebook tells the market this content is not worth protecting with real money. A $97 guide signals substance and seriousness before anyone reads a word. Underpricing undermines every other element of your positioning.

The Framework

Every digital product market has three natural tiers. Most solopreneurs ignore two of them.

Understanding where your product fits, and why each tier attracts different buyer psychology, changes how you build and price everything.

Tier 1
$7 – $37
Impulse / Entry

Buyers decide in seconds. Low commitment, higher refund rates. Works as a lead magnet or entry point. Not a business model on its own.

  • Checklists and short guides
  • Quick-win templates
  • Top-of-funnel entry
Tier 2 — Core Market
$47 – $197
Considered Purchase

Buyers read your sales page. They have a specific problem. They research and then decide. Lower refund rates. Higher implementation rates. This is where most products should live.

  • Comprehensive guides and courses
  • Systems and frameworks
  • Multi-format bundles
Tier 3
$297 – $997+
Investment Decision

Buyers treat this as a real investment. They research you, read the sales page multiple times, and consider the ROI. They show up and do the work.

  • Cohort courses and programs
  • Transformation-oriented systems
  • High-touch or live components
