Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On keyword research

Comment sections are the best keyword tool you are not using

When a video ranks for a keyword, the people who find it through search leave comments that reveal related questions, adjacent interests, and follow-up searches. Read the comment sections of high-performing competitor videos and record every question and phrase that appears multiple times.

On metadata

Your description should be a short article, not a keyword dump

Write the first two sentences naturally with your primary keyword in them. Then outline the main topics using natural language. YouTube indexes description text and weights the opening sentences heavily. Your description is a content signal, not a tag list.

On Shorts

A Short that tells viewers to watch the full video performs worse

A Short that partially answers a question and redirects to a long-form video produces worse retention metrics than one that delivers complete value on its own. Deliver the full answer. The viewer who trusts you will find the long-form content without being prompted.

On thumbnails

Thumbnail text should expand on the title, not repeat it

If your title says what the video is about, your thumbnail text should add what makes it worth watching. A specific outcome, a surprising number, or a qualifying phrase that speaks to the exact viewer you want to click. Title plus thumbnail is a complete pitch.
