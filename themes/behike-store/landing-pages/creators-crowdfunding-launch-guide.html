The real issue

Most Campaigns Fail Before They Go Live

60-70% of successfully funded projects hit their goal in the first 48 hours. That is not luck. That is a pre-launch list built over 6-8 weeks. The platform does not drive your traffic. Your audience does.

No pre-launch list

Going live without signups means starting at zero when the algorithm is watching. The first 48 hours determine whether the platform promotes you to strangers or buries you.

Reward tiers priced wrong

Round numbers feel arbitrary. Too many tiers create decision fatigue. Most creators leave 30-40% of potential revenue on the table through poor tier structure alone.

Campaign page that describes, not converts

Features are not benefits. A visitor who arrives skeptical needs a structured argument for why this exists and why you can deliver it. Description is not that argument.

No communication system post-fund

Backers who funded a project and heard nothing for two months become detractors. One honest delay update keeps 95% of backers on your side. Silence costs you reviews and referrals.
