The Problem With Most Attempts

Deep expertise does not automatically translate to consulting income. The packaging does.

Most people with genuine expertise never build a consulting practice. Not because they lack knowledge. Because they lack the structure to present it, price it, and sell it.

The patterns that kill consulting practices before they start:

  • Positioning so broad that no specific client sees themselves in the offer
  • Pricing by the hour instead of by the value of the outcome, which signals service provider, not authority
  • Waiting for referrals instead of engineering a client acquisition system from the beginning
  • Accepting every client who is willing to pay, then building a reputation for mediocre results with wrong-fit clients
  • Treating consulting like freelancing, which creates a ceiling based on available hours rather than expertise leverage
  • No productized offer, which means every engagement is scoped from scratch and client acquisition never becomes systematic
