The Problem

Three ways producers waste AI inside FL Studio

The AI tools are already in the ecosystem. The gap is workflow knowledge, not access.

01

Using AI mastering as a final export step only

Most producers run a master through an AI engine at the end and wonder why it sounds the same. AI mastering works differently at different stages of the mix. Using it only at the end is the least useful application.

02

Treating stem separation as a gimmick

Producers download it, separate one track, listen to the artifacts, and never open it again. Stem separation is one of the highest-leverage tools for sampling, mixing reference tracks, and creating variations. Most people never learn the actual workflow.

03

Ignoring AI melody and automation assist

The melody generation and automation clip tools inside FL Studio are underused because they require a specific setup to produce usable output. Without knowing the input conditions, the results sound generic. With the right setup, they become a creative accelerator.
