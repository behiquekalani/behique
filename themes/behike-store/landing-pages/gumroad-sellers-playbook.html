The 7-Part Product Page Structure

1
Headline: Specific Outcome + Time Frame + Without Common Objection

Not your product name. A clear statement of what changes for the buyer. Written for a stranger who knows nothing about you.

2
Problem frame (2-3 lines)

Name the exact situation your buyer is in right now. Specificity here signals you understand their problem better than they do.

3
Why the usual approach fails

One or two sentences. This is where you disqualify bad alternatives and position your guide as the direct path.

4
5-8 benefit bullets (not feature bullets)

Each bullet names something the buyer will know or be able to do after reading. Not chapter names. Not topics covered.

5
Social proof (reviews or beta reader quotes)

Five honest reviews on a Gumroad product page measurably increase conversion rate. The guide covers exactly how to get them fast.

6
Who this is for (3 specific personas)

Narrow targeting increases conversion. Buyers who see themselves in your description convert at higher rates than buyers addressed generically.

7
Price anchor and CTA

State the price and what they get one more time. Make the action completely clear. No clever phrasing. Just the button.
