The Problem

Good products fail because of bad launches

The product was not the issue. The launch process was. Most creators build something real, then spend two days "launching" it. They post once, get twelve sales, and call it a flop. The product had nothing to do with it.

A launch is a system. It has a before, a during, and an after. Each phase has specific work. Skip the before and the during feels like shouting into a void. Skip the after and you leave 30-60% of your revenue behind.

⚠

No pre-launch phase

They announce a product their audience has never heard them talk about. Cold traffic to a cold offer.

⚠

One-day launch

Post on Day 1, go quiet, close on Day 2. Most buyers decide on Day 7. They were never given the chance.

⚠

No post-launch system

The cart closes and the creator moves on. The 40% of buyers who needed a nudge never got one.

⚠

Unaddressed objections

Objections in DMs and comments go unanswered. Every unanswered objection is a sale that did not happen.
