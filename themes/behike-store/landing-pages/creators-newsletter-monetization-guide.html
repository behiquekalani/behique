Inside the guide

The frameworks, explained.

Model selection

Four models. Most newsletters should use two or three.

Paid subscriptions, sponsorships, product sales, and affiliate revenue each have different ceilings, different minimum list requirements, and different trust prerequisites. The guide maps which model fits which stage of list growth, and how to layer them without splitting your focus.

Product sales carry the highest long-term margin. Sponsorships are the fastest path to revenue at small list sizes. The sequence matters.

Welcome sequence

Five emails. Two weeks. The window closes after that.

New subscribers are at peak attention when they opt in. The guide covers each email in the sequence: orientation, origin story, best work, original framework, and soft offer. Including the exact spacing, the framing for each, and why the sequence falls apart when you wait too long.

Email 5 introduces a paid product in context. Not as a pitch. As a natural extension of what they already received.

Sponsor pitching

Small lists can get sponsors. Most are pitching wrong.

Sponsors care about audience fit and engagement, not just list size. A niche newsletter at 1,200 subscribers with a 45% open rate is more valuable to the right sponsor than a generic list at 8,000. The guide gives the exact four-paragraph pitch structure and rate benchmarks by list size.

Under 1K subs: $50-$150 per placement. 1K-3K subs: $150-$500. These numbers assume open rates above 30% and a defined niche.
