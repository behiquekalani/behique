The Problem

Three ways traditional
networking backfires

Most networking advice optimizes for the wrong thing. It teaches you how to ask, not how to give. It builds volume, not trust. Here is where the standard playbook breaks down.

01

The transactional trap

You reach out when you need something. The person on the receiving end immediately reads you as someone trying to extract value. There is no prior deposit, no history of reciprocity. The ask arrives in isolation and it fails.

02

LinkedIn is a cold calling platform

The platform's architecture trains people to treat outreach like volume sales. Most inboxes have a two-bucket filter: people I know, and people trying to sell me something. Template messages land in the second bucket regardless of intent.

03

Deficit networking

People network when they need something, which means they are always operating from a position of urgency. Urgency reads as desperation. Desperation is the least compelling posture you can bring to a new relationship.

The Framework

The 3-Tier Network Map

Not everyone in your network deserves the same attention. These three tiers create a structure for who to focus on, how to engage differently, and where the real leverage lives.

Tier 1

Peers

15-30 contacts. Monthly touchpoints.

People at your current level or within one tier. Building in the same space, facing the same problems. The most undervalued relationships in most people's networks.

The person who is your peer today is a key decision-maker in three years. Build now, before there is a power differential.

Tier 2

Aspirational

5-10 contacts. Quarterly touchpoints.

People 1-3 tiers above your current level whose work is specifically relevant to where you are heading. Not generically impressive. Specifically relevant to your trajectory.

Quality over quantity. One strong Tier 2 relationship outperforms fifty dormant connections with senior people.

Tier 3

Connectors

3-7 contacts. High reciprocity required.

People whose value is the breadth and quality of their own network. Community builders, podcast hosts, organizers. They can compress the timeline from months to days.

One introduction from the right connector is worth more than a hundred cold messages. These relationships require genuine reciprocity first.
