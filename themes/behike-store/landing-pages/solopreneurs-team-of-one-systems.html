Inside the guide

How the systems actually work.

Client intake

From inquiry to signed contract in five steps and 15 minutes.

The first email response is a template. It acknowledges the inquiry, sets a response window, and asks for the three pieces of information you need before a call is productive. This filters poor fits before you invest call time. The guide covers every step, every template, and when to use each one.

Do not start work before the contract is signed. Every time you have, you have regretted it. The system removes the pressure to skip this step.

Finance system

The quarterly review is the session most solopreneurs skip.

It is also the session that generates the most revenue over a one to three year period. Two hours, end of each quarter. Review the income statement. Confirm tax estimates. Evaluate pricing against the market. Assess which clients are profitable. The guide gives the exact structure and the decisions to make at each step.

Most pricing decisions get deferred indefinitely without a scheduled forcing function. The quarterly review is that function.

The sprint

Four hours. Three systems. Everything out of your head.

Hours one and two: write the process document for each task identified in the audit. Every step you currently take, in order, including the tools and the decisions. Hour three: build the supporting templates. Hour four: test the system from start to finish using only the document. No memory allowed. Any gaps found during the test get filled immediately.

By the end of hour four, the document is usable by someone who has never done the task. That is the test.
