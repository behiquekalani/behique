The Problem

Most solopreneurs build first and figure out legal later.

That works until it does not. A dispute, a copycat, a platform ban, or a tax notice can cost more to fix than it would have cost to prevent. Legal basics are not a burden. They are infrastructure.

?

LLC or sole proprietor?

Most people default to whatever is easiest. The choice carries real consequences for liability and taxes that are worth understanding before revenue starts.

?

No contract, no protection.

Clients who do not pay. Scope that keeps expanding. Work delivered but never used. A clear contract is what prevents each of these from becoming your problem.

?

Who owns your work?

Copyright, trademark, and licensing are not just for big companies. If you create digital products, you need to understand what you own and how to protect it.

?

Terms of service as an afterthought.

Copied terms from another site. No refund policy. No IP ownership clause. These gaps are where disputes start and where you have no ground to stand on.

?

Privacy policy requirements.

GDPR, CAN-SPAM, and state-level laws apply to you if you collect email addresses. Not knowing the requirements does not exempt you from them.

?

Brand with no protection.

Someone registers your business name as a trademark. Someone sells a product using your brand. Without basic brand protection, you have no clear recourse.
