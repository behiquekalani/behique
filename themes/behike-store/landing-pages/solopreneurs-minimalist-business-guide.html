Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On complexity

The audit question

If you could only keep three things in your business, what would you keep? Not what you think you should keep. Not what you have the most clients for. What, if you had to start over, would you build around because it produces the best results, uses your best skills, and earns the most with the least friction?

On offers

Fewer clients at higher prices

A solopreneur who needs 20 clients at $500 per month lives a fundamentally different life than one who needs 5 clients at $2,000 per month. Same revenue. The second model has fewer relationships to manage, lower delivery overhead, and more time per client to produce exceptional work.

On time

The single daily priority

Each working day begins with one answer: what is the one thing I need to complete today for this to be a successful day? Not a list. Not five priorities. One. Do that thing first, during your protected morning block, before any reactive work begins. Everything else is secondary.

On enough

Designing for "enough"

Know the monthly revenue that, if you earned it reliably, would genuinely be enough. Then design your business to hit that number with the simplest possible model. Anything beyond that is a choice, not a requirement. This clarity changes every business decision you make.
