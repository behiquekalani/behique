Why Referrals Don't Happen

You're treating referrals as one-off asks. They're a system failure, not a relationship failure.

Most solopreneurs know they should be building a referral pipeline. Most aren't. The problem is almost never the quality of their work. It's five specific system failures that kill referrals before they happen.

Failure 01 Wrong timing
Asking before results are clear, or after the relationship has gone cold. The window closes fast. Most solopreneurs miss it every time.
Failure 02 No concrete action
"Let me know if you think of anyone" is not a request. Without something specific to do, intention evaporates. The client meant to refer you and never did.
Failure 03 Wrong framing
The ask is framed around your pipeline needs instead of the referred person's benefit. This turns endorsement into favor. People don't refer enthusiastically when it feels transactional.
Failure 04 No follow-up
Client says they know someone. You say great. Nothing happens. No follow-up because you don't want to seem pushy. The referral dies in the gap between intention and action.
Failure 05 No close loop
Introduction made. Project won. The referring client hears nothing. No reinforcement. The behavior that produced the referral is never rewarded.
