Inside the Guide

What the Chapters Actually Cover

The Voice Audit

Pull 20 pieces of your best content, read them without your name attached, and ask one question: is there something here that could only come from one person? Most creators discover that about 20-30% of their content is genuinely theirs. The rest is learned scaffolding. The audit shows you exactly where the line is.

Opinion Calibration

Two failure modes exist on either end. Under-stating: "some people feel that X while others argue Y." This communicates nothing. Over-stating: permanent hot-take mode where everything is the most important thing ever said. The calibration that works is earned confidence. Specific, defensible, useful. You can be definitive on what you have earned the right to be definitive on.

The Language Patterns Framework

Every distinctive voice has a list of words it uses and words it refuses to use. The ban list is as important as the preferred words because it creates a productive constraint. When you cannot reach for the easy word, you are forced to find the accurate one. That accuracy is what makes your writing feel precise rather than generic.
