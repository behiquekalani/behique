Why most attempts fail

The three reasons nomad life collapses in the first 90 days

The lifestyle is not the problem. The missing foundation is.

Reason 01

They left before the income was stable

A pipeline is not the same as a paycheck. Most people leave with one or two clients and optimism. Six months later, the savings are gone and the clients have drifted. The income floor has to be real and consistent before departure, not projected.

Reason 02

They chose destinations on aesthetics, not infrastructure

Bali looks great in photos. So does a cafe in Lisbon. Neither matters if the internet drops every afternoon and your banking setup charges 3% on every transaction. Infrastructure decisions made before booking save months of friction.

Reason 03

They underestimated the compounding overhead

Every new city requires re-learning basics: transport, food, internet, language, safety. In small doses, this is energizing. When you are moving every two weeks across multiple countries, it becomes a cognitive tax that erodes the quality of your work and your presence.
