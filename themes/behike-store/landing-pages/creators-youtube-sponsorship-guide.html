Section Previews

A few excerpts from the guide.

On the pricing formula

Most first-time creators either wildly underprice or use an arbitrary number they pulled from a forum post. Both approaches lose deals. Underpricing signals inexperience and attracts brands that will require rewrites, endless revisions, and pay late.

Sponsorship Rate = (Average Views / 1,000) x CPM Target. For mid-rolls in a tech niche, use $20-40 CPM. Add a production fee of $100-300 depending on integration complexity. That is your number.

Finance, business, and software content commands $30-60 CPM. Lifestyle and entertainment runs $8-18 CPM. Your retention rate and engagement rate move you up or down within that range.

On negotiating without backing down

Most creators negotiate from a posture of gratitude. That posture gives the brand control. The correct posture is peer-to-peer: you have an audience that fits their customer profile, and you are evaluating whether this is a good fit for both parties.

If a brand comes in below your rate: "My standard rate for this format is X. If the budget is fixed, I can offer a shorter integration instead of the full mid-roll." Never drop your rate without getting something in return.

On retainer structure

A single sponsorship deal is a transaction. A retainer is a business relationship. After a successful single deal, send the brand a performance summary and one line: "I enjoyed working with this and I think continued visibility with my audience would compound well. If it makes sense, I'd be open to discussing a quarterly arrangement."

That is the entire pitch. You are not begging for continuity. You are offering a business case: compounding familiarity with your audience over multiple placements is more effective than a single shot.
