The 7-day plan

One task per day. One deliverable per day. No exceptions.

1
Day One

Choose your product

Complete the knowledge audit. Pick one topic. Write the one-sentence product description and commit to it.

2
Day Two

Build the outline

Write 8 to 15 chapter headings. Each covers one question or one step. If the outline takes longer than two hours, the scope is too broad.

3
Day Three

Write the first draft

Write without editing. 1,500 to 3,000 words. Messy is fine. Missing is not.

4
Day Four

Revise, polish, export

One pass. Fix confusing sections. Add one specific number or example to any section that feels vague. Export as PDF.

5
Day Five

Set up the product page

Gumroad account. Upload file. Write description. Set price. Cover image. Go live.

6
Day Six

Write the launch post

Three versions. Pick the best one. Post it on your most active platform. Pin it if you can.

7
Day Seven

DM outreach

Find 10 people who have expressed the problem your product solves. Send a personalized, non-pitchy message to each one. Log every response.

Questions

Do I need an existing audience to make this work?
No. The guide covers outreach to people who already have the problem you solve, not content marketing to a following you have not built yet. You can make your first sale with zero followers using the DM strategy in Chapter 7. An audience helps later. It is not required to start.
What if I do not know what to sell?
Chapter 2 is the knowledge audit. It is five questions designed to surface a product idea from what you already know. Most people who say they do not know what to sell have not done this exercise. After you do it, you will have at least two or three concrete options to choose from.
Is $14.99 the right price for a first product?
The guide covers this in detail in Chapter 5, including the math at different price points. The short answer is that $12 to $15 is the sweet spot for a first product with no reviews, and underpricing often hurts conversion more than it helps. There is a detailed explanation of the psychology behind this.
What format does the product have to be in?
The guide covers five formats that work at the first-product stage: guide, template, checklist, swipe file, and resource list. All of them are document-based. No video, no membership site, no software. You can build the simplest version of any of these in a single day.
What if I follow the 7-day plan and nothing sells?
Chapter 10 is the troubleshooting section. It covers the five most common reasons a first product does not sell and the specific fix for each one. Most first products find their first sale between day 7 and day 21. The guide tells you exactly what to check and adjust if you are past day 7 with no sale.
