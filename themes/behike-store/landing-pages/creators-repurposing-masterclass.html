Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On ideas

A core idea is not a topic. It is an argument.

"Content marketing" is a topic. "Most content marketing fails because creators optimize for volume instead of depth" is a core idea. The second one can be developed into 30 pieces. The first one generates generic posts that blend into every other generic post in your feed.

On the workflow

Batch by task, not topic

Write all long-form drafts in one session. Adapt all pieces to Twitter in one session. Schedule everything in one session. The cognitive mode for writing is different from editing, which is different from formatting. Staying in one mode for an extended period is faster and produces better output.

On platforms

The newsletter is where repurposing starts, not ends

Write the newsletter version first. This is the longest, deepest treatment of your core idea. It is the source document from which all other versions are derived. Your newsletter reader has given you permission. Write as though you are in conversation with them specifically.

On metrics

High consumption and low action diagnose different problems

High consumption with low action means the content is interesting but not useful. High action on low consumption means the content is valuable but not reaching enough people. Both diagnoses suggest different responses. Most creators track only consumption and wonder why their audience does not buy.
