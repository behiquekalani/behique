What it costs you

Mental overhead
A client who can reach you at any time lives in your head at all times. Chronic low-level availability eats the cognitive capacity you need for focused work.
Revenue dilution
Unchecked scope creep can cut your effective hourly rate by 30-50% over a six-month engagement. Invisible in the short term. Compounding over time.
Burned-out resentment
You build resentment toward clients who are often not doing anything wrong. They are operating within the norms you established. The poison attaches to the relationship.
Wrong client pipeline
No limits attract clients who value limitlessness. These clients are disproportionately difficult. They refer you to similar clients. The cycle perpetuates.

The language that works

The guide includes specific phrase replacements for the most common enforcement situations. Examples:

Avoid
"I don't work weekends."
Use
"Let me take a proper look at this on Monday when I can give it the attention it needs."
Avoid
"That's not what we agreed."
Use
"That falls outside our current scope. I can put together a quick change order if you want to add it."
