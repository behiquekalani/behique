The Problem

Most newsletters are built backwards.

The standard advice is to grow first and monetize later. Build trust, get to 1,000 subscribers, then think about money. This advice is responsible. It is also why 90% of newsletters die before issue 20.

📉

They optimize for growth before revenue

Six months of free content trains the audience to expect free content forever. Introducing a paid offer later feels like a betrayal to both sides.

📊

They track the wrong metric

Subscriber count is a vanity metric. A list of 300 buyers is worth more than a list of 10,000 passive readers who never click anything.

🔥

They run out of runway before compounding kicks in

Building an audience takes 12 to 24 months of consistent effort. Most people can not write for free for two years waiting for something to eventually work.
