Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On referrals

Good work is necessary but not sufficient

Most satisfied clients do not refer because they never think to do it. Not because they do not want to. Their lives are full. Your name does not surface naturally unless you stay visible, make the ask at the right moment, and make the referral easy to deliver.

On asking

The direct ask that works

"Is there anyone in your network who is dealing with the same problem you had when we started? I am looking to help two or three more businesses like yours this quarter, and a warm introduction from you would mean a lot." Specific. Direct. Framed around their network, not your need.

On network

Ten deep relationships beat a hundred shallow ones

Depth is built through consistent, genuine engagement over time. Know what the people in your referral network are working on. Check in with no agenda. Send them something useful when you find it. These small, consistent behaviors build the trust that eventually results in referrals.

On tracking

Four numbers to track monthly

Referral conversations that occurred. Referral conversations that converted. Names of referral sources. Revenue attributed to referred clients. These four numbers, tracked over six months, tell you exactly what is working and where the system needs attention.
