Part 1

Why people follow: the identity layer

People follow creators who reflect the version of themselves they want to become. Not the version they currently are, but the aspirational one. This is why instructional content alone is not enough. Teaching people how to do something is less powerful than showing them who they could be. The creator who says "here is a tactic" gets a save. The creator who says "here is how I see the world" gets a follower.

Part 2

The trust arc and why you are selling too early

Nobody arrives warm. A new follower found you because of one piece of content. They do not trust you yet. The trust arc has four stages, and most creators rush or skip stages two and three. The most common mistake: selling to people who are still in stage one or two. The sale disrupts the arc and sends them back to skeptic.

Part 3

Controversy gets engagement. It does not get conversions.

Controversy activates the sympathetic nervous system. It creates arousal, not connection. People share controversial content because it confirms something they already believe or because they want to argue with it. Conversions require trust. Trust requires safety. Controversy and psychological safety are in direct tension.

Part 4

Specificity is the most underused tool in content

Vague content does not create trust because it does not require the creator to actually know anything. "Be consistent" is generic advice anyone can give. "Publishing every Tuesday at 9am for six months resulted in three sponsored deals and a 40% increase in my list size" is specific. Only someone with direct experience can say that. Specific stories connect because the details signal truth.
