The Problem

You built something real. Selling it feels wrong.

You know your work is good. You have seen it help people. But when someone asks "how much?" something contracts. The pitch sounds hollow. The follow-up feels desperate.

This is not a character flaw. It is a training problem. You were taught sales through the lens of people who needed to push commodities on strangers who did not want to buy. That is one model. It is not the only one.

"Nobody likes being sold to. Everybody likes making good decisions. Your job is not to sell. Your job is to help someone make a good decision about whether your offer is right for them."

The solopreneur operates in a different context. You are not chasing strangers. You are helping people who already have a problem understand how your specific work solves it. The sale is downstream of the trust.

When you understand this, selling stops feeling like manipulation and starts feeling like clarity.
