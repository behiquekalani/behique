What you will actually read

On control

Process goals have a binary result

Done, or not done. That clarity is psychologically different from being 40% of the way toward a goal that has no clear path to 100%. Process goals are the only goals that give you genuine daily feedback on your execution.

On accountability

Self-generated accountability is more durable

Systems that rely on someone else to enforce your goals are fragile. Systems that you run yourself, designed to fit how you actually work, hold up over months. The goal is to stop needing external enforcement entirely.

On dead goals

A shorter list beats a longer list every time

Keeping a dead goal active is not commitment. It is accumulation. Every goal on your list that you are not actually pursuing is consuming cognitive load and generating quiet guilt. The quarterly audit clears that weight.

On execution

Goals do not come with a schedule

"Grow my email list to 1,000 subscribers" does not tell you what to do on Tuesday morning. The execution bridge translates the goal into a time block, the time block into a weekly commitment, and the commitment into completion tracking.
