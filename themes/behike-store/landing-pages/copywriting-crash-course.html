The Problem

Why most digital product copy does not convert

Most creators build great products and write copy as an afterthought. These are the three patterns that show up on almost every failing sales page.

01

Written for the writer, not the reader

The copy describes the product's features from the creator's perspective. What it includes, how it was built, why it matters to the person who made it. The reader never sees themselves in it, so they leave.

02

No clear problem diagnosis

The page jumps straight to the solution before establishing that the problem is understood. When readers do not feel seen, they do not trust. When they do not trust, they do not buy.

03

Weak calls to action that create friction

Generic CTAs like "Buy Now" and "Submit" remind the reader they are spending money. They create resistance instead of momentum. The reader hesitates. The hesitation becomes a no.

The Frameworks

Proven structures. Not guesswork.

Three of the core frameworks from the course, applied to real copy problems that every digital product creator faces.

Chapter 3

Pain-Agitate-Solution

The sequence that mirrors how every purchase decision is actually made. Name the problem, make it feel real, then introduce your product as the logical answer. In that order, every time.

Chapter 4

Features-Benefits Bridge

Every feature you list is a disguised benefit waiting to be translated. The "so what?" test forces you to find the real value the reader cares about. Most creators stop too early.

Chapter 5

CTA Anatomy

The call to action is not just a button. It is the moment your reader decides whether the copy worked. Action verb selection, placement strategy, and friction reducers. All three matter.
