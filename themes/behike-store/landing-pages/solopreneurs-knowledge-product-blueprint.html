Why most people never start

The knowledge is there.
The system is what's missing.

Three patterns keep capable people from building products from what they know.

👀

Can't see their own expertise

When you've solved a problem long enough, the solution feels obvious. That familiarity hides the value. What's obvious to you is not obvious to the person who hasn't solved it yet.

📄

Wrong format for the knowledge type

Building a course when you need an ebook. Launching a template when the knowledge requires explanation. Format mismatch kills products before they get to market.

💰

Pricing anchored to effort, not outcome

A guide that helps someone add $5,000 to their income is not worth less because it took 30 hours to write. The outcome determines the price. Most solopreneurs never make that calculation.

The format question

Six product formats.
One right one for your knowledge.

The guide covers every format, what it's best suited for, and the pricing range each format supports.

Format 01

Ebooks and Guides

$17 - $97

Fastest to build. Best for process documentation, frameworks, and reference material. The right starting point for most first products.

Format 02

Templates and Toolkits

$9 - $49 standalone

Packages your process into reusable assets. Sells well as a bundle add-on. Quality is judged by how little setup is required before it works.

Format 03

Email Courses

Free - $97

Sequenced delivery over 5-21 days. Behavioral change, multi-step processes. Creates more relationship than a static document through repeated contact.

Format 04

Online Courses

$197 - $2,000

Highest effort, highest ceiling. Best for skills requiring demonstration, community, or complexity. Build an ebook first, upgrade after you have buyers.

Format 05

Workshops and Cohorts

$47 - $5,000

Live access. Real-time feedback. Community. The highest-touch format and the best market research tool for building what buyers actually need.

Format 06

Coaching and Consulting

$800 - $5,000+

Highest income per hour. Most valuable as the top tier of a product ladder, not as a standalone acquisition model.
