The Problem

You are not lazy. You are running the wrong system.

The productivity industry is built on a single assumption: that humans have a consistent, reliable ability to direct attention on command. For most people with ADHD, that assumption is false.

The ideas are constant, flooding, often brilliant. But the execution is erratic. You hyperfocus on the wrong thing for six hours and cannot touch the right thing for six days. You build systems that work for two weeks and then abandon them completely.

The creators with ADHD who produce consistently are not the ones who finally got disciplined. They are the ones who stopped fighting their neurology and started building infrastructure that fits it.
