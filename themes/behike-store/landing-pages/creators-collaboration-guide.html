Why collaborations break down

The 6 reasons most collaborations fail

  • Misaligned expectations set before the project begins. Each party imagined a different scope, timeline, and outcome.
  • The size imbalance problem. When one creator is significantly larger, the relationship becomes asymmetrical faster than either anticipated.
  • Ghosting after launch. The collaboration happens once and both parties disappear, wasting the relational capital just built.
  • Values drift over time. Creators change. Not acknowledging a divergence directly produces quiet resentment.
  • Over-collaboration that dilutes your distinct voice and confuses your audience about what you actually stand for.
  • The favors mentality where the relationship turns into a ledger of debts and credits rather than genuine mutual interest.
