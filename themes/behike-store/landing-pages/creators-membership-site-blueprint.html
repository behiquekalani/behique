The Problem

Most membership sites close before year two.

Not because the creator ran out of content. Because they built a membership around producing instead of around a system. They set unsustainable cadences, priced on instinct, and built community by accident.

01
No transformation statement

The membership exists, but nobody, including the creator, can clearly state what it actually produces for members. Churn follows.

02
Burnout content cadence

Five pieces in launch week because the energy is high. Three months later, the energy is gone and the expectation remains. Creators abandon what they built.

03
Price set by feel

No understanding of LTV, churn math, or how price signals filter member quality. Most memberships are either too cheap to be taken seriously or too expensive without the retention mechanics to justify it.

04
Community dies in month two

No structure, no norms, no facilitation system. The community channel becomes a ghost town. Members who joined for the community cancel. The content-only members follow.
