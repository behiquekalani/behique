The Real Problem

Most businesses are more location-dependent than their owners realize.

You do not notice until you change locations. A client expects a same-day response across 8 time zones. A file upload takes two hours on a slow connection. A payment question requires you to walk into a branch. A process that ran automatically in your home office now requires you to be present to execute.

These are not scheduling problems. They are design problems. And they compound fast.

Communication

Clients who expect synchronous availability at specific times

Delivery

Work that requires specific hardware, software, or network conditions

Financial

Payment systems that require local banks or physical presence

Process

Workflows that live in your head instead of documented, portable systems
