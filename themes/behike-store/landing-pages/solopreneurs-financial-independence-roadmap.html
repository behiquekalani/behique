Inside the guide

Chapter previews

Chapter 1

Your Independence Number

The number is your annual expenses multiplied by 25. This is the 4% rule. If your annual expenses are $36,000, your independence number is $900,000. Calculate yours now. Write it down. Everything else in this guide moves you toward it.

Chapter 2

Fixing Income Before Fixing Finances

Investing $200/month while carrying $15,000 in 24% APR credit card debt is mathematically a loss. The sequence: stabilize income, build emergency fund, eliminate high-interest debt, begin investing. In that order.

Chapter 4

The Investment Strategy

A SEP-IRA allows contributions up to 25% of net self-employment income. Every dollar contributed reduces your taxable income by a dollar. Max your tax-advantaged accounts before investing in taxable accounts. The math is clear.

Chapter 5

Building Passive Income Streams

Before targeting full independence, target $500/month in passive income. Each $500/month increment reduces the pressure on active income permanently. The path: build a digital product that generates $500/month, then build the next one.
