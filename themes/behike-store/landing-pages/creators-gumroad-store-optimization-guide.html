The Problem

Publishing a product is not the same as having a product that sells.

Most Gumroad stores convert at one to two percent because the copy is a table of contents, the pricing sends the wrong signal, and there is no system to turn one-time buyers into repeat customers.

▲

Description that reads like a menu

A table of contents tells buyers what is in the product. It does not tell them why they should care or what changes after they read it. Copy that converts names the problem first, not the contents.

▮

Underpriced and under-valued

Price is a signal. A $7 product attracts a different buyer than a $27 product, and the higher-priced buyer is more likely to implement what they paid for. Most creators price for comfort, not for value.

◀

One-time buyers left on the table

Buyers who have already purchased convert at dramatically higher rates than cold traffic. Without a post-purchase sequence and a related product offer, every sale ends at the download page.
