The Real Problem

Most Freelancers Have Been Burned. None of It Was Random.

Late payments. Scope that expands without the budget moving. Clients who disappear after you deliver. These are not bad luck. They are predictable outcomes of starting work without a proper agreement in place.

The freelance contract is not a legal formality. It is the document that determines whether you get paid, how much, when, and under what conditions. It defines who owns the work, what happens when either party wants to stop, and what recourse you have when something goes wrong.

Without it, you are working on trust. And trust is not enforceable.

This is for you if...

  • You have worked without a contract and paid a price for it
  • You use informal agreements or handshake deals with clients
  • You have been ghosted after completing work
  • You have experienced scope creep without additional pay
  • You want to look professional and protect your business
  • You are starting out and want the right foundation

This is NOT for you if...

  • You have attorney-reviewed contracts already working well
  • You want templates without understanding the clauses
  • You are looking for jurisdiction-specific legal advice
