The Problem

Remote work is not the same as a remote-first life.

Most people who go remote recreate the office at home. Same reactive posture. Same surveillance anxiety. Same burned-out evenings.

Location changed. The design did not.

The real opportunity in remote work is not where you sit. It is the ability to design exactly how you spend your time, who you spend it with, and what kind of work you actually do.

Signs you are still in office mode:

You start every morning by opening Slack or email and reacting to what is most urgent.
You feel guilty stepping away from your desk during "work hours."
You attend meetings that could have been a written update.
You end the day feeling busy but uncertain what you actually completed.
