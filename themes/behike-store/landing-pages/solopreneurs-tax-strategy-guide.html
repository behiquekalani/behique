On taxes

You pay tax on profit, not revenue

A solopreneur who earns one hundred thousand in revenue but has forty thousand in legitimate business expenses owes tax on sixty thousand. The deduction game is not a side feature. It is central to how business taxation works.

On structure

The S Corp saves money at the right income level

By separating your income into salary and distribution, you pay self-employment tax only on the salary portion. At certain income levels, that difference exceeds seven thousand dollars annually. The overhead costs two to three thousand. The math is clear.

On retirement

Every retirement dollar reduces taxes this year

Retirement contributions are not just long-term planning. They are current-year tax reduction. A solopreneur contributing the maximum to a SEP-IRA can remove tens of thousands from taxable income every year. The two benefits are not separable.

On accountants

Once-a-year is not enough

The decisions that reduce your tax liability most significantly are made during the year, not after it closes. Entity elections, retirement contributions, equipment purchases timed before year-end. One meeting at filing time misses all of them.
