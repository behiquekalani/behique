The Problem

One product is not a business. It is a lottery ticket.

Most creators build a product, launch it, make a few sales, and then watch revenue go quiet. Not because the product is bad. Because there is nowhere for the buyer to go next.

Without a stack, every new sale depends on new traffic. You are running a sprint every month instead of building a machine.

01

No upgrade path

Buyers finish your product and move on. There is no logical next step, so they take their trust and their money somewhere else.

02

Inconsistent revenue

You have a good launch week, then silence. Your income depends on your posting schedule, not on a system that runs independently.

03

Wrong pricing

You priced by effort or by what others charge. Both are wrong frameworks. The result: you leave money on the table or undercut your own perceived value.

04

No internal funnel

Your products do not point to each other. Buyers who finished Tier 1 never hear about Tier 2. The funnel is broken at the source.

The Solution

A stack is an architecture. Build it deliberately.

Every tier solves a specific problem, reveals the next one, and points the buyer upward. The architecture does the work. Your job is to build it correctly, sequence it correctly, and price it for the transformation you deliver.

This guide gives you the exact framework, the correct build order, and the pricing logic at each tier so you are not guessing.

  • Tier 0: Free lead magnet

    Brings the right buyer in. Makes the Tier 1 problem visible.

  • Tier 1: Entry product ($7 to $27)

    Converts readers to buyers. Dense, specific, immediately actionable.

  • Tier 2: Mid-ticket ($47 to $197)

    Solves a cluster of problems. Where most creators make real money.

  • Tier 3: Transformation ($297 to $997)

    Complete infrastructure. Requires the trust the lower tiers built.

  • Tier 4: Recurring ($17 to $97/month)

    The layer that changes the math. Income before you open your laptop.
