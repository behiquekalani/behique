The Problem

One income stream is not a strategy. It is a bet.

Most solopreneurs know they should diversify. What they do not have is a framework for when to add each stream, which stream to add, and how to manage multiple streams without losing the focus that made the first one work.

Concentration risk

One client at 60% of revenue is not a business. It is a job with a different title. A slow month for them becomes a crisis for you.

Platform risk

Every platform has changed its terms, fees, or algorithm. Building on one platform without backup streams is building on borrowed ground.

Capacity ceiling

Service income is capped by your available hours. Digital products remove the ceiling. Most solopreneurs have neither built nor sequenced this transition.

The distraction trap

Adding the wrong stream at the wrong time splits attention and stalls both. Sequence is the difference between leverage and chaos.

The Framework

Income stacking is a sequence, not a simultaneous build.

Each stage extends what already exists. Each addition leverages the audience and infrastructure already in place.

Stage 1

One Stream, Proven

Build one income stream to stability before adding anything. Stable means: covers core expenses, runs for three consecutive months without requiring all of your attention.

Stage 2

The Capture Layer

The second stream does not require a new audience. It extracts more value from the one you have. Almost always a digital product priced between $9.99 and $49.99.

Stage 3

The Accelerator

Premium services, productized packages, or group programs. The same expertise, delivered more selectively, at better rates, with clearer scope.

Stage 4

The Multiplier

Affiliate revenue added to an existing content library. No new audience required. The content is already being created. The affiliate links make it earn on an ongoing basis.
