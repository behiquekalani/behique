The five-phase call structure

Every high-converting discovery call follows the same structure. Here is what it looks like.

  • 1

    Opening and Agenda Setting (5 min)

    Establish what the call is for, how it will run, and that neither party is obligated to proceed. This reduces the pressure that kills honest conversation.

  • 2

    Problem Exploration (20-25 min)

    Four levels of questioning: surface problem, business impact, timeline, and desired outcome. The prospect understands their problem more clearly than before the call.

  • 3

    Assessment and Fit (10 min)

    You reframe their situation with precise language. You demonstrate that you have seen this before. You determine honestly whether you can deliver.

  • 4

    The Approach (5-10 min)

    High-level explanation of your methodology and what working with you looks like. Price in the proposal, not on the call.

  • 5

    Closing the Call (5 min)

    A specific commitment to the next step before the call ends. Not "I'll be in touch." A scheduled proposal walkthrough.

The five objections you will face — handled

Every objection in high-ticket sales falls into one of five categories. The guide covers all five.

Objection 1

Your price is too high

Almost always means: the value has not been made concrete. The response creates a comparison conversation, not a price negotiation.

Objection 2

I need to think about it

The least informative objection and the most common. The right response surfaces the actual barrier hiding underneath it.

Objection 3

I'm not ready yet

Either a real logistical barrier or a comfort objection. One question distinguishes the two and tells you exactly how to respond.

Objection 4

I had a bad experience before

A trust objection that requires a specific sequence: listen fully, acknowledge genuinely, then differentiate with evidence.

Objection 5

I want other quotes

Normal and appropriate for high-ticket purchases. The response builds confidence rather than creating pressure, and frames the comparison in your favor.
