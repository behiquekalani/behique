Inside the Guide

What each section covers.

Why Plans Fail

Three architecture problems that kill most annual plans before February. Wrong altitude. Outcome-focus instead of system-focus. No review mechanism built in. This section names each one specifically.

Theme vs Goals

Goals are fragile. A theme survives disruption. "The year of distribution" is still a useful direction even after your revenue goal becomes unreachable in Q2. This section explains how to choose a theme that works as a decision filter all year.

Quarterly Rocks

Three maximum, with specific completion definitions. At the start of each week you ask: what am I doing this week that moves each rock forward? If a rock has nothing scheduled, that is a signal, not a coincidence.

Revenue Forecasting

Three numbers and an honest assessment. Current monthly revenue. Revenue per unit times estimated volume. Growth assumption tied to specific actions. Bottom-up forecasting that forces you to identify the actual drivers.

Plan Drift

Plan drift is not a failure. It is a guarantee. This section gives you the mid-year review process and the specific questions to ask when strategic drift requires updating your direction, not just your sprint plan.

Year-End Review

The lessons section is the most valuable output of any year-end review. Not vague takeaways. Specific principles derived from specific evidence, stated precisely enough to become operating rules for the next year.
