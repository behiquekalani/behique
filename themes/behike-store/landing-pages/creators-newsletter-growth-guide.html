Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On positioning

Your positioning statement before your first issue

Write one sentence: "My newsletter is for [specific person] who wants to [specific outcome] without [specific obstacle]." This sentence becomes your tagline, your subscribe page headline, and your filter for every piece of content you ever publish.

On growth

The first ten subscribers come from people who already trust you

Message them individually. Not with a mass email. Tell them you are starting a newsletter about a specific topic. Ask if they would want to be on the early list. Ten personal invites yield five to eight subscribers. That is your foundation before any system kicks in.

On writing

The subject line is a contract

Readers who feel tricked unsubscribe. "The one pricing mistake killing your conversion rate" outperforms "This week's issue: pricing." The specific version tells readers exactly what they are getting. Specificity signals that you have thought carefully about what you are delivering.

On monetization

Build the audience before you monetize it

Rushing monetization damages trust. A subscriber who has received ten issues and then gets sold to aggressively will unsubscribe. A subscriber who has received fifty excellent issues trusts you enough to pay. The newsletter is the asset. Monetization is what the asset enables.
