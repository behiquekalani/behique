The Problem

The solopreneur who does everything themselves has a permanent ceiling.

It is set by the number of hours they can work and the number of tasks they can hold in their head simultaneously. No amount of productivity optimization removes that ceiling. It only pushes it slightly higher. Outsourcing removes the ceiling. But only when it is done with a system.

"The solopreneur who cannot outsource anything is not free. They are the highest-paid employee of a business they own."

Outsourcing horror stories come from missing systems

The contractor who disappeared. The VA who misunderstood everything. These failures were not outsourcing failures. They were SOP failures. Documentation failures. Selection failures.

The true cost calculation is almost always wrong

Most solopreneurs compare contractor rates to their average hourly rate. The right comparison is to their marginal rate: what an additional hour of their highest-leverage work is worth.

Micromanagement is a systems failure, not a people failure

When you are checking everything before it ships, the problem is not the contractor. The problem is that you did not build the quality system that makes that level of oversight unnecessary.
