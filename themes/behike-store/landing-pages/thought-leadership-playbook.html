The Problem

Why most experts stay invisible

The gap between what you know and what you are known for is not a skills gap. It is a systems gap. Three structural failures keep skilled professionals off the radar.

01

They have opinions without evidence

Bold claims with nothing behind them. The audience senses the thinness. Engagement comes once. Trust never follows.

02

They have evidence without a point of view

Curators and summarizers who share other people's research without a distinct perspective. Useful, but interchangeable. No one seeks them out specifically.

03

They have both and never distribute

Sharp thinkers with rigorous research who publish occasionally and inconsistently. The association never forms. The audience never builds. The authority stays private.

The Framework

The 3 ingredients of public authority

Every thought leader who has built genuine market authority has all three. The playbook builds each one systematically.

💡

Point of View

A specific, arguable position on a topic your audience cares about. Not a category. Not a preference. A stance with a mechanism and an implication.

📊

Evidence

Data, case studies, primary research, and expert citation layered behind your claims. The habit of evidence is what separates commentary from authority.

🔥

Distribution

Consistent, platform-native publishing that builds the association between your name and your topic over a sustained time horizon. Without this, the first two are invisible.

Point of View + Evidence + Distribution = Authority
