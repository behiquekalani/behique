What you will actually read

On psychology

What buyers think when they see a low price

High-value buyers have been burned by cheap providers before. They paid the real cost later in redoing the work or replacing the provider. When they see a high price, their initial reaction is interest, not resistance. A high price says this person knows what they are worth.

On positioning

The three-part positioning formula

"I help businesses grow" has no positioning value. "I help independent professional service firms acquire their first five corporate clients through a targeted outreach system I built over three years of running it myself" has very strong positioning. Specificity on all three components builds the premium category.

On price

State your price without apology

The qualifiers most solopreneurs add before stating price signal uncertainty. State the price flatly. Then stop talking. Let the silence sit. The discomfort in that silence is what causes most solopreneurs to start negotiating with themselves before the client has said a single word.

On scaling

If everyone says yes immediately, your rate is too low

Premium pricing creates a small amount of friction. Some prospects say no. That is expected and healthy. The goal is to be at the price point where you win the clients you want at a rate that sustains a profitable, selective practice. The market will tell you where that line is.

"Premium pricing is not a tactic. It is a decision about what kind of business you are building and what kind of clients you are willing to work with."
