On the algorithm's distribution stages

When you publish a Reel, it is first shown to a small test audience, a slice of your followers plus non-followers who share behavioral overlap. Completion rate carries the most weight at this stage because it is the hardest to fake and the most indicative of content quality. If it clears the test, it enters the broader recommendation pool.

On tension in the middle section

Tension means the payoff has not arrived yet. The viewer knows something is coming and stays to receive it. Tension collapses when the middle becomes filler. Structuring the middle as a series of micro-revelations, each slightly satisfying but pointing to something larger, is the most reliable technique for holding attention past the halfway point.

On caption strategy

A viewer who watches to the end and then reads the caption has given you extended attention in a single session. That sustained engagement signals quality to the algorithm. Write the caption as if it is the second act of the content. The Reel is the first act. The transition should feel continuous, not like promotional text below the video.
