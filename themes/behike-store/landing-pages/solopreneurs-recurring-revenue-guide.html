What you will actually read

On compounding

Why recurring revenue changes everything

If you add five new monthly clients and lose two, you end the month with three more than you started. The following month, those three are still there when you add more. Your revenue floor rises with each net positive month. Project work never compounds this way. There is no accumulation.

On retainers

The conversion conversation

Converting a project client to a retainer requires one direct conversation. "I have noticed that we keep working together on similar things. Have you considered structuring this as an ongoing engagement?" Most clients who receive ongoing value have already considered asking. They assumed you preferred projects.

On productizing

Same scope, same price, same process

Custom work requires you to define the scope and negotiate the price for every client. Productized services solve this. When you offer the same thing to everyone, you can build a delivery process that improves with every repetition. You price once. You deliver efficiently. You keep the upside of your expertise.

On stability

The revenue stability threshold

Identify the minimum monthly income you need to cover all expenses without stress. The goal is to build your recurring income base to cover 70-80% of that number. When that happens, the pressure to generate income from scratch every month drops dramatically. Business decisions become clearer. You stop making choices from desperation.
