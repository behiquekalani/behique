The Problem

Three mistakes that kill YouTube channels before they start.

01

Picking a niche based on passion, not market fit

Passion keeps you going. Demand determines whether anyone shows up. Most channels fail not because the creator quit, but because nobody was searching for what they were making.

02

Waiting for the perfect setup before recording

The camera does not matter in year one. Your first 20 videos will be imperfect no matter what equipment you use. Waiting to record is not preparation. It is fear wearing a productivity mask.

03

Treating monetization as something that happens later

The 1,000-subscriber threshold is not a starting line for income. Every day you delay building affiliate links, digital products, and service offers is revenue left on the table from videos already live.
