The Real Problem

A collection of products is not a portfolio

Most creators build products in isolation. Each one is its own separate business. The result is a catalog that looks like a list, not a system. Revenue stays flat no matter how many products you add.

01

Single point of failure

One product means one traffic source, one conversion rate, one price point. Any variable that moves against you hits the whole business at once. There is no cushion.

02

No customer journey

Buyers purchase once and leave. You have no architecture to move them from a $12 entry product to a $39 core product to a $127 premium offer. Revenue per customer stays low.

03

Revenue ceiling

At $22.99 with a 2% conversion rate, you need 6,800 monthly visitors to hit $3,000. With a portfolio, the same traffic earns more because each visitor has more places to spend money with you.

The Architecture

Three tiers. Every product has a purpose.

Assign every product to a tier and the entire system comes into focus. Revenue per customer increases. The catalog earns trust instead of creating confusion.

Tier 1

Entry Products

$7 – $19

Convert strangers into buyers. Their job is not revenue. It is trust. A buyer who pays you $9 and gets $40 worth of value is warm for your $39 product. Over-deliver at this tier and everything downstream gets easier.

Tier 2

Core Products

$19 – $49

The backbone of the portfolio. Your clearest frameworks, deepest expertise, best demonstrations of what you know. These carry most of your revenue and serve as the components for your bundles. Each should address a distinct problem.

Tier 3

Premium Offers

$97 – $500+

Where the economics change completely. One premium sale equals 10-15 core sales in revenue. Buyers who reach this tier already trust your work. A portfolio without a premium tier is leaving its highest-value segment without a home.
