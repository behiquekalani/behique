The Problem

Most creators have licensable assets and no idea what to do with them.

You have built a brand. An aesthetic. A relationship with your audience. When a licensing opportunity shows up, you freeze. You have no documents, no pricing model, no structure. So you accept bad terms or walk away from money that was already yours.

Brand licensing is not a corporate concept. It is the mechanism by which you take what you have already built and turn it into repeatable income. This guide exists because most creators never learn the structure of licensing until they are already in a room where they are outmatched.
