Inside the guide

What you will actually read

On the offer

Work backwards from the deliverable

What does the client have in hand at the end of the engagement that they did not have at the start? Name it. Make it concrete. Can you hold it, read it, run it, or measure it? If yes, you have a deliverable. If the answer is vague, the scope is still too broad.

On pricing

Run the value math explicitly

A service that helps a professional services firm close two additional clients per month at $5,000 each is worth far more than a service that produces a detailed report, regardless of which took more hours. State the value math in your sales conversations. Move from cost negotiation to ROI evaluation.

On delivery

The first time you deliver, you are building the template

The second time, you are testing it. By the fifth time, you are running a system. The investment in building the template correctly is an investment in every future engagement. Deliver the first five carefully. They are not just client work. They are system construction.

On scaling

Your process documentation is your training curriculum

A new contractor should be able to read the documentation, complete a test project, and deliver to your standard without a week of onboarding calls. If that is not possible, your documentation is not complete. The investment in documentation is an investment in every future team member.
