The Problem

The problem is not that hiring failed. It is how you hired.

The freelancer disappeared. The work came back unusable. The back-and-forth ate more time than doing it yourself would have. You concluded that hiring is not worth it.

That conclusion is wrong. What was not worth it was the way you hired. A bad brief attracts low-quality applicants. A missing onboarding document creates drift you spend weeks correcting. No vetting process means no filter.

The infrastructure for building a lean, high-output one-person business with a network of freelancers is already built. This guide teaches you how to use it.

The Framework

Four questions before you hire anyone for anything.

01

Does this task recur? One-time tasks are rarely worth the overhead. Recurring tasks amortize the investment.

02

Can I systemize or eliminate it? Many solopreneurs pay freelancers to do tasks that should not exist.

03

Would a freelancer do this better than me? Either time savings or quality improvement is a legitimate reason to hire.

04

What is my hourly rate? If you can delegate a task for less than your rate, the math usually makes hiring obvious.
