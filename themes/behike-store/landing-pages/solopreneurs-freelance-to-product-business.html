The Model

The 60/40 transition.
Income protected the whole way.

The fatal mistake is cutting services too early. Without service income, you create financial pressure that ruins every product decision. You rush the build. You under-price to get sales fast. You skip validation because you cannot afford to wait.

The 60/40 model keeps 60% of your capacity in services while 40% builds the product side. Service income funds the build. No pressure. No scarcity thinking. Just strategic allocation.

The Six-Month Timeline

Month by month.
Nothing skipped.

Each month has a specific focus, a specific output, and a clear signal that tells you whether to continue, adjust, or rebuild.

Month 01
Audit and Extraction
Map your knowledge. Identify the productization path. Write the product concept. Do not build yet.
Month 02
Validation
Minimum viable landing page. Share with your exact buyer. Five purchases in two weeks means build. Zero purchases means diagnose first.
Month 03
First Build and Launch
Minimum viable product. Launch to existing network at launch price. Collect testimonials. Evaluate buyer feedback.
Month 04
Refinement and Distribution
Revise based on real feedback. Set up one distribution channel. Publish consistently. Drive traffic to the product.
Month 05
Revenue Evaluation
Read three numbers: monthly product revenue, service income stability, and actual time allocation. Consider a second product.
Month 06
Decision Point
Product revenue above 30% of expenses: move to 50/50. Below 15%: diagnose. The guide tells you exactly what each scenario requires.
