The Problem

You are using the wrong map.

Most content advice was written for consumer brands. Viral hooks, emotional storytelling, mass reach. When you apply that model to B2B, you build an audience of peers with no budget and wonder why the pipeline stays flat.

📷

Optimizing for engagement, not pipeline

Likes and impressions feel like progress. They are not. Decision-makers do not click "like." They send DMs after doing due diligence on your content history.

🎯

Generic positioning that blends in

"I help businesses with content strategy" is a category, not a position. It puts you in comparison with fifty other providers and leaves you competing on price.

📋

Educational content with no case studies

B2B buyers need evidence, not education. Tips and frameworks attract aspiring practitioners. Case studies attract buyers with budget who need proof you can do the work.

📈

Proposals that compete instead of close

If your proposal looks like everyone else's, you are in a commodity race. The proposal itself is content. Most solopreneurs write forms, not persuasive documents.
