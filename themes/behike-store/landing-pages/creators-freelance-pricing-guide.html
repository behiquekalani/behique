The real problem

Your rate is a positioning
statement. Most freelancers
don't know that.

Most pricing mistakes aren't about math. They're about the three broken frameworks freelancers default to without realizing it.

💬

Copying other people's rates

Forum research produces average rates for average positioning. Your rate isn't a median. It's a signal about what category you belong in.

📈

Pricing backward from survival

Monthly expenses divided by expected hours produces a subsistence number, not a market number. It has no relationship to what the work actually delivers.

🤔

Guessing and adjusting down

Proposing a number and lowering it when clients hesitate builds negotiation into the process before it begins. The hesitation is about communication, not price.
