Inside the Guide

What the guide actually says

On the content calendar

"A calendar that shows you missed Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday is a guilt machine, not a productivity tool. Build your calendar from the Pipeline database. Use a weekly target, not a fixed daily schedule. The weekly target survives a chaotic Tuesday. The fixed schedule does not."

On keeping the system minimal

"Every time you feel the urge to add something to the system, ask: what decision does this help me make, and how often do I need to make that decision? If the answer is unclear or infrequent, do not add it."

On idea triage

"The triage session should take fifteen minutes. If it is taking longer, you are over-thinking ideas instead of committing to them. Not every idea deserves development. The triage is where you practice saying no."

On revenue tracking

"Once a month, spend five minutes asking three questions: Which source generated the most? Which type of revenue is growing? Which source surprised you? Write the answers as a dated note. Over a year, these notes become an honest record of how your business actually developed."
