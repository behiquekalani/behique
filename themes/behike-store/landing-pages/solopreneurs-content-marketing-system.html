Why Most Content Fails

Solopreneurs burn out because they're playing the wrong game.

They use content to build an audience when they need to generate demand. Those are different objectives. Building an audience optimizes for reach. Generating demand optimizes for buyers.

A creator with 500 followers and a 20 percent email conversion rate is building a more valuable asset than one with 50,000 followers and no buyers.

  • Choosing platforms based on what their favorite creator uses, not what their buyer uses
  • Publishing attract content only, no convert or retain content
  • Creating content with no pathway to an offer or email list
  • Tracking impressions and followers instead of leads and revenue
  • Daily posting schedule that's sustainable for 6 weeks, then collapses

The 3-Pillar Strategy

Every piece of content serves one of three functions.

Most solopreneurs are at 90% attract, 10% convert, 0% retain. Rebalancing this changes revenue without changing volume.

01

Attract

Brings new people into your world. Visible, broadly useful, speaks to the problem your buyer has before they know you exist. 60% of your content calendar.

02

Convert

Moves someone from curious to committed. Case studies, offer breakdowns, testimonials with context, posts that name who your offer is for. 30% of your calendar.

03

Retain

Deepens trust with people who already follow or have already bought. Creates conditions for repeat purchases and referrals. 10% of your calendar.
