What you will actually read

On format

Why email wins on completion

Video courses demand synchronous attention. An email arrives in the inbox people already check daily, at a length people can finish in five minutes. That match between format and behavior is what produces 40 to 60 percent completion rates instead of 10.

On topic

The specificity test

Can someone who is not your ideal customer read your course title and immediately know it is not for them? If yes, your topic is specific enough. If everyone thinks it might be for them, it is too broad. Specificity is what makes someone feel the course was made for them specifically.

On writing

Opening lines that earn the read

The first sentence determines whether the subscriber reads or archives. Do not start with "In today's lesson, we are going to..." Start with the idea itself. The subscriber should be 50 words in before they realize they have committed to reading the whole lesson.

On upselling

The final lesson as a natural handoff

Buyers who complete your email course are the most qualified leads you have. They know your teaching. They trust your judgment. They have already paid once. The next sale is the easiest you will ever make, if the final lesson is written correctly.
