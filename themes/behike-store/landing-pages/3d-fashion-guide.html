The Problem

Digital fashion is a billion-dollar market. Most 3D artists have no entry point.

The pipeline is not taught anywhere coherently

Cloth simulation, pattern design, fold anatomy, retopology, texturing. Each piece lives in a different tutorial. Figuring out how they connect takes months of trial and error.

Simulation artifacts kill the work

Garments clip through the body. Folds look artificial. Topology from simulation is usable in sculpting software but broken in game engines. Most guides stop before addressing any of this.

No clear path to professional output

You can simulate a shirt. Getting it to a portfolio-quality, game-ready asset with correct UVs, baked normals, and a render that gets you hired is a different level of work with no clear guide.

Ch. 1

The Digital Fashion Pipeline

Core tools (Marvelous Designer, ZBrush, Blender or Maya), essential terminology, material properties (weft, warp, bias), and how to build a four-category reference library before touching any software.

Cloth Simulation Fundamentals

Drawing patterns, sewing panels, particle distance, first simulation, internal lines, seam folds, layered outfits, fabric presets, the 200% scale technique for higher fold detail, and common simulation problems with fixes.

Topology, Retopology, and Game-Ready Meshes

Why simulation meshes fail in game engines. Polygon budgets. Automatic, semi-automatic, and manual retopology methods. Topology flow principles, projection workflows, adding thickness, and decimation for optimization.

Texturing, Detailing, and Final Presentation

UV layout, texture blockout, leather and denim workflows, stitch maps, fabric damage, normal map baking, posing for presentation, three-point lighting, and building a portfolio that gets hired.
