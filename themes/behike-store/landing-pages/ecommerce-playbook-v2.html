Why Stores Fail

The problem is not marketing. It's not branding.
It's product.

Most ecommerce stores fail in the first 90 days because the owner picked a product before they knew it would sell. There are three failure modes that cause almost all of it.

01

Low demand products

The product exists on AliExpress. It looks cool. Nobody is searching for it. You list 50 units and get one sale in six weeks. The product was never validated against real buyer behavior.

02

High demand, wrong positioning

The product sells, but your listing title, price, or photos are wrong. Buyers scroll past you to a competitor with 300 reviews. Data would have told you the right setup before you launched.

03

High competition, no margin

The product is popular. 200 sellers have compressed the price to near cost. Your $4 profit per unit makes no business sense after fees and returns.

The System

eBay first. Shopify second. Always.

eBay gives you access to 130 million active buyers and completed listing data that shows what people actually paid, not what sellers asked. Use it as your free validation engine before you build anything.

Step 1

Research on eBay

Check completed listings for sell-through rate, price stability, and consistent volume across 30 days. This is your proof of demand.

Step 2

Test list before bulk

Source a small batch locally or dropship a test. List it, see if it sells, see at what price, see what buyers ask. Real signal before real investment.

Step 3

Move winners to Shopify

Once a product proves itself at a margin that works, it earns a spot on your Shopify store. eBay stays the testing ground.
