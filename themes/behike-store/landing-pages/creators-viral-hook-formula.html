The Problem

No one is waiting for your content. The hook decides everything.

The average person scrolling their feed is in a passive, low-resistance state of consumption, swiping through dozens of pieces of content per minute. Every piece is competing for the same scarce resource: the decision to stop.

The hook is the first three seconds. The first line. The first frame. It is the only part of your content that matters to someone who has not yet decided to consume it.

Creators who understand this build their entire workflow around the hook. They write ten hooks per piece and pick the best one. They test systematically. They build libraries that compound over time. This guide teaches exactly that.

The Five Hook Types

Every strong hook uses one of these mechanisms.

  • 01

    Contradiction

    Opens with a statement that directly contradicts a common belief in your niche. Signals immediately that you have a perspective worth hearing.

  • 02

    Specificity

    Uses unexpected precision. Specific numbers feel true and earned. The reader's brain asks how those specific numbers happened.

  • 03

    Reversal

    Takes common advice and inverts it. Acknowledges what the audience has heard before and immediately questions whether they should believe it.

  • 04

    Unexpected Connection

    Pairs two things from different domains. Connections the audience has not seen before feel like discoveries. People stay for discoveries.

  • 05

    Harsh Truth

    States something true that the audience would prefer not to acknowledge. High risk, high reward. Strongly attracts readers ready to hear hard truths.
