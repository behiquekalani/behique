Revenue

Habits

Streaks, completion rates, ADHD-adapted.

Tracks streaks without punishing gaps. Missing a day surfaces a pattern question, not a shame spiral. Completion rates show your honest average, not your best week.

Content

Calendar view, batch tracking.

Weekly posting slots filled from a content bank you build over time. Tracks consistency rate, which is the only metric that matters in the first 90 days. Not views, not followers.

Projects

Active builds, blockers, timeline.

Each product idea moves through five stages: Idea, Validated, In Production, Launched, Active. Ideas that sit in Validated for more than 30 days without moving get flagged. You either build or archive.

Morning briefing

Three priority slots per day, one per energy block. Yesterday's incomplete tasks carry forward automatically. Tasks that carry forward three times get flagged for a decision: is this actually important, or something you are avoiding?

Wins log

A running record of things that went right. Solopreneurs skip this because it feels indulgent. It is not. The identity of "someone who ships" builds from evidence, not aspiration. The wins log is the evidence.

Why a dashboard

Three reasons a dashboard beats a spreadsheet for this type of work.

A spreadsheet is a blank canvas. A dashboard is a system with memory.

01

It carries context forward automatically.

A spreadsheet starts blank every Monday. You have to remember what you did last week, reconstruct your numbers, and decide what to do next. A live dashboard remembers your previous session. You open it and pick up where you left off without any reconstruction. For ADHD brains, the cost of context-switching is high. Removing it is not a convenience, it is a structural fix.

02

It shows you what matters without requiring you to build the analysis.

Most solopreneurs who use spreadsheets spend more time formatting and maintaining the sheet than using it to make decisions. A dashboard pre-builds the views that matter: revenue trend, habit completion rate, content consistency, project pipeline. You enter your numbers. The dashboard handles the interpretation. The separation of input and analysis is why it actually gets used.

03

It connects your modules so they talk to each other.

Thirteen separate spreadsheets are thirteen separate maintenance tasks. In the OS, your Vision Builder feeds into your Content Engine. Your Financial Pulse connects to your Monetization Map. Your Energy Manager calibrates your Daily Operations. The modules are a system, not a folder of documents. That integration is where the value lives.

ADHD design

Built for variable energy and variable focus.

This is not a productivity system that assumes you are always running at capacity. It assumes you are not.

Visual, not textual.

Status indicators, progress bars, and color coding throughout. You read the state of your business in seconds, not paragraphs. The cognitive load of interpretation is minimized by design, not by accident.

Dopamine hits for completion.

Tasks completing, streaks extending, modules going from empty to filled. The OS is designed to make forward movement feel satisfying in real time, not just at the end of a quarter. Small rewards sustain behavior where delayed ones do not.

No shame for missed days.

The Energy Manager module tracks your actual energy pattern over 30 days. On low-energy days, the OS calibrates expectations downward. A day with one priority completed on a 2-energy day is not a failure. It is the system working correctly.

Questions

Is this a spreadsheet?

No. It is a browser-based web application. You open it in any browser, on any device. No install required. It is not a Notion template, not a Google Sheet, not an Airtable base. It is a purpose-built dashboard for solopreneurs.

Can I customize it?

The module inputs are flexible. You set your own goals, your own categories, your own content pillars. The structure of the 13 modules is fixed by design. Unlimited customization creates an overhead that kills usage. The OS is opinionated on purpose.

Does it require creating accounts on other platforms?

No. The OS is self-contained. You access it from your Gumroad receipt link. No third-party accounts required to use it. You can optionally connect certain modules to external data sources, but none of that is required to get value from day one.

What format is it in?

Web application. Browser-based, no install. Works on desktop and mobile. Your data is stored in your browser session. The setup guide and data templates are PDF and spreadsheet files that download from your Gumroad receipt.

Does it update automatically?

The dashboard reflects whatever you enter. It does not pull live data from external sources automatically. Revenue, habits, and content entries are manual inputs. The value is in the structure and the connections between modules, not in automation. Manual input means you actually process what you are entering, which is where the thinking happens.
