The Problem

The announcement is not a transition. Most creators get that wrong.

Most creator migrations follow the same pattern: a single announcement post, a surge of "love you, will follow you anywhere" comments, and then 70% of those commenters never actually show up on the new platform. The gap between intention and behavior is real.

Moving your audience is not hard. Losing them in the process is. This guide is the architecture for a migration that actually works — because it is designed around how behavior change happens, not how announcements work.

The Framework

Four phases. Ninety days. One complete move.

Phase 1
Days -30 to 0

Seeding

Before any public announcement, you warm your audience to the destination platform by talking about why you have been spending time there. You build desire for the move before asking anyone to make it.

Phase 2
Announcement

The Announcement

A specific structure: why you are moving, what the audience gains by following, and one frictionless action to take right now. One link. One step. No ambiguity.

Phase 3
Days 1-60

Reinforcement

80% of content effort toward the destination. Regular migration references without spam. The content cascade system keeps both platforms alive without doubling your workload.

Phase 4
Days 60-90

The Close

A two-week "final call" communication push with increasing directness. Then a deliberate wind-down of the old platform — not a ghost, but a proper close.
