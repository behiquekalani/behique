Why Most Cold Email Fails

Wrong model, wrong copy, wrong ask.

Most people treat cold email as a numbers game. Blast enough and something sticks. What actually happens: your domain gets flagged, your reputation takes damage, and the few responses you get are the wrong kind.

The other failure mode: writing so cautiously that nobody knows what you want or why they should care.

01

Generic copy

Mass-blast language signals immediately that this is not written for them. Deleted without reading past line two.

02

Too long

Four paragraphs of credentials and case studies before you have established any relevance. The reader owes you nothing yet.

03

Ask too big

Asking for a 45-minute discovery call from someone who has never heard of you is friction they will not overcome.

04

Deliverability ignored

No SPF, DKIM, or DMARC. Sending from a fresh domain. Unverified emails causing hard bounces. The spam folder catches what you wrote.
