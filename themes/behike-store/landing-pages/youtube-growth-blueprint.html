The Problem

Most people treat YouTube like social media. That is why they fail.

YouTube is a search engine with a recommendation layer on top. Chasing trends, posting daily, and hoping for virality is the wrong mental model entirely.

They optimize for views, not systems

A viral video with no pipeline is just a traffic spike. A consistent system with no viral videos builds a business.

They wait for 1,000 subscribers to monetize

AdSense at 1,000 subscribers pays for lunch. The real monetization infrastructure should be live before your first 100 subscribers.

They neglect the thumbnail-title system

CTR and retention are a unit. A great title with a bad thumbnail, or the reverse, underperforms both. Most creators never test them as a pair.

They burn out chasing frequency

One deeply considered video per week outperforms three shallow ones. The algorithm rewards watch percentage, not post count.
