The Problem

You are not making bad decisions because you lack intelligence. You are making them without a system.

Decision fatigue is real. By mid-afternoon, after dozens of small calls about what to work on, what to post, whether to reply, whether to discount, your decision-making hardware is running on degraded capacity. That is when you accept the bad client, drop the price you should hold, or commit to the pivot that costs you three months.

The solution is not to make fewer decisions. It is to build infrastructure: automatic rules for the low-stakes calls, a defined process for the medium ones, and structured frameworks for the choices that actually move the business.

This guide builds that infrastructure.
