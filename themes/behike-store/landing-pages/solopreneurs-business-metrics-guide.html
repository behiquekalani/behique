The Problem

More data equals more time not spent building.

The internet has convinced solopreneurs that tracking everything is the path to clarity. It is not. For a one-person business, every hour interpreting data is an hour not generating revenue. The real problem is not lacking data. It is tracking the wrong data.

x
Tracking gross revenue instead of net profit margin. A $20,000 month with $18,000 in expenses is worse than a $10,000 month at 65 percent margin. Gross revenue without margin context is a vanity metric.
x
Optimizing social followers instead of email subscribers. A list of 500 engaged email subscribers consistently outperforms 50,000 social followers for solo product businesses. Follower count is a media company metric.
x
Watching open rates that are no longer reliable. Apple Mail Privacy Protection has made email open rates directional at best. Solo operators using open rate as their primary email metric are making decisions on flawed data.
x
No cash runway visibility. Most solo operators do not know how many months they can operate without new revenue. This is not a bookkeeping problem. It is a decision-making problem that creates unnecessary anxiety and unnecessary risk.
x
Tracking metrics they cannot act on. If a metric cannot trigger a specific decision, it is noise. Most solopreneurs spend hours per week in analytics they never act on.

What You Will Track

Six metrics. One dashboard. Twenty minutes per month.

Every metric in this guide is selected because it either directly measures revenue, predicts future revenue, or protects against financial failure. Nothing else makes the list.

Revenue
Net Revenue

Revenue after refunds and platform fees. The actual top-line number, not the number you invoice.

Profit
Net Profit Margin

What the business actually produces after all operating expenses. Healthy solo digital businesses target 40 to 70 percent.

Conversion
Sales Page CVR

Direct traffic converts at 5 to 15 percent. Cold traffic at 0.5 to 3 percent. Below benchmark, something is wrong with problem statement, social proof, or price framing.

Email
Revenue Per Subscriber

Total email-attributed revenue divided by list size. The most important email metric most solo operators never track. Benchmark: $1 to $3 per subscriber per month.

Content
Net New Subscribers

The leading indicator of future revenue for content-driven businesses. More reliable than pageviews, follower count, or any social metric.

Financial Health
Cash Runway

Months of operating capacity without new revenue. Below 3 months: build the buffer first. Above 6 months: position to take calculated risks.
