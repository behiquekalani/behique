The Real Problem

Underpricing is not a confidence problem. It is a math problem you have not solved yet.

Most freelancers set their rates by looking at what someone else charges and picking a number slightly below it to be competitive. That is not a strategy. That is how you end up overworked at rates that do not support a real business.

The rate you charge is not a number you find. It is a number you calculate. There is specific math that determines what you must charge to stay in business, what you should charge to be profitable, and what you can charge based on the value you create. Most freelancers only know the first number vaguely and ignore the other two entirely.

The Three Numbers You Need
Minimum Viable Rate: The floor below which you are subsidizing your clients, not running a business.
Profitable Rate: What you need to charge to build real margins and handle bad months without crisis.
Value-Based Ceiling: What you can charge when you price on outcomes, not hours. Usually much higher than you think.
What Changes When You Know Your Numbers

Every negotiation gets easier when you know your floor, your target, and your ceiling.

Minimum Viable Rate

Stop taking losing trades

When you know your MVR, you can evaluate any project in seconds. Below that number, you are not running a business. That clarity is not optional.

Profitable Rate

Operate with margin, not desperation

At your Profitable Rate, slow months are manageable. You can say no to the wrong clients. That selectivity signals quality to better clients.

Value-Based Ceiling

Charge for outcomes, not hours

When your work generates $40,000 in revenue for a client, charging $1,500 for it is not humility. It is a pricing mistake. Know what your work is actually worth.
