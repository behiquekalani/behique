The Problem

Three mistakes that keep builders broke

Most personal finance advice assumes you have a system. You probably do not. These three failures are why.

01

No tracking means no visibility

Most people know roughly what their salary is. They have no idea what their actual take-home pay is, what their monthly expenses total, or where the gap goes. You cannot manage what you cannot see.

02

Random allocation creates financial chaos

Spending without a system is reactive. Money flows to whatever is urgent. Rent gets paid. Subscriptions accumulate. Investments happen when something is leftover, which is almost never.

03

No protection means one emergency destroys everything

One car repair or medical bill without an emergency fund means debt. Debt at 24% APR means the $1,500 problem becomes a $2,200 problem by the time it is paid. The absence of a protection layer is the most expensive mistake.
