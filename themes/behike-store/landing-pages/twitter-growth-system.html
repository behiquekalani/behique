The Problem

Why most Twitter advice fails

The advice is optimized for the wrong goal. Virality is a one-time event. Compounding is a system. Most guides teach you to chase the first thing and wonder why the second never arrives.

📈

Viral content decays

A viral tweet brings in followers who followed you for one specific moment. Most will never engage with your work again. Viral growth is rented, not owned.

🔄

No system, no consistency

Most people post when inspired and go quiet when they are not. Without a daily structure, the account never builds the content density that makes compounding possible.

💵

Monetization feels forced

Product announcements that look different from regular content break trust and trigger sales resistance. The method for plugging products into content naturally is learnable.

🤔

Optimization for the wrong metric

Impressions and likes are ego metrics. Follower quality, email list growth, and product revenue are business metrics. Most Twitter guides confuse the two.
