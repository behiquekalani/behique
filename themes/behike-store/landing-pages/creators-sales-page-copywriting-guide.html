The Hook

The most reliable hook formula names the exact situation your reader is in right now. Recognition creates trust faster than any claim. This section shows you how to write a first sentence that earns the second.

Problem and Agitation

Naming a problem creates mild recognition. Expanding it creates urgency. The difference between a 1% and a 4% conversion rate is often this single section, done wrong. This guide shows you how to do it right without manufacturing false emotion.

The Offer Section

Most creators list what is included and state the price. That is not an offer. A strong offer has four components: what you are getting, what that gets you, what it would cost to achieve that outcome without this product, and the actual price.

Price Anchoring

Price is always relative. This section covers ethical anchoring that makes your price feel obvious, and the difference between real urgency and manufactured scarcity that erodes trust the moment the reader notices it.
