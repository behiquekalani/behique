The Problem

Most solopreneurs are using
AI tools wrong.

Not wrong in ways that produce bad outputs. Wrong in ways that produce dependency. The distinction matters for your business long-term.

Tool collecting

Every week brings new AI tools. The instinct to try everything produces a fragile stack of subscriptions, most of which duplicate the same capabilities and none of which are used well.

Skill atrophy

Using AI to replace skills you should be developing, primarily writing and strategic judgment, builds a business that functions worse over time, not better.

Commoditization blindness

If your primary value is a skill that AI can now replicate cheaply, your revenue is at risk. Most solopreneurs are not thinking about this clearly or acting on it quickly enough.

No workflow structure

Using tools is not the same as having a workflow. Without systematization, AI tools add friction instead of removing it, and their value is inconsistent and hard to scale.

The Core Thesis

The tool is neutral.
The strategy is yours.

AI is a tool that does what you design it to do. If you design it to replace your thinking, it will. If you design it to extend your capacity while you continue developing the things that genuinely differentiate your work, it will do that instead. This guide is about building the second kind of AI-augmented business.
