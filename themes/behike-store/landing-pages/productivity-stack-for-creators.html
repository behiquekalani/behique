The Problem

GTD was not built for creative work.

Standard productivity systems fail creators. Three specific places it breaks down.

□

GTD fails for creative work

Getting Things Done was designed for knowledge workers with repeatable tasks. Creative work is nonlinear, energy-dependent, and resistant to inbox-zero thinking. Applying the wrong system makes you feel more behind, not less.

▢

Energy mismanagement kills output

Scheduling deep creative work during low-energy windows is the most common reason creators cannot stay consistent. Time management that ignores energy is just a better-organized failure.

▣

The editing loop that never ends

Perfectionism is not about standards. It is about the absence of a decision framework. Without clear publish criteria, good work dies in draft. The editing loop is a productivity problem with a systems solution.
