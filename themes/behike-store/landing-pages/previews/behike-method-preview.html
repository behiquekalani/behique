Chapter 1

The Vision (What Are You Building and Why?)

Most people skip this chapter. They want to jump straight to "how do I make money." I get it. I did the same thing. I spent months bouncing between eBay reselling, Shopify, Telegram bots, AI video content, automation services, and ebook publishing. All at the same time. All half-finished.

The problem was not that I lacked ideas. I had too many. The problem was that I had no vision pulling all of them together.

A goal is "make $5,000 a month." A vision is knowing exactly what your Tuesday morning looks like when you have already won. Goals are checkboxes. Visions are magnets. A goal tells you what to do. A vision tells you who to become.

When you have a clear vision, the daily decisions get easier. Should I take this freelance gig? Does it move me toward the vision or away from it? Should I post on Instagram today or build the product? Which one gets me closer?

Without a vision, every option looks equal. And when every option looks equal, you do nothing. Or worse, you do everything at 10%.

Here is what changed for me. I stopped asking "what should I build" and started asking "what does my life look like when this works." The answer was specific. Working from home. Supporting my family. Building things I care about. Having time for my faith and the people who matter. Running a business that does not require me to trade hours for dollars forever.

That vision did not eliminate the chaos. But it gave the chaos a direction.

Exercise 1: Write Your Tuesday

Exercise 1

Write Your Tuesday

Not Monday (too much pressure). Not Friday (too much relief). Tuesday. The most normal day of the week.

Write out your ideal Tuesday three years from now. Be specific. What time do you wake up? Where are you? What is the first thing you do? When do you work and on what? Who do you talk to? What do you eat? When do you stop working? What does the evening look like?

Do not write what sounds impressive. Write what sounds peaceful. Write the life you actually want, not the one that gets likes on Twitter.

My Tuesday (for reference):

Wake up at 7. No alarm. Coffee on the balcony. Check my systems, not my phone. The AI agents ran overnight. New sales came in. Content got posted. Nothing is on fire. I spend the morning building something new, a tool, a product, a piece of writing. Lunch with my family. Afternoon is reading, learning, going deeper on whatever has my attention. Evening is mine. Faith, rest, presence. I made money today without selling my time.

Exercise 2: Name Your Three Non-Negotiables

Exercise 2

Name Your Three Non-Negotiables

What are the three things that your business must never compromise? These are the things that, if violated, mean you are building the wrong thing. Even if it is making money.

Mine are:

  • 1. Faith. My foundation. If the business requires me to compromise my values, it is the wrong business.
  • 2. Family. The whole point. If I am too busy for the people I am doing this for, I have already failed.
  • 3. Freedom. Not "laptop lifestyle" freedom. The freedom to choose what I work on, when I work, and who I work with.

These three things are your filter. Every opportunity, every project, every partnership gets tested against this list. If it violates one, you say no. Even if the money is good. Especially if the money is good, because that is when it is hardest to say no.

Why Most People Skip This and Fail

Because it feels soft. It feels like journaling instead of building. And the internet has convinced everyone that the only thing that matters is execution.

Execution without vision is just busy work. You will build the wrong thing fast. You will optimize systems that should not exist. You will wake up six months from now having "worked hard" on something you do not actually want.

I know because I did it. I built a scraper bot, got it banned, rebuilt it, got it banned again. Was it useful? Yes. Was it part of my vision? I had not checked. I was just building to build.

Do the exercises. They take 20 minutes. They will save you months.
