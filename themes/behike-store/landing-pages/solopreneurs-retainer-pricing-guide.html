The Problem

Most retainers fail before the first invoice. The design was wrong from the start.

A freelancer finishes a project. The client says "we'd love to keep working with you." The freelancer names a number that feels right. Nobody defines scope. Nobody defines success. Nobody defines what happens when the client asks for more.

Three months later, the client treats the retainer like an all-access pass. The freelancer is doing twice the work for the same fee. The retainer dies quietly, often without either party understanding why.

This guide fixes the root cause: retainers designed without structure, priced on time instead of value, and sold to the wrong clients through the wrong conversation.

The Framework

Three types of retainers. Most solopreneurs only know one.

Type 01

Access Retainer

Sells ongoing availability. The client pays monthly for the right to reach you, ask questions, and have your perspective in their corner. No deliverables. Advisory value only.

$1,000 - $5,000/mo
Type 02

Deliverable Retainer

Sells a defined set of outputs per month. Specific volume, clear format, defined revision cycles. Both parties know what "done" looks like every month.

$1,500 - $8,000/mo
Type 03

Advisory Retainer

Sells access plus deliverables plus the value of accumulated context. The most durable retainer type. The relationship compounds because your thinking gets more calibrated over time.

$2,000 - $10,000/mo
