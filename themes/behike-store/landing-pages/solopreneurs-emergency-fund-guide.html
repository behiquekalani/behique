The Problem

The standard emergency fund advice was written for people with predictable incomes.

Save three to six months of expenses. Keep it accessible. Do not touch it unless something goes wrong. This advice works when you know, within a small margin, what your paycheck will be every two weeks.

You have clients who pay late. Projects that fall through. Months where every lead converts and months where the pipeline is silent. The W2 emergency fund model fails solopreneurs in three specific ways, and this guide corrects all three.

What You'll Walk Away With

A financial safety net that actually fits your income reality.

  • A clear method for calculating the right emergency fund size for your specific income pattern
  • A framework for where to keep the fund and why the account type matters
  • A decision system for when to use the fund versus when to protect it
  • A rebuilding protocol for after you draw on the fund
  • A percentage-based savings model that works with irregular income instead of against it
  • A healthier mental relationship with financial uncertainty
