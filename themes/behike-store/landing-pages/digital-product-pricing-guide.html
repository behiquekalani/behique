The problem
Three mistakes killing your pricing.

These are not theory. They are the exact patterns that keep digital product creators at $7-$12 when they should be at $47-$97.

🕐

The effort trap

You count hours, calculate a rate, add a margin. Buyers do not pay for your hours. They pay for what changes in their life after they use your product. These are different numbers.

🔍

The comparison trap

You find a similar product on Gumroad for $12 and price yours at $10 to compete. The $12 product is probably underpriced too. You are anchoring to a broken reference point.

🧐

The access fallacy

Pricing at $7 does not make your product accessible. It makes it ignorable. A $49 product at 3% conversion generates more revenue than a $9 product at 4%, and reaches the same number of people.
