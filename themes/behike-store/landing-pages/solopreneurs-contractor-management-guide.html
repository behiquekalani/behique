Inside the Guide

What the playbook actually says

On the decision to hire

"Track every task you do for one week. Flag anything that does not require your specific perspective, takes more than two hours per week, and you could document with a written brief. That flagged list is your contractor shortlist."

On test projects

"Always run a paid test project before committing to ongoing work. Brief the test project exactly as you would a real assignment. The brief is part of the test: can they execute on what you produce?"

On giving feedback

"Never say: it does not feel right. Always say: the intro section is too formal for our audience. Give all feedback in a single message or document, not in a stream of separate messages over several hours. Fragmented feedback creates fragmented revisions."

On building the bench

"When capacity allows and you find someone impressive, run a small test project even if you do not have ongoing work. The cost is one test project. The benefit is a vetted contractor available when you actually need one on short notice."
