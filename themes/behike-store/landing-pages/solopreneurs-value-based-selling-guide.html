The Problem

The pricing gap is not a skills gap

The difference between a solopreneur charging $500 and one charging $5,000 for similar work is almost never quality. It is almost always how they communicate and defend their value.

💸

Pricing based on competition

If your strategy is "cheaper than the expensive one," you have no strategy. You are triangulating around other people's wrong decisions.

😞

Optimizing for not hearing no

A client who said yes because you were cheap is not a client who values you. They will resist scope, push back on increases, and not refer better clients.

🔄

Selling time, not outcomes

Hourly rates tie your income to hours, which are finite. They penalize efficiency. The faster you get, the less you earn per project.
