The Problem

Most people who want to build a digital business do not know where to start. Or they start in the wrong place.

They build products before they have payment infrastructure. They grow social media before they own an email list. They skip legal setup and create expensive problems later. They do things out of order and waste months on work that has to be redone.

⚠

Wrong sequence

Building a product before you can accept payment. Growing an audience before you have something to sell. Sequence determines speed.

⚠

Skipped foundations

Legal setup, email authentication, payment processor verification. These are boring and critical. Skipping them creates problems when you have revenue to protect.

⚠

No system, only hustle

Posting without a content system. Building without a product roadmap. Hustling without direction produces exhaustion, not a business.

⚠

No milestone to aim at

Without clear 30, 60, and 90-day targets, progress is invisible. Invisible progress kills motivation. This checklist makes progress concrete.

What's Inside

Seven sections. Every major decision, in order.

Each checklist item includes a brief explanation of why it matters and what good execution looks like. Not just tasks. Understanding.

Section 1

Legal and Business Foundation

  • Business structure decision (sole prop vs LLC)
  • Business name registration
  • EIN setup and business bank account
  • Basic accounting system
  • Terms of service and privacy policy
Section 2

Domain and Hosting

  • Domain purchase and registrar selection
  • Hosting or site platform setup
  • DNS configuration and SSL
  • Professional email address
Section 3

Payment Processing

  • Payment processor selection (Stripe, Gumroad, PayPal)
  • Identity verification and banking setup
  • Tax obligations for digital sales
  • Refund policy creation
Section 4

Email List Setup

  • ESP selection (ConvertKit, Beehiiv, MailerLite)
  • Domain authentication (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
  • Welcome sequence creation
  • Lead magnet and opt-in page
  • Sending schedule and consistency plan
Section 5

First Product

  • Topic validation before building
  • Outcome statement and format selection
  • Content creation with a deadline
  • Sales page with the six-component structure
  • Product delivery testing
Section 6

First Sale and First 100 Subscribers

  • Warm network outreach list
  • 20 direct personal messages
  • Lead magnet distribution across platforms
  • Community participation strategy
  • First sales email to your list
Section 7

Content System

  • Primary platform selection
  • Content pillars definition
  • Minimum viable content calendar
  • Repurposing workflow
  • Monthly content strategy review
Roadmap

First 90 Days

  • Days 1-14: Legal and infrastructure
  • Days 15-30: Email list live
  • Days 31-60: First product launched
  • Days 61-90: Content system running
  • Day 90: Full business audit
