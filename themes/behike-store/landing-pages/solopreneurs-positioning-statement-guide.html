The Core Framework

The 3-part positioning formula

The guide walks through each element in depth. Here is the structure you will build.

Working Formula

"I help [specific who] achieve [specific outcome] through [approach that differs from alternatives]."

Who

Specific enough that the right person recognizes themselves and the wrong person clearly does not.

Outcome

The result the client actually cares about, not the mechanism you use to produce it.

Differently

What is actually true about your approach that alternatives cannot credibly claim.

Inside the Guide

On the cost of sameness

When you do not know who you specifically are for, you make conservative pricing decisions, write content for an imagined broad audience, and design offers that try to solve every problem rather than one problem very well. Positioning affects everything downstream.

On the "differently" element

This is where you have to be honest about what actually distinguishes your work. Not what sounds good. What is actually true. The differentiation should be real and defensible. Pick one primary differentiator and make it clear. Multiple differentiators at the same level of prominence dilute each other.

On positioning against alternatives

The relevant alternatives are not other solopreneurs in your space. They are: doing nothing, doing it themselves, using a different type of provider, or using a different approach entirely. These are the actual options your prospect is weighing. Positioning against them is more relevant because it meets the prospect where their thinking actually is.
