# Creator's TikTok Monetization Blueprint

**How serious creators build income from TikTok without touching the creator fund**

By Kalani Andre Gomez Padin

---

## Who This Is For

You are posting on TikTok. Maybe you have a few thousand followers, maybe you have a few hundred thousand. You have figured out that the creator fund pays a fraction of a cent per view. You have done the math and concluded it will never be a real income source.

This guide is for the creator who wants to treat TikTok as a traffic engine, not a paycheck source. The platform gives you reach. You build the system that converts that reach into money you actually control.

If you are looking for tips on going viral or dancing into brand deals, this is not that guide. This is a business document for people who think like business owners.

---

## Part One: The Core Problem With Creator Fund Thinking

### Why the platform does not owe you income

TikTok's creator fund was designed for one purpose: to keep creators producing content. It is a retention mechanism, not a revenue model. The rates are low by design. If TikTok paid meaningful money per view, it would lose leverage over creators. You would post when you want instead of posting constantly to chase views.

Understanding this changes your relationship with the platform. TikTok is not your employer. It is a distribution channel. The moment you stop expecting a paycheck from it, you start thinking clearly about what it can actually give you: attention from a specific type of person.

Your job is to figure out who that person is and what they need, then build something they will pay for.

### The fundamental math of creator fund income

The TikTok creator fund pays between $0.02 and $0.04 per 1,000 views for most accounts. This means a video that gets one million views generates between $20 and $40. That is not a typo.

A creator who consistently posts videos that hit 500,000 views might earn $200 to $400 per month from the creator fund. Most people with a full-time job earn that in a single day. The creator who got 500,000 views spent hours scripting, filming, and editing.

Now compare this to the math of directing those viewers to an owned product. If 0.5% of one million viewers visit a landing page for a $29 product, and 2% of those visitors buy, that is 100 sales. $2,900. From a single viral video. The conversion rates are not guaranteed, but the economics are fundamentally different.

The platform controls the fund. You control the funnel.

### What platform dependency actually costs you

Every creator who builds their income primarily on TikTok is exposed to a specific set of risks: algorithm shifts, policy changes, account bans, and platform shutdowns. The United States government has already attempted to ban TikTok once. That is not a hypothetical.

Creators who survived the Vine shutdown, the Instagram reach collapse, and the YouTube demonetization wave of 2017 all had one thing in common: they had built an audience on a platform they controlled, typically email. The ones who had not lost most of their income overnight.

Platform diversification is not a side project. It is the foundation of a sustainable creator business.

## Part Two: The Four Revenue Structures That Actually Work

### Structure 1: Traffic to owned digital products

This is the highest-leverage structure available to a creator. You create once, sell repeatedly, own the customer relationship, and set your own price.

The model works like this: TikTok content attracts people interested in a topic. The content positions you as someone who knows that topic deeply. The content includes a call to action pointing to a free resource or a low-priced product. People who take that action are self-selecting as your best future customers. You now have their email address or their purchase history.

The key insight is that TikTok content does not have to be a commercial. It should be genuinely useful content that happens to establish your expertise. People do not click because you told them to. They click because they trust you know what you are talking about.

Product types that work well with TikTok traffic: guides and playbooks on specific problems, templates and systems people can use immediately, video courses on technical or practical skills, and niche communities built around a shared interest or challenge.

Pricing for these products should be tested but is typically most effective in the $15 to $75 range for entry-level digital products from creators with modest audiences. The goal is low barrier to first purchase, high value for the buyer, and a clear path to a higher-priced offering.

**Implementation path:**

The first step is identifying the single question you get asked most often in comments or DMs. That question is your first product idea. Do not overthink the format. A 30-page PDF that answers that question completely is worth more to the right person than a free blog post that scratches the surface.

Create a simple landing page. Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, or a direct Stripe integration all work. You do not need a Shopify store. You need a page that explains who this is for, what they will get, and what to do next.

Put the link in your bio. Mention it in relevant videos. Track your click-through rate from the link-in-bio click to the purchase page.

### Structure 2: Affiliate integration without selling out

Affiliate income is often dismissed as low-quality revenue, but that characterization misses the actual opportunity. Bad affiliate strategy is when you promote products you do not use to an audience that does not trust you. Good affiliate strategy is the opposite.

The structure that works: you use a product or service genuinely, you show it working in your content without making the content about the product, and you have a link available for people who want to learn more. You do not have to be in an affiliate relationship to do this, but when you are, you earn a commission.

TikTok content is well-suited to this because the short format rewards specificity. A video that shows you using a specific tool to solve a specific problem is more compelling than any sponsored post. The tool is part of the content, not an interruption.

Affiliate programs worth building around: software tools in your niche that pay recurring commissions, physical products you document using in your workflow, courses and educational resources you have personally completed, and services that fit naturally into the problems your audience is solving.

The key is never promoting something you would not tell a close friend about. The moment you do, you trade long-term trust for short-term commission, and you never fully recover it.

**Commission structures to prioritize:**

Recurring software commissions are the best compounding affiliate structure. If you refer someone to a tool and earn 20-30% of their monthly subscription, that customer generates income every month as long as they stay subscribed. Twenty customers paying $100/month means $400-600/month in passive commission, growing as you refer more.

Physical product commissions are typically 3-8% but can be significant at volume. If you are a lifestyle creator who influences purchase decisions, the volume can make up for the lower percentage.

### Structure 3: Consulting and service intake via content

This structure is most relevant for creators who have professional expertise. An accountant who creates content about tax strategy, a developer who creates content about code, a designer who creates content about visual systems. The content is a portfolio and a demonstration simultaneously.

TikTok works for this because it compresses the trust-building timeline dramatically. A potential client who has watched 40 short videos of you working through real problems trusts your expertise before they ever contact you. That shortens your sales cycle and increases your close rate.

The intake process does not have to be complex. A simple form, a link to a calendar booking system, or even a DM-based intake can work at the beginning. What matters is that the content builds the case for your expertise clearly enough that people arrive pre-sold.

Pricing for this structure: your TikTok presence allows you to charge more than a freelancer who discovered on a job board. The content creates context. Clients who come through content are more familiar with your thinking, more aligned with your approach, and less likely to micromanage. Premium pricing is justified and expected.

### Structure 4: Community and membership models

This is the highest retention model because it creates recurring revenue from people who want ongoing access, not just a single deliverable. The challenge is that it requires significant trust to sell and significant engagement to maintain.

The TikTok-to-community pipeline works when your content creates a sense of shared identity or shared pursuit. Fitness creators who build around a specific methodology, business creators who focus on a narrow industry niche, and creative creators who build around a specific tool or art form all have built successful communities this way.

The best community models give members something they cannot get anywhere else: direct access to you, peer accountability, proprietary frameworks, or early access to new content. The price of membership is secondary to the value of belonging.

Platform options for community hosting: Discord for high-engagement real-time communities, Circle or Skool for structured learning communities, Patreon for ongoing patronage, and private Slack for professional communities. Each has different dynamics and suits different creator types.

## Part Three: Traffic to Owned Assets

### Building the funnel architecture

The funnel between a TikTok video and a paying customer has multiple steps, and each step has a conversion rate. Understanding the architecture lets you identify where the friction is and improve it systematically.

Step one is reach: how many people see your content. This is determined by your posting consistency, content quality, and alignment with what TikTok's algorithm rewards. It is the least controllable step but the most visible, which is why creators overinvest in it.

Step two is profile visits: what percentage of people who see your content visit your profile. This is determined by your video's call to action and your content's ability to create curiosity about who you are.

Step three is bio link clicks: what percentage of profile visitors click your bio link. This is determined by your bio clarity. Your bio should answer three questions in two lines: who you help, what you give them, and why they should click.

Step four is landing page conversion: what percentage of visitors to your landing page take your desired action, whether that is signing up for an email list or purchasing a product. This is determined by your landing page quality and offer alignment.

Step five is fulfillment and follow-through: how effectively you deliver value after someone takes action, and how effectively you make subsequent offers. This determines long-term customer value.

Most creators optimize step one and ignore steps two through five. The math is wrong. A 2x improvement in bio link click rate doubles your leads from the same traffic. A 2x improvement in landing page conversion doubles your customers. These improvements compound.

### The bio link optimization framework

Your bio has one job: turn profile visitors into people who click your link. Everything else is secondary.

The mistake most creators make is treating the bio as a description of themselves. "Content creator. Coffee lover. Helping people be their best." This tells the visitor nothing actionable and gives them no reason to click.

An effective bio makes a specific promise to a specific person. "I help new freelancers land their first $2k client. Free guide below." or "Daily tools for solo business owners. Start here." These work because they are addressed to someone specific and offer a clear next step.

The link itself matters as much as the text pointing to it. A single, focused link converts better than a link tree with six options. If you must have multiple destinations, prioritize the one that starts the highest-value customer relationship.

### Email list as the core owned asset

The email list is the most valuable asset a creator can build because it is immune to platform algorithm changes. If TikTok cuts your organic reach tomorrow, your email list still exists. Your subscribers chose to give you direct access to their inbox.

The typical exchange for email signup from TikTok traffic: a free piece of high-value content in exchange for an email address. A PDF guide, a checklist, a template, a mini-course. The free resource should solve a real problem and demonstrate that you are worth paying attention to.

Once someone is on your list, you have a fundamentally different relationship with them. You are no longer dependent on an algorithm to reach them. You send an email, they see it.

The economics of an email list: industry average open rates for engaged creator lists are 30-45%. A list of 2,000 engaged subscribers who open at 35% means 700 people see your email. If 2% of those buy a $39 product, that is 14 sales from one email. $546. A list of 10,000 generates 3x that from the same activity.

Building an email list requires a dedicated platform. Recommended options: Kit (formerly ConvertKit) for creators who want automation, Beehiiv for creators who want the newsletter distribution built in, and Mailchimp for those starting with a free tier. The platform matters less than using one consistently.

### Conversion optimization for creator landing pages

A landing page that converts well from TikTok traffic has specific characteristics because TikTok visitors arrive with different context than search traffic.

Search traffic arrives having typed a specific query. They know what they want. TikTok traffic arrives having seen a short video. Their intent is less defined. They are curious about what they just watched, and they may or may not be ready to buy.

This means your landing page needs to re-establish context quickly. The headline should speak to the same problem your video addressed. The first paragraph should expand on the insight you began in the video. The visitor should feel like they are continuing a conversation, not starting a new one.

Elements of a high-converting creator landing page:

A headline that names the problem or outcome specifically. Not "the ultimate guide to growing your business" but "how to land a $3,000 freelance client in 30 days without a portfolio."

A short paragraph that expands on the problem and why existing solutions fall short. This shows you understand the problem at a level most content does not.

Three to five bullet points describing what they get, written as outcomes rather than features. Not "20 chapters of content" but "a complete system for qualifying clients before you waste time on calls."

Social proof, if you have it. Testimonials from customers, screenshots of results, number of people who have already taken action.

A clear, single call to action. One button, one next step.

## Part Four: Product Integration Without Being Promotional

### The content-product alignment framework

The most common mistake in product integration is treating the product mention as an interruption to the content. The creator says "before I get into today's topic, I want to tell you about this product." The viewer feels the shift and checks out.

Content and product align when the product is the logical extension of the content. If your video is about how to write a cold email that gets replies, your product should be a collection of proven templates or a complete outreach system. The content creates the demand; the product satisfies it.

This alignment should be deliberate. Before you create a video, ask: "If this video works and someone watches it twice, what do they want to know next?" The answer points toward the product.

### Soft integration versus hard calls to action

There are two ways to mention your product or link in TikTok content:

Soft integration: you mention or show the product naturally as part of the content. "I put together a full system for this, the link is in my bio if you want the detailed breakdown." This works well for audiences that are already familiar with you and have built some trust.

Hard calls to action: you directly tell the viewer what to do and why. "If this video helped you, I have a 40-page guide in my bio that goes deeper on every one of these points. Click it, it is $19." This works better for viral content reaching new viewers who need a clear direction.

Test both. Track your bio link clicks and purchase conversions to see which your specific audience responds to.

### Structuring content series for funnel depth

A single video can introduce someone to your work. A series converts them.

Content series work for funnel building because they create return visitors and compound trust. A viewer who watches your three-part series on freelance pricing has invested more time, learned more from you, and is far more likely to buy than someone who watched a single video.

The series structure that works for monetization: video one addresses the problem and why it matters. Video two presents a framework or approach. Video three goes deeper on implementation and mentions the product as the resource for full execution.

You do not have to plan every video in advance for this to work. You can identify which videos are performing best and create follow-ups that serve the same audience more deeply.

## Part Five: Affiliate Mechanics for TikTok

### Platform-specific rules and workarounds

TikTok has specific rules about affiliate links and disclosures. You cannot typically include clickable affiliate links in video captions. Your options are the bio link, the TikTok shop affiliate program, and verbal mentions with bio link direction.

TikTok Shop has its own affiliate program that allows you to embed direct product links in videos. For physical products in categories TikTok supports, this is the most direct path to affiliate commission. However, TikTok Shop has higher rates on some categories and restrictions on others.

For software and digital products, the most reliable path is directing viewers to your bio link, which points to a dedicated page on your website that includes your affiliate links with proper disclosure. This also allows you to build an email list as an intermediate step, which is the higher-value outcome.

### Choosing affiliate programs strategically

The affiliate programs worth building your content around share several characteristics: high relevance to your audience, genuinely useful products you have tested yourself, reasonable commission rates, and reliable tracking.

In the digital creator and business category, software tools pay 20-40% recurring commissions on monthly subscriptions. Design tools, email platforms, project management tools, and AI software in this space consistently pay these rates. One referred customer who stays subscribed for two years is worth hundreds of dollars in cumulative commission.

In the education category, online courses in your niche often pay 30-50% commissions. If you create content about the same topics a course covers, your audience is pre-qualified for the recommendation.

Avoid affiliate programs for products you have not used and products your audience does not need. The short-term commission is not worth the long-term trust erosion.

### Disclosure requirements and credibility

Disclosure is not optional. The FTC requires clear disclosure of material connections, which includes affiliate relationships. "This contains affiliate links" or "I earn a commission if you purchase through my link" satisfies this requirement.

Beyond legal compliance, disclosure is often better for conversions than hiding the relationship. An audience that trusts you already knows you have commercial relationships. Attempting to hide it signals that you think they would stop trusting you if they knew. That is a bad signal. Transparent creators who disclose their relationships and explain why they stand behind a product often have higher conversion rates than creators who hide the affiliate nature of their recommendations.

## Part Six: Account Structure for Business vs. Content

### The case for separate accounts

Many creators who start monetizing eventually face a structural question: should you have one account that does everything, or separate accounts for different purposes?

The answer depends on your audience composition and what you are monetizing. A content account that serves entertainment-focused followers is different from a business account that serves people who follow you for expertise. Mixing the two creates confusion and dilutes the trust that drives conversions.

Consider separate accounts when:
- Your personal content and your professional content serve fundamentally different audiences
- Your personal brand has associations that might undermine your business positioning
- You are building toward a brand that will eventually be larger than your personal name
- You want to be able to sell the business account someday

Consider a single account when:
- Your personal story is central to your value proposition
- Your audience follows you specifically because of who you are, not just what you know
- Your business and personal identity are aligned and complementary

### Building a business account that converts

A business account on TikTok differs from a personal creator account in how it is structured and what it prioritizes.

The profile image should be professional and consistent with your other brand assets. If someone looks up your brand after seeing your TikTok, every touchpoint should feel like the same entity.

The bio should be focused on the benefit to the visitor, not a description of your identity. As discussed earlier, the bio is a conversion mechanism.

The content strategy should balance two goals: reaching new audiences and deepening trust with existing followers. New-reach content is broader, more problem-aware, and shareable. Depth content assumes some familiarity and goes into more specific territory.

Pinned videos are valuable real estate. Pin your highest-performing video that also drives bio link clicks, your most compelling explanation of who you help and how, and optionally a direct explanation of your product or service for new visitors.

### Content posting frequency and the conversion connection

More posting does not linearly increase conversions. The relationship is more nuanced.

High posting frequency increases reach because TikTok's algorithm rewards consistency and active accounts. But reach alone does not drive conversions. Trust drives conversions, and trust takes time and depth.

Creators who post twice a day often have lower conversion rates per view than creators who post once a day with higher production quality, because the twice-daily posting compresses the trust-building timeline in a way that feels low-effort.

The model that tends to work best for monetization: post daily or near-daily for reach, but include one or two higher-effort pieces per week that demonstrate genuine expertise and drive the product connection more explicitly.

## Part Seven: Building Off-Platform Funnels

### The complete funnel map

The TikTok-to-customer funnel, mapped end to end:

Content discovery: a new viewer finds a video through the For You Page or a search.

Profile investigation: the viewer checks your profile, reads your bio, looks at other videos.

Bio link click: the viewer decides to learn more and clicks your link.

Landing page: the viewer arrives at a page that matches the promise of the video and bio.

List capture or purchase: the viewer either subscribes to your email list for a free resource or purchases a product directly.

Nurture sequence: if they joined your email list, an automated sequence delivers value and makes offers over time.

First purchase: the viewer becomes a customer.

Repeat purchase and loyalty: a good customer experience leads to additional purchases and referrals.

Every step in this chain can be optimized independently. Most creators only work on step one.

### Setting up the infrastructure

You do not need a complex technical setup to make this work. The minimum viable infrastructure:

A link-in-bio page. This can be a simple landing page you host yourself or a service like Carrd, Framer, or even a customized Linktree. The goal is a single URL in your bio that directs people efficiently to your most important destination.

An email platform. Kit, Beehiiv, or Mailchimp. Your choice. Set up one landing page for list signup and connect it to your bio link page.

A product delivery mechanism. Gumroad or Lemon Squeezy handle digital product delivery and payment processing with no upfront cost. Connect these to your email platform so purchasers are automatically added to your list.

An analytics setup. You need to know where your traffic is coming from and what it does when it arrives. Google Analytics 4 is free and works well. TikTok's own analytics show you what content drives profile visits.

### The email sequence that converts TikTok subscribers

When someone joins your email list from TikTok, they know you from short video content. The email sequence needs to do something the videos cannot: go deep.

Email one: deliver the free resource immediately. No delay. Thank them for subscribing and tell them what to expect from you.

Email two: share one piece of genuinely useful information that was too detailed for a TikTok video. This establishes that your emails are worth reading.

Email three: tell a brief story about a problem you or someone you know faced and how the approach you teach solved it. This creates emotional connection and demonstrates results.

Email four: make a soft offer. Mention the paid product with context. Explain who it is for and what it does. Do not pressure. Give them a direct link.

Email five: answer a common question your audience asks. Deliver pure value. This reinforces that you are worth paying attention to even when you are not selling.

Email six: follow up on the offer with any additional context that might help someone decide. Testimonial, new information, limited-time framing if you have a genuine reason for one.

After this sequence, send regular emails at whatever cadence you can sustain. Weekly is standard for creator lists. The content should be similar to your TikTok topics but at greater depth.

## Part Eight: Monetization Timelines and Realistic Expectations

### When to expect first income

The timeline from starting a TikTok account to generating meaningful income varies significantly based on your niche, the quality of your content, and how efficiently you build the funnel.

Most creators who follow this playbook see their first non-fund income between months three and six. This assumes consistent posting, a product or affiliate setup in place, and some basic funnel infrastructure.

The factors that accelerate this timeline: a clear niche with a defined problem, existing expertise that allows you to create high-quality content quickly, and a product that maps directly to the problem you address in content.

The factors that slow it: a broad or unclear niche, content that is entertaining but not expertise-demonstrating, no product or funnel to direct traffic toward, and inconsistent posting.

### The $1,000 per month milestone

This is a useful first milestone because it proves the model works and gives you data to scale from.

At $29 per product, you need about 35 sales per month to reach $1,000. That is roughly one sale per day.

To generate one sale per day, you need enough daily traffic to your product page. If your landing page converts at 2%, you need 50 visitors per day. If your bio link converts at 10% of profile visitors, you need 500 profile visits per day. If 2% of video viewers visit your profile, you need 25,000 views per day.

Twenty-five thousand views per day is not a viral number. That is a consistent posting schedule with solid content. It is achievable within six months for a creator who executes well.

These numbers are illustrative, not guarantees. Conversion rates vary significantly by niche, offer quality, and audience trust. The point is that the math works at numbers that are reachable without viral scale.

### Scaling beyond the first milestone

Once the funnel is working at small scale, you have a system you can scale. The levers:

More content volume increases reach. More reach means more profile visits.

Better content quality improves engagement and sharing, increasing reach without increasing posting frequency.

Higher-converting bio and landing page extracts more value from existing traffic.

Higher-priced products or product bundles increase revenue per customer.

Email list monetization generates revenue independent of ongoing TikTok performance.

Affiliate commissions compound as your list grows and your historical content continues to generate clicks.

Each of these levers can be adjusted independently. A creator who masters all of them builds a business that is largely independent of any single platform's decisions.

## Part Nine: Platform Risk and Long-Term Strategy

### Building the asset that survives platform changes

The TikTok ban scare of 2024-2025 was a useful reminder that no social platform is permanent infrastructure. The creators who would have been most affected were those with large TikTok followings and no off-platform presence.

The creators who were least affected had already built email lists, were active on multiple platforms, and had owned products. Their income would have been temporarily disrupted but not destroyed.

Building a business that survives platform changes requires treating TikTok as a customer acquisition channel, not a business. The business is the email list, the products, the brand. TikTok is one of the inputs to that business.

This framing changes how you allocate attention. Instead of asking "how do I grow my TikTok following," you ask "how do I efficiently convert TikTok reach into owned relationships." The first question optimizes for a number that TikTok controls. The second optimizes for an asset you control.

### Cross-platform content repurposing

A TikTok video takes time to produce. Getting maximum value from that time means repurposing the content across platforms efficiently.

TikTok content repurposes well to: YouTube Shorts with minimal editing, Instagram Reels with minor adjustments, and Twitter or X video with a caption that makes it work without sound. The distribution multiplier from posting one piece of content to four platforms is significant.

The added benefit of cross-platform presence is reduced risk. If TikTok's algorithm punishes your account tomorrow, you are not starting from zero elsewhere.

### The exit strategy for the platform itself

Serious creators think about their exit strategy from platforms even while using them. What happens if TikTok becomes irrelevant, gets banned, or changes its economics in a way that makes it unprofitable to produce content for?

The answer is the same in every case: if you have built an email list, a product catalog, and a brand that stands independent of the platform, you are not exiting. You are simply reducing your investment in one distribution channel while maintaining your business.

The TikTok work you do today is worth doing precisely because it is building something that will outlast the platform.

## Summary: The Operating System

The creator who wins financially on TikTok does these things consistently:

Creates content that establishes genuine expertise on a specific problem, not content designed to go viral.

Points that content toward owned assets, primarily an email list and a product or service.

Builds and optimizes the funnel at every step, not just the content creation step.

Builds affiliate relationships strategically and discloses them transparently.

Treats TikTok as one channel in a multi-platform content system, reducing dependency over time.

Charges real prices for real products that solve real problems, and invests in the quality of those products.

Measures conversions, not just views. Views are vanity. Conversions are the business.

This is not a complicated system. The complexity is in execution: showing up consistently, improving your funnel incrementally, and treating the business decisions with the same seriousness as the content decisions.

The creator fund will never make you financially free. A well-built funnel can. The difference is in how you think about what TikTok actually is and what you are building with it.

## Appendix: Tools and Resources

### Recommended stack for TikTok monetization

**Email platform.** Kit (formerly ConvertKit) is the standard recommendation for creator email. It has visual automation, landing page builder, and product delivery in one tool. Beehiiv is excellent if you want a newsletter-first approach with built-in distribution.

**Product delivery.** Gumroad remains the simplest path to selling digital products with zero upfront cost. Lemon Squeezy has better VAT compliance for international sales. Both are adequate at early stage.

**Landing page.** Carrd is the fastest way to build a clean, minimal landing page. Framer is better if you want more design control. Your own domain and hosting gives you the most control but requires more setup.

**Link in bio.** A custom page on your own domain is best because it does not add a link tree intermediary. If you want something fast, Carrd again or a customized Linktree Pro.

**Analytics.** Google Analytics 4 is free and sufficient. TikTok Analytics in the Creator Center shows you which content drives profile visits.

**Affiliate tracking.** Impact, ShareASale, and PartnerStack are the major platforms where software companies list their affiliate programs. For Amazon products, the Amazon Associates program handles tracking.

### Key metrics to track weekly

Profile visits from content: tracked in TikTok Creator Center under Analytics, then Followers, then profile views.

Bio link click rate: your link-in-bio tool should show you click data. Divide by profile visits for your conversion rate.

Email list growth rate: new subscribers per week. Track the source if possible.

Landing page conversion rate: tracked in Google Analytics as goal completions divided by landing page sessions.

Product page conversion rate: tracked through your product platform, Gumroad or Lemon Squeezy dashboard.

Revenue per content piece: a useful metric for understanding which types of content drive the most commercial activity, not just views.

These six metrics, tracked weekly, give you a complete picture of funnel health and tell you exactly where to focus your improvement efforts.
